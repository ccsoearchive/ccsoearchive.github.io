08/15/2011 10:33 AMPosted by Dráke
Also how anyone hasn't gone clinically insane from playing AV STILL AFTER 5 YEARS! Every expansion should bring in new arena maps and new BGs. Heroics and raids go with the new story and theme of an xpac why not with PvP?


Yes, yes, YES. This shows their disregard for PvP. "Oh, but it will spread the player base too thin if there are too many maps to queue for!" It wouldn't if there was enough of each map so a person could queue for a type of battleground. The system could then randomly choose which map of that type to put players into. Of course there needs to be enough of each type; if this system was put into place now and I queued for CTF, there'd be a 50/50 chance I'd get WSG. How sad is it that after all these years the pool is seven maps deep?

As if the enormous wait between patches to fix obvious class imbalances isn't enough, or the illegitimacy of Gladiator/rank 1 titles due to how long Blizzard takes to police win-trading/MMR exploiting (when they do at all), we don't even get a change of scenery. A new CTF map could be set in a different part of the world, a different conflict between the Horde and the Alliance, but have the same exact dimensions as Warsong. It'd be awesome if it was a little different, but a simple change of scenery would be preferable to the mind-dulling we suffer when play the same map for the thousandth time. That kind of takes the wind out of the sails of the argument, "But what if we make an imbalanced map and it upsets PvP'ers?"

If I make the mistake of turning on WoW's music, I hear the same stupid song that's been around since Vanilla (and I think finally one or two new ones were added *golf clap*). I'm sure a lot of PvP'ers don't care much about music, but neither do a lot of raiders. It just shows a complete disregard that time can't be found to make unique songs for different arenas/battlegrounds.

Why is a lot of line of sight in battlegrounds still not LoS after all these years? Anything which looks like LoS in instanced PvP damn well should be LoS. It's so obvious and simple, and not fixing it shows an utter disregard for PvP. Why was Ring of Valor left in the rotation for the entirety of WotLK when 99% of the arena community despised it? It was made for arena players, was it not? The developers can be so damn stubborn sometimes. They say they won't add new maps because all PvP'ers want are a few balanced ones, but that's bullsh!t because they leave in imbalanced maps for years. Dalaran Sewers also completely screws hunters. It's only one class, but when it's THAT imbalanced it makes it a bad map.

I can probably go on for as long as the OP on this, but I'll cut it here. PvP gets very little development time and that's unfortunate because it has always had a lot of potential. This is very personal to me because the group of friends I played high-rated PvP with have all quit already, and it wasn't from burnout. I can promise you they'd still be here if Blizzard allocated the resources needed to make PvP decent. A friend of mine who is a pro Counter-Strike player (as in he lives off of the game) is interested in WoW PvP. I keep telling him not to get into it because it's terrible for competitive people. I'd love to have the company seeing as how my network of PvP buddies is drying up, but friends don't let friends get into WoW PvP.
