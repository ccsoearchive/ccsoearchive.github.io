I don't really expect an answer to this but I want to throw it out here since you're rocking this thread :)

I've often wondered if there will ever be any changes to the focus of the PvP game. Right now, and since release, it's been very grind-centric. That's all well and good, but it makes it hard to branch out. Here's a good example... in my personal opinion, War Games are about the coolest thing to be added to the game ever, but can I ever get anybody to play them? Nope... they all want to grind out their gear and since War Games reward nothing, folks would rather do things that reward honour/conquest points. As most of the people I play with are fairly casual, it's unlikely that everybody will be finished their set and want to do a War Game. It also doesn't make sense to add rewards to War Games because hey, they're just for fun.

Now, if the PvP game were a little less focused on grinding out gear, perhaps people wouldn't be so worried about doing other, fun, PvP related activities that don't reward anything. The counter argument to this has always been the rather bland (imo), "MMO games are a grind, deal with it!" but I can't help but wonder if there's an alternative. Maybe there is, maybe there isn't... but either way, I wanted to write this here in hopes that these thoughts would make their way to someone who might have some ideas. I do know that unless something changes, amazing features like War Games will go relatively unnoticed, and that makes me a sad bear.

I want to know why Blizzard has done its PvP balancing by making huge drastic changes to a class (so much so that it can completely alter how the class plays) instead of making minor adjustments to tone things down. So far in Cataclysm it has just been one gigantic sweeping change after another, patch after patch. If that's how you want to balance things then that's fine, but only if you actually do testing beforehand.

You guys have said in the past that you do not use PTR's to determine class balance in PvP. You wait until the patch goes live and see how things are. That means, to me, that you observe people after a patch, then just make giant sweeping changes, don't test them for balance on the next PTR, and then see how they do once they are live. You treat live as your test environment for PvP balance and when you decide to make a change it pushes classes from one end of the spectrum to the other.

Class balance shouldn't be a see-saw patch to patch.

Hunters come up short compared to other dps classes in a couple key ways: (1) no ability to clear debuffs (such as cloak of shadows or ice block) & (2) lack of repeatable, targetable CC.

Just a brief comment about hunters & CC. At the high-ends, CCing is practically mandatory. One might expect hunters to be the ultimate trappers but hunters must combine 2 abilities on "long" cooldowns--scatter shot & freeze trap--to "guarantee" a successful CC. Meanwhile other CC classes are free to chain CC, in a targeted fashion.

Perhaps adding insult to injury is classes like mage and frost dks out-trapping the hunters with area-wide freeze abilities.
