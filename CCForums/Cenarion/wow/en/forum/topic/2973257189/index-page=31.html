TLDR: Any thoughts on Priests in PvP, Holy as a PvP Spec or PvP healing?


Hey Zarhym,

In the past I've spent a lot of time playing my Warrior main in PvP, but recently I've come to take a liking the the healing scene. I'm not the most experienced Priest persay, however I would like to think that my experience through my time playing a Warrior in PvP can lend me some insight when it comes to playing a different aspect of WoW's meta-game.

As a precursor, let me first say I appreciate you taking the time to talk with the PvP community, you're prescence is a great reminder to the community that Bizzard does take the time to listen. Secondly in no way do I claim to know the game in it's entirety so my opinion is just that; an opinion.

A few questions I have relate mostly to healing in pvp, more specifically from a Holy Priest perspective.

I find that interrupts during PvP encounters (specifically 3v3) can be quite fruitful and being a Holy Priest taking advantage of Serindipity can be quite taxing. While I make a strong attempt to manage cooldowns, instant cast heals and juke attemps, Often I still find it quite difficult at times. Perhaps the potential review of Wind Shear will be enough (as it tends to be the worst in my experience).

Recently I have taken some time to explore the Discipline tree to better understand why it's the perfered tree in PvP (mostly in arena anyways). During my early encounters with the spec immidiately noticed the difference in Mana regen (much better) , the power of mastery (actually feels like it makes a difference) and it's nice set of utility cooldowns.

As a result, ultimately I'm wondering if you have any thoughts on Holy priests in PvP, Healing in PvP in general or the differences in the above between both Priest Healing specs.

Let me state for the record that in no way do I think the Priest class is broken!

Thanks again for taking the time to speak with the community!
