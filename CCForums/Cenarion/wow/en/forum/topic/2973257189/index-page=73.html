now that pretty much everything is coming out in this thread, I wanna give you my feeling of pvp during BETA's.

I got to be part of cata beta, and while it was a neat experience, PvP just didn't feel like it was important. And I think the dev's need to approach PvP more seriously during beta periods. They need to invite a heck of a lot more pvp'rs to test things out.

I made a few posts on the beta forums about it and the responses that the thread received were that there's not enough data to make balance decisions basically. Meaning you have to wait til the game launches to start getting the data you need.

I think this is a bad philosophy and needs to change. Could have avoided mmr bugs, could have avoided the way TB got off on a bad start. When you decide to take Bloodlust out you leave ele shaman hanging in the wind and after the game launches its too late to give meaningful replacement in a timely manner. I can't imagine playing a spec for years, and getting excited for a new expansion only to find out the relative balance of your spec has regressed heavily rather than progressed in competitive balance. Ditto for Hunters. Sheez. There were plenty of beta forum posts about the strength of dispels and interrupts. How much sense does it make for steady shot to be affected by casting slows?

You should have gotten the data you need to see that bloodlust was gonna need to be removed. You should have gotten the data you needed to see what was gonna happen with resto shamans after giving them defensive dispell. Should have gotten the data you needed to see that spriests shouldn't have started out with dispell. Should have gotten the data you need to find what hunters really need. 1/2 way through an expansion and hunters are relative worse off than when the expansion launched when they already started weak! I just don't get why that can't be sorted out during beta. Get more pvp'rs!

Why do you keep yo-yo'ing ret? They start out terrible, then feel overall very balanced @4.06, except what is keeping them balanced is too strong of heal, so its nerfed, now ret is back just a little better than where they started terrible. When an ability is too strong for a spec, but the spec is equally competitive with others and you justifiably nerf it, why is not replaced with something else when the intent isn't to nerf the spec's competitive position, just the annoying, too strong of an ability. Its so damn frustrating to wait on a major patch to get back to that competitive position. Can't this figured out during beta? You had to have known how much ret was capable of healing during beta. Did you just have a change of heart of philosophy when people whine about it? or did you just not think they would wanna heal, or just not anticipate the healing capability to be a crutch for weak utility?

When people here are asking for pvp changes, or pvp content, and you get the feeling devs are too busy creating new pve content to be concerned with the balance needs/system bugs (mmr) to make timely changes is just eventually wears your patience thin. I've lost a number of pvp friends I've had since Vanilla and tBC who have went to other games out of frustration. Consequently I've been logging in a lot less as of late. That's why I say more attention needs to be given to competitive pvp during Beta so that PvP launches with excitement rather than frustration and anxiety of when that "patch" is gonna be good or bad.
