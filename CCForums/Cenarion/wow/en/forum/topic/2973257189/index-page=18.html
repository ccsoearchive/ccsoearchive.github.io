Hey there, Zarhym.

Care to talk about why there is a gear progression in place for PvPers? From what I understand is that since PvE oriented people have to work for their gear, that PvPers should have to work for theirs too.

What I would really like to see in the PvP scene is less reliance on gear and more about everyone being on an even playing field right from the get go. So in that regard you could just hop into a battleground or arena knowing the fight will be purely based on skill, not gear.

Now, I get that raiding the current content and getting those 'phat lewts' is a good feeling. You feel a bit more accomplished on your character. However, I don't feel like this when it's in a PvP setting. Grinding out conquest points slowly for my next set of gear feels like a bit of a chore and it's just one more piece to even get me on par with the rest of the players to try and find that even playing field (once everyone is full Ruthless, etc.)

Now yes, there are a few issues that arise with this system. Like would every player just be given a set of PvP armor from the start of the season? If that were to happen, there would have to be a system in place to make sure that players couldn't just grab this gear then join PvE content with it.
