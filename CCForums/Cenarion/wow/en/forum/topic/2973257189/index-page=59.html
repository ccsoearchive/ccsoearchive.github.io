Holy paladins need, need a way to control our space better. No other class with a healing spec is doing so poorly in high end arena and it's easy to see why. We are the only ones in Cataclysm both without preemptive spells or abilities that make other healers fear getting out into the open. Lack of the former means CC chains hit us the hardest, and the latter means we're constantly forced into LoS issues as we're herded around which compounds our lack of HoTs or shields.

In addition, being the healer most reliant on RNG by far means even the few high ranked ones tend to have horrible win/loss ratios. Sadly it also leads people into believing huge instant heals are a constant for us through confirmation bias. We need a good deal more to make up for these huge flaws. Things like tremor and grounding totems would be perfect, making us the anti-CC healer to make up for no preemptive spells and little utility. It would also allow us to make use of our melee abilities with greater frequency.

Faerie Fire (Feral) lasts too long.

It creates somewhat of a hard-counter for Rogues, and to my knowledge, hard-counters, or 'rock-paper-scissors' balance is something that Blizzard is trying to avoid. Reducing the duration to ~20 seconds would have negligible affects on game-play, while reducing some of the unfair circumstances that can arise between a Rogue and Feral Druid.

For something that is so easily applied, and given the severity of its effect on Rogues, it lasts too long. The CR/CoS nerf was a sizable indirect buff to Faerie Fire, especially in the hands of a Feral Druid.

While I understand the game is not balanced 1v1, or even 2v2, if a problem that exists can be fixed without harming 3v3+, why not do it? (Not assuming you agree with my opinion, just saying in general).
