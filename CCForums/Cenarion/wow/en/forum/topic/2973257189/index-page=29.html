One of the things that made PvP rewarding in the past for me has been the slower pace of games and the ability to fake cast and control games with superior positioning. I am of course not saying this is completely dead but I can't imagine a time where I had less success beating hard counters than now, even against lower teams.

In the past classes had less options as far as gap closing, rooting, snaring and interrupting. In my opinion this made the game easier to balance and more fun and rewarding. These days it seems like every class has multiple ways to do the before mentioned things. Fake casting while being trained now is more of a risk than a reward. It seems to be better in most situations to just hope they don't interrupt you rather than interrupting yourself 3-4 times waiting for them to 'bite' on one of your casts.

What I am really asking is: Are there any plans to reduce the number of snares, interrupts, gap closers and roots currently available?

Hey Zarhym,

I'm glad at least one person cares about PVP at Blizzard.

Here are some quick points about PVP:

-Battlegrounds should have some sort of in-game reference to explain what players should do, similar to the dungeon journal. I think players would fight on the road less often if they didn't have to look up what to do on websites.
-Arathi Basin should generate points faster, the games tend to drag on forever.
-Alterac Valley could be made more enjoyable if you were forced to destroy all 4 bunkers/towers before attempting the boss. Remove the Warbringers/Marshals and put a big door at the boss room that prevents entry until all the bunkers/towers are capped.
-Strand of the Ancients is far too reliant on snares and roots. Make the demolishers immune to snares/roots but reduce their movement speed to 100% normal run speed.
-Isle of Conquest is a miserable experience for everyone involved.
-Eye of the Storm should have the large holes near the flag filled so people don't die instantly if they get knocked off by instant cast spells.
-Knockbacks should not cause fall damage. I'm fine with people being knocked out combat but when it also kills that person then its just too much.
-A full duration fear takes a melee character too far away from their target. It effectively adds the time it takes for you to run back to position as additional CC. Please consider making players effected by Fear run slower so they don't have to spend so long running back.

Class balance issues:
-Frost Mages have too much control and too much survivability. They should not have access to Improved Blink, Impact, or Blazing Speed.
-Feral Druids have too much survivability in Cat Form when you consider how powerful Bear Form is. I wish I had a "Shift into Protection Paladin Form" button.
-Protection Warriors are the only viable tank for RBGs and it is really hurting the game. Please increase the viability of Paladin and Feral tanks in RBGs (Blood DKs already have a cool niche).
-Instant Cyclone from Feral Druids is ridiculous and has to be removed.
-Warlocks have too much passive defense. Consider changing Soul Link to an activated defensive cooldown that transfers 50% of all damage taken to
-Affliction Warlock pressure on multiple targets is too high. Unstable Affliction should be limited to one target.

-Retribution has a big problem with Crusader Strike being dodged. It locks us out of Holy Power generation for 4 seconds. A dodged or parried CS should have its cooldown reset to the global cooldown.
-Retribution has too much burst (both damage and healing) during its cooldowns and really poor damage outside of them. Unlink Hammer of Wrath from Avenging Wrath for the same reason Execute was taken off Sudden Death. Retribution needs another melee attack in its toolbox to fill rotations.
-Seal of Justice is really clunky and awful to use. Can we please please PLEASE PLEASE have the old Judgement of Justice back?

Thank you for taking the time to read this!

Z Axis maps giving clear advantage to certain classes / comps.

What’s the rationale behind re implementing Ring of Valor? Personally, I don’t think dynamic line of site should be in the game. While it may add an additional layer of skill, it’s far too penalizing when playing against certain CC heavy comps and can severely reduce your team’s ability to turtle.

Casting speed reductions – in an environment where getting casts off is quite a task, why make it harder with casting speed reductions. For instance, if you manage to juke a kick, some classes (rogues and dks for instance) can lock you down solo with other abilities until kick comes off of cooldown. Surviving through this lock down only to cast a 2+ second “fast heal” then suffer spell pushback seems excessive and just puts more emphasis on instant cast spells. Also, dot classes seem to be over-penalized by casting speed reductions.

Wind Shear CD – Combined with the number of other interrupts / ways to lock out casters in 3v3, the short cooldown on wind shear can be overbearing especially if you're not running a wizard cleave.

“When stars align damage” – With the introduction of proc based enchants and proc based trinkets, stars align damage is becoming more of an issue. Classes that get a Power Torrent Proc and a DMC:Volcano proc at the same time, can put out some ridiculous damage that can completely change the direction of a match based on nothing but extremely good RNG. Is this issue at all on your radar?

One of the biggest offenders of this type of RNG gib damage seems to be mages. Damage variability of a mastery-stacked mage compared to a non mastery-stacked mage seems to be overlooked. Mages that stack mastery and use the Moonwell Chalice trinket can put out some absurd damage “when stars align” that seems out of tune with what other classes can output and what mages themselves can output on a consistent basis. Are their any changes on the board that could reduce this damage variability?

Tremor totem – Warriors and Priests seem to be countered too hard by tremor totem. Against a shaman team, Warriors that don’t run with another fear class basically use Intimidating Shout as an interrupt or can only use it effectively as a CC when the shaman is in a CC and can’t drop tremor. Do you think tremor totem on a 1 min CD is too penalizing to certain classes?
