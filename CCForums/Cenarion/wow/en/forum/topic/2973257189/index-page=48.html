Class balance will never be perfect, so there is no point in commenting on that, beyond mentioning that (imo) Blizzard wasn't entirely consistent when they hotfix nerfed DK's multiple times within days of releasing a patch, but left warrior burst damage in an arguably OP state until the next patch came around. I'm not bitter though.

The place you can improve pvp the most is with respect to map variety. Part of the fun of dungeons/raiding is exploring new areas and figuring things out on the fly, but in PvP we don't enjoy that aspect of the game. I think that current battleground templates (capture the flag, hold nodes, conquer the keep) could be easily translated onto new maps, giving players a variety of things to look at and explore but not confusing them about what they are supposed to be doing on the map. Why can't I do capture the flag in a Firelands-esque map, or defend nodes on a flying air elemental map? The possibilities are epic and fun.

I don't agree that maps have to be perfectly balanced so that neither side has a slight advantage; if starting areas are randomly selected then any given player would not experience a net gain or loss of advantage over time.

I think there are other PvP battleground paradigms that could be explored too...

BGs with flying mounts. This could be interesting because holding a node high up could give you the advantage of being able to see further.

BGs where killing npc monsters is the objective. Killing a monster rewards your team with points that are proportional to its difficulty. Of course there would be one huge boss monster, mini-bosses, and various trash mobs. This could potentially be a lot of fun because you would have to balance between killing for your team and wiping out the other team while they are occupied. Maybe it doesn't work in practice but it would be worth a try.

BGs where each team has to aid their huge godzilla-like monster in a fight to the death. All things being equal, each monster kills the other at exactly the same time, so players could fight by the monsters themselves to deal damage/heal directly, or fight for other objectives that rain down artillery or whatever on the other teams monster. Again I think this could be an incredibly epic and fun BG experience, but maybe it doesn't work.

A true gauntlet BG, where players start at opposite ends of a huge corridor filled with traps (swinging pendulums of doom, flame gouts, slimes ala patchwerk tunnel) and score a point if they can make it through the other team and into their starting area, at which point they are instantly teleported back to their start to try again.

My ultimate vision of the true endgame of WoW is one with randomly generated maps and map editors for players, for all aspects of the game, so that people can explore an endless variety of dungeons/raids/bgs. Well, maybe I'm being too optimistic in that respect.

But overall, I think Blizzard should be much more liberal and try lots of new and interesting and variable things, even if they don't work out and everyone hates them. Thanks!
