Well first let me preface this by saying that (once completing the initial 'honor grind'), I spend 3x the amount of time in world PVP than in battlegrounds. I'm about bored with random battlegrounds, and frustrated with how the RBG mmr system is setup in a way that punishes players for grouping w/ anyone that doesn't have equivalent rating. (But thats a known issue)


Any chance we'll ever see statistics or achievements added pertaining to world PVP? Both personal and guild could be interesting

such as:

- Kill 50/100/500/1000/5000 players outside of battleground or ranked matches
- Kill an enemy player in each zone in Eastern Kingdoms/Kalimdor/Outland/Northrend/Cataclysm

Its really just bragging rights, but still gives something visible to work for. Bragging rights without is well... just bragging.


Another issue is the capital cities. There's so many reasons to just stay in Orgrimmar. I can't access the AH, mailbox, trade-chat or PVP vendors if I'm anywhere else. I know it would be counterproductive in many ways, but if players were able to 'do their business' while being out-and-about, they may not feel so inclined to homestead.

I love the idea of having a zone like Tol Barad that was designed to initiate PVP, but the majority of people have no reason to go there or do the dailies.

I don't think its ground mounts vs flying mounts. I get more PVP in Hyjal where players have a reason to be on the ground, yet others can drop in from the air. Players need a reason to gather, and a reason to PVP. I know it never used to be this way, but with so many things to work toward, world pvp seems like an empty endeavor.


So then, rewards? There's a 'reward' for damn near everything, whether useful or cosmetic. Both motivate, but having neither is a recipe for...well, nothing.
Honestly just about anything could work, but I would like to see it be non-specific. I don't want to be forced to PVP in a certain zone, a certain time, or in specified groups. World kills should be the defining factor---but as for the 'reward', anything from achievements, titles, mounts, gear, conquest points, vanity gear/pets, or unique heirlooms otherwise unobtainable could do the trick.

Note that I mentioned conquest, but not honor. Honor, for many, has zero value, either useful nor cosmetic. However, for many players world PVP is the endgame--its what they work thru BGs to gear for. What if taking down an opposing factions' city leader granted 150 or 250 conquest to the raid members? What if killing an opposing player granted 1 or 2 conquest per kill? It would be much more time consuming and tedius as compared to RBGs or Arena, yet would still generate a tangible purpose to world pvp.

PvP needs to be fair. So long as there are gear imbalances and severe class imbalances this will not happen.

To address gear imbalances, simply give everyone a balanced gear set for their spec when they enter a BG.

This will do a number of things. Puts everyone on an equal footing gear wise every single match. Takes away the gear grind for pvp, allowing people to simply pvp for fun. Gives more people incentive to join in PvP simply for fun, not to grind out a new set of gear just to be competitive, just to end up grinding out another new set of gear. You can still allow people to buy gear, vanity items ect for use outside of bgs/arena.

Class imbalances is a different beast. One thing that can be done is to stop balancing for arena. If arena was around in vanilla, there are certain classes that would never ever be represented simply because they had no viable specs for small, kill before killed battles. However, all classes and most specs had a place in battlegrounds. Things simply balance out better in group and objective based pvp.

Another way of dealing with Class imbalances is with the premade gear. You will be able to balance that gear around the classes. Class X has too much survivability ? Lower it's stamina/Damage Reduction ect till it's more inline with other classes. Class Y outheals too much damage ? Lower there gears intellect/mana regen ect. And so on. This would be especially handy balancing lower level pvp without having to mess around with the core abilities.

The other thing is BG's. You simply need more maps. You don't need new/original objectives, eg SotA. Just more maps that follow the rules of the tried and true formula's of WSG/AB/AV. Battle for Gilneas is an example of what could have been so good, but IMO went so wrong. It's an interesting and beautiful map, but let down by being small and only having 3 nodes. It would have been so much better sticking the AB mode of having 5 nodes.

No need to be inventive or overly creative but you could add added perks to these maps. Eg with Battle of gilneas. If you control the lighthouse you get a bonus to your attack range. Control the mines a bonus to your stamina.

You could even make these things random at the start of each bg so they are different. You could zone in to an AB and have it be a normal AB one match, but the next match have a bonus if when you own the mill. Another match the bonus could be the mine. Make the bonuses random.

You could also make instances of existing zones as new bg's. Take the old silithus sand. An instanced BG of the silithus(possibly slightly miniaturised) map. 40 V 40. Sands randomly spawn all over. Goal is to be the first to get X number of sand back to your base.

The advantage of this is those who like to achieve the goals of a bg will go after the sands. And those who spend most of AB fighting on roads, and WSG chasing down random people in the middle of the map will still be able to do that, but it will actually benefit the group. Possibly even reduce the amount of crying and carrying on you often get in bgs :)

The possiblities are massive. But the point is, you need many more maps to keep pvp interesting. Keep them 'simple' as the original 3 are, but add varying tactical aspects to keep it different and fun.
