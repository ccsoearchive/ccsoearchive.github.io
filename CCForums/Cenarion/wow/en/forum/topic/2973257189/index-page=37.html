A few things. (And I sure hope you see this.)

1. A cast time for Hungering cold was.... just uncalled for. Too heavy handed a solution. I agree that it needed a nerf, but this was NOT the way to do it. Nerfs should recude the power of abilities, not make them completely ineffective in an entire sphere of gameplay. Maybe a smaller radius, or smaller time frozen, but we are not casters. We are melee.

2. Battlegrounds are BoA or be a wasted player slot to the team. Needs serious rebalancing. Heirlooms were created to ease leveling time, and remove having to spend hours getting the right set of gear. They are now the only option if you want to do ANYTHING with other people, not just BG and pvp, but PvE too. Perhaps a slight rollback in their stats to match what the average non-BoA would be wearing to balance it.

3. Hunters. They are overpowered in lower level brackets, complete honor farms in higher level ones. If any class deserves a little A-Team loving it is the hunter. Needs far more utility in higher levels, and reduced power (read: NOT REDUCED UTILITY.) in lower levels. Hunters should also have the melee range removed, or their melee abilities GREATLY ENHANCED if the melee zone is to stay.

4. Rogues. From personal experience (and most people on the rogue section) vanish is having a couple of issues with its stealth that needs fixing. Rogues are also suffering from a similar problem, too powerful against some targets (clothies), almost pathetic against others (armored players).

5. An interesting issue reguarding open world pvp. Like others suggested, an intrusion of lower level players into a lower leveled opposing faction zone spawns lvl 85 guards. Heavy handed is too small a word for it. I don't roll out an Abrams for a child that walked on my lawn. Guards should spawn which have the appropriate level of the person in the opposing faction zone. 85 guards also have a nasty habit of single-shotting players just passing by on a flying mount. Strength of defense should escalate as long as the intruding player is in the area (think like warning shots, then roll out your Auto-tracking AA turrets if you feel like it). Another option is to lower the shooting altitude of the guards.

I havent covered all the issues with PvP, and maybe covered a few that don't need retouching perhaps, but I hope these things get fixed.

If you do read this, thank you.

I would prefer the elite conquest gear to also be allowed in 2v2. My hunter friend and I are decent in 2v2, however not very good at 3v3, so I am a bit unhappy because I can't access the gear.


As for direct pvp changes... some things I read on the forums that, IMO, would be fun to play.

A: Revamp AV to be similar to the old 1. I saw a thread on this somewhere.(Pretty sure someone else posted this alrdy)

B: New battlegrounds. Ideas I am fond of: Underwater bg's, in the air bg's, maybe an underground bg, and I saw an idea of a 40v40 BG that involved vehicles.

C: A simple deathmatch battleground. This is for all those who just like pure pvp. Maybe each team has a certain number of reinforcements, however the numbers only go down by killing members of the opposing faction. First group to reduce their enemy's reinforcements to 0 wins.

Finally, I would suggest a BG with many mechanics similar to some raids/dungeons. Basically, it is completely in air but you cannot use any flying mounts. Instead, you use vehicles to fight. The vehicles would be: Gunships, choppers, riding on dragons, and catapults to launch you from 1 Gunship to another, including enemy 1's.

How it would work is the goal is to kill the enemy's flagship, which is a HUGE gunship with flags and npc's. There would be 2 ways to accomplish this. A: Killing all the major npc's on board, or B: doing enough dmg to it to make it crash. However, this vehicle is defended well with anti air turrets to fight smaller vehicles, and heavy artillery to fend of gunships. The flagships are stationary in the sky and are the default resurrect points of each faction.

How gunships would work: They would be npc piloted, move fairly slow, and have good anti air and decent anti enemy gunship abilities. These are destroyed like a flag ship, but these are weaker and there are 2-3 per faction. These carry catapults on board, as well as choppers, bombers, and some dragons, and can be set as rez points, like in Wintergrasp and workshops.

How choppers would work: They have a fast firing anti other flying vehicle gunfire, and bombs that do a little damage to gunships and flagships. The choppers are fairly fragile, but move quickly. These are also very good vs enemies not in vehicles.

How dragons would work: You mount them, and each color has a different ability ad type. Red dragons would be higher in defense but weaker in attack and shoot fire, which does a little dmg to all enemies and vehicles. Bronze dragons have moderate dmg and defense, and can put on a quick burst of speed for 5 or so seconds. These can drop time bombs that explode after a short time, dealing a bit of dmg to gunships and flagships. Green dragons would leech HP from enemies and also can heal allies, however are very weak vs gunships. Blue dragons can launch moderate dmg attacks to players and most flying vehicles that also slow them. They can drop a freezing bomb which would freeze all players on small area of gunship or flagship for 5 seconds. Dragons would move at a speed slightly slower than choppers.



How catapults would work: You would use them to launch onto an enemy gunship or flagship.

How bombers would work: They have fairly heavy HP, but move relatively slow. The have slow, moderate dmg shots vs other vehicles, but have good dmg vs gunships and flagships by dropping bombs on them.

How anti air turrets work: They shoot moderate dmg shots at air vehicles, and are manned by players.

How anti gunship/flagship turrets work: These are below deck, manned by players, and do decent dmg vs big enemies, and 1 hit smaller vehicles.




Well, just my 2c.
