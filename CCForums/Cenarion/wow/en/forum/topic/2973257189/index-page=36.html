Skirmishes, sir?

Skirmishes are a weird phenomenon. When designing Cataclysm, we looked at the data and saw an extremely fractional percentage of the entire playerbase actively using Skirmishes. So with all of the other changes happening, along with BG War Games, we decided not to polish up Skirmishes and keep them in.

The last time we talked to the developers about it, they mentioned the possibility of these going back in to compliment War Games. But given they saw very little evidence to suggest coding for Skirmishes paid off for players, it seems a low priority.

No. Bring them back. War games are lame and take too long to find people. Skirmishes were instant queues, and you could do them when your partners are offline.

Hi Zarhym.

First some credentials: I have reached 2000/2200+ on three different toons this season and have only ever focused on pvp since I started playing years and years ago.

I haven't read all of the comments but most are about class balance issues - and while I agree some tweaks would be welcome I would like to raise an issue that is important to all pvp-enthusiasts.

The issue I am talking about of course is, content.

The last new arena was released at the start of Wotlk, and we have been getting two new BGs per expansion for a while now. There was a post about this recently and one of the blue responses was that it was 'technically difficult to program BGs' - which is mildly insulting to our intelligence to say the least. As someone who has been playing since late vanilla, I am getting very tired of doing the same BGs over-and-over-and-over again. By comparison, if there were only two dungeons released per expansion this game would have died long ago.

Arena maps are in a worse state - last one we got was Dalaran sewers and we were even down one arena for almost a year (Ring of Valor). This makes serious pvp feel stagnant and non-progressive. There is nothing new to learn, nothing new to master.

Please consider this important issue and fellow pvp-ers, please raise it again to increase the chance it will be seen.
