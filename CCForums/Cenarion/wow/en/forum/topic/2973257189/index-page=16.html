A while back Blizzard decided to push/coax players away from 2v2 arena because of the inherent balance concerns that come with so few players on each team. I understood at the time that 3v3 was felt to be much easier to balance.

With the introduction of rated battlegrounds, I got the impression that one of the things that Blizzard really liked was the idea of balance being even more achievable with the larger teams that came in this new venue. 10v10? So much better! With that many players it should remove most of the balance concerns, right? Now each team should have no problem picking up X buff or Y crowd control.

Here's the thing though, I don't think that works. As the play in rated battlegrounds is becoming more competitive, I believe that we're going to increasingly see balance issues coming more and more to the forefront. Look at flag carriers. I keep hearing from highly rated players that a warrior is the * only* way to go. No one else even comes close. Then, how about smoke bomb? I know players who have got into the top .5% of RBG players last season by using the strategy of "Smoke bomb on the FC and blow him up." That's it. That's the whole strategy.

How does Blizzard feel about balance issues in rated battlegrounds? Are things playing out the way that you expected? Or are you finding that, regardless of the size of the team, class balance is class balance and it's a darned tough needle to thread?
