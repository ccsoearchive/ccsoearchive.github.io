Certain tank classes (Death Knight, Warrior) are far more competent in PvP than their counterparts (Paladin, Druid).

This is due to a variety of factors, like the multitude of Crowd Control abilities Warriors have (AoE Stun, single Target Stun, AoE Fear, etc...) or the sheer versatility that Death Knights have (Self-healing, Magic absorption, Death Grip, Chains of Ice).

Druids do have versatility with shapeshifting but we are at a severe disadvantage when it comes to other types of crowd control. Fear is an incredibly potent type of Crowd Control in PvP and it's rather unfair that only one of the four tanking classes happen to have it.

Are there any plans to remedy this? Please don't say "BUT DRUIDS HAVE INNERVATE AND BATTLE RES!!!" as a response. Death Knights now have a Battle Res as well and Innervate is a completely worthless spell that restores less mana than a level 85 Mana Potion.

I think a another look needs to be taken at what a class brings to PvP. Some classes such as mages/rogues were given additional utility in RoF/smokebomb while the plate dps specs gained nothing or even lost or received nerfs to utility (intercept, hungering cold, freedom removing stuns, etc). Casters outside of balance druids (not counting demo, fire, etc) are too strong and the leather wearers are dominating the melee scene. Plate melee are just too easily controlled (by casters and leather wearers) and they have become "tunnel vision" damage dealers with very low control/utility.

Double melee is not performing well right now compared to melee/caster/healer and double caster teams. It's fairly obvious who is dominating 3v3 (resto, aff, frost) which spills over to RBGs where casters are preferred. Also, some specs (ret and i believe enh as well) are still relegated to running cleave teams which is rather limiting in today's arena.
