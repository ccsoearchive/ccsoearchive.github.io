Skirmishes are a weird phenomenon. When designing Cataclysm, we looked at the data and saw an extremely fractional percentage of the entire playerbase actively using Skirmishes. So with all of the other changes happening, along with BG War Games, we decided not to polish up Skirmishes and keep them in.

The last time we talked to the developers about it, they mentioned the possibility of these going back in to compliment War Games. But given they saw very little evidence to suggest coding for Skirmishes paid off for players, it seems a low priority.

I probably fall under the category of casual PVP'er at best, but I do feel like hunters are quite underrepresented in high-end competitive PVP. If memory serves, about 3-4 hunters compromise of the "top" 200 PVP players and the class has ~2-3% arena representation in 2200+ brackets. That seems low compared to other classes, but without strong numbers and hard evidence, it's hard to determine whether the class truly is underpowered or whether the playerbase just tends to gravitate towards different-styled classes for the game.

Personally, I do feel like this class is easy to pick up but nigh-impossible to master. It seems to rely much heavier on keybinds than other classes. For example, I have around 80 keybinds total on this toon, but on my level 85 feral druid that's used for tanking I have about half that at most. Some abilities just seem like they could go for some good ol' consolidation, but once again, that's from my (possibly biased) view.

Everybody is going to see classes in a different light. Rock says nerf paper, scissors says paper is fine sort of thing. That said, how do other players - and possibly the guys over at Blizzard - currently feel about this class? Is it fine? A bit on the weak side? Possibly overtuned? I do see a lot of threads about how "terrible" hunters are, but that's likely because I visit the hunter class forums more than any other; I'm sure the situation is comparable for rogues, druids, or any other class out there.

I'm genuinely curious about how others see this class. I have seen quite a few suggestions on how to "improve" the class, many of which were great in my view, but as this is my main it is quite difficult for me to solidly maintain objectivity. (:
