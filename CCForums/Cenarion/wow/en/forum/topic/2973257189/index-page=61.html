When finally in a good spot -- second half of S9, for example -- Ret always gets nerfed because some mechanic is too powerful. I agree Word of Glory without a cooldown was OP, but not any more than Frost Mage control and it didn't actually make Ret overpowered. Back around the start of S8, Ret was already fairly poor in high-end arena and was nerfed because it was overpowered in regular battlegrounds!

I agreed with the nerfs in both instances. Unfortunately this doesn't happen when certain privileged specs are overpowered. For example, Frost Mage is the best non-rated BG spec, required in every RBG group, and strong in high-end arena. What Frost needs is a rework to some of its mechanics to make it strong yet not OP across the board. What Frost got is a band-aid slap on the wrist (Deep Freeze/Ring of Frost now share DR). What Frost would get if it was treated like Ret is a 10 sec cooldown on Poly.

"But Ret can go Holy! Frost is the only good mage spec for PvP!" I won't even go into how Ret and Holy fill completely separate roles. The bottom line is I'm tired of being treated like a second class citizen. That may sound dramatic, but it's the truth. If Ret was in the Affliction/Resto Shaman boat, it would have been nerfed months ago. Ret was nerfed for an overpowered mechanic which, while silly and not ideal, didn't actually make it overpowered. In fact, WoG made Ret work. It didn't make sense to give it a cooldown without compensation elsewhere. That's what I call half-assed class balance.

Be nice for the PvP casuals to be able to obtain more mounts and titles. Being a casual is actually tough work and doesn't provide many incentives towards playing once you obtain your "welfare" gear.

In my case for instance - I consider myself a casual pvp player. This is because I personally choose to level up characters and do regular BG's with them. The problem is - I can level a guy 1-85 in a week and then completely gear them out in honor gear the next week. In two weeks I can be fully set to doing arena for points, which turns out to being a slow grind incentive, unless I can some how find partners with the same goal and playstyle as me to grind upwards of 2200+ in 3's or 5's for the better gear.

Finding these types of players can be very hard. Mostly because many of them are already established within guilds and have a set of friends. And secondly, having to find those people with a relative fresh toon makes it even harder - no one wants to play with a player that might only be 1800 material, when they themselves are 2000+.

What I would really enjoy seeing in the realm of the PvP community is more casual player mounts and titles.

When was the last time a casual had the option of obtaining a new mount? - All pvp mounts for the casual players haven't been updated since WoW 1.0... And then to top it off, the only real titles available are at 100k and 250k player kills? <- This is not very casual friendly, even more so when this can take months to years to achieve...

I don't quite understand why - it seems - that the same people are constantly obtaining the Gladiator titles and mounts AND these people are also taking the rated BG titles. It seems that the top of the top are in a constant competition with each other and the 90% of casuals are getting the left over scraps.

It almost is as if the only incentive is to 'hope' for the chance to find like minded players on the same server and be able to compete at the top 10% Level with a small chance of taking in a new title or mount.

I think it would be kind of a cool idea to implement a Kill to Death ratio title - Where if you have say a K:D ratio of 2. With over a 10k kills, then you have the obtain to wear a title. Or if you have over 100Wins in a certain BG you get a title. Or if you heal/dmg more than say 4million in a single BG you can obtain a title. Or if you say go 100 kills and 0 Deaths you get a title.

I think instead of trying to always balance things out. Make the game more desirable through aesthetics to keep those incentives going to keep actually playing and not complaining.
