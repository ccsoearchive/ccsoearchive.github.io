Tol Barad v2.0,
by Helsbane

As the battle is set up to be a siege of a fortified position, the defenders should have some form of advantage, the “high ground” as it were. Tol Barad is essentially a converted prison for the purposes of the battle, making for a tough defensive position for the attacking force to crack. In taking it’s prison origin into account, my version has limited access points, after all, nothing was supposed to get out that shouldn’t.

Additions to the zone (made to be friendly to upgrade using existing in-game models)…
*Gates in IoC fashion on all outlying forts and the keep itself.
*Guns mounted on the upper walls for defensive use.
*Various types of siege vehicles to replace the giant useless mini van currently in play.
*A gunship to act as a secondary starting location on the southern side of the island.
*Bombs as seen in IoC or SotA.

Offense…
The offensive team has two starting locations, the bridge at the north end of the zone (already in place) and a new gunship on station at the southern end of the island. This gunship was rushed into service and has no functioning cannons, but as it is out of range and serves only as a starting point, it doesn’t need them. Players drop into the zone via parachute. This gives attacking forces two areas to work from initially and causes the defenders to work on two fronts to try to intercept them at this stage.

The towers now unlock the siege engines, now parked at the base of said towers. New engines spawn after a given vehicle is destroyed, limiting the number and preventing a swarm of tanks. Type of vehicle is random for more flavor. The tower node is at the top of the structure, and is captured as in AV or AB, cogwheel style. There are also a limited number of bombs at these towers used for assaulting the main keep or the outlying forts.

The Outlying forts (WV, ICG, and SW) become important aspects in taking the main fortress. Each fort provides the main fort with a 25% reduction in siege damage. Initial damage to the main keep is a mere 25% of max, but increases by 25% per fortress captured till the attackers are at 100%. This gives both forces reason to assault or defend these areas. Once taken, these areas are fixed. No back capping the nodes. Given time constraints, this ability could lead to a hours long battle and I believe it should be avoided. Larger supplies of bombs are located in these outlying forts, giving the attackers a better chance to assail the main keep.

Taking the main keep is accomplished WG or SotA style. Get past the defenders and touch the goal

Defense…
Defenders have multiple ways and opportunities to stop or slow the invaders. Simply keeping them from taking the outlying forts, destroying the siege engines, etc. for long enough shall win the day, but feel like a true siege should. Outlying forts have defenders portals as in SotA, IoC, and WG allowing defending units access. The defensive team spawns ONLY at the main keep, allowing them to hold it rather effectively, but they will have to run to the outlying forts to take up positions there. This lasts till all three of the outlying forts have fallen, then defenders start spawning at the outlying graveyards and must use the defenders portals or whatever breach is present.

Thank you for your time,
Helsbane
