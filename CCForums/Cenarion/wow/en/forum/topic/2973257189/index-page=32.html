Overall, I like the way PvP "feels" in Cataclysm. I appreciate that combat was slowed down a bit and that CC is less crushing. I honestly have very few balance concerns -- but I rarely play arenas, so here I'll defer to those who do. (For the record, I play a Resto Druid these days.)

My major complaint with WoW PvP is that there is currently no system that feels suitably "massively multiplayer."

One possibility would be something along the lines of Dark Age of Camelot's RvR, or the upcoming Guild Wars 2's WvWvW. It seems like you took a stab at this with Wintergrasp, and again with Tol Barad (a slight step backwards, in my opinion: the map is detached from the rest of the game world, and doesn't provide the satisfaction of actually sieging a fortress). The key element you're missing is that the map should always be contested.

A second possibility would be to do something epic using the existing instanced battleground system. The obvious starting point is to revamp Alterac Valley (http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2952885503), and make changes to the queue system to reflect "normal" vs. "epic" battlegrounds so players know what they're getting into.

A third possibility is to truly embrace open-world PvP within certain areas. Throw players of both factions into the same area and give them conflicting goals. Spice things up with simple mechanics like kill broadcasting (http://forums.riftgame.com/rift-general-discussions/pvp-warfronts/163442-suggestion-zone-wide-pk-messaging.html) and player bounties.

Now, to come back down to earth, I have one very minor request for WoW PvP: please turn the "first win of the day" bonus into a "first seven wins of the week" bonus, like you did with PvE. :)

I thoroughly and unreservedly support Foury's efforts regarding a restoration of the "epic" style Alterac Valley. I think that it is a smart idea to have large-scale battlegrounds with significant PVE elements (AV, IoC, SotA) removed from the random BG, and to leave skirmish-scale battlegrounds (AB, WSG, EotS, etc) as the "random". I think that there should be a distinction between these two formats.

I am very, very disappointed to hear that "it is doubtful that AV 1.0 will be resurrected". Like many others, I miss the "epic" nature of those battles. I acknowledge that the time investment and format are not for everyone. However, I believe that the map and mechanics are largely present and barring some tweaks, action could be taken.

I would like to see additional themed arenas built out of the Dark Iron Stadium, Dire Maul, the Orgrimmar Arena, Amptheatre of Anguish (etc), and the Gurubashi Arena. The bottom line is that you have a variety of lore Arenas scattered around the world and I'd like to see more of them used.

I'd like to see a whisper of redevelopment toward the Gurubashi Catacombs. The Catacombs could be added to the existing world map as a level 85-incentive area under the Gurubashi Arena or sorted as some new Arena map, I believe that there are options.

I would like to see Wintergrasp adjusted as 85 content. I'm not certain that the Vault would need to be adjusted with it, but another large, scheduled PVP match, server-wide seems promising. Being able to see full-scale fights in both Tol Barad and Wintergrasp seems reasonable, and, frankly, at each expansion, each time the level cap is raised, these zones should be retuned, not just left to collect dust as people level past them. I think these large-scale zones are good for the individual server communities - you're exposed to more people on your server, it's not like a RDF or BG where you never see anyone again. Rivalries and coordination have time to develop. Leaders - on both sides - can evolve. And be targeted.

I personally do not enjoy the small-scale battlegrounds, arenas, and similar fights. I like the push and pull of BIG engagements. I sincerely miss the early AV - I liked seeing elites and needing to work with others to advance. I don't really miss Korrak and the IBS.
