The major issue I've had with PvP is player skill and group composition. I don't know the specifics of how random battleground matchmaking works; however, I would imagine it's not as effective as most would like. Why am I put in groups with 5 rogues, 4 dk's, and I'm left as the only healer? Between this and general baddies, random bgs just aren't fun anymore.

I'm not against being in groups with people who are bad, after all, how else would they learn how to get better? But getting crushed 100-1600 in EotS several times a day just isn't fun.

It's worth noting that I'm not trying to say I just want to win more, since we all know that isn't fair nor practical. It's the people who are so against, for whatever reason, following strategic directions in order to win a match. The people who need experience and the help of a WoW veteran most in these matches are the ones cursing out and ignoring the people trying to help them. People seem to just do randoms for the honor, not the practice.

To end, I guess I really don't know how to fix this. Only suggestion I can think of is making the voice chat feature available for random dungeons and battlegrounds (and consequentially make 11.4 million people deaf). Many people use Vent or Skype or whatever, and have headsets ready for use. Just saying what my qualms are at this moment.

PS: Also, while War Games are nice, they're freaking annoying to set up. I, for one, have never seen a group looking to do war games in trade or anything like that. We should be able to do random battleground premades again. What's at stake after all, some honor? It's not like you can cap conquest for the week from randoms. My guild isn't large enough to pit 20 people against each other so most of our "what works for us" experience is done in actual rated battlegrounds.
