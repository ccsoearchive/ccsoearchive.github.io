Also, a brief summary of some general concerns raised by the PVP community:

Feral - damage is too high; instant cyclone every 5-10 secs is overkill; survival is too good

Mage - burst damage is too high (though evened out not as much); too many ways of applying inevitable ccs (nova -> sheep / counter spell -> sheep / deep freeze -> sheep / ring of frost should be taken off cold snap)

Warlock - dots application and fear application needs a revisit

Resto Druid - out of tree form very hard to live through pressure when focused

Rogue - burst damage is too high; survival is too good (even after cloak / combat readiness nerf)

DK - survival against melee cleaves is horrible; damage as frost is too high; mobility as frost is too low; pet / garg for unholy is very cluncky

Resto Shaman - healing efficiency is too high

Balance Druids:

Some Ideas:
1. A PVP talent that would increase our healing (not make it resto-level, but make it decision-worthy). I think it could be done where you could have a balance druid that could trade off some damage in exchange for being better at healing in a pinch.
2. An instant button for significant damage mitigation. (or a buff to barkskin) Currently, if you get death-gripped by a DK with AMS up, it's really tough recovering from that.
3. The option to use Solar Beam as an AOE or as a single target application silence. It would also be good if there was an easier way to keep people in solar beam for longer than it takes tp just walk out of it. Casting roots before beaming is an option, but roots doesn't seem to keep people in it for more than a second or two.
4. Can we do something fun with treants again? Like a tree stun or the option to explode them for damage/sacrifice them for hp or a buff?
5. An instant CC. We have two easily interrupt-able CC's. The old Glyph of Roots that made roots instant, but gave you a cooldown on it was fine. You could something similar for Cyclone.
6. Buff Typhoon. Currently, there are multiple classes whom, when typhoon-ed off you in an attempt to get away, can just jump right back on you. It either needs more damage, a short stun, or a farther knockback.

Anyway, just some ideas. Currently, Balance in arena is very difficult to play successfully outside of a couple of gimmicky comps.
