I honestly can't answer a few of those because I don't actually know the answers myself (Warrior stat weights are still kinda up in the air) but I'll do my best.

1. Yes.
2. I honestly don't know whether or not hit is better than parry (I'd say it's probably better than dodge at this point), so it's kinda up to you. Are you getting annoyed with your rage generators not landing? Get more hit/exp.

1. Honestly, you're probably fine with whatever you think looks best and what is nicest for your situation. For example, in the first tier, do you think you'll need a decentish stun, or charging twice rapidly, or charging more often? In the second one, do you want an emergency, automatic low health heal, or a healing CD that is either a heal over time, or an instant heal.
2. Again, pick which ones you like. I'd suggest Unending Rage, for the increased rage pool, and also recommend steering clear of the Glyph of Shield Wall (Usually, with cooldowns, having them available more often is much more valuable than having them reduce slightly more) and Hoarse Voice (Which just seems like a loss to me in any situation). Beyond that, up to you.
3. Honestly, I'm not really sure. Go for strength and parry if you want your passive mitigation to be stronger, and for hit/exp/mastery if you want your active mitigation to be stronger.
4. We've got basically the same priority system we used for most of Cata and Wrath really. Use Thunder Clap as often as you need to to keep Weakened Blows up, then Shield Slam whenever it's up (Especially if Sword and Board procs), and if not Shield Slam, Revenge. If you don't have anything to hit, Devastate. As for Shield Block vs. Barrier, in general, you want to be using Shield Block for your steady, average boss damage, and Barrier for the big hits that block doesn't work on such as Stomp, Focused Assault, Hour of Twilight, Impale and so on.
