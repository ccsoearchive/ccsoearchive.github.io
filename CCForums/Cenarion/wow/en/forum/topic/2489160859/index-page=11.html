It'd help if I knew what exactly you were confused about but I'll try...

Basically, if you want to know your overall chance to avoid an attack, you add your avoidances together. Everyone has a 5% miss chance, then you add on your dodge and parry chance. Using the numbers I gave in that example, if you've got 15% dodge and 15% parry chance, you've got 5 + 15 + 15 = 35% chance to avoid an attack.

Pallies have slightly lower base strength, so use this (It doesn't matter that much, but you'll get slightly more accurate numbers):
/run d=GetDodgeChance() p=235.5*d/65.631440-((235.5/65.631440)*5.01-3.19) DEFAULT_CHAT_FRAME:AddMessage("Ideal parry for current dodge: "..string.format("%.2f",p))


These are still off. You adjusted the base parry for pallies but are using the old incorrect C values, and not changing them per class.

Parry (for warriors, pallies and dks): c = 237.1860
Dodge (for pallies): c = 66.56744
Dodge (for warriors) c = 90.6425
k is just a constant that controls scaling and is also based on class:
For pallies: k = 0.886
For warriors: k = 0.956


From your scripts, 235.5 is the old parry C value and needs to be updated.
From your scripts 65.631440 is the old dodge c value. It needs to be updated and changed between both scripts.

Basically, what it's telling you is how much parry/dodge you should have to minimise diminishing returns. So, say, you've got 1000 dodge rating and 2000 parry rating, and it says your ideal parry for current dodge is 2500, it means that your dodge and parry are too close.

Just keep in mind that that's a fiddly thing to do and one of the absolute last things to do on your gear and usually, you use it to determine whether or not you want to reforge out of dodge or parry to a better stat.
