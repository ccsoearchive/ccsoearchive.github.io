To understand my song choices, you have to understand a bit of Meri's character creation, I think. Meriste is a little bit of me as a player and a little bit of inspiration from two other characters. The two songs I picked are both sad, but contain messages of strength and hope. Draenei in general have a horrific past, but I wanted to play on Meriste's unwavering willpower.

The first source of inspiration - part of the backbone of Meriste's concept - comes from the song, "I Move On (Sintel's Song)."

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VAubLFMVXRY

"As the dawn
Breaks through the night,
I move on,
Forever longing for the home
I found in your eyes
Your voice
saw me through the night."

Sintel is a 15 minute film about a young woman - Sintel - who finds an injured dragon hatchling and nurses it back to health. When the hatchling is taken, she goes on a journey to recover it. I won't spoil the ending - it's worth watching: (Fair warning - it's terribly sad.)

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eRsGyueVLvQ

At the time that I made the Meriste, I had just finished reading The Hunger Games. I admired the qualities of Katniss Everdeen - young, but resilient; a resourceful provider; courageous in the face of unspeakable horrors; fiercely protective. I decided I wanted those qualities in Meriste. I really like "Safe and Sound" from the new movie.

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YFEDTtKaFzU

"Just close your eyes;
The sun is going down.
You'll be alright;
No one can hurt you now.
Come morning light
You and I'll be safe and sound."

For Lahkin, this worked well just a few months ago:
"I'm Still Here" by Goo Goo Dolls.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ba1UFP186F8

I think the lyrics say it all. It's classic misunderstood teen, wanting to be taken for a man and for who he is, yet also craving a mentor-type to look up to. It also has some hints of his fighting through depression as he inevitably comes up short and feels brushed under the rug anyway.

Now that's he's more confident and "centered", I'm not so sure. This one might work, speaking more to his spiritual and shaman-y side.
"He Lives in You" by Lebo M.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vyRvF9lYlng

The lyrics reflect him as he's coming into his own, fulfilling the legacy of family (Narnicka, Genevra, and Cyrus). I also find the overall ambiance of it tribal and edging on mystery, reflecting his family's shamanistic background. It's also hopeful and similar to soul music, which is the same contrast Lahkin and his Light magic brings to his family, particularly with his "uncle" Cyrus.

These lyrics in particular have meaning:
And a voice
With the fear of a child
Answers
Mamela (Listen)
...his spirit guide is a child's soul, but it could also be used to describe Lahkin at times.
