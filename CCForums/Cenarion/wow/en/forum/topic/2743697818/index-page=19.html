Read your thread of suggestions, Drelnu. It's a well thought out post and there are portions of it I agree with, but personally I prefer the compilation of suggestions in this thread. However, what you have proposed is better than current AV. Indeed, just about anything would be better than current AV.

Some of your proposed changes revolve around buffing the General and his "battlemasters," as you call them. I agree... 250%.

However, I take issue with the inclusion of a 1 hour time limit on AV. And many of your proposed fixes revolve around reinforcements. While in theory they would improve the BG, I fear the effects of more band-aids on AV. I think its time to rip off the existing band-aids first and examine the wound.

An example to illustrate my point:

The Alliance storms south out of the gate. The defense that Horde setup at the outset is enough to slow them some, but the Alliance overwhelm the Horde and methodically move south. They take and burn IB and TP towers. IB GY and FW GY are flipped to Alliance control. Ten (10) minutes into the battle the Alliance are midway through capping the relief hut, the Horde are stuck at SP GY and the dreaded bridge. The call for Horde defense goes out, and a number of us use our trusty trinkets (or whatever has the hearth) and return to our base.

The next 15 minutes are a battle royal, with the Horde defending Drek in the keep and trying to take back the relief hut. Finally, the Horde succeed in overwhelming the Alliance and taking back RH. The fight moves to FW GY, where the Alliance offense is rezzing. Eight (8) minutes later the Horde caps it. Over the next 10 minutes the battlefront slowly pushes north to IB GY. Five (5) minutes later the Horde succeeds in capping it. It has been grueling, but the Horde have fought back to control their half of the map.

The Horde sets up defense at the IB chokepoint, while Alliance rez at snowfall and try to bust through. After 5 minutes of intense fighting, the Horde send small groups forward to contest the SH and IW bunkers that were saved by Alliance backcappers back in the beginning. The Alliance is now on the run, forced to split their numbers between defending their towers and trying to restart the push south. More Horde push north as the Alliance offense softens, trying to keep the momentum..... <<game timer hits 1 hour, AV ends>>

This example is not a fantasy of mine. It happened a number of times "back in the day" (and formed some of my fondest memories of WoW PvP). How does this story end? With a game timer we'll never know, but in my experience sometimes the Horde were able to continue the push north; other times the Alliance was able to rally and stop us. This is why I oppose explicit timers and implicit timers (e.g. reinforcements) in AV.

And if a stalemate develops that cannot be broken? That's what the NPC summons are for.

This post got longer than intended, but I couldn't resist the trip down memory lane. My apologies if I lost anyone.

However, I take issue with the inclusion of a 1 hour time limit on AV.

Thank you for taking the time to read; I know its quite a lot to get through.
I understand your dislike of the idea of time limits. I DID express my own reluctance for this to be applied to something as large-scale as AV, but I feel its a necessary evil to keep AV appealing to the current player-base (and many people who may want, but don't have time for a BG that runs all weekend long). My hope is that under your scenario as we reach the "More Horde push north as the Alliance offense softens, trying to keep the momentum..." line (approx 50 minutes into the battle) the next phrase reads: "Alliance realizes time is growing short, and abandons defense of their towers in a desperate last-ditch strike at the Horde General". Or perhaps instead it reads "the Horde realize that the alliance defensive positions are to strong, and gather their team together in an attempt to kill enough alliance players that they will run them out of reinforcements before the battle ends, or they will be able to summon the Ultimate Unit".
Although it may not be to your personal taste, once you have a timer on a Battleground, it becomes a valid part of strategy. Plus, once implemented, the timer is easy to adjust if too many battles are bumping up against it.
Alternatively, we take AV out of the random BG queue (as some people have suggested) and once players are free to choose it rather than be forced into it, I would easily agree with removing any kind of timer.

And many of your proposed fixes revolve around reinforcements. While in theory they would improve the BG, I fear the effects of more band-aids on AV. I think its time to rip off the existing band-aids first and examine the wound.

As I said in my post, I like the idea of reinforcements because I think they add an interesting level of complexity. We already have one BG where the only real goal is to kill the enemy general, IoC. My goal was to allow players in AV to do WHATEVER THEY WANT, with the idea that anything except sitting in a corner helps your team in some way.
Also, that thread did not start out as the wall of text it is today. Originally, it was an attempt to rework AV without requiring a great deal of effort on Blizzards part, so I prefered the so-called band-aid approach to entirely redoing the BG. I'm trying to temper my enthusiasm for change with a bit of realism.

Now that we've moved away from the token-model entirely, many people have suggested reverting AV, and merely adjusting the honor gain so that honor-per-hour is similar to other BGs.

Drel (May I call you Drel?),

I agree with Hezza on the points about having a timer. With AV off the random BG queue and having an "exit BG without Deserter" system in place, players don't need to spend more time in AV than they feel comfortable with, but neither do they need to be in there for a shorter time than they'd like. While killing Drek'thar or Vanndar as the only final objective might sound limiting, it still allows players to get to their strongholds in a variety of ways.

Alterac Valley was awesome before all of the band-aid fixes began to be applied to it. While you've done a great job of playing around with reinforcements, the reinforcement system is one of those band-aids that needs to be ripped off.

Without reinforcements, killing players still moves your team toward victory (crystals, flesh, blood, medals, etc), but removes that implicit timer. You mentioned keeping AV in line with other battlegrounds, but remember that that's the reason it was bastardized in the first place. Sometimes the experience can take a hit for the sake of gameplay, and sometimes it can't.

Sometimes it dies horribly, and drags the gameplay down along with it. This is one of those times.

Again, within the reinforcement system your ideas would certainly help to greatly improve the current iteration of AV, but a lot of us would rather see that system removed entirely.
