A good idea, imo. However, given how long its been since AV hasn't been largely a zerg-fest (and all the players that have joined the game since), a large scale education of the zone is needed. Maybe quests can do the job. But for example:

  • Each tower we have up adds to General's defense (should be obvious even now but you never know)

  • Each Commander gives dps buffs to their respective team every so often (one reason why its a good thing to protect him/her)

  • Armor scraps can be turned in to buff up NPCs

  • Blood can be turned in to eventually summon Lok (I've only played Horde; no idea what you silly Allys turn in to summon Ivus [Troll mojo perhaps? I hear it's pretty strong, lol])

  • Wing Commanders (3) can be rescued from opponents side of the map (I seem to remember a quest to go find all three). Important to note to newcomers: If they make it back to your base you can turn in flesh to initiate aerial assaults.

  • Trinket (or tabard if our suggestion is taken) can be used to return to your home base for defense

  • Horde-side: taming wolves and collecting ram pelts allows you to summon wolf-riders (Allys have the equivalent)

  • Collecting mine supplies allows for a squad of reavers to be summoned (they were quite badass in classic WoW; if they're not now they should be)

I'm sure there is more, but you get the idea. It would take a lot of quests to handle all this information. And for any of you reading this who did not get to play in AV pre-zergfest: Yes, this BG was very intricate. With all the moving parts very little was bugged, if memory serves. The only complaint I remember was Lok's summon being bugged sometimes if people didn't click correctly (similar to how you can screw up a mage table click).
