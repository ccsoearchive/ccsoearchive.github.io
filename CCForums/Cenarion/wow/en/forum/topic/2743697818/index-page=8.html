While it does, as a large-scale battle, feel similar to those open-world combat zones, I think that most of the people advocating these changes would prefer to be able to queue for AV at any time.

Another thought I had about AV. What if it were possible to rebuild the towers after they had been burned (and therefore restore the associated warmaster/marshall)? Allow me to explain my thinking...

If the changes/reversions we are discussing here were to happen, then chances are that the average length of an AV would go up, which most posters here agree would be a good thing. However, it means that the likelihood of joining an AV already in progress will probably go up. I remember joining AVs "back in the day" where my team was losing pretty badly, and people were leaving the BG in response. I think it would be interesting in this case to allow a sort of reset to the BG.

This doesn't need to be (and shouldn't be) something that's easy to do. Maybe put it on a timer (like an hour or more), or have it require a large amount of turnins (similar to how we turn in armor scraps to buff up the NPCs), or both.

I'm not sure if this is a good idea or not, but I figured I'd throw it out there to continue the discussion. If the queue times for "classic AV" are relatively short, then this probably wouldn't be needed. But if the queue time for an AV is long and I join one already in progress that's been heavily one sided, I'd like the chance to recover from the mess the previous AVers left.

Anyway, just an idea I thought I'd throw out there.

Yeah, I miss original AV with opposing sides actually fighting.

The gear just got to the point where people were able to kill Vann/Drek without destroying the towers and zergfest began, and destroyed AV as a BG, now it's one big pug PvE zone.

A few times, Blizzard did buff up the NPC's, which was only a temporary fix. Then last year Blizzard did two things right not only did they buff up the NPC's in the general's rooms, but had the damage taken and dealt by the NPC's scale with how many towers remained standing. This brought back the need for both sides to actively defend and engage other players, which started to make things fun.

A few times one team or the other would experiment with ensuring to get flying NPC's, the elemental lords, or the elite wolf riders/stormpike berserkers, but those NPC's weren't even a challenge anymore. I miss seeing one side or the other supporting the elementals to keep them alive and kill alliance so you pretty much got a guaranteed base capture if they lived.

They definitely need to rebuff/revamp the NPC's. I would suggest that at the beginning of each time new content is added with higher item levels, the NPC's difficulty increases automatically instead of us needing to wait for Blizzard to manually go change the code.

While some of those old 4-32hour matches were interesting, it did get old when it was happening every day for every match. I did like the resource addition so one side couldn't turtle for hours in their base and not even try to leave to get to the other side of the map.
