I absolutely LOVED the epic feel to old AV, pre reinforcements. I LOVED also the rare, but awesome epic WSG battles (though I have come -- very slowly -- to understand the reasons for the timer there).

I know exactly what you mean, Myrandrien. I miss AV pre-reinforcements. And I too was a late convert to the concept of a WSG with a timer. But it also makes me realize: WSG has the mapsize and teamsize where at the end of 25 minutes of play if one team hasn't capped 3 flags, you usually don't feel like it was a cheapened experience. Assuming you don't have a team loaded with bots (rofl sure seems that way sometimes) chances are that during the 25 minutes of play there were some terrific battles, whether they be trying to break stalemates, flag escorts, etc. You can call it after 25 minutes and still feel largely satisfied.

That isn't true with AV. While it doesn't have the explicit timer of WSG, it has an implicit timer in the form of reinforcements. Is it possible to have a more immersive experience in AV? Yes. Blizzard isn't forcing us to play a certain way, but I'd argue they are encouraging quick play with the current setup/rules of AV and with the way honor is awarded. That is what ruins the AV experience for me. I wouldn't expect Lok to be summoned every game, but there should be more hard fought battles over the various objectives. Otherwise, what's the point? Its possible to zerg-win an AV before any GYs turn over, so why bother to fight over them? Usually when AV ends its after 15 minutes or less of gametime, and 25% of that was probably spent just running from one end of the map to the other. That cheapens AV far more than the timer cheapens WSG.

/bigsigh
