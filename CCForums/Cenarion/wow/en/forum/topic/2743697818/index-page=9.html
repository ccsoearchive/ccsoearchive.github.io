2. Don't take AV out of random rotation. It is a catch 22 and a terrible irony that people PvP to get PvP gear, but bitterly complain that they must spend actual TIME PvP-ing against players. It's absurd, and they can all go jump in a lake.

I agree that it's a sad and bewildering state of affairs when people who want to grind honor to buy gear for PvP have such an aversion to PvP. Although, perhaps that just underscores the point of this thread which is that AV isn't really PvP anymore, at least most of the time it's not. Food for thought, Blizz :) (in case you're still reading this).

However, if AV were to return to its classic form, I think it should be removed from the random queue for 2 related reasons. First of all, not everyone who queues for a random will have the time to spend in what could potentially become a long and epic battle. Since all of the other BGs in game are tuned to last at most 25 min or so, a player can queue for a BG if they have a half hour to play or so with the expectation that they'll be able to finish up their PvP for the day/night on that timeline.

Secondly, some WoW players simply don't have the attention span to appreciate classic AV. I don't mean this as an insult to anyone. Classic AV existed in a time where things took longer to do in the game. Five-man instances, for example, could take a whole afternoon (remember Scholo, Strath, BRD?). I don't want to derail the thread with that, but I simply point out that for a few years Blizzard has put a premium on everything non-raid lasting an hour or less. And when something takes longer, such as the latest incarnations of ZA or ZG, people start complaining that instances take too long to run.

In short, if someone doesn't have the time to invest in or have the desire to spend a chunk of time in classic AV, I'd rather not have them there. And I feel that if classic AV were a possible outcome of the random queue, then some people might feel "duped into" or "stuck" playing a BG they don't want. I personally would have no problem joining a classic AV, playing for a couple hours, realizing I have to go and logging out. For me the fun isn't so much the win, but the battle that takes place on the way to the win (or loss, yes losses can be "fun"). But I realize that not everyone is like me. And that's probably a good thing. :)

Just my expanded thoughts on the matter.

A hard-fought near loss is more fun than an easy win, any day.

With the current AV it's too easy to feel disheartened by the dwindling reinforcement count. If you're a few hundred behind, you're just fighting until the game's over; There isn't time to take back the lead, and once your reinforcements are gone they aren't coming back (unless you have the mines and nobody on your team ever dies).

With classic AV there's a chance you might summon your elemental champion or call forth an aerial assault, and that gives you hope. If you can take back that one graveyard, you can hold the enemy at bay long enough to stabilize again. They can only win when they kill your general, and they'll only do that over your dead body!

Sometimes all you need is a Deus ex machina, and Classic AV delivers exactly that.
