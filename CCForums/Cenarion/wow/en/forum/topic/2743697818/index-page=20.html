I don't really know about that. Certainly we both agree that the AV battle should be slowed down, but if you do it with mini-boss NPCs, that just makes the battle more like a PvE race rather than PvP. I prefer having fewer pre-spawned NPCs, and instead force players to generate the NPCs through PvP combat (via killing, looting, and then turning in the drops).

If the goal is to remove all PvE from AV, then we should remove the tower/bunker archers too. But I don't think any of us would advocate that. AV really can't ever be free of all PvE for the simple fact that even with 40 players to a team, its not enough for a map of that size. But even with the archers there, you can solo cap a tower even as Horde by finding the sweet spot between the walls. Putting a lieutenant back in the tower would ensure that it would take more than 1 person to take it. Putting an elite back at snowfall makes taking that GY less trivial as well.

Part of the reason that playing defense in AV right now is unpopular is because unless you commit half your team to a spot, you're going to get run over. Elite NPCs serve as a pacing mechanism. Yes they're technically PvE, but with them slowing down the offense just a bit, it allows for defense to make more of a stand there and for PvP to occur. In this fashion a bit of PvE leads to more PvP, which is exactly what we all want for that BG.


Also, under Foury's plan, I worry about the possibility of endless turtling. Assuming you force the enemy back into their base, they have a closer GY (particularly horde), making it very difficult to force them out again. If the enemy never has to worry about losing, why shouldnt they just sit on the Aid-Station/Relief hut and use the NPC boss to compensate for the difference in player strength? Under my scenario, you can hold your base for the entire game and still lose because your opponent controls the entire rest of the battlefield. Maybe you don't like that, thats your opinion, but I don't think making unwinnable BGs is very good game design.

I remember a few games that turned into this. Horde were stuck either at the bridge or at AS, unable to cap it. Lok was summoned, but unfortunately did not get guarded well enough and was killed by the Alliance. So I decided to run mine supplies back to the base. It took a few minutes since no one else answered my call for help in chat, but I eventually was able to summon the reaver squad. They spawned as lvl 62 elites (back when lvl cap was 60) and charged north. They tore through the Alliance turtle like butter and it was all over within minutes.

The moral of the story is that if one team is simply turtling, the offense still has the advantage due to summoned units. Technically they are PvE, but AV was never marketed as pure PvP. As Foury has said, classic AV was a great blend of PvP and PvE. I suppose its a matter of opinion and we can agree to disagree, but I enjoyed the experience.
