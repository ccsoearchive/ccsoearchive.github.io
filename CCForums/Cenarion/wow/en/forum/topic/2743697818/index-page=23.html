I'm working on the next thread's OP, but could use a hand. Are there a few people who played AV a lot at level 60 during its golden age (Patch 1.5-1.8 or thereabouts) who could provide a brief description of how incredibly awesome Old AV was? Newer players could use a little summary of the "good old days," and when I say old I mean old. My descriptions of Korrak are second-hand only.



There were a few great battles in old AV. Having one of those rare occasions where 90% of your team worked together to achieve an objective was pretty special. Managing to line up the cavalry and infantry and using them to crash through an opponents graveyard gave a real sense of accomplishment.

But unfortunately most of the time old AV was pretty awful. Just two walls of players mindlessly throwing themselves against each other with organisation being the usual PuG standard (i.e. none). This would typically happen for hours on end because the two key chokepoints (the bridge for alliance and the narrow path to the final towers for horde) were almost impossible to break through.

Summoned NPCs would mostly be uselessly kited away to Vann/Drek who would promptly splatter them. There would be people fishing and farming the caves for loot/xp.

In many ways I think that the tedium for much of the time made those rare moments of accomplishment that much more special.

And I know that old AV sounds awesome with all of the NPCs, summons and different ways to help your team. And just occasionally it was amazing. But I'm not sure that's enough to make up for the mindlessness for the rest of the time. Ultimately Blizzard didn't change AV because they wanted to deprive us of an awesome BG. They changed it because the vast majority didn't like it much.


Oh, and as for Korrak, he was rarely killed. He was basically a 25ish man raid boss in the middle of a PvP zone. He was tank & spank so you could do him with less, but that increased the risk of the other team interfering.

95% of the time he would be kited away while someone capped the GY. The few times he did get killed was when the winning side got bored of pushing against the chokepoint and decided to kill him. You didn't gain anything for killing him - in fact you always lost a lot of ground because of all the people who needed to pull off the front lines to do it.

It is ironic that adding more PvE elements actually increases the PvP inside AV, but it's tried and true. I remember when storming a bunker/tower was an all-out effort. And who can forget all the fights over the great Stormpike Bridge.

In addition to what you mentioned in the OP, you also had the wyvern/gryphon riders who you had to rescue in enemy territory. They would then go on bombing runs.

And in the early days, Field of Strife was aptly named so because there were landmines. This prevented players from just casually riding on through on their way to the enemy base.

You also had the Witherbark Troll faction/city across from snowfall. There was a quest to rescue a goblin engineer who had been kidnapped. If you were successful, he would come back to your town and if you supplied all of the materials, he would make you a Goblin Shredder which was pretty epic.

You also had the Sindicate faction in play, though it's been too long for me to remember what their purpose was.

And, of course, you had to loot the corpses of your opponent to get items like armor scraps which you would turn in and eventually make your NPC's stronger.

Yep, those were the days....
