Firstly, Ascendance sounds awesome so yay for that.

I'm just going to list several things and hopefully you guys can talk about 1 or 2 of them.

1) Ancestral Guidance seems to fall flat when compared to like talents (specifically Vampiric Dominance), is there a possibility that this could be adjusted to 25% and effect more targets?

2) Fortifying Waters doesn't quite compare to the other two healing options for DPS Shamans. Is there a chance we can see it also make Healing Rain instant. The cast time is a DPS loss for Ele and Enh has to use MW5 to make true use of it which is a DPS loss. The cooldown on HR should be enough to ensure the spell doesn't get over used. It would also add a nice small HPS increase to Resto to make it more comparable to HTT.

3) Nature's Guardian is still incredibly underwhelming. For PvP and Solo play the odds of it saving the Shaman would be quite low and for PvE it is really just a safeguard for players that can't manage their aggro.

4) Astral Shift, the output reduction seems overkill for a class that has no other defensive options. Unless there are baseline defenses in the horizon I feel this needs another adjustment.

5) I'm wondering about the thought process behind the limiter on Totemic Restoration. Call of the Elements seems quite powerful in comparison.

6) Totemic Projection, a great many (but not all) Shaman dislike this talent. Could we please see it go baseline as a toggle (on - place | off - drop). Considering our totems are a mix of defenses, healing, offensive damage, offensive utility, defensive utility, etc. a skill that relocates all of them isn't that great. It also doesn't seem to fit the tier anymore as the other two options are all about getting lots of up-time on totems.

7) Not sure what to say about our output talents, Elemental Mastery got significantly more boring. It would be nice it also increased Nature spell power for its duration.

8) Removing the "ignores movement impairing effects" part of Ghost Wolf seems to greatly diminish its use in my mind. Elemental and Resto can cast while moving, Resto can run and Cleanse itself in caster form, Elemental has a Knockback. I wonder if it would still be dispellable.

9) Elemental Totems. It's nice that you cut their cooldown and duration in half but they are still totems and this is a huge issue. We have no control over them, they leash, don't swap targets, can't stop their DPS if you need to, sometimes stop for no reason, are easily destroyed, etc. Enhance can't even use GFE because of Searing Flames. GEE is only good for soloing content and even then marginally so. There is just so many things wrong with them.

10) I am confused as to why Lava Burst no longer scales with Shamanism. Wouldn't this be an issue at higher SP levels if Lightning Bolt begins to outscale it?

11) Please for the love of Enhancement Shaman everywhere do not continue to do anything with Searing Flames. Remove Searing Totem and create a better, less infuriating system. Imagine if Rogues had to rely on a 5HP stick in the ground for combo points.

12) I'm quite disappointed to see Purification still gives Resto Shaman bonus healing and now also gives increased healing from Water totems. This puts DPS Shaman at a bit of a disadvantage for self healing and greatly devalues Healing Tide Totem (if it is supposed to be a Tranquility for Resto then it would be half a Tranquility for Ele/Enh). Restoration Druids lost their passive healing increase and it was baked in so DPS Druids have equivalent healing potential with a much smaller toolkit. All we have is a much smaller toolkit.

I will close with my 2 biggest issues,
a) Shaman defenses and self healing do not appear to be up to par with other classes, factoring in very low base HP and lack of escape tools this very worrisome.
b) Enhancements reliance on Searing Totem needs to go, it does not and will not work.

First is with the new Level 87 Ability “Shroud of Concealment” – Is this intended to be a Group Vanish or should we view this as more of a Stealth (and if so is it only usable out of Combat)? This is a reoccurring question, so please lend some clarification to this new Ability.

With the removal of the Thrown Weapons (and I believe it’s our MH Weap that will take it’s place) will this affect our Poison Ability? How will Poisons then be affected Deadly Throw and Shuriken Toss? I’m expecting for this to be a minimal change, if at all, but several people are just hoping for clarification on this.

I saw this asked earlier about Warriors and Death Knights, but since I am a representative of the only class that does not have a choice between Dual Wielding or not, I am curious how Blizz intends to handle their removal of the +Hit Talents? The current setup has PvE Rogues picking up +4% Hit from Precision as a minimum, with most grabbing the full +6%. I’m curious if Blizz has decided to address this multi-class issue with Gear Itemization or an Adjustment to Stats or if any other plans are in the works regarding this.

With the new designation of Lethal and Non-Lethal Poisons, does Blizz have any thoughts of introducing some new Lethal ones to the mix? With the latest adjustment to Deadly Poisons, it is hard to see why anyone would (or should) use Instant Poisons outside of some extreme situations. Since we also get Deadly Poisons 20 levels earlier, are their plans to revamp Instant Poison as well?

I’m out of questions, so time for some feedback:

Tier 1: Subterfuge seems like a wonderful Talent, allowing Rogues to score a double opener. This allows us to surprise opponents (PvP or PvE) with deadly openers again, something we have been lacking for awhile now. Sadly I think it will be the clear choice for most Rogues. I see Shadow Focus playing a very close 2nd, with everyone’s favorite old “filler” Talent of Nightstalker falling by the wayside. I don’t think this initial Tier has enough strong competition in it.

Tier 2: I agree with an earlier comment that Deadly Throw doesn’t seem to be in the right spot here. I remember reading that a hope for the new Talent setup was to cause “category choices,” but these 3 choices do not seem to be in the same category. This next thought may not sit well with anyone, but I almost feel as though Dirty Tricks (Tier 5) should be moved to this spot and have this Tier supply Rogues with 3 different choices to avoid damage. If not that, then some other (new) Talent to replace Deadly Throw that makes more sense in its place.

Tier 3: I feel like this is a pretty solid Tier. My only complaint (as previously mentioned by others) is that we have 2 Survival Tiers in a row. Perhaps swap this and Tier 4?

Tier 4: Similar to Tier 1, I (and many other Rogues have voiced this) think that Preparation is the obvious winner here. The other 2 are on pretty equal footing, but they cannot compete with Prep. Many Rogues have suggested either to Baseline Prep or to get rid of Prep over the years, and I honestly have no preference either way on the matter. However, I feel that this Tier needs to be addressed one way or the other if Blizz truly wants the Tiers to have valid choices and promote diversity. I for one support your goal, but this is one of your obstacles that have built up in the 7 years of WoW.

Tier 5: I think this Tier competes well and have nothing to add.

Tier 6: This is the Tier that I honestly am upset about the most. To explain why takes some time though. First, I love Shuriken Toss. I think this is wonderful that we have a Combat Point generator on our Ranged as an option now. This is a consolation prize for the times that Rogues are stuck at range and all we can do is toss a dagger hoping to keep our Deadly Poisons stacked (ie Morchok). But that’s it, a consolation prize. This should not be a Tier 6 Talent. I will not go so far as to beg for a Baseline Ability here, but definitely lower on the Talent Tree would be better appreciated. Next, Anticipation… THANK YOU! If I understand this Talent correctly (and I think I do), I need to kiss someone on the mouth in California. I’m over-reacting of course, but this little gem goes a long way in smoothing out PvE rotations for rogues that it beats out the other guys. No more 4+ Combat Point Finishers cause this makes them all 5 Points. This also lets Combat Rogues use Revealing Strike more reliably. I’m pretty sure that after some actual testing gets done that this will be the “cookie cutter” Tier 6 if there are no changes to this Tier (I know you don’t want to hear that, but let’s face some reality here).

Now, I saved Versatility for its own paragraph. I am going to climb up on my high horse for a minute here, so please do not think too ill of me. I have spoken on this topic (as have many others) several times over the years. I have been around since almost the very beginning of WoW. I’m a Vanilla Raider of MC and BWL on my Rogue. One of our oldest quality of life complaints has been Combat Points stacking on our target instead of us. In years past it was a discussion that Rogues would disagree about from time to time, but we assumed for the most part that Blizz wasn’t addressing it due to some sort of limitation similar to our problems with Vanish. We dealt with it, but then an expansion comes out and Pallys are redesigned with a mechanic very similar to what we had been asking for since Vanilla. Blizz did award us Redirect, but it wasn’t received as well as could have been. It has been viewed with disdain as more of a “stop-gap” after seeing what Pallys received. Now we are close to another expansion and we get a Tier 6 Talent that removes the Cool Down from Redirect. I know non-rogues (hell, probably even some rogues) will not see the big deal since we get what we’ve wanted for so long, but think about it for a second. Rogues have to click an ability after switching targets while in battle to utilize the extra Combat Points left on the previous target (if they don't disappear to quickly) when it died before we could dumb them into a finisher. And in MoP if we use up our last Talent we can do this with no Cool Down. All the while Pallys have been able to do this without a Talent and without an Ability while suffering less Combat Ramp Up penalties than Rogues.

I’m not saying it’s not progress. I’m just saying that when things are handled in certain ways it leads to disdain more readily than to appreciation.
