Qustion: What are the developments for the Cat and Bear versions of Mangle/Swipe/Thrash/etc.?

Answer: Several abilities for Cats and Bears are slightly different depending on Cat or Bear Form. The abilities you’re seeing in the talent calculator are the base versions that show up in your spellbook. Here are some additional details about their different effects in each form:

  • Mangle: In Cat Form, has no cooldown, costs energy, and generates a combo point. In Bear Form, generates 5 rage, and has a 6 second cooldown, which can also be reset by a proc from Lacerate and now Thrash ticks.
  • Swipe: In Cat Form, has no cooldown, and costs energy. In Bear Form, has a 3 second cooldown, and is free.
  • Thrash: In Cat Form, has no cooldown, and costs energy. In Bear Form, has a 6 second cooldown, and costs 15 rage.
  • Skull Bash: Costs 15 energy or 10 rage, depending on form, and has a 15 second cooldown. The current talent calculator says that it has a 10 second cooldown, but that is a mistake. It is 15 seconds like most other interrupts are now.
  • Stampeding Roar: Costs 30 energy or 15 rage, depending on form.
  • Faerie Fire: Has a 6 second cooldown when used in Cat or Bear Form.

    We’ve just updated our [url="http://ow.ly/94f5N"]Mists of Pandaria Talent Calculator[/url] to reflect many of the most recent changes and renovations currently being made to the next expansion of World of Warcraft.

    We’d like to discuss these and other changes to talents and specializations in this thread. Here are a couple of thoughts to start with:

    • For shaman and hunters, we still have a ways to go with the revamp of talents, and this latest calculator doesn’t have some of the changes that we’re currently working on.
    • If you don’t see a favorite spell in the talent list, there is a good chance it is something you get automatically for choosing a particular spec. For example, all Fury warriors learn Raging Blow without having to choose it as a talent. All Discipline priests know Divine Aegis. Chances were almost certain that you were going to choose those anyway so we just gave them to you.
    • If you don’t see a favorite spell or mechanic as a class ability, spec ability or talent, then there is a good chance it is becoming a glyph. Examples include Atonement (priest), Gag Order (Warrior), Rude Interruption (Warrior), and the Acts of Sacrifice Cleanse component (Paladin).

    Feel free to have a look at the updated calculator, and then discuss it here!

    I'd still like to get an idea of what Blizzard's intention is with Melee Paladin Rotations.

    Currently, I would consider Protection to be very fun. It has a rhythmic nature to it due to the 3-second "builder" ability, and then you weave other skills around that. Procs and Holy Power usage keep it from being too linear, but it definitely has a nice flow to it. (For the record, I consider Fury warrior to be the same way, but Fury is not raid-viable right now so my alt -former main- has been forced to going Arms)

    Currently, I would consider Retribution to be absolutely terrible and in no way fun to play. Granted, that's personal preference, but all of the flow and smoothness that exist for Prot are just destroyed by the 4.5-second cooldown on your primary builder. In an earlier blue post, it was stated they were okay with having open GCDs for melee classes as long as it didn't happen with "frustrating regularity" and that blue poster must have never played Retribution.

    Ret has 4 primary attacks. 4. Divine Storm or CS (4.5s), Judge(8s), TV(varies with procs and judge availability, let's say about 10s), and Exo(varies, only cast on a proc). That's it. We have Wrath on a 15sec CD and the near-worthless Consecrate on 30sec, and a ~35 second buff to maintain. Wrath and Cons aren't even good, they're just things to push if they happen to be up one of the dozens of times per fight that Ret has an open GCD.

    I benched my Pally early into the T11 content and picked up a warrior because I was the OT for my guild and I couldn't stand Ret's jank, unintuitive, open-GCD-filled rotation. Now, in 4.3, with the gutting of Fury (I'm not fond of Arms' rotation, I like it better than 4.0.3 Ret but not by much) I've dusted the Pally off again. As of right now, there are only 3 things that make playing Ret remotely tolerable, and all three have to exist or it goes straight to hell:

    1) 2T13 lets Judgement pop a Holy Power. This helps with the flow a bit and allows you to get to your TVs faster, reducing some of the open GCDs. Will this behavior become baseline in MoP, or can I expect my rotation to get crappier once I lose that extra HP? EDIT: I see Judgements of the Bold does, in fact, grant this effect baseline.

    2) Exo procs can help fill gaps in the rotation, giving me the impression that I'm doing something more frequently. Exo procs appear to be going away in MoP, dropping my "core abilities" from 4 to 3, and my rotation was already kind of crap when I had 4. EDIT: Art of War looks like it still retains the over-all functionality, but the MMO champ info says otherwise. Was there a bugged/non-updated tooltip that was later fixed?

    3) The "Holy Power Procs" that allow me to use my finishers at max power look like they're going to become ridiculously less frequent. Again, this is something that helps fill gaps in the rotation, and help to make it feel a little less static while at the same time giving me buttons to push. I like having buttons to push.

    TL;DR: My main concern about Melee Paladins is this: I like 4.3 Prot. MoP Prot looks like it will play more like 4.0.3 Ret (which I can't stand). MoP Ret looks like it is going to remove or severely diminish the small handful of things that makes 4.3 Ret tolerable.

    I'm trying to see the glass half-full and reserve Judgement, but I'm a Paladin and can't help it. Please tell me our rotations aren't going to suck in MoP. I'd rather not have to bench this character again.

    Edit: Minor comment, it feels a bit contradictory to state that the design for melee classes is to sometimes have an open GCD while simultaneously releasing a new class that doesn't even have auto-attack. I'd assume/hope Monks won't have any open GCDs where they literally sit at their keyboards while their character does absolutely nothing -- not even white swings. <strong/>
