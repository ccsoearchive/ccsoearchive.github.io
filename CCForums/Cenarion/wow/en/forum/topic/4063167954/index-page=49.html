I know shamans haven't been "worked on" as much as of this update, but I have a few concerns/questions based on whats shown in the recent update.

1. Burning Wrath: This grants the +10% spell power buff, but since the 4.3 changes Enhancement no longer benefits from this buff (not that it NEEDS to benefit from it, the increased atp to sp conversion scaling pretty much negates the need for the buff). So, I'd like to know if enhancement will once again benefit from this buff, or is it possible to make Burning Wrath into an Ele/Resto spec ability and give Strength of Earth (as +10% atp) to Enhancement? I would like to be bringing raid buffs that also actually benefit "me."

2. Primal Wisdom: the amount of mana returned has been reduced by 3%. I don't understand this honestly. Enhancement shouldn't ever have to really worry about its mana, also I rather like what Retribution paladin is receiving with Sword of Light (5% max mana returned every 2s). Is Enhancement going to receive the same treatment as Ret? (I would much prefer permanent fast mana regen than to be stuck HAVING to melee my opponents and now for even less return).

3. The AoE situation: I noticed the Imp Lava Lash synergies have been placed back in (on LL itself and Searing Flames). Mostly my complaints with Enhancement AoE is still that it takes too long to set up: FS>LL>FN and while the damage is not super crap now, often by the time I do get to fire my AoE, everyone else has fired off two AoEs. Warrior's AoE situation especially as Prot and Arms has been simplified with the removal of Rend and changing of Deep Wounds and Blood and Thunder. Can something like this not be done for Enhancement?

4. Giving the double damage crits back on spells will actually make crit more attractive than it has been in Cataclysm, but how do you plan to make haste worth anything? Elemental Mastery is a haste buff and the new "Ancestral Swiftness" talents are almost completely worthless as Enhancement because of the near null value of haste for Enhancement - haste is worthless because of all of the hard CDs or Internal CDs on our abilities, and it does not quite give the same "resource benefit" it does to other specs, MW procs are not quite what they used to be, Searing Totem and Searing Flames do not scale with haste, and so on. On the level 60 tier of talents only Echo of the Elements is a viable choice for Enhancement.

5. No more Greater Healing Wave: Is it your intention to weaken Enhancement's self healing? As it stands, Greater Healing Wave and Healing Rain are the only spells I cast with Maelstrom for healing, the others are too weak to be useful. Are you planning to make Healing Surge heal for as much as a Greater Healing Wave when used with MWx5?

6. Two questions about the new ability Ascendance: Will the nature damage for Enhancement scale with our mastery? Also, will we end up turning into the Twilight Ascendant Model, or will you give us a new model/graphic for the ability? (Please say it is the latter, because I do not want to look like the Twilight Ascendant). If this ability scales with mastery, I see it being something Enhancement has lacked for quite some time: a decent DPS CD. However, 15s with 3 mins CD seems a bit steep.

7. Can you go back and make Spirit Walker's Grace into something cool like Camouflage instead of something totally useless? Shamouflage would be nice. (lol) But seriously, can it be reworked into a passive that allows the casting of various spells all the time, much like the glyph for Lightning Bolt? Make it so it allows the casting of Greater Healing Wave, Chain Heal, and Lightning Bolt. I think that would make it a better ability and remove a VERY situational spell from the action bar, especially since we might have to make room for all these gimmick totems now? This would not even particularly make the ability useful for Enhancement, but it already was the worst level 85 ability given out (especially from Enhancement's point of view).

8. No more +15% movement speed for Enhancement? I do not want to lose that, I think that was one of the best changes Enhancement received in Cataclysm.

9. Can you please, when you make more of a pass over the shaman talents, make talents actually desirable by a PvE Enhancement shaman? I know I'm probably going to get a "situational" response to this, but honestly the talents feel just plain weak from an Enhancement perspective, they seem very Resto and Elemental geared still (and more useful in PvP than PvE). I really only see a clear use for Echo of the Elements, and when I look to other class trees, I see a lot more interesting choices for them.
