Classic AV is in my top 5 list of favorite things in the game, up there with classic Southshore vs. Tarren Mill.

Multi-day AV battles are not some nostalgic myth. They actually did happen. I remember many cases of starting a AV battle at night, going to sleep, waking up before work and jumping back in, then quitting again and going to work then coming home from work to find the same battle still raging. There was the odd player who would stay for the entirety of it too and have insane amounts of kills. I recall AVs where I personally had 500-1000+ honorable kills in a single session. Now I am lucky to get 10 HKs in a AV battle.

Following and protecting the druids to the field of strife, then helping them summon Ivus the Forest lord, then cheering when the summon finally went off and Ivus went storming to the enemy base. The damage Ivus dealt is deadly even by todays standards, he would do 30k+ a hit which is a lot when the average player had 3k-4k hp but since he would only attack 1 person at a time he could still be dps'd down. Players could also buff and heal him to make him last longer. A well-timed Ivus was often essential for victory.

As soon as you saw these words /yell'd throughout the zone you knew sh*t was about to get real:

"Wicked, wicked, mortals! The forest weeps. The elements recoil at the destruction. Ivus must purge you from this world!"
