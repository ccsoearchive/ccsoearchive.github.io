Look, Rogues at the moment are borderline OP. They have a tool for every situation, while very few weaknesses. The intent of this design decision is to level the playing field a little more. So, instead of buffing everyone to a Sub Rogues current mobility, defensive, and control levels, they're limiting Sub Rogue choices in regard to those.

This is the smart decision on their part. Ghostcrawler wrote a blog not too long back about the PvP "Arms race", and this is an excellent example of that in action. If you buff other classes to Sub Rogue levels, then what? They then have to give other people more effective tools for what will happen after, and it just ends up being a cascading effect that is impossible to balance, and is a general headache.

Rogues did well before mid Cataclysm in PvP, and they'll be fine if these changes go through as well. It's not like they simply made us choose between Prep and Shadowstep, and gave us nothing. There are a fair amount of good things they added with the recent talent changes, and keep in mind, nothing is finalized yet.

I think people are putting the cart before the horse, and simply spending their time focusing too much on this one change. But again, this is just my opinion. To each their own.

The talents are meant to give interesting choices to the player. Any role bonus is meant to similar in any given tier. Why? Well, firstly, because vanish as a dps cooldown in pve silly. (Personal preference.) But mostly because preparation then needs to be balanced against the instant mobility of Shadowstep (without a dps buff, so we mean the dps bonus of extra time on target) and BoS (again, more time on target, but with a significant energy cost, which is only partly negated by regen during transit).

Overkill is the odd man out because its dps bonus is significant, especially combined with a garrote. Find weakness is << 1% damage. Same goes for Prep. Overkill is over ~2.5%, and that is with 1 vanish, so its effect per vanish is much higher (I'm wildly guessing closer to 3.75% total benefit with prep overkill). Preparation as assassination would be required except the most extremely heavy switch/movement fights.

Ok, so now that we've "established" that prep would add another 1.75% damage to assassination, the other options, BoS and ShS would need to add ~175% damage from target uptime. In a 5 minute fight that means an extra 3 seconds of target uptime. Ok, thats pretty easy to do.
