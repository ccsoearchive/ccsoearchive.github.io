I'm really disappointed in people's support of this. Trying to argue the equality of Step vs Prep seems asinine, bordering on shill territory. Giving Blizzard too much credit and wearing rosy glasses. In PvP, rated BGs or Arena, how does Shadowstep hold equal weight with an extra smoke bomb? Or vanish, dismantle, cloak, etc? Target swaps rarely require me to Step currently, I can just stay aware and position myself properly. The added mobility is certainly not worth the utility sacrifice in most cases, save for a Focus Step / Kick or Step / Redirect / Kidney, perhaps, but that wasn't much of an issue during Mut / Prep.

And in PvE, Prep is a DPS cooldown, at least for Sub and Mut, while Step is mere mobility. The winner is obvious - unless you want to gimp your DPS. There may be limited cases where Step could be better, but Prep is by far and away the clear winner, both in PvP and PvE. It offers so much more. It eclipses the other options.

People who support this current design are far more optimistic than I. Not to invalidate your opinions, but I think your opinions are ignoring history. Blizzard might have good intentions, or even good theory behind this idea, but that hasn't prevented altruistic ideas from being flops when implemented into practice in the past. Objectionable designs like this have made it to beta, through beta, and to live servers before the outcry became loud enough that the approach was changed. And all along the way, many people were complacent and even supportive, saying Blizzard knew what they were doing when in the end it was proven otherwise, even by Blizzard's own admission.
