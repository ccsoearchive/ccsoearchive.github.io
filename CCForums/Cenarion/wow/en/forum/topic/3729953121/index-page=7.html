It may be contrary to the dev team's "motivation," however it's still how a vast majority of the player-base feel.

I can appreciate exposing the story to casual players, but Blizzard, please understand:

The story presented isn't worth exposing.

Surely, the WoW devs aren't too proud to actually acknowledge that Cataclysm is a far-cry from what they were able to originally produce, in terms of quality of content. To say otherwise just... does not make any sense, and is more than likely due to inexperience or ego. But that's simply what I think, obviously doesn't apply to everyone. :p

However, it is simply my, personal, opinion that in an effort to cater to more of WoW's player-base, you have, in turn, sacrificed original/compelling content. I have never been more under-whelmed by an expansion's final boss than I have with Deathwing, which I think is a terrible waste and shame, considering DW's lore.

The fact that I see threads repeatedly popping up on WoW's forums, as well as others, makes me think that maybe other people agree with me. I don't know, though. We all handle our own business, so if it's more profitable (as it clearly is) to adapt the game for a less involved audience, then more power to you all.

I'm happy to say that I probably won't be there to see how it ends up.

PS: Which would you rather have? A good portion slice of a delicious pie? Or 10 bowls of dry rice?

This is what has been presented to WoW's subscribers as options.
