I think we need to be clear on what does, and does NOT constitute competitive progression raiding.

Competitive Progression raiding (CPR) is all about being the first to kill a boss. Period. End of statement. All of the demands that CPR leaders make are specifically to achieve this goal.

Storyline doesn't enter into it.

No one should be prevented from seeing the story. no one should be treated like a second-class citizen and have to wait.

By the same token, not everyone should be able to get EVERYTHING.

To wit:

Gear is important for CPR because stats are the tools CPR raiders need to do their job effectively. If you're just looking to see the story, then you don't NEED the powerful gear.

Personally, I do not think that tier gear should be available at LFR levels. I see tier gear as something needed if you have a team looking to do traditional raiding. LFR isn't raiding by any stretch of the imagination - it is the pve equivalent of battlegrounds - a bunch of people doing their own thing and hoping that somehow they end up killing a boss. Even if you're fortunate enough to find someone willing to provide a strat or two, chances are, not many are going to listen.

So how would I break this down?

Heroic raids - Legendaries and Heroic Tier gear
Normal raids - Normal tier gear
LFR Raids- No tier gear but LFR level epics and Valor Points.

This gives the widest opportunity for folks to see the story, rewards players for making the commitment to a raid team (and before the howling commences, there are a lot of community raid guilds out there for those who do not want to raid at a progression level. I fail to see how agreeing to raid on predetermined nights is such a horrible request) and rewards those who raid at a competitive level with items that are not easy to get.

So for those who have their panties in a bunch because "OMG everyone's seeing Deathwing dead!" need to chill. This is content, and it's perfectly fine for a lot of people to see the story play out.

For those who are going to complain that my suggestion on gear availability is "mean," progression raiders put A LOT into raiding - they understand their classes on a very deep level. They chose to put great effort into optimizing their toons and their computer systems. They deserve some kind of shiny for the effort - much like the top Arena players get unique mounts and titles. And frankly, the higher ilvl gear means nothing if you have no intention of utilizing the benefits those stats give you.

The rewards for one's gameplay of choice should be commensurate with the gameplay choice. If you wish to raid casual, then the reward is content. If you wish to raid semi-hardcore or progression, then the reward is unique-skinned gear.

Just a couple things that the resident short-bus rider would like to voice.

The main complaint is simply that "Deathwing exploded in a glitter shower when he died!" That's pretty much it. Now, if he'd been disintegrated, or if he was dismembered, drawn and quartered, and tossed into the Malestrom, or had some other epic death animation instead of simply going "POOF!!" and vanishing, I doubt we would be having this conversation. The fight itself? Epic. Having to choose which order you do your platforms to suit your raid composition is a brilliant, BRILLIANT idea and makes the fight interesting. The ending, however, is lackluster. I just spent 12-15 minutes fighting this boss, killing his many adds, and he explodes into EPIC GLITTERZ? Please. Blizz, you can do better than that.

Second? Another reason people are complaining about this boss is that Dragon Soul, as a whole, is far too easy. We got to Madness on the second week in my little 10-man guild. However, it took us a month to get to Ragnaros, and even longer to down him. Final bosses aren't as satisfying as they used to be. It was an ACCOMPLISHMENT simply to MAKE it to the Lich King, and judging from the stories I've heard (I've only been raiding since late Wrath), Burning Crusade bosses were even more brutal. I've heard of Mu'ru single-handedly breaking guilds, and he's not even the final boss in Sunwell. Long and short is, I long for the days where you actually had to WORK to make it to the final boss, instead of having the final boss handed to you on a silver platter. The first few bosses in DS are a joke, and none of them were genuinely difficult, which is a shame, because you had some good ideas in there. (Yes, Zon'ozz, Mr. Ping-Pong boss, I'm looking at you.)

And before you say anything, LFR doesn't count. This doesn't have anything to do with the story, it's more about progression raiding. More about saying, "I killed this boss when it was relevant, and it was awesome." I'll always treasure the moment I got my first Lich King kill.

Oh, and I'm not talking about heroic modes, either. My server got realm first Heroic Ragnaros 3 days before the DS patch hit. Paragon wiped 500 times on him. I have no doubt that heroic modes are brutal and satisfying, but I'm talking normals here. You shouldn't be able to take a pug in during the second week of the patch and down 6/8. Blizz, there IS such a thing as TOO easy. Please keep that difficulty setting in LFR (where the arc mage in full PvP gear doing 12k DPS wins all the tier), thank you very much.

Just a couple thoughts from a retarded dude, don't mind me.

If you take the cover off Dragon Soul so to say, I really liked the instance. The fights definitely had a creative feel to them. Actually, the same could be said for the entire expansion.

However, when Blizzard said back before Cataclysm that Deathwing wasn't going to play an intimate role with the players, I guess they weren't kidding. Sure, from 1-85 you have a lot of interaction between yourself, the Twilight Cult, and Deathwing (be it the sky turning red or an actual quest ending). As a player who went from 80-85 though, there was hardly any interaction with Deathwing at all. In comparison to Lich King loling at you for 2 years, there was no real drive to hunt Deathwing down, destroy him, and teabag + spit on his corpse. In fact, I really felt no connection between himself, Ragnaros, Cho'gall, Nefarian, or Al'akir. Actually, I think Cho'gall was the only boss in the expansion that questing made me want to go after him. I remember being upset when the Abyssal Maw was canned. There was no real representation from the Water element aside from Throne of the Tides and honestly, I was looking forward to some epic showdown with Ozumat and potentially Azshara, but whatever. Maybe in MoP. Aside from the opening cinematic, there wasn't any real display of power from Deathwing at all. Also, why were the resources used for a human model if it served no real purpose in the finale?

Looking back, it's like the entire expansion was just a really bad excuse to remake Azeroth. Granted, the objective was accomplished, the new world looks amazing, and is a fun experience. As a long time player, a lot of good things came into the expansion. As a raider though, I was thoroughly disappointed. 4.1 was a waste of time. 4.2 was fun, yes, but it was Ragnaros reinvented. 4.3, as I said above, was just a big letdown. I really can't help but feel that it spreads the message of "Oops, we messed up big time. Let's get this over with so we can move on."

Optimism tells me to look towards the future though. History has told me before that Blizzard will deliver. Regardless, I'll still be playing until they stop making content.
