I both agree and disagree with the OP. The fight does feel like a huge "here's some trash, guest-starring Deathwing" fight...rather than a boss fight. Phase 2 has no mechanics whatsoever other than switching back and forth between burning Deathwing's head and killing some adds...and maybe popping a defensive cooldown (Dream) periodically. I like the interactive feel of the first phase - choosing how to proceed - but the differences really are minimal no matter what platform you choose.

Still, I don't agree with the idea of different endings for different difficulties. People aren't taking into account that just because THEY can down heroic-difficulty bosses doesn't mean everyone - or even the majority of players - can. Letting all players have the ability to experience the end content has no bearing on whether or not you can see it on a higher difficulty - LFR difficulty wasn't made for those of us who aspire to beat it on Normal or Heroic. It was made for people who can't. I've run some LFRs, myself, and I've noticed that what seems face-rollingly easy to me is genuinely difficult for other players, particularly those players who've had no experience with raiding and leveled up in current content, which does nothing to prepare players for the level of awareness required for some of these fights.

If one were to compare what the upper-end raiders lose by allowing "lower-end" (for the sake of reference) raiders to beat Deathwing to what lower-end raiders lose by being unable to complete content because it is simply too difficult (or time-consuming, or whatever), it should be obvious that the lower-end raiders would lose out on more.

But maybe that's just because I never understood why elite raiders are so upset that other players can down the same content they can, if on a lower difficulty.
