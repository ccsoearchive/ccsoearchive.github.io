The OPs activity feed shows he is the type of player the LFR was designed for. I doubt he ever experienced heroic content until he outleveled and outgeared it. And that is fine. Not everyone has the time or inclination to take on heroic challenges when the challenges are relevant.

I can take it a step further and say TEN man heroic mode is the only fight that matters. In comparison, most 25 man heroic encounters are "easy" mode.

Where do you draw the line?

The answer is exactly where Blizz drew it with LFR. WotLK thru FL I had the time and the guild to complete normal and most heroic modes and achievements as a tank. Now, being able to login for an evening here and there and casually experience the end of the storyline is AWESOME.

Also, many casuals, as well has hard mode players, have multiple alts (and in my case multiple accounts). LFR's (lack of) difficulty level is perfect for gearing said alts and experiencing raid encounters with other classes without the need of a dedicated guild.

Of course LFR was crazy easy, especially for my experience and gear. I am OK with that. It was aggravating enough to scream at the 2nd tank repeatedly when an occasional mechanic required a taunt switch.

(Note to Blizz: avoid taunt switches due to stacking debuffs in future LFRs. People with little raid experience aren't looking at debuffs, nor do they have boss mods to tell them what is happening.)

This was Blizz's first pass at the LFR concept. And I say they hit the nail solidly on the head. Feedback about your enjoyment of a specific encounter (and its ending) will probably be welcomed so they can fine-tune the concept.

QQing about LFR not being Heroic? Worthless!

Vast numbers of paying customers getting to experience the resolution of the overarcing storyline of an expansion? Priceless!
