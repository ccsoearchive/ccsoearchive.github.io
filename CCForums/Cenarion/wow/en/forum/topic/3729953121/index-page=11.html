There is a reason that games with multiple endings and different difficulty 'game over' scenes were so popular. REPLAY VALUE!

getting ending A to ending C to ending #10 all took you playing the same game, but with a different strategy, character sets, story progression, harder bosses, or you being limited, amongst other things. and the reward was sometimes better items, yes. But seeing more of the story was why we did things like that. Ragnaros was a great example. When I beat him in normal mode on my paladin, I was all "wait, that's it?! guess i need to do heroic to see the full ending" ...sort of how i felt when I first saw the ending for the Dragon Soul raid. "wait...that's all that happens?"

Someone mentioned the Lich King battle and how it felt more complete when we finally did put him down. It was also a harder encounter.(reportedly, i didn't get there at all myself) so it felt like 'the end'.

Playing through Maverick hunter X on Hard mode, there was different dialogue, the story sort of made more sense, and you felt more accomplished doing it all with bosses that are faster, stronger, have more attacks, and you can only have the base health bar from the beginning (no life-up items, limiter).

Right now i'm trying to get my warrior into a guild that raids DS. and...having already seen the 'true ending', i'm not all that excited about doing it on harder difficulties really.

Just seems like after Ulduar and Icecrown, the raid/story designers have gotten soft. :(

You, sir, win the internet.

Though, my issue isn't even with LFR: it's that DW's death was so unspectacular that I felt compelled to say something on the forums about it.

Seriously, he didn't die fighting. He died PANTING FROM EXHAUSTION...

The most baddass, epic, deadly creature in all of WoW lore died like a fat man after a 100 meter dash...

There are no words...


So here's the thing: This expansion, instead of the players being the heroes who thwart the big bad guy, the players actually aid Thrall and the other dragon aspects (who are the heroes) in defeating the big bad guy.

The entire encounter with death wing is of the players playing the support role/distraction while thrall gives DW his best shot.

After which, isn't enough to completely kill Deathwing (because he's so bad !@#) and it's up to us players to finish him off.

Of course he still puts up a fight, and in the end, it's like Arnold Swarchenegger (us players) riding the crank lift to put a 40 mm grenade into T1000's (deathwing) chest to mess him up, causing him to fall into the boiling pool of steel (the maelstrom).

Was a good ending? Yes. It was very fitting. Could they have staged the fight in a manner that shows more destruction of azeroth and a compelling reason for players to take on more of a leading role in the fight?

I don't think so. Environment destruction is really intense, and it would take a lot for them to make it palpable for a 10-25 players to handle at once while still conveying the dramatic feel of it. If it were a single player game? Sure, environmental destruction is easy to handle. But because this is an MMO and you need 9-24 other people to be able to perform their role while staying on the same page as you, no, I don't think it would have worked out well.

Outside of that, everything you do as a player this expansion, is part of the storytelling. They did a really good job of shifting the story telling from the last raid of the expansion into the entire experience starting at level 80 and leveling your way to 85.

On top of that, Deathwing is the Aspect of Death. He breathes destruction and chaos. He's not going to put on this huge dramatic dying sequence when you finally kill him. No, instead he's going to depart almost as abruptly as he came.

So when I read your OP and saw the point you were trying to make, using the LFR as a scapegoat for the 'blandness' of the encounter, I found it kinda silly that you would make a case for your opinion on the story telling when it seems like you just ignored most of it, deciding to pay attention only during the 3rd stage of the fight.
