Celinne, you might not like this. Sorry.

(My) Mut perspective lands only on the first two tiers.

Reducing damage of Mutilate (and Hemo) and buffing again in Combat tier 1 doesn't just increase the desirability of Combat off-spec, but also reduces it for Subtlety off-spec. Really, it isn't needed. If you look at Sub tier 1, there aren't any buffs for Mutilate, so Combat tier 1 doesn't need them to be attractive.

Instead, compare tier 2 talents Puncturing Wounds, Aggression, and Opportunity. Some abilities are missing and damage buffs are not the same. If we want to balance tiers 1 and 2 of all three specs for off-speccing, then we need to follow a few simple rules.

(1) Every talent that improves a damaging opener, improves all damaging openers.
(2) Every talent that improves a damaging cp builder, improves all damaging cp builders.
(3) Every talent that improves a damaging finisher, improves all damaging finishers.
(4) Damage increases in one tree are approximately equal to those in other trees.
(5) A talent that improves multiple abilities does not have to improve multiple abilities equally.

There is one more rule that needs adding, specifically in regards to Combat tier 1, but to a lesser extent to the other two trees too.

(6) Each tree tier 1 must have at least 5 points worth taking for Rogues playing each aspect of the game.

That is, right now Precision is only good for Heroic Mut (and Heroic Sub? Do they exist?) Rogues, and Improved Sinister Strike is useless.

That's a lot of TL;DR, so I apologize for the rest of this. Lets look at some problem talents individually.


Coup de Grace: Gets the main two, but misses Deadly Throw.
Lethality: A small damage buff only useful to crit builds.
Mut Tier 1 Issues? There aren't enough talents in tier 1 attractive to PvP builds.

Improved Recuperate: Great talent, only one in Combat tier 1 taking for most Rogues, but is complemented by Opportunity and Elusiveness (survival talents). Should probably be in Combat tier 2 (which makes this harder).
Improved Sinister Strike: Useless to non-Combat. Should either be removed and baked into Combat as a whole (and replaced), or swapped into Combat tier 2.
Precision: Useful mainly for Mut Heroics, leaves out most everyone else.
Issues? Combat's tiers are nearly completely backwards!

Improved Ambush: Is missing Garrote, anyone think extra damage and crit on a Rogue bleed is a problem?
Issues? Besides Relentless Strikes, there are not enough talents in Sub tier 1 attractive to PvE builds.

Puncturing Wounds: Missing Sinister Strike (and arguably, Hemo).
Blackjack: This could easily be pushed into tier 1.
Issues? Not a lot, Mut tier 2 is pretty solid, "only" missing 2 primary Rogue cp builders.

Improved Slice and Dice: Good talent, could be safely moved to tier 1.
Aggression: 7/14/20% where Opportunity is 10/20/30%. Also missing Mutilate, Hemo, Envenom, and Deadly Throw.
Improved Kick: Nice talent, could easily be moved to tier 1.
Issues in Combat tier 2? Inequality in Aggression, and again, Combat's tiers are nearly backwards.

Waylay: Can't be anywhere else, too bad it's useless for Mut/Combat.
Opportunity: Missing Sinister Strike (and arguably Hemo).
Initiative: Useless to Mut and Combat, and many argue to Sub.
Issures? Mostly just Opportunity missing 2 primary Rogue abilities

[EDIT: I wish I knew what to do with some of those, but I don't have a suggestion for every issue]

Overall, talents are either in the wrong place or unbalanced in damage or abilities modified. Besides just swapping them around between tier 1 and 2, some either need to be replaced, swapped into a higher (3+) tier, or given a buff for the other two trees (looking at you Improved Sinister Strike, Waylay, and Initiative).

Finally, rule 6 above would be served better if each tree's tier 1 had 4 talents, and tier 2 had only three: one survivability (Quickening, Improved Recuperate, Elusiveness), one damaging (Puncturing Wounds, Aggression, Opportunity), and one something really really nice/desireable (Ruthlessness, I don't know, and I don't know).

OK, so I gave you my llllllllooooooooonnnnnnnggggg (Mut) opinion on off-speccing, can I tell you what I don't like about Combat?

I don't like auto-damage in general, so the whole Combat Potency (and Main Gauche) thing bothers me. The problem is that it builds continuously off everything else. Haste and attack speed buffs (SnD, Lightning Reflexes, Adrenaline Rush's speed buff) all play into both Combat Potency AND into Main Gauche (which increases with mastery) which itself feeds back into Combat Potency. Then, Adrenaline Rush doubles at least base+Vitality+haste (if not Combat Potency's) energy regeneration.

It's no wonder why Combat has energy capping issues. Each level of mastery and new tier's worth of haste multiplies (sometimes more than once over) into the whole thing. In other words, it keeps Combat increasingly spammy, but only while Combat is sitting there banging away on auto-pilot. It's annoying as hell, and I have no ideas on how to fix it. Maybe Combat should be the auto-attack damage spec?


Like most others, I don't like Bandit's Guile. It's an unnecessary ramp-up mechanic on a class that already has two of excessive length. You had a good suggestion for swaps. Another good one I heard is allowing players to force the issue with Revealing Strike. Give each tier of Guile a duration (they said 10s, I imagine it'd need to be longer) and allow Revealing Strike (or another ability?) to force Guile into the next highest tier.

Of course, the faster you get there, the more you're wasting getting there and the faster it would fall off again. It would however make burst in PvP a little more possible, requiring player input for set-up instead of just player timing of game-generated mechanics.


Talking about player/game generated mechanics, I hate how Killing Spree is designed (we've had this discussion). Yes, it's pretty, but you set it up and press a button and it runs itself for 2.5 seconds. I like player input. If I were to redesign it, I'd:
- Remove the "invulnerable" buff.
- Give it a time limit (say, 6 seconds)
- Give it 5 charges and allow players to decide when to jump by hitting the button again.
- Allow players to make other strikes between jumps.
- Seriously consider (you're suggestion) Blade Flurry on/off for multiple/single targets.


So here's what I love about Combat:

Restless Blades is an extremely well thought out alternative to a reset button (Prep). I think Evasion should be on it (but not on say, Reinforced Leather). I'd consider your "offensive finishers" suggestion, but I'm not sure how it'd play out. Hopefully, Blizzard will give it a shot on their internal servers.

Throwing Specialization is pretty cool, unfortunately Deadly Throw costs cp's, allowing only Sub Rogues to use it without first hitting someone in melee range. I think enough baseline damage could be given it by simply adding it to Coup de Grace and Aggression. However, I'd consider the "double's damage of Deadly Throw" only in the case of "when it hits a player in the back."

Why? Two reasons. First, because it's counter-able (and abilities SHOULD BE). Second, because it's in the freakin' back! It's got a lot of class appeal, a lot of feel that I think would complement WoW quite well. (In the same way, I'd love to see WoW weather change when Shaman dance, or Resto Druids be able to turn into a more realistic looking tree).

I like how Combat's control in Blade Twisting, Improved Gouge, and Revealing Strike is unique. As much as I'd love these abilities as Mut, I think it's good that they set Combat apart.

Finally, I like the stand-up & fight playstyle. I wish Reinforced Leather improved this. Deadened Nerves is in a lot of ways a better talent, though I agree that the Combat talent should increase armor vice reduce damage (make a Rogue a little more like a Warrior, though not completely). Maybe what Reinforced Leather should do is give a larger armor bonus, OR allow chain (though it's not very Roguish), or provide spikes (a sort of "Retribution Aura"), or something more than what it does. Unfortunately, like Initiative, it's just not quite useful enough.


I don't know if that helps you any, but that's what I like and dislike about Combat. Some of your suggestions compliment that, some oppose, and some neither.
