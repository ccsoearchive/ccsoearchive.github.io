Well, from a Combat PvP rogue's point of view, it's not really the damage I deal that I'm supposed to be focusing on, but rather the control I'm applying on enemy players / teams. And I do feel it when I land a 9 second Kidney Shot, or watch a healer die because I jumped out of nowhere and hit him with Improved Kick, or save someone else's life thanks to Improved Gouge.
The damage buffs are there to remedy Combat's ( more precisely Sinister Strike's ) extremely low damage in PvP, because no matter how much control I have, I should also have enough damage to actually kill someone who's at 10% Hp when I'm out of DPS cooldowns, and not spam Sinister Strike to watch it's damage outhealed by Recuperate or some other weak over-time heal.

The Armor Penetration buffs ( In the new mastery, Blade Twisting and Glyph of killing Spree ) are also there to remedy the problem that the one spec that relies the most on physical damage has the least ways to deal with armor.
Other class / specs that are largely affected by armor would be Subtlety rogues and Warriors. However, when dealing with armor, there are two ways to proceed: Let's take for example guns vs armor:

1- Get bigger, more powerful bullets. ( Higher damage per strike, not DPS )
2- Get special armor-piercing bullets. ( Not as strong as the big bullet, but designed specifically to penetrate armor )

Compared to Combat rogues, warriors and sub rogues have both bigger bullets AND armor-piercing bullets. Both of them have attacks that deal much more damage-per-strike than Combat and tools to get around armor.


So yes, in the end, it's all tweaks. Tweaks that should be, and should have been in the game long ago.

Looking over your proposed changes. You seem to want to force Assassination and Subtlety to spec into Combat by nerfing their abilities and "restoring" the nerfs by specing into Combat.

Improved Sinister Strike, renamed to Improved Strikes: Increases the damage dealt by Sinister Strike, Hemorrhage and Mutilate by 30%, and reduces the energy cost of Sinister Strike by 2 /4 /6 energy. ( Hemorrhage and Mutilate base damage reduced by approximately 15-20%, so that they are not too strong with the talent, but not penalized too hard for not having it, but let's be serious, Hemorrhage is much stronger than it needs to be already )

Aggression: Increases the damage dealt by your Sinister Strike, Mutilate, Backstab, Rupture and Eviscerate 10 / 20 / 30%.


What you've done with these 2 talents is reduced Mutilate by 15-20%, added 30% damage from Improved Strikes and another 30% from Aggression, (also making it impossible to get Opportunity -30% damage). What you gain is approximately 6% damage to Mutilate (100%-15%)*160%(damage bonus of 3/3 IS and 3/3 Agg), and you lose 2.5% Mutilate damage specing 2/3 Aggression.

Backstab and Rupture stay the same (Aggression replaces Opportunity) and can only spec 2/3 Relentless Strikes.

As an Assassination Rogue I think you should keep Backstab, Mutilate, and Ruopture bonus damage on the same talent (for PvE) either Improved Strikes or Aggression. That way it will be equal to Opportunity.

With the Poison hit cap at 17% you haven't given any Assassination Rogue a reason to stop specing 2/3 Precision, and 3/3 Relentless Strikes (80/100% chance to regain 25 energy on finishers is too good). So if you don't change Mutilates damage and you add 30% damage for Backstab, and Mutilate to Improved Strikes, Assassination rogues are left with a choice between 2 points in either Nightstalker, Improved Slice and Dice, Improved Kick, Improved Sprint, or Aggression (without Mutilate, Backstab, and Rupture bonuses). I would probably spec Improved Slice and Dice (PvE) and Improved Sprint (PvP).

Added: Having talents in Combat and Subtlety that increase poison damage dosen't feel right.
