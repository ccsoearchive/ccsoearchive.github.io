Two reasons:

1. Because most of the time there is something else worth reforging, e.g. Hit or Expertise.

2. Because at their gear levels they actually experience non-trivial diminishing returns. So with those kinds of +dodge and +parry, they want to keep Parry and Dodge within 1-2% of each other, with parry always leading.

Example: My tanking gear has 51.5% block, 11.5% dodge and 14.5% parry. Reason? Hold the line of course, but also because the diminishing returns from 12-15% parry are negligible so I'd rather have more parry. Sure, I lose a LITTLE to diminishing returns, but it's made up for by HTL.

A lot of people will say keep Parry within 1 % of dodge. I don't think it matters that much, as the diminishing returns shown for parry/dodge in the low-teens (10-15%) are negligible.

Say you have 12.5 dodge and 12.5 parry. You could go 13/12 an experience no diminishing returns, or you could go maybe 14.8/10 (lost .2 due to DR... that's an example, not a real DR calculation).

You are losing 0.07% avoidance by reforging to parry. Since that amount is negligible and parry is worth more than dodge, I would choose the latter option of reforging to parry.

I'll agree with Dellingr for the most part, but I happen to despise Leaden Despair. The proc is near useless. If you're a tank and sub-35% health in a raid, an extra 3k armor isn't going to do anything (I speak in generic terms).

Since you insist on stacking Stamina in your gems (wrong), Throngus's finger becomes even MORE valuable.

If you're the off-tank for raids and you have Add duty, you want your mitigation as high as possible. If you've got 9 Aberrations hitting you, your block/dodge/parry chance becomes much more important than if you have a single raid boss...

On the flipside, if you're tanking a raid boss, generally, as long as you have ~75-77% total mitigation, extra mastery won't help much. In that case, total health pool becomes more valuable.

Example: on Maloriak I have add duty. Our MT uses a Steelskin flask and his Stamina trinkets... he has ~175k health. I use an Elixir of the Master and my mastery trinkets, so I have 158k health but 78% total mitigation without shield block PLUS a proc chance for mastery and a proc chance for Parry.

But generally speaking, stacking Stam as your gems at sub-359 ilvl is frowned upon.
