No. This goes back to the EHT vs Avoidance argument. Especially with Cata and the changes to healer mana and mechanics, as a warrior mastery is your most valuable rating. Block evens out the spikyness of your incoming damage. With avoidance stacking you may not take some hits, but the hits you do take will cause your healer to use more mana to heal you back up to full in the fear that you may take another big hit again. Over the course of a fight you may take more total damage stacking mastery than avoidance but your healer is less likely to OOM.

You should be reforging your dodge into mastery and leaving your parry where it is due to its synergy via the Hold the Line talent.

-----------------------

In terms of this guide overall I'd have to say well done, very informative. I have a couple nitpicks/concerns. If the tone of your guide is for raiding I would disagree with you very strongly on your assessment of Safeguard. 15% Damage Reduction to the OT is 15% Damage Reduction. I would drop 2/2 BaT for the 2 points in Safeguard any day.

As far as your concern regarding Impending Victory goes, from a raiding standpoint the pervasive opinion among those pushing the edge of progression (not to say i'm one of those people by any means) seems to be that it ranges from a very very useful talent to have when a boss is sub 20% and healers are OOMing to an absolute must have. Whereas Vigilance and Concussion blow are not really necessary in any way in a single target raid boss tanking fight.

Other than that great guide :D

It is pretty baffling and hard to understand. Makes me wonder why they got rid of arp for being "too mathy".

Until you're near full raid gear, you're aiming for the "soft" block cap (77~%) without going over so you're reforging your stats around to accomplish this as effectively as possible. Further down the line, you'll be pushing towards the "hard" block cap, but for now you can ignore this.

For example: 16% Parry, 15% dodge, 46.4% block (Don't forget to take into account buffs, ie battle shout/kings)

Obviously this is hard to accomplish and a huge pain in the ass. Please don't quote those numbers as some ideal set, either. They're just an example.
