I believe there are some important missing PVE details from original post, hence Blizz is refraining from stickying it. While it may be just anecdotal, my own research and testing have taught me some things that I didn’t see in this thread. I’ve included some WHYs to explain my choices, because none of the info makes you a better tank if you can’t think and adapt for yourself.

FOR HEROICS:

Prioritization should be stam > mast > dodge = parry (with the edge to Dodge since you can’t Parry mobs from behind during those messy multi-mob pulls).

Your gems should be: blue=Solid Ocean Sapphire, red=Defender's Demonseye, yellow=Puissant Dream Emerald.
Reforging is not terribly important at this stage, but if you’re compelled to, reforge Expertise/Hit/Crit/Haste on items into Mastery or Parry/Dodge and enchant for Stamina and/or use Heavy Savage Armor Kit.

WHY? Heroics = multiple short fights where healers can drink between pulls. Endurance is not important, so stamina is still king.

FOR RAIDING:

If/when you get to around 145-150K+ hp unbuffed, change prioritization to Mastery > Parry > Dodge. Parry edges out Dodge because of Hold the Line talent, but keep them about 2-3% apart.

WHY? Stamina is normalized on gear now, so it can be largely ignored. Tank gear has the same Stamina as healer/dps gear for the same slot, i.e. your plate tanking belt has the same Stamina as the cloth healing belt. You will earn your bonus Stamina from trinkets, “obvious” enchants (like the helm, legs and shoulder enchants) and by virtue of choosing a tanking tree, which has an automatic health multiplier that scales very well with gear.

Wherever possible, reforge ALL your expertise/hit/crit/haste to Mastery. Reforge Parry<>Dodge as best as possible to keep Parry around 2-3% higher than Dodge.

WHY? Because Taunt cannot miss anymore, and threat is currently a non-issue once you’re hitting the right buttons. After 10-15s you’ll never lose aggro. The only exception to this is if you’re the only person doing mandatory interrupts. In that case you’ll need to be closer to the 8% hit cap.

Change your gems to: blue=Puissant Dream Emerald, red=Fine Ember Topaz, yellow=Fractured Amberjewel.
Enchanting: back=armor, chest=stamina, bracers=dodge, hands=mastery, boots=lavawalker (mastery+runspeed), shield=blocking.

Consumables: Eat Lavascale Minestrone. I also believe it was already stated correctly in the original post that the Elixir of the Master plus Prismatic Elixir combo is better than using Flask of Steelskin, but depending on the fight, replace Prismatic Elixir with Elixir of Deep Earth if there's very little magic damage being taken.

WHY? Stacking Mastery gives you greater endurance in 10-min boss fights because of the flat damage reduction it provides. And Mastery doesn’t have diminishing returns so the more you stack, the better it gets at making your incoming hits more even, more predictable and more manageable for healers, giving your healer’s hots and passive spells the most bang for the buck. Healer mana is a premium now and they CANNOT spam big expensive heals trying to top off your 240K health pool that’s taking 60K hits each swing. I can’t speak for every tanking type, but in Cataclysm, the prot warrior is designed around his SHIELD. How effective your BLOCKING is will separate the men from the boys in the end. That’s why Mastery is your new best friend.

Watch your Recount/Skada addons for Damage Taken totals on certain bosses and try to improve those numbers each week.

I’m not professing any of this to be the new reigning dogma, but it’s my current working formula and it may evolve as gear, content, experience and knowledge evolve, so caveat emptor.
