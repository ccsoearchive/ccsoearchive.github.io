Yes, I would advocate the same stat priorities for heroics. The reason for this is within my answer to the rest of your post:

"Running around landing no hits" is a bit of an exaggeration. Miss % is probably about 20-25% total (miss/dodge/parry) because there is still hit/exp on some of our items, and since we can't reforge everything away, there is residual hit/exp which helps us, and is all we need. [Edit: I'll admit that I have occasionally (maybe once every run depending on the dungeon) found myself with not enough rage to Thunderclap or something... in that case, you need to improvise and charge, heroic throw, taunt, intercept, intervene, heroic leap, etc. to round up the mobs].

Single-target vengeance/threat for warriors is almost never an issue. I can only remember maybe half a dozen times where a DPS has pulled aggro off of me in the first 10 seconds of a fight. If that's the case, I should have noticed a high-threat DPS by the time we get to the first boss, and I should have Vigilance on him already for insta-taunts.

From pack-to-pack Vengeance does indeed wear off, and multi-target aggro is, I admit, tricky... but it's not bad with some practice. Rend followed by TC = instant and recurring AOE aggro. Then, before the DPS can peel aggro off of you, you should be hitting your Shockwave to stun them. Once stunned, you can cleave, TC, and tab-target taunt them so that you maintain threat. Yes, this is more vulnerable to threat loss, but you have heroic throw to grab stragglers, taunt (and Vigilance may help here, also), and Intimidating Shout and Commanding Shout if you lose all control.

Mastery + Sta is good for EH against physical damage. But the only way to mitigate pure magic damage (other than CD's and raid buffs) is with raw HP.

This is why its nice to have extra HP especially if your going to be in front of a dragon-type boss that's going to blast you in the face with fire/arcane/frost.

As for mastery caps, what I understand is that the soft cap on mastery is when your block chance reaches 100%. Block chance reduces damage taken by 30% or 60% if its a critical block. Thats why parry is such an important sub-stat, because it has a chance to proc 'Hold the Line' which increases your critical block chance by 10%.

There's actually a nice forum about stat weighting here:

http://maintankadin.failsafedesign.com/forum/index.php?f=6&t=30504&rb_v=viewtopic

When you consider trinket stat weights though, consider this conclusion from the above post:

"Or in other words, it takes 7 armor to give you the same damage reduction as one point of mastery rating (or 1255 armor to match 1 point of mastery skill)."
