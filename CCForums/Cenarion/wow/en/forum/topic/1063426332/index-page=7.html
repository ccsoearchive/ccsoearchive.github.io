Updated for 4.0.6.

  • Spell power coefficients are still the old ones from 4.0.3a. I'll update those when I can sit down and figure them out.

  • Since Prayer of Mending now returns an error if the priest attempts to cast it at a target that already has a stronger Prayer of Mending, it should no longer be possible for the spell to fail to place the priest into Chakra: Sanctuary by failing to overwrite the stronger one. I'll test it just to make sure.

  • Lightwell probably still won't function at all with buffs that increase casting speed - just haste rating.

  • Someone pointed this out the other day, it seems the actual reason Renew gets another "mystery" tick when refreshed by Chakra: Serenity is because it sets Renew's base duration to 15 seconds, instead of 12. I wasn't sure how to go about testing it at first, so what I did was I took off all my gear and specced out of any haste talents so that Renew was a 12 second duration with a 3 second tick interval. Then I spammed Heal on myself while in Serenity. The duration that Renew was set to was always between 15 seconds and just under 18.

    Then I decided to try spam refreshing Renew while under the effect of a massive haste buff, for which I used the one from Altairus in VP. When periodic abilities reach huge amounts of haste their overall durations are only very, very slightly off from their original duration at 0% haste (Even when you add on the tick that's preserved by refreshing the spell), and sure enough Renew's duration was always set to about 15 seconds. I didn't test this on the PTR, so we'll see if it still does this here on live today. Ideally I'd like to try it after I've eaten a bunch of good food during the Captain Cookie encounter. This could also mean that Chakra: Serenity alters Renew's haste thresholds to that of a spell with 5 ticks at 0% haste, instead of 4.


If you see anything outdated other than those outdated spell power coefficients. that I forgot to take out/correct let me know.

I'll be honest with you, math isn't my best subject. I'm okay at it, though. For clarity I'm okay if the margin of error is only, say, a tenth or so.

The reason it's not a lot of work is because I can simply use a spreadsheet to do the work for me so that I don't have to sit at a dummy for long periods of time or fumble with a calculator/unclean numbers and risk making a silly math error. The only tedious work I actually have to do is finding a reasonably accurate median for spells that do x to y damage/healing rather than assuming that what the tooltip states is correct as it is often not.

For Shadow Word: Pain, my steps went more like this:

2175 spell power: 619*6 = 3714 total damage, and a 619 tick value.
189 spell power (naked): (255*3)+(256*3) = 1533 damage total. 1533/6 = 255.5 tick value.

So, I gained 1986 spell power which increased SW:P's tick value by about 363.5. Dividing the difference in tick value by the difference in spell power gives me a coefficient of 363.5/1986 = 0.183031, or 18.3%. While that's within my acceptable margin of error, I updated the OP. I'm thinking that when I ultimately have to redo the list for 4.1 that it would be a good idea to round to two digits though.
