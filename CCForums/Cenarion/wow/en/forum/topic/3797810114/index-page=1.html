We're not dps; we don't have breakpoints like that. Prior to block cap, mastery is always a warrior's best stat, and ought to be found natively on all their gear, or reforged into if not. That includes reforging into mastery on double avoidance pieces.

Post-cap, the survivability benefits of both pale in comparison to stamina, and the question becomes whether you chase additional mastery so you can maintain passive block cap while using a stamina trinket, or just regem everything towards stamina. I have done the math you are looking for OP.

This is the basic equation if you want to set it up long hand for a paladin (not gonna do warrior...you can if you want)

BV*2.25*X/179.28=((0.956*176.7189/(X+Y)+1/65.63144)^-1)-((0.956*176.7189/(Y)+1/65.63144)^-1)

Now for this calculation we want the point where 1 increase in rating of mastery is equal to one increase in avoidance. We also have to take into account the various block values. So:

X=1

Minimum BV=0.3
Maximum BV=0.5
Average BV=0.3667

The first one is regular blocking. 2nd is blocking with holy shield up. The last one is the average block value of using Holy shield on cd.

Solving for Y gives you the answer which in this case is

Y (Maximum BV)=always. Its always better. It would take negative rating to make it equal.
Y (minimum BV)=18.28% avoidance/2813rating.
Y (average BV)=12.76% avoidance/1487rating.


Plug this into your own spread sheet to double check cause this is too tedious of math to do by hand. After this point mastery is more damage reduction than dodge/parry until the block cap at which point mastery is worthless to paladins and nearly worthless to warriors.
