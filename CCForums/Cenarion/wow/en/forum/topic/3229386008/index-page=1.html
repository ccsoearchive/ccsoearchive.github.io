The set bonuses are pretty bad compared to most of the other classes. From a final tier of the expansion perspective, these are probably the worst ones we've ever had.

T3: (good at the time)
2 pieces: Your normal melee swings have a chance to Invigorate you, healing you for 90 to 110.
4 pieces: Your Backstab, Sinister Strike, and Hemorrhage critical hits cause you to regain 5 energy.
6 pieces: Reduces the threat from your Backstab, Sinister Strike, Hemorrhage, and Eviscerate abilities.
8 pieces: Your Eviscerate has a chance per combo point to reveal a flaw in your opponent's armor, granting a 100% critical hit chance for your next Backstab, Sinister Strike, or Hemorrhage.

T6:
2 pieces: Increases the haste from your Slice and Dice ability by 5%.
4 pieces: Increases the damage dealt by your Backstab, Sinister Strike, Mutilate, and Hemorrhage abilities by 6%.

T10:
2 pieces: Your Tricks of the Trade ability now grants you 15 energy instead of costing energy.
4 pieces: Gives your melee finishing moves a 13% chance to add 3 combo points to your target.

PERSONALLY, what I think might be better is if they didn't something like this for T13 set bonuses:

2 pc: After triggering Tricks of the Trade, your abilities cost 10% energy for 20 seconds
4 pc: While Shadow Dance, Adrenaline Rush, and Vendetta are active, your damage it increased by 10%.

This way, it would be similar to what the developers were wanting with t13 bonuses, only less terrible.

Both bonuses scream Subtlety. The 2pc is terrible for Combat. It's just begging to energy cap yourself, and then once you do it won't matter if you're receiving a discount on abilities or not. Self-negation in a set bonus, awesome. For assassination it's not bad by any means but not amazing. Sub, well couple that with the 4pc and..

The 4pc is terrible for assassination. We don't glyph Vendetta for a reason, it's hard enough to find 30 uninterrupted seconds on a target in a lot of fights. Add 6 to that? Even worse, 9, what a waste on anything that isn't tank & spank without a lot more strategic deployment than a simple plus % damage boost should take. 3 more seconds of Adrush, when we're going to have even more haste next tier? You're going to cap, and then you're going to negate part of the Adrush buff, just like the 2pc. When buffs start cancelling themselves out, that's an issue. But Sub, you couple significantly reduced ability cost with an increased ShD duration? There's just no downside whatever. Unlike the other two specs which each have negatives weighing down the positive aspects of these bonuses.

Maybe these are more placeholder than finished product. Maybe there's some other class tweaks yet to tumble down the pipeline. It's really early, and I don't want to go all Chicken Little on this. But as it stands right now with the information at hand. it seems Sub is getting thrust forward by virtue of holding back Assassination and Combat. Take all that with a grain of salt and apply critical thinking. If rash develops, consult your local physician.
