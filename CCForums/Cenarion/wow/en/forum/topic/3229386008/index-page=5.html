My only real complaint is that it's another tricks related bonus. They got rid of hunger for blood because it was a button you hit every 30 seconds to maintain your dps output. Now tricks is a button you hit every 30 seconds to maintain your dps output. Correlation anyone?

Seriously, since the threat transfer aspect of tricks of pretty much a non factor, why don't they either reduce tricks' power and make it a permanent buff we stick on someone to buff their dps by 2 or 3 percent; OR, increase the cooldown (significantly) and make the buff fairly powerful. Buffing someone's dps by a whopping 2% by hitting a button on cooldown isn't that fun, being able to increase someone's burst by buffing them by 30%+ but only every few minutes changes tricks from a minor buff lost in RNG to a powerful burst buff to be coordinated with fight mechanics rather than used on cooldown. Longer cooldowns also tend to encourage saving that cooldown. In a 5 minute fight you can use a 3 min cooldown twice. This actually leaves you roughly a minute to a minute and a half leeway on when to use it without wasting a possible use - something to think about to say the least.

Finally they could just scrap tricks and take it in a different direction entirely (redirect incoming damage to new target usuable on medium length cooldown sounds interesting to me).

lol Whats wrong with Combat?! It's the top performing spec currently.

So, T13 4pc gives 3 extra seconds on Adrenaline Rush, our strongest cooldown. The glyph of Adrenaline Rush, which gives 5 extra seconds, is worth about 815 dps. So the 4pc would be worth 489 dps or so for Combat, give or take, which isn't bad at all. EJ projects it at 587 DPS for Combat.

The 2pc would have to be used correctly, such as at points where you aren't energy capping. But, it's still a relatively strong bonus. To get a better idea about it, I'll quote Aldriana from EJ:

The 2pc reduces the energy cost of abilities by 20% while active; this effectively gives us 25% more energy to spend (1 / (1-.2) = 1.25). It has 20% uptime, so thus gives us about 5% more energy to spend (more, if you pool to optimize the buff - but lets ignore that for the moment). Hence, the set bonus is equivalent to roughly a 5% energy regen increase.
