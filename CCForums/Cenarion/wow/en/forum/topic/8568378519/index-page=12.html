This response is to the OP. Hopefully you read this.

Durumu is a good example of a boss with a lot of abilities. I can imagine how a beginner level player would have issues with this boss, there is a lot going on no matter what your role is. You will probably get CRUSHED by the Eye Beam the first time. You might even die a couple more times to that til you learn not to, one or two weeks down the road. On the opposite end of the spectrum, lets say you are a seasoned raider of many years...even Durumu with new and original abilities is pretty predictable, so generally we know what is bad, and what is good.

My short term memory is terrible, but I rarely just forget about one of any bosses many, many abilities.

The abilities in Throne of Thunder LFR are forgiving. There is no DPS check and enrage is non-existent.

That being said, I believe most LFR raiders (casuals) will have pretty much mastered all the abilities after 3 weeks of repeat killing each boss. You can expect LFR to be pretty damn painful the first week. What "casual" reads icyvein boss guides, watches video guides? Realistically can't expect the people you run into in LFR to be able to master a boss without even seeing it, or even seeing it once.

Week 1, wing 1 of Throne of thunder was terrible, lots of wipes. Now? 1 shot on all bosses with, just god aweful dps and heals.

Let's use my logic on normal or heroic level raids. My 25man guild has killed Durumu 3 times now on 25 normal, we are generally average-above average players. We raid very little time each week, only 7.5 hrs per week. Week 1 was full of wipes to things we would consider really stupid in week 3. By week three we had no wipes and zero deaths of course. The ability to perform in LFR - normal seems to be consistent, but now dps actually matters and any mistakes will kill you instantly, unlike in LFR where some instant kills will simply just stun you for 3 seconds.

I feel like the the 30 players on the roster in my guild are probably on average about the same as the random people you will see in LFR in terms of mental capacity. The big difference is that we are a tight knit group obviously.

I hope the intention of Blizzard is to give even casual LFR raiders the feeling that they are doing something truly epic, and not just some mundane task that a 25man heroic raider like yourself is doing, just because they HAVE to for their main raid.

Bosses typically have some kind of fire to be avoided, some sort of tank swap, adds that need to be killed, and possibly a debuff to be managed. These mechanics have been around in some form since vanilla and are the cause of most wipes. Aside from these basic mechanics, most bosses really only have 2 or 3 others that your role needs to be concerned with (on normal).

Counting items in the dungeon journal is a terrible way to measure complexity, since most mechanics are just variants of the basic things I listed above, and many others amount to "you will take unavoidable damage from time to time".
