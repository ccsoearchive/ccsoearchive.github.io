I agree with the OP in that the demands of WoW raid fights now, for whatever reason-be it the increasing proliferation of boss mods, the increasing general skill base of raiders, whatever-have, in this tier, passed beyond the realm of complex and entered the realm of overly complicated for no apparent reason other than "add this cheap mechanic right now to make the fight harder." Jin'rokh is in theory a simple fight, until you get into it and see things like the lightning balls being put on one of your healers just before lightning storm goes off and you wonder who just thought a cheap shot like that was a good idea, or the sheer genius of the tank debuff move ALSO doing raid wide damage. Every fight has one or two abilities added to it that seem to be there just to make things much complicated, and removing them wouldn't make the fights cakewalks.

Throne of Thunder is the largest raid since ICC, and I'd hazard a guess that a quarter of ToT has more encounter mechanics to learn than ALL of ICC. That's faintly ridiculous. The raid design team is mistaking complicated for complexity, and forgetting that sometimes all a fight needs is one single, well designed ability to make it a challenge. Look at the Lich King. The raid wiper wasn't "six people getting killed at once by an AOE while the tank dies from mistiming a taunt on the fourteen adds that attacked during subphase 2." It was "oops, stood in Defile." I think Blizzard took the criticism of the relative simplicity of Dragon Soul-and yes, it was relative, on servers where there aren't a lot of heavy progression raiding, people were still earning Destroyer's End when the buff was at its max-and in typical fashion let the pendulum swing too far the other way.

And I'm not even going to get into the brain rattling stupidity of the purple on purple with purple telling you where to go of Durumu's maze. That's a special breed of stupid on its own.


Other than Durumu, what mechanics do you need to know as a dps in LFR?

Primordius, kill adds till a lizard then kill boss.
Dark Animus, aoe. Run away with crimson wake.

I see a lot of arguments being made about Blizzard assuming people are using DBM/BigWigs/etc. I agree that that's what they're doing, and it's a problem. You literally cannot be successful at end-game raiding without a boss alert mod. Not only that, but Blizzard is mixing necessary responses to partially remove the 'advantage' of a boss mod. How many fights can you think of that have two special abilities, one requiring the raid to stack up and the other to split up?

Since Vanilla, raid mechanic complexity has completely gone out of control. If I traveled back to 2005 and showed Younger-Me a video of Durumu, Younger-Me would say 'forget it' and go back to playing Final Fantasy. The impact doesn't really hit most of the dedicated raiders because we've been raiding for years. I've spent more time (in terms of years elapsed and man-hours invested) learning how to raid than I spent getting a degree in biology. What Blizzard is completely missing is the segment of NEW RAIDERS.

Imagine your younger brother or sister picks up WoW for the first time next week. Give them a month to hit 90, then toss them into MV 25 normal. Try to get them to remember everything they have to do to not die on Stone Guard. Move out of purple pools (easy). Move away from blue crystals that spawn (ok). If you get chained to somebody else, stay close to them (ok how do I know where that other person is going to be moving to?). Use damage reduction cooldowns to help survive overloads (how do I know when an overload is coming? what's DBM?). And by the way, you can move away from the person you're chained to and stand near blue crystals, but only when the right boss is gaining energy (huh?). Oh, and keep all this in mind while doing your normal dps or healing thing (uh...). God help you if you're a tank. Actually, you can be a newbie OR you can be a tank...you can't be both at the same time.

People make fun of 'LFR heroes', but it's Blizzard's fault that those people exist. If I were starting off in the game today, I'd be utterly confused by the boss fights and probably ostracized by other raiders for 'being a bad' (when did adjectives turn into nouns anyway?) because I lacked thousands of hours worth of practice. Blizzard seems to treat every raid tier as though everybody running it has done every tier previously, so they add ever-increasing amounts of complexity. It's gotten to the point where actually explaining the mechanics of a new fight can take upwards of 15 minutes, and that's just on normal mode. New folks can get really intimidated by the complexity; LFR doesn't actually teach them anything because the mechanics are nerfed to the point of being ignorable.

The thing about increasing memory demands is that rarely is everything in the DJ even relevant to your particular role. There are so many things you can simply ignore on any given encounter depending on what class you play or how your raid is approaching things.

A lot of mechanics are also recycled and should be ingrained in peoples memories anyway. Instead of a red void zone that does fire damage, you now deal with a bigger void zone that does frost damage.

In a world where they disabled add-ons or raid alerts actually supported by the game I might be inclined to agree. But current raiding is nothing like many memory straining exercises we take in school (exams, tests, whatever). Vanilla raiding was like taking a history test on your particular state. Current raiding is like taking a history test on your entire country, except it's open book and they told you three quarters of the country wouldn't be on the test.

Experiencing something firsthand is a way better way to learn than simply reading a strategy though. For most encounters you can safely go into it telling people to not stand in stuff that looks dangerous. (fire, frost, poison, lightning).

Yeah but I just don't see this as necessary. There are 10's or maybe 100's of thousands of people that already down content as is - it's hard for me to swallow this memory demand argument when people are already blowing these raids out of the water. A quick look at my history will tell you that no, I haven't done it, but i'm 99% sure I can if I put the time in. That's the crux of the problem - it doesn't need to get easier or capped at a certain number of things to remember. If anything, I would encourage Blizz to stretch those imaginary boundaries a bit in the name of a challenge.

Also, although fights all have multiple mechanics, it's pretty rare that there are a huge # of them all to remember at once. Breaking fights down into stages allows one to remember a lot easier which mechanic belongs to which phase of the fight. Your brain learns to associate certain mechanics with certain phases of the fight. For example, in Elegon I know that twice/fight I'm going to need to GTFO out of the middle. I also know that I'm going to need to dps down my pillar and dps down adds immediately after the floor drops out.

None of these mechanics constitute something I'm constantly aware of and watching out for every 10 or 15 seconds in the raid. Once learned, those major mechanics like the disappearing floor or dpsing the pillars become second nature, and something that most raiders push to the back of their mind as they focus on other things that DO require constant attention (stacks on Elegon for example).
