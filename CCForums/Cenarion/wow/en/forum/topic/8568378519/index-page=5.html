People can learn it, but it's not really intuitive, and it could have been better just in terms of the fact that the maze smoke is about the same luminance as the ground and that makes it really hard on the human visual processing system and slows down reaction times.

Yes, and in this case, if learning the boss was faster, people wouldn't wipe on purpose.

Keeping in mind that the buff was their way of compensating for difficult fights where people would drop out of LFR entirely and give up, of course abusing the buff is faster than sitting around an hour waiting for people to join after others had dropped.

Just removing the buff makes people have to choose between just not running the LFR or dealing with the LFR taking hours to complete that they may not have before their baby wakes up or whatever real life time constraints prevent them from spending 3 hours on one LFR boss every week.

So, making fights that are intuitive to learn quickly, but still difficult and challenging for the average LFR raid, is something that Blizzard may never quite find the right balance for. How do we make it such that people won't just give up and drop raid after the first wipe? My solution would be to have a limited number of mechanics that are intuitive and easy to learn, but still allow some room for complexity. In some cases, that may mean dropping mechanics from the normal version (maybe entire phases), but still allowing heroic-mode raiding to tax the extent of your abilities to wipe over and over again until you learn it (and having normal-mode fall somewhere in the middle).
