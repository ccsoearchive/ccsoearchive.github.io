The OP makes some good points but I have to disagree with her stance on LFR. LFR was meant to be a way for people to "see" the content in a "raid" setting without having to be committed to a certain schedule etc and was not meant to be the free loot fest it is now. I'm going to say this before I go further, understand that despite what my tone may suggest this is not meant to be a swipe at the OP or anyone in particular.

Heaven forbid people actually have to be conscious and do a little work for their loot in LFR or in general. Personally I think LFR is one of the greatest disasters to befall this game just behind CRZ. If they actually required people to put forth some amount of effort then LFR would have its uses but it does nothing in its current form but teach people to put in the least amount of effort possible to get free loot. This has caused that attitude to bleed over into normal raiding as well. The only thing I've seen out of LFR is people who will leave after a single wipe because it's "too hard" or complain because they can't just sit there and pull substandard dps and get loot.

The facts are there are just some people out there who simply for whatever reason, aren't able to handle and aren't cut out for certain difficulty levels. No matter how often you lower the bar there are going to be those certain people who can't make the cut, some through no fault of their own other than they can't do it, and some because they refuse to learn and get better. As for a game I'm not cut out to play it would be Halo. I can run the story mode or goof around with friends but I'm just not cut out to play that game. Same thing with wow. there are just some people who can't handle raiding or much more than questing or 5 mans. some people are that way through no fault of their own and some are that way because they refuse to learn and get better. Lowering the bar won't fix that as there will always be people who don't make the cut which is just a fact of life and not just video games. Lowering the bar does nothing for the game but teach those people to wait for the nerf instead of giving them something to work for. Either they're going to get better and as such the playerbase be better off for it or they're not going to make the cut and not much is lost. I hate to say it that way but it's the truth. If someone isn't going to put in a little effort I don't want them in my raids but if you're at least willing to learn and you show me that by hanging in there then I can work with that.

Overall you're not helping the playerbase or the game with the constant lowering of the bar but instead teaching people to just wait for the nerf and hurting your playerbase in the long term. There is a difference between nerfing something for being game breaking and nerfing to lower the bar. When you constantly lower the bar you're going to get to the point where you can't lower it anymore then what do you do when you still have people who don't make the cut? Where does it stop and when is the bar so low that you've irreparably damaged your player base as a result? I'm not saying stuff should be so hard that only 5 people in the world can do it but it shouldn't be so easy you can afk for 20 minutes come back and get loot. You can only lower the bar so far and I'm concerned if it's lowered much more that there will be no hope for this game.

The thing is - the Human brain is extremely good at pattern recognition. So good, that people throw up false positives all over the place and see patterns where they don't exist.

"1 Wastewalker jumps down, then 2 Wastewalkers jump down 20 seconds later" isn't a complex enough pattern that even not awesome human beings can't "get". They may take a couple of pulls to get it, but they will get it.

Similarly - interweaving the pattern of mechanics on Horridon to "Here's little adds, a big add jumps down, move out of stuff, kill X" is a simple pattern that doesn't take much for Humans to get. It's on the level of decisions people take every day with no difficulty while driving.
