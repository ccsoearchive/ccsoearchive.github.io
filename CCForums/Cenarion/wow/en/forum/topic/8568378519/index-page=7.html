Unlike other posters I think the encounter will trend towards doing it correctly, because if you can beat it with 2-3 stacks it is manifestly much faster than just praying to be able to zerg it, but there will definitely be the odd groups that continue to death zerg it.

I agree with this. What the buff does is it smoothes out the LFR difficulty curve over time. Last tier if a fight wasn't killable for a group because the group was subpar or the group was unable to figure out the correct strategy the group just died. This tier the buff makes things killable those first few weeks until LFR groups learn/figure out proper strategies. Within a few weeks I don't think you'll see people determination zerging durumu, the maze is much larger than normal and once you actually have someone guide you through it it'll be pretty easy to see going forward.

Make bad mechanics influence individual loot chance, without causing raid wipes.

Also I think this idea has a lot of potential. The trick is to apply it to things that are blatant enough and minimally griefable. Things like taking more than X damage from avoidable mechanic or triggering a stomp on garalon could be easily measured and would encourage correct execution.

Based on what?

Bear in mind that for every Normal mode fight that actually has several lines, you're talking about either multiple phases or split-raid-roles.

E.g., for someone doing the Nests on Ji'kun, your care point is
- When Nests spawn, go down and kill them.
- Come back up.
- Don't get taken out by Down Draft.
- (Optional) Intercept Feed

Talon Rake, Infected Wounds, Caw, and even Feed since it's entirely optional in Normal mode isn't really something they are ever going to care to memorize.
In Multi Phase fights, they become simply things where only one mechanic ever actually matters at any given time. For Horridon it's

- Move out of Charge/Double Swipe
- Door 1: Kill WAstewalkers, don't stand in Stand Trap
- Door 2: Kill Venom Priests, don't stand in Poison, maybe chuck an interrupt
- Door 3: Kill little things, move out of Orbs (probably never have to do this later on)
- Door 4: Kill Flame casters, Kill Beast Shaman, (optional) Don't stand in front of Bears.
- Kill War God
- Kill Horridon

This isn't exactly complex stuff for an adult to handle.

But if you're actually trying to imply that this is a trend, you're barking up the wrong tree. We've always had many multi phase fights with several mechanics. Kael would take up SEVERAL notches on the Dungeon Journal tree.
One of the easiest fights in BC - postrework Solarian, would sit there with a longer DJ entry than Lei Shen or Durumu.

Well, Kael in TK was the 17 point boss in the data spreadsheet, just for the record. My argument wasn't that Kael in TK was easy, or that end-bosses have always been mechanically easy. In fact, it's not the maximum difficulty that shows the trends for a real increase over time, but the moving average for the raid dungeons over time that does (and to some extent, the minimum moves more linearly, as well, though there are outliers in that trend set, as well). Traditionally, the full spreadsheet that I haven't posted with the raw data shows that end-bosses tend to be outliers in terms of more mechanics than others - except in TOT and a few other raid tiers. In theory, if I really wanted to prove my point, I wouldn't have stayed with normal-mode counts when they started having normal vs heroic (showing actual increases in the maximum number of mechanics), but my concerns aren't at the heroic level.

In fact, the bigger problem overall is the huge jump between last tier and this tier in terms of the attention/memory requirements. It's going to make it harder for guilds to survive transitioning from TOES to TOT.
