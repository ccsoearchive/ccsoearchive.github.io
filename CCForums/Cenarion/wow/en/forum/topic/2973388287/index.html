Joachim followed the rest of the staff as they exited The Blue Recluse, planning to enjoy a full night off for once. As the others began to walk away Joachim peered around looking for someone, the owner had said he would be stopping by to do some renovations today, but had never shown up.

A few minutes later the lanterns illuminating the patio of the Recluse began to flicker lightly, then more violently as they finally blew out; despite the cool night's air being still. If anyone was even awake at this hour, they might just brush off the low thump that followed, hitting the walkway. A dark figure followed dropping from the air with two large glowing green orbs where eyes should be. The figure began to fiddle with the locks before the door creaked open and something was dragged inside the building before the door was shut and locked once more.

As the figure began to drag whatever the object was towards the cellar a low steaming hiss escaped from the figure itself, "There, now if anyone wus in 'ere, tha' gas should 'opefully knock 'em ou' till openin'." As he reached the final barrel in the cellar that he had prepared the night before, he stopped dragging the object and placed his right hand out to his side and created a small flame to illuminate his work. It was Zaium Duskmyre, the owner,"Time to finish settin' this up I guess," his mechanical left arm began to click, clank, and whir as the fingers withdrew and what appeared to be a motorized saw slid out to take their place. He dragged his right hand across the air creating a small circle of flame above him that would stay in the air; then with a quick end to the motion cracked the flat of the barrel as his claws dug into the wood. He quickly cut out the flat of the barrel and pulled it out exposing the glowing runes within the barrel lining it's sides and the runic circle on the backside; he pulled over te object he was dragging, the light exposing it as a smaller barrel sitting atop and tied to a metal circle with a runic circle design on one face.

Zaium opened the smaller barrel and splashed it against the backside's runic circle; then quickly lifting the new metal cover placed it in the barrel and with a few clicks and hisses it adjusted itself to fit tightly, shutting the barrel. Inside of the barrel, the liquid caused the runes to flicker brighter and faster, the the liquid began to float towards the center and swirl violently; eventually stabilizing into a new drink portal that Zaium uses for all the drinks in the Recluse.

He had already headed back upstairs and was setting up a new portal dispenser for the new drink, craving the necessary runes under the counter and filling them with magic. Zaium then pulled out a note which he set under the counter informing Joachim to try and push the new drink; as well as a flier which he hung upon the beam next to the counter.

" Try our new drink!
Passion Punch
You're sure to love it.

Sponsored* by Gentyl D'Amond."
((By now, the flier is gone, and has been replaced, look to my next post for new one.))

Zaium chuckled as he glanced the flier over and walked towards the door, dismissing the circle of flame,"Oh I'm sure they'll -love- i'." he let out a hearty chuckle as he exited the building, locking the door until the morning.

Unfortunately I don't actually have a drink to represent it, so if you want to participate, just ICly order the drink. Since it's experimental and it should effect everyone slightly differently anyway, feel free to come up with how it will effect you, if at all.

Feel the love!

"Try our new drink!

You're sure to love it.

Sponsored* by Gentyl D'Amond"

He just had to refine his mixture a bit, and remove Gentyl's name from the new flier. As well as a few surprises of his own.

The talk with Zaium had gone well, or as well as it ever goes with someone from Lluchduu Ocheliad. Sometimes she thought Imperon put something in their drinking water to make them purposely not realize the potential for disaster.

Of course, Gentyl seemed to attract chaos as well, so perhaps it was just the combination of Gentyl and Lluchduu to blame. They had a meeting almost as soon as she stepped off the ship. As Taelanas said, fortunately there were no unplanned children on the way or unwanted marriages, but Zaium's brew definitely did stir up feelings of lust.

According to the Regent Tenwit, limes enhanced the "romantic humours" and lemons incited more warlike responses. She wasn't sure she believed it since she drank lemons in her tea all the time and didn't feel any more warlike. She did refuse the limes the Regent kept offering her just in case, though.

She still thought the plan would work. Get the Grims full up on the punch and they would be more than ready to marry Vloth off to Spricket. With any luck he would be faunching at the bit to marry the imp. With even more luck, they would be more interested in making love than war and it would be one more horde war party off the battlefield. With the best of luck they would be so busy tending to their growing families they wouldn't have time for war for years.

"Sepha, I don't think sending the horde this potion is a good idea," Catrella said. "I'm rather uncomfortable with the idea of an orc or tauren full of love potion on the battlefield."

Gentyl squeezed another wedge of lemon into her tea. "I don't really think a random orc or tauren is going to have the overwhelming desire to marry you on a battlefield," Gentyl replied.

"Marriage isn't what I'm worried about," she replied.

"I don't really think it will have that much of an effect, do you?"

Taelanas shook his head. "You don't understand what this drink does. It isn't a love potion, it's a lust potion."

"Interesting. Are you sure? How do you know?"

He shifted uncomfortably in his seat.

"Oh."

She had to think about this some more. Now all of the women were agreeing with Catrella. It wouldn't be good if they all refused to take the field due to fear of a romantic Grim. What would happen if Abominus drank some?

Poor Red Earth. Well, maybe it was time she settled down and raised a family.
