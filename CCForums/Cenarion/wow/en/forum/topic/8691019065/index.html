The Sin’dorei huntress looked at the evening sky of Ashenvale and sighed briefly before tending a small campfire. Her pet lynx was taking a nap nearby as Coriani grabbed a stack of parchment papers from her brown pack. Why was she getting out those? Simple, she needed to write letters in a plea for scrying aid since the person she originally contacted for the job ‘bailed’ out. Yes, she needed the help of mages in a plight of hers that involved a missing relative of the Arrowind House.

It had been months since Coriani had started searching for her cousin, Failiani and she was getting desperate. Not only was the young elf still missing, partially due to her falling for the tricks her cousin had created with fake letters to throw the huntress off the trail, she had now had strong evidence that a high elf alchemist with nefarious intentions wanted Failiani to be used in lab experiments. Nisensi…. Cori cursed as she thought of the name.

This high elf had recently hired experienced mercenaries to track down and capture Cori’s cousin. While she didn’t think Failiani had been located by mercenaries; it was only a matter of time before that could change. Based on what Lor'eth Dawnshadow, Fail’s guardian and others had told Coriani about her cousin’s fighting skills, she didn’t think the young one would have much of a chance against them. The huntress was doing her best to locate her cousin, however; Coriani had suffered an injury to her left ankle weeks ago that prevented her from searching on foot until it fully healed.

She looked at the root cast around her left ankle and sighed, it might have healed by now if she hadn’t tripped and fallen several times in the past few weeks. Using a staff to help keep her balance hadn’t helped either as one would have hoped. And she wasn’t the only one injured in the search for Failiani. Lor’eth, her cousin’s guardian had been severely injured falling off a mount in Winterspring almost a month ago. Coriani’s eyes blinked while she thought of the old rogue.

He apparently had found something in his search for Fail and was going send the evidence to Cori by mail. The huntress had waited for the mail and wondered why it hadn’t come until she got a letter from a druid explaining the situation nearly three weeks earlier. While he had survived his injuries from the fall, it’d take him months to recover and it was unknown at this point if he’d ever be able to walk again. As it was when she had seen the venerable elf almost two weeks ago, he couldn’t feel his toes let alone move them or his legs. That diagnosis didn’t look good for Lor’eth.

Her eyes blinked again. Cori had gone to Winterspring for a few reasons. One was to check up on Lor’eth and another one was to deliver an ammo order to the goblins at Everlook she had received over a week earlier. After she had delivered the ammo, she traveled to the place in Northern Winterspring where Lor’eth was recovering and find out what happened to the evidence he was suppose to send. The evidence he had acquired was a letter written by Failiani. Unfortunately for Coriani, Lor’eth couldn’t remember if he had sent the letter to Ratchet, Gadgetzan or Silvermoon city.

Speaking of letters, Cori shook her head and cursed silently after she accidently dropped the parchment papers into the fire; her clumsiness wasn’t helping in this matter as she started to look for more parchment in her packs. Granted, she should have written and sent the letters over a month ago but finding out about Lor’eth’s injures and the ammo order had greatly distracted her at the time. The ammo order had been finished and delivered and she figured she’d have the time to write them now with her pet taking a nap.

That is if she could she parchment paper to write the letters, the red haired Sin’dorei thought she had more somewhere in her bags. The huntress shook her head and started, she couldn’t write a plea to ask for help if there wasn’t anything to write on. Luckily, she found enough parchments papers to write with a few minutes later in the same pack where she also found her Bloodthistle. She wasn’t sure how the papers ended up where they did but at least she had some, even if the parchments had the scent of Bloodthistle on them.

With the papers in hand, who was writing to? Coriani had asked Sidonwy, a member of the Da Doctas in a letter for recommendations in scrying and after meeting with the Sin’dorei, she had got three names. ~Coriani Arrowind

Cori was in a state of panic. Her pet lynx, TigerCat had been asleep for over a day and still hadn’t awakened. She was certain from examining him earlier that it was a magical effect from a potion or something similar and it was too powerful of one for her to have a chance of dispelling with her mending spells. Either a healer would be needed to dispel the effect or she was going to have to wait it out and see if it dissipated on its own. The latter being something the huntress didn’t want to do considering where she had landed.

How had her pet managed to get affected by a powerful magical effect? Simple, it was from the mountain trout she had bought for him from a blue haired female goblin salesman’s near the border of Felwood. She had taken another look at the fish and noticed it had turned a dark brown color from the green color it had been before. Not only had it turned a different color, it also had an alchemy type smell to it. The trout now was sealed up in a container as she was wanted to keep it as evidence for warning others from buying anything from the goblin tradesman.

The blue haired goblin (Cori forgot to get the salesman’s name) that had sold her the trout didn’t appear to be an alchemist type or a fisherman but she could have been wrong about that. Then again that goblin had mentioned they had a ‘reputable source’ for the fish. Coriani figured she was going to have to track down the goblin tradesman and find out who had given them the fish. Whoever had tainted the trout was extremely skilled with alchemy and a danger to others considering her pet’s state.

The huntress’s thoughts got interrupted as she heard something hit her flying machine. What could that been? She knew it wasn’t an arrow or bullet based on the sound but it wasn’t a moth, either. She winced as she got to her feet and inspected the area briefly before noticing what seemed to be a paper airplane lying on the ground next to her camouflaged covered flying machine. Where had it come from? The elf didn’t see anyone else nearby thankfully but the paper airplane probably had magical properties that made it travel far distances. She better go investigate it.

She winced while she gingerly walked over to her machine and pick up the paper airplane before sitting down near the sleeping lynx. After inspecting the paper, Cori noted it even had a message written on it that was addressed to her. Who had written this note? That did bother Coriani that someone had managed to track her down to this part of Ashenvale but considering the way the message had come, it had to be important and needed to be read right away.

With that in mind, she started to read the note on the paper airplane, her face paling a bit after she had fully read it. According to the note, her life was now in grave danger due to her being in Ashenvale and an unpleasant woman intended to find out the reasons Coriani had come though means she wouldn’t want to experience. That meant torture for the huntress and no; she didn’t want to experience that. The huntress had been held prisoner before a few years earlier by Sin’dorei rogue she wasn’t to name at the moment and was lucky to survive that incident. She didn’t want to get herself in a similar situation again.

That being said, who was the person after Cori? Were they someone in the Alliance, in the Horde or was this someone Nisensi? Why Coriani had just thought of the wretched high elf’s name, she wasn’t sure but it did occur to her that there was the chance that alchemist could decide to go after her, too. It also wouldn’t surprise her if this person was Alliance considering she had landed near a night elf retreat. There could be patrols spying on her now…
