What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE

Normal and some heroic raids

What makes playing your class more fun?
The ability to switch from either a bursty, single target execution, high aoe mute; to a high single target, high cleave combat spec.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Some people quote energy starvation for mutilate specs, but that's probably due to the few seconds here and there floating energy due to the envenom buff. I personally have no problem with energy.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Excellent, though it would be nice if rupture were buffed a bit for combat since rupture with bleed debuffs is on par with ruptureless specs +/- 1% overall dmg. Without that, the combat rotation is very, very, very simplistic. Mutilate feels nice since there's a lot of on-the-go choice as to when and how much to float your energy based on what ability timers are coming down to 0. To counter this being a buff to mute specs, maybe do a slight nerf the rupture buffing talent 2% per point.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
A very minor rupture buff for combat rogues to bring it back into the rotation and fill it out a bit more.
With transmogrification coming soon, bringing back fast offhand/slow mainhand pairs of agility swords/axes/maces/fists like back in wotlk would be GREAT. Not every rogue wants to run around dual wielding maces, or 1mace/1axe. Having the option to transmog your favorite combo of the 4 weapons would be pristine. I loved being a dual wield sword combat rogue in ulduar and being able to skin that to my character would feel amazing.

What spells do you use the least?
I use pretty much all my PvE toolkit.

Having to use a thrown weapon instead of a gun/bow for moves such as fan of knives/deadly throw. I feel like rogues are being pigeonholed into using just daggers/thrown weapons when in reality I think they should have the most variety of weapons after warriors ofcourse.

What makes playing your class more fun?

What makes rogues so fun to me is that they have such good utility. The problem with this I think is that it excels for them in PVP but in PVE it doens't add much at all. Most rogues will agree that they play rogue to PVP and not to PVE.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Finishers such as SnD and ReCoup. I love them as skills but having to keep them up is very anoying in fights. I understand they are part of the rotation but it is a lot less fun for your rotation to be keeping up buffs rather then actually executing skills on the enemy.

Assassination should focus more on poisons and Subtlety more on bleeds/finishers.

Also on my wishlist for rogues is an execute type ability. An under 20% HP type thing maybe, similar to Execute and Hammer or Wrath, ect.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


    Combat
    I really don't like all of the excess energy during adrenaline rush or hero. I can't find the gcd to use feint or even tricks without energy capping when either of them are up. When they're down I often find myself below 50 energy more often than above 50 energy, which is kinda slow with bad procs but definitely alright. I like the energy pace I'm at with 1 haste rating proc up... it's generally fast but still leaves me with a gcd to use on utility.

    Killing spree is the number 2 bane of combat imo. I don't like having to think about not dying/ending up on the other side of the room or w/e when I go use to a dps cool down. I want to be thinking about using it optimally based on my rotation, not optimally based on whether or not it's going to kill me or someone around me (baleroc!).

    I like the finisher prioritization combat has right now (though buffing rupture in some way so we can use it w/o mangle/trauma/hemo should be a high priority for 4.3), and I like BG, RvS, and restless blades. I thought I would hate bg at first, but I was totally wrong. I like it a lot. It needs to switch targets without redirect or redirect needs a much shorter/nonexistent cd though.

    I feel like combat's cleave is way too strong and its ae is somewhat too weak. I see how there's some merit to having a cleave spec that's not very good at ae, but I think the cleave is too well pronounced and the ae not well enough. I feel sub par being any spec other than combat on a fight where you can cleave, even if the cleaving is mostly padded but kinda justifiable. Like domo cleaves. I don't need to be combat for that, the adds die so fast anyways... I just feel and look inferior as sub/mut on that fight because I can't 'steal' the add damage from others. ryolith when cleave hits the adds is also a good example. On the same note I feel really bad as combat on a fight like sinestra or when I need to be doing some burst ae damage to the molten seeds as they spawn on rag.

    Subtlety
    Hmm, I don't feel like having to keep up recuperate is as bad as people try to make it out to be, but I also don't feel like it fits a dps spec. I'm really just indifferent to recuperate as sub.

    I really enjoy the burst damage (or at least it really feels like a lot of burst) that [vanish -> prep -> shadow dance -> vanish] puts out. It feels insane, and I really like seeing how high I jump up in damage when I can time something like that with hero and a potion at the start/endish of a fight.

    Shadowstep presents some problems on fights like al'akir/ragnaros, but it's such a small dps increase that I can not use it and not feel bad about it.

    Hate the complete lack of ae/cleave dps. I can do really well on the single target portion of a fight but end up way below everyone else at the end of the fight because of the ae or cleave/multi dot portion of the fight.

    Energy capping is rarely a problem unless I'm pooling for combo points from hat and the boss suddenly turns toward me and I cap before I notice and hit hemorrhage (AHEM, RAG). or even if I do hit hemorrhage I get parried and cap anyways, but that's an encounter design flaw not a rogue flaw.

    I don't really like how many ways there are to make your first rupture of the fight really strong. If you could find a way to make rupture update dynamically with buffs then that'd be great. Right now there's MoS+prepot+tricks+4p bonus if not haste+ap procs at the time. That feels like a lot of gimmick, and it's a steep penalty for rupture dropping off mid fight on like rag submerges or something like that.

    The talent tree is so bloated with dps increases that I can't take enveloping shadows or cheat death w/o losing 500 dps, with that number obviously increasing as gear increases. I think being able to put in 2 points into either cd or es while only having to sacrifice dps for the third would be the best way to structure the tree.

    Haven't pvp'ed since the beginning of Wrath. Just more of the same and kind of boring at this point.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    Ramp up time on target switches is atrocious as assassination. By the time an add has rupture and 5 stacks of deadly poison applied it is often dead. Either give assassination the ability to swap deadly poisons onto another target, or drop the 5 DP stack requirement to proc instant poison from offhand attacks.

    I thought "cleaving" classes were going to be put to rest in this expansion and sadly this did not happen. Specs such as combat rogues, shadow priests, and affliction locks absolutely dominate multi-target fights and leave nearly everyone else in the dust. This forces me to play combat on progression fights when I would much rather play assassination. I strongly suggest that the cleaving aspect of these particular specs get toned down significantly for the next expansion so I don't feel forced to go into combat to compete.

    As assassination our dps suffers significantly if we do not have the opportunity to open with garrote on the boss at the start of the fight (Alys, Domo, etc.).

    There is too much discrepancy in aoe damage between the three specs.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Assassination: I like the feel of the rotation, requiring precision and timing rather than spamming of one button like combat. I also enjoy the talent the provides energy to be returned when a target dies with rupture still on it. The ability makes things kind of interest when fighting multiple targets that die relatively quickly.

    Combat: I like how the cooldowns for combat provide some burst and actually give the rogue some major damage output when needed.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Vendetta needs a serious overhaul. More damage is nice, but the conditions necessary to maximize throughput of the ability is absurd. I hate that the damage increase applies as a debuff to a target rather than a buff to the rogue while the duration is too long and the damage increase doesn't really feel like much in a pve standpoint. In comparison, adrenaline rush for combat "feels" powerful whereas vendetta just feels like another necessary button to press. Cold blood, while fun as a pvp ability, really needs an overhaul to improve it in a pve standpoint somehow. The ability itself is also relatively weak as a cooldown.

    Even with the changes in Cataclysm combat feels too spammy and the rotation is still painfully simple. Combat insight needs to either be overhauled or needs to go.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    Assassination - I love the rotation of assassination. It's fluid but requires proper timing to play well. Damage cooldowns need a serious overhaul however. Change overkill back to the way it was in Wrath (since burst has been toned down) and make some big changes to vendetta to be more user friendly and give more burst than it currently does.

    Combat - I like how the cooldowns in combat play more into the rotation and synergize with one another. The standard rotation however is still fundamentally the same as it has been for years.


    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Better target switching cabability

    Lower ramp up time

    Better, more useful, more interesting cooldowns for assassination.

    Better, more interesting rotation for combat.


    What spells do you use the least?

    Shiv (Pve)
    Expose armor
    Combat readiness
    Smoke bomb (Pve)
