The reason for this is pretty well known at this point: A rogue's melee attacks were actually made "ranged attacks" within the game code specifically so that they could not be blocked, dodged or parried. They were meant to always hit.

So it seems like a simple oversight: Rogue (and kitty) stealth openers were coded as ranged attacks purely so they could receive the same "unmissable" buff as ranged attacks. Camo was meant to block ranged attacks, not melee attacks.

I'm not sure what is even technically possible (or simple) that could address this. But I don't think it's correct to say that allowing stealthed classes to open on a hunter in Camo is somehow "nerfing" Camo. What I'm suggesting, and have not heard otherwise from a dev as far as I've seen, is that this immunity against stealth was an unintended consequence in the first place, so removing it would be a fix, not a nerf.
