What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Mostly PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Normal raids, then heroics

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Normal random BG's and the occasional world pvp

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Even though mana is gone in many ways we are still treated as casters. The number of things I can't do while silenced is downright shocking. PvP hunters stacking spell pen is also rather ridiculous. That and bugs of course. But overall mechanically I think we're in a pretty good place, I've actually seen far more QoL changes recently than I thought we would, it was pleasantly surprising.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Pets, our unique status as the only ranged physical damage class, and all of our utility

What makes playing your class less fun?
The handful of counter-intuitive and broken mechanics that linger from earlier days (like the aforementioned spell pen issue)

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
All 3 are pretty solid. Much more interesting than they used to be.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Cobra shot was a failure in design, get rid of it and roll the SS refresh effect into Steady or something, as long as all 3 specs still have it one way or another. Baseline Silencing Shot! Also a baseline self heal, this would alleviate a lot of small problems (nothing major, and usable in combat, but not easily). Let's see what else... The first 2 tiers of each tree need refinement, and they should only contain talents that fit the theme of that tree, no more sic 'em in MM, it's confusing for those new to the class. Get rid of widow venom, and roll the effect into Serpent Sting, talent it if you have to (maybe the second tier in SV?)

BM - We miss our improved mend pet, this NEEDS to come back in one form or another. Don't be afraid to make Kill Command our signature, and move intimidation to a talent (the other specs can live without it). Redesign our 31 pter "Beast Mastery" to instead of giving extra talent points, unlock a new talent/ability/skill in your pet's chosen tree. Something like a bleed from ferocity pets (applied and refreshed by KC crits), a focus return or maybe an extra CC for cunning, and a survivability thing for tenacity. "Unlocking the pet's true power", as it were. As a follow up to that, redesign the 3 pet trees from scratch please.

MM - Put stuff like sniper training and wyvern sting here (ranged CC, should be MM). Move readiness to SV as it should be more of a survival tool than a dps tool these days. The current Chimera Shot is BORING. Bring back the old functionality, either get rid of venom(s) and bring back some other stings, or attach it to our aspects instead (good opportunity to really evaluate aspects).

SV - Redesign this entire tree. The first 2 tiers are a mess, counterattack is a joke, and black arrow is the worst 31 pointer in the game. Move trap talents deeper in the tree, and maybe figure out a way to make the lock and load effect the 31 point talent. Seriously, this tree is functional, but a mess. Also, bear trap... make this happen.

Beyond that I DON'T want to see minimum range removed, but rather our melee made meaningful. The Raptor Strike glyph was a huge success, think along those lines. I'd like to see some trap overhauls too. Explosive trap should stun all effected targets on the first tick, right now absolutely nothing about the trap says "explosion" to me. Snake trap is a joke, fix it or get rid of it already.

What spells do you use the least?
Aspect of the Wild, widow venom, snake trap, camouflage. and depending on spec, either steady shot or cobra shot (please, please remove one of these, I can't tell you how many other hunters I've had to explain proper use of these to. It's confusing and redundant).

What makes playing your class more fun?

Finding unique solutions for problems, making me a bit of a "Johnny," (versus a Timmy or Spike). Hunters have a large toolkit that impacts threat, CC, and raid buffs via their pets. Tossing out traps, dropping aggro, using Camouflage: All great, unique problem-solvers.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What makes playing your class less fun?

  • Patch 3.3.0 added the Dungeon Finder, the best new tool since the founding of Azeroth. It was a double-edged sword, however, as server groups practically vanished and queues for DPS skyrocketed in Cataclysm. I started a Tank alt out of hatred of the RDF queues. I absolutely hate only being able to perform one role in a heroic as a pure DPS class, and would kill for a tank or heal off-spec to reduce queue times.

  • Second was when the class (Survival spec) was intentionally broken due to an pending expansion. The lack of Cobra Shot was a major problem for Survival Hunters between WotLK and Cataclysm once patch 4.0 was released, but before the level cap was raised. It was a painful, painful period that really made me hate the game, and I only listed it second because it was temporary. Any change of this nature is, in my opinion, game-breaking. Current raiding should not be impacted by a pending patch or expansion.

  • Finally are ability bugs (e.g. Having a pet summon with low health or without their pet bar, auto-shot stopping during Trap Throw, etc.) disrupt game immersion and, in rarer cases, can cause player death. Nothing is worse than bugged abilities.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The rotation for both Survival and Beast Mastery at the level cap are, theoretically, great, though I am still getting used to the new ability queue. At the moment I strongly dislike it, but I believe this is more a player issue than a game issue.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What’s on your wish list for your class?

  • The removal of minimum range.
  • Trap Launcher improvements to allow macros
  • The ability to modify our weapon - even if by profession - to one the allows the use of a racial bonus (guns < - > bows).

  • The ability to use thrown weapons. They are already well-itemized for Hunters, and there is no reason a Hunter shouldn't also be a spear-thrower or dart-tosser. It fits with the class from both a lore, aesthetic, and functional standpoint, they already exist in-game, and it gives us another gearing option.

  • Pet summon bug fixes (two): Low pet health and no pet bar.

  • Revert pet stance changes, or add additional stances to include the old versions of aggressive and defensive.

  • A "Stampede" ability, allowing one or more pets from my stable to be called temporarily on cooldown.

  • Buffs for Snake Trap and Immolation Trap.

  • A new trap of some sort, possibly one that benefits teammates where they step in and are healed, or removed from combat. A disarm or (cone of) silence effect would also be welcomed.

  • Two pets active at the same time (think Shannox) either on cooldown (aggressive-only) or as an end-tier ability allowing dual pet management, such as for Beast Mastery; BM could use a buff, anyway.
  • Purly cosmetic, the ability the sheathe our ranged weapon on our back rather than our stat stick, or - less cosmetic - the removal of our stat stick entirely. A bayonet would be fine!

Happy gaming,

I am frustrated at the number of stable slots. While I am hugely grateful for the expanded stables we were given in the Shattering, I am frustrated that I cannot add more pets to my stables unless I get rid of some. While I do not use every pet every day, I enjoy going out and trying out different pets or having specific pets that I enjoy running with.

I am also frustrated with not having Eyes of the Beast and Volley anymore, because certain aspects of the game made both of those abilities extremely useful. Eyes of the Beast was great for PVP, and Volley was great for when you were stuck in melee or trying to aoe down something that had aggroed to you and not your pet.

It would be nice to have the ability to call any of the pets in your stables at any time. I change my pets in my active call list based on what I am doing, but sometimes it would be nice to be able to access the stables within a dungeon in order to grab out a pet that I would like to bring for a specific buff to help the group.

Taming pets and getting to run with them. The best part about being a hunter for me is going out and hunting for rare or unique pets. Being stuck in a melee situation with very little abilities to use, and the current cap on stable slots.

I started playing in BC, and was Marksman/BM forever! Once the Shattering hit, I ended up switching to Survival/BM because I absolutely hated the new MM rotation. I have tried going back to MM but it irritates me. I like the flexibility of Survival and I will not give up BM because of the pets that I currently have (and will keep) in my stables.

    *More stable slots. I would be happy to purchase these like we had to originally when we wanted 3 pets instead of just 1. Just the ability to have more stable slots and be able to continue looking/using the unique/cool pets, or even some of the lesser loved pets like the coyote wolf model.

    *Volley and Eyes of the Beast coming back.

    *Glyph of Greater Proportion

    *Glyph of Camouflage (Would allow your Camouflage ability to be permanent unless attacked/take damage, or you attack). Not having to refresh camo every 60 seconds would be awesome.


What spells do you use the least?
Immolation trap, Raptor Strike, Wing Clip, Widow's Venom is only used in certain situations
