I've been continuing to read a lot of the "Class Feedback" posts all over the hunter forums, and there's another excellent point worth addressing here.

Hunter's are, bizarrely, alternatively counted as casters, physical DPS, or both.

For example:
Many of our abilities use spell crit rates rather than physical crit rates.
Many hunter abilities are affected by silences, others by disarms, some by BOTH.
Debuffs that affect casters AND debuffs that affect physical DPS both can cripple hunters.
In PVP, hunters must gem and enchant for spell penetration.

Blizzard should fully switch hunters to being regarded as physical dps.


All hunter abilities should be based on physical stats, not things like spell crit.

Disarm should affect hunters, silence should not.

Attack speed debuffs should affect hunters, but cast speed debuffs should NOT.


Bear in mind that a huge part of current hunter DPS is magical, which is why hunters need spell penetration. However, a solution is needed so that hunters don't require spell pen.

I've recommended that Arcane Shot be replaced with "Piercing Shot" which is physical dps that bypasses all armor. This wouldn't affect DPS at all, but would be more class appropriate and wouldn't need Spell Pen. Regardless of the Spell Pen issue, it is too weird to have so much of Hunter DPS be "Arcane", and this should be done just for flavor reasons.

Still, Serpent Sting, Chimera Shot, Cobra Shot, Explosive Shot, Black Arrow, and traps would all still require spell pen, so this doesn't fully resolve the issue.

Consider offering some baseline hunter ability that negates all resistances. Perhaps have Hunter's Mark negate all resistances on the target? That would be a simple and easy fix.

To Summarize:
Make Hunters function as a fully PHYSICAL DPS CLASS.
Remove all Caster attributes from Hunter abilities.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


Movement/range penalties which make us truly competitive at high-end only when we turret, sub-standard utility abilities that are too often resisted or to which mobs are immune (FD, interrupts, traps, etc), and ongoing, long-term, unresolved class bugs.

What makes playing your class more fun?


The basic, core concept for the hunter class is very well-conceived. Being a physical ranged class and having a pet definitely makes the class feel unique and I think that, for the most part, you guys have done well in helping us to diversify our pet choices, which was a welcome change. I think the focus change was a positive one, even if I think it needs a bit of a tune-up.

What makes playing your class less fun?


We're losing a lot of ground to classes with less-demanding rotations, which is particularly frustrating when it's a hybrid that can (and does) fill in on the same raid to heal or tank. We suffer some pretty hefty penalties to both boss proximity (which can have a negative impact on fights like Gatekeeper, Rhyolith, and even Rags during seed) and movement (nearly every fight). Simply put, our only competitive spec (MM) suffers disproportionately in anything other than turret situations compared with other classes/specs, some of which have less demanding rotations to boot.

Secondly, I don't like having a ton of core abilities that are essentially useless in a raid setting. Most of our CC and interrupts are unreliable, unnecessarily expensive and, as a result, a substantial DPS hit to use. FD is particularly frustrating, not only in that encounter mechanics often bypass it (face rage, drink magma, etc), but successfully using it often screws us on bloodlust timers.

Lastly, crippling class bugs seem to linger in game for far too long. How long have we had a problem with our pets summoned at 7k health? How many events do our pets bug out and refuse to attack? Now we have the issue with pet action bars disappearing.

# How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

MM and SV rotations are mostly fine. My complaint isn't so much with the rotations themselves (both of which are interesting), but with the fact that MM takes too big of a movement hit and SV is too low DPS and too focus-starved. I can't comment on BM because I haven't played that spec in a long time because theorycrafting would suggest it's still not competitive for raids.

What’s on your wish list for your class?


I'd love to see the minimum range go away. It's outlived its purpose and now it just seems unnecessarily punitive. I'd also like to see steady shot changed up so that it can be cast on the move. Those two changes would go a long way toward restoring the class to where it needs to be.

On the "fun" factor front, I'd still like to see further pet customization options such as visible (and perhaps distinctly functional) armor.

What spells do you use the least?


Tranquilizing shot (It's fun when we have a gimmicky fight that uses it, but it's too expensive and isn't needed for most fights), Widow's Venom, Master's Call (range issues make it problematic), FD (on any fight where bloodlust is used, you're hurting yourself to use it), Distracting Shot (it would be useful if we had an event that we could/should kite), Aspect of the Wild. Most of these could be useful if their cost, utility, and frequency with which they were needed were tweaked. It's not the abilities themselves that are ill-conceived.

With this kind of an adjustment you can remove the ability to drop a trap at your feet.

It would be really nice if Freezing Trap didn't break from a DoT. Personally I would like to see Freezing Trap freeze/pause any Dot on the unit until the trap expires. So once the frozen unit in the trap is free (any new/additional damage applied to the frozen unit would break the CC), the DoT resumes from its last tick. This would give Surv hunters a great emergency, long term CC with Wyvern Sting followed up with Freezing Trap (that wouldn't break from the Wyvern Sting DoT).

Focus:
I don't mind the focus mechanic but in PvE and PvP if a hunter is focused starved at a key moment - they're going to die or kill the raid. Need some way to very quickly get enough focus to get off a multi-shot (if the hunter has to cast cobra shot to have enough focus for that vital shot at an exact moment, bad things are sure to happen). If the BM ability Fervor were available for all 3 hunter specs, this would be eliminated as an issue.

Healing:
MM and Surv hunters don't need any healing in PvE. But in PvP a reliable, significant self heal would be fabulous. For MM, Chimera Shot's 5% is laughable and Surv doesn't have anything.

Minimum Range:
This is the fundamental weakness of MM and Surv in PvP and results in us spending most of our time trying to get back at range rather than fighting. In PvE its very annoying that hunters can't stack reliably with the rest of the raid. Too often I can't shoot the boss/adds if I have to stack right on top of everyone else during an AoE healing/burn phase.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Doing things at range. Able to put out consistently high DPS while on the move. Having a wide array of pets that can be called upon to provide benefits to raids and huge functionality to solo play.

What makes playing your class less fun?

PvP:
Minimum range, no self healing, clunky CC that is too easily broken (i.e. DoTs).
Glyph of Kill Shot is too unreliable in PvP. If a player drops below 20% and you get a Kill Shot off, only for them to be immediately healed and jump back up to slightly above 20%, the second Kill Shot (from the glyph) fizzles. This happens overwhelmingly in Arena/RBGs.

PvE:
Minimum range, clunky traps (two keys for a trap is annoying). Overall I think hunters are in pretty good shape with PvE.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

PvE:
Its okay but it can get too repetitive. It would be really nice if we had at least 1 other shot--totally separate from steady/cobra shot--that we could use to generate focus. Having a couple options to generate focus could allow for more flexibility and individuality in developing a shot rotation.

PvP:
Cobra/Steady shot is terrible in PvP. Giving your opponent 3-5 seconds to recuperate while you get your focus up with some relatively weak Steady/Cobra shots gives anyone with a self heal too much of an advantage/escape. Having another way to regenerate focus (other than being hit with Aspect of the Fox up) would be great.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

No more minimum range (maybe a 50% ranged damage penalty when opponents are <5 yards away)
Changing all traps to work like Ice Arrow did in WotLK (get rid of Trap Shot).
More abilities to generate focus.
A reliable self heal for all hunter specs.
An incentive/buff for MM and Surv hunters to not have a pet around.
An updated Snake Trap.

What spells do you use the least?

Counter Attack
Beast Lore
Widow Venom
Distracting Shot
Aspect of the Wild
Immolation Trap
