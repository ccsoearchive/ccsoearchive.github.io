Seeking a home; feeling as if you were a stranger in your own lands? Deep Forest Caravan seeks those who would not walk alone through this world.

The Caravan is leveraging this time of peace. While others may be content to sit aside and rest on their laurels, Deep Forest knows that now is the time to hone abilities to a keen edge.

All are welcome, regardless of the current level of training. Deep Forest Caravan has the breadth of knowledge to ensure your skills grow. Veterans welcome. Deep Forest has a long tradition in the sharing of knowledge.

Be aware that the Caravan does not seek to field an army, but rather a tight-knit circle of companions that can count upon one another. To that end we are seeking:

Eight fighters that can draw danger’s attention and hold it.
A dozen field healers, two positions open for long term care healing in matters of the mind.
Twenty fighters; ten comfortable in close fighting styles, ten at range.
We also require various support personnel, to be offered a position on a case-by-case basis.

Post to Sajala, the Caravan’s Quartermaster, for consideration.

OOC:
Deep Forest Caravan will be running a full immersion campaign into Warlords! Grounded firmly in RP, we will be progressing through the leveling process at an RP pace, gearing up through Heroics, stepping directly into normal and pushing forward to Mythic.

To that end we need to grow our roster to around 30 – 40 members.

Who are we looking to attract? Cutting edge RP’ers interested in the next gen form of RP. Deep Forest is an experimental RP guild that is seeking to intelligently and uniformly extend the lore to encompass gameplay. The goal of the system is 85 – 95% of the game world incorporated into our RP.

If you are a PvE’er with a healthy interest in RP and your compulsion to race to the finish line firmly under control, now you can have both!

If you are an RP’er with zero interest in raiding, but enjoy being part of a team focused on an objective, we would love to have you.

Raiding is not required to join the Caravan, but RP is required to Raid!

Those interested in World PvP welcome. We have a guild rank for you, but you will need to be a self- starter. (aka – my hands are full ) World PvP must have accompanying RP with it.

We are generally an evening guild, although there are members on throughout the day at this time. Raids are running currently from 7 – 10, twice a week. 10/14 Normal. Earliest time we will start a raid in Warlords is 6pm server, group schedules depending. RP events run a minimum of twice weekly, more often when a story arc is active.

Interested candidates should first scroll down to post 16 - 18, then drop into our Visitor's Forum @ dfc.guildlaunch.com Contact Mossclaw in game and/or throw down a app on our site and I will find you. ~.^

Mechanics

The greatest shift an incoming player will make when joining Deep Forest is leaving all OOC communications out of the view of the general population at large. This concept is not to be confused with “never having an OOC chat again”, but does take some getting used to as you re-route OOC comments out of /s, /w, /gchat and all game channels (Gen, Trade, etc ).

That, on the face of it, seems bleak. However, because we integrate the game into our RP, what it really means is that you will just no longer be talking about the latest movies, Blizzard, and the like in /s /gchat /<fill in city/world channel here>. Nor, because our characters are speaking, will you be correcting typos in /s or /gchat. We have an OOC channel and that is where such activities as corrections, clarifications, edits, and brbs take place. When you are interacting with an RPer using a different system of RP, you’ll just direct needed OOC edits/additions into /w with (( )), regardless of what they care to do.

In addition we do ask that you separate /s from /em. This is actually important from a back-end perspective.

The next shift revolves around how your character gets around and interacts in the game world. Once gameplay is no longer OOC, you naturally would not run everywhere, nor ride your mounts to overlap NPCs, fly into buildings, and the like. Your goal is to make your character as natural as possible at all times. This does not mean never running in non-combat areas! Often times, characters are in a hurry ICly and your speed would reflect that. The offset is to switch to walking as you approach your destination. It is a given that there is no walking from quest mob to quest mob or walking tours of active dungeons. The more walking when not fighting the better, but new members may have to ease into it.

Those two areas are mechanics and execution. If you can attack a mob or maneuver your character through a city, you'll be fine.

RP

Where to start? I suppose at the beginning – getting the guild tag floating over your head.

We require an application. It helps us gauge the level of support an incoming member will need.

DFC does not throw invites, and this is an area of confusion for most. We expect to have your character meet someone of the Caravan and RP expressing the wish to join us. Some players take months, others use the handy-dandy flier on the Call board and send a post to Sajala and are in the next night. It is truly up to the player how they want to intertwine their story with Deep Forest’s. We are more than willing to assist, of course, and we encourage the longer road.

If you are RPing a level one character and wish to remain so – there will not be a lot of difference between what you are used to doing and the DFCsys. However, if you enjoy playing the game, then there is one key point of integration for your storyline and that is that you are a “Chosen” (as it is called in Common). This is DFCsys’ device for holding space for traditional RP/RP’ers and those who wish to take full advantage of the system. It is not a method under which your character is better than another.

The DFCsys is a wonderful system for those new to RP, as now, the majority of what is happening to your character as you play the game – is actually represented in RP.

Veteran RPers will find that as the game-world is included, immersion and depth will increase accordingly. While there are no hard and fast rules, the system’s goal is to include the game world; so the less you modify your character that takes reading an XRP profile to intuit, the better; transient conditions aside (injuries, mood). The tighter the integration between your character and the game-world, the better. Keep in mind that once the game world is included, every game feature your character participates in, is actually something your character is doing. You cannot level/raid/run bgs/areana/professions in DFC and not acknowledging that fact ICly.

After that, there are a handful of terms that translate to OOC functions to learn. A player new to the system can either read up on them OOCly and then have knowledge of them ICly, or have the information revealed over time. It depends upon how you’ve constructed your character.

PvE

You are not required to engage in current content raiding. Do be aware, however, that DFC’s story arc revolves around delving into Raids – which are referred to as “pockets”. What this means is that if you do not raid, you will want to create RP that supports that primary function, or attach yourself to a member of the raid team. The Caravan holds ranks for those who do not raid.

For those interested in Raiding, we enjoy helping our members grow into their class, or having members that can mentor others of their chosen class.

To PvE in DFC is to hold an open mind towards trying new things. We do not discard players based upon the composition of our raid (ask Gnimbus!) once we’ve secured 2 tanks and 2 healers we sweep up the rest and go.

The plan as it stands now for DFC is to build up to a Mythic raid team, while continuing to run Normal/Heroics for those who wish a less exacting raid environment. We encourage our players to give back to the community, and Warlords will have some sort of Community raid event once we get settled.

I do not see DFC passing 40 members in total during Beta.
