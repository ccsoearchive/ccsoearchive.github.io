Grymm made his way around the great round of the Undercity. Everything here reminded him of his dark birth in Northrend. Full of the sounds unique to undeath. The bubbling of liquids from the the labs, the smell of embalming fluids, the fluttering of breeze from up above. Grymm had been summonined to the rogues quarter by one of the dark guards. The purpose was a mystery, but Grymm had found service to the dark queen most satisfying. Even though he had once been Orc, before his dark birth, Grymm did not relate to his living cousins. He had embraced his new being, with darkness. His name was given to him by the other Death Knights. For indeed his was the visage of terror on any field of the living.

With ease he had reached his destination. A simple alcove with in the rogue area. He stood and watched as one of the shadowy figures, standing with hands clasped over chest, as if reposed in death, came alive and opened its eyes. A familiar reddish glow looked at Grymm. He imagined his own blue glow to be somewhat similar.

" You have come in a most timely manner, Dark Knight. You are as prompt as they have reported" the rogue master began. " Wonder must have crossed your mind as you made your way here I am sure. I shall answer all of the purpose, for the Dark Queen herself has made a request."

Grymm listened to the rogue master, a face of stone, as unreadable as most of the Forsaken. This seemed to please the rogue master. He continued on accepting Grymm's silence as understanding.

" Not so long ago, a certain society arose that helped to foster understanding of the shadowy and the darker paths. Places that the living fear to go. Knowledge called forbidden by the Light Blessed. We who have crossed to a higher experience in this living world feel those shadowy places, hear those whispery voices and feel the beat of the void in our very being." The rogue master paused to circle Grymm. " You know of what I speak, for that very essence has given you life. You have shown no fear to tread those paths in your thirst of understanding, your hunger for its knowledge. The Dark Queen wishes to see this society spark again. It needs to continue discovery of the voids secrets, and the voids power. Are you intrigued Grymm?"

Grymm did not have to wait to answer " Yes."

The rogue master ceased his circling and stood in front of Grymm. The difference in height was great, yet this master rogue exuded his own power. Grymm could not help but be impressed. " I give this challenge to you then. Find others of like mind. Form a network, protect those who join the ranks. I have two blood elves to aid in specialized tasks. I wish you to reform the Black Hand Society." The master held out a ledger book in a gesture of acceptance.

Grymm took the book without looking down. His thoughts raced with the excitement of dark purpose. He held his stone face but inside felt the swelling of honor the rogue master had given him, that the Dark Queen had asked of him. The rogue master smiled up at Grymm and stepped towards the alcove where he had been reposed. He turned his head back in Grymm's direction." We do not give support to your endeavors Grymm. I shall not be in contact with you again. The Black Hand is only a shadow upon the wall, a mark on paper. Do you understand?"

Grymm knew exactly. This Society was not official to the Undercity, and most especially to the dark lady herself. " Understood" was Grymms only reply as he turned from the rogue master and made his way out of the Undercity. He would now be on his own, but only until he had found the others. Others that fear not the shadows, the void, and the cold dark powers that extended to this world and beyond. Grymm paused to look upon the ledger, its opening page was a hand print of ebon ink. He could feel the tingle of power from the book. The Black Hand Society is reborn.
