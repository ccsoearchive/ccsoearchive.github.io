What a lot of people do not realize, is that MMR is what it stands for: Match Making Rating.

This isn't like some lucky boss fight kill. It's more like your guild finally figured out how to beat it, and now they can continue to beat it again and again.

If someone just recently got to 2200, they most likely have improved over time and are now of that skill level. The way the system works, rating is your skill in relation to everyone else that pvps.

What this means is that if they were to reset the ladders tomorrow, you would be able to get back to whatever ranking you were previously at seamlessly once everyone has started playing and had been factored in to the equation.

What has been happening since last season and into this season was a bugged mmr system that severely inflated the rating system. By consequence, everyone, and i mean EVERYONE was at a higher rating than they should have been. So this 2200 that someone may have worked hard for this season is technically tainted. It is no longer the 2200 of previous seasons where the MMR was better calculated.

The system needs to be reset because people who grinded up team in the bugged system are at a huge advantage over the people trying to grind up teams now because gaining mmr is immensely harder to do now.

It is rather unfair NOT to reset the ladders to the many people who did not exploit the system(knowingly or not), than to reset them so that everyone is on an level playing field.

Also, it is not "too late" to reset them. Every season your team rating goes back to zero and you have to get it back up, so why isnt everyone complaining about about their rating being reset for every new season? Because it's a "new" season.

The point is that the ladders and MMR needed to be reset when they changed the way MMR gain/loss worked. Plain and simple. If you cannot see that simple fact, i have no idea what to tell you.

Edit: I would also add that for those who have worked hard for their rating and do not want it taken away, you do realize that you working back up to that rating is the same thing as playing AT that rating right now. Meaning that if you are planning to just hit a rating and then just stop pvping altogether just so you do not lose rating, you are just proving how little you actually care or know about pvp and should then just stop making concerned remarks about it in opposition to people who DO play it and are genuinely concerned.
