This thread is wholly unrealistic and impractical. You're suggesting changes of a magnitude that would require a huge amount of work on the game's engine itself.


Nothing I read seemed unreasonable or out of reach. Most people hopes would be for models that are boosted up to WoW Cataclysm standards (ie, Thrall, Worgen, Goblins). Like how feral druids were upgraded, and that didn't break the engine. I agree that it would take tons of work, but this is entirely realistic to ask for. The Blizzard art team are an amazing bunch, and do tons of model upgrades.


Character models in WoW are designed to be simple and to have a relatively low polygon count. Why? Because when you have two hundred characters doing all kinds of things in a small section of Stormwind or Orgrimmar, those characters need to be as simple as possible or everyone without a multiple-GPU system is going to enjoy watching their box ignite.


Cataclysm shows this engine can handle current Cata-models. Goblins were added to Cataclysm too, they are all over Orgrimmar now as npcs and players. I think that's a vote of confidence from Blizzard their population can technically handle it.

More, adding extra bones and other details like that aren't things that can be scaled, which is going to add a lot more workload for people playing the game, and people that can only run the game at low framerates (25 fps or less) might end up not being able to play the game at all anymore. You can't scale it so that those bones and moving bits and pieces suddenly don't move or appear anymore.


What scaling are you referring to? Size? Bones/skeletons work fine on different scales, otherwise our characters couldn't grow from Heroism. I think they go invisible too when not in use, like Goblin Pigtails that bounce, as they change can change hair styles without problem.

FPs shouldn't be any worse for than it currently is. I've never heard of a raid ask for less Worgen because they lag everyone up. Let alone ask for simpler raid boss models. We always experience lag in high mob situations (onyxia whelplings *cough*), and those models are simplistic. If anything, it comes down to how many are crowded in one spot of any model/player. Like 40 mans on City Bosses.

EDIT: Also consider, that the more things that are stitched, cut, and outright crammed into WoW's aging engine, the more likely all different kinds of bugs start cropping up. This means more time spent with QA to ensure the damn thing even works, more time on PTRs trying to identify various bugs, and then more time on live to fix those bugs.

All kind of bugs do crop up. Just about anything added causes them. Like that mounted riding bug recently or the /attack + /dance bug (which was hilarious fun, by the way). This is exactly what PTRs are meant for, and what would be quicker to find then bugs on your own character model?

Personally it came across more like a job application. If she knows so much about it, why doesn't she work for them doing what she proposed? Just a thought.

A valid thought! ;) While being part of any creative development group would be engaging, this thread was made solely for the community in hopes of communicating our ideas/desires for Model Updates.

Most of the information collected is available publicly, with WoW Model Viewer being a great supplementary learning tool. The rest comes from creating alts, learning their customization options and watching how they animate.


This Compendium was fashioned over a number of weeks, reading over similar threads and collecting ideas from friends and in-game voices. Importantly, this thread is a work-in-progress as it needs everyone's constructive ideas, works, and extensions.

If anyone has thoughts on how to improve our current models, please go ahead and write it! I check this thread often, and re-fit the opening posts to be more cohesive and concise.
