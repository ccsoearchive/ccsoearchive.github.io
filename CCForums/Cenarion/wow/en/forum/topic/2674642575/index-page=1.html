List of Update Suggestions


-Rigging Updating-

1. Bones
The lines that hold your character's stance. Like a stick figure, there's a line attached to each vital limb.

a. Consider adding bones for extra mobile pieces.
    Example 1: Troll ears that bounce like the blood elves.
    Example 2: A dwarf beard that flops around.
    Example 3: Various toes that wiggle.
    Example 4: Draenei Facial Appendages that quiver with anticipation.
    Example 5: Forsaken Jaws that dangle.
    Example 6: Long hair that flows, with a drifting delayed reaction time.


b. Wildcard bones!
Consider specifying certain 'sticks' within the model's construction to move under environmental effects. It would be amazing to see a pony tail or hair curl bounce in the direction of a gust of wind. It could also be a round about way of adding a 'wind' effect in game.

2. Wireframes
Enhancing the Polygon count could smoothen out unnecessarily jagged forms. This does double duty, as improving these curves add to the flow of Skin Texture values and gradients.

3. Height/Size
Pygmy Oil can be a great meter for this! It shrinks you in small degrees, and many roleplayers already utilize it (along with its giant growth alternative). The change is almost unnoticeable, but the scaling technology is certainly in game.
Consider adding this to our Character Creation screens! Example, clicking through 5 options for increasing and decreasing size: ( -2, -1, 0, +1, +2 )
To implement it, allow each character a free size shift on the log in screen or make it an ingame customizable feature. (Witchdoctor Voodoo/Gnome Size-combobulator!)

X. Pitfalls to Height/Size.
a. When implemented, keep NPCs simply at their default (aka 0 degrees of change).
b. Keep the range noticeable but not too exaggerated. An large (+2) Orc matching a smallish Troll is fine. A Goblin towering over a Forsaken may be too much, as far as identifying a race quickly goes.


-Posture Improvements-

1. Spines
Adding a curve to their wireframe/bones would radically take away those squarish looks of the older models! In addition, making it bendable would contribute to more fluent emotes/movements.

2. Concerning Walk v.s. Run Visuals
a. Walk is generally the NPC/Rper's movement.
Walking is often aesthetic, and changing an animation in this would have minimal effect on the player base's gameplay perception. It will also make all the Warcraft heroes look good in their leisurely stride. Perhaps add a sway to some, a bounce in their step to another. We all walk the same road but do so a little differently.

b. Running is a PVE/PVP key mechanic.
We all do it! 99% of the time. Consider updating the visual queues players take to know their character is running! It has to look different from /walk. If it isn't, it becomes hard to distinguish from when your character is lagging/slowed down in motion.

There are four main contributors to the act of running: Leaning, twisting, leg kicking, and bobbing. Twisting/bobbing are key in many models, and is absent from the older model variants. This is when the shoulders swing opposite to the legs.

Example 1:
    Draenei have a Waggle to their run, partly because their body twists slightly as it does! Which is natural for them and becomes visually evident with their tails, capes, and weapons.

Example 2:
    Now, compare it to the Human model who appears to lean, but it is only the arms/legs that give the semblance of movement. Also, the small twisting they are doing is improper. The proper motion is with the leg and arm coordinating together but on opposite sides of the body. Not right arm and right leg together, or viceversa. This may be due to their lack of hip to torso motion.


-Body/Skin Textures-
The art department has always excelled at this. With updated and smoothened wireframes this department could reach new heights.

1. Crisper Body Details and Smoother Gradients
I can see almost every speckle on a Goblin, but then on an Older Model they look pixelated or roughly blended. These may be inherient in the flattened skins or the nature of a model's wireframe geometry.

2. Tattoos, Scars, and You!
Think Hair Salon. Tattoos/Scars would be wonderful. One could imagine it implemented like the Glyph menu! Perhaps have the character's stand spread eage, ala Da Vinci Diagram, with places to insert the design you want (like the Operation Board Game). Backs, Faces, Arms, Legs, Stomachs, and Upper chests are all viable places.

Tattooing could combine with the Inscription very seamlessly! It could be both a flavorful use of pigments and an enterprise for future profiteers! The type of Tattoos available may vary depending on race, best left up to the Skin graphic artists to help determine what will work.

Example:
    Nozdormu is a good example of Tattoos:
    http://www.wowpedia.org/images/d/d0/Nozdormu_Elf.jpg


Cosmetic Scars could fit in with Skinning, following this line of thinking. Instead of simply overlapping with Tattoos with designs, they could be more distinctive. Facial or body marks. Claw marks, sword grazes, and anything else one can imagine!

As for NPCs that teach or facilitate it: Any number of colourful npcs/groups could be created!
Not necessarily all Goblins either. Trolls and Orcs both appear viable. And for the Alliance: Dwarves, Humans, and even Worgen.


-Emotes-

1. Idle
Many races still shrug as their idle emote. The current direction of idle emotes towards "looking around" are a great replacement to this. Take the Draenei, Forsaken, or Worgen for example. The female Worgen sniffs the air, which is a nice addition!

Another opportunity would be adding variant Idle animations for players to choose from. Perhaps a simple one of three selection. Some may be creative additions, or simply the lack of an unfavorable animation.
Examples:
    Choosing to opt out of the Worgen sniffing idle, adding a stretching animation to a lithe night elf, or having no animation for Forsaken wishing to creep others out by standing perfectly still.


2. Dances (specifically a Dance Studio)
Two variants of this service commonly come to mind:
a. A studio where you pick each motion and arrange them.
b. Browse through dances, select one and keep it until switched again.
Players enjoy the tradition of taking from Popular Culture. Though don't feel limited, there are many traditional/world dances one can draw from.

X. Pitfalls of the Dance Studio
a. Copyright infringement when using Pop. Culture.
Take note of Blizzcon and the absence of offical music to each dance during the contests.

b. Forgetting the race's roots.
This would be like forgetting to add flips and movements to a troll, or adding ballet to Tauren's repertoire.

3. /Wink
This wouldn't be too hard to animate, would it? /winks slyly


-Facial features-

1. Retaining Expressions
An important aspect not to neglect in an update, players spend alot of time familiarizing themselves with their avatar's looks and those of their friends. When updating these, look for key feelings the expressions give. Are they scowling and look upset, are they grinning maniacally? Pouting and about to cry? Smirking like a brat? Easy going, laid back?

In addition, note muzzle-type models (that Tauren and Worgen) may like expressions too. Perhaps some smiles, some frowns, and the vanilla neutral expression.

2. Retaining Unique Traits
Features that shouldn't disappear from each face. Things like Freckles, Blush, Cheek/Chin definitions, Eye narrowness, etc.

3. Retaining the 'vague' Ethnicity
The model designers have always done a great job toning this down to a degree where it is an aesthetic and not literal racial features of real life. With updated models, do your best to keep these identities both visible yet somewhat indistinguishable. A balancing act for sure, but a great touch to this world-wide game.

4. "Where is the X face?!"
Add a few more faces, to fill in the gaps left in the race's spectrum of expressions.
Here is a rough list of common notable faces we often see:
    -Aged
    -Scowler
    -Madness
    -Gladness
    -Placid
    -Perky
    -Sad Puppy
    -Confident Smirker
    -Perplexed
    -Grim


In addition, balancing the number of monstrous/fierce versus comely/handsome faces is appreciated(more on that further on!)


5. Eyes
Most races have an colorful spectrum of eye variants, each connected to the face type chosen. If new face type variants were added as mentioned earlier, so too could more eye colors conceivably! Some races are notably lacking in variation.
Example:
    The Troll Female model have one orange eye color and no blink animation.

The other exceptions are the beings with glowing eyes. Draenei, Blood Elves, and Nightelves. Ontop of the glacial eyes of Deathknights.


6. Beards
A great addition to any race lacking them, Forsaken/Troll to name two. This graphic can be tied right into hair color. Allowing the clean cut version is always handy too.

7. Hairstyles
We have seen a peek into what Blizzard is cooking for Hairstyles. Previously mentioned was the idea of adding more 'bones' to the hair, allowing more dynamic movements. To expand our imaginations, below is a great thread that samples a variety of fanmade hairstyles:

Fiorelle/Haerle's Hairstyles
http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2548998084

Model Education


A -vast- resource lays in wait to support Blizzard developments:
It's own Community! With enough resources and offical support, we the community could better support the game we love!

You likely have thousands of aspiring artists and designers, many with computer skills and 3D development understanding. Perhaps some don't prefer Fanart or Comics, but still wish to submit to the greater good! Maybe some want to make a special portfolio piece?

Why not put us to work?


-Skins-
Provide us with the tools and/or templates!
For example, here is what we look like flattened out!

    http://i.imgur.com/Lzh4b.png
    http://i.imgur.com/xm3BE.png
    http://i.imgur.com/ud1gH.png
    (source: WoW Model Viewer)


A simple perspective, no?

Why not create a java interface, where we can upload template creations onto blank 3d models? (Inappropriate submissions removed of course, we all can't have poptarts for Torsos. ;) )
Perhaps host a contest at the end of a month, or have Developers hand chose a number of submitted skins. Even a +like vote system could be viable.


-Bare Bones-
Examples of model bones:

    http://i.imgur.com/oiYk0.jpg
    http://i.imgur.com/tvU5c.jpg


For most bone structures, the straightest parts are arms, legs, and spines. You will notice extensions falling down the back for cape and hair. The pieces extending up out of the spines hold form for models with mane hair or with visible tropezius structures (aka hunching over).

In newer models, we have seen improvements for more complexity. Female Draenei, for instance, support a two-tier arch in their spines. Worgen males also carry a subtle hunching three-tier arch, accentuated by their outstretching muzzles.


-Wireframes-
    http://i.imgur.com/i5FOP.jpg
    http://i.imgur.com/kUJI9.jpg


Complex, but still manageable.

As a few general rules of thumb, the face is more detailed and complex than the body. Sharper angled forms distinguish joints and bone. Round steady forms represent places of muscle and fat. As an overall picture of each body, we can think of each major part in their elementary geometric forms. Spheres, cylinders, squares, pyramids, etc.


-Animatronics-
    http://i.imgur.com/OKEpo.jpg


183 animations in total. Sampled from the Worgen Male.

Each animation can consist of 1000+ frames of motion. However, it may not be as complex as it seems on first glance. Recall the bones? They are the main structure of your character, move one piece and the wireframe warps and distorts accordingly, in a good way! With keyframes and re-positioning, this can create fluid movement.

Of course, there are always unforeseen errors. Editing and smoothing these out may take up the bulk of time doing this, Ontop of already having great emote ideas and planned out movements. Even the process of posing a 3d character is a job onto itself.


-Model Geosets-

To put on armor that isn't snug to the skin, each character model has built in wireframe 'extensions' to support different armor skins. Each part of the body that holds armor currently comes with 2-5ish~ variants, depending on the part of the body.

List of Geosets (Night Elf Female)
    http://i.imgur.com/yqhZA.jpg


In the following models, note that armor-skins shape onto these extension wireframes. The armor itself, besides specific models or weapons, do not have innate wireframes structures of their own.
Example:

    36 [Trousers, 1] replaced with 39 [Trousers, 2]
    http://i.imgur.com/Yaqs1.jpg


    Night Elf with Plate, proper geosets versus default
    http://i.imgur.com/N4vk4.jpg
    http://i.imgur.com/Y3r1G.jpg


This shows that not every piece of armor requires coding for location. They appear to fit under a number of general archetypes: Large boots, Skirts, Narrow Gloves, Thick Gloves, Etc. Instead, most armor pieces need a Geoset listing. One that could tell them which parts of the body needs to be turned on/off in order to make the armor look correct (large hair disappearing for a helmet, etc).

-Overview-
Allowing the player base to contribute would be an immense boon to production, specifically in regards to updating current visual content. No spoilers done, no content leaked. Simply fans/players contributing to the game we love.

Aesthetics of the Avatar and the Overall Picture


1. Customization enhances racial visual queues, not impede them.
Adding a plethora of customization will further enrich the game, with miniscule resistance as the fanbase adapts. For instance, attempt remembering which hairstyles were the originals and which were implemented later with the Barbershop. A wider range of customization helps build player identity and uniqueness too, without affecting game balance or lore issues, if they are not overly farfetched.
Example:
    Take the Worgen and Troll druid forms. Each feel fairly customized, each unique. Trouble knowing which they are? No, because you -know- it's a troll druid that passed by you! Despite whatever customization it had, it's style/theme shined through! How could you not, you were blinded by that flashy mohawk! Or how about those rich Gilnean colors and spikey collars around that Worgen?

This same concept could be applied to racial models, enhancing visual queues!


2. Players want to choose the Race they like, not the Superstar.
As an example, many players of the Horde love older races: the Tauren, Troll, Orc, and Forsaken. They love their story lines, they love their flavor/atmosphere, air about them. However they find the models difficult to avate themselves as, and often feel a falling out into what are deemed the "prettier/updated" races to fulfill their hope of exemplifying the hero inside themselves to their friends/community.


3. Don't undersell races as outdated or unpopular
With marketing specifically. The poor Draenei Male has been in two ads pointed against him. One a re-customization, the other a faction change. I personally see them as charming, but one suspects the Marketing department is aware of these disparities in model designs. With re-vamped models, the whole range of races would become available to properly showcase Warcraft.


4. Players -love- Large Customizable Menus
Fewer options can be just as much a curse as having more options, but the later makes those that need it happy. Many players would give up a clean customization screen for a million buttons. It is a short (though exciting!) portion of creating or re-customizing characters. Those that don't mind much can be quick and random, those that do are will spend as much time as they need.

To be economical with the space and/or not intimidate new players, an advanced option could always be added as a tab. Another suggestion was leaving certain customizations kept in game, incase players wished not to commit until later.


5. Monstrous Versus Comely/Handsome

a. Don't reach a middle ground. Met them both ways!
Aesthetics are difficult, especially when contested. The development of Worgen females is a prime example of this. Multitudes of players spoke out on this, and the results still leaves some players unsure how to feel about the models in their current shape. Some share a confusion over the Worgen female's appearance. They are savage yet attempt to be comely at the same time. Later, the reasoning for such narrow looks may have been due to the race being a "muzzle" model , like the Tauren, that are limited to one expression.
If it is within power to allow a model both furious and comely expression choices it will satisfy the majority, as it has for the other races.

    mmo-champion.com/threads/827548-Female-Worgen-What-would-you-improve/page40

An offsite thread; however very worthwhile. It contains an amazing collection of fanmade work done for Female Worgen. Visual displays, thorough discussion, and artwork. Well worth browsing through.


b.Sexual Dimorphism
Exaggeration is an important tool, as with small avatars it quickly conveys a message rather economically.
Visual queues like hips/breasts versus muscle definition/wide chest shouldn't be marginalized by any means, but various other methods are available as well.
Examples:
    -Chins
    -Noses
    -Overhang of the Brow
    -Posture (The way one stands/walks can vary by gender!)
    -Thickness of Fingers
    -Arch of the Back

In the lieu of (or combined with!) these overall customizations, consider allowing players access to the variant skins or functional models that exist.

Concluding Words


-The great Re-Vamp! Where to start?-

1. Refitting all those NPCs!
Two ways!
a. If npc models are connected directly to player models, the transfer should be automatic!
b. Catalogue all the racial Npcs (ex: ".. was Bonethunder an Orc or Tauren?")
Wowhead is a great resource for this. Plus the entire community would contribute to cataloging any racial models needed to be defined!

2. Generate the Npc's likeness
This is where managing those model appearances pays off! The closer they looked, the better this works!

3. Voting for NPC Appearances?
Stuck? Or too much man hours to do? Why not let the fans do the pulling? Consider this: Have Wowhead or whichever website add a voting system to each NPC needed to shift. At the end each are tallied and distributed to each npc correspondingly.

X. Pitfalls

Racial Variants: Be careful to not mix these up! This is where we can help. Sub-races could be a tricky case as there are many subtleties in some character stories. See the Sub races list a page back for a sample listing!

Q u o t e:
Q: Still no word on updated player models for classic races makes me angry. 3 expansions with not a single update on it.
A. We definitely want to do it. Our artists would love to - believe me. The worgen and goblin took an enormous amount of art and animation time for Cataclysm. It's definitely on the list though. We recognize the problem with old models in shiny new armor.


4.2 new hairstyles update!
We did have a lot of new hairstyles we had created over time up on the patch 4.2 Public Test Realms. The problem was that, even though these hairstyles were added on the test realms, many of them were not finalized or approved by our art team. Because of this, a good portion of them (about half) were pulled shortly before patch 4.2 was released on live realms, leaving only a partial implementation of new hairstyle choices.

Unfortunately, this means some races/genders now have more hairstyles than others. This result had nothing to do with intentionally giving some races more choices than others. It was based purely on which hairstyles we felt were ready to go live, and which we felt were not.

These new hairstyles are also not available at the Character Selection screen and can only be chosen via the Barbershop. We definitely plan on going back over the hairstyles which did not make it into the game and adding them at a later date.
