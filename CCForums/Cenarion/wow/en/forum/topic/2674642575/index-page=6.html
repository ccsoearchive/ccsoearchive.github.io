The issue with adding such fine detail to the character models is the fact that you really won't see any of it during gameplay because WoW does not use true to life character models. We are cartoons and we wear big armor. Not to say that's a bad thing, as the style is what keeps WoW from looking as dated as it is.

Yes the newer models look better naked than the classic models, but in armor you see none of that and to be honest is not worth the investment. Anything skin related like textures and tatoos is basically worthless and even changing the models to smoother model shapes would be next to invisible unless you were RP'ing naked or perhaps if they decided to start giving females more revealing armor sets again.

The only significant change they could make to our characters that would be immediately noticable during gameplay would be changing the animations. Some races have better and more fluid animations than others, even some classic races like Night Elf females have very well done animations while others like Humans look a little unnatural.

Otherwise putting a large amount of effort into anything else body-related is would be a gigantic waste of time. Face, hair, armor, and animations, those are the only things that will make a difference.

Haven't read all the original posts yet, but I support new models for us. Thrall's newest looks great, Kalecgos doesn't have the human hare lip, Sylvanas looks great, Malfur....um...Sylvanas looks great!

While I don't like Malfurion (drop the mustache and wings and he looks right), having him stand next to Tyrande (one of the few leaders without a custom model) and it's glaring how badly it's needed.

OP put a LOT of work into this! Well thought out and presented just by skimming it. Time to start reading! :)

Having read more fully, I will comment further on a couple points:

The OP is very in-depth and thought out...perhaps too much so. While many of the ideas and suggestions would be quite impressive, they would also put a tremendous strain on the resources required to play WoW. Keep in mind that Blizzard wants WoW operating on low end computers and the more you add, the more your requirements bump up.

Not to say none of this is possible. Compare the facial expressions on the goblins during their attack animations to even their more recent "kin" the blood elf models and you'll see quite a step up in detail.

Moving ears associated with animations, facial expressions, higher polygon count, more natural appearance with apparent curvature of spine, etc are all things you might see in the near future when they update the original models (a project the art team is eager to start on, according to the art team at 2010 Blizzcon).

But the flow of hair, the twitch of tendrils, different heights/weights, and other smaller nuances are possibly pushing the low end computer past the point of friendly performance. If you clean up and trim down the post, I'd consider those sort of factors and focus on what WoW can do with its current field in mind. BE, Dranei, Worgen, and Goblins prove there's a lot they can still do, as well as the racial leaders and dragons having unique-ish models.

Re: Sub-Races
I don't really see sub-races as being necessary, but each race getting two models per gender would be pretty cool. Mostly each race could have a muscular version more like the current ones and a slender version for those wanting to look less built (a lot of casters I know would like a slender model) and more lithe.

Wonderfully helpful support, criticisms, and ideas so far. Thank you all!

I hope the Subrace section is not throwing too many off. It was made more to show-off the current state of humanoid models. The faction/race sequences are a close estimate, but meant more as a visual display for model possibilities then a definite for those peoples' direction.

Mutterscrawl mentioned the Vrykul, which many could agree may not translate well story-wise or in their current states.
