It had been a long day for the pretty witch as she had been studying and shopping since dawn. Her head was full of new found knowledge, her eyes hurt from reading the faded ink of several large tomes, and Zyss' feet hurt from wandering about the bazaar, finding some bread, and other sundries she needed for a nice soup.

As Zyss came to her street, she looked around, noticing the shadows had grown darker and longer as the sun began to set behind the walled city of Silvermoon. Her arms were full of groceries and books as she headed for the small cottage she called home. She stood before the old wooden door, and tried to get the key into the lock, her tired arms trying to lift the key to the lock. She stopped as a dark shadow fell over her, and an arm reached out to grab her.

Books flew, and the bag of food stuffs was knocked to the floor, potatoes rolled about the porch, as well as several eggs, while a few lay broken close to the door, and the head of cabbage rolled down the front steps. A hand covered her mouth, as the arm pulled her close to a tall skinny body. Her teeth bit down hard on a finger too close to them, and a loud yelp resounded in the fading light, for that brief moment she whispered a few hurried syllables, and fell towards the door from the back handed smack she received for biting the assaulter. She looked up in fear as a fist came up to strike her again. But it never fell on her.

The demon she had called picked the man up and threw him several feet into the air and almost clear across the road. Even as the attacker was slowly getting up, the claws of the demon ripped across his chest and then lifted him up. The dark pools that served as eyes for the large demon gazed into the eyes of the attacker, and the demon almost laughed as he smelled the sour smell of fresh urine.

“Let him go, Krillnac,” the warlock said.
