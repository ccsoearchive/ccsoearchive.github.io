-You walk the streets of the Dwarven District, Stormwind, in the dead of night. The streets are all but pitch black, the darkness only being broken by the flickering light of your torch. You finally find what you are looking for: Flintcrag’s Arsenal, an unremarkable little store sitting on a dusty corner of a Dwarven District street. Tentatively, you push on the old, creaky door. The hinges protest as it slowly opens, revealing a dimly lit store, with an elderly dwarf standing behind the counter-

“Bit late tae be doin’ some shoppin’, innit?” The dwarf says in a gruff and irritable voice. He puffs smoke from his pipe absently, while cleaning out the barrel of one of his guns. “What do ye want?”

-Your mind is cast back to a few days ago, and the interesting little flier that caught your eye as you glanced at the billboard in your local town. Red, with a single black eye, it said simply “If you wish to protect this world, seek out the Eternal Watchers.” You tore a slip of paper from the poster, and writing burned into the paper, and your mind. “Travel to Flintcrag’s Arsenal, Stormwind, at midnight.”-

Now, you have found it, and the dwarf raises an eyebrow as you respond to his question. “The Eternal Watchers, ye say?” He beckons, and opens a door at the back of the shop, behind the counter. He holds out his hand, and points. “In there if ye want tae know more. If no’, it’s best ye turn back now.”

-If you were to walk in, the door would slam shut as if by a gust of wind. BANG! You are left in utter darkness. Suddenly, a male voice would speak. It seems to surround you. In the darkness, you cannot identify its source.-

“Welcome, seeker. I am your guide. You have boldly sought out an order that has a past shrouded in mystery, an order that has long been a shadow of its former self. But this shadow, is growing…”

“You must think that with all this talk of shadows, we are some form of evil cult.” The deep voice laughs, a surprisingly happy sound.

-You would sense amusement in is voice at the next comment-

“Well, let me assure you. Not everything that works from the shadows is evil.”

-Suddenly a dim light would illuminate the room. Despite the fact that the lighting is dull, in contrast to the pitch black you were formerly experiencing, it still seems momentarily blinding-

-The source of the voice is revealed before you, however. A reasonably young, bearded human clad in red and black armour. On his chest is a tabard, proudly emblazoned with a large black eye upon the deep blood red background. It is finely crafted, ornate even.

-You would also now see around the room. It is circular, and three small statues stand on pillars at about waist height to the average human male. Each has a feel of some kind of magic; there is more to these statues than meets the eye. One is simply a statue of an open book, its pages covered in an indecipherable language. It pulses with an eerie purple light. The second is some form of gun, technical, precise, and engraved with an image of crossed swords. It gleams a brilliant gold. The third is a fist of black iron, clamped hard and strong, and appearing unbreakable. The man speaks quietly and calmly, a gentle smile on his face-

“We hide in the shadows, because that is where our enemies hide. There are forces of evil in this world that nobody notices, that elude the eyes of armies and nations, that go on for centuries as a secret blight upon society, with nobody ever noticing. The Cult of the Damned, the Twilight’s Hammer and many other such organizations were not discovered by nations and the world until it was too late. If only there were Watchers active, people who lingered in the shadows, and engaged the enemy on their own level… then perhaps Lordaeron would never have been plagued. Perhaps the Cataclysm would never have occurred. There is much that could have been prevented….”

-He sighs-

“We are an anti-cult, you could say. The Eternal Watchers lingers in the shadows so that we can prevent darker powers from crawling out. We hide within society to notice those who also do so, for darker purposes. We engage enemies that can move fast and stealthily, too fast for armies, but able to be reached by those who can also move fast and stealthily.”

-He looks at you intently-

“If you wish to join us…. I will not lie. It is not easy. You will be in danger. But… you will be able to fight to save this world. To protect it from the evil lurking in the darkness that we cannot see.”

-The statues pulse with light-

“There are many paths we take. We have a long and detailed history, and we fight towards our shared goal in a variety of ways.”

-He points towards the book-

“The Keepers of the Lost, the Lor’Verloren. These are the seekers of knowledge. They search for and record all manner of things, and are invaluable in the pursuit of justice. Without knowledge, we are blind.”

-He points toward the gun-

Well, assuming you got through that wall of text and remain interested, here’s some FAQ’s and OOC info for you!

Who are we?
The Eternal Watchers is an ancient secret society with its origins in human prehistory. We are dedicated to safeguarding the world against the many threats that come against it, from behind the scenes, no matter the cost.

Are you an active RP guild?

Yes, I’d say we are! We have weekly RP events, and average 15 or so participants per event. So rest assured, if you want to join, it won’t be a case of “Join my awesome guild, we have no members and will probably abandon our entire concept within a week.” I promise, we’re pretty good! :P We also are active throughout the week, having sporadic guild RP pretty much daily.

When do you actually RP?

We tend to be a slightly late night guild, due to a population of workers, and two Australians. We aren’t utterly unattainable though, with our events happening every Saturday server time, 8pm. They usually go until about 10 or 11. Our weekly sporadic RPs are much more spaced out, ranging anywhere from around midday server until about 5am. So, we aren’t TOO late, and should be fairly easy to reach in RPs.

Do you have Ventrillo?

Nope, but we have mumble! We’re pretty active on our speaking, but it isn’t mandatory. So if you’d rather chat than type, we’ll definitely let you into our mumble server, and give you the details when you join.

Do you have a guild website?

Not yet, but it’s a work in progress. We’re not planning to go with the signing up route, however. I mean, who needs MORE passwords. We’re working on a guild tumblr, and potentially a guild wikia, to chronicle all of our lore and our wonderful characters! Yaaay history!

Are you a lore abiding guild?

Yep! We’re not utter !@#$’s who believe all Night Elves must love Elune (why would the Twilight’s Hammer be full of Flame Druids if that were the case?) but we aren’t a total fanfic either. So please do not join us if all you want is vampire ERP. We don’t vampire RP. Okay? Ever. Please, no vampire ERP. You can give us doughnuts, but not vampire ERP. We clear? Good!

So how's it work? What are the paths?

The paths actually have their origins in a number of ancient human tribes, formulated not too long after the Vrykul dropped their babies off in Arathi. Interestingly, in our particular branch of human prehistory, four distinct tribes sprung up, and joined briefly together to face a great enemy. As such, the Eternal Watchers were born as a union of four incredibly diverse tribes, that later slipped from tribal society to a much more ideological group of people, united, but following certain traditions left by the original tribes, and acting for many centuries as a small group in service to the Empire of Arathor. One of these tribes, the Black Claw, separated from the Watchers in its infancy, and as such are not part of the current Eternal Watchers, actually serving as an antagonist group in a few of our RP plots. The three remaining tribes are listed in the guild post above, and are fairly summed up here. The Lor’Verloren are the lorekeepers. They love knowledge, learning and exploring, often for its own sake. They can be quite grey in terms of morals, some being harsher than others, because ultimately they will go to great lengths to attain the knowledge they seek. The Gunnaroth are, ideologically, our resident paladins. Their focus is on honor, virtue, protecting the innocent and the weak, showing bravery, exercising mercy, and so on. The Mer’Catharn are our resident Sith Lords. They’re very focused on strength, power, and passion. They’re the most likely to stoop to their enemies level when there is need, which often puts them at odds with the Gunnaroth. There’s a certain discipline in the Mer’Catharn, however, as they often walk dark paths to prevent evil. This, as we’ve seen with many characters in WoW like Arthas, Maiev, Illidan, etc, doesn’t always lead to good things. As such, their mental strength is perhaps their most important feature, as they must walk a razor’s edge between good and evil.

Do certain classes have to join certain paths?

Nope! As much as we’re conditioned to think that warlocks must be evil, mages must love knowledge, and paladins must roflstomp the wicked, we honestly don’t mind if you have a super pious holy priest, for example, sitting in the Mer’Catharn. Hell, the Scarlets had some pretty dark priests, you can too! The focus of our guild isn’t on abilites, so much as ideologies. You choose an ideology that fits your character. Class and abilities mean absolutely nothing unless something you do is in direct violation of said ideologies. (So a Gunnaroth Death Knight who tortures children PROBS ain’t gonna fit. But if said Death Knight deals with their addiction by killing something less sentient, there’s more of a place for them in that particular branch)

How do I join?

Simple! Whisper or mail one of the officers, and we'll get back to you asap! Whisper our Guildmaster, Kalden, or one of the three Thanes, (Myself, Amaai, and Kåluu). The other option is of course to type /who Eternal Watchers and whisper us! We’re a connected guild, so you can reach us through anyone!

So I've got access to the guild.... then what?

When you're invited, you'll be immediately bumped up to initiate rank. You'll have an IC interview and remain an initiate for a time. Then, you'll be given a trial to prove your worth at the path you select, be it Gunnaroth, Lor’Verloren or Mer'Catharn. That will facilitate your full membership.

Any other questions? Ask them below in the comments!

Thanks for reading!!!

- The Eternal Watchers Team
