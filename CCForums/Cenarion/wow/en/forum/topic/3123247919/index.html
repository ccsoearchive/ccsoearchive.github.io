The priestess walked over to the human sitting by the moonwell, her face clearly showing looks of wonder and amusement. This particular human had been known to visit the Temple of the Moon from time to time, and often displayed a fervor towards Elune that was highly uncharacteristic for one of both her race and her path in life. This time, however, she looked far more serene and at peace.

"Dear lady, my apologies, but I have often seen you here, and then with such a look to your person that you appeared distressed, even relieved. Today I sense the goddess has done something special in your life?"

"Indeed, priestess. My heart today has found another to rest with, and things are most enjoyable. I came to offer the goddess my thanks for this, her latest of many blessings to me."

"The goddess is glad that you have come to this place and made your act of thanks, but she... I... have wondered why."

"Ah, you ask why I do not offer prayers in one of my own cathedrals? The Light, yes, I hold to its philosophy of the virtues, and am thankful for its deeds and presence, but it was Elune who has been my strength and shield ever since I have reawakened into this world. It is also she who has found the heart I long for and returned this wayward son back into the arms of her people."

The priestess immediately understood that Vanressa spoke of one of the Highborne. Confusion gave way to fear and distrust, but the solemnity and tranquility of the Temple and the joy radiating from Vanressa could give pause to even the most hardened heart.

"How unusual. How very fortunate as well. The one you pursue, does he find room in his heart for the goddess as well?"

"I would hope so, for we both plan to be wed here in Darnassus, in the presence of the goddess, this place, and the priests. We have found that our hearts can conquer much together, but we also know that we are alive and well because of blessings from on high."

"Then make your preparations, and in a month's time, we shall bind you into a union."

Vanressa nodded at the priestess, and then returned her gaze to the moonwell. For the first time, she no longer stared blankly into its waters. She no longer saw a shimmering pool of arcane energies. She understood why she was in the temple and had been drawn to this place for so long.

"Keyesh... it finally makes sense now."

In her revelry and celebrating the holiest day of the dwarven year, alive with the great spirits of the ale elementals coursing through her veins, Brenri joyfully sprouted flames from her finger tips. Sadly, one such tongue landed on the stack of invitations for a certain wedding, invitations she was supposed to be taking to the central mailbox in Ironforge.

"Och... well... maybe I can find a scribe or something. We need t'have th'wedding before the bad weather sets in anyhoo. Aye, that and I can surely send out more invitations and really give th'happy couple a surprise."

Aloft on the winds of her high altitude homeland, Brenri ran with as much grace as could be expected of a lass with thighs the size of small tree trunks. Not surprisingly, she was far more upright and lucid than many of her fellow folk of the mountain, and whizzed past rambling crowds of drunken brawls and laughter. At last she came before the arcanery in the district of the Great Forge and tried her best to communicate with the sober gnome proprietor.

"A set of invertashuns madam? Yes, we can have these ready for you. I would suggest sending invitations instead though. HEE HEE HEE!"

Brenri just kept her mouth open trying to comprehend the joke and then simply smiled and pulled one of the remaining invitations from her braids.

"Quite the talent you have. What sort of device do you use to braid so much storage space into your hair?"

"I dinnae know. I think it was just the AquaGnome hair spray m'man Timeus suggested to me long ago. Grand stuff really, waterproof, climate controlled, even... aye, I keep m'frogs in there when I go flying."

"I see. How truly horrible. Now, about these invitations..."

"Aye?"

"As you know, we handled the last batch for that Vanressa lady of yours, and she left us explicit instructions regarding the guest list. I see here that you wish to disregard such things?"

"Aye! What's a party if only one or two entire clans show up?"

"Madam! Well... it is up to you I suppose. I did notice that she had a few names listed here with skulls and void ink scrawled over them. I wager she would not want them invited."

Brenri put on a red colored monacle to better read the names, and after squinting and trying her best to read, she took far more amusement in the various notes and pictures drawn by certain names.

"Better invite them all. No need to let a few past insults and bickering scare us out of a grand old wedding brawl. Ah heh... she really hates Cyrus. Unless three burning skulls means love."

With that, she slammed a bag full of gold on the table, along with several choice gems. She need the work done quickly, and knew full well that a good tip can mean the difference between wedding and mere mixer.

Come one and all at 7pm server on the 30th of this month to the wedding! This includes Horde, we could always use a good fight to make Darnassus more interesting.

Arlston began seriously boarding up his mailslot at the SI:7 barracks. Especially now that another letter sat there, slightly stained with ale...

It had always been empty from the first year, after all, he had nobody who cared for him, nobody concerned about how he was doing, and he had nobody who KNEW him. And he would've kept it that way. Nobody wanted to get to know a man who danced with death every time he stepped outside the barracks. A man who would die suddenly and be forgotten the next day.

But now, ever since the Pillar had recruited him, he was forced into situations he was always uncomfortable. Weddings, just another word for a large social gathering and raucous activities. Arlston sighed slightly, keeping his face perfectly emotionless.

"I...guess...I have...security...overwatch."

There would be no celebration for him. He had dedicated his life so others could be happy.
This wedding would go off without a hitch, even if he had to shed blood to do it.

Vanressa tossed and turned in her sleep as she endured what seemed to be one night after the next full of nightmares and the faces that she had left behind to embark on this new life. Most disturbing of all, she kept seeing Venyn. Clearly she was far from being over him, even if their worlds now seemed to be extremely distant. Keyesh had seemed so appealing to her, so much like what she had wished Venyn could have been had he not chosen to follow a darker path and cast off most of the former vestiges of his life in the Alliance.

In her dream she walked through the valleys of Orgrimmar and walked with apprehension past the orcish guards that used to be a common sight in her life. She saw trolls selling trinkets, tauren conversing with one another, and... green, oozing, decrepit fingers.

She awoke in a cold sweat, and found that her condition was very much that of a woman alive, and that she rested in a bed in the Exodar. This relief would be short lived, as she found her thoughts racing.

"Why will the Fellowship not offer us a priestess for the wedding?"

"Why does Keyesh constantly get surprised in public by various women claiming to have... known him?"

"Why... do I still dream about such things...?"

Reaching into her satchel, she pulled out a small flowerpot with a totem jammed into some dirt contained within it. The totem was a little bit of help from Brenri, given so that she might get some sleep. It had numerous frogs etched on the sides, and a note attached that she had not yet read. She opened it and came across a short message from Keyesh:

"You will know when you are ready."

He was not angry with her for calling off the wedding, but they both knew that it was not an easy thing to do.

The totem began to softly ribbit and croak like a frog, and a half smile came across her face as she fell asleep again beneath the dimmed crystals of the inn room.
