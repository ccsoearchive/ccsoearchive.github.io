So, a few things that have occurred to me in trying to turn my pve Survival success into pvp Survival success, and that might work as easy fixes.

1: Remove the cooldown on Explosive Shot. Right now, Survival hunters have two realistic pve rotations; excluding Serpent Sting/Arcane Shot/Black Arrow, we Cobra Shot three times and Explosive Shot once, or we Cobra Shot four times and Explosive Shot once. Because of the way Rapid Fire, Bloodlust/Heroism, Haste procs, and Resourcefulness work, it can get a bit random at times, and we end up spamming Arcane Shots frequently, when we'd much prefer to be dumping our focus into more Explosive Shots (I've found Arcane Shot to do about a little over 2/3 of the damage-per-focus of Explosive Shot in raids, and that's a pretty steep penalty to take for an ability that still occupies a global cooldown).

2: Fix Black Arrow and Serpent sting to scale with our physical crit chance. That this isn't true already is very odd.

3: Remove Black Arrow from the shared cooldown with Explosive Trap, and have it frontload a large proportion of it's damage. If it still procc'd Lock and Load from it's dot, that'd be fine, but it represents a dps cooldown and a large investment of resources in pvp, and is easily negated by defensive dispels.

4: Change Lock and Load to no longer make your next two shots free, but increase their damage by some reasonable percentage. This, as well as removing the cooldown from Explosive Shot, would allow a sharp increase in pressure (something Survival very desperately needs) as well as preventing us from vomiting fire all over those poor pvpers with our new and improved Explosive Shot that scales with weapon damage without regenerating our focus responsibly.

5: Drop Silencing Shot to a reasonable place in the Marksmanship tree so that both the other specs can acquire it should they choose to. The issues with Hunter cc in pvp are many and varied, and unlikely to be fixed soon or satisfactorily. Silencing Shot being either baseline or available to everyone would give hunters of the other two specs some reasonable ability to interact with casters in pvp (notably, interaction that allows damage on the target to continue, rather than Trap/Scatter/Wyvern Sting, which in all but the most rare of situations must be used to mess with someone other than your dps target).

I am wholeheartedly behind your proposed Raptor Strike/Counterattack/Wing Clip suggestion, as well as most of your others. My concern is, admittedly, more pve- than pvp-centric, but I feel that there's a LOT of room for improvement in both.

One thing I do disagree with is Careful Aim being reverted to 80%. At 90%, it's still a very, very potent pve talent for all specs, and simcraft over at Elitistjerks suggests that it will edge out One with Nature by the middle of this tier for pve dps, and that seems dumb, given that it only affects 10% of a fight. Really, the whole idea of the reverse-Execute talent is wierd and introduces a lot of bizarre issues. You could easily just change it to improve Steady Shot/Cobra Shot damage while in Aspect of the Fox while providing an increased Aimed Shot crit rate or something, and it'd be fine and less of an "occasionally great, frequently worthless" talent.

Speaking as a raiding hunter, I both agree with most of everything put out here, but at the same time think that focus, at least from a PvE perspective, does work nicely in terms of balancing resources versus damage output and disagree with the notion of changing back from focus to mana as a resource as a lot of other (read: bad) hunters commonly whine about. However, as noted, using Steady Shot and Cobra Shot, in their current forms, as a means to regain focus is indeed to easily punishable in PvP. Even with Aspect of the Fox and aspect dancing, it's not fun to know that all my target has to do is run behind a pillar while I'm trying to recover to punish me for trying to burst them. Especially when in arenas, half of the battle is pushing positions around various objects involving line of sight. However, having been around the block and played a few MMOs in my time, as well as other games from nearly every genre out there, I've been thinking about this a lot from a gameplay standpoint and thought of a potential solution;

The idea of focus, its reduced regeneration rate when comared to energy, and Steady Shot and Cobra Shot as a means of recovering/rewarding focus, by itself, is a good mechanic. The problem as I see it is the casting time with those two shots. In raiding/PvE, this isn't a problem because majority of the time, we have and can easily maintain LoS with our targets. So we can easily maintain a steady focus intake during our shot rotations and therefore have plenty of focus to work with in a fight. In PvP, we move around a lot, not just to keep up with a target, but also to save our own hides against someone trying to kill us. And LoS isn't always easy to maintain, both in world PvP as well as in competitive PvP. So, why not simply remove the casting time of these shots and instead give them cooldown timers equal to what the base casting time of Steady Shot and Cobra Shot are now that scale with haste? (ex. Instead of my Steady Shot having a cast time of 1.78s after haste benefits, it now has a 1.78s cooldown after haste benefits) This would, or at least should allow damage levels to stay where they are in PvE while reducing the amount of punishment against us when we need to recover focus in PvP since we can then instantly fire our recovery shot while our target is infront of us instead of having them run behind a pillar while we're winding up only to have the ability fail because we lost our LoS. This would also be a large QoL improvement for us, as it would remove the need for aspect dancing macros and eliminate what is (in my opinion) a very poorly thought out and clunky aspect that also punishes us for needing to recover (Aspect of the Fox). In its place, Aspect of the Monkey could come back, or perhaps even Aspect of the Dragonhawk to give us additional dodge. Or, we simply lose an aspect, which if this change happens won't be a terrible loss, at least in my opinion. As for talents/glyphs that affect steady shot's casting time, those also would be switched over to reduce its cooldown time.

As for other mentioned ideas...
-Seperating Freezing Trap from Ice Trap: Brilliant. But instead of it being a Freezing Trap, why not give us Crystal Prison Trap from Shannox or even just the shiny chain trap the night elf wardens use in the Molten Front? Call it 'Binding Trap' or something similar. To prevent Lock and Load from breaking, give the talent an internal cooldown to prevent a hunter from chaining procs with both the chain trap and the freezing trap.
-Making Black Arrow harder to dispel or undispellable: It still doesn't solve the problem of it being overly expensive in PvP for the damage it deals. It should be given an additional effect of some kind. Maybe reducing the target's resilience by a set percentage while its active?
-Removing Wing Clip and Raptor Strike: Since it looks like Blizzard is leaving us with few options for melee as a means to give us a weakness, this wouldn't be a bad idea so long as we have our minimum shot distance removed. This would also be a nice QoL improvement in the raiding environment for some fights, and would eliminate two more keybinds for us. That or they expand our potential for melee a bit more and make dual wielding a viable choice for us. I'd say the former is far more likely.
-Removing minimum shot distance: See above.
-Merging Widow Venom into X ability: Also a good idea. But MS effects now are nearly worthless in my opinion, so I wouldn't see any harm in this being merged back to Aimed Shot. But then that locks out other specs from obtaining it. Maybe Arcane Shot instead?

I'm going to post my suggestion about Steady and Cobra Shot where hopefully the devs will see it.
