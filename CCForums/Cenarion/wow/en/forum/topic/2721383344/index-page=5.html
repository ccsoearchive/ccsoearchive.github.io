The original thread got me thinking and I like the idea of making the aspects meaningful as an option to making them meaningless - either way is better than now. I'll repeat the outline of what I was thinking.

Make Fox a "close in" aspect, increases wep strike dmg, limits range, reduces ranged damage a little maybe for balance (I don't think it needs to, just makes you get closer - 40s go to 30s, 30s go to 20s, etc.), but creates an "almost" viable hand to hand to remove the deadzone. Apply some of the mechanics suggested in the thread to the shots and it gives you a choice for LoS and Pillar issues, when you get in close you aren't worthless, maybe you can actually finish off on a slightly sub-par skill and overcome the zone of worthlessness that exists now. Maybe it can increase dodge and such as well to par levels. That overcomes some of the big holes at least.

Not a substitute in any way to the excellent expose and work in the thread but a different take on a way to maybe lift the bar in PVP. My rogue buddy and I have the same weps and the hand to hand damage difference is about 100% each hand. Make that more like 40% in fox and perhaps we could eke out those close wins and overcome that dead zone. It would require switching aspects and doesn't fix all the problems, but seems in keeping with what a "hunter" should be. (Clearly you can't be handling a ranged weapon and hand to hand, so when doing one, nerf the other, using aspect as the trigger is the general rule in mind.)

Kudos to the thoughtfulness in the analysis. Good thread.
