As a S1/2 Glad and S3/4 Duelist, I have to say that from all expansions and the original game, BC had the best shot at keeping Hunters in a "manageable" design. All comps with Hunters in them were drain teams and stall teams, and our class had the tools to ensure that a certain target couldn't escape the drain. A Hunter and Priest could make quick work of any Healer's mana, and I loved playing my Hunter competitively. Ever since BC, Blizzard has played around with the class as a whole, changing talents sporadically, moving them into different trees entirely, or giving talents that had long been in one tree to all trees. Changing a class for the better is good. Completely reworking them without any specific reason or explanation is horribly, horribly wrong.

They really have no clue what to do with our class, and a lot of this stems from the fact that we had no Development Team for the entirety of Classic and TBC. We were designed by, mostly, Warlock and Mage Devs. The current virgin Hunter Dev Team doesn't understand the History of the class. They are not sympathetic with our cause because they did not design the problems that create the anger we hold. I can remember a time when the class forums were the only place Blues went, and for the entirety of S3/4 the Hunter forum itself had 3, maybe 4 INDEPENDENT blue posts outside of the usual "these are the changes we're making to your class" stickies they have. Every single other class had very, very active Blues. Ours were dead silent, just as they are now. It isn't that they don't care, it is that we have so much anger, so much hate, that if they post anything we don't like (even the tiniest change like making cats slightly bigger or smaller), they incite an army of angry nerds that will rally against them at the drop of a hat (or the call of a Glad).

It's just the way it is.

Fantastic post, thanks for putting all this together!

However -- if I may play devil's advocate for a moment -- from a developer's perspective, there is also a lot of noise in here. The fact that hunters are a popular class that's rarely found in top-level PvP could just reflect the types of players who are attracted to hunters (pet collectors, for example). Complaints about how specific abilities measure up against other classes can easily be waved away by saying that different classes have different strengths and weaknesses.

Again, this is a great post and I'm not arguing with any of your points. I just think it might be received move favorably if we can separate the wheat from the chaff.

I would suggest creating an additional "short version" that cuts right to the heart of the main issues:
- actual bugs (e.g. trap resists)
- clunky, difficult-to-use abilities (e.g. trap launcher)
- designs with zero gameplay value, just pointless complexity (e.g. aspect macros)
- balance issues that could OBJECTIVELY be considered "unjust," not just different (e.g. having our resource regeneration able to be locked down)

That would be a list of issues that could not possibly be considered QQ, and which no self-respecting community team could let sit unanswered. ;)
