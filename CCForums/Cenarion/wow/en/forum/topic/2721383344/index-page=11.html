Having played this game since Vanilla Beta as a hunter, I can tell you don't hold your breath. The only consistent thing with hunters is that if and when changes do come, they wont come without a fair share of concessions. While we might gain viability in one area, we will lose somewhere else 'to keep things fair'. After that, its just a matter of time before the rest of the WoW community catches up and learns to exploit that.

Lets also not forget that the new stance on class changes voiced by GC is one in which many of these issues will not be addressed until the next expansion. Which in all honesty is such a horrible approach for a popular MMO, it baffles me why its even on the table to begin with. Especially after all the "WoW is like a train with no destination" quotes by designers, in an effort to explain why there have been so many class changes over patches. And I get it. No one wants to relearn and regem with every patch, but some of these issues are so blatantly broken that to leave any class with that state of dysfunction is nearly criminal.

I know its admirable to hold out hope, but the writing is on the wall. All the contradictions, inaccurate statements, bizarre design decisions and complete lack of communication all leads me to one conclusion: Hunters will never, ever be fixed to a point of being a viable PvP class. Ever. Even if Blizzard wanted to, they wouldn't know where to begin. Accept your fate. We will always and forever be the platypus of WoW. A mash-up of failed design ideas, horrid implementation mixed with a complete lack of regard for people who play them, leaving them in no better position than a fat kid in a wheelchair in the middle of a freeway.

Life is rough. Get a helmet.
