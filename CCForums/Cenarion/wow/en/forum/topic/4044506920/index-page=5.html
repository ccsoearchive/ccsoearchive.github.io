Safe fall isn't the only thing missing, we are also missing detect traps.


Safe Fall and Detect/Disarm Traps are Rogue classics that need to remain, but I'm much more worried about Ruthlessness. I had hoped it'd show up after the NDA release, but it hasn't.

Ruthlessness can seem overly random on the surface, but it has a much bigger impact on rotations than other overly random abilities (see Venomous Wounds), and not just for PvE!

HaT doesn't just allow Sub Rogues to generate combo points from other players (a huge advantage), but it also allows Sub to generate cp's from non cp builders. Seal Fate does not do the same thing for Mut. At issue? Where do you get combo points for ranged finishers?

Typically, you build 3-5cp and use a finisher. If you want to have a cp or three available later, when you're not on your target (for say, Recuperate, or Deadly Throw), you need an after-finisher combo point. That's been either another cp builder, HaT, or Ruthlessness.

With Ruthlessness gone, Mut's only option is an after-finisher cp builder ... at a cost of 55 energy. I know it doesn't sound like much, but it has a tremendous impact on PvP. That last cp is extremely important.

I certainly hope that Blizzard sees the issue and either re-implements Ruthlessness or allows Seal Fate to trigger off of non-cp-builder crits.


I wouldn't be to mired in the details of the current specs to project on how things will work in MoP.

First, we potentially have a ranged CP builder. Its a level 90 talent.

Second, we still have Shiv and expose armor is also becoming a combo point builder both of which are "cheap" CP sources.

Third, there is no reason that seal fate couldn't be modified to proc on all special attacks, which neatly solves your issue as you stated, or they could allow each hand to individually proc via seal fate, granting between 2 and 4 combo points which effectively doubles the chance for CP generation off of seal fate procs. Or they could modify mutilates crit rate. The list goes on and on.

The only time HaT procs from the rogue's crits are really an issue is when grouped with only a couple of players, or solo. This could be fixed by simply baking in the old talent, Setup, into HaT. Or it could reward CP every two seconds in combat. That is, if it is even an issue.

Remember, now that we have to choose between non-dps areas, the devs are free to let us melt faces (or backsides as the case may be) with gusto again. I wouldn't get bent out of shape over a minor dps talent and call for a moratorium on MoP just yet.

Things will be different. They will also be fine. The developers now how to make a great product. Let them do their thing.

I've finished a sweep to update a bunch of stuff, and to add a new section that lists some of the most noteworthy reports from players about rogue changes in the Mists beta that we haven't seen officially reported by Blizzard (in the talent calculator or elsewhere).

Two other quick notes:

-- GC posted in the beta class forum to note that rogues will have a Throw ability (it originally wasn't included in the beta, causing a lot of frustration for rogues who had no way to pull flying mobs while questing), but that it will do only a "tiny" amount of damage -- it'll literally be like a warrior's Throw, just used to get an enemy's attention.

-- GC also posted in a monk thread to remind people that they shouldn't put too much stock in the amount of damage that certain abilities are or aren't doing. Quoth the crab:

It is worth pointing out that one of our goals is for nobody's damage to drop when you convert your Cataclysm level 85 character to a Mists level 85 character. The sources of your damage may change as rotations change somewhat, but once you're comfortable with those new rotations, your damage should be similar.

Also remember that number tuning is one of the last things we do. There is no point in spending hours testing numbers if we decide that a particular ability or mechanic was a boneheaded idea and needs to be redesigned. We try to keep the numbers sane enough that players still feel like they can give feedback though.

(source: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/4366249705#3 )

Recently got my beta invite...we do have throw and it does about 500 damage in my gear, but has a .5 sec cast time and no cooldown so can be spammed quite fast. Doesn't apply poisons as far as I can tell, but will proc vial.

Thrown weapons also still exist in the game (my razor saronite chip was in my bags) but thrown weapons are in red for rogues (can't equip them) and there is no slot for them.

In other news, I'm busy testing out my beloved assassination spec. It has a nifty new proc called blindside that allows mutilate to proc a use of Dispatch (the new backstab for assassination) at any health. Dispatch also can be used from the front so our execute phase doesn't have a positional requirement anymore.

My poisons also seem to be critting a LOT more...not sure if it's a bug or what but it's quite nice.

Since the rupture glyph has been removed, the duration is incredibly short on your first 1pt Rupture after a garrote...I usually have it falling off trying to fit a SnD and a 4+ envenom in there, I've never been good with numbers but it might change the order we begin the rotation with for assassination.
