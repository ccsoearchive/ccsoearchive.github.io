Cata came in hard with needing CC, but towards the end, there isn't a call for it really in DS.

I wonder to what extent Blizzard became leery of CC burnout. We know that, with Hour of Twilight, the devs largely went out of their way to avoid forced-CC scenarios, in large part because at this point in the expansion -- thanks to the introduction of the Raid Finder -- heroics have been redefined as the "easiest, quickest" route to Valor. I don't expect that reality to change in Mists.

Regardless, though, no raid fights in Cata came anywhere even remotely close to requiring the level of unusual-ability mastery of Faction Champions -- a fight that infuriated a lot of people back in its day *because* it was so fundamentally different. Sure, interrupts were pretty heavily prioritized in many fights within the first raid tier and Firelands, and Ragnaros' transition phases forced many of us outside our comfort zones, but those moments were few and far between.

And, to play devil's advocate, why shouldn't they be? If we're a pure DPS class, does it even make sense to call for a greater demand on our utility in raid fights? Sure, it might make the fights a lot more interesting for those of us who like the idea, but look at how many rogues were infuriated simply by the fact that Ultraxion can't be backstabbed. I'm not saying it's a orange-to-orange comparison here, but how many more rogues would be up in arms if we were forced to spend entire fights in Mists whipping out various tools in our kit at the expense of DPS, even if it's for the good of the raid?

Ghostcrawler's newly-posted rundown of upcoming stats changes in Mists includes a whole mess of stuff that will change the way we approach certain stats in both PvP and PvE. Since I don't know crap about PvP, I'll ignore the resilience and spell pen stuff for now; you can read more about that within the blog entry itself. I am gonna wholesale copy this stuff on hit and expertise, though (some of which I'll integrate into the main part of this thread shortly):

We still think having stats that can be capped is a good game design. Rather than focusing solely on stacking your best stat, you have to decide how valuable it is to hit your target before you go back to stacking your best stat. However, we are making some changes.
  • Hit and spell hit will no longer be separate stats. The hit stat negates melee miss and spell miss.
  • Expertise will negate dodge and spell miss, then parry.
  • Expertise will be listed as a percentage, just like hit, instead of having an intermediary stat.
  • We are normalizing hit with expertise, so that 1% of each stat will require the same amount of rating.
  • We are normalizing melee and spell hit, so that spell hit is equal to miss plus dodge.
    • Against an equal level creature: 6% spell miss, 3% melee miss, 3% dodge, 3% parry (from the front only), 3% block (from the front only).
    • Against a +1 level creature: 9% spell miss, 4.5% melee miss, 4.5% dodge, 4.5% parry (from the front only), 4.5% Block (from the front only).
    • Against a +2 level creature: 12% spell miss, 6% melee miss, 6% dodge, 6% parry (from the front only), 6% Block (from the front only).
    • Against a +3/boss level creature: 15% spell miss, 7.5% melee miss, 7.5% dodge, 7.5% parry (from the front only), 7.5% block (from the front only).
    • Ranged attacks will be able to be dodged. Hunters will benefit from expertise and will have it on their gear, which will also allow hunters and Enhancement shaman to share gear more easily.


Also included in the blog: Poisons will work off *melee* hit, not spell hit. Which marks the end of rogues' long flirtation with spells-that-aren't-spells.

More at: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/blog/4544194

I don't think I saw you mention that rogues now offer certain buffs? I noticed it on one of MMO's recent posts...

So, I'm struggling with how to note this within the thread, or whether to even bother. GC gets into the details in his buff blog earlier this week, but the gist is that rogues will be able to bring any of five different buffs to a party or raid group:

  • 10% increased haste (buff to allies within 100 yds) through Swiftblade's Cunning, a passive ability we receive at level 30
  • 12% armor reduction (debuff on target) through the redesigned Expose Armor spell, which does no damage, awards 1 combo point and applies a 3%, 30-second armor debuff that stacks 3 times
  • 8% increased spell damage (debuff on target) through... well, I don't see it in the talent calculator, but presumably through a baseline passive ability similar to the Master Poisoner talent Assassination rogues currently spec into
  • 30% casting speed reduction (debuff on target) through Mind-Numbing Poison
  • 25% healing reduction (debuff on target) through Wound Poison


The thing with all this is that almost all of our "raidwide" buffs 1) are things we already offered before Mists, 2) are things we theoretically offered before Mists but didn't use because applying a utility poison to a weapon in PvE was usually stupid, or 3) have potentially serious caveats on them, such as Exposed Armor requiring us to sacrifice 3 global cooldowns worth of damage-dealing special abilities at the start of a fight, and another 1 every 29 seconds or so. I feel it's a stretch in several of these cases to say we're truly bringing a raid buff.
