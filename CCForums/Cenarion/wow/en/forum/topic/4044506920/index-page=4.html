Gonna recopy what I typed in post #60, since the page flipped immediately after:

GC gets into the details in his buff blog earlier this week, but the gist is that rogues will be able to bring any of five different buffs to a party or raid group:

  • 10% increased haste (buff to allies within 100 yds) through Swiftblade's Cunning, a passive ability we receive at level 30
  • 12% armor reduction (debuff on target) through the redesigned Expose Armor spell, which does no damage, awards 1 combo point and applies a 3%, 30-second armor debuff that stacks 3 times
  • 8% increased spell damage (debuff on target) through... well, I don't see it in the talent calculator, but presumably through a baseline passive ability similar to the Master Poisoner talent Assassination rogues currently spec into
  • 30% casting speed reduction (debuff on target) through Mind-Numbing Poison
  • 25% healing reduction (debuff on target) through Wound Poison


Master Poisoner is being removed as an Assassination talent, yes, but either it's becoming a baseline passive buff associated with all specs, or it'll be associated with some other sort of classwide ability/spell/what have you.

Honor Among Thieves is indeed losing its 5% raidwide crit buff. But Sub effectively gains the 8% spell damage buff, so... not sure how the math works out for that. Actually, none of us do -- there's no math to work out yet, since we haven't yet seen how everything is being balanced out in the Mists beta.

Safe Fall and Detect/Disarm Traps are Rogue classics that need to remain, but I'm much more worried about Ruthlessness. I had hoped it'd show up after the NDA release, but it hasn't.

Ruthlessness can seem overly random on the surface, but it has a much bigger impact on rotations than other overly random abilities (see Venomous Wounds), and not just for PvE!

HaT doesn't just allow Sub Rogues to generate combo points from other players (a huge advantage), but it also allows Sub to generate cp's from non cp builders. Seal Fate does not do the same thing for Mut. At issue? Where do you get combo points for ranged finishers?

Typically, you build 3-5cp and use a finisher. If you want to have a cp or three available later, when you're not on your target (for say, Recuperate, or Deadly Throw), you need an after-finisher combo point. That's been either another cp builder, HaT, or Ruthlessness.

With Ruthlessness gone, Mut's only option is an after-finisher cp builder ... at a cost of 55 energy. I know it doesn't sound like much, but it has a tremendous impact on PvP. That last cp is extremely important.

I certainly hope that Blizzard sees the issue and either re-implements Ruthlessness or allows Seal Fate to trigger off of non-cp-builder crits.
