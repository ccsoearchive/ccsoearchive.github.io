This is a quote from Mezasu's sticky on this subject

A. Stampede
Courtesy of Adarant

When I use Stampede, what abilities can I expect my pet to use?

During Stampede, you control your active pet as you normally would. The 4 summoned pets, however, are restricted in the abilities that they can use. Any abilities that are set to auto-cast when you last dismissed your pet will be used according to their normal usage rules. Abilities that are not set to auto-cast will not be used by your summoned pets. Abilities that cannot be set to auto-cast, Ancient Hysteria and Eternal Guardian for example, cannot be used by the pets summoned by Stampede. You cannot use macros to bypass the auto-cast restrictions.

Here are some examples of what you can expect during Stampede:
1. If you leave Growl on auto-cast for a summoned pet, that pet will use Growl on cooldown if they are not at the top of the threat table. If Growl is not set to auto-cast, your summoned pet will never Growl. If you have a macro that contains the line "/cast Growl", then your active pet will Growl, but none of your summoned pets will Growl. This goes for all pet abilities that can be set to auto-cast, including but not limited to Growl, Rabid, Web, Demoralizing Roar, and Furious Howl.

2. Assume your active pet is a Corehound and you have "/cast Ancient Hysteria" in a macro. If you use that macro during Stampede, then your active Corehound will use Ancient Hysteria as expected. Assume your active pet is not a Corehound, but one of your summoned pets is a Corehound. If you use your Ancient Hysteria macro during Stampede, your Corehound will not cast Ancient Hysteria. This goes for all abilities that cannot be set to auto-cast, including but not limited to Ancient Hysteria, Eternal Guardian, Froststorm Breath, and Burrow Attack.

If you are solo a good tip is to macro misdirection to all of your shots.

/cast [@pet] misdirection
/cast arcane shot

etc.

and make separate macros replacing arcane shot with each of your abilities.

so you will always automatically misdirection everything to your pet without having to constantly press it.

I also use this in pug dungeons since if I burn MD on the tank I will get aggro whenever it is on cooldown whereas my pet can tank for a while in a dungeon.

I didn't make this macro but I find that as many times as tank whine about hunters and growl that this needs to be listed on this page.

For anyone who does have this problem (at any level), here's a macro that you can whack on to your most often used ability (e.g. Hunter's mark):
#showtooltip
/stopmacro [noexists]
/cast Hunter's Mark
/petautocaston [group] Cower; [nogroup] Growl
/petautocastoff [nogroup] Cower; [group] Growl
(If you're in a group, pet taunt is turned off; if you're solo, it's turned back on.)

/sidenote

Should you often party with someone for questing outside LFD and need your pet to tank, this will specify a group as 3 or more and leave growl on if you only have 2 people:
/petautocaston growl
/petautocastoff [@party3,exists,group:party] growl

-Credit for this goes to Tentigers of Dath'Remar
Source: us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/7592399700?page=2#39

For anyone who hates having to switch buttons around whenever they swap between silencing shot, wyvern sting and binding shot, I have this macro (based on an example I saw on shadowpriest.com) which makes one button which will use whichever you currently have:

#showtooltip
/stopcasting
/cast Silencing Shot
/cast Wyvern Sting
/cast Binding Shot
/script c=GetSpellInfo(34490); d=GetSpellInfo(19386); e=GetSpellInfo(109248)
/run SetMacroSpell("30 Talents", GetSpellInfo(c) or GetSpellInfo(d) or GetSpellInfo(e))

One macro I find most useful for solo playing is for Misdirect if you have the glyph that resets when you cast it on your pet.

#showtooltip
/cast [target=pet,exists,nodead] Misdirection
/petattack
/cast Serpent Sting

One click misdirects to your pet, Get's him to attack the mob and fires off serpent sting then it resets misdirect because you used it on your pet with the glyph so you can use it constantly to keep attacking while keeping the mob on your pet the entire time.

You can put in trinkets or engineer abilities in there too if you wanted

Oh here is the same but for multi shot for pulling groups all onto your pet, Same deal as above one click attack.

#showtooltip Multi-Shot
/cast [target=pet,exists,nodead] Misdirection
/petattack
/cast Multi-Shot

My guess is that because Hunter's Mark is on the GCD, it casts that and the pet attack command, but stops because it must wait 1.5s before executing the next GCD command (your Mend Pet).

You'd need a castsequence command. See if this works for you. You'll need to hit the button twice:

#showtooltip Hunter's Mark
/petattack
/castseqence reset=14/target/combat Hunter's Mark, Mend Pet


It'll set the pet on the target and mark it with the first use, then cast mend pet on the second. The reset conditional should reset it after 14 seconds of no use (the duration of mend pet, minus 1, assuming you'll be rolling the HoT), or you switch target, or you leave combat. Disclaimer: I didn't test it though =)
