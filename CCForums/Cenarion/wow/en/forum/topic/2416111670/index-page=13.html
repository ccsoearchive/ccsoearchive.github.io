@Anainiel:

/startattack [@focustarget,harm,nodead] [harm,nodead]

The first set of brackets checks to see if the target of your focus (a tank in this case) is both an enemy and not dead (for efficiency's sake, since they may or may not have switched off a dead target when you set this again) - if this is the case, you'll switch target to them and begin using your autoshots; if not, you'll just start autoshotting your current target (or continue attacking, as is more likely the case).You can throw in something like /cast Arcane Shot instead of /startattack if you want to open up with something else, it's a very slight dps increase if it's an instant cast.

As survival I would cast black arrow first (before i had the T17 set bonus) and since I would open with it I would bind it with the rest of my opener (beating heart and berserking). I don't use this macro to reapply black arrow since I might want to save my trinket/racial for a certain situation.

To be honest in raiding you won't need too many macros for DPS, I hardly use any macros on damaging abilities other than combining CDs and trinkets. You should get a good misdirect macro to save time and not have to click on any of the tanks. A macro to help tranq shot other targets might also help.

But for the most part the important ones for damaging abilities are those that you add /stopcasting, /cancelaura or /petattack to. For example adding a line to stampede to be sure that the pets attack your target. Or so that you can cast a spell without having to remove deterrence or BoP manually.

Now that I switched to MM for this patch, /stopcasting allows me to interrupt my aimed shot, focusing shot, for kill shot or tranq shot without having to waste time interrupting my cast. The same goes for attacking out of deterrence before the buff wears off and without having to look at your buffs and right-click it.

I didn't read all 13 pages, so I don't know if these are already here, but I'll put them in anyway. Here are some macros I use for PvP that I find to be useful:

#showtooltip Master's Call
/stopcasting
/stopcasting
/target Harryhart
/cast Master's Call
/targetlasttarget

If your pet is cunning or adaptation:

#showtooltip Roar of Sacrifice(Cunning Ability)
/target Harryhart
/cast Roar of Sacrifice(Cunning Ability)
/targetlasttarget

If you're using a spirit beast:

#showtooltip Spirit Mend(Exotic Ability)
/target Harryhart
/cast Spirit Mend(Exotic Ability)
/targetlasttarget

Simply replace 'Harryhart' with whatever your name is.

Does anyone have a good Spirit Mend target macro?


Spirit mend macro:

#showtooltip spirit mend
/cast [@player, nomod] Spirit Mend
/cast [@focus, mod:shift][@pet,pet, mod:shift] Spirit Mend
/stopmacro [@focus,noexists][nomod]
/e 's pet casts Spirit Mend on %f
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

again focus the tank. The tell lets them know they're getting a little extra boost. Lets me alternate between healing myself or healing my pet.

I put some of the other macros I use here: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/16283528447#6
