heres some macros i use... some are spec particular.. i keep it simple and useful

kill command and bestial wrath macro.. with bw on alt aswell

/castsequence [nomod] reset=3 Kill Command, Bestial Wrath
/cast [modifier: alt] Bestial Wrath

mouse button dependent for tranq or distrac.. alt mod for scare beast.. helps condense rarely used abilities into a single multipurpose button.

/cast [nomod,button:1] Tranquilizing Shot
/cast [nomod,button:2] Distracting Shot
/cast [modifier: alt] Scare Beast

your simple MD macro.. md focus.. if no focus.. md on pet

/cast [target=focus,help] [target=pet,help] Misdirection

simple macro that puts marking and flare on one button

/cast [nomod] Hunter's Mark;[modifier:alt] Flare

my trap macro i created and tested. its a simple cast sequece to set the traps down.. however if you want to shoot one.. right click the macro first to activate trap launcher.. you can shoot them in sequence or just set them at your feet in sequence. i found a little problem with this macro.. sometimes it bugs and stops the sequence.. doesnt progress. another small inconvenience i found is sometimes the macro just wont work.. to alleviate that i found that putting all the traps that are included in the macro on your hotbars somewhere.. even a bar you have disabled and dont use.. helps fix that issue.

/castsequence [button:1,nomod] reset=12 Ice Trap, Explosive Trap, Snake Trap
/cast [button:1,modifier:alt] Freezing Trap
/cast [button:2] Trap Launcher

Figured I'd share some macro lovin'.

Complete pet summon/dismiss control through one button:

#showtooltip
/cast [pet,mod:ctrl,mod:shift]Dismiss Pet;[@pet,dead]Revive Pet;[nopet,mod:ctrl,mod:shift]Revive Pet;[mod:alt,mod:ctrl,nopet]Call Pet 5;[mod:shift,nopet]Call Pet 2;[mod:ctrl,nopet]Call Pet 3;[mod,nopet]Call Pet 4;[nopet]Call Pet 1;Mend Pet

The functionality:
With no pet out:
Summons pet
Shift: Summons pet 2
Ctrl: Summons pet 3
Alt: Summons pet 4
Ctrl+Alt: Summons pet 5
Shift + Ctrl: Revive pet (in case of dead-pet bug. Yay for bugs persisting through 3 expansions so that you have to CODE for them in macros!)

With pet out:
Mend Pet
Shift + Ctrl: Dismiss Pet

With pet dead: (in the rare case where there ISN'T a dead pet bug.)
Resurrect Pet

Here's the button I press at the start of every fight in an instance (tank is focus always):
/stopmacro [nofocus]
/target focustarget
/petattack
/cast Bestial Wrath
/petassist
/petautocastoff Growl
/petautocaston Cower
/Aspect of the Hawk
/cast [@focus] Misdirection

First line is VERY important to prevent the game from sending your pet after random targets and accidentally pulling something you don't want to. Don't be a Huntard.

Sets all the necessary settings, sends your pet after the tank's target, and misdirects to the tank. Here's my PvE version, misdirecting to the pet instead, and turning growl on:

/target [target,help] targettarget
/cast [@pet] Misdirection
/petautocaston Growl
/petattack


My trap macro: requires two presses to launch a trap
#showtooltip
/castsequence [nomod] reset=15 Trap Launcher, !Explosive Trap; [mod:shift,mod:ctrl]!Explosive Trap;[mod:ctrl,mod:alt]!Immolation Trap;[mod:alt]!Ice Trap;[mod:shift]!Freezing Trap;[mod:ctrl]!Snake Trap;


NoMod = Trap launcher, then Explosive trap
Shift+Crtl = Explosive
Shift = Freezing
Ctrl = Snake
Alt = Ice
Alt+Ctrl = Immolation
Using any mod keys overrides the Trap Launcher in case you want to drop a trap the traditional way, or if the reset has expired. There's a bit of a delay after arming the launcher before you can select your trap, so either be patient or spam the key until you see a targetting disk.

And finally, dealing with speed:
#showtooltip
/click [nomod:ctrl/shift][outdoors]BT4Button61
/cast [mod:ctrl]!Aspect of the Pack;[indoors][mod:shift]!Aspect of the Cheetah;[swimming,mod]Abyssal Seahorse;[swimming]Sea Turtle;[mod:alt][noflyable]Swift Gray Wolf;Red Drake

Intuitave mounting macro that uses Aspect of the Cheetah if I'm indoors. I can also select Cheetah, Pack, or my ground mount through Shift, Ctrl, and Alt respectively.

"BT4Button61" clicks the first button of Bar 6 (in Bartender). This button has another macro on it (Who says a macro can't call a macro?) that looks like this:
#showtooltip
/cast !Aspect of the Hawk

Why didn't I just put that in the original macro? Because /click doesn't affect #showtooltip. I could have used /click BT4StanceButton1 (which is Aspect of the Hawk directly), but you can't turn off toggle with click so every other time I mounted it turned my aspect OFF. This is the only way to be able to turn off toggling AND not have Aspect of the Hawk show up as the tooltip.

Btw, the bar this is on is hidden with Bartender, but the call still works. In theory, I could have dozens of hidden macros that other macros call when needed.

To find out the name of a button, type /fstack in chat and mouseover the button.
