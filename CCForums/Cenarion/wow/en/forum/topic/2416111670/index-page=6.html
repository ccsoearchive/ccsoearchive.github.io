Not to be persnickety, but on a side note /use 10 and /use 15 are not "engineer only" although the engineer tinkers is the only reasonable time I can think of that you would want them hotkeyed...

They both will use any item with a on-use ability on that slot (gloves and cloak respectively).

The difference may not be important now, but for accuracy you should amend that. In the future they may add some sort of wacky "use" ability to other slots that is not engineer only. To clarify, each of our equipment slots has its own /use # associated with it.

Was checking the trap macro out for my hunter and found it would not show tool tip. There was an extra space in there somewhere. I prefer Explosive Trap increases dps against multiple mobs so changed it to following and added the option to turn sound off and back on to dump any annoying feedback.

#showtooltip Explosive Trap
/console Sound_EnableSFX 0
/script UIErrorsFrame:Clear()
/console Sound_EnableSFX 1
/castsequence reset=9 Trap Launcher, !Explosive Trap(Fire - Trap Launcher), nil

You still must double click, once to select launcher and one to select trap then position as normal. If this is done for all traps will get rid of one item on tabs, the launcher.

Misdirect
#showtooltip Misdirection
/cast [target=focus,exists,nodead] Misdirection; [target=pet,exists,nodead] Misdirection

Casts misdirect on your focus (which should be your tank). If you have no tank, it casts it on your pet.


Trap launcher + traps
I have two different trap launcher macro's (just makes my life more simple than trying to remember which mod key is what for 1 macro):

#showtooltip
/castsequence [nomod] reset=3 Trap Launcher,!Freezing Trap
/castsequence [mod:shift] reset=3 Trap Launcher,!Ice Trap
END

#showtooltip
/castsequence [nomod] reset=3 Trap Launcher,!Explosive Trap
/castsequence [mod:shift] reset=3 Trap Launcher,!Snake Trap
END

(You can always make this into 1 macro, just make sure to use [mod:alt] or [mod:ctrl] for the next lines).

Multi-purpose macro:

#showtooltip
/cast [nomod] Feign Death
/cast [mod:shift] Wyvern Sting
/cast [mod:ctrl] Scatter Shot
/cast [mod:alt] Camouflage

This I mainly use to clear up a bunch of clutter on my interface. I think there might be a way to do a mouseover on this, but I haven't figured out how.


Start attack/send pet in macro:

#showtooltip Explosive Shot
/targetenemy [noexists, noharm,dead]
/stopmacro [noexists, noharm, dead]
/petattack
/startattack
/cast Explosive Shot
END

In the last line, you can change Explosive to whatever shot you start out with. I made this macro when Blizzard "fixed" the auto targetting bug.




Anyways, just some of the macro's I have for my hunter. Figured they'd be helpful.

I took a quick glance and was surprised to not see this in here. Normally, when you're in camo, if you use your pet's special ability it will take you out of camo by default because it puts your pet in combat. However, if your pet's special ability is a ranged ability that doesn't do damage, you can have your pet return to you after using it and not take you out of stealth. So, make a macro like this:

/cast [pet:spider] Web; [pet:monkey] Bad Manner; [pet:wind serpent] Lightning Breath
/petpassive

Using this, means that you can use your spider web from stealth, the target will be rooted, and you're still in stealth! Your spider will pop out of stealth for <2 sec and be right back in camo. You can position your pet away from you and they will not be able to tell where you're standing!

After they are rooted, you are free to cast Aimed Shot while still stealthed and they stand there completely helpless!
