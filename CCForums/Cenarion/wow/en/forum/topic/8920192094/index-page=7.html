* Oh God folks, it's 5.4. We've got a LOT to talk about. I'm going to break down things by category. Some stuff will stay the same, some won't. Let's begin with my introduction...

Ladies and gentleman, I'm going to be brief. Patch 5.4 is here, and that means I need to get back to work. Whoever's pulling strings backstage has been rather kind to us this time around.

I'll attempt to denote changes as I go along, but not being able to use colored text might make that kinda difficult.

I'd like to start from scratch, so here we go:

------------------------------------------------
5.4 CLASS CHANGES IN THEIR ENTIRETY
------------------------------------------------

General
• Ambush damage has been increased by 12%.
• Evasion now increases dodge chance by 100% (was 50%), cooldown has been reduced to 2 minutes (down from 3 minutes), and duration has been reduced to 10 seconds (down from 15 seconds).
• Eviscerate now deals 10% less damage.
• Fan of Knives damage has been increased by 25%.
• Recuperate now restores 4% of maximum health every 3 seconds, up from 3%.
• Sinister Strike deals an increased 240% of weapon damage (up from 145%), but now costs 50 Energy (up from 40 Energy).

Assassination
• Assassination Rogues should no longer be eligible for any other weapon types except daggers in situations where loot is automatically awarded(Raid Finder, Bonus Roll, Heroic/Normal Scenarios, and the upcoming Flexible Raid difficulty.)
• Dispatch now deals 645% weapon damage (up from 460%).
• Mutilate now deals 280% weapon damage (up from 200%).

Combat
• New passive ability, Ruthlessness: This passive ability is learned by Combat Rogues at Level 32. The Rogue gains a 20% chance per combo point (100% chance at 5 combo points) to immediately regain 1 combo point while performing a finishing move.
• Killing Spree targeting has been changed. If Blade Flurry is not active, Killing Spree will now hit the Rogue's target 7 times or pick the closest eligible target if none had been selected. Killing Spree will continue to work as it has while Blade Flurry is active.
• Revealing Strike now deals 160% weapons damage (up from 125%).
• Vitality now increases Attack Power by 40% (up from 30%).
• Fixed an issue where certain conditions can cause Blade Flurry to not deal damage to additional nearby opponents.

Subtlety
• Backstab now deals 380% weapon damage (up from 275%).
• Hemorrhage now deals 160% weapons damage (up from 140%); 232% weapons damage if a dagger is equipped (up from 203% for daggers).
• Sanguinary Vein now increases damage against targets with bleed effects by 25% (up from 20%).

Talents
• Burst of Speed cost has been reduced to 15 Energy (down from 30 Energy).
• Cheat Death now reduces damage taken by 85% after activating (up from 80%).
• Cloak and Dagger now allows the Rogue to use Ambush, Garrote, and Cheap Shot from 40 yards away (up from 30 yards).
• Nightstalker now increases damage dealt by abilities while stealthed by 50%, up from 25%.
• Paralytic Poison now stuns the target at 4 applications of the poison (down from 5 applications).
• Shadowstep now has a reduced cooldown of 20 seconds (down from 24 seconds).

Glyphs
Major Glyphs
• Glyph of Adrenaline Rush's effect is now baseline and has been replaced with Glyph of Redirect. Glyph of Redirect now reduces the cooldown of Redirect by 50 seconds (down from Redirect no longer has a cooldown).
• Glyph of Blind now only removes damage-over-time effects that would cause Blind to break. For Rogues that also have the Dirty Tricks talent, this means Blind will no longer remove the Rogue’s poison or bleed effects.
• Glyph of Crippling Poison has been replaced with Glyph of Sharpened Knives. Glyph of Sharpened Knives now causes Fan of Knives to also damage the armor of its victims, applying 1 application of the Weakened Armor effect to each target.
• Glyph of Debilitation has been replaced with Glyph of Recovery. Glyph of Recovery increases the healing received by the Rogue while Recuperate is active.
• Glyph of Recuperate now increases the healing of Recuperate by an additional 1% (up from 0.5%).
• Glyph of Sap has been replaced with Glyph of Hemorrhaging Veins.
• Glyph of Hemorrhaging Veins causes Sanguinary Veins ability to also increase damage done to targets affected by Hemorrhage. Glyph of Shiv now reduces the cooldown of Shiv by 3 seconds (was 2 seconds).

New Minor Glyphs
• Glyph of Improved Distraction: Distract now summons a decoy at the target location.
• Glyph of the Headhunter: Throw and Deadly Throw abilities will now throw axes regardless of the Rogue's currently equipped weapon.

Class Armor
• Rogue Tier-15 4-piece set bonus now causes Shadow Blades to reduce the cost of all abilities by 15% (down from 40%).

------------------------------------------------
ASSASSINATION SPEC-SPECIFIC ABILITIES/PASSIVES
------------------------------------------------

-- 5.4 -- Mutilate -- Now deals 280% weapon damage (up fro 200%). Requires daggers in both hands. Attacks with both weapons. Generates 2 combo points. Generates 3 combo points if either weapon strike crits. Each mutilate has a 30% chance of proccing "Blindside". Main CP-builder when the target is above 35% health.

-- 5.4 -- Dispatch -- Now deals 645% weapon damage (up from 460%). Replaces Sinister Strike. Essentially our execute ability. A stronger version of Backstab with no positional requirement. Does have a health requirement though. Can only be used during Blindside procs, or when the target is below 35%. (That means 34.99% or lower, cannot be used right at 35%.) It can be to your benefit to pool some energy if the target is near 35% health, so you can spam Dispatch when they fall below.

Envenom -- Replaces Eviscerate. Finishing move that deals nature damage based on CPs. Also applies a 2-6 second buff based on CPs that increases poison application chance by 15%.

Assassin's Resolve (Passive) -- Increases your energy by 20 and all damage done by 25% while wielding two daggers. Necessary. Using anything other than daggers as a Mutilate rogue is simply not an option.

Improved Poisons (Passive) -- Chance to apply Deadly/Wound poison increased by 20%.

Seal Fate (Passive) -- Gain an extra CP when you critically strike with any ability that builds CPs.

Venemous Wounds (Passive) -- Your Garrote and Rupture have a 75% chance when dealing damage to do additional nature damage and supply you with 10 energy. Explained in better detail further down.

Cut to the Chase (Passive) -- Your Envenom will refresh your Slice and Dice to its full duration. Works with any strength Envenom (ie. A 1-point Envenom will fully refresh SnD.)

Blindside (Passive) -- Generated by Mutilate. Will pop up two orange slashes on either side of the screen. Your next Dispatch is energy-free and can be used even if the target is at or above 35% health. Does not stack, so use it before your next Mutilate. Triggers the global cooldown.

Mastery: Potent Poisons -- Increases the damage done by your poisons by a %. Affects Envenom as well.

TALENTS
------------------------------------------------

Nightstalker....|....SUBTERFUGE....|....SHADOW FOCUS

Deadly Throw....|....NERVE STRIKE....|....COMBAT READINESS

Cheat Death....|....Leeching Poison....|....ELUSIVENESS

Cloak and Dagger....|....Shadowstep....|....BURST OF SPEED

Prey on the Weak....|....Paralytic Poison....|....DIRTY TRICKS

SHURIKEN TOSS....|....Marked For Death....|....ANTICIPATION

Tier 1

-- 5.4 -- Nightstalker -- Increases your movement speed while stealthed by 20%, and the damage your abilities deal while stealthed by 50% (up from 25%). This ability's 25% damage buff still doesn't make it worthwhile, even considering Ambush's 12% and Mutilate's 40% buffs. PvP openers are concerned more about utility or control as opposed to damage. The only time I would consider this talent is MAYBE for carrier-killing in Kotmogu, and that's still a stretch.

Subterfuge -- Your stealth breaks 3 seconds after dealing or receiving hostile actions, rather than doing so immediately. A solid talent. Allows for openers that other players will have a hard time interrupting. Also makes for more effective getaways if you've got DoTs/bleeds on you.

Shadow Focus -- Abilities cost 75% less Energy while you are stealthed. Another good choice, and my personal recommendation. Increased burst potential, and it melds very well with talent choices in other tiers.

Tier 2

Deadly Throw -- Finishing move that reduces the movement speed of the target by 50% for 6 sec. If performed with 3 combo points, also interrupts spellcasting and prevents any spell in that school from being cast for a period of time. Good when you're up against ranged opponents. Essentially lack-luster in any other situation. You will almost never find yourself timing the throw correctly to interrupt/silence anyone. Main use is a ranged snare.

Nerve Strike -- A successful Kidney Shot or Cheap Shot also reduces the damage dealt by the target by 50% and healing done by the target by 10% for 6 sec after the effect fades. Sounds good on paper, performs well in practice. The choice between this and the next talent really comes down to whether or not you're selfish. Would you rather peel for your healer, or save yourself? Hrm...

Combat Readiness -- Enter into a state of heightened awareness, deflecting enemy weapon strikes with increasing effectiveness. Successive attacks will deal 10% less damage per application, stacking 5 times. Lasts for 20 sec, but if 10 sec elapse without any incoming weapon strikes, this state will end. One of your "Oh !@#$" buttons. Only effective vs. melee, and it sure ain't gonna help your team much directly, so it has its down sides.

Tier 3

-- 5.4 -- Cheat Death -- An attack that would otherwise be fatal will instead reduce you to no less than 10% of your maximum health, and damage taken will be reduced by 85% (up from 80%) for 3 sec. This effect cannot occur more than once per 90 seconds. Sounds cool, but it simply under-performs. It can save you, sure, but it will not be often enough to warrant its use. It is easily overpowered and you will likely die in the next GCD. Works great on Hexos though in the Brawler's Guild. Gives you an extra shot at killing him if you screw up, so hey...knock yourself out.

Leeching Poison -- Coats your weapons with a Non-Lethal Poison that lasts for 1 hour. Your melee attacks have a 50% chance to poison the target for 10 sec. Your subsequent weapon strikes against the poisoned target will heal you for 10% of damage dealt. Hilariously terrible. All your damage is from poisons, not weapon strikes. Of all 3 specs, Assassination makes the least weapon strikes against a target. You are hurting yourself with this talent. Move along.

Elusiveness -- Your Feint ability also reduces all damage taken by 30% for 5 sec. Hands down. Elusiveness' 30% mitigation, and 100% up time when you need it just can't be beat. You can stack it with other damage reduction abilities (smoke bomb and/or glyphed cloak) to buy yourself time for a heal if situations are dire.

Tier 4

-- 5.4 -- Cloak and Dagger --Your Ambush, Garrote, and Cheap Shot abilities can now be used from 40 yards away (up from 30 yards), and will cause you to appear behind your target. I still can hardly fathom why someone would want this talent. It serves some purpose when paired with Subterfuge for a multi-target Cheap Shot, but that's about it. I'm not using Vanish just so I can port 40 yards. No thanks.

-- 5.4 -- Shadowstep -- Step through the shadows and appear behind a target. Movement speed is increased by 70% for 2 sec afterwards. 20-second cooldown (down from 24). Such a wonderful ride it's been with Shadowstep. Truthfully it's still a very strong talent considering the present choices. The cooldown reduction isn't exactly stellar, but it's something. Great for reaching players on ledges above you, and saving your skin in BGs like Eye of the Storm (I'm looking at you, Shaman).

-- 5.4 -- Burst of Speed -- Increases movement speed by 70% for 4 sec and removes any movement-slowing effects. Does not break stealth. Costs 15 energy (down from 30). Great choice here. Doesn't exactly carry Shadowstep's surprise factor, but you're going to be hell to kite or catch. Works amazingly well with Shadow Focus (costs 3 energy in stealth; spam it), and it stacks with Nitro Boosts from Engineering. ZOOM ZOOM.

Tier 5

Prey On The Weak -- When you disable an enemy with Kidney Shot, Cheap Shot, Gouge, Sap, or Blind, they suffer 10% increased damage from all sources for the duration of the effect. Boring talent is boring. I am not a personal fan. You lose the control of the other 2 options for a straight 10% damage boost from all sources. Serves its purpose in 3v3 or 5v5 I suppose.

-- 5.4 -- Paralytic Poison -- Coats your weapons with a Non-Lethal Poison that lasts for 1 hour. Each strike has a 20% chance of poisoning the enemy for 15 sec. Stacks up to 4 times on a single target (down from 5), and upon a fourth application, the enemy will be stunned for 4 seconds. As an Assassination rogue this talent is the prettiest it'll ever be. We have so many modifiers that increase our poison application chances; this talent was essentially made for us. And now it procs faster. Hilarious. And my oh my is that shiv bonus sexy. A solid choice.

Dirty Tricks -- Your Gouge and Blind no longer have an Energy cost, and no longer break from damage dealt by your Poison and Bleed effects. Yes, things have changed a bit with this talent due to glyphs. We'll get to that later. For now we'll just talk about how cool it is alone. Boy is it ever. It has the potential for so much control (on demand, unlike Paralytic Poison). Some pufu this talent because your teammates can screw it up so often (eg. removing your Gouge or Blind with damage). But I say get smarter teammates, because the free Gouge is wonderful as an extra interrupt alone.

Tier 6

Shuriken Toss -- A ranged attack that deals 2493 (+ 60% of Attack power) Physical damage to an enemy target. If the enemy is farther than 10 yards away, your autoattacks will be replaced with ranged shuriken for up to 10 sec, granting them a 30 yd range. Shurikens are coated in your Lethal Poison. Awards 1 combo point. Replaces Throw. Everyone seems to ignore this talent. I have no idea why. Quit spamming it and starving yourselves of energy and you'll be just fine. Needs a disclaimer: to be used in moderation. Great for ranged opponents.

Marked for Death -- Mark the target, instantly generating 5 combo points on them. If the target dies within 1 min Marked for Death's cooldown is reset. Works pretty well with Combat and Subtlety because they can use it for back-to-back Eviscerates among other things. Back-to-back Envenoms don't translate quite as well for Assassination rogues. You'll end up clipping the initial Envenom's 15% poison application buff. We don't like that. It's good for 5 free combo points if your target is about to die. Can also be used for instant 5-point Kidney Shots or Ruptures, but you're better off finding that utility elsewhere.

Anticipation -- When one of your attacks generates a combo point on a target that already has 5 combo points, you gain an Anticipation charge, up to a maximum of 5. When you perform an offensive finishing move on an enemy, any Anticipation charges are consumed to grant you an equal number of combo points on that target. Solves a pretty major quality of life issue with the Assassination spec: 4-point finishers. Without this talent you will CONSTANTLY find yourself debating whether to use a finisher at 4 combo points, or risk wasting combo point procs in order to get 5. Problem solved with Anticipation, and the unspent CPs above 5 will automatically transfer to your next target. Great burst, happy rogue. Win-win.

------------------------------------------------
GLYPHS
------------------------------------------------

Okay, so 5.4 brought a lot of changes to the table in this regard. Let's knock 'em out one by one.

Glyph of Adrenaline Rush's effect is now baseline and has been replaced with Glyph of Redirect. Glyph of Redirect now reduces the cooldown of Redirect by 50 seconds (down from Redirect no longer has a cooldown).

Okay, cool. Good for Combat rogues. That needed to happen sooner or later. Not much of a concern for us though. Let's be honest here. No one chose the redirect talent when it had no cooldown. No one's taking this glyph when it has a 10-second cooldown. Moving on.

Glyph of Blind now only removes damage-over-time effects that would cause Blind to break. For Rogues that also have the Dirty Tricks talent, this means Blind will no longer remove the Rogue’s poison or bleed effects.

Sounds good, but that isn't necessarily true. Only really works with Dirty Tricks, so it limits your talent choices. And when it does work, it prevents you from using Blind, stealthing, and then sapping the opponent. Your DoTs will break the sap. That ain't cool. We don't like this glyph change. RABBLE RABBLE RABBLE.

Glyph of Crippling Poison has been replaced with Glyph of Sharpened Knives. Glyph of Sharpened Knives now causes Fan of Knives to also damage the armor of its victims, applying 1 application of the Weakened Armor effect to each target.

Well that's cool and all, but it serves very little purpose in PvP unless you wanna waste a major slot so you can Leroy Jenkins a crowd and maybe weaken their armor by 3 stacks before you die.

Glyph of Debilitation has been replaced with Glyph of Recovery. Glyph of Recovery increases the healing received by the Rogue while Recuperate is active.

Sounds very solid. Everyone loves 20% more heals, but is it worth a major slot? My playstyle has me using Recuperate in short spurts though, so I personally haven't justified it yet. If you love using Recup, you love this glyph. That's the short of it. Will definitely see a lot more action in arenas where you can be the center of attention. Worth a recommendation for sure.

Glyph of Recuperate now increases the healing of Recuperate by an additional 1% (up from 0.5%).

Now comes the debate over how significant 0.5% really is. We already got a 1% boost to the ability itself. Is a major slot worth 0.5%? I can't bring myself to think so. I'm not recommending for the time being. Convince me otherwise.

Glyph of Sap has been replaced with Glyph of Hemorrhaging Veins. Glyph of Hemorrhaging Veins causes Sanguinary Veins ability to also increase damage done to targets affected by Hemorrhage.

Significant quality of life improvement for Subtlety rogues. This is sausage to us Assassination rogues. Moving on.

Glyph of Shiv now reduces the cooldown of Shiv by 3 seconds (was 2 seconds).

How often did you find yourself wishing you used Glyph of Shiv? Never? Imagine that.

Okay now that that's outta the way, let's talk about some important major glyphs that haven't changed.

Glyph of Gouge -- Amazing with Dirty Tricks. Enjoy your free gouge without positional requirements. Casters will loathe you. High-five, bro!

Glyph of Feint -- Pretty straight-forward. Maximize your uptime with Feint for that sexy 30% damage reduction through Elusiveness. If you're using glyph for any other purpose you're just silly.

Glyph of Cloak of Shadows -- I'm not much a fan of dying, so I like this glyph. Tell both casters and melee to shove off. All with one button!

Glyph of Garrote -- I don't personally use it. If I take the Subterfuge talent, Garrote ends at just the right time for a Cheap Shot before stealth breaks so I find little use for it. Others are absolute diehards over this glyph though, and I can't say they're wrong so it still gets a recommendation.

------------------------------------------------
REFORGE STAT PRIORITY
------------------------------------------------

3% Hit > ~3% Expertise > Mastery > Crit > Haste

Things haven't changed to my knowledge in 5.4 Hit should be as close to 3.00% as possible. Expertise beyond 3.00% is still considered beneficial so don't sweat it if you pass 3.00% by a large margin; just consider focusing on your other stats from that point on.

------------------------------------------------
GEMS
------------------------------------------------

Everyone gets their knickers in a twist over this, so take what I have to say with a grain of salt. It is my PERSONAL OPINION that ignoring resilience is no longer detrimental enough to cause any major grief.

There has been a blue post about PvP Power/Resilience gems that follows the same mantra.
http://eu.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/7527742704#17

Okay, so now Grievous gear is the new hotness. Tyrannical gear stands to be replaced in a relatively short amount of time. There is no longer a need to min/max all your gem slots in Tyrannical gear unless you have already during the previous season. Having said that, the following setup originally meant for Malevolent gear can now be followed for people just starting to earn Tyrannical.

Meta -- Agile Primal Diamond
Red -- Delicate Primordial Ruby or Perfect Delicate Pandarian Garnet
Blue -- Assassin's Imperial Amethyst or Perfect Assassin's Roguestone
Yellow -- Adept Vermilion Onyx or Perfect Adept Tiger Opal
Prismatic -- Delicate Primordial Ruby or Perfect Delicate Pandarian Garnet

Also, I highly recommend filling your socket bonuses.

The ideal mastery to hit in Tyrannical gear is 79.5%. We know this thanks to the fine folks over at ArenaJunkies.

http://www.arenajunkies.com/topic/238477-how-to-play-mutilate/page__st__40#entry3895637

It's dated news though a bit of a moot point as you'll start earning Greivous gear as you go along. I'm sure the appropriate amount of mastery investment will change, but for the time being here is the most effective gem layout for a full-Tyrannical rogue working on their Greivous gear.

Meta -- Agile Primal Diamond or Tyrannical Primal Diamond
Red -- Delicate Primordial Ruby or Perfect Delicate Pandarian Garnet
Blue -- Effulgent Wild Jade or Perfect Effulgent Alexandrite
Yellow -- Fractured Sun's Radiance or Perfect Fractured Sunstone
Prismatic -- Fractured Sun's Radiance or Delicate Primordial Ruby

OPTIONAL ITEM(S)

------------------------------------------------
(DE)BUFFS TO MAINTAIN
------------------------------------------------

Slice and Dice -- Directly increases your auto-attack speed, indirectly increasing your poison application rate. Easily refresh-able to its max duration with any strength Envenom so you don't have to start it off with 5 combo points. Use it often.

Bleeds -- Garrote & Rupture both provide a buff Called Venemous Wounds while they're active. Each tick of your bleeds has a 75% chance to deal nature damage & give you 10 energy. Garrote is considered slightly inferior to Rupture as it ticks every 3 seconds while Rupture ticks every 2 seconds. Venemous Wounds does not stack if you have both bleeds on a target, but you should have at least 1 on somebody, somewhere. The more people you have bleeding, the more energy you'll get.

Elusiveness -- 30% damage reduction. 100% up time if you're carrying the flag/orb, or when you're being tunneled. All other times, you can save the energy for something else. Glyph of Feint can help with this.

-- 5.4 -- Recuperate -- Restores 4% (up from 3%) of your health every 3 seconds (4.5% if glyphed). While you can argue it's not the greatest or fastest heal in the world, you can't really turn your nose up at it either. Use it when flag carrying or being tunneled along with Elusiveness, or whenever an encounter is going sour. I use it every time my target dies and I have leftover combo points that I won't be transferring to another enemy.

------------------------------------------------
COOLDOWNS
------------------------------------------------

Vendetta -- A wonderful 30% damage boost. However, be aware this is not considered a buff for you. Rather, IT'S A DEBUFF PLACED ON THE TARGET. Use it with caution on a Rogue; both Cloak of Shadows and Vanish will remove it. (I personally believe Vendetta should be considered physical to avoid this; the way things are now is retarded.) If you absolutely must, try to hold onto it until you know the rogue is out of defensive options.

Shadow Blades -- Auto-attacks cause 100% shadow damage, and abilities that generate CPs now generate one additional CP. Nice to use when an enemy player becomes immune to physical damage. I'm looking at you, Paladins.

It is subject to debate as to whether you should macro these two abilities together into one giant "!@#$ you" button, or keep them separate so you can use them when you need them. I'll leave that up to you folks. For what it's worth, I will mention that I keep them together and I don't have any survivability issues when they're off cooldown.

------------------------------------------------
TRINKETS
------------------------------------------------

For the sake of those starting to gear, I'll be linking the tyrannical versions in my examples. Your most readily-available trinket choices are as follows:

-- 5.4 -- Tyrannical Gladiator's Medallion of Tenacity -- OH GOD NO MORE MASTERY WHYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYYY? Because we need HEALTH, AMIRITE? Sadly this is no longer a competitive option.

Tyrannical Gladiator's Medallion of Cruelty -- Critical strike "alternative". Now it's more-or-less the new default. Reforge what you can to mastery and daydream about the good ol' days because it JUST AIN'T THE SAME.

Tyrannical Gladiator's Badge of Conquest -- On-use trinket. Slightly less powerful than its insignia counterpart but grants you control over its use. Until recently this was my bread and butter, and I highly recommend it if you'd like to make a giant all-in-one DPS macro as shown in the next section. Using synapse springs at the same time as this trinket is not possible as it shares a 20-second cooldown with that tinker. Engineers be warned.

Tyrannical Gladiator's Insignia of Conquest -- Proc-based alternative. In taking this trinket you trade control over its use for a larger agility bonus than the on-use trinket. Synapse Springs from engineering can be used during this trinket's proc.

Vicious Talisman of the Shado-Pan Assault -- This trinket will (hopefully) be a non-issue as the new patch progresses and people gain grievous gear, but I'm keeping all this info here just in case. ALL RIGHT, time out; let's sit down and have a chat about this thing. We're going to iron this out once and for all. This trinket used to be amazing. Too amazing. As such it's undergone a bunch of nerfs spanning the whole of 5.2 and ending with the release of this patch, so we're gonna take this slow. We'll start from the beginning:

*** Vicious Talisman originally provided 8,800 agility for 20 seconds irrespective of instanced/world PvP. It was hilarious.

*** April 10th, patch 5.2: Talisman is nerfed down to 6,908 agility when activated in instanced PvP. Duration remains 20 seconds. Tooltips update to show this change.

*** April 18th, patch 5.2: Talisman is nerfed further down to 4,400 agility when activated in instanced PvP. Duration elongated to 40 seconds. Tooltips do not update to show this change.

*** May 21st, patch 5.3: Gear is now capped at 496 in instanced PvP. Talisman's proc reflects this change, and is nerfed further still to 3,454 agility without an announcement specific to the item(s). Tooltips do not update to show this change.

So let's soak this in. As of RIGHT NOW the Talisman provides 3,454 agility for 40 seconds in BGs and arenas. The tooltip still reads 6,908. It is incorrect, and so is anyone that tells you otherwise. The internal cooldown for this item is 105 seconds, so that's roughly 1 minute between procs in BGs/Arenas, and 80 seconds in world PvP.

Bottom line, I DO NOT RECOMMEND THIS TRINKET FOR INSTANCED PVP.
I do, however, recommend this trinket for world PvP.

Upgraded twice, this trinket will provide 9,483 agility for 20 seconds when used out in the world. It packs quite a punch, and that's something you just can't ignore.

Use it, but if I see any of you folks running around in BGs with it I'mma give you such a pinch.

------------------------------------------------
MACROS
------------------------------------------------

Now, I'm one of those super cool guys that uses a Naga mouse so I don't have many macros that aren't something simple like a rebound key: shift-1 or ctrl-5.

I will tell you however, that I use Engineering for both the Nitro Boosts and Goblin Glider. If you're seriously considering going the PvP route, I suggest you do the same.

The reason I say this in the macro section is that as of 5.3 the Goblin Glider will NOT FUNCTION WHILE THE NITRO BOOSTS ARE ACTIVE.

Because of this, you'll have to make something along the lines of the following for your Glider if you plan to use the Nitro Boosts just beforehand:

/cancelaura Nitro Boosts
/use Goblin Glider

With that out of the way, let's talk damage.

We've got your two cooldowns mentioned previously: Vendetta and Shadow Blades. We've also discussed your trinket options. We need to make them work together.

If you're the kinda player that likes to control your damage and you chose the badge trinket:

/use <badge of conquest goes here>
/cast Shadow Blades
/cast Vendetta
/use <gloves with synapse springs go here>
/use Virmen's Bite

This can be used every 60 seconds for the Badge and Virmen's Bite buffs (even if your SB and Vendetta are on cooldown). At two minutes you'll get Vendetta along with the badge and potion, and at 3 minutes SB is good to go again so everything's back up and running. You can also use it every minute, 20 seconds after the first use (while everything's recharging) in order to use your Synapse Springs. Recall that the badge and Synapse Springs share a 20-second cooldown. It's not the most fluid for Engineers, but it works all the same. Virmen's Bite stays at the bottom so the macro remains usable in arenas.

It is essentially spammable, is what I'm saying here.

If you did not take the badge trinket:

/cast Shadow Blades
/cast Vendetta
/use <gloves with synapse springs go here>
/use Virmen's Bite

This is where Engineers rejoice. Synapse Springs can be stacked with the proc-based trinkets. Wait for your insignia (or better yet your shado-pan trinket) to proc, hit this macro and go to town. People will die.

Remember, VENDETTA IS A DEBUFF ON THE TARGET. You need to be targeting something for it to be used; it will not activate with everything else otherwise.

How about a sap macro?

#showtooltip Sap
/cleartarget [nomod]
/targetenemyplayer [nomod]
/cast Sap

This will target the nearest enemy player and sap them, duh. Because of the cleartarget line, do not target a player before hitting the macro. You'll deselect them and sap the player next to them. But the cool thing is if you hold down any modifier key (shift/control/alt) it will cast sap normally so you can still use it on mobs or players you want to hit specifically when targeted.

------------------------------------------------
OPENER(S)
------------------------------------------------

Your opener largely depends on the target. However, there is a cookie-cutter option if you are spec'd into Subterfuge. It's most effective on casters/healers.

I use Mutilate instead of Ambush in stealth as it costs 5 less energy, has an additional chance to poison the target, and procs Blindside. Some consider this a gamble, and like to stay with Ambush. It comes down to personal preference. The possible benefits of Mutilate outweigh Ambush in my opinion.

For most casters/healers I open with Garrote -> Mutilate -> Cheap Shot.

Since I do not have Garrote glyphed, its silence effect only lasts 3 seconds (which is the length of subterfuge) and therefore lines up perfectly with my Cheap Shot.

If the player does not break either the silence or the Cheap Shot, this opener makes for 8 straight seconds of lockout. It's very effective, but we're not done.

At the end of this opener, you will have 5 combo points. Sit on them and do not use them right away. Just let your auto-attacks unload on the player while they're sitting in the Cheap Shot stun. If you proc'd Blindside in your opener, use it now.

If you're spec'd into Dirty Tricks, as I suggested you be, Gouge them the second Cheap Shot fades for 4 extra seconds to build energy. Your DoTs will continue to damage them while you stand there.

/emote at the player while they sit in Gouge. This step is ESSENTIAL but highly variable; which emote players use is subject to personal preference. I prefer /fart.

When Gouge fades, use your 5 combo points on Kidney Shot.

You'll have pooled a very nice amount of energy at this point. Pop your cooldowns and go to town.

That's one dead player you got there.

And if your aim isn't to kill...all said and done, this method tallies as follows:

3 seconds -- Garrote
4 seconds -- Cheap Shot (4.5 if glyphed)
4 seconds -- Gouge
3 seconds -- Kidney Shot at 50% diminishing returns

14 seconds of lock-down. 14.5 if you're using Glyph of Cheap Shot. Highly recommended against healers.

------------------------------------------------
POISONS
------------------------------------------------

Lethal

As you just begin to gear, you will make a bigger impact on an enemy's health using Wound Poison as your lethal.

As you obtain gear and stack your mastery, however, Deadly Poison is going to be your bread and butter. I use it nearly exclusively, save for a particularly pesky healer.

Non-Lethal

These tend to be highly situational. Once your get your 2-piece bonus, you'll be applying Crippling Poison every time you apply your active non-lethal poison.

We've already discussed Leeching Poison's uselessness, so I swear I better not see any of you out on the field with it active. You'll hurt my feelings. And I've given my opinion on the 5th tier talent set, which did not pick up Paralytic Poison, but let's take a moment to go over it anyway.

Paralytic Poison is a stacking stun and its shiv ability, Partial Paralysis, is a 4-second root...And it's !@#$ing awesome. HOWEVER, Paralytic Poison also relies heavily on stacking applications to do its dirty work, and it has a LOWER application chance than every other non-lethal poison (20% as opposed to 50%). That also means you'll have a lower chance of applying Crippling Poison through your 2-piece set bonus. We don't like that, and that's why Paralytic Poison ultimately loses in the end.

Crippling Poison may seem useless once you get your 2-piece, but that's where you're wrong. Shiv only activates your ACTIVE non-lethal poison. So if you have, say, Mind-Numbing and the 2-piece, you will not get a 70% slow and a 100% increased cast time. You will only get Mind-Numbing's bonus. This is why Crippling Poison is still useful. That 70% slow from Shiv can be dead sexy in the right light. I use this poison for games like Strand of the Ancients for slowing demolishers. Give it some thought.

Mind-Numbing Poison will likely be your most common non-lethal poison you use. Its passive 25% increased cast time debuff is annoying for casters, and the Shiv bonus (100%) is a nightmare. Just watch out though, an instant-cast ability will negate the Shiv bonus. You may have to try predicting what the player will be casting next when you use Shiv with this poison. Not the easiest of tasks.
