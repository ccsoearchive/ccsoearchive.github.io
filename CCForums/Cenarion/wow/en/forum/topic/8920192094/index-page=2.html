This is one of the things many forget to mention. It's not simply Shadow Dance that makes Subtlety shine in arena, but also the ability to heal in stealth using Premeditation and Recuperate.

Sadly Mutilate rogues cannot do this, and so we're left with simply using recup out of stealth. If we want to use recup again we have to go attack something.

For leveling though, encounters with mobs are so short that a simple recup with left-over points after a fight should do the trick. If it doesn't, some cheap food from any vendor will be a far faster heal than relying on Subtlety's premed/recup combo.

I highly disagree with not having Paralytic Poison even as an option.

It actually works BEST with Assassination, as Envenom will increase its proc chance to 35%. Dirty Tricks is still amazing, so I can't say to DEFINITELY take Para. But... Para is absolutely solid, especially for Assassination.

After that, I definitely say Burst of Speed. 30 energy is nothing. And, frankly, Assassination has the best energy management of all the specs. You can absolutely spare 30 energy whenever you need a quick sprint.

All of my talent choices are recommendations; none of them are absolute. If you read my poisons section you'll see that I still gave it plenty of good word. It's true that Assassination works best with it due to Envenom's buff giving you limited control over how fast it stacks, and for that reason I could consider editing my post.

But I'm going to disagree with you that Assassination is the best at energy management. We were given a large energy pool simply because we AREN'T good at it, and would be starved in no time without. We have the most expensive abilities of all 3 specs, and a mechanism of energy regen that is RNG. Venemous Wounds is not guaranteed to give you energy every tick, and though you can bleed multiple targets it will only last so long before you have to reapply more people which would spread your attention and pressure too wide to be effective.

Initially yes, BoS makes little impact when we've got a full 150/150 energy bar. But when you've already spent all your energy and are at the bottom of the barrel 30 energy for BoS is not something I want to rely on when a 75% chance for more energy is all I got, especially when a snare can just be reapplied to me and then I have to spend another 30.

Anyone tried haste>crit instead of crit>haste?

I feel like I do a lot more damage since I reforged out of crit and into haste.

Crit should be valued above haste due to Sealed Fate, as far as PvP is concerned. Extra haste will not make up for the reduced amount of combo points you would generate otherwise. Crit has a lower value for Subtlety because generating extra CP is not entirely dependent on the rogue like Sealed Fate is. Anyone in their group/raid can contribute to their combo points when they crit, which is why Honor Among Thieves also has a 2-second internal cooldown.

Hi Toryk,

Just a question on reforge, I'm wondering how much crit I should invest on? Should I always reforge my surplus hit-expertise to mastery?

Anything that can be mastery, should be mastery. Anything that cannot be mastery, should be crit.

Say you've got an item with crit and haste on it, leave crit as it is and convert haste to mastery.

Say you've got an item with only crit on it and nothing else. Convert that crit to mastery.

Say you've got an item with mastery and haste on it. Leave the mastery as-is and since you cannot convert the haste to additional mastery, convert it to crit instead.

If you have an item with both crit and mastery on it, it doesn't need to be reforged.
---------------------------------------------
The same rules apply to extra hit/expertise. Reforge surplus to mastery when you can, and crit when you can't.

My opener considers a fair amount of gear though. Don't expect to destroy people if you're just starting out, but you can definitely lock down a healer this way. In the "(De)Buffs to Maintain" section you will see I suggest keeping a bleed active on at least one person as often as possible.

Taking a look at lastrogue.com (updated 6/6/13) it looks like they only differ from my gemming suggestions in the meta socket.

They suggest either Tyrannical Primal Diamond or Fleet Primal Diamond.

Tyrannical Primal Diamond provides 1.66% damage towards players and ~2% damage reduction. Not to mention it costs 1,000 conquest points and requires 27,000 CP earned during the season. It's not something I'd recommend.

Fleet Primal Diamond's run speed increase does not stack with the boot enchants, so half its value is lost outright. I do not recommend this either.

Agile Primal Diamond would still be my best bet as an all-around choice.

I dunno. From my experience you don't have enough contact time to DR too much, but if it does happen then there's enough up time on target that the point is moot; they will be dying shortly OR it's a class that is so tankey [*cough*disc priest*cough*) that you will have enough time for the DR to reset after it immunes.

For me, the DR'ed stuns are freebies. By its nature it's a rng based mechanic and it would be unreasonable for us to expect it to be controllable such that it will give you a full 4sec as you want it without DRs. It's classified as an uncontrolled stun for a reason, which is why it's justifiable to put it on a different DR table than KS and CS.

Part of the skill of using PP is watching stacks, anticipating the stun proc, and adapting to what happens when it does/doesn't proc. It's something that, personally, enhances the fun of the spec as a whole.

Also, the 4 sec root is pretty underrated. You can use it to keep someone in place for up time to get the last stack for the stun, you can use it to peel, and you can use it keep someone in a bomb or out of LoS of their healer if they make a mistake and push in too far.

Bottom line though is that the idea of the "best" talents kinda went out the window with how Blizz made changing talents so cheap/easy and allowed us to do so in the beginning of an arena match no less. The best talents are what fit your comp and counters the comp you're playing against the most effectively. Really.
