I'm afraid I don't quite follow your numbers. Using Cheap Shot with Shadow Focus would indeed be 10 energy, but that means you saved 30 energy as the ability costs 40 by default. If you were to open with Mutilate, you'd be saving 41.25 energy, and 26.25 with Envenom.

The reason I make a note of most damage abilities instead of CC is because SF doesn't truly shine via standard openers like Subterfuge does. It's meant to streamline your burst.

Here's a post I made on the subject:
http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/15509151172#3

Slightly updated & re-written, we have...

1) Stack to 5 CPs.
2) Pool Energy.
3) Vendetta for Cold Blood buff.
4) Envenom.
---Mutilate/Dispatch during the 20% buff for ~5 more CPs.
------Envenom again to clear these CPs. The 4-piece doesn't seem to add
------Anticipation stacks. The closer you are to 0 CPs before step 5 the better.
5) Vanish for 5 CPs from your 4-piece and to gain another Cold Blood buff.
6) -75% cost Envenom out of stealth or -75% Cheap Shot then Envenom.
---These two options minimize energy loss through Relentless Strikes and
---Shadow Focus. 20 energy spent to do CS>Env@5CP, & 16.25 energy
---gained to just Env@5CP.
7) More Mutilate/Dispatch spam during the 20% Env buff.
8) Mecha-Blast Rocket
9) If you know the kill can happen, Prep and repeat #5-#7.
10) Health should be low if not dead; Dispatch spam.

SF is meant to maximize energy retention while you work your way down this list. Including steps #9+, you can reduce overall cost by 60, 56.25, or 52.5 energy depending upon how you break Vanish. Even more if for whatever reason you decide to open with Mutilate.

01/06/2015 08:47 AMPosted by Siidestep
Which pvp trinkets should assassin be using? Also is there such a thing as to much mastery?

Since the +Mastery trinket no longer exists, you should be using the +Crit free-action trinket and your choice of proc/on-use damage trinket.

Mastery has no soft/hard cap, but despite this, back when I first made the guide (MoP S13/14) there was a break-off point where stat weights shifted, and it was better to just focus on stacking agility. Right around 79.5% or so if I remember correctly.

Since we can no longer gem main stats (agi/int/str), this is no longer the case. Mastery should be your one true love, now and forever.
