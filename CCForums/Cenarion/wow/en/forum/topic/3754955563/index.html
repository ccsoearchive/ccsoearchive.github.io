The current plan is for the Looking For Raid system to detect specs in MoP to prioritize gear types. However, this will not prevent people from rolling on gear they don't need because they have something better, either to vendor or to give to a friend.

This is a simple problem with a simple solution. Assuming a given piece of gear has a value X to someone who can use it, by rolling for that item there is a chance they will get X value if they win the roll, and 0 value if they lose the roll. Everyone who doesn't roll gets 0 value, but someone who wouldn't use it and rolls anyway gets a smaller value if they win and vendor it or give to a friend, something like X/2 (if there is a chance their friend will do the same thing in the future).

To fix this and prevent everyone from having to join as a group to exploit the maximum amount of possible rolls for each item, anyone who wants a given item must estimate the value X of the item and split that amount with the rest of the raid. For a 25-person LFR group, each person in the raid would receive X/25 in value for every item that drops, including the person who wins the item (in actuality the game would be to underestimate the value as much as possible so they would receive somewhat more value than anyone else). People would probably be less excited about winning an item they have to pay for, but would also be less disappointed about losing an item to someone else with a better dice roll.


This is a simple concept but hasn't become more popular in Western countries simply because consecutive bidding is a terrible way to determine value when the proceeds are split between all participants, and prone to exploitation and collaboration to influence the price. So instead of multiple bids, each player has only a single bid that's only announced when all bids have been completed (this is how the MMO Aion does it) but furthermore, anyone with a low bid can match the highest bid to have a chance to win the item (at that higher price) — they just have a lower chance to win the RNG, similar to the bonus to need roll based on class role in the LFR system.

EXAMPLE
A weapon drops in a 25-person LFR group. It is usable by four people.
Player A secretly bids 3000g. (According to AHSpy.com, lvl 85 epics go for about 7000~10,000g on the AH so 3000g is substantially below market value)
Player B secretly bids 500g.
Player C secretly bids 1500g.
Player D secretly bids 40g. (sell price to vendor)
Player E can't use the item, but is friends with player B and secretly bids 500g as well for a chance at another dice roll.

All this takes only a few seconds. After everyone has either bid or declined, the top bid of 3000g is announced without stating names, and the other players have the option to match it (if they have at least 3000g in inventory) or drop out. Players B, C, and E decide to match at 3000g, and once they have done so the dice is rolled:
Player A has no RNG penalty due to being top bid and rolls 14 (1~100).
Player B has an RNG penalty based on a standard formula, like 70% of the proportional gap between his bid and the top bid subtracted from the maximum roll: [0.7 * (3000 - 500)/3000] = 0.583, meaning a maximum roll of [100 - 58] = 42, and ends up rolling 31 (1~42).
Player C using the same formula has a maximum limit of [100 - (70 * (3000 - 1500)/3000)] = 65 and rolls 28 (1~65).
Player E has the same limit as player B of 42 and rolls 42 (1~42).

Player E wins the item and pays the raid 3000g, with everyone receiving 120g. Player E then trades the item to player B who can use it.

In this case the value X of the item was determined to be 3000g, as no one felt the need to place their initial bid higher than that despite knowing that other people wanted the item. If a guild, or even several guilds, raid together with PUGers used to fill extra raid slots either in 10-person or 25-person raid modes, there would be no reason to be upset at, verbally abuse, or kick any of the PUGers if they won the roll on any item, because they paid the full, nominal value X of the item to the entire raid and everyone, including the person who won the roll and anyone who lost the roll, has theoretically received the same value from the item dropping.

No drama. No players being kicked for rolling on something they "shouldn't" have rolled on, whether for main spec or offspec or even transmogrification.


Disclaimer: other than PTRs and the lvl 70 Arena Tournament realm, my highest character in WoW was lvl 61, who only reached that level because I panicked and force quit WoW when several guards in AV were about to die instead of /afking like a good Alliance PvPer. I have also never received any loot from a raid boss. But this seems like it could fix a lot of problems.
