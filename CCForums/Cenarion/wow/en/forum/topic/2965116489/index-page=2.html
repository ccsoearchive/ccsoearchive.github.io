08/12/2013 03:49 AMPosted by Merdrah
All discussion of the casino, even just giving the rules cannot be done on any of Blizzard's channels.


Just to clarify, giving out the rules would likely be fine through party or private channel. Giving out the rules in public channels. i.e. Say, Trade, General, Yell, etc... would not be.

If you just invite someone to a group out of the blue and advertise your casino that is another matter.

We understand that there are folks out there that like to participate in these types of activities, I know a number of guilds during their get-togethers would "raffle" off prizes. That is often innocent fun, designed to bring a guild together.

These policies are not designed to greatly impact those types of activities, and is one of the reasons why the policies were changed to allow casinos, just not the advertisement of them. So that guilds and other groups could put together some fun activities like that.

Unfortunately, there were simply too many scams associated with allowing the public advertisement of casinos, to the point that it greatly impacted the support we were able to provide players overall.

I hope that helps. :)

In-Game Casinos

While running or participating in casino activities in-game is not a violation of our policies, we do not allow advertising for such activities in public chat channels or on the forums. Players found advertising these activities in chat or on the forums are subject to penalties.

There are two ways to report casino or gambling advertisements in public chat channels:

Right-click the character's name in chat and select Report Spam.
Submit a ticket.

Note: When submitting a ticket, include the offending character's name, realm, and a brief description of the chat.

To report casino or gambling advertisements on the forums:

Click the thumbs down symbol in the upper right corner of the post.
Select Report from the dropdown.
Select a report type from the Reason dropdown.
Enter additional details in the Explain section.
Click Submit.
