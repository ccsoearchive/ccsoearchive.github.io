As long as they're not actually spamming (the same message back to back more than the occasionally accident), and not posting in trade chat, they're not breaking the rules.

Just to say, it goes a big beyond not posting in Trade chat.

Players are not allowed to advertise casinos in ANY manner in-game or on the forums. That means whispers, say, yell, Trade, General, Party, Raid, ANY channel in-game is a no-go for letting others know a casino is being run. That also means Character Name, Guild Name, Pet Name cannot be used to blatantly imply a casino. It means posting on the Blizzard forums in any way to announce one's casino is against the rules. That even means playing the game publicly can be against the rules, as the purpose of such would be to attract other players. Getting winners to advertise is also not allowed.

So if a proliferation of casinos is visible to someone who doesn't want to see it? That would mean it's being advertised (aka made public knowledge) and whatever method is being used can be reported.

===================

Gambling itself is not against the rules because sometimes players can choose to do little things between themselves. I remember doing chain heroics in BC days and betting with my friends at the beginning of each how long we would take. We were all friends and there was no advertising to anyone else. Similarly, guilds will often have raffles and contests that are essentially gambles. Make rules against gambling and suddenly that sort of fun that is mutually agreed on within a small group isn't allowed.

Thus, the rule currently is against advertising - in any manner whatsoever. Guilds can advertise on their guild websites and be fine. Friends can make friendly wagers in Vent and be fine. And "Casino" runners, to be allowed to continue, must find their sources of gold external to WOW communication.
