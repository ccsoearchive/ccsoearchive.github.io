NAME(S): Red Earth of the Blackhide, Matriarch of the Ishnu Por Ah; AKA Meshkwahki in her tribal dialect

FAMILY HISTORY:Swifthorn tribe, family Blackhide. Eldest daughter of Dark Sky (father, now deceased), shaman and clan elder, and White Hoof (mother), hunter. One sibling, a younger sister, Kick Feather.

ACTIVITY BRIEF:Appearing in Mulgore not long after Thunder Bluff was established, this Tauren travelled alone until becoming involved with a social occasion in Ashenvale hosted by a Kal’dorei named Aroree. Started out as an open invitation walk no matter the faction, the event became seen as an effort for peace as both Aroree and Red Earth rose to become its figureheads, refusing to fight against any who would attempt to harm them during these nightly walks. Growing friction between the two factions resulting in open warfare in Ashenvale, as well as assassination attempts on them, and a growing difference of opinion about the Walks objectives caused a split which eventually led to its demise after four months.

During this time, she became acquainted with the Ishnu Por Ah (IPA) through an intimate relationship with a founding member, a Tauren warrior, Getharn. Involved in a few of IPA's early activities and courted to join them a few times, she refused until sometime after the death of her father. She moved up in prominence within IPA, gaining shared leadership with Feren Longstrider, its founder. When personal reasons force him to step down, she became its sole leader. Presently, she is still leader.

Though the reasons for Feren’s removal are mysterious and tragic, there seems to be no foul play involved in the takeover. The current whereabouts of Getharn are unknown. He is presumed dead, lost at Blackrock Mountain. There has been no further love interest, though she is fanatically pursued by an eccentric Forsaken, Abominus.

As a member she continued to call for peace. However, under her leadership, IPA also became a more Tauren focused group, as she advocates the Will of the Earthmother and the preservation and sovereignty of the Tauren nation. These sentiments are not surprising. She does not consider herself a member of the Horde. This stems from the beliefs of her home tribe who have refused to ally themselves with the Horde for fear of losing their Tauren cultural identity. The tribe's majority remain nomadic, separate from Horde society. Red Earth is known to visit them at least once or twice a year.

As a shaman, she is considered a special circumstance. Tribe tradition dictates that those in the Blackhide family born with pure black fur are meant to be the Swifthorn shaman. Red Earth, whose fur is red, discovered the call of the Spirits howver and learned from her father. She lives apart from her tribe as they believe her differences determine she was not to live among them. It is believed that this separation has made her faith in the Earthmother’s guidance and her reliance on visions paramount to her actions and teachings.

Visions overwhelmed her in her early years. But after spending time in the Outlands with the Mag’har of Nagrand, she seems to have a better control of their effects on her. It is also in her years in the Outlands that this “simple shaman” rose to become a leader of war parties. It is believed that this development and the following participation in the battles in Northrend are the seed for the more militant attitude that is developing in concerns to the protection of Mulgore and her drive to bring prominence back to the IPA which has weakened in the aftermath of Lich King’s defeat and the eruption of the Cataclysm.

Her strong belief in the Earthmother causes her to despise Fel magic. Her belief that all people are Children of the Earthmother compel her to attempt to treat all with compassion and fairness. This is juxtaposed with her traditional upbringing which makes her biased towards the Tauren people before any other people or nation, Horde or otherwise.

THREAT ASSESSMENT: The impact of her activities have had their effect, though nothing long lasting. But she is able to build strong ties which has garnered respected friends from both factions, has allowed her to absorb different members from now defunct groups such as the Horntotem Tribe and the Mt. Hyjal Alliance, and retain relationships with former members of the IPA like ranking members of the suspiciously entrepreneurial Razortalons. Also with her independent stance, a growing militant attitude and past accounts of erratic behavior based on her calling, the seeds are there for something to occur. She should continue to be watched.

NAME(S): Arothand "Aro" Lightsworn, previously Executor of the Razortalons, previously Guardian of the Household Zharikov.

FAMILY HISTORY: Most of not all of the Lightsworn family appears to be deceased. Parents confirmed deceased, possible unconfirmed sightings of siblings within the past year. Present status unknown.

ACTIVITY BRIEF: Lightsworn's activity hasn't been difficult to trace. Heavily involved in the Silvermoon army when he came of age around a hundred years ago, he excelled in most forms of armed and unarmed combat, but was shown to be a poor leader when given the opportunity.

Eventually, apparently fed up with the politics of Silvermoon, the Elf went rogue, pirating a ship and crew. Remained a minor threat, moreso of a nuisance for quite some time. Eventually, driven by remorse, he took up the path of the Paladin. Activity between then and now is fairly typical for an Elf his age, a veteran of several wars.

When his people defected, so too did Arothand, joining the Blood Knights in their feeding off of the Naaru M'uru. Eventually, however, it appears he grew sick of politics once more, taking his leave of Silvermoon once more.

He made quick friends with the Archmagus, Raoul Zharikov, and joined the ranks of Da Doctas as a "Protecta". His time with the Doctas was relatively short, ended by a dispute over a demon-possessed woman, and the treatment of those who reside on the campus. Enraged, Arothand enlisted in the military once more, killed in action, and raised as a Knight of the Ebon Blade.

It was at this point Arothand began to delve into more suspicious territory. Furthering his friendship with Archmagus Zharikov, he gave an oath to the man that he would protect Raoul and his family. At this point, Arothand picked up what appears to be a dangerous romantic relationship with possible cultist Sizer Williamson, the self-proclaimed "Titansteel Warrior", the three becoming an organization of sorts, their fingers dug into a few events including a form of magic rabies that plagued Da Doctas campus.

Eventually, whether by contract or otherwise, Zharikov entered the service of the once-again suspicious business organization known as the Razortalons. Arothand was quick to follow. Hardly business savvy, and not the type to take on a leadership position, Arothand appeared to be some sort of high-ranking Bruiser/bodyguard, calling himself an "Executor". His work with the Talons has been particularly hard to track, however it can be deduced that he is a muscle of some sort, and appears to do a good job of keeping mouths shut.

This took an odd turn, however, when due to a dispute in beliefs, or possibly honor, Arothand betrayed the Talons, killing an estimated four-five of their rank, and himself, in the process. For unknown reasons he has been resurrected by the Razortalons, and has resumed his work for them, as well as reuniting with his presumed love interest, Sizer.

THREAT ASSESSMENT: Arothand has proven himself capable of taking down large groups of enemies, and friends alike, having recently killed a substantial amount of Razortalons in one fight. The means of such are unknown. His kill count beyond that is suspected to be much higher, however, many of the deaths he has been involved with have quickly been covered up by unseen forces. He has connections within the ranks of the Razortalons, and appears to be close to the leader of Da Doctas, Sidonwy. Often seen carrying Old God artifacts, his loyalty to the Horde is questionable due to his close relationship with a possible cultist. While his skills as a soldier are unquestionable, he appears to make rash decisions, and is likely temperamental. While this makes him less likely to lead an organized assault, he is unpredictable. He should be watched.

Recent years has seen the Troll return to his previous voodoo practices, proving proficient in multiple types of rituals. While he keeps it mostly private, his habits have increasingly taken to it's use to supplement his aging body.

THREAT ASSESSMENT: Despite being of advanced age, the Troll remains in significant physical condition, further supplemented by voodoo rites and blessings. In addition to himself, he retains the companionship of a number of animals, all of whom have shown to be fiercely loyal to the Troll, and fully capable of operating completely independant of the Troll's direct influence. His vicious response to the deaths of his family, and complete willingness to practice voodoo arts are both notable. Should continue to be observed with great care.

NAME(S): Kick Feather of the Blackhide; Full name-Kicks the Feather Walking

FAMILY HISTORY:Swifthorn tribe, family Blackhide. Youngest daughter of Dark Sky (father, now deceased), shaman and clan elder, and White Hoof (mother), hunter. One sibling, an elder sister, Red Earth

ACTIVITY BRIEF:
Kickfeather left her tribe in pursuit of her sister, Red Earth, of whom she has shown to be very protective. Her concerns were not unfounded as after a few months of search she found herself helping her sister’s then lover, Getharn, rescue her from the quillboar who had captured her while she was in a state of visionary delusion. It was also with a troll witch doctor’s aid-Hrookzhin, then leader of Da Doctas-that Kickfeather was able to be reunited with her sister. Because of this, she joined Da Doctas to help pay off the debt she owed to Dr. Hrookzhin for helping her save her sister’s life.

As a Protector, she stayed happily with Da Doctas for several months. Then a dispute between Hrookzhin’s daughter, Arjah, and Kickfeather arose. The dispute stemmed from Arjah’s accusations that Red Earth hated and tried to kill the troll, something Red Earth flatly denied. Kickfeather sided with her sister and challenged Arjah for spreading such “dishonorable” rumors. When Hrookzhin would not help resolve the issue, Kickfeather then left the service of Da Doctas considering her debt paid and joined the Ishnu Por Ah, a Tauren focused group which her sister now led.

Kickfeather is a young, strong warrior. After joining the Ishnu Por Ah, she resumed the protective role of her sister. This spread to any of the Ishnu Por Ah whom she considers a second family. She has been known to offer to “take care of” and “beat up” any who threatened any member of IPA in anyway. As far as we know, she has never had to carry out such threats. She also aided the Horde in their struggle to maintain their borders and protect their resources and so was seen frequenting battles at Warsong Gulch, Alterac Valley and Arathi. When many went to the Outlands after the Dark Portal reopened, Kickfeather stayed behind to continue to protect the home front. Though never a true member of the Horde forces, she was given the honorary recognition of Sergeant for her actions on the battlefields. When the Lich King arose to attack the nations of Azeroth, Kickfeather joined the multitude of armies and headed to Northrend.

She is known to have a very wild nature, acting before thinking. Some have called her crazy. She has shown to have an extreme curiosity, quite naïve about things outside her tribal education, though her years spent in the Horde lands has taught her much. She is never shy, her nature is certainly brash and she shows no fear in battle. But she seems to have a strong sense of honor and a deep respect for her faith and those she calls family, especially her sister whose words she lives by. This has helped temper any extreme actions she may have or will take.

THREAT ASSESSMENT : Though sharing the faith of her sister in the Earthmother, it is not as fanatical. And though she is a berserker in battle, there is no record of her being a danger to any she does not consider the enemy. Alone she is not a threat. She is, however, deeply loyal to her sister and many of her actions stem from what her sister advises. And so may be considered a deadly instrument, a ‘right-hand man', in whatever machinations her sister may devise.

PRE-DEATH ACTIVITY BRIEF
(Note: The following was compiled from multiple local sources and is presented here as a foundation to possibly explain future activities.) Born in Pyrewood Village before the Scourge overtook the area, Vincent was raised primarily by his mother while his father ran a small mercantile fleet. Vincent was found to be a quick book learner (especially in the arcane and medical sciences) but lacked the social ethic to be a productive member of the community. Conversely, his brother Isaac became a civil engineer, helping to maintain the buildings and various utilities. Several years later, Isaac discovered that his brother had joined a local cabal of warlocks to further his learning. During the Scourge outbreak, Vincent had fallen ill and was bedridden. Isaac took this opportunity to suffocate Vincent with a pillow, to remove the "corruption" his brother represented. For reasons that have yet to be identified, the suffocation and subsequent death served as a catalyst for the Scourge plague.

POST-UNDEATH ACTIVITY BRIEF: Several weeks later, Vincent became re-animated by the Scourge plague, but retained some degree of free will. Returning to Pyrewood, he learned that his Last Will and Testament was found but was not carried out. While Vincent was dead, his cultist activities were brought to the Mayor's attention. The Mayor chose to not honor the Will and instead distributed the inheritance to repair the damages he felt the Cult caused. It is unclear if Isaac's position as Chief Engineer held any influence in this series of events, especially since preparing for the inevitable reprisals of a demon-worshipping enclave normally does not require the addition of a patio and gazebo on The Mayor's summer home.

Vincent somehow learned of this, and began to "collect what he is owed", by killing each person that he felt would have his inheritance. Upon reaching his home, he found his wife Claudia and children waiting for him. Instead of defending themselves, they were ready to sacrifice themselves, since they thought at the time it was the arrival of their demonic lord. Instead, Vincent asked how much of the inheritance they got. After they responded that they were given the house but none of the money, Vincent told Claudia she would now inherit a thirty minute headstart and suggested she use it wisely. He left the house and tracked down the Mayor and Isaac. After a series of heated arguments, they both fought Vincent but eventually lost after a protracted battle. When Vincent returned his house, he found Claudia and the children gone, but found all of his possessions intact. He would jokingly refer to this as "the cheapest divorce ever". It is rumored that they contacted one her father-in-law's old merchant friends to seek passage on a ship, but it is unclear what happened with Vincent's family since then.

He also appears to have been involved in the reclamation of Thunder Bluff after the Grimtotem revolt plotted by Elder Crone Magatha. From this point to the Cataclysm, he was involved with assisting in cleaning up the city.

She appears to have met the bull Nishk Winterspear through an AAMS mail mix up at the Doctas clinic. Her romantic involvement with NW lead her to cut ties with her family over an arranged marriage. It also introduced her to the Tauren separatist organization, Ishnu Por Ah (please reference the IPA files for further explanation). During Benna's time with the IPA, there was a price on her head, and the contract was taken by Stefan Pryak. Given that Benna never showed any particular talents as a shaman, or seemed to possess anything very interesting, it is likely that the contract was due entirely to her relationship with NW.

It was during this time that Benna started her seed collection. It is likely that she has seeds of most plants growing Azaroth or the Outlands before the cataclysm. After saving an elfin child from a Modas Il Toralor raid and delivering the child to a foster family through the AAMS, she developed strong ties to the orphanage in the lower city of Shattrath, where she tutored and volunteered until her breakup with NW, at which point she moved in and began full time work. Before the break up, Benna resided primarily in the apartment of NW in Booty Bay and in a small house she had purchased in Nagrand.

Benna became pregnant and retired to the jungles of Stranglethorn with Arjah and Hrookzihn. After that her whereabouts are unknown for a period of close to a year, but it is known that the baby was lost. Her relationship with NW quickly dissolved after that period. Benna has been known to engage in long bouts of meditation and perhaps this was an example. Benna left the IPA once NW introduced the druid Ebonbreeze, leading us to believe that her interest in the group was romantic rather than political.

Benna followed Arjah to the Homeland, a group with no coherent ideology, of which she remains a member. She reconnected with her sister Ninah through this group and with her Brother Dern through the tournament. Shortly after reuniting with Dern, she moved out of the orphanage and into Dern and Ashra's apartment in Dalran along with Ninah. When it became clear that Azaroth was unstable, Benna returned to the Bluff to offer to take family members to her home in Nagrand. Two elderly ladies, five children, and one cousin were sent with her. In addition all members of the apartment in Dalaran moved. Benna appears to be the main breadwinner for this entire family group, and has been working almost constantly for the horde since their arrival at her home.

THREAT ASSESSMENT: Benna poses no physical or rhetorical threat. However, her efforts to grow exotic plants particularly the violent ones of the Underbog in non-native locations requires observation.

APPENDIX A: NINAH RAGETOTEM

Ninah dyed her fur black and ran from the Bluff shortly after a coming of age ritual at the beginning of her warrior training. At the beginning of the semester Ninah gained admittance to the RAS apprenticeship program and is currently studying alchemy full time. She plans to specialize in the creation of flesh golems.

THREAT ASSESSMENT: As a up and coming student of the RAS, Ninah should be watched with the rest of them.

ACTIVITY:
Brief:
Plainswander has been a loner all his life, beginning when his family arrived in Mulgore with the rest of the tauren after the Great Migration, and continuing on even now into his later years. Despite that, he has been to all points of the explored cosmos, and with sometimes companions in the Grim Maw Clan, AAMS, Eternal Sorrow, Order of the Ebon Shield, and Ishnu Por Ah, fought most of the great threats to the world. He is almost supernaturally resilient, has survived everything two worlds can throw at him, and has been active longer than most other subjects detailed in this record.

Early Life:
Plainswander (né: Ahanu) was completely average in all respects but size, mostly notable only for his lack of tragedy in a time where tragedy dogged so many. Raised by his mother and father in a small and so far unidentified settlement in Mulgore, his life was largely an uneventful series of long sun blessed days and nights spent around the cookfire telling tales, jokes, and lies around the events of the day. Contact with the greater group of Tauren society was limited to market days and the occasional visit from a roaming hunter or shaman. It was not until he was almost fully grown that Plainswander encountered his calling in the form of a druid. After nearly being slain by a marauding raptor pack, and subsequently being rescued by a fortuitously nearby druid, Plainswander found himself consumed with dreams and wanderlust. The newfound obsession grew to the point where, after his 10th week of unanswerable questions, stories, and pestering, his parents finally gave up hope and sent him to Bloodhoof to train with the other youngsters.

Plainswander showed strong aptitude for the way of the druid, and was soon "released into the wild" as it were, to begin his own path.

Early Adulthood:
Plainswander spent much of his early adulthood wandering Kalimdor and the Eastern Kingdoms with no real direction or purpose, simple absorbing as much as he could of life and the world. He attempted, for a while, to become a social creature, cultivating friends on both side of the Horde-Alliance conflict. It was during this time his ongoing contempt for the hardline partisan stances of both the horde and alliance was set. To this day he refers to himself not as a member of the Horde, but as "just a druid", holding his allegiance to the various Cenarion orders to be a higher calling than any political concern. It was during this time as well that, in partnership with a dwarf named Fil, Plainswander founded the diplomatic/mercantile company known as the AAMS (see separate dossier).

Also during this time, Plainswander began what was to become one of his signature hobbies, the fomenting of unrest, confusion, and outright conflict, all for his amusement. The combination of political, diplomatic, and mercantile connections with his natural tendency to bend the truth and tinker with events for amusement's sake has on more than one occasion landed others in considerable trouble. Plainswander has, however, so far evaded the consequences of his meddling.

Donald Mund's life until the time of his death was that of an accomplished, but otherwise completely unremarkable scholar and writer of fiction. Armed now with only a pair of knives and his obsession with revenge, he has taken up a career as a rogue, and while his skills were rudimentary (at best) when he began, he is swiftly learning the tricks of the trade, and displaying an almost unnerving aptitude for the language of blood and urder.

Observation of the subject has led us to conclude that regardless of who perpetrated the murder of this man, the deed itself was one of exceptional violence. Owing to the nature of his mortal injuries, Donald is lacking certain properties common to most forsaken. Most notable of which is a large portion of his skull, and the brain matter within it, which seem to be missing. Most of Dolmund's (as he prefers to be called) higher faculties have been left intact, what seems most affected are the centers for language use and recognition, emotional control, and empathic perception.

The subject, once a even tempered polyglot with a wide range of knowledge and a passionate love of literature has been reduced to a mostly illiterate monster with only the most basic grasp of spoken common, furious psychological storms, and full knowledge of what, exactly, he's lost. The result is an unstable, and extremely angry forsaken who is, as most of them are, hell bent on what his damaged mind perceives as 'justice'. Couple this with a lack of self control, and an almost complete excision of moral restraint, and you have a cause for concern.

Threat Assessment:
At present, Dolmund is extremely dangerous to those in his immediate vicinity, beyond that he can be safely disregarded. Dolmund seems to be primarily obsessed with finding who killed him, and is embarking on a search, using whatever means he may, to determine the identity of his killers. At present, owing to the missing portions of his brain, the unfortunate side effects the trauma that accompanied his first death, Domund's search is proving largely fruitless.

It should be noted that as a result of his resurrection, he possess the almost singleminded focus on his goal common to all forsaken, and should the parties he seeks be of sufficient importance, his crusade to find/destroy them could have lasting repercussions. Observation and possibly containment are warranted.

Research has revealed that for many years this Tauren was a gladiator slave to the Amani trolls in Zul’Aman, surviving by providing murderous entertainment to his brutal masters. Sold to them by his own father in an attempt to gain funds and favor to abet the suffering of his dying tribe, it is the last known act of his father and what was left of that tribe. Dark Sky won the duel and so won Mishkwaki’s freedom and he returned with the shaman to his tribe, the Swifthorn, in Kalimdor. It is believed that because Mishkwaki knew nothing but slavery for most of his life, he offered to serve the Blackhide family for the rest of it since he knew nothing else and to repay them for his ‘freedom’. After many years, he did eventually leave their service to live the life of an ascetic and it is during this time the it is believed he heard his calling to take up the hand of healing and druidism. Though the Tauren puts forward a bit of an eccentric and peaceful façade as an old, doddery druid, there are signs that his years of slavery have left their scars and not just physical ones. He has expressed great hatred for trolls and a wariness of other races, especially blood elves, who were more than likely his most prolific victim in the gladiator ring. It is only his unwavering dedication to Red Earth who encourages a more open acceptance of other people that prevents him from acting on his aggressions.

THREAT ASSESSMENT : The danger of Mishkwaki is directly related to whatever commands Red Earth gives to him. It is said he has killed for her without a second thought, but there is no evidence beyond rumor. There is also the question of his possible activities as an underground arms dealer and this should be further assessed.
