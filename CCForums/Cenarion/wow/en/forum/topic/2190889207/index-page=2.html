Vasilia
Profile
Vasilia
70 Draenei Priest
AAMS Alliance Branch
0
NAME(S): Vasilia Starfallen AKA Vas.

FAMILY HISTORY: Parents: Unknown. Siblings: Unknown. Previous Relations: Unknown.

ACTIVITY BRIEF: Next to nothing is known about Vasilia's history prior to the awakening of Deathwing. She resided in the coastal town of Auberdine prior to the event and was in Darnassis when the village was destroyed.

Vasilia suffers from an unknown, and seemingly uncorrectable disability that affects both her short term and long term memory. As far as we can tell, she has always suffered from this disability. Learning to write at a young age greatly increased her quality of life as she was able to write things down so she would not forget them later.

By writing nearly everything down Vasilia is able to use her "notes" to help refresher her memory. Her ability to remember is based on numerous factors. She might be able to remember someone's name for a few days at most, but if she goes longer than that without seeing the person she will ultimately forget about the person. Her notes, both amazingly efficient and accurate help her keep her life in order.

Her last name was given to her by a night elf family that she stayed with after Auberdine's destruction. "Starfallen" being a literal translation of the Dreanei's ship, Exodar, falling to Azeroth from the stars.

Upon moving to Stormwind she has gained employment with the AAMS. Once disorganized, the AAMS has benefited greatly from her organization and attention to detail. While she has many friends she often finds the most comfort in her work and making sure the AAMS is running smoothly. Very career minded.

She has recently starting to form relations with another Draenei named Cyrus, who has a less than honorable past and employer. <See Cyrus's file for details>

THREAT ASSESSMENT: A capable healer, Vasilia does not pose much of a direct threat. She is known to favor the stave, wand and spells in combat and normally has a collapsible riot stick on her person at all times. While bound by the AAMS's strict neutrality policy she is often given greater access to the higher levels on both sides of the law. Of particular concern is how closely she operates with the Horde AAMS branch, often socializing with members of the Horde willingly and without provocation.

He claimed to have wound up on an island shrouded in green mist with a large substructure, where he'd make daily broadcasts from, reporting his going-ons. After the Sundering, it's not certain what happened, but his first physical sighting was off the coast of the newly reconstructed Azshara in a small motorized boat roughly a month after Deathwings emergence. Since then he's somehow established a company called "Payload Incorporated" and has begun supplying the Horde with military grade munitions and trivial inventions for day to day life.

It appears he was recently involved (soul perpetrator) in a series of mailbox bombings through out the Horde cities and towns. It appears this isn't the only time he's caused unrest amongst his Hordsemen, having reportedly causing trouble at social events and every day activities. He's also believed to be involved [this section is blacked out]

THREAT ASSESSMENT: HIGHLY DANGEROUS.
Regardless of what his past actually was or not, Chachaa is with no doubt a dangerous individual. An unstable mental state combined with an alarmingly fast learning rate and access to high grade explosives raises alarms in several circles. He bombed a great number of public Horde (his allies) locations on a whim, raising what he'd be capable of with a grudge. Along with his continued projects to advance his own powers through bio-enhancements and power-suits and his other inventions created with the soul purpose of destruction, Chachaa is without a doubt a target of interest.

Profile
Vecht

Modas il Toralar
6445
Name:
"Vectus" aka Vith Maldonst


Family History:
Son and sole heir of a Lordaeron nobleman. Only known survivor of a Scourge attack occurring late in his teenage years which killed his family and destroyed most of their estate.


Activity Brief:
After the attack, he fled south and joined the Stormwind infantry. Here, he rose to the rank of Lieutenant Commander after several years of distinctive service wherein he demonstrated his affinity for battle and tactical operations. Eventually, his commanding officer had him transferred to SI:7 to make better use of these skills.

Shortly after receiving the highest levels of training and being commissioned as a Covert Personnel Elimination Agent for SI:7, he was dishonorably discharged when information came to light about his personal life. Apparently, he had taken to staging the death of his marks, only to keep them alive so he could torture them at his leisure.

For four years thereafter, he worked as a freelance assassin and headed a group of elite mercenaries known only as "The Organization." The Organization quickly gained a reputation for its violent and often criminal operations, and was systematically shut down after key information about its members was leaked to Stormwind authorities. After this, the man then known as Vith Maldonst went into hiding. He resurfaced two years later under the alias "Vectus" as an undead within the Horde.

The exact circumstances surrounding his undeath are unknown, although the fact that to date he still displays none of the usual symptoms of undeath suggest it was not an accident, and that he achieved his current state purposefully and through unusual means.

Currently he is the commanding officer of the Ebon Reavers, the military division within the infamous and esoteric organization, "Modas il Toralar." No useful information has been gathered recently, as any reconnaissance agents sent to investigate the subject tend not to return.


Threat Assessment:
Vectus is a seasoned veteran of many battles, a highly skilled and resourceful assassin, and is well known by all who have fought alongside him for his exceptional displays of combat ability and tactical prowess. He often employs creative uses of his engineering and alchemical knowledge during battle to gain the upper hand, and is completely ruthless in his pursuit of victory. Although normally level-headed, he has been known to have bouts of impulsive sociopathy, making him highly unpredictable.

Vectus is wanted by most factions for a variety of heinous crimes, including mass murder, arson, torture, extortion, and the assassination of public officials. He is especially fond of abducting and torturing his victims, usually ritualistically carving various symbols into their flesh after he is finished with them.

Additionally, he is one of the highest-ranking members of the infamous and esoteric order, "Modas il Toralar."

Confrontation of any kind is not advised. Approach at own risk.

Verogoth

NAME(S): Rillik Verogoth AKA: Sarge

FAMILY HISTORY: Parents: Thaler Verogoth - father, Arewien Verogoth - mother Siblings: Eowon Verogoth - brother

ACTIVITY BRIEF: Rillik Verogoth comes from a humble background, the son of the owner and operator of a textile mill near Andorhal. According to accounts from neighbors and local informants, the subject was a hard-working young man, but lacked any sense of self-advancement, seemingly content with living out the rest of his days as a miller and amateur tailor. Local lore holds that during the Third War, business for his father's mill boomed trying to keep up with orders from local militias and larger armies, and the subject and his younger brother, Eowon, were sent out to recruit extra laborers from what is now the Eastern Plaguelands. In transit, the pair was caught up in a battle between human defenders and a Scourge incursion force. The subject was killed defending his brother. As far as the citizens of the subject's hometown are concerned, his story ended there.

Subject disappeared from the known world after that, and did not resurface until the recent rebellion of the Knights of the Ebon Blade. Apparently, the subject's body was chosen for reanimation as one of the Lich King's death knights. He has not revealed much about his time under the sway of the Scourge, so little to nothing is known of how his time was spent.

However, it should be noted that the subject now displays a very healthy respect (almost obsession with) for decorum and order, suggesting that he took to the militaristic aspects of the Ebon Blade very well.

After returning to human civilization, the subject maintained a low profile for a while, fearing the repercussions of what he had become. Intelligence suggests that he did attempt contact with his family, but local informants report that they maintain the their son is dead and remain tight-lipped if questioned further.

Recently, however, the subject has been seen publicly more and more often, likely a direct result of his employment with the Alliance Branch of the AAMS. Also notable is the fact that after recent upheavals at the AAMS branch offices in Stormwind, it was the subject who oversaw the renovations, and he was witnessed purchasing materials for, and hiring craftsmen that specialize in, improved security measures. It can thus be inferred that his duties among the Ebon Blade included fortification security.

THREAT ASSESSMENT: While no accounts currently exist of the subject initiating violent behavior, his training among the Ebon Blade cannot be ignored. All successful (read: surviving) recruits among the death knights have undergone rigorous combat training and tests of the capacity for violence. Additionally, the subject is a member of the AAMS, and organization with ties among both the Alliance and the Horde. (see report on the AAMS for more details) Therefore, the subject may or may not have access to resources and/or contacts unavailable to most among the Alliance.

Further observation is recommended.

Name: Grammok a.k.a. Gram; No family name.
Family: Unknown (Deceased)
History: Little is known about this orc past before he sneaked through the Dark Portal. Before the end of the Second War, he was a peon, of unknown family, of the Thunderlords who escaped the brutal savage warrior culture of the Horde, in part because he was mocked and threatened for his relatively non violent temperament. Very young, he tried to make his way to the exiled Frost Wolves in the hopes that they would take him in. Inexperience nearly cost him his life had it not been for an eccentric old dwarf who took the youth in after after seeing him struggle through the wilds of the Eastern Kingdoms. Why the dwarf would do this is not known; Perhaps he sensed a kindred soul in the young orc. They lived almost as family for years and slowly the orc himself became a hunter.

Then Gram's world came tumbling down as he was seen by a near by patrol on one of his outings. He lost them and quietly made it back to the cabin he had with the old dwarf. As he got there he realized that the Patrol had had trackers of their own and followed the well worn Hunting trail back to home. His mentor refused to talk to the Soldiers and to turn in the student who was like a son to him. The sergeant of the patrol summarily executed the old dwarf as a traitor, beheading him with a sword stroke. Gram fled into the wilds and stayed there biding his time for years, harboring a loathing of humans. If it were not for Thrall's exodus, very likely he would have stayed in hiding.

He spend the third war as a simple scout, getting food to feed this new Horde and relearning his race's noble heritage as well as that of other races in the Horde. After the Battle of Hyjal he decided to see the world. He joined the Winds during the war in Outland after finally getting his over fear of loss, and has been with them ever since.

He explored Outlands and fought with distinction in Northrend. The Cataclysm and the so called Fourth War has brought out his old fears. This place in the world he has so struggled for. Thrall is gone, Cairne is dead, and hte Horde is going back to what seems to be its violent ways. Grams seems troubled, as his old prejudice of humans is coming back.

Threat Level: Gram seems relatively simple and easy going. If not engaged he is unlikely to fight. However, his long years as a hunter have made him a master of guerrilla warfare and small unit tactics. His simple ways hide a cunning mind and a nigh implacable will. His beast are fearsomely loyal to him and will stop at nothing to defend him. He will not fight face to face and always strikes from an advantage. Avoid combat or outmaneuver if engaging him.

Name : Shattering (Original name unknown)
Family : Records lost

Known history : Shattering was one of the faceless nameless masses of the priesthood, toiling away at his duties serving a belief he did not share for day after identical day.

He had come to the priesthood as so many do, through accident of birth. Perhaps he was the youngest son of a large but not wealthy noble family, perhaps a farmer wanted something better for his child. His senses faded from the agonizing light of unknowable pain to the eternal and cold embrace of death. His body fell laughing to the cobblestones wet with lethal residue and the running blood of his congregation. He had finally found his faith.

Threat Assessment: Shattering is determined and fanatic, what he lacks in raw power he makes up for in a patient unwillingness to leave any target alive. He could become quite dangerous as his strength grows.

Likes : The sounds of bones snapping, the final breath of a dying man, rot and decay, flowers.

Dislikes : The living, sunrise, any who would oppose his new queen, and chickens.

FAMILY HISTORY: He speaks fondly of a remembered son, sister, and nephew, though even he is not certain of their current whereabouts.

ACTIVITY BRIEF: Little can be gleaned about Dustwing's past, although the night elf is conjectured to be more than 1000 years old. What little written below has been taken out of the records of one Kildrad "Wildmane" Al'sharen, a deceased night elf lieutenant who bears significant resemblance to Dustwing, in manner, bearing, and appearance. Note that their relation and shared history is conjecture only, and the latter two paragraphs bear the only past that is known for certain.

Expected heir of his House, Kildrad was trained from a very young age to be a leader, especially in combat situations due to the military traditions of his upbringing. He experienced trouble within the family, and a schism between would-be druids and soldiers trained for more mundane combat. For this reason he never picked up druidism like most male night elves are expected to in kaledorei society, though his sister and later his nephew went against the conventionally accepted means of their family and became druids later in life. This caused an uproar in the family, for most females are typically not allowed in the druidic ranks, and the nephew is suspected of delving into the forbidden arts of the Druids of the Scythe.

Kildrad fought and commanded forces in many wars, most specifically the war with the Silithid one thousand years ago, the battle at Mount Hyjal, and the more recent wars against the Burning Legion, the Scourge, and the Horde. He worked extensively with the Argent Dawn and the Aldor as such, and is considered an honored name in both organizations.

This is where the records become uncertain. Kildrad was reported to have led a mixed group of Argent Dawn and Alliance troops deep into Icecrown. The entire platoon disappeared, guessed to have been wiped out in a surprise attack by the Scourge, their bodies buried in a freak avalanche. It is uncertain how many people, if any, survived this catastrophe.

Whether or not Dustwing and Kildrad are one and the same, Dustwing refuses to speak much about his past. For now, the night elf loner wanders the world, pitching in to help stranger travelers or communities as he sees fit.

THREAT ASSESSMENT: A competent archer, skilled in training animals for combat. Remains calm and rational in a fight. Believed to also be an experienced commander, and with the right encouragement, a valuable ally to have in any kind of organized warfare.
