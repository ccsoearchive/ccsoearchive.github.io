Raine'ka 'watched' the scene, her feet pressed firmly into the dirt. Her eyes had been removed years ago, but her connection to the elements allowed her to see through them. She felt the vibrations of the earth when one took a step, felt the curve in the air as one's body moved through it. Because of this, even from a distance she was able to 'see' what was happening. She allowed the scene to play out until the general began to shout, only then taking action. She charged forward, pulling the old, worn skull she wore down over her face. She was garbed in a mixture of plate and leather, protection and movement. For now she kept her sharp spear in hand, closing in the distance between her and the militia quite quickly. Each forward step she took allowed for her to pull the wind beneath her feet, allowing her to move further with each step. She 'watched' as the first few Frostwolves were executed, snarling beneath her skull of a helmet as she sent the bottom of her spear into the dirt, the air beneath it suddenly swirling into wind strong enough to launch her at her foes. Luckily the militia had been too busy bashing in skulls to notice a single Orc's advance, but caught on the moment she landed behind the first of many. She swung her spear around, aiming not to smash the sharp edge into the Orc's armor, but instead to send a powerful wave of air at his legs. The blackrock was trained and had rooted himself, but the blast was enough to send him off balance mid-bash, allowing Raine'ka to follow-up her swing with a swift plated kick to his leg, sending the armored Orc to his hands and knees before her.

The others turned, their attention drawn from the execution to her instead, the general himself glaring at the masked Orc. " A Frostwolf, alone against the might of the Blackrock? Kill her! " The general shouted with a dark chuckle. Raine gave them little time to advance, swinging her spear around to bash the fallen Orc's exposed head with the sharp tip, effectively knocking him unconscious with a hard enough bash to the temple. She stayed silent, charging closer before spreading her legs out to root herself in a defensive stance, knees bent. The earth at her feet reached up, allowing her to dig her boots into the ground, rooting herself further. The first Blackrock wasted little time in charging the frostwolf, snarling himself as he raised his mace high, only to bring it down at her skull when she made no attempt to move out of his way.

Raine held her ground, gripping her spear with both hands as the mace was brought down towards her skull. She raised her own weapon, held sideways, to block the attack. - The mace came in contact with the hard shaft of the spear just at the end of the handle, inches from the mace's thick metal head. Normally this would do little more than block the incoming attack and damage the spear, but Raine knew what she was doing. The moment the two weapons collided the mace's shaft all but shattered from the impact, to the surprise of the orc wielding it. He had no time to recover before the blunt end of the mace suddenly took a turn and flew through the air, bashing into his forehead with a tilt of Raine's head in his direction. The dazed Orc stumbled back, giving Raine the chance to swing her spear around and jab his plated chest with its pointed bottom, blasting his dazed body back with another strong burst of air, which sent him tumbling into the crowd.

The other Orcs were shocked at the sight of one of their weapons being broken by the shaft of a spear, but it only halted their assault for a moment. The next two Orcs rushed in together, one wielding a sharp axe while the other used a mace similar to the first. Raine, still holding her ground in silence, waited for the larger of the two to reach her and swing his massive axe before making a move. She brought her spear up, tilting it and bashing its strong shaft into the sharp edge of the axe, redirecting its strong swing off to her side with a burst of sparks from the contact. But, there was more to the attack than that, something the Orc did not expect. - Not only was his swing deflected, but his axe acted almost as if it had a mind of its own, using the force he put in to the initial blow against him. His swing continued further than planned before twisting upwards, the sharp edge ripping through the flesh and bone of the Orc that had advanced with him, decapitating him in one fatal blow.

One Orc fell dead while the axe-wielder backed away, looking down at his axe in shock. She was turning his own weapon against him.. But his mind was drawn to more pressing matters the moment he heard the loud rip of rope, his gaze lifting from his weapon in time to see Raine, still rooted, swinging her spear and sending a flurry of wind along the still living Frostwolf capitives, tearing through their bonds. Time for an execution.

Kargris looked over his shoulder from the boulder he was laying against, peering over its edge into the clearing below, and sighed at what he saw. All he'd wanted to do was find a portal to Mulgore (even though traveling via magic often gave him a head ache), order some deep fried Kodo ribs from the nice Tauren gal in the inn, and pass out in the grass while stargazing. Theoretically, he could leave, he could shuffle away, and turn a blind eye to the whole spectacle.

However, he knew his pride, his honor, and his sense of duty wouldn't survive the scathing of his conscious. So, he did what any battle hardened Warrior of the Horde would do, one that was an Orc as well. He leapt over the top of the boulder, crashing into the earth below, and let out a bone shaking roar. The fourteen - well, fifteen if you counted the big one - Blackrock Orcs turned with wide eyes. Only seven prisoners left. He'd have to make quick work of this if he wanted to be back in Mulgore in time for the ribs to come out of the oil. What? He liked his food, and anyone who tried to spoil it for him could kiss his green !@#.

Kargris pulled his blade off his back, taking several steps backwards as at least seven of them advanced, and realized quite quickly that charging in without a plan might not have been the best idea. Ah hell... He put his sword on his back and ran. Yes, that was right, he - a battle scarred, hardened, and strong willed Orc of the Horde - ran like an idiot across the clearing cursing under his breath, and scrambled around a corner. The ease with which he clocked the first two Blackrock Orcs, decapitating them in one blow, was almost pathetic. What was even more pathetic was him continuing his jog around the camp, and picking up a bottle of boiling oil to throw on three others. Right, he'd taken down five. Five? Yes, that meant nine left as he watched the oil sizzle, and eat away at the flesh of their faces. He shook his head, his nose wrinkling, and snorted at the smell.

"Oops." He turned his head, frowning when the remaining eight goons came barreling around the corner, and stared. They snarled, growled, roared, and foamed at the mouth at the sight of their four dead comrades. Kargris, to his credit, simply smiled, and waved... Before bolting around the side of a caravan. Bad idea, bad idea... He checked his watch. Damnit, twenty minutes! Why did he have to be so honorable? He knew he should have been a Rogue! At least he could have smoke bombed and shanked all these idiots to death if he was!

The red haired Orc dove through a thicket of three grey skinned Blackrocks, their blades scraping over his armor, but none of them managed to penetrate. He kicked himself back to standing, bringing his sword out in one move, and parried a quick succession of strikes from all sides. Kargris felt one of them nick the mail under the plate of his shoulder, slicing through, and he grit his teeth in pain. Two to the right of him leapt and he chose to make a move that would either doom or save him. He rolled backwards, slicing upwards, and split open both of their stomachs. Their intestine splattered in the dirt, blood spraying over his face, and he faced the last seven of them.

They looked nervous almost, their eyes darting between each other, shifting stances, and weapons still bared. Kargris wiped the sweat from his face, frowning at the feel of the sticky grime from the dirt. He flicked off the debris, rolling his shoulders, and smirked. "Who's next?" A shadow loomed over the men before him, causing them to make room, and the general stepped forward with a snarl.

"You killed seven of my best men."

"If those were your best this is going to be easier than I thought."

"Stand and fight coward!" He practically screeched. Kargris rubbed at his ear in annoyance.

"$%^- off, I'm not in the mood."

By the end of the night he was sitting cross legged in Bloodhoof Village with a very cold platter of ribs, his armor dented, and gouged. The mail would need to be restrung, the grill of his helm screwed back in, and his sword needed to be sharpened. The Blackrock General's tusks, however, had been added to the necklace around his neck. He grumbled, munching softly, and started bandaging a deep cut on his arm. Oh well, there was always tomorrow...

Grel'kar, having watched this scenario from afar, considered it with a slight narrow of the eyes. His lips twitched back, baring a fang-displaying sneer in the direction of the slaughter. His gaze settled on what had appeared to have been the commander, giving the distant figure an idle once over. Simultaneously, his right hand adjusted it's grip on the simple steel spear he wielded, his left hand idly padding to assure that his axe remained strapped just over his left shoulder, and that his twin knives where still at the back of his belts.

"Lok'tar Ogar." The monk huffed under his breath, and with one last quick check, broke out into a sprint.

The position he'd picked had kept him just out of view from the group, which now seemed fully engrossed in their slaughter. His sprint was sounded off with a dull smack of his boots against dirt, but was certainly not enough noise to overpower the jeers of the attackers and the ever distinct CRACK of metal and the SPLAT of brain content. Hefting up his spear mid sprint, he threw the spear towards the general's head.

His belief in the dream that drove him to the acts that would forever brand him as "The Fang of Dranosh-Ar" still burned as brightly as they did in the beginning.

This went against those dreams, those ideals which he held so dear. As Kurtog watched silently while the Blackrocks cut down the helpless Frostwolf Orcs one by one, he felt his stomach churn uncomfortably. Orcs killing orcs, it was counter-intuitive. He understood the necessity of it, in wartime, but not like this. If these were men, or even elves, Kurtog might have never even spared them a thought, but these were Orcs! Give them axes and let them die with honor! His mind screamed out in tandem with each blow of a hammer or mace against Orcish flesh.

His warchief had brought them here, he had promised a new dawn for his people, and he had signed it all in an oath. The oath of every warchief. "Blood and Honor." He growled out once again, the timber of his voice raising ever so slightly, though it was still drowned out by the laughter of the executioners. The hunter's mind was at war with itself. He had walked through fire and iron, braved the dangers of armies and an impossible war, all on the back of a belief which was inspired by a single Orc. His Warchief.

"Not my Warchief..."

He heard himself say the words, and the levy broke. The Blackrock orcs were setting themselves upon one of the few remaining prisoners, a den mother, when suddenly there was a soft click and one of the Blackrocks dropped in a heap. The rest jerked their heads in unison to the hunter, standing alone with his crossbow trained. Another click, and a second body crumpled to the dirt. Then, chaos erupted as, with their attentions occupied by the turncoat hunter, the Blackrock unit had no defense as the boar came crashing into them like cannon fire.

With that, it was but a quick-step in and a powerful thrust to send the orc to his grave.

Then his path opened, and his eyes settled on the leader of this little warband. He saw the Orc's grip tighten on his warhammer, and saw the fear and anger mingling in his eyes. "Traitor!" He spat. "You turn against your own, against the Iron Horde?! Against your Warchief?!" To this, a slow growl began to emanate from the Hunter, one that set the Blackrock leader back on his heels. "...This." He pointed the blade of his spear at the Frostwolf bodies laid low in the dirt. "...This is not my Horde." He growled, jerking the blade of his spear back to face the Orc as he stalked in for the duel. "...And Hellscream...Is not my Warchief..."
