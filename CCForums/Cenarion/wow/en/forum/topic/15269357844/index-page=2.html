Pressing Onward - Draenor so far

Months prior, a fraction of the Phoenix Guard's total forces led by both Commander Dawnblade and Lt. Commander Ronaestrider pushed through the Dark Portal to take the war to the Iron Horde upon the savage world of Draenor. Difficulties beset upon them immediately as injuries were commonplace, dangers were everywhere, and difficulty was not a word but a way of life. They battled their way to Frostfire Ridge, establishing a makeshift base and digging their feet into the dangerous climates of the ice-covered region.

Meanwhile, those remaining in Quel'Thalas did their best to reconnect with their inter-dimensional comrades. Led by Captain Dawnstar, the Guard's forces found assistance with Mechanist Zeke and Magister Runeweaver to establish a portal connecting the garrison in Frostfire with the headquarters on Quel'Danas. The road was dangerous as the materials required took them from Northrend to Pandaria, but the mission had proved successful.

The Guard's first offensive campaign led them to the jungles of Talador where they aligned themselves with the Blood Knight contingents under Lady Liadrin's command. From fighting minions of the Shadow Council, saving their fellow Sin'dorei, working alongside the defenders of Auchindoun, and even butting heads with local ogres, the Phoenix Guard was kept very busy.

Fate would weave its own path, however, as the camp in Talador was destroyed by the machinations of the Shadow Council and the Burning Legion, incinerating the camp and destroying their makeshift outpost. As the surrounding areas were overrun with demons and Shadow Council alike, the Phoenix Guard was forced to make a quick retreat back to their garrison in Frostfire Ridge in order to ensure at least a fraction of relative safety in this dangerous foreign world.

Now back within the snowy plains of Frostfire, the Phoenix Guard tends to the defense of the garrison as well as resupplying themselves with goods from Quel'Thalas via the arcane portal that sits in the center of the outpost. Dangers are everywhere in Draenor and reports continue to pour in from even the farthest reaches of the foreign world as the Iron Horde, the Shadow Council, the Burning Legion, unwelcome natives, and many other threats continue to terrorize those who have traveled once again through the Dark Portal.

Though the next moves for the Phoenix Guard have yet to be revealed, the time is now for the unit to prepare themselves for the inevitable trials of conflict.


Now is a great time for anyone interested in coming aboard as we plan for our next campaign! Head on over to our guild site at phoenixguard.shivtr.com or get in touch with Inathia or Ruthar in-game for more information!

Membership of the Phoenix Guard:

After discussions with our Arakkoa Outcast captive, Haa'tor, the decision has been made to move our current course of action to the south, into the Spires of Arak. During the course of our meeting, Haa'tor has informed us that the artifact we have recovered is a missing piece of the ancient apexis golem known as Mox'iss, a powerful creation that the Adherents have large plans for. Haa'tor has offered to help us form an allegiance with his kind as we move onward to the Spires of Arak.

The Horde has already created an outpost known as Axefall to the north of the territory - it is in our best interest to make our way there and settle in before moving with Haa'tor to associate ourselves with the Outcasts. He claims it is in our best interest to move the artifact out of our garrison to keep it hidden from the Adherents, a sentiment I cannot help but agree with. We will take it and discuss its future with the Outcasts, potentially using it to negotiate if necessary. It seems they are open to an allegiance, at least if Haa'tor is to be believed, so luck seems to be on our side in that regard.

This being said, we will make our move to the Spires this upcoming Saturday evening under the cover of dusk at 7:30pm sharp. I do not know the safety of our route, so be prepared for anything.

Arrival at Axefall

Ruthar trudged around the Horde encampment of Axefall with his hands behind his back and his eyes narrowed into slits. His fel green orbs analyzed the safety of the outpost, scrutinizing the soldiers milling about, making mental notes of structural weaknesses or possible issues but fortunately finding very few. It was clear that the presence of the short yet stoic Ranger Captain did little to ease the feelings of the Horde forces within Axefall, but Ruthar couldn’t care less - the Guard was here to see to the defeat of the Adherents and their fortress of Skyreach, and he would do everything in his power to ensure that came to pass.

“You keep a tight ship here, Taskmaster.” Ruthar removed his helm with a nod to Gornek, the orc in charge of the contingent here at Axefall. “Much obliged for housing our forces.”

The orc smirked a big tusked grin. “For the Horde, Lieutenant Commander - we all do our part.” The large muscled orc planted a closed fist against his thick chest in an honorable salute.

Ruthar returned the gesture as he stood side by side with Gornek, his arms folded over his chest. The sight was certainly one to behold as the tiny Ranger Captain stood next to the orc who was significantly taller and perhaps twice as wide, but Ruthar paid it no attention. His eyes scanned the immediate surroundings as he watched the Phoenix Guard begin to settle into their new temporary base of operations here in the Spires of Arak.

“I have two guards stationed outside the hut for the artifact at all times - it will be safe.” Gornek’s words had a strong finality to them which comforted Ruthar. “We have seen few issues here at the encampment. The surrounding areas, however - well, that’s another story.”

Ruthar nodded, his left hand running over to his right shoulder. The rock that assailed him during their conflict on the way south had slipped his mind completely, replaced with the necessity of getting settled into the camp. He would have to see to it soon - it was sure to be badly bruised at the very least. “We will do what we can to assist, Taskmaster - you have my word.”

Gornek nodded appreciatively. “Gornek, Lieutenant Commander - I’ve never been one for titles.”

Ruthar smirked approvingly at the orc. “Ruthar, then - a pleasure.”

Gornek chuckled roughly and spread his arms wide with a hint of pride. “Welcome to Axefall, Ruthar.”

Calm After the Storm

After an arduous time in the Spires of Arak, the Phoenix Guard has returned to their garrison in Frostfire Ridge after dealing heavy blows to the problematic Adherents. Using their ancient technologies against them in combination with new allies and their own superior sense of combat, the Guard was successful in eliminating the avian god Rukhmar, destroying sun beam technologies, and killing High Sage Viryx within Skyreach itself.

Now, the collective attention of the Phoenix Guard turns inward once again. New tales begin to trickle in from other places around Draenor as Commander Dawnblade and Lieutenant Commander Ronaestrider discuss where to affix their attention. The Iron Horde strongholds in Nagrand continue to pose their own threats while the conflicts of Ashran tear through superheated skies; Tanaan Jungle remains shrouded in mystery while the plant life in Gorgrond continues to terrorize passers-by; even the beautiful landscapes of a very different Shadowmoon Valley hold their own secrets and dangers.

Now that there is some downtime between guild campaigns, the time is right to get on board! Recruitment remains open, so do head over to the guild's site for more information on how to get involved.

The tide of war is beginning to turn; many orcish clans of Draenor have been shattered by the might of the Alliance and Horde’s Vanguard, and the Iron Horde has retreated into the temporary safety of Tanaan Jungle. The Phoenix Guard, fresh from their victory against the Adherents of Rukhmar, have been alerted to an area of concern along the western coast of Nagrand.

Nestled in the shadow of the great Oshu’gun, Ironfist Harbor continues to serve as a stronghold of the Iron Horde, a prominent naval port and supply hub key to their sinister operations. The seizing of the Harbor would deal a heavy blow to the Iron Horde and greatly diminish the Warsong support that is being offered to the Iron Horde by sea.

The problem, however, is getting there. The seas toward Ironfist are perilous, and the jungle pathways are near impassable with Warsong shamans, wolf-riders, infantry, and other dangers lurking within the rolling greenery of Nagrand. Even worse, the sinister activities of the Warsong have put the elements of Draenor itself into unrest around Oshu’gun, causing the land itself to rise up in turmoil.

The Phoenix Guard must cut a path to Ironfist Harbor and take it from the Iron Horde without being cut down themselves, or worse. Final preparations are being made at the garrison in Frostfire as the elite sin’dorei look to march upon Nagrand, but first they must gather supplies and prepare their march back at their former outpost at Retribution Point in Talador on Sunday, June 14, 7:30PM ST.

The last campaign in the Spires of Arak was a huge success, so thank you to all who participated! We look forward to digging in and returning the fight to the Iron Horde directly in Nagrand as well as pressing forth into Tanaan in Patch 6.2 and beyond.

For the Glory of Quel’Thalas!

-Ina’thia & Ruthar
