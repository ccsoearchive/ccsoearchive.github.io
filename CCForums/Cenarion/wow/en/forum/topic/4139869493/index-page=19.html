HOTFIX: Glyph of Vanish no longer will let Subterfuge proc twice. In other words, we get a new glyph slot!

It's also worth looking into whether or not Subterfuge still is the best talent for you considering how high FW uptime is with AoC and the t16 4pc. I'm still suggesting it in the guide since at the very least it is probably better for lower gear levels and is a solid choice no matter what.

EDIT: Quick note on the talent thing: The DPS delta between the various t15 options is quite negligible. This is not to say that the talents don't matter, but that they are well within the margin or error for ShadowCraft. Personally, I have no idea which talents it favors. On one hand, Subterfuge is great for the opener which ShC considers negligible. On the other hand, it might exaggerate the additional FW uptime that Subterfuge grants.

It kinda confuses me that you can't do keybindings since they are through the game menu, but what about your mouse? I can try to tell you what I think for just 1 through =, but especially for sub being able to bind your keyboard and/or mouse buttons/wheel is really useful.

EDIT: I checked out your armory and noticed that you are way over your hit and expertise caps. The max you will ever need of those stats is 7.5%, which is the cap against level 93 mobs and raid bosses. I suggest using ShadowCraft to help you optimize your gear.

Link: shadowcraft.mmo-mumble.com

Put in your character name and realm, make sure you are in the right spec, and click "Auto-Reforge All". You can also have it optimize your gems. Keep in mind this tool is specifically for raiding, though I recommend it for PvE gearing in general.

EDIT 2: Other tips:

I don't know what your gold situation is, but getting Dancing Steel on your weapons (at the very least your MH) will significantly increase your DPS.

Check out the glyphs section of this guide, there are a few that might come in handy, most notably Hemorrhaging Veins.

When you get the valor, upgrade your MH to 2/2.

Hey, sorry it has taken me so long to reply. Been a busy weekend what with school ending and my brother graduating. I'm just going to reply paragraph by paragraph.

05/18/2014 10:40 AMPosted by Vallagard
Based on the current (alpha) perks that we are going to get as a class, do you think the some of the perks given to sub rogues are solid enough? Having ambush, hemo and backstab do more dmg, a shorter CD on Vanish and more up-time on SD are great. The fact that FoK gives 1 CP per enemy struck implies that in fights with lots of adds, the CP generation is insane.

Interesting question. I talked specifically about each perk when they came out in my blog (link: http://theshadowydancer.wordpress.com/2014/04/04/warlords-alpha-first-impressions/ ) but I'll give you the short version here.

It's hard to know how much to read into the perks. If in 6.0 Subtlety is unchanged except for these perks, then we'll be getting a lot more active damage at 100 via the Ambush, Hemo, and BS perks, which would be cool.

I'm pretty ambivalent about the Vanish and ShD perks, since especially combined with readiness (CDR stat) our FW uptime is looking to be quite high. Personally, I like the uptime throughout MoP, since it hit a sweet-spot between being a very noticeable DPS increase and continuing to be a bonus (rather than something like SV, where the expectation is incredibly high uptime). With my 2/2 upgraded Flex AoC uptime feels a bit high, but considering it is the end of the xpac I think that is ideal.

I can't wait to see how the FoK one plays out. If it is balanced, it will be so much FUN!

05/18/2014 10:40 AMPosted by Vallagard
However, is the 5 less energy for Evis that much of a boost, when combat gets 20% more dmg on Eviscerate and envenom gets two perks? Although the final equations for the main finishers (Evis and Rupture) are under continuous changes, it appears that these two abilities remain pretty much the same for sub rogues. What is your take on this, given that SV was decreased to 10% increased dmg instead of the current values?

First, some clarification: We have no idea what SV will be. Damage tuning has not begun and the SV change was not in the patch notes, which means it can and should be completely ignored.

I think comparing perks cross-spec is a fallacy. To me, the only significance of this perks is that I might be able to fit more Eviscerates into a ShD or Stealth window than before, which I suppose is pretty nice. With regards to the other specs getting more powerful finisher boosts, Sub already has hard hitting Eviscerates due to its mastery and already has a lot of active damage, which means further increase to active damage is less needed. As such, I'd say it only makes sense that the other specs get more noticeable buffs to the active portions of their rotations.

05/18/2014 10:40 AMPosted by Vallagard
Also, isn't that "Enhanced Premed" restricting the freedom of use of the 2 CP from the original premed? Shouldn't the "Glyph of Hemorrhaging Veins" be baked into SV, as it was during some part of Cata (or MoP, I cannot remember exactly).

The Preparation perk is bad and Bizzard knows this and is coming up with solutions. Like the HaT one, they wanted this to be a QoL improvement. Unlike the HaT one, they failed. We'll see what happens with it.

Evidently Glyph of HV is not viewed as required since it is technically not useful on every fight. This, IMO, is silly, since even in a Patchwerk style fight it offers redundancy so that if Rupture falls off you don't lose SV. I suppose I don't mind the glyph, although it would be nice to have that glyph slot for something funner like Deadly Momentum. Personally I don't care whether or not Hemorrhage applies SV.

05/18/2014 10:40 AMPosted by Vallagard
Currently, I think that we are the most active of the rogue specs, with the majority of our damage coming from special abilities instead of auto attacks and passives (poisons/main gauche). The trend seems to be maintained in the new build and with the changes to HaT, does this might imply the return of crit chance as a clear stat to aim for, even with the removal of reforging?

Interesting. I hope not, because I like both haste and mastery more and think that readiness will be pretty fun too, but that's not a real answer. Blizzard has often stated that in Warlords secondary stats will be much closer together in value than in previous expansions. Since we don't have the full picture and even if we did I have no confidence in a human's ability to determine stat priority and EP values (http://theshadowydancer.wordpress.com/2013/11/16/the-stats-connundrum/comment-page-1/ ), I am going to have to trust Blizzard on this one and say that they should all be pretty close.

05/18/2014 10:40 AMPosted by Vallagard
Lastly, the rogue class has to be the one that remains the closest to its actual state, which might be a good thing. However, we still have the positional requirements, which might make the spec undesirable depending on fight mechanics and with the added fact that we are one of the classes that the brings the less utility into a raid, what is your take on the future of sub rogues (PvE) in WoD?

Oof, that's a good question, and probably deserves its own blog post. But I'm not going to write a blog post right now.

First off, I think Subtlety will remain the underdog spec. This is partially because of positional requirements, partially because of its difficulty, and partially because it has always been considered less viable than the others, which causes fewer people to play it, which means fewer great parses, which reaffirms its supposed inferiority. I don't mind this actually, since it means that people are all the more surprised when I kick their butts on the meters. Of course, there have been times when I have not been nearly so zen about this (http://theshadowydancer.wordpress.com/2014/04/09/backstab-positional-requirements-but-seriously/ ).

The changes to our AoE will help a lot with our ability to deal with many adds, especially combined with how Crimson Tempest bleed will now be preserved upon refreshes. I for one am super excited to single target a boss using FoK as my generator because I am surrounded by adds. While Backstab is keeping some positional requirement, the lessening of them on BS and the removal of them on Ambush will go very far towards making Subtlety competitive on a much wider range of encounters.

In terms of actual viability of the spec, there is no question that for all levels of raiding besides mythic (currently heroic) progression raiding, all specs will remain viable. In terms of mythic progression raiding, I think it is fair to say that even if fewer people play Subtlety, a good Subtlety rogue will not be asked to change spec for the vast majority of encounters.
