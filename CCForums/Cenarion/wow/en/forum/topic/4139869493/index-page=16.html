actions+=/pool_resource,for_next=1,extra_amount=45
actions+=/vanish,if=energy>=45&energy<=75&combo_points<=3&buff.shadow_dance.down&buff.master_of_subtlety.down&debuff.find_weakness.down

You actually interpreted the code a bit wrong here. I'm going to assume you understand the conditions on Vanish down, but its important to note the energy restrictions in order to understand the previous line.

What "actions+=/pool_resource,for_next=1,extra_amount=45" says is do whatever is in pool_resource if "for_next" succeeds with a boost of "extra_amount" energy. In the vernacular, this is what is happening:

Line 1: "'pool_resource'? Alright, let's do this! BUT WAIT! I have a 'for_next' clause, which means that I'm only pooling resources if it will allow me to use whatever's on the line below me, so that means we have to check its conditions. Hrm, there's energy as one of the conditions. Usually I would assume that it isn't an issue because I'm pooling for it, but I have this 'exra_amount=45' bit which tells me to act as if I had another 45 energy."

From there, two things could happen.

Case 1: With the boost of 45 energy, the energy conditions are not met (meaning there's either less than 45 energy or greater than 75* with the 45 energy addition). The result is that the conditions for the next line fail, making "for_next" fail, and the line will be skipped.

Case 2: With the boost of 45 energy the energy conditions are met, AND all other conditions for the next line are also met, meaning the next line's conditions succeed, making "for_next" succeed, and thus causing "pool_resource" to happen.

*I'm not actually sure if it checks the upper-bound here, since without an "extra_amount" I believe it will default to 100 (make it no issue), basically semantics but this is a bit advanced and I'll have to ask or check the code myself.

Just some non-scientific observations...
