My opinion is that this is kind of an annoyingly convoluted change (It essentially means you have two classes of RPPM trinket), but that annoyance should mostly be felt by theorycrafters, and when only a few people need to deal it's...ok.

But that doesn't answer your question, so here: I'm pretty sure haste never reduced the ICD of abilities. Most RPPM effects don't actually have an ICD, which is a mechanic that strictly limits the amount of procs/min to a certain amount. In other words, it is literally a hidden cooldown. Rather the direct effect of haste to RPPM procs is to straight-up increase the chance to proc - both by increasing the %chance to proc in the forumula, and by increasing the frequency of attacks which might proc something. The problem area (as Bliz sees it) is where the proc effect also increases the value of haste, which creates double dipping. What they are doing is removing the "haste increases proc chance" part of the formula for the procs that exhibit double dipping. Obviously removing that will reduce the value of haste if you have one of those trinkets (Or, possibly, Dancing Steel too?), but it's hard to say whether or not that will be enough to change our stat priority.

So basically the answer that it is confusing and you should in the end just check out the new weights ShC/theorycrafters give you after they work out the math.

Hailsan, I think it's actually the opposite: This latest change is not meant to reduce the effect of haste on proc chance. The "double dip" is, I believe, referring to procs that are themselves enhanced by haste. The example that was given was a proc that gives a big Agility bonus for several seconds. Since Agility makes our attacks hit harder, and more haste means more attacks, a proc like that inadvertently makes haste more valuable than they'd intended.

I don't think this is doing anything to create two "classes" of RPPM proc. They're all being treated the same. None of them will be able to benefit twice from haste, is all.

Patch 5.4 Subtlety Write-up

Subtlety-specific notes:

  • T16 is out! That means some really good bonuses that I'll put into one of the theorycrafting sections.
  • Nightstalker now increases damage while stealthed by 50%, up from 25% - But you should still shouldn't be using it.
  • Sanguinary Vein now increases damage by 25% (up from 20%)
  • Eviscerate damage reduced by 10%.
  • Backstab and Hemo damage increased by 15%.
  • Ambush damage increased by 12%.
  • Fan of Knives damage increased by 25%.
  • Glyph of Sap replaced by Glyph of Hemorrhaging Veins: Allows Hemorrhage to apply the SV damage increase (This is game changing).
  • Glyph of Crippling Poison replaced with Glyph of Sharpened Knives, which makes FoK apply the Sunder Armor debuff.
  • Glyph of Adrenaline Rush was made baseline, now we get Glyph of Redirect, which reduces the cooldown of Redirect by 50 seconds (down to 10 seconds).


***Note the last minute buff to Ambush***

Analysis:

Subtlety is getting buffed a lot, which will make it much more competitive in every type of situation. While it will still be behind in terms of AoE, it should be a lot easier to match the single target output of the other specs even with positional issues for small portions of the fight. The addition of Glyph of Hemorrhaging Veins and Glyph of Redirect will make Subtlety much better at switching targets, though how target swapping compares to other specs remains unknown. The buffs to FoK and SV will also help bring our AoE closer to that of the other spec's but it is still looking quite weak.

All in all this is a happy day if you want to play Subtlety in Siege of Orgrimmar. While we don't yet know what the best spec will be, Sub is definitely worth a try, as it should be strong on any fight without crucial AoE or long-lasting times where we must face our target.

EDIT: According to Fierydemise, the single target dps increase for Subtlety should be 7% if you don't have the t15 4pc, 5% if you do. (Source: https://twitter.com/Fierydemise/status/377434010105831425)

While applying Rupture is certainly less urgent if SV is already applied, it is still a definite DPS increase over Eviscerate. Rupture fait effectivement plus de damage mais l'écart est globalement faible (entre override le Garrote avec Rupture ou spam Eviscerate jusqu'à ce que Garrote s'apprête à fade). La seconde option, celle que j'ai jusqu'ici priorisé, m'a en fait toujours paru plus logique, et plus 'harmonisée' avec le 4pt15 et son bonus caché. According to google (Or is it defaulting to Bing? Eww...) this means:
[Rupture] actually does more damage but the difference is generally low (override the Rupture or Garrote with spam Eviscerate until Garrote is about to fade). The second option, that I have so far prioritized, I actually always seemed more logical and more harmonized 'with 4pt15 and hidden bonuses.
