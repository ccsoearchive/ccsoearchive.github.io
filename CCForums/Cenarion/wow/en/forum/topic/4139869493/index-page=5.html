There are a few options for opening, though I think using a 2 CP SnD is...promising. Ambush does a fair amount more damage than Garrote, so we'd only open with Garrote if we are not going to get a rupture up soon. No matter what, we want to end our subterfuge with an ambush to increase FW uptime. I'm actually planning on doing some tests for the opener once I get a chance (hopefully I'll get to it in the few hours I have now), but as I see it we have four options.

Prem>SnD>Openers>5CP Rupture (first opener might be Garrote)
Prem>SnD>Opener>any CP rupture (opener here is def Ambush)
Prem>Ambush>Rupture>Opener(s?)>SnD
Prem>opener>SnD>opener(s?)>Rupture (first opener would probably be Garrote)

Which one is best will depend partially on how many GCDs we can fit into this sequence (I actually don't know), and how much of a loss it is to have less than 5 CP finishers, as well as which is straight up best.

Starting with 100 energy and SnD running (8 energy per 2 secs) and assuming 20% haste the energy gained should be around 12 energy/sec.

So in 3 secs you should get: 100 + 36 energy (haste) + 16 energy (SnD) = 152 energy.

Cheap shot = 40 energy.
Garrote = 45 energy.
Ambush = 60 energy.

Garrote (45) > Ambush (60) > Ambush (60) = 165 energy. Not possible.

Cheap Shot (40) > Garrote (45) > Ambush (60) = 145 energy. Totally doable. And after this, you end up with 4-5 CPs available. Although, not viable for bosses or mobs immune to CC.

09/02/2012 09:15 PMPosted by Willikins
My thinking was that in a single point comparison between rupture versus an eviscerate to get equivalent damage you'd have to successfully crit 8 times for the rupture ticks and only crit once for an eviscerate... however if you look at that over time then it'll be evened out won't it?


Yeah. Say over the course of a raid you use 100 eviscerates and get 1000 ticks of rupture (these are probably very very incomplete). The percentage of times both rupture and eviscerate crit will probably be your crit percentage, plus or minus maybe 5 crits. The plus or minus isn't (necessarily) plus or minus 5%, and more importantly the variation will on average be the same for eviscerate and rupture.

So, say we have a 50% chance to crit: Through the course of the raid you had 45-55% of eviscerates crit and 49.5-50.5% of rupture ticks crit. Of course it is as likely that you will be above 50% as below 50%, so you can say both have 50% crit. That means when you are calculating the damage of the ability you get (non-crit damage)*(2(crit percent)), or the damage of a non crit multiplied by the crit damage bonus times the percentage crit.
