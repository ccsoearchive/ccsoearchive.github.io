We could absolutely make it so limiting that it won't place you into a game until X, Y, and Z standards are equal and you could sit in queue for ... maybe ever, but as you still receive rewards for losing, and due to the (intended) quick nature of games, we don't feel that makes sense for how battlegrounds flow.


I completely and totally understand this, however I have one itsy bitsy issue. :)

I don't have Cata on this account and don't foresee my getting it anytime soon due to real life issues. Onmy here is my bg toon and that means I'm stuck at lvl 80 bgs. Now I don't mind getting pounded and dying to those whose gear and skill levels I can compete with/against. That's just a matter of me needing to become more clever than they are. However, what I do object to is getting pounded and dying due to toons whose gear and skill level I can't compete with. In other words if they are wearing lvl 81+ gear and have lvl 81+ skills and above I'm toast with no hope of an ability to compete against them. I know this an xpac issue. But for those of us who don't have the ability to get the newest and best xpac anytime soon, I offer this suggestion. LvL 60, 70, and 80 (and perhaps soon to be lvl 85?) bg breaks. You get to move up into the next bg tier when you level up to 10+1 (or 85+1 if it comes to that) once you hit 60. That way those who don't have the next xpac (for whatever reason - for most of us it's rl financial needs outweighing our gaming wants) like myself, we can compete, have fun, and enjoy what we have and look forward to what is to come rather than feeling these toons we've loved to play have to be retired.

The reason I suggest this is because the ability to keep those of us who don't have Cata out of Cata content areas and abilities are possible (ie: flying mounts in Azeroth, not able to go into the new Cata content areas, can't access archeology, can't level profs past 450, etc). I admit I don't know how much of tweak this would require but it's a thought, a suggestion, for the devs to chew on and consider.
