I have thought about that, removing raiding gear will solve so many problems that exist in the game.

First off Blizzard wont have to worry about how the stats affect the talents and abilities throughout the entire expansion anymore. Everything is set at the beginning and all they need to do is balance the fights around those stats and make those fights harder as expansion progresses and new raids are added to it. I read somewhere that in the Firelands raids there are as many as 5-6 times more triggers per raid boss that the number of the triggers for bosses from the expansion starting raids - there is the challenge right there.

People will no longer complain that their hard work to get a certain gear tier is now worth less since old raids were nerfed and better gear is available from the latest raid. If all stats are the same killing Cho'gal at the start of the expansion will be as hard as killing it at the end of it, except for those who have learn the fight - their is the constant challenge, plus it feels more realistic, I very disliked the fact that Heroics could be run through, literally, with the latest gear upgrades, after some point knowing the fights become irrelevant as gear carries you through them, skill and knowledge become second hand players.

Each raid though will provide its own gear sets, that has different looks but same stats. So after killing that raid's bosses certain amount of time players can purchase the sets and parade in town - "Look at me I have done this and this that many times and this is how I have earned my gear, no random drops, no BS, just hard work"
