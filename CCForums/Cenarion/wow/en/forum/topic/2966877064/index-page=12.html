We think battlegrounds work best when people are able to get into them quickly.

While the matchmaker starts out being stringent it fairly quickly loosens up its search requirements to get people into games within a reasonable amount of time. We could absolutely make it so limiting that it won't place you into a game until X, Y, and Z standards are equal and you could sit in queue for ... maybe ever, but as you still receive rewards for losing, and due to the (intended) quick nature of games, we don't feel that makes sense for how battlegrounds flow.

Getting people in, playing the game, win or lose, and then quickly into the next match is what most players tend to want - and not long queue times or drawn out matches.

I think the initial poster's comments were way too abrasive. He was complaining that people in the BG would not listen to him... I wonder why... if he approaches things like that and focuses on gear score.

I do enjoy random battleground as a way to get honor points... as a means to an end... to do rated BGs and arenas. For those that enjoy merely the random battlegrounds, I would probably try to queue with friends and have one or two be healer(s).

In general, it does seem that the main limiting factor on wins is the number of healers in the battleground. Even so, there is skill involved and some level of cooperation.

From a practical standpoint, the one thing blizzard can control is keeping the queue times low... they can't control the quality of the players involved in terms of level of cooperation, skill with class, and skill in the battleground. Overall, they can control the gear level indirectly by the manner in which honor is distributed.

On some level, I have accepted the system. I have several alts. Sometimes I will play on one alt until I win a random BG that day, then switch to another alt. That way, I'm maximizing my honor points in the time I have to play the game. By losing several in a row before winning, I am actually gaining considerable more honor. Also, it would be hard to say that I learn nothing in these bg's.

Often, I will make suggestions or even flat out directions -- and people generally respond -- it's all in the approach. If you come across abrasive or talk about gear score, then you will lose credibility and people will see that as a maturity issue on your part. There are leadership resources out there.... there are definitely ways to influence people. That being said, even with a skilled BG leader who is able to take charge... there are still some situations that are really hard to overcome. The smaller the BG... the easier it is to control and influence the outcome, though. And always, scan for afk'ers. That is a HUGE part of winning or losing.
