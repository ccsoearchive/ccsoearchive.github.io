This post got a bit long; TL,DR is at the end.

Part of the appeal of PvP in this game is that when the battlegrounds are constructed well, the game is rarely the same. I'm not trying to belittle PvE (as I raid as well) but its fun to participate in a very fluid environment. You see the loading screen and recognize the BG you're going to be in. You may have played a particular map for years, yet you do not know how the "story" will unfold, only what needs to be done to win.

Take WSG/TP: such a simple concept of capture the flag. Yet the experience can be wildly different depending on your team's makeup (# of healers), the enemy team's makeup, and the strategy they follow (i.e. do they play defensively and keep lots in their FR or offensively and zerg your FR). There are other factors as well of course: gear levels, skill levels, cooperation, etc.

Or AB/BfG: Which nodes do you go for first (believe it or not there isn't one "correct answer"), how many of your team play defense, how well does each team communicate, etc.

EotS: Do you play for 3 nodes or 2 nodes + flag. Team makeup, level of communication, coordination, etc.

Even in the flavor-of-the-month BG to pick on, IoC: From game to game the differences may be slim (Alliance tends to rush docks, Horde takes hangar & WS) yet over time the PuG strategies have changed. I remember when Horde took docks and Alliance always went hangar. The sameness of each game is more due to an ingrained "we do this because we always do" mentality than due to a flaw in the BG. And there are X factors such as the quarry and refinery, even if half the team doesn't know what they do..... but I digress.

But AV: With a few exceptions, AV plays more scripted than a typical raid boss encounter. I see the AV loading screen and the only question I ask myself is, will I try and hold IW/SH bunkers until they burn or will I run all the way north to give our team a head start on the DB bunkers? I rarely even consider defense because I don't feel like being a lamb at the slaughter.

AV has become stale. And in PvP (or what little PvP is left in that place) when a BG becomes stale it becomes boring, repetitive, and uninteresting. There's no mystery in what's going to happen. Everyone who has been in there knows the script. Its important for BGs to retain a level of unpredictability. As a Blue post correctly pointed out, constructing a BG is an arduous affair. PvPers will get less content than PvEers for that reason, and it is understandable. Therefore the goal is/should be to make it last by allowing each iteration of it to evolve in different ways. A single BG should be like a snowflake, no 2 games are alike.

TL,DR: Except for AV, all other BGs (even IoC to an extent) have a degree of unpredictability in them. No two games will be played the same on the same map. AV in its modern form does not, and that staleness needs to be addressed.

Edit: Apparently I forgot about Strand of the Ancients. Ummm, yeah that's gotten a bit stale too, but its not nearly as much of a disappointment as AV has, IMHO. As in, it hasn't deviated away from how it was when it launched too much.
