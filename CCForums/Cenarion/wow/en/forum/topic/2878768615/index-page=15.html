My sentiments exactly. Anyone who writes code and has halfway decent habits keeps old versions in case you need to revert a change (I program simulations in RL and do this all the time). It must be easier to hunt through old iterations of AV to reorganize the BG than it would be to create a whole new BG out of nothing.

Besides, as I noted in an earlier post (not sure if it was this thread or another one) battlegrounds should be somewhat timeless. Unlike PvE where content is dished out for use only until the next raid, PvP battlegrounds persist even as new and shinier gear is introduced. Therefore, while AV may be old, it is still quite relevant as all BGs can be played above the player level they were introduced at. Blizzard Devs and CMs should not be comparing a BG to a raid instance in any cost/benefit analysis (as I fear they do). Even if it took twice as many man-hours to fix up AV as it would to create the 4.3 raid (which I highly doubt, but w/e), it still would be a worthwhile venture because while the 4.3 raid may be current for perhaps as long as 6 months, a shiny, refurbished AV would be played for as long as WoW is still around.
