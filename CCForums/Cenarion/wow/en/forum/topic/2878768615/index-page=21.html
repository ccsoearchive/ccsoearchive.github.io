I like some of the ideas, but others I think are ill-advised. Changing Balinda, for example, to allow reasonable defense and prevent line of sight issues and the like are good changes, but removing reinforcements or creating a 3-hour raid-like battleground I don't think are good ideas.

Reinforcements force an end-game condition on the battle that I think is sorely needed. I do agree that making the battleground more epic and less a zerg-fest is good, but the reinforcements give a good incentive for defending towers and bunkers as well as give a goal when the other team is hunkered down in defense. It needs to stay.

I also think pvp should have a fast-paced feel to it, so trying to arbitrarily draw out some of timers is a bad idea, imo. Having certain points be critical to defend and throw back attackers is good, but not every. single. tower. That's just excessive. I think a game where 30 minutes is considered 'fast' would be an adequately epic battleground.

creating a 3-hour raid-like battleground I don't think are good ideas.

Reinforcements force an end-game condition on the battle that I think is sorely needed.


This is the role that summoned NPC's used to fill. Can't make your way into Tower Point for a cap? Send the cavalry through. Alliance bridge too heavily defended? Lokholar will lead you straight to Dun Baldar.

As the game progresses, the tools available to players increase in power until one side gains a decided advantage, in a way that feels direct and natural.

For example:

"Oh, @#$!, Ivus is coming! Quick, fight harder!"

As compared to:

"We're down 500 to 350, just let them kill Drek already!"

Reinforcements are something that happens far away, to somebody else. You can hardly see the effect you are having on that number. Buffing up your NPC defenders to level 86 so they can defend graveyards while you go on the offensive is something both subtle and tangible that lets you feel like you personally could end up carrying the day. Because you just might.

I do agree that making the battleground more epic and less a zerg-fest is good, but the reinforcements give a good incentive for defending towers and bunkers as well as give a goal when the other team is hunkered down in defense. It needs to stay.


Such an incentive was included in the original version of AV - If you let the enemy capture your bunker, not only do you lose a Marshall, but they would gain an extra Warmaster! For example, if Horde captures Dun Baldar North, they get a Dun Baldar North Warmaster.

Again, if the enemy is hunkered down in defense, there are four different kinds of summonable NPC's that can smash through those defenses.

I also think pvp should have a fast-paced feel to it, so trying to arbitrarily draw out some of timers is a bad idea, imo. Having certain points be critical to defend and throw back attackers is good, but not every. single. tower. That's just excessive. I think a game where 30 minutes is considered 'fast' would be an adequately epic battleground.


Then you are perfectly welcome to enjoy such 20-40-minute battlegrounds as every other battleground in the game. There are a lot of us who want the PvP equivalent of a raid. In Classic AV, the "Zerg Vanndar" strategy required incredible teamwork and coordination, but it was doable. You could still play a 10-minute AV, but it would be a rare exception.

Classic AV was still a race, but it was a race to get back to the front lines, a race to gather resources, a race to summon the first cavalry assault. The point of creating a strong defense was so you could unleash an unstoppable, coordinated beatdown.

Note that we are asking for AV to be removed from the random queue, with the idea that players queueing for AV intend to play AV for an hour or two.
