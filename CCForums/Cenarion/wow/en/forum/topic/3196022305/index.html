Greetings Direct Damage. Wanted to see if anyone shared or noticed the same concern I brought up in one of the other threads.

As we saw yesterday in a GC post, melee will be receiving a zone wide melee buff. Now, I'm not stating that this isn't needed. It just brought up a growing concern in regards to the Hunter class.

Currently, Marksmanship is sitting at middle of the pack. MM is the highest scaling of our 3 specs, but even then it is appearing to be outscaled by most classes and specs, as apparent by it's current position and the fast that it has stalled in damage output while others below it grow at decently fast levels (Enh and Frost DK closing in rapidly.)

Going into 4.3, we'll see many casters with Dragonwrath, something that boosts the impact of your ranged DPS but not Hunters. Most melee are merely a stones throw of equaliing or surpassing Marksmanship in damage potential and now are receiving a buff to keep them competitive with the caster dps.

Now my question is, where does this put Hunters? If our best spec is merely midpack behind most casters with the gap widening due to Dragonwrath, and will be passed by melee post buff, does this mean all 3 specs of Hunters will be at low-mid pack or bottom?

Also, what about the possible effects of this on pets? It could be a temporary solution to the Beast Mastery woes if allowed.

Hunters are in an odd position, being the only Ranged Physical DPS class, as well as relying on a Melee Pet.
I can't say what MM and SVs problems are, because i don't regularly use them.
But I think what we're seeing here is that the Devs are simply unsure of how to fix our PvE DPS without screwing PvP up.

Casters having Dragonwrath and Melee getting an AP buff leaves Hunters in a strange position. We're not Casters, but we're not Melee. So which numbers nob can they fiddle with to bring us up to par? It's a difficult question. If they just gave us an AP boost like Melee, suddenly our damage shoots up because AP for us is AP for our pets, and now we're double dipping. Its fine for Melee, more or less, since they don't usually use pets as a main source of DPS (Unholy DKs aside. But don't they need help anyways?)

I think the Hunter community would be better served in discussing the *why* of our poor performance, rather than simply pointing at the Devs and saying *fix it*.
Is it scaling?
Is it poor stat -> Pet stat conversion?
Is it our ability base damage?
Is it a flaw in Focus?
Is it a flaw in our Rotation design?
Do we not have our Pets doing enough for us?
I'd like to see some proper discussion on these sorts of things. For my own curiosity, as well as for the goal of improving Hunter DPS across the board. Having all 3 specs at comparable Raid DPS would be amazing.

Beast Mastery and Survival has extremely poor scaling, both in relation to Marksmanship and even other classes. Marksmanship has mediocre scaling in relation to most classes as well, so not a whole lot better.

Beast and Survival rely heavily on just AP and less on weapon damage. Beast uses quite a bit of Arcane Shots yes which scale from weapon damage, but Kill Command and pets in general have relatively poor RAP scaling values, making only armor upgrades boost them, and by relatively insignificant amounts.

Survival uses near nothing BUT RAP except Cobra Shot. Survival cannot use Arcane Shot as much as MM or BM get to because of being Focus starved a majority of the time. Same boat as Bm where really only armor gains will give you any noticable difference.

Marksmanship is entirely weapon + RAP formulas. Problem is that it brings up an issue that Blizzard noticed in Wrath and that's Hunters still don't scale all that well even with their ranged weaponry. They tried to alleviate that issue in Wrath but stated that they would keep a close eye on it. Problem is that Armor Penetration in Wrath masked this issue and it's rearing its ugly head again.

Even ake a look at our stats. We stack Agility obviously, hit cap, then Crit. From there, Haste and Mastery are both largely worthless except Haste in plateau numbers which upon reaching, makes them both useless again. Look at Survival and Beast Mastery. Survival wants 758 Haste (so easy to reach) then Haste and Mastery are both complete effing trash. Beast Mastery doesn't even have a Haste plateau to reach and has Mastery and Haste as equally crappy period. The only stat Hunters actually care about is Crit.

The end result is Hunters not having the raw damage of ranged casters and having pets hindered by melee mechanics (significantly hurts Beast Mastery) while also not having the scaling melee has, which is obvious when you look at Beast and Surv sitting at the bottom of the pile in DPS.

It causes concerns because casters will largely be running with Dragonwrath next tier due to being able to pump them out like an assembly line, then the added factor of a buff to all melee, when melee are already rivaling the effective power of Marksmanship which is supposed to be our "go to" spec for good numbers and competitive damage.
