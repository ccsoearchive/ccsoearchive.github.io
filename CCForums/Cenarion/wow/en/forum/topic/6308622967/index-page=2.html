Special Adviser's Report - Early evening, sixth night of the last quarter. Verronia Fay

Role of the Special Advisor
I, as a special advisor to the Sentinel Army, am charged with giving guidance on specific matters where a precise body of expertise is needed (in my specific case, on matters related to commerce and trade), and with informing appropriate members of the Sentinel Army and affiliated or allied groups of information that comes to my attention.

Type of Report
The following report is intended to convey information that has come to my attention to appropriate members of the Sentinel army and/or affiliated or allied groups, in this case specifically, this report is intended for use by Warden Kyalin Raintree.

Explanatory Note
Please consider that when taking action, due to my proximity to and involvement in the event to be described that my independence is materially impaired.

Basis for opinion(s)
Keyesh Shadowstorm and I had visited the Feathers of Iron tavern and were, not soon after arriving, verbally accosted at length by one Jalou Nightingale. During the exchange, I verified that Ms. Nightingale was a tavern employee from one of the management representatives who attempted to stop her. Following a long period of continued insults of a crass and obscene nature, Mr. Shadowstorm physically attacked Ms. Nightingale, blasting her into a wall and causing a moderate amount of damage to the property. Ms. Nightingale laughed, and drew her attention immediately to a nearby cartel bruiser. Mr. Shadowstorm noticed this movement as well and immediately removed himself from the room by means of an arcane spell. Following the exchange, I left the tavern as well.

Opinion(s)
Based on the facts described above, I have come to the following conclusions.
1. Management was not complicit in Ms. Nightingale's actions.
2. Ms. Nightingale was attempting to arouse anger in myself and in Mr. Shadowstorm.
3. Mr. Shadowstorm suffered a momentary loss of control as a result of Ms. Nightingale being successful in arousing anger in him.
4. Ms. Nightingale expected Mr. Shadowstorm and/or myself to suffer that momentary loss of control, and as a result, have appropriate action taken against him and/or me on the part of the Steamwheedle Cartel Bruiser.

Recommended Course of Action
Based on opinions listed above and the evidence on which they are based, it is my assessment that Ms. Nightingale is a threat. I do not advise sharing this report with cartel officials, as it is unlikely that they will act upon it without considerable "grease" payments. However, as stated in opinion 1, management was not complicit in Ms. Nightingale's actions, and will likely hand her over to your custody if you should request it.


I declare, under the pain of imprisonment, that the explanations, facts, and representations detailed above are, to the best of my knowledge, representative of the truth and reasonably free from material error, omission, or misstatement.
Signed,
-Verronia Fay

The following is an addendum to the previously issued travel advisory for Darnassian Citizens:

The first case of the activities described in the advisory has been resolved, resulting in the apprehension and subsequent imprisonment of the subject. This is notable because extradition was accomplished through eastern mechanisms of law, demonstrating firmly that nations under statute based systems cannot be used as shelter for committing hostile acts against the Kaldorei.

While I will pursue remaining and future acts of incitement, this notice should not be taken as license to give in to the inciter. If you encounter an individual who is attempting to attack you in this manner, it remains your responsibility to bring the matter to the relevant authorities instead of taking direct action yourself. If you are visiting foreign lands, it is furthermore your responsibility to know the local laws beforehand.

I must finally add that during the aforementioned arbitration, that facts became apparent to support the notion of the belligerent using the law in a nonlethal manner to harm the target. While I had accepted this as a possibility, the original notice erroneously implied an assassination attempt, and I apologize for the lack of clarity.

-Warden Kyalin Raintree

Epilogue:

A tattered notice blew past her face as the sentinels swarmed the open, two-leveled building in the Craftsmen's terrace, but in all of the noise, the target woke. A hairy-black blur flew from the second floor, landing on another rooftop before it bounded off. The worgen was no stranger to narrow escapes, but then, neither was the Warden.

Four hippogryph riders gave chase, and as if on queue, Kalil'shae brought Kyalin airborne as well. The worgen tore into the tradesmen's terrace, weaving through crowds of people that had filled the streets in the early-morning hours. Kyalin knew that the sentinels could get off clear shots, but she had instructed them earlier not to. To them it was the same old nonsense: she wanted to bring him in alive.

The worgen made a sharp right turn. Ordinarily, a wall of roots and branches may have deterred a criminal here, but up them he climbed, using his unnatural advantage to move further into Teldrassil. The Warden's assistance was deterred, the speed of the chase rendering them unable to weave through the branches as she did, her eyes unerring on the target, instead of the two worgen hiding in the branches on either side for but a split second before they pulled a rope taught in front of her.

She caught the rope, or the rope caught her, and perhaps the two worgen who had attempted to stop her didn't consider this just before they found themselves tumbling down through the branches themselves, but it was enough to send the Warden tumbling down as well. She fell into a small clearing, soon joined by her once-prey, and apparently, about fifteen of his friends.

Plucking a smoldering cigar out of his mouth, a well-dressed one took prominence among the pack. She'd seen him before: a former criminal who she had tried to rehabilitate, a man who was never too far away from her scrutiny, or at least she thought. She had been cordial to this man, respectful to this man. She thought she had made progress, clearly she was wrong.

"Do leave her alive, boys." He said, grinning. There was a disconcerting chuckle echoing through the forest. The Warden felt a fear that she hadn't felt in several years, like she had in Nendis when the Myrmidion's trident had cut right through her armor. But it was the bubbling fury that soon took over as her mind raced through dozens of conversations, objections from petulant outlanders, idle threats that would have been met with a slash across the face but a few years ago, bending over backwards to accommodate, to be fair, and of course, the blood covered cell from earlier in the evening.

She gripped the edge of her cloak, allowing the daggers that had poorly - yet intentionally so - been sewn to the hem to flare out, but the warning went unheeded. She whirled around, allowing the knives to detach and fly out in every direction, catching many of the advancing worgen in multiple places as she gripped her umbra crescent for the first arc. Ten seconds later, the gang had been decimated, and she set her eyes on the ringleader.

Never again, she told herself, would outlanders walk over her. There would be no more appeasement to their methods, their customs or their protests of "fairness" or process.

Her eyes locked with those of the terrified worgen at the other end of the clearing as her comrades swept in from the canopy, finally having caught up. Her hand tightened around the grip of her crescent as the worgen nervously drew his own sword. He held it out for a few moments, trembling terribly, but looking as if he were ready to attack.

Kyalin raised a brow. "Ash falah no?"

The gang's leader dropped his sword, raising his hands above his head.

Epilogue II:

From the journal of Jalou Nightingale:

For the act of abusing law for personal gain, and imprisoning me falsely as such, she will pay.
For her horrid acts against me, and for every other man and woman she's wronged the same, she will pay.

I’ve come to reconsider my vow of bloody revenge, or rather, the severity of my actions to come. Unlike some, I know true justice, and shall abide by it. Roughly one year ago, Scary, Hairy, and Sandy, met their ends; two of which at my hands. They paid for their crimes, blood for blood, I reflect, and for this, reconsider the Warden’s punishment.

I had sworn one death per day of unjust imprisonment, as I was taken into her dominion. She desired a criminal, a killer, and I could give her such. If she wished to see the effects of imprisoning an innocent, I would show her. To let her find a pile of gored Darnassians would do nicely; perhaps have them walking again after death, for added effect. Again, though, I have come to reconsider. This course of action would not be direct enough. The Warden herself would remain unharmed, and would not know the true meaning of my actions. There should be no blood spilt but her own.

I await her next actions. This sudden release from imprisonment, for reasons already posed to her, time and time again, by many a person, made no sense. Most likely, she would spread word she had not granted release, but rather, that I had escaped. So be it.

More hopefully, she saw logic at last. I would have escaped before my sentence was complete anyhow, by force, and the longer I would have stayed, the more severe her punishment would have come. Perhaps she came to realize this, and chose to surrender.

My own actions shall not, for they ought not to, be taken immediately. In time, I shall have an appropriate punishment chosen for her crimes, and I shall put it into motion. Perhaps I’ll cut off her precious toes.

I shant have my revenge today. I shant have my revenge tomorrow. In time, however, all shall be made right. I shall suffer no injustice in the false guise of law, and merely shrug it off.

An eye for an eye, an eye for a tooth.
