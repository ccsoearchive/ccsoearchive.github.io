I think part of the problem is there are mixed messages.

Blizzard has made efforts to restore a guild bank when a guild master empties the guild bank, shifts guild leader, and then jets to another guild/server/faction etc. This is done to prevent guilds from having their entire guild bank stolen, thus negating the efforts of the people still in the guild.

However, if the guild master disbands the guild, the guild bank's contents are mailed to the guild master. Not distributed evenly among the guild. That's built into the game itself.

So, if you disband the guild, you get all the items. I haven't seen them restoring the guild and its guild bank to someone else when the guild disbands (nor would I expect Blizzard to become the arbitrator of when a guild is officially finished or not). But if you take all the items and leave the guild in tact, then that's treated as stealing from the guild.

Personally, while I appreciate the effort to attempt to "protect" guild members, I think ultimately the best policy is one of interjecting only in extreme situations. I think in most cases Blizzard doesn't want to be guild masters, which is what they would become if they inserted themselves in every guild dispute. I think they need to be able to step in and quash obvious scams, but in cases where it's not then I don't think it's up to Blizzard to determine which side is right: that's something that should be settled within the guild itself. I think that guild members should understand that the Guild Bank is not an entirely secure entity, and that contribution is not without some risk. The level of risk varies by guild, and should be part of the consideration a player takes into account before joining or remaining in a guild.

Thank you for contacting the World of Warcraft Game Master Department.

After a thorough investigation of the guild bank loss you reported, it appears the member who performed the withdrawal(s) was the legitimate leader of the guild, based on the Guild's founding and promotions. We were unable to locate evidence that his or her leadership arose from circumstances involving fraud or scamming, and as a result, have concluded that the use of the guild's bank was legitimate.

Please be aware that goods contained within a guild bank are managed at the discretion of the Guild Leader. Like all Guild Functions, including Guild Transfers, Faction Changes, membership, permissions, and other administrative functions, management of the Guild Bank is left solely to Guild Leader. As such, the Guild Leader may adjust any or all of these functions at any point. This includes adding or removing goods or adjusting access to the bank.

We encourage you to exercise caution when choosing to deposit into a guild bank. When you choose to contribute to a guild bank, you relinquish your personal claim to any deposited goods and acquiesce to the managerial decisions of the Guild Leader for those goods.

As our investigation did not uncover evidence supporting the use of fraud or bug exploitation to perform the withdrawal(s), we will be unable to provide restorations to the guild bank or recover the reported goods from the member who withdrew them. We apologize for any inconvenience this may cause.

For additional information, please review our Guild Bank withdrawal tips at: http://us.blizzard.com/support/article.xml?locale=en_US&articleId=20459

Thank you for your time.
