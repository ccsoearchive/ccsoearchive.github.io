Gentyl looked up from the reports as the office door opened. The Commander stomped in, shaking the rain from his cloak. "Damnable weather, mi'lady, but the ship lies easy."

He hung up his cloak on a peg near the door and eyed the stack of paperwork. "Shall we order dinner in then?"

She sprinkled sand over her signature to dry the ink then poured it back into the small shell she had picked from a beach on their honeymoon. "If you don't mind, sir. I need to get these action reports done and we have some more to go on the Wanted List."

He glanced through the notes and nodded. "Pang is back? Let me order that dinner and I will write up his notice. Know him well and happy to put him on the list."



Pang, revenant of the order Half Past Twelve. A merciless scamp, his preferred hunting ground remains the city of Stormwind where he has claimed no less than 60 souls, most if not all being young ill-provisioned guards or those untutored in war. He rarely leaves anyone injured, his work is clean and precise. When a defense has been mustered, he can be elusive, especially when working the labyrinth of corridors within the Stormwind walls. Known to leave behind a particular malodourous air that many have associated with vegetable rot. He has no purpose beyond delivering suffering, works under no known directive. Little is known of his order. He is not shy, has no fear of taking a death, and has never been witnessed taking the coward's blue. In the mold of Sadiq and Chaele, names still whispered in Elwynn and Westfall, the rogue Pang is one of our Most Wanted.

Gentyl sank wearily into the chair beside the fire. Steam members are professionals without the rude gestures and coward blue often seen on the field. Approach with blades drawn and expect no quarter.

She stood up at last and began removing the armor. It was too late to warm water for a bath and she was too weary to wait for one, so she simply sponged off from the wash basin and slipped her sleeping gown on. It was pale blue, the color of her eyes, and one of her husband's favorites. She suspected that was because it was also the same color as the sea in the bay where they had honeymooned.
