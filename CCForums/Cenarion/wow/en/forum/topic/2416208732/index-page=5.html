So why does the time invested have to be the same as in PVE? Didn't the point that the attraction to pvp was that less time was required to be invested for those of us who no longer have many many hours to raid.

I used to raid all the way from mc to icc, but since cata I just haven't had the time, I haven't even step foot in a 5 man dungeon at 85 on any of my characters one because I can never be sure I could stay for the entire dungeon and because dungeons don't appeal.

But I could login and earn stuff in arena based on my skill level at a pace which wasn't extremely fast (that's what the damned conquest points per week is for innit?) The appeal of arena was the ease of organisation (don't need to schedule) because you don't have to commit an insane amount of hours, by throwing in a point limit per match you've taken that away. Right now all the combos I play are healer/dps in 2's. Some matches can seemingly go on forever, 20 minutes+ and you can still end up losing them, so you add those games together and assume you win 60% of games and you're looking at many many tiresome hours to the point where I'd get more reward per hour by raiding than doing arena. I'm sorry if I come off as a bit of a whinger here, but I just don't understand (I legitimately mean I don't understand) how this change is beneficial to the game whatsoeve. The change goes against the very thing that makes arena such an attractive part of the game to people who don't have a large amount of time.

I don't want to raid, I prefer pvp but it really looks like it's the easiest way to get a decent amount of pvp gear now.

I rarely ever post on forums these days, but as someone who has played the game for quite a while though, you guys have always done pretty well at keeping things even between pve and pvp but this change I don't think (if that's what anything) is beneficial to my own enjoyment of the game, the same I think could be said for anyone else in my position.
