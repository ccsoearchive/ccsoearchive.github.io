180 points/win isn't that big a deal if your cap is only 1343. In my opinion, complaining about spending an hour of time for an essentially effortlessly gained epic is just whining about the game getting a little tougher--try playing arena back in BC when a 1500 rating got you 300ish points and it took almost a month at that rating to get your epic. And you couldn't teamhop before the week ended or you would lose your points. All things I liked, personally.

To be constructive: why can't the system be changed so that people with higher ratings receive more points per win than those below 1500? That's the real problem I'm seeing--the higher rated players with high caps (not me) are the ones who got the shaft, as they now have to play an inordinate amount of difficult and possibly very lengthy matches to get their point cap. There's no reason they shouldn't get more points per win--at a higher rating, they've earned it in my book.

And finally, this abrupt drop hurts most the people who are doing arenas now and didn't jump in right at the start of the expansion. It's not exactly fair. I understand that it gives more incentive not to team-hop and limits the rapid overpurpling of the realms, but changes like that should only happen at the end of a season. Not everyone starts at the same time, and this change merely slows the pace at which other people can gain equal footing in the competition.

It's not about the time you have to spend, but it is about time for PVPers.

In PVE, you don't general have the bosses gearing faster than you do. Bosses don't change; you gear up for the boss or instance. In PVP generally one side gears faster because on most servers one side has more pvpers than the other side has. On mine, it's horde. On some others, it's Alliance. In PVE you also don't have people faction changing due to the other side gearing faster or being more PVE oriented generally speaking. (Correct me on that point if it's wrong as I am PVP.)

Because PVP DOES have this problem, the side that gears faster is already geared under the old point system. The, let's say, slower to gear side was starting to catch up, but still wasn't there yet. Then suddenly it has been made even more difficult for that side to catch up to the other.

PVP is most fun when both sides are geared equally and the battle or arena match is about skill and strategy and is close and exciting; however, by making this change mid-season for pvp, this isn't the way PVP is. As it is, PVP is a pwnage of one side or the other depending on the server. There may be a few servers that are an exception, but they are few.

The fun of PVP is playing after both sides are geared.

PVE'ers want it to be fair, then I say the boss needs to continuously scale upward as you are trying to gear. Then it would be more like PVP. You just simply cannot make the two the same. PVP an PVE are completely different. Time involved in the gearing should not be equal between PVP and PVE. period.

I am starting to wish they would just completely separate them - no mixing, different servers, etc. That way rebalancing could be separated, speed of gear attainment could be separate, etc.
