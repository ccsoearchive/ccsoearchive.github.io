How would 2 way conversion make sense if the time investment required for each respective currency isn't even close to equivalent? Personally, I dont think they can easily make it equivalent, the problem as I see it is mostly mmr and the time an average match takes.

mmr is designed to get you to the point where you should win 50% of your games, more or less. This means if it takes 20 games to cap points, then on average it will take you about 40 played games to cap. What's average game length, maybe 3-5 minutes currently? I know some can go long, especially in 2s, but that's 200 minutes or so to cap. Let's call it 4 hours.

The odds of you capping valor points in 4 hours are quite low, and that's not even considering the fact that pvp has a dynamic cap as opposed to the static pve cap. In an ideal world, it would be bidirectional, but I can't think of a good way to get there, at least until the effort input is point max is normalized. You can evade the cap issue by just having a 1250/week cap on conversion, but the other issues are a little more problematic.

However, I still have two issues with this:
1) From previous Q&As, Blizz has mentioned that RBGs were not rewarding enough for their effort. How is something that requires more people, arguably higher skilled people (at least on average because most lower skilled, non group oriented people steer away from it), as well as games that last from 3-4x longer, yet the point gains you receive only 2.2x more. If this is your way to encourage RBGs, this makes absolutely no sense. With this change, we'll most likely have to supplement our RBG playstyle with arenas, something most of us have done for 8 seasons that we don't much care for.

2) If you feel that people were getting gear too easy for time investment, that's one thing. Do you however feel that you should disproportionately punish higher rated players with a higher time sink? One of the hallmarks of arena was supposed to be that you could get rewards based on skill, rather than time investment. Of course a team who put in more time would more likely improve to be higher ratings, but now you've sort of forced better players to play more often. If you felt that 5 wins weren't enough, its understandable to increase it to 8 or 10 wins, but why would you not have that scale based on everyone's rating so that its fair for everyone?
