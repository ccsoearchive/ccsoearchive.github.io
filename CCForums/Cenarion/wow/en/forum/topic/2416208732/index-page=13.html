180 points/win isn't that big a deal if your cap is only 1343. In my opinion, complaining about spending an hour of time for an essentially effortlessly gained epic is just whining about the game getting a little tougher--try playing arena back in BC when a 1500 rating got you 300ish points and it took almost a month at that rating to get your epic. And you couldn't teamhop before the week ended or you would lose your points. All things I liked, personally.

To be constructive: why can't the system be changed so that people with higher ratings receive more points per win than those below 1500? That's the real problem I'm seeing--the higher rated players with high caps (not me) are the ones who got the shaft, as they now have to play an inordinate amount of difficult and possibly very lengthy matches to get their point cap. There's no reason they shouldn't get more points per win--at a higher rating, they've earned it in my book.

And finally, this abrupt drop hurts most the people who are doing arenas now and didn't jump in right at the start of the expansion. It's not exactly fair. I understand that it gives more incentive not to team-hop and limits the rapid overpurpling of the realms, but changes like that should only happen at the end of a season. Not everyone starts at the same time, and this change merely slows the pace at which other people can gain equal footing in the competition.
I agree with you on the 180 per win is fair for the lower end cap. The issue began when it first came out it was 135 per win. From there is was why did they not tell us this before so we could of put feedback instead of getting this suprise.

I also agree about when you get higher up they should give you more but then it goes to why don't they make it like 8-10 games per week to cap out so then it will balance itself out that way.

I tried posting this in the customer service forums, but was promptly told by a blue that the only service I was getting was to move my post elsewhere. So here it is: I am a paying customer, and I am currently unsatisfied with this game.

I would like a response to the following question:

Why does Blizzard repeatedly short-change the pvp community in favor of PVE? I am speaking of two things, the conquest point awards being changed and the ability to convert valor points to conquest.

Changing the amount of points awarded for a win was a simply horrible idea, as you can see by the outcry on these forums, arenajunkies, etc. NO ONE likes this idea. For those of us with multiple alts, it will take hours upon hours each week to simply reach point cap. Some of us have lives, jobs, and responsibilities that prevent us from playing WoW 20+ hours a week. In addition, this change being implemented without it being in the patch notes is simply underhanded. I am sorry, but I don't buy the "it was an honest mistake" excuse that is being tossed around by Bashiok and others. Blizzard has been revising these patch notes for months now; it's obvious that it was simply omitted due to the outrage it would cause.

Secondly, why is it that I can now raid...and use my valor points to get pvp gear? Can I arena and use my conquest points for pve gear? No...it's a one way street...I can change valor to conquest, but not the other way around. How is this not a bias? Again, not all of us have time to raid three nights a week; gearing up through arena was a viable(until now) alternative. With the changes to the arena points award it's potentially going to be faster for hardcore raiders to get pvp gear than for arena players. How does this make any sense?

These decisions are simply killing the casual wow players. Not all of us want to kill imaginary dragons, and not all of us have 20+hours a week to devote to a game. I have been a paying customer with(at times) 4 accounts for nearly four years, and I would like some answers.


You are saying they shortchanged pvp with the conquest cap change, when its the exact opposite. If you are truely a dedicated pvper, you will get your conquest cap easily each week. This post you made is exactly why blizzard shortchanges pvp, because you whine and make pvp seem different from pve. If everything were consistent, then that would mean that if you can get the top tier of pvp gear doing only 5-15 arena matchs a week (assuming you arent absolutely horrible) then i should be allowed to do 1-2 heroic a week and then in 6-7 weeks get the top tier of pve gear. If you think thats not the case and you dislike the conquest change, then you are the reason blizzard shortchanges pvp.
