My 2 cents worth.

I think Blizzard is on the right track to accomplish their goals as I understand them.
1. They have trimmed all the fat and fluff from the talent trees. Example, there was no need for Cut to the Chase to be a 3/3 talent.
2. The specializations have consolidated all of the "must have" abilities that focus on DPS.
3. The talent tree now focuses on utility and force us to make some interesting choices.

This should eliminate the "cookie cutter" specs that we have all cherished over the years. Thus, allowing us to make choices based on the "fun factor".

Or will it?

The specializations will have to be VERY VERY close in dps. If so, we get to choose our spec based on person preference. If any one spec out performs the other two, it will become the default spec. Because that's what rogues do.

As for the talents, many of them don't seem to have much impact on PvE and will force other choices by default. For example, Level 45 Tier: Leeching Poison seems to be the practical choice for PvE. Which will make it the default for all three specializations. I can see Killing Spree also becoming the default for PvE. And, as many have expressed, Preparation being a default PvP talent.

It's still early so we will have to wait, at least until beta, to see how this pans outs.
