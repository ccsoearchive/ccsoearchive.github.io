Saying that placing an item into the guild bank means that it is no longer yours isn't exactly accurate. Most players place items into the guild bank as a contribution to the guild as a whole, which includes themselves as well as friends and fellow guildmates. I doubt that there exists a guild with a strict rule where any and all items placed in the guildbank belonging solely to the guildmaster him/herself and will only be used by the guildmaster. In that scenario, I doubt that anyone in the guild actually makes deposits into the guildbank as some of you have suggested. Furthermore, most guilds have levels of access to the guild vault; ours included. It is simply illogical to claim that all items within the guild vault are sole properties of the Guild Master, as different ranks within the guild has access to various tabs in the vault. Technically speaking, the items in the various tabs in the guild vault belong to anyone with access to that tab.

The argument that Selveird and some others have made where if a player places an item into the guild vault, they must realise that it will automatically be a property of the Guild Master's is a fallacy. No member of the guild forfeits an item of their own to aid the Guild Master solely. Rather, no member of the guild is signing a contract whereby they use the Guild Master as a middleman to handle and redistribute their goods and gold by making a deposit into the Guild Bank. Even if it is intended by Blizzard to use the Guild Master as the middleman in this case, it should not be legal by WoW policy for the Guild Master to take all the deposits for his own. As an analogy, if I make a donation towards an Orphanage, I would expect at least a good sum of that donation to be used for the goods and benefits of the children and the foundations of that Orphanage. In contrast, it would be considered a scam if the said owners of that orphanage takes them money for themselves and leaves nothing for the children of the orphanage; a breach of contract; a scam. Guild deposits can also be viewed as investments. I agree with Selveird that at the end of the day, WoW is a videogame intended to provide other players with enjoyment. When I contribute to my guild, ultimately I get out of it guild progression and therefore fun (at least for me). Every copper I donate to a guild I do it for the purpose of obtaining fun and enjoyment in the long run. However, having the GM ninja the guild bank undermines a majority of my investments made towards the guild. It's not exactly legal IRL for a bank or an organisation to take the money you've invested without giving you anything in return, and I don't see how it should be okay in WoW.

Ninja looting in a raid has repercussions where if a breach of contract between the raid leader and the fellow raid members is made and the raid leader takes an item by changing the loot policy without the approval of the rest of the raid for that change. The concept of ninja looting in a raid cannot be directly applied to the concept of ninja looting in a guild, as it would be impossible for any GM to track any contracts made between a Guild Master and any subsequent members who join the guild, assuming that such a contract even exists for most guilds to constitute a breach of contract. However, as I've stated in the previous paragraphs, there is no defined rule that any and all items deposited into the guild bank belong to the guild master solely. And most if not all players deposit into their guild banks hoping that it would benefit the guild as a whole, and not only the guild master. The Guild leveling process is something that isn't accomplished by the guild master alone, but rather every member of that guild. Therefore, the Guild Master ninjaing an entire guild should not be allowed.

The Guild transfer process is still a very new feature and is simply flawed at the moment. Of course, the guild bank feature is aged and while we may be expected to have seen the possibility of this happening, having a Guild Master ninja an entire guild is rarely precedented and the Guild transfer process simply allows it to happen with ease.

It would be useless to point out the flaws in a system and propose no changes to it.

- In order for a guild to transfer servers, at least 5 other members in the guild having been in the guild for no less than one months period and belonging to different accounts must agree to the transfer and transfer with the guild.
