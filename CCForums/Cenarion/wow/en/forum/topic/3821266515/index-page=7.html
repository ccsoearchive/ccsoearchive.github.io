That would still not cover you for what the GM did here. In your case, you said the GM moved the whole thing. He did not take out items that were put there by others and keep or sell them. The guild bank still holds the items. He moved the whole guild to another server. So the items that belong to the guild went with the guild. When the guild gets to the new server, it will likely have new members who will be able to take those items out. So, if the GM does not remove them and sell them for his own personal benefit, the items are in the possession of the guild and still technically belong to the guild. I think you are trying to come up with a way for the items to belong to the collective of people, but the issue there is that the makeup of that collective changes constantly, so who would have rights to what when. If they belong to the guild, which is a thing (almost like a corporation in your example), then they are still with the guild who owns them. The policy change would not protect you.

Now, if you are saying a GM should not be able to ninja an entire guild bank, sell it, and keep all of the money; that is different. I do not know what a system that both allows central control of the guild function, including the bank, but also provides checks on that central control would look like without becoming a slippery sloap.

Again, I don't disagree that what your GM did was wrong, and that a workable prevention measure would be nice. But I don't currently know of a measure. Certainly does not mean someone more brilliant than I could not come up with one though.

I've had guild banks ninjad from me and mine 3 times over the past couple years. Always got them back.

The trick is to report the GM for account-sharing, if you know that he has ever had anyone else accessing his account. Hope that helps - always remember that if someone didn't break a particular rule, just find a rule that they did break and report them for that instead.
