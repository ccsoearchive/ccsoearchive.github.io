I like the idea of the role bonus; it is necessary for LFR to function. Of course, there are always things that can be improved. I will introduce the notion of flagging items for the purpose of this post.

Currently, it seems items are flagged as any combination of Tank, DPS and Healer, along with a primary armor type requirement. If you meet the role and primary armor type requirements you are eligible for the role bonus.

While on my Balance Druid I was eligible for a role bonus on a leather agility item. This is because the item is flagged as DPS (and probably Tank for feral druids), and leather armor. While on my Mage, I witnessed a cloth chest piece with spirit drop and all cloth users were eligible for a role bonus due to it being tagged as DPS or Healer, and cloth armor.

You can see that the role bonus is a little too general. Items could be further tagged for 'agility users' or 'spirit users' to prevent people from acquiring these items over someone they would benefit more. Perhaps spirit items are not flagged as such because non-spirit items aren't prioritized to non-spirit users. But, hybrid classes with two DPS trees (shaman and druids) should not be eligible for a role bonus on items that don't benefit their current specialization.

I think this idea could be easily applied in an upcoming patch. However my next idea is something that would require rigorous testing and would be better considered for the next expansion.

The rough idea is that for each class and role there would be an expected level of performance. If that level of performance is exceeded, that person would receive a role bonus based on the amount (as a percent) it was exceeded.

Let's say a markmanship hunter should be expected to do 15000 DPS. If said hunter was able to do 20000 DPS on a boss, then the hunter would receive an additional roll bonus of 33. This is gained using the formula ((actualDPS - expectedDPS)*100)/expectedDPS. The role bonus would only be considered if the expectations were exceeded.

Healers, of course, could be expected to reach a certain amount of HPS, while Tanks could be expected to reach a lower threshold of DPS. There could also be a maximum roll bonus attained from this feature.

Another factor that could be considered is minimizing damage taken from avoidable boss abilities. Let's denote X as an avoidable boss ability, and say a person should take no more than 100000 damage from X. If a person only took 75000 damage from X they would get an additional roll bonus of 25. The formula for this is ((expectedDamageTaken - actualDamageTaken)*100)/expectedDamageTaken. Again, I do not promote the use of this feature to hinder someone's roll.

I think if LFR is to be used in the upcoming expansion that these features should definitely be considered

Blizzard is planning on checking specs for the role roll bonus in MoP. However, this wouldn't fix vendoring of items, trading or giving to friends which would only be fixed with bidding: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/3754955563

Giving a role roll bonus as a way of rewarding performance only works if someone actually rolls. (Can anyone roll on tier tokens with a roll bonus for the classes that can use it, or is it not allowed and only equippable items get the role bonus?)

So if someone already has everything they need from boss X, this would be ineffective in preventing them from going afk. And everyone who didn't go afk will feel justified in rolling for everything that drops because they get a bonus.

Basically it would be used as a standard for judging people that wouldn't always be accurate. What if a healer decided to keep spamming heals instead of dispelling a debuff, because it gave them a better roll bonus?

My personal view is that the role bonus doesn't really work. For starters I blame it for the current shortage of healers:
Last time I saw the stats, 10 man guilds far outweighed 25s. Furthermore, a large quantity of 10 man groups are 2-healed. So that makes the general raiding population closer to 2 tanks (20%), 2 heals (20%) and 6 dps (60%) than the 25 man numbers which are tanks 8%, heals 24%, dps 68%. I would hazard a guess that the non-raiding (ie - people who don't set foot into normal+ raids) would be weighted even further away from healing (because let's face it, its just not that popular a role).

Now, that's just for people's primary spec. I'm sure there's a fair chunk of people who would be willing to offspec heal LFR, or even respec just to heal.

BUUUT

If you do, you don't get mainspec gear. LFRing as a healer is completely worthless to me, because if I do that, I will get zero gear that will contribute to my guild's normal raid progression. We have enough healers, and we don't need to gear up more ahead of other specs.

And that's all squarely on the head of the current need+ mechanic. I would happily queue as heals if I could roll on feral gear. Even if that was a trade from being able to need+ on healing gear. My healing gear is more than sufficient for LFR, and that would be all it would get used for.

And don't even get me started on when I have to use my dual spec on feral and guardian. Hell, I suspect the current feral set bonuses are sucking a lot of healers out of the pool, simply because tank/dps now takes up 2 spec slots.
