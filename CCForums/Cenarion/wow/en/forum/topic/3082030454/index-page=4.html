Problem is that the mechanics that keep Hunter (MM Hunters) performing well in PvE are the same mechanics that keep us behind in PvP. Focus is a fine as a resource. The problem isn't necessarily pillar humping, as that hurts everyone. Its the fact that the focus regen of Steady/Cobra Shot is attached to damage being done. So, much like Warriors, absorb effects nullify our focus regen. Pillars interrupting the cast nullify our focus regen. And, of course, stepping into melee range nullifies the focus regen. 2 focus per second on melee attacks via Aspect of the Fox does nothing and requires 3 talent points in BM to get what you'd regen naturally anyway. And none of that takes into account that the base haste, after reforging, for teh honor PvP set only gives you ~ 2.5% Haste (I think full S10 at 2200+ rating gives around 6%) which drops Steady/Cobra Shot down to ~1.9s draw time, give or take depending on the haste level.

So, disregarding everything else that may or may not be wrong, ANY class who's resources are so constrained is going to struggle.

LEGALLY ENFORCEABLE PROMISE

To win a breach of a contract lawsuit, you must first have a legally enforceable promise. The types of promises that are NOT generally enforceable include:

1. Jokes, or promises the reasonable person would not take seriously.

2. Promises made by a minor (someone under 18 years of age in most states).

3. Promises made by a mentally unsound person.

4. Promises for gifts only, when there is no reasonable reliance on the promise resulting in harm to the person to whom the promise is made.

5. Promises to perform illegal acts.

6. Promises to not report alleged wrongful acts to the authorities in exchange for benefits (except for lawful plea bargains by proper government authorities, etc. ).

7. Promises that are not in writing when a writing is required (e. g. , transfer of real property or services that cannot be completed within one year).

8. Promises that are rejected by the person to whom the promise is made.

9. Promises that can't be kept due to acts of God, acts of war, sudden lack of supplies or laborers, or other things beyond the promisor's reasonable control.

10. Promises made in duress or to someone with vastly superior bargaining power, as with most binding arbitration agreements.

So, a legally enforceable promise is a serious promise made by a mentally sound adult to do or not do some legal act in exchange for someone else doing or not doing some legal act (or if nothing was anticipated in exchange for the promise, as in the case of gift promises, but the person receiving the promise reasonably relied on receiving the gift anyway and would be harmed if the promisor is not forced to give it). If you have a legally enforceable promise from someone, what can you do if that person breaks that promise, or breaches the contract?

Signing a TOG doesn't excuse a company from violating basic contract law

If a representative of a company makes a promise written or verbal it is a contract

Now if that representative doesn't uphold that promise it is called a breach of trust

For example

"I need to talk to the devs about the balance feedback I'm seeing echoed in here. That still doesn't guarantee I'll get right back to you tomorrow on this stuff, but it's on my radar. I'm sure it's on theirs too, as they regularly read our forums and AJ. I just need to discuss it with them."

Now this promise is a verbal contract
Any reasonable person would assume that this company representative would respond back in a reasonable amount of time. Now it would be up to a judge or jury to decide what would be a reasonable amount of time

As a contractor i deal with verbal promises/contracts all the time and if the party doesn't fulfill that contract in a reasonable amount of time. i as a consumer of the other parties service/product i have the right by law to look for compensation for my lose as a result of his broken contract
