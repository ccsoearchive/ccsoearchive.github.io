In your example you compare charging for in-game services as charging for popcorn at the movie theater.

When you go to the movies, you probably buy popcorn, right? Why? Because it's something a little extra that helps you enjoy your experience while watching the movie. However, it is purely optional, and in no way is popcorn that you have to buy forced upon your wallet.

The same goes for these services; You can pay for a service, such as a race change, a faction change, and coming soon, a guild transfer, only to help you enjoy your experience while playing the game. The fact that Blizzard is charging money for this is not outrageous. They are offering you an optional service to help increase your experiences while on the game, but they are not forcing you to buy it.

And the fact remains that Blizzard, like movie theaters that charge for popcorn, is a for-profit organization.

If you were to have a business where you could pay someone to write a script, wouldn't you want to make money off of it? No.. I bet everyone in this thread who is complaining about how this is going to be a PAID service is just a billionaire philanthropist in real life, right?
