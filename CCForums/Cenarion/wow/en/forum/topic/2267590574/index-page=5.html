I think this has the potential to be great for players, but I feel like it’s opening up vulnerability for people who scam guilds for their banks and cash. We see it in the Customer Support Forum where people complain that they’ve been suspended for scamming when all they did was “take stuff from the guild bank,” when in reality they’ve violated the Scam Policy:
Guild Bank Theft
This category includes:
    Removing goods from a guild's bank with malicious intent

If a player is found to have perpetrated a guild bank theft, he/she may:
    • Be temporarily suspended from the game
    • Have any money or item gains removed


http://us.blizzard.com/support/article.xml?locale=en_US&articleId=20459

While Game Masters can intervene to reverse any damages done to the guild, the service somewhat blurs the lines of defining a guild bank theft. I’m sure your Policy teams have juggled around the idea and have something in the works, but I feel that it’s worth restating: If the service is abused, what recourse remains for the original guild? What will be considered malicious intent under the new service? What happens to the transferred guild on the new server?

I think the service is a good idea and will benefit a lot of players in the long-run. My concerns are how policy will adjust to protect players from having their hard-earned items and donations to the guild stripped away. What would happen to guild members who are inactive at the time of transfer; are they still notified? If their account is reactivated and transferred, how long do they have before their guild reputation no longer carries over?

I’m very curious to see the FAQ for the transfer service once it’s released. I know it’ll be a ways off still, but I can’t wait to see how Blizzard will address some of the issues. Can’t wait to see what they come up with.
