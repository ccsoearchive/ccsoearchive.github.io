One thing you need to keep in mind is one of the big problems with hunters is they don't synergize with any other class at all. Warlocks can enable shadow priests and boomkins with DI. Just the 3% haste is enough to push some pretty extraordinary numbers. Rogues are always best off putting tricks of the trade on the class/raid member that is going to pull the highest numbers, thus inflating those numbers across the board. Marksmanship hunters aren't a good target for DI. Hunters are possibly a good target for tricks of the trade, but if you have a class that's almost guaranteed to outdamage a hunter, you put it on them instead. The problem is unless you're the top performing class/spec in the raid, you're simply not going to get things like tricks of the trade because it's a net raid dps loss.

So when you're looking at raidbots charts, make sure you keep in mind that our class synergy is pretty weak and therefore on most encounters we're simply not going to be above casters. It has much less to do with the legendary as it does with synergy.

Now as far as the original topic is concerned, we're just not scaling well. You can see it in the numbers if you have some old logs laying around. My marksmanship numbers from an average ilvl of 368 to an average ilvl of 381 has scaled by about 5%, but it's comparing 378 to 391 weapon damage as well. They should have been able to predict this with some decent modeling, but they never seem to figure it out.

Also keep in mind that we started the tier with easy to get items that were instantly large DPS upgrades. That was a really big boost for MM in the early part of this tier. But we're still not scaling for squat right now.
