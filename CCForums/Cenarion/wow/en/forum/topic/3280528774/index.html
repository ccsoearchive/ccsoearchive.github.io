"Overall, we think melee DPS in dungeons and raids is still too low. We think melee DPS is fine in PvP, and we think melee DPS is competitive against ranged specs against target dummies, but in actual encounters with all of their moving pieces, melee falls behind. To attempt to adjust for this imbalance, we have improved the benefit to attack power given by the various raid buffs (Blessing of Might et al.) to melee only. Hunter DPS is fine, or even high in 4.2, so we didn’t want this buff to extend to them, which is why the AP benefit is now 20% for melee attacks but still 10% for ranged attacks."

8th place according to raidbots based on the top performing people playing in top guilds which is the best measure for the highest potential a class has to do damage. Raidbots normalizes dps for each fight and then ranks each class against other classes for that same fight and calculates a score. As such, gimick fights such as alysrazor are equalized. While all attempts to rank classes against other classes over a variety of fights with varying mechanics is impossible to do with 100% accuracy, It can be done within a reasonable margin of error with enough parses.

raidbots also has a setting to calculate scores given all parses. This means calculating using the lower performing parses which will result in a spec not ranking well if it has a high skill curve, a low skill cap, or both. Given all parses, the best hunter spec still puts us at 8th place, with a major difference being that locks then have two specs above us instead of just one.

As it stands now, Shaman are in the same boat as we are. Their best spec is essentially tied with our best spec. Death knights' best spec is a good amount below ours, but they will be benefiting from the melee buff while we will not.

The point is that for a class that has no other options but to DPS, our DPS is not "fine, or even high". Our DPS is ehhh or even poor compared to other classes.
