I think somewhere along the line people started taking this game way too seriously and feeling like Blizzard owed them something. Back in the day, if you played your Super Mario game and were unhappy with it, did you write the dev's and tell them to change Mario or else? No. You just didn't buy the game or any of the sequels. Not sure when video gaming turned into "give me what I want or else!"

Blizz has caved to this far too many times. The only time I really felt it was warranted was the whole using peoples real names on the forums as that was a major privacy issue. It's nice to see them taking their game back steering the story and the game play on their own.

I am personally looking forward to the next expansion as well. The new zones have me terribly excited.


SMB was a fixed console game with no prospect of updates. WoW is a fluid ever-changing game with open requests for feedback.

When Cataclysm was announced, I was tremendously excited. I'd never been able to fully experience an expansion launch, having started playing too late. I'd heard stories of the opening of the Dark Portal, of the Gates of Ahn'Qiraj, of the Zombie Invasion. I looked forward to seeing what they would do.

But then information started to trickle in. One of the interesting things, to me, a new way to customize our characters was scrapped. Then I learned one of the things I loved most about the game, treeform, was going to be removed in exchange for a cooldown form. I posted about this frequently, trying to maybe convince them to change their minds. On the last night we had treeform, I rallied the druids of my realm to give the beloved treeform a last hurrah. There were about 100 druids, and several hangers-on, parading around, taking a trip in the TB/Org Zeppelin, hanging out with Applebough. And then it was gone.

And then there's more. Hybrid talent trees? Nah. Gone. I don't care if it was sub-par, I had a build I liked and it worked. And it was fun to customize if I felt like it. Now, we're stuck with one tree and a smattering of extras, and in MoP, we'll all be even MORE alike. Anyone who thinks those trees will offer any real variety or choice is fooling themselves.

Separate raid lockouts? Nah. Gone. No longer could I pug multiple runs of OS or Onyxia or Ulduar or Naxx or VOA every week without worrying that someone might've already done it with their guilds that week. Even though ALL that gear was obsolete, and there was no need to have old raids on shared lockouts, but they did it anyway.

Our faction leaders. Thrall, quit "off-screen". Cairne, KILLED "off-screen". Wanna know what happened? Too bad unless you buy a book, which you shouldn't have to do for a self-contained game. Because they sure as hell won't give you any backstory aside from a brief mention on the starting character intro video. Now we have Baine and Garrosh.

And the thing I was looking forward to, the pre-expansion event. After all the hype. It turns out to be a timed event, which then results in us QUEUEING for a dungeon to kill a boss and have him drop... gear that would be obsolete in Cataclysm. They could've had the boss invade the cities instead, or they could've had the boss drop gear as a "preview" of what things will be like, but they did neither of those things. It was rote, dull, and lifeless, and a shadow compared to how things COULD HAVE been. I'm not holding my breath for MoP's pre-expansion event to be any better, no matter how they hype it up.

Now, you can say, Mishotem, you're level 85. You clearly bought the expansion, despite your laundry list of things you didn't like. And you'd be right. I did buy it. And to this day, I still hope they'll change their minds on some things they've done, some of which I listed here.

I still have hope. That's why I bought the expansion. That's why I signed up for the yearly pass - so that I can get into the beta, and unlike SOME people, I won't be there to hunt down raiding bugs I can later exploit. I'll be there to make the game better, which is what I also try to do here by pointing out where I think they went wrong. Because unlike SMB, they actually have the power to change things if people don't like it. But they won't change things if we don't say anything. And they sure as hell won't change things if all we do is make sycophantic posts about how great their game is and how everything they do is the bee's knees.
