The intent of the talent calculator was to give players something to fool around with. We wanted everyone to get a feel for the idea we're going for in Mists so that it wouldn't sound as crazy or shocking when seen on "paper."

The release of the prospective 5.0 talents and abilities was a largely unprecedented step for Blizzard, wh has historically kept much of the new things under wraps until beta, and it's a step I'm glad they took. It shows that the developers believe that community feedback and discussion is an important part of the development process through a larger portion of the cycle. It would also make little sense for them to post the talents and abilities they are working on (had to have taken time from multiple people/departments) and then "ignore" player concerns, ideas, issues, and feedback. We will go into 5.0 beta knowing a ton more about class changes than perhaps ever before in a WoW expansion beta.

That each thread with solid feedback does not receive a shiny Blue tag is a good thing; Blue responses tag Blue trackers, which bring a swarm of players who may have had little or no interest in the original thread but feel it necessary to leave their two cents (or just mill around) anyways. Then come the trolls, the cheerleaders, the nay-sayers, the nee-sayers, and all other manner of posts that detract from the original discussion and can even get a thread locked despite the original post being a well-stated argument over a legitimate issue. I often wonder if there are any Blues posting in our discussions on their personal accounts so as to contribute unobtrusively. That we, the player community, do not see every design iteration is also probably healthy for both us and the developers. It gives them space to create and think of cool new things without having to deal with (or at least sticking the CMs with) the constant forum outcry that I am sure would happen if we could somehow peek at the latest changes every day.

I, for one, am grateful that Blizzard took the time to show us their works-in-progress rather than hide behind the curtain until only the last few details need polishing.
