My opinion differs about the upcoming talent system. When I look at what is given to us so far, and what is given to us about Diablo 3's skill system... I find similarities a bit disappointing.


They emphasized the wrong parts. A lot of your core abilities will be there, but you just won't choose them. When you choose your spec at level 10, they'll be progressively unlocked for you. This makes sense considering right now there's little choice in talents. With the new talent trees, there'll actually be CHOICE.

3(3^9) = the amount of different VIABLE combinations of talents that each class will now have.


Viable is a bit of a stretch. While the talents haven't been settled out yet for any class, just a quick glance at the Shaman tree shows you what they're leaning towards.

As it is now, I can spec for interupts. Spec for shocks. Spec for PVP (in several different ways I don't even want to get into) to match the encounter, playstyle, or overall feel of how you want your character to be. Are all of them the "best" options for raiding? Nope. Best for PVP? Nope. But you have options. Take a little extra toughness here, some extra heals there, etc etc etc. Those are options. Not "This is what you're getting every 15 levels... take it or leave it." The only way to force any Enhancement Shaman (again, just using that as an example) to feel the need to take ANY talents before level 75, for PVE, and REALLY feel good about that choice is to custom tailor encounters to MAKE those talents viable. ... Which is, in effect, REMOVING choice from the tree.

"Oh, this boss does a movement effect to the party/raid... gonna need that Windwalk Totem."

Meanwhile, the tree as it is now... I can customize the hell out of my lower level toons (I have several Enhancement Shaman, on the same server... just because I love the style of the spec so much). I can put more into my Lightning Shield, or more into Flurry, or more into Elemental Devastation, etc etc etc... I have that choice. Baselining all talents like and giving them out one or two at a time is a little bland. Add into that the choices we get to pick from being AMAZINGLY useless for level appropriate encounter; I know at 15 or 30, there's nothing more I want than the repulse the mobs away from me to make them even harder to hit. Or to root them into place just to screw up the tank a little more... Or reducing my damage by 50%... good times.

I know this is a rough draft of the tree... but that's just it. It's a draft... that's what they're already leaning towards, so that's pretty much how it's going to be in the end.

You can't honestly look at a tree and be like "oh, all 3 of those are good choices and will serve me well, I'm sure. I could pick any of those 3 and be viable for most encounters..."
