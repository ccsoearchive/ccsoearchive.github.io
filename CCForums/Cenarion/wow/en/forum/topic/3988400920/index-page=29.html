The best thing about this game being around for so long is that Blizzard has an enormous amount of data and history to work with.

The same has been shown in history with almost everything man made. Sure we can play Devil's Advocate and assume that every move they make is a calculated response in order to maximize profitability. That's the main core goal of any global business - to make the most amount of profit possible. However, just like almost every global business, they aren't stupid. They do understand that there are breaking points, dull areas, and things that are downright unacceptable to potential and veteran customers.
With each new patch and expansion across numerous titles, not just WoW they learn from success and mistakes. They make changes and adjust. It will and I repeat NEVER be perfect. We just have to continue to play a part in the future. By simple communication, subscription count, gains, losses etc. over the years, we are the main ones directing the changes that Blizzard is implementing.

Whether we choose believe it, try to understand it or not, we still have a hand in this game's future and help pain the picture to guide the ever changing constant refinement of the entertainment that Blizzard produces for us to purchase. Have faith, and even if they don't get it exactly right, they will make adjustments based on what they learn from us once we get time to play it.

One example is found usually in the movie industry. Most of us can name titles where the first of a movie series seemed to be pretty decent, while the first sequel ends up being awful and overdone and by the third and fourth movies becomes improved, while maybe not your cup of tea individually, it ends up being a more successful product as time goes forward.

Examples in movies -
The Fast and the Furious
Batman movies ( all of them )
X Men
Iron Man - Avengers storyline

Basically my message is to have some faith. If they mess things up, we let them know and they make improvements. Kinda like an ongoing beta test. Just be positive.

Rome wasn't built in a day. :)
