From what I've seen so far, I actually agree, and I know why I disliked Cata. Wasn't the content, though T11 was pretty "meh". It actually had more to do with the "return to Azeroth" theme than anything else. After playing War1 and 2 when I was younger, there was NOTHING like zoning through the Dark Portal for the first time, and BC for me will always be a special point in this game, and gaming for me in general.

That said, some parts of Northrend will always give me great memories as well. Grizzly Hills, Howling Fjord, Storm Peaks, ULDUAR...

The feeling of epic newness, new continent, new content, new adventures in store... Cata didn't quite provide that since it was more of a "return", though given WoW's age I'm not saying it wasn't necessary.

I'm just very much looking forward to expanding once again into a new and mysterious land, that's what RPGs are all about.



OH and now that the art team actually has a solid theme and direction in which to head once again... yeah, it's going to be epic. They rarely disappoint, I just don't think they had much to work with in Cata, but Outland/Northrend had some truly spectacular scenes, music, moments, and art. I honestly believe Mists will deliver on that quality benchmark.
