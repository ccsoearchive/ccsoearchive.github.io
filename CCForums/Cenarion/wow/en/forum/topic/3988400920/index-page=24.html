Imo, primarily because Blizzard didn't include enough end-game 5 man content for all levels of players. Normal dungeons were ok, but they were more designed for leveling up. Once you hit end-game, Heroics was where it was at. However they only introduced 1 level of challenge, and that was Heroic.

Since 5-mans are the most accessible form of PvE group content at end-game, it's natural everyone wanted to get in on it, problem was not everyone was good enough. So instead of creating a new level of end-game 5-mans to help please both crowds, they simply brought their 1 level down to a level where everyone and their dog could do it, thus pleasing the majority but leaving others out in the cold.

The second issue was the introduction of the badge system. Before the badge system, running heroics was an occasional activity if you wanted to pick up a side-grade here or there, to have a challenging dungeon run to test your skills, or get some gear for raiding (though it wasn't required when Heroics were first introduced, as Karazhan could be done straight after regular dungeons). However, with the Badge system and the requirement for Heroics to be run in order to raid, everyone now had to do them, and everyone wanted to do them a lot (over, and over, and over, and over, and over again). This means making them super easy and super quick.

With MoP they're introducing "Challenges", but it's a minor step that will only help to slightly alleviate the larger problem, and instead of new content it's more "Do this content again and again, but try and do it faster!" (as if Heroics weren't short enough as it is :p)

The Heroic label is now a relic of an era long gone. They should really rename them to "Grind Dungeons", because that's all they're really there for.
