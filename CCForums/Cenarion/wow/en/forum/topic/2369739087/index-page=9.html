I believe that Zeherah has said that Haste is a difficult/tricky stat to simulate properly. Some of the results from femalwdwarf regarding Haste could be slightly off. Also there are a few settings that Whitefyst suggests you make adjustments to when determining your DPS on femaledwarf. There is a section in his EJ post describing how he has the settings configured when using Femaledwarf. You should check those out if you havent already.

Regarding the 2 specs, I was in the same exact spot. I carried both MM and SV as my two primary specs. (Just recently our raid comp has changed and I have been going BM for fights where my AoE dps is not needed.)

What I did was optimize my gear the best I could for MM, since MM was my main single target DPS spec. Doing so means that I was giving up a little bit of DPS in my SV spec, but I really only used it for AoE/movement intensive fights anyway, so it didnt matter to me as much.

You will have to either pick one spec to Min/Max or find a happy medium that works for both specs. In either case you really wont be loosing all that much *REAL* DPS.

I was playing around with your gear at wowreforge and FD. All of the following was done without raid buffs, so you probably want to repeat this process with full raid buffs to confirm my results.

First, I reforged your gear as close to 8% Hit as possible, which got me to 963 rating, or 8.01% Hit. Then I reforged to Crit when possible and Haste otherwise. The results of this setting was 17823.44 DPS. The raw stats were: Agility - 4197, Stamina - 5082, Attack Power - 190, Hit Rating - 963, Crit Rating - 1824, Haste Rating - 1149, Mastery Rating - 1016.

This puts you at about 200 Haste below the recommended rating. So, I then started reforging into Haste and ended up with 1400 total Haste, which is slightly above the recommendation, but enough to account for latency and other issues. The results here was a net loss of about 120 - 140 DPS. (I accidently hit the Update button before I meant to and lost some consistency). I'm not going to bother listing these stats because they are worse than the above configuration.

After this, I went back to the full Crit build, with the raw stats listed above and tinkered with your glyphs. Anything other than the Chimera Shot glyph was about 350 - 400 DPS less than using Chimera Shot glyph.

So, what this says to me is that you have the haste to support glyph and the quicker rotation. Now the thing is, your SS cast time should be 1.27 seconds, fully buffed. That means a cycle with 5 SS and 2 AS will come in 8.35 seconds, delaying your next CS by 0.35 seconds (see Mendia's post at the top of this page). Even though you are not at the ideal spot for Haste, you would have to give up too much Crit to reach that spot. If you can naturally acquire Haste without sacrificing Crit, you should see the largest increases.

If my calculations (not shown here) are correct, this rotation is fairly focus positive, so you'll have to burn an AS every third cycle. That is, you'll need to run 5 SS > 2 AS > CS > 5 SS > 2 AS > CS > 4 SS > 3 AS > CS to prevent focus capping. Again, see Mendia's post above.

To answer your second question, I did all of this with a MM spec in mind, and completely ignored your SV spec. It has been my experience that when my gear has been optimized for one spec, it is good, but suboptimal, for the other spec. I rotate between all three specs (for PvP, single target, and AoE when needed) on a weekly basis but do not bother reforging each time.

You're probably either going to have to choose one spec to optimize for and deal with it in the other spec, or find some happy medium that is not best for either spec, but good enough. Suffice it to say, though, that you are doing it right.

How much more Hit and Crit did you lose when you did your reforging? Swapping either of these for Haste, even to get to a plateau, will be a definite DPS loss. My guess is that your Hit Rating tanked when you did that. If that was the case, the number of shots you were missing outweighed the extra benefits of Haste.

Looking at your gear though, it appears as if you are shedding too much Haste to reach the Hit cap. You can get Spear of Trailing Shadows from Tol Barad rather quickly, and that would give you a big chunk of Hit and Haste to play with. Also, there are Key to the Endless Chamber and Fluid Death, which can replace Unsolvable Riddle. Again, these give you more Hit than you need, enabling you to reforge out of Hit and into other stats, as opposed to what you are being forced to do.

Separately, I would undo the reforging on the Viewless Wings and the Mantle of Moss for 5 more Hit.

Also, I'm not sure about your gem choices, you should usually be going for +40 Agility. The rule is this:
A) Find out how much Agility you would get from all Agility gems,
B) Find out how much Agility and other stats you would get from from activating the socket bonus,
C) If the amount of Agility in B is greater than or equal to A, go for socket bonus. If A is greater than B (yes, you ignore all other stats), go for straight Agility.
