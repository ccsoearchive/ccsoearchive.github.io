Hey nice guide. I"m leveling a hunter. I got a few questions. Should i use serpent sting all the time in the first phase. Second, what do you mean when use CS on cooldown. Third, Should i use CS to refresh serpent sting to maintain it through out the standard phase or use CS-2x arcane-5x ss for rot in standard phase? Thanks for your time.


While you are leveling, the majority of this guide is useless to you. It is designed for Hunters at level 85 doing heroic dungeons and raid content.

That said, here is some advice that you can apply while leveling:
1. You almost never need to use SrS (Serpent Sting) while questing and during trash pulls while in a dungeon. If the dungeon boss you are fighting will last more than 10 seconds (some people say 15), then it is worth using. However, most dungeon bosses hardly last that long anyways because leveling dungeons are meant to get you in and out as quickly as possible.

I haven't gone back to reread the information, but after a couple of discussions about best practice for openers, opening with SrS was determined to be a net loss in DPS. The ideal thing to do is begin casting a Misdirected AiS (Aimed Shot) as the tank is running to the target. Follow that with a pair of SS (Steady Shots) and then repeat AiS > 2x SS until the boss dips to 80% health. At this point, you apply SrS and revert to the standard phase rotation.

Misdirection is a level 76 talent, so it won't be available through most of your leveling experience. So, you should probably not used and AiS opener while leveling to save your tanks a lot of hassle.

2. You won't get CS (Chimera Shot) until level 69. CS is one of your spec-defining abilities and is one of your biggest hitting shots. Because it is so good, there is a 10 second (9 if glyphed) cool down on the shot, meaning you can fire it only every 9 seconds (8 if glyphed). The reason that is not 10 seconds is because the global cool down, which limits everything we do, begins ticking immediately after firing the shot and is 1 second long.

'To fire <ability name> on cool down' just means that you are firing it as soon as it is available because not doing so would be a waste of DPS.

3. When you get into longer fights, you will be firing CS on cool down, so you shouldn't need to think about using it to refresh SrS. It does that task automatically and you just need to remember to fire CS on cool down. The rotation that is given (and is flexible depending on circumstances) is designed to fire CS on cool down with the appropriate levels of Haste. It takes care of both steps at once.

4. Practice using Rapid Fire and Readiness (when you get it) while you level, because it is a great tool to use in end game content.
