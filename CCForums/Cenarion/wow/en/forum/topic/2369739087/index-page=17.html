You'll get better answers in the Professions forum then you will in here when it comes to profession advice. If you are really concerned about maximizing your DPS, Jewelcrafting paired with either Leatherworking, Blacksmithing, Alchemy, Inscription, or Engineering will give the most benefit. Of those, the cheapest are typically Alchemy and Inscription, while the most expensive are generally Engineering and Leatherworking. Blacksmithing and Jewelcrafting can be expensive, but there seems to be a better supply of ores than skins at lower levels.

If you are truly concerned with money, I'd suggest going on a collection spree and gather a bunch of ore to dump on the AH. Then, go skin a tonne of lower level mobs to get a bunch of lower level leather. Drop Mining, pick up Leatherworking and supplement what you gathered with stuff from the AH. There are plenty of ways to make money with LW that do not require Chaos Orbs (PvP gear, Leg Armors, Leather Conversions, etc) that you can use to fund your next profession. If you go Alchemy, I'd recommend Transmute Specialization, as this is the best at making money.

If you're still worried about gathering, roll a DK and make him a Skinner/Miner. He can go about gathering all of those low level mats for you.

The add-on Auctionator will help you get an idea of what is profitable on your server.

The short answer is to enchant those 7 items and follow the gemming guide laid out in this guide. The long answer would take longer than I am willing to give seeing as I'm about to head to bed.

The longer answer:
1. Your shoulders are gemmed for Haste, to activate a +10 Hit bonus. Ditch the Haste gem and get a Delicate Inferno Ruby. (-10 Hit)
2. If you're not exalted with Therazane, get to that point for your shoulder enchant.
3. Use the Critical Strike enchant on your cloak (+65 Crit).
4. Use Deadly Ember Topaz in your chest instead of the Agi/Hit Gem. (-20 Hit).
5. Enchant your gloves.
6. Use Delicate Inferno Ruby in your prismatic socket on the belt.
7. Those legs are deceptive. Usually, you want to go for a +20 Agility socket bonus, but not in that case. I would use two +40 Agility gems there, but it'll be close and you'll want to use femaledwarf to confirm.
8. Enchant your Weapon.
9. Use a tool such as reforgecalc.de or wowreforge to get yourself to the 8% (961 Rating) Hit cap.
