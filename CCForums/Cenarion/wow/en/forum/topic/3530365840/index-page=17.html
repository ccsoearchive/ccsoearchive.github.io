I'm not sure why we're confusing RPG with world of officecraft. I don't really don't remember grinding as a part of DND.

The entire way you're thinking about this is wrong. "Rewards" shouldn't be easy or hard to acquire. Epic gear shouldn't be a reward. It should be a tool. Maybe have mounts and pets as rewards, whatever. Getting an epic shouldn't make one feel "OH YEAH I HAVE PURPLE TEXT", which is almost existentially absurd.

It should be "!@#$ yeah, I have the tools I need to go do all these things I couldn't do before and explore all this new content". Nothing is really more rewarding then that feeling of possibility.

This game is far too much about rewards right now, coming from a pretty seasoned raider. I'd suspect this is because the feeling of reward is far easier to manufacture then a feeling of wonder and epicness.

That above all else is why people sit in ogr, why people hate dailies, etc. Because people aren't nearly as reward driven as you may think, and when they do care about rewards, its only because some previous experience with really solid, well designed content that is intrinsically just fun and awesome has made them feel invested in the gameworld in which they are receiving these virtual rewards.
