There needs to be something about dailies that keeps them fresh. A very spoken complaint in this topic is that they become quickly redundant. The same can be said for the old repeatable quests, but one had the option of sitting down for half a day and plowing through a huge portion of your workload towards whatever goal you were after. Dailies don't have that luxury, and are just as boring as repeatable quests, making them inferior in design when invoking fun and efficiency for the member base. It doesn't matter that I'm throwing bears if I've been throwing bears for the past two weeks. It's only fun the first, second, third, or maybe fifth time.

The other clear complaint coming from those that are voicing against dailies is that it creates an invisible wall to block alts and new players from catching up to the pack. Dailies also have the distinct drawback of limiting you to what other reputations and rewards you can go after. It was said in the topic already, and it was a good point: the growth of the number of factions that require daily quest attention to raise is astounding at this point in the game's life, and it's punishing for those who might just want to get the painful bits over with and enjoy their rewards. People will get bored with their rewards, but with the number of factions to grind for, it's going to take a while to overcome all of the trials for those shiny collectibles.

That said, if people are getting THAT bored with the game in between patches that your answer to get them to log in every day is daily quests (a forced task, as there is apparently no alternative route of play for the people who really can't find anything else to do), then you definitely need to introduce harder content that will take longer to push through, including both group and solo content. This is also (off topic) the reason why I wasn't keen on the idea of regular/heroic 10/25 mode raids. If people want to see content, make them work for it. The developers who manage this sort of progression have created the problem of boredom between patches themselves by making everything in WoW too accessible, which has led to the rise of more and more redundant sets of daily quests in an attempt to keep people logging in. This surely must be a mistake, and perhaps PvE scenarios and Challenge Mode dungeons will help to solve some of that boredom in MoP.

Those two additions still don't address the need of the solo players, though, who continue to lose out on fresh and engaging content because of a distinct lack of end-game level quests and rewards that don't require a group. The "this is an MMO" argument isn't sufficient for my tastes, because I don't want to play with a group each and every single time that I log in to WoW. Many times, sure, if my friends and contacts are on. Unluckily, LFD has made even grouping for dungeons tasteless and disinteresting because there's no need to communicate at all with the group anymore as long as everyone does their job. Dailies do not fill the hole of solo content, because, as already stated, they are redundant, boring, and I'm limited on just how far I can go with them each day.

What is "good for the game" really has to be, at the end of the day, what players will enjoy, and not what will keep them strung along for months at a time to achieve some arbitrary goal like a +30 stat bonus enchant, a majoritively useless recipe, or (outside of the aforementioned rep rewards) useless reputations. Keeping members strung along with artificial barriers such as dailies is good for business, certainly, but it doesn't equate to a fun or engaging game for virtue of the stringing.

First of all I hate dailies. That's just a personal preference and play style, but I do. I hate them so much. I feel no sense of progress doing dailies because most of them never unlocks anything new, and I hate repeating the same thing over and over again. I say let machines handle repetition, and let us focus on decision making, creative things, and tackling challenging content.

That said, I didn't mind doing Molten Front dailies on my main (besides lag, obviously). I felt a sense of progress, and it was bearable for the first time in my several years of wow life since the introduction of daily quests. Even then, it would have been worlds better if I only had to do each daily once and be done with it for the eternity. The fun factor decreases exponentially each repetition, and the story only makes sense for the first time.

Now, I have every class at 85 - one main, and a couple of other alts that I tend to focus on, and other alts sit on ZG/ZA level gear. Molten Front rewards are still very attractive to alts with no raiding opportunities (probably not any longer once 4.3 hits), especially trinkets and the caster offhand just to name a few. However, those shiny rewards aren't worth spending another months and months worth of time doing dailies, enduring the painful, repetitive content which I have already experienced on my main. There's absolutely no sense of progression for the second or the third time around, and those Molten Front dailies get old REALLY fast. Regardless of how exciting/fun it may have been for the first time, everything becomes a chore after a while.

I would like to see alts having easier access to stuff that have already been experienced/uncovered/unlocked by one of my other characters, whether it be achievements, vendors, reputation, etc. I consider that it is the player (the human player behind all the different characters) and not the character who earned whatever reputable thing it may be. Just like "skip the cinematic" option, there should be an option to "skip the repetitive dailies and unlock" feature. I just want my alts to have access to stuff I can already get on my main. Is that too much to ask?

I'm not suggesting to make everything shared among all characters. There are certain things that should be on a character-by-character basis, such as profession, normal quests, gear itself, etc - they inherently make sense to be "bound" to each character. Then there are things that should be shared among the account, such as reputation, unlocked vendors, certain achievements, etc. If these elements can't be made account-wide, making rewards BoA I think is a good alternative, like the BoA Arcanum head enchant. Therazane rep on the other hand... ugh.

If that were the thought I was expressing, I might be inclined to agree. That's not what I was getting at.

It's easy for a critic to make the mistake of confusing their opinion on a system--solely in the context of their own preferred playstyle--for whether or not the system in question is well or poorly designed. Often the assessment is levied without any argument for why a given item isn't well designed, it "just is" and the rest of the argument flows from that assumption.

Merely because one dislikes something, does not necessarily mean that it is also poorly designed. It could be, certainly, but starting a discussion by stating an assumption as though it were fact isn't gauged to have an open discussion where an actual exchange of ideas takes place.


It's true that the current daily system sometimes does not suit my playstyle.

But, using MF as an example. What if it was changed so you must commit a minimum of 3 hours (which I can do on weekends) to obtain something like 40 marks? It's totally opposite to what we have now. It would cater to people with my playstyle but then players who never have time to play for more than an hour would find it impossible to make progress. They would most certainly feel the design is bad for their playstyle.

The current daily system is a bad design because it's lacks flexibility.

If it was changed to be like RDF rewards, it caters to more playstyles. People who can only play in short sessions can still progress their toons daily in bite size. People who can't play everyday but can play long sessions during weekends can make relatively substantial progression and catch up in a day or two.

Now I understand some people would rush to complete all the things, find nothing to do, then cancel, but it's not like I'm asking Blizzard to change dailies into "yearlies" where I can finish progression in a month then cancel and not pay for the other 11 months until new contents are released.

"Weeklies" is a fair middle ground. I haven't seen anyone who doesn't like the dungeon reward change...

.....and in the end, I still need to spend roughly 30 days to get the MF rewards, and 1+ year to get all JC recipes (which is still daunting when I think about it).
