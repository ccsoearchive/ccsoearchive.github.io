what is a proper description for rehashed content?


A response to many, many requests over the years from the community to revisit old dungeons and characters?


Not the best response, perhaps. There is a difference between rehashing and revisiting... do not confuse them.

4.1's ZG has a feel somewhere between a revisit and a rehash -- the bosses were new(er) and the zone's storyline moved ahead (with a fairly tight integration into Stranglethorn as well).

4.1's ZA, however, was simply rehashing without any real energy seeming to be put into it -- no new bosses or boss mechanics, only a few changed voiceovers for Dakarra at the end.

4.0 Deadmines, SFK... also partway between revisit and rehash, with new bosses and a new storyline.

All in all, I would much rather have new content than anything recycled/renewed/refreshed/rehashed. Nostalgia is just that, a fond memory of the past -- I really didn't want to see Ragnaros, Onyxia, Nefarian or ZG/ZA again in this expansion.

We still appreciate the feedback we receive, but when it comes to feedback, details are important. That's one of the reasons we solicit thoughtful, constructive feedback: because it's more useful than blanket naysaying.


Regardless of what I think about "bad design", this is a viewpoint I get, so here.

I see a lot of people saying how we could make dailies more interesting. There suggestions are all pretty good ways for making quests more interesting, I'd love to see harder quests and some group ones (that would be fun to solo!).

But there all pretty bad for daily suggestions. Theres basically nothing you can do to dailies that won't make them a tremendous grind, just because of there intrinsic nature, quests designed to keep you logging in every day to advance your character in some way.

People get bored for heroic dungeons for chrissakes, and look at all the design effort that went into those.

My suggestion is to scrap the current focus on dailies. Sure, keep a few profession dailies and rep grind dailies, but just design them for what they are, grinds, and don't spend that much effort on them. TB was good MF was too much.

Then replacing that framework, you guys should go back to realm wide, mass events like AQ opening events. These should be designed ahead of time to last 3 months or so, and should be spaced between major raid content.

Why? Because looking back into classic, the spontaneous events that we love and are now mostly dead were really about a sense of real progression. You wouldn't get TM versus SS in hillbarhad if you made a cool worldpvp daily there with awesome rewards, you would get halfhearted zombies farming for loot. Classic wow relied on a sense of "epicness", that you were doing something nobody else was doing. Massive scale world pvp happened because of the awesome feeling most players got when that participated in something so big.

Now you have zone wide pvp with tons of vehicles and BGs centered around it (and all which are collectively awful to actually play), and nobody gives a !@#$, because that sense of "epicness" is completely gone.

The more you try to push players into that mode of play with dailies and rewards the more that feeling of epicness evaporates.

So if your looking for a way to get people out of ogr, you can't just give them ways to progress there character, all streamlined out of them. Go here, do that, get stuff, get mount. It just feels hollow, empty and done before, because it literally has been done before by millions of other players, possibly including there other toons. Give them ways to feel as if they were progressing the game through what there chars were doing. Thats what epicness is all about.

In terms of actual quests, it could be anything. Kill a rare mob, do a bossfight a certain way (kinda like how you had to make regents by getting pieces infused by bosses in the epic staff questchain).

But tbh it shouldn't be that quest based. Let the playerbase be forced to explore and discover things on different realms. Maybe you need a certain item in the world, but its somewhere completely random on every realm.

Basically, the bottom line is that players, above all, love a sense of progression in not just a gear sense, but in a sense of an evolving game, and being part of that evolution. Whether this is accomplished in hardcore raiding or just messing around killing allies and doing stupid stuff doesn't matter. Dailies alone don't address that at all, precisely because of how insanely streamlined they are. You need to create a world that is actually changing, and give players the chance to be part of that change. No, I don't mean more phasing or whatever. When you have that down people will naturally want to leave ogr and do things. But I guess this is quite a design challenge at this point, designing something that is still "new" and has yet to be done in this game.

-------

Wow, that was really long.

To sum it up more concisely, make the game more of a sandbox, and fill it with toys. Don't tell people how to play with them. Make no instructions. People will naturally go out of ogr to play with them. Then I guess add a few retard helmets via dailies and heroics to grind for people who actually like there games that way.
