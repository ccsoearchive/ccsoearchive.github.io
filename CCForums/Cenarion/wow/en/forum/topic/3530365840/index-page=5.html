We typically provide some boundaries because, as you've illustrated, something that we allow, is something that can all too easily seem mandatory. That's not what we want, but we do want to provide a reason to come into the game, be in the world, and see what's happening on a frequent basis. It's nice to also be able to offer some rewards for doing that. Naturally, dailies shouldn't be the only way to accrue rewards, and they aren't. Dailies were, in part, a response to a World of Warcraft where there wasn't a lot of incentive to come play on non-raid days, since for many players, the only way to progress became dungeon runs and, for a few, raiding. We also wanted to provide another means of acquiring currency aside from professions, and new ways to acquire reputation with important factions too. They're designed to hit a lot of notes (I'm probably missing some), and I think that they're pretty successful. You don't have to hit your cap, (indeed, one of our fears about a raised daily cap is that players might feel compelled to hit the new, higher cap) but you can if you want to put in the time. Naturally, we also want to continue to add other means of progression to the end-game, and we're looking for ways to do so in a fun and compelling way.


Some of the dailies I believe are good at what they want to accomplish, while others feel more like a chore, in my opinion.

Dailies such as the ones for Dragonmaw/Wildhammer are a good use of them as people can work on faction rep each day instead of grinding dungeons if they wish, or even both. Something that would help with dailies like that is to use a system much like Molten Front/Tol Barad where they have a chance of rotating each day. Doing the same daily over and over can be a bore and turn people off from them. Even if it was 2-3 quests for each one would be infinitely better than the same old one (usually a repeat of a quest you did).

Crafting professions is where they get mixed a lot I think. Dailies such as cooking work perfectly because there's a small amount of recipes to actually obtain. However, dailies like Jewelcrafting it becomes a major pain where if you want the patterns it will take almost a year or more just to get them due to the way its setup. I think a good portion of the JC recipes could've been set up like the Tailoring/Leatherworking/Blacksmithing/Engineering, where you make items to turn them in. This would pertain moreso to the Rings, Necks, and Chimera gems (instead of wasting a daily on the gem) and could work for some of the mixed cuts such as green, purple and orange.

Molten Front was setup somewhat decently. The first week it was fun, but after that started going downhill fast. One question I have to ask myself everytime is "How are we able to uphold the front but that Spire has to be rebuilt everyday or the flames need recleared". Dailies like that should just be a one time thing and built into the other dailies. Most of the dailies there just aren't fun after doing them several times. Even going back in there after a two month break I'm still dreading it when I did them earlier. Something like after you opened up a new section all the dailies you do after that offer +1 or +2 extra marks than they did before. This way people are still experiencing everything at a slightly faster pace and doing the same dailies. I'm surprised that the other phases haven't received the same treatment as Phase 1 did where it went from 3 days to 1 day.

Next we have Tol Barad as it is setup decently as well. We can earn tokens from doing the dailies or winning the battle. Some system like this works as a middle ground for people as well. Something like you get 1-3 tokens for doing the BH bosses would also add an incentive for people to do them as well and help out people who can't stand doing the same dailies over and over.

Lastly we have the Argent Tournament. Looked good at a quick glance, but once you dive into it you want to pull your hair out. The jousting mechanics are not fun in any way, but in order to maximize your tokens you need to do them. It was a nice change where the coin bags always dropped an additional token. The tournament also added in reputation for city factions and Argent Crusade (I think, been since wrath), but in essence offer no other reward for those who were already exalted with them. Argent Tournament is one of the designs I really hope never comes back in the form it is now. If tokens were doubled for each quest then I might consider it being worth it. At some point I need to work on them for the mounts and pets, but I just can't bring myself to doing so.

Overall, some dailies are fine I believe if another daily was added to make it a rotation base. Some could use a currency increase to make it worth the time. Then there are some I think should be scrapped completely. As some mentioned I think if most of the dailies had an alternative source that you could physically grind (like turn ins) could help out most areas as well.

Disclaimer: Everything here is my opinion and not stating that is what the playerbase thinks.

They ask nothing - they merely reward a choice.

We typically provide some boundaries because, as you've illustrated, something that we allow, is something that can all too easily seem mandatory. That's not what we want, but we do want to provide a reason to come into the game, be in the world, and see what's happening on a frequent basis. It's nice to also be able to offer some rewards for doing that. Naturally, dailies shouldn't be the only way to accrue rewards, and they aren't. Dailies were, in part, a response to a World of Warcraft where there wasn't a lot of incentive to come play on non-raid days, since for many players, the only way to progress became dungeon runs and, for a few, raiding. We also wanted to provide another means of acquiring currency aside from professions, and new ways to acquire reputation with important factions too. They're designed to hit a lot of notes (I'm probably missing some), and I think that they're pretty successful. You don't have to hit your cap, (indeed, one of our fears about a raised daily cap is that players might feel compelled to hit the new, higher cap) but you can if you want to put in the time. Naturally, we also want to continue to add other means of progression to the end-game, and we're looking for ways to do so in a fun and compelling way.

I get concerned when I see players throwing out words like 'bad design'. Perhaps an individual dislikes a design choice, and that's fine. We do our best, but World of Warcraft can't be all things to all people, all the time. That said, making a value judgment about whether the design is 'bad' or not is not only un-constructive, but in the vast majority of the cases I've seen, such an assessment reveals that the design was not well understood to begin with.

These forums represent an opportunity to have a dialogue about the game. I think that choosing words that have context and meaning, and offering alternative solutions, makes for feedback which is more readily useful.


I don't think I'd call them a bad design, but I wouldn't call them a good one.

If your goal is to give people a reason to log in every day, daily quests certainly accomplish that. On the other hand, I doubt many would agree that running the same quests every day for a month to get whatever reward you're shooting makes for very compelling gameplay.

I think it's safe to say that people only enjoy doing daily quests the when they do them for the first time or do them for the last time. Everything in between is just a grind.


If your goal is to get everybody to log in every day, then sure, it's a good design.
