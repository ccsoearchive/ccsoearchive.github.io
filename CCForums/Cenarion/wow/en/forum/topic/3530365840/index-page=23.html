Choice. For me, it is all about choice. If I come upon a daily hub and have to do the exact same quests for the 23rd time, I have 0 choice and just about as much interest. Daily quest hubs needs to have 50 potential quests, and 10 handed out. Yes, repetition will come, but much later.

Silithus offered some form of choice. We could choose, as a player, which path we wanted to take. If you chose a path you hate, here is your mirror. Modern day dailies tend to be very static. The moment I finish my last achievement, I never return. The money is pointless.

If you cannot look at a quest hub as being "fresh" for the duration of a tier, that is the definition of stale. Might as well admit that up front. Yes, it is more coding to change status quo, but anything in life worth doing has a cost.

With the MF dailies, some limited effort was made to change the dailies given to players. My personal take on that matter is that the effort was so minimal, it came across as "extra grind" instead of "fun factor". It felt like a choice of "less suck" instead of "more fun".

I do not know that my "feedback" will ever be read by blue, but when I read posts that ask for feedback with a logical and sincere opinion, I get motivated to give it, so here it is.

I really wish people would stop calling dailies bad design. You may personally find them annoying, which is all well and good, but bad =/= annoying.


I, personally, do not.


As a relatively new player who just started current-content raids this last week, dailies give me a number of options. One, I hate balancing my checkbook- anything that involves playing money games makes me want to punch babies in the face and kick small puppies. Dailies gives me an alternative source of gold, particularly since I also hate dungeons and will sacrifice various innocent beings to avoid joining them. Dailies give me a decent, not amazing, source of gold, especially since I quested my way through Cata-content and there are barely any left for me to do.


Two, also as a new player, doing dailies is repetitious enough for me to test out healing rotations if I'm playing with someone else, or damage rotations if I'm soloing. There's wiggle room to experiment without having to hear the wrath of some angry dpser who stood in stupid and then died from it.



Three, I did play Vanilla for a while, but didn't drop back in until the end of Wrath. Dailies allow me to go back and grind reputation, which I don't mind, with old BC content. It's how I got to exalted with most of the people I have rep with. Going back and rediscovering stuff, in small pieces, works with my schedule and is more relaxing than zerging through a Zan.

I agree that more content, especially non-dungeon related content, would be nice, but that's not the fault of dailies. That's a decision fro Blizzard we have to abide by, and let our opinion be known.
