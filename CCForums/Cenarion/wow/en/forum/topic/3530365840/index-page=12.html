Making the dungeon vp rewards to weekly-based was a good change. It allows players to focus on a specific toon on a chosen day rather than making players log in every toon every day.


While I agree that the OP's message here is at times poorly articulated I believe they have a genuine concern here that is shared by many within WoW, plus a constructive suggestion that I've highlighted here, because I competely agree with a week-based focus, it's been a positive change in other aspects so far and it is a trend I completely support.

Now, if I had a dime for every time I've heard someone say "I don't do dailies, they suck." or something similar, I would be rich. They were actually once pretty popular, at least in BC I remember the first push where they felt meaningful. Of course, the gold they provided relative to the economy at the time was very good and some of the dailies were unique at the time and were considered fun in their own right. Although I was never the kind of person to cap dailies, the bomb runs and simon says were both quick and a fun and different kind of quest than were originally available.. "kill x and collect y".

I am sure that tastes evolve, and not only that but some of the newer daily quests just genuinely do not seem as engaging. The two I do fondly recall (bird calling and turtle punting) are easily outweighed by the many uninspired "kill x and collect y".

But I don't ultimately believe this is the only reason why dailies are becoming a dirty word among some circles. That is more likely a combination with fluctuating interests and expectations along with the very length of time that it can take some of the newer (or not so newer) dailies to provide a meaningful reward.

The current system, the rewards most people are after are at the very end of a minimum month-and-a-half long grind that for those less inclined/active players will easily take months to complete. Compared to other facets of the game, the time investment seems unrewarding. I know myself and many others who prefer to play alts as a way to pass time, instead.

What to do? Change up the type of quests a little more, back off from the lengthy pursuit and instead offer some sort of replenishable reward for the more consistent questers out there. Or don't, really.. just so long as you understand that it's going to interest far fewer people than it otherwise could. I appreciate that there are design goals in WoW that aren't directed toward whatever grouping I may fall under. Since questing is a side attraction and not my main goal in WoW, if they stay as is I will continue to happily ignore them in favor of other things.
