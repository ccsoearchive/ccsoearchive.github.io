Molten Front dailies are a starting point for how the non-raid progression experience could be enriched.

All of the progression was hinged around the accumulation of Marks of the World Tree, a currency. This streamlines the core progression path, while leaving room for any number of currency sources to be implemented. Unfortunately for 4.2, the only such sources were daily quests. They were good dailies, I think, but they were still the only available option.

What would have been wrong with, say, adding a few repeatable quests in 4.2 - e.g., albeit a potentially unrealistic example, turn in 40 Eternium Ore for 1 Mark. Or better yet, rather than eating into the trade mats supply, add BoP drops from all Hyjal invader NPCs and let players exchange stacks of those for Marks. Sound familiar? :)

This would only complement dailies, not replace them or feel like a requirement alongside them. Players who were gung-ho about their progression could work feverishly to acquire enough currency as quickly as possible, while some players would much prefer to stick with dailies; players like me could casually farm some currency here and there without ever feeling like I'm falling irreversibly behind: I could put in 8 hours of farming on a Saturday and make up for 5 days of missed dailies.

This is really about putting the pace of progression into the players' hands as much as possible. An equal amount of effort is still there, but its distribution is more malleable.

It would be different if other lines of progression (e.g. raiding) were contingent upon this sort of content, because then absolutely you could expect players to feel compelled to participate in whatever daunting activities were necessary to maximize progression per unit time; but that simply wasn't the case with Hyjal/FL and hopefully won't be moving forward.

So I don't see the problem.
