I think what a majority of people are forgetting here is that one of the reasons the Molten Front dailies were added with 365 gear was because people wanted a way to obtain better gear than what was available in dungeons and nerfed raids without being forced to raid current content, which may be too difficult to do or get a group for.

Blizzard achieved success in this. They still have the model of "If you want gear, then you have to work for it." However, instead of having to learn a fight and wipe week after week on it for only a chance at loot, you can now opt for doing dailies everyday to have a weaker-yet-still-powerful item guaranteed to you.

I did Molten Front dailies everyday. I did Tol Barad dailies, I did Wildhammer dailies, Therazane dailies, and even old Argent Tournament dailies. Did I necessarily enjoy doing the same things over and over again? Not always, though I got through it. To me, the reward was worth the effort I would put into it. Would I have done these dailies if there was no 'powerful' reward? Most likely yes. They add another aspect into the game that doesn't require me to wait in a queue or go into super-serious raider mode.

The trouble is that some people here are just asking for handouts. When some people are stating that they are 'forced' to do dailies to get rewards, they are incorrect. Yes, if you want a specific item only available from dailies or rep, then you may have to grind some of them to get it. However, there are other methods of acquiring gear, and this is exactly what Blizzard wanted - multiple methods of obtaining gear that is relevant and still 'powerful.' As I said, there is still the model of having to work for the gear.

I believe that the current system as it stands is in a very good place. Raiders may still raid and obtain the best gear available; non-raiders may still do dungeons, dailies, and older (T11) raids to obtain still-relevant and good gear.

As for the daily cap, I'd say it's also in a good place. While I never felt 'forced' to do more dailies, I would have liked to do more, though I can understand why the limit is in place. The Molten Front dailies definitely stressed your cap and made you choose what was important to you. I think, once again, that this was one of Blizzard's intentions and they did this correctly.
