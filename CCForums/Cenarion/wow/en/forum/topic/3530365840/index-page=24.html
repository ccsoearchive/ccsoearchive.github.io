Some people only want to do the JC dailies and only feel like doing so on a Thursday night. With something like this, they could log on Thursday night, do a JC daily, hit the reset, do another JC daily, etc, until they've done 7 'days' worth of JC dailies in that one single night.

If the point of dailies is to be an alternate means of getting gear, that's not just bad design, it's ignorance. Molten front dailies aren't an option, they're just optional. Dedicated players will do everything they can to get gear, meaning killing every boss they can, capping valor points, and getting any solo items they can. Molten front items are simply another quest that dedicated players must accept that leads them to 3+ weeks of farming daily quests. It's not even that these items are supplemental to raid items, for some class/specs these are best in slot items; for instance, the moonwell chalice is better than trinkets from heroic firelands for a demo lock.

Dailies suck for 3 reasons:
1. You can only do them at the established pace. Same as everything else in this game except for heroics via the random queue. If I can play 10 hours, I want to spend 10 hours trying to get the items I want. Lockouts and limits on quests are simply annoying and don't lessen the gap between dedicated and casual players, they simply postpone the time to completion regarding progression.
2. The rewards for dailies are essentially required. If you're a demo warlock, you do molten front dailies for the trinket, otherwise you're bad. If you're a fresh 85, you do Therazane dailies or run dungeons with the tabard for shoulder enchant, otherwise you're bad. Dailies, fun or not, are being done because of incentives, but why do the designers WANT us to do dailies? Did they decided dailies would improve the gameplay, so they made them and added incentive to do them? Or did they simply want to put obstacles in our way to keep us playing more. Of course they want us to have a reason to login other than to raid, but perhaps they should give us a lot of options of what we want to do when logged in, rather than a laundry list of 5 things we have to do, each of which gets checked off leaving us in the same situation after getting our items and rep we wanted, waiting for 3+ months for the next content patch or expansion.
3. Most dailies aren't fun. Not even a slight majority, the vast majority. The best dailies are the ones that are so stupid easy then you roll through them, like "killing" 8 generic flame guys by hitting them once and letting npcs do the real work. The worst dailies are the ones that are painfully easy, gimmicky, and just take a few minutes of boring recipe-following to finish, such as "aiming" bear cubs at the trampoline. A few dailies actually have an interesting mechanic that is fun for a few times, and some are relaxing "kill 8 of this guy," but the majority are just not any more fun that killing monsters without a quest.

I'm not the person you're responding to here, but I'm going to take a stab at this one anyway, because I think it's the same thing that I recently found myself wishing to be brought back.

It's been a long time, so my recollection may be a bit fuzzy, but way back when, Silithus was a zone with a lot to do and a lot of rewards to obtain. There was a mini-set for each class, consisting of (I think) a weapon, a ring, and something else (chest?). Obtaining these items meant raiding AQ20.

Another part was collecting tokens (now called currency) of one of three different types: combat, logistics, and tactical. Combat tokens and quests were straightforward: Go kill X number of this specific type of bug. Tactical quests and tokens were of the "go to this location type": rescue or check up on an NPC, usually buried deep in a cave surrounded by the bugs you needed to kill. (I loved doing these as a rogue. :) ) Logistics quests and their tokens were perhaps the most interesting: head for a capital city, and get some items crafted, or buy them off the auction house. This created demand for otherwise overlooked items from each profession, and they were often items that had patterns difficult to obtain to begin with.

I forget whether you bought the items with the tokens, or simply turned in tokens in sets to increase your reputation, but either way, I remember you needed a balance of tactical, combat, and logistics. I also remember you could have several quests (perhaps 3?) at once, but beyond that, you had to turn in a quest before you could get another. The next quest you got was random from a group of roughly 10-12, and you never knew what you would need next.


And then aside from all that was the cultist farming. (Hey, some people like farming!) This was pretty simple. You farmed cultists until you found 3 items of clothing. Then summon a minor elemental who dropped an item and some stuff, usually essences or something. Collect 3 of the special items and another set and you could summon a mid-level elemental, designed to be fought by about 5 people. They dropped another item and better loot. Collect 3 of those special items, and another set, and another of the first item and you could summon a raid-level boss that required 20+ people, and this one dropped some pretty amazing loot.


So Silithus, back in the day, had ways to advance your character through raiding, solo questing, and farming, including using player-summoned world bosses. The zone really had a lot going on in it.

And none of this was restricted by the use of "daily" quests.
