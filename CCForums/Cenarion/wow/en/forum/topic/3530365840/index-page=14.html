The problem with the current crop of dailies is there is no bulk alternative to them. Say what you will about the rep grinds of old -- you had your dailies, and you had repeatable turn ins to increase your rep. If you wanted the rep but got sick of the dailies, you could substitute them for grinding mobs, or turning in items, or running dungeons or whatnot; you didn't have to do dailies. Molten Front, and to a lesser extent, Argent Tournament, were different in that you had a limited number of rep/rewards to gain per day, and that was it. If you missed a day, you were a day behind everyone else, and there was absolutely no way of catching up. That is bad design, and suggesting, "Oh, you don't have to do it," as a means of an excuse is not only silly, but disingenuous to the actual complaint being leveled.

I like dailies. But I'd like an alternative to them without running them into the ground. Netherwing had eggs and gathering profession turn ins, which was the perfect mix. More rep/daily hubs should have that, not less.

First Daxxarri I appreciate your explanation to his question but let me digress for a moment...

Perhaps this is too far off topic, but in respect to 'bad design', yes there is such a thing. I spend 75% of my waking day designing buildings and indeed there are bad ones.

As Daxxarri put it:
'That said, making a value judgment about whether the design is 'bad' or not is not only un-constructive, but in the vast majority of the cases I've seen, such an assessment reveals that the design was not well understood to begin with.'

On the contrary having your design critiqued by the ones using actually using it day to day is very constructive, and I might argue the very point of some of these forums, even when the feedback is negative. Critiques can provide perspective that you may not have considered as well as a number of other things. Furthermore, It is the responsibility of the designer to make sure that the design can be comprehended. Unfortunately, I'm not always around to explain the intent of my design to a client and it must stand alone, as easily understood as if I was there, perhaps not the subtleties, but the big picture. I was once told, and have found very true over the years, that 'if you have to put a sign on your building to direct someone, you've done it wrong'.

Perhaps the OP doesn't understand the design intent behind dailies, however, it's hard to explain how it's his fault. As indicated, he doesn't approve of the current daily system and offers up that the current VP reward system is a better method. This seems to be constructive even if he called the design 'bad', when 'inconvenient' might have been a more applicable term.

I would like to see more repeatable optional quests in addition to daily quests. Like if you want to go out into the world and collect 100 bad guy heads you should be able to return to the hub and turn them in.

The current questing structure seems to be focused on completing a story line chain-quest until you reach the end. To me this locks the player into only the current objective and discourages exploration and sandbox game-play.

On one side, chain quests give more story and are easier to develop the game-play perspective the player will use. But another option would be to give players some freedom in which part of the zone they explore and give them some reward for doing so. At least give some simple quests that can be done right away( or repeated) in addition to the big story chains that stretch across the zone.

You could display these quests on the map. You could even expand the interface to be the quest giver in some situations by using the map to acquire or complete the quest. Give more player freedom to play the way they want to and then give the reward for the amount of time invested. To keep it from being grindy, keep the story in order to get the better rewards but also match it with a repeatable generic quest.
