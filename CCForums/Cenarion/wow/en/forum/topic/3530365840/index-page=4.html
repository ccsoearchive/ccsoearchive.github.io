I remember before dailies, doing the hellfire pvp quest over and over for a fraction of the reward it is now. Part of the reason for dailies was the fact that 'grinding' mobs for loot or rep wasn't a fun or compelling gameplay choice, and dailies were offered as an alternative for spending hours grinding.

Remember that part of design is to limit progression by various means; it keeps people playing the game. If, on one extreme, things were absolutely impossible to get without hours of work every day, a very small percentage of the population would ever try. If things are too quick and easy to get, they're not valued, and people run out of things to do, and can stop logging in except to raid or whatever. Blizzard wants people in the game actively playing, and dailies is the method of choice to strike the balance between effort and reward.

That said, and I'd like to give my 2c on the molten front: I think it was too quick and easy, there wasn't much reason to continue once you'd unlocked it all. I would have liked to see more things you could buy with the tokens you get, much like the argent tournament.
