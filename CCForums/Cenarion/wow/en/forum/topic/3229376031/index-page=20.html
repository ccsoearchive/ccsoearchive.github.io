I understand these aren't final, but I just wanted to give my opinion on a few of these bonuses.

Tier bonuses across the board are (mostly) outside of the box, I applaud you for that. Unfortunately, a lot of these bonuses in their current implementation seem lackluster / have a lack of appeal. I'm glad to see some outside thinking here, on these bonuses. For the most part, it is refreshing to see a bonus that isn't "increases damage of x ability by 10%", but I'd rather have that for the rest of WoW, than a very under-performing bonus.

Protection Warrior 2 piece: I like it, but it seems rather strong. Revenge has a short cd, it's pretty much 100% up and it deals a nice chunk of damage. Something I would probably lower.
Protection Warrior 4 piece: Not a bad concept for a bonus, but having another ability that can become used for the raid, as opposed to the tank, is quite annoying. Other than the cd being used for the raid, as opposed to my dying self, it's a pleasant change.

Dps Warrior 2 piece: Don't know if this will be a good bonus or not. From the looks of it, pretty weak. Perhaps reduced rage cost by 5/10 while Inner Rage isn't active, and then increase damage dealt by 5/10% while it is active.
Dps Warrior 4 piece: Please tell me this is a typo and this actually reduces the cd of Colossus Smash. The only way I can explain this is... you took the RNG of both Shadow Priests and Retribution Paladins and managed to set them as our 4 piece bonus.

Again, a lot of bonuses are outside of the box in terms of thought, but some just have no appeal to them.
I'd really love to have an amazing looking tier set with amazing tier bonuses. The art team did a fantastic job with this set, and I'd expect the team who develops the bonuses would have followed suit.
If the bonuses went live like this, I'd truly only take the gear (for dps) if the stats were better itemized than offset pieces.
I'm happy I get to look like Deathwing... but I also want to partially feel like him, through our set bonuses. Please go the full mile and make it happen!

Shadow Priest 2 piece: As long as mana is still returned, that's fine.
Shadow Priest 4 piece: RNG is less of an issue, but it seems like it could be OP. I wouldn't change this bonus, though, but I would put some mechanics in there that limit the orbs gained based off of (all) currently active SW:P.

Healing Priest 2 piece: For Disc, this doesn't seem terrible. Holy, on the other hand, got Chris Brown'd.
Healing Priest 4 piece: First portion seems neat (not good or bad, just neat). Is bubble spamming some how going to occur again?

Rogue 2 piece: Besides one major problem, another TotT bonus? That's like the third bonus revolving around that in ~4 tiers. I understand it's among the easiest to balance for all specs, but I'm sure rogues are finding any bonus revolving around that to be annoying.
You take the time to give Rogues the very first (one) class specific legendary, but can't take the time to give them a bonus that feels unique in the slightest bit?
Rogue 4 piece: Could be lackluster for other specs, like Combat, but not sure! Seems like a simple number change if it's not performing well.

Now for the problem with the Rogue bonuses: They revolve around energy. Perhaps the other specs would truly love these bonuses, but Combat will greatly see next-to-no benefit from these.You've done other tier bonuses based off of spec, so please do the same for at least Combat. Since they are always at a surplus of energy, anything that reduces energy cost or increases the duration of a cd that gives back more energy makes that particular thing feel less important to the spec. Currently, it feels like Combat rogues do not have any resource mechanic and an entire cd is used to make the non-existing resource buildup faster.
Please add a Combat only portion for these bonuses that doesn't revolve around giving back / reducing energy.

Balancing has become on thing you have been working on in this game, and here is another place you need to balance around: tier bonuses per spec (make sure all specs are completely viable with them).
It will be slightly more work, but overall, players should be happy to have bonuses that reflect their specific spec.
This is one thing you try to do every tier, but it is something you haven't been improving on... and truly should be.
I hope there are many Blizzard employees reading the responses on these to help fine-tune the bonuses. Players don't want bonuses that suck, and developers don't want players to be unhappy if they can easily please them.

Warrior DPS set bonuses were not well thought out.

The 2-piece encourages us to stack hit to make use of it, unless you plan to ensure we hit 27% hit in 4.3, it's a pretty bad/worthless bonus.

The 4-piece's has a bunch of issues.

1) It favors Arms over Fury, since Fury can only CS once every 20 seconds, and has more opportunities between 20-0% to take advantage of it from random refreshes.

2) Warriors are @ 25% or so crit in Heroic 4.2 Firelands gear, I doubt it's going to go up significantly for 4.3.

3) It's a proc of a proc of a proc. Only usable past 20% hp on a boss, it requires CS to already be up, CS lasts 6 seconds, of that 6 seconds, only 3 of your globals could be possible executes, of those 3 executes, one of them has to crit, of those that actually crit, it has a 20% chance to refresh it. Let's go through that one more time:

Requires sub 20%->Requires CS to be up(20 second cooldown)->3 execute opportunity->Requires execute to crit(25% chance)->20% chance on your 25% crit execute to refresh.

That's way too many requirements and too low of a chance to be a signifigant dps increase for a 4 piece.

Personally, i believe if you were to change the Hunter 4 set from
"4P -- Your Arcane Shot ability has a chance to grant 25% haste to you and your pet for 10 sec"


To something along the lines of

4P -- Your Arcane Shot and Chimera Shot ability has a chance to grant 25% haste to you and your pet for 10 sec.

Or

4P -- Your Serpent Sting Ticks have a chance to grant 25% haste to you and your pet for 10 sec.

What this does is accommodate the set bonus for all types of players, whether it be because of their spec, play style, or choice of rotation. Obviously the percentage of the proc rate would have to be tweaked to correspond with the change if it were to change to serpent sting ticks.

For example, here are three different rotations that are commonly known by most hunters who have half a brain. First there is the arcane shot as a focus dump, second there is hard casting aimed shot(not using arcane shot At all aside from movement), thirdly there is arcane shot as a dump with aimed shot only being used during RF/lust/heroism. Obviously the current 4 set would be nice for 2 of those 3 rotations, but then the other is boned.

Forcing hunters to weave arcane shot to gain a buff that may not even proc just doesn't gain my interest. There are many different rotations as a hunter some do not even use arcane shot at all like I've stated above. So to implement a set bonus that would force someone to use a shot that is rarely used aside from during movement and in the long run, hindering their performance just because they have to add a shot to the rotation seems rather counterproductive to me.

Now before, someone replies to my opinion, cause that's what it is. My opinion. I'll just make sure i cover two things which i know will be a common response.

First, anyone who says they have focus management problems should possibly consider re-rolling a new class. No hunter that is somewhat decently geared should have any problems managing their focus.

Secondly, Yes i understand that the 2 set is paired with the 4 set, giving you more focus in order to use arcane shot as a focus dump. But that extra focus gained, can be used more productively rather then forcing us to sit there and dump some arcane shots on a target knowingly wasting focus and time to gain a buff that we may not even receive, when in fact that very same focus can be used to dump our normal shots into the target therefore increasing our dps that much more, buy shortening the rotation in a sense. Honestly if you think about it, the T13 2 set is Almost like the T12 4 set, the only difference is its a consistent focus regen rather then a chance on hit free shot, which might i add procs quite a bit. The current 4 set has numerous uses as well, its not limited to one shot, so why should the T13 4 set be limited to arcane shot. When i decide to actually use my T12 4 set, i find myself gravitating towards using that proc for a free chimera or free aimed shot that coupled with the Aimed! proc its rather nice. But I'm going off topic here.

Back on track, really what I'm saying is just include more spec viable shots into the T13 4 set.

I could go into an Even more long winded explanation comparing rotations and stat preferences ect but id rather not, people can go look this stuff up on their own, the information is available from numerous sources.
