09/25/2011 12:02 PMPosted by Diatenium
By 4.3 prot paladins will have so much avoidance that the barrier will actually be more beneficial than you give it credit.


Prot pally avoidance going into 4.3 is going to be somewhere around 40% (5% miss + ~35% dodge/parry). Higher avoidance doesn't make a absorb shield more powerful though. It is more likely to make it less powerful - depending on how long the absorb shield lasts. If a priest casts PWS on a tank, and the tank then gets a long string of parry/dodges the priest just wasted his mana because the shield wears off before he actually took a hit.

As I stated it is a 1-5% damage reduction. Anything that reduces the amount of damage I take is a good thing and ultimately it is small pieces of that all adding together that give tanks survivability. But it isn't a very interesting or fun ability. It isn't something you can look at and go, wow, that actually makes a difference in fights when I got my 2pc (granted, few 2 pc bonuses have every been that big of a deal...). So again, it is a so-so ability. It's not downright bad, but it's not all that great either. I could take it or leave it.


It's also a sign of things to come, you're using an offensive ability to increase your survivability and chances are the 2-set won't work if your judgement misses.


An ability that we use on cooldown isn't very reactive for increasing our survivability. It kind of sort of leads in the direction blizzard wants to go with tanking being more interactive, but it is a long step from that, because we judge on CD (or nearly on CD). They should push for the reactive tanking when they are really ready to make all the changes so it makes sense, not some crippled version of it that doesn't actually change anything about how we play.

Actually, every tank's 4-set does a raid-wide thing, not just the warrior's, and the reason it appears fairly lousy for a prot paladin is because we already have a raid wall, but I think you're not giving the set it's due credit.

One, the ability is separate from your personal cooldowns, a warrior has to burn his shield wall to protect the raid and that means he can't use it for himself at a key time, where as it doesn't affect us and two, the ability is now back to a 2 minuet cooldown whereas a warrior's shield wall is still 3.


I'm aware all of the tank 4 pcs are designed for raid wide damage reduction/healing. It is nice to have it linked to an ability that we use separate from our own survivability. But that is so NOT a fun ability for a 4 pc bonus. It is kind of like a druid bonus that says "Everytime you cast innervate caster DPS in the raid gets a 5% dps boost for 5 seconds, unless you are boomkin specced in which case it does nothing", or "As a mage, every time you cast evocation, the boss takes 5% increased physical damage for 5 seconds". It just doesn't really fit. Its not something you get excited about using.

And as I mentioned tying the warriors raid wall ability to their major CD is just bad. The warrior now has to decide to save it to save the raid or save it to save themselves.
