2 PC bonus represents anywhere from 1-5% damage reduction. Judgement hits for 11-12k non-crit, and over the course of a minute should be used about 6 times. (8 sec cooldown, but 9 second GCD locked, plus being delayed sometimes because AS proc'd). Damage intake can vary wildly from fight to fight. Unfortunately the fights where the damage reduction would be most beneficial are the ones where it represents the least amount of reduction - that is on the bosses that are hard hitting tank fights. A 3k absorb shield every 10 seconds isn't enough to allow a healer not to cast a healing spell on you if you need healing - so it doesn't really free a healer up to allow them to heal someone else in need. I'd put this as a so-so bonus. It's not terrible, but it is also neither interesting nor any reason to take the 2pc bonus if the set pieces are not BiS already.

4 PC needs something new. A major set bonus that does nothing for us personally is not fun at all.

I don't think the warrior model of tying this to our GoAK is a good idea either. You don't want tanks using their major damage reduction CD to reduce incoming raid damage - they need to be able to save that CD for when they are in trouble and need to give the healers some breathing room. Also past experience of raids shows that raid design usually has either heavy tank damage or heavy raid damage. Fights are rare when both heavy raid damage and heavy tank damage are occurring at the same time.

The 4 pc needs to either apply the 20% to the paladin as well, but I'm not to keen on this, we already have a lot of damage reduction abilities for our self. Or tie it to our divine shield (and maybe make the raid get only a 10% reduction while DS is active) or be scrapped in favor of something else entirely.

Likewise the warrior 4 pc needs to be decoupled from their shield wall if it is going to provide raid damage reduction.


By 4.3 prot paladins will have so much avoidance that the barrier will actually be more beneficial than you give it credit.

It's also a sign of things to come, you're using an offensive ability to increase your survivability and chances are the 2-set won't work if your judgement misses.

Actually, every tank's 4-set does a raid-wide thing, not just the warrior's, and the reason it appears fairly lousy for a prot paladin is because we already have a raid wall, but I think you're not giving the set it's due credit.

One, the ability is separate from your personal cooldowns, a warrior has to burn his shield wall to protect the raid and that means he can't use it for himself at a key time, where as it doesn't affect us and two, the ability is now back to a 2 minuet cooldown whereas a warrior's shield wall is still 3.
