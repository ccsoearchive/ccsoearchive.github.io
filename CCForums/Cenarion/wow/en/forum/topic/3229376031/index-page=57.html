I love Blizzard's approach on the bonuses this tier from a tanking perspective. They've been talking for a while how they want to make tanking more reliant on "active" mitigation rather than "passive" mitigation, but they've also said that they don't want to force that large of a play style change during an expansion. I think this approach of adding some more active things like, "Protection, 2P -- Your Revenge ability now also grants a physical absorption shield equal to 20% of the damage done by Revenge." is a great way to compromise between changing the mechanics now or waiting till the next x-pack!

Although I do have to say, as much as I love that they're adding some active mitigation for the other tanking classes, I'm slightly saddened that all they came up with were different skills to add physical absorb shields (judgement, revenge, and whatever the druid one was) seeing a form of absorb is the DK and Druid mastery. I just get a stale feeling in my mouth when I see my unique-tanking style being copied by classes that also have block mastery - I have absorb, and they get absorb and block now.

Not saying it's a bad change, I am very happy with it - I just hope in the next expansion when they switch to active mitigation that they don't simply give absorbs to all tanking classes' main tanking skills. Maybe something like active self-heals for DKs, active dodge and avoidance for Druids, active absorbs & block for Warriors, and active non-block-based mitigation & stronger tanking debuffs for Paladins? The first three seem to make perfect sense to me as heals, avoidance, and soaking hits are respectively what DKs, Druids, and Warriors are good at. The Paladin idea makes sense to me because they aren't as "tough" as warriors, so less soaking is fine - but they're HOLY protectors, they should be able to smite an enemy's offensive powers down a good bit.

Anyways, those are my thoughts - just trying to participate in a discussion. :3
