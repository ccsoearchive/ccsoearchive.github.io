There's a couple problems the priests on EJ see on the Healing Priest t13 that I thought I'd
post here.

Both Specs: Two piece proccing from Divine Hymn is pretty meh, considering you'll lose 8 seconds of reduced mana cost DURING the hymn. That means disc will have TWO seconds of reduced mana cost after finishing the channel.

Disc:

The two piece (assuming it is multiplicative like every other % bonus is this game) will lose us about 5% mana cost due to PI reducing mana cost, and the 2set. If it's additive, well then.

The four piece is terrible. You can't time a 10% chance during the 12 second rapture ICD on a target you KNOW will break the bubble - actually giving you the rapture mana back.

For instance, I could throw a shield on the tank every CD, but there's no guarantee that he will get the double bonus, or that it will break during the double rapture ICD.
Then if someone spikes low enough to need a shield, that shield could proc the double absorb, but they might not take 60kish damage to break it.

Holy:

Four Piece: A 20% longer sanctuary is situational at best. 3.6 seconds extra on a placed AOE heal, which is not nearly as large as healing rain, can be hard to actually maximize. Sure there are fights where the raid can be stacked for 21 second, but those are few and far between in encounters. In addition, Sanctuary is not very high on priests overall healing, except for fights that demand constant stacking. Compare that to the chance to proc double duration rejuvs... Druids number 1 healing spell....

Compare that to a 30% haste buff for shaman, Flat 20% more healing on HR for the entire encounter, and a chance to proc double duration rejuvs (which is spammed in 25s), and you can maybe see where I'm coming from. The 4p affects two healers bread and butter spells (new HR = amgawd), while the other healer gets an entirely new cool down out of it.

Priest

  • Healer, 2P -- After using Power Infusion or Divine Hymn, the mana cost of your healing spells is reduced by 25% for [10|23] sec. (10 sec for Discipline, 23 for non-Discipline.)
  • Healer, 4P -- Your Power Word: Shield has a 10% chance to absorb 100% additional damage and increase the mana granted by Rapture by 100%, and the duration of your Holy Word abilities is increased by 20%.


Please take a hard quantitative look at the 2pc/4pc bonuses for Holy Priests compared to 4pc/2pc bonuses on Firelands gear.

With Firelands gear, the 2pc Divine Fire proc was close enough in value to Chakra Flow that we could upgrade asap without much stress over mana. Also, the awesome 4pc Cauterizing Flame was something everyone could look forward to without a backward glance at the laughable Heal +crit bonus.

In contrast, the proposed 2pc bonus on the new tier provides no throughput and a really quite questionable mana bonus (what if DH leaves everyone at full?), and simply isn't in the same league as a 55k+ free smart healing proc that happens more often during a fight.

As some others have noted, the intent of the new 4pc bonus seems kind of hazy and not very valuable. My experience in Firelands heroic modes is basically that "Hah hah, now you can't stack!", but you want to make the new tier's 'awesome' 4pc effect improve my stack-healing-only spell? In any event, stack healing is one of the things Holy Priests are already good at, and it would be nice for the 4pc to not be limited to those situations. This is another thing the Firelands 4pc did well.

Also, should any class designers be laboring under the misconception that HW:Serenity provides a good direct healing buff please communicate the following to them:

Even assuming the buff actually works and isn't bugged (which is not a safe assumption), the crit bonus is low enough and short enough that for a short healing window it has a significant chance of not improving healing done. Compounding this is the fact that HW:Serenity heals for less than Flash Heal, and, crucially, does not stack serendipity like Flash Heal. This means in any short healing window you always heal more reliably with pure FH/GH and, unless you get very lucky, heal for more than if you tried to cast and take advantage of the HW:Serenity buff. Actually, I think the failure of this mechanic is a large part of the truth behind the perception of Holy's tank healing issues, but that's getting into another discussion... The point here is we have a good reason for reason for reading a 20% duration buff to HW abilities as only affecting HW:Sanctuary - it almost doesn't matter for HW:Serenity.

As things stand I'm really not looking forward to 'upgrading' from my firelands gear and losing my cool healing mortar. =(
