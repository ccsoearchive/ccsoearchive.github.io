DPS, 2P -- Sudden Doom has a 30% chance and Rime has a 60% chance to grant 2 charges when triggered instead of 1.
DPS, 4P -- Runic Empowerment has a 25% chance and Runic Corruption has a 40% chance to also grant 710 mastery rating for 12 sec when activated.


After multitudes of Frost DKs providing feedback in the class forum saying that we are tired of having a majority of our damage tied to RNG, the new tier bonuses introduce more RNG. I know this is pre-PTR and all but I really don't like the direction this is starting out at.

With our current rotation there are really not a lot of open GCDs to use the extra charge of Howling Blast. The extra charge of Howling Blast will also compete with Killing Machine procs for GCD. When Killing Machine procs it is in our best interest to use it as soon as possible to not waste potential Killing Machine procs to comes afterwards. With the proposed 2pc bonus, I will potentially sit on Killing Machine procs longer to dump extra Howling Blast charges if both Rime and Killing Machine procs close to each other. This cuts into the GCD that I could’ve used to dump my Killing Machine procs which in turn causes me to lose potential Killing Machine procs and dps. I just don’t feel there is a great deal of synergy between the current Frost "rotation" and the T13 set bonuses.

The 4pc bonus also feels lackluster from a Frost DK point of view. I know some people are confusing Runic Empowerment (40% chance to refresh a fully depleted rune when using Frost Strike, Death Coil, or Rune Strike) with Empower Rune Weapon (refreshes all runes and generate 25 Runic Power; 5 minute cooldown). But making our bonus a proc only adds to the randomness of our damage, not to mention that 4pc proc is tied to another proc. This proc on proc pattern with many of the T13 bonuses feels redundant and Frost DKs don’t need more RNG in their damage.

In contrast, the 4pc bonus is more beneficial for Unholy DKs as they at least have a higher percentage chance at proc’ing the 4pc bonus and mastery benefits a majority of their attacks. After skimming the replies on this thread, it feels as if Blizzard is trying to balance out different specs within the same class by making class bonuses more beneficial for one spec than the other. For example the Rogue’s bonus penalizes Combat Rogues but is beneficial for the other two Rogue specs. I am not sure I can agree with this form of balancing if indeed this is what Blizzard is trying to accomplish with the T13 set bonuses.

If the developers want more players to pick up classes that are unpopular at the moment, the best solution is to find out the reason why they are unpopular and fix it so that they are more compelling to play. Making the dominant spec (Frost) less attractive in hopes of people will pick up a less popular spec (Unholy) is a lousy solution that does nothing to tackle the underlying issues surrounding the unpopular specs.
