The enhancement set bonuses are good from a numbers standpoint. They're stronger than the T12 bonuses, and that's appropriate when you consider that enhancement's T12 bonuses are fairly weak.

Many people have brought up the fact that the set bonuses encourage hard-casting lightning bolt with less than 5 stacks of maelstrom weapon. I can't emphasize this enough: please disregard any and all suggestions that people make in an effort to limit the 2-set bonus to 5-stack maelstrom weapon. Doing so will result in a significant number of wasted maelstrom charges every time we use feral spirits in combination with the 4-set.

* EDIT 1: Even with hard-casting lightning bolt, a large portion of the maelstrom charges we gain from the 4-set bonus are wasted (based on preliminary simulation results over on elitistjerks). We simply don't have enough spare GCDs to fire off that many additional lightning bolts, and that puts the value of the 4-set bonus significantly lower than first anticipated (only about ~0.9% extra DPS, which seems to be significantly lower than the target most set bonuses achieve of +2-3%).

* EDIT 2: It's worth emphasizing that the results on elitistjerks are still preliminary. There's potential for gains to the 4-set bonus in response to modifying the priority list. Still, it's probably worth keeping an eye on this set bonus' in-game performance relative to its "intended" performance.

As a raid healing captain, who tries to understand what the various healing specializations bring to the table, here are my initial impressions.

First let me say that, mana reduction tier bonuses don't really make healers stand up and cheer. Yeah, I know it's part of the whole cataclysm theme make healers try to manage their mana, but managing mana is not why healers decided they want to heal.

As a raiding Discipline Priest I strongly dislike both of the bonuses. As discipline only the first 10 seconds of the 15 second Power Infusion has an increased mana reduction. As a Disc Priest, I rarely use Power Infusion for the mana reduction. I use it for the haste effect. Reviewing my logs, I'd say that this 2P bonus won't help me at all. It won't increase my healing and I can only use it once every 2 minutes. I'll be "fortunate" if I see it used twice in an encounter. 4 piece -- looking at some of my logs over the past month -- if I'm lucky I might get an additional 0.5-0.7% of mitigation based healing. Numerically, this is pathetic for a 4P for the final tier of content in an expansion. Spam shielding is not a viable strategy and healers don't like RNG effects they can't control. In all liklihood the rare time I can afford to cast a shield on a non-tank is the one time it will proc in a fight and be entirely overheal.

The way holy priest currently works, any mana reduction they can get is a benefit, but this one won't be. Some Holy Priests don't bother speccing Lightwell, others only use it before the start of a pull or during low damage when they can afford to spend the global trying to place it. They will not be getting any useful return on investment with how this tier set is structured. 4p for holy would be a little less disappointing, if Blizzard first re-tunes Holy so it's competitive and in the process beefs up both of the Holy Word spells so they are worth casting on cooldown. Holy is currently only brought if you need raid healing and there weren't any druids or shaman available. A raiding Holy is typically only found in the Chakra Sanctuary. However the only time they cast Sanctuary is if they have extra mana and the raid doesn't actually need healing immediately but hopefully might need some in the near future. Holy Priests are not casting Sanctuary on cooldown, and for a 4P bonus you're decreasing the cooldown?

Is it bad if Holy Paladins are looking at their ret counterparts jealously for the 2P bonus? (Ret would gladly give it to them by the way.) In contrast, it looks like the Paladin 4P should offer about 2% boost to their healing, assuming they do about 10% of their healing with Holy Radiance. Again, not exactly exciting, but it does strengthen their raid healing which is a good thing. However most paladins will probably hate this bonus because if they're still healing as a paladin it's because they enjoy tank healing, so this bonus does nothing to appeal to their interests.

Restoration Shaman may or may not have been screwed. Tying their 4P to a 2-minute cooldown is disheartening on the surface, but if it turns out that Spiritwalker's Grace becomes a key mechanic to dealing with movement in the next tier content, it might be a blessing in disguise. However if there is a mechanic that requires them to use this, the other healers are going to be severely lacking. So in general, I'd be surprised if raiding shaman are happy to see their new 4P bonus.

Druid 4P probably looked good on paper to somebody at Blizzard but having Timeslip occasionally double the duration of Regrowth and Rejuv will most likely just mean that the spells will be either overwritten or entirely become overheals. Regrowth ticks are already pretty pathetic, doubling the duration is a lot less useful then doubling the amount of the tick. Rejuv is a fire and forget, and druids anticipate that the last few ticks won't ever be useful because their targets will be full health by other spells.

I honestly think that they have some dps person who doesn't really understand the motivations and desires of healers designing the healer set bonuses. After some really lackluster bonuses so far in Cataclysm, I was really hoping that they were saving the best for the final tier.

To summarize: scrap them all, find someone who actually understands the motivations behind why raid healers choose the role they've selected for themselves and try to reward them for sticking with you through Cataclysm.
