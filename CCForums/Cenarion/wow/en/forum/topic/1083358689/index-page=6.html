World PvP can't work because PvP in general can't work in World of Warcraft.

There is too much discrepancy in power between players of different gear and character levels.

PvP is only meaningful if there's a chance of losing or winning. A level 81 player could never hope to contribute in a meaningful way to a PvP breakout involving lvl 85 players, nevermind all the level 1-80 players.

If I see that Goldshire is under attack, I'm assuming its a max-geared Horde contingency, and unless I happen to be on one of my max-geared characters, I know that any effort I make will be futile and meaningless.

Nobody will respond to any kind of call to participate in a known world PvP event without knowledge of the level of competition.

Blizzard answered this by having battlegrounds and max-level zones designated for PvP, to help the competition remain balanced (which still doesn't work).

So in the end, no matter what mechanics, rewards, or information systems you introduce, world PvP will remain viable only among max-level characters with reasonable gear, who might as well be participating in existing PvP zones and events, since it already offers what is being proposed for WPvP.

Sorry to burst the bubble.

To fix world PvP here is what I think would be needed.

1) Honor-giving npcs in some towns.
- Npcs like Rayne Wolfrunner, who are elite and hard to kill (or at least, she was hard to kill back at level 60). They would call for help and some would not spawn until enough guards were killed. To be fair the respawn would have to be about 1 day.


Having certain guards, no matter how elite and hard to kill they may be, produce honor when killed is just going encourage farming of these elites. This type of World PvP is indirect, as you are killing an NPC for your honor, not a player.

2) Bonus honor for defending towns.
- Players who save lowbies from being griefed and protect their own towns should gain signifigantly mroe honor points then someone who is just ganking a random level 85 leveling herbalism. I think a buff similar to honorable defender should be given out to players who are inside their own towns or near allied questgivers.


Very nice suggestion.

3) Flying combat
- Some sort of system where you can use your own flying mount (or just normal ranged attacks) to force an enemy player to dismount from their own flying mount. You already made a similar suggestion but I think it would be far more fun if each mount class (Dragon, Gryphon, Proto-drake, wyvern, ect) had their own set of 3 or 4 abilities which can only be used on players riding flying mounts. The player who forces the other to dismount would gain a slight upper hand in the fight, so as to maybe convince the other player to land early and give an advantage to the victor (I think the person who has their mount defeated should either be stunned or lose 20% of their maximum health (but no fall damage)).


I like it, giving each flying mount its own unique set of abilities is a fantastic idea.

4) Rewards
- Killing special npcs on the other faction and players outside of battlegrounds should grant a form of currency that gives fun "Alliance vs Horde" stuff. I like the idea of a mark or something for every time you kill a player or certain elite npcs, and these marks could be used to buy weapons and armor (probably rare items, not uncommon or epic) that looked like town guards, and maybe some "-use" items that can summon alliance or horde guards to protect you from players ect. It wouldn't have to be high-end gear or overpowered items but something fun and maybe even useful would be great. Though, I still am a firm believer that capital city leaders should have a pool of loot that can drop from them or be earned for saving them.
