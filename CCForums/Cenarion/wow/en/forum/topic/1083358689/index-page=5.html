To fix world PvP here is what I think would be needed.

1) Honor-giving npcs in some towns.
- Npcs like Rayne Wolfrunner, who are elite and hard to kill (or at least, she was hard to kill back at level 60). They would call for help and some would not spawn until enough guards were killed. To be fair the respawn would have to be about 1 day.

2) Bonus honor for defending towns.
- Players who save lowbies from being griefed and protect their own towns should gain signifigantly mroe honor points then someone who is just ganking a random level 85 leveling herbalism. I think a buff similar to honorable defender should be given out to players who are inside their own towns or near allied questgivers.

3) Flying combat
- Some sort of system where you can use your own flying mount (or just normal ranged attacks) to force an enemy player to dismount from their own flying mount. You already made a similar suggestion but I think it would be far more fun if each mount class (Dragon, Gryphon, Proto-drake, wyvern, ect) had their own set of 3 or 4 abilities which can only be used on players riding flying mounts. The player who forces the other to dismount would gain a slight upper hand in the fight, so as to maybe convince the other player to land early and give an advantage to the victor (I think the person who has their mount defeated should either be stunned or lose 20% of their maximum health (but no fall damage)).

4) Rewards
- Killing special npcs on the other faction and players outside of battlegrounds should grant a form of currency that gives fun "Alliance vs Horde" stuff. I like the idea of a mark or something for every time you kill a player or certain elite npcs, and these marks could be used to buy weapons and armor (probably rare items, not uncommon or epic) that looked like town guards, and maybe some "-use" items that can summon alliance or horde guards to protect you from players ect. It wouldn't have to be high-end gear or overpowered items but something fun and maybe even useful would be great. Though, I still am a firm believer that capital city leaders should have a pool of loot that can drop from them or be earned for saving them.

I had posted this idea a while back and got no real response. It's a rare occasion wall of text posts get any real traction when they're not complaints or rants.

~~~~~~~~~


Cataclysm really reinforced the idea that there's a war going on between Alliance and Horde in Old Azeroth. Some zones were drastically altered to accentuate this point. Areas like Ashenvale, Stonetalon Mountains, and Southern Barrens are filled with quests almost entirely about fighting the opposing faction.

Open PVP was a damn fun thing back in vanilla, and battlegrounds have since killed it. Given Cataclysm's many zone changes, it needs to come back. I propose this:

In "Contested Zones" like Ashenvale, all, some, or one of the outposts and an opposing faction counterpart would have repeatable PVP-oriented PvE quests. They would offer some modest rewards, even a bit of honor if you do each one or repeat them often. So many of these would need to be done to prepare for an assault on an opposing outpost. Southern Barrens, for example, would pit the two outposts bordering the Battlescar against each other. Once the assault is prepared, the zone is notified, and a group of say three players can trigger the assault itself. There's a five minute preparation window allowing others to join the attack and defenders to prepare before it begins once initiated.

When the assault officially begins, the attackers have only so much time to complete a tier of objectives. "Gain Control of {Area}" might be Objective #1 and they have five minutes to do it. Then they have another five minutes for, "Defeat XX Outpost Guards" and then another five minutes for "Defeat Outpost Leader" or something similar. Once the assault is over, "Spoils of War" vendors spawn that, through a special currency or quest system, allow players to buy gear, resources, recipes, honor, and other goodies that were pilfered and pillaged. A possible implementation would be that killing players and guards during the assault provides players automatically with the Spoils of War currency and the winners are also given some. These vendors despawn after 15 minutes or so. Once despawned, another assault can be made in the zone once preparations are completed.

If the attacking side wins, the opposing outpost is "held" for a short period of time. Defenders can reclaim it sooner by defeating the NPC assault leader that now spawns and this will provide a Spoils of War vendor for the defenders once the outpost is reclaimed. If the assault NPC isn't killed, powerful reinforcements eventually drive them out, the outpost is restored, but no Spoils of War vendors spawn for the defenders. Spoils of War vendors provide more and better merchandise based on the success of each side. They don't spawn for attackers if no objectives are completed nor for defenders if the outpost is claimed and the assault NPC isn't killed. This balance of available spoils then depends on how well the attack went or how well the defenders worked. Naturally, the most spoils go to the attackers if they take the outpost the keep the assault NPC alive or to the defenders if they prevent the completion of any objectives.

In order to participate in this system, you must be within the level and iLevel cap of the zone. If you outlevel the zone, you cannot participate in PVP nor the assault prep quests until you speak to a "deleveling" NPC for that zone. Similar to City of Heroes' sidekick system, you'd level down to the cap and your stats, effectiveness of gear, and available talents and abilities would then reflect the level and iLevel cap of the zone. Leaving the zone or speaking to the deleveling NPC again restores your level. This prevents high level griefing but allows high levels to participate.

We need an open pvp system. I really miss how fun it was.

You've hit it on the head: there's no incentive for players to seek out world pvp instead of simply pressing "H" and letting the system find you teammates, opponents, and a place to fight.

That incentive could take the form of some combination of positive or negative reinforcements:

-One important thing I think is that negative reinforcements in a game will not produce the desired outcome. IE if you tell alliance players if the horde sacks goldshire, then they'll all receive 5% less honor across the board, the natural reaction will be to simply stop pvping.

-Another important thing is that on many servers, world populations are out of balance, and players are well aware of it (perhaps even perceiving the imbalance as more than it is). While the horde on Thrall would welcome another free honor faucet like Wintergrasp, realistically it's unfair to the underdog to provide incentives in a way that the overpopulated faction receives disproportionately.


Now, what I think would be a WONDERFUL fix for all this, would be for faction leaders to do a call to arms every 3 hours or so, where one would basically call a crusade on one of the other's towns. During said CTA, forward camps could appear on each side, siege weapons could become available for lowbies to participate, guards would spawn for both sides to balance numbers, guards could award honor, player kills could award increased honor, and the winning faction could get a roll on some pvp epics similar to Baradin Hold.

Now you could tweak the mechanics a bit as needed - maybe siege weapons don't need to be involved, or an anti-flying field needs to be raised for the duration. But to me this would solve the three major things plaguing world PVP atm:
-The rewards are less than BGs, while taking considerably more effort to organize.
-There is no consistent "place" for world pvp to meet. In other words as the world has expanded, there's no SS vs TM hotspot anymore.
-Faction imbalance makes it much less likely for both sides to want to show up
