Perhaps the debates should work similar to the old question-and-answer sessions?
Any who wish to speak a point on the subject line up, and may say however much in opinion to subject and other responses?

As question-and-answer was limited by a one-question rule, these would likely have to be limited to prevent plodding speeches from forming as others stand in line. I don't see harm in a Kyalin-moderated approximate length, each to be halted by our Warden if they drag on too long. (After all, it is her event)

In such a case, I feel that the line-based turns work better than the hand-raise order, as I can see people missing their call in text, and left without a " I'm at the front of the line now, it must be my turn" reminder; especially as speakers would likely be taking longer to end their turns, and one's position in a queue could be forgotten or lost in a sea of chat.

However, with an established order of speech, I'd predict there to be less confusion, as the text could be read in a more linear manner, rather than having to sort through Guy #1 and Guy #2 talking back and forth, while one or both are also responding to another person(s).

Still could come worry, and inevitable happening, of people speaking out of turn.
Verbal correction would be helpful, but if you happen to want to hire some mercenaries as manner-bouncers.... (I'm joking about that, of course. A little.)

I hope that a satisfying system can be found, so we may keep enjoying the forums.

All right. This week is going to start with yet another debate, with apologies to the gnomes:

http://i1092.photobucket.com/albums/i415/Kyalin_Raintree/Darnassianforum-9.png

I'm changing the debate format again, hopefully for the better. I will lead us off with a short address, laying down the ground rules and casting a hopefully more focused lens on the prompt before the debate itself begins.

The debate format will go as follows. The first person who wishes to speak will take the floor at my indication, and give an opinion not longer than three complete posts. This should be more than enough space, but I want to be generous. After answering three questions (yes, we are moving back to the line. One question per questioner, no follow-ups or two-parts) the cycle will repeat with the next person.

As for why I'm giving up follow-ups and two-parts, just about everyone is going to have them, whether the speaker 'dodged the question' or not. What I don't want is a back and forth between two people. I'd like to get more participation from others.

So, the event again is scheduled for this Sunday, August 5th, at 7:00pm in the usual spot.
