(UPDATE 9/1: Added something about the accidental addition of untrainable Mutilate and Mutilate Off-Hand spells with Patch 4.2.2.)

This thread lists all official and as-yet-undocumented Patch 4.1 and Patch 4.2 rogue-specific adjustments, without bothering with all the useless clutter that's relevant to all of the inferior classes. (Patch 4.2 stuff is in post #2.)

Rogue Changes That *Are Not* in the 4.1 Patch Notes (Yet)
(I'll list them as "confirmed" if reliable sources report that it's legit, or if other folks report it and I can personally corroborate it.)

  • Melee openers -- Ambush, Cheap Shot, Garrote (but not Sap) -- can now be used against Camouflaged hunters. (Reported by several)
  • Traps laid by a hunter while in Camouflage can now be disarmed. (Reported by Kyrrion)
  • Revealing Strike now correctly increases the damage of Deadly Throw. (Reported by Celinne)
  • Sap can no longer be used/refreshed on an NPC that has entered combat but has yet to begin its attack. (Confirmed)
  • The cooldown of Stealth begins when a rogue enters stealth mode, rather than when s/he leaves it. (Confirmed, but this will be short-lived: According to the current official patch notes for 4.2, when that patch drops, the stealth cooldown will once again begin when a rogue leaves stealth.)
  • Shadowmeld (night elf racial) now immediately drops the caster from combat in both PvE and PvP environments, even when in a party/raid. Rogues with this talent can thus use it as a quick re-entry into stealth mode.(Reported by several)
  • When you enter stealth, you will be unable to voluntarily unstealth for 2 seconds. This "stuck in stealth" cooldown is not triggered when you use Vanish, however, and stealth-breaking effects will still work. (As noted by Dert and Mif, this is caused by a UI setting that you can remove by opening up the options menu in the game, selecting Interface, then Action Bars, and unchecking the "Secure Ability Toggle" box.)
  • Non-combat companion pets now stealth with the rogue, though to the rogue the pet will appear unstealthed. (Confirmed; first reported by Tantojutsu)
  • Smoke Bomb has a new sound. It is far less burpy, and far more smoke-bomb-y. Oddly, the same sound can be heard when switching specs. (Confirmed)
  • Some rogue gear has received a design overhaul. Among the changes: Many off-hand daggers appear smaller, Vicious Gladiator's gear appears more rogueishly ominous, and the Belt of Nefarious Whispers now looks like something you get for becoming the world's undisputed heavyweight boxing champion. (Reported by several)
  • For additional changes of particular relevance for Assassination PvE rogues, check out Verelyse's sticky: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/1658707015?page=1#2


Rogue Changes That *Are* in the 4.1 Patch Notes
(These are copy/pasted directly from the official patch notes.)

General
  • Recuperate base effect now heals 3% per tick, up from 2%.
  • Stealth base cooldown has been reduced to 4 seconds, down from 10, and the movement penalty from being in Stealth has been removed. (Ruffy's note: The patch notes are incorrect here. The base cooldown is 6 seconds, not 4.)
  • Tricks of the Trade now has a 100-yard range, up from 20.
  • Talent Specializations
    • Combat
      • Improved Recuperate now adds 0.5%/1% to the health restoration effect of Recuperate, down from 1%/2%.
    • Subtlety
      • Cheat Death now reduces damage taken by 80% while in effect, down from 90%. Its internal cooldown has been raised to 90 seconds, up from 60.
      • Nightstalker now reduces the cooldown of Stealth by 2/4 seconds, and instead of negating the movement penalty from Stealth, it adds a 5/10% movement speed bonus (stacking with other effects) while Stealthed.
  • Rogue Bug Fixes
    • Kick can no longer be blocked.
    • Certain non-damaging AoE attacks cast by NPCs will no longer break Stealth.
    • Hostile pets should now be less inclined to follow rogues that are in Stealth.

    Patch 4.2: Official Rogue Changes
    (Anything in quotes, unless otherwise noted, has been copy/pasted directly from the official patch notes or hotfix notes.)

    • Spec-Specific Changes:
      • Assassination
        • "Assassin's Resolve damage bonus has been increased to 20%, up from 15%."
        • "Vile Poisons now increases poison damage by 12/24/36%, up from 7/14/20%."
      • Combat
        • "Savage Combat now increases attack power by 3/6%, up from 2/4%."
        • "Vitality now increases attack power by 30%, up from 25%."
      • Subtlety
        • "Elusiveness now reduces the cooldown of Cloak of Shadows by 15/30 seconds, up from 10/20, and now also reduces the cooldown of Combat Readiness by 15/30 seconds."
        • "Hemorrhage base damage has been increased by 40%."
        • "Sanguinary Vein damage to bleeding targets has been increased to 8/16%, from 5/10%."
    • "Cloak of Shadows cooldown is now 2 minutes, up from 90 seconds. In addition, Cloak of Shadows is no longer on the global cooldown."
    • "Combat Readiness and Cloak of Shadows now share a cooldown."
    • "Blind duration against enemy NPCs is now 1 minute, while the duration against players remains 8 seconds."
    • "Stealth now once again begins its cooldown once the rogue leaves Stealth."
    • Hunter pets have lost their "aggressive" stance, which means they will no longer automatically target and attack you if they spot you while you are stealthed.
    • In a PvP-related hotfix on July 6, this is listed in the Druid section: "Faerie Fire and Faerie Fire (Feral) will now remove Stealth, Invisibility, Vanish, Shadowmeld and Prowl from players."
    • Ghostcrawler explains a few of the 4.2 changes: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2416158935?page=7#127
    • Trinket-related post-patch hotfixes:
      • Ricket's Magnetic Fireball now shares a burst cooldown with other on-use trinkets.
      • Unheeded Warning now grants 1926 attack power, instead of 680 weapon damage.
    • The Tier 12 set is here!
      • It's pretty wicked cool-looking (all you haters can suck it): http://www.wowheadnews.com/blog=188631/patch-4-2-tier-12-armor-variations-and-3d-models
      • 2-piece set bonus: "Your melee critical strikes deal 6% additional damage as Fire over 4 sec."
      • Zarhym: "The Fire damage-over-time effects from some set bonuses work like Ignite, and will accumulate and refresh damage just as it does."
      • 4-piece set bonus: "Your Tricks of the Trade ability also causes you to gain a 25% increase to one of your combat ratings at random for 30 sec."
      • Zarhym: "The rogue 4-piece bonus never picks hit or expertise, and never picks the same rating twice in a row."
    • Verelyse has compiled this excellent rundown of all rogue gear that has become available with Patch 4.2 (and how to get it): http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2721374612


    Patch 4.2: Unofficial Rogue Changes
    (Stuff that has only been noted by players)
    • Not sure when this happened, but apparently, night elf rogues can now use all the abilities in Shadowmeld that they can use in regular stealth: http://elitistjerks.com/f78/t122173-4_2_changes_discussion/#post1984586


    Patch 4.2: Bugs and Known Issues
    • As of Patch 4.2.2 on Aug. 30, "Mutilate" and "Mutilate Off-Hand" now appear as trainable spells for virtually all rogues. This is a bug, and will be fixed in a future patch; those spells are not supposed to be there and cannot be trained, as noted by Blizzard here: http://bit.ly/oZHIAL
    • Lots of rogues are reporting issues with stealth and Vanish. Prob the best place to go to add your own reports is this thread in the Bug Report forum, which includes a blue post confirming something's up with Vanish and asking for more info on stealth in general: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2795122866
