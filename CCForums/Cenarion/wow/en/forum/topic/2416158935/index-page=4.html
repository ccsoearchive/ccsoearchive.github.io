    Enough people have posted about this now (see http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2416159042 for more) that it's becoming harder to discount, but I still have a hard time wrapping my head around it or accepting that it's an unreported change (or even truly a change at all). We've got a smattering of anecdotal evidence, but we also know that in PvP resilience has been significantly altered and many classes' defensive capabilities changed, and we also know that in PvE or PvP, RNG can be RNG.

    Couple that with the fact that we all are going to be scrutinizing our numbers much more intently in the days immediately following a patch than we had before... It's just a hard thing to really get a handle on.

    In my mind, the only way to really prove a change one way or the other is to use exactly the same gear, enchants, gems, buffs, etc., that you were using before the patch, then go to a training dummy and spam Backstab on it for an hour. Then look at your numbers, and compare them to an identical experiment you ran before the patch hit. That way you can (ideally) remove any possible variables from the equation, and just look at whether Backstab in and of itself is any different.

    Obviously, that's a bit of a tall order, especially because it may involve time travel to be able to put together that pre-patch data. But other than that, I think the best we can do is wait for data to keep rolling in from raidbots and stateofdps and see if, according to their (admittedly flawed) measurements, Sub PvE has somehow seen a huge DPS spike since the patch that hasn't been matched by other classes.
