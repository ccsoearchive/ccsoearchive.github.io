Just gonna toss this in here. Battle of/for w/e Gilneas could use some fixes.

1. The score should go to 1600, not 2000. Unlike it's counterparts, AB and EotS (which are both 1600), Battle of Gilneas suffers from a small number of point nodes. It's not an issue of the number of points per tic, it's the issue that they are constantly in contest. And when a node is in contest, nobody is gaining any points for it.

2. It's like AB for terribles. Instead of juggling 3 nodes, thinking about going for a 4th, needing to know where to pressure etc, in Battle for Gilneas there's 2 nodes to think about. So teams just run back and forth between their 2 nodes, with a zerg, and whichever team has the better zerg (better healers) wins.

Everything about the map promotes zergs. Attackers that die are sent back across the map, which in itself the distance isn't unmanageable, but when you tack on an average 15 seconds of sitting in the GY, and then on top of that when you spawn at the Light House >50% of the time you actually spawn at the starter GY which adds another 10 seconds to your journey, the attacker is constantly at a huge disadvantage.

So defenders have a huge amount of time to zerg a node, and then make it back to their other node before anything is wrong because the attackers are suffering through such a longer amount of time before being able to get back into the action.

I used to think that SotA and IoC were bad. Now I realize the worst BG in the game is Battle for Gilneas. Honestly, it's probably the closest thing to team death match in the game, and really highlights why this game cannot have a team death match battle ground.

a DotA-like BG would be (imo) a good idea. it is inherently balanced. just have a main base that spawns 2-3 troops every 10 seconds that run towards the enemy base (maybe have two lanes since it would have to be 10v10) and fight the opposing faction's troops in the center until the players arrive to turn the tide of battle. have towers in the lanes similar to AV towers that can be burned down after holding them for x minutes. getting x amount of kills or looting armor off of the opposing faction's troops would let you upgrade your troops armor/health/attack/speed etc. (similar to AV except no one actually does that anymore). once your troops reach the enemies base the players must kill the commander in the barracks to stop troops spawning in that lane. the barracks commander would have guards relative to the number of towers still alive in his lane that would buff him (again similar to AV). once you have destroyed your enemies barracks in your lane, your troops would then either assault the main base (which could have a garrison of say 100 troops to help the losing team not instantly lose once their barracks is gone) or attack the other barracks to help the other lane(s). during the battle, the players will be right next to their troops, killing the enemies footmen but also constantly encountering the enemy players, since they are attempting to give their forces the advantage in a lane as well.

in addition to upgrading your forces troops, one could opt to send out support units, siege weapons that (if they make it to an enemy tower) decrease the time it takes to burn down by 5sec per hit the weapon gets off (most likely needs to be tweaked for balance). a bomber could be bought once per team that eliminates ALL troops, and players with less than say 30% health, in ONE lane.

TL;DR
make DotA a BG

The other part of the reason is that Battlegrounds (and Arena maps, for that matter) require a higher degree of polish than a PvE map does. If a bit of geometry causes you to hang up, or blocks line of sight in a dungeon or raid, then in the majority of circumstances you can probably just work around it until it's fixed. If the same flaw exists in a bg or arena map, then that imperfection might be exploited for an unfair advantage, which runs contrary to the spirit of fair competition we want in that kind of PvP.
