Hunter Beast Master Macro Compilation 6.0.3

Firstly, I will say that I am NOT a master of macros. Many of these I found online, and some I either updated or altered to my own preference. I apologize if there are already big lists of Hunter macros on the forum, but the only lists I found were on other websites.

This is dedicated to the Beast Master Hunters, so don't expect to see any Marksman Specific macros here because there are none.

I also recommend using Macro Toolkit because it's the bomb.
http://www.curse.com/addons/wow/macro-toolkit

Now for the macros, I'll try to explain the best I can what each of them does, and what the benefit of them is. Let's start off with Kill Shot.

Kill Shot
#showtooltip
/stopcasting
/stopcasting
/cancelaura Deterrence
/use Kill Shot
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

When your target is under 20% HP, Kill Shot will start flashing. It does massive damage, and becomes top priority when available. You also gain HP if you kill the enemy with the shot. /stopcasting is written twice, because the first one stops your autoshot. And the second one stops any casting, like Cobra Shot. /cancelaua Deterrence will remove the buff from you, because if you have Deterrence on, you can't attack. At the end /run UIErrorsFrame:Clear() is there so you don't have a constant spam about the spell not being ready yet.

Kill Command
#showtooltip
/stopcasting
/stopcasting
/use Kill Command
/startattack
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

This is basically the same as Kill Shot. Kill Command is another top priority spell, so you never want to waste possible cooldown time while you are casting Cobra Shot. /startattack is at the end of this so that the player starts autocasting on the target after Kill Command is cast. This is useful for when you send your pet in first with Kill Command.

Counter Shot
#showtooltip
/stopcasting
/stopcasting
/cancelaura Deterrence
/use Counter Shot
/startattack
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

This again is very similar to the above two. It cancels any casting and kills off Deterrence so that you can cast Counter Shot. This is very handy when you need to stop a the targets heal or spell, because now you won't be stuck midway through Cobra Shot.

Focus Fire
#showtooltip
/stopcasting
/stopcasting
/cancelaura Deterrence
/use Focus Fire
/startattack
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

This lets you cast Focus Fire any time, just like the others. /cancelaura Deterrence may not be needed, but I put it in there because I think it is. I haven't tried without.

Pet Control
/petfollow [@pettarget,exists]
/petattack [@pettarget,noexists]
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

Being a BM, your pet is very important to you. So it's a good idea to have a macro to make your pet assist you, and return to you when in danger. The way it works, is /petfollow [@pettarget,exists] makes your pet follow if it has a target, thus dropping it's current target. /petattack [@pettarget,noexists] makes the pet attack again. Which if it's on assist, it will attack your target. This macro is very handy for things like big AOE's. You can call him back to you, then send it back to the target after the AOE is done.

Misdirection
#showtooltip
/use [help][@focus,help][@pet]Misdirection

I like this one a lot. First, it will try to cast Misdirection on a targeted friendly. If your target is not friendly, it will target your focus. If you don't have a friendly focus, it will cast on your pet. This is the way I prefer it, that my target is priority over focus. That way, if the focus I have is struggling, I can click on the second tank, and cast on that one without having to change my focus. If you would rather always cast on focus, then remove [help] completely. But without [help], you would need to change your focus if you want to switch tanks.

Bestial Wrath
#showtooltip
/stopcasting
/stopcasting
/cancelaura Deterrence
/use 13
/use 14
/petattack
/use Bestial Wrath
/use Kill Command
/startattack
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

This is the almighty Bestial Wrath, but even mightier. Behold it's power! Just like the others, it stops casting and cancels Deterrence. /use 13 and /use 14 are for trinkets. 13 is the top trinket, and 14 is the bottom. If you're trinkets have an ability that you can activate, this will use them along with BW. If your trinkets DON'T have an ability you must activate, then you can either remove the two lines or keep them for if you ever do get usable trinkets. Kill Command is cast right after BW, that way you can squeeze in 3 Kill commands.

Spirit Mend Self Heal
/use [@player]Spirit Mend

If you have a spirit beast (recommended), this simply lets you cast Spirit Mend on yourself whenever you like. The pet only uses Spirit Mend on you once you are below 50%. But I don't want to wait that long, because at that point sometimes it is too late. I use this around 70% HP as a rule.

Spirit Mend Heal Selection
/use [help][@player]Spirit Mend

The difference between this and the self heal, is simply that if you select a friendly target, it will heal them instead. I myself don't use this, because I keep one macro for myself, and use the pet bar Spirit Mend for targeted heals. Here is where this could mess up.

You're using Misdirection on the backup tank, so you have him selected. Right as you are casting it on him, you get hit hard and drop down to 10%. You spam the healing macro. Oh no! You casted your chance of survival on the tank you had selected. Yes, you could have pressed escape to lose your target first, but you panicked.

By having a healing macro set for just yourself, you can avoid this situation, because it will ALWAYS heal you.

Aspect Selection
/use [button:2] Aspect of the Pack; Aspect of the Cheetah

This will cast Aspect of the Cheetah on left click, and Aspect of the Pack on right click. Good to have if you need to make room for another macro.

Notes
A few people have been known to add Focus Fire to all of their spells. This way its one less thing to watch, but it does lower the benefit because you will be activating it at 1-2 stacks always. If you notice you aren't using Focus Fire on 5 stacks often enough, you could try doing this. To add it to a macro, simply paste /use Focus Fire into the macro before the other spells.

Feel free to experiment! Try switching out Kill Command with Barrage maybe, or add Kill Command to arcane shot so that it auto casts while you spam Arcane. It might not work, but worth trying things out!

If you have any suggestions/ corrections, feel free to mention them!

This is my kill command macro. I got it from somewhere, but I don't remember what post right now.

#showtooltip Kill Command
/startattack
/cast [nopet]Call Pet 1
/cast [nogroup,@pet][group,@focus]Misdirection
/petautocastoff Growl
/petautocaston [nogroup]Growl
/petattack
/cast Bestial Wrath
/cast kill command

I focus the tank in dungeons/raids. If you use the macro toolkit you could add in the use trinket lines too. I love not having to worry about growl.

Spirit mend macro:

#showtooltip spirit mend

/cast [@focus, mod:shift][@pet,pet, mod:shift] Spirit Mend
/stopmacro [@focus,noexists][nomod]
/e 's pet casts Spirit Mend on %f
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()

again focus the tank. The tell lets them know they're getting a little extra boost. Lets me alternate between healing myself or healing my pet.
