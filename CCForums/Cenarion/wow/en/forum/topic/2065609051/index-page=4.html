My absolute favorite thing when I started playing 2.5 years ago was exploring. It was during one of those excursions, around level 12, when I found a way out onto one of the outside branches of Teldrassil. Then I fell off. And kept falling. For a long time.

Then I died.

Then I resurrected nearly all the way down the tree. I tried jumping down the rest of the way.

And I died.

Then I resurrected and saw this enormous, mind-numbingly vast ocean I'd never seen before. I decided to swim out into it and see what I could find.

I got fatigued. I didn't know what it meant. I died.

You'd think this kind of thing would've taught me a lesson. But 10 levels later, I decided to swim north from Darkshore, because there was clearly stuff above it on the world map but the game wouldn't let me walk to it. So I swam. And swam. And swam. And occasionally came across little portions of shore that led nowhere, or led through hills that took me tantalizingly far inland, but still nowhere. And I swam and swam and swam, all the way around to Azshara.

Where a level-55 mob saw me swimming and I died.

I resurrected and immediately stealthed, thinking that would keep me safe from those inexplicably powerful "Level ??" creatures.

I was wrong. They saw me. I died.

Repeatedly.

I don't remember whether I ever got to shore before I gave up. Pretty sure I eventually took the angel res and hearthed the heck out of there.

It was a good soul-hardening experience for my repeated attempts to run from Duskwood to Booty Bay while under the pirate buff I got in Stormwind on pirate day. At level 24.

Spoiler: I died.
