I should start off by saying that I canceled my WoW account for a couple of months, played a different game, and re-subbed to WoW to try out the new Zandalari stuff. There are a lot of complaints on here these days, and there has been a loss of subscribers to WoW. There are a lot of debates about why people are becoming dissatisfied. I want to submit my personal explanation and/or complaint, which is really a comprehensive aspect of the game design.

Simply put, it requires too much time, and too much monotonous, repetitive activity, to achieve anything in WoW's end game. It's not a problem unique to WoW, it afflicts other MMOs as well, but it is significantly worse in Cataclysm, and I think that's a big reason for the dissatisfaction. I know it is for me.


It applies to pretty much every aspect of end game play:

- The amount of dungeons/raids you have to run to buy a piece of VP/JP gear.
- The amount of BGs you have to run to buy a piece of Conquest/Honor gear.
- The amount of mats/gold you have to farm to make a piece of crafted gear.
- The amount of farming you have to do for Archaeology (I stopped leveling this after I saw what it was about. It's the most boring thing I've ever seen in WoW, and I hate RNG).

The above aspect of game design is entirely arbitrary. The developers can set those values at whatever level they want. I believe this is the underlying reason why most people start to get fed up. Cataclysm feels like less content than previous x-packs, and the bar has been set higher on grinding. It's hard not to come to the conclusion that they're trying to milk you for subscription dollars by stretching you out over a smaller amount of content, by setting the bar higher on how much time you have to spend grinding points/mats/etc.

What I would like to point out, in case this thread gets read by Blizzard employees, is that you do not need to increase time/reward in order to keep people subbed. On the contrary, I would be more inclined to keep my sub if I could log on and feel like I accomplished something without having to play the game 24/7, run the same dungeons hundreds of times until I hate them and never want to see them again, circle around zones for hours mindlessly farming mats, and so forth.

I like what new content there is in Cataclysm. I think the dungeons are the best you've ever done. I like the fact that they're harder as well. What I don't like is the amount of rewards you receive for completing them. Grinding =/= challenge. It feels like you're trying to rip me off. There are numerous examples I could give. The rep/quest grind for Therazane. The 346 blue Blacksmithing weapons that require 6 Truegold (going for ~600g each on my server).

This aspect of the game has become absurd. I have not yet reached the point of leaving WoW and saying "I'll never go back". But the game is definitely on probation with me, on a month to month basis. There are other issues: the poisonous culture of much of the "WoW community" that you've taken no steps to combat. The lame toilet humor and often directly ripped-off content in some of the quests (Indiana Jones, Rambo). After giving it some thought though, the grinding is the real reason why I'm on the fence about staying with the game. I've stopped raiding because of the culture of the players (I've come to despise most of the people who play this game), but I would keep my sub if I could get reasonably geared through other means without spending what feels like 40 hours a week to get some minor upgrade.


It's called business.

Blizzard doesn't want you to get fully end-game geared in 1 day. They want you to take your time, paying them every month.

It's really that simple.

And.... OP, that pretty much sums up why I am taking an extended break from WoW. So much time must be spent grinding if you want to really do well end game that it has become like another job.

Yes, I know it's all "optional", and no one is holding a gun to my head to make me do it. But really, how many of you that say that also say, quite bluntly, that if I want to improve myself, I have to "take the time to do it", "work at it", "put some effort into my character", or some other such thing.

I always find quite ironic that the people who shout down posts like this for "QQ", are the very same people who also complain that they don't want to run with people who don't take the time to "learn the fights", learn how to properly gear/gem/enchant/glyph, work at getting decent gear. You want to run with people who have spent time improving themselves so you don't have to suffer through wipefests and subpar performances, but you have no sympathy for people who struggle with having enough time to do exactly that.

I have taken the time to max out 3 toons, get them properly geared, gemmed, enchanted, and glyphed. I have been taking time to read/ watch fights, read EJ, etc. And you know what? After finally reaching a point where those toons are not only ready to raid and run the troll heroics, but do it well. Know what else? After only a few raid nights, I'm tired. I'm sick of looking at my screen. I've reached a point where I do not give a flip that we downed the boss, and I even passed on rolling for an upgrade that dropped because I realized that after the endless grind to get there, that sparkly bit of pixel candy felt hollow.

For those of you that enjoy the grind and spending massive time making sure you are the best, yipee! I'm so happy you are happy. I just miss the days when I could play, make some progress, yet in a relaxed manner that let me focus on enjoying my downtime with my friends. Do I deserve a full set of epic on dinging 85? Nope, not remotely. Should I have to spend weeks of hours per day doing dailies, farming massive quantities of mats, and sitting in 30 minute queues waiting to do a troll heroic which will probably take more than an hour with today's pugs for very few rewards? Not if I want to not glow in the dark. Don't even tell me that those of you in full tier play "less than an hour at a time every few days", that is complete BS, and I don't mean blacksmithing.

In the end, most people simply do not have the substantial amount of time (or energy) you need to dedicate to pursuing excellence in this game. "Go play something else then", you say? Yup, that's what we are doing. And it is slowly bleeding away Blizzards profits, which is going to bite the rest of you in the rear someday.

TLDR: I agree with OP. I'm exhausted and bored. FWIW Blizz, I would have preferred to pay a little more and gotten more content with less grind to reach the brass ring.
