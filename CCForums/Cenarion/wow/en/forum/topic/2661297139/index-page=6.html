This thread about "grinding" is really about wanting to be 85 instantly. And that is such a bad idea.


No that is not what this thread is about.

Leveling is probably the best example of how to make a grind fun. There are multiple ways to gain experience from gathering, archaeology, pvp, dungeons, or questing. While many quests are similar there is still enough variety to break up the monotony.

Max level is when grinding really takes over. Grinding is using the same strategy over and over again in order to progress in order to use a new strategy over and over again on more difficult content.

Grinding isn't necessarily bad, it actually helps level the playing field among players of different skill types, it's only when it become pervasive and excessive that it starts to become detrimental to the fun of the game. When used excessively grinding sucks the fun out of a game and makes the only true challenge: "Can I stand to do this for X number of times."

The fact that other games have had more grinding or that wow required more grinding in the past doesn't really make a difference to those who feel that wow is too grindy now.


Indeed. Endgame is the problem. When you're leveling you're constantly progressing, constantly getting new gear, and you never have to do the same quest or dungeon twice unless you want to.

Just to add clarity -- there are a couple of additional reasons why the problem is worse in Cataclysm:

- There are 5 new levels and 5 new zones, compared to 10 of each in LK.

- They made raiding less accessible. They put 10 and 25 on the same lockout, and made them the same difficulty level with the same gear. I wonder if they thought through the fact that they pretty much eliminated an entire tier between heroics and 25 man raids. In BC and LK, 10 man raids provided that connector. Now it's gone, and the shared lockout means raiding options are cut in half. The time spent developing raids that a minority of players will see adds to the lack of content for non-raiders, and the elimination of previous 10 man difficulty makes raiding even harder to get into.

I've already addressed this in the thread. The problem is not that there is grinding. It's that they set the numerical values of the grinding too high. It's an arbitrary bar they set, and they can set it however they want. The purpose is to drag things out so you continue running the content you have, instead of having to give you more content to keep you playing. Some amount of that is fine. But the amount of grinding is inversely proportional to the amount of new content they give you. So when the amount of new content goes down, the grinding has to increase to keep you playing at the same rate.

Get my drift?

Bottom line: if the purpose of endgame content is to give you the feeling that your character and the story are continuing to progress, the current design is failing. The progress you make is excruciatingly slow for small rewards, and you don't get to see the story if you don't raid.
