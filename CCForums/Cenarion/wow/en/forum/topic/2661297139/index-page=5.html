This thread about "grinding" is really about wanting to be 85 instantly. And that is such a bad idea.


No that is not what this thread is about.

Leveling is probably the best example of how to make a grind fun. There are multiple ways to gain experience from gathering, archaeology, pvp, dungeons, or questing. While many quests are similar there is still enough variety to break up the monotony.

Max level is when grinding really takes over. Grinding is using the same strategy over and over again in order to progress in order to use a new strategy over and over again on more difficult content.

Grinding isn't necessarily bad, it actually helps level the playing field among players of different skill types, it's only when it become pervasive and excessive that it starts to become detrimental to the fun of the game. When used excessively grinding sucks the fun out of a game and makes the only true challenge: "Can I stand to do this for X number of times."

The fact that other games have had more grinding or that wow required more grinding in the past doesn't really make a difference to those who feel that wow is too grindy now.

This is a stupid complaint and I'll tell you why.

Blizzard cannot develop content fast enough to keep players busy at the endgame without having grinds. All these people asking for epic quest chains at the endgame do not understand how much development time is required for a daily, let alone a month long epic quest.

There are ways around the grind, but none of them have worked particularly well. Randomized content is not yet polished enough and usually becomes just as grindy. City of Heroes went the dungeon creator route, but this only created more problems at the endgame. Guild Wars 2 appears to be on the bleeding edge of this, although we don't know how well that'll work either.

There needs to be something to slow down a players progress or they will accomplish everything they want and stop playing until the next content patch. For a game to have an active endgame, there needs to be players still around after 6 months of the same content.

Maybe calling it stupid was harsh, but this is the reality of MMOs right now. It isn't something that Blizzard can just fix without making strides forward in terms of technology.

- The amount of dungeons/raids you have to run to buy a piece of VP/JP gear.
- The amount of BGs you have to run to buy a piece of Conquest/Honor gear.
- The amount of mats/gold you have to farm to make a piece of crafted gear.
- The amount of farming you have to do for Archaeology (I stopped leveling this after I saw what it was about. It's the most boring thing I've ever seen in WoW, and I hate RNG).

The above aspect of game design is entirely arbitrary. The developers can set those values at whatever level they want. I believe this is the underlying reason why most people start to get fed up. Cataclysm feels like less content than previous x-packs, and the bar has been set higher on grinding.
