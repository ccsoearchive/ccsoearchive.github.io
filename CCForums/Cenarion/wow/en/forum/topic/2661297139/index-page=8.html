The real problem is that when you have a thempark style MMORPG where all the content is developed for the player by the devs, this "grind" is the only way to gate it so that the players don't run out of "stuff to do". With sandbox style MMORPGs the player never runs out of things to do because the world is alive and the players can actually effect the environment thus allowing the players to use their imagination to create their own content.

SWG pre-nge had it right, but their game's engine was terrible. No third axis, and terrible creature AI are just two of the things that come to mind. However, the ability to be creative was unparalleled.

Eve also is very good in the same way but they lack a ground game which kills it for me.

WoW simply isn't like this and there is no meaningful "world". Therefor the best you can hope for is that the devs can pump out content fast enough to keep you playing.

Uthben (OP),


It would probably be better if there was less of a distinction between dungeons and raids. The difficulty level of, for example, the new Zul'Gurub, should be enough for any content in a game like this.


Says who?

Just because ZG may be challenging enough for YOU, doesn't mean it's enough to satisfy the majority of the playerbase. You are making a lot of assumptions in many of your points that simply aren't validated.

There is no reason to put anything harder into it. So you could have 10, 25 and even 40 man "raids" that are of roughly the same difficulty level as current heroics, all pug-able through the LFG. I've also said in another thread, and I'll say again here, that they should get rid of guilds, and design the game so that they aren't necessary. The whole concept of guilds and "hard core end game raiding" is a flaw, not a strength.


First of all, will you get out of the mindset that all guilds are for raiding. THEY AREN'T. There are all different types of guilds in this game. Hardcore raid guilds. Casual raid guilds. Family/friend real life based guilds, pvp guilds, levelling guilds, social guilds. Whatever part of WoW you most enjoy, anyone can find a guild focused on that aspect.

Getting rid of guilds would be an ENORMOUS mistake on Blizzard's part. Guilds are about the only source of community left in WoW now since cross-server dungeons and BGs killed server communities.


But for now reducing the grind would at least be a step in the right direction.


Yes and no. WotLK was the most monotonous and grindy WoW has ever felt. Cata is about the same.
Individual grinds back in Vanilla and BC were far more time consuming than either Wrath or Cata...and yet the overall game back then felt WAY *LESS* grindy.

Having players able to obtain rewards for minimal effort can actually be a detriment and make the game as a whole feel MORE grindy, not less.

i.e. Players very quickly achieve one reward, then start grinding for the next, get that, grind for the next, and so on.

The grinding nature of WoW (or MMOs in general) is not going to change anytime soon, or at least not until MMO developers start making their games require less repitition and have more immersive things happening throughout their worlds.

And a lot of the repetition simply boils down to a lack of sufficient content.

Most things in WoW are fun the first time, or first few times, but after that they can turn into a chore.

Heroic Dungeons, raids (especially early bosses and trash), daily quests, pvp, etc can all turn into a chore after a while and feel GRINDY (like you're only there for the VP/tokens/Honor, etc) and not fun.

Part of the reason for this is lack of content and more things to do in game, as well as a loss of the feeling of adventure with much randomness taken out of the game. Class homogenisation also hasn't helped.

Nor has the fact that WoW hasn't really introduced anything completely new style of content since Arenas back in BC. Everything else has been just fine tuning and enhancing on things already implemented in-game previously.

e.g. New dungeons and raids. It's new content, but not different contnet. It's more of the same.

New battlegrounds. Same thing as above.

Crucible of Carnage. Rehash of Ampitheater of Anguish which was a rehash of BC's Ring of Blood in Nagrand.

Daily quests. More of the same.

Blizzard needs to stop just creating NEW content and actually start making some DIFFERENT content.

In an old game like WoW, the luster has worn off of much of the existing STYLE of content, not just the specific content itself.

No it isn't. My guild, which I have been a part of for close to 5 years now, has always peaked at about 18 active member. As you can imagine, pugging out the remaining 7 spots to hit the magic 25 people to even step foot into a raid past Kara (and later ZA), was a hassle and would end in failure because so few people had actually seen content most didn't know what to do.

Then there was the tedious grinding of rep... except back then you didn't have the luxury of putting on a tabard and doing random runs. No sir, you had to do specific instances for specific rep. Auchindoun for Lower City, TK for Shattar, Hellfire Citadel for Thrallmar/Honor Hold, etc...

The only major difference was the craftable items via professions. You got more bang for your buck. Sure, the grind was as long, if not longer, but you got to possess epic level gear, and if you were a weaponsmith, you got to upgrade those epics.

That being said, the res of you saying crap like, "Other MMOs are grindier so be happy" or "Things are better now than previous expansions" or "You grind in real life too" have fail arguments.

Yeah, WoW is better now than say Vanilla or TBC, and yes, other MMOs (especially older ones) require far more grinding, and yeah, IRL you have to do repetitive monotonous tasks (I'm doing some myself with data entry at my job) but how the hell does that imply that there isn't room for improvement?? And to those who say it isn't a grind, period. You're delusional. But if chores is what you like, come on over and mow my lawn and do my laundry.

Professions have taken a dive. You get less bang for your buck than before while requiring ridiculous mats.

Reaching exalted takes an insanely long time. for some reason it feels longer than Wrath, but maybe that's just my perception.

Then there's just running heroics over and over and over and over for JP... just so you can step foot into a raid. If you get a lucky string of drops, then your time grinding is shortened.

The reality is, there's not enough end game content, nor are there enough zones to offer players real choices while playing through 80-85.
