If you really think WoW is grindy right now, don't.

Classic:
Argent Dawn was 5rep per kill up to Honored, then only instance drops that you fought against everyone else to collect first for an additional 25 rep was grindy. Most quests gave 100rep and the single repeatable was 50rep, and only dropped in Scholomance.

Magram/Gelkis Clan Centaur was 5rep per kill until you hit level 45, then only 1rep per kill was grindy.

Timbermaw Hold was 5 rep per kill, 25rep per headdress/bead turn-in was grindy.

Wintersaber trainers, I won't even start.

Hydraxian Waterlords sure you got 10rep per kill, 100 for bosses, up to Revered, then you were limited to 1200 per week on a full clear. That was grindy.

TBC:
Dungeon Reps gave you about 1200 per Heroic, limited to one per day of each Heroic. About 1000 per run of Normals, and you had to hit Revered with a given rep from it's normal before you could run its Heroic.

Violet Eye: quests about 10k, full clear about 3k...per week.

Ashtongue Deathsworn and Scale of the Sands: about 9k per week if you did full clears.

Shattered Sun Offensive was 4k rep per day as a non-human.

Wrath:
Dungeon Reps give you tabards that allow you to decide which rep you'll get for that Dungeon/Heroic at 10-20rep per kill and 100/250 for bosses. Also added infinite Dungeons/Heroics per day with introduction of RDF.

Cata:
Continued trend. Questing will get you most reps to Revered, and at least halfway through Honored.

Some like Therazane give you 1850 per day even if you're unguilded and not a human, plus having the benefit of a Tabard if you want to do it faster.

Once you hit Heroics you're unlimited in the amount of JPs you can gain per week, and you can spend them on crafting mats once you've filled out all the gear.

I think I'm getting repetative here, but my point is this: WoW is nowhere near as grindy as it was in previous expansions even though it may seem like it is more grindy due to the nature of the implementation.

It isn't necessarily a good thing, but the grind these days is so simple and quick that it's actually kind of sad.

Since WOW relies on subccriptions for cash flow they need to keep people playingTo keep people subscribed longterm they need to do one or more of the following.

They could try to constantly add new content but this could lead to rushed content or burn out. Neither of which would be good for the life of the game. The current methods of releasing content are best for the game.

They could try to increase replay value. This is probably the best and worst way to go. If they do something which works we are happy and keep playing. On the other hand, if it fails we are unhappy and stuck with nothing to do and they have also wasted resources which could have been used elsewhere.

They could try to slow us down by turning things into a grind. Too much grinding can be bad for reasons already listed. Grinding can also be bad if it is not spread around enough. Doing the same thing over and over again is not fun. However if you spread the grind out to different things it becomes more bearable and even fun in some cases. In TBC you could do your daily dungeons then go work on rep for Skyguard, Netherwing, and Mag'har/Kurenai. We would be doing four different grinds but they broke eachother up so you were not exactly doing the swame thing repeatedly. And the rewards for the three factions were well worth the effort.

So the explanation of most of the responses here is "it's worse in other games" or "it was worse in WoW in the past". That's irrelevant. Just because you've become accustomed to monotonous grinding doesn't mean it's a good or desirable part of the game.

But to repeat myself again...it's not the fact that you have to grind, it's the numerical values of the grinding being set too high. The increased difficulty of fights in Cataclysm is fine. The increased cost of purchased items is not. They don't seem to understand that setting prices higher, forcing people to grind more, will make people sick of the content they're being asked to repeat that much faster. If you think about how many times people will have to run these dungeons with each successive tier, it's mind-numbing.

From a monetary standpoint, it makes no difference to Blizzard how much time people spend playing. It only matters that they subscribe month to month. If you can't log on and feel like you accomplished something, eventually you're going to cancel.

The thing that really gets me is the people who are saying "you have to work for things just like real life". THIS IS A GAME. Why would I PAY to have a second job? Why should a game be WORK? That's crazy.

Difficulty is what makes something a challenge. Grinding is not a challenge, it's a chore.
