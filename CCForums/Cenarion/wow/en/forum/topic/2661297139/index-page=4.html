So the explanation of most of the responses here is "it's worse in other games" or "it was worse in WoW in the past". That's irrelevant. Just because you've become accustomed to monotonous grinding doesn't mean it's a good or desirable part of the game.

But to repeat myself again...it's not the fact that you have to grind, it's the numerical values of the grinding being set too high. The increased difficulty of fights in Cataclysm is fine. The increased cost of purchased items is not. They don't seem to understand that setting prices higher, forcing people to grind more, will make people sick of the content they're being asked to repeat that much faster. If you think about how many times people will have to run these dungeons with each successive tier, it's mind-numbing.



Could you give a specific example of something you think takes too much time to get? Because you've said multiple times that you think things are too expensive, but you have yet to give an example of what grind you find too painful.

You mention crafting. Do you seriously find it to be too much to farm a couple stacks of ore and run a couple heroics to get a crafted item? You mentioned points. Do you seriously not remember when someone who didn't raid would have to run the daily FORTY FIVE TIMES to get a T10 BP, as opposed to two weeks and two days to get a T11 chest?

They've largely removed the grinding from this expansion. If you quest out all the relevant zones, you can get exalted with all the factions that matter for your class in like a dozen instance runs.

Honestly, the three major changes I've noticed in this expansion are:

A) The five mans aren't relentlessly boring to run long before you stop needing stuff from them,

B) All the starter factions/crafted gear are much more useful than in previous expansions, and

C) All the starter factions/crafted gear are VASTLY easier to get done.

If you find WoW:Cata to be too grindy, or too much like a job than you are burnt out, or playing in the wrong genre. Or both. The time spent/reward ration right now is stupendous. I can see where you might feel overwhelmed, because it's actually worthwhile to level your reputations and craft gear this time around. Don't be fooled though, if you don't want to you can absolutely skip it. There's nothing from factions or crafting other than head enchants that you can't do without.

And how flamingly stupid do you have to be to think that abolishing guilds would be a good idea at all?

I'm going to go out a bit on a limb and say that WoW is as grindy as we choose to make it. If we "hate" questing, and the only reason we quest it to get rep or loot or experience, then we consider the quest our grind to get to our reward. If, on the other hand, we quest because we enjoy questing, then the reward is a bonus and we don't feel like it was grindy getting there.

One of the things I saw a lot of players doing as Wrath came out and, even worse, as Cata came out, was voraciously poring over all the little data-mined bits to the point they didn't really care about the game as a whole - the ONLY point was the ultimate goal, geared up, BiS, doing the hardest raids, getting the show-off mounts, getting the new profession rares.

Slow down, take the scenic route, find what is fun in WOW other than getting a slightly increased iLvl, and it might stop feeling so grindy.

Jump into the wheel and rat race for server first, rare drops, the highest iLvL, and we've forgotten to play for the entertainment called a game.

There is a site that describes how MMO's are addictive and similar by using the rat lab techniques of making people do repetitive actions for a carrot at the end.

From what I am aware I am not supposed to link other sites so I'll just sum it up.

- One way to keep people playing over and over is to give the semblance of achievement either doing slot machine kind of actions (close to gambling addiction) - that would be the type of things like doing archaeology when it's not fun anymore but you want that next tolvir dig site and you wait in case it will finally pop up.

- Activities like grinding very hard material over and over for tiny rewards in the end.

- Making an achievement system that makes you feel like you accomplished something by doing something not that much important but you got point for it.

- accumulating things like mount and pets (takes advantage of people who are ready to spend a lot of time, sometimes to addiction). Similar to people collecting useless items in real life. it gets to a point where you "need" it bad when you will probably never use it much in game once you get it.

Those are techniques used from lab test result on rats where they know that if you push the lever over and over your brain will know it will get a reward and will push through boring dailies to get it.

Now the wonderful thing that seems to happen is that many people are revolting against this system and want a fun game and not a game that make them act like rats.

I am not saying that everyone who is playing is being a victim of this system. Some genuinely like to raid and some genuinely like to collect and have fun doing it.

But a large portion of the community is just grinding without fun and now is rising and protesting. This is good good news.

Maybe it will be time for WoW and other MMO to reconsider how they make end game content.

PS. In that articcle about MMO using rat-like maze techniques they were mentioning a chinese MMO that has people using real money to buy keys in game to open chests. The person who opens the most chests everyday gets a price... and it's working like crazy over there. Just happy we are not to that point here.
