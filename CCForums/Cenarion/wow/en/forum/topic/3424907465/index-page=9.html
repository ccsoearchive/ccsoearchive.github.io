Blizzard: You are losing older subscriptions because you are ignoring older subscribers.


The "challenge" aspect of WoW has been all but entirely removed. The main objective now is to get to level cap because that's where the party's at (Why?? --might I add), but it has been so streamlined and dumbed down that no one gets a chance to enjoy the scenery anymore. Was it completely arbitrary to have, while leveling, elite crocolisks in Dustwallow Marsh and dragonkin in Swamp of Sorrows that could eat my face? Yes, but it made the journey thrilling and an example of what WoW is lacking now. No threat of a giant elite dinosaur the size of a house (or Fel Reaver or Dragonmaw Ascendant) stomping on me while questing.
There exists nothing to engage the player along the way at random levels, it's all just a matter of short-cutting one's way to 85 and being herded into major cities where one can queue for anything to your heart's content. Elite mobs are non-elite now, group quests have been obliterated, class-specific quest lines are extinct, secrets and speculation have been silenced (the Shatterspear troll hideaway, randomly stumbled upon escort quests throughout the world, the Ironforge Airfield), mounts drop from bags, quests are insta-logged, reputation grinds are nerfed to the ground via dailies/tokens/tabards/guild perks, the list goes on...
What is happening in WoW is a perfect microcosmic representation of what is happening in society as a whole: the thirst for instant gratification and the fast track to convenience. A video game is not a microwavable meal, FFS. Yes, change is inevitable, but it should be change for the better. Easier does not mean better! Homogenisation does not mean better! I want my experience to be different than that of the player standing next to me. Watering down content and removing options has made it so our experiences are universally shared and universally dull. Naysayers shout "special snowflake" as an insult because everyone wants everything handed to them and criticise those who want to own a piece of WoW unique to their individual adventures. What made the game great was feeling like it was yours, only now there is no sense of discovery and the risk versus reward model has been made obsolete in favour of making everything accessible to everyone with the slightest bit of effort.
No thank you.
