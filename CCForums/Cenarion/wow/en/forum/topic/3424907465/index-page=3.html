(1) I don't have 5 hours to complete an instance. It took me forever to clear slabs some days because it was over 3 hours with bad groups.
(2) I get bored with BGs that last longer than 20min, plus I can fit them into those little breaks that happen in between real life.
(3) I like world danger, on that I agree. In vanilla it was kinda hard for a warrior to solo more than one mob and you had to clear an area and pull stuff back to you for fear of aggroing more.
(4) World pvp on unbalanced servers isn't much fun. Faction transfers pretty much gave Horde the victory (4:1) on this server.

Structural homogenization happened with the character classes. Remember back when druid tanks stacked stam as their primary, pallies had AoE abilities and warriors complained? Homogenization also happened by using the same old quests over and over. Just throw a different wrapper on it and sell it. There was also a smaller content for endgame in cata. How many interesting dailies are there? Now, it's pretty much just MF for 4.2, and they're hard to do on more than 1 toon. The Thundermar dailies in 4.0 were at least interesting.

I miss several aspects of the game that Blizzard has taken away, most notably old Alterac Valley and the incredible fun of Tarren Mill/Southshore wars. People didn't fight in those to grind honor....most honor grinders did Warsong Gulch or other BGs, and before the honor system even existed, the battle in Hillsbrad raged on for nothing more then fun. Old school AV to me remains the most fun i've ever had in an MMORPG and i played my first one in early 1999.

A lot may not agree with me on this, but I still think Molten Core and Blackwing Lair were two of the funnest raids i've ever done. They didn't present ultra gimmicky encounters like dodging the red beam while throwing blue fire at an add while you shifted into the shadow realm at the same time as dodging flying snakes. It has gotten totally out of hand, and the difficulty of those old school raids was just the raw power of the mobs. A single mistake by one player didn't wipe the entire raid as encounters are famous for today.

The bottom line to me is as Foury said, NEW IS NOT ALWAYS BETTER.
