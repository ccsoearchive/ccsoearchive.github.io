As for "Hybrid Specs", half of them were overpowered and eventually got nerfed, usually because of oversights and/or talents working with each other in ways that were not intended (and usually overpowered). It also made for very confusing choices for new players, you'd see players in dungeons with one-third talents in one tree, one-third talents in another tree, and one-third talents in the other tree and they would do only a tenth of the DPS they should because they're missing core abilities. You'd see the 15/15/15 rogues or whatever. Don't know about you, but I prefer this current system of Talents. Nice, easy, and I don't have to read volumes of EJ crap on what talents I'm supposed to take. There's millions of combinations, but <5 "correct" combinations. The only difference is you have to dig through posts on EJ to figure out which are correct and which are not.

You made some excellent points in this thread, Foury, and I had an epiphany while reading.

I want to be lost again.

I want the devs to let go and stop holding my hand. Let me wander off into the wilderness and get lost on the way home. Give me a chance to challenge myself on my own - without having to join a raid.


I remember being scared that I might be lost forever the first time I left Teldrassil for the big strange forests of Darkshore. I had been playing for a day and didn't know what would happen if I did get lost. One of my early deaths, I took a spirit rez simply because I was lost.

What I miss is that incredible feeling of being immersed in a foreign place that is huge, imposing, and dangerous. The scope of the game has been shrinking for me and I don't believe that it is because I'm a wow veteran. I recently did BRD on my baby druid and after the group broke up, I stealthed around reacquainting myself with the city. I'd missed it and I remembered a bit how I felt the first time I realized that you wind all the way to the bottom of the city on your way through. I felt the same thing at the top of UBRS when I first realized you could see down to the bottom of LBRS.

These places were huge, but more than that, they felt huge. Now, you can't get lost in an instance. They are too linear. And while I love the portals that allow faster rez runs and the ability to walk out a shortcut at the end if you choose, I think it would be possible to incorporate these things in a large-scale dungeon without losing that feeling of being a very small fish in a very large pond.

Would I like all vanilla style content back? Not really. Would I like some elements in the game that rekindle that enormity? Yes, and when I needed to feel small again, I could choose to do them.
