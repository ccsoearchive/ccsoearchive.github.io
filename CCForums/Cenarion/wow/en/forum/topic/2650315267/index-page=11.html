    Q: Are there plans to address the fundamental contradictions in rogue DPS design which disallow us from playing to our full potential in encounters frequently populated by target swaps and intermittent boss DPS phases? Between redirect being a 1-minute CD, and losing all our (vital) debuffs on our old target, rogues are at a significant disadvantage in Cataclysm raiding content.

Expanded background of the above question:

Between assassination’s poisons and long-term CD (singular), combat’s Bandit’s Guile, and subtlety’s entire PvE rotation, we’re in a pretty tough place in Cataclysm-style encounters where target switching and intermittent boss DPS opportunities are the norm. Plus, all three specs are suffering from scaling issues.

Rogues are apparently designed to do well at tunneling consistent damage into a single target over significant time, and our ability to do burst DPS or effectively switch targets in PvE has become even more hampered than it was in Wrath of the Lich King, while assassination continues to suffer from a bland rotation.

While I understand how this was most likely done in order to avoid rogues becoming overpowered in PvP, assassination and combat lack survivability to the extent where it's almost a moot issue for those specs, while subtlety (which is the most common PvP spec) has the best burst of them all - comparatively speaking.

I want to say right now that I think Cataclysm-style encounters are really, really great, but it’s being driven home harder every week how poorly rogues can cope in these sorts of environments compared to other DPS classes/specs (ESPECIALLY H:ODS). And Firelands is promising only to bring us more of the same.

I realize all three rogue specs are getting moderate buffs in 4.2, but can we expect any real changes to address the issues with the basic, underlying rogue mechanics that are significantly constricting the ability of the class to really shine in Cataclysm PvE? In a nutshell this has just been really discouraging for us.
