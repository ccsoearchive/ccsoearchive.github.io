BM damage potential is pretty underrated. A buff of about 10% constant to Kill Command through Killing Streak and upping the range of Kill Command by 3 yards to make it a reliable attack should be enough. Most of BMs issues are in the mechanics and dealing with pet AI.

Nice list! Though, because I started this earlier today and had to finish it, I see your list and raise it with mine. Will take a few posts.
Beast Mastery

Specializations:
Intimidation: Removed and made baseline for all Hunters.
Why? Intimidation has long been a staple of the BM tree, but as the level 10 bonus ability, it’s just plain useless. A 3-second stun would help Hunters of all specs, so let’s give it to Hunters of all specs. It can be trained at the level Kill Command is currently trained at.
New Specialization: Kill Command: 40 focus, 15 yard range, 6 second cooldown.
Why? Kill Command is BM’s nuke, their focus dump, and one could say the crux of being a BM Hunter. MM and SV have limited use for it (Resistance is Futile and a focus dump during Lock n Load, respectively), so let’s just let BM have it all to themselves. MM and SV will have certain talents adjusted accordingly, as you’ll see. Also, the 15 yard range allows Hunters to almost-always be able to use their nuke ability without having to wait on their pet.
New Specialization: Beast Mastery (the talent)
Why? MM and SV both have a nice talent waiting for them at the bottom of their talent trees that is something worth working towards. BM, on the other hand, gets something that might as well be a specialization. So let’s make it one.

BM Talents in the next post.

Talents:
Improved Kill Command: Removed and combined with Killing Streak
Why? Having two free talent points offers BM some extra options and extra utility, which is always nice.
Pathfinding: Moved to Tier 1. Now also increases your movement speed by 4/8%. Increases the movement speed bonus of Aspect of the Cheetah and Aspect of the Pack by 5/10% (up from 4/8%) and increases mount speed by 10/20% (up from 5/10%)
Why? Pathfinding is a crappy talent through and through. This change gives Hunters some extra mobility without having to give up 10 DPS for a particular enchant, makes AotC and AotP better when used, and gives Hunters the speed boost provided by Crusader Aura. Mobility is never a bad thing in this expansion.
Focus Fire: Redesigned. When your pet gains a stack of Frenzy, your haste is increased by an additional 3%.
Why? It seems unfair that a MM Hunter can get their Haste buff rolling within the first five seconds of a fight, SV gets it constantly, yet BM is forced to wait 15 seconds (assuming Rapid Fire is off cooldown). While this does still create a ramp-up time for the full Haste effect, this ensures near-100% uptime when it does stack to five, and gives BM one less button to push. This also lets your pet keep attacking like a machine gun without you have to take that power away.
Cobra Strikes: Your Arcane Shot has a 7/14/20% chance (up from 5/10/15%) to cause your pet’s next two basic attacks to critically hit.
Why? Increased proc chances are never a bad thing, especially for the spec that is hurting the most.
Killing Streak: Redesigned. Increases the critical strike chance of Kill Command by 5/10%. When you score two Kill Command critical hits in a row, your next Kill Command will deal 10/20% more damage and have its cost reduced by 5/10 focus, and allows the use of one Kill Shot as if your target was below 20% health.
Why? Rolling Improved Kill Command into this seemed to make perfect sense to me, and the free Kill Shot helps improves the Hunter side of BM damage (as opposed to the pet side).
Bestial Wrath: Effect now lasts 15 seconds, up from 10.
Why? More time to do extra damage? Why not?
Ferocious Inspiration: Also reduces the cost of Multi Shot by 15 focus, and your pet’s Basic Attack now hits up to two additional targets for 50% damage.
Why? BM suffers greatly in sustained AOE. Worms are the only viable option since the Chimera pet still can’t AOE in a circle, only in a cone (and I’m waiting for Blizzard to fix that). On top of this, outside of Bestial Wrath, BM does crap for AOE damage. This addition helps alleviate that problem.
The Beast Within: Effect now lasts 15 seconds, up from 10. While under this effect, you do not know pity or remorse or fear and cannot be stopped unless killed.
Why? Brings it in line with Bestial Wrath, and the immunity to certain CC effects was very useful in PvP. Why they removed it when Hunters are already suffering in PvP (at least, that’s what I hear, I can’t say that definitively) is beyond me.
Invigoration: When your pet scores a critical hit with a Basic Attack, you and your pet both instantly regenerate 3/6 focus (up from just you).
Why? I think that a BM Hunter should get better Focus returns for their pet than MM and SV. This is a way to ensure that.
New 31-point talent! Stampede: 10 min cooldown. You can now summon a new active pet without having to dismiss your current pet first. Also, when activated, you call forth all of your active pets to assist you. Pets summoned this way will only use their Basic Attacks, buff/debuff abilities, Dash, Charge, Thunderstomp, Rabid, and Wolverine Bite. Lasts 30 seconds.
Why? Let’s be honest, BM needs something cool to cap their talent tree with, and this, in my opinion, is the epitome of being BM. The passive part allows for an easier switching between an AOE pet and a single-target pet. The pet ability restrictions are in place so that all other talented pet abilities aren’t wasted.

MM and Surv next post.
