I look forward to the day when we stop patting people on the back for hurling insults at fellow players, but I'm not naive enough to keep my hopes up too high. Anyway, to give a less succinct response that actually answers the OP, the reasons people play BM in PvP include:

  • An extra trinket;

  • A stun;

  • More efficient focus usage;

  • An anti-LoS tool;

  • On-demand burst damage (I find this overrated, but that doesn't mean it's not a reason)

  • Crouching Tiger, Hidden Chimera (an underrated talent, except by high-level non-BM hunters, who want to cannibalize BM for the talent);

  • Spirit Bond (not for the crappy regen, but for the extra healing received); and

  • Exotic Pet abilities tend to have PvP uses. Even if you don't use a spirit beast (which I also think is overrated), exotic pets tend to be souped up versions of the pets that other hunters use. Silithids are spiders with the stamina buff, Devilsaurs bring the MS debuff without the hassle/wasted focus of widow venom, Chimaeras have a free snare, etc. These abilities may overlap or be useless in raids and even small scale arenas, but not necessarily in BGs where you haven't established your comp beforehand.


    • Obviously there are several reasons why players don't think these tools are enough. But those are some concrete answers for why you do see BM hunters in PvP, just in case "pokemon" or "idiots" felt like hollow explanations.

    I never said the entire class or the entire spec are like that.

    Hunters who play the game in any competitive environment are the minority of our class. Sad but true. Go look at the Hunter forums for about an hour. It's nothing but new posts about "this dbag killed my spirit beast!" or "name my pet" or "taming pet X, Y, and Z." Now go inspect every Hunter you find in Stormwind/Orgrimmar. You'll notice 90% of them are using stamina gems and are Beast Mastery.

    Most of our class are weekend warriors, casual players, and less skilled players. Period. End of story. It's the same way with Paladins. The ranger and holy warrior archetypes are generally the most popular in any game out there. The entire playstyle and spec flavor of Beast Mastery caters to that audience.

    You have

    1. Ease of soloing. Your pet plays a minor role in the leveling and soloing experience. Pet tanking is much more "noob friendly" since it requires no knowledge of or ability to kite. To a new or bad player, "send pet and shoot" it's a much easier concept to understand and it's easy. Teaching a new or bad player to kite is like trying to teach physics to a middle schooler.

    2. Pet skins. Most of the people who fall into that bit I said earlier are attracted to this. If you aren't raiding or PvPing competitively, you're probably hunting pets. A lot of casual Hunters are pet chasers which is why we got new pet skins. It appeases more players than addressing balancing concerns would, and it's much easier to do.

    3. Ease of play. Beast Mastery is a spec that falls under the "easy to use, difficult to master" mantra as it has a lot more dependence on your pet damage and thus deemphasizes individual player skill at the basic level in comparison to the other specs who only have 10-20% of their DPS tied to pets.

    I personally love Beast Mastery with proper play. I find it to be an engaging spec and I can do respectable damage in PvE scenarios. But it doesn't change the fact that it's still a drastically inferior spec and as such, most of those who do use the spec are unknowledgable and unskilled players, new players, or the weekend warrior type. Notice the word most.

    When we do get to the facts, however, they tend to get twisted or ignored. Take the 4.2 taming challenges, for example. This often gets represented as catering to Pokemon-loving BM hunters. The problem with this is that they added 10 pets in that patch, and 7 of them are pets that BM hunters have no reason to use. Those spiders, the cat and the crab are for Pokemon-loving MM and SV hunters. A BM hunter would use a Silithid over a spider, and a spirit beast over a cat. They just wouldn't use a crab very often, period. I play BM and I was even less excited about those new pets than the rest of you.

    Another "fact" that comes up is that BM has been ignored this expansion. The past two patches haven't done very much for hunters in terms of outright buffs -- there have been mostly QoL changes and bug fixes. But the last patch that actually gave significant buffs to hunters (4.06) gave those buffs to BM (and MM), while SV got nerfed. Singling out BM as proverbial black cat (no pun intended) because it isn't getting buffed every patch doesn't make sense when no one is getting buffed.

    The facts are that BM has issues and Blizzard hasn't yet addressed all of them. The facts are that there are some players who aren't happy about the first fact. The facts are that the last time anyone at Blizzard said something about their goals for BM, they said their goals were to make it a viable spec. The facts are that they haven't succeeded at that goal, because the players don't consider BM to be viable. Somehow we've taken all these facts and arrived at some questionable conclusions.
