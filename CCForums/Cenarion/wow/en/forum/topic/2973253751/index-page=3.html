This is the most succinct summary of BM hunters I have ever read. Well done, Bullet, well done :)


I look forward to the day when we stop patting people on the back for hurling insults at fellow players, but I'm not naive enough to keep my hopes up too high. Anyway, to give a less succinct response that actually answers the OP, the reasons people play BM in PvP include:

  • An extra trinket;

  • A stun;

  • More efficient focus usage;

  • An anti-LoS tool;

  • On-demand burst damage (I find this overrated, but that doesn't mean it's not a reason)

  • Crouching Tiger, Hidden Chimera (an underrated talent, except by high-level non-BM hunters, who want to cannibalize BM for the talent);

  • Spirit Bond (not for the crappy regen, but for the extra healing received); and

  • Exotic Pet abilities tend to have PvP uses. Even if you don't use a spirit beast (which I also think is overrated), exotic pets tend to be souped up versions of the pets that other hunters use. Silithids are spiders with the stamina buff, Devilsaurs bring the MS debuff without the hassle/wasted focus of widow venom, Chimaeras have a free snare, etc. These abilities may overlap or be useless in raids and even small scale arenas, but not necessarily in BGs where you haven't established your comp beforehand.


    • Obviously there are several reasons why players don't think these tools are enough. But those are some concrete answers for why you do see BM hunters in PvP, just in case "pokemon" or "idiots" felt like hollow explanations.

People who play the game in any competitive environment are the minority -- assuming that by competitive you mean high arena/RBG ratings or heroic mode raids. I have no idea whether the proportion of hunters who aren't competitive in any aspect of the game is drastically different from other classes (note, by "any" I don't mean "just PvP") but I do know that the derogatory labels probably weren't needed to explain that.

I did find one hunter who didn't have any of his stuff socketed yet at all, and he was BM.

    As a comparison, I also inspected all the mages (six total), and I found two players using spirit gear. One of those had zero enchants on anything, and the other had only two enchants, one of which was a stamina enchant on a set of non-PvP legs.

    I also inspected the warriors, but there were only two of them (most were out doing stuff in the world). One was decked out in PvP gear, and the other had blues/greens, no enchants, and an ilvl 200 trinket from Brewfest.

One answer is that the "word on the street" hasn't gotten out to everybody, and that seems to be part of what Bullet was saying.

    Another answer, however, is that the word on the street doesn't apply to the level that OP saw, because the comments about how horrible BM is are focused on very high level play. At other levels, BM has strengths -- mobility, survivability, efficiency -- that are outweighed by its (and the other specs') weaknesses. So it's possible that not all of the hunters the OP saw that day were pokemon/weekend warriors/absolute idiots. They almost certainly weren't highly competitive players either, but there's something that exists between those two points.
