I've led every RBG i've ever done, playing 2600+ MMRs last season and hitting rank 21 in the US overall. I've had a ton of compliments on my leading in pug games, lots of people saying I'm easily 10x every other leader they've seen. I'll share some basic strats with you. :)

Generally, I always run with 4 healers unless you have very, very skilled dps players.

In wsg / tp, you're going to always want to have the jump on the other team. What i mean by this is that if they are going to get on your fc before you get on theirs, you should send your entire offense back and wipe them completely then head up with your O. You're free to run whatever setup you want, whether it be 5/5, 4/6, 3/7 D/O, I've done well with all of them but my favorite from last season was a 5/5 because it almost never scared a team into counterwiping you after you wipe their team, effectively giving you a 30+ second headstart depending on their rez timer.

In AB, what I generally do is always have one pet class (hunters are ideal) at farm, send myself to mine, and the remaining 8 either split up as 3/5 or 4/4 at LM / BS. Reasoning for sending myself to mine is that giving up a neutral base for free is not a good thing because you are then hoping that you cap BOTH of the other bases which can be a hard task, and also I go as a rogue because if they send one person, I will be able to 1v1 them 100% of the time due to being a very strong dueler, or else I can sap cap -> blind cap on them if they aren't a warrior, mage, hunter, paladin or lock (pets, bubble / block to stop the blind cap after they trinket sap). If they send two or more, I am able to stall them there through sitting in stealth spam sapping and when DRs are high, just popping out to control and mitigate damage on myself as much as possible until sap DR is up and i can vanish out and repeat, stalling teams for a very long time and buying my other two base squads time to get a cap. The stronger of your two groups should generally go to blacksmith because it is the base base to own in AB.

After the bases have all been established, it's important to always have a designated AOE'r wherever they are attacking and let your healers know they should be dispelling said person out of cc as a priority over EVERYTHING else. Best AoE'rs are generally rogues, or a prot if you decide to have your FC stay prot in ab. A great way to cap bases is to have a CC group set up. This works best with either rogue/mage or rogue/druid. Say you have two bases and really need a 3rd. You can have one person defend each of your two bases, and have a group of 6 hit what appears to be the weakest defended, while the rogue and druid/mage stealth and invis to a 4th base which has one person at it.

When you're there, the secondary CC will either sheep or pounce/cyclone depending what they are when the rogue starts capping, forcing a trinket. When they trinket, the rogue can then fully blind them and start recapping, having the other person cc afterwards. Simple strat, very effective. This can be used in gilneas and EotS as well, however it's not as effective in gilneas simply due to most teams defending with more then one person per base after they are established.

In gilneas, as an opener I generally use the same kind of strategy. One pet class at mine/LH, a CC group to the other teams starting base, and the rest to waterworks. Your cc group can either kill or CC depending what is there, however CCing is far more ideal if possible because attempting to kill someone can give the other team time to send backup. If you decide to send everyone to waterworks, have your DPS generally focus on disc priests / resto druids first as they are easy healers to kill, and afterwards work on killing DPS with cc on whatever other healers they have, or get your priests (shadow / disc) to burn them. As a rule of thumb, NEVER kill oom healers because it almost always does more harm than good. Just keep tabs on them and don't allow them to drink while they waste their time being useless as you pick off their dps. Once again, always have a designated AoE person at whichever base is being attacked and have healers dispel them when needed over anything else. When things start to go in your favor at a base, it's always best to set up some kind of cc on the remaining few people there to allow you to cap quicker. I've seen teams attempt to kill off our last few members buying us time for all of our rezzers to get back there and stop them from capping, winning us games due to their stupidity.
