over 50% of the rogue population most likely does not read these forums.

So how can you know that "85% are complaining",

Heres another fact for you. Most WoW players will not see patch-current raid content until they grossly overgear it via free epics, or it is no longer patch-current. meaning that it is no longer impressive to down the boss, because there is a new raid(s) that are harder and available.

Most WoW players will not get past 1700 rating, most don't even do arena at all.

For every rogue that has none-heroic nef/cho'gall on farm, and working on heroic BWD and TB, there are probably thousands, if not hundreds of thousands of rogue's game-wide that haven't beaten omitron/magmaw, much less stepped into TB or BWD.

For every rogue that knows his class to the core, there are a ridiculously large amount that have no clue, or at the least, aren't being optimal at their class.

This being said, you don't take everyones opinion into consideration, you would only take into consideration the people that would actually know their class.

So, who's opinion would be correct.

The one that has raid content downed, and/or is a high rated arena player, that says the class is not half as bad as what people say?

Or the 1500- player, who has not seen raid content, complaining about the class.

Obviously the former, because he's experienced the class in content that the ladder has not, he knows how to work his class, while the ladder doesn't, or else the ladder would most likely be at the same content as the former.

Most QQ'ers on this forum are the ladder if you were wondering.

Most good players only really get frustrated at things like vanish bugging, frostfire orbs having true-sight, ETC, bugs that are a solid reason to cry.

However, these players usually will just struggle on with their bugs, because they know that it isnt the CLASS is fundamentally broken, its that there are bugs.

Make sense?

A bad player will always blame someone else for his mistakes, the easiest is blaming his own class or friends, the hardest is accepting blame.

Good players know that they are doing something wrong. The reason why they are good, is because they notice that something went wrong, they find out what made the error, and fix it. Bad players either shrug off the error, or blame the wrong party, neither of which progresses their skill level.
