I'm saying that the two things are equivalent.

If you reduce your opponent's damage by 35% he'll be dealing 65% of normal damage. It takes 1/.65 = +54% longer to kill you, which is the same as if he had the same damage, but you had +54% HP and Heals.

Basically I'm showing you things in terms of the Target Dummy Damage it takes to kill you in order to show you the proper scale of the benefits that resilience gives.


Similar, not same. And as has been said a number of times, it doesn't matter how little damage you take, or how long you can stand there and get beat on. If you can't put out damage, you aren't helping your teammates. At all. That's what gemming straight resil does, reduces your own output and makes you a useless lump on your team.

You gem resil in yellow sockets with agi/resil, and only up to a point.
They ARE the same. They result in the exact same overall effect with the exception that it's now clear how useful resilience is compared to other talents and stats. And what I'm saying is that if you're going to stack resil, you should go all the way or not at all.

For example,

2000 resil is equivalent to +26.6% HP and Healing.
4000 resil is equivalent to +72.44% HP and Healing

By doubling the amount of resil, you tripled the benefits. With the majority of the benefits of resil reserved for those who stack it as much as possible, it's simply not possible to "balance" damage and survivability in the way you describe. Someone who stops halfway in terms of resil is paying half the price, but receiving less than half of the benefit. This is especially true if you stop at close to the maximum amount of resil you can possibly get. You get significantly more overall benefit by either going all-out on resil or not getting any at all.

Frankly I strongly doubt that the relative damage increases you could potentially get from sacrificing resil are anywhere near close to the relative survivability increases you can get from stacking resil.

Regarding arenas:

A team with 4000 resil will take less damage, and deal more overall damage to a team with 3000 resil and the rest offensive stats. You're mitigating more damage from them, than the increase in offensive stats is yielding in damage for them. Thus, you end up doing more damage to them than they do to you.

Well, I guess this will bring this discussion to a close (4.1 PTR notes):

Resilience scaling has been modified for linear returns, as opposed to increasing returns. Under the new formula, going from 30 resilience to 40 resilience gives players the same increase to survivability as going from 0 to 10. Resilience now scales in the same way armor and magic resistances do. A player with 32.5% damage reduction from resilience in 4.0.6 should see their damage reduction unchanged in 4.1. Those with less than 32.5% will gain slightly. Those with more will lose some damage reduction, increasingly so as their resilience climbs.

Just when I thought it was kind of cool that there was at least something still up for debate and subject to personal preference. Now, at last, we can all look/gear, spec, enchant, and gem the same.
