If you want to see awesome dps in heroics, get an addon to track your DoT's, and Multi-DoT on all of your pulls. 3 is a good number, Put Sw:P and VT on about 3 different mobs, and keep those dots up. Keeping your dots on one or more adds will make your dps go way up. What I do, is I always keep Sw:P on all 3 targets, by using a full mind flay on one of them, then mind flay the next one, then the next one, and just keep rotating between each one. You should refresh VT, if the mobs still have quite a bit of health left.

Also, use Shadowfiend and Archangel off cooldown every opportunity you can. (If you can, time it so you use both when you get any int or sp procs, it stacks)

DoTs are full of win, always keep DoTs up on as many targets as you can, as long as you can.


When starting out Heroics, I would not recommend doing this. In leveling greens/blues and normal dungeon blues, you'll find yourself going OOM multi dotting every trash pull. But for trash and usually any multi dot fight, its keep SW:P/VT/DP on the main target and SW:P/VT on off target(s). MB on CD and MF on the main target and refresh DOTs when needed on all targets, SW:P refreshed through MF.

SF and DA should be saved as mana CDs until you have no mana problems in your current gear. This is usually when you get to around full Heroic blues which is when you can start using SF and DA as offensive CDs.

This guide, however, is mainly on how to handle yourself on day-to-day raid/heroic boss encounters. Trash DPS typically doesn't matter imo as long as you aren't pulling ~4k as a single MB can bring you up to that, which in turn means someone, somewhere is doing something wrong.

But to the OP of the post you quoted, he really needs to read other sections of this guide. Gems are completely out of whack, reforging is wrong, and no enchants. I'm here to help, not hold peoples' hands ;)
