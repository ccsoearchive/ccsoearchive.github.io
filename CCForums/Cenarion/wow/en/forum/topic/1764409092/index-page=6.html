Some thoughts on some of your points--

Glyph of Spirit Tap--much like the original Spirit Tap, this is pretty much worthless and should be skipped in any raid format. Although it has some use for leveling and maybe 5-mans, in raids there are not many mobs and the odds of you timing and SW:D perfectly to kill one (and considering most fights are boss fights where you'll have plenty of time to mana up after anyway) are very very low. Shame on Blizz for only have 1 useful major glyph for shadow...we, more than any other spec, really got the shaft when it comes to glyphs. I find Mass dispell a pretty useful glyph and chose Psy Horror by default which is extremely situationally useful, but there are fights where disarms can help.

SW:D--I know the common "rule of thumb" is to not use SW:D until mobs are in the execute range. I for one disagree with that point. For the small amount of dps loss (1 GCD for a spell that does 5-8k normal and 10-15k crit is still not bad), the mana return can be very useful, especially in long fights. It allows you to blow shadowfiend and Eva from the start and use them offensively on the CD as opposed to when you need mana regen. I use it on the CD, but for sure you should use it during mobility phases.

Mind Sear--is just about useless atm. I can do more single-target dps to a mob than I can using mind sear on 8. It is rare that mind sear is a better option than multidot at this point unless you really need AoE dmg fast (like for Maloriak).

more to come, out of time...good post though = )

Catay, I'm not sure if you're using that gear for heals as well as shadow, but I'll go through your gear and what's wrong with it just purely from a shadow perspective. Of course, you don't have to follow all this down to the point if your Main Spec is Holy.

You have 21% hit, that's 5% over hitcap, and hit cap isn't essentially as black and white as it was in WotLK, so being a bit under is fine as well. Instead of reforging all that Hit into Spirit, reforge it into Haste or Crit, whichever one doesn't exist on the gear already. Gems are wrong too, the only three options that you should be using are Purified Demonseye, Reckless Ember Topaz, and of course Brilliant Inferno Ruby. Gemming/enchanting for spirit/hit is a loss of DPS, and you shouldn't be doing that. Again, this is from a purely DPS standpoint, so if your main spec is Holy, and obviously aiming for spirit, you don't need to change everything around :)

@Ricky - The only DOT that automatically refreshes according to our buffs is SW:P through MF. Other than that, VT and DP need to be manually reapplied. I've personally been doing what you said, reapplying DOTs after gaining Empowered Shadows. I have no math to back it up as of now since no one's brought it up which is why I haven't posted anything of it, but I've asked around and gotten people's own personal doings about this, and most of them are refreshing after getting Empowered Shadows.

IV. Reforging

Cataclysm introduced a way for us to even customize our gear to our liking.. Well, 40% anyways. Reforging is a new thing in Cataclysm, where you take 40% of a secondary stat (Haste, Crit, Spirit, Mastery) and convert it to another secondary stat that is not present on the same item. For Shadow Priests, Haste is by far the best stat to reforge to. The stat weighing is still the same for reforging as you would be picking out upgrades. Mastery should be reforged out of as much as possible, whether it be to haste or crit. Crit should be reforged to Haste if possible. And spirit should be reforged to either Haste or Crit, whichever is possible. The best way to get hit capped if you are a bit short, is to reforge Mastery and Crit (maybe even Haste) into spirit.
