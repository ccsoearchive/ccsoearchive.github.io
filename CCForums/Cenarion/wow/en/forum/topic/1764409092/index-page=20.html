One point I would add is that it's always worth recasting DP if you get bloodlust/heroism. Simply because DP (due to improved DP) does marginally more damage than MF so you might as well refresh it immediately and take advantage of the 30% haste buff.

Recasting VT on the other hand is a bit harder to judge. Because you don't do any instant damage when recasting VT, you are always wasting some cast time by recasting it early. I'm not sure whether the extra 30% haste is worth the loss of the cast time.

While you are doing the two MF's and 3 Mind Spikes, your VT, SW:P, and DP could already be ticking. Sure, your initial DPS would skyrocket, but the longer a fight, which is the case in HM's and generally most raid encounters, overall DPS matters over spike.

PS - When you switch to adds, such as on Cho'gall, your DOTs should never be falling off when you learn the fight better. What I personally do is keep VT and SW:P on Cho and then focus on adds and in P3, I keep all DOTs rolling on Cho while Mind Spiking or VT'ing adds.

Yes, Shadowfiend is effected by either Int or Spell power, honestly cannot remember which, though I'm putting my bets on Spell power since Int gives SP. If you're not having mana problems, you should run through opening rotation (VT -> SW:P -> DP -> MFx2 -> MB with Orb) and by then most trinkets/enchants have proc'd already, so its a good time to pop SF.

Regarding VT/DP scaling, it contains all the buffs that you had when it landed on the mob/boss. So yes, if you cast it while there's 3 seconds left on DMC:V or Power Torrent or whatever, it keeps it until you recast the spell. However, it is not worth recasting if you -get- a proc. Just keep doing what we've been doing since god knows when, refreshing DOTs when they're about to fall off.

Letting DOTs fall off? Nevar. I usually refresh VT right before its last tick, which is right as it falls off, and the second to last tick being at 1 second, so generally I refresh VT between 0.1-0.8 seconds left.

This is a great guide! It's good to have all of this information in one place, especially for new players. I've had this character since vanilla and the Priest is my absolutely FAVORITE class in this game. While I was installing Cataclysm for the first time, I started browsing the web forums for info on the new SPriest mechanics, dps rotations, BiS lists, etc and I got really nervous. It seemed like there would be new rotations and new mechanics to worry about during each fight but I've found now that I do exactly what I've always done with the addition of clicking Archangel every time its' up (but since it's off the GCD it doesn't make any difference in my rotation). That bieng said, I do have a few questions/comments:

1) Now I'm not a huge theorycrafter, I've never run a simulator on my character or anything like that, but I do appreciate the effort others have put in to help me figure out stat priorities. My biggest question is why is INT prioritized higher than SP? You mentioned during your post that our mana recouperating spells/abilites are based upon our mana pool and so, as such, INT is an important stat for THAT... but I'm just not understanding how mana regen can be prioritized higher than dmg for a dps role. Am I just missing something? I'm not finding any abilites/talent/spells that give us any kind of dmg increase based upon INT...The way I see it is this:
more INT = more mana = more mana regen = longevity
more SP = more dmg dealt = mobs die faster (doing your job better) = less need for longevity
Now of course I recognize the fact that if you run out of mana you can't do squat, but providing you use your mana regen effectively, and aren't having mana issues, why wouldn't you want to stack SP more so than INT?

2) According to the stat priorities: Haste > Mastery. Why do you recommend the +35 Mastery and minor run speed enchant to boots over the +50 Haste?

3) You mentioned waiting for full Shadow Orbs before casting your first MB and I wonder why? My standard rotation goes as follows: VT>DP>SWP>MB>MF>MF>MB>MF>VT>DP>MB. With an 18% chance to proc an orb, I usually have Empowered Shadows by the 2nd MB; 3rd at the latest; unless the proc gods are frowning on me. The ONLY risk I see in doing this is that SWP might proc an orb and that orb will be burned by the 1st MB. Otherwise, it seems to me that the damage from the 1st MB will outstrip the dmg of the 1 MF you're replacing.

4) These are just a couple of comments i have about rotations: I've found that using this rotation works a little bit better for aggro reduction in randoms or with a new/undergeared tank: VT>SWP>MB>DP>MF. This gives my tank 4-5 seconds to get solid aggro before my DDs hit and my DoTs start ticking away. If it's a running start: SWP>DP>VT>MB>MF. Again, this makes sure that my tank can get an attack or two off before my DDs hit, BUT, sometimes the imp DP will cause a huge aggro spike so I will sometimes forego the DP until I have my VT out.

5) I've also found a fairly usefull way to utilize Mind Spike. When the boss is low (600-700k) I will usually toss out 3 Mind Spikes. With 2/2 Mind Melt this allows me to get off an instant cast, +90% crit chance MB. MS>MS>MS>MB>SWD>SWD. Depending on your haste, this should take around 7 seconds and if any of the MS or SWD crit, you can get ALOT of dmg out to finish them off quick.

@Skrog

1) Int = SP, mana pool; spirit = mana regen, "longevity"

2) Haste, Haste, Haste. Or Earthen vitality if you really like the speed increase for getting out of stupid (which I do).

3) don't bother to get all orbs before casting the first MB. Generally I cast after 2 MFs w/ no orbs.

4) Generally if I pull aggro, I just pop fade and keep on keeping on. Mob will be dead before it wears off if I keep maxing out on it. Really though, if your tank is losing aggro before your first DoT refresh, your DPS isn't the problem, it's the tank's skill.

5) I generally just SW:P all mobs ->3xMS -> MB -> 2xSW:D on all trash now. Higher DPS than multi-dotting. I probably wouldn't MS in the middle of a fight though. Tested MSing about 3 minutes into DPSing a dummy to see how it would look on meters. Had a 1K DPS gain followed by a 2.5K DPS loss while I was re-dotting. Better to just keep DoTs up rather than having to re-DoT on bosses.

1) What he said. Except for the fact that spirit does not give us combat regen. With Cataclysm, Spell power was removed from all items except for weapons. Int is on a 1:1 ration with spell power.

2) Haste is better from a purely DPS standpoint. However, alotta raiders like the speed boost to get outta stuff faster, which means more DPS time.

3) What he said. Kinda. You say that by your second MB you have an orb, that's delaying the uptime of ES, which is bad.

4) This guide isn't written with every single situation in mind. If you have a bad tank, just wait a second or two and fade then start up. If you pull, fade. However if you're running with a competent tank, you really shouldn't be having aggro problems.

5) The OP is talking about at the end of boss encounter. I actually do not have evidence to prove/disprove what you're saying, but it all depends on when your DOTs will tick before the boss dies. If all of them tick when the boss is ~500k, dropping fast, then you're costing yourself DPS/dmg by ripping them off with Mind Spike.
