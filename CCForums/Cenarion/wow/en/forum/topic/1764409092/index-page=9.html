As I mentioned on the first couple main posts of the guide, hit isn't as black and white as it was in Wrath. It's both viable to run either at hit cap or under like I am. Being under just requires more awareness and somewhat more gear since mana might get tough. Keep running Simcraft however on your character, and just adjust your gear according to what stat scales show as your priority.

Just a reminder: Being capped or not being capped does not automatically result in a DPS loss or boost, seeing as there's no definitive math - Though its viable both ways.

I wouldn't honestly. 30% dmg buff to something that does ~1% of our total damage really isn't worth losing what you can replace tier gloves with. BiS for gloves is probably Brackish Gloves, helps you with the hit as well.

Our gear is pretty similar and for me, my stat scaling as of now is Int > Haste > Crit > Mastery = Spirit/Hit - So the values of Mastery and hit are pretty much the same with crit being marginally better, so I'm only reforging my Mastery into Spirit/Hit. As you're doing, reforging all your Mastery into Spirit, is the ideal thing to do :)

Also, don't gem or enchant for hit unless you really have a problem with watching timers and seeing if your DOTs missed or not and a bit slow to reapply them. The Int from gems and enchants greatly outweighs the need to be at hit cap. So being a little bit below is fine as well so you don't sacrifice Int just to reach hit cap.
