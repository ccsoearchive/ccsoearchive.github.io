Even though by the time you throw up VT/DP, you'll have the majority of your buffs, the longer the fight goes, the less your opening rotation matters. Also, the entire time you're MF'ing for an orb, you could have had multiple ticks of VT/DP go off already giving you a good start. After you MB to get ES, you should refresh DP as well.

MB on CD is a DPS increase. There was some work being done about MB'ing with 1+ orbs, but apparently the DPS loss/gain is so miniscule that it doesn't even matter. So for flat out DPS gain, MB'ing on CD while being smart about not letting ES fall off is the best way to go.

Is there still a debate on whether or not to hit cap?


Yeah, its still the same as pre 4.2, but spirit/hit's value has increased over Crit and Mastery to the point where it matters now. MB is now a larger part of our DPS rotation, so a missed MB could result in a DPS sink. I personally am going for hit cap now, though its because of my current gear setup.

If I had the choice, I would not sacrifice any Haste/Int/Spellpower for hit regardless of being capped or not.

So for a straight forward answer, yes its still an on-going debate and there is no right or wrong way to go about it.

Mind Sear:

With fights that have 5+ adds, just straight up use MS. With fights that have 2-4 adds plus the boss, VT each one and then use MS. Mind Sear got a pretty huge buff with 4.1, so it is completely viable to use in this situation now.

From what I see, Mind Sear is counted as a DoT. Because of this, it also benefits from Empowered Shadows and Evangelism (I do 2.8k ticks before these and 3.7k after on dummies) and so just spamming MS from the beginning might not be the way to go. When I AoE, I find doing a normal opener to get ES and 5 stacks or Evangelism before MS to increase the damage output of MS. I usually keep SW:P on at least 1 target to generate Shadow Orbs while casting MS so when ES is about to drop off, I can refresh it and continue casting. It does make our AoE a bit RNG based with failing to generate orbs and a bit harder but it does increase damage output of our AoE.
