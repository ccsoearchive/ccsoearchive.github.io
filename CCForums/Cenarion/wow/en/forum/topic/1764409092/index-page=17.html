I've been playing around with Mind Spike in the main rotation ONLY at certain times. But the rotation I used is so easy to remember, and it gives you some run-around time to help proc apparitions. Here's what I did:

MSx3 -> VT -> SW:P -> DP -> MB -> MF until Archangel -> Archangel + ALL CD's -> MSx3 -> VT -> SW:P -> DP -> MB -> MF away to a normal rotation.

Notice that it's exactly the same at the very beginning as it is after the Archangel + CD pop. Here's the neat part about it. SW:P, DP, MB are all one after the other and instant after the Mind Spikes. This allows 3 global cooldowns of run-around time to proc an apparition. Also notice that VT duration is nearly expired anyway by the time you're casting your second round of spikes, so you're hardly losing any VT DoT time.

I know it's dirty. It's feels extremely filthy to be casting Mind Spike anywhere near our precious DoTs, but try it out for yourself. Play with it, give it a few tries before screwing it up and saying "That guy is freakin' nuts." Granted RNG will be the major deciding factor in how much DPS is lost or gained. But what I like about it especially, is that its the same exact rotation before and after Archangel. And being mobile with those 3 globals have actually come in handy.

I personally think there might be something to squeezing in Mind Spikes and also allowing an assured MB crit while every one of our cooldowns are popped; that's a lot of damage. I not only managed to meet my previous rotations DPS(one from shadowpriest.com), but surpassed it. I'll be keeping an eye on this thread, and if anyone's actually interested and wants some more hard numbers, I'll share 'em.
