"Thanks for our talent Twisted Faith, any spirit that we have on our gear is converted to hit on a 1:1 basis. So basically if you have a choice between, lets say, 50 hit rating or 50 spirit with all the other stats being the same, choose the spirit because it provides mana regen as well as hit."

1. What is the hit cap?

2. What should I reforge to? (I think I saw was haste then crit, but I am tired and its late.)


Hit cap's 1742, which is 17%. An easy way to quick look this is the little tooltip bar when you mouseover hit on the character menu. There's been some on-going and also some outdated discussion about shadowpriests and how the hitcap isn't as important to us anymore, but I've yet to see definite math that < Hit Cap = More DPS, so I'm not changing any of my posts as of yet.

2. It depends, are you reforging for hit cap or reforging to extra DPS stats? If hit cap, try reforging Mastery to Spirit/Hit, if not mastery, then Crit to Spirit/Hit, and if not Crit and its unavoidable, Haste to Spirit/Hit.

If you are hit capped, and can sustain it, reforge Spirit/Hit to Haste > Crit, whichever is available, and Haste is more valuable if there is neither of the stats onthe piece of gear. Then Mastery to Haste > Crit, same deal as above. Crit to Haste as well if there is none of that stat available on that piece of gear.

Stop claiming that hit cap is mandatory, please. I see you mention EJ as "lacking math" but I've posted my personal dps tests regarding the hit cap issue on both of their shadow priest threads (which are, incidentally, full of math) so I know you're mistaken on that point.

It is extremely viable to run as much as 10% under hit cap in raids. This does not mean it's always a dps gain to do so or that every gearset reflects this; your mileage may vary, but the idea that hit cap is as black and white as it was in Wrath is fallacious.

Capping hit, at current gear levels, can be far too restrictive of SP and Haste gains to be worthwhile. That said, there are plenty of arguments in favor of sacrificing some slightly "superior" stats such as crit or mastery for extra spirit or hit: namely, the potentially-exacerbating human reaction factor when recasting dots, the risk of mana starvation/dps loss due to more Dispersion uptime or pre-25% SWD use when multidotting adds during boss encounters, and the fact that many encounters, such as P1 Nef when Ony is not always available for DoT recasts or Rohash/Nezir swapping during Conclave, make instant recasting of DoTs following a miss impossible.

But please alter your main post's simple "get hit capped first" rule. It's misleading, in my opinion, and only priests with ample access to lots of raid gear (much of it spirit and therefore gear that should be prioritized to healers) should even consider trying it.
