Figure I'll put forth the short story of my character's transition from Wrath to Cataclysm. If you know my characters, probably not so much as I(or even you, maybe) would like then up until The Sundering I called my rag tag group of 9 the 'Emissaries of Neltharion' a silly old venture I thought I could make work. The very beginning was so Marry Sue-ish it hurts to think about now...but over time I turned it into a respectable gathering. Over time I got better at writing and story crafting, so by late Wrath I had the present concept that they were all part of the Twilight Hammer, though there was tension between the Twilight and my characters. We were outcasts, anti heroes, drifters without a solid faction or allegiance.

Along came the sundering and my group of now 10(Gilneas Priest, good background to it) were marked for death and all that by the Twilight. Rounded up with other 'traitors' and put to death... Luckily some craftiness, level thinking, and skilled members allowed us to escape. The event that took place after however we do not lay claim to being the cause, but if you read it I worked it in such a way as to say "It could've happened, maybe we caused it, maybe not but it fits and isn't a Mary Sue, just coincidence"

I'll leave it for you to decide, please enjoy the short story of how my group went from the Emissaries of Neltharion to the mercenary group 'Knights of Blackwing'... I had fun with this. Story below in many parts....

"Knights of Blackwing"

It'd been two weeks since I returned from Gilneas with Saelin and my new ally Elizabeth, we weren't back for a day when a messenger came. With him was a fine piece of parchment with the Twilight Hammer stamped into the wax seal. Upon reading it turned out to be a summons from the council, most likely a report on my work in Gilneas and an inquiry as to the Worgan curse I now bore. The one thing that bothered me was that as I found out all my Emissaries had been summoned. Not once since I began the group I called 'The Emissaries of Neltharion' did we get summoned all together. I was aware the Twilight knew about us but the fact they would want us all made my stomach uneasy.
It turned out my suspicions were right though; the summons had been a trap. Through a Twilight Gate we all went and ended up in some dimly lit underground assortment of rooms with no visible way out and any portal out was ineffective. So there we were for a total of three days trying to figure a way out of this damned place before the true agenda had been carried out. I lay most days in the bed they gave me and pondered our choices, many times I'd end up falling asleep and wake up in what I presumed was morning. The same happened tonight, I had been thinking about how now that the Twilight had cast us out we would carry on finishing our plans. Deep in thought all manner of time seemed to pass me by and I fell asleep. I ended up being woken up by a very eccentric voice I knew only too well, "Darrian, vake up Darrian, zis isn't time to be over sleeping, vee are being summoned by Cho'gall; don't doddle"
I could hear Mivius's voice through the haze of sleep and woke up to him sitting at my desk gathering up various papers, his small spectacles balanced precariously upon his bulbous nose as he flipped through our collected stack of documents frown barely masking the worried look on his face.
"Why are we being summoned? We have not been sent out or called upon since I returned from Gilneas with Elizabeth" I managed to stifle my shocked acknowledgement as I sat up, the impact of the Gnome's statement casting a shadow over my rough features, "Wait, Cho'gall is summoning us; personally?"
"Yes, and it iz for zat reason we cannot doddle, if our suspicions are correct then we must gather all we can; zis is it my friend, if we succeed then we have grounds to appeal our case, if not zen ve vill die by 'his' hand or those who we'd ask for refuge from'". The gnome explained; head buried in a sack of parchment I knew contained names of Twilight spies.
We knew the day was coming. When Deathwing as we were ordered to call him; would rise forth into the lands once more, reshaping the world in a vision as twisted as his mind. The preparations had been going on for weeks, tremors from his restlessness spanned the globe and only we the Twilight Cult knew their source. It was since then we had been compiling data to give to Varian and Garrosh to prove our trust, whether we were accepted or not that was up to them. I quickly got out of my bed and dressed in my usual attire, slipping on my shirt, fastening all the buckles and clasps, and attaching my swords to their rightful places. I walked to the other side of my room and picked up my old hat; as I stared into the worn cracks of that aging leather I looked to Mivius and said, "The others are they ready for this? Our fates are balanced on but a wire, the slightest falter would mean destruction" He only nodded at my words.
"Do not underestimate zem Darrian. You sought zem out from all corners of Azeroth for zis mission of yours. Each one of us could've killed you were you stood and you knew zat; you did your research. Yet you found us one by one and laid out your sheer unbelievably fool hardy plan and one by one you von us over. So here we are at the end of one mission, you've vorked with the other flights and aspects, braved the unbearable and gazered your allies close." The Gnome ranted on, his hands as expressive as his accent, he put his hand to his face, running his fingers through his white hair he gave me a look such as a teacher would give when his worst pupil surpassed his every expectation. "You managed to put a group togezer of such conflicting personalities as would kill each other in different circumstances, yet here we are under your word and have in some far stretch of zee word become friends. It is zat that makes me question vat your true greatest accomplishment is."
His words brought a smile to my face as I placed my old hat on my head; I grabbed my rifle from its resting place against the wall and looked back. "Are you ready?" I ask as with one last glimpse he places all the papers into his satchel.
"I am ready, vee best be off quickly so as to meet wiz zee ozers before our meeting wiz Deathwing" He said while walking past me into the dimly lit corridor, followed by me closely behind.

For one ridiculous moment I thought this was yet another tremor, but the wave of fear rolled over me as I realized what was about to happen. I pushed at the unstable ground with all my might and made it to my feet, two words screamed past my lips as I began to run full tilt, the earthquake long forgotten, "Deathwing! Run!"
I did not need any more motivation for my Knights to understand as they all used their abilities to quicken their paces. Be it the ethereal wolf ahead of me, the great crow above me, or ahead of me the hulking Draenie with Aikaena under one arm and Mivius clinging to his shoulder. We ran with every ounce of energy we could muster, mid stride I looked back at the now running civilians, and what they were running from. There on wings so large as to cast the entire park district into shade was Deathwing in all his fury, I could see the rage in his eyes and for a brief moment I wondered if this was our fault. We ran as I heard him draw breath, expanding his massive chest, I had to think fast. "The canals jump into the canal!" I called over the screaming of people.
