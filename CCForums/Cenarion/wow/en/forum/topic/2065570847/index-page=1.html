This is going to change in 4.1 but as it sits fury warriors start out with 8 points into their mastery. This gives them 44% bonus to all abilities that require you to be enraged or cause you to be enraged. A naked fury warrior can regenerate 52% of their health over 10 seconds. This number increases pretty quickly as we gain 5.6% to the master 'per point'.

So what likely happened is the warrior you were fighting was mastery stacked or had a mastery trinket. I think I've gotten mine to regenerate something stupid like 80% of my health without too much effort.

I have a rogue as well as a warrior and am decently adept at both. Best way to fight a fury warrior that has a lot of gear is to almost literally shiv spam them. If they can't keep an enrage up they can't use their hardest hitting ability. Also watch for their recklessness and either KS/disarm/evasion or something because that's when they most powerful. They get pretty easy if you time your defensive CDs well and keep enrages off them as much as possible. Also side note I think there is an addon somewhere that will highlight buffs that can be dispelled. So you can more clearly see when and what to shiv.

Though sadly since berserker rage is off the global its pretty difficult to catch and remove that before he uses enraged regeneration. Hope this helps a bit.

Snikt is correct. Once I pop enraged regeneration, you can't stop it. It also gains healing from Field Dressing (6%... so 31.8% of our health... further increased if we have a buff that increases our health, which we'll all have in the next patch via rallying cry) and mastery, which is being nerfed (i.e. our base amount in fury is being dropped). Arms warriors don't get any benefit to it from mastery.

Lucky for you, I cannot activate Death Wish or Berserker Rage while it is running. I can still pop Recklessness though. That being said, if you see it running, there is no need to shiv (unless you are sub and lose your crippling for some reason) until it fades. Luckily for non-warriors, it is on a 3-minute CD.

My advice to a rogue would be to frustrate me as much as possible and get me to make a mistake. This typically doesn't work well in high brackets as players are fairly controlled at that level, but pissing off lower ranked players can usually force them into a mistake... that suggestion with bleeds, bleeds, and more bleeds. Many players will push too hard and over extend themselves if they can't reach you and see their health ticking away with no recourse.
