How do you co-locate servers that have to communicate with one-another in real-time for events that are timed down to the millisecond? (Cast time: 1.1 seconds) There is a lot involved with a seamless MMORPG world (unlike EverQuest, where you had to "zone" and zone servers could be disparate).

Think about this, while you're sitting in Stormwind, you can literally observe the activities of an entire raid happening in real-time while you're not even there. Imagine the network traffic involved just to your machine alone. Now imagine coordinating that on a millisecond level between 25 players AND all of the servers involved on the backend (databases, messaging queues, et al).

Imagine if you co-located those servers 20 to 30ms apart... So much for that 1.1 second cast time - they'd have to always round up.

This isn't a web-site where you load a page and the server gets a break from you. Real-time has its drawbacks - network latency being one of them.
