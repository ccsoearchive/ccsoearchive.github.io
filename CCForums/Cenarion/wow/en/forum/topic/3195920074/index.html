The once highly populated, vibrant, highly progressed in PvE, and active in PvP realm of Skullcrusher is dying. Our server population has dropped to dangerously low levels. Recruiting has become virtually impossible. Many good players are transferring off the server in droves leaving guilds unable to fill their 25 man raids. We have spoken to GM's and they have directed us to the forums and I was chosen by my guild to start this thread.

Immortality is a day one Skullcrusher guild with an extensive history of world firsts (I was there for all of these) and cutting edge progression. Over time, the loss of original members, replacement members, and a drastically reduced recruiting pool has all but destroyed the guild and the server. Recruiting previously was easy as the player base for the server was massive, even without paid transfers at the end of original WoW. Skullcrusher endured multiple rounds of free transfers off due to the worst lag I have ever seen in any game. Raiding became impossible in peak times. Both horde and alliance sides have suffered as our server population has been decimated. 25 man Raiding guilds especially now lose members and cannot replace them. It's quite depressing.

In recent months and days full guilds and quality players have been transferring off the server due to frustration with poor progression simply because raids cannot be filled. This was never a problem until the end of WOTLK and Cataclysm. I would move my guild from the server, but we are day one and have many inactive players who were a huge part of our guild in years past still on our list and we refuse to abandon them.

We ask now for you to fix the server. Give us free transfers to bring in players, merge another server with ours. Anything would help.

Regards,
Dante - Former Guild Master of Immortality
Once a 40-man raiding guild
Now a 25-man raiding guild stuggling to survive as one.

In the grand scheme of things, Skullcrusher is not actually that bad off. While it is true that it is not at it's once marvelous glory of vanilla or even BC, there are servers in far worse predicaments than ours.

The server of which I once played Horde on, Blackwing Lair, is a fantastic example of what is truly a "dead" server. As that server stands, it is not only low population but it's Horde to Alliance ratio is around the levels of 12-1 or 13-1. Not an exaggeration.

While I can agree that this server is not as highly populated as it used to be, it's still not so bad off that it needs some form of assisted transplant. Also which, even by coming up with a solution to "merge" another server with us, the true problem of the server could still remain, which is the very unbalanced faction numbers. Even by the average server's standards, 7-1 ratios between the two sides is incredibly imbalanced and leads to two things.

1. New players who do come to the server join the "majority". Mostly due to the perceived idea that being a part of the majority makes things easier for your overall experience.

2. Current players get tired of being the "minority". This was most prevalent in the previous expansion, when population balance determined success or failure in Wintergrasp battles. While that issue is at least fixed (as sides in Tol Barad must be equal unless they cannot be so), those that transferred likely did not switch back.

If anything else, two solutions seem to fit the scenario our server faces best.

1. If mergers begin to happen, a server merge must include a server that has a higher Alliance to Horde population. This would assist in evening out the numbers of the server's population, and would not give the perception that one faction is "better" to play than the other.

2. Free faction changes must be offered to the majority faction for a limited duration. While this may not help the true problem of the server as much as solution A, some Horde players may dislike the choice they made to be Horde, but simply do not have the funds available to them to reverse faction.

In the grand scheme of the game as a whole, I do agree that something must be done for servers that are in either of two situations. Extremely imbalanced population totals, or extremely low population totals in general, or perhaps even both. While I do not agree that our server is quite in the latter stage yet, I can definitely state as fact that we are (and have been for quite some time now) in the first position.

I do hope that at some point in time, Blizzard takes both of these dilemmas seriously and offers a plausible solution to correct it.
