Over the years feral druids have been given different tools and tricks to try and augment their weak performance in the pvp theatre. Examples of these are the before mentioned Predatory Strikes and also Nurturing Instinct.

Out of concern that my pve days might come to an end when this pvp issue is addressed let me say that these utilities that once were needed to make the class better are no longer needed. Ferals have been given only one thing, dmg, which many other classes have so lets look at trimming down some of the unneeded bonuses we have.

I think the instant cast talent has been talked about enough and should be removed. If we want to cc someone that badly outside of our current stuns and slows then we need to pay the price of a cast time. We're a melee attack class, leave the casting for the birds.

I like Nurturing Instinct for the +20% healing received by others but I've always found the added bonus of spell power from our base stat out of place. Now that AP is no longer in game and Agi is on all gear this talent has been buffed a lot seeing how I can currently get over 5k spell power in catform.

Survival instincts should be only usable in bear form. Being able to reduce all incoming dmg by 60% in conjunction with barkskin while maintaining all damage capabilities as a cat is a little much in my mind.

I agree that the Berserk glyph needs to be removed. Even in a pve setting I feel it's a little rediculous at times. To counter the removal of this glyph would be the already present reworked tiger's fury glyph which is comparable but gives much shorter burst capabilities.

Ah yes, Unheeded Warning. Throughout the history of wow static numbers and buffs have had far reaching unseen uses that often show up months after they are released when they are exposed or compared to higher level gear. Examples: Thunderfury, Stormherald, Lifegiving Gem, Hand of justice, Darkmoon Card: Greatness. In this case this was just very bad timing, the trinket came out and feral druids were buffed up. I'd say that adjusting some feral mechanics should come before this trinket is reviewed and altered. It's not like half a dozen classes are using it, just one and notably well, which lends itself to being the class that's at fault and not the item.

Long but readable. TLDR at the end for all you fifth graders;)

The basic issue generating this thread (ferals are really strong right now) is genuine, and deserves a look. However, there's also a lot of hyperbole and misunderstanding on display. A couple concerns:

1. Cyclone

"Instant" Cyclone isn't instant. It requires a MINIMUM of 6 gcds (3 of which must be within melee range of the same target). I need 3 gcds (with 2 crits) to generate 5 combo points. I need another gcd to use a finisher. If I'm lucky, I'm still in melee range, and I get to use something other than Savage Roar. The 5th gcd is used casting the instant cyclone. For the sake of completeness, you need to realize that now I'm standing there in caster form, exerting no damage or healing pressure on anyone or anything. The 6th gcd gets me back into cat form. That's a lot of setup time (and luck, considering that ferals aren't sitting on anything like 66% crit in present gear).

Cyclone itself is a strange beast. What my opponents see is a period of complete helplessness, and I agree that can be enormously demoralizing. Of course, you're immune to both damage and healing over that period. Both your allies and MY allies can find that to be super annoying.

Apart from the double-edged effects of cyclone, there are some notable built-in balances. Cyclone is much shorter range than the abilities of our most important counters. An intelligent, observant enemy healer or (shudder) frost mage is rarely close enough to have to worry. On top of that, Cyclone has a shorter duration than most other garden variety cc abilities. At 6 seconds (before any DR has kicked in), I only have 5 seconds to actually do anything to your partner after I use that one gcd to get back in cat form.

Where does that leave us? Cyclone is different. It's powerful but it has some hard-wired shortcomings, and it requires a great deal of thoughtful play to deploy in a match-changing way. Frankly, MORE abilities should work this way.

If we HAVE to nerf cyclone, I recommend making it dispellable. The problem is that this would really hit my resto and balance siblings hard. Maybe we could just make feral cyclone dispellable. Or if that's too clunky, we could make the Predatory Swiftness proc dispellable. Oh wait... that's been dispellable for a long time now. Maybe give it a big red flag animation, and some DBM-style warning text? Fur-covered wings have some precedent, I guess.

2. Mobility

This has been qq'd to death (on both sides). There are enough spammable or aoe snares in this game that anyone who wants to make a feral waste every single gcd powershifting already does so. Move on folks. Ferals have tools to increase our time on target, but the fearsome abilities everyone blanches at (Predatory Swiftness procs, bleeds, and giant trinket-boosted Shred crits) rely utterly on that time-on-target.

3. Damage

Scrolling combat text is funny. Those fat 5 digit purple numbers imprint in our brains and make us toss and turn at night. The far-more-expected 4 digit numbers get purged from our memories pretty quickly, though. This is true no matter who we face, and I'm certainly guilty of some excessive"wtf"moments myself (giving and receiving).

Feral IS bursty right now by design. But we've tried the reverse (slowly ramping big bleeds), and the only people who seemed to like that were ferals and our twos partners. Maybe bleeds need to be dispellable? Bring back the Loofa? Or maybe we could steal a page from some existing PVE debuffs (http://www.wowhead.com/spell=38801): a target healed to 100% is purged of a bleed. Any measure used to scrub a debuff that requires so much ramp up should similarly require some effort or tradeoffs.

Others have mentioned that Unheeded Warning is an issue, and I'm sure that's true. Because it increases weapon damage (not dps), it's the last refuge of an older non-normalized world. Combine this with a feral cat's 1.0 claw speed, and we have the makings of a problem. I think this area right here is a clear nerf waiting to happen. Weapon dps is worth something like 3 times as much as our primary stat (Agi)... is anyone else in the game in this boat?

But before we nerf damage (elegantly or otherwise), I have to say I'm skeptical. I'm unconvinced that feral damage in the PVP environment is too high or too fast. It might be, but all I've seen are anecdotes (my screenshots of 45k shred crits are anecdotes with pixels... Sorry!). I'm keeping an open mind! Bring data, and I'll line up for my nerf pills. But if feral PVE dps is lackluster (a better-supported but still imperfect assertion), what mechanism explains such astronomical (perceived) PVP damage? Do raid bosses have hidden anti-feline defenses that we can't document? Everyone I know feels like they occasionally get gibbed by someone. Are ferals really disproportionately represented on one side of that relationship? Prove it to us...


TLDR -

"Instant" cyclone takes a long time to set up and doesn't last long enough to do much with. Observant players can already prevent it.

Feral mobility was successfully nerfed not long ago, and the mobility we have left is absolutely required to execute any part of the class playstyle.

Feral damage might feel big and bursty, but it's not obvious that we're actually bigger or burstier than anyone else at present. There's ample evidence from PVE that feral damage is in fact lackluster outside of Patchwork-type scenarios.
