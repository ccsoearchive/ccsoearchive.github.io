Instead of rehashing all the obvious points from everyone before me I just want to give my opinion of how all this constant feral changing effects me and maybe other feral players...

It's damn exhausting.

I started playing in BC. In BC I remember playing a power shifting game to be competitive in PVE... until high gear levels when there was no way I could keep up with other DPS classes. I remember being a bear mostly in BC, cause well, that's what were pretty good at. At least in BC I don't remember any fight or fights where I couldn't use shred when I actually got to be a kitty.

Power shifting went away in ICC, I believe we had a period of FAP on weapons, bleeds became decently important, or at least more so than BC with rake becoming a factor. All I remember about ICC is we were actually competitive though we lacked the utility of say a rogue, nothing new there. We were fine tanks at the beginning until the SD debacle (which is still a concern) ArP became king and we were actually worth bringing to a raid for DPS. We had one fight where shred was impossible, and maybe one or 2 others where positioning made us less effective. (Yogg positioning comes to mind, tentacles, ect)

Cata comes, ArP is gone, we seem nerfed. We get an awesome trinket that drops like candy that helps us out. More fights where shredding is a pain (for various reasons) or our lack of AoE makes us less than desirable. Strength nerf, more balancing... Right now I think we are competitive for PvE DPS, just barely.

Exhausting I tell you. I am not even getting into all the gear changes that have happened over the years with staves and leather.

The only other level 85 toon I dislike playing more is my lock, I know locks that quit the game over the early changes that happened in this expansion.

My Druid is my main. My Druid is invested in the game. It will be hard to want to continue playing in this expansion if I am constantly regulated back to the end of the line in PvE DPS.

Thanks for the staff that makes me look cool, hopefully I will get it soon and be going fire kitty!

This is one of the things that makes me hate this game sometimes.

The devs and the playerbase equate "doing well in PVP" with "overpowered in PVP." I think that's an immature and fallacious equation.

This game's (very lofty) objective is to balance 30-ish specs with one-another. It's not going to happen, but that's the goal. When people notice that a class which has previously been a non-threat is suddenly beating them often, they come here to post about which aspects of that class need nerfing. They might post constructively, accurately pointing out the strengths of the class. And, at what seems to be complete random, the devs swing the nerf bat. (If you don't think it seems random, Mages are over ----> there.)

The problem, of course, is that there is a HUGE gap between being "overpowered" and being "capable" in PVP. Feral druids have long complained that, the moment they become capable, everyone screams "OP!" and we get nerfed. Do you think we're making this up? We're not that pathetic. Trust me. Here are some drawbacks to being FERAL that the "whiners" are overlooking:

1) In your damage form, you have incredibly limited survival capabilities.
2) Those "40k" Ferocious Bites cost 5 combo points and a crapload of energy. Mages and warriors crit for higher numbers, more often, more easily.
3) Lol @ whoever said we have infinite mana.
4) Predatory Strikes requires burning *5 combo points* to use. If you're letting a Feral just sit on you all the time building CP's, you probably deserve to die.
5) Berzerk provides no CC protection, and can be EASILY mitigated by stuns, incapacitates, or even--*spit*---ROOTS. Blizz's logic for nerfing Berzerk's fear-immunity was that it was impossible to mitigate for some classes. Now it's not only possible, it's EASY! We need that long duration on Berzerk to play our deadly game of "kite me or die."

Sad thing is, this is posted on page 25 of the discussion... so not a soul will read it or care.
