I arena quite a decent amount with friends as well and I actually have noticed a few things as well. This is a completely unbiased list as I have played just about every class and almost every spec to at least 2k MMR (I currently have 7 level 85s in at least 3.3k resil).

... as a rogue I rely heavily on being able to peel a target with stuns and slows, feral druids are UNSLOWABLE they have infinite mana to shift out of every single slow without ANY REPERCUSSIONS...


This is something I too noticed before but especially recently upon playing today on my death knight in the 2v2 bracket. The problem is that ferals have a nearly 100% uptime. I thought about why this is, and the thing I came up with is that they have their 30% increased movement speed from feral swiftness. This makes them unparalleled in their speed (other classes max out at 15%).

Added to this the feral shift which removes slows, and ferals become virtually unpeelable. They also can now break their own roots (which is not too big of a deal) however when paired with a healer leaves them little to no consequence of tunneling and LoSing their healer across the map, because unlike other melee classes, they can take off their own roots if their healer cannot get to them, then stay in range if they don't have skull bash or stampeding roar to break themselves out. There is no penalty for being outplayed and making "bad decisions".

The final component to this problem as I see it is the feral slow effect which is up literally 100% of the time coupled with their stuns and instant cyclones. I realize that the speed is not that big of an issue, as it is the same as a warrior's hamstring and lesser than a DK or rogue's slows, however when coupled with their movement speed, their ability to peel, uptime on targets, and stuns are seemingly, "overpowered."

In fact I noticed the only real way to keep them away from me even a little bit was kiting them over desecration over and over with freedom, but with their 2 gap closers (charge and skull bash) it still was impossible to stay on their partner.

My math on it was about as follows:
Feral With a DK on it:
30% increase on speed - 50% slow = -20% movement speed (assuming desecration, due to shifting chains)
DK with a Feral on it:
15% increase on speed - 50% slow = -35% movement speed

My math may be slightly incorrect, or I may not have the exact grasp on the mechanics, however the feral is moving at least 15% faster from this example, assuming he is constantly on desecration. This is the best case scenario as rogues and warriors have it much worse since their slows can be shifted out of.

This paired with their stuns and instant cyclones are just too much.

If I had to propose changes (aside from those already suggested changes from hotted) I would say slow the movement speed on feral swiftness, and/or add some mechanic where the feral must make a conscious decision to apply the slow, not just apply it constantly by spamming damage without any forethought.

Then if i do try to gouge or stun them the chances of a dodge or parry are MUCH higher then most other classes...


This is also a painfully obvious problem, especially on my rogue (although it is more than a slight pain on my warrior and DK as well). I cannot tell you how many times I have had a teammate die because I was unable to land a peel on a feral.

I get a lot of unlucky parries on gouges on other classes, however the amount ferals dodge is simply insane, and makes it not even worth attempting to use (read as wasting) gouge to try and peel.

The same thing goes for kidney shot, throw down, etc. The problem is these are essential peels that must go out. It is so out of control that I decided the other day that I was going to shadow step the feral to ensure that I was 100% behind the player then was going to land a kidney shot on him directly after. What happened was that he DODGED it from behind! Literally a global after shadow step, him tunneling my partner, and my KS was dodged???

I am not sure if it's just the multipliers for their agility that sends their dodge levels through the roof, and I would be fine if they had a decent amount of dodge in bear form (however I think them being able to survive so efficiently in bear form is kind of ludicrous as well) as that is sort of their "go defensive" time but that much passive dodge in their DPS cat form is more than slightly unfair to other classes.




The final thing I have noticed (which has been slightly addressed) is the insane burst damage. I played against a ret/feral (on both my DK and warlock) who ran in, popped every single cool down, trinkets, everything and literally took me from 100%-0% in the time it took for my healer to be cycloned and trinket it, in which I was also stunned the whole time. Even if my healer did manage to get a heal off, he could have been repentanced and I still would have been dead.

The changes to burst have already been brought up though, so I feel that it is unnecessary to beat a dead horse with repeated suggestions for damage reductions, however the point was brought up to show another person's real, relevant, current experiences with the burst and how out of control it is.
