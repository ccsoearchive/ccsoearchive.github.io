I've laughed pretty hard at all these threads, and will continue to do so. I laugh especially hard when I see a resto druid and rogue complaining about Berserk. If only there was some way to remove it from a feral (hint: soothe and shiv), making it an annoying gnat for the next 3 minutes...

For anyone who cares to armory me, its fairly evident that I'm not a hardcore, or in anyway a serious PvP'er. I do offer some thoughts abouts druid, specifically feral pvp though.

Anyways, incoming wall of text crit; at least I warned you.

1) Ferals, once a class that was king of mobility, has become a staggering crochety old man in terms of movement. We have 2 root breaks. We can be slowed by any number of abilities (again, seeing a rogue complain makes me giggle; if only rogues had some way of slowing targets). Once we've broken 2 roots, without dispells, we're basically immobile for the next 2 minutes. Don't complain about ferals teaming up with a class that can provide freedoms/dispells, any player can do that.

2) Our CC is fantastic. We can root melee, and 'clone healers/ranged. We can even do it instantly in some cases. For everyone who is complaining about ferals' CC, perhaps you don't understand how it works. It takes considerable time to pool energy, and our casts only become a surefire instant when we use a finisher with 5 CP's. While its not the hardest thing in the world to set up, its very resource intensive. If only there was a way that CC didn't always hit you for full duration...

3) Our energy regen (in PvP) is abysmally slow. A Feral Cat in PvP gains something around 9-10 energy a second, since haste is a garbage stat. Shred costs 40. Thats correct, the spell you all are complaining about hitting for so much, costs 40% of our energy. Outside of a Berserk, you should only ever have to eat 2-3. You can in fact eat none however, and just not give us your back.

4) Against pure melee classes (who derive most, or at least a greater portion, of their damage from phsyical damge) we have great defensive cooldown's. Bear form is so effective because of the armor bonus, couple with a moderate, but still present, damage output. However since the armor does nothing against spells and spell damage, its only effective versus melee.

5) This might have been better placed with my bit about energy regen but we're awful at target swapping. Because our attacks are so energy intensive, it takes time to set up damage on a target. I understand that all melee function in a similiar way, but you'd be hard pressed to convince me that ferals are better at target swapping than you.

6) Berserk no longer breaks fear, or makes us fear immune. You can fear us into oblivion, and laugh as we scamper across the arena/battleground.

7) Our self healing is laughable. I routinely get outhealed by Rogues, DK's, Warriors, and Rets, basically every melee class but Shamans.

To be honest, I expected the sheeple to revolt when the feral damage changes came out. I say changes, because they're not all buffs. Our net AP was decreased. Our bleed damage was decreased (again). All our abilities had their base damge decreased. While I'm not going to lie, and say there was no increase of damage, to a large degree all that happened is we suddenly scaled with gear. To people who don't know/research/understand feral dps, i could see how it looked like amazing buffs. To those of us who actually do get feral, it was anywhere from a 3-9% buff to direct damage, assuming 372+ gear/weapons.

I think the biggest reason that all the outcry is focusing on feral presently, is because of the parity of classes. DK's and Warriors did recieve nerfs, and they're no longer the only ones who can put out a truckload of damage in a hurry.

Another possibility is the lack of viability for the other two druid specs in small scale PvP. In RBG's, World PvP, and regular BG's, Balance and Resto druids do quite well. Currently at least, they're rather weak in arenas. Because so many druids don't feel their other specs are viable in small scale PvP, I would surmise that some are switching to feral. Our class has a high skill cap, but its not impossible to play. People are just seeing more ferals than they did before, and probaly more skilled ones.

The end point of this, is that, at least to me (and I admit I'm at least partially biased), feral is in no way overpowered. Is it strong currently? Sure is. But (and yes I realise I'm leading with a preposition, deal with it) there are a myriad of ways to control, mitigate, or otherwise frustrate ferals and their damage. I feel like if people had concrete discussions about tactics, abilities, and strategy, it would contribute a lot more to their playing than chiming in with "OMG YES FERALZ ARE SO OP, PLEASE NERF TO THE GROUND."
