Let's please not devolve too much into a discussion of "get your PvE/PvP balance out of my PvP/PvE game!" It's just not beneficial to anyone.

That said, we're happy with Feral performance in PvE overall. It we were to nerf Predatory Swiftness, it'd have a minor impact on PvE. Unheeded Warning is both a PvE and PvP problem. The particular mechanics of the trinket keep it disproportionately powerful for Feral druids. Nerfing Berserk would have PvE ramifications, but we'll take steps overall to make sure your dps doesn’t drop in PvE. We do everything we can to ensure we're considering all aspects of gameplay when approaching class balance.

i know you were talking about resto vs feral, but it's also because ferals, at least the long term ones, are used to being beaten into submission any time we get competitive in pvp. check my PSA post from a few weeks ago ;).



anyways, i've been feral forever in wow terms, and i have a pretty strong grasp of the class, as well as high level (but *far* from pro) level of play. i've also carried a bunch of friends / guildies to 1800/1850 for weps, or similar ranges for points in cata, so i have a pretty solid idea of play across all rating ranges up to 2.2k (pre-MMR inflation). i've played far more 2s then 3s, mostly due to my own weakness in tracking a 3s match well, but i've still been 2k+ in 3s in 4 seasons (+s3 as resto, but hated it). done my share of pve at both the top 50 world and casual levels as well.

when 4.2 notes were announced, i predicted a wave of forum QQ on fero bites. when the scaling math was posted, i felt the unheeded warning trinket would end up far too strong, and cause all kinds of problems. i'm just glad the first set of numbers didn't go live, before they moved a bunch of that damage into savage roar. those numbers + UHW would have been madness, i tell you.


i do think the current feral is too strong. i just want to see the nerfs hit the right aspect, and not gut the spec that i know and love (more then already has been done - QQ shapeshifting nerf).


we've had instant cyclones for a very long time, and while some have complained about them (of course), nobody really thought it was OP until now.

why is that, do you think?

well, it's because feral never really had the burst potential to be lethal during that 6s cyclone, of which only 3s are actually useful to the feral, before now. more accurately, it's the 5-10s following the instant cyc then partner's CC off of, but the point is the same:


feral burst damage needs to be nerfed

and it needs to start with the UHW proc.

it's ridiculously, amazingly OP for feral, so much so it's almost hard to explain how good it is with numbers. it's akin to giving a feral a weapon from 2 seasons in the future for 10s every ~minute.

think about that for a minute..... i know all the warrior's eyes just went wide ;).


it's soooo good for feral mechanics it's OP in pve too (which is really why it's even on bliz's radar tbh, but whatever). it's a 359 ilvl trinket that compares favorably to fireland trinkets, even heroic versions*.

with this trinket in play there is no possible way to balance feral pve and pvp damage. it's probably not as gamebreaking as the legendary staff will be, but it's damn close. unlike the legendary, it's a boe trash drop from BoT trash, so almost any feral with 10k gold to drop has one (ie: every competitive feral).


it is my sincere hope that the *first* thing blizzard does in adjusting feral is to effectively remove this trinket from play.


the next target should be berserk duration. 25s is silly long. it's completely unreasonable to try to peel a target for 25s in pvp.

this is further compounded by the UHW proc (of course) as even with no management of the ICD at all it has a ~50% chance to be up during your 'zerk, and if you get significant uptime during the proc with 'zerk you simply merc folks.

berserk duration should be shortened, and it's cooldown moved to 2 mins to compensate.


next up would be fero bite. i've written a bunch on it across many posts, but the short version is that you can't make the current incarnation pve viable without it being broken in pvp.

moving a hefty portion of it to a short duration bleed effect is something i've recommended probably a dozen times since pre-cata notes were announced, and would work. barring such a major restructure of the ability, it honestly needs the colossus smash treatment, with separate pve / pvp damage.


if the above changes result in feral pve damage being too low, further damage should be shifted into SR, as that has minimal pvp ramifications.


Predator's Swiftness

if you put a shortish (10-15s) ICD on the PS proc it doesn't really change much. yes, it will limit those rare times where a feral pulls a double instant cyc out of nowhere in 5 seconds, but those are truly very rare. i'd be fine with such a change.

if you put a longer (25s+) ICD on the PS proc you just denied the feral a huge amount of his utility - it's not just cyclone we use the proc for. i'd really hate to see this.

if you remove cyclone from PS you'll pretty much kill the spec in high level play.

if you nerf cyclone directly you hurt restos and further kick boomkins to the curb.


it's primarily burst damage that changed in 4.2 (well, along with dk, warrior, and rogue nerfs) that made feral seem like such a beast since patch.

this is compounded by balance being generally awful (as always) yet getting nerfed, and restos being ill-equipped to deal with the burst damage in game available to pretty much all arena viable specs, so good arena players of those specs drift towards feral, especially at the beginning of a season when re-gearing is easy.


i agree there are too many ferals now. i'm used to being a rare and special snowflake that has to get friends to run arena with them because nobody wants a feral. these days i get random tells from hpallies and wars "u wantz make gud 3s team?" :/


but please, nerf the stupid burst, don't take away my predator's swiftness.


thanks for reading (if any of you did).


edit: it's worth noting i strongly agree with Datah's post on instant cyclones in spirit. i just think the reality is that ferals will end up non-competitive again, while whatever other broken stuff will stay.
