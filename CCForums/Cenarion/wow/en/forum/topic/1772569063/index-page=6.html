You actually have it backward - the IC channels are /say and /yell (conversational distance, essentially), while /general and /trade (zone-wide and cities-wide - distances our characters could not converse across without the use of specialized magic or technology) are not required to be in character. Most roleplayers prefer to do our roleplaying face-to-face, so it makes sense to require those channels to be IC. (Why /party chat was included by Blizzard as an IC chat channel I'll never know...)

So, if we're roleplaying in /say (sitting in a tavern, standing on the docks, whatever), and a couple of people next to us are using /say to discuss Tron, yes, that does break immersion.

This. Though a veteran of RPers in other games, forums, etc., I've never actually brought myself to RP on WoW. But I have experienced some RP servers briefly, and I never understood why people insist on making channels in-character. How would your character be communicating with every other character in their zone or in every town? "Well, technology/magic, duh!" Sure, but I highly doubt that every single character in existance would have access to such a novelty. It's just not "realistic", even in a fantasy setting, and for me, it would definitely break immersion to have people RPing in General and Trade. Use yell if you must, it at least makes some sense.
