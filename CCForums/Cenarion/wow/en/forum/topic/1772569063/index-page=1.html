(Since there is no "Suggestions" forum, I thought this would be the closest place to post this)

At the launch of World of Warcraft, Role-Playing (RP) and (Later) Role-Playing PvP (RP-PvP) servers were intended for role-playing. What would separate these servers from Normal and PvP servers? They were intended for greater immersions in the game world. Naming was stricter and there were real penalties for griefing role-play. It even flourished. Then something happened. The player base grew but the Game Master support base did not keep up. Now the GM staff largely ignores the RP and naming policies while RP has been marginalized on the realms that have those two letters listed in their server type. The real power to reclaiming RP servers does not just lie in the GM staff, but also in the RP player base.

What players can do

1. RP in the open!

Walk when turning in quests or going to forges in towns. Emote about auction house prices while browsing. When you see a fellow RPer, /salute or /say a greeting to them.

2. Put verbal harrassers on ignore and report them.

RP Griefing is a power trip. When the RPers scatter or leave, that only encourages the offender. Be sure to emphasize in the ticket that this is an RP or an RP-PvP server.

3. Report physical harrassment

Ignore only works when the person /says or /yells something. What happens when they decide to strip and dance? Ignore does not work there. Then the report key will also help.

4. After you've reported the griefers, RP their presence

Griefers will stop griefing when they see that their intended targets are still on the site and are talking about how addled the minds of the offenders are. When they don't have the results, their power trip is cut short and they look for another target. At the same time, they have a pile of ignores and reports on their account and a group of roleplayers laughing at the griefer. From here, it's up to the GM staff.

(Amended 16 Jan 2011) 5. Create a cross-guild RP channel and maintain it

This will create a channel that is player-moderated. This will allow for in-character RP and a coordination of RP events. If a griefer finds their way on the channel, they will be banned from it very quickly.

(Amended 19 Jan 2011) 6. Create a mass-report names list

Too many players are disheartened by a lack of enforcement. The best way to get the staff to enforce the naming policy is not to slate report names in small groups, but mobilize and mass report.

Suggestions for the GMs

1. Implement the realm type ban

Players who come to RP and RP-PvP realms to grief RPers have no business being on such realms. If they grief RP, they should be restricted from using RP-type realms for no less than 24 hours. The pile of tickets against the offender's account would be a testimony complete with transcripts from the /say and /yell channels as evidence.

2. Increase the opportunity costs (penalties) of RP griefing

Anything less than 24 hours is nothing. A person could be banned from the game for three hours and not know it, which is not a real penalty. The increasing scale of the RP realm ban should be 24 hours, followed by 72 hours, followed by one week, followed by account banned from playing on RP realms. That would force the habitual offender to pay for a transfer from the RP servers.

What else could the player be doing if s/he were playing during those ban periods? PvE raiding, running arenas, rated battlegrounds, farming, doing dailies. 24 hours means they are unable to do daily quests. 72 hours means they have to miss 1-2 nights of raiding, especially if they are a tank or an healer. One week means they will be behind gearwise. Permanent ban means they would have to shell out $25 per character for transfer. That is how the opportunity costs work.

3. Enforce naming

Part of the intention of immersion was to head into a fantasy world without running into someone with the name of "Stabgank" or "Pwninjoo." That enforcement has gone down. The first penalty should be a rename but of the player's choice. The second penalty means a GM-assigned name. If the person does not like the GM-assigned name, that's $10 for recustomization.

(Amended 16 Jan 2011) 4. Patch in an in-game role-playing mod

This will allow players to give titles, last names, and allow for biographies rather than relying one a plethora of addons. This would be a sign that the development team does care about role-players.

Conclusion

Players have more power than what they give themselves credit for. GMs only have the power when the players give them that. I encourage role-players to stand up and unite to reclaim the realms.
