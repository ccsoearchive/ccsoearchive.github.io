While I really enjoyed the original post, and agree with its message, I have some issues with Role Play in this game as it stands now that were not addressed in that post.

The first issue is with the naming policy debate. On one hand we have players who want others to use RP sounding names (which I agree with), but on the other hand we have a system in the game that does not allow for spaces or capital letters in the middle of a name.

For example, I can have a character named Allisynne.. but I can not have one named Allisynne the Knife, or Allisynne Darkhammer.. or even Allisynne Dar’Norne. This also assumes that the name Allisynne is even available.

There are a limited number of decent RP names available with only having a single connected string of characters to choose from. After numerous (failed) attempts to name a character (because the chosen names are already taken), players often get frustrated and settle on Ihealzyou as their name.

Bottom Line: More flexibility in the way a player can generate a name is required before any hard-core naming policy enforcement can happen.


Second issue! No space for a character background (bio) tab. We need this. This can be a source of endless fun as players can generate a background for their avatar that other players can read when they examine your character. Your gear is available for review by any passing player, so should your character bio.

Also, in order to assist new players, and in order to help ensure that certain RP mistakes are not made (such as a human warlock being the long lost twin of the King of Stormwind), a drop down and select menu can be used to generate that character bio. As further fun, certain options for your character’s bio might not be immediately available, and can be opened up as you go along, or even be influenced by whether or not your character has a legacy item in their possession.


Third issue! Not enough time between what happened in Warcrafts (1, 2, &3), and World of Warcraft. New players looking to RP need to get a degree in WoW Lore before they can adequately play their character. Any character who is thirty years old (or so), would have been alive and active during most of the events of the Frozen Throne, WC2 and WC3 (if not even more). They would be aware of the Orc revolt, and of Thrall’s rise to power in the Horde. They would probably even have been there to help build Ogrimar!

If we allowed for a lot more time between games, and built into the game a two or three hundred year buffer where not much happened, players would have a lot more flexibility in their character’s histories and origins.

However, the drop-down bio idea in the previous section would help address this.

TLDR: Policy enforcement will only go so far to encourage RP in WoW, but the implementation of new features (the change to how players can generate names, and the bio tab) would go a lot further to encourage this style of play.
