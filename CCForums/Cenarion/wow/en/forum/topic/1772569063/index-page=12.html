Agreed. I was given grief the other day, based on how I RP my character. My character is RP'd as having been blinded in combat a fair bit ago. Given her lack of sight, her other senses have worked to compensate... as such, she hears better than the average elf (elves are notorious for having superb hearing).
My night elf was called a “Mary Sue” for daring to be an orphan raised in the StormWind orphanage by humans. I did not see that my character idea would offend anyone, but apparently it did.

Let’s see… Night elf orphan.. no ties to royalty, no past family, no weird connection to other characters or realms of existence.. she was just a poor, impoverished adventurer trying to make ends meet and discover her heritage as she went along…

But that idea was harshly received by at least a few RPers. Plus the whole time frame issue was again raised (as Night elves were not discovered early enough to allow for a Night elf orphan… etc… ) and the whole “you need a degree in WoW lore just to play” garbage came up.

So I continue to RP.. but do so on my own terms, and mostly I do it privately in my head.

What I notice a lot now is the fear that some Roleplayers have towards other players. Being someone who went without RP addons for a long while, I'm guilty of this too. I couldn't tell who was an roleplayer, and who wasn't. So I was generally afraid of roleplaying in the public eye, even on a RP server. I didn't want to be griefed, or harassed, so I just went about the game minding my own business.

Eventually I downloaded MRP, and noticed that there are tons of roleplayers, even people I've never seen RP publicly before, that rp. Which opened myself to be more outgoing within the Roleplaying community. Nowadays, I could careless whether you're an roleplayer or not, everything I'm /saying is going to be how my character would respond to you.

Admitingly, I didn't read the whole thread, but I've seen enough of these to know the general message, which I greatly support.

Even though WoW is aging, as well as it's roleplaying community, we need to set a standard for all future and current Roleplayers. Any realm with "RP" unders its realm type should welcome open roleplaying and shun those out to ruin it for others. The community as a whole can band together and make their realm a better RP-friendly environment, even if you don't RP.

Blizzard can also give a helping by giving a message pop-up whenever someone goes to make a character on an RP and RP-PvP realm explaining where they can get information about the standards of the RP community (shown in Forum stickies, ect.).

EDIT: Blizzard could also encourage greater immersion by adding that Custom-armor idea where you have a stat set and a display set (display is only for show rather than stat bonuses).
