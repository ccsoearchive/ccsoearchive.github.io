I think RP in a computer-based world is fundamentally flawed to begin with, to be honest.

A big part of what makes something like a tabletop RPG fun is that the rules, while clearly defined, can always be bent or broken if it would make the game better. That's what a Game Master/ Dungeon Master is supposed to do, not just blindly enforce the mechanics as written. However, that blind enforcement is all a computer is capable of.

Go read any good WoW-inspired webcomic, or dig up the old Flintlocke comics on GameSpy. Virtually every strip will involve the characters doing things that simply can't be done in the game (such as Flintlocke's tendency to use teammates or small woodland creatures as ammo or weapons). You can't "think outside the box" like this in an MMO, because it would have to be programmed, balanced, and tweaked to the point where it was just another skill available to everyone of your class.

Think about Hunters' snake trap and trap launcher abilites. They are throwing a spring loaded trap full of live snakes at someone! That should be awesome! But it has to be balanced, debugged, and incorporated into the game in such a way as the computer can handle it every time. So it's no more remarkable than a fireball spell.

I'm not trying to chase RP out of WoW, but I really wish folks fighting for it would think about what they're fighting for. Names and speech are a pretty trivial part of any game, RP or not. If the gameplay is set in stone (as it has to be for an MMO), then there's really no room to RP in the first place.
