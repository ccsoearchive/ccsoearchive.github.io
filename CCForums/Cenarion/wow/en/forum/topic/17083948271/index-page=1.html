Kinarra stopped her wolf on the mountain trail to give the pack kodo she was leading a chance to rest – and to silence Greatmother Moon who had been complaining incessantly for most of the journey. She jumped down from her mount and looked west as An’she neared the end of his journey. They were not far the Tribe’s camp, but if the old druid thought it was important to stop here then they would stop. Kinarra let her thoughts drift to the events of the past days as the elder continued to talk at her. “Leaving wasn’t easy,” she whispered without realizing.

The elderly druid was silent a moment before reaching up to put her hand on Kinarra’s arm, “The most important things – the right things - usually are. Not everyone walks the same path – you know this. Do not start this part of your journey with this burden. It is as Jodank said – be joyful for a new tribe is born.“ Greatmother leaned on her cane and spat a few sunflower seeds at the ground. “In this case, an old tribe is reborn. The Skyfather’s call is answered once more, and our ancestors begin to rest easier again.”

Kinarra smiled softly, never moving her eyes off the horizon as she watched An’she’s last light start fade. “Aye, that is true. I have my own way to go. The ancestors are right, it is time to remember the old ways. Slowly we have turned away and forgotten, Greatmother.”

Greatmother Moon cackled and turned to hobble toward the pack kodo, “Oh child, just because a trail has become overgrown, that doesn’t mean it’s been forgotten. Don’t be so maudlin – it doesn’t suit you. Now hurry up, you have a tribe lead.”

Kinarra snorted gave a soft laugh. She shook her head as she turned to help the old druid up onto the pack kodo. “I know, old woman, I know.” She gently made sure Greatmother was safely tucked in before securing the lead to her saddle. “Try not to fall off, I’d hate to lose you again. Took us two months to find you last time.” The Chieftess smirked as she heard a rude noise behind her, from Greatmother’s general direction.

“Don’t imagine that I’m too old and frail to give a proper smack with my cane, Miss High and Mighty!” The elder made a threatening gesture toward the back of Kinarra’s head before laughing. “And, Kinarra – don’t worry. They will find us. The ancestors will lead them.”

The Chieftess turned her wolf back on to the trail, “Aye. When the Skyfather calls his children, the faithful will answer… And we will be ready. Now, to get you camp! I sent word ahead with Maha that we would be late. She promised to make sure Sister Unika kept some soup hot for you.”
_____________

The Whitehorn Tribe is a mostly Tauren, tribal RP guild with a heavy focus on service to the Skyfather, the teachings of the Earthmother, and honoring the ancestors. If your character has grown tired of constant warfare and feels that the Shu’halo people are losing touch with their traditions – then we might be the guild for you! If your character hears the spirits call and wants to stand ready to protect the Earthmother and her children – then yes, we do have a place for you.

FAQs :
What do you mean by “mostly Tauren”?
That we do accept non-Tauren members! As long as the character’s beliefs are in line with the Earthmother’s teachings, then yes we accept them. We call them ‘Kindred Spirits” and believe that they were led to us for a reason, either to learn from us or to teach us. ICly, They are considered “provisional” members with the idea that once that reason is done, they will continue their journey and leave us. OOCly, they are welcome to stay as long as they want.

Who the heck is the “Skyfather”?
The Skyfather, to my knowledge, is not mentioned in Tauren Lore. The concepts Earthmother and Skyfather occur together frequently in tribal cultures around the world however. To my point of view though, since the “Earthmother” is mentioned, it seems to imply the Skyfather. It’s not hard to believe that the different Tauren tribes would have different variations in some of their beliefs and customs. So, The Whitehorns are a bit on the fringe of the Tauren mainstream. Which might seem downright cultish to some. *hides the Koolaid*

I noticed many references to “journey”, “trail” and “path” – is there a theme here?
Yes there is! The main focus of the guild are “Spirit Paths”. This is the method by which you honor the SkyFather and Earthmother. There are four paths that represent different focus areas your character can choose from. They are: Brave, Healer, Teacher/Lore master, and Mystic. For RP purposes, these replace your in-game class. How you choose to RP your character determines which path you choose.

Basic Information:
Horde Friendly* Guild: The Whitehorn Tribe (*Because “Neutral” isn’t an option.)
Guild Site: http://thewhitehorntribe.shivtr.com/
Contacts: Kinarra (Guild Leader),Ossyka, Kureya, Raiaga, Rakarhain, Wachiiwi or Springmoon.
