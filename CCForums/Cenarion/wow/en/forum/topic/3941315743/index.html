Similar to its sister thread, http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/3424793138, this is a list of all rogue weapons that utilize their own enchant instead of an enchant you place on the item. It is still a work in progress; specifically, I still need to add quests, BoE greens, and anything in Naxx, Ulduarr, and Cata raids. In the meantime, I’d suggest checking the old thread for daggers and TBC content for other weapons (TBC seems to be the best source of such weapons)

The items listed are those that any rogue can obtain. If you have an item that has been removed from game or is restricted in transmogg use, use this script to determine if the item will or will not display enchants.

/script DEFAULT_CHAT_FRAME:AddMessage("\124cffa335ee\124Hitem:ITEM#HERE:4099:0:0:0:0:0:0:0\124h[ITEM NAME HERE]\124h\124r");

Simply replace ITEM#HERE with the appropriate item number (but do leave the colons in place), enter the script in the chat box, and look at the item in the character modeler.

It is worth noting that the only enchant I'm looking at is landslide; therefor, in some cases, it might be possible that a weapon might have the capability to show most enchants but for some reason can't show landslide. Fortunately, for rogues, this isn't a particularly pressing issue since we ultimately have to use landslide anyway.

It is also worth noting that the descriptions were determined by looking at the in-game model viewer (ctrl + click an item); to that end, there may be some difference between what the model viewer displays and how the enchant actually looks.

Updating the list tonight (rest of WotLK raids and TBC / WotLK quests). I'll check the stuff mentioned in the EU thread, though some of the items listed there actually do show enchants.

My personal theory for pre-enchanted weapons is that it was a means for bliz to make an item look somewhat special or different without actually having to go create a new model for it; additionally, it's also possible that there were limitations in the past on what could be done to create new models. To this end, this is why I'd say that a lot of the models with enchants either share the same model as other weapons or would otherwise be quite plain themselves. It's obviously not true in all cases, but it would offer an explanation of why TBC has so many while Wrath and Cata have so few.

EDIT: Well what WotLK lacks in the dungeon and raid department, it makes up for itself in the green quest reward department.
