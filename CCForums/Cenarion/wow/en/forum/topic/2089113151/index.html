I noticed a lack of an outline of rogue abilities as it pertains to pvp.

The purpose of the thread to consolidate information about other classes abilities and how to counter them. Not every rogue has played mutiple classes significantly or have been playing long enough to reconize other class skill which can make or break a win. This will be a work in progress. Most of this stuff will be known to experienced PvPers, but to the newer players, I hope that this guide can be helpful when encountering specific abilities which the hope that by understand their interactions, the player will be better positioned to understand what the opponent will most likely do next. Which is your single greatest advantage in PvP.

Most of my experience is mutilate, but I intend to include all specs in the analysis.

This guide will for the moment be separated by a few categories:

General inventory of skills and viable glyphs for rogues in PvP. Thier non-obvious uses and tricks.

Spec specific listing of major abilites for other classes as they pertain to rogues

How other classes abilities affect your teammates effectiveness.

A short list of best practices on gearing decisions, and the trade offs they they require.

Note, I don't expect to get the full outline fleshed out today, but after I open the post, feel free to go ahead and start adding your insights. We have a great community here that I am sure will be able really help this class excel. Later on, I would like to add video guides to illustrate the visual ques that should be looked for for for each opponent spec.

I hope that we can make this a community project.

Sap
http://www.wowhead.com/spell=6770

    Notable Talent Interations:
    • Blackjack (Assassination, Tier 2)
      • http://www.wowhead.com/spell=79125
    • Shadow Dance (Subtlety, Tier 7)
      • http://www.wowhead.com/spell=51713
    Glyphs:
    • Glyph of Sap

  • Has a PvP duration of 8 seconds.
  • It also shares diminishing returns with Gouge.I am unclear as to whether it shares with other "Disorient effects". If so, I would like to know if the wowwiki site's list is accurate. I noticed that Cheap Shot is still listed as an opener stun.
  • With Shadows Dance, it is possible to sap a caster while out of stealth before they have a chance to enter combat, since casting a CAST TIME spell doesn't not put the in combat immediately.
  • As the glyph text says, the increased duration does not effect player chacters. It also does not have increased duration on player controlled pets or guardians.
  • Players can circumvent sap proactively by keeping their pet in combat, which will keep the pet owner in combat as well.
  • Healers typicly immune themselves by immediately casting an instant heal on a target in combat, bringing themselves in combat.
  • Any damage, including existing DoTs or AoE will break this effect.
  • See the macro section for some modifications that may enhance this ability for your needs.

    • Gouge
    http://www.wowhead.com/spell=1776

      Notable Talent Interactions:
      • Improved Gouge (Combat, Tier 3)
        • http://www.wowhead.com/spell=13793
      • Sanginary Vein (Subtlety, Tier 5)
        • http://www.wowhead.com/spell=79147
      Glyphs:
      • Glyph of Gouge

  • Is very handy for CC an off target or allowing for a sprint>stealth getaway. This is an advanced tactic and requires knowledge of what counters a class has against this. Best used where there is an LoS opportunity available.
  • It also useful for a second "interupt" in casting when kick has a couple of seconds left on cooldown, so that you can save Kidney Shot for a more opportune moment.
  • Like Kick, it is best used near the end of a cast.
  • It shares diminishing returns with Sap. I am unclear as to whether it shares with other "Disorient effects". If so, I would like to know if the wowwiki site's list is accurate. I noticed that Cheap Shot is still listed as an opener stun.
  • See the macro section for some modifications that may enhance this ability for your needs.

    • Shiv
    http://www.wowhead.com/spell=5938

  • You can Shiv a rogue though evasion to generate combo points.
  • Useful for applying an offhand poison, should you absolutely need that poison applied now.
  • Typicly used to apply Crippling Poison for Subtlety or a Deadly Poison for Assassination (rarely).
  • This ability is useful to dispel enrages, which can be quite significant, as some class's depend on them for their rotations and defensive abilties. Removing the enrage significantly hampers their ability to fight effectively.

      • Enrage
      Wrecking Crew
      Savage Roar
      Unholy Frenzy
      Berserker Rage
      Vengeance
      Death Wish

  • Specific uses will be outlined in the class/spec specific section.

    • Feint
    http://www.wowhead.com/spell=1966

      Noteable Talent Interactions:
      • Enveloping Shadows (Subtlety, Tier 4)
        • http://www.wowhead.com/spell=31213
      Glyphs:
      • Glyph of Feint

    • Useful for moments of significant AoE/mutli-target damage as it reduces all damage from AoE/multi-target sources by 50%.
    • Requires a melee range target to use.
    • Use pets if no player is near.
    • The glyph is mostly useful for PvE. As there are much better choices for PvP glyphs.

    Blind
    http://www.wowhead.com/spell=2094

      Notable Talent Interactions:
      • Elusiveness
        • http://www.wowhead.com/spell=14066
      Glyphs:
      • Glyph of Blind


  • Has a PvP duration of 8 seconds.
  • Shares Diminishing returns with Fear. (Howling Terror, Intimidating Shout, Psychic Scream, etc.)
  • The glyph will remove all DoTs from the target when it is cast so that it will not break. Subsequent reapplication of dots or incidental damage will still break its effect.
  • Its duration can allow for a restealth.
  • Can be used from any direction. You do not have to be facing the target.
  • Useful as a "ranged interrupt" on a kiting class, to either stop a cast or close the gap.
  • Can allow for a clean get away should the need arise.
  • A mouseover and focus blind macro has been provided in the macro section to enhance this ability.


    • Garrote

    Notable Talents
    Glyphs

    Extremely useful for silencing classes that use spells (not abilities).
    Silence effect shares diminishing returns with all other silence effects. (Avenger's Shield, Strangulate, etc.)
    With Venomous Wounds it does additional poisons damage and can proc energy returns.
    Can be applied from the rear 180 degree arc, not just from behind.

    Handy macros:

    Auto clears your current target and auto-targets an enemy, (tab targeting behavior) and saps them. Great for sapping stealthers. Or sapping reenforcements without detargeting your current target first. Add /targetlasttarget after the /cast line to return to the previous target.
    #showtooltip
    /cleartarget
    /targetenemy
    /cast Sap
    Auto-targeting (tab targeting behavior) gouge, just face what you want to gouge. Use with a mod key to gouge an in-range focus target for some cheap and easy CC. Works extremely well with the glyph. Add /targetlasttarget after the /cast line to return to the previous target.
    #showtooltip
    /targetenemy [nomod, exists]
    /cast [mod, @focus, exists] [] Gouge
    The essential mouseover blind macro. If you don't use this, you are missing out. Also included focus behavior with the use of a mod.
    #showtooltip
    /cast [mod, @focus, exists, harm][@mouseover, exists, harm][] Blind

    Hi Aeriwen,

    Great start on the guide. I would add that gouge+smoke bomb can be used for a "free" restealth if LOS is unavailable.

    Also for the sap macro, I have found that it is better to have it like this:

    #showtooltip
    /cleartarget [nomod]
    /targetnearestenemyplayer [nomod]
    /cast sap

    This will do 2 things differently. 1) it will select enemy players only (no pets, totems, elementals etc). 2) If you really need to get a sap on one dude in a crowd, simply hold a mod key (shift, ctrl, alt) and it will not clear target and sap whoever you have targeted.
