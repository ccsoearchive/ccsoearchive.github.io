Not so much the GCD as the energy/cp usage.

In heroics I seriously doubt EA is worth the effort, 1) Because there's only three DPS there to benefit from it (or less), and 2) most fights are too dynamic to get EA up without gimping your DPS time significantly. In raids, for Wrath the mantra was to only use EA if there's no warriors around to sunder, which I assume still holds true in Cataclysm.

Possible exceptions: extremely quick burn phases, like the head-phase on Magmaw (like the heart-phase on XT). Popping a 1-2cp EA at the start might be worthwhile, but I'm not sure. As for the reforging issue, I can't think of any way to avoid it. Either you'll need to pick one spec, or reforge between them constantly, or wait until you have two sets of gear. :(

Mostly because it's just... awful. DPS doesn't matter much in your offhand, the EP value for it is about a third of the EP value for mainhand DPS. I'm ridiculously confused about what you're trying to say.

The stat itself is weaker in Cataclysm, for two reasons: 1) white damage is no longer a majority source of our damage after our mastery was added, and 2) with the changes to the talent trees, critical strikes no longer gives back energy. That last one is incredibly important and it's also the reason crit isn't as valuable as it was either. I forgot about this earlier since the changes happened all the way back in October, I can't even remember the name of the talent anymore to be honest.

So while white hits are a decent portion of our damage, and more white hits would mean more poison hits, it breaks down that it's more valuable to stack haste for energy regeneration (that's no longer supplied by white hit/crit) and mastery (so that the poisons that do hit do more damage). I guess it's a miss-more-but-hit-harder situation. And while stat ratings might be weaker, we also get TONS more, especially considering this is just the first raiding tier. Example:

Belt of the Tortured: 1.63% hit at level-80 (25m Naxxramas drop).
Belt of Nefarious Whispers: 1.53% hit at level-85 (crafted BoE epic).

So while we're not swimming in stats like we were at the end of Wrath, the progression in the itemization doesn't seem to have devalued hit rating at all. It's just its basic usefulness to us as a class/spec that's dematerialized in this Expansion because of changes to our core mechanics.
