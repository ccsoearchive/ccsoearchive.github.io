I haven't looked closely at any of the MoP dungeon run-throughs yet, but just from Blizzard's past design philosophies those sorts of abilities are usually present to give the healer something to do besides heal the tank the whole run. It's something for them to be responsible for handling, not for you to be responsible for avoiding (with exceptions, like Alizabal's whirlwind in BH, etc.).

We also have feint, though; reduces AoE damage taken by 50% for 5 seconds, instant-cast, no CD. Increased to 80% if you also choose Elusiveness from the 2nd talent point tier. And the glyph increases its duration to 7 seconds. It's not spammable without a significant DPS loss, though, since it'll cost 20 energy per use (on live the glyph voids the energy cost entirely, so you can use it on CD if beneficial).

That was actually a topic of a rather long blog/rant by Aldriana at EJ:

http://elitistjerks.com/blogs/10672-aldriana/567-mists_beta_thoughts_feint/

Bit of a work in progress.

If there's any disagreement on the details I'll gladly consider criticism in making corrections.

Talents:

15: Shadow Focus, maybe subterfuge if ambush beats mutilate as our opening move.
30: Personal preference, deadly throw may have some applicability in PvE at times.
45: Usually leeching poison, elusiveness if it makes sense for the fight.
60: Whichever of the three fits the fight best. Preparation, shadowstep, or burst of speed.
75: Personal preference, I like Prey on the Weak for fights like Blackhorn, etc.

Glyphs:

Major: Vendetta if you can guarantee 39 seconds on target, expose armor if you're using it. I'd recommend feint and sprint as well. Pick whatever you want if you have an empty glyph slot after that.

Minor: Do NOT use the tricks of the trade glyph, it's still a DPS gain to tricks another DPS player during a fight in order to boost their damage. Glyph of poisons is useful when you have to switch mid-fight.

Stat Priorities:

7.5% hit (769 rating) > 7.5% expertise (769 rating) > Mastery > Haste > Crit > Extra Hit Rating

Actual stat weights aren't known yet, but that's the priority list at least.

Weapons:

Two 1.8-speed daggers (all daggers have been changed to this speed, so it's all we've got).

Poisons:

Your lethal poison should be deadly, non-lethal should be leeching unless another is better for a specific fight. Other non-lethal poisons include: crippling, mind-numbing, and paralytic (if talented).

Crippling is useful on the final phase of heroic madness, I can't think of applications for the others.

Rotation:

Open with: mutilate > rupture > mutilate > slice and dice.

Always refresh rupture immediately with whatever combo points you have available. It's not yet known if it's better to open with ambush, if it is it might make subterfuge a competing talent with shadow focus.

Use mutilate on targets from 100-35% health and dispatch from 35-0% health. Aim for 4-5 combo point envenoms while using mutilate and 5 combo point envenoms while using dispatch.

Mutilate has a 30% chance of proccing blindside, which gives you a free dispatch (no energy cost) in the next several seconds. In this situation dispatch should always be used before your next mutilate.

Envenom will automatically refresh slice and dice, but if it drops get it back up immediately. If switching targets consider using redirect to save a GCD getting rupture and slice and dice back up.

Use vendetta when appropriate during the fight.

AoE Rotation:

Fan of knives to 5 combo points, and hit crimson tempest - repeat until dead.

            Mutilate Phase: from 100-35% health, mutilate is your combo-point builder. Use envenom at 4-5 combo-points, and rupture at 1-5. Higher combo-point ruptures are better, per se, but even a second of downtime negates that value, so use whatever you have to keep it up.

                      Blindside Procs: Each mutilate has a 30% chance of proccing Blindside, which allows you to use dispatch once in the next eight seconds. This dispatch will also not have an energy cost, and should be used before your next mutilate when you are at 4 combo-points or less.

            Dispatch Phase: At 35% health, dispatch replaces mutilate as your combo-point builder. You will also aim for 5 combo-point envenoms when using dispatch, although you can switch back to mutilate if you're having energy capping issues (like during bloodlust/heroism).

                      Clipping Envenom: Try to avoid clipping (overlapping) the buff from two envenoms. You CAN do this if you're in danger of energy capping, but envenom timing is an important element in maximizing your assassination DPS. This is something to keep in mind all the time.

            In review, get rupture and slice and dice up as quickly as possible, and keep them up constantly. Slice and dice is simple since it's automatically refreshed by envenom, but rupture will require your attention. You can also overlap ruptures by 1-2 seconds without losing a damage tick.

            Lastly, be VERY aware of your energy bar – NEVER let it become completely full ("energy capping"). If you're ready to use a finisher but will energy cap before rupture is ready to be refreshed, cast envenom. Then rupture as quickly as you can with whatever you have available.

First off, congratulations for this guide. Awesome work.

While I don't question most of the elements, I might however put a star over the Haste/Critical strike impact over DPS. Critical Strike might have a increased value since 5.0.3, taking in consideration several aspects :

- Seal Fate (Passive) grants an extra Combo Point if the ability crits (excluding FoK and CT).
- The loss of the Critical Strike Chance enhancement from our previous tree.
- The haste scaling at 85 for Energy regeneration is absolutely -terrible- as it is right now, it takes more than 1.2-1.3k Haste rating and plus to get a single EPS (Energy per second).
- And, finally, the +100% to our abilities in case of a critical strike (Including Venomous Wounds procs, and the application of Deadly Poison excluding the DoT)

While it might be not reliable, I've done field testing on a dummy for a 15 minutes period for each reforging priority, and it wielded better results with prioritizing Critical Strike rather than Haste by a small margin (500-600 DPS on my Rogue current gear). If haste is superior to Critical Strike mathematically (I have yet to see true calculations exposed, and real weight), it leaves me to think that the difference will be greatly marginal.

If I am unaware of the state of 90, due to my absence on the Beta, I can sure tell by the first look of it that the Talent Anticipation will greatly benefit from Seal Fate, and might be a DPS increase for Assassination.

Now, concerning your AOE rotation, I'd suggest mentionning applying an Energy return before commencing the FoK => CT (5CB). If you got Shadow Focus, opening with a free Ambush/Mutilate then applying Rupture will wield best result. Currently, with my Nightstalker talent (...Lol), it's ideal for me to open with Garrote instead before going to the AOE. Might be the same for those specialized in Subterfuge (Limited protection against AOE that might damage your character).

But that's only my humble opinion. =)
