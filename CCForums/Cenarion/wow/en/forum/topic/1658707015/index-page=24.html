There's two kinds of trinkets, basically. Trinkets like Fluid Death, which you keep up constantly, and trinkets like Prestor's Talisman of Machination, that have a significant proc tied to a cooldown. You can further subdivide "proc" trinkets between ones with an internal cooldown, like Prestor's Talisman of Machination, and on-use trinkets with a triggered use/cooldown, like Ancient Petrified Seed.

There's no real difference between the two types of "proc" trinkets in this case, except that an on-use trinket requires intentional use while the other triggers itself off an ICD. In terms of mechanics, they're practically identical (although an on-use trinket might prove to be stronger, everything else the same, since you can choose when to use it to maximize the proc, syncing it with other DPS cooldowns or heroism/bloodlust, etc.).

The difference is that trinkets like Fluid Death can be stronger, since they're up almost 100% of the time when you're attacking anything, while "proc" trinkets have a CD/ICD to deal with and might go off at awkward times when it's hard to maximize them, reducing their estimated value. But the other trinkets can also lose value on fights when you're not hitting anything for awhile (like Atramedes's air phase).

I remember quite a bit of talk about this on EJ a few months back, and whether or not it was better to swap trinkets before each fight based on the encounter mechanics. In the end I'm pretty sure they simply decided it was best to just use the best trinkets you had at your disposal. Someone also created a chart that represented different trinkets' EP values over time, though, which is also useful:

    http://i.imgur.com/YxCHv.jpg

Basically, a "proc" trinket will go off right away, syncing nicely with personal CDs and other related burst damage immediately at the beginning of an encounter. Trinkets like Fluid Death perform worse at the start in comparison, but on average perform better overall. Regardless, though, some trinkets just have procs that make them obscenely good, even if the proc mechanic might not be exactly optimal.

I mean, hell - Darkmoon Card: Greatness was good for most of three raid tiers in Wrath.

Unsolvable Riddle has been considered since its introduction one of the worst trinkets even with its on-use 1605 boost in Agi being handily beaten by Tia's Grace. It makes sense to consider a stacking agility trinket as more advantageous given that you have the boost even during the times that you are not using Heroism/Blood Lust/Time Warp ending having more damage output in the long run.
It's not so much that it's an on-use trinket, it's just that it's worth less regardless.

Unsolvable Riddle: 321 mastery, 1605 agility for 20 seconds every 2 minutes, so...

(321 × 1.3) + [(1605 × 2.6) × (20 ÷ 120)] = 417.3 + [(4173) × (0.1667)] = 417.3 + 695.5 = 1112.8

Tia's Grace: 285 mastery, and 340 agility indefinitely when stacked, so...

(285 × 1.3) + (340 × 2.6) = 370.5 + 884 = 1254.5

ShadowCraft can do more in-depth modeling for more exact figures, but that's the gist of it.
