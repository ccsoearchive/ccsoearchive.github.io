Can anyone explain what this means? Like why do I want to use low CP ruptures?
Actual damage from rupture is very low, so the main reason to have it up is for venomous wounds. Using low-cp ruptures, you get more rupture ticks out of the energy you spend, making it more economical (and saving energy for more envenoms). It's more important to make sure rupture is up all the time though, so if it drops and you're at 5cp you should still refresh it immediately.

I'll try and make that more clear in the guide soon, have a raid starting in a moment so I can't now. :(

I'm not understanding how t11 chest is better than stark or morrie's. I mean yeah theres the 40agi difference from the socket bonus and gem but wouldn't the 218 mastery loss affect that in the least bit?
I also assumed blue-quality gems and optimal reforging practices, so it broke down like this:

Wind Dancer's Tunic, reforging expertise for mastery:
(381 agility × 2.6) + (113 expertise × 1.1) + (228 haste × 1.2) + (75 mastery × 1.3) + (20 hit × 1.4) = 1514.0

Morrie's Waywalker Wrap / Sark of the Unwatched, reforging crit for haste:
(361 agility × 2.6) + (119 crit × 0.9) + (79 haste × 1.2) + (228 mastery × 1.3) = 1475.9

If either one had an agility socket bonus it'd be less of a gap, but they don't, so it isn't.

Aldriana:
Here's the best guess we have at the moment:
1) Rupture uptime is more important than rupture size.
2) However, if you can avoid downtime either way, larger is better.

That is: a 5pt rupture just as the previous one drops is better than a 1pt rupture just as the previous one drops, but both are way better than a 5pt rupture with a 2 second gap from the previous one.

This is not based off of the formal Shadowcraft model, but some scratchwork calculations I did based off numbers I pulled out of Shadowcraft. At some point I will try to put real modeling for it in Shadowcraft, but I've just been too busy to worry about it lately.

Patch 4.1.0 (Pending):
  • Non-damaging interrupts are off the hit table, and now can never miss (aka: kick).
  • Baseline recuperate now heals 3% per tick, up from 2%.
  • The Improved Recuperate talent has been slightly reduced to break even with this change.
  • Stealth CD is 4 seconds, down from 10; no movement speed penalty when stealthed.
  • Nightstalker reduces Stealth's CD by 2/4 seconds, and increases stealthed speed by 5/10%.
  • The speed increase from Nightstalker presently stacks multiplicatively with Quickening.
  • Tricks of the Trade now has a 100-yard range, up from 20-yards.
  • Epic agility leatherworking patterns will now have sockets (some stat reduction to compensate).
  • Assassin's Chestplate will have an EP value of 1447.0 (up from 1392.6).
  • Belt of Nefarious Whispers will have an EP value of 1136.2 (up from 1084.6, now BiS for 359s).
  • Lots of new gear, etc. (352/353s from heroics, 378s from raids, 391s from hardmodes).

These changes are not guaranteed, many things can change on the PTR.
