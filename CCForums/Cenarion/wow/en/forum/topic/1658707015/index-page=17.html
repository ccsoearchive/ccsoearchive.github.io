1st of all, glyph of garrote doesnt add another tick from garrote. it just adds 2seconds to the silence effect that it gives. making it a 4.5second silence when you use garrote.

now why is glyph of backstab better than glyph of garrote?

It falls down to how many times you could use the skill in an encounter (lets say a cataclysm raid boss). you only get to use garrote during the start of the encounter and whenever you pop vanish during an encounter (which is usually around 2 times, total of 3). while you get to use backstab starting when the target's hp drops down to 35% (youd be using it more than 30times til the target drops.) together with murderous intent, you'll be drowning in energy.

03/24/2011 12:24 PMPosted by Silverwere
Why Glyph of Backstab over Glyph of Garrote?
Even if the garrote glyph did everything you thought it did, which it doesn't, it still wouldn't be nearly as useful (pre-4.0.1 the garrote glyph was more similar to what you described, you gave up 3 seconds of duration in order to increase its damage by 20%). Either way, backstab becomes your primary combo point builder on targets under 35% health, so it's used an awful lot. Garrote you get to use two or three times each encounter. It doesn't even have a remote chance of being as viable as the backstab glyph.

I have an unrelated question: When is the proper time to use Fan of Knives? I don't see it in the rotation listed by the OP. Should it just be used when there is energy to spare? It sounds like putting Deadly Poison on it and using it right away is a good way to help max your Deadly Poison stack, but the OP does not suggest when to use it.
FoK is only useful in AoE situations, and there are barely any of those a melee DPS can help out on without being horribly brutalized. An exception is the vile swill adds during hardmode maloriak's black phase, that's the only raid boss scenario I can think of where you'd be using FoK.

Plus, if you have "energy to spare," you're doing something wrong. The only time you should be in danger of energy capping is if you're off target for an extended period of time, or you're using backstab while under the effects of heroism or bloodlust. If that happens, switch back to using mutilate for a bit until your energy becomes more manageable.
