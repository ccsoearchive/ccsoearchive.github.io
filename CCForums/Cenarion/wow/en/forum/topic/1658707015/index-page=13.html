Pooling energy before envenoms: there are two situations where you should do this. The first is if the envenom buff is still active from your last envenom, and the second is to pool to 80-90 energy to fit two mutilates/backstabs into your next envenom buff. You should NOT pool energy if slice and dice is in danger of falling off, rupture needs to be refreshed, OR if you are going to cap energy.

You should probably mention that during bloodlust you will cap energy must faster and should not pool as much if at all.

VII. Gems:

Check the socket bonus before matching the color, sometimes the extra +20 agility from using a Delicate Inferno Ruby is a better alternative. Currently, suboptimal gemming in order to activate a meta is a net DPS loss, so use the best meta that already matches the gems you are using.

You may have missed this during the rewrite, but there's only 1 meta people should be using now and you should always meet the requirements if you have the minimum number of sockets in your gear.

While passive, our t11 set bonuses are both relatively strong this raid tier. The 2pc set bonus generally depends on which pieces are the strongest updates for you, while the 4pc with either the Poison Protocol Pauldrons or Wind Stalker Leggings (of the Zephyr) offsets is optimal for pre-heroic raiding

It might be worth noting here: using the engineering helm may be better than either of these alternatives. I haven't really done the math, but neither has EJ because they're only talking about heroic gear. From the little analysis I did, I've decided to use my helm as the non-set piece.

Lastly, it may be worth mentioning bows. Bows are viable and can be swapped out for a thrown weapon in a FoK macro. If you can get a bow with higher stats, it's worth using for most encounters.

Raid spell hit cap is 17%, 2/3 precision is 4%. Melee hit and spell hit scale differently, so you need (roughly) 120.11 hit rating per 1% of melee hit and 102.45 hit rating per 1% of spell hit. So, while I have 11.12% melee hit, my spell hit is 13%, which, added to 2/3 precision gets me 13% + 4% = 17% for the spell hit cap. Short answer: 1332 hit rating to be spell hit capped for raids with 2/3 precision.

The only cap you really have to be concerned with is spell hit, which is the level of hit rating required to stop our poisons from missing. The hit cap for special attacks is far below this, so you should have it as a matter of course. And the hit cap for white attacks is not presently valuable to assassination rogues.

if you notice in this chart, it takes 1972 rating for the 2nd extra tick, 1977 rating for the 3rd, and 1975 rating for the 4th. This is pretty much the same amount. Additionally, the difference in haste % is the same between each tick gain, 16.7% haste. This is what I was talking about. It takes the same amount of haste, and haste rating, to get an extra tick when you have 2000 rating as it does when you have 4000 rating (approximately). The value of haste does not go up or down. What changes is the value of other stats.

Say your spell ticks 10 times for 100 damage over 10 seconds, and if you gained 10 spellpower, it would tick for 101 damage. This would be a dps increase of 1. If you get enough haste so that the same spell ticks for 11 times over 10 seconds, the same 10 spellpower now ups your dps by 1.1. This effect is shared between all dps stats for all classes unless there is a stupid talent mechanic. Some stats do decrease in potency with increasing amounts, such as crit or stats with soft and hard caps.

I'm reasonably sure it came from an uninformed explanation of haste and weapon speed:

Haste Weapon Speed # Attacks / 3 Minutes
0% 1.80 (180 seconds ÷ 1.80) = 100
25% 1.44 (-36) (180 seconds ÷ 1.44) = 125
50% 1.20 (-24) (180 seconds ÷ 1.20) = 150
75% 1.03 (-17) (180 seconds ÷ 1.03) = 175
100% 0.90 (-13) (180 seconds ÷ 0.90) = 200
While each successive point of haste gives less of an absolute increase to weapon speed, the number of extra attacks still scales in a perfectly linear fashion (25% more attacks at 25% haste, 50% more attacks at 50% haste, etc.). Reasonably sure this is where it all originated from.
