For some reason the EP value for Gilnean Ring of Ruination was ignoring the amount of haste I reforged to mastery, which brings it up to 816.3, making it BiS before heroic raiding. The Lightning Conductor Band is listed correctly, and Signet of the Elder Council was as well. Thank you for the catch.

PS: Also another note as to how I calculated these values, I assume the character is reforged perfectly to the spell hit cap, so I use the 1.4 value for hit rating. However, I do not allow for reforging into hit rating on any of the items, as this would generally cause over-inflation. The highest stat I'd either reforge to haste (1.2) or mastery (1.3), depending on which was available.

The drawback to this is that items that -could- be reforged to hit rating are slightly understated as to their highest potential EP value, but only by a small amount. However, when you have a close cluster of similarly-valued items, like the three rings you quoted, this may be enough to tip one above the other just because of the rules I've applied to the simulated reforging process I'm using to generate the EP values.

I just wanted to mention this so there weren't raised eyebrows when Gilnean Ring of Ruination surpassed Signet of the Elder Council, which would actually be worth 821.5 if haste was reforged to hit rating. I could avoid the discrepancy by listing two EP values for each item, one allowing for reforging to hit and one assuming you're already at the hit cap and don't need any more, but I thought that'd be more confusing and counter-intuitive than anything.

tl:dr, the assumption is that if items are within ±10 EP, there's not really a material difference between them.

This is unrelated to your question, which was definitely a mistaken omission at my end. I just wanted to take the opportunity to explain where the values were coming from; if you're going to reforge an item to hit, its EP value will probably be raised by 5-10 EP. I've been trying to think of a way to account for this effectively but I haven't figured out a solution that'd be perfectly forthright as yet.

Not a problem, but my answer would simply be to keep reading. :)
The table above gives the amount of expertise rating required to reach the expertise cap for dodges. When a combo point generating ability (Mutilate, Backstab, etc.) or a finisher is dodged you are refunded 80% of the cost. When Envenom is dodged, you still get the Envenom buff but depending on how much time you have left on your SnD, your SnD may fall off and have to directly refresh your SnD. These mechanics play a part in expertise's relative poor value and it is likely most Assassination rogues will not gear for expertise cap unless they have cycle stability issues. The expertise cap for Gnomes is given because they are the only race that has dagger expertise, playing an Assassination rogues with non-daggers is ineffective.
http://elitistjerks.com/f78/t110134-assassination_guide_cata/#Stat_Weights

Underlined for emphasis. If you're having trouble maintaining your rotation (i.e., slice and dice is falling off before you can refresh it because of dodged envenoms), then it might be a good idea to get some expertise. But honestly I've been ignoring expertise for mostly all of Cataclysm without any issues. I'm presently at 3/3, and heroics/raids are going fine.

In fact, and this is something I haven't covered well, and I should have, you really don't need as much hit rating as you have right now either, unless you'll be raiding in the very near future. In heroics you should only need 6% spell hit, which is 2/3 precision + 205 hit rating. Hit rating above that point doesn't hurt, but you shouldn't need it in terms of hitting with special attacks and poisons.

Something else that's probably inhibiting you dramatically is trying to use the current (badly broken) meta gems. I'd suggest going with one of the three lower ones on this list and gemming normally in your other sockets, which will add a significant amount of agility to your current set-up. When the new metas are added in 4.0.6, then we'll have a + crit damage one worth using.
Meta Gems:
    54.6 + damage: Relentless Earthsiege Diamond.*
    48.6 + damage: Chaotic Shadowspirit Diamond.*
    70.2: Fleet Shadowspirit Diamond (do not use if you don't meet the gem requirement normally).
    48.6 + spell reflect: Destructive Shadowspirit Diamond.**
    57.6: Thundering Skyflare Diamond.
    * Neither of these metas are worth using until their gem requirements are fixed.
    ** According to EJ, this is the best PvE meta if you don't have 2 yellow gems socketed.
http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/1658707015?page=1#9
