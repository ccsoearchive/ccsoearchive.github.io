SB before and after capping

Assumptions 35% avoidance (15% parry/15% dodge/5% miss). You need 67.4% block to cap...so lets say you have 42.4% block. When you use SB this puts you at 102.4% exactly.

HtL uptime is found by this formula 1-[(1-Parry%)^(10/boss swing speed)]

Average debuffed boss swing speed is 1.8seconds so uptime is 59.41% of the time.

Crit block without HtL or SB is block%-20% so 22.4% block. With HtL factored in:
22.4+10*.5941=28.341


Damage taken is
1.024-avoidance%-(block%*Block value+block%*Block value*critblock)*(2/3 normal block uptime)-(SBblock%*Block value+SBblock%*Block value*critblock)*(1/3 Shield block uptime)


1.024-.35-(.424*.3+.424*.3*.28341)*(2/3)-(.674*.3+.674*.3*.28341)/3=0.478664998
This is
52.1335002% Damage reduction

Now adding one mastery so we have SB overflow.
1.024-.35-(.439*.3+.424*.3*.29841)*(2/3)-(.674*.3+.674*.3*.31341)/3=0.472370998
This is
52.7629002% DR

A 0.6294% dr difference.

Now lets not add the overflow and lets just say you COULD get DR from block past the cap
1.024-.35-(.439*.3+.424*.3*.29841)*(2/3)-(.689*.3+.674*.3*.29841)/3=0.471881998
This is
52.8118002% DR

A 0.6783% dr difference.

As you can see 1 mastery LOSES value after you reach the soft cap. Reason being is that crit has an additional multiplier reducing its contribution to your damage reduction.

http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2489160859




DR can mean either diminishing returns or Damage reduction. I tend to use a capital DR for damage reduction and a small dr for diminishing returns.

The formulas for determining dr for avoidance for a plate tank is:

Avoidance=5%+[(168.9432684/Avoidance rating)+1/65.63144]^-1

1% avoidance=1% damage reduction for all intents and purposes.



There is 179.28 mastery rating in one Mastery. For each Mastery (NOT RATING) you get 2.25% block as a paladin and 1.5% block and 1.5% crit block as a warrior.

With holy shield down a paladin blocks for 30% and with holy shield up we block for 50%. Used on cooldown it averages to the 36.67% number i used in my calculations.

Damage reduction from pally block=(Block chance*block value w/no HS)*(2/3)+(block chance*block calue w/ HS)/3

I already gave Warrior DR calculation formula above.



TTL=time to live. This is the amount of time you can live with no heals on average. It can be calculated many different ways but i prefer to assume 1 blockable damage per second model in which it becomes.

TTL=Total buffed hp/(1-total damage reduction)

Generally stamina gives the most ttl of any stat, but if you have enough DR avoidance gear can beat it.

So lets say i have 75% total damage reduction and 175k hp.

175k/(1-.75)=700k seconds to live (if taking 1 blockable damage per second).

Total damage reduction=1-(1-DR from talents/stance)*(1-DR from armor)*(1.024-avoidance-DR from block)




EH=effective health. This is the amount of health you EFFECTIVELY have after mitigation...or simply put the shear amount of damage you can take without any heals before dying.

Lets say you had 100k hp with no armor. You have 100k EH. You can take 100k damage and not die. Now lets say you had 100k hp and 50% dr from armor. That means you can now take 200k damage and not die. Its EFFECTIVELY the same as having 200k hp with no armor....hence the term Effective health.

EH against Physical damage=HP/[(1-DR from armor)*(1-DR from talents)]

IF AND ONLY IF you have 102.4% block+dodge+parry+miss you can also calculate block in your EH calculation

EH against physical damage if block capped=HP/[(1-DR from armor)*(1-DR from talents)*(1-block value)]

EH against magic=HP/[(1-DR from resistance)*(1-DR from stance/talents)]

So if i have 175k hp and 55% DR from armor

175k/[(1-.55)*(1-.1)]=432k EH.

Block never affects magic damage.




DR gotten from talents/stances: 10% from sanctuary (paladin), 10% from defensive stance (warrior), 8% from blood presence (Deathknight).

For completeness sake
HP=healthpoints.
