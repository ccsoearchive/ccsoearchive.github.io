1) Blizzard merges several low-pop realms together.
Pros: higher pops, closing of extra servers.
Cons: name conflicts, guild name conflicts, tendency for low-pop realms to be PvP and Horde-favored would not resolve faction imbalances, forces players to leave "home" server against their will.

These cons are completely unacceptable except for forcing players to leave their "home" server. You go from forcing people to pay for transfers to forcing people to change names which just doesn't sit well in anyone's book.

2) Blizz offers free transfers from high-pops to low-pops.
Pros: an influx of toons of all levels and factions.
Cons: people who take these transfers rarely stay, proven to be ineffective.

This is, unfortunately, the only solution as we'll see later.

3) Blizz offers free transfers from low-pop AND high-pop realms, TO medium-pop realms that can sustain both.
Pros: allows friends to transfer together in groups to keep guilds & networks together, ensures high-pop transfers by guaranteeing decent economy & playerbase on medium-pop server.
Cons: forces players to leave "home" server against their will, players have to abandon/restart guilds on new server.

Free transfers off of low-pop is the same as option 1. It forces low pop players and guilds to rename which is completely unacceptable.

4) Blizz offers Cross-Realm Raid Service (all but promised for 4.3)
Pros: allows endgame access regardless of server.
Cons: opens a whole new level of competition, unsure of whether people can guild cross-realm (and therefore be an equal "team member" to a raid/guild).

This will help, but the flight risk level of cross-realm raiders and ninja looting is scary. Guilds will use this as a last option, not a first option. The Raid Finder Tool will be more useful than this for low pop players, but they still won't get to see Rag with that tool... at least not in normal cases.

5) Blizz consolidates all realms into "one" (an illusory shared realm where players from all servers mingle in a shared realm-wide instance).
Pros: would do away with the outdated "realm" paradigm and move WoW toward a truly united world (by region, I imagine).
Cons: if this is something they plan it would be an enormous overhaul.

Impossible. Did you see what happened to Darkspear when Swifty had a massive PvP event? The best the server can handle is an 120 v 120 battle, and even that's a wee bit laggy.

As for merging realms, there's a way to get two realms together and avoid the naming issue. The fact is, people won't accept something without getting something. That something is that anyone with a duplicate name can, upon first login after the merge, create a last name. For all intents and purposes, the player will have to be whispered with their full name, and a new character without a last name will not be able to be created.

As for the faction balance issue, that can only be solved by allowing horde => alliance transfers from high pop to the newly formed low-pop merged servers. Players simply need incentive to move there, and that could be a number of rewards. Taking a free transfer with a max level character could result in a gold reward or aesthetic item reward. It's a lot easier to find a guild on a new server with the guild finding tool!

One of the blues started a post(Neth) titled: Happy Friday, Let's talk! I tried to get attn brought to the low pop server issue. Reading through the post I found this from one player, I thought it was awesome and wanted you guys to see. Obviously the blue started the thread and is reading through it and responding to a few things, so I want to believe they saw this.

I've been playing WoW since 2006 and cleared Naxx60 every week, killed Mu'ru pre-nerf, and have been through the good times and the bad.

At the launch of Cataclysm I had the opportunity to transfer my characters from Mal'Ganis (I just couldn't wait in queues any longer) to a low-population server. I now have 10 characters on this server (9 85s and 1 70), and I don't think I will be paying $250.00 to transfer them anytime soon.

My server keeps shrinking, in the last month three of the "top guilds" (you can barely call them that as two of them had only killed 1/7 heroic) were pretty much forced to transfer off because there are less than 100 active raiders on the server, only 40 of them with any real experience or understanding of the raid environment.

With the subscription base shrinking (it was bound to eventually, no one's fault at all) I don't see a solution to this problem. I think a lot of what makes WoW really enjoyable is the server community and ability to interact with many different individuals and try them out in a raid environment. My server has a dreadful Horde : Alliance ratio, I NEVER can seem to find Alliance, and I believe there are reports of less than 20 people in Stormwind at any given time.

I am convinced this is not a unique situation on my server.

Since the overall population of players is diminishing, what is the plan?

This problem is much more significant than 90% of what is communicated to us by the good people at Blizzard. I understand that this issue is not part of the content, art, design, development, or community team's list of problems, but to be honest, this is everyone's problem. Every single employee of WoW should be thinking about this problem 100% of the time, as they should be more than concerned about the quality of experience someone in a low-population server is experiencing compared to a medium server with a balanced representation of players on both factions. This problem is getting worse, not better, and there are far too many unhealthy servers, unhealthy being a conservative term.

I will not be threatening to cancel my subscriptions, or using any other tactic to persuade you to read this or demand a response, I am much more reasonable than that. I simply need Blizzard employees to start working on resolving this issue, I think the players deserve it.

What is planning to be done about this problem? I think I have the support of your entire community, and I think we deserve a thoughtful discussion about this without being told to accept the status quo for the time being.

Please help me to better enjoy my experience in Azeroth.

Sincerely,
A long-time customer.

lol Torv! We surely did, but they spared us for some reason. I thought it was weird that I couldn't check my auctions mid-afternoon.

So I'm posting from this toon with a few observations from leveling on a high-pop server. I will say this -- AH health is way more integral to fast leveling than I thought. There was someone in Thread #1 saying that a good way to revitalize dying factions would be to implement cross-faction AH. I didn't believe them then, but I do now. Being able to log on and see that your stuff actually sold, because of this weird thing called "demand", is very convenient and speeds up the leveling process. In fact, the steady demand means that prices are steadier -- they don't gouge too deep because of all the competition, but likewise prices don't plummet due to the competition either. Finding what you need when you need it is very handy. My Fireland group's resident DPSDK still hasn't found 2 x Obsidium Cleavers on our server's AH, and our faction's not even the dying one!

The capitols and Trade Chat are really bringing back memories. Literally, memories. I haven't seen such loud, capslock'd spam in over a year I wager. Both servers that I played on used to be that boisterous and crowded. Chromaggus is like a silent void now in comparison. It really is shocking. On a similar note, it's weird seeing so many people in Stormwind at peak hour. Org on Chrom can hustle and bustle, but most people congregate in the front by Garrosh's place, and of course some diligent traders who wisely hearth to the Tauren Ghetto instead. In Stormwind, I'm surprised to find that the Rear Gate (near the Monorail) is often BUSIER than the classic Front Gate area. Both are packed to the brim with toons, flying mounts streak through the air regularly, and there is a real sense of bustling and life that isn't as apparent on, say, Chromaggus Alliance. 30 people on at peak? You can't tell me there's no problem there.

Anyhow I'll have more reflections and they may or may not be worth reading. Those are the things that really struck me right off the bat.
