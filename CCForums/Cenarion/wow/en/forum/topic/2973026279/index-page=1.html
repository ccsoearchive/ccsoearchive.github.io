Our previous thread capped without an official response from the Powers that Be, however it was a source of great discussion (and only mild trolling compared to most angsty topics).


SUMMARY of Thread #1 (http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2912882209)

In short, the Realm Population issue about which we're so concerned has to do with the current situation of having many low/dead-pop realms that are becoming unable to sustain endgame guilds.

One example is Chromaggus-US-Alliance, which has fewer than 500 active toons across all levels. The only "raiding" guild left on Chrom-A is Para Bellum, currently 1/7 after 2 months of 4.2 content access. Even Chrom-H can only sustain about 8 raiding guilds at the moment, and the server's last remaining 25s guild transferred during the first week of August 2011, rendering Chromaggus a 10s-only server.

I and others conclude that Blizzard has a responsibility to its paying customers to provide the same access to content across all servers. This is not the case.

Furthermore, the population control problems have been made worse by the Paid Transfer and Faction Transfer services. While I completely agree that Blizz can and should offer these services for profit, they must also recognize that these services are having a negative impact to the game, so much so that these "optional" transfer fees are becoming mandatory for many players if they wish to see endgame content.

Therefore, our logic suggests that Blizzard has an ethical obligation to use their profits from these services to help remedy the problem they worsen -- the slow steady migration of players to high-pop servers.

I and others in the previous thread have ourselves used transfer services, on our own terms -- to join RL friends, for example. However, we do not feel that our realms should die because Blizzard needs to please its shareholders. Conversely, we recognize the duty Blizz has to its shareholders and new corporate overlords, but feel that the balance is tipping away from quality customer service and toward profit at the cost of quality assurance.


Some Common Arguments and/or Trolling

"Quit whining and pay the transfer fees for your toons."
- No. We should not be obliged to have to leave our server at all, let alone pay to leave, as we did not cause this population issue. If anything, those who have taken transfers and moved to larger servers are making the problem worse for those who refuse to leave.

"Just quit whining and move already. Why would you play on a dead server when you can pay $100s to be happy?"
- First of all, on principle, as stated above.
- Many people refuse to leave their home servers because they have played there their entire lives and feel bound to the realm's fate.
- Many of us have lots of friends and social connections here (I daresay that it's usually "lone wolves" who only care about themselves who are the first to leave). Those of us who remain are the most loyal, stalwart, stubborn team players in the World of Warcraft. These are not the players who should be (indirectly) punished because so many others are willing to take the coward's way out and transfer.

"You should roll new toons on a bigger server and level a new guild all together."
- A nice thought, but we've already spent months leveling our current guilds and toons, making friends here, and establishing ourselves. The only thing worse than transferring all of your toons is giving up the ones you have. The guild is lost in either scenario but as someone who signed the charter of mine, I won't give it up without a guild transfer.


Suggested Solutions and their Pros & Cons

1) Blizzard merges several low-pop realms together.
Pros: higher pops, closing of extra servers.
Cons: name conflicts, guild name conflicts, tendency for low-pop realms to be PvP and Horde-favored would not resolve faction imbalances, forces players to leave "home" server against their will.

2) Blizz offers free transfers from high-pops to low-pops.
Pros: an influx of toons of all levels and factions.
Cons: people who take these transfers rarely stay, proven to be ineffective.

3) Blizz offers free transfers from low-pop AND high-pop realms, TO medium-pop realms that can sustain both.
Pros: allows friends to transfer together in groups to keep guilds & networks together, ensures high-pop transfers by guaranteeing decent economy & playerbase on medium-pop server.
Cons: forces players to leave "home" server against their will, players have to abandon/restart guilds on new server.

4) Blizz offers Cross-Realm Raid Service (all but promised for 4.3)
Pros: allows endgame access regardless of server.
Cons: opens a whole new level of competition, unsure of whether people can guild cross-realm (and therefore be an equal "team member" to a raid/guild).

5) Blizz consolidates all realms into "one" (an illusory shared realm where players from all servers mingle in a shared realm-wide instance).
Pros: would do away with the outdated "realm" paradigm and move WoW toward a truly united world (by region, I imagine).
Cons: if this is something they plan it would be an enormous overhaul.

Amazing summary to the previous thread. Nice work!

My general thoughts are "the concept of realm is the problem" as you saw in the previous thread. Long term realms have to go. Blizzard is doing a nice job with the cross realm LFG and the addition of LFR. This is a start.

Here are my ideas on where this should head for cross realm features:

- Guilds. Ahh but conflicts in names right? No. <Forty Spoke - Chromaggus>. We can take advantage of the outdated realm system to remove name conflicts.
- Mail / guild bank. This will help with lower population auction houses. Thrifty auctioneers can profit off of supplying a dead realm with product. Blizzard already has botter / gold seller scripts in place to catch those people. Nothing would change.
- Realm ID. Torvalds - Chromaggus. Cross realm friend lists without the privacy issues.

Changes that could help strengthen a realms auction house:
- Buyer side posting. Allow people looking for items to place an order and sellers be able to fill it. Open the API up so that web pages can exist to show where demand on a realm.
