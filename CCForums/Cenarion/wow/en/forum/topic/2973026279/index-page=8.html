Question here (I read most of the "version 1" of this topic, and am firmly in the camp that Blizz needs to do something to address the situation); there have been some hints/rumors of a cross-realm raid finder being implemented, much as we have a cross-realm dungeon finder. How much would this help things? Low-pop realms would have a vastly improved access to endgame raid content; obviously it's not ideal to have to PuG every raid, but currently the low-pop servers can't even do that. It also fails to solve many of the other problems (e.g. stagnant economies, deserted cities, etc), but it would seem to be a single step in the correct direction.

I read this and all I could think was - How Terrible!

I actively play on 4 different realms with my 9 level 85's. I play on different realms for a reason. They all have a different community feel that allows me to be in an environment that I like no matter what my mood.

I have one realm that is low population, RP and mostly all adults. You never see any "orrifice" spam. Trade chat revolves around the stock market and what to do about teething babies.

Another realm is PvP and what we call the "dead end kids" realm. It's filled with nastiness in trade and orrifice spam.

Another is a high pressure, PvE raiding realm.

Why would we ever want to homogenize the communities that have built up over time?

Plus the incredible technical changes that would be required. Realms usually have < 10,000 online at any time, but many frequently have > 5,000 online. So to have a realm that could support 241 (the number of NA realms) times 10,000 as a max online at any time - that would be a feat.


I just think that Blizzard does not have the playing customer base to support 241 NA realms anymore.

I understand where you're coming from, each realm does have its own flavour and community. Since starting these threads, I've rolled an alt on a high-pop server to find out just what all the fuss is about. All I've noticed is that Trade Spam is even more atrocious than cap-city lag on a high-pop server. It made me appreciate how all of the rabid little monsters who couldn't put in the work on a low-pop have migrated away, and it made me appreciate how empty and sensible Trade Chat on my home server has become. That said, there are a million guilds LFM and they're all progressing. More than I can say for Chromaggus-A and their fleeting 1/7 in FL10.

Different realms for different reasons... but there's a difference between picking the realm/community, and having it change irreversibly over the course of weeks and months. Chromaggus is a shadow of its former self.
