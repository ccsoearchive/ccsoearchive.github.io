My general thoughts are "the concept of realm is the problem" as you saw in the previous thread. Long term realms have to go. Blizzard is doing a nice job with the cross realm LFG and the addition of LFR. This is a start.

Here are my ideas on where this should head for cross realm features:

- Guilds. Ahh but conflicts in names right? No. <Forty Spoke - Chromaggus>. We can take advantage of the outdated realm system to remove name conflicts.
- Mail / guild bank. This will help with lower population auction houses. Thrifty auctioneers can profit off of supplying a dead realm with product. Blizzard already has botter / gold seller scripts in place to catch those people. Nothing would change.
- Realm ID. Torvalds - Chromaggus. Cross realm friend lists without the privacy issues.

Changes that could help strengthen a realms auction house:
- Buyer side posting. Allow people looking for items to place an order and sellers be able to fill it. Open the API up so that web pages can exist to show where demand on a realm.
