I do not know why Blizzard doesn't just have you select the type of server you want, time zone, and country. Then assign you to a realm that is low population when you create your first character. After that character (or main charactier) hits lev cap, allow the player to switch realms if they so choose. But then you charge them. No more free server transfers and a quick solution to the population problem.

Also more tutorials explaining the choice of type of realm and to make sure you get onto a realm that may have friends on it. Alot of people that buy a game will not go online to research the game and find things out like the difference between a PvP server and RP server. Instead they load up the game and jump right in.

Maybe a prompt that takes them to a welcome to wow forum with alot of information about the ui and how to set it up. Keybinds, macros and all that stuff so that new people can at least have a chance at coming into the game somewhat prepared.

Also another thing that can be done is to have sub catagories with realms like PvP-progression or PvP-battlezone RP-progression/RP-battlezone and so on. That way people that like to progress in game content will not end up on a realm with 1 to 4 progression guilds. And people that like PvP will not end up on a server where everyone hides in a dungeon instead of forming arena teams, and doing world PvP and such.

I would like to see some server's to get mergered or at the very least offer players 1 free xffer per toon so we can just go to where ever.

1 free xffer per toon (and I mean 1 ever not 1 per month year or anything else) would remove most of the cons to anything posted. Sure it means we would hafta leave our dead server but even if blizzard didn't have paid server transfers and such players would have left the servers anyways and just made a new char on the other server they xffered to. none of this is really the result of anything blizzard has done but I do believe they should do something to help out the players on those dead servers.

Pros to allowing 1 free xffer per toon
1.the ability to close dead servers altogether once everyone xffers off. Saving Blizz $$

2.Players get to choose where they go so they can chat with their friends in order to make the choice easier

3. Once players xffer they might need to spend $$ to switch to their friends faction thus generating more $$ for Blizz.\

4. Players suffering from playing on dead servers usually either quit the game altogether or just talk in trade all day becoming trolls or other kinds of jerks so this would give them more to do being on a decent server which will help the community as a whole (god knows it needs it in alot of cases)

Cons

1. Blizzard wouldn't make money off the xffers the first time

2. Can't really think of anymore cons outside of making medium pop servers become high pop servers and such

First off, let me say that the idea of buyer-side auctions is nothing short of brilliant, and not just in therms of the low population server problem, but as a quality of live improvement for players in all realms. I find it very annoying to check the AH every time i log in for those illusive items or mats I need, or worse, spamming trade chat. As a seller, being able to browse what people are looking for and taking the highest offer price is great.

Now on to the low population server problem. Admittedly i didn't read the entire first thread but I think the most practical and likely solution is to merge realms. Dungeon and Raid finders are great and all that but you still have the problem of the capitol cities looking like ghost towns. And I find it very unlikely that they will abandon the idea of realms all together. When it comes to merging realms, I think they should take the really low population realms and roll them into low-medium population realms instead of taking 5-6 lows and making one medium. This way you're not taking players from 6 realms and smashing them together into a disjointed realm, your just augmenting the low-medium realms with some new blood.

Now admittedly, I am not on one of the very low population realms, my realm, Misha has been recently labeled "New Players" Now Im not sure what this exactly means but there a hell of a lot of new low level players traipsing through Orgrimmar.

When it comes to the name conflict issue, I think that both players should be given a choice to change. For example, if 2 players with the same name are on merging realms, they are both given the option to choose a name change. The player who takes the option (or takes it first) is also given a free race/faction change for that character, giving a bit of incentive.

Let me know what you think!
