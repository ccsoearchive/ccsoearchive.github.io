As stated previously.. Blizzard is in this to make Money.. the product they provide are computer games..

Now.. someone who pays the $$ to transfer to another server is most likely going to play for some time to come.. So.. this means that blizzard gets the $25 off this person PLUS their continued monthly contribution.

Someone who is sooo upset about being on a low population server and quits the game for this reason, most likely would not be playing much longer anyway.
For example.. lets say 5 people from a low population server quit .. that's 5 x $15 = $75 per month that blizzard loses.

Now lets say 5 people from a low population server transfer ooohh 2 characters and they continue to play the game for some time.. so that's ((5 X $25) x 2) + (5 X $15) = $250 + $75 per month. In the second scenerio Blizzard collects a one time fee of $250 and continues to collect the $75/monthly fee. So when you see how that $250 offsets the loss of the 5 peopole cancelling subscriptions, it should be easier to understand from a business perspective.

Why don't you think they offer any kind of bulk transfer rate? Again Money.. if you have 5 characters and you want to move your main raiding character you are most likely going to want to move your alts (if high enough level) rather than starting new characters.

Of course some people will bring up the point of nearly a million lost subscriptions.. How many of those left only because of being on a low population server? I'd bet a lot less than were willing to transfer and continue playing for a while.

When you have more people cancelling their subscription due to low population servers than people transferring, blizzard will then step in. Untill then it's gravy..

I totally agree it would suck to be on a low population server.. All I'm trying to show is that nothing will be done about it as it's an extra source of income for Blizzard.
