IC INFORMATION

===================================================================

ATTENTION ACCOMPLISHED HORDE CITIZEN:
As an able-bodied member of the Horde, you are hereby requested to report to Runemaster Wailakee Dawntreader at The Crossroads in the Northern Barrens.

The recent display of your various skills and talents has not gone unnoticed, and your assistance is needed by the Dawntreader Caravan to secure the Northern Barrens and surrounding territories against the ever-threatening presence of the Alliance, quillboar, and centaur.

The Caravan is a defensive operation lead by the Barrens’ own native Dawntreader tauren tribe, but served with honor by all Horde citizens. Your duties would involve using your specially honed skills to gather resources and intelligence from the enemy, and to discover and develop weapons and defenses that can be delivered to the Horde’s militia against encroaching enemies throughout northern Kalimdor.

It is important to note that you are not being drafted into battle, but instead are being requested to supply the front line. This is a defensive operation with your goal being to provide the rest of the Horde forces with the tools they need to succeed in times of war. Without operations like yours providing the necessary supplies, our warriors are but warm bodies on the battlefield.

Lok’tar ogar, citizen. Destiny awaits!

OOC INFORMATION

===================================================================

*Purpose & Theme*
The Dawntreader Caravan has existed since the Cataclysm and originally consisted solely of the Dawntreader Tribe of tauren. Once a neutral party making their home in the Southern Barrens, they were recruited into the Horde by Garrosh Hellscream and offered protection against the increasing Alliance presence in exchange for their servitude to the Horde. However, due to a decrease in the tribe’s numbers from a recent mass-fatality, Vol’jin has requested all able-bodied Horde citizens to join the tribe in their initiative, thereby bolstering their forces with numbers and diversity. Now, they wander the plain territories of northern Kalimdor in search of any resources that could be used to serve the Horde’s front lines against the Alliance in an attempt to drive them from the land.

Unlike in the past, the Dawntreader Caravan now employs all Horde races in its ranks, from disgruntled undead Alchemists to savvy Blood Elf mages, though the tauren tribe still remains in a leadership position as they know the areas best.

Every employed citizen has something of use to offer the Caravan and ultimately the Horde, whether it be an eye for picking out sturdy wild Wind Riders to tame, or bring able to detect the subtle hints of nearby ley lines to draw upon. The Caravan seeks all of these things and functions as a resource operation first and foremost, though they must be well equipped to handle any physical threats which they may come across during their travels.

From an OOC perspective, the Caravan offers such diversity to all players who want to roleplay with us. We are confident that no matter what profession your character has taken up, he or she will be a valuable member that can jump in on ANY storyline, at any time. We feel as though Horde RP tends to lean on the segregated and limiting side simply due to the way the Horde is represented through the lore, and we wanted to help eliminate that.

===================================================================

*Our Vision*
After nearly seven years of partaking in roleplaying guilds in which she never seemed to meet the time requirements and demands of, Wailakee decided it was time to venture off on her own. Her idea was to create a no-stress, pressure-free roleplaying guild in which the members were free to participate on their own time and at their own pace. More specifically, she wanted to target players that other guilds didn’t seem to want to accommodate – those with busy schedules or high real life demands. Our members are not required to log on for a certain amount of days, nor are they asked to attend a certain number of events, to keep their membership – making this guild perfect for those who have an ever changing or demanding out-of-game schedule.

In addition, she wanted her guild to be a warm embrace for those suffering from anxiety (social or other) or similar issues, as she personally knows all too well how those often consuming emotions can interfere with a player’s enjoyment of an MMORPG.

Because there are no high social demands placed on our members, we are hoping that every one of them can feel as comfortable as possible when playing with us. We do encourage those with anxiety to reach out and push their boundaries as we believe that this is how self-confidence is instilled and grown, but we also realize that these things cannot be force. Instead, we want to work with players with anxiety to make them feel welcome, so that they will feel comfortable enough to come out of their shells on their own. How long this process takes is up to each individual – but if we can help at least some players feel more comfortable in their own skin, then that is all we could ask for.

*Membership Requirements*

  • Remember the roleplaying Golden Rule: IC=/=OOC. Events that occur during an RP session are not to be taken out of context. There is always the possibility that your OOC best friend’s character turns out to be a vengeful mage bent on the destruction of your IC family, but that doesn’t give you the right to be angry at the individual OOC. Conflict in RP is normal and should be welcomed, as it makes things infinitely more interesting.
  • Become familiar with basic RP rules and guidelines. In addition to the above point, we expect members to be familiar with the basic rules of RP, or to reach out for help if they are not. These rules are to be followed as strictly as possible to prevent issues from arising.
  • Show respect for all those you interact with. This includes players outside of your guild as well.
  • Remember that we are a guild that supports players with anxiety and similar issues. Staying true to our vision, this guild is intended to be a welcoming second home to those with social anxiety even if you are not a sufferer yourself. Just remember that we are all going through the walks of life together, and if you are not willing to offer a helping hand to your fellow guild members then you have no place with us.
  • INTERACT! If you are going to hit up your daily heroic dungeons, it is polite to ask your guild mates if they would like to join you on your adventures. We should all adopt a friendly, welcoming attitude towards our members and encourage people to play together. This is especially important for those with anxiety.

    FAQ

    ===================================================================

    Q: Are there any race, class, or level restrictions?
    A: NO! We believe that diversity plays a very important role in keeping the storylines interesting and thus all Horde races and classes are welcome. We also do not impose any level restrictions.

    Q: “Dawntreader”… Didn’t I read that this was the name of a Tauren tribe? Are you sure this guild isn’t exclusive to Tauren?
    A: Although the guild grandfathers its name from the Dawntreader tauren tribe (due to the caravan being lead by tauren), the name itself has much more meaning to it that applies to any character who joins.

    The Dawntreader Tribe received its name because of its tendency to be active during the early mornings. Because the tribe makes its home on the arid desert plains of The Barrens, the tauren often take to their hunts during the dawn, taking advantage of this sacred time to escape the blistering heat.

    The Barrens have changed much over the years, and the Tauren are no longer the primary inhabitants of these plains. Since the Cataclysm, the very earth has been shattered and the Alliance have developed an ever increasing presence which threatens not only the tribe, but the established Horde forces as well. The tribe, once neutral, was recruited into the Horde following the events of the Cataclysm out of mutual necessity: the tribe for protection, and the Horde for resources and extra bodies.

    Now, the tribe is raising another promise to hold to its name: to embrace their nomadic history and take to the land for resources which may be used to supply the Horde's militia, bringing with them the dawn in times of darkness and scarcity.

    Q: So does that mean you play in the early mornings?
    A: The guild does not have set times for participation or events. “Whenever we can” is the motto around here, however that doesn’t mean that the members are not active – it just means that their schedules can be unpredictable.

    Currently, most members (and the GM) tend to play in the evenings and throughout the weekends at any time.

    Q: So, this is a military guild?
    No, and yes. The Caravan’s central focus is to bolster the Horde’s main military forces against the Alliance but not directly lead or serve on the front lines.

    Q: So what is your specialty, exactly?
    A: The Dawntreader tribe know the prairie territories like no other, and the Horde desires to take advantage of their knowledge of the land – this was the purpose of their recruitment. They are hunters first, and stealth and espionage have been a large part of their tribe’s culture for decades as these skills allowed them to survive against the threat of quilboar, centaur, and now the Alliance.

    The Caravan wanders the plains daily in search of many different resources, including but not limited to:
  • Resources (stolen perhaps from local enemies)
  • Herbs (for their employed alchemists to make use of)
  • Ley Lines (to draw upon as a magical resource)
  • Wind Riders, Kodos, and other animals to use as military mounts
  • Etc!
    • Truly, the possibilities are endless and this list could go on and on. I can guarantee that any race or class can find a way to fit into the guild and serve the Horde from an IC perspective, and everyone has a say in what resources we should hunt next.

    Q: What do you mean by ‘prairie territories’? What territories do you protect?
    A: The Caravan is centred throughout The Barrens (North and South), Mulgore, Stonetalon Mountains, and Durotar. They may even wander as far as Feralas and Desolace.

    Q: I have a tauren character that I would like to join the Caravan with. Is it possible to become part of the tribe as well?
    A: Of course! If this is the route you want to take, ideally we prefer if you start a fresh tauren character with a backstory that includes being born into the tribe (a retcon is acceptable too), but we are absolutely open to tauren hailing from different tribes forsaking their birth tribes and joining ours. How you would like to proceed is up to you!

    NOTE: Please keep in mind this does NOT mean that tauren characters must join the tribe! It is simply a suggestion.

    Q: What type of roleplayers are allowed to join?
    A: Any and all roleplayers are welcome. Whether you para or one-line RP, or whether you are new or a veteran, you will find a welcoming home in our guild.

    We do ask that you keep in mind that we are beginner friendly, and you are expected to be patient with those learning the lore and how to RP. Those expecting a full-time RPing, veteran-filled guild with eloquently thought out storylines that are perfect in every way will be disappointed and we are likely not the guild for you. Our focus here is fun for all, and many of us have been soured by previous full-time RP guilds with high player demands. These types of environments are simply not for us.

    That being said, the GM has been playing for eight years and has been a lore enthusiast and RPer for at least six of those. Just because we are beginner friendly does not mean many of us are not knowledgeable, simply that we have a desire to educate those who want to learn.

    Q: Do you have a website?
    A: Not at this time, nor are there are intentions to create one in the near future. We feel as though too many OOC mediums can result in lack of in-game participation and can hinder RP, so for now we will continue to function without a website but are open to the idea should the need arise.

    Q: Are there any other facts I should know?
    A: Fun fact! The letter addressed to the Horde Citizen in the IC information section was written with inspirations from the various “Warchief’s Command!” quests which send players to new questing zones. This gives the character a choice in the matter: they can either ignore the drafting request, or answer the call to arms.

    Q: How can I apply?
    A: You will find no complex guild applications here! If you are interested in joining, please have your character send a brief IC letter to Wailakee explaining the recruitment letter you received from Orgrimmar, and that you are ready to join the cause. She will then reply and have an official IC recruitment arranged.
