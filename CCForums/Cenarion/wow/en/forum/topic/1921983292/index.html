I know countless posts are out there in regards to this but I would like to throw in my 2 cents.

From my experience ferals do have an advantage in stealth, hands down, tested it personally with a guildmate. The developers have said that the stealth levels are equal because rogues get it at an earlier level, I believe this to be true but the following may be what is making the difference.

Back Story: I played a gnome mage in BC and most of Wrath. I noticed that on a pull at the top of a ramp or stairs I would have to stand further up the ramp than fellow (taller) casters or would have LoS issues because I’m shorter. This tells me that LoS is associated not only with the body (X –Y axis) but with the head (Z axis). Same thing is verified with mounts. Ever notice how a gnome mount is larger than an identical mount used by a different race? It has to be to keep the eye level the same while mounted.

From what I understand feral’s hit box is in the center of their body. Since they are elongated along their forward axis the head sticks forward of the hit box thus shifting the “perception envelope” forward as well. A rogue on the other hand would have their “perception envelope” dead center of their hit box in the X-Y axis.

Again, my $0.02.
