Some tips from a 9/9 gold holy paladin:

  • Reforge for haste. Flask for intellect. You want big fast heals. Don't worry about spirit too much, you can use restorative amber after almost every pull
  • Talents: Speed of light / justice / EF / Clemency / Holy Prism
  • Glyphs: Blinding Light / Sac / FoL
  • Be prepared to use flash of light like it's TBC. LoD / HR are pretty weak in CM's.
  • Avoid stacking healing CD's where possible
  • Hand of Salv & Hand of Prot are great if your tank is having aggro issues on a large pull (i.e big monkey pull in SSB)
  • Hand of Sac on the tank whenever he's taking brutal dmg
  • Voice comm between you & your tank re: healing CD's is critical. If we're about to pull a nasty trash pack, and everything is on cooldown i tell him before the pull
  • Communicate with dps that have off heals to help (i.e shaman) when things get out of control
  • Make a macro for SSB last boss to switch to both your dps set AND change spec so you don't have to fiddle with the UI

    We got four golds in one night just screwing around, didn't watch any guides or anything. Our tank hadn't tanked up until now, our healer hadn't played resto on his druid in 4 years, and I just hit 90 about a week ago.

    One of the things that's absolutely huge are gem slots. Gems don't get reduced down at all, so it's best to stack gear with as many sockets as possible. This is easily accomplished with the new siege gear, considering all of the tier have a ton of sockets for every class and spec.

    Heart of the wild is also especially powerful. If we ever needed to use it (mainly for our resto druid to dps), our tank would pop cooldowns and if I had them up I would drop AG and then tide so that healing wasn't an issue.

    Paladins can also bop off bleeds and there are some mobs a hunter can pull and then feign death to reset them and pull you out of combat. We did this in Mogushan Palace, right before the pack after the first boss. The saurok automatically puts you in combat, so our hunter pulled it and then feigned, while the rest of us stood back so we didn't get stuck in combat.

    A huge thing for challenge modes isn't necessarily doing the most damage, to me it's being able to make as few mistakes as possible, or to be able to recover from mistakes made. For example, in Temple of the Jade serpent, we used invisibility pot on the bridge before the twin bosses. We killed the last pack (the sha all count as mobs for the 45 needed to complete it) but we invis'd most of the bridge. Being the woman I am I completely forgot my bind for invis pot and ran right into some mobs. I had ank up, ank'd, and popped invis pot as fast as I could. We ended up getting gold that time. Again, it's being able to make as few mistakes as possible and being able to recover from them.
