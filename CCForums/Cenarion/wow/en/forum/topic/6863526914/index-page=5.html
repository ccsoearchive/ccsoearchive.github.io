I tanked everything personally with a DPS flask, food, runeforge and pure DPS gear. I know other DKs tanks used tank gear though, or at least a mix. I think it's universally accepted that you should be at least using the strength flask though.

It largely depends on your composition and what CDs you have available to you. We needed more DPS from me because a lot of pulls are very melee unfriendly (we ran with 2 melee DPS and a demo lock), and their up time isn't all that high. The reason I got away with it however is planned CDs and very tight stun/interrupt rotations.

Some pulls also aren't possible unless you kite with chillblains. It was common for me on "SOME" pulls to open with our full stun rotation, get as much threat and damage as I could in the ~10 seconds then proceed to kite with blood boil (virtue of rolling blood) spam with chillblains snaring all the adds.

Your mileage may vary, but I personally did everything in DPS gear. You're at greater risk of dying on SOME bosses and SOME trash packs, but we just sucked it up and reset the instance if I went splat because of unhealable damage.

I like haste in challenge modes TBH. Although to be perfectly frank I was incredibly lazy when it came to reforging for CM, so I don't even remember what I did. Haste is still really strong for Blood DKs though.

We never had a hunter or a mage, nor did we ever feel it necessary. It takes some practice because the cushion for blood boiling and keeping chillblains up is rather narrow, but you can effectively kite as a blood DK (and do crazy damage because diseases aren't part of an AoE cap).

I normally crushed everybody on trash DPS with full DPS gear on, and on some of the very hard hitting bosses typically did the most damage (or competitive at least).

The only downside to doing it this way is that your healer can't really contribute to damage on a regular basis. A lot of compositions will have healers assisting and while ours did a fair share of damage on a lot bosses and SOME trash packs, she couldn't do it as often as some healers could if their tank was indeed worrying about survival more than damage.

Trade off for sure, but it worked for us.

I stayed full tank gear except for trinkets and sometimes fallen crusader runeforge when we initially did these (more to do with remembering to re-forge it after raids before CMs). I would highly recommend, if not full dps gear, the dps flasks/food/potions/runeforges/trinkets. It's one of those things, the strength you get from each individual piece of gear is sort of a drop in the bucket compared to vengeance if you're getting hit a lot, but when your vengeance is low it's a large dps gain, and of course you have to be survivable (if you have to kite a pack more because you're in dps gear that's probably not a gain, for instance).

All things told, I'm pretty sure IN GENERAL dps is better, but it's not as large of a gain as you'd think. I also did about half of these before bone link fetish got nerfed so my dps in the videos is higher than it would've been without (that trinket represented upwards of 15% of my total damage in many instances)
