Most crafting professions provide 80 extra agility (inscription, enchanting, leatherworking), in the cast of Engineering if used on cd the synapse springs average out to 80 agility, as well as their other tinkers, though the malfunctions of the tinkers make them a double-edged sword and can potentially cause far more harm than good. Tailoring's swordguard embroidery is generally considered to be worth somewhere in the range of ~75~80 agility making it roughly equal as well.

BS provides 80 extra agility until you get to the point that you're putting the new epic gems in every slot at which point it goes up to 100 extra agility making it the best.

Jewelcrafting provides 81 extra agility, until you get to the point that you're putting the new epic gems in every slot at which point it drops to only 51 extra agility making it the weakest crafting profession.

The combinations of professions doesn't really have any real impact or increased effectiveness on the professions. For example using just blue quality gems being a BS/JC still only provides 161 extra agility, 80 from bs and 81 from jc (in all epic gems the combination only gives 151 extra agility, 100 from bs and 51 from jc). The only exception I can really imagine would be a combination of Tailoring, Engineering, and maybe Herbalism since they are procs/cooldowns and so stacking them may compound their effects (though I'm not sure on this).

Engineering is the best, because you can pool the 300 Agi on-use instead of having it spread out. For example, for Sub Rogue, it's the same cooldown as Shadowdance, so all that AGI can go to your shadowdance, which is more valuable than just having a flat 80-81 over the minute. I also use it for my initial rupture, but I do not know if the agi bonus carrier over on subsequent refreshes, I'm assuming not.

In addition nitro boost on your belt is another excellent gap closer, and if it backfires, you can cloak out of the rocket fuel leak.

Also, goggles are very cool looking and one should never go without them. :)

Jewelcrafting for a flat 81agi, Enchanting for a flat 80agi. I prefer the latter, as you don't have to do crummy dailies for the Enchanting. And as someone said ealier, BS beats out both with full epic gems.
