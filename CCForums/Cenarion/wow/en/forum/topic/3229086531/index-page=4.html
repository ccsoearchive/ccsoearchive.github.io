In other words: There's nothing here yet to rend garments over or keen wildly into the air about. It's less than half the story of how our class will change in the next expansion. It's a first look at the possibilities. I find them fascinating. And I look forward to learning more before I pass judgement.

Continuing the theme of noteworthy stuff that I have no idea where to list or mention, I wanted to include a transcript of the only noteworthy rogue-related question that was asked during the live Q+A panels at BlizzCon with the game designers and other bigwigs. Fittingly, it was asked by one of roguedom's most visible members, Chase Christian (Madsushi@Azgalor, and the man behind the Encrypted Text column on WoW Insider).

After taking a moment to thank the devs for our upcoming legendary (which elicited more boos than cheers from the audience -- it sounded like the rogue representation numbers in-game were matched in the BlizzCon audience), Chase asked:

"My question is regarding the new introduction of the Windwalker monk class, and also the formalization of cat druids as an official spec. With all these new melee DPS hybrids, what do you guys have planned to make sure that rogues are competitive as the only pure melee class, where we can't really bring some of the utility that a healer or an offspec -- they can bring those abilities. What do you guys have planned for rogues to make sure that we still bring some utility?"

And here was the answer from game designer Ion Hazzikostas (Ghostcrawler was on the stage, but didn't offer his take). It probably won't satisfy many of you. :)

"I think we've moved away from a model where being a pure meant that you simply did way more damage than everyone else and that only hybrids brought utility. I think everyone brings a little bit of everything. Right now some of the best reasons to bring a rogue -- aside from their excellent, top-tier melee damage -- also include things like the ability to fan Crippling Poison onto a bunch of things, or excellent interrupts, or stuns. Those are things that are useful in a number of encounters and will continue to be useful in a number of encounters going forward, as well as, of course, in PvP.

"We're clearly going to try to maintain the rogue gameplay niche. Just because you have a monk class that has some maybe similar mechanics -- in terms of energy bar, being melee -- doesn't necessarily mean they're going to be stealthy. They're not going to have rogue poisons. They're not going to have all the little tricks that make being a rogue awesome and that make rogues rogues."

Meanwhile: Celinne, I duno if you saw this, but a different guy tried to ask a question about Killing Spree and Bandit's Guile. I *think* he wanted to try to get them to comment on how disjointed the Bandit's Guile mechanic is with Combat's cooldowns and with the rest of the spec as a whole, but he got tongue tied, then horribly flustered, and the poor guy didn't end up managing to ask much of a question at all. Ghostcrawler ended up assuming the guy just wanted to know when the best time was to use KS, making the entire thing super-awkward and ultimately pointless.

Keep in mind, though, that even if you do get to start the chain, legendaries are typically created as a reward for pretty dedicated end-game raiders. So unless you really want to be one of those, getting these daggers to evolve from their blue/purple form to their orange, tentacley form may not be in the cards.

Here's how the new legendary section looks, for those of you too lazy to jump back to the first post (I'm one of them):

==The Legendary Weapons: Fangs of the Father==
The Basics:
  • Set of daggers, one main-hand, one off-hand.
  • Only rogues can get them. They will be our precious(es).
  • Will be usable by all specs. Ghostcrawler sez: "We have a little tweak in there so that Combat Rogues will be able to Sinister Strike with the daggers without it being a big DPS loss." This tweak appears to be a spell called "Weighted Blades," which on the PTR right now increases Sinister Strike, Revealing Strike and Main Gauche damage 45% (this may change before the patch goes live).

How You Get Them:
  • Long quest chain, which appears to begin with the killing of a Dragon Soul raid boss. You get the daggers relatively early in the chain, but they become progressively more powerful (in three stages) as you move along the chain, with some steps requiring you to complete tasks within the Dragon Soul raid (possibly including the cold-blooded murder of additional bosses).
  • You must raid Dragon Soul in normal or heroic modes; you won’t be able to get components of the daggers by raiding through the Raid Finder.

What They Do:
  • Damage effect: Stacking agility bonus. On the PTR right now, this bonus increases each time you upgrade the daggers to the next stage of their evolution. In their first stage, they'll increase your agility by 2 per stack; in the second stage, by 5 per stack; and in the final (legendary) stage, by 17 per stack. (In each case, the max number of stacks is 50.) How long it takes for stacks to accumulate may vary by spec.
  • Legendary proc: On the PTR right now, if you have the final-stage legendaries and your agility bonus has stacked 30 times, each stack after that has a chance to proc "Fury of the Destroyer," which, according to the tooltip, "immediately grants 5 combo points and causes your finishing moves to grant 5 combo points" for the next six seconds. When it procs, however, your agility stacks reset to 0.
  • On-use effect: The final-stage legendaries will allow you to unfurl wings that can be used to slow descent (a la priest's Levitate or mage's Slow Fall).

For More Info:
  • Read the official blog for additional bits, including the lore background: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/blog/3579611
  • Spoilers: Wowhead has also datamined a bunch of goodies that appear to be part of the quest chain; here's Rogue Resource's summary, but please keep in mind that *none* of this stuff has been officially announced yet and may thus still be in flux: http://www.rogueresource.com/?p=2744
  • More spoilers: Roguey rogue Nik has been recording PTR videos of his experiences with the legendary questline thus far: see http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PW0Bvg4YZAo and http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Kfaqu014WI for more, but remember, as always, that all of this stuff is likely incomplete and will change as we get closer to the launch of Patch 4.3.
