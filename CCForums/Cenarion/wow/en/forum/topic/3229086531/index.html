  • Post 1 lists stuff on rogue class adjustments and the legendary daggers.
  • Post 2 discusses new gear, analysis/theorycrafting and other miscellany.
  • Post 3 *was* all about the Mists of Pandaria expansion, but I moved it all to this new thread so it'll have more room to grow: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/4044506920

Anything in quotes is lifted directly from official patch notes, Blizzard posts or transcripts of interviews with Blizzardfolk.

==Rogue Class Adjustments and Fixes==
  • ”Wound Poison now reduces the target’s healing received by 25%, up from 10%.”
  • "All raid and party buffs which grant group members 10% increased attack power have been changed slightly. They continue to provide 10% increased ranged attack power, but now provide 20% increased melee attack power."
  • The above change impacts Abomination's Might, Blessing of Might, Trueshot Aura and Unleased Rage. (The AP buff from Runescroll of Might also is doubled for melee, to 16% from 8%.) This was done to address what Blizzard feels is unacceptably low DPS for melee in dungeons and raids.
  • "Main Gauche should no longer break effects such as Gouge."
  • "Tricks of the Trade has a new sound effect." (Yep, that's an actual patch note. Someone's got a sense of humor.)
  • Bug fix: "Secondary debuffs will now more reliably apply to targets which are knocked out of Stealth or Invisibility."
  • Deadmines: "Helix Gearbreaker's Stickybomb should no longer break Stealth."
  • Deadmines: "Vanessa VanCleef's Vigorous VanCleef Vindicator achievement should no longer break Stealth."
  • Well of Eternity: "Starting the Mannoroth encounter should no longer remove Stealth effects."


==The Legendary Weapons: Fangs of the Father==
The Basics:
  • Set of daggers, one main-hand, one off-hand.
  • Only rogues can get them. They will be our precious(es).
  • Will be usable by Combat rogues thanks to a passive ability on the main-hand dagger called "Weighted Blades," which increases Sinister Strike and Revealing Strike damage 45%.

How You Get Them:
  • Long quest chain that you pick up inside the entrance to the Dragon Soul raid.
  • The daggers become more powerful as you move along the chain. They evolve in three "stages": Jaws of Retribution; Maw of Oblivion; and finally, Fangs of the Father, which are the actual "orange-text" legendaries.
  • You get the Jaws of Retribution by completing a series of quests that require rogue abilities, including pickpocketing the fourth raid boss, Hagara (which can only be reached after killing the first three bosses and the trash leading up to Hagara).
  • Several steps in the chain require you to complete tasks within the Dragon Soul raid, including the repeated, cold-blooded murder of bosses to collect quest items that only rogues can get.
  • You must raid Dragon Soul in normal or heroic modes; Raid Finder does not mix with any stage of these daggers.
  • Icy Veins has an outstanding, detailed runthrough of the entire quest chain here: http://www.icy-veins.com/fangs-of-the-father-legendary-daggers-quest-line

What They Do:
  • Damage effect: Stacking agility boost that gets better as the daggers "evolve." In their first stage, they'll increase your agility by 2 per stack; in the second stage, by 5 per stack; and in the final (legendary) stage, by 17 per stack. (In each case, the max number of stacks is 50.) How long it takes for stacks to accumulate appears to vary by spec.
  • Legendary proc (final-stage legendaries only): Once the agility boost has stacked 30 times, each stack after that has a chance to proc "Fury of the Destroyer," which, according to the tooltip, "immediately grant[s] 5 combo points and cause[s] your finishing moves to generate 5 combo points" for the next six seconds. When it procs, however, your agility stacks reset to 0.
  • On-use effect: The final-stage legendaries will allow you to unfurl wings that can be used to slow descent (a la priest's Levitate or mage's Slow Fall).

For More Info:
  • Read the official blog for additional bits, including lore: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/blog/3579611
  • Rogue Resource's summary of just about every worthwhile bit of info out there on the legendaries: http://www.rogueresource.com/?p=2744
  • Roguey rogue Nik recorded three videos of his experiences with the legendary quest line on the PTR. Part 3 lives at: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_nNQfhb1XwY
  • Datamined music that appears to be associated with the quest chain: http://www.youtube.com/watch?feature=player_embedded&v=pcYul9mFsDA

==Hints & Allegations==
Look, everybody! It's unconfirmed rumors! Hooray! But for really: Don't trust anything below until it's been confirmed by Blizz.

  • Combat rogues: Fan of Knives appears to no longer be usable during Killing Spree.

  • Movement-while-stealthed animations appear to have changed for a number of races.

  • This is not in the patch notes, but Mutilate appears to have been tweaked. According to the tooltips, the hurty it deals will increase from 150% to 179% of weapon damage, but the 20% damage buff against poisoned targets is being removed. The end result might amount to a small damage buff for Assassination rogues.

  • The Ambush and Backstab tooltips have also changed in a way that initially made some think the abilities were being nerfed. But brain-using rogue Aldriana writes: "I have a sneaking suspicion that the Backstab and Ambush "nerfs" are just tooltip changes. ... My guess is that they're storing the damage calculation a little differently but the actual damage done is not changing."

  • Though no preview has yet been released by Blizzard, Wowhead News datamined the next season's PvP set: http://www.wowheadnews.com/blog=195681/4-3-preview-season-11-pvp-sets

The first incarnation of the official notes for Patch 4.3 is up! (As is an explanatory blog from Ghostcrawler.) Nothing specifically about rogues is in the notes yet, and we're not mentioned in GC's blog either -- looks like we get saved for part two. But what *is* in the notes should make any raiding or dungeoning rogue happy: All attack power buffs will be doubled for melee damage.

Per GC in his blog:
Overall, we think melee DPS in dungeons and raids is still too low. We think melee DPS is fine in PvP, and we think melee DPS is competitive against ranged specs against target dummies, but in actual encounters with all of their moving pieces, melee falls behind. To attempt to adjust for this imbalance, we have improved the benefit to attack power given by the various raid buffs (Blessing of Might et al.) to melee only. Hunter DPS is fine, or even high in 4.2, so we didn’t want this buff to extend to them, which is why the AP benefit is now 20% for melee attacks but still 10% for ranged attacks.


And in the patch notes:
All raid and party buffs which grant group members 10% increased attack power have been changed slightly. They continue to provide 10% increased ranged attack power, but now provide 20% increased melee attack power.
  • Abomination's Might: The melee attack power bonus from this raid buff has been increased to 10/20%, up from 5/10%. The ranged attack power bonus remains 5/10%.
  • Blessing of Might: The melee attack power bonus from this raid buff has been increased to 20%, up from 10%. The ranged attack power bonus remains 10%.
  • Runescroll of Might: The melee attack power bonus from this consumable has been increased to 16%, up from 8%. The ranged attack power bonus remains 8%.
  • Trueshot Aura: The melee attack power bonus from this raid buff has been increased to 20%, up from 10%. The ranged attack power bonus remains 10%.
  • Unleashed Rage: The melee attack power bonus from this raid buff has been increased to 10/20%, up from 5/10%. The ranged attack power bonus remains 5/10%.


Will there be rogue-specific changes in store as well? Stay tuned, bat friends -- same bat time, same bat channel.
