Q: A big concern for me and a large part of the Rogue PvP Community has been that Preparation and Shadow Step were put into the same Tier of Talents, along with Burst of Speed. While Burst of Speed and Shadowstep are a great two talents to choose between, Preparation is a very powerful cooldown, and instead of feeling like it’s making a hard choice between abilities, it feels like we’re getting nerfed, because we already have Preparation and Shadowstep currently. Are there any updates to this situation and what was the reasoning behind grouping these abilities together?

Watcher: “We definitely understand why it’s a source of concern, but ultimately the Subtlety tree offering the combination of Prep and Shadowstep is the core reason why it has become the de facto (and perceived mandatory) PvP spec. If we moved Preparation to a different tier, then the community is clearly saying they’d all take it, and that runs counter to our aims with the new talent system. And if we add a baseline class-wide ability that resets multiple cooldowns, then we may as well just adjust the underlying cooldown lengths directly and save people a keybind.
“In defense of that rogue tier as it currently stands, if we know that rogues with Prep are sacrificing a high-uptime movement ability, then we have the flexibility to balance (i.e. buff) the class as a whole around that restriction. If rogues can (and thus will) take both abilities, then the class as a whole gets balanced around the survivability and mobility of having both.”


It's not like the intentions behind the abilities being in the same tier wasn't made extremely obvious. No, they haven't implemented a *how* to that yet, but that doesn't mean it isn't planned, especially when it's very obvious that abilities have not had their final numbers (damage/damage reduction) passed over at all. A cursory glance at...backstab, for example, makes it abundantly clear that we're still under heavy WIP. Call me a heretic, but I believe they'll mostly come through with those changed needed to make the talents being in the same tier workable.

People seem to mistake our 'being ignored' as of late as an issue of being looked over, where in reality the reason we haven't received massive changes often this expansion is because *they're quite happy with our current performance, and relative spec performance.* You don't rebalance the gyroscope. Last time they promised to fix something (combat at the beginning of the expansion), they came through quite well. Most would prefer hyperbolic anger over changes rather than reasonably considering them, though, as most threads on this topic will show.
