Got it on the second try, made it to the last corner on the first go, and got too greedy on the timing around that last worgen.

My advice, be patient. I didn't change my spec to sub and I didn't distract other than the pack to the left of the sleeping dog in the middle of the courtyard. Just go slow and be smart about timing. The folks failing are just rushing this. The design is clear - if you are trying to get through two mobs you are doing it wrong. Blizzard designed this so that you need to time your sneaking between mobs, and sap the single pats, move to them, then carry on. You don't need sub, even if it might help some, you just need eyes, your sap and patience!

I thought it was rather fun :)

The hardest part for me was...finding the limitations of the mechanics. I can't un-stealth, vanishing doesn't help, and you only get ported after they do damage to you. I opted for the long way around. After going across the bridge, I went straight into the training grounds, and used distract to hold large packs at a distance. Sap is more like making gaps in impassible walls. The most confusing part for me was trying to figure out how to get passed the guards at the canal. I walked down to the gate, and counting 4 guards from the right, I sapped him, and jumped up on the ledge and hugged the wall 'til I was in front of the courtyard. The rest shouldn't be too hard.


Vanishing does help. You're near impossible to detect in imp stealth.
