Stamped in black ink on cheap parchment, these fliers can be found in every major Horde and neutral city across Azeroth.

Has da war against da Alliance got ya down? Would ya sleep bettah at night knowin’ dat ya got a couple extra seaforium charges or goblin land mines hidden around your front yard? Do ya need a couple of litres of phosphine or chlorine gas to finally get rid of dat nasty dwarf infestation in your basement? Or have ya just been too nervous or tired of late dat ya feel like ya gotta try our new line of black lotus narcotics to function on a daily basis? Take my word fer it – ya’ll feel so good aftah ya try it, ya’d swear it was illegal! Allegedly addictive! Or is it extra labour ya be lookin’ fer – night elves and hoomie ladies fer ya harem, dwarfies and ogres fer yer menial labour, worgen fer yer freak shows and circus.

What evah it is, da Modas il Toralar got it! Come on down to our Undermarket, and we’ll hook ya up with what ya need fer a bargain price dat ya won’t find anywhere else on Azeroth, Draenor, or elsewhere! We got weapons – biological, magical and mundane – slaves, drugs, liquors, all kinds of services ranging from assassination contracts to da kinda stuff ya wife would nevah agree to, and much, much more! And for a limited time only, buy one barrel of Forsaken Blight, and get another free! Das right – a second barrel of Forsaken Blight ABSOLUTELY FREE!

And if it ain’ product ya be lookin’ fer, but ya be offerin’ services, da Modas il Toralar be hirin’ RIGHT NOW! If ya got experience smugglin’, or arms or drug traffcikin’, we got ya dream job in da Undermarket!

Da Undermarket: accessible by portal from da Cleft of Shadows in Orgrimmar or Magic Quarter in da Undercity, and soon to be many more convenient locations!

My name be Dree’jin, and I give ya my solemn promise dat if ya experience occasional bouts of antisocial personality disorder, den yer gonna walk out of da Undermarket happier den ya been in years! I guarantee nobody walks out of da Undermarket disappointed! Nobody!

Da Undermarket, da Modas il Toralar’s official place of business: enabling everyone on Azeroth to kill each other more efficiently for five glorious years and counting! We hope to see you there!

Please note: the Modas il Toralar is not responsible for any legal conflicts arising from the purchase or subsequent sale of any product obtained from the Undermarket. Please refer to local laws and regulations regarding the proper use of obtained products. Refunds to be considered on a case by case basis. It is strongly recommended that those of a morale or ethical nature consider an alternate place of business.

Drakehide sighs as he stands at the mailbox, about to drop a plain envelope inside. This was not originally what he had planned. When he had heard initially that slaves were being kept in the Undermarket, it had troubled him deeply. Drakehide was not one who solved his problems with words, or with thought. He solved his problems by the sword, by tests of strength and endurance, by one's conviction in battle.

But...Modas il Toralar were not his enemies. The work that they did was work for the Horde, and there was honor in them. The son of Rukh was walking a sword's edge - as many of his colleagues prepared more radical solutions to the Undermarket's trade of slaves, solutions that bordered on the outright treasonous, Drakehide hoped desperately that the situation might be disarmed through dialogue, and that diplomacy might prevail.

This new issue carried with it the promise for a whole new level of open warfare, between the Alliance and the Horde. When the Alliance learned of this, their retaliation would be swift. It would be a great battle - a GLORIOUS battle - but, when it was done, the son of Rukh feared many of his friends, his allies, and his loved ones would be dead, lost forever.

I did not survive torture at Tyrynna's hands only to watch my home destroy itself.

He cast the message inside. Soon, it would arrive, and would be brought to the attention of the Modas il Toralar. Silently, he prayed to the spirits that this situation would be resolved soon, without the need for a single shot fired, on either side. Resolved, before it consumed them all in flames...

The note, contained inside the plain white envelope, is as follows:

To Whom It May Concern, among the Modas il Toralar

Throm'ka. My name is Drakehide. As a warrior have fought many battles for the Horde, and my clan, in its time, has delivered many Alliance scalps to our Warchief. The name of your organization is known to me, as are your deeds. In many respects, I feel it necessary to state that I applaud you - while I may not always agree with your methods, your purpose in defending the Horde's claim to its lands and keeping its enemies at bay is honorable. The safety of our homes and families is testament to your success. For this, Modas il Toralar, I honor you.

I write to you as this alone - a friend. An ally, concerned by troubling tidings. I write in the hopes that a rising storm might be averted, before it strikes, and brings down fire and disaster on us all. It came to my attention recently that you have reopened the Undermarket. I do not condemn your trade in narcotics, or your trade in weapons, but I am alarmed by one other specific brand of stock in the Undermarket's lineup. Namely, the trade of live slaves.

Recently, I had the misfortune of being seized by the Alliance. During my captivity, though brief, I was tortured, stripped of my dignity, and shamed. It will take me some time to recover from the many disgraces of my capture and reclaim my honor, it granted me a special insight into a group we as warriors too-often overlook: the prisoners of war.

These slaves in your keeping...are they warriors? Honorable warriors? Say what you will of the Alliance, but I have always found that many of them - specifically the Kal'Dorei, the night elves - have always proven staunch, honorable opponents, and they fight well and die well. I can understand the use of slaves as laborers, provided the work is reasonable and they are treated with decency. The use of Alliance races as harem slaves, however, and worgen as sideshow acts is a dangerous gamble.

It will not take long for the Alliance to learn what is in place here. It has come to my attention that the Alliance's Pillar of Honor is aware that night elves are being kept as harem slaves, and they are most displeased. I would not be surprised if news spread in the near future to other groups - Pia Presidium and the Reclamation, for example, assuming they haven't heard already.

I do not fear the Alliance, do not misunderstand me. I have brought many of their hides before Hellscream and I will undoubtedly bring many more before I die.

But...giving the Alliance an excuse for a unified front and an honorable cause in their eyes creates the possibility, however unlikely, that they will bring fire and steel to our gates, and to our homes. It also creates the possibility that they will escalate their own treatment of prisoners of war. What I experienced in captivity was depraved enough - I would not care to see the Alliance's treatment of their prisoners descend any further into madness.

Let honorable warriors keep their honor. Let them die with some dignity left. If you must keep your slave trade, I implore you to abandon the sale of harem or sideshow slaves. Treat these captives with honor, decency. Perhaps if it comes to the Alliance's attention that their people are being treated with respect, they will be less quick to retaliate. Perhaps our actions will also inspire them to treat their prisoners with a little more respect as well.

My greatest hope is that no one else will suffer the indignity, torture, or loss of self that I experienced through my own captivity, at Alliance hands. For, truly, there can be no greater insult to an honorable warrior - Alliance or Horde alike.

I would be pleased to speak with you further in person, at the next possible opportunity. Again, I write to you a concerned friend, with only the greatest respect for you, and the work you do for the Horde.

Aka'Magosh - spirit's blessings be with you all.

- Drakehide, son of Mok'Rukh
