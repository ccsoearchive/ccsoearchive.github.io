whats a ddos


A DDOS is when someone with a LOT of computers (usually infected with Malware to do their bidding) send a bad request to one of our servers. When your computer talks to someone on the internet, it sends bytes of data. A byte has something in it that tells a computer where the byte starts and ends. People who DDOS send bad bytes, that have had their data corrupted.

08/18/2015 10:19 PMPosted by Freakaleek

They are saying its a DDoS against their game services, so I am guessing maybe someone figured out the ip address and port numbers that the game services are running on and launched the attack.


Kind of. We both publish our IPs for performing things like traceroutes and pathpings, but there are also ways to figure out what IPs your computer is talking to while a connection is open. We have a our ways to mitigate bad requests to our addresses, but it does take time to get them in place and then properly configure them to address specific attacks. To protect our processes though, I can't go into more detail on that.
