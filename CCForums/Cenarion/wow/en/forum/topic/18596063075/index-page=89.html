So, a couple things.

Blizzard isn't dumb - they're here to make money and they do a reasonably good job at that. While I'm sure they have their share of management reality <> IT reality disconnects, a DDoS attack knocking (by my last count) 56 realms offline repeatedly is a big business problem and it's unrealistic to pretend they're going to sit back on their heels and pretend they don't need to do something.

If DDoS attacks were easily solvable and preventable across the board, they wouldn't be an issue for anyone. Not only do you need to deal with your own code and infrastructure (which is basically guaranteed to have bugs), but everything that ever connects to you (and in many cases the basic infrastructure of the Internet can be turned against you without too much work). Amazon and Google have massively distributed networks, which adds a lot of durability, and they (can afford to) sink a lot of money into researching how to deal with systems failures and attacks (ChaosMonkey is a good example of something interesting that came out of researching failure).

Software is also a moving target. Every patch no matter how well tested is likely to introduce some new unexpected behavior or vulnerabilities. Tech does what it can in the time it's allowed to mitigate this but you can only do so much. :(

For the interested, CloudFlare has a (slightly technical) overview-explanation of some types of DoS attacks here: https://www.cloudflare.com/ddos

Humans are demonstrably terrible at writing software, which as a result often has unexpected behavior that only shows up under certain conditions (like when the system is massively overloaded). In theory you might be able to exploit a race condition by massively over-working routers or other servers during a DoS attack to break into something specific (say an account server) and harvest information that is profitable like credit card information. I expect the break between the WoW realm servers and the Battle.net account systems makes this a moot point in this instance, but simple theft for profit is a motivation.

Or it could be simple unethical exploration. Or malice. :(
