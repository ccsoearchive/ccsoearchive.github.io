Everyone looking at the norsemap and speculating on where these attacks are coming from keep one thing in mind, its not really that hard to re-route an IP address to make it look like it came from somewhere else. Proxy servers, VPNS, or just some good old fashioned hacking can accomplish this without even breaking a sweat. If they are really good they can bounce signals off 30 different countries before you could even attempt to trace it. The very nature of a DDoS attack makes it difficult to pinpoint a country of origin, which is why it takes a while to fix these things. Sony and Xbox were down for nearly a month each when they were hit with massive DDoS attacks. Yes, we have learned a lot about mitigating these attacks since then, however these hackers are like hydra. Cut off one head and 2 more grow in its place. The only way to completely guarantee prevention of a DDoS, or any other intrusion, is to stay off the interwebs completely, and since that kind nullifies the MMO part of the game, its not really an option. The fact that something of this scale rarely happens shows that Blizzard does take security seriously. I know ive said it before, but thank you Blizz.

On a not completely unrelated side note, I dont think im alone when i say that Glaxigrav has done an amazing job tonight. Not just in keeping us updated, but also in dealing with peoples frustrations, staying positive, and trying to be lighthearted. Most times people only report bad things to employers, and positive feedback doesnt occur nearly as often as it should. Glax linked his feedback survey in one of his posts, and I think everyone who agrees with me should take advantage of the WoW downtime and shoot blizzard a note letting them know how well he's done. It doesnt take long and he deserves a pat on the back for dealing with the children and trolls as well as he has.
