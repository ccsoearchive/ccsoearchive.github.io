There's quite a few reasons why the old AV didn't work out too well, and probably still wouldn't, but say we thought it was an awesome idea...

Development time on reimplementing everything could be better spent on new and already scheduled content, such as the new battleground/arena/etc.

Would you rather have old AV and lose a siege weapon? Lessen how robust the new battleground could be? Have one less Northrend city and associated quests/rewards? Have a new or updated holiday be scratched? That's how it works. Even with a development team as large as ours there is a finite number of resources. Adding development time on something new/else means that the scheduled content is either delayed or just won't ever make it in.

In any case, Lake Wintergrasp in Northrend is intended to be the huge, epic, never ending battle


so all the development time it took to bring Lake Wintergrasp...and all of its patches and hotfixes...it now sits idle...

exactley in the same state as how much other content? that took how much development time from the 'finite' resources.?

but then the motivation for the allocation of these 'finite' development resources has nothing to do with bringing any level of satisfaction to your current customer base...it is motivated by the need to expand the existing customer base and increase revenue and profit margin(s).

wasted...absolutely wasted resources...

AV is crippled...but still rolling...wintergrasp is dead, you could delete it and noone would notice. you could have revamped AV back then and it would be alive and well, word of mouth advertising between friends about how much fun they had in an epic AV battle from the night before...

wintergrasp is generating...nothing...and friends are talking amongst themselves about some other game...from a different company...

ya, i would rather you made the decision to scrap wintergrasp and revamp AV back to its original form...

Though I've not played the AV of yore,

And Blizzard will say: "You just didn't ask!"

Foury, your post has made me want to yet again delete all my toons, switch factions and servers. Just so I can /salute you in honesty.

As the oldest player of WoW in my office, having spent hundreds of dollars on character transfers for myself and my wife, having bought well over half a dozen copies of the game over time for various enthusiastic hobbies, I constantly strum on about how great this BG was at one time.

Some understand, either from experience or perspective, others just gape in awe. Every time I queue for a battleground, every time I renew an account, every time I log onto the game it is in memory of this BG and the days of playing it.

Every character of level had their mailbox overflowing with excessive marks for this BG. This BG is the scale I weigh all other BG experiences. The releases since, in this game and others, seem to suggest it was awesome by accident, and not by design.

Restoring this BG is the one gift I would ask Blizzard could give to an avid fan of the history of Warcraft before it became a vending machine.
