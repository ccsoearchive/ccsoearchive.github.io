I just got done playing WSG with 8 Hunters.

I'm not asking for much blizzard; Anyone can reason with 3 Hunters, 3 rogues, 3 paladins; that's not too overpowering; but to have 8 Hunters in a single team running around in midfield is the saddest thing in the world to watch.

Put a class cap on battlegrounds. Limit each number of classes allowed in a single BG by 1/3 of the max number of players allowed in the bg.

WSG(10 players): 3 Max of the same class.
AB(15 players): 5 max of the same class.
EotS(15 players): 5 Max of the same class.

Etc etc.


Obviously this rule wouldn't apply to Wargames and Rated Battlegrounds, but even if this ruins the que times and how many games are going at once; it's not asking for much after playing a game with 80% of the team is just hunters. You wouldn't even need to nerf/buff any classes, just fix the number of classes that can be in a single team and that will solve everything.

You can be the best player in the game, but there is no way you can expect a PuG to beat a team of 8 hunters.

Give us a break from the class stacking.


Heck I'd even settle for Limiting it to no more than 1/5 of the same class in a single BG. But that would ruin BGs for everyone.


This kind of change would be helping more than it would be ruining battlegrounds. and Like i said this would not apply to RBGs or Wargames(since those are not PuGd games). But please; end this chaos. Hunters/rogues/paladins will complain about the longer que times for BGs, but hey; if they want a faster que then roll a class that isn't overplayed.




This can be exactly like Dungeon ques as well, Tank/Healers get faster ques because they are not as popular as a DPS role. That being sad, they get faster ques than DPS. DPS get longer ques because they are playing a role that is OVERPLAYED.

That same rule needs to be applied to Battlegrounds. People who choose to pick a class that is overplayed should have to sit in que longer(only if that class is currently overplayed) while others who dont pick the classes that are overplayed; will not have to wait as long.

Not only will the class-ratio be balanced, but the games will be a lot less chaotic as far as class-stacking goes.


EDIT: Another thing I would like to add: Not that this was ever an issue for me personally, but lately I've been seeing people multiboxing and reading complaints about multiboxing; this is the cure to that. Obvious it's super easy to beat a multiboxer(kill the one they are all following); but the only issue with that is they all focus fire the same person. I'm not even sure if blizzard has been trying to figure out a way to stop multiboxers or if it's even on their radar; but if blizzard is trying to figure out a way to stop multiboxers then this would be the way to do it.
