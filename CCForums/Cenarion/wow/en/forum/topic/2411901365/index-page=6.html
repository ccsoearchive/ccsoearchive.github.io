So you /who the zone? If you know they are there and suspect they are spying, and have no control over what they do with the information they gather, the chances of changing how you act ICly are dangerously high (even subconciously).

I completely understand if you want to have control over your events and storylines, but if you cannot accept some level of outside influence when you're rping in the open, then I suggest you make your storylines closed so they pan out the way you want. I understand you have had events ruined in the past by random and crappy rp charging in and screwing it all up. It's unfortunate and sad that it happened but it doesn't mean that anyone should have to tell someone they're being watched.

It works two ways and that should go without saying. Only the most anal and hardcore rpers can completely cut meta-gaming out completely (not even sure they can because of the mechanics of the game). If I have a contract to spy on someone, I will check to see if they're online because I'm not going to travel around to places they might be if there is zero chance of them being there. This is meta-gaming on a small level but I don't think it harms anything if I am very strict about IC/OOC information. If they are online and I have IC knowledge of where they have been seen before, or like to hang out, I'll check those locations out. If I'm by myself I'll do it in a random order, if I have others to help me I'll scatter them about to check all the locations quicker.

If I happen to run into OOC knowledge that someone is spying on me, then I do my damndest to make sure that doesn't influence the RP. I've had my character leak vital information, get ambushed several times, and all other sorts of bad things happen despite the fact that I KNEW OOCly how to prevent it (stop talking, leave, magically spin around and out the spy that I could see with my camera way behind my character etc).

Open rp has some major risk involved in it. With that risk can come great rewards, surprising twists and unforseen consequences. This is why we strive to not meta-game. No one is perfect, but I think we can all agree that it's worth striving to do our best.

I would like to make it clear before the parade of white forum knights rides to battle, that I appreciate this thread and think it has some good information in it. I support Dala's guild and the active and interesting RP they bring to our community. What I posted above is simply my opinion. The entire point of being a good spy is that the target has no clue that you're there. ICly for obvious reasons, and OOCly to prevent meta-gaming (intentional or unintentional).
