What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Poisons should probably be an ability, that's really it. Annoying, but nothing gigantic.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Combat and Mutilate are pretty different, in general the gameplay between both is pretty fun in PvE. Mutilate is about maximizing envenom uptime, pretty easy rotation, big AoE numbers is fun. Cleaving as combat is fun, AR is a really good cooldown.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Killing Spree. Seriously. On demand burst and target swapping is pretty bad compared to every other melee.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Mutilate is a little boring, although it's nice to be out of the days of hunger for blood.

Combat cooldown management is good given restless blades and it's relation to gear level. Bandit's Guile is a little underwhelming, perhaps making it interact with KS in some way would be interesting (instead of 'when deep insight, hit the button).
Energy capping inside AR is bad, there needs to be some way to dump energy or make the 'energy regen' portion of the cooldown actually mean something.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

The only legitimate thing PvE rogues even want is a redesign to killing spree. It's bad. There is nothing fun about losing control of your character for 2 seconds as part of a 31 point talent. It also doesn't do that much damage over the course of a boss encounter. I mean, it's meant to be burst and 'on demand', but it's still a bit underwhelming considering.

I realize the difficult part is maintaining the 'fun' aspect of the ability while making it considerably better. Here's just an idea:

Channeled, 2 seconds, can move your character and perform actions that are not combo generators/finishers (burst would be too high if you could). Attacks three enemies in a cone in front of you during the duration (similar to hammer of the righteous), still affected by blade flurry. Includes a graphic that shows the cone of 'knives' or swords or whatever in front of you so you can aim it accordingly. Does more damage than it does now to a single enemy.

Purpose of redesign: Give combat rogues more on demand cleave burst, ALLOW FOR MOVEMENT considering the nature of boss mechanics this expansion, be better for a 31pt talent.

What spells do you use the least?

Expose Armor and Deadly Throw, but they have PvP uses so it's not like I don't have them bound :p

What makes playing your class more fun?
On my rogue, when I get the sense that as assassination or subtlety that my toon really is intended to hit my enemy where they are weakest, and is not just supposed to antagonize healers, or do insane amounts of dps.

More directly, when I use different abilities or time my abilities differently depending on how the situation changes in a single environment. Example, it is nice to use backstab as assassination on mobs below 35%; there should be more abilities or situational changes to rotation depending the state of your toon, and the state of the enemy.

What makes playing your class less fun?
A stale rotation that hardly changes, except to occasionally be on interrupt detail, or when the boss is in execute stage. Or for PVP, to rely on stunlocks or be forced into futile games of tag with casters and healers with very limited means of chasing them down, or forcing them to close the distance themselves.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
For assassination, there is too little to do for too long on fights where I don't have to move around too much, and not enough time to build up on high mobility fights. For sustained dps fights, the only choice I need to make is how to time my envenom so that I get the most out of rupture and not let slice and dice fall off. There should be a reason to use abilities like Expose Armor, Eviscerate, Deadly Throw, sinister strike, etc in every tree, but that the conditions under which they should be used be different as each tree should change their use. These are abilities I forget I have in PVE as assassination because they have no relevance in that environment, with the rare exception of Expose Armor.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1. More abilities, especially ones that can be used while stealthed, or reliant on having been in stealth recently. And more ways of utilizing stealth in PVE.
2. A greater means of benefitting the group I am in, and not just being reliant on high dps. Considering that the dps between pure dps classes and hybrid classes are so close, that rogues are the only pure melee dps class, and that they have limited and situational survival mechanics, makes most rogues the cog in the wheel of any raid. There are enough equivilents of heroism, auras, etc. why not introducing specific debuffs to an enemy on a long cooldown that other classes can't do more easily.
3. More uses for abilities such as sinister strike, eviscerate, deadly throw etc. As assassination, they have almost no use, ever. By and large, whether I am playing my shadowpriest, fury warrior, resto druid, or rogue, I have so few abilities that there is rarely a decision to be made about how to approach or leave a given situation.
4. That my class have fewer shiney or glowing pieces of gear. That their gear be less bulky and more sleek, dark, and resembling someone who doesn't want to be seen. As two out of three specializations of the class are geared toward making them assassins, spies, etc, their gear should look as if utility and functionality take priority over epicness. Even as combat, it should be visually clear that the rogue as swashbuckler places more of an emphasis on their skill and adaptability rather than what they are wearing. A lot would be gained if the rogue were the visual and functional antithesis of the warrior, as the ninja were to the samurai in Feudal Japan.
5. A greater variety or functionality of poisons, as they are the signature tool of the rogue in every specialization.

What spells do you use the least?

1. Eviscerate, sinister strike, deadly throw, expose armor, shiv, combat readiness (since it shares a cd with cloak of shadows), pickpocket, distract.
