What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Poisons: Use DK, shaman, or glyph adjustment model. Set only 1 poison for damage. The specs can be used to modify poison damage (DoT, bonus effect, bonus damage, etc). Other poison could fill a buff or debuff function (attack speed redux, increased physical damage, etc).

Combo Points: Stick on the Rogue (adjust other abilities/damage to compensate). See paladin model.

Ranged Weapon: Should not hinder abilities (like FoK, etc). Rogue should feel comfortable using the best ranged item we have.

Killing spree: Needs adjustment or positional glyph for PvE. For a signature move to the spec, far too poor performance in raids. Puts you out of position, gets you killed, etc, etc.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What makes playing your class more fun?

Stealth, ninja feel (Vanish, Shadowstep, SAP/Blind, Smokebomb, Lockpick/Traps) . Class needs more of that showcased in all forms of content and abilities.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What makes playing your class less fun?

Positional requirements. PvE is not kind to these. Change "Backstab" to "Shank" and get rid of all position requirements on attacks. Most classes lost these. Hit table is enough to promote "behind when you can" positioning.

Combo points not sticking. Makes ramp up times and target swapping painful compared to playing other melee when combined w/ our other mechanics.

Having PvE functionality changed/nerfed due to PvP issues.

Feeling - "Why should we take a Rogue when a Hybrid melee character can do it better and bring more to the table?" This is largely why I just use my DK for melee DPS even though his primary purpose (for me) is tanking.

Feint/TotT being on the GCD.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

In general still far too much passive damage. Combo point system conveys a sense of build up to a big reward, but instead the bulk of damage is white/poison with small finishers or finishers that add to 'more' passive/DoT damage. -- Rogues can scale nicely with weapon damage, but they also don’t see that effect very easily on their abilities. The buttons I actually press should make up more of my damage than my autoattacks. Slice and Dice is a HUGE culprit here. Should probably be removed.

Masteries: All of them essentially read "adds more passive damage". No (managed) procs, gameplay, or rotational change added. Each spec should look and feel very different, both to the player and the observer (and that shouldn't be defined by just daggers). The difference between a combat rogue and an assassination rogue is technically there, but relatively non-existent compared to a mage being frost vs. fire or a warlock being affliction vs. demonology, Both in looks and rotations.

In specifics:
Assassination is in best place (though somewhat dull).

Combat - Bandit's Guile seems to clutter the play and Killing Spree has aforementioned issues. Also the energy capping when using all your special abilities is counter productive.

Sub has the most "rogue-like" tools, but the number of things to manage for PvE makes it a poor choice, especially considering non-sticky combo points. Shadow dance is too detrimental to UI as well, hence people just peg it as the "pvp spec".

Sticky combo points

More 'ninja' in all aspects. Where's the rogue's signature (army of the dead, paly wings, demon form, elemental, dual weild 2handers) eye catch ability? Could Shadowstep be the right choice for this?

More use of abilities like in TBC. (Locks/Traps returning to PvE, Pick Pocket having interesting returns, even if cost added back in other ways, etc)

Viable off-hands beside daggers. (And/or transmogrify allowing axe/mace/sword interchangeability -- Combat spec is oft considered the swashbuckler spec and people want that look back.)

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Raiding, specifically current content heroic raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

It's a mixed bag, I enjoy BG's the most, both rated and not. however it seems im doing more arena than BG due to logistics

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

Target swapping as assassination is very painful and difficult. In addition when I do switch to a new target, specifically an add, it usually dies before I'm able to do any meaningful damage because of how deadly poison stacking works coupled with the fact i need a rupture on the target before i can envenom (which itself takes time after the fact before it's effects full manifest)

In addition "burst" damage is some what minimal, I cant really do anything to sacrifice my long term damage for a short term gain of damage to kill a high priority target save from using a cooldown

What makes playing your class more fun?

Being able to use my "roguey" abilities, disarming traps in ICC and the traps in BWL and the shannox encounter is fun. Feeling like I'm systematically destroying my enemy as the fight goes on as assassination is fun, it feels like im a trained killer beating my opponent with my superior fighting skill and knowledge of anatomy. stealth is fun because well, we rolled a rogue to be sneaky =o

as a side note being forced into a dagger offhand as combat is lame, i want to be like illidan, not captain blackbeard. also before you even think about it, axeassins and mace rogues are even sillier (in short, more fast agility swords and slow agility swords please)

as sub it is a blast to be able to fly around the battle field with shadow step, i wouldn't go so far as to suggest shadowstep be baseline but it does help with the feeling of being a rogue

What makes playing your class less fun?

the combo point system doesn't feel fulfilling, since it scales linearly the benefits for doing a 5 point finisher and a 4 point finisher (specifically envenom but rupture as well) seem to be interchangeable. it feels like wasted potential when to be optimal i have to envenom if i have 4 points instead of pushing that 5th point not because it is the best decision to envenom at 4 points at that time, but because its the best decision all the time and it is confusing to new rogues when i have to explain why they need to envenom at 4 points and not 5. rupture on the other hand is so powerful (thank god its finally useful) that the accepted method is to maintain a 100% uptime on it at all costs, even if its a 1 point rupture, but that if you can get it higher then that's better. that's confusing to new rogues as well, especially when i have to explain it to them

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

the rupture change (venomous wounds) was a great change for assassination but it still feels like its missing something. it would be more interesting if we could add things like backstabs and perhaps another finisher to the mix, perhaps something with expose armor becuase tht would fit assassin's theme

on another note, murderous intent, it's fun when it is active becuase it feels like ive gone into a focused state where im much more effective at killing, the game speeds up and you just hammer away at the enemy with backstabs till it is dead. that is fun. however what is not fun is when i cant do this until 35% or lower, which means on a progression fight where we are lucky to get something to even 50% for a few nights im out in the cold unable to use 2 of my talent points. it would be better if it was some kind of mode that triggers and we had to react to it (it would need to last a while tho, to be able to actually capitalize on it)

What’s on your wish list for your class?

poison vial, just like that rogue boss in trial of the champion, targeted aoe that cracks a bottle of poison onto the ground.
the anatomically correct cheap shot.
some kind of scorpion wire that could be used to trip, "death grip", entangle or something along those lines
more support for thrown weapons (more abilities and a reason to use those instead of bows xbows and guns)

What spells do you use the least?
shiv, gouge (in pve at least) expose armor, combat readiness(in pve), smoke bomb (in pve)

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Poisons: Needing to dedicate 4+ bag slots to the variety of poisons and having a duration. If my poisons fall of as assassination my dps drops from 17k to 4k and I need 6 sec to reapply them.
Target swapping: As assassination I do incredible if there are five or more mobs but do incredibly poorly if there is a need to CC and take out trash one at a time. Allowing combo point generation on the rogue could help to alleviate this problem, and it is a problem when the tanks can pull more dps then I can.
Hit box size: On some mobs I feel as if I can stand as far from the boss as ranged while on others I have to hide myself inside the graphics of the boss to be able to hit them.
Positional requirements: Abilities which require being behind an opponent, such as Backstab, are tricky in PvP, due to network latency and how some players are always mouse turning very rapidly.
Stealth and Vanish not working: with most of our burst abilities and control tied into stealth the loss of it through some random bug that has yet to be aknowleged is frustrating.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Control through stuns and blind. And of course stealth.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Not being able to solo a lot of the older content. The very long ramp up times on trash.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
As assassination for pve I love my rotation. I wouldn't change a thing.
As assassination for arenas it really does need more mobility and or less reliance on dispels.
As sub in arena I also love it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
I would love to have less ramp up time on trash mobs. Cloak and combat readiness not share the same CD. Be able to remain behind every boss in the game for the majority of the fight. Less difference in damage if I must fight face to face.

Expose armor and Deadly throw.

It just seems like we would be next in line for a minor quality of life buff, seeing as our DPS is so heavily dependent on our poisons, Assassination especially, and such a minor and honest mistake can severely hinder our performance.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    Finesse, what keeps me playing a rogue is not only the fast paced game play, but also the ability to control the flow of combat and dance circles around our opponent. This, I feel, is what really separates us from the other melee classes.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Positional Requirements, this isn’t a terrible thing to deal with, but can become exceedingly annoying against moderately skilled players. The Hemorrhage buff was a step in the right direction and is in fact quite affective in some cases, but having our ability to apply pressure to a target depend on both our ability to stand near a target and get behind it seems like a major disadvantage. This is, of course, a PVP focused issue, but also has repercussions in PVE. It is an extreme issue when an already limited spec is also limited by whether or not they can position themselves behind the boss. All rogues need to get behind the boss to maximize damage, but subtlety gets severely punished on fights where it’s difficult to do so. I would not propose that moves like backstab be removed, they add needed flavor to the rogue class as a whole, but I would suggest that the advantage of attacking an opponent from behind be shifted away from damage and more toward tactical advantages like Waylay.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    Assassination’s rotation, in my opinion, is an incredibly fun rotation. Some would argue otherwise, but only because on the surface it is terribly easy. What I like about it though is that it is easy to pick up, but it also has enough minor intricacies to it that make it fun to play (Envenom clipping, Rupture rolling, etc). In contrast, I believe subtlety is “broken.” I don’t like using that word but it is the best way to describe it. A rotation should not take so much concentration to execute properly, note properly not perfectly, that it becomes difficult to focus on anything else. The basics of any good rotation should be easy enough that a new player can pick it up and be moderately effective. It is the little details of a rotation that should make it challenging.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Baseline Shadowstep……I kid. But, Assassination and Combat could use some mobility. Subtlety is a blast to play, but the other two have some interesting mechanics that they could bring to the PVP table if they only had the ability to stay on target. They, of course, have some other issues keeping them out of the PVP scene, namely combat’s horrendous ramp up, but mobility would at least be a step in the right direction. Also, I personally haven’t touched combat in a while, but I’m sure our swashbuckling friends would appreciate the ability to transmogrify their off-hand daggers into something more suiting to their more straightforward nature. Oh, and swirly ball please :).

    What spells do you use the least?
    Expose Armor, I almost looked up the name because I couldn’t remember it. I don’t think I have had this bound or even used this spell in my career as a rogue. However, since I think this will be every rogue’s answer I suppose I’ll say my second. Cheapshot, only because the DR changes have made it ridiculously situational. It would be nice to see some use out of it again other than stopping some jump turner so Shadow Dance isn't wasted.

    P.S. I see a lot of rogues putting things like distract and pick pocket here. Those kinds of spells are the fun kind of situational; they are rarely used, but are fun tools to have when the situation calls for it.
