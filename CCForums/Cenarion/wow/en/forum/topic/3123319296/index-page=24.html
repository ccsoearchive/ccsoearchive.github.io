What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Maybe poisons, combo points and ramp up time.

Though, I would not like it if poisons were done away with. I find the poisons mechanics to be a good aspect of the game that helps to make the rogue class stand apart from the others. It is nice to be able to select from a range of effects be it a tactic choice to a straight DPS gain, to apply to your weapons. Poisons taking up bag space is a minor problem, the duration of poisons is something I feel could be extended.

I concur with the other rogue players in the thought of switching targets is a waste of combo points - losing those established on your previous target, having to restart the combo point building over again on your new target. Redirect helped with this to some degree, but is it enough?

Ramp up time for assassination rogues can be a bit slow once the Overkill buff has faded in prolonged fights. Combined with switching targets and having to restart the combo point building it can get frustrating at times.


What makes playing your class more fun?

Stealth, and the all the feel to the class that comes with it, being the sneaky saboteur, or elusive assassin. Quests in which there is a specific mob to be killed it is great being a Rogue because you can infiltrate the location of the target, sneak pass the other mobs and ambush the mark, ninja assassin style. Vanish. Ghost. The elusive shadow. In and out, like a half remembered dream.

One of the prime reasons I enjoy playing a rogue stems from the teachings of Sun Tzu
in The Art of War. There is a common undertone outlined in The Art of War, that teaches warfare is deception. For me playing a rogue is more than just a game mechanics choice; it is a philosophical one. To be the assassin both in the enemies’ mind and on the battlefield. The stealth mechanic combined with the class’ technical variation in abilities is excellent for this.

“O divine art of subtlety and secrecy! Through you we learn to be invisible, through you inaudible; and hence we can hold the enemy’s fate in our hands.” - Sun Tzu

“Let your plans be dark and impenetrable as night, and when you move, fall like a thunderbolt.” - Sun Tzu.

"Deception is the point! Any fool can calculate strength. That one has been doing it since we arrived. Now he has to calculate what he can't see." - Herger the Joyous

How can one strike against what one can not see? How can one make ready for what they can not predict? The stealth mechanic allows for time to plot and lay in wait for an opportunity to present itself. As a rogue I am able to choose my fights in battle, picking off weaker combatants & those separated from others. Stealth allows me the time for selecting keystones that will cause the enemy to crumble when removed; killing healers or keeping a stronger opponent from joining their comrades in a fight at an objective by keeping them distracted or controlled.

“Therefore the clever combatant imposes his will on the enemy, but does not allow the enemy’s will to be imposed on him.” - Sun Tzu

Sap, Cheap Shot, Blind, Gouge, Kidney Shot, Dismantle, Kick, Distract, even Cloak of Shadows; all forms of control or deflecting the enemies will over you. I find that Rogue abilities to control the opponent are a great example of Sun Tzu’s teaching. In 2v2 arenas I have found that control is more important than dishing damage. If you can control the fight, you ensure victory. Have the enemy believe their decisions are their own, make them use their cooldowns while preserving your own resources, then move in for the kill.

The class mechanics for rogues makes for the class most inclined to execute these teachings within WoW. This is what makes the class fun for me. If there were anyway to improve upon these ideas it would be giving Rogues more utility and ability in the future to execute such slight of hand and deceptive tactics.


What makes playing your class less fun?

Frost mages. Being energy starved as an assassination rogue in prolonged fights.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I do not feel strongly about my primary spec rotation one way or another. I like how it plays out for the most part. So long as there is not much target switching mid-fight and I can get back into stealth every now and again to reacquire the Overkill buff it works fine.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

More skills and abilities that exemplify deception, and of course cool “ninja” like capabilites.

One idea I have had before is for rogues to have the option, maybe a glyph, which when the rogue vanishes it would leave an attackable doppelganger copy of them left standing in the position where they vanished.

Maybe something that would allow for feigning being wounded, akin to a hunter’s feign death, but with the illusion of the rogue’s health pool being dangerously low complete with a wounded animation (although that would probably require for all classes to have a wound animation when their health is low for it to be viable).

What is a cool “ninja” capability? Well, like the minor glyph for sprint. It allows the rogue to run across water while sprint is active. That is awesome. More awesome things like that, inspired directly from ninja pop culture and/or movies with ninjas would be excellent. Like running up or briefly across walls. Leaping from precarious perches down onto unsuspecting victims. Hanging upside down from a ceiling. Use of grappling hooks? We have smoke bombs and blinding dust, what about a blow dart-gun to apply a poison or effect from stealth? Like a dart gun could be another form of CC useable from stealth other than Sap; maybe it could cause the target to fall asleep or apply an invisible debuff, or buff for the rogue against that target.

New options in poisons would be cool. Maybe a poison that when applied temporarily effects the target by sending them into a confused or berserk state causing them to attack friendly targets. Or a poison that inflicts a muddled state to the opponent’s mind causing the effected target to see multiple visions of the rogue, kind of like a mage’s mirror images, but only visible to those effected. For PvP in addition to this effect maybe have it temporarily cause a player’s screen to take on the blurred effect like when a character gets drunk in the game.

I wish so much for fist weapons to work for mutilate/assassination rogues in the same manner as daggers.

What spells do you use the least?

Shiv, Redirect, Expose Armor, Min-numbing poison, Crippling Poison, & Sinister Strike (I play primarily in Assassination specialization).

    What type of content do you focus on?
    PVE

    If PvE, what type of PvE?
    Raiding mostly, though also Heroics and I enjoy just questing as well

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
    I think the biggest one is how inconvient combo points can be when target switches are required (esp. frequent switches such as Majordomo's cat form). Redirect helps but it has such a long cooldown that it's almost a non-factor (plus its use is somewhat clunky). It would be really nice if combo points were tied to the Rogue and not the target.

    Others have covered poisons to death so I won't go into that.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    I love opportunities to sneak around - sneaking beyond mobs to kill a named quest mob, or to pick up a quest items. It's a lot of fun to feel like I have some control over when I get into a fight and to explore an area without fighting every single mob in my way.

    I also just love the fast-paced combat of the Rogue. It's fun to unleash a flurry of attacks on a mob and not have pauses in combat (i.e. cast times, etc).

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Overall, the fact that we are melee. It just feels like melee are kind of the pariahs of WoW - many fights have mechanics that encourage stacking ranged (or lazy deterrents like "the boss does a super ability if not enough are in melee range" which only encourage the bare minimum of melee). We suffer mobility issues, target switching issues, positioning issues (especially fights that prevent us from getting behind the boss to avoid parries, or require us to stand outside of melee range where we can do no damage) and in general, don't really bring anything to compensate for that other than sometimes maybe topping the dps meters if our gear is good enough and the current patch doesn't have us underperforming.

    Rogues in particular don't really bring any raid utility - we have a *few* things like Master Poisoner or Savage Combat, but these largely go unnoticed since they don't put a big buff icon at the top of the screen, and of course, other classes bring these bonuses too (and in particular, Expose Armor is such a DPS loss for us that it's better that other classes bring that).

    I've been playing a Rogue since the original Vanilla beta, so I've had the experience of being sat for the raid several times over those years, whether it be because Rogues were way behind during that raid tier, or we were a liability to the raid, or what have you. It sucks every time. I will say Cataclysm seems to be a bit better in this regard, but there is still definitely room for improvement.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”?
    Overall I'm ok with it (this is for Assassination at least). I don't really care for having to spam Tricks of the Trade every 30 seconds (esp. given the T12 4pc bonus) given the implications of dumping threat on a dps. There could be more interesting things to do, like a reason to press something different every once and awhile (I do like the addition of Backstab sub-35% at least), but it's overall ok.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Really I'd love to see something *new* to press in combat. It largely feels like we have been hitting the exact same buttons (if not necessarily in the same order) since TBC, and even many of those abilities just felt like "upgraded" versions of abilities we had (i.e. Envenom = fancy Eviscerate, Mutilate used to be a fancy Backstab/now maybe Sinister Strike). About the only real significant combat ability since then has been Fan of Knives which itself is only really used in AOE situations - the rest have mostly been support abilities like Tricks, Recuperate (which I love BTW!), etc. I'd love to see new CP generators or finishers - something different to press, maybe in harmony with Mutilate/Envenom depending on the situation.

    I'd also like to see some kind of tangible utility that we could bring to groups/raids - something that would make us desirable to bring along.
