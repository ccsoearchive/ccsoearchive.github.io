What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

As a Sublety Rogue, I accept the fact that my dps is going to be slightly behind, on some encounters, the dps of my fellow Combat and Assassination Rogues. I trade this dps for more mobility, a more complex rotation (That I find more enjoyable), and raid buffs.

However. The buff I bring, 5% crit. While nice, is shared by several classes. Rather popular classes. Give me something else, Something unique. Something that a raid leader will look at, and say "Damn. I'd like to find myself a Sub Rogue, that buff would really fill a hole."

Also - The fact that raid encounters are far more ranged friendly these days, is not helping rogues out. Don't take this as a QQ for nerfing - Take this as a - please stop making encounters that favor ranged dps over melee, make them balanced.

The last thing that irks me; I always get a bit envious of classes that don't have, a rotation per-say, instead they have a priority system. That sounds alot more enjoyable. Alot more difficult. More challenging. Instead I get to do the same. moves. over. and. over. Give me some chaos.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I really don't know. I enjoy my spec, not my class. Once upon a time I enjoyed all three specs, but now the other two are just boring.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Raid encounters that all but force me to be in front of the boss. I understand that I choose a spec that requires me to be behind the boss. However, thats one of the little challenges I enjoy about my spec. Positioning is something I'm always focusing on. However, we'll look at Majordomo - Yes, it is certainly possible for me to move in and out on the scorpion phase, however, a little lag spike, and I cause a big problem - hence why I spec combat for the fight, not because I enjoy it, but because its more practical.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?"

Its challenging, and I enjoy it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Unique raid buffs. Make TotT into something better - I mean, seriously. We BEGGED for that ability for years - Rogues QQ'd for so long that we didn't have any real raid utility, and you gave us an MD, and it was great. But then with the recent threat nerf, it's little more than a damage buff. Fine and dandy, but I feel that something like that takes something that we offer from the plate - Quid Pro Quo. If you're going to take something, give us something in return.

What spells do you use the least?
Shiv, Deadly Throw. Gouge, Garrote, Expose Armor (Thats been a joke for what, 6 years now?), Smoke Bomb,

What type of content do you focus on?

-Mostly PvE. My answers will be based on PvE unless pointed out otherwise.

If PvE, what type of PvE?

-I mostly do raids, but will do heroics when gear is needed. However, if levels were (ideally) randomly generated or had some other significant form of randomness i would enjoy running them more often.

If PvP, what type of PvP?

-PvP is mostly arena. I feel like the class is too 1v1 based to be able to risk getting caught by a group of players so battle grounds feel stacked against me.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

-The times i get frustrated is when poisons wear off my weapons in fights (my fault, i know... but sometimes i just get busy paying attention to other things). Also when a fight starts and everyone is beating on the boss and i'm still stealthing around to behind it.

What makes playing your class more fun?

-I like the feeling of uniqueness. Rogues can distract pathers that are about to agro the group to save from getting big adds. We can stealth up to mobs and walk all around them while everyone else has to be careful. We can Vanish and sometimes save the raid from a corpse run with mass rez. We can pick locks in dungeons to save the group a tiny bit of time (ok, this only happens in deadmines.. once). I love being able to go immune to spells from raid bosses that everyone else has to fear.

What makes playing your class less fun?

-I dislike that im disadvantaged for being a melee. I try to think of how you could make boss encounters to favor melee and its hard to come up with logical events. I dislike having abilities with positional requirements but the encounter makes it extremely difficult to use them.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

-I feel that the rotation for Assassination is comfortable. It takes some time to get used to and perfect, but isn't a big hassle or too simple. It's frustrating how dependent i am on the rotation because fight mechanics that make the fight fun and unique can really kill my dps which makes me not enjoy the fight very much cause im struggling to carry my weight.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

-I think a shadow step type of transportation for all specs could make things much more enjoyable. Considering all my damage is from melee range, it feels needed lots of the time.
-I would like to be able to sap and resap mobs that are in combat as long as they haven't hit or been hit by anybody
-I would like cheap shot to not work on a mob if it wont stun them so i can still use a different opener.
-I would like combat and assassination to feel more unique from each other and viable in pvp and Subtlety to be more viable in PvE so i can count on the spec for all fights in a dungeon.
- This is mostly for PvP but sometimes pertains to PvE. I would like it if vanish would make it so currently existing DoTs wouldn't break stealth for the rest of their duration. I hate having to use 2 cooldowns just to get out of the fight and utilize one of my main class attributes. I pretty much have to save cloak to go before vanish and with that, i cant use combat readiness.

What spells do you use the least?
-Shiv in PvE,
-Expose armor - Too costly compared to other classes
-Deadly Throw - If something is running away, i would rather save my points for getting my rotation going again or debuffing
-Smoke bomb in PvE. It's good for pulling in the ranged mobs to the tank but it's very situational.


EXTRA:
Ultimately, i love the Rogue class. I feel that it has more uniqueness than other classes but that uniqueness isn't much of an asset in the WoW community. I think if things were adjusted some, we would be able to stand out from the rest in certain situations that makes us more desirable and able to shine by showing personal attention to what we do. As it stands, i don't feel like i can excel over another class in any department...... except opening lock boxes. Damage is dependent on gear and player ability so i feel that is moot. We cant crowd control better (has to be out of combat, not have see invis guys around and we cant refresh it). We dont bring any substantial group buffs or items (mage food, health stones, etc).

Instances need to have parts were trivial mobs can be skipped by a rogue picking a lock, or sneaking up to a lever and opening a door for the group, or pickpocketing a certain key. Bosses should be able to be able to have some smaller abilities negated by rogues by smoke bomb or other abilities. I don't want to make the fights substantially easier, but i want to make an affect that the other party members will appreciate. It would make us help be a mitigation class without being a tank. Other classes have utility and i feel something like this wouldn't be outrageous and would only benefit other players.

I also miss weapon offhands as combat that are not dinky little daggers, the whole reason I play combat was to hit people with swords and axes and such, and I feel combat has lost that flavor in this expansion. # What makes playing your class more fun?

Bandits guile is also another band aid that really doesn't do us any favors..it forces us to sit on all our CDs until we can use them in a small window, then its like a chinese fire drill to get them all off in time.

We also have a huge toolbox of things we can use, unfortunately a lot of it is so limited to barely be useful 99% of the time. Of all the cata abilities we gained this exp pretty much most of them are more or less useless. I think a lot of the many abilities need to be removed or redesigned, as they just don't work or are dated and uselss. Although I would love to have the top dps class since that is what we used to be known for, I think a good buff or debuff would be huge in getting people playing rogues again after we pretty much disappeared after cata came out.

*What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Several people have mentioned poisons running out and the bag space they occupy as being an issue. I would have to agree with this as well. I feel that similar aspects to the game that can at times be an annoyance or inconvenience actually add to our overall experience. Requiring cloth to make bandaids, vials to make potions, requiring a skinning knife... I think it is a fine line between these things that make sense and keep things from being too simple and something like poisons or ammo. Running out of poisons is just a pain and can really hold things up. Poisons are a huge part of what rogues do but the process of applying them and having them drop off (sometimes unknowingly even after all this time) is just annoying. And not in an endearing way. To be honest, I think that the days of needing blinding powder for blind to work made more sense than keeping poisons in your bag and needing to reapply them when they suddenly need to be reapplied.
I want to stress that I would not want to see all the aspects of the game like this removed. Any long lasting relationship needs some inconvenience and annoyance ;)
Having said that, I also think that the poison vendors throughout the world and the areas they occupy help to define rogues as unique in the game and reinforce some of the key character concepts that help us remember our place and roles in the game.

*What makes playing your class more fun?

Stealth and vanish. PICKPOCKET and the fun, specific items we can grab out of this. Being the sneaky class everyone loves to hate. The selfish class with few raid utilities but big damage. Rogue's drought and lockboxes from pickpocketing.
I have really enjoyed the changes that came with cataclysm like the current talent point system and I have always enjoyed the glyphs and how they allow for us to customize, to some degree, our characters. I also appreciate that Sub is becoming more raid worthy for those worthy rogues who can master it.

*What makes playing your class less fun?

Not enough room to customize a viable spec with talent points and glyphs! As stated above, I love how much more we can do this with cataclysm, but I think being able to make decisions on our talents and glyphs that will ultimately affect our rotations and possibly make them a bit less standard would be fantastic.


*How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I am pleased with it currently (mutilate). I would like to see rotations a bit less "standard" based on our glyph/talent choices but I imagine this is difficult to balance and implement. I am very, very happy with the changes that cataclysm brought. It involves more than a few buttons thankfully and some thinking/awareness. It moves quickly and is fun to play. It can be simplified for those that find it too complex and still do fairly well.

*What’s on your wish list for your class?

I kind of feel like the "lurking in the shadows," swashbuckler aspect of the class is being lost and I would like to see the core values that make this class unique maintained. I think this is particularly important when it comes to the manner in which they are visually represented. While I love the variety of gear art that comes out, much of the recent tier art feels less rogue-ish and could pass as another class entirely. Big bright shoulders don't feel very sneaky! I really think this is more important to peoples experience in the game than they may admit. Most of us were hooked the moment the character creation screen came up and we picked a class.
I miss the old rogue specific quests and the ravenholt zone (i think its still there but not much use past a certain level). I would love to see more class specific content.


*What spells do you use the least?

deadly throw. gouge.
