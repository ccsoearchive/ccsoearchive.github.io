What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]:

BGs/Arenas

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

  • Poisons

    • - Stacking (Bags): Amount per stack should be 100. I do not mind carrying them, but please let me only have 1 stack per type of poison.
    - Duration: One hour duration could use an increase, perhaps two hours.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Stealth, controlling enemies, fluid combat.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Target Switching (PvE) - For all specs, switching targets, especially often, is not fun at all. Having to generate completely new combo points, restack poisons, and apply any other debuffs to the target is not fun. This is especially apparent in Heroics, many of which require DPS to be on "add duty". Rogue DPS suffers quite a bit from having to swap off of a boss to kill an add. It also does not feel rewarding to do so.

    Mobility Issues (PvP) - Assassination and Combat suffer from high mobility issues in organized PvP. Even with talents such as Improved Sprint, the amount of snares and roots in the game keeps a Rogue from having good uptime on a target. Being kited is not fun and does not feel rewarding.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”?

    Assassination - Slightly Complex. Because Mutilate can generate a different amount of combo points from Seal Fate, you can never know exactly how many points you will end up with. This wouldn't be a problem, but when you have 2 combo points, then Mutilate for another 2 combo points, it doesn't feel rewarding to waste a Mutilate on one more combo point. Because it is a DPS loss to Mutilate for the last combo point instead of using a 4 point Envenom, the design seems flawed. Having a mixed rotation is nice, but I should not have to glue my eyes to the combo point bar. Having the combo points that would have been wasted from a Mutilate roll over after the Envenom would feel more fluid and rewarding.

    Combat - Simple. While the rotation for Combat is simple, it is not fun. As someone who's played Combat since 2005, the rotation has not changed much, with the exception of Revealing Strike. Bandit's Guile makes target swapping feel unrewarding. Redirect is not available as often as I'd like it to be, even with Restless Blades. Having a rotation based on RNG (Glyph of Sinister Strike) is not appealing. Only using Revealing Strike if you're at 4 combo points is not fun either. Suggestion is to change Revealing Strike entirely. Allow it to only be used after a Sinister Strike, do more damage, and apply a stacking armor debuff. Remove Expose Armor completely. This way the rotation go from mashing Sinister Strike to trading off between both attacks. The stacking armor debuff would be a nice addition to the class.

    Subtlety - Very Complex. Keeping Slice and Dice and Recuperate up while trying to maintain Eviscerates to refresh Rupture is not fun. The rotation feels like a juggling act. In raids, this isn't as bad since Honor Among Thieves provides a healthy amount of extra combo points. In Heroics and PvP, it's very difficult to do all of this. Subtlety also suffers the most from having to target swap, since you have to get a brand new Rupture up while maintaining your other buffs.

    (cont)

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Combo Points

    - Number of maximum combo points changed to 4. This would make all specs feel a little more fluid, allows for more breathing room in maintaining buffs like Slice and Dice and Recuperate, and makes target switching less of a pain. (Try it in your head...1 2 3 4 *SMASH*)
    - Combo points allowed on more than one target at a time. It would be extremely fun, and rewarding, for encounters where multiple NPCs need to die at the same time. Being able to quickly swap targets and stack up your combo points on each one feels much more rewarding than having to back off on your DPS because your NPC is dying too fast.

    Energy

    - Rogue's need more ways to generate Energy. There are many times in both PvP and PvE where I feel energy starved or am just shy on enough energy to interrupt an ability. Combat Potency is a great talent for Combat to increase their generation. I would consider making this a baseline passive, though changing it to be Proc Per Minute based.

    Off-hand Weapons (Combat)

    - From 2005-2010 I enjoyed being able to use two swords/maces/axes for my Combat spec. I enjoyed having the cosmetic distinction between the other specs. Having a big sword in one hand with a little dagger in the other feels off for the class. Please consider adding more fast Agility off-hands (1.4 speed) OR make Combat Potency Proc Per Minute so that I can use slow weapons in both hands.

    Killing Spree

    - This ability causes a lot of problems, like trying to use it on Magmaw and falling into the lava, etc. I think it would be interesting to make it like the Stoneclaw Flayers in The Stonecore when they use their Flay ability. Have it root you in place and do a cone of slashes in front of you for a 2 second duration. This rewards players for lining up multiple mobs in the cone, and provides synergy by allowing players to Kidney Shot the target prior to using it so they can ensure the target takes full damage.

    What spells do you use the least?

    Expose Armor - Waste of Combo Points when other classes can apply this for a lower DPS loss.

    Shiv - Only using to remove Enrages makes me feel like I waste a keybind.

    Deadly Throw - Used to use it all the time in PvP for interrupts. Combat is not viable for PvP otherwise I might still use it.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    PvE

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

    Heroic raids.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    None

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    I love the little things rogues can do to eek out as much damage and utility as possible, in the right hands and the right situation, few classes can have as much control. The fairly high skill cap also does a very good job of separating the good from the very skilled in PvE and PvP.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Combat's ramp-up time, it really is an issue on any fight with a target switch that happens more often than redirect is available. Without redirect, you're losing
    - 10 seconds of 5 stack deadly poison ticks.
    - 5 applications of instant poison when you're re-stacking DP on the new target.
    - Combo points, without redirect these should go into refreshing slice and dice, but it still does lose effective damage.
    On top of all of the above, you can lose up to 30% damage just from bandits guile.

    Even in the bast case scenario, with bandit's guile, you're still losing a large chunk of damage from poisons. It's a bit much, combat's ramp up time is by far the slowest for all rogue specs.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    I have several top 100 WoL parses for each spec, so I feel that I know them all fairly well.
    I love the rotation for both assassination and subtlety, although for all specs I do feel like the cooldown on redirect is too high.

    I like combat a lot, the rotation is simple enough but with enough nuances that really separate the good rogues from the exceptional ones. There are a few things that I feel could be improved to make it feel less clunky.

    Adrenaline Rush is a great cooldown, however because of how combat stacks haste, energy capping is impossible to avoid. In theory as long as you're using a damage ability every GCD you're not losing anything from energy capping, however energy is still being wasted every time you use your best ability. With rising levels of mastery, haste and hit, all of which increase energy gain, this problem will only get worse. Also during AR rogues are severly punished for using any of their utility, such as tricks to another dps and feint to reduce damage; using any ability that does not cost energy will waste energy..

    Killing Spree has had issues since its introduction a few years ago, almost all of the time you can work around them, but there are way too many fights where killing spree is a hindrance and really needs to be planned and micromanaged. It really has many nasty tendencies, just to name an examples, on heroic Beth'tilac it can easily get you killed by porting you too close to a tank, so that you take damage from their widow's kiss. Most of the time, these situations can be avoided but it is a bit of a nuisance to need to plan raid positioning based on this ability.
    On top of all of this, the fact that it jumps to another enemy if it is in range seems counter productive, since you only have bandit's guile stacked on your current target, every jump off of this target only losses you damage from both bandit's guile and poison applications.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Adrenaline Rush, Feint, Tricks of the Trade, and Redirect taken off of the GCD.

    To avoid energy capping issues during Adrenaline Rush, increase both the energy cost and damage of Sinister Strike, Revealing Strike, Rupture, and Eviscerate while Adrenaline Rush is active.
    Note: I do not think AR would be made overly powerful this way, I think it could be balanced so that has the same amount of damage increase that you SHOULD get if you were able to use all of your energy during AR.

    Make Combat Readiness equal to cloak. As in, make it viable to use in any situation, as it is currently, its just a button that gives you a fun visual effect in exchange for putting a useful ability on cooldown.. I don't know the best way to go about it, possibly remove the ramp up time?

    Rupture should do noticeably more damage than eviscerate. At the moment, the main reason rogues use it is for the lower energy cost. Completely ignoring one of our main finishers only costs the rogue a couple hundred dps.

    Bandit's Guile should be stacked on the rogue. It is simply too much of a damage loss if Redirect is on cooldown. I like the part of bandit's guile where you do increasing damage, it makes timing cooldowns more important. However as it currently works it severely limits rogues target changing abilities.

    What spells do you use the least?

    Combat Readiness

    Most of my time is spent in-raid as Combat. At the moment, I don't find any real issues with the rotation, though incorporating Revealing Strike took some getting used to (and sometimes not used at all, depending on Ruthlessness procs).

    Using Redirect/transferring Bandit's Guile is still clunky, as you can't always predict if you'll have enough time to use your 5CP in a finisher and get another CP on that target before it DIES from raid-crits. The cooldown for Redirect is also too long.

    I would very much like to see BG be a product of situational awareness rather than time-on-target awareness, ie. This additional effect to Smoke Bomb would encourage more use deliberation towards using it offensively or defensively.

    Not having unglyphed Poisons take 5 secs; the Glyphed time-to-apply should be standard. Also having poison duration buffed to 2-3 hours.

    Better AOE damage for Fan of Knives, or just better aoe-dmg in general.
    Less ramp-up time for Bandit's Guile.
    Friendly-target Shadowstep.

    Removed penalty from Glyphed Kick, or a lower Kick cd in general. Six seconds would be awesome, and put us on par with Shamans, but we'd still have to be within melee range.

    What spells do you use the least?

    From a PvE pov: Deadly Throw, Backstab, Gouge, Expose Armor
