■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PVE exclusively.

■If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Heroics and quests.

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Poisons are an annoyance and it's extremely embarrassing to forget them. But they're not a huge issue.

■What makes playing your class more fun?

The mobility. I love stealthing to get into perfect position to strike a boss, I love hitting sprint at just the right moment to escape a fire or get in place to make that last body slam take down a chain at the end of a ZG run. Stealth is awesome, but I think you can group it under the larger umbrella of "mobility" and still be correct.

■What makes playing your class less fun?

Feeling like I'm not contributing to an encounter. It's frustrating how your DPS and buffs fall off when you have to leave melee range.

■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For Assassination, it's great - fast paced, focused, masterable. Combat would be much better if Bandit's Guild ramped up fast enough to make it useful - I've almost never seen Deep insight.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Even more mobility. Seriously. The class is at its giddiest fun when I feel like I'm channeling Jackie Chan and engaged in a running, zipping around at top speed fight. I know that i'm doing the most damage when I'm staring at boss's posterior, but that doesn't mean I admire the view. I would love to have a version of the warrior's Heroic Leap, especially if I could macro it in with Vanish or Deadly Throw.

More debuffs or interrupts. I wouldn't mind losing DPS when I leave combat range, if I could debuff that boss as a lovely parting gift. A poison that deals 0 damage but debuffs a mob hard would be a way to do this; apply it to a thrown weapon and Deadly Throw/FoK regain some utility.

As time goes on, I've come to realize that a well-timed interrupt by a DPS is more important than being top of the damage meter; being able to contribute to a raid by shutting down a boss or applying "just in time" debuffs could be a way to feel potent without having to stay in a constant DPS race. (Which isn't to say we shouldn't have high DPS, but we can't all be #1.) Being able to (for example, number pulled out of my tush) cut a boss's attack power by 50% for 1 second would be a gem. It's also very rogue-ish; it could feel like a lock without actually being one (if you keep the window of opportunity small).

As TotT loses importance, I'd love to see the threat redirection be turned into a dodge bonus for the tank. TotT doesn't just help our threat; it helps the tank by giving him the threat he wants. Finding another way to gain synergy with the tank would be excellent, and a bonus to dodge or block would be superb. Any and all tank synergy would be good; it would be awesome if we became valued in a raid not just because we brought strong DPS but because we made the tank's job easier.

■What spells do you use the least?

Smoke bomb. Lovely idea, useless in PvE. Feint. Dismantle. Deadly Throw.

A quick intro before I answer the questions. I just want to let whoever is reading this to know that the following answers are from the perspective of a player with only about 2 months of game time to her name. I also want to thank you in advance for seriously looking at the players thoughts.

■ What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Solely PvE.

■ If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Mostly heroics, although I'm working up the courage to ask others to help me get raid experience. Also anything related to earning achievements.

■ If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

I personally dislike PvP. I only engage in it if its quest or achievement related - which is why I may begrudgingly start engaging in PvP activities in the future.

■ What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

1) Many others have brought up the issue of poisons. I don't have much problem with it except for those rare times when I'm in the middle of a boss fight and I'm button mashing Envenom like crazy going nuts on why it's not working only to see that my weapon poisons have run out.
2) I love shadowstep. I just had to put that out there. It's the ultimate of all rogue abilities. Ok scratch that, stealth is the ultimate, but shadowstep is a close second in my heart.
3) I always feel like I'm wasting my combo points. I've gotten into the habit of using recuperate after each kill just so I don't feel that way... but I still do. I like the idea that others have proposed of storing them on your own portraits. Maybe make it so that you need to use them in a min or 30 seconds before they go away (like with premeditation).

■ What makes playing your class more fun?

I will be honest - I actually read the class descriptions on the website before choosing my first class. And I have to say that I fell in deep, deep obsessive love after reading the one for rogues. Beholden to no one, the stalker in the darkness. Leaping behind an opponent to strike them in the back, then vanishing back into the shadows before they've even hit the ground. That concept alone gives me soooo much fun as a rogue.
In terms of actual game play, subtlety is the only spec for me. If it wasn't for stealth and shadowstep I'd have been severely disappointed. I actually never even bothered trying another spec until I hit 85. Since assassination doesn't appeal to me at all I did combat, but I don't like it.

Another thing I love is the insanity when playing a rogue, especially during instances. When the tank pulls, I SS and am immediately thrown into a life and death situation. Everything's whirling around me, lights are screaming, voices are blinding me... Since rogues are what I consider super, super close combat I just don't get that same intense feeling with other classes.

■ What makes playing your class less fun?

I don't know if this is actually something that can be worked on, since it has a lot to do with player's perceptions, but if by balancing the specs this could be fixed I'd probably die from a love overdose for Bliz.
As I said earlier, I dislike PvP. I also said Subt was the only spec for me... a dichotomy I know. What I highly dislike is random people asking me to PvP with them. Yes I'm a rogue. No I do not PvP. No I am not strange in the head (though there may be those who'd beg to differ).
There's one conversation that stuck with me that went something like this:
*ding*
Random whispers - hey wanna do 2v2 with me
Me - soz no im not into pvp
Random - but ur a rogue
Me - so?
Random - rogues pvp, its what they made for
Me - i like rogue style, doesnt mean i have to pvp :/
Random - well then kindly suck on your <insert male exclusive body part>
Me - back at you
Random - no u

Which at this point I'm highly irritated (somewhat at my inability to make witty come backs) so I right-click ignore that person.
And ok I realize that I'm sort of ranting, so I'll just answer the rest now.
