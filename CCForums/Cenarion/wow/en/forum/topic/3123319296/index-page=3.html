What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Mainly PVE, with a good bit of pvp.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Arena and Rated BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo
could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

One of my biggest issues with the rogue class is our ability, or rather inability to switch targets effectively. As combat Bandit's Guille is a nice addition, but a real pain as well because we simply can't switch targets briefly without taking a hit. For Assassination poison ramp up time is really a pain for that regard as well.

What makes playing your class more fun?

For PVE I simply enjoy tackling an encounter as a swashbuckler. Blade Flurry, and Killing Spree(to a lesser extent due to positioning changes) just make me feel like a general master of dual-wielding.

What makes playing your class less fun?

For combat, bandit's guile ramp up is very annoying, not being able to switch targets effectively isn't very interesting, and killing spree's position swap isn't very fun either. It can be very dangerous to a raid (Heroic Baleroc for instance) since I get placed directly inside of the boss and often can't use it due to mechanics.
For assassination the general lack of cooldowns isn't very engaging. Vendetta is honestly a weak cooldown, and cold blood is more of a joke for use. Something like Adrenaline Rush would really help the spec in terms of both energy, and speed.
Subtlety just isn't worth the time to play competitively. It would be really nice (in the future of course) to adjust the tree and play style to potentially fit raiding better.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For combat, the rotation is great. Like many players I preferred it before Revealing Strike, which basically gives us an extra key bind. Other than that, I very much enjoy it because I always have to pay attention, and get a nice work out for my fingers to prevent energy capping.
I'm not very enthused about Assassination's general rotation, but it works. It's often slow, but mastering it puts you leaps and bounds ahead of others.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I would love another choice for offhand weapons aside from daggers as combat. I would also like changes for bandit's guile to allow target swapping (maybe increase the up time of it, but reduce the damage gain a little). Killing Spree would be wonderful if it would let you stand in your current position when used. Most of all, a baseline shadowstep for all rogues would keep the class interesting.

What spells do you use the least?

For PvE Expose Armor, Shiv, and Combat Readiness.
For PvP just Expose Armor.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE, a little PvP now and then

Heroics, Raids, Old-tier content (as in MC, BWL, etc).

The occasional BG with guildies.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Having the combo points stack on the Rogue, similar to a Paladin with Holy Power. It would cut-down on the ramp-up time involved with switching targets, Honor Among Thieves in the Subtlety tree would not cause any more issues, and Redirect would need to be changed. Let the points disappear 5 to 10 seconds after combat has ended to prevent stacking them for very long.

Have "Throw" be an instant cast with the weapon speed as the cooldown. It would give us a slight mobility buff to help with being kited as opposed to having to stop for half a second to throw while risking our target getting out of range.


What makes playing your class more fun?

Being able to stealth around, sneak past certain mobs, having a certain level of control over encounters by use of positioning and CC

What makes playing your class less fun?

Hearing from others "Why should we take a Rogue when a Hybrid / Ranged character can do it better?" for starters, having out PvE play nerfed due to PvP issues, and such,

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Assassination: Rotation is fine, albeit simple. Change to Backstab @ 35% and continue on the same.
Combat: The rotation is ok, though Killing Spree needs some work. Bandit's Guile and Blade Flurry don't get along too well. Revealing Strike is a little lack-luster / cumbersome.
Subtlety: A lot to keep track of, though definitely keeps you on your toes. Honor Among Thieves, though, does make Redirect somewhat useless if the timing isn't right.

Overall: The rotations don't really leave much room for error, nor doing things "on the fly" without sacrificing damage.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Combo points stacking on the rogue, as mentioned in the Quality of Life changes

Throw being instant cast, as mentioned in the Quality of Life changes

Since we're a debuffing class, 2 new poisons to round out the mix would be a good start and make us more raid-friendly. One to provide the "Spell Critical Strike Chance" debuff (which is only provided by Warlocks and Fire Mages) and a "Physical Damage Reduction" debuff similar to Demoralizing Roar or Curse of Weakness.

Also, for poisons, remove Instant Poison and bake the damage into all the others. The talent Deadly Brew essentially does that with Crippling Poison. A change like that (allowing ALL poisons to do damage) would give us more options on what to use when, and not feel like we're gimping our damage output. It would be useful for both PvE (group utility) and PvP.

What spells do you use the least?

Expose Armor (due to all tanking classes having the same effect for much less of a cost and is already a part of their rotation)

Smoke Bomb (Haven't really found much use for it, and I usually forget I have it. My own fault, really)

Redirect (I usually pop Recuperate or Slice and Dice to use the points after a mob dies. On fights where there's a boss & adds, I may use it unless I don't have any points when my target dies and I need to switch)

Combat Readiness (Mostly due to the change with Cloak of Shadows. Cloak is more useful)

I play a rogue in a second-tier progression guild. While not my only toon, or my only raiding toon, he is the one I focus the most time and effort on.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE almost exclusively.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Raiding: firelands.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
N/A

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Long ramp-up. As combat, my ramp is over 15 seconds under haste effects, as much as 30 without. Target switching is a pain when ramped up. As assass, my ramp is rng dependent, from 5 seconds all the way up to 12. Target switching, while less of an issue, makes my brain hurt.

What makes playing your class more fun?
The sound effects, the ability to survive damage that most toons die to (3rd concussive stomp Hc Ryolith), running a class with exclusive weapons and low-demand armor.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Long ramp-up and clunky target switching mechanics. Positional requirement for max dps in 2/3 specs. Dependence on rng and passive sources for over 50% of my damage.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Combat: engaging, easy enough to do while progression raiding, always room for improvement. /thumbsup
Assass: simple, not mind-numbing, rng dependence in many ways... /meh
Sub: Hard. Especially on a non-farm boss. Not competitive in any situation that involves anything except standing in one place and dpsing. /thumbsdown

What’s on your wish list for your class?
The fact that something like 15% (combat) to 45% (assassination) of my damage comes from poisons makes the Deadly stacking mechanic extremely frustrating, especially in a target-switching environment. I just checked a Baleroc log. A total of 57% of my damage is melee+poisons+t12 bonus. All passive damage.

Stack CPs on the rogue, or leave cps on old targets. Make DP last longer/lessen number of max stacks/increase proc chance dramatically/make stacking poisons an ability. (Possible: make evis refresh DP stacks for combat and sub, and give envenom a Shred-glyph style effect on rupture.)


What spells do you use the least?

Blind, Disarm Trap, Combat Readiness.
