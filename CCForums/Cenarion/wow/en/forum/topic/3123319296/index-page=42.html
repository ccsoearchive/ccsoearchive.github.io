What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both, but if I could only one, PvP

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Still gearing up for PvE, so Heroics. I have trouble finding the time necessary to raid.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

All of the above. But I prefer Arena over RBGs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Carrying around poisons is a minor annoyance, but I almost enjoy it from an RP point of view. The most annoying thing is probably that they only last for an hour. I've gone into Arena with 30 minutes left on the application and had them wear out during the match.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I love sneaking around and attacking unsuspecting enemies. It can also be fun to just disrupt enemies by sapping them and leaving without ever attacking.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I hate fighting classes that can kite effectively. As subtlety, I can Shadowstep to an enemy, Sprint, and use my rocket boots, but after that, there's not much else I can do. I don't want Rogues to become ridiculously over-powered, but I cry whenever I feel forced to use Cloak of Shadows or Vanish simply to break a snare.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Subtlety rogues are fun in PvP.
I feel that Assassination rogues, which I play for PvE are a bit boring, though. I feel like I just mash Mutilate and keep my finishers up until the enemy is below 35% health. Then I get to mash Backstab for the rest of the fight. Plus, energy regeneration is slow enough that I feel as though I'm hardly using any abilities.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I wish that other specializations, besides Subtlety, were more viable in PvP. Shadowstep is so important that I feel forced to subtlety. Don't get me wrong, I enjoy subtlety, but I wish that being a poison-master or an agile swordsman were more valid choices. In my humble opinion, without Shadowstep, there aren't enough enough ways to close distance with enemies.
Also, I think Combat Readiness should be on a separate cooldown from Cloak of Shadows. I never use Combat Readiness except maybe in a duel with a Warrior or Feral Druid, because Cloak of Shadows is so much more important.

What spells do you use the least?

As I said above, Combat Readiness, because of the shared CD with Cloak of Shadows.

Shiv. I don't always use Shiv... but when I do, it's to dispel an enrage effect. I use Crippling Poison on my main hand in PvP, so I rarely use Shiv.

Sinister Strike. As I play Subtlety and Assassination, there are much better and more efficient combo point builders available. It might be nice if Sinister Strike was made more viable. It could force Subtlety rogues to use Sinister Strike, Hemorrhage, and Backstab to be effective.

I also rarely use Distract or Pick Pocket as I am too lazy.

Having to keep many buffs up. (SnD, Recuperate,) and keeping rupture going on an enemy. Also the fact that rogues aren't that unique.     What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    Poisons expiring. Having poisons fall off my weapons in the middle of a battle is highly annoying. On a bad night it doesnt happen much, because you always check/redo buffs during the downtime between wipes or close kills, but on good nights where you go an hour straight without a single stop you suddenly find yourself doing half your normal dps with no warning or indication that your poisons have or are about to drop off.

    Having many limiters on some major abilities. Some abilities have double limiters, like all finishers are limited by energy and combo points, which is fine but rogues have several main abilities that are triple limited. Envenom is limited by cp, energy, and poison stacks. ambush and garrote are limited by stance, energy, and positioning. Kidney shot is limited by energy, cp and cool down. It just seems that the payoff for meeting the requirements is non existant

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Having a fast paced ability rotation. The feeling of using many small attacks to build potential and suddenly releasing that potential in a sudden, devastating strike.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Being an disposable member of my group. Rogues provide no benefit that cannot be provided as well if not better by another class. Our buffs are inefficient and unstable to provide. Our survivability is reliant on short effect, long cool down abilities; without which we become mana sponges. Our damage can be eclipsed by classes with greater survivability and more utility. The one niche that we did fill, being low threat dps, has been completely negated by the recent changes to tanking. Now that threat generation barely matters, all dps classes can go full throttle without concern.

    Lots of passive damage. The last time I checked recount my top three sources of damage came from Instant Poison (~22%), auto attacks (~20%), and deadly poison (~13%). Thats right, I have no say in 55% of my overall damage.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    I normally play as an assassination build, and the overall feel of the rotation is solid but at points can feel sort of rough or artificially impeded. There are times where I end up wasting combo points or capping energy because I cant refresh a 5pt rupture with 2 secs on it with a 4pt rupture. This leaves me with two options while waiting for the rupture to clear; do nothing and cap energy costing me dps, or throw another mutilate and waste a cp costing me dps.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    I would like to see a revamp of how combo points work overall with the rogue class. I would like to see them work in a manner similar to a super meter in a fighting game. Points would no longer cap at 5 they would go to 100. You would get 1 per auto attack, and more for doing combo building special attacks. Finishers would have a defined max effect and the level of your combo meter would represent how effective a finisher would be when used, with certain finishers only usable with your meter at a defined level.

    What spells do you use the least?

    Never use smoke bomb.
    Never use gouge.
    Never use deadly throw.
    Theres two relevant encounters in the game where I use shiv, and thats only for the baked in tranq.
