•What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Mostly PvP.
•If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids, when I PvE'd in the past[Wotlk era]
•If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Everything.
•What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

1) Haveing a very, very slim chance of seeing a feral druid in stealth. We are the primary stealth class and it never made sense to me as to why a 'mimic' class gains better stealth, or for what purpose. Though I understand some classes require counters so I've grown to accept this fact.
2) Most rogues are engineers for PvP, so not being able to use many of the engineering things makes us sad pandas, though this is slightly off-topic. ;)
-- Aside from these two things, with one being slightly off-topic and silly? I think rogues are in a perfect spot at the moment and require very small chances for meer effect rather than balancing.
•What makes playing your class more fun?

Simply playing a rogue is extremely fun. Flaws and all. The thing about rogues I absolutely love the most is we always seem to find a way out or into something. A way around an obsticle. I love that I am able to put some thought and strategy into play and see the difference when I do so, which is unable to be done with some of the more linear classes.
•What makes playing your class less fun?

When the 'fun', no redundancy intended, little things are taken away! While they are not always class-based[engineering becoming more limited when it comes to PvP viability, for example], I can speak for all rogues whom played in vanillia, we miss our swirly ball[Detect Traps]
•How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I LOVE, I will say it again, LOVE. The spot that Subtlety is in at the moment. It is absolutely perfect. Don't change anything about it! The damage in Shadow-dance is slightly low after the Unheeded Warning nerf but it is still performing on-par when it comes to burst requirements in arena. Combat PvP, while I know it is a long-shot to ever see combat as a viable pvp spec, requires some work in that area. I think if combat Cooldown-based burst was buffed it would perform far better in PvP.
•What’s on your wish list for your class?

I'm not sure, to be honest. I'd like to see rogues gain some new spell/ability animations. They have always been fairly boring when it comes to being show-y. We kinda just hit things and look cool when we shadow step. Thats about it. But that is pretty minor, as to buffing? Nothing is needed in my personal, PvP-based opinion. We're in a very good spot at the moment. Oh! Maybe some minor additions to the class. Things that wont specifically hinder/buff the class in terms of PvP/PvE/Ect. But give us something to goof around with when we're bored. Detect Traps from vanillia worked perfectly for that. ;X

I'd also really like to see poison-crafting returned to rogues. The class was much more engaging when we had to level lockpicking and work on our poisons. I really miss the poison crafting.. It made me feel more.. Rogue like!
•What spells do you use the least?

Aside from the spec-based abilities[Sinister Strike, Backstab, Mutilate], which only one of the three would be used depending on the spec, Expose armor. For subtlety and as far as I know, all the other spec's, Expose armor is near useless in terms of PvP. It requires too much energy and combo points to get a full duration up, which is the only viable duration, and just isn't worth the sub-par damage increase we gain from it. My suggestion to fix expose armor in PvP terms is this: Instead of durations, perhaps change it to how Hemo used to be, but rogue-only. 5 combo points could give something like 30 hits. Maybe more or less. Buff the armor pen to 30-60% on players but 12% on PvE NPCs(Range given because it would have to be tested and balanced). That way it acts as a smaller Find-Weakness that is able to be put up while we are not in stealth. That is the only thing I could think of for expose armor. It's really up in the air since Ignore-Armor is pretty sub par at the moment. Find-Weakness takes care of our damage since we gain it again during our shadow dance. Who knows! You devs. will think of something ;)

Also, Pickpocket. The boxes rarely give anything worth-while and using the ability is fairly taxing. Perhaps increase the distance of it to 10 yards unglyphed? I'm not sure.

- To close, I think rogues are in a wonderful spot at the moment in terms of PvP and I'd like to say thank you to the developer(s) who read all these threads. I am very impressed with the effort of trying to see what the community's concerns are. Thank you. :)

4. What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
A: My Rogue has been my main since classic WoW. I raided MC, BWL, AQ40, and everything since those days, and 2 things have always stuck out at me as, "Wow, this would be so much better if..." issues.

Firstly, poisons. Poisons have come a long, long way since the days of doing multiplication to figure out how many of each mat I had to buy to craft them or leveling up the skill... but still: Warlocks no longer blow bagspace on Soul Stones, and Hunters no longer waste 1-2 bags on ammo... Yet because Blizzard feels it adds "something" to the Rogue-ish-ness of my class to visit a vendor once in awhile, I have to blow 8-16+ slots on poisons? And I only PvE! I can't imagine what the PvP'ers feel like.

I don't have a problem with the duration. I don't have a problem with how many poisons we have total. I don't have a problem with the price. I don't even have a problem with one poison relying on another (Deadly proc'ing the other hand, for example). ... What I can't stand is having to go to the poison vendor every so often and fill up an entire bag full of poisons to last me another week. Why can they only stack to 20? Why not 200? Or why not just make them a spell we train at the appropriate level (Instant @ 10, Crippling @ 20, etc)? Heading to the vendor isn't TOO bad... but blowing bagspace after how far WoW has come to save other classes their bagspace is a little annoying.

Secondly, and I'm sure you'll get this one a lot: Combo Points. Combo Points are the core of how a Rogue functions. We build them up to blow them on self-buffs or damage to our enemies. Yet in many fights in raids, and especially in PvP I'm sure, switching targets renders all of our Combo Points completely useless. Across all 3 specs. Oh, but wait, we have 1 ability on a ridiculously long cooldown (for it's function, not in general) (Redirect) that can give us what we've needed and asked for all along? Combo Points need to stack on the Rogue, not the target. I'm no expert on other classes, but I feel that a Rogue's ramp-up time on a new target is a little ridiculous sometimes, and too often do we find ourselves getting to the perfect time, and then having to switch to a different target and starting all over. At the very least let us keep our Combo Points.

5. What makes playing your class more fun?
A: Stealth. Being able to stealth to the back of a cave and kill the quest-NPC I need is more valuable to me than any other class-centric perk I've come across in my experience. Though Stealth has seen some nerfs in its days (we no longer have a talent to increase our stealth, and it's easier to see people in stealth now than it was in classic WoW), it's still an incredibly valuable tool. Don't nerf the entire essence of my class.


6. What makes playing your class less fun?
A: 2 things. First off, True Sight from enemies seeing through my Stealth! lol... Secondly, and most importantly: Rogues bring very little to a raid, especially Combat-spec'd ones. We don't give out food, we don't buff folks for an hour, we don't heal or tank, and we're the only melee class who has 1 function: dps. I know this post isn't suppose to be about damage, but you asked the question: What makes MY class less fun is being stomped on damage-meters by a hybrid class who has another choice in their role. A druid (cat), warrior, or death knight shouldn't do more damage than a rogue (equally skilled and geared). We should be the best melee damage-dealers, no question. It's all we can do!


7. How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
A: As Combat, I love my rotation. Current (4.2), Rupture is factually a DPS-loss to use without a bleed-debuff on the target. Even when a bleed-debuff is on the target, it's less than 1% gain to use it. I *love* this aspect. I've played a Mage and Death Knight in end-game raiding and I'm sick of looking at DoT timers. I'm perfectly fine managing my Slice 'n Dice, but I have enough on my plate with Combo Points, Revealing Strike, cooldowns, target-switching, and staying in melee-range. I don't have time to worry about a DoT timer as well. My current "rotation" as a Combat Rogue consists of 5-point SnD, then 5-point Eviscerates until I need a new SnD, with cooldown-popping in the mix as well. I love the "simplicity" of leaving Rupture out of the mix. Keep it this way.


8. What’s on your wish list for your class?
A: -- Combo Points stacking on the Rogue, not the target. See question 4.
-- Change how poisons stack in bags. Make them stack to 100-200, or make them a spell. See question 4.
-- Please do something about Killing Spree. It's pathetic for a 31-point talent and literally gets a Rogue killed in numerous boss fights.
-- Give us more offhand options other than daggers. I know you did this to simplify some loot tables and stuff, but seriously, how many people other than Rogues were rolling on 1.4 speed swords and axes? Everyone knows it's made for a rogue. Please stop constricting us to daggers in the offhand. It looks like crap for Combat. At the very least, let us transmogrify our dagger in the offhand to something else, like a sword or axe.


9. What spells do you use the least?
A: As a Combat rogue, i hardly ever use Rupture. I LOVE it this way. See question 7. I also very infrequently use Redirect. It's on a long cooldown and is sometimes just a pain to think about before I've already started building a new set of Combo Points on my target. See question 4. Expose Armor is never used. Ever. At least not in PvE. Even without a warrior or druid providing a 12% sunder-like effect, my combo points are better spent in my rotation than blowing them on Expose Armor. I can't remember the last time I used it, let alone even had it on my bar.


That's about it. Hope this post is taken seriously and that the bit I've said is taken into account... especially the Combo Point thing, lol...
