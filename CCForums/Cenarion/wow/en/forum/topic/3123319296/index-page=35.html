  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    • PvE

  • If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

    • Heroic Raids

  • If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

    • While not my focus in any way what so ever, I do like to participate in Rated BGs and Arena every now and then, nothing serious.

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    • I can't really think of anything that I dislike on a day to day basis. I suppose poisons are annoying, being that they take up several bag slots. I find that sometimes I don't pay attention to my poison duration during raids and they fall off at inopportune times, but that's more my fault than anything else. I suppose though, given the time commitment of raids, it would be nice if poisons lasted a wee bit longer.

  • What makes playing your class more fun?

    • I enjoy the "quick on your feet" thinking required to play a rogue well, and the need to often quickly different evolving situations in raids. On the whole the class plays at a pretty fast pace, I enjoy that the most.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?

    • Honestly, when it comes down to it I feel rogues bring the almost nothing to the table in raids. With the advent of Hybrid Classes that do amazing dps, are great tanks, and can heal like there is no tomorrow, I feel like my place in raids is no longer a sure thing, no longer a given, many classes now provide overlapping buffs or benefits, which makes sense for 10 mans on paper, but the end result is that, for example, 10 mans often opt to bring more of these hybrid classes to a raid, rather then a straight DPS class. There have been a few times I was passed over for a ten man spot in favour of a feral druid, because not only can feral druids do damage on par with most rogues, they can also OS tank. I have no desire to tank anything or heal anything, that's why I play a rogue, but I am unhappy that rogues don't bring more to the table, something to make us a more valuable asset to a raid team.

    Another thing that I don't really like is that I feel, especially with this tier, that Blizzard is forcing me to play a certain spec. I know you keep saying this is something you want to avoid, but it's not hard to tell that this tier as a rogue heavily favors Combat and Subtlety. When I saw that secondary stats attached to my Tier 12 gear, I knew right away I would be dropping the spec I really enjoy, Assassination, in favor one I don't enjoy at all: Combat. I'm sure you can sit there and tell me "No, it's not like that." But lets be honest, classes are designed with certain secondary stat priorities, Mastery is, for example, one of the best secondary stats for an Assassination rogue, yet it is, outside of the shoulders, non-existent on my Tier gear. While things like Haste and Crit, two stats that are not as strong, are plastered all over Tier 12, Haste is awesome for Combat, and BOTH are amazing for Subtlety. Bottom line is, I now raid with two specs that aren't my favourite, and that I feel forced into playing. However, for what it is worth, Subtlety is pretty fun on a few fights.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    • I don't really have a problem with any of the rotations for any of the specs. Outside of Subtlety, which can be a bit complicated at times, none of the rotations require too much thinking, however I feel that they all do require a certain knowledge of class mechanics in order to be able to maximize their potential. So while I may not have the most intricate rotation as a Combat rogue, I do have to pay attention to many things in order to make sure I am doing the DPS I am capable of, and that is something I like.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

    Arena (bgs to get honor but not focus) and a rated BG or two.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    Not a big fan of having to purchase and carry around poisons. I also feel that we have the least interesting armor sets out of all the classes (be it pvp or otherwise). Warlock shoulders are always so much cooler... Also, the positional requirement on backstab can (at times) be extremely annoying (ie: Someone putting their back to a wall).

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    I love how our class can completely change the flow of an arena match. You can be falling behind and losing momentum, but immediately gain it back through a redirect 5 pt shadowstep kidney into a smokebomb shadowdance. The ability to change the tides through SKILLED actions and timing is what makes our class fun. Though I find this to be a double edged sword.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Most, not all but most, of the comps that are viable and FUN to play for rogues require more coordination and understanding of your partner's play style than any other class. For example I play a RLS variant (using holy pally at the moment) and I find that without perfect play from myself and my partners, we'll lose every time (last night's losing streak can attest to that). But on my Priest, running a spellcleave (which I heal) I find that my mage/eleshaman can just pick a target and train it for the most part. My priest which is my alt is higher rated than my rogue because of this imbalence.

    I'd like to see mindless cleave teams made un-viable somehow, though this may just be a pipe dream.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    Rotation's do not really exist in pvp. The little bit of PVE i do I find the rotations easy and usually out dps PVE geared people, this may or may not be a concern.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Someone else said New Posion, I'd like to be able to use something more interesting as Subtlety rather than wound/crip or wound/numb. But I'm not sure what you could add.

    I'd like to see a slightly less bloated spellbook and trees. Why not combine Evis/envenom into one skill, which changes attributes based on the tree you're in?

    Keeping spells like SnD, Recoup, and rupture (though rupture isn't used by me personally), is far less interesting. I'd rather Recoup not be in the game (far too many people QQ about it) and have Rupture do more damage so I don't feel like I've made a mistake when I see it tick for 400-600 damage.

    I'd like Combat Reediness to be reworked, I've died far too many times through CR and Evasion.

    What spells do you use the least?

    Expose armor, Rupture, pick pocket, distract (very rarely in pvp), sinister strike.....I try to use all the tools I'm given, bu these few just piss me off ^^.

    Thank's for reading I tried to be thorough and as helpful as I could be.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

    I've been known to harass healers in TB on occasion.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    I'd like redirect to be an effect that automatically kicks in after we spend a few seconds hitting a new target. The current incarnation is too clunky for my tastes; combat energy regen is so fast that I might as well just use the time it takes to hit redirect to add new combo points instead.

    Alternatively, make combo points not tied to a target, similar to how holy power is.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    The speed and intensity. The constant struggle to not let my energy bar fill. The visceral thrill of getting in close and disemboweling my enemies. Sprint. Being able to jump off cliffs and not die. The sheer ninja stylishness of it all.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Encounter design that punishes melee. There are a lot of fights in Cataclysm that are not only harder to play as a melee, but harder for the entire group if there are more melee than ranged. Corla is probably the best example of this, but the first boss in Lost City (whose name escapes me just now) is also very painful to do as a melee.

    There are also several encounter that seem to punish combat spec in particular by making it unwise or impossible to use killing spree. If I use it during the second phase of Glubtok, it will likely port me into the fire and kill me. If I use it on Corla, it will probably screw up the debuff stacks (not to mention the fact it wastes hits on the cultists). I can't use it at all on Ozumat.

    I love killing spree, and I don't want to see it changed. Encounter design just needs to take it into account better.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    I love the rotation for combat. Don't ever change it.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    More weapon options, especially for the off-hand. Daggers are boring. I miss my axe/sword combo from WotLK; I was like a fury warrior, but more awesome.

    The redirect changes I mentioned above.

    Shadowstep for all specs would be nice. It's just a really fun ability.

    Maybe remove recuperate, or buff it but give it a long cooldown. Rogues are about killing, not healing.

    Otherwise, I think rogues are pretty much perfect. I love this class, and I don't see the need to change much.

    What spells do you use the least?

    A lot, honestly. Disarm, blind, gouge, smoke bomb, redirect, expose armor, envenom, shiv. But it doesn't bother me. I like how rogues seem to be more about using a small number of abilities well than spreading themselves between many abilities.
