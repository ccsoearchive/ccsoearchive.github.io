Content I focus on, and type:

PvE, mostly. 10 man raids, and some heroics. I do BG's every once in a while, because they are fun and it is a nice change of pace.

Quality of life issues:

Like some have said, picking locks takes a while when unglyphed (mainly a lazy issue, and not wanting to sacrifice a minor glyph in either spec for it... though I do have one at the moment). Other than that, maybe the expense of poisons (no other class pays for a mechanic that they rely on so heavily, now that ammo has been removed) and having them drop off a weapon unexpectedly (but those are easily countered annoyances).

What makes playing a my rogue fun:
Stealth, popping out and dealing quick painful attacks. I like the sleek moves, after garroting an enemy and going in for a mutilate with a jump in between. Also, the sprint is fun for running back to the boss and beating my buddies after a wipe.

What makes it less fun:
Long ramp ups on short fights, being energy starved when needing to interrupt something. Also, relying so heavily on another class for maximum damage (not talking simple buffs, but class mechanics... primarily a 10 man raid concern).

How I feel about the current rotation:
For assassination (my preferential pve spec), the rotation is enjoyable, mutilating an enemy, keeping our speed up and bleeding them to stay up on energy, poisoning them at every chance. The aspects I dislike are the down times waiting for energy to come back... kind of takes me out of the combat more than is enjoyable, and the long time to get into the good damage, waiting on those deadly poison stacks, or waiting til the 34% mark for back stabs. Waiting makes it feel sluggish.

Wish list:

Something to do, some button to hit when the energy is coming back, to keep me on the edge of my seat and in the combat (or a speed up on energy regeneration for assassination).

Maybe a slight reduction in deadly poison stacks needed for maximum damage.

A possible mechanic to improve our physical portion of damage (most of what assassins bring is help to casters)... maybe incorporate expose armor into another more commonly used rogue ability.

Less reliance on combo points (I know, I know a staple of the rogue class), I'd like to see the fights and watch things unfold rather than be glued to the little dots building on the enemy raid frame. Don't mind the combo point deal, it's just a tunnel vision fix I'd like to see.

An overhaul to the combat rotation (I know asking a bit much), I just don't like watching the buff bar as slice and dice is running out and making sure i have enough combo points to keep it up or losing out on a lot of damage from letting it fall off for a global, and I don't think revealing strike is as fun as it sounds. Also for combat managing energy is about as painful as it is for assassination, but by having too much too often and losing out on the full effect of a cooldown or two.

Least used abilities:

I have to say I utilize almost every ability in my spell book currently, mainly depending on type of game play (bg's on occasion, helping a friend in arenas, raids, and heroics), but the one ability I don't think I've ever used is deadly throw. There's not much call for a slowing throw that uses combo points, as to get those points you most likely hit them (and if there's a need to slow the target, most likely there is crippling poison on a weapon).

Granted I'm not the most proficient PvP rogue out there and I hardly do arenas, but it seems that ability is for an extremely limited player-base.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

My life as rogue would be much, easier if positioning requirements of some openers were revised. I mean, if I prefer to open with Ambush why do I have to ambush from behind? One can jump off the hedge or break stealth facing the enemy with the same success as they could sneak from behind and sink daggers in. Ambush should remain ambush whether from the front or the back. Considering PVE world, eliminating such positioning requirements would actually speed up mob pulls and general pace of Rogue gameplay (same applies to Garrote, btw).

Poisons are a tough nut. You have eliminated the ammo from the game as being redundant, but left the poisons. What is the ammo if not a natural extension of the ranged weapon? And what is the poison if not the natural extension of Rogue's melee weapons? I mean, we deal the majority of our damage via poisons, so why not integrate them within our weapons systems, make them castable (Alt + Click to select the type of poison, or what have you), remove the inventory items and let us happily envenom henceforth? You will be removing the actual items, which would potentially would free bag space, vendor space, and hell lot of the code that you can apply to different other things (later on this).

Stealth is not what it should be. I mean, what is the most charismatic feature of the Rogue? Yeah, STEALTH. Any rogue not using stealth is prone to realize he is slowly regressing into a bad warrior, and this is not where we should be heading. Give us stealth that we can actually embrace. Keep us in stealth if you have to, but allow us to use it properly. Rogues do not pull mob in happy bunches, 2-3 (competitive level) enemies is what we can handle, and every time we aggro, we break steal only to realize that we have to stealth-click again and again after each pull. Why not allow us to activate the ability once, and forget about it during consecutive pulls? For example, if I activate the feature and pull, I break the stealth, but am put back in automatically after the mob is dead, so that I dont to reclick the stealth button again if I want to pull another mob. Moreover, there are few people as is that are happy to see any given Rogue in the city running around on SI7 errands, so why not answer those people's prayers and keep us in stealth all the time? My quality of life would be certainly improved if I become a ninja that only shows up for vendor/AH trading, and is otherwise bloody unseen.

If you want Rogues to be fun, allow them to keep low profile, after all, we are as close to ninjas as it gets in the whole game. Hell, I am even farming my ninja-set-alike for the new patch. So, I don't really understand what's the deal with that flashy armor and gems shining all around the gear? Turbans and shoulder armor that screams of noobism. Rogue should be synonym of balaclavas and tight leather armor - which visually highlights his/her focus on agility, movement and speed. You have the concept there, and it works, so why not implement the design in the same way? Being low profile is part of Rogue mentality. Being sneaky and unnoticeable is what we are. Why would anyone want to be a Rogue, if they want to wear shiny armor?

What makes playing your class more fun?

Mechanics are great, and you can twink them allowing for both raiding and world PVE rotations. For example, I specked improved Armor Pen, which is fun to throw in against mob, and occasionally against bosses when other classes forget to apply armor reducing spells. As such, I couldn't care less if that gives me net decrease in DPS of 200 points, all I care is that it's fun.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I am a Rogue for many years now (if not since day one of the game release, certainly from day two), and unfortunately, I feel that the class is becoming less so unique as it was before. What your game designers have done is converged every role more or less to the same standards: Rogues, Warriors (dps), DKs (dps) are melee - they have to do a, b, c... and nothing more. And while we do those As, Bs, and Cs in a slightly varying ways, on a larger scale, there is no difference if in any given raid composition you have a Rogue for melee or a DK. Back in vanilla, Rogues were feared for PVP, during crusade, we were leading DPS race, hell, even in ICC we could at least disarm traps and push levers from stealth. Where, do I ask, is the unique value-add of having a Rogue in a raid nowadays? So, surely, it is less fun knowing the class/spec/gameplay that you dedicated so much time and development is suddenly just an average same when compared with the other toons.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I play assassination mainly, and remember the rotation be heart. It's easy, it's universal, it's flexible to some extent. There is absolutely no need to change anything about the rotation other than implementing the above adjustments to how the stealth works. My understanding and full appreciation is for the developers who created the class rotation and polished it to the sickness. Keep it as is. Don't change it. As a matter of fact, stop tinkering at the bloody thing for all the clases. The rotation and class mechanics are good. Decide the priorities of where you want each class to be (on the dps/health meters) and stop re-working it. Crash the bugs, and let the players actually enjoy the gameplay without nervously waiting for every patch expecting nerfs. Focus on something else. Focus on the fun aspect of the game, let people enjoy the product that they bought form you, and are paying you on a monthly basis to use. Essentially, what you are doing is not just selling a toy to us, but also are charging us regular fee to use this toy, to play with it. And inevitably, if you neglect the fun/novelty aspect of that toy, you will be loosing dedicated players to other games. Focus on the archaic graphics that do not hold any reasonable criticism. Substantially, the game graphic feels and works the same way as it deed on the release date. This is not enough. The whole idea of releasing Cata as a limited leveling expansion (only 5 levels) was presumable to allow you focus on other things in the game. What you ended up doing is working, re-working, and re-reworking balancing without clear-cut message of where you want to see the classes and specs. What is of the promise to actually improve the game? Your models hurt eyes and the self-esteem of a player to look upon. You argument that modeling is not just poly counts, but what have you done to actually improve the underling design structures? Focus on rethinking the UI. Kill the memory leaks, make the UI consume less resources than it currently does. Challenge your programmers and designers alike. The players need the functionality in a lean UI, not the griphons and lions painted over the cast bars. How long did it take you to implement movable UI elements? And there's so much more that you can do to improve it further, and by doing so, trim the code, kill bugs, make the game better.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Better daggers. I don't really know if it's just my luck, but after killing Trons in BWD almost on weekly basis for the last 4 months, I am yet to see the Inverter drop. I am really serious about this wish. I wish for the dagger to finally drop! It shames me as I'm considered to be an OK DPSer, an I have to run around still swinging the blue (the ZA/ZG do not apply aesthetically as they look so far off from what a DAGGER should look like, so I refuse to equip them).

I wish for Rogues (as much as I wish for any other class) to remain unique, and bring particular value to the game. If you want us to be pure DPS class, let us be more than just average DPS. If you want us to be sneaky-slimy ninjas, let us forage unseen and unnoticed. If you want us to be ROGUES, let us follow the mindset of a rogue (not a hunter, or pally, or DK).

What spells do you use the least?

Had to actually look those up (in no particular order, and considering mine is the Assassination spec):

Feint (because true Rogues don't Feint)
Deadly Throw (seriously, what was this designed for? did I miss something?)
