Abusedgoat

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both and I play a lot.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raiding and heroics for fun.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Mostly arena, world pvp and BGs. Sometimes I do rated BGs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Poisons

A long shot, but removing the positional requirements of backstab/ambush/garrote. At least in a pve setting :)

Bosses you don't have to face and get parried for a majority of the fight. Allah Akir is on that list, and others like Rag and domo deserve at least a mention.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Control and damage are always on the list.

I love blade flurry with it's toggle on and off.

Despite how much I (and most of the community) mocked smoke bomb, I really like it. So much potential with it.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I mainly raid as combat and there are two things I can't stand. First of all, energy capping happens all the time. The playstyle is so spammy that it doesn't feel like wrath or what little I saw from BC. There's no fun in mashing sinister strike with maybe 1 revealing strike to avoid energy cap. This also means using feint/tricks will cost you damage since you used a global on them while energy capped.

Bandit's guile is probably the worst thing I have ever experienced on any of my characters. Yes there is some skill managing it, but it's SO punishing for swapping targets when redirect is down. Chances are if you are swapping targets in a fight, redirect won't be up for all of them. Then you have to manage saving redirect for a good amount of insight, or not using it when you have none built up at all. Not only is this ability frustrating to manage, but it encourages tunneling one target.

I miss wrath PVP where rogues could spec assassination because of prep. Combat was actually viable (not necessarily good) at non elite brackets and it's completely terrible now. What made combat playable was dumb (killing spree) but at least you could choose which spec to play. Cata pvp is sub and nothing else. Doesn't matter what you are doing, sub is the best choice without any competition.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I really enjoy the sub rotation, and I love the fact that our most complex rotation is also potentially the most rewarding. I'm sure people will complain that it's too complicated but the other two specs are more straightforward. On one hand it encourages tunneling to keep rupture rolling, on the other it offers great (relative) burst for target swaps with backstab.

I rarely play assassination because the other two specs offer more IMO. However, it's nice to see a change from mut/mut/envenom spam for an entire boss fight. Making rupture viable as well as an execute make it more interesting.

I enjoy the combat rotation except for the issues I listed above.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Complete removal/rework of bandit's guile.

Killing spree doesn't cause you to lose control of your character. Anyone that's raided at any level knows how frustrating this can be. This may prove to be a double edged sword in that spree will no longer let you immune through fears/stuns/etc but that doesn't get me killed.

A way to deal with combat energy capping. My suggestion is to let eviscerate have the option like ferocious bite to cost double energy for double damage (for combat only). This should also remove spree/ar/sprint/redirect cds accordingly with restless blades. The combat tree offers 14 spots to place talent points that offer no direct damage gains, players have to choose 4. I'm sure something can be removed or changed.

Feint/tricks taken off the GCD.

Making kick duty appealing. No one ever wants to do this on a boss, and if you do I wish you apped to our guild last tier. If imp kick gave a small (~2%) damage buff for a short amount of time players might actually want to do it or spec for the talent.

This is more of a change for another class, but faerie fire is silly in a 1v1 setting when it lasts as long as it does. Perhaps a 10-15 second debuff on players? A feral doesn't sacrifice much to use it.

What spells do you use the least?

Rupture as combat. My guild almost never has a 30% bleed debuff so it's pointless to use.

Shiv obviously. It serves it's purpose however.

Combat readiness since it started sharing a cooldown with cloak.

What type of content do you focus on? If PvE, what type of PvE?

PvE as Assassination. Raids/Dungeons.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

The biggest issue I have is still fights that have me do a lot of target swapping, especially if the adds are short-lived. You put in Redirect to move combo points periodically but this is really only the tip of the iceberg. The biggest issue is still having to rebuild my poison stacks, not to mention getting rupture back up, refresh SnD if it has fallen off, only to have to do these things all over again on the main target. I sometimes end up running without rupture, with half stacks of poison, and barely keeping SnD up. It's not fun, and it's not good DPS. I understand that there is a penalty for everyone with target swapping, but it feels so much more punishing on a rogue than any other DPS class I've played end-game.

I really like having more buttons to push than CP/finishers, but I am not sure I like the idea of Rupture being my energy regen ability for this same reason. It makes situations where you don't use it effeciently or at all - which is not just add swapping but short fights like some trash, five mans, solo play - worth double the loss (from both bleed and lack of energy for other abilities). I would rather energy regen be passive or fixed into poison damage, and rupture just be a straight damage ability.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I like rogue utility, provided it's only a small DPS loss. I like handling interrupts or being responsible for things that require sprint or slowing or evasion tanking, dismantle, disarming traps, etc - all those kinds of things that make me feel useful and all roguesque. I like stealth, vanish, distract, stuns. And, of course, doing awesome damage.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The frustrations with handling target swapping is the biggest.

But I also am not very happy when I am expected to provide a particular utility that is a huge DPS loss; ie, hypothetically having to kick is fine but having to use Crippling poison instead of Instant is not. I feel like a lot of other classes that can provide these same abilities can do so at a much smaller personal DPS loss.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

I like having multiple buttons to push and several things to track finally. I also love having an execute with Backstab. I like using Vanish as a DPS cooldown. I like feint being useful again. I like having some cooldowns to watch and some situational moves I can use for "smart play."

I wouldn't mind gaining another ability or cooldown to watch, provided my rotation was made flexible enough to allow it with effort but not frustration.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Something to help poisons with target swapping would be nice-no, mandatory.

I would like for poisons to be an imbue type effect rather than item in the bag. It was a fun flavour thing when we brewed our own poisons (which I liked!) but now that we no longer do that it is sort of unnecessary for it to be taking up bag slots. If you want to keep flavour, have us apply it via our spellbook and have it last until 15 minutes of logging out like a conjured item, and have it require a single 'poison vial' type item in our bags (not a reagent, but like an enchanting rod or arclight spanner). This way we can at least keep it the whole time we're online but still keep the flavour of poisoning our weapons. I understand wanting to avoid permanent enchants that remove the 'feel' of being a rogue, but it's really a pain to be carrying stacks of a bunch of kinds of poisons (just in case you need a weird one) and have to be applying every hour, and hope it doesn't fall off mid-fight because you misestimated the fight length and just screwed your DPS.

Ultra wishlisting: I would like something that subtracted or lowered kick's energy cost (talent or glyph or baseline). I would like Rupture to not be our energy-regen ability (as per above). I would like to find an elegant solution that doesn't make rogues too strong in PvP that allows us to use specialty poisons when the raid needs without a massive a DPS loss. Tricks is a little stupid to just push on cooldown; I wish it was more fun. I would love to finally have a non-passive/not-spec-specific group buff, be it an aura or something like Battleshout or Bloodlust.

What spells do you use the least?

I also never use: Gouge, Deadly Throw, Ambush, Sinister Strike or Eviscerate. I rarely use Shiv, Expose Armor, Disarm Trap, Combat Readiness or Smokebomb. They are all cool abilities just too situational.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE. PvP scares me to death.

If PvE, what type of PvE?
I'd say a mix of about 10% raiding, 50% heroics, and 40% questing (dailies). At least, that's when I'm actually engaged with the game. I care most about my performance when I'm raiding though.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?.
Having to reapply poisons in the middle of a fight because I forgot.

Also, for some inexplicable reason, my off-hand weapon breaks faster than any other item of my gear. I have no idea why, but it makes repair trips much more frequent. It's been going on since early Wrath.

What makes playing your class more fun?
I like the cooldowns themselves, on their own. They really make me want to go "Yeee haw!" in a horrifically bad Texan accent. They're absolutely kickass and have been since the 3.0 patch. Mix with fan of knives, and I start to sing "Nuuuummbers numbers numbers!" in the raid's vent.
...Seriously, I do. They love the song, too. They think it's cute.

The new blade flurry is also wicked amazing. I think I'll have another hit single called "Iiiiinfinite cleaaaavvvee!" coming out soon.

Definitely, absolutely, 100% love combat for those three buttons.

And Vanish. The fact that Vanish isn't (much of) a dps cooldown means I can use it to avoid dying when a wipe is called. Saves on repair bills, and the feeling of doing that and then mass-rezzing the group makes me feel loved.

What makes playing your class less fun?
The moments when you energy cap during Killing spree and have to use FoK.
The moments when Deep Insight run out before you had enough time after Adrenaline Rush to empty your energy a bit.
The moments when the boss gets off a spell while you were on kicking duty (glyphed kicks need clutch timing during Alys).
The moments when Deadly Poison runs out of stacks. Seriously, that's not something that should happen.
The moments when you need to switch targets and Redirect is on cooldown. 'Sup Omnitron.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
Right now, since I'm combat, it's got to be the juggling of cooldowns and finishers to mix with Bandit's Guile. Adrenaline Rush and Killing Spree don't mix, and Killing Spree is only worth using at Deep Insight, and a glyphed Adrenaline Rush (being glyphed because I'd get ridiculed if it wasn't) lasts only a little shorter than a full Insight rotation so that there's a small window to use Killing Spree and it's a long time to reach the next level of insight and if you miss it then you'll have to wait to use KS again and that means a waste of a cooldown! And, if you haven't taken your energy pool down a notch you'll be forced to spam fan of knives to stop from capping while you're in Killing Spree mode, and you should pop your activated trinkets at Deep Insight, and you should only rupture if you're *not* at Deep Insight and *only* if there's a bleed debuff up.
Oh, and the energy boost from Adrenaline Rush mixes too well with Heroism, so I gotta predict when heroism is called and save Adrenaline Rush until after it so I don't energy cap like a [whoopsie].

So overall, about 6 different things to worry about. I have to worry about getting 5 combo points, whether I've used Revealing Strike, whether I'm in Deep Insight, whether Slice and Dice is low enough to refresh, energy-capping, and spacing out cooldowns evenly. And most of those things tie in with each other - can't RvS at 5CP, can't space cooldowns while Insight's a smacker, and so on.

Asides from that, the rotation itself is alright. I don't mind it at all. More complicated than Wrath, but not too much so.
