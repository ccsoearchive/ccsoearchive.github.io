    What spells do you use the least?

    - Combat Readiness:
    Ever since the change to make it share a cooldown with cloak, I find myself hitting this ability less and less in pvp. Nine times out of ten, even against double melee teams, I will use cloak over Combat Readiness. With Cloak, I can actually get a restealth off against ferals and DKs, I can break magical roots such as by druids or shamans, and can use it for both offense and defense. Combat Readiness is an extremely situation cooldown that only really works as a defense.

    - Expose Armor in PVE
    For every spec but Assassination, it's more expensive for a rogue to put up an armor debuff compared to any other class. DPS warrior only have to sunder twice and can keep up the debuff with their standard rotation the rest of a fight. A Feral Druid only has to use a GCD once in a while to keep it up. A rogue without the talent deep in assassination (which provides not other benefit except refunding CPs on EA) has to spend five combo points roughly once a minute. There's a very large disparity among the classes that bring an armor debuff here.

    Overall, I'm pretty happy with where rogues are, but some quality of life changes would be definitely welcomed.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Stealth. I love stealth. When I was leveling, I loved being able to sneak right past mobs and get to my quest target. I love being able to have the freedom to pick my battles.     Fights like Ascendant Council Phase 2 in BoT. I don't mind fights that require me to move around with some frequency; I don't equate "fun" with "no risk of my rotation ever being disrupted," and Patchwerk, Festergut and Chimaeron are not exactly thrilling fights for a melee DPS. Between Arion teleporting himself, the tornadoes and grounding fields sometimes being entirely on the opposite end of the (very large) room from the boss I needed to hit, and the need to suddenly run out of the group and twiddle my thumbs when I was unlucky enough to have Lightning Rod cast on me... I spend most of that fight just feeling frustrated.

PvP ups my heart rate enough as it is; I don't want to risk a heart attack due to the added stress of not knowing whether my stealth will fail at an inopportune moment when I wouldn't otherwise expect there was even a risk of it failing (e.g., 30 yards away from the enemy, or just after Vanishing while attempting an escape).

    How do you feel about your “rotation”?

    I like the Assassination rotation. I've often equated it to a dance; I like that there are multiple buffs/debuffs I need to keep track of and keep up, and I like that there's a lot of finesse and timing involved in syncing up my cooldowns with key moments in a fight (and with each other). The rotation itself is somewhat simple and can be boring, particularly on tank-and-spank fights. But I love not feeling obligated to spam a button every GCD, and I like having a small amount of active problem-solving involved in some of the decisions I have to make during a fight.

    That said, I do wish our ramp-up time weren't so massive. On fights like Cho'gall -- and I know this is bad and I shouldn't do it -- I'll actually find myself deciding to stay on the boss rather than run 20 yards over to interrupt the person who's worshipping, and instead hope that someone else interrupts him/her before we take a large amount of badness. And it's because I worry I'm going to have to restart my rotation and lose significant DPS by going to help a fellow team member. If I knew the DPS sacrifice was less, I'd be more inclined to heed those mechanics.

    09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    I'm sorry to jump on the bandwagon, but I think combo points should stack on the rogue. It would make high-mobility fights a little bit less infuriating. Similarly, changing Redirect so it could redirect poison stacks instead of combo points would be outstanding, though I realize it'd be all but impossible to do from a PvP standpoint. I just wish Assassination rogues didn't have to deal with such a lengthy ramp-up time; it feels like an unfair penalty for us simply doing our jobs.

    Similarly, I'm increasingly finding myself in the camp that believes that, as a pure melee DPS class, we should be the ones who easily top DPS meters on tank-and-spank fights, period. It is literally what we do best; why would we not be the ones who do best at it?

    09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
    What spells do you use the least?

    Besides the obvious ones that aren't part of my rotation (Sinister Strike, Ambush) or that I don't use because they're largely made for PvP rather than end-game PvE (Deadly Throw, Gouge)... Smoke Bomb. Disarm Trap. Distract. And of course, Expose Armor (which I don't miss using anyway). I do wish the first three had more obvious/frequent utility in PvE situations, though. Especially Disarm Trap and Distract. I left this out of my "what makes playing your class less fun" answer, but I enjoy having to do CC, or having to do special little things that help my group that only I can do. CC is so universal now that, in my 10-man group, I'm almost never asked to do it. We have a sheeper, we have a bind-elemental-er, we have a trapper. I'm also rarely the person who's put on interrupts, since the shammy can do it more frequently. I feel less useful today than I did at this time last year. I know things like the traps in ICC were a pain in the !@# to have to rely on a rogue for, but I do long for the feeling that I brought something unique to the group, even if it's just a small thing that only applies during a third of the fights in a raid tier.

It's one thing to be vaguely aware that some of the things we say may or may not find their way back to someone within Blizzard who can act on it. It's another to know specifically that it's likely to happen. I wish one of you had the time to periodically come in here and shake it up with us. You are missed.
