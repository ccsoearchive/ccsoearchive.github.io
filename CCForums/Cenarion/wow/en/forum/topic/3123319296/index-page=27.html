What type of content do you focus on? PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? Heroics/Other (dailies)

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters. Recuperate is probably the best one that I can think of, as it makes me unkillable to a point while soloing PvE content. Other than that, did we even have any poor quality-of-life things besides buying poisons?

Though, the recent nerf to Combat Readiness/Cloak of Shadows strikes me as making my quality of life... lessened. I thought I'd notice it a lot more than I do, but I don't, though.

What makes playing your class more fun? Sneaking around and CC, and doing moderate-high damage.

What makes playing your class less fun? When using my CC isn't necessary and my DPS is vastly outranked among my gear level.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) As Combat, I feel that my rotation is among one of the most solid, easiest to play rotations in the game. It's why I play Combat. But it has far too long build-up time (though admittedly less than Mutilate) and heavily discourages target switching.

I do feel that Rupture could be a more necessary part of my rotation. As it is, I can leave it out of any fight I feel doesn't need it and only lose a small amount of DPS. In my mind, Combat's rotation should feel... messy. Like I leave a bloody mess when I DPS.

What’s on your wish list for your class? A shorter cooldown on Redirect. I know, Combat has the lowest cooldown on that anyway, but I still need to use combo points to get it sooner. But it would make it easier to switch targets in add fights, which is where rogues falter hardest anyway.

What spells do you use the least? Shiv, Expose Armor, Smoke Bomb and Dismantle. I still use them, granted, but they're situational. That's sorta the rogue theme, though, so I don't particularly mind it.

    •What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    As many have already pointed out, it'd be awesome if poisons no longer took up bag space. I'd love to see poisons become like a shaman's temporary weapon enchants, in that they're something you can instantly cast onto your weapon(s). I'd also love poisons to become indefinite in their duration, though that's probably asking too much.

    •What makes playing your class more fun?
    Stealth. I love being able to sneak around, sapping and pickpocket my enemies with ease. I also love the offensive pressure that rogues bring to the table, as well as the many utility tools at our disposal.

    •What makes playing your class less fun?
    I hate how our DPS gets totally screwed when we have to switch targets mid-fight, due to our combo point system and the ramp-up time of Deadly Poison and/or Bandit's Guile. Redirect has helped out in this regard, but the one-minute cooldown of it is brutal, even with the Restless Blades talent. I think it would be absolutely fantastic if the base cooldown of Redirect was reduced to at least less than 20 seconds. It might even help if combo points were stored on the rogue rather than on the enemy, though that may be too radical to implement right now.

    I also don't like how we're the only pure melee DPS class and yet we regularly underperform in terms of damage compared to other melee specs, including those of hybrid classes (Enhancement shamans come to mind.) I'm not saying we should always be at the top of the DPS charts, but so far throughout this expansion, our damage has been subpar in comparison.

    •How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I play as Combat in PvE, and the rotation is fine. But like other Combat rogues will tell you, the mechanics of Bandit's Guile are simply too erratic right now. I'm all for spec uniqueness, but the cyclic "low-dps-followed-by-high-dps-back-to-low-dps" nature of the Combat spec as provided by Bandit's Guile isn't exactly ideal when it comes to sustaining damage during key moments, such as during raid boss fights. And we simply can't skip the talent either, because the precious moments of Deep Insight are too good to give up as well. See the problem? Reduce the ramp-up time of Bandit's Guile, and/or give us more control over it.

    I used to play Assassination, and while the rotation of it is fine as well, I find that the main problem facing the spec (aside from the aforementioned ramp-up time of Deadly Poison) is energy starvation. Compared with the other two specs, I often feel too energy-starved, with the only exception being when my Overkill buff is up. That's the primary reason why I switched to Combat during this expansion.

    Subtlety's rotation in PvE is complex, which is good. But one small mistake can cost the player majorly in terms of sustaining good DPS, which is bad. I believe that a few small changes can be made to the Subtlety tree to improve the quality of life of those who want to raid as Subtlety, which I'll bring up in the wishlist up ahead.

    •What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Above all else, please alleviate the issue of target-switching, preferably by greatly reducing the base cooldown of Redirect and/or by making improvements to the ramp-up time of Deadly Poison and Bandit's Guile.

    I also believe that the Subtlety tree could use a few quality-of-life improvements. For starters, you could make the Energetic Recovery talent add 10-15 seconds to the max duration of Recuperate, so that Subtlety rogues have more leeway in their rotation. Also, the Serrated Blades talent should be like Assassination's Cut to the Chase talent, in that casting Eviscerate should have a 33/66/100% chance of refreshing Rupture to its 5-point maximum instead of simply its original duration. Subtlety's Mastery could also use at least a slight buff; as a few others have pointed out earlier, it's bizarre that Subtlety's combo-building moves (Backstab, Hemorrhage) do more damage than its big finisher.

    I'd also like to see our bleeds (Garrote, Rupture, glyphed Hemo) do more damage than they currently do. Rupture in particular really needs a buff.

    •What spells do you use the least?
    Disarm Trap, Expose Armor, Combat Readiness (now that it shares a cooldown with Cloak of Shadows), Shiv, and Deadly Throw. I'd use Deadly Throw a lot more often if it didn't have such a lousy travel time, though.

    I've read that buying and applying poisons were going to stay because it makes us feel like a rogue, something unique to our class. I think that is fine, but I think making the duration infinite duration would be nice. You still get to keep the theory of applying poisons making us feel like rogues while we have a better quality of life.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    I like this class because it is the only pure melee class in the game. Theoretically, we should be one of the top dps in any situation, any boss fight, with the exception of a few fights due to mechanics. Our class has a feel to it that we are needed in raid progression because we make it so that the dps on progression is of no worry.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    What makes playing a rogue not fun has nothing to do with the class design, but rather the current boss designs. In Cataclysm, every end heroic boss for both tiers have been extremely, extremely melee unfriendly in the 10 man setting. Sinestra's orbs and the spread of wrack in 10 man made taking a melee a complete pain, almost impossible in some 10 man guilds if they wanted to go for realm first. Heroic Ragnaros is the same in the 10 man setting: the only viable melee in a 10 man setting would be a frost DK due to howling blast. A rogue is very rarely brought for 10 man kills unless the guild has adequately brought and geared the required specs that are needed to kill the seeds in Phase 2. The design to make the seeds to tremendous damage when grouped together completely removes melee from participating in that part of the fight, which, of course, makes it impossible for progression if you are a 10 man guild that has problems recruiting and gearing the specific specs that are required to AoE the adds. It is extremely frustrating in Cata so far that the boss mechanics for the final end heroic boss have been a disaster for melee in the 10 man setting. It isn't fun to be part of a realm first progression guild, help the guild get 12/13 or 6/7, and then cannot participate in the final boss because the mechanic has too much stress to bring any melee, so much so that if this is not fixed for Deathwing and Heroic Deathwing, cancellation is most likely to occur.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    My wish list is said above: making sure that the final end heroic boss in the 10 man setting is designed so that it doesn't completely negate melee dps' role in the fight. Being a pure melee class, this issue must be addressed; warriors, ferals, and ret have the same issues in the 10 man setting, the only exclusion would be a frost DK (but that negates unholy DKs).
