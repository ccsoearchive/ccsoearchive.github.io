What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]


PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]


Heroics. My schedule has made it very difficult for me to raid. I'm hoping the raid finder can help me out in that regard.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


Running out of poisons and forgetting to reapply them. I suggest that rogues need only one of each poison, like a shaman's totems before 3.0

Also, combo points need to stack on the rogue, not the target. Unless your future plans for boss fights are styled like Patchwerk and Deathbringer, rogues are going to need help staying competitive. Putting points on us rather than the enemy wouldn't fix everything, but it would be a good start.

What makes playing your class more fun?


Competitive raid damage and utility.

What makes playing your class less fun?


I feel kind of left out in a raid setting when rogues have no buffs to give out. In protracted fights, stealth also plays a very limited role.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


I really hate my rotation. It's too simple and has too long a ramp-up time. I think it says something is profoundly wrong with rupture that the only reason I'm supposed to use it is for venomous wound.

What’s on your wish list for your class?


This isn't just an issue for rogues; I'm concerned about the future of the pure classes. It used to be that we were the only classes that could do damage (obviously that was a flawed system). Then we were still by and large the top damage dealers. "Bring the player" however started the push of the pure into obsolescence.
What's the point of playing a class that can only fill one role when there are two classes that can fill all three? We no longer do top damage, so that's out. Our specs aren't balanced very well, so that one's out too. Finally there's the fun factor, but endgame players go for numbers above all, and the numbers don't conform to Blizzard's current design philosophy. You don't publish max level activity by class, but I know you have that data. Mages can't be underrepresented because of their insane burst; I'm guessing we don't have a shortage of hunters either. But it should be telling how small a segment of endgame players are rogues and warlocks.
This can be fixed in one of a few ways. You could make pures the undisputed top damage dealers, but that risks destroying the whole point of the hybrid class. You could tweak all specs to make them competitive with each other, but Blizzard has proven itself less than capable at this task. Or you could get rid of the convoluted pure vs hybrid business and give rogues a tanking tree


What spells do you use the least?


Distract, pick pocket, expose armor, and feint.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Raids.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Arenas and BGs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
1. Target swtiching is clunky for various reasons in all three specs within the PvE environment. Switching Combo Points to the Rogue (much like Holy Power) would be one welcome way to address this.
2. Frequent stealth bugs. Namely being seen by players and mobs alike from much further away than usual. (I've been targeted by hunters from max range while stealthed with no debuffs, etc. to cause such things.) Vanish not working correctly, leaving pets following me and marking me easily for a quick AoE to drop my stealth. Creatures completely ignoring Vanish and ripping me a new one anyways... Those sorts of things.
3. Poisons. The least of my worries, but it'd be nice to shift poisons over from actual items to spells that I can apply to my weapons instead. With reagents seemingly going the way of the dodo as time goes on, this just seems like a natural change to me. I like applying poisons to my weapons however, I feel it gives me a degree of control that is lacking in other classes.

What makes playing your class more fun?
The use of all my non-DPS abilities to affect the outcome of PvE encounters as well as PvP encounters. For example: Feint used on cooldown to lower the often times enormous amount of raid damage going out, Evasion used to tank a boss for those crucial last seconds, Sap used to fiddle with the minds of the Alliance and hear the lamentation of their women. That sort of thing.

What makes playing your class less fun?
1. Stealth malfunctioning. It's frustrating to lose arena battles or get caught out in a battleground when you really shouldn't have. All due to someone seeing you that shouldn't be able to.
2. Forced positioning. There are quite a few boss fights nowadays where standing behind the target just isn't always viable.
3. Special Snowflake Syndrome. I don't have some sort of special feather in my cap that screams to a raid leader, "Bring that guy!" It forces us to work twice as hard to get into 10 mans vs. the guy who brings bloodlust and does 9k DPS.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
1. Assassination, I'm fine with it. Although when the raid leader calls for burning something down, I feel a little silly since nothing really changes for me.
2. Combat, ehhh, it's alright. I feel like Combat is an aerobics class for my fingers, but I'm only hitting like 3 keys. Energy capping is a real issue and I don't really feel the oomph behind Revealing Strike, although I can see it on meters.
3. Subtlety. I love this spec, but it's just too much work for just slightly less DPS than I can pull in the same encounters with Assassination and Combat. Which leaves me wondering, why work so hard when for less than half the effort I can do a little more DPS? I love the challenge, but when fighting for a raid spot as it is against other classes that claim to bring more to the raid, I can't afford to ditch that extra 1000 DPS.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1. Some sort of damage boost to Subtlety that doesn't break PvP but makes it worth the effort in PvE.
2. Combo points on the Rogue rather than the target to ease target switching.
3. Stealth and Vanish effects working as intended, rather than letting me be seen from 40 yards away by any Joe Schmo with eyes.

What spells do you use the least?
1. Smoke Bomb. This spell is essential in arena, but in PvE I find myself making excuses to use it. It's come in handy for dragging spellcasters to the tanks, who I've trained with special tank treats to stand inside just for that purpose. That's about it though.
2. Redirect. I've tried my best to use this ability as well, but sadly it's always on cooldown when I really need it. I've found more luck using it in Arenas than anywhere else. PvE the new targets are either coming too fast for it to matter after the first or it's simply a better use of my time to memorize the timing and dump all my combo points right before they appear, either in a well timed damage finisher or refreshing my slice n' dice or... Recuperate if I'm not at 100%.
3. Expose Armor. Even in five mans, I've yet to find a reason to use this since Cata hit. If there's not a warrior or druid, there's a raptor saying, "Man, why are you even here with that stuff? Put it away!"

  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    • Mind Numbing poison is something I never use, it could probably be baked into the wound poison or crippling poison debuff to reduce the kinds of poisons needed to carry.

  • What makes playing your class more fun?

    • I find managing timers pretty fun, while also reacting to procs such as seal fate or sinister strike glyph procs.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?

    • It's possible to swing the above too far and have too many timers to manage. It's also stressful trying to avoid energy capping during adrenaline rush.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    • The rotations are fine as-is, in terms of spell sequence. Ramp up could be reduced

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?

    • Since I mostly play combat this seems like a good time to address the energy regeneration concerns with combat.

    I'd like to see combat's damage-per-energy go up and the energy regeneration take a slight hit. Reduce vitality from 25% to 15% increased energy regeneration. Have adrenaline rush give a smaller energy regeneration bonus but give it a shorter cooldown, say 70% increased energy regeneration on a 2 minute cooldown. I'd also like to see combat potency have a higher proc chance for less energy to smooth out the energy income. Instead of a 20% chance for 5/10/15 energy, make it say a 20/40/60% chance for 5 energy (talent affects proc chance not energy amount).

    Killing spree can be dangerous to use, it needs some more work. It's very fun so something along its present line, just some way that it reduces the chance of putting you in harm's way.

    Combat's baseline damage needs to be raised by about 10% with bandit's guile stacking for 5/10/15% damage, and stack more quickly. Either that or don't tie bandit's guile to a target.

    Fan of Knives damage needs to be increased to 100% of thrown weapon damage and slaughter from the shadows needs to reduce the energy cost even further for subtlety to have any semblance of passable aoe.

    Change the blade flurry glyph so that it no longer reduces the energy regeneration penalty, but instead causes no threat on the secondary target.
