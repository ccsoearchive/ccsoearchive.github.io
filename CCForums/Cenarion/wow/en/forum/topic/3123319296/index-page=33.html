Mutilate Rogue's perspective; it's the only spec I play.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Lots of BGs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
No complaints in PvE. From a PvP standpoint, player pets being able to follow you around everywhere even after a vanish or restealth shouldn't happen.

Having CloS share a cd with CR is not enjoyable; I've removed CR from my binds since this change was made. There's just no situation where CR would be better to use than CloS, and as CR was of rather limited use to begin with (not saying it was bad, just that the times when it was of great benefit to use were very few), it's just not worth using over CloS.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Rogues were irritating to manage in Wotlk, or to me it felt that way; this has been cleaned up quite nicely in cata and allows for an incredibly fast-paced feel to the class, whether you're moving in to sap and open or just laying out the dps. Knowing that if things get out of hand you can bust a ninja move and vanish/reset is very signature to the class and I enjoy this.

In organized PvP, smoke bomb is such a massively important tool, even without Subtlety's prep>re-bomb strategy, it's one of my favorite tools and can even be incredible in PvE if used right.

Also even with tank threat being a non-issue now, Tricks of the Trade still has use as a wonderful 'misdirect' when used in conjunction with FoK on anything involving lots of adds, or in bringing a lone big guy over to the tank.

EDIT: Also I just wanted to add that the movement speed buff in stealth is a great improvement to our quality of life, but that my Feral friends could probably use a similar tweak, since that's how we spend our lives - in stealth. And this is imperative: unlike us, Ferals don't have a reset button. So as much as I love my Rogue and as much as I hate being bled out by Ferals, I could make the argument that they (kitties) probably needed the in-stealth movement speed increase just as much, if not more, than we did.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Can't think of anything that makes my class less fun, aside from the pets following you in stealth thing.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Smooth, no complaints.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
I'd like to see the shared CD between CR and CloS removed. I understand this was done as a balancing issue and I don't envy you guys and gals for having to find a way to tweak classes without making everyone mad, that's a rough job. But by forcing a shared cd on these two abilities, pretty much every Rogue worth his or her salt just stopped using CR, which given our (comparatively) smaller toolbox versus other classes, basically equated to "here's this new ability! But you'll never want to use it."

Also, this suggestion might be asinine because I haven't played sub or combat in a while now.... but you might consider whether removing crippling poison and just making the movement speed reduction a natural byproduct of deadly or instant poison is a good idea or not. My reasoning for this is that generally speaking, when not shadowdancing, Sub's output is simply kinda low, and combat basically feels like playing a less-armored dual-wield 1h fury warrior in TBC. I'm not saying these specs are bad, but adding the slowing effect to deadly or instant (NOT to both, and NOT to mind-numbing, that would be OP) would help these specs out a long way I think, without being overpowered since those specs lack the Assassination talents to buff their poison damage.

To go hand in hand with the above, Wound Poison really needs to either be re-worked or just removed I guess.... I don't want to say healers are just OP, but they're stronger than they've ever been, and the current state of Wound Poison makes it not worth using compared to the alternatives. For the most part anything that died while being attacked by a rogue using WP, was almost certainly going to die anyway if that rogue had been using a different poison; it simply doesn't make enough of a difference to matter when stacked against alternative choices.

Finally, Redirect is a great tool but I think the cd should be reduced; I am in the habit of recup or SnD my extra cps due to just not having much of a reasonable opportunity to use redirect.

What spells do you use the least?

Combat Readiness, Expose Armor

Thats 5.78-7.84s per finisher for Mut, 8.4s for Hemo, and 11.4s per Backstab! How many finishers is a ramp-up? 2? 3? 4? No other class/spec has a ramp-up even half as long.

    (3) Introduce some more Rogue flavor.
    - Player Pick-pocketing. Not from target bags/bank, but from a mob-like respawning "pocket." No (targeted) player loses anything, no one's game experience is hurt. Make the Rogue (but not target) flag for PvP FIRST to increase challenge and reduce ganking.

    - Re-introduce poison brewing. Don't replace poison vendors, just allow players to brew other, unique poisons. Maybe some only useful for (solo?) PvE or strange/funny effects to prevent balance disruption.

    - Introduce Climbing. We can already fly almost everywhere anyway, just prevent climbing in flight-prohibited areas (Arenas, etc). Make the maximum grade 90%, it'll make Climbing (and Safe Fall) interesting. Even if it is trainable before level 60, I cant imagine it'll change balance at all.

    - #1 above: the only thing more Roguish than running with knives, is throwing knives while running.

    (4) Fix Rogue damage sources. This is a process, to be done in order.
    - First, reduce the energy cost of Mutilate, Hemo, and Backstab. Example: 42e Mutilate, 21e Hemo, and 28e Backstab would reduce time between strikes to 2.21/3s, 1.5s, and 2 seconds respectively, and 5pt finisher time (with 1 free cp) to 4.42/6s, 6s, and 8s respectively, A SIGNIFICANT IMPROVEMENT to both player down-time and ramp-up time.

    - Second, tie poison procs to player-generated strikes. 100% proc chance on yellow attack (opener/builder/finisher), 0% on auto-attack. "Your Backstab did X damage + Y Instant Poison damage (Z total)" is a lot more satisfying than a server-generated floating number on the screen ... because the player did it, not the server. This will also reduce DP ramp-up time significantly (10-20 or more seconds currently is still too long). [[EDIT: This would also change MH/OH weapon choices, probably slow/slow Mut, slow/either Combat, Slow/either Sub.]]

    - Finally, overall damage will have to be reduced. Take it out of auto-attack damage first. Then, take it out of IP/WP ... DP damage is capped at a 5 stack, but IP/WP will be proccing a lot more often. Finally, rebalance cp builder (and maybe finisher) damage so that each Rogue spec is balanced with the rest of WoW.

    - ONE MORE NOTE: The sheer genius behind Shadow Dance is that for 6-8 seconds, it fundamentally changes the way a player plays. Vendetta is a great buff, but boring. Killing Spree has a nice graphic, but no player action. SnD is a player generated buff to server-generated damage. These things could be more fun. SnD could be part of lowered energy cost or reduce GCD, Killing Spree could have 5 charges and let the Combat Rogue jump when s/he wants to, Vendetta ... I don't know.
