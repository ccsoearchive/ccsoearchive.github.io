What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids (10m)

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

I don't mind reapplying poisons but I think they should last more an just one hour.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The main part for me is that the rotation contains very few RNG elements as I don't like to get unlucky and thus under-perform. I also like the amount of synergy between my talents, abilities, and stats as well as the fast paced elements to the combat rotation (I don't like to sit around waiting to use a spell).

What makes playing your class less fun?

I know it's been brought up many times, but being melee can be frustrating at times, especially when I imagine how much easier something could be if I played a ranged class. I think that there are many more RDPS favored encounters than there are MDPS favored encounters. Also, combat rogue mastery is very boring in my opinion, as we basically share the same mastery with several other classes.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I like it's overall feel and fast pace, but it's kind of annoying when I have so many buffs up that I can't spend energy fast enough and some of it's being wasted (this usually happens when I have to be in front of the boss and I have Adrenaline Rush and/or Heroism up). Additionally, Killing Spree feels too reckless to me. I like the ability but I wish I could control my character during it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

A more interesting mastery for Combat, way more Fan of Knives damage, a way to use energy faster like how warriors have heroic strike (like when I have Heroism and Adrenaline Rush up), less encounters where I have to be in front of the boss (Majordomo Scorpion Form) or have to run around so much that I can't do my rotation half decently unless I'm ranged (phase 2 of Ascendant Council), and I'd like to control my character during Killing Spree. It feels to reckless to me and I'd like to use it at my leisure instead of having to wait until I can do it and still live.

What spells do you use the least?

Dismantle, Expose Armor, Combat Readiness (because Cloak of Shadows is so good), Smokebomb, Shiv (I let my raid's hunter dispel stuff), Rupture (only worth using if my raid has the 30% bleed damage debuff because I'm Combat), Deadly Throw, Gouge, Cheap Shot, and Kidney Shot.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both
PvP

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs
Arenas and Rated BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
First, Redirect having a GCD. Considering that combo points are the only resource system that stacks on targets, which has the inherent disadvantage when target swapping, shouldn't have the skill meant to diminish this disadvantage cost the most important resource of all i.e. time. I realize there might be some limitation with client/server communication that makes the rapid redistribution and use of the combo points without a delay impractical, but as it stands now the class must spend 1 sec doing nothing to move a resource it's already generated and paid for to another target.

Second, Assassination and/or Combat specs not having viability in PvP. When it comes to the specs it's a clear and simple case of a lack of mobility. Some kind of skill/mechanic that has a >30sec CD is absolutely necessary in today's meta-game.

Finally, Deadly Throw is beyond outdated. It's nothing more than a very low damage ranged snare. It's actually a waste to use anything, but a 1cp Deadly Throw. That doesn't makes no sense. Either the damage needs to be calibrated so that it's worth using a 5cp or it needs to have some beneficial effect for using 5cp like an Imp. Hamstring root effect.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Having a class that when played poorly or when making poor decisions it shows. It is not enjoyable when a majority of a class's effectiveness or benefits to a group are almost a given rather than requiring knowledge, effort, and maintenance.

What makes playing your class less fun?
A lack of distinguishing play style between the specs. When swapping from Subtlety to Assassination I change 4 buttons and 3 of them are mirrored when it comes to type i.e. Hemo/Mutilate, Eviscerate/Envenom, and Shadow Dance/Vendetta. Only Cold Blood and Shadowstep are dissimilar other than Shadowstep is still technically usable for increasing damage. The point being that I'm still pressing the same combination of buttons and a slave to a nearly identical rotation and priority. The majority of difference between the specs are mechanics like poisons and procs that a player doesn't control.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
The rotations are particularly stale because there are very limited points or situations where a secondary attack is used over spamming the primary attack to build 5 combo points and then using the highest priority finisher. All the maintenance and decisions are thus on the finishers end of the rotation. If specs had a secondary attack that had a vastly different combo/energy and damage/energy ratio when compared to the primary then priority and choice wouldn't be limited to just finishers.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Remove prep and rework cooldowns and mobility to compensate.

What spells do you use the least?
Feint, Pick Pocket, Expose Armor

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Bandits Guile: Horrible the way it is, needs to be a personal buff that allows target changing without the use of redirect.

Combo Points; Same logic as above, why are they target specific? If you are so adamant about the state of combo points being on 'targets' then allow us to build combo points on a multitude of enemies. Allow Redirect to not trigger a GCD and have no CD, and allow it to transfer 1CP at a time from surrounding enemies to the target.

Poisons; Totem-like UI system is required.
Poisons 2: We need to be able to spread poisons over multiple targets efficiently without the use of FoK. There is an awesome way for assassination rogues to dps Multiple targets through Venomous Wounds stacking which is never fully realized.

AoE: Assassination triumphs above all for AoE Damage, it’s where the other specs should be.

Energy: With energy overflowing some solutions are as follows; Allow Killing Spree to consume a max buffer of Energy to buff the damage of the ability. Nerf Adrenaline Rush’s Energy Regeneration Rate and give it an extra buff like increased attack speed.

Combat: Overall boring, with the only fun aspect being the numbers pulled with Flurry. I’d really like to see some stance sets used for this spec, similar to priest Chakra, interchangeable for situations like buffing FoK Damage.

Utility: We have some minor de/buffs brought to the raid, and some very uncommon gimmicks. Mostly in personal threat and damage taken reduction. I’d like to see PvE use of Smoke Bomb.

Roles: As it stands, Rogues are damage dealers. They aren’t the most versatile of role fillers but I’d really like to see them fill the role of a tank. A lot of the framework is already there without jeopardizing what we already have.
I really do think that adding some extra’s into Subtlety would synergize well with a tank class, and offer a new, exciting and challenging way to play the class.
Some Examples;
Rogues would get an ability which makes them more Shadow like, increasing the effectiveness of their worn armor aswell as lowering their damage taken by 10%, but also lowering the damage they deal by 30-40%. Call it Shadow Armor, Subtlety Rogues would be able to gain Vengeance while it is active, while the other specs would have other gimmicks. Just the start of an idea.
