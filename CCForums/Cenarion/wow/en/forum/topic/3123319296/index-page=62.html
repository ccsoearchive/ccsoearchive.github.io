What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both PVP and PVE: Sub and Combat, respectively

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
BGs and Arenas

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
It seems very whiney and whatnot... but having to constantly reapply poisons and visit the vendor to purchase more is extremely annoying. Also the sheer amount of poisons, most of which have very limited usefulness can be somewhat of a nuisance.

What makes playing your class more fun?
As a Sub specc, sneaking around unseen is the most exhilarating part of my job. Also the sheer amount of CC sub rogues have to use and "stunlocking" is enjoyable.
As a Combat specc, Blade Flurry is a blast, and Ad. Rush is super fun when burning large bosses.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Lack of variety of moves in general. All fall under very broad categories (added damage, cc, self buffs)

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
With Sub being a mostly pvp-oriented specc, the rotation is great because every encounter is different. We get to work the rotation and move priorities around the specific enemy's skills, and that makes every fight fresh and exciting. This in itself highlight's the combat rogue's largest flaw: there is absolutely NO variety in our rotation. Ever. I find myself spamming SS, RS, SnD then Evisc over and over and over again... I need variety in the way I play, and I just cant find it in Combat.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
On my wish list for Sub would be a stronger viability for PvE play. I love the mobility, micromanagement, and situational awareness needed to play Subtlety, but it's sheer lack of DPS and abundance of Crowd Controls make it not useful at all as a PvE specc.
For Combat, What I really would like is some excitement. I dont really have any thoughts on what form that should take (they pay YOU to do the thinking) but I would like to not feel like that teenager that sucks at fight stick games and just sits there, mindlessly pushing buttons. Combat needs a broader set of skills that dont just end up with us hacking and slashing our way to mediocre dps. Our entire existence revolves around the uptime of one ability, and it would be nice if the Combat specc required me to actually think :)

What spells do you use the least?
In Sub (PVP) - Gouge. and this is just so wrong! Also makes Blind a bit pointless.... any time I'm not soloing someone in mid in a bg, both these abilities become useless because I can turn off my own dps but not my buddy's.

In Combat (PvE) - Expose Armor, Rupture (?!?!?!?!) Gouge, Shiv. And I should mention - I love the idea behind Shiv, but its lack of crit possibilities, and generally negligible benefit to... well, anything make it really just kinda lame.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Progression Raiding


If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Arenas, BG's


What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Poisons. At the minimum poisons should stack to 100 and last longer when applied. Ideally they should be class abilities that buff our weapons not consumables that we buy.


What makes playing your class more fun?

Rogue mechanics are based around energy management, cooldown timing, and cooldown synergy. I welcome not having wack-a-mole proc mechanics like some dps classes do. I much prefer being able to focus on timing/combining my abilities and cooldowns to maximum effectiveness.

Stealth and sprint are fun. Shadowstep is really fun. The mystique around wielding dual daggers and *swords* is fun.

The things I enjoy most about rogues are the abilities which make us unique.


What makes playing your class less fun?

1) I feel that our dps penalty for target switching fights is too severe at the moment. DPS rampup time is ok but it's not fun being in a perpetual state of ramping up and never being able to actually deliver the dps. Many encounters demand constant target switching. It seems the damage redirect problem has been addressed for other classes more effectively than it has for rogues.

2) Tricks of the trade and set bonuses based around tricks of the trade. I want my dps potential to be tied more to how well I play and less to how well another rogue plays. When there is only one mage in the raid he can throw focus magic out, get some benefit, and forget it. When I'm the only rogue in my raids I take a 1000 dps loss *for not having another rogue present*. This isn't fun.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Compared to other classes I like rogue dps rotations. It allows me to focus on the timing of cooldowns for maximum dps on boss encounters. I prefer combat because it provides me the tools to do this. I much prefer timing cooldowns and finding optimizations in how they are used together versus reacting to random procs.

Combat:
Energy capping at high levels of gear causes a problem with combat rotations under bloodlust/haste procs. This needs to be addressed.

Bandit's Guile as currently designed doesn't allow rogues to adjust their play in any meaningful way to adjust to the state of insight. As such it seems like a purely cosmetic ability since we redirect on cooldown anyways

Rupture makes almost no meaningful contribution to our rotation at high levels of gear. There should be a greater dps reward for using it.

Otherwise I enjoy the combat rotation primarily because I like having cooldowns to manage and use as the encounters demand.

Assassination:
Slow ramp up, dps falls off the map when there is target switching. Like driving an 18 wheeler in a dune buggy race.

Subtlety:
Would be a lot of fun if it was useable on more than one encounter. It's simply too complex a rotation to use on any fight with a lot of movement. But it's much more challenging and fun than assassination. I'd love to see it's rotation refined where it doesn't fall apart anytime there is movement. The biggest single change to make raid encounters more fun for rogues would be having shadowstep available to all rogues.

Other rotations:
Tricks and interrupt rotations are just stupidly annoying . Full time interrupt rotations on bosses are not fun. Tricks rotation + interrupt rotation + dps rotation.. this defines every fight in the game that is not fun for me.



What’s on your wish list for your class?

Get rid of bandit's guile.
Get rid of redirect.
Get rid of tricks of the trade dependence on another rogue being present, alive, and as competent as you every 30 seconds.

Make shadowstep trainable.
Make shadowstep also redirect. Make it glyphable for PVE where it can also redirect poisons and bleeds.
Keep tricks of the trade threat transfer but makes its damage buff a self buff.

Agility and quickness (not slow dps ramp up time) should be our class defining abilities.


What spells do you use the least?

In PVE I rarely use dismantle, shiv, disarm trap, expose armor.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


Mut PvP "rotation" is too demanding.
- A Sub Rogue normally keeps up one finisher, Recuperate, to handle both energy return and survivability, Mut Rogues need two, Recuperate and Rupture. Yes, that extra finisher also provides pressure/damage (at the cost of CC), but its still the case that Mut Rogues normally need to maintain two.
- a Sub Rogue uses 1 or 2 finishers for burst, Eviscerate as a combo point dump and optimally a Kidney Shot for setup. Mut Rogues need two, Envenom (which is one hit, compared to 2-4 Ambushes and 1-2 Eviscerates) and SnD to make use of the Envenom debuff. Even with CttC, it's more difficult to set-up. The catch? Forget about using Kidney Shot for that set-up (to keep the guy from moving, as critical to Mut's Envenom debuff as to Sub's Shadow Dance) because you need those points for Envenom (where Ambush makes combo points).



What’s on your wish list for your class?

What spells do you use the least?


These two are related, and to the rotation above. Improved Expose Armor is one of the least useful Assassination talents because Mut builds rely on poison damage ... a fact Combat Rogues often point out. They'd LOVE IEA, but if it was given to them, not only would it be a big boost to Combat PvE dps, we'd have to replace it in Assassination. The question is, what could we replace it with?

SnD is already on both Deadly Momentum and CttC, and Recup on Deadly Momentum. Rupture would lead to a DPS increase, and Envenom or Deadly Throw would lead to spamming (a broken talent tree). There is one option though, Kidney Shot.

Turning Improved Expose Armor into Improved Kidney Shot has three major advantages.
1) KS is on a 20s CD, so it can't be spammed.
2) It does not increase PvE DPS, because KS isn't a damaging ability. It does improve Mut utility on non-immune mobs.
3) This would allow Mut Rogues to set-up burst in PvP the same way Sub Rogues can.

------------

While we're comparing, here's another issue.

Shadow Dance: a single burst Cool Down, 1 minute.
Cold Blood and Vendetta: two CDs that need to be popped for burst, each 2 minutes.

Putting Cold Blood on a 1m CD WOULD be a DPS increase for Mut, but only by a single Envenom's damage per minute. Vendetta could easily be reduced to a 1m CD, if it's duration was also halved. That results in a net zero DPS increase.

-------------

OK, so we're still comparing. Mut and Combat simply can't keep up with Shadow Step, and I'm among those who'd hate to gank Sub and homogenize the class. Earlier, I suggested replacing the hidden cast time of Throw with a GCD and short CD (make it available on the move for a ranged snare with Crippling Poison). I said "the only thing more Roguish than running with knives, is throwing knives while running." I have since heard a suggestion that can compete.

As nice as the Vendetta utility is, it's extremely difficult to use. How can you know when someone will stealth? If they do first, you've already lost them. The better idea? Change the utility to "While Vendetta is active, you can not be slowed below 100% running speed, and your target can not move faster than 100% running speed."

Sounds pretty powerful, right? Even for a reduced 15 seconds? Here's why I like it: For the Assassin, it gives the feel of running down your target. For the target, it gives the feel of being run down. **shudder** VERY Roguish. Consider it a "wish-list" item alongside that Throw.

Maybe more later.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Just PVE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Pretty much everything PVE related

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

- Poisons. I understand buying poisons adds flavor to the class, but I would really like to see poisons become trainable spells and have an infinite duration until they are canceled our switched to another type. Having to reapply poisons mid-boss fight or having to scramble to a vendor in the middle of a raid is not fun.
- Low level dungeons/leveling. We need a AoE accessible at low levels. Blade Flurry works with Combat, but Assassination/Subtlety contribute almost nothing in low level dungeons. I would suggest making Fan of Knives trainable much earlier.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The Combo Point system works well. It needs some tweaks and maybe some new spells thrown in because it is very old, but it's a fairly simple, easy to grasp system that generally does it's job.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I feel like Rogues are having a bit of a identity crisis in PVE. We have low armor, low burst, and are lacking in the target swapping department. We don't really utilize many of the key Rogue feats, such as stealth, powerful openers, and powerful finishers. Combat feels like a cheap Fury Warrior, Assassination feels like a cheap Feral Druid, and I feel like the devs really don't know what they want to do with Subtlety in PVE at all. I've played my Rogue for many years now, and even though we are viable in PVE, I really don't feel like I'm playing a ROGUE anymore.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Assassination - This specs rotation is fine. It's not too simple, yet not complex so that it results in "tunnel-visioning". I would like to see this rotation left lone.

Combat - Revealing Strike needs to go. I feel like this ability was thrown in simply to make the rotation more complex, but it feels clunky and out of places. I also feel tying in 35% of our finisher damage to an ability that we don't even use before every finisher is a mistake. I'm fine with making the rotation more complex, but not with abilities that feel non-synergistic and out of place. Adding "reactive" abilities to the rotation seems like the way to go, as I will mention later in this post.

Subtlety - This rotation is too complex and not practical in most raid encounters. Removing Recuperate from the rotation and perhaps finding a way for Hemorrhage to become the sole PVE CP builder would likely solve this problem.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Crit needs to be a more valuable stat.
- Ramp-up needs to be toned down for all three specs. Removing Bandit's Guile and making Deadly Poison stacking quicker/easier would help a lot.
- Finishers need to be more meaningful. They don't necessarily need to do more damage, but added effects (Ex: Talent: Your Eviscerate and Rupture abilities causes your next 3 Sinister Strikes to deal 100% more damage) would work wonders.
- More reactive abilities. We don't need more CP generators, we need "Overpower" type abilities. Perhaps allowing Deadly Poison ticks to have a small chance to allow a free, instant Ambush for Assassination and off hand attacks having a small chance to proc a free, instant Eviscerate for Combat.
- Overall just better identity. Move our passive damage into active sources, make openers and finishers stronger and more meaningful. Perhaps try to better utilize stealth in PVE. Make PVE'ers feel like ROGUES again.

What spells do you use the least?

- Combat Readiness (the CD share with Cloak of Shadows makes it useless in PVE)
- Rupture (worthless for Combat)
- All openers borderline worthless for Assassination and Combat in PVE.
- Expose Armor is worthless in PVE.
- Smoke Bomb is worthless in PVE.
- Deadly Throw is worthless in PVE.
