What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
There are two really big quality of life issues for me

1) Deadly Poison & Poisons in general: It takes too long to stack, and the duration is significantly small. Even though Blizzard did a better job on making switching targets not as punishing, it just becomes frustrating that this DoT we spend time applying doesn't last nearly as long as the time it did to create the stack.

The other issue being that poisons don't act like our melee attacks. I call this quality of life, because it doesn't particularly matter in relation to balance. It's just disjointing that we need to get "Yellow Hit" and then get "Spell hit", if Assassination is supposed to be using poisons, then they should be more integrated with our stats. So the suggestion would to make Poison crit be based off of our melee crit (including the 2x factor).

2) Expertise consolidated with hit: This is a pet peeve of mine. Truthfully I love the idea of hit rating. I like how it can be a very useful stat to a certain point, and then still useful after...but not as good as other stats. The problem is expertise makes this whole process redundant. The spec I currently use doesn't really care much for the stat to begin with...so I never get it. However, it becomes problematic when a boss mechanic forces me to stand infront of the boss. The rotation becomes chaotic, and I feel like I'm just rolling the dice. In general for Melee, it feels unfair to be punished in their DPS for following a strategy. I know you want to reward players for standing in the right spot, but this is already partially handled with harmful mechanics like cleaves. I just dislike having so much of my itemization budget being placed on "making physical contact with my target".

Basically let's skip all of these and go straight to the last one, Rotation.

Combat has the slowest Combo point generation of all three rogue trees. In PvE, that's not a problem, however in PvP, there are several Finishers to keep running or to reapply very often. These would be Recuperate, Slice and Dice, Expose Armor and Kidney Shot. With all of these needing primary attention, there's very little left in the way of damaging finishers, this brings us to my point.

Restless Blades, I love this ability. It rewards sustained onslaught by reducing your cooldowns, allowing you to maintain your onslaught and boost it more frequently. However, the cooldown reduction effect is only triggered by Damaging finishers, which are severely lacking in PvP, thus getting very little use out of this talent.

A very simple solution for making this talent functionnal in PvP is to change the triggering finishers from "Damaging" to "Offensive", adding Kidney Shot and Expose Armor to the list. Both these finishers are used in PvP, neither are used in PvE, it's an entirely one-sided buff that will not affect the other facet of the game and not even risk unbalancing the PvP aspect, for such a small buff will not bring Combat rogues to the level of Subtlety rogues, simply make it more enjoyable to play.

You've probably heard ( or indeed not heard ) that Combat in PvP is pretty awful right now. That is enough justification to consider this idea, as it would increase damage, the primary culprit of Combat's mediocrity in PvP, mobility, the second culprit, and target switching, which complements Bandit's Guile.

What makes playing your class more fun?
In general, I like it when I feel like I'm doing things in line with my class. A Rogue is someone who is neither physically strong, or capable of magic. They need to use cunning and finesse to match their peers. They're vulnerable, and yet extremely dangerous.

In PVE. My class feels fun when I'm forced to either be creative with the damage I'm doing, or helping the raid in a way that's beyond just doing my standard rotation. An example of the first instance, would be Heroic Maloriak or Rhyolith. In Maloriak, during the Black Swill, I can redirect 5 combo points to a Slude and put rupture on it. I then start spamming my Fan of Knives, following up with Vanishing into Garrote with another rupture. Being able to creatively use my Energy Regeneration to improve my overall AoE damge feels awesome. On Rhyolith, It's just fun trying to keep a rupture on both legs with Deadly poison on each...I feel like I've turbo boosted myself, while avoiding the the common things that harm my DPS. To me that's interesting. An example of the second, would be using Kidney Shot on one of the Sons of Flame in Ragnaros, and then saving the raid by Cheap Shotting another add that's getting close to the hammer.

In PVP, it's most fun when I get to use all those nice tricks to either keep myself alive, or surprisingly kill a target. I'd much rather be able to use Gouge to fend off the killing strike from a DK than say...pop Deterrence.

What makes playing your class less fun?

In PVE. A few things are either tedious, or frustrating. I like the idea of Tricks being used to boost other's DPS, but I hate having my DPS dependent on using it on coolodown. This makes it feel very much like Hunger For Blood, and it just feels "forced". I wish there was something changed about Tricks in general, so that it wasn't a "use on cooldown" kind of move.

Bladeflury has also added a lot of misery to my gameplay. I like playing Assassination, and it's all that I ever will raid with. However, Bladeflury is so situationally over powered, that it feels like I'm obligated to have to play both specs "just in case". In the future, I'd like the mechanics built in such a way that there's usefulness for both specs in all fights. Or at least such that I don't feel like I'm dragging down my raid team by opting not to dual spec.

In PVP it's less fun, when I feel "countered". Before 4.2, I PVPed as Assassination...quite successfully so. However, changes kept getting made to the game and the spec fell so far behind the meta that I stopped PvPing. It was just upsetting to see a hunter and go "Well that class has my number...there's really not much I can do". Recently I've returned trying out Subtlety, and it's a lot more tolerable. The only problem being, is that I feel like I'm forced to play the spec.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The Assassination rotation is good, but not without it's problems. I like the fact that there is some randomness to deal with, but getting unlucky isn't gruelingly punishing. I also enjoy switching to backstab when 35% hits, because it's not an absolute thing to do. I have to use my discretion as to either use Mutilate or Backstab when I may not have access to the back. It's a rewarding experience.

The issue I have, is that it's a rather binary rotation. I get Combo Points, then I rupture, and then I envenom...repeat until dead. I dislike how most of the output ends up as Passive Damage. It feels like there is very little to react to, or to optimize besides trying to keep my envenom up time. When I think "Assassin" I think fast critical hits, with touches of very powerful damage. Instead it feels like I'm prodding the boss with a fork till it gets really irritated. The cooldowns, only hurt the process and they verge on the horribly boring (Vendetta) to weaker imitations of other cooldowns (Overkill to Adrenaline Rush). Neither of those introduce any change to the rotation, and have very little to do with the spec. I wish were some way to throw like 3 straight Envenoms, or incorporate Eviscerate.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

    I cannot think of many, though I feel like the rogue class is either very circumstancial, or or has a high-ramp-up time.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Stealth is key to the rogue, and it's also it's more valuable tool. It's what I miss the most when playing alts: being able to just skip half the mobs when questing to get to the target.

    And when it's there, the feeling of utility that sparks rarely that no other clas could do. be it putting a Smoke Bomb on the flag carrier on crucial moments, cheap shotting and killing the spark before it reaches Ragnaros' hammer just in time, or yelling in vent at someone who is standing on Shannox's trap but saving them regardless because I disarmed it.

    There is also the matter of survivability. I will mention this though it was heavily reduced from BoT/BWD to Firelands transition. Feint and Cloak of Shadows where great survival tools that trivialized (for one person, not the whole raid) certain abilities, allowing that much-needed extra DPS in. Examples are Cloak of Shadows for Heroic Nefarian, where you didn't have to jump into the lava, Heroic Valiona and Theralion, where you could stay longer in the twilight realm because you could remove the shadow damage stacks...

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    As a melee class, bringing a rogue is almost condemnable. Thru all of the fights save Heroic Fandral Staghelm, rogues don't really shine or have any luster. Ranged classes have to move more, and they are often more affected by bosses' abilities and they have to have more coordination. But thru this, the vast majority of them can still DPS. A melee class has to avoid a boss ability and they have to stop DPS altogether for a considerable period of time (Example: Arion and Terrastra phase for Heroic Ascendant Counciil)

    The uptime and target-switching is considerably annoying, as well. As mutilate, DPS is severely hindered if the mob does not have a sizeable stack of deadly poison and prefferably, rupture. Combat loses all its stacks of Bandit's guile if it has to switch. Redirect is not the tool that it was meant to be: 1 minute is a cooldown too large for all of the bosses to really allow it to shine for combat. Mutilate has the same issue. I really, really, really dislike Bandit's Guile in general, since it sort of feels like a randomizer.

    In PvP. I also feel like it's a bit circumstancial. Now, PvP is not my forte, but to dish out some damage, I need to have recuperate, slice and dice, and the enemy to not have diminishing returns on stuns and to not have the trinket up in order for me to do some harm with Shadow Dance. If I dish him out with just backstabs it simply meant that I outgeared him but the circumstancial prowess to pull all of those at the same time seems a bit outlandish in comparison with PvP i've done on other classes. Yes. Stealth allows me to pick the time of my battles but often, this doesn't do that much. Also survivability is too damn low.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”?

    Combat DPS' is the most simple minded one, I believe. But that is without applying the problems that arise with Bandit's Guile. Trying to maximize DPS with Bandit's guile uptime seems very hard. if you consider boss mechanics and other procs.
