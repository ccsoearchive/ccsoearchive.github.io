What type of content do you focus on?

Both PvE- heroics & raids and PvP- BG's, Arenas & Rated Bgs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

Carrying around poisons. Please update us and make poisons into a skill/buff that we choose instead of having to take up space in our bags.

What makes playing your class more fun?

It is 'more' fun when I get to actually be a rogue instead of just another melee in the party/raid/bg. Unlocking treasure chests in dungeons, unlocking doors that present alternate routes, stealthing past mobs, sapping in PvP, incapacitating another player's movement to the point they are useless in a PvP skirmish. When someone hates me because i'm a rogue, that is when i'm having the most fun.

What makes playing your class less fun?

It is 'less' fun when I show up to raid early and get sat out because I bring nothing to the raid except my fairly adequate dps. It is less fun when a boss fight in a raid is designed in such a way that range dps can excel and shine to the fullest, but there aren't any bosses designed in a similar way where range must move around while melee can simply concentrate on doing dmg.
Furthermore, in comparison to other melee, we are the only pure dps class with, thats right, 3 dps trees. Whereas the warrior, paladin, death knight, shaman, and druid can not only bring buffs and possible offspecs to a raid or group composition, rogues currently bring nothing but another player to fill a slot. To add further injury to insult, it is overwhelming to see that the 'non-pure' melee dps classes listed above, often do MORE dps than the rogue on single-target. If that is the case, why would anyone bother to bring one to a raid? Why would anyone consider rolling a rogue for endgame PvE?
Every melee class listed above has the option to spec into a tank or healer, providing flexibility in group composition or at least bring a competitive amount of dps to the raid. But the rogue's only option IS to do damage. The rogue's offspecs ARE damage. Doesn't it make sense that the rogue should ALWAYS be at the top of melee dps. Doesn't it raise eyebrows or at least trigger some sort of silent alarms when the rogue, whose sole purpose is to provide melee dps, is considered bottom tier, unnecessary, optional class option for a raid composition?

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The rotation for each spec is fine, but it is insulting that most other melee classes were given 'proc' based bonus skills that could be triggered and easily identified with the new ingame 'power auras'.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

1. Give shadowstep to all rogues as a skill/ability. If going subtlety is the only way to enjoy shadowstep, then it is broken because only the smallest amount of rogues will play that spec (except for PvP).
2. Give rogues an ability to set traps (similar to hunters), but only visible by other rogues in stealth. What's the point of being able to disarm traps but not be able to employ any of them?
3. Give rogues an aoe that is competitive in damage. FoK is so undertuned that it suggests you are waiting to give rogues something better. How about dropping a poison canisters/vials that create deadly fumes to enemies standing near it?
4. Give rogues a 'cloaked/shadow' form similiar to shadow priests. This would be different than what cloak of shadows provides; giving instead a damage buff while 'cloaked/shadowed'. Something similar to what metamorphosis is to a demonology warlock or what tree form is to a restoration druid.
5. Let a rogue be more than it is now in the game. For goodness sake's, it is YOUR rogue. In the real world and fantasy nothing draws as much speculation and fascination as a silent assassin that can escape without notice. Update your rogue to a competitively fun class to play, let it be larger than life. To quote a recent popular movie, "...You mustn't be afraid to dream a little bigger, darling...".

What spells do you use the least?

Smoke Bomb, Shiv, Expose Armor, Redirect, Deadly Throw, Disarm Trap.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    • For Combat, the rotation is simplistic, boring, and spammy. Spamming Sinister Strike over and over is not fun. Due to Rupture's poor damage, it's competitive to drop it from the rotation all-together, making it even less engaging. Killing Spree should be changed to hit your current target only, since health pools are so big now that it's not very effective on multiple targets, meaning it spends alot of time off cooldown on boss fights, especially those that are add-heavy or have mechanics that don't agree with KS (Magmaw for instance). Energy capping is a serious issue for any end-game Combat rogue, rendering Adrenaline Rush useless during Heroism/Bloodlust, and leaving us GCD-locked. Cooldowns should be meaningful and should make you feel like a beast for however long they're up, and Adrenaline Rush currently fails in this respect.

    Assassination relies too much on poison damage, and since mastery was introduced, expertise has been greatly devalued which leads to cycle stability issues and IMO gives the rotation a very clunky feeling. If you can't get behind your target, you aren't doing optimal dps. If you aren't getting lucky with Venomous Wounds procs or if Rupture is down, energy regen goes right down the tubes, which makes the spec particularly weak on add-heavy fights like Beth'tilac (if you are assigned add duty). Again, too much of the spec's damage is passive.

    For Subtlety, juggling 4 finishers kills the spec's viability in PvE. Having either Slice, Rupture, or Recup fall off kills it's damage output. In the unfortunate event that 2 or even all 3 drop off at the same time, you have to struggle to get them all back up and rolling. An easy fix to this would be to tie Sub's Energetic Recovery to Slice n Dice rather than Recuperate. As it is right now, Sub rogues in PvE are rolling a self-healing ability 100% of the time in combat just to get more energy back, and to me from a design standpoint, that doesn't make a whole lot of sense.
