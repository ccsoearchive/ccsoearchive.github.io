What type of content do you focus on?

PVE raids primarily.

Quality of life issues

Poisons. Needing to dedicate 4+ bag slots to the variety of poisons required sucks.
Target swapping. We don't need to be great at it but we certainly need to be better than we are relative to other melee and even casters.
AOE. The FoK-uses-thrown-weapon experiment is a pretty big failure if you're not Assassination and Combat's perma-cleave falls flat once you pass 2 targets.

Why I enjoy playing a rogue

I like the frenetic pace of Combat. Hitting Adrenaline Rush and going to town with SS or using Killing Spree and jumping all over the place still entertains me.

What I don't enjoy about playing a rogue / What I feel about the rotation

These kind of go together. Overall, I find the predictability of a rogue's rotation to be boring. Even with RvS added to "break up" Combat's SS spam, it's still as mindless to hit at exactly 4 CPs as it would be to just hit SS. There's nothing to really react to; just spam, spam, spam, finisher. RvS is also a cheap band-aid to make our finishers hit relatively hard after the loss of armor pen and Bandit's Guile is so miserable that I just turned it off on my UI so I could forget how hamstrung I am when switching targets. I'd go as far to say that BG is a horrible talent in that it makes me want to be a selfish player and tunnel a target instead of helping the raid if I'm needed to switch; that's not the type of mindset it should promote. Also, pretty much every finisher outside of Envenom could use some love. For the most part, they're as plain and boring as the day they were created 6 years ago.

Wish list

More impressive finishers, less of a dependency on SnD or at least making SnD more interesting, Bandit's Guile not making me want to tunnel a target and ignore switching, Killing Spree being a little less lethal for the rogue and a way to make my OH look like something cooler than a dagger. On, and for our new abilities for the next xpac, could we get more than 1 that has use in PVE?

Least used skills

Expose Armor. I use it now but that's because we have no warriors or druids in our raids anymore. I still think it should be changed to be a personal Combat-specific finisher in lieu of using rupture.
Smoke Bomb, Combat Readiness, Deadly Throw. PVP-focused skills that are next to worthless in PVE.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
The biggest quality of life change I think would be one that addresses poisons. Yes, I know we've come a long way from having to brew our own, but improvement could still be made. A pull-out interface for poison application similar to how Hunters and Warlocks select their pets or Mages select their portals would be a very nice quality of life improvement. Stealth.

Having skills I use for PVE be nerfed because PVP needs balance. Example: Cloak of Shadows CD nerf and then further nerfed to share CD with Combat Readiness. I wish PVP had its own player mode spec with its own rules. This is hands down the one thing about the game that bothers me the most.

I mostly play Assassination because of the rotation. It's not too complicated and it's not too boring. Don't fix it; it's not broke.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1. Combo points on the player, not the target.
2. Throw to be usable while moving or alternatively, Deadly Throw to not require combo points.

What spells do you use the least?
Expose Armor, Deadly Throw

What type of content do you focus on?
Only PvP

If PvP, what type of PvP?
City PvP; mostly invading Stormwind and trying to gank any Alliance in Stormwind that happen to be PvP flagged (and preferably AFK). I also do BGs and Arenas.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Keep in mind these all have to do with PvP, not PvE:
1. Remembering to buy poisons. Why can't we just have them all the time?
2. Why do poisons have a 60 minute timer? I have to use an addon which reminds me when my poisons are about to expire. That doesn't seem right. Why can't the poison just stay on my weapon forever until I decide to change it?
3. If Kidney Shot misses, it still goes on full cooldown. Very frustrating. I think that's a situation where RNG is too much of a game changer.
4. Abilities which require being behind an opponent, such as Backstab, are tricky in PvP, due to network latency and how some players are always mouse turning very rapidly.
5. Being stunned 0.01 seconds after using PvP trinket. I would change the trinket so that you cannot be stunned for 2 seconds after using the trinket. I would make it so that if someone tried to stun you during those 2 seconds, then no cooldowns would be spent. Example: I use PvP trinket to get out of a stun. Opponent tries to Kidney Shot me 0.1 seconds later. His Kidney Shot would fail, but it would not go on cooldown.
6. If many of my abilities are on cooldown from a previous battle, I sometimes feel like I have to sit around waiting for cooldowns before I can do anything.
7. Way too many Stormwind Guards spawning out of thin air, even when plenty of human-controlled Alliance players are already on me. I think just 1-2 guards are enough; that'd keep me from using Vanish. Also, why do guards' melee/arrows need to hit for 5,000-6,000? I think City PvP would be more fun for both factions if it was up to human-controlled Alliance players to defend Stormwind, and not a gazillion computer-controlled guards.
8. Lack of Alliance players in other cities besides Stormwind. Ironforge used to be fun for me to invade before Cataclysm; now there's hardly any level 85 Alliance there. Darnassus and Exodar have never been worth invading due to lack of level 85 Alliance. I think it's because those Cataclysm portals are only available in Stormwind. If those portals were available in other cities, then there'd be more level 85 Alliance in those cities for me to try to gank!
9. When I die in Stormwind City, the ghost run from the east side of Elwynn Forest is way too long, even with the guild perk "The Quick and The Dead". Also, you have to ghost run from there if you die anywhere in Deeprun Tram; even if you die on the "Ironforge side" of the Tram.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Stealthing in Stormwind City and trying to gank level 85 Alliance players who happen to be PvP flagged. Especially if they happen to be AFK. It's challenging to pull off a gank, which makes it really satisfying when I pull it off. I would love to invade other cities too, but it seems like all the level 85 Alliance are in Stormwind.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Situations where I feel forced to "wait and stand around" as I wait for all my cooldowns.

Also, I wonder how people without addons/macros set up their Shadow Dance bar. You have to use Shadow Dance to get the bar to appear, then you only have 6 seconds to try to drag abilities onto that bar. If you can't get all your abilities on the bar in time, you have to wait another minute. It feels like addons/macros are mandatory to use Shadow Dance effectively.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1. Poisons being unlimited like hunter ammo. Poisons staying on your weapons forever until you decide to change poisons, instead of the pointless 60 minute timer.
2. When graveyard rezzing in a Battleground, cooldowns being reset or at least shortened.
3. Would be nice if Preparation also reset Cloak of Shadows.
4. Would anything bad happen if you removed those "truesight" elite guards from Stormwind? If you removed Officers Brady, Pomeroy, Connelly, Jaxon, etc. from Stormwind, would anything bad happen? It's frustrating to accidentally get too close to them.

Type of content you focus on: PvE, I feel PvP does not fit my personality and how I want to participate in the game.

Type of PvE: Questing. Raids. Dungeons. I've been raiding for ~4 years, but retired raiding for this expansion due to time constraints. I casually do heroic dungeons just to keep my gear up to pre-raid standards should things change.

Biggest quality-of-life issues: That vanish still doesn't work as intended all the time (firelands dailies are a big example of places where it has issues). I find that buying and applying poisons is annoying, though when I had to brew my own poisons I felt more engaged in the process and enjoyed applying them to my weapons - now that has changed, it feels like an old and outdated way to do things. I don't like that my stuns are put on a diminishing return by other classes while other classes don't have the same rules in place. Dying by killing spree, randomly falling through objects (bridges etc) and getting attacked by things 'too close'. Bandit's guile being a dps loss instead of a boost, as we're constantly switching targets and our redirect has such a long cooldown in comparison to when we actually need it in fights like that.

What makes playing your class more fun? The combo-point system. I don't have to deal with mana and cast times or a complicated system like runes or pally's power stuff or constantly lose my resource like warriors. I'm like an energizer bunny, I can keep going and going. I have ways to get myself out of trouble if I don't want to deal with a random attacker. I can steal money and objects, which is amusing (I keep a snuggly starfish in my bank because it's such a fun item). I can stealth around and discover things people normally couldn't. I enjoy being able to heal myself instead of relying on bandages. Shadow Stepping. If I could shadowstep in all specs, I'd be a happy girl. It's so iconic and fun, and such a good way to keep our damage up since we'd be on the next target quicker than running there. I love that I'm actively pushing buttons with this class rather than waiting for cast times or passive damage and certain procs, it makes me feel like I'm more in control and that my character is actively participating.

What makes playing your class less fun? The fluctuation in PvE viability. We're high damage, then we're low, then we're middle, then we're low, then we're high. It's annoying. For raiding, it feels like we're dating a bi-polar schizophrenic. I get slightly depressed when we're not near the top of raid damage, but other dps-only classes are. When hybrid classes out do us on a regular basis, I feel like we're being rejected by the dev team. No one likes being sat on progression because you're middle of the pack or lower and you don't have any specs available other than dps. Class mechanics don't make me upset, but the fluctuation in desirability of the class makes me upset and not have fun.

Our tier gear I find to be a problem. Our bonuses are stuck on ToTT for some reason, or weird procs. The look of a rogue is supposed to be sleek and stealthy. I hide my helm constantly, shoulders are a clunky embarrassment and why do we have bright colors? I feel like it is detrimental to the class, all these weird huge armor designs. The set bonuses I've felt have not been particularly useful for 3 expansions. It's not overwhelmingly desirable to have a set bonus in this xpac - sure, it's desirable, but not Overwhelmingly desirable which I think is a design flaw.

How do you feel about your rotation? As combat, I like it. It keeps me active and has a lot of variation on when cooldowns are used. It feels like I'm putting out enough effort as a player to get decent results, right amount of work for the right amount of damage.
