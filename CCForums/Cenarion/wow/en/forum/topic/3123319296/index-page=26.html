PvE Subtlety - Serious Raider

while sub is constantly considered to be a pvp only spec, but there are people out there (like me) who would prefer its unique play style - being a 1-on-1 single target focused spec.

but ever since the big nurf on sub (because of the QQ in pvp - which DOES affect pve blizz why won't you listen ><) a sub rogue's life has become an ever downward-going spiral. i mean, seriously? i'm doing the most complex and technical rotation known to roguekind (tricks on tank > recoup > premed > sstep > ambush > hemo > rupture > bstab x5 > s&d > bstab x4 > hemo > evis > bstab x5 > recoup - and then repeat) just to maintain that poor 22k at ilvl 370, while an assassination rogue at ilvl 360 can easily par (and sometime OUT dps) me by spamming mut and envenom. the balance is way off, and pve'ers nowadays will generally look down on sub rogues (and the few times that i got kicked from dungeon groups and raids just because of my spec).


also another QoL issue is that fan of knives requires a Thrown weapon. this alone killed the ONLY AoE for sub rogues who want better stats. we only get one thrown weapon from Valor Points, which has Hit and Expertise on it. but what if people (like me) who want other, more beneficial stats? like crit and haste from the lava bolt xbow (which is what i'm using - as my protest to this issue - despite losing the ability to use FoK). i feel like i no longer have options, i feel like i'm forced to use what everyone else is using, for better or worse. everything in the game now has been streamlined to make EVERYONE the same. there's no more fun/ways to be unique.

Combat Readiness is the thing that i use least. putting it on same cd as cloak only made it worse. people still choose to save that cd for cloak for it's diversity.

also Expose Armour should be in the spotlight a bit more. the concept for it is really nice, but the cost for it (as a combo finisher for that poor 12% by-pass) is simply not worth it.

as for a wish list? well here it goes:

Redirect has had its cooldown reduced to 10 seconds! Rogues now should feel less penalized when they switch targets. Have fun in PvE!

Expose Armour has been redesigned. It is now a Combo Point generator that does no damage, but puts a stacking debuff on the target that allows the attacking rogue to by-pass its armour by 3% per stack. Stacks up to 5 times and lasts 30 seconds.

Fan of Knives now no longer requires a Thrown Weapon to be equipped. It now does 50% off-hand weapon damage. But if a Thrown Weapon is equipped it will deal 100% Thrown Weapon damage instead.

The Combat perk, Vitality's 25% bonus to Energy regeneration is now a new Passive you can learn from your class trainer at Level 85. Although Combat rogues are still the only ones that receive the 30% bonus to Attack Power.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    Both, though I spent more time on PvE content, I enjoy both styles of play.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Raiding.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    Arenas and Battlegrounds. I would like to do rated BGs but I am waiting for my guild/friends to get something together.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    General poison maintenance is the most annoying and boring part of my class, especially my specialization. As assassination, you are severely punished for using anything but instant poison and deadly poison, so applying these requires no thought and is just something done every so often. Other poisons that might end up helpful sit in my backpack taking up room. Poison has no measurable cost in purchasing it, so it has become a rote exercise in memory and inventory management. Changing each poison into a spell, and reforming poison to have both a primary effect (instant damage or damage over time) and a secondary effect (movement speed slow, cast slow, healing debuff, etc.) would largely alleviate this quality of life burden.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    In general, being able to do great damage to a boss with a calculated, precise rotation is plenty of fun. However, anytime a rogue's unique abilities are able to be showcased- such as trap disarms for Shannox/ICC/BWL, tricks to a hatchling tank on Alysrazor, smoke bomb on...whenever you'll finally allow us to use smoke bomb on a serious fight- fun will increase.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Gimmicky boss mechanics that either remove melee from the equation, make it excessively difficult to function, or dampen damage considerably. Rhyolith is a good example of this, delegating melee damagers to stressful leg-steering duty without so much as the benefit of being able to do noticeable damage for a large portion of the fight. These kinds of fights are interesting to talk about, but in practice lose their sense of wonder after only a few attempts.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I am throughly content with the assassination rotation. It's concepts are easy enough to convey, but mastering it is a rewarding and entertaining endeavor. I would make no serious changes to it's rotation at this time- though, I wouldn't mind some tweaks to Cold Blood and Vendetta. Cold Blood is a languishing holdout from the old burst-heavy assassination tree, and it's use is largely out of rote numbers than interest. Vendetta is infinitely more interesting than it's successor Hunger for Blood, but suffers from it's single-target status. I would suggest reducing the cooldown of Vendetta if the target dies while the buff is still active, and making Cold Blood more interesting of a cooldown in general.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    I've revealed a few of my wishes in my remarks above. To summarize: Comprehensive poison reform, and tweaking the assassination cooldowns a bit. I would also like to reveal a couple more in the following question.

    What spells do you use the least?
    While I use smoke bomb a lot in PvP, it enjoys almost no effect in a vast majority of all raiding encounters. While I realize full use of it would be overpowering in some unforseen situations, it would be nice to have it work the way it says it works at least some of the time while raiding. However, my least used spell is Combat Readiness. Yolked to the cooldown of Cloak, it is now a 'you lose' button when pressed in a vast majority of arena, and even unrated battleground, encounters. In PvE it was almost never used to begin with as it is. It was sold to the public as something to press when you are being focused in PvP, but this is no longer useful except against a team of warriors that feature no other living classes. I would see it completely redone, changed into something completely different.

    For me, poisons are a complete hassle. I have to constantly buy new ones, I have to use a mod to tell me when they fall off and even then I sometimes fail to notice and they drop off mid boss fight, causing horrendous dps loss until I manage to reapply them. I think poison variety should be maintained, but perhaps we could switch poisons over to trained abilities rather than purchased materials. This would be especially nice since I currently have to guess whether or not my poisons will run out in a boss fight given their current time remaining. I've often had to reapply with 10m remaining on my previous application to avoid the risk of losing poisons mid combat. If poisons were trained abilities rather than purchased materials it'd be no big deal to reapply before every single boss attempt and the issue of them falling off would be gone.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    I love the fast paced button mashing style of my particular spec.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Well other than the poison issue I mentioned before, more than one raid encounter forces you into a position where you can't be behind the boss at least some of the time. This only causes parries for my spec, which are annoying and lead to energy capping, but for assassin this means a huge dps loss due to the inability to backstab effectively during the execution phase. We should be behind our targets most of the time at the very least. Mechanics that force us in front of the boss on occasion are great, but bosses like domo where we have to spend half our time in his face.... no thank you.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    Mine is a little simple, but it works well with the incredibly fast paced button mashing style of my spec. It can also change up slightly depending on raid composition and fight mechanics so I never feel bored or overwhelmed with it.
