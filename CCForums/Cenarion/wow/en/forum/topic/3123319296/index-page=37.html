What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

1) Targets dying and wasting all those combo points, e.g. killing the cats on staghelm is very cumbersome. I feel like the paladin "combo point" system is less frustrating since you will always get to use your Templar's Verdict finisher. I feel like I miss out on the fun part of the rotation when I spend so much time building up combo points and don't even get to use a finisher because the target already died.

2) Super small hitboxes on targets. The frost DK that I raid with find ourselves cursing at the Fireland developers who "have clearly never played melee before" when I literally have to be directly on top of something to be in range.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Stealth, managing cooldowns, juggling blade flurry, poison utility, ToT utility, managing damage mitigating abilities like feint and cloak.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Wasting combo points (see above), any encounter where "we need more range dps" really infuriates me. There needs to be a compelling reason to bring melee dps or you might as well just remove them entirely. Lich King was really bad about this, but I think it has gotten better with Cataclysm although there are still a few exceptions (try doing Cho'gall with too many melee).

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For assassination, losing combo points when a target dies can make the rotation very painful when there is a lot of target switching because you're constantly fighting to save enough energy to envenom to refresh slice n dice. But if the target dies they're just wasted.

For combat, I feel like I'm going to break my keyboard smashing sinister strike or revealing strike during adrenaline rush because I just can't use the energy fast enough. Every GCD ability that doesn't use energy feels like a total waste like tricks or feint.


What’s on your wish list for your class?

Since rogues are limited by energy, I think there are times when removing or diminishing the GCD on a few abilities would not be overpowering and would make the class less frustrating. Removing the GCD on sinister/revealing strike during adrenaline rush would be nice.

Assassination feels limited when you have to switch targets a lot because of the ramp up time on deadly poison. Also, while I think Vendetta is a really cool ability, it also suffers when there is target switching involved. It feels similar to warrior death wish, but just more limited. Any reason it can't just mimic death wish some?

What spells do you use the least?

expose armor, combat readiness, shiv, never use deadly throw in pve, rarely use smoke bomb in pve

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Heroics mostly. I was a hardcore raider in previous xpacs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

Poisons I feel should be abilities more similar to shaman weapon enchants. You've taken us from it being its own profession to simply buying it, Thieves Tools are gone, Blinding Powder and Flash Powder gone.

Be nice if Recuperate would tick once immediately on using it

What makes playing your class more fun?

Sneaking around and pickpocketing things. The cool little pickpocket treasures like the rogues diary and the romance novels.

What makes playing your class less fun?

In the name of 'fairness' you've taken away all reason for the banditry part of being a rogue. We don't steal anything aside from pickpocketing. Why be sneaky when combat rogues, while admittedly are closer to fighter archetypes than traditional rogues, can just sprint in and blade flurry everything down

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For Assassination it feels a little slow, but its also more involved than Combat. Subtlety I know is not intended to be a pve spec, but its max dps rotation is hideously complex compared to the other two

Revealing Strike I feel should be more powerful, either as an individual strike or it being a 'sixth combo point.' I'm honestly OK with Rupture not being important for Combat

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Raid utility. Rogues bring pretty much every kind of debuff to the table but it costs much more resources than other classes. Applying the 12% armor pen debuff for example is something a druid can do in 1 global cooldown, a prot warrior does it with devastate, a hunter pet can apply it at global cooldown costs.

I feel that making Shiv apply the poison on your thrown weapon instead of your offhand would make rogues much more willing to use Mind-Numbing, Wound or Crippling Poison.

Perhaps, introduce a new Glyph of Shiv that makes it extend the duration of Expose Armor significantly.

It makes sense for rogues to not bring any buffs to the table, but I feel we should be able to bring any debuff. A new kind of poison that applies the -10% physical damage debuff like demoralizing shout or vindication, etc.

Redirect I feel should be received a loot sooner, as well as Tricks of the Trade so people will actually use them.

What spells do you use the least?

Disarm Trap. Remove the 'Requires Stealth' aspect and increase the cast time by half a second.

Shiv. I've only used it for two things in the last year. Dispelling Mind Control in the Kael encounter and dispelling Enrage from the Lynx boss in ZA.

Smoke Bomb. Accomplishes absolutely nothing in PvE as you can't prime it and it lasts far too short a time. In PvP its easily defeated by any class that it should be useful against. Make it do significant poison damage, an aoe disorient, something.

Combat Readiness and Cloak should not share a cooldown. Cloak is far too powerful to give up, especially when Vanish will usually take care of any problem Combat Readiness would help with.

Make Dismantle useful in PvE. Mobs lose very little to no damage while disarmed.

Deadly Throw is useless in PvE. Give it a 20 second cooldown, cost 30 energy, a 100% chance to apply the Thrown Weapon poison and deal 100% thrown damage

Killing Spree needs work. Pathing I know has never been the strong point. Just make it reduce swing timers to near nothing for 5 swings on both weapons and a damage bonus, along with the CC block

Rework backstab for combat so it transforms into a combo point-less energy dump. If the manual combo idea is too taxing, re-work that into just number of strikes rather than order. 3 SS and 2 RS = instant 5 = point rupture off the GCD so you don't have to worry about the finishers which were never all that exciting for combat to begin with. This also makes revealing strike more interesting to use rather than applying that passive buff.

    That kind of change to combat would make it far more exciting to play. Rather severe and intense but I would look forward to that kind of individuality for the class.

    Quality Of Life Concerns:
    There really isn't much here for me that's not about how a spec plays. I know a number of Rogues don't like having to buy their poisons, and that we're the last spec to have to "buy DPS", but it really doesn't bug me. Then again, maybe I'm just conditioned to deal with it with years of playing this character. I don't think learning a spell like Shaman or making poisons permanent DK enchants would be a decent alternative due to the immersion factor, but there's probably a middle ground somewhere.

    I'm not a fan of positional requirements. Now, I'm not referring to the positional requirement of things like Backstab, but the requirement for Sub's and Mutilate's cooldowns. A requirement gives abilities some added flare, and gives good rogues a chance to squeeze out a little more DPS like, for instance, Al'Akir. Mutilate doesn't gain too much from using Vanish on CD, but cooldowns are kind of a big deal for Sub. Therefore, it seems fair that rogues should be able to cast Ambush while in front of their target in some capacity due to the spec's reliance on good CD usage. Be it while Shadow Dance is up, a major glyph, talent, whatever. Using Shadow Dance / Vanish optimally requires enough planning as it is, expecting players to cope with bosses turning for whatever reason and maybe even losing a cooldown is more annoying than it is interesting flare.

    I've tried to like Killing Spree, I really have. But it's one of the few abilities in game that can get you killed, even when it should be a perfectly valid use. There needs to be something worked out here, I don't care too much what it is, but anything would be better than what it is now. There's just too many problems with it in my opinion.

    This one is going to be a little strange, and I very well might be in the minority here, but I don't like it when my MH weapon is used for a cap-able stat. For example, Gatecrasher has expertise, which I have to consider to get to my expertise cap, yet if I switch specs, I won't be at the same place in my cap-able stats since my new MH most likely won't have the same amount of expertise.

    Making Rogues Fun:
    I enjoy decisions, but I much rather enjoy timing my abilities outside of being GCD locked. It should then come as no surprise to hear that I enjoy playing Assassination and Subtlety a lot more than I enjoy playing Combat, Sub a little more so than Assassination.

    Combat Rogues are somewhere in the realm of 45-50 actions per minute right now. I don't think they're at the top of the APM chart, but they are -very- high up there. Combat also deals with ridiculous amounts of energy capping. It's perfectly fine for specs to change their stat priorities over time, but Combat still biases toward haste simply because of how the spec works. Haste is worth too much, and Combat has to deal with the GCD cap and energy capping more than any rogue spec probably should. For that reason, I would suggest either retuning Adrenaline Rush so it's less likely to be an instantaneous energy cap, or reducing vitality and converting it into finisher or CP generator damage.

    Combat could also use some depth to it. It certainly has complexity, but it's almost in vain because of how much it actually impacts your DPS. The usage of Revealing Strike and Rupture is nearly irrelevant, and some Rogues just drop them from the rotation all together for the sake of simplicity. Some abilities just don't matter enough. It seems that Bandit's Guile could be more interesting if it were tied to Rupture, rather than SS & RvS. This would also probably reduce cleave damage, but I'll leave it at that since this talk would diverge from "Making Rogues Fun". Combat just needs to have the rotation matter.

    Assassination isn't too bad, but it's very simple, very, very simple. There should be something else to manage here (or maybe a couple things), since Envenom, Rupture, Vanish, Vendetta and arguably Slice and Dice are not much to track at all. It's a pretty dull spec. It's slow which allows you to focus on the encounter at hand, but it definitely lacks complexity or tricks that would make it a hard spec to master, which means it's both not rewarding, and fails to differentiate good rogues from average ones.

    Sub is pretty close to what I want in a spec. It falters with any cleave or AOE moment though, which is not fun. Sub has one of, if not the worst AOE in the game. And Sub and Mutilate could both use some form of cleave. Assassination can get two Ruptures up at once for some added damage, but Sub is left out in the cold for cleave. I'll get back to FOK in a moment though, again.



    Wish List:
    -Poisons need to be 200% damage crits. They're one of the last remaining spell abilities in the game that does 50% more damage, and it's the biggest reason why Mutilate despises crit rating.
    -Fan of Knives needs to stop proc'ing poisons on the thrown weapon. Poisons are not AOE capped, and the damage from FOK is therefore horrendous and requires an obscene number of targets to become a decent AOE. Removing the poison gives non-Mutilate Rogues a chance to actually have a decent AOE.
    -Buff thrown weapon stats. Having to use Hunter weapons to achieve optimal single target DPS is somewhat annoying, especially since we're forced to use a thrown for FOK.
    -Convert Deadly Poison into something more akin to Ignite, in the process reduce ramp up time for DP, and buff IP (and maybe WP) damage in the process.



    Least Used Spells:
    Blind, Expose Armor, Combat Readiness, Smoke Bomb, Shiv, Kidney Shot + Cheap Shot, Gouge.
    Enrages are rare so Shiv is neglected, only Sinestra has had a real use for Gouge, Kidney Shot + Cheap Shot are really only used on Heroic Ragnaros for me, Smoke Bomb tends to get ignored by most encounter mechanics, Expose Armor is redundant, Blind is never used, and Combat Readiness has drastically less utility than Cloak of Skill.
