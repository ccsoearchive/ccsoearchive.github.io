What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

- PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

- Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

- N/A

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

- Poison Management could be better, as with other comments. Having to use up space in bags to do something while other classes (e.g. Death Knight, Enhance Shaman) have other ways to do something similar.
- Unglyphed Pick Lock cast time of 5 seconds, while trivial, seems to take forever when you have a few lockboxes to open.

What makes playing your class more fun?

- Providing consistently good DPS for the raid group, while having abilities to actively mitigate damage to stay alive (e.g. Feint, Cloak of Shadows) and being able to position myself quickly with Sprint.

What makes playing your class less fun?

- Ramp up time being too long, primarily due to the Combo Point mechanic (target based) and the long stack time of Deadly Poison, even as Assassination (when considering the "quick target switch"-type fights).
- Combo points being target based.
- Having hybrid classes do more or less similar damage.
- The penalty for switching targets is too high; limited burst damage and high ramp up time being the main contributing factors, particularly as Combat with Bandit's Guile being a target based debuff (and not a self buff).
- On that note, Redirect is handy, but the cooldown is too long in PvE for it to negate the target switching issues.
- Fan of Knives mechanic; it takes too long to ramp up as Assassination and is useless in Combat or Subtlety.
- Cooldown usage for both Assassination and Combat aren't regular enough to break up the rotation.
- Killing Spree kills me if I use it at "inappropriate" times.
- The effect of Parry on overall DPS is too high, particularly frustrating on fights where being behind the enemy is not always an available option.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

- Assassination rotation is reasonable, but could do with a bit more energy regeneration to spice things up a bit.
- Combat rotation seems rushed, particularly during Adrenaline Rush. Revealing Strike seemed like a good idea, but is simply an extra button to press when you're at 4 CP.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Combo Points to be attached to the rogue, not the target.
- Quicker ramp up time.
- Ability to continue to do at least a reasonable amount of damage when target switching.
- Ability to give more buffs to the raid.

What spells do you use the least?

Deadly Throw, Gouge, Dismantle, Smoke Bomb, Combat Readiness (since shared cooldown with Cloak of Shadows)
