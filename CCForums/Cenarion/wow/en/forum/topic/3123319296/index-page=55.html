I'm not posting on my rogue, but I do have an 85 and a 33 rogue. I loved combat and I love assassination.

I PvP a lot. (made the rogue to "get some of my own back" - was getting tired of the sap and ambush in BG's)

I do a little raiding, but mostly PvE and solo dungeons and quests, getting some acheivements.

My 85 rogue is currently at ilvl 362, and is a JC, so she is pretty well-geared and I understand how to play the class. Combat is easier, but does less damage, it seems to me, and assassination is great but all I hear when I'm fighting is "I don't have enough energy I don't have enough energy I don't have enough energy I don't have enough energy I don't have enough energy I don't have enough energy I don't have enough energy." It gets old quick, and in a 5- minute fight I might hear it well over 100 times. A small energy reduction cost to Mutilate might be very helpful.

Wishlist: Better poisons - or poisons that scale more - or a different set of poisons for up to lvl 25, lvl 25-50, and lvl 75+, like the glyphs occur - that have more powerful qualities. It would also be nice to not have to apply every hour - perhaps an inexpensive alchemist potion or a buff food that would make them last 2 or 3 hours, that would not interfere with other flasks or potions?

I pretty much never use shiv, mind-numbing poison. I don't use wound poison a lot either, although i did at lower levels. There are a couple of others I didn't even bother to drag to my spellbar, because they just aren't worth taking up the space.

The rotation is fine - but perhaps things like rupture and slice 'n'dice could last a bit longer - trying to keep track of keeping up those two, recuperation on myself, and envenom on my target are a LOT to keep track of, for how fast they wear off, and how hard it is to regen energy to get more points on and get them back up. Again, if you don't wish to reduce the cost of mutilate or buff its damage, perhaps consider allowing it to proc a continuation of one or more of those spells we have to keep up on targets - or recuperate. Lengthen the time of envenom, not just 5 seconds.

You could perhaps ditch having to purchase poisons altogether if you made them part of the spellbook. At lvl 10 you learn one, at lvl 14 another, along with the regular spells, and so on, until you've learned perhaps 12 different poisons, or different levels of the same poisons. (Deadly Poison IV, Instant Posion VI, etc. kind of like Toughness or Master of Anatomy)

I think also the thing that most frustrated me was that I did not get an AoE until I believe lvl 75 - Fan of Knives. Blade Flurry just isn't the same, and neither is killing spree. That would've been a nice spell to have learned around level 45-50, and have it scale with the character from the on up. The only other problem I see with that spell is again - energy regen. For the damage it does, cost should be lower. I know it's not part of the regular rotation, but it might be interesting to let it have similar effects to Blizzard and Multi-Shot and Hurricane, especially in large mobs. I often hesitate to use it because of the inevitable "I don't have enough energy I don't have enough energy"

The energy I guess then is my biggest issue. On the one hand I relize you don't want me comparing classes, but my hunter doesn't spend all her time saying "I dont' have enough focus I don't have enough focus" nor my mage "I dont have enought mana I dont' ahve enough Mana". They DO say that a little bit into the fight, but my rogue is saying it 6 seconds into the fight, and it becomes a Zen chant, as it never lets up for the remiander of the fight, between trying to get enough combo points to do something, and then trying ro regen enough energy to actually execute the spell. If it's a spell that requires combo points - rupture, Slice 'n'dice, recuperation, envenom, expose armor - if you HAVE the combo points availoable you should be able to execute the spell, not wait around to be able to hit the target. Other spells that do not require combo points need the energy (Dismantle, for instance). I get very frustrated, I've built up 5 CP, and now I can just stand around waiting for the energy to get high enough to actually DO what it is I need to. That might be the most frustrating thing about playing this class - the constant waiting, while everyone else around me is hitting away, and I'm having a quick break waiting to be able to do my next hit.

Hope this was helpful. I love my rogue, and I pretty much love the class as is, but a few tweaks might make it way more fun to play!

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

I primarily focus on PvE content, though friends have recently gotten me to PvP.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

I raid more than anything else, specifically current content, though I enjoy going back to the older raids for neat lookin' gear. I do heroics as needed, more so on alts than on my rogue.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

When I PvP, I usually do so with friends. We do BGs and some Arena, but no Rated BGs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Many people have stated issues with poisons, but coming from the days where rogue poisons had to be crafted (which I liked) and had charges (which I didn't like), I'm happy with how they are now.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Stealth, escape mechanics, being resource limited rather than GCD limited. A whole host of things make playing a rogue tons of fun for me. I like the aesthetic of the abilities and the general feel of the class. I like how important timing is for our DPS. I've played a rogue since early in Vanilla, and I've always loved the class. There's always more to learn about rogues.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Vanish not working properly and you guys telling us it works as intended, haha. Caster-clysm has been a little irritating, though 4.2 isn't quite as hard on melee as the first raid tier this expansion. Having my crits on finishing moves be jokes is really irritating.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Since I play Subtlety for PvE content, I *love* my rotation. I actually have one and I find it engaging and rewarding when I do it right. Combat and Assassination barely have a rotation to speak of, which is why I don't really play them anymore. I keep a combat spec simply for utility.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

1. Vanish. There are so many times this accursed ability fails and it's incredibly irritating. When your survival hinges on an ability working and it fails, it sucks all the fun out of it. You eventually become jaded and cynical, expecting it to fail even before you push the button.

2. Abilities need to have caveats for PvP, rather than trying to adjust the game for both. It's been done with Colussus Smash and CC abilities, it can be done for everything presumably.

3. Killing Spree needs to kill the enemy more than the rogue, that'd be nice.

4. Cloak of Shadows and Combat Readiness need to be on seperate cooldowns again. Having them like they are makes you almost never hit Combat Readiness and completely wastes one of our newest abilities.

5. Cold Blood needs to give some kind of short damage boost, like 10% damage for 5 seconds or so after you use it. It's a really boring ability.

6. Combat needs to receive some kind of damage bonus for capping energy while in Adrenaline Rush. Energy capping while that's up is extremely hard to prevent.

7. I want the Combat tree to have something akin to the Druid Feral tree, where we have a possible tank spec mixed in. Rogue tanking would be incredibly stylish and fun.

8. Combo Points stacking on the rogue rather than the target would be wonderful, and would alleviate some of the target switching issues.

9. Deadly Poison needing to stack to 5 is another thing I would like to see change, since it would help shorten our insanely long ramp up time.

10. Finshers for Subtlety really need to hit harder if the spec is going to focus on them. My Backstabs typically hit harder than my Eviscerates. Rupture also is barely noticable, I'd like to see it get a 100% damage increase as Sub.

11. I'd like to see Shadowstep become baseline, to give Assassination and Combat more mobility, especially if Combat were to have a tanking tree built into it. (Which I really really hope for)

12. I would like for fast daggers to be marked as Off Hand, and for those items to be able to transmog to any other weapon type. This would allow us to appear as though we're dual wielding swords, maces, axes or fists without actually having to itemize for these. We know this capability is there, since it's allowed with guns, bows, and crossbows. Also, if they don't have to be Off Hand only, that'd be nice.
This is one I'd really love for people to quote me on and push.

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    • For me, it is all about positioning and Mob longevity. Sub Rogues take a HUGE DPS hit from having to fight a boss in the front. Our entire rotation is based on Backstab. Trivialize that and our DPS plummets. The buff to Hemo is nice, but it really doesn't address the base issue.

    We have to worry about being dodged and parried. That is an aweful lot of Expertise. And frankly we just don't have the item budget to spare. We have to stack haste so much to keep up with the high cost of our rotation that Expertise quickly goes by the wayside.

    And then there is boss longevity... we have to do a LOT of setup before ramping up our full dps. Ambush, Backstab, SnD, Backstab x2, Hemo, Rupture, Backstabx2, Recoup. And that is all needed BEFORE I start doing competitive DPS. I tend to not even bother to come out of stealth with small mobs or low hp multi packs (but that also has to do with abysmal AoE dps... FoK spam ftw....)

  • What makes playing your class more fun?

    • The ability to chose our targets, the mobility and the utility my particular spec brings to a raid.
  • What makes playing your class less fun?

    • No real AoE. And this next thing is more of a general Melee thing than strickly a Rogue thing.

    We tend to be punished more for minor mistakes in positioning than ranged. If a ranged gets in front of a boss/mob? Changes are no big deal. Melee gets in front? We are doing the death crawl. Also we get caught in Tank Spiking moves a lot. AoE spells, whirlwinds, Cave ins, etc... These force us to run out (loss of DPS), or Die (BIG loss of DPS). These effects tend to no bother Ranged as much. An AoE spell on ranged means they just move a bit and go right on DPSing. Less loss of DPS over melee.

    It makes people not want to bring us.

    • I am of two minds about our rotation. One it is complex, which keeps me interested (I HATED the combat rotation of Vanilla and BC. Mindless to the extreme.) and it separates the good Rogues from the bad ones.

    On the other hand, the complexity of the rotation limits our effectiveness in small hp, multiple mob pulls.
