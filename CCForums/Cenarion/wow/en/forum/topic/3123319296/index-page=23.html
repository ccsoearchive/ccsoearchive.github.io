    Part II
    What makes playing your class less fun?
  • Too much passive damage. It was said this would be reduced come Cataclysm, but passive damage is still a significant chunk our our damage output for every spec. While I don't necessarily mind it being a part of it, I think it is currently way too much as active damage is more fun and allows for players to distinguish themselves.


  • Rogues are currently too dependent on Agility as a stat. It would be nice to see it scaled back a bit some of that scaling rolled into some of the secondary stats to allow us to make some more interesting choices with our gearing.


  • Cooldown variety and design. With a couple of exceptions, our cooldowns are currently badly designed, situational, or just boring. For example, I like the idea of Killing Spree, but there are more fights where I find it annoying than fun; specifically, there are fights where it cannot be used at proper times due to certain mechanics preventing it, which is a shame because it takes away some of the enjoyment of said mechanics as well as in the use of the ability or spec. Additionally, Adrenaline Rush, although a good idea for a cooldown, is scaled badly at the time. I feel as if it should at least be able to be used during Bloodlust/Heroism or in combination with haste buffs without making it impossible not to cap energy. Possibly reducing the increase and cooldown of the ability could help this problem. Shadowstep doesn't work on Heroic Rag until Phase 4. Further, I feel Vanish shouldn't be used as an offensive cooldown as Assassination as I like having it up to perform utility for the raid (i.e. Cheap Shotting Sons of Flame on Rag).


  • Target swapping for the specs is absolutely ridiculous, especially when there are ranged classes that can swap in ~3 seconds and be at full DPS capability on a target. While I don't like comparing classes, I think that something needs to be done with this area for Rogues. Currently, in order to target switch you lose BG, DP, CPs, Rupture (or any other debuff you have to apply), and you inherently lose energy from having to reapply these things. Whatever it is, something needs to be done about this.


  • The class does lack the ability to control its burst when necessary, perhaps increasing the damage done at higher combo points but also increasing the relative energy cost would be something to look into in this regard. Thereby allowing us to choose when to unleash the extra damage and having energy management be a more important skill, at least at higher levels of raiding.



    • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    Assassination:
  • I mentioned this earlier, but Assassination currently has too much passive damage, a good chunk of this damage could be rolled into active sources.

  • I like the execute mechanic with Backstab and its associated Energy regen through Murderous Intent; however...

  • Being behind bosses can suck at times. While I understand and like the mechanic (to an extent) it's currently troublesome on some encounters.

  • Vanish as a DPS cooldown should be somehow reworked. I like having Vanish available for utility.

  • I like the idea of Mutilate being used to generate multiple combo points at a higher energy use in a lower energy regen spec, but I do think the rotation could be built more around the energy management aspect than it is currently. Essentially, a strategic decision to hit a button rather than, say, Combat which is built more around keeping you active with hitting buttons in your rotation.

    Part III
    Combat:
  • Bandit's Guile currently penalizes Rogues more than they already are with target swapping and is a frustrating mechanic on fights where you can't utilize Deep Insight. I propose making Bandit's Guile relative to the Rogue instead of the target and finding a way to make its application more in control of the player (something similar to the Boomkins' Eclipse has been suggested), if this happened, it would be a perfect place to change Combat's current passive Mastery.

  • Currently, Combat is a tad bit spammy overall. While I would like more control over distinguishing myself from other Rogues through my rotation decisions, I do like there being a Rogue spec that utilizes high energy regeneration in its rotation, as I believe it creates diversity of gameplay among the specs.

  • Rupture is not strong enough at the moment. I'd prefer Rupture being a DPS increase regardless of whether or not there's a bleed debuff on the target as opposed to it being only a 1% DPS increase under ideal circumstances. Ultimately, Combat's too focused on Eviscerate as its finisher, moving Rupture more strongly into the spec would be one step toward getting away from this pitfall.

  • Killing Spree does deserve a change, as mentioned earlier.

  • AR is so powerful it can't be used in combination with Bloodlust/Heroism without energy capping; in fact, even without another haste buff, AR can cap with a string of Combat Potency procs. Even if it is a DPS loss, it does feel better to line up my cooldowns with Heroism rather than have to wait until the raid-wide burn period is over to pop them.

  • Blade Flurry is good in that it provides a nice niche for Rogues, and while it is a bit powerful in its current state, it would be nice to keep a high up-time on it rather than see it go back to low up-time high potency model.

  • Revealing Strike doesn't add as much to the rotation as it really could.


    • Subtlety:
  • Slightly too involved for most fights, while it's fun on a fight like Baleroc, many fights just don't support the ramp up time involved.

  • Shadowstep has similar problems as Killing Spree on some fights to the point it is literally unusable on Ragnaros in phases 1-3 without going into his pool.

  • Shadow Dance has an interesting design, but I think it could be more involved. Unfortunately, I don't have many good suggestions on this one.

  • Vanish shouldn't be a DPS cooldown. While I understand its use as a DPS cooldown more in Subtlety than Assassination, I would prefer it not being that way for either spec.
    I already mentioned being behind bosses, but I figured I'd reiterate it one more time as it's even more important for Subtlety.



    • What’s on your wish list for your class?
  • AoE viability is currently all over the place between the specs. While certain specs being better at AoE than other specs is perfectly acceptable, all specs should at least be able to do some type of AoE when required.

  • Fan of Knives should be moved back to MH and OH weapons. Currently, in order to optimize one's gear-set, a Rogue has to collect a Bow and a Thrown Weapon which also happen to be itemized differently enough that the reforging changes entirely when switching between weapons (for example on a fight that requires Fan of Knives as opposed to a fight that doesn't). It would be nice to have the main weapons we go after also scale our AoE and allow us to collect one type of ranged weapon and subsequently stop forcing us to swap sets of gear on a per fight basis.



  • Mastery should be fixed to be more interesting for the specs and, hopefully, also be more centralized to the spec's primary 'feel' or purpose, and it should present interesting gear choices as well as change the way you play the spec or, at least, scale from something that the player is actually involved with in their rotation.
    1. Combat's Mastery (Main Gauche) - is a perfect example of this. Instead of designing the mastery in such a way that it acts on its own, a better solution would be roll it into Bandit's Guile so that it increases the Bandit's Guile increase by a certain percent allowing differentiation in cooldown use across levels of mastery or depending on the amount of burst you need. Alternatively, it could be rolled into cooldowns either making them stronger in a way or possibly allowing the cooldowns to give a damage buff for at time after their completion that scales with the mastery. I'm merely brainstorming here, but anything that requires us to play differently or at least react to our mastery in some way, even with gearing, would be better than what it is now.

    Part IV
    2. Assassination's Mastery (Potent Poisons) - While I like the idea of it scaling with poisons, it's also a completely passive mastery. At the very least, doing something like Priests have with their Divine Aegis and Mastery scaling would be instesting; for example, our mastery increases our critical strike damage bonus of poisons instead of a flat modifier to them, which would allow crit to be somewhat more useful for the spec and have an interesting twist on the mastery's application.

    3. Subtlety's Mastery (Executioner) – While Subtlety's mastery makes sense to the design of the spec, it could use a buff to make mastery a stat Subtlety actually wants to have on their gear.

  • Tricks of the Trade currently is an annoying ability. Presumably, our DPS is balanced around having another Rogue to trade with; however, that's not always feasible. Furthermore, many Rogues don't trade it simply because they don't get a DPS increase from it. While this isn't a problem at high levels of raiding, it is annoying to play with a Rogue who may not be as selfless and decides to not give it. The 4 pc bonus is a step in the right direction, but I wouldn't be opposed to the ability's damage bonus being removed all together.


  • Poison variety across specs could be improved. Currently, poisons are one of the things that differentiate us from every other class, but there's little to no variety between specs. It would be nice to see different specs require different poison setups to increase the feeling of uniqueness to each spec.


  • Target swapping is currently a pain for Rogues and needs to be fixed somehow. I've mentioned it in various ways throughout this post, so I'll keep it brief here.

  • Ramp-up time is also horrible for Rogues. While I think ramp-up time should be a central part of the game, I also believe it needs to be true of every class if that is the case. As it stands right now, it's not, and Rogues suffer because of it (especially when target switching).

  • More rotational control and burst capabilities in PvE would be nice. Specifically, rolling more damage into our special abilities and making energy management more useful would not only allow us more control over our damage output and rotation, but it would help solve some target switching problems and the over-abundance of passive damage in the class at the moment. Obviously, it wouldn't fix all of these problems completely, but it would certainly help.
