What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Quality of life overall for Rogues is fine. The worst I can think of is reapplying poisons but that doesn’t bother me as I I’m not as lazy as other people seem to be who will go charging blindly into the fray without their poisons up. In fact, ignore this paragraph. If a rogue is bad enough not to keep track of his poisons in some way, he deserves to take a dive on the meters.

What makes playing your class more fun?
The fast paced feel of combat makes things exciting. Having cooldowns like AR give you this burst of what legitimately feels like adrenaline as you try to burn as many solid rotations in that small time period.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Ramp up and horrendous target switching for Combat:

To maximize DPS we need to charge up Bandit’s Guile, which takes a tremendously long time, and it’s incredibly easy to lose it. Sure you can throw BG to another target with redirect but that ability has it’s limitations. The cooldown is too long to make it usable as often as we desperately need it, it REQUIRES combo points. I wish it didn’t so that if I just used a finisher, I can throw my BG stacks to another target. Overall I just wish BG was a buff tracked on myself and not my target. We have enough issues with switching between combo points, and deadly poison stacks not following targets (which really hurt assassination a lot more than combat).

Killing spree seriously needs to be fixed. Right now more often than not, it’s a suicide button (worms before rag, Alysrazor (it’s teleported me from the druids, TO Alysrazor on the air, and I landed on the opposite end of the field, 500 yards away from where I started), a detriment to raid mechanics (completely borks steering on Rhyolith), or a complete raid wipe (Heroic Baleroc). The teleporting is unreliable and because of the timing, it can break and not even do all 5 attacks on moving targets. Just remove the teleport function of it, and do 5 dual wield attacks to whatever is in range of where I’m standing.
Assassination feels incredibly sluggish. Energy regeneration is so slow that it just doesn’t have the same pace and feel of combat or sub. Also, redirect is borderline useless for us since it doesn’t move deadly poison stacks. Which means on targets that have 5CP, you switch… and can’t envenom because you need to throw away combo points to keep from energy capping as you wait to hit 5 stacks of poisons. Assassination would be perfect if there was a way to spread deadly poison to a new target instantly so we don’t have to wait for new target ramp up to even use envenom, and an active form of energy burst for when the spec just feels like it’s slugging along.

Combat Readiness and Cloak of Shadows sharing a cooldown for the sake of PvP really hurts some scenarios for PvE. Losing one for two min is understandable, losing both is brutal. Have it like trinkets where they share a 30 second cooldown, but have individual 2 min cooldowns. Otherwise buff combat readiness to be useful, otherwise I disregard it and stick to saving the CD for Cloak.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I think combat has a good feel to its rotation, and not completely unforgiving. Assassination is good albeit slow. Sub could use some work to make it viable in PvE. As it stands it’s the worst of the three to maintain, and probably one of the harshest specs to play to its fullest potential in the game.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1. Fix killing spree, dear god please fix it. It’s probably the worst 31 pointer in the game.
2. Allow Bandit’s Guile to follow the rogue rather than the target. Or allow me to redirect BG without combo points.
3. Give assassination a little bit more energy regeneration.
4. Do something about Combat Readiness and Cloak of Shadows for PvE.
5. I’d absolutely love some form of raid utility in Combat and Assassination. Essentially every other DPS spec has some form of buff it can give a raid BUT combat and assassination. Even DK’s have multiple raid buffs.
6. Make shadowstep a baseline ability. Even with having rocket jump and sprint, I find myself blowing a speed pot during a raid encounter just to get from point A to point B in extreme situations.
7. Make Tricks of the Trade a little more exciting. Right now it’s not even used on tanks, we throw it on DPS to give someone else a 10% boost and to keep our 4 Pc. Buff going. Ultimately without the 4 Pc. It does next to nothing for the rogue. Feint and Vanish do enough for threat dumps, and tanks are holding up just fine.
8. Please bring back 1.4 speed offhand weapons that aren't daggers.
9. I'd like to see some type of PvP nerf to Fairie Fire and Hunter's Mark. I don't see how it's fair that spammable abilities can almost break us in PvP has been allowed to go unchecked for so long. If you suggest blowing cloak for either, you should be slapped.

What spells do you use the least?

• Shiv (it’s not worth the tremendous amount of energy to force a poison, and it’s few and far between I need to dispel an enrage).

• Deadly Throw (I don’t see the point in having it cost CP, you’d never use it with more than 1 anyway since it’s damage is negligible).

• Dismantle in PvE (the dudes in ZG that whirlwind do exactly the same damage unarmed as they do armed, and they still WW without weapons).

• Combat Readiness (It’s just too weak of an ability to risk putting Cloak on cooldown).

• Expose Armor (And I’m happy I don’t have to keep it in my rotation anyway unless it didn’t require combo points!)

• Smoke Bomb (I wish smoke bomb could be used on a target area, not on the rogue itself, and last some kind of meaningful duration for PvE)

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE; PvP very casually.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids (current and previous tier), 5-man dungeons, questing, soloing previous content.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Random BGs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

A rogue's quality of life has come a long way, with the speed penalty removal from Stealth, Recuperate, Blind being actually useful, 1-min cooldown Sprints, actually being able to rely on Vanish... What I really didn't understand though was the change on Combat Readiness/ Cloak of Shadows. It's not like CR was an overpowered ability, with its ramp-up time and long cooldown, and now that activating it may actually result in your death (since you might miss a CoS on a crucial time) I only ever use it when I'm soloing old dungeons.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I love everything about Stealth; being able to avoid combat when you don't feel like it, sapping, picking pockets, ambushing, the thrill of not being spotted. I love disarming traps, and I wish there were more of them (like in ICC - it just felt great back then). I love Mutilate, its sound effect, and how it might give me 3 combo points with a single button press. I like doing very high damage, topping the charts (though I don't feel the need to be on top every fight) and the feeling of a job well done it provides. Also, though not a lot of players pay attention to that, I love being last on the "Damage Taken" charts! (Feint really helps with that. And raid awareness).

What makes playing your class less fun?

Being melee. (Still reading? Good, now let me explain). Don't get me wrong, I love melee damage (it's why I chose to play as a rogue in first place) and want to stay as melee, but right now I can't think of a single advantage melee classes have when compared to ranged classes. Do players in melee get hit less by boss abilities? Hunters aside, nothing stops ranged DPS from staying on melee range during an entire fight, and they don't even need to make sure they're behind the target at all times. The boss is on the move? Sure, we can keep our full rotation while moving, but we also need to move much more than ranged classes do, and we have a much higher risk of getting out of range of our target. The boss is a dragon and it took off for flying phase? Too bad: avoid fire, attack those adds (if there are any) and wait until the boss lands.

I can do competitive damage, but most of the time I feel that my raid group has to make some sort of compromise because of me (i.e. on Cho'Gall they'll have me seat on the boss the entire time, while everyone else switches to adds when they spawn and help with interrupts; on Shannox all DPS will switch targets when my leader calls, but I just stay on Shannox lest I'll be outrun by Riplimb on every opportunity -- and so on). It even prompted me to ask my raid leader a while back: "why exactly do you want me on this team?" Interrupts? Shamans can do them from ranged distance. AoE and slows? Pretty much every ranged DPS class has a reliable AoE spell with a slowing effect, while ours (FoK + Crippling Poison) requires melee range and a dose of RNG (even with 2/2 Deadly Brew and Glyph of CP we may not get every target after a couple of casts). Throughput? This used to be the case back in ICC when rogues were indisputably best on single-target DPS, but currently we're being consistently beat by mages, hunters and (gasp) warriors on Baleroc.

To sum things up, rogues are a great asset to any raid, but for every role we play there's some other class that does it a little (if not a lot) better. Rogues are the only pure melee DPS class in the game. Playing as a rogue is immensely fun, but what do we bring to the table to compensate for our shortcomings? If it's not top single-target DPS (and it doesn't have to be), then what?

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I play as an assassination rogue ever since BC. What spells do you use the least?

Gouge (does it even have a use in PvE?), Smoke Bomb, Shiv (I actually liked how this ability was changed, trouble is I can only name about 2 or 3 encounters where it actually has any use; not to mention that it doesn't work on a lot of enrage effects from higher level mobs, and I find it annoying that there's no way to tell which effects can or can't be dispelled on the regular UI).

Wound, Mind-Numbing and Crippling Poison; Instant Poison and Deadly Poison are so central to my damage and rotation that I rarely ever use the other options, even when I PvP. I feel poisons should be more of a choice and utility and less tied to a spec's viability.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Mostly PvP at this time.



If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs

Arena and Rated Battlegrounds.



What are your biggest quality-of-life issues

Poison application is an outdated system that should be revamped. While devs feel that they prefer rogues make the options of which poisons to use, inventory consumption isn't the greatest system. A new tab should be created for rogues with all poisons listed. A player should then choose which poison to apply at the time and where, however, after applying the poisons should always remain. (Such as inner focus now does) The expiration date and inventory slot usage just needs to be removed.


What makes playing your class more fun?

Playing Rogue at a higher level takes practice and a much better understanding of your class than others. Stealth remains enjoyable and the changes to vanish were great. A greater diversity of abilities used while in combat. (No 2 button spams)


What makes playing your class less fun?

The class still is CD reliant on not just it's defensive abilities but it's offensive. As an example Rogue damage in BGs is much farther behind other classes due to the lack of splash or multi dot damage. The class highly relies on Shadowdance to perform any type of significant pressure to a single target. While great, consistent damage needs to be slightly raised. (Such as on bleeds) If not for the smoke-bomb ability, rogues would be quickly replaced by other melee in RBGs and Arena's (especially 5s)

Our ranged CC is extremely lacking and too easy to counter. Blinds cooldown is too long for an 8 second duration that breaks on any damage. It's range is also extremely poor as it falls behind the range of many other ranged CCs. Minor changes need to be made.


How do you feel about your “rotation”?

I for the most part enjoy our rotation not just in PvP but in PvE as assassination as well. Having to decide on recups, hemo, backstab, sdance rotations, evis, stuns etc etc I think gives the class an overall unique feel. Rotation wise I think our class is enjoyable. However, based on popular pvp specs, reapplications of bleeds such as rupture should be looked into.



What’s on your wish list for your class?

Right now our class seems to be on a fairly "close to" balance level compared to other melee. Yet some tweeks need to be done in order to keep up. We're a pure DPS class and all specs should show this.

Rupture damage as Sub pvp is very very poor. An experienced rogue knows that you're better off using the combo points in either evis, recup or SnD. Hemo only is used to prevent Rogue restealths and damage buff. Otherwise our bleeds are too minimal for the combo points used towards them. As it stands SDance is our reliant high burst DPS CD in pvp, but once down our consistent damage is slightly low compared to other classes. I think bleeds would be the best option to look into in order to change this. Also a better refresh/reapplication system afterwards.

Eviscerate damage is very low as well. While still preferred above rupture, an ability that does less damage as a finisher vs our builders obviously needs to be looked at. Our ambush and backstab damage is great right now, but finishers need improvement in pvp.


Blind CD as previously mentioned needs to be slightly reduced. 8 second duration on a 2min CD isn't acceptable. Nor is such limited range on the ability either. Many classes have far superior ranged CCs and Rogues needs to be somewhat improved.

Defensive CDs. The change to Readiness and Cloak combined was probably a good call however, at the same time it was not. Not having evasion on Prep CD anymore along with the readiness/CoS change hurt rogues defensive ability slightly too much. CD on CoS and Readiness needs to be slightly lower if now combined. I'd also suggest potentially bringing evasion back on Prep CD if not. Either or needs to be done. Recup IMO is fairly balanced based on it's combo point usage. Though regeneration from warriors is less restrictive and provides an much larger return. My suggestion would be to just extend recups duration based on CPs. (it doesn't need to be stronger, just longer)

Smokebomb. I would like to see this ability having more use in PvE. It shouldn't be limited to a PvP only ability, more raid encounters should be effected by this.

Expose armor needs to be buffed and it's almost never used.

I think every rogue still wants shadowstep as trainable still.



What spells do you use the least?


Rupture(in pvp), Expose Armor , smokebomb (in pve), feint(in pvp)
