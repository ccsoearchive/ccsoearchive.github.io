How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Assassination: Clunky. Constantly having to decide if I should go for a 2 count rupture or hold out for more CPs. Then turn around and have to take a 2-3 count Envenom to refresh S&D, then I have to get rupture back up then maybe I can get a 4-5 point envenom in. All this while dodging fire. I prefer to use a "priority" system rather than a set rotation. Wouldn't want to change any of that. Rotations are boring. EJ has a chart that shows relation between actions (2 point envenom, energy capping for 1 second, etc) and estimated damage loss. The chart is meant to show you which is the lesser of two evils when making decision like this. I live in this chart.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Deadly throw breaks pally bubble.

What spells do you use the least?
Expose Armor - Never used. Always covered by other classes. Smoke bomb isn't even on my PvE bar and rarely used in PvP because it doesn't work. If I could drop a smoke bomb, break combat with whoever is targeting me, then stealth, totally. But they just keep me targeted and wait five seconds. Breaking combat outside of the smoke would create an awesome tactical dilemma. Either close with me to maintain combat or I stealth again. Maybe do the same for PvE - use it to regain stealth for Overkill. Maybe. May not be worth it to drop off target for 3 seconds for the buff.

# What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvP, little PvE except to get specific gear

# If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

All types of PvP

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Ramp up time is really awful for both combat and assassination specs. I feel like the game has in a good way become more complex, and as a result rapid and well timed target swapping is less of a nice to and more of a need to ability in both PvP and PvE. Unfortunately, however, for two out of three trees this results in crippling dps drops due to our ramp up time. As combat this is extremely frustrating in PvP since the most important part of being a damage dealer in pvp is picking your moment to apply all the damage you possibly can. The way combat works right now, this essentially just cycles in and out giving you no control and frequently offers you no benefit since the time you need to apply burst just is not in sync with bandit's guile. Assassination similarly suffers when switching targets since your first mutilate or two will deal 20% less damage and you will need to build up DP stacks for envenom.

# What makes playing your class more fun?

Absolutely bar none the most important thing for me is having multiple specs to play that are actually viable for both PvP and PvE. I know this gets hard to balance but it really was one of the big promises of cataclysm and part of the impetus for gutting the trees down to 31 points and removing hybrid specs if I recall.

# What makes playing your class less fun?

PvP viability being limited to one tree of our three. There are certainly tweaks and balances that can be made to allow viability in PvP without destroying PvE balance. Just about every pure dps class has been forced into using the same one spec for PvP for the entire expansion now after being told that one of the great things about Cataclysm would be that all three trees would finally be viable in both PvP and PvE- that having a spec for PvP and another for PvP would be a thing of the past.... this is not cool blizzard -_-

# How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
This takes me back to bandit's guile, which is of course when I should use my burst if I want it to hurt someone. The ramp up time is TOO LONG for combat to be viable in PvP. This could easily be balanced simply by making bandit's guile ramp up after less sinister strikes/revealing strikes, merely hastening the cycle rather than making it stronger. Since this would change the ratio of time spent in 0%/10%/20%/30% damage boost in favor of the 30% time; an equivalent duration nerf to the 30% boost time (I believe it is 15 seconds now) could balance out this change to leave DPS unaffected while making combat much for viable for PvP, and far less complained about in PvE.

# What’s on your wish list for your class?
Major changes to bandits guile, restless blades working off slice and dice and kidney


# What spells do you use the least?
*Expose armor, used to be one of my favorites- now its so bad that people won't even use it if it costs them zero combo points, AND zero energy (mutilate rogues if they are silly enough to waste two talent points)

*Mind numbing poison- the mechanics for this are outdated, no one really casts except healers and wound poison has become to necessary for damage to use anything else in pvp (was this ever used in pve?)
