Now the things feint helped with are not such a big issue and you can sit waiting for energy to pool watching envenom and rupture drop off because you have got an unfavorable proc and no energy to build cp's and refresh either of them

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Cloak and CR put on seperate cooldowns, that can only have been a PvP complaint. also CR was our level 83 ability which we can now only use if we havent used another attack
Attach the single CD to a pvp glyph and the problem fixes itself if you feel it is necessary. Gear that doesnt have either crit/expertise, crit/hit, or hit/expertise. it is so radically unhelpful and there seems to be so much of it in 4.2. The problem with them is that because 2 of them are useful till capped you get to a point where you have to maintain weak stats to avoid going over the caps with reforging.

Also none of our 80-85 abilities can be glyphed which is probably why they arent as effective as they could be.

maybe we could
glyph redirect for target swapping encounters to a shorter CD
glyph CR so its not sharing CD with CoS
glyph smoke bomb so it has a wider radius or acts like a raid defensive cooldown

What spells do you use the least?

expose armour and distract. will occasionally use smoke bomb but it is a dud. good idea, good mechanic but not something to look forward to at level 85

# What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PVE, exclusively.

# If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids. I seldom do heroics to avoid burnout like I had in Wrath.

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Limited time on Poisons. Re-applying them is just busywork, and serves as a disproportionate punishment for forgetting a menial task.

# What makes playing your class more fun?

I enjoy the button-mashing aspect of the class, at least the way I have my hotkeys set up (mut on 3, envenom on shift-3).

Building up cooldowns and pouring everything into one big crit is usually the highlight of any given encounter. I think something could be done to further enhance this effect somehow. I could probably think of it myself given a few days, but I want to reply to this while it's still relevant... but I know there's something there that can be done.

The survivability bonuses we can give to ourselves via Cloak, Evasion, and Feint are phenomenal as well. It's not just enjoyable to me, but to the rest of the raid when the tank dies, the boss attacks me, and I manage to hold on to the boss for the 10 seconds needed for the raid to finish it. Nothing I want changed there, just saying.

# What makes playing your class less fun?

Fights with forced ranged elements. We currently have NO ranged PvE abilities, not even a tiny little one. This makes us completely useless in certain situations, like phase 2 of Nefarian, or the Ascendant Council achievement. When ranged is necessary, it feels like a very personal, direct insult.

# How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I kinda covered this earlier - I like the button mashing element. It allows me to kinda form a rhythm in sync with the rhythm of my daggers. And the mix of timing and decision making when using combo points gives me a consistently compelling reason to continue the game and enjoy it.

I'd like to mention, though, that I've tried Subtlety spec, and it feels more like a juggler class than a stealth class. It really doesnt match the job description.

# What’s on your wish list for your class?

Like I said earlier, a ranged ability, even a laughably weak one, for fights where it's impossible to get in melee range.

I've also seen the genius you can do with, say, Frost DKs, and would love to have such a brilliant meta-rotation for one of our specs at some point.

On that note, making Subtlety raid viable and NOT annoying as hell to use is a big want for me as well. I'd rather be exploiting the shadows than fighting up close and personal or relying on poisons, but that chance keeps being denied from me.

# What spells do you use the least?

Smoke Bomb. I was jumping with joy when I first heard of it, only to find that in practice, it's nothing but a graphical effect (for PvE at least). I've never seen it work as it was described since it was implemented.

Ramp up time with switching targets.

Q: What makes playing your class more fun?

A: I love the play style of rogues. Being able to stealth around and feel like you have control over the flow of battle. As a rogue we can decide what fight to pick and when. We also have a large varity of tools at our disposal to really annoy an enemy . I love the idea of rogues being ninja assassins that kill a target before you and anyone else even know he was there.

We have great survivability in raids as well. Feint. My god I love that move. 50% aoe dmg reduction is so handy. Then Cloak of shadows for spell immunity.

Q: What makes playing your class less fun?

A: PvP - The fact that if you want to compete in pvp you are forced to take sub. I used to love sub back in BC but to me it’s not what it used to be. I would love to see assassination back as a viable pvp spec.

Taking away stunlock has imo made rogue a lot less fun to play. Take away some of our defensive cd’s and bring back stunlock! The fact that we were the class that could lock you down in stuns made us unique.

Putting Cloak of Shadows and Combat Readiness on the same cool down was a massive mistake. We shouldn’t be forced to choose between the two because they are so different. 8 times out of 10 cloak is going to be more useful. But the times you need that extra physical dmg reduction against ferals/hunters/warriors we are screwed. I mean ferals my god they just go bear form and pop cooldowns and laugh at you while you are hitting them with a couple of wet sponges.

PvE: - When we have to hit a boss in his face man it is a pain. I am forced to skip out on my entire execute phase on rag because it’s easier for everyone else in the group if I’m with the ppl hitting him in his face and not behind him. With rogues and ferals the only classes that have positioning requirements for abilities and with quite a few encounters that require us to be in front of the boss at certain times it really screws with our dps.

Getting taken out of stealth by an ability before we are even able to start on the boss.
Also the fact our target switching is so terrible because of how our abilities work where you have fury and frost dks having no issues what so ever. It is certainly not fun watching our dps plummet because we have to attack adds with pathetic dmg while we wait for our ramp up time.

Q: How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

A: The rotation for assassination seems fine expect for the fact we are forced to use the lamest and weakest of our abilities ‘Rupture’ just so we can gain our energy back and get a poison dot. I can’t really comment on combat as I haven’t played it since Wrath.

Q: What’s on your wish list for your class?

A: First and foremost our target switching problems need to be fixed. Redirect was a nice start but really it’s not enough. I believe redirect needs to be reduced to a 10sec cd that also swaps our current bleeds and deadly stacks onto the new target. Alternatively deadly poison could no longer stack to 5 and instead just apply once. And we could have combo points stack on the rogue rather than the target. (Much like holy power for pallys)

A big problem with rogue aoe is how our energy regen works. Now for assassination we have to have bleeds up on multiple targets so we can spam FoK. And even then we need the targets to stay alive long enough to get 5 stacks of deadly going. I would suggest changing how we get our energy back from instead of stupid rupture to we get energy back when ever our deadly poison ticks. This way we aren’t spending so much time and energy getting combo points on 2-3 targets to put up rupture and by then the adds are half dead.

Another important change I want is poisons having 200% crit instead of 150%. DK’s got it. Its rogue’s time. I also feel like the talent trees need to be reworked. For example the fact that Imp expose armor is in deep assassination is silly when most of our damage bypasses armor. It would be much better off in combat. Then we have imp sprint in combat which really is a pvp talent that I feel would be a huge fix to assassination’s weak pvp mobility.

It would also be nice to bring something to the raid other than just dps. ToTT was useful before the threat change now it’s just a 30sec cd to increase someone else’s dps slightly.

Q: What spells do you use the least?

A: Sinister strike, Expose armor, combat readiness (because of the way you get stacks for dmg reduction, doesn’t work if you’re not taking hits) Smoke bomb(hardly ever used for pve/used a lot in pvp obviously) Shiv(outside of the odd mob that enrages or pvp)

More dagger availability. Also, some sort of significant raid buff would be nice for this spec as well, since we bring nothing to the table other than damage. More big, flashy cooldowns.

What spells do you use the least?
Combat Readiness. Cloak of Shadows is almost always superior, and since they share a cooldown, CR falls by the wayside. Most other moves occupy some sort of niche however. Expose Armor doesn't see much use either, especially when Poisons ignore Armor anyway.

Poisons not taking up bag space anymore. Maybe bring back the poisons "skill" and let us swap them that way? Also for mutilate, when murderous intent would kick in and benefit backstab, a glowy indicator around the ability would be nice, like moonkins with their eclipse or other execute abilities

What makes playing your class more fun?
The ability to pick my fights with stealth and the ability to break combat to put pressure back on my attacker. Though some of these are a double edged sword, as explained in the next question

What makes playing your class less fun?
As much as self healing is nice, recoup honestly doesn't make the class fun. Sure, it's nice to see those 6k ticks, but a rogue is supposed to be about killing something quickly, or controlling them to the point that they get worn down before the rogue can. Cooldown dependency also detracts from the fun. Yes, it's cool to "be a god" by popping your cooldowns, but you feel much less effective when they're down or unavailable.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Rotation should be simple, but some periodic procs to look out for (like maelstrom for enhancement shamans) would make the mutilate rotation much more fun. Otherwise, I'm relatively happy with the ease of mutilate.

More "active" procs as explained earlier to make the rotations a little less static. Deadly Throw actually being deadly instead of doing damage equal to my thrown weapon and costing me my combo points
