What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics, raid, dailies, soloing old content

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Arena, bgs and Tol Barad

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
More mobility. The removal of speed penalty for Stealth is nice but I still feel slower than other melee which is ironic since we are a class that is supposedly fast and elusive.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Stalking my enemies from afar and striking with fast and deadly precision before they know what happened and disappearing into the shadow after.

I love that unlike most melee I don't have cooldowns for my core abilities and I can choose to dump all my energy into a quick assault or maintain a slow consistent rotation.

For PVE it use to mean I could go all out on the boss and not worry about aggro because I'm a sneaky bastard who can tricks others and vanish. But lately it's more like a contest of who can chase down the add and kill them the fastest or holding back my dps because too much might make the boss miss that volcano.

What makes playing your class less fun?
When other melees trivialize my defensive cooldowns. i.e. Evasion is useless against warriors , druids' bleed, a dk and shaman's spells. I feel that rogues should be more about avoidance instead of mitigation.

When target switching is so inefficient for my dps because of loss of combo points and rotation momentum.

When I'm forced to play a certain spec because that tree has significantly higher AOE than others or certain ability that is more suited for target switching.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I would like to see some procs added to our rotation. Assassination and Subt have problems with energy regen so a proc which reduces energy cost for next ability would be nice.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Our gear should make us look more sinister and seedy. We're thieves and murderers with no honor. People who see us should lower their heads, avoid eye contact and try to walk away. I don't think a turban and neon lights on our shoulders quite inspires the same sentiments as a cloaked figure with visible tattoos and scars.

Combo points on the rogue instead of the target.

More glyphs for pvp oriented abilities. For example a glyphed Smoke Bomb might instead of obscuring vision be turned into a corrosive gas which leaves a boss with reduced armor for a few seconds. Or a glyphed Deadly Throw which applies Expose Armor instead of a slow. On that note I would love to see more range options for rogues.

Finishers revisited. I find it silly that our combo point generators do more damage than Rupture and Eviscerate. I think Eviscerate should be streamlined and have it's effects change depending on the spec we choose. So if we remove Envenom and replace it with Eviscerate for Assassination only to have it do the same thing that Envenom does, but if we're combat Eviscerate might grant us a slight melee haste buff. Subt could have an armor debuff or bleed effect.

Less penalty for not being able to dps behind bosses. Backstab should be replaced with Stab and allowed with use of swords and daggers. Stab would have significantly reduced damage but do 2x damage if hit from behind enemies so as to emphasize the importance of melee positioning.

What spells do you use the least?
Expose armor. Combat readiness

You don't ever see Fury warriors in PvP.
You don't ever see Combat rogues in PvP.

These are just 3 specs that you never see in arenas or bgs. Because they aren't even close to being competitive. I can't speak for mages or warriors because I know little about the intricacies of their specs but I will make a quick comment on rogues.

Mobility. Sub has it, Assassination and combat don't. BG's

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Much like the others have said, carrying all those poisons. They take up a lot of space, and that hour goes by pretty quickly. Also, Fan of Knives relying on a thrown weapon being equipped is a bit of a downer. Ideally, you'd be able to use it and have a bow or gun equipped. I mean, what good rogue wouldn't carry some extra knives anyway? :D

What makes playing your class more fun?

Being able to sneak into places that others would have to spend much more time fighting to get into is great. I also like pick pocketing; I've probably made around 2K gold with it, and the healing potions are nice. Granted, I've robbed over 10000 mobs or so? Not sure of the exact number. It all makes me feel very....rogue like.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I feel absolutely useless at range. When a boss or mob goes airborne or moves beyond where I can move with it, my dps tanks since the majority of it is based on Instant Poison and Melee damage. I know it's a melee dps class, and I think it should stay that way, but something to fill in the gap would be nice.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I love the rotation for Assassination. I do wish Subtlety were more viable for PvE, as I was a subtlety only rogue from before BC to only a few months ago. I hate that I essentially had to trade Shadowstep for DPS. That being said, the rotation of Subtlety is great, just doesn't have as high a dps output, so Assassination trumps it for me usually. I've never even tried a combat rogue, but I don't really have any interest in it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

A viable ranged attack, maybe one or two that generate combo points? Make them live Shiv and require more energy for slower weapons. We have a ranged finisher, but we have to be at melee range to build the cp for it, Premeditation not withstanding. By itself, it just hasn't proven that useful. I think making Fan of Knives usable regardless of ranged weapon equipped and adding a few abilities to generate cp at range would be great. This way, my dps doesn't nearly flat-line when an enemy goes out of range.

What spells do you use the least?

Deadly Throw (when I'm able to use it, I'm close enough that I can use Envenom instead), Combat Readiness (once or twice maybe), Smoke Bomb (2nd worst offender), Expose Armor (Once, to test it out. Not once since.)

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PVP

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

I no longer PVE on my rogue, but I have a great deal of raid experience on him. I dislike feeling like a fury warrior.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Arena, 2s, 3s, 5s. Rated BGS

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Human racial superiority - which is evident by examining the racial breakdown of top rated arena teams. If you're a rogue, being horde just isn't worth it if you want to actually compete at a higher end.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Rogues are where it's at. My raiding main is a tanking paladin and I spend very little time on him outside of raids. Rogues are just fun. You get to stealth around. You get to jump all in someone's personal space and ruin their day. It's a class that just screams, "Have fun walking back to your corpse, buddy!"

Rogues are fun. In the pvp environment being able to peel multiple people. Lock someone down. You don't need a healer, have the rogue mess with him, and when it's time for the kill, you call a swap, step over that direction, drop a bomb, and dance behind his back.

What makes being a rogue fun? Putting frost mages in their place - under your knife.

Surprise redirect full kidneys into a healer.

Fear into a full sap with a blown trinket.

Scoring a kill with deadly throw while you're in a snare.

Shiv's interaction with enraged warriors.

disarming hunter traps then disarming the hunter.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Poison utilities no longer feel necessary. Necrostrike being a prime example, it's a better than wound poison, mind numbing.

I might eat some heat for saying this, but burst is low. Finishers don't feel like finishers. I'd rather hemo you than eviscerate you. Combo points are better dumped into SnD than an eviscerate, or used to top off a recoup stack. Burst may seem high at some levels. But this is due to human rogues double trinketing. Yes, I've got a plate of sour g%@@*s, no I'm not faction changing.

Overall the posts so far talk about low PVE damage. This is because PVE damage is balanced around pvp damage. It's a necessary evil, I'd say, given that rogue control is outlandish; if we were to hit harder in general there would be major imbalances.

Oh, also, Faerie fire is rogue breaking. As is the fact that druids still have increased stealth detection when it was removed from our talents. Lately, when they open on you, it hurts. Bad.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Revealing strike is boring. Rupture is boring. Eviscerate puts you to sleep. Finishers don't finish. Adding complexity with revealing strike reduces performance. Target swapping is painful.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Helmets that show off my spikey hair. It's a big deal, trust me.

I accept the cloak of shadows nerf as a necessary balance. It still upsets me. The fact that combat readiness is no longer readily available is basically the loss of one of our cataclysm level skills. That's a big deal. Especially with nothing to replace it.

Racial parity for pvp. You shouldn't nerf unheeded warning. You should nerf the fact that their racial talent encourages them to stack pve trinkets and double damage trinkets. Really, an extra 1200 agility matters. Lots. The same can be said about warriors, dks, et al.

What spells do you use the least?

Uh, pickpocketing?

What makes playing your class less fun?

"Rogues are a class for aggressive players" - I remember reading that in the original WoW manual.

I felt that rogues were supposed to be glass cannons, a high risk high reward class that was reliant on skill and timing in order to win, not playing like a fury warrior...

Combat readiness/ cloak being on the same cool down (considering we have to cloak to get away free when we vanish)
