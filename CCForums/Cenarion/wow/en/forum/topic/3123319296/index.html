We’re looking for feedback on your class as we work on changes and adjustments for a future game update. While we may be making some specific class changes in 4.3, what we’re looking for in this thread is overall feeling on the class as a whole for more long term changes in the future.

Here is the kind of feedback that is NOT helpful in this particular thread:

  • Concerns that your current damage, healing or tanking is not competitive. While this kind of feedback is often useful, it also is the kind of thing that can change from patch to patch, and so it isn’t as useful for long term vision changes.

  • Concerns that your feedback is being ignored or that we don’t take these kind of posts seriously, or otherwise posting anything that is off-topic or not in the spirit of this thread’s intent.

  • Requests for responses to your suggestions or feedback. The purpose of this thread is to get information to us, not the reverse.

We hope you take these threads seriously, as we’re going to be reading them very carefully. We ask that you take time to think and formulate a useful and objective reply, as this feedback could be used to alter your class in substantial ways.

We won’t be replying here addressing your concerns. This is purely for us to read your answers to the below questions.

Please copy the below questions into your reply and answer each one.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

I typically focus on PvE play, however I occasionally do arenas or random unrated BGs.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

My raid group focuses on 10m Heroic Raiding.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

There are several quality of life issues for rogues. Limited duration of poisons, coupled with their consumable nature and the need to have several different utility or DPS poisons available can be tedious.

Moreover, the use of an extremely fast offhand dagger in order to maximize dps output, causes durability loss at a faster rate than the rest of the rogues gear.

What makes playing your class more fun?
The frenetic pace and feeling that I am swiftly whittling health away from my target is extremely enjoyable.

What makes playing your class less fun?

There’s a large disconnect between how it feels to play a rogue, and the result. Primarily, I feel that my actual button presses have less of an impact on my performance because most of my damage breakdown appears to come from passive sources like autoattack and poison proc damage.

More importantly, I constantly hear friends playing other classes talking about their great numbers on crits or hits (between 60K and 100K) whereas I can never seem to get that “wow” factor, saying "I'm attacking 5% faster" doesn't have the same ring to it.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The combat rogue rotation feels fine on paper, but in practice it feels very spammy and less controlled. That isn’t necessarily a bad thing, but it does not jive well with the Bandit’s Guile talent’s Insight mechanic. This cyclical damage buff coupled with the speedy feel of combat’s rotation don’t gel well together.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Better target switching is the utmost requirement: I feel that my ability to perform well in the current style of raid bosses requires more target switching than I can do while maintaining my buffs. Adds die or spawn at a fast enough pace that I cannot maintain or build up my buffs in order to actually start doing damage.

Lessening of passive damage sources: Autoattacks and poison damage make up a vastly disproportionate amout of rogue damage. This means rogues can scale nicely with weapons, but they also don’t see that effect very easily on their abilities. The buttons I actually press should make up more of my damage than my autoattacks. Slice and Dice is a HUGE culprit here.

Set bonuses that aren’t tied to Tricks of the Trade: This is an ability that I hit every time it’s up, and tying a bonus to this skill is boring at this point.


What spells do you use the least?

Shiv, Gouge, Expose Armor, Rupture, Combat Readiness, Smokebomb.

A mage forgets to throw on his Arcane Intellect and he'll still do decent damage, but a rogue who forgets poisons is half a rogue until he realizes his mistake and puts them on.
