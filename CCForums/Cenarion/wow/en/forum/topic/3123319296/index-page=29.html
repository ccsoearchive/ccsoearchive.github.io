■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Poison reapplication takes a bit too long to apply and needs to be done too frequently.

■What makes playing your class more fun?
- I love picking pockets. It's an innocent little thing that sometimes gives unique, neat, little items that can only be gotten from PP. Plus it's one of the only truely unique things we can do. (as with any character I play, it's the unique class-defining things that make the class fun to play)
- I like helping with crowd control, though it's a skill seldom asked for since it has a short duration and can't be reapplied.
- I love the sneaky, pokey, whirlwind of blades, light-armor, high skill, cat-burglar/swashbuckler/assassin character.

■What makes playing your class less fun?
- not being able to get behind boss mobs. This isn't so bad in assassination spec, but it does eliminate the good openers from stealth. As subtlety, this severely hampers damage.
- target switching fights are a bother as sprint doesn't compare to pounce/charge for switching to another target. Shadowstep is great for this, but subtlety's lack of raid viability eliminates this as an option.
- low quality CC, no raid utility, no unique contribution to the raid
- too low of dps. As a class that brings nothing else to the table but dps none of the three specs should be behind any spec from a warrior/shaman/dk/priest (I'd say paladin, too, but ret has been destroyed already). Certainly none of the mele classes.

■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
- Assasination rotation is fine.
- Subtlety rotation is too precarious of a balance to be sustainable in most boss fights.

■What’s on your wish list for your class?
I like assassination fine the way it is I just prefer the fun of subtlety, and don't like that I'm forced to play a different spec if I want to be the most competitive.
so, for Sub spec:
- more efficient talents so that there are some "extra" talent points to spend on some of the fun talents like cheat death without further hampering my dps.
- faster combo point generation. Cut the crit-combo point generation from HAT down a bit from 2 seconds.
- Make Serated blades more like cut-to-the-chase. the chance/combo point is fine, but it should refresh it to it's 5 point duration.
- either boost Hemo to be the main attack, or boost Shiv to replace backstab as the primary attack. This will eliminate the positional element. OR stop making boses you can't get behind!!
- more pockets!! It makes no sense to me that raid mobs don't have pockets! Humanoid bosses should have pockets with unique loot tables.
- The return of class-specific quests, and unique class-specific items.

■What spells do you use the least?
- shiv, don't use it in any spec.

Arenas, BG's and occasionally RBG's.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Maintaining poisons. I don't care about the hour long duration, and having to have a lot of poisons in my bags, for me it's where your poison buff is located. In pve when you have a lot of buffs it's easy to miss one or two poison buffs falling off. I wish there was a more obvious warning when your poisons only had 10 or so minutes left on the duration.

Combo points...It seems like almost every class has some sort of combo points now. While some of them are more obvious, holy power, some are less obvious like Meat Cleaver from a warrior. With the exception of Rogues and Ferals, these other classes do not suffer from mandatory target switches like rogues do. I'm not asking for CP's to be treated like HP either, maybe just give us a "grace period" or something of that sort. Like, in pvp some times a rogue will have to gouge a focus target to interrupt a spell from being cast. You don't always have time to refresh slice or recup, so maybe give us like a 1cp grace period where it won't take the cp's off the previous target if switched back after a single ability. That would remain unique from other classes, and definitely help rogue target swaps.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The complexity of combat is great. You can find rogues with far better gear doing way less dps because they do not understand the class mechanics as well as you do.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Having several aspects of your class given to other classes. This not only makes the class less unique, but some times these other classes get better variations of our class abilities.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

PVE: Assassination bores me to tears, sadly. I think Combat is far more fun due to restless blades, and having to be more involved in your rotation to maximise it's effectiveness. Assassination needs a huge buff in the "fun" department, because the only time you even have to think is when your SnD and Rupture are both about to fall at the same time. That is, literally, the only attention you have to pay with the spec, which definitely makes it less fun overall.

PVP: I love how there is no rotation and basically all your finishers should be used at different moments. I think rogues are the most situational class in the game, and it should remain that way.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I just wish the devs would decide what they want rogues to actually be. In pvp we lack damage all in the name of having so much control. I'm ok with not doing as much damage with control as a tradeoff, but there are other classes that have just barely less control with signigicantly more damage as well. Either giving us more damage, or taking CS off the same DR as KS would be great. Also what I've noticed is that rogues in current pvp gear, really don't have stats to shoot for. Once you have 30% crit and met your expertise and hit caps, there's nothing to reforge "wasted" stats into. Maybe if you made haste or mastery more interesting for sub rogues this could be prevented. I think, in general, that sub rogues could definitely use some sort of energy regen buff for bursting in a dance, so making haste more valuable could definitely help with the wasted stats on current pvp gear.

For PVE purposes I'd like to see fast sword/axe/mace off-hands. You're already making it so hunters will be able to use transmogrify to change bows to guns, and reverse. So even if you don't want to add more 1-hander agi weapons for itemization purposes, could you at least consider allowing us to transmog them?

What spells do you use the least?

I use everything depending on the circumstance. I don't think rogues have any abilities that are never used, rather abilities that are used in specific circumstances. This gives the class complexity so hopefully this question isn't designed to get rid of rarely used abilities, as I suspect it is.

    What type of content do you focus on? - PVE
    What type of PvE? - Heroic-level raids
    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
    - Needing a thrown weapon to use FoK was a terrible change
    - Ramp up time on target switches through DP stacking
    - Non-existent burst damage option for assassination/combat (tied in part to huge ramp up time)
    - Limited AoE (combat/sub have effectively none, and assassination's is only useful for sustained damage on targets that are alive an extended period of time).
    What makes playing your class more fun?
    - Energy is an enjoyable mechanic, and the whole 'sneaky assassin' archetype appeals to me
    - Having a range of damage reduction cooldowns to mitigate/avoid damage, which helps make me feel useful
    What makes playing your class less fun?
    - Limited raid utility; we have a bunch of cool personal cooldowns but nothing to help out the raid like other dps classes (even the other pures get several more useful utility abilities than rogues)
    - Complete inability to switch targets/create noticable short-term burst. Warriors, Frost DKs, Rets, all these classes are able to switch to another target for quick burst far better than rogues are; with the exception of Blade Flurry there's absolutely no way for a combat/assassination rogue to do respectable damage to short-lived targets, whereas a DK can just save some RP and chain two Frost Strikes into the burst target for huge burst.
    - Limited AOE ability; combat and subtley's FOK is completely undertuned and bordering on useless, and assassination's FOK is only dealing decent damage because of how OP stacking deadly on all the targets and proc'ing instant is. Remove the assassination talent making FOK apply poisons, and just BUFF the overall physical damage of the spell back to an acceptable amount to make it viable for all specs. Also remove the requirement to have a thrown weapon equipped, or somehow buff thrown weapons to be better itemised for rogues than bows/xbows. There's no other class that has to choose their weapon based on 'do i want to aoe or single target'.
    - Lack of an assassination cleave; atm Blade Flurry makes combat's cleave TOO good, and assassination has NO cleave. Balance the two specs out slightly - sure combat can be better at cleaving, but not obscenely good whilst assassination has none. Perhaps some sort of multidot spell similar to Bane of Havoc that would cause your poison applications to be duplicated on a nearby target would be useful, allowing assassination at least a slight cleave potential.
    How do you feel about your “rotation”?
    - I love the assassination rotation. It's very smooth and fluid, everything seems to flow well. My only issue is the passive aspect of the rotation, where using rupture and envenom only increase your damage by affecting other parts of your rotation. I'd much rather prefer rupture and envenom had lower secondary effects, but did more direct damage themselves, instead of feeling like 'buff' moves that do insignificant damage themselves. The AOE rotation is annoying, having to wait 5-10 seconds to stack DP on all targets before being able to do noticable damage feels clunky, but this was addressed earlier in my post.
    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    - Combo points generated on-player instead of on-target
    - A cleave/multidot ability for assassination, to allow the spec to do more than 'tunnel one target or suck at damage'
    - A class-wide buff to FOK
    - A useful utility buff, now that ToTT is just a boring damage boost on a 30sec cooldown some kind of raid-wall or hand-of-etc ability would be nice, perhaps a change to make smoke bomb work similar to PW:B and reduce damage or perhaps make the next ability from the target a guaranteed miss
    - Some sort of burst ability, something that doesn't generate combo points but deals instant damage to a target to allow a very brief target swap (think along the lines of Bloodthirst/Frost Strike). It would obviously do less damage than say a combined 'mutilate, the poisons that would proc from the mutilate, and the damage value of the combo points generated from the mutilate' would, but the damage would be instant and have no negative effect on sustained damage (such as losing all cp on your primary target.)
    - Shadowstep as baseline would be excellent, seeing as warriors get charge, ferals get feral shadowstep AND skull bash, rets get Long Arm of the Law, etc. It could be given a long baseline CD (45 seconds or something similar) but reduced considerably through subtlety talents and/or subtlety baseline bonuses.
    - Remove Cloak and CR from shared cooldown, or make CR an instant shield-wall effect instead of needing to be hit in melee to stack it up. Right now there's effectively NO situation in PVE where CR is more useful than cloak.
    What spells do you use the least?
    - CR, since it's completely pointless to give up cloak and CR almost never stacks in a raid environment
