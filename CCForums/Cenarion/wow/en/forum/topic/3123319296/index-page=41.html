* What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both, mostly PvP
* If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics/Other. Raids are another story. Questing doesn't teach people that fire is bad, which it did back in Vanilla via elite mobs as well as difficult vanilla questing. Without this, your playerbase has gotten less skillful, while raiding continues to try to provide a challenge. I have found a lot of players have difficulty at 85 doing TBC raids. Gear can't fix that. Only a harder game altogether can fix that. Raids are fine, every other PvE experience needs to get on that level.
* If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Majorly into PvP. Combat PvP in Battlegrounds. I only do arena for points, as arenas simply do not feel rogue like. Those players mentioned above are extremely fun to corpse camp at their gy.
* What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Poisons wearing off. Sometimes you'll have 10 mins left on your poisons, get caught up in an epic BG melee, and suddenly you need to vanish because time got away from you. Same on PvE bossfights I could see that happening too if it's a long fight.
* What makes playing your class more fun?
The thrill I get when you do something that other classes cannot do. (Pick Pocket, split mobs via sap, ride magmaw via the chains in his encounter, disarm those traps back in BWD I believe while your raid runs through them. Killing spree kologarn and falling into the abyss and dying, etc.)
* What makes playing your class less fun?
Other players. It's hard to work with someone as a team when the questing experience is so easy they end up in my pugs or on my team and don't know a frost mages ring of frost doesn't freeze you if you stand in the middle, or that green puddles of ooze is generally a bad idea to stand in. Harder questing content or content that would require you to group would fix this earlier on. EverQuest started using mercenaries to fix this. I'd love a pocket tank and healer to keep me up while I have the monsters not paying attention to me. Rogues like being in the shadows.
* How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I've played a combat rogue for years. Really, I would like to see an ability that lets you jump onto an enemies back like the kobolds in Trial of the Crusader, or a mirror image setup similar to frost mages, except the mirror images are all in melee and stick to your target. Let rogues actually confuse people. You've shown the last few years you don't like me stunlocking people. (I still do, revealing strike + 5cp kidney = awesome.) Mirror melee images that do damage while I vanish, hopping on enemies. A grappling hook that lets me pull myself to the roof in WSG. SO many fun things you could do. Standing around an enemy makes a rogue based on agility and dodging feel like a sitting duck. Let me swing from a rope hanging from the ceiling and kick a boss into a pit of lava. Anything.
* What’s on your wish list for your class?
I listed a lot under the last asterisk, but TL:DR rogues are resourceful. Why not have blue lights spawn around the boss in a safe place that lets rogues have a damage buff if they stand in it because that's how the bosses armor shifted. Make ONLY rogues able to see it, make being a rogue require THOUGHT. It'll help us with dps problems in the future too, and not overpower us in PvP.
* What spells do you use the least?
Disarm trap in PvE. Expose armor. Backstab, Ambush. All others I don't use really belong to another spec, and I play combat hardcore.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Heroics, trying to find a raiding guild.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

BGs and TB until I find an arena/RBG team.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

It's not a huge issue, but I think at this point, poisons ought to just be equivalent to a warrior's stances. We already got rid of blind/vanish powders, hunter ammo, etc... might as well finish the job.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The element of surprise. I think I speak for all rogues when I say that in a PvP situation, ambushing the enemy or subtly tilting the odds in your team's favor is the most fun one can have; case in point, picking off the enemy healer while his teammates are too busy tangling with mine.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Losing my momentum. This includes my rotation in PvE, and bosses that force me to lose my mobility - nothing like trying to run back to a boss and watching yourself slowly drop on the DPS meter.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I've only played Sub and Assassin so far. In PvE, Assassination definitely has a smoother pattern than Sub; it's practically second nature now. As there's no real "rotation" in PvP, I suppose I have no complaints.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

In general, I'd like to see the rogue given more opportunities to throw his opponent off-balance, and quicker methods of gaining inertia in combat. I'm not suggesting a return to the days of pure stunlocking, but I feel like Blind, Vanish, Kick, Sprint... things that change the fight suddenly are what make the rogue so fun.

What spells do you use the least?

Sinister Strike was on my hotbar for maybe a day.
CR is a liability unless I KNOW I won't need CoS.
Evisc, but that's a spec issue.

BG's, world pvp (*if* that happens), casual arenas, since I don't particularly enjoy current rogue pvp(, I just need the gear for my pvp server).

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Having to apply poisons each hour is an inconvenience, when generally there isn't much reason to change them until you're done doing whatever you're doing - something like the Runeforging tab would perhaps not be a bad idea - it'd free up rogue bag space, too. (We can't really complain about that though - we don't have reagents or many extra gear sets to worry about). Considering that I always use the same poison set-ups for PvE on every fight, and that I always use the same poison set-up in just about all PvP situations, it'd make just as much sense to not need to replace them 3-4 times before being finished. Also, something that's bothered me for years..... can't we find *some* way to combine shoot and throw? We use guns/bows/thrown, whichever happens to have the best stats (since they're stat sticks right now, especially), but we need to use thrown weapons for pvp - for poisons, deadly throw, etc.... but what happens when we're in our PvE spec, and we need to fan? Simple, you swap to your thrown - then what? Then you can fan of knives, but you can no longer range things until you either swap back to your other weapon, or you have to swap your action bars. Or, alternately, you need to keep both on your bars - when you can only use one at a time anyways. It just seems a bit silly, and I'm certain there must be some future way to combine them.

I also love the active mitigation we get from proper feinting, evasion tanking if something goes wrong, and the ability to cc/silence adds when we need to. Making blind last an entire minute was a nice touch to this.

# What makes playing your class less fun?
Killing Spree is, really, a failure for PvP, because of how unreliable it is. I don't mind not being able to use it on certain boss fights, and you've really come a long way in making it work - on Magmaw, Rag, etc - but when it's 50% of our dps cd's in PvP and it also only works some of the time, that's problematic. If a target is moving, it's often a wasted cd, and that makes pvp as combat not fun - especially in world PvP when targets are mounted most of the time. This is unfortunate, because PvP as combat is pretty fun otherwise - there's a good balance of mobility and damage.

The diminishing returns on stun, in my opinion, also makes rogue pvp less interesting. The choice between damage and CC makes sense, but being able to stunlock someone at the cost of your damage gave you more flexibility... instead, we have a choice between doing damage and... basically interrupting them for a few seconds. It's not usually a compelling choice, and it makes our ability to stun people early in a fight less interesting.

# How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I like the combat rotation, though I think Rupture should be a more important part of it (~1% dps loss to not use it at all), and I like the addition of Revealing Strike - it makes you have to pay attention to your cp's instead of just spamming SS. I think the assass rotation for PvP is alright, if a bit slow compared to the other specs - the sub rotation is perhaps too complex in both PvP and PvE, though in a sense, that's what makes it attractive for PvP. You have a lot of options - how you use them is up to you.

# What’s on your wish list for your class?
I miss picking locks. I miss opening doors. I miss pick-pocket being profitable (getting gems and such). Those things made being a rogue interesting and useful in dungeons. Having disarmable traps in the Shannox fight was a really nice; more things like that would be wonderful. Not necessarily in boss fights, even - a trap door to let you bypass trash, being able to disarm traps that damage/kill raid members would make having/being a rogue for your groups worthwhile. I.E. ICC trap trash.

# What spells do you use the least?
Since no one is ground mounted outside BG's anymore, distract is something I miss using. As I mentioned, pick lock, pickpocket. You also can't use backstab when you're in combat spec, so I'm not sure why it's in the combat tree still. Expose Armor could also use some tinkering; it's a personal and sometimes raid dps to use it if you're the only source of it, but... really? The CP cost is boring and ridiculous to me. Also Combat Readiness, as many other people have mentioned.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Combat is my favorite, and is currently in a bad state.

Whatever, I wish it was like it used to be.

1. Get rid of bandits guile

4. CR + Cloak on same cooldown – get rid of it.
5. Shadowstep – give it to everyone. Use the space in subtlety’s spec to increase pve damage.
6. Bring back burst damage. We’ve got specs that are built with the idea of big burst damage…but health pools are just too high, and our actual damage is too small, for it to matter.
7. Give me a permanent, lasting reason to use rupture in PVE. In combat, it’s just not interesting.
