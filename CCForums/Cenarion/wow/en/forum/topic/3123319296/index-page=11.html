Whooo y'all have a lot to read thru, g'luck.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PVE nowadays.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids mostly; Heroics are just a gateway to raids.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Retired, heh.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Movement. A lots been done re: poisons and things like Shiv; being movement-dependent, 1 min Sprint is a good start, but even then it needs the glyph. The stealth changes were pretty awesome as well, but more CD or fight-dependent movement utility would go a long way.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Being more involved with active abilities; less dependent on being a white-hit or even poison buzzsaw.

What makes playing your class less fun?


Buzzsaw at one extreme, perfect micro necessary (*cough* pve sub *cough*) at the other. You're getting there, tho.

I'd also have to agree with the armor concerns (tho I like T12 a lot): everyone else gets animations and glowy bits, we get... dark static stuff. Fair enough, but I'm sure more can be done with this.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


Prefer priority over blind rotation, and needing to be aware and actively adjusting according to the situation (and not just per fight). I like the timer-juggling of Assassination, and knowing when to pool energy etc. Changes to Feint make it pretty awesome for AoE fights with only the GCD as tradeoff; enough to make it usable without being spamable, and knowing where to put that 4 second window of vulnerability.

What’s on your wish list for your class?


I'd hope that our next Ultimate Skill for an expansion not be something used for PvP only (and even then, get nerfed!). As above, more mobility; something to offset dps loss from movement or knockback, a little. More emphasis on PvE utility and desirability.

What spells do you use the least?


Smoke bomb. Disappointing to get it at 85; sounded cool, but a terrible mix of OP in pvp and Useless in pve. Combat readiness, especially as it shares a CD with Cloak, never gets touched either (i cannot speak to its pvp uses, where the tradeoff CD might make sense). Edit: oh yeah, Expose Armor. Yeah, it's time to put that one to bed, guys ,-)

Thanks for lissenin'!

First, I want to thank you for asking for our feedback! I appreciate all the time and effort you spend trying to make millions of complaining players happy ;-)

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    PvE


If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Heroics, normal raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    occasional BG


What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    I wish you would at least get rid of the confirmation window when applying poisons. That is pretty annoying sometimes, and makes it feel really clumsy and awkward. A glyph for permanent poisons would be kind of fun I think.


What makes playing your class more fun?

    Fast attacks, cool animations (the Garrote and Envenom animations rock), stealth not slowing me down. Stealthing up to a mob and destroying it in a flurry of fast attacks is just so fun, no other class compares to that (and I've rolled them all).

    Also, poisons are fun because they are unique to us. I don't mind using them, and don't mind the one-hour duration (just hate the confirmation window, as mentioned).


What makes playing your class less fun?
    Feeling like a target...when other classes see a rogue doing quests, they go out of their way to attack me. I keep feeling there is something wrong with the design if this is happening so much. People know we are weak and don't have the survivability we used to have.

    Also, leveling to 85 was very painful, more so than for other classes I've played. I would often get taken to half health just killing one mob, and if I pulled two by accident would often die. This was very different than leveling from 70-80, where I had no difficulty. You blow cooldowns and if it doesn't die, you are meleeing in leather and get hammered while waiting for your energy to build back up.

    Also, I don't think our gear is often very cool...certainly not distinctive of our class


How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I love the Assassination rotation in raids. It's not so difficult that I can't focus on what's going on around me (which melee needs more than ranged).


What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Yay! You finally asked! I would LOVE to have more "roguey" abilities, such as Shadowstep, available to all specs. I know it's a balance, because you want each spec to feel unique (and they don't, really). I don't want to feel like a dual-wielding warrior, in other words.

    More "roguey" looking gear - think "secret", "stealth", "shadow", "assassin"..... We should have more of that "I never heard anything, and just found him dead!" kind of gear. Instead, I have shoulders that sparkle. Yeah.

    And now for a silly request, maybe: a stealth animation. Whipping your cloak around and disappearing...something like that.

    I think people play rogues because of the IDEA of a being a rogue is appealing. I don't think people are attracted to other classes for quite this same reason. People play Paladins because they like what a Paladin can DO, not for what it IS. People play rogues because of what they ARE, not so much what they can DO, and anything you can do to give us more of that feeling will be really appreciated!


What spells do you use the least?
Smoke bomb, Gouge, Expose Armor, Deadly Throw.

    Let me start off by saying that I have played a rogue from TBC up until 4.2 of Cataclysm. My rogue was by far the most fun for me to play, but in Cataclysm many of the "changes" to rogues seem forced just to have something different. (ie. Revealing Strike). I geared up my DK during 4.1 and I've been raiding/pvping on my DK while my rogue has sat inactive since the launch of 4.2.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]


    Mainly PvE, but I also try to do a fair amount of PvP too (Did more in TBC and WotLK)

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]


    Raids (progression guild). I enjoy doing the occasional heroic & cleaning up a quest here and there too.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]


    Now, primarily BGs, but in the past I participated in Arena and RBGs.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


    1. Changing the way poisons interact with your weapons would be better. Perhaps having poisons act like DKs runeforge where you will keep deadly on one and instant on the other until you decide to change the poison on a specific weapon.
    2. AoE: Yes, Combat has Blade Flurry, which is a great way to pad the meters, but Sub and Combat lack the damage that Assassination gets from Fan of Knives. The other problem being Assassination's AoE is reliant on the mobs staying alive long enough so deadly poison can stack up to 5.
    3. Stuff dies with combo points on it. Yes, you can use redirect/snd/recup if you pop it quick, but if you quickly target switch you will lose the aforementioned combo points
    4. Ramp-up time while switching targets. I'm not referring to just combo points, but poison ramp up time, Combat insight, putting rupture back up, all while trying to juggle SnD (and Recup if you're Sub) I've been playing a frost DK for most of Cataclysm, and target switching is a joke since I can Howling Blast, then plague strike, and not miss a beat (sometimes frost fever is already on the target if they're nearby) I cannot begin to explain how clunky rogue target switching feels in comparison with nearly every other class. Yes, redirect is a simple solution on a 1min cd, but does not carry your poisons, rupture, and combat insight over to your new target.

    What makes playing your class more fun?


    1. I enjoy how stealth factors into PvP and you can jump into or out of a fight when you please. I'm not really into RP, but stealth adds a lot more strategy to the game when you compare a rogue's strategy to that of a DK/Warrior (CHARGE IN BEFORE THEY START CASTING!)
    2. Safe Fall: This passive ability (and the glyph) has been a deciding factor as to what I would like to play before. I love being able to jump from high places and take little to no damage and it really is one of the few unique passive abilities in the game that makes rogues stand out. If possible, I'd like to see the "safe" distance increased even more. There is nothing better than seeing an enemy below you, stealthing midflight, falling to the ground and attacking.

    Subtlety: Briefly, I feel that the spec is too complex to play. It requires an extremely skilled player to pull off reasonably competitive numbers for the spec. This problem is compounded by the fact that the damage is too far behind that of the other two specs.

    Mutilate:

    - An extension of the former problem is the inability to effectively attack small groups of multiple targets, either through target-switching, cleaving, or multi-DoT.     1. Target switching as a rogue (see "Quality of Life Issues" section) is a joke in comparison to nearly every other class. That being said, rogues combo points need to be handled differently because we are punished if we decide, or need, to switch targets. Please, figure out a balanced way that combo points are attached to the rogue rather than the target so we can at least be on the same playing level as ret pallies and Enh shaman and we are not penalized quite as much for switching targets.
    3. Combat insight/Bandit's Guile. I played combat for 20 months from the release of WotLK until 4.0 when mastery was introduced which made Assassination the clear favorite in h ICC. It all goes back to ramp up time and target switching. Although I understand adding something to Combat could have been seen as necessary to break the WotLK feel to it, I feel Bandit's Guile was a failed attempt to add something to the class, and actually pushed me away from the spec, and eventually the class altogether.

I would really enjoy if the 10% haste buff could be worked in somewhere amongst the Combat tree, or any of them for that matter, because in my experience as a rogue, it's very hard to find a 10m group that will bring over 2 melee, and if the other melee isn't an Enh Shaman or Frost Dk, then their will be no 10% haste buff (unless the hunter is playing survival, which is quite unlikely given the current state of MM). Feeling like I only have Combat and Assassination for PvE really scared me away from sticking with the class for another tier because 1. I didn't like Combat because of Bandit's Guile, and 2. Assassination, as I will explain next, is pretty boring.
