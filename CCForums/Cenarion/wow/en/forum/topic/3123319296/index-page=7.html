What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Raiding

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

I find that poisons can be a drudgery to remember to buy and apply. I always put them on, I always restock them, and I will always have to do this. There is no choice to go without poisons; you MUST have poisons. Which means you MUST keep them in your inventory, you MUST remember to re-apply them once every hour (or 45min generally, if you're worried they'll fall off in a fight).

What makes playing your class more fun?

I enjoy stealth, I enjoy being the fast blade wielder in leather, and knowing I can bring an enormous amount of damage to the fight. I've recently switched to combat, and I simply adore how rapidly my cooldowns are available (because of Restless Blades)... being able to throw down a cooldown and really go nuts is a lot of fun.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The ramp-up time for our dps (poison stacks, combo points, Slice and Dice, Bandit's Guile, etc)

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I have to tell you a secret; I macro Revealing Strike in to happen after every third Sinister Strike. I know you put Revealing Strike in there to make the rotation be more active, but it's silly. I have very competitive DPS, and I never even need to think about RS.

And another secret; I never use Redirect. I either refresh Slice and Dice or put it into a Recuperate (which is macro'd in with my Evis when out of combat).

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Shadowstep as a BASELINE ROGUE ABILITY. Even if this means JUST the teleport, and you put an Improved Shadowstep into the sub tree that grants the damage bonus, etc, I feel that rogues need to be able to not only get to their targets faster, they need to get BEHIND their targets faster. Sprint on a one-minute cooldown was meant to address this, but it does not cut it.

I would like Adrenaline Rush to provide a GCD reduction, similar to how Unholy Presence works for DKs. It is far, far, far too easy to become energy capped because the damned GCD is *too long* while AR is up.

I would like teleport mechanic entirely removed from Killing Spree. It's an unsafe DPS cooldown; it throws you in lava, it teleports you into unpredictable places. it jumps you to not-in-combat passive enemies and leaves you far away from where you need to be. Losing control of my character over and over again just for a few fast hits is terrible.

What spells do you use the least?

Redirect. Expose Armor. Deadly Throw. Combat Readiness now that it shares a cooldown with Cloak of Shadows. They may as well not exist.

@edit I am seeing a lot of rogues mention they do not use Shiv or Dismantle or Smoke Bomb. During Heroic dungeons there were enrages to shiv, some bosses needed to be disarmed, and the smoke bomb brought all the casters happily into melee range. In raiding, it seems NONE of these abilities are useful. Shiv doesn't even work on Maloriak's heal (but Tranq Shot does??). They need some love.

I'll go one step further and say it would be cool if in addition or instead of the above, that your melee crits applied rupture. Much like Ignite. It only makes sense that if you hit someone hard enough you would rupture something and they'd bleed a while. Either one of these choices would make it so you didn't have to choose between Rupture and Eviscerate while at the same time having a cool feel to it.

Assassination being all about poisons. I miss the old Assassination where it was more physical. This isn't that big of a deal though.

What makes playing your class more fun?


When my GCDs are fairly tight, but without energy creeping up, outside of cooldowns/bloodlust. It's a really enjoyable rhythm to get into.

When the stars align and 4pc T12 procs in conjunction with these two trinkets. More for lolnumbers than a mechanic I really like, though.

Three different rampup mechanics. I recall Daxxarri(I think?) responding to a concern about Deadly Poison, saying that it was something that was being looked into. I'll glue my take into the wishlist.

Getting parried is kind of a downer though that's more of a problem with the current content than with the class itself. It ties into your obsession with putting big bosses in spas. :3

PLEASE STOP PUTTING ME UNDER RAG'S HAMMER/FIRE/OTHER FORM OF DEATH, KILLING SPREE. I have a difficult time convincing myself to even push the button on certain fights. Alysrazor Normal? Flip a coin, tails, you get to enjoy a 100yd drop. Al'akir? Into his hitbox you go. Sinestra? Hope you don't mind getting decapitated by a Cutter. It can be argued that it's an interesting mechanic in that you have to use it at a type that won't make you die horribly, but I personally find it to be simply un-fun.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


Revealing Strike on an oceanic connection while GCD locked is probably the most annoying thing.

Energy capping as Combat is a less than favorable situation, especially when it's necessary to use Adrenaline Rush in conjunction with Bloodlust. Having two haste-proccing best in slot trinkets, and the ability to win the lottery with a Future on Fire proc while they're up, doesn't really help this. (I do love it when you win that proc string, though.)

Our cooldowns are still on GCDs auuuuuuugh.

The specs have a problem with their AoE. Combat is leaps and bounds in front of anything when there's something to cleave onto, but its effectiveness tapers off compared to Assassination's very powerful Fan of Knives when there are larger numbers of targets. Subtlety's crying in a corner.

What’s on your wish list for your class?


I would really, really like it if a mechanic similar to Inner Rage were offered to Combat. Aforemented "Every Haste Buff Humanly Possible" concerns.

Delete Backstab and roll it into Sinister Strike. Do something similar with Shred, but that's a druid problem so not really the point here. I don't see the point in having a distinction between the two abilities, and if Sinister Strike were changed to an arrangement akin to Ambush(more damage if dagger used for attack), it'd open up the gear range for both Combat and Subtlety, and keep Subtlety from being immediately discarded on fights which you cannot get behind the boss.

Those target switching problems. Turn Bandit's Guile into a targeted debuff rather than a self buff, so I can attack flamespirits on Majordomo. Aldriana's suggested this before; turn Deadly Poison into an Assassination-specialised ability.

If that suggestion of Deadly Poison as a specialisation is taken heed of, it couldn't hurt to take it one step further- Why not remove Instant Poison as well, and roll the damage into the other poisons? This'd make poison selection a tradeoff between debuffs, rather than the no-brainer which is damage vs debuffs.

Killing Spree- I think this ability should be given a good hard look as to whether it should remain in the game as it stands. My suggestion would be to heavily rework it to instead create a clone of the Rogue which does the bouncy-jumpy-stabby, while still proccing Combat Potency, Main Gauche, poisons, and increasing damage dealt by the rogue. Combat isn't really working at all in a PvP environment, so I don't think that's a big concern.

Revealing Strike is neat. Expose Armor is useless. Let's glue them together and give Warriors the finger by depriving them of unique Shattering Throw.

What spells do you use the least?


I really have a hard time finding a use for Expose Armor.
