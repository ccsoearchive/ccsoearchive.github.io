What type of content do you focus on?
Both, although more recently PvP only

If PvE, what type of PvE?
Heroic Raids until my guild quit. I'm enjoying some time off, but I'll get back into them Soon(tm).

If PvP, what type of PvP?
Mostly regular BGs and arenas, but some rated BGs when I find a group.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Purchasing poisons before a BG or arena. When raiding, I have enough stuff to grab from the bank that I usually remember to restock poisons, but if I'm logging on for a quick BG I usually forget.

What makes playing your class more fun?
In PvP, control. Being able to do competitive damage is never a bad thing either...
In PvE, I like the intensity of combat from the random combat potency procs. As assassination, it's nice to have fairly consistent damage.

What makes playing your class less fun?
PvP: High armor classes and either server lag or other latency. It's incredibly frustrating to try to hit someone who is persistently "out of range" no matter how hard you try. If I'm on top of someone on my screen, how is he 10 yards away?
In a way I feel bad for resto druids and disc/holy priests when I fight them. The damage I do to them compared with the damage I do to resto shaman and holy pallies isn't even comparable due to the difference in armor. Also, fighting feral druids who can match my damage but infinitely go defensive in bear form is disheartening.

PvE: Bandit's Guile. If there's one thing from my post that gets noted, I hope it's this.
Cyclical damage is fine, as long as we have some control over it. The worst part is that it's 30%. This is way too high. I feel completely gimped when I'm out of insight or in shallow insight. This talent alone has made it very, very hard for me to play combat since Cata's release (which is sad since I've played combat since vanilla with a two month exception in Naxx, but we don't have to talk about that).
Also, Tricks of the Trade on cooldown for the sake of using is way too similar to Hunger for Blood from days past. Since the threat component is trivialized, I'd really like to see this talent reworked somehow. Maybe make it a 30 minute buff that has a continuous threat value (previous 10 seconds only) and 3% damage buff instead?

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
Assassination is fun because it's somewhat consistent with a few cooldowns to use wisely.
Combat is fun because it's chaotic with some very powerful cooldowns when you need them.
I think vendetta, killing spree, and adrenaline rush could all have a slightly longer reset period. Bandit's Guile would maybe be more compelling if you were rewarded for using it well, like having a rupture stay at whatever insight value you executed it for as long as you keep it refreshed.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Rework Bandit's Guile and Tricks of the Trade
Increase the effectiveness of Expose Armor
Make poisons spells instead of items
Let me use a sword offhand again D:
Pick Pocket reworked to summon a pet monkey with a fez hat to pick pocket for me.

What spells do you use the least?
Pick Pocket and Pick Lock
possibly Expose Armor and Disarm Trap, although they get a fair amount of use

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Heroic 25's

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    Poisons stacking to 20 taking up 4 slots of inventory Space minimum.
    1 hour application.
    Assassins step enchant is useless for Assassination Rogues.

What makes playing your class more fun?
    Energy. We don't ever run OOM, and our Rage is never starved ;)

What makes playing your class less fun?
    Lack of competitive DPS on specific fights or having to respec/reforge to MAKE it competitive for every fight.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    Baseline Shadowstep<<
    Firstly I'd like to say I play all 3 specs in a raiding environment and I play them well with top 5-10 parses for each spec in current heroic tiers.

    Assassination:
    Easily the most boring rotation we have, it's extremely easy to play and not very embracing at all. I'm extremely disappointed in the rotation. The ramp up time is aweful when target switching, and you literally only hit 3 buttons (Mut, Rupture, Envenom) after your initial opener. I think you could spice it up by making a change to Shadowstep, and making it baseline. Something along the lines of "Increases your next envenom by X" in addition to the Ambush bonus you already gain. This wouldnt change the rotation too much but atleast gives you another button to hit, and a small window to close off those envenoms before losing that ShS buff. Assassination is the bottom of the 3 specs currently in PvE for single target, and I think an increased envenom every 20s would be a nice addition.

    Combat:
    Very bulky rotation, however it's immensely fun for soloing, and a large portion of the current tiers fights. The cleave in combat I think is great. For the rotation aspect its similar to mutilate in only requiring the use of 3-4 buttons outside of CD usage, however the larger energy gains makes it seem more fast pace and doesn't mimic the same "boring" feeling that assassination gives you.
    I don't think the energy capping is an issue during AR, since we are able to use an ability on every GCD, small price to pay and regardless wields a dps increase. The AoE is relatively weak, FoK is practically useless and I'm not sure what it would take to fix this, but it would be nice to see an improvement in this regard.
    The biggest issue with combat is the talent Banduits Guile. The ramp up time is rediculous and indirectly encourages a rogue to ignore fight mechanics and tunnel vision a single target. I think it needs to be decreased the amount of stacks required to reach your 30%. Also for friendlier target switching, allow Shadowstep(Baseline) to transfer your stacks of Insight along side of redirect.
    Also wtb 1.4 speed swords :|, having a massive main hand with a puny dagger off hand looks ridiculous, we want swords.

    Subtlety:
    Easily the funnest spec to play and the most rewarding once mastered. Subtlety is currently our highest single target DPS spec in a raid environment. The rotation is amazingly fun like I said, the eviscerate refreshing rupture just feels right, and recup restoring energy makes sense as well.
    The two issues I have with this spec is the maintaining of the Hemo debuff (yes it's 1 minute I know) and the lack of multi target abilities. I'd like to see Serrated Blades refresh your hemo buff as well as the rupture, with such a complicated rotation, having an extremely short 3s window to refresh 3 of your finishers, it can easily all go to waste when throwing a single hemo in. Your Slice can fall or even worse your Rupture. It's already complicated enough, adding in the need for the hemo debuff is extremely frustrating when it causes you to lose your Rupture, Slice, or Recup.
    Lastly, the lack of AoE or cleave for Sub makes it absolutely useless on a few fights. The ability to spread Hemo/Rupture bleeds to multiple targets around you I think would do us a great deal of improvement for sub rogues on those AoE trash fights. This would still have a ramp up if not preemptively prepared for the AoE phase.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Baseline Shadowstep
    1.4 speed OH swords
    Reliable FoK for combat
    Some form of cleave/AoE for PvE subtlety


What spells do you use the least?
    Expose Armor
    Pick Pocket
    Shiv

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

My focus is purely for PvE work.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

All kinds of PvE. Heroic dungeons, raids, and regular raids

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could
be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

I've seen a lot of people already quote this one, but poisons are a definitive pain to me. I have multiple bags filled to the brim with all sorts of items for dungeons, raids, crafting, quests, etc. Poisons do tend to take up space in my bags. I'd think that you could possibly fix this by making poison applications a spell of sorts, similar to how shamans have weapon enchants.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I don't have the same feel of it since cataclysm, but I loved feeling like... well, a rogue. That feel of sneaking up on the enemy, then surprising them with a devastating attack for PvE. The rigid rotation for the class also feels very smooth.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The only major complaints I have is the minor issues with energy and target stance requirements. Energy (especially with assassination spec'd rogues) tends to be eaten up extremely quickly with no real noticeable return of energy in a timely manner. After a couple of mutilates to get those 5 combo points needed for an envenom, I'm literally on zero, meaning I have to wait a bit before I can actually hit the target with a spell, whereas another class just rolls through their rotation without hesitation. Also, haste doesn't seem to have a very noticeable affect on the energy bar like it does for other classes. The last issue is that there are quite a few boss fights where we cannot actually stand behind the boss due to positioning, or simply due to a massive amount of movement. For us to actually position ourselves behind the boss for the last 35% to use murderous intent could be suicidal, such as in the case of Chimeron. I feel that abilities such as this give the class a unique feel, but unless there are changes to boss fights were we can position ourselves behind the boss safely and not be interrupted, then this talent needs to be applied to a different ability, or to have the ability re-worked to be a bit more convenient.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The Combat rotation for rogues seems a little jerky, but it does work pretty well in bursts. the Assassination class has a very streamline style to it, making it very ideal and powerful. All in all, the rotations aren't the priority.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Here's what I'm hoping for:

    Poisons to no longer require reagents, and instead to be used as a spell.

    Another AoE ability for rogues to use OTHER than FoK.

    Better energy gains or return during fights. (Since i've heard so many combat rogues complain about too much energy, just give us adrenaline rush).

    A re-working on how rogues must position themselves for cerain abilities.

    Better damage overall (we don't tend to match up as well against other melee classes)

    Re-establish separate CD's for Combat Readiness and Cloak of Shadows (since CR is useless in PvE)

    more agi off hand weapons for combat spec'd rogues, like maces, swords, and axes.

    A change in the requirements of hit and expertise. both of these numbers are so high, it has shied me away from playing my rogue anymore. If we could get the numbers lowered just a little bit, it'll mean we can spend our time re-forging into more useful stats, like mastery and possibly haste.


What spells do you use the least?

Combat Readiness is one of the top spells i don't use. Cloak, when glyphed into it, takes both melee damage reductions, and "cleanses" most poisons and spells off the rogue. It has much more utility than combat readiness.

Smoke Bomb. This spell is supposed to interfere with targeting. It's a helpful PvP ability, but sort of wasted in PvE.

Deadly throw. Again, another PvP ability, not PvE.

Gouge. I'll say it again, it's PvP, not PvE.

On a side note, mind-numbing poison seems like a wasted poison now-adays, since no one ever really uses it.

    Energy capping as combat under adrenaline rush, heroism, or even getting unlucky combat potency procs when you use no energy cost ability (feint, tricks) . The issue only increases every time a new tier is released and we have better gear.

    Poisons not acting in a similar way to Shaman imbues. Most classes have had their reagents completely removed through glyphs or just in general, but I still lose 10 bag slots to poisons.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    The main reason I have been playing a Rogue for 7 years is because I enjoy the energy/combo point system. I find the most fun times I've had playing a Rogue was not when I was spamming something every GCD, but rather intelligently planning out my course of action with energy pooling. The only real example of this was TBC Mutilate with the Ashtongue rep trinket, where you had to rotate 3 finishers with Mutilate and you had to maximize your ability usage with your two buffs (trinket and Find Weakness). It wasn't possible to keep them up 100% of the time, so it felt like you had a lot of control with your damage.

    Something like that doesn't happen anymore. Now, you can completely get away with:
    a) Mutilate: get to 4-5 cp, if rupture is down, put it up, otherwise envenom
    b) Combat: use cooldowns. spam combo points. if snd is down put it up, otherwise, get to 5 cp and eviscerate

    It's not completely optimal to do the rotations I've listed, obviously, but the difference between a skilled player and the person who just follows this rough outline is miniscule.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    As a pure DPS, having uncompetitive damage on a multi target fight bothers me. These types of fights are obviously the norm now. I also think it's unfun to completely demolish damage meters by leaving blade flurry on for 80% of the fight (Halfus, Magmaw, Staghelm), although I understand that Rogues need a niche like that. I feel as if the damage could be moved around somewhere else.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    Combat and Mutilate are too simple. Sub suffers too much from swapping targets, but has a decent rotation.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    - Poisons like Shaman weapon imbues.
    - Mutilate making a return to work like it did in TBC (non refreshing SND's, find weakness).
    - Combat getting a way to deal with bursty energy gains, or an ability that is only worth using if you're energy capping (like a Warrior's Inner Rage)
    - Deadly poison stacks being reduced to 3 instead of 5 to lower ramp up for all 3 specs, since we already have combo point ramp up, debuff ramp up, etc.
    - Rupture getting a damage buff to make it actually a significant choice
    - Revealing Strike could also use a buff
