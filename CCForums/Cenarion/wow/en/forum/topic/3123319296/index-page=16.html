What type of content do you focus on? PVE

If PvE, what type of PvE? Heroics and Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? BG's / Rated BG/s

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Movement and positioning!

Shadowstep is great fun, and makes you feel awesome, and really helps with both positioning and movement... but is in a spec that is so hard to play, they consider it a son of gun to play even on EJ!

Shadowstep should be base (and sub should be changed to not be so positional)

What makes playing your class more fun?

Using stealth and rogue utility abilities in pve and not only ever in pvp. Feeling like a fast moving rogue and not a mindless button masher running around trying to catch mobs while ranged sit back and easily do the same thing.

Also hitting harder, and not death by 1000 paper cuts.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I sometimes don't feel like any individual button press I do means very much. What I would do, if I could redo the class would be to make what you now consider builders to be more like most classes normal yellow attacks, and finishers to be truly epic, or maybe even more for flavor so the combo points matter.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

It's kind of boring to hit what feel like meaningless basic attacks and then occasionally spin a rutpture or SnD plate, followed by a finisher that feels like a normal warrior or paladin attack.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I would like Shadowstep to be base.

I would like the combo system changed so that you have to use different builders, that hit harder for more interesting finishers.

For example, you would use Shiv, Eviscerate and hemo to then allow you to give a damage bonus to your party, would use Eviscerate, Hemo and Backstab to allow you to give a dot to the mob that lasted a minute, etc.

Check out the dual blade system in CoH.

What spells do you use the least?

Shiv, Combat Readiness, Eviscerate

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE

Raids

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Poisons lasting only an hour, should just be permanent, lazy I know but forgetting to refresh poisons then having to do it mid fight is a dps loss and very annoying. Also as combat I rather liked having an offhand that wasn't a dagger, some fast offhand swords/fists/axes would be nice again, or just make combat like Single Minded Fury and Enhancment and use two slow weapons. Using two swords or two axes is what gave combat it's powerful feeling in my opinion.

What makes playing your class more fun?
The utility, being able to do or avoid things any other class would die from, example: falling from greater hights without taking damage, avoiding massive damage intake with skills like Feint, Evasion, and Cloak of Shadows, being able to stealth almost anywhere, and running at greater speeds.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Not much on this end except I can't stand having leather boots that look like socks, I know it's kinda stealthy and all but socks are lame.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
I'm combat mainly because I don't like the Rupture ability. I don't mind dots from poisons and what not, but having to refresh and ability constantly and not really getting any interaction out of it besides it ticking is boring. I'd rather have more melee direct damaging abilities, maybe one that does damage and cause a dot effect, so it doesn't feel like i'm just refreshing a dot like a warlock or something.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Katars! Not like getting two fist weapons, but an actual Katar set, one weapon that when you equip it takes both slots and counts as two, and maybe some Katar specific abilities to match would be absolutely amazing. Ever play an Assassin class in Ragnarok Online, kinda like that. Also I loved using duel swords/axes/fist/maces, then the only offhands for rogues in Cataclysm are daggers, and they feel weak and tiny. Would love to have that option back, I know it makes more loot that is class specific, but I personally would be ok with combat using two slow weapons to accommodate. I'd also be happy if we could transmogrify the look of our offhand dagger to look like a sword/axe/fist/mace, like hunters can change the look of their ranged weapon, it'd be a nice compromise for not being able to use any of those weapons in my off hand.

What spells do you use the least?
Rupture, I dislike this spell, it's boring. Redirect, a nice spell but with combo points persisting through death most of the time is just more efficient to use them up with SnD or Recuperate. Expose Armor is very situational and I don't ever use it outside of pvp once in a great while. Shiv, maybe if it dispelled one random magic buff or something along with enrage i'd use it more but otherwise stays off to the side.

High damage, a focus on bleeds/DoTs/keeping abilities up, having a unique "toolkit" to use in many situations.

While effective, the mut rogue rotation feels slow/kind of clunky with a fairly large dependency on energy procs from venomous wounds in order to keep things interesting. Combat is much faster and more enjoyable, a lot more abilities are being thrown around and it's fun.

MOAR DOTS! Finishers with effects other than damage: more things like Slice and Dice, using combo points to buff yourself is fun and makes me think twice about how I want to unload those 5 CP.

What spells do you use the least?
Sadly, lots of really cool spells. Combat Readiness would be a great cooldown if it was useful/not sharing a CD with CoS. Distract, disarm trap (y helo thar Shannox, though) Never using Eviscerate as mut is kind of weird, too. Maybe if Evis had another mechanic to it...hmmm

    Single target burst and utility combined with Shadowstep for PvP as Sub, strong single target boss dps for PvE, increased movement speed as passive abilities. Evasion tanking!

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    1.) For PvP against a (good) geared healer, burst damage (or sustained DPS) as sub is not high enough in some cases where management of all CD's are necessary to kill them leaving no CD's available when switching targets.

The PvE benefits are obvious.
    3.) Make distract able to be cast while not in stealth and have it essentially be a "taunt" to change your focus's target to you. (In PvP) i find myself refreshing recuperate or SnD before the buffs run out after an enemy kill quite often because the CD is down. In PvE on bosses like Majordomo I find generating CP's on the add to be a waste because it dies so fast I cant switch back to the boss fast enough. I ususally just tab target and autoattack and pool energy to get back on the boss when the add dies. If redirect was always available, mutiple target DPS will go up quite a bit.
    5.) Make Sub raiding a viable spec (i.e. remove pve backstab positional requirements).

    What spells do you use the least?
    I use almost all the spells for both PvE and PvP. Shiv is great for pvp to neutralized enrage effects and slows, but bad for pve unless you are without a hunter. Feint is great for pve, but I find myself not using it for pvp that much. I probably use distract the least.

    BGs.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

    1. Weapon Choice:

    Visuals are incredibly important to me. I chose to play a Combat Rogue when the iconic Combat Rogue was dual-wielding swords. As a raider in Cataclysm, I am forced to use either a mace or axe in my mainhand and a dagger in my offhand due to both mechanics (I need a fast offhand weapon) and unavailability of weapons (there are no 1-handed agility swords beyond ilvl 365, and there are no 1.40 speed non-daggers in the game at level 85). This has been a sore spot for me since WotLK, and is at its absolute worst right now.

    Swashbucklers shouldn't be using shaman weapons.

    2. Raid Buffs:

    We're too reliant on getting them from others while not bringing any of our own. In the past, this wasn't an issue, because we were generally in 25-player raids and our damage was high enough to make us wanted anyway. Nowadays, our damage is middle-of-the-pack and 10-player raids are my endgame, so buffs (and the lack thereof) become much more important.

    3. Stealth-Detectors

    I use my V-key to show health bars over the heads of nearby enemies. Unfortunately, these frames often cover up the spinning indicator that an NPC sees stealth. A better way of getting this information to a stealth class would be appreciated, I'm sure.

    4. Killing Spree

    It gets me killed. A lot. Especially on Ragnaros, because it teleports the rogue directly into the hammer-smash range. Al'akir was even worse, especially on Phase 3 when it instantly killed me for having the gall to use my 31-point talent on a burn phase of a fight.

    Aside from Rag, half of the time when I'm using it, I jump to some other target that shouldn't have been in range, and that target then moves, so the ability fizzles. It's bad.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Being useful to my raid/party in unique ways. Tricks of the Trade is an example of this uniqueness, as is disarm trap (though really only on Shannox). Pulling off a double-CC with Sap and Blind is fun, but it's been incredibly rare to do since we got a 1-minute blind because everything and its cousin in Zandalari heroics can see stealth for some reason.

    Feint is a good example as well, as it's a unique way for me to help healers.

    I wish you guys made Smoke Bomb do SOMETHING useful in PvE Raids, though.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Lack of diversity in weapons. Lack of an alternatively-viable PvP spec that could compete with Subtlety. Melee-unfriendly encounters. Bandit's Guile.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”?

    Overall, I'm okay with the difficulty level of Combat. I don't (and never will) play Assassination or Subtlety for PvE. However, there are a few problems:

    1. Bandit's Guile gives Combat a nasty need to tunnel-vision. Having to use Redirect to work around this also clumsy. I am a highly combat-aware player, but Bandit's Guile forces me to ignore adds (unless they are tanked right on top of me and I can hit them with Blade Flurry). Have it stack on the rogue, not the mob, or get rid of it. Please.

    2. Adrenaline Rush causes too much energy-capping. Especially true because we are encouraged by mechanics to stack a lot of Haste. I have enough to pay attention to with trying to keep up with all my timers (SnD, Rupture, Bandit's Guile, Tricks of the Trade, Glove Enchant CD, Killing Spree CD, and Adrenaline Rush CD). I should not have to worry about wasting a GCD to hit Feint when Adrenaline Rush is running or risk energy capping.

    3. Killing Spree is dangerous to my health at worst and hard to weave into the rotation correctly at best.

    4. Revealing Strike is a nice idea, but it's way too underpowered in PvE to the point that a Sinister Strike glyph proc rules out its use and it can be ignored entirely by some rogues for minimal dps loss.

    4. Overhaul Bandit's Guile to let us switch targets again.
