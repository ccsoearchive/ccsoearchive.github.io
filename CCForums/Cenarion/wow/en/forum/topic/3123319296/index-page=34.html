Before starting: I only play combat, so opinions expressed come exclusively from that standpoint.


What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

N/A

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Switching targets. Redirect has waaaaaay too long of a cooldown to be useful, and I usually just use recuperate or slice and dice to burn off the extra CPs instead. It'd be nice if combo points stacked on the rogue instead of on the target, but if that's not feasible, then at least cut redirect's cooldown in half and make it usable without requiring combo points (In case we want to transfer bandit's guile to a new target, but don't have combo points on our previous target).

On a lesser note, it'd be nice if we had an easier way to tell when SnD is going to fall off, rather than have to keep searching our list of buffs at a glance to look for the tiny duration number (I remember one of those flashy powerauras was labeled as being tied to SnD. What happened to that?)

like many other people have said, it'd be nice if poisons were an ability rather than an item, and lasted until cancelled.

In addition, it'd be nice if there was an easier way to tell which boss attacks are nullified by Cloak of Shadows and which aren't. The following is a very common situation:

Guildie: Hey Lance, can you handle [insert boss ability here]?
Me: Let me see if I can cloak it.
Lanceon dies
Me: Gah, I guess not...

What makes playing your class more fun?

As a combat rogue, I love delivering quick strikes, generating a lot of energy, and getting my attack speed as high as possible. Killing spree is my very favorite ability. It'd be nice if we could see more attacks as cool and visceral as KS. Also, giving us unlimited blade flurry was an awesome move. ^_^

What makes playing your class less fun?

Generating more energy than I can use, and the aforementioned quality of life issues.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I'm pretty fine with the combat rotation. It's simple, but not boring.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

It'd be nice if haste could lower the global cooldown on our attacks. I know that sounds a little selfish coming from a class that already has a reduced cooldown compared to other classes, but sometimes I generate so much energy that it's impossible to use it all, especially during Adrenaline rush (geez, that just makes me sound even more selfish -_-).

Hmm... maybe you could make it so that adrenaline rush provides a 50% global cooldown reduction instead of 20% attack speed? That'd actually be pretty cool.

Also, see the quality of life issues

And finally, the ability to transmog any 1h weapon into any other type of 1h weapon.

What spells do you use the least?

Smoke bomb, redirect, deadly throw, rupture, gouge, and garrote.

Technically also dismantle, but unlike the other 6 it never feels like dead weight on my bars, because hey, you just never know. It'd be nice if more weapon-wielding bosses were vulnerable to disarm, though.


I love the combat spec. My rogue has been combat for his entire WoW career, and I can't wait to see what's coming down the pipeline next. Keep up the good work! ^_^

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics, raids when I can.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Poisons. Much like ammo was, I have multiple stacks of poison sitting in my bags taking up space. It is never really an option to not use my poison, and in PvE there's never really a reason to change my poisons up. Its always Instant/Deadly on my main weapons, and when I bother to poison my throwing weapon its usually Crippling for CC. Nobody else can use poison, and we've already gotten rid of poison ranks, so why not just finish the job and make them a baked in option? An on/off toggle ability like Blade Flurry where you could just pick the poison you want to apply and it stays on until you decide to use something else? Or at least just make it an ability with the same durations if need be, its not like the few silver we spend on a stack of poison is an effective gold sink anymore.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Putting out large DPS numbers. We're the only purebreed melee dps in the game, and raw dps is the only thing we really have to offer to a raid, so its nice when we can bring it.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Lack of variety in weapons we are forced to use. I'm looking at things like throwing weapons primarily here. There are hundreds of different swords and axes, but in the entire game there are less than a dozen throwing weapons. It wasn't so bad back when we didn't have to use them to FoK, as we could just use one of the nice pretty bows or guns. Now we're forced to use a throwing weapon with a vanilla model. Seriously, top of the line throwing weapons are using vanilla or BC era models while everybody else is getting cool new stuff all the time. We got what, 1 new throwing weapon model in all of Cataclysm?

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Rotation is pretty good right now. There are "major" abilities that I just never use as combat though. Rupture is worthless to me unless there is a druid in the party buffing bleeds, for example, but its good for other specs, so can't say anything too bad about it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Cool roguish gear. By roguish I mean "stealthy", not "I have giant freaking lighthouses on my shoulders that can be seen from a mile away".

What spells do you use the least?
As a PvE player, I almost never use stuns. They have been nerfed down so badly for PvP balance that most of the time they just aren't worth the energy in PvE. Bosses are immune to them, and outside of some rare instances (some of those guys that power up in the zuls, or sapping my way through Vortex Pinnacle to bypass some of the encounters for the group) I'm better off just burning them down with that energy. All in all, any non-damage increasing ability doesn't see much use in my PvE toolbox (besides Kick) because they've been so nerfed for PvP balance that they are no longer useable in PvE.

- Disarm Trap: I used this skill on a quest once, which I didn't really need to. And perhaps a couple of times in BGs.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PVE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

All types, from current tier raiding to fun world events etc.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

No major issues here, although when I have been caught out forgetting to reapply poisons I feel like a damn fool.

Blade flurry being an active ability. Why not stick on a toggle as a special ability like stealth and shadow dance. Would take up one less slot on my bars. With no real change in mechanics.

What makes playing your class more fun?

When I am preforming my class role to the best of my ability and it easy to see that I am making a notable contribution to my groups success. Specifically I enjoy ability to deal a constant stream of smaller amounts of damage, choosing between a mixed physical/spell or purely physical theme. I say theme because that seems the main form of difference between Assassination and Combat, barring minor stat weights plus modifiers and multipliers.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Loss of a unique feel to the class. I should clarify that its not that the class is not unique, because rogues I feel are on the more unique classes. More the loss of that uniqueness is something that makes my class less fun. Case in point, where I have had a career of raiding where I was relied upon for interrupts I suddenly find myself in Alysrazor on the druids where I am forced into only having one interrupt every 10 seconds. So I find what was once a key feature of my class is simply something I can do along with every other melee and regularly not even perform as well as them.

Also, the negligible raid buffs that I bring to a group, in my ten man group there is a Warlock causing the Curse of elements debuff and a Death Knight granting the 4% melee debuff (plus Horn of Winter and the passive haste buff.) I grant tricks, with a heavily nerfed threat mechanic. Partially I am grateful as it allows me to spec how I chose, it does however frustrate me that I am not bringing anything hugely helpful to the raid. Unless rapier wit and good looks counts... :S

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I hate the way rupture is utilised in it's current form. At the moment it feels like a band aid in Assassination so I have something to use besides envenom. In combat the problem is even more aggravated. You are required to make a quick mental checklist: do i have the mangle debuff on the target, is it appropriate to use Revealing Strike this combo, is the target going to be alive long enough to make the move viable?

Seeing as I don't raid with a warrior or a feral druid I simply don't use rupture anymore, although even with those debuffs it is easy to ignore rupture for the loss of say 2-3% dps, by my estimates. This leaves a spec that has left the traditional cycle style rotations of combat pre -cata to priority based rotations. Except, I'm prioritising S'n'D and eviscerate once every 45 seconds.

Ideally my preference for the use of Rupture was back in tier 8, where it felt as an integral part of the rotation but was required to be timed appropriately.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Another raid wide buff. We get ToT, with an arguably nerfed threat mechanic and one raid wide debuff/buff per spec. Bring our raid wide utility to parity with other classes would be a big plus.

Making rupture more useful or even changing it all together. There are numerous blogs around the interwebs discussing this so I won't bother to much here.

One a more personal note, making garrote awesome, to long has my namesake been a sometimes ability that doesn't awe a lot of people. :P

Make fast versions of all weapon types. Please. :) Axe and dagger or mace and dagger doesn't even make sense as a style of fighting... bludgeoning and parrying with your dagger maybe, the mind boggles.

What spells do you use the least?
In PVE-
Smoke bomb
Gouge
Evasion (with the diminishing returns of dodge and the advent of Combat Readiness this seems lackluster 999/1000 instances)

I really enjoy my class and will continue to play the rogue into the future. But these are some of the things that frustrate me about roguery in it's current form.

*Edit* Forgot to mention Expose Armour, that is how little I use it.

I like relying on the stealth mechanic for bonus damage and being able to get closer to bosses at the start as well as avoiding trash packs or taking routes that are shorter while the rest of the raid goes around.

What makes playing your class less fun?

running out of energy in the assassination spec because your rotation slows to a halt and it gets very annoying if rupture doesn't proc frequently. -------What type of content do you focus on?

Both PvE and PvP

Arenas, BGs.

Having to reapply poisons while in a raid group is boring, specially when we are in a long fight, in hope our poisons would last long enough until we kill the boss gives rogue a feeling of despair that isn't needed.

-------What makes playing your class more fun?

Poisons, along with shadow gives rogues the true aspect of a stealthy assassin, and that's why I won't abandon my rogue even thou the mechanics might be a little messed.

-------What makes playing your class less fun?

PvE:
The fact that we have 3 specs that only differs from one another by the fact that assassination brings poisons, combat brings adrenaline rush and sub brings shadow concept(less than it should).

When playing the 3 specs (and I played them extensively) there's no big diference in our rotation, always having to hold Slice and Dice up for all specs, Rupture for 2 of them(maybe 3), and the rest is basicaly: Assassination hitting Envenom, Combat Hitting Revealing Strike and Eviscerate and Sub hitting Recuperate and Eviscerate (Vendetta, Adrenaline Rush and Shadowdance being hit on CD, respectively).

It is hard to look at other classes like Mages, Warlocks (comparing to pure dps) or Shamans, Paladins, DKs when the playstyle of each spec is different (not considering healing specs, of course), making a frost dk having a completely different playstyle then an Unholy DK has. Same for prot pally and ret pally (pvp) and so on.

PvP:
The lack of mobility against some other specs is making Sub right now the only real PvP spec.
I like Assassination mechanics, but having to stack 5 Deadly Poison to REALLY do damage with Envenom kills the spec.
Combat is bad for PvP.

BOTH: (AND REALLY IMPORTANT FOR ME)
As mentioned before, the lack of "big numbers" for rogues sometimes makes the class feel inferior to others, which is sad. Even thou we are a "preasure" class, I can say that I feel bad when we have to reengage in combat during an arena and CAN'T hit for true, having to build up poisons/CP to TRY big numbers (same for PvE).
This definately makes playing almost all the other classes/specs more fun than playing a rogue.

-------How do you feel about your “rotation”?

PvE:
Assassination: It is a good rotation, but hitting more buttons would be better than hitting 3.

Combat: Combat rotation is boring. SS, SS, SS, (RS), Finisher and so on. But that is due to the fact that combat right now is just a warrior wannabe and should not be a rogue spec in the way it is sitting right now. The last time combat was fun to me (I started playing on the release of WOTLK) was when we had to stack ArPen to make combat a legitimate combatant. The rotation was almost the same, but the feeling of ArPen was great. (I think that this was the only time in these 3 years that I felt combat truly being a rogue spec. Ignoring armor is a rogue feeling, finding the weaken spots and hitting hard even thou the enemy is wearing plate was great!!!).

Sub: Sub rotation is ok. We have to really focus on our rotation in order to max its efficiency and it is great. BUT the fact that we have to keep 3 buffs up (speed, bleed, energy) and still worry about doing damage with Eviscerate sometimes kills the feeling of shadow we should have while playing Sub. (shadowdance every minute is not enough).

PvP: The only rotation I can think in PvP is the fact that you have to stack 5 DP as Assassination to do damage, and it is boring and broken.
