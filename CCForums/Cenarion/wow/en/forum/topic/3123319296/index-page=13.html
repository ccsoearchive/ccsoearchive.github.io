Part IV:

  • What spells do you use the least?

Expose Armor - Improved Expose Armor is an absolute waste in its current position. Assassination rogues already bypass armour with a significant portion of their damage breakdown, and to use Expose Armor is an unequivocal DPS loss. Although it can be a raid DPS increase should you find yourself in a physical damage heavy group that doesn't already have access to the debuff elsewhere. Given that druids and warriors both have access to, and can apply the debuff at significantly less personal cost, a group makeup where this situation would occur is nigh impossible. Therefore talenting for it is a poor choice in any and all conceivable situations.

Whereas both Combat and Subtlety have a higher focus on physical damage and could both make better use of Expose Armor. Under the right circumstances in can be a DPS increase for Combat if there is no other source available. So if not a removal a re-adjustment of postion for perhaps allowing Combat/Subtlety access to it might be a worthwhile change.

Deadly Throw - While has niche uses in PVP is almost always a poor choice in PVE. Even though it is usable on the move the damage is weak even though it costs your valuable CP, and the slow is of negligable value in a PVE setting.

Combat Readiness - Given the now shared CD outside of tightly specific niche use, Cloak of Shadows is far too important to ever put on CD like this.

What type of content do you focus on? PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? N/A

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? My biggest issue here is the way in which poisons are presented in the game. I personally would love a permanent poison enchant. I know that you are worried that this would make the class feel less unique, but buying reagents (poisons) from a normal-looking, bland vendor and doing a few clicks every hour isn't unique at all. I think a much MUCH cooler way to make them unique is to make them permanent (and therefore less clunky), and make new "shops" in Orgrimmar and Stormwind in which only rogues are allowed entry. You give your new weapon or a weapon that you wish to change poisons to a really shady guy that poisons them for you for a fee, and they stay like that. If you're concerned that people will wish to change poisons frequently, make then current mechanic "Temporary Instant Poison" or something that removes the current enchant and replaces it with a temporary version of another poison. I think it will accomplish 2 things: make it a lot cooler and more unique, and also make poisons less clunky. They're frankly boring the way they are.

What makes playing your class more fun? I love stealth. It's certianly something that makes me feel unique. I also like having two resources in combo points and energy. Energy as a whole is so much nicer than mana. I never feel starved for energy!

What makes playing your class less fun? Other classes are getting the mechanics that used to make my class feel unique. Druids having these mechanics made sense. Now Hunters have a stealth and their version of energy, and shadow priests and paladins have their versions of combo points. I don't feel like the class is as distinct as it used to be.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) That depends. For assassination, I like it. Some people say that combat is "harder," but I think that requiring proper cooldown timing is far from difficult, it's just another thing to keep track of. I like that in assassination I get to use 2 combo point generators based on the target's health, and I'm glad that I can't ignore rupture like I did in Wrath (because of Venomous Wounds). It's not a difficult rotation, but there's more stuff to keep track of than there used to be, and I think it's just right. Back to combat though, I think that Bandit's Guild and Restless Blades are just clunky talents. Restless blades gives combat dependence on cooldowns for sustained DPS. Isn't this a contradiction? This problem is exacerbated by how potent combat's cooldowns are. For Bandit's Guild, target switching without redirect is truly a nightmare. It also provides a certian level of inconsistency, making our damage a "wave" instead of a straight line, and a dramatic one at that. This brings up another problem: we already derive so much of our damage from sources that are completely out of our control, why do we need more? This has been a problem with the class for a long time, and it never seems to be addressed. Why can I get most of my damage from abilities that I hit directly? Why does so much of it have to come from melee autoattacks and poisons? Of course this is worse in Assassination because of Venomous Wounds' high damage, and the increased poison damage. I won't even get started on what's wrong with Subtlety's clunkiness.

What’s on your wish list for your class? Less clunky poisons, and easier time switching targets (less ramp-up time).
What spells do you use the least? Smoke Bomb, Shiv, Expose Armor.
