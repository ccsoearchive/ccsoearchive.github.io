09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


I think as many have pointed out, poisons are pretty high on the quality of life issues. As assassination, you pretty much go to dead weight for any kind of duration that you don't have instant and deadly on. For PvE, probably PvP as well, utility poisons may as well not exist.

The ramp up time and loss of damage for target swapping for assassination, and combat as well are pretty steep. (I haven't really played subtlety other than the Twin Dragons spec and encounter, so no good feedback there) It's usually not a choice on whether or not you're going to switch off of the boss or attack an add, it's just something you gotta suck it up and do, and we're not very effective at it. Combo points are probably okay, but needing to stack up a bleed, waiting on poisons to restack, etc. hurt. I don't recall any other dots that when you have to freshly apply them, you're guaranteed to lose a good margin of direct damage (instant poison procs from deadly poison).

This could be a non-issue as everyone requires buffs to be effective damage, but I feel like if I'm in a 10 man and we're low on melee buffs, I drop a pretty significant amount. Due to our highly passive damage breakdown, windfury still feels pretty crucial.

Another issue for me is the number of defensive cooldowns required to survive PvP, though some may like it and PvP isn't my main focus, though I'd like to enjoy it more.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What makes playing your class more fun?


I like the pace of the assassination rotation, the fact that it's largely about precision in timing rather than complete button mashing and global cooldown locking.

Stealth is also one of those things that I find it difficult to live without on new characters, though sometimes I wonder if our survivability and burst are too reliant on it as a crutch. If you can get back into stealth, you're pretty safe for a little bit, and you can't make someone who can pop out of nowhere be too insanely bursty.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What makes playing your class less fun?


Little utility to bring to a raid. Tricks of the trade was pretty fun back in the day, I suppose. Nowadays, it's just a button to increase someone else's dps that you push every 30 seconds, it gets pretty tedious, I suppose it could also be a quality of life issue as well. Other than that, we don't bring a whole lot aside from our damage. We have a few buffs that are really handy, but for a ten man for example, it doesn't feel great bringing more than one of us.

Not being good at PvP (My skill, not the potential of the class). I really wish there were an alternative playstyle that was viable. I'm not really big into subtlety and it seems it's the only one that can get around a battle field enough to be effective. I've heard rumors of some of the best arena rogues in the world using joke weapons when they PvP because their damage wasn't very important for their team's success. It'd be nice if there were a way I could play the spec I enjoy (assassination) and be effective, but since most of our damage is melee and instant poison and nobody stands around and lets us beat on them, we're not very bursty. I just want a role where my sole purpose wouldn't feel like just being on lock-down duty.

I'm not a major pvper nowadays, so take that last paragraph with a grain of salt, but that's just how it feels to me and it feels like a barrier.

09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


I enjoy the assassination rotation, it's pretty effective, timing based, and not too spammy. It could maybe use something else to give a tiny bit more complexity.

I don't play combat, but it seems a little too spammy/gcd/energy capped, but people like the fast pace involved in that, so I'm happy for them, bandit's guile seems to be the thing people complain about for that spec.

When I used the subtlety spec for Twin dragons, I played around with its rotation for a bit, it's pretty complex, but I don't feel like it gives the numbers to be worth it to me to play.

What’s on your wish list for your class?


Something new. I feel like I've been the same for a very long time, change for the sake of change isn't always a good thing, but I'm sure the development team has the creativity to find a game changer that doesn't change everything up too much to not recognize the class, but will make things feel a little more fresh.

Tricks of the trade seems like it could be a handy 30 minute buff like dark intent or focus magic, we could have something else that feels more meaningful to do for those every 30 seconds. The misdirect portion isn't bad, but aside from the pull, it doesn't really get used for that very often any more, it's just a damage increase we hit every time it's up.

An alternative, more bursty playstyle for pvp that has at least decent mobility. Subtlety has okay burst for shadow dance and has decent mobility with shadow step, but I just want something more for the spec that I play, something that has the ability to, but doesn't feel like it's only purpose is to be on lock-down duty.

Feint and vendetta not on the global cooldown.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Feeling like a rogue when I play, like using vanish to escape, using a smoke cloud to save someone in PvP, or sneaking around in general. Poisons and their respective affects, and having to choose between them.


What makes playing your class less fun?

In PvP, when I feel helpless against a target, for example being kited mercilessly or chain-cced. I also dislike not being able to get away from a target (for example a feral druid or a hunter) no matter what I do or how many cooldowns I blow
In PvE, the lack of rogue-unique utilities.


How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The rotation is somewhat challenging and engaging, I constantly have to watch and keep my timers up. All things considered, it's reasonably forgiving, and when you pull a perfect rotation it's nice to see the numbers soar. However, sometimes through lack of crits or other mechanics (like being away from the target) make it difficult to pick up my rotation again or make me feel energy-starved. Also, it's painful to switch targets at times (poisons don't switch, rupture doesn't switch) and I feel like every second I'm not on the target is a major hit on damage.


What’s on your wish list for your class?

For PvE, some more utility, I don't like the fact that the only thing I really bring to the raid is damage, especially as a melee class. Damage-wise we're competative, so I have no problems there.
For PvP, something that would help me more reliably escape a target. Vanish is awesome, especially with the delay for stealth breaking, but it becomes tricky when I have to choose between Cloak and Combat readiness. If I save my cooldown for cloak, then I'm more secure that I can get away, but if I use Combat Readiness, then I stand virtually no chance. However if I don't use Combat Readiness, there is no way I can live against some targets to even put up a fight. (1v1, or otherwise) Being able to use throw while running would be nice, just for something to do against frost mages. Maybe a shift in combat talents for that two points in sprint breaking roots, which I would be very inclined to spec into.


What spells do you use the least?

Expose Armor, Deadly throw ( it brings almost no benefit aside from a physical based slow as opposed to poisons, and you have to blow all your combo points to use it). Feint in PvP.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The dumbing down of our class, reducing assassination to nothing but an opener into a pve rotation. This is not involving gameplay and does not make me want to play assassination. I cannot comment on combat, it's always been a pve spec in my eyes and I could care less how it's handled. However, assassination has a soft spot in my heart since I played it for so long in previous expansions. Because we only have one viable pvp spec, I actually do get bored of my main which is a rogue.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

Subtlety is perfect, it has little to no investment on targets aka poisons or "Stacks" of anything and everything is built in or requires a single button press. This might sound like it's broken or needs to be fixed, but the continuing trend in pvp is to swap when necessary. Having no investment on targets means I can swap when I need to with only needed to apply a single Hemorrhage debuff. This essentially allows me to roll Recuperate and Slice and Dice as need be, with Slice and Dice being an optional dps increase and not something mandatory. I feel like this is exactly how a pvp spec should be played.

Assassination is on the other hand a joke and bothers me to no end. It in it's current form requires me to use Garrote as my opener, I can use Cheap Shot (Ambush is absolutely awful as assassination) but I want Garrote rolling to proc VW. Immediately after my opener I need to Rupture, I then need to make sure Slice and Dice is active as it's how I stack my poisons. I then need to either find a way to squeeze in a defensive finisher, Kidney or Recuperate, before I spam Envenom. This sort of game play in pvp is not viable, the first five seconds of a rogue opening on someone involves the other character regardless of spec or class to begin kiting and trying to disrupt your opener especially if you're assassination. With it's heavy ramp up time and investment on targets assassination is just terribly slow and inadequate.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I'd like to see assassination be fixed and made viable right along with subtlety in terms of pvp viability. Removal of heavy investments into poison talents, possibly even the ones baked into the tree (Bring back flat physical damage), access to Gouge, and even possibly a copy of Preparation. The ability to disregard the pve rotation required to do damage would be a plus. I do not mind using Rupture, however the stacking on Deadly Poison is awful and requires you to use Slice an Dice to maximize your damage which in turn means you might as well take Cut to the Chase. This is fine in pve, but in pvp you do not have the ability to juggle this many finishers; Recuperate, Rupture, Slice and Dice, Envenom, and Kidney Shot when necessary. That is almost EVERY finisher the class has. In addition, ever since the removal of Preparation and the robust requirement to do burst damage assassination has been the heaviest kited spec in the game. I'd like to see something done for this spec, it went from essentially Gladiator status to Challenger/Rival if that.

What spells do you use the least?

Rupture and Expose Armor are terrible, EA has it's uses against some healers and Rupture is only viable if you want to keep something out of stealth. But by themselves, the damage or effect is miserable.
