  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]


    • Both, though more PvE these days.

  • If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]


    • I've enjoyed all aspects of PvE.

  • If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]


    • BGs, and I was a big fan of assorted world pvp.

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


    • S'pose inventory poisons are the most obvious answer, and applying them is jus a lil more of a deal than it needs to be.

    Otherwise, the reliability of vanish in both PvP and PvE has always been aggrivating.

    Oh, and setting up Shadow Dance hotbar.

  • What makes playing your class more fun?


    • The pace is fun, and the ability to counter assorted situations uniquely or provide help to a battle other than dps really gets me feeling heroic. Whether it be temporary tanking, blinding a loose mob, ease of stuns/interrupts, or the like.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?


    • Being kited in PvP when cooldowns are down is a terrible feeling of futility. Having less opportunities to provide more than dps to battles, and having relatively less answers to assorted problems in raids or the like. Ranged simply seems more useful and tends to have better utility.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


    • I enjoy Assn and Sub rotations. Assn could use a tad more complexity perhaps. Sub feels like its bleeds need to be made higher priority. Combat feels significantly dull and slow though; Revealing Strike feels like it needs to be more interesting rather than what may amount to a slightly altered passive finisher improver(see below).

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?


    • More poisons with more effects, preferably ones that counter or cause additional situations,

    A new mechanic with poisons for better effects (Had an idea a while back, sort of stacking poison debuffs as an alternative combo-point-finisher technique, just causing unique effects rather than our current actual finishers.).

    A dispelling ability. (Perhaps through Revealing Strike or Expose Armor, balanced by ability alteration or talents?)

    More ranged capabilities (Debuff-oriented or the like, a combo-point ability would be fun, though I have dreamed of an almost complete ranged-alternative rogue...but that's a kinda big overhaul to balance).

    Revealing Strike to be more interesting (I did miss the reactive effect of oldschool Riposte, perhaps this could react to something else and cause an additional effect? Maybe tied to Bandit's Guile?)

    Distract to be useful in combat.

    Companion pets stealthing with us.

    And I don't know why or how it'd be reasonably useful other than messin with people in pvp...but I have long desired a knockback.

  • What spells do you use the least?


    • Mind-Numbing Poison (Just rarely seems enough, considering other poisons).

    Wounding Poison (For intended purposes, that is. For now it seems best suited just for Sub as it procs more often, thus benefitting HaT).

    Combat Readiness (Its usefulness is marginal and uninteresting. And as a decision between it and Cloak, I'd rather save the cooldown even on the rare offchance I may need Cloak)

    Shiv (I like Shiv and both of its effects, but its so rare to need them, I think an improvement to poisons would be the best route in improving Shiv as well)

    Deadly Throw (Too weak, and too minor for just one throw. For that short period in BC where it could refund CPs and be reused, I found a good bit of fun use in it.)

    Expose Armor (I like the utility, but it's rare to need it, thus hard to wanna talent it, and a bit of a pain untalented)

    Redirect(Often just seems easier to hit a new target and restart CPs)

    Distract(It would be such an interesting PvP skill-move if useable in combat)

    Disarm Trap (I would totally support giving it an additional application. I've always wondered the effectiveness of havin a stealthed, close-range "cast-time" ability. Little extra risk for a little advantage through sabotage? Maybe tie their shoe-laces together from stealth! :O)

    Poisons are obviously a pain. They take up bag space, money, and time to get. I understanding needing to monitor buffs on the rogue, but constant vendor trips for an item that integral to our class is annoying after awhile. I know there have been vast improvements in the poison system, in fact I remember when rogues had to buy mats and level a secondary poison profession, but that doesn't mean it can't get better. Making your own poisons was not a fun or interesting mechanic, and got quite tedious after awhile, so it was removed. Same is true for constant trips to the vendor: Neither fun nor interesting, but very tedious. They should be a learned spell, similar to shaman weapon imbue.

    # What makes playing your class more fun?
    I think the game is more fun when I feel like my character is the one causing the actions, and can respond or prevent enemy actions if I anticipate correctly.

    # What makes playing your class less fun?
    Things like RNG, while I realize can be necessary to an extent, are overdone in other areas. For example: Warlock or Priest instant AoE fear can send me miles from my healer. Why is the other team rewarded with my then terrible positioning for using an ability that in all likelihood cannot be prevented outside of blanket silences, stuns, or general CCs? Nobody can be CC'd 100% of the time unless the teammates are not helping at all. This is just one example, it exists in other areas but I just want to be able to do stuff on my character that feels like it matters, and not feel cheated because they other guy got lucky while I wasn't in control of my character.

    Also, Faerie Fire needs to function like hunters mark. Its ridiculous I cannot use a 2min CD (3min untalented) because of something that is instant essentially spammable. That is NO FUN.

    # How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I like it!

    # What’s on your wish list for your class?
    I want to be able to play more than just subtlety in arena. I like assassination and combat, and I honestly feel like those two specs were just given up on from a PvP perspective, which is frustrating to say the least. If those specs are to have any hope, Preparation needs to be a baseline talent, otherwise double smokebomb will almost always outweigh whatever the other specs bring to the table.
    # What spells do you use the least?
    Expose Armor is hands down the biggest absolute joke of a finisher in the history of WoW in its current state. Not a single rogue I know has it on their action bars except for maybe novelty purposes or a massive surplus of combo points. Its such an unbelievably minuscule damage increase, I am likely better off refreshing my SnD or Recup instead. Same is true for combat or assassination. The ability is just god awful, unfun, uninteresting, and pretty much an afterthought to most rogues.
