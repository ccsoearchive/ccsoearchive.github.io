[edited because I almost forget to mention some stuff]
What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics, hoping to start raiding after spending most of my time in PvP

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Battlegrounds, almost entirely objective-focused (EFC kills, ninja caps/recaps, etc.)

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Having to carry and re-buy all my poisons (especially with different battlegrounds having different priorities for poison usage) and all of the 20 coppers greys filling my bags when I Pick Pocket Cataclysm targets. Especially the latter, since the stuff you get off PPing level 30 mobs is more valuable. Also, having DoTs break stealth- it sucks in PvE where we have to either blow one of our most powerful survivability cooldowns or not use stealth (which often means not doing anything or getting almost killed by a single target while leveling) for the duration of whatever the mob we just killed put on us right as it died, and it sucks even more in PvP when even blowing Cloak of Shadows doesn't get rid of bleeds. I can live with Hunter's Mark sticking because that's a counter and a class feature, but a class that can nullify some of the world's strongest magic with "minor mysticism" should really be able to do something as relatively simple as hiding a bloody wound for more than three seconds.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Shadowstep and crit streaks, and the fact that Sub almost never starves for energy.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Having to keep up my finishers, especially in PvE, especially in PvE fights where there's time off the boss (Anhuur, for example, usually pops his shield before I even get Recup and Slice and Dice up). This is really a two-part problem: The rotation is so tight it gets screwed by the smallest things, including bosses who turn a lot at the wrong time or even using Shadow Dance and replacing Backstab with the more costly Ambush for six seconds. The second is that Subtlety cannot function without using 5 combo points for every single finisher but gets energy starved if I have to provide too many of those points myself (essentially when my group isn't getting enough crits, an especially common problem in Heroics or 10 man raids where the average player's crit chance is relatively low, due to either poor gear or class stat priorities). These problems feed into each other and make Shadow Dance very hard to use and impossible to use on cooldown, since it requires my finishers to somehow all have enough time that I can afford to be using 3 or 4 point Eviscerates for energy regen between Ambushes if my group doesn't give me two combo points during the GCD- which is even more annoying when I think about how I could have that kind of buffer consistently in a group with enough crits to make the whole "can I get away with a 3-point finisher" question a non-issue. Also, bleeds.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I like it when it works (i.e. when I keep my finishers up and have enough time on each to deal with boss movement, target swaps, etc. without something dropping), but I hate how rarely it works, especially in Heroics and 10 man raids with certain class combinations where HaT can go up to 5 seconds without proccing while the whole group is alive and on the boss or healing actively.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
A HaT buff, a talent to make Backstab sometimes proc an extra combo point, a few extra seconds on my finishers, a way to make Shadow Dance or Ambush refresh a finisher or two, a way to make Eviscerate refresh another finisher- really anything that would let me keep up my finishers and use Shadow Dance on a regular basis with less dependence on other players' RNGs to create ideal conditions. Also a cooldown or other mechanism to let me keep my finishers up or instantly apply all of them from the same combo points during/after boss phases where low-health adds or other mechanics force me to let all of them drop off. Finally, some more valuable Pick Pocket loot, something to address my issues with poisons, and a way to stealth while dotted and/or clear bleeds.

What spells do you use the least?
Combat Readiness, because I don't engage players directly and Cloak of Shadows is way stronger; Smoke Bomb, Gouge, and Shiv, because I forget I have them and can't think of a really good use for them; and Deadly Throw, because I forget I have it.

Also, I don't see the point of Mind-Numbing Poison.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Being able to come out of nowhere and crawling all over some poor unsuspecting fool before he knows what's going on >=D

What makes playing your class less fun?

Shield Abilities (Power Word: Shield, Ice Barrier, Mana Shield, Sacred Shield, etc) and the fact that we have no way to circumnavigate them or effectively deal with them as a melee class. Warriors have Shattering Throw, Paladins and Death Knights and Feral Druids can outlast them by merit of being tank classes, and Enhancement Shamans can get rid of them, but the other thing is that if those aren't options all of those classes are capable of delivering a consistent damage amount that lets them burn the shield down and keep going. Rogues on the other hand are primarily spike damage, so what ends up happening is we use a spike or 3 getting through the shield (critting a disc priest for a whooping 2, that's something I love to see on my screen), but by the time we can hit them with our next spike they've got the blasted shield up again. It's incredibly aggravating.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Nothing too strenuous here, although I would love it if some of our Combo Point Builders had more potent damage and effects (Hemorrhage getting a bleed effect was excellent, but maybe bump up its damage a bit?), that way it wouldn't feel like I need to use a weaker ability 4-5 times before I can let a good attack off the chain.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Well, I'd certainly like to see that shared cooldown revoked, but I bet you're tired of hearing that. Personally, I'd like some improvements to Evasion, like putting it back on the Preparation list as well as giving it something to make it more viable against Paladins, Death Knights, and Enhancement Shamans (who can simply get around it by using spells). I'd also love to see an increase to the Cloak of Shadows duration, because while that 5 seconds is awesome it really only buys us immunity from 2 cast time spells (assuming between 1.5 and 2 second cast times, which seems to be the average) or 3 instant casts and overall I just don't feel like it makes enough of a gap in a caster's rotation o' pain for us to get in there and cut them. Hell, if the Cloak's duration was increased to 10 seconds like Combat Readiness, I'd be alright with the shared cooldown. Lastly, I really wish we had some proc abilities/effects a la Shadow Trance, Killing Machine, Arcane Missiles/Hot Streak/Brain Freeze, etc. Rogues already have a pretty unique style that's fast paced and edge-of-your-chair exciting if done right, and I think something like this (maybe even something like the Hunter Master Marksman Talent and it's Ready, Set, Aim... mechanic) could be just the spice it needs to really blast into an adrenaline-flooded realm of its own.

What spells do you use the least?
Gouge and Expose Armor are the only ones that I don't use for a non-utility reason (I don't use backstab because I'm a sword rogue, Hemorrhage makes Sinister Strike obsolete, etc). I also don't use Redirect at all really, simply because its often more useful to use those leftover CPs to pop Slice And Dice or Recuperate.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
The class, and especially combat, is genuinely un-fun in the raiding environment. I've raided the other two specs as well, but they really were't much of an improvement. I really like the excitement of progression raiding, and I miss it at times, but raiding as a rogue in Cata was just drudgery. Yes, yes, I know I could always re-roll, and I used to alt-raid fairly extensively on my DK back in Wrath, but it's not the same. I've always been a rogue.

What makes playing your class more fun?
The unique touches of the class like Stealth and Pick-Pocket and Sap. Omg-last-second-tank-is-dead-2%-to-go evasion tanking. Oooh, and back in Wrath, when it was a lot safer to use, I loved Killing Spree.

What makes playing your class less fun?
I'm a combat rogue at heart. I've almost always been a combat rogue. I still want to be a combat rogue. I hate playing the current energy-starved Mutilate spec. But Cata combat is the most clunky, moose-on-rollerskates spec imaginable.
--Bandit's Guile.
I think about some of the fights I did before I went casual...25-man heroic Throne, heroic Omnitron, even goold ole Al-Akir...and how ungodly, /headdesk-ingly frustrating it was to watch every...single...Deep Insight tick down completely unused because by the time it FINALLY came up, it was also time to jump to a different platform, switch targets, stop DPSing, dodge tornados, or otherwise run in to, out of, or around some fire or other. It's a mechanism that sounds reasonable in theory, but is terrible in application. It's clunky and frustrating, the kind of frustrating that just saps all the fun out of raiding. Raiding is supposed to be about fighting against a boss, not fighting against your spec. It's the antithesis of what it used to mean to be a rogue. Rogue-ing used to be about precision, about leveraging abilities to the greatest advantage. Now it's about how to mitigate the disadvantages of the spec.
--Mastery
Granted, the new and improved mastery is much better than the old mastery ("Original Mastery: So bad it makes +Spirit look good!") But it's still not all that good for combat, which raises the question of why it even exists as a stat. I mean, mastery was supposed to simplify the stat system in general, for all classes, but when it winds up being the junk stat that always gets reforged away, that really isn't much of a simplification.
--The fussiness and lack of precision that comes from mixing BG with Restless Blades. "Hey, let's try to time the unknowable against the uncontrollable!" I think of all the times the raid leader said "save CDs for X or Y" and all I could think was "Save them? I don't even KNOW them." Throw in more fussiness with Revealing Strike, Eviscerate vs. Rupture, energy-capping with AR, and the fact that Killing Spree ranges from completely unuseable to downright dangerous and the whole thing just turns into one gigantic cluster.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I miss having a genuine rotation, like I did back in BC and Wrath. With Cata combat, it's impossible to have an "accepted order in which abilities are used to MAXIMUM EFFICIENCY" because a large portion of the spec is either uncontrollable (the insight states of BG) or unknowable (the effect of Restless Blades upon cooldown timers). Instead, combat has an "if/then/otherwise" flow-chart of questions and answers made to MINIMIZE DISADVANTGES. It's exhausting and frustrating.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Put it back to the way it was. Please. Just put it back. Or at least get rid of Bandit's Guile and find a better way to balance the class. Give us back the precision and control that we used to have. If you insist on keeping BG, then swap it from a mostly-passive cycle to an active set of cooldowns. That way rogues can consciously and actively decide when to use Shallow Insight, when to use Moderate Insight, and when to use Deep Insight, instead of being a dancing monkey slave to a cycle that has no correlation or relevance to the actual fight.

What spells do you use the least?
