What type of content do you focus on?
PvE primarily, PvP on the side.

If PvE, what type of PvE?
Normal 10-mans.

If PvP, what type of PvP?
BGs.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
I don't mind having to refresh my poisons every hour, but it is rather frustrating having to carry a stack of each poison, if not more, for the purpose of situational encounters. With having to poison 2 (3 if I do my throwing weapon) weapons every hour, my inventory space fills and empties quickly since I need to regularly replace what I've used. I would be much happier if each poison had infinite uses, at least then I can dedicate X bag slots to one item, and never have to worry about it again.

Since Poisons are not an optional reagent by any means, I believe we are the only class that has to dedicate inventory space for reagents that are used 100% of the time. It would be even better if poisons were simply part of a skill, like Death Knight's runeforging.

Pick Lock takes too long. I believe the glyph should just become baseline, since rogue minors are actually useful.

The biggest combat-mechanic "quality-of-life" issue I have involves Shadow Dance. It is a very powerful cooldown, however it is a pain to set up. The bar does not exist anywhere else except in Shadow-Dance state, meaning you only have the six or so seconds to modify the bar to your liking before you must wait another minute to get to play with it again. This makes it difficult to set up the proper abilities that require stealth in addition to the combo point dumps that will inevitably need to be used to get the most out of Shadow Step. I could always add these buttons elsewhere, but that's an unneeded hassle that only clutters my already maxed out action bars further. The solution? Allow Shadow Step to use the Stealth bar. Rogues everywhere have been asking for this for a while...

Also, for the sake of other classes, give Shadowstep some sort of effect. In PvP there is far too much to watch out for to also read all of the many buffs the rogue will have to see whether or not he has a 6 second tear-you-to-shreds buff.

What makes playing your class more fun?
I play Sub for PvP and PvE, and it is by far the most fun I've had with a rogue. I enjoy the high damage output combined with the many utilities the spec has. When played correctly (and it is the most challenging rogue spec to play properly), it is an absolute amazing experience since the challenge provides good reward!

What makes playing your class less fun?
There are only a few major complaints I have that weren't listed in the Quality-of-Life section.
With the adjustments to threat where it is no longer a real issue, Feint is no longer a threat-dump utility. Technically it is, but I ONLY use it (and I'm also very proud of myself for this), to take the reduced AoE damage. So many rogues out there get their faces ripped apart because they don't use their full arsenal. To get to the point, this is now a damage reduction ability and is extremely situational. Not only does it cost 1/5 of your total energy bar, but it is on the GCD. One of the two should be adjusted so it isn't such a high penalty to use. Off the GCD, or no energy. I may suggest upping the cooldown slightly as well so that it isn't something rogues just macro or click at the slightest hint of danger. Require thought!

Preparation is one of those big cooldowns that a class gets, and it's on the GCD. I believe that ability-refreshing cooldowns should all be off of the GCD, across all classes. Assuming it has a reasonable cooldown, of course.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
As a sub rogue in both PvP and PvE, I feel as though the rogues are at a very fun spot. Their rotation involves micromanaging several buffs, and squeezing in as much DPS as possible when everything is active. It requires the most skill to pump out good damage than either of the other 2 rogue specs, making it a challenge that can be extremely rewarding when properly played.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
There isn't much new or exciting I could ask for rogues. Having played every class at end-game, they are among one of my favorites and everything about them just feels like it played great.

What spells do you use the least?
Distract, Deadly Throw, Expose Armor, Shiv, Dismantle are the big ones. The issue with this question and rogues is simply that they are full of utility abilities that are highly situational. Most of their abilities are rarely used, and those who focus solely on their DPS use even less.

Distract I feel could get some good use out of PvP. It should definitely function in-combat, making the players turn wherever the rock landed. It can be extremely useful against casters and, in some cases, melee.

  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PVE.
  • If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Casual levels of nonheroic current raids. Heroic dungeons. Interesting quest lines and leveling, at times.
  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Not a ton of quality of life issues on a Rogue. I guess if I had to pick something, I'd say that with poisons scaling with level and costing mere copper, it feels like they might as well be a skill rather than a reagent that you have to go out and buy.
  • What makes playing your class more fun?
One of the things I honestly like about being a Rogue is not being cooldown-locked, by which I mean that you have time to think and pool energy between attacks and you don't always lose overall DPS by not using abilities back-to-back-to-back (as long as your energy doesn't cap and your buffs don't fall off). I like that I have time to look over the battlefield as I'm playing a rogue because of these little breaks, as opposed to being locked onto watching my resource/buff bar.
  • What makes playing your class less fun?
In contrast to the above, in certain specs and at certain times, there are occasionally times where I DO have to watch my energy/buff durations/CPs closely as I pool energy or wait for just the right time to refresh a buff/envenom/etc. I don't like taking my eyes off the battlefield too much, and though Rogue may be one of the best classes for it, there are still times where you have to.

I hate Shadow Dance in particular because of this. You have to pool energy before you use it, and your cooldowns/buff timings/energy regen rate/etc. are so close that you have to keep a close eye on it while it's up- and if you stumble out of position because of the focus on the UI rather than your positioning, or because the boss moved or something, you probably lost a very important fraction of a second to repositioning and will get one less ambush out of Shadow Dance.

Subtlety in general has this problem, I feel. Or, at least it did at the launch of Cata- I haven't tried it much since then. I feel like Sub just has too many buff/debuff durations to watch.
  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I'm pretty much fine with Assassination's rotation, though I suppose I have a little gripe in that it just feels like Rogue rotations have been some of the most static ones for a long, long time. I played Assassination in BC and I can't really recall it feeling much different than it does right now.

I'm not a big fan of Subtlety's rotation, as I mentioned above. There's just a lot to keep track of.
  • What’s on your wish list for your class?
I'd like to see stealth incorporated into active combat a little bit more, even if it is just in the Subtlety spec. Subtlety always felt like the stealth/opener focused tree to me, and the new finisher-oriented stuff in Cata just seemed... off. I know in-combat stealth is tricky, especially when it comes to keeping PVP balanced, but I'd love if there was a way the design team could implement the feeling of being stealthy into combat as Sub spec. Shadow dance and the occasional vanish just doesn't seem to cut it for me.

Back in the Cata beta days, I was always a proponent of Shadow Dance somehow causing temporary combat stealth, though I don't remember exactly how my proposal went anymore... I think it was something like "Shadow Dance is a passive talent that causes your Shadowstep to make you enter stealth for a maximum of 4 seconds on use", with the hope that a limit of 4 seconds wouldn't be enough time to let you drop combat and use real stealth, so you couldn't use Shadowstep as an escape/vanish mechanism. Also, it probably would trigger the stealth cooldown upon dropping the temp stealth, so that wouldn't be an option regardless.

I'd also like to see Slice and Dice not be such a core, necessary move (at least, not for every single spec. I'm fine with it in one or two.), and to see at least one spec use mostly active damage-causing finishers. Shifting the damage away from passive/autoattack bonuses (Slice and Dice, Poison procs) to active, directly-caused damage (More rupture/envenom/eviscerate/CP Builders) would be a nice thing to see.
  • What spells do you use the least?
In PVE? Probably Stealth (see above), Expose Armor, and Shiv (aside from enrage, which I guess is enough). Plus some of the PVP oriented stuff like Gouge/Smoke Bomb.
