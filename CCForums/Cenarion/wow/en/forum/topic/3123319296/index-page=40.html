Is Combat single target dps going to be kept mediocre to compensate for having one very situationally over powered dps ability? Well, at least it's off the gcd.

    Killing Spree: The ability that you can't use this expansion for fear that you may kill yourself or others during it's teleports. Does ok damage when you can find good times to use it. I was never a fan of the target switching (what exactly is the point of this?) as I'm sure others aren't. This ability also adds to the a fore mentioned energy problems. It is on the gcd and prevents you from using your energy to sustain your rotation during it's duration, short as it is. This of course causes you to cap your energy and that energy translates to lost potential damage.

    Shadow Step: Great ability. The only reason people have been playing Subtlety for like 4 years. Make it baseline and make Sub a legitimate spec.

    Redirect: That ability is, quite frankly, a total joke. One of the new Rogue abilities. At a 1 minute cd this ability is supposed to offset the newly implemented penalties for switching targets. But does it really? Sure, it's supposed to transfer your combo points and insight level (oh, we will get to this), but no poisons. But think about it for a moment. When you switch targets, what is happening? You're attacking a primary target (lets assume... hmm... the boss) and you need to do a quick burn on some add. Sound familiar? Well, you target the add, burn that gcd to use Redirect, most likely capping your energy, spend at most 10 seconds killing the add, and switch back. Wouldn't it be nice to Redirect back onto the boss? Oh, but you just used it 10s ago. Sure, maybe you get more damage on the add depending on your level of insight and assuming you didn't lose too much energy oh that switch, but you're really back to where you were without Redirect then with. This seems to be a common theme for Cataclysm. Most abilities, more complexity, and what do you get for your trouble? Nothing.
