Subtlety PvE
Heroic dungeons, rarely alt raids

Quality of Life:
Spinning mobs. Why must they spin? Find it in your heart to cure their vertigo, oh blizzard devs. Find it in your heart to weep for the sub rogue energy capping and frantically running in circles trying to get that ambush off before shadowdance ends.
Nameplates : this isn't rogue-specific, but the way nameplates are anchored right now seems to make it harder to see the sap and blind animations.

Fun:
Ambush and backstab are just made of fun. Positioning behind mobs and feeling like you are using strategy at all times to produce more damage is fun. Positioning is only a problem because those pesky mobs are all about the eye contact when they hurl their spears. Weaving in vanishes and shadowdance is fun. Watching your energy and maintaining a somewhat complex rotation is fun.

Not fun:
Why, oh why do they spin?
Our finishing moves, as sub, just don't hit very hard, and seem lackluster relative to our combo point generating moves. This is anticlimactic in terms of playstyle, and counterintuitive relative to other rampup systems like holy power.
Forgetting your keybinds and then being mortified because you just hit the Fan of Knives button. Then removing Fan of Knives from your bar. Aggravating the tank when tricks isn't up because you single target things on aoe pulls. Explaining that you single target because Fan is so bad you took it off your bars. Being kicked from party.

Least used:
Fan of Knives. Expose armor. Mind-numbing poison. Deadly throw.

Wish list:
Make them stop spinning!

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

      PvE (with light PvP)

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

      Heroic Mode raiding, with a focus on 25-mans.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

      Casual BGs

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

      The only quality of life I can associate to the class is Poisons as a reagent rather than an application from our spellbooks. Given we no long have reagents for blind or vanish, having stacks of poison filling our inventories feels like an unnecessary burden.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

      Mobility and versatility are my greatest views on the rogue. While the class specializes in very little and offers little more than the power of substained DPS, we have measures and answers for most every issue. Enrage? We handle it. Sunder? We can do it. Emergency tanking? I've saved raids from wipes so many times.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

      Currently, I have roughly 6 macros dedicated to Premeditation alone. They are all linked to my various stealth abilities because, in the midst of everything, it's an annoyance to have a talent that requires it for practical use.

      Rupture, of all my bleeds, feels like it's just tossed in there for the sake if being there for a spec like Combat. Now, to maximize my DPS, I must use Rupture, but that's maybe a hundred extra DPS. However, without it, I have a very boring DPS rotation, which isn't significantly impacted with the added ability in the first place. If anything, I would like to see something added to this ability to warrant its use in the Combat spec or dismiss it entirely with a feature entirely unique to Combat.


    How do you feel about your “rotation”?

      Assassination: This spec deals with a dull rotation from the get go. It's summed up with the least amount of abilities: Mutilate / Backstack, Slice and Dice, Rupture, Envenom. Of all three specs, it is the most boring and remarkably easiest rotation. One rarely even needs to pay attention to their energy with this spec. I tend to steer clear of it entirely.

      Combat: Combat is a bit static in and of itself, but it has more dynamic in regards to the need to keep track of several factors. Spamming Sinister Strike is fine and well, and I like that Revealing Strike is thrown in to add to the mix; players are rewarded for using it properly, and punished for using it improperly (by way of less damage). Eviscerate is fine and well. It's direct reward for an arguably slow build up. As I mentioned before, however, I don't like how Rupture is handled for the rotation. It's the only spec where Rupture is used for the sake of being used rather than having additional benefit for the player

      Subtlety: While I do not often use the spec, I love its rotation. It's dynamic, more so than any other class' rotation in the entire game. There are more variables in the rotation, and it changes nigh constantly because of them. Its rotation is unique, challenging, and satisfying when the player pulls it off properly. The buff to Hemorrhage's upfront damage has made it even more viable, albeit not optimal. I think, in a casual raid setting, a Subtlety rogue is viable because of these options. Unfortunately, rogues looking to maximize their DPS still steer clear because of the limitations the backstab presents. Overall, however, Subtlety feels the most roguish of the specs and rotations.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

      Killing Spree to be unaffected by the location of a mob / target. While I enjoy this skill, I have find new and exciting ways to kill myself with it on every single boss encounter.

      Rupture for Combat having something added to it, or for it to be affected by something. Anything is better than the nothing that it is currently.

      Bandit's Guile to build up regardless of the target it's on.

      A New Damage Finisher: Our current ones are instant gratification (Eviscerate / Envenom) and gradual gratification (Rupture). I've no real problem with that, but I think something new could spice up rotations across the board for the class; perhaps a mix of both of a finisher that has effects based on the particular spec of the class.

    What spells do you use the least?

      Gouge: It can be a decent clinch move for PvP, but I don't like how it can interrupt the combo points on the current target of choice when it could serve so well as another interrupt. Also, it's useless in PvE.

      Smoke Bomb: Again, great for PvP, but very limited in PvE.

    Q1. Both. PvE more than PvP.
    Q2. Heroics and raiding.
    Q3. Mostly BGs. I have done arena in the past and try to as much as I can but it is not my focus.
    Q4. I don't have many that leap out at me. The only things I can think of belong on my wish list, more so than are quality-of-life problems.
    Q5. Being able to cheat death with the rogue's cooldowns has always been a huge thing for me. It feels so cool, and fun. Though I have my problems with it, I also really enjoy the rogue's unique resource system, and not being worried about global cooldowns.
    Q6. I play Combat, though I played Assassination through Wrath. I don't like how Combat suffers from poisons dropping off. Though it's not as severe as Assassination, and they are, of course, given passives to ensure this doesn't happen, it feels off, since poisons are the focus of another tree. Granted poisons are still a rogue baseline, of course, but as there are still 'proper' poisons and a 'proper' way to use them, I don't like the feel of losing the damage I could be doing from a passive source. I also feel that 'finisher' abilities don't have much weight. Despite the mechanic behind rogues being to build up a large amount of power and then release it on the target, Eviscerate, the primary damage finisher, just feels sort of boring. Slice and Dice I don't have as much of a problem with. Though it is passive, it is a significant buff and very important. Kidney Shot feels slightly awkward too. Bandit's Guile is also a rather frustrating mechanic. While it can be moved around, more often it feels like a shackle on the rogue, making me not want to use my more powerful abilities because it would not be as good as it could be, and very often in raids I'll be cycling between targets without Bandit's Guile ever reaching Moderate. Still, despite my complaints I am largely satisfied- these are not huge concerns, simply things I thought up when asked this question.
    Q7. As Combat, it feels rather simple, but is good enough. Tinkering with it would likely not be worth it. Though it may not be considered part of the rogue's 'rotation', I do feel all rogue specializations could do with more offensive cooldowns, particularly shorter-term ones. The existing cooldowns also don't feel particularly exciting, with the exception, I think, being Killing Spree. Cold Blood, while useful, is extremely passive- as is Vendetta. Vendetta also relies on you being on one target for, though it may not seem it, a huge amount of time, and as we no longer make fights like Patchwerk, it both feels very boring and passive in PvE and also is very difficult to use to its full potential. Adrenaline Rush also feels rather boring, and can frustratingly easily cap out your energy, which makes me feel as though I'd used it wrong and am wasting resources, though there is only so much energy I can burn.
    Q8. More fun, powerful-feeling cooldowns, like Killing Spree. I like that cooldown for its extremely powerful feel, even if it doesn't hit extremely hard, and for the fact that improper use can actually be your death. Not every time, like Koralon was in Ulduar, but say when the back of the boss happens to be in fire. Perhaps rogues having more raid utility. I feel sad that many other classes can extremely easily benefit the raid, especially with buffs and debuffs I could provide but are too inconvenient for me to try, like Expose Armor when a druid can much more easily Fairie Fire, or my poison debuffs I could potentially provide, but can't because again, other classes can give them easily and these debuffs would hurt my damage to use- for Assassination, significantly so. Though I do like Tricks of the Trade and its uniqueness, however, I wish I could give a very significant, impactful buff or debuff to my raid, rather than simply my spec's one passive debuff. I also find myself slightly wishing for a bit of a more fun ability, like the mage's Slow Fall. Something that isn't huge, but is enjoyable to have for running around, and for people who decide to level a rogue. I consider, say, Safe Fall to be fun-ish, as it allows you to drop for quite a distance, but it's passive and not particularly noticeable until you glyph it.
    Q9. I try very hard to use them all, but though I do occasionally use things like Blind in a pinch and other such spells, I must say there never seems to be an opportunity to use Smoke Bomb. In PvE, any uses it may have on bosses are usually processed out because they would be too powerful and unfair to groups without rogues. I also never use Combat Readiness, for since it shares a cooldown with Cloak of Shadows there is absolutely no point in using it, for Cloak will always be better and more helpful, while for damage reduction Evasion, also rarely used, will be better at helping me survive.
