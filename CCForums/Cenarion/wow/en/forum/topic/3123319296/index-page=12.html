How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


*I'm going to leave out pre-potting/trinkets just so I don't spend one of the last days of Summer sitting inside. Let's not argue about my "opening" rotation either. It's probably sub-optimal which is beside the point right now.

Assassination: Garrote, mutilate, rupture, mutilate, snd, mutilate to 4-5, Vendetta, (ColdBlood) Envenom. Keep up Rupture (refreshing at 1.5 sec or less). Keep as high an uptime on Envenom as possible without overwriting the buff (pool energy without energy capping).

1. Assassination is pretty boring after the first few times you go through the rotation. You're not reacting to any procs (like KM/Rime with frost DK, Sudden Doom- Unholy DK). It is one of the only specs I know of that is still a ROTATION instead of a PRIORITY system like many other classes have shifted to in Cataclysm. It's dull, isn't interactive, and isn't as fun. My dualwielding frost DK is 10x more fun to play because of Procs that I have to react to.

Combat: Sinister strike to 4-5, if you're at 4, use Revealing strike. Keep up SnD, Keep up Rupture. Use Blade Flurry if their is a target capable of being cleaved. Use Killing spree when Bandit's Guile stacks to 30%. Eviscerate if SnD/Rupture timers are good to go. Add in Adrenaline Rush when it will benefit you.

1. Combat is a slight upgrade in fun from Assassination because Restless blades does add a nice "reactive" feel to the spec so you actually feel like you're doing something with your rotation. However, it is still a rotation and like assassination, you don't get to be nearly as reactive like many of the other classes (Killing Machine for Frost DKs, Master Marksman & Lock N'Load for Hunters). Reacting to talent procs like the aforementioned is what is fun about World of Warcraft in both PvE and PvP. Rogues didn't get the reactive feel to them in Cataclysm which is really unfortunate. Target switching really sticks out with Combat. Bandit's Guile dropping is the equivalent of the boss turning and taking away your weapons while you build bandit's guile back up to 30%.

Subtletly: Still has some of what made this spec so much fun in TBC and early WotLK, but really was forced into a PvP only niche in Cataclysm.

Why doesn't it work for PvE:
1. The amount of debuffs/buffs to keep up are nearly impossible on any current boss encounter. Getting off a 5pt rupture, keeping up SnD, keeping up recuperate, and then getting off a 5 point Eviscerate is only possible in a Patchwerk style fight. For instance, the Alystrazor encounter you have quite a bit of downtime between the Initiates and "Burnout" phases which means each time you get on a new target, you will be forced to start over, 5pt rupture, SnD, Recup, then, FINALLY, Eviscerate. Having a 20-30second ramp up time multiple times per fight is not viable. Having to keep up recuperate just for an energy boost is not even close to ideal. Perhaps roll the energy regen into Rupture like it is with Assassination.

What could be improved for PvP:
2. Serrated Blades talent is nearly useless for PvP since it is rare you get a 5pt rupture off on your target and then get consistent 5pt eviscerates to keep rupture up. Recuperate and the energy revitalization does work well/right for PvE, but Recuperate has no interaction with Mastery, making Mastery less fun.

What’s on your wish list for your class?


1. 3 viable specs for PvE and at least 2 for PvP (combat could be left the way it is for pvp)
2. Fix the ramp up/target switching issues that rogues have by allowing rogues to carry combo points on themselves rather than the target
3. Deadly poison no longer stacks, there is only 1 stack (reduce ramp up time). Still procs the MH poison.
4. Subtlety being a viable spec for PvE like it was in TBC. I DW swords and ran around using Hemo, KS, Eviscerate.
5. ******Having the class be more reactive to Procs like that of a Frost DK, Survival Hunter, Fire mage, etc so it doesn't feel like a rotation. It should be a priority so boss encounters do not feel like a meaningless grind to the end.****
6. Bigger Safe Fall "safe" distance drop.

What spells do you use the least?


Shiv(has its' uses for PvP, but still), Expose Armor, Deadly Throw, Combat Readiness, Smoke Bomb (no use for PvE)

***I understand changing anything is hard, but please, do something to bring the rogue up to speed with the other classes. I feel like the game is evolving without rogues and changes like Bandit's Guile are just hindering our ability to play at a proficient level.

more fun?

I love having a mechanics that involve whether yourself or an enemy is in or out of combat (stealth, sap). That's not to say that I think stealthing is cool or a good ability or whatever, but basically being able to CC someone when we're both out of combat is cool and being able to 'change stances' (stealth) when out of combat (only) is cool and fun.

I hate RNG in PvP. I feel like a lot of rogue stuff allows the elimination of RNG or doesn't involve it at all so rogues make me happy. For instance, being able to reach 100% crit for ambushes is really nice. It means I can for the most part not get screwed by RNG while shadow dancing in a burst situation in PvP where critting or not critting could win or lose a game (aka where RNG could screw me).

I'm also very pleased that sub. is currently the best PvP spec for most situations. I think the player skill cap on sub is very high compared to the other specs. less fun?

    Increased stacks for poisons to decrease bag space. Some would also suggest instead of a physical item they be changed to an entry in the spell book and applied from there rather than an item taking physical space.



    • What makes playing your class more fun?

    Ability to stealth and dictate what and where engagements take place. Class skills that emphasis control over an opponent and victory through subverting enemy tactics in lieu of brute force. The playstyle and general thematics of the specs attract me to the class.


    • What makes playing your class less fun?

    Throughout all of tier 11 it felt like you were always at a disadvantage being in melee, and by consequence where disadvantaging your raid group. It's not fun when you feel like you could possibly be holding back 9/24 other people. Simultaneously it felt like the majority of fight mechanics gave preference to ranged classes. While it was never so bad that raids entirely forewent all melee DPS there were severe limits to the numbers that could be realistically taken while conversely more ranged was never to the detriment of a raid and almost always a boon. Tier 12 has however seen a rectification of this (driving Rhyolith, Shannox traps, solo-interrupt Alysrazor, etc). It is a thing to be vigilant not to return to.

    Ramp-up time has always been an issue for a rogue. By their very mechanics rogues excel at tunneling to and pouring large amounts of damage into a single target. However with the prominence of target switching through most of Cataclysm (and how it's more mechanic heavy, less tank & spank fights are the foreseeable future as has been hinted at in the Dev watercooler as to why tank threat has been changed) having to re-stack poisons so high and the introduction of new mechanics like Bandit's Guile have only taken matters to a level where you cannot help but feel a little impotent next to some classes that can swap targets almost at will with little or no appreciable loss in their cycle, while rogues must start from zero. Often without the aid of early rotational stabilizing utilities (i.e. Overkill, or Premeditation)

    Redirect was made as an attempt to help differ these issues, but has many of it's own. First it does nothing for poison stacks. While Assassination has talents which increase the rate at which it applies poison, Combat and Subtlety do not. So even though Assassination sometimes cannot re-build poison stacks on new targets fast enough, the other specs are far more deeply plagued by this issue. Second, the cooldown is quite freqently too long. During many add phases swaps must be made to and then back from an add, or from add to multiple adds and back, and all too rapidly for the cooldown. Third, is the fact that Redirect is on the GCD. As Combat with Bandit's Guild it's imperative to keep that buff running and not reset an already clunk cycle and key to maximizing damage output even though the loss of a GCD can easily mean capping energy. This is true of lost function for the other specs as well.     Part II:

    Oft times too heavy reliance on cooldowns. Without which, especially in PVP, we can feel quite impotent. It was announced previous to Cataclym's launch that the developers agreed and shared this sentiment that rogue play was far too binary in either CDs were available and we felt very strong or they were not and we were severely weakened. This has basically seen very little change in the pre to post Cataclysm launch environment. In addition to having another defensive cooldown added to our bars (in Combat Readiness) Recuperate helps us with longevity and to stay alive in the open more, but also seems to be costing us adjustments in our burst or cc to account for our staying alive and open longer, which was perhaps a shift of the pendulum too far in a direction we didn't want to go.


  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


    • Assassination:
    The majority of our activity is a means to facilitate our passive damage (poisons). While poisons as a whole are an interesting and thematic mechanic, this can sometimes lead to feeling like you're relegated to positioning your character while the game itself takes care of the actual task of providing damage. Although overall output would drop if we were not correctly utilizing our abilities to increase poison damage the perception of it can be an important factor. Perhaps a better balance can be struck between active and passive sources of damage while still maintaining an overall focus on poisons.

    The number of abilities used and the way in which they are used, in my opinion is by and large a success. Assassination is not GCD locked like some class/specs and so maximizing DPS is not as much about ensuring you never miss a GCD or how quickly/efficiently abilities are spammed but in the nuances of when to use what secondary abilities inbetween maintaining primary rotational tasks and effective energy management. Perhaps some of these secondary abilities could be pushed to the fore or there is room for other types of proc based play that could be weaved into these openings in the rotation.

    There also exists some merit in the debate about dodge capping expertise as assassination. I wouldn't under normal circumstances do this (although certain fights under the right conditions make it advantageous), but the debate itself intrigues me. I'm not sure if this was an intentional thing, or some happy accident. But it's something I think should be explored more in the future and with more specs.

    The overall DPS loss is actually fairly minor; can actually be a gain if you're spending significant time in front of a target. And allows those who are ok with the trade off of performance to a stable rotation to do so without having to worry about contingencies for dodges. Depending on your ability to process and respond to dodges in your rotation this could actually serve as a personal DPS gain. But regardless of that, you still must run the rotation correctly or your DPS appreciably suffers. Lenience or flexibility in stat priorities makes for interesting choices or individuality while at the same time rewarding proper play.

    Combat:
    Combat runs at the opposite end of the spectrum from Assassination. In higher gear tiers energy capping is a very real risk with correct buffs, or even just a string of good procs. It is a playstyle that routinely GCD locks, and so maximizing every key press is your means to maximum potential. And in its style I think it is a success, but there are problems with execution.

    Unfortunately the difference between knowing and correctly applying your rotation and maintaining Slice and Dice or spamming Sinister Strike & Eviscerate, ignoring entirely Revealing Strike & Rupture, and hitting CDs as they light up while Slice and Dice's running is in the ballpark of ~3% variation. RNG from encounter to encounter can have a bigger impact on DPS than that. Lack of accountability in actual play is poor design and offers no reward to good play. Enough to bar entry to highest level play should one be so lax? Most definitely. But even at moderate skill levels a player should feel engaged by their rotation and feel that what they do matters and be rewarded accordingly for playing well. When you can do quite nearly everything wrong and still achieve same or similar results it detracts from this and in the end promotes poor play.

    Part III:

    Bandit's Guile - This while interesting mechanic thematically. Opening up your opponent and taking advantage of his/her weak points, in practice can be a hefty gate on Combat rogue performance. The mechanic (most prominently in PVP but pervasively so) is clunky, adds increased ramp-up time to a class already taking issue with its ramp-up, and inhibits target swapping when we've now been through two raid tiers with prevelant target swapping and PVP environments where swaps can be both frequent and critical. Redirect attempts to account for this but falls short (examination above).

    Subtlety:
    While on paper one of the strongest rogue specializations, rotational complexity and stringency show it to lag appreciably behind in practice on all but the most straight-forward (tank & spank style) encounters. And in truth Subtletly is generally the spec of choice on a movement-light fight like Baleroc just for this reason.

    The rotation has so many abilities involved and the window for maintaining everything is incredibly narrow. On any fight with complex mechanics, heavy movement, or frequent target swapping it becomes nigh impossible to effectively do so. Coupled with the requisite for positioning in order to maximize rotation due to restrictions on core abilities further exacerbates the situation. Given that these types of fights have become the standard (again hence the recent changes to tank threat and proposed changes to more active mitigation as outlined in a recend Dev's Watercooler) and fights like Baleroc have become the outlier Subtlety suffers greatly in the majority of instances/encounters.

    Recuperate - all three rogue specs must actively maintain a finisher in order to keep energy regeneration at acceptable levels. Assassination through Rupture via Venomous Wounds, Combat through Slice and Dice via Combat Potency as well as passively through Vitality, and Subtlety through Recuperate via Energetic Recovery. It gives us an active regeneration mechanic built into all rogue trees. Instead of our energy regeneration being completely passive, we must build to and maintain finishers in order to maintain our expected energy rates. And this is partly engaging but the way it is handled in Subtlety is a culprit in the bloat and complexity of the rotation.

    Assassination and Combat both have their energy regeneratin tools tied to offensive finishers, and in the case of Assassination actually deals more damage in and of itself. Whereas Energetic Recovery actually forces rogues to invest CP in a self-heal instead of offensive abilities whether we're taking damage or not. So regardless of the combat state or need for it; twice a minute a rogue must dump all CP into a HoT. In PVP this binds us, it is unequivocally bad to lapse Recuperate, even while at full health and not being focused due to the loss of energy. Regardless of what we need to be doing to further our teams goals we must stop to activate a self-heal. Even if much of the time it is advantageous to have this running, it is not all the time.

    In PVE this feels even more nonsensical. As a DPS class we are meant to be maximizing DPS on our target. We have a dedicated healing team there to take care of inbound damage. So again regardless of combat state, and where in only the most raid AoE intensive periods with our healers struggling would either of the other specs think to use Recuperate, Subtlety must maintain it no matter what. Energetic Recovery should be uncoupled from Recuperate and streamlined better for a more efficient rotation.

    In PVP the emergence of two simultaneously viable specs in Hemo Sub & Backstab Sub is a success on par with or greater than the Assassination Expertise question.



    • What’s on your wish list for your class?

    -Quicker/shorter stacking poisons on targets with increased duration so they will not always drop to zero in the space of a single target swap
    -Bandit's Guile redesigned to be less of a barrier to ramp-up time/less clunky mechanic overall, better fitting to PVP
    -Recuperate uncoupled from Subtlety energy regeneration for a more decisional and intelligent use rather than maximum upkeep regardless of combat state
    -A less punishing rotation for Subtlety to give it wider PVE viability
    -AoE tools for PVE Subtlety
    -Greater level of reward for correctly playing Combat's rotation
    -More specs having room for personalization/customization (as per Expertise and Assassination in PVE or Subtlety: Hemorrhage vs. Backstab in PVP)
    -Less punishing target swaps
    -Redirect off GCD & shorter CD
    -Assassination/Combat being given another design pass for PVP viability
