PvE and PvP

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

items that require taking up bag slots after 6years (poisons, thieves' tools)

What makes playing your class more fun?

knowing that I am still playing one of the least active classes after many years

What makes playing your class less fun?

applying poisons, auto attack still being 30-40% of my damage, rupture only being used because it procs another talent, being refreshed by other abilities, or just being removed off of my bars when I play combat, and a glyph (Hemo) ticking harder than a finisher. Combo points still being bound to a target and not the rogue (see holy power). Trying to AoE with FoK.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I feel that a rotation such a Sub should out preform Assassination and Combat simply because of the requirement to track all of your finishers, Hemo, and cooldowns.

Assassination - nice flow until you proc too many haste buffs and/or Hero/Lust/TW is used.
Combat - changes to BF are nice. remove Revealing Strike it is far to clunky with the use of the revamped Sinister Strike Glyph, energy capping through AR/procs.
Sub - amazing flow and usage of almost all your rogue abilities. every spec/class should be more like sub.

Coming into the expansion I thought Sub would still be the worst spec talent/rotation/performance PvE wise but somehow the 'PvP' rogue spec could turn into the best PvE spec if the developers would treat it more than just a PvP spec.


What’s on your wish list for your class?

Poison like profession tab (Runeforging), thieves' tools removed, combo points tied to rogue not target, auto attack damage not being 30-40% of overall damage, change sub and combat mastery, remove Revealing Strike, fix the bottom of combat tree (BG and KS), increase base amount of energy regen but decrease haste effect on energy regen (to avoid capping energy), and remove rupture (give all specs Hemo and make glyph baseline. change Serrated Blades to refresh SnD or Recuperate. Make Venomous Wounds work off of Hemo bleed or Garrote). Make FoK back to what it was in Ulduar.

What spells do you use the least?

PvE - Gouge, Deadly Throw, Expose Armor, Dismantle, and Combat Readiness.

PvP - Deadly Throw and Combat Readiness

I've played since early BC days so I know the poison/bag space/duration issue has become WAY better than it was. Only thing I would suggest is allowing poison to stack higher than 20, because I'm always running out. It also does take up a good amount of bag space when you're carrying around 5 different kinds.

What makes playing your class more fun?


Anything that makes me feel sneaky/stealthy/tricky, like a rogue should.

What makes playing your class less fun?


slow damage ramp-up (that's if the ranged didn't kill everything before I actually got to the mob in the first place), encounters where I'm forced to stay back from aoe or it's too far up for me to use anything but my thrown weapon.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


Assassination feels fine to me. Combat feels a little more clunky. I don't feel like it is worthwhile to plan my attacks around Bandits Guile, and Revealing Strike is underwhelming and I was happier before it was added.

What’s on your wish list for your class?


1. Some kind of raid buff I can give, other than being in Subtlety 2. More ways to incorporate sneaky/roguish things into pve. Personally I would love to see Shadowstep be a base Rogue talent. If you ever see a rogue NPC, it's the one distinctive thing that you expect they will do. 3. Better target switching, less ramp-up. 4. For those inevitable encounters where I have to stand there and do nothing but use my thrown weapon (which does maybe 1200 dps if I'm lucky), something that can give me even a small, temporary boost...a cooldown maybe? I always thought it would be nice if Throw actually built up combo points so I could use deadly throw in those situations. I also liked the idea that someone else put out about Shiv applying the poison on your thrown rather than offhand, so I can have a utility poison without sacrificing my damage/rotation.


What spells do you use the least?


Distract, Expose Armor, Throw, Deadly Throw, Mind-numbing Poison, Gauge, Smoke Bomb (I know it's good in pvp, but I haven't found use for it in PVE), Combat Readiness.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

New Raids, Old Raids and Heroics

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Arena, Rated Bgs, Bgs and World pvp.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Poisons falling off after one hour. While there are addons to help with that I don't feel I should need to download an out of game program to maintain these. It's annoying to refresh then at 15 minutes but if you don't you might have them run out in the middle of them. Allowing us to stack poison buffs to increase the time would be great.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The mobility and control we have. While others hate us.. having both of these makes it a ton of fun for us. Everyone hates being controlled but with our somewhat weak ability to stand up to anyone else in fair combat, this gives us a very very unique feel. The rogue is very much different from any other class.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Healers. While I understand we need a few classes that have advantages against us. Healers have a distinct advantage. We are designed to control and burst, I love that... We can kill lesser healers, but in the long run if a healer(such as a priest spamming fear) is any kind of decent it ruins our day. It would be nice to have a little more balance. Harder to kill dps classes and a little more utility against healers.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

We have great single target rotations. I love them a lot. Though when we have to switch to multiple targets, though we should lose some dps it's just not a lot of fun. We get our combo points up just in time to barely or not be able to use them. It would be great in the pvp builds if we could maybe have a talent that refreshes or reduces the cooldown on redirect each time our current target dies. With a short 5 or 10 second cd just to avoid exploiting it and doing terrible amounts of dps.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I would like sap to be considered a melee attack. It's sort of annoying to have hunters sit in flare in camo and not be able to touch them. I also feel like blind should be less of a go to button. While throwing dirt seems effective, Throwing it 20 yards or 60 feet into someones eyes just doesn't seem believable. I would love to see blind be another cooldown that reduced the chance to hit while we were given something like flash grenade that could effect multiple targets at long range and have a similar effect. The great thing about playing the rogue class(other than the mechanic aspects) is feeling like you're an assassin, ninja or thief. It's what every little boy wanted to be. I want to see those aspects expanded upon.

I also would love a little more utility in raids. Not in dps, but in ways like disarming shannox's traps. Things that would make bringing a rogue more useful and playing one would be more fun.

What spells do you use the least?

Disarm traps. I use it to open vs hunters then that's about it. I don't understand why we need to be in stealth for that. Certainly stealth could offer a buff to that by allowing us to remain undetected or it could just remain the same and hunters could give us their own debuffs while we sit there and channel. I know you opened this up in shannox. I loved that fight just because of that. Heroic was changed a lot by having a rogue in it.
