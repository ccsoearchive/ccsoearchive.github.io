  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    • About 90% PvE, and 10% PvP. BUt it depends highly on the expansion cycles. Slow cycles mean more PvP usually.

  • If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

    • Mosly Heroics with friends and guildies. I raid some normal modes from time to time. Usually it will be a PuG even with guildies. I do a lot of dailies, holiday stuff, solo old content and generally mess around.

  • If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

    • Almost exclusively random battlegrounds for logistical reasons. I've done some arena with guildies that is hardly worth mentioning. I don't have time to the commitment for rated bg. I also do a little world pvp when it comes along.

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    • Definitely the poison maintenance. I feel like the are always dropping off at the worst possible moment.

  • What makes playing your class more fun?

    • Skulking around in the shadows is definitely the best part about the rogue. The smaller but varied ability set makes for better play than say my paladin or druid's huge toolbox of largely situational spells.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?

    • Melee feels like a liability. It is frustrating to be running around avoiding stuff off target while the ranged pelt away at the boss. Even with my many abilities to reduce or avoid damage, I still take a ton of incidental damage compared to ranged classes in my opinion.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    • Assassination is very smooth, and I like it. While not difficult, it also isn't trivial to maintain while keeping proper situational awareness. The cooldowns are kinda lackluster. I don't being stuck to a target for a long time to make use of vendetta.
    Combat has a very nice "in your face" style, though it is a bit "spammy" hitting sinister strike over and over. It has some nice flexible cooldowns.
    Sub is just a mess though. There is just way too much to do and keep track of for my tastes.

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?

    • Combat daggers - All rogues should be able to use daggers effectively.
    Swords, axes and maces for offhand - Really I would like every spec to be able to use any of our equip-able weapons in either hand.
    Tank spec - I hate having to switch from my main because there isn't a tank around. Combat would make an excellent choice for this.
    Poison parity - I'd like to choose between poison effects, not damage potenial.
    Poison variety - I'd like to see more different poisons. Maybe rogue could be the go to guy for raid debuffs. Things like attack speed reduction, physical damage reduction or sunder effects.
    Duration-less poisons - You apply it and it stays there until you change it.
    Poisons as an ability - No more carrying around all your poisons in your bag or running out.
    Combo Points on the Rogue - We have a lot buffs and debuffs to put up before we get rolling. This shouldn't be that big of a deal. Pallys already have it. And you know...we've been screaming about it forever.
    Redirect should allow the next finisher to use no combo points (preferably) or to add 5 combo points on the target (bigger badder premeditation).
    Abilities that hit harder - Hey! We like big numbers too. We shouldn't have to look at recount to get a giggle. I didn't understand the fascination with crits until I rolled a warrior and started giggling every time I crit for some huge damage. It really is quite fun seeing those numbers pop up or those health bars drop.
    Deadly Momentum should be a baseline rogue ability that will refresh at max CP on a killing blow. The problem is that the talent is largely ignored outside of leveling, even though it makes playing a rogue so much more fluid. This is a really fun talent that turns you into a killing machine instead of playing the buff maintenance mini-game non-stop between pulls.
    Suspend/Refresh Slice and Dice and Recuperate while in stealth. Partly to help with the buff mini-game and partly because it encourages rogues to be in stealth.

  • What spells do you use the least?

    • Expose Armor - This little bugger is hardly ever used.
    Distract - Eh. I use it. It is just so...eh.
    Combat Readiness - Cloak is sooooooooo much more important in a wider variety of situations than to risk having it on shared cooldown. Plus, evasion works counter productively with this cooldown anyway. I'll press it mostly cause it looks kinda neat or I'm doing something where it doesn't matter if I have cloak ready.
    Redirect - Useful for putting a Rupture up a bit faster or swapping a Kidney Shot to a new target. So with the cooldown, its not all that useful.

    It is time for poison items to go away. Make them like enchants in a way. I don't see how packing poisons in your inventory adds anything to the game other then maybe a technical issue to where making poisons like enchants conflicts with real enchants.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    All the control you give this class. It's insane. Between Kidney, Gouge, Cripple poison, Smoke, there is just so much we can offer to a team other then just damage.

    I wish you could do the same for other melee classes like warriors or paladins. My main is a warrior but I moved to rogue for PVP because its just more fun and you aren't a waste to the team.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Poison purchasing. Being funneled in Sub for PVP because of Shadowstep.

    We are still a little too cooldown reliant, but I really don't see how this can be resolved since its been with this class since Classic. I will admit its come a long way for the better.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    For Mutilate and Sub I am fine. Haven't bothered with combat. I find Sub the best because you do not run out of energy much provided you have Recup up. Mutilate is good, if not a little too energy heavy for my tastes.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    - Decouple slightly Cloak of Shadows and Combat Readiness. I still believe that what you did was the right idea so we don't just blindly use cool downs but completely linking them was a bad idea. Maybe put a 20 second cool down between both but don't shut the other skill out completely. All you did there was make Cloak of Shadows used more then Readiness. It was as if there was no thought behind that. I get the "we have to make a choice" but seriously at least for me the choice is obvious.

    - No more poison items. Poisons are like skills in your spell book.

    - Maybe less cool down reliant, but as I said before we are in decent shape still. It's just that having Prep is like being twice as good in PVP because of all the extra cool downs you get.

    - Shadowstep baseline for all specs. Remove the damage buff it provides for Assassination/Combat, Sub still gets it.

    - Combo points on the rogue, at least have them sustained for 20 seconds or so after combat. I understand it might be OP to kill something then use your 5 combo points immediately on another target... which is why I assume you have not gone this route yet.

    - Redirect. I see the purpose, but honestly we all want points on the rogue, not the target.

    - Combat Readiness since the nerf. Casters are everywhere. I find COS more useful then Readiness since we have passive mitigation vs melee already as well as Evasion.

    - Expose Armor. There are usually just better abilities to use.

    - Sinister Strike - spec based. I imagine if I was combat I'd be using it.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    Subtlety's rotation being complex and hard to execute. Having a ton of tools for every situation that can come along. Being a non-stop killing machine while leveling.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Long Ramp up times for Assassination. Subtlety PVE rotation requiring near perfection yet not really giving any advantage to playing it. Combat and Assassination's rotations being 3 button specs that get rather boring.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    As Subtlety: Its a hard, complex rotation which is in stark contrast to the relative ease of Assassination and Combat. Having to keep track of multiple cooldowns, debuffs, and buffs makes the rotation a challenge to play. However, in order for Subtlety to keep up damage wise with the other specs, it requires near perfection. The little bit of utility you gain with Shadowstep and Shadow Dance doesn't feel adequate compensation for the complex rotation. Also, trying to keep up perfection on the rotation can cause some awareness issues on certain bosses, which can wipe raids.

    Of course the whole point above me is moot if the mob has significant amounts of time attacking from the front, since we cannot Backstab or Ambush from the front and Hemorrhage isn't cut out for carrying the spec itself. In that case I am forced to swap over to Combat.

    As Combat: Its simple, hasn't really changed since Vanilla. Maybe increase the bonus Revealing Strike has on Rupture since it isn't a huge loss to just ignore rupture during bosses. The rotation itself is fine, its just the ramp up time due to Bandit's Guile which is the worst part of the rotation.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Something to make Subtlety's rotation either a little eaiser or more rewarding. Right now the only reason I play it is because I enjoy the challenge. Something like Serrated Blades refreshing Rupture on any finisher used instead of just Eviscerate, or having it work like Cut to the Chase and it will always refresh Rupture on use of Eviscerate and set it to its 5 point maximum. Just something needs to be done to make Subtlety less of a joke spec in the eyes of the community and give it the spotlight it deserves.

    Also, somehow reduce the positional requirements on Subtlety. Would making Backstab & Ambush not require rear positioning as a talent in Subtlety really cause that much PVE & PVE imbalance?

    Fan of Knives could use some love for non-assasination specs too. It really does pitiful damage compared to a lot of other AoE options out there.

    Make Killing Spree & Shadowstep not be suicide on certain bosses. Although it seems only Ragnaros is affected this tier, it still should be unacceptable to have abilities which will teleport you into lava.

    Unlink the Combat Readiness & Cloak of Shadows cooldowns. Or make them only linked in Battlegrounds & Arenas. I don't think I've used Combat Readiness once.

    Shiv, Fan of Knives, Combat Readiness,

Redirect does address this issue, but it's not enough sometimes (specially to mutilate rogues).

Perhaps we could have "poison bottles" that have an unlimited amount of charges, instead of x amount of y poison in our bags.

    But being a rogue since classic, i really can't complain much. Our quality of life is substantially better now that we don't have to buy vanishing powder, re-apply poisons every time we zone in, or hesitate to cast blind on someone because of the absurd amount of gold it took to make blind powder.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    Mostly PvP

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Heroics, old world achievement/loot farming. I used to raid in WotLK.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    BGs mostly, but arena and rated bg's whenever I can.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    - I dont mind using poison reagents, however it does feel a bit outdated considering how many other reagent dependent abilities for other classes have been removed.
    - I dont know many rogues who pick pocket every mob before killing them. Especially when playing with other people. I love the ability, but it needs to be changed to be more convenient.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    We have a large toolbox of tricks. I love that I can heal, evasion tank, vanish to drop aggro/escape, misdirect a lone mob to the tank with Tricks of the Trade, stun and interrupt, CC like blind and sap, sprint, and even simple features like picking locks and reduced falling damage. I've enjoyed other classes, but the broad versatility of the rogue has earned my love. No other class in any other MMO I've played has been as fun as the WoW Rogue.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    - Being beaten for DPS by classes that are hybrid or can play alternate roles. If you can change spec and play a healer or a tank as well, your DPS should not be higher than those who only have DPS specs. This is all we have.
    - I loved being able to use two swords, but now there are no quick offhand swords anymore. I feel forced into using daggers, and while some rogues love daggers, others don't. One of the main reasons I enjoy Combat is because I get to use larger weapons.
    - Fighting healers is humiliating. Originally rogues used to be the class that would cross enemy lines to target healers, but now geared healers can practically ignore us. Our wound poison is used more for the higher proc rate than for the actual healing debuff.
    - Our specs are too similar in PvP. I have friends who loved Assassination who have had to give it up because it doesn't bring anything unique to the table anymore. I think Subtlety should be more about control and focused burst, while Assassination should be about high sustained damage in PvP. Most people would rather take a warrior than an assassination rogue. They should hit alot harder, like they used to.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    As a Combat rogue I love my rotation. I have a good variety of tools to use - I'm stronger against group pulls than an Assassination rogue.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    - Increase the healing debuff of Wound Poison, or change its effect. Make it damage mana, or combine it with Mind Numbing, etc. At its current state it has very little effect.
    - Remove the shared cooldown of Combat Readiness and Cloak of Shadows.
    - Nerf Ambush for rogues level 1 - 70. They're extremely overpowered, and now we have alot of bad level 85 rogues, and a reputation for being a cheap or overpowered class.
    - A rogue NPC to look up to.
    - A few cooler animations. Everyone has gotten some nice looking animations these days, but the rogues best effects are still Fan of Knives and Cloak of Shadows.
    - More opportunities in PvE for Pick Locks, Stealth or Pick Pockets to be useful. I loved in ICC when people would actually be grateful to have a rogue around. For the first time in PvE I felt like I brought something special as a rogue.

    What spells do you use the least?
    Feint (hardly need to use it as often in Cata. I still use it sometimes, but mostly to reduce AoE damage), Expose Armor (I love this ability, but there seems to always be warriors and druids using it instead), Pick Pockets (Fun ability but not practical)
