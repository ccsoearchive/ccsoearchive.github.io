■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

■If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Mostly raiding with some heroics thrown in.

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

The only real quality of life issue that still bothers me to this day is the fact that we still apply poisons, which is something all the other classes no longer deal with. Something similar to the shamans weapon enchants would be preferred. Also, lowering the cooldown of misdirect would be lovely.

■What makes playing your class more fun?

I like the versatility and the ability to choose how I approach a fight. Things like dropping combat at will, lowering aoe dmg, and cloaking to give me an edge against certain mechanics.

■What makes playing your class less fun?

Having to switch targets constantly and not be able to keep my damage at max while doing so. Lacking mobility in anything but the sublety spec considering how many mechanics effect melee. Having a 4pc bonus that is amazing in party or raid and absolutely useless solo.

■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I am actually liking the changes to the assassination rotation, and while some find it slow or odd, I find it to be fluid and symbiotic. I also love having an "execution" mechanic now with backstab and murderous intent.

■What’s on your wish list for your class?

First of all I will say that I am in the camp that believes that Shadow Step needs to be given to all rogue specs. I dont feel its inherently a sublety skill considering it didnt exist until TBC and as far as I am concerned you can take the dmg bonus away and we'll call it even. I feel that what Assassination and Combat lack in mobility Shadow Step makes up for with little tuning needed. Warriors now have charge in combat and every stance, ferals have their pounce, hunters were given disengage in all specs so I fail to see how all rogues having Shadow Step breaks the game. Secondly as I am primarily assassination spec I dont want to say that we scale poorly but more that I dont think that one set is enough now with things like mastery and reforging. I lose so much by having things like expertise and no mastery on my chest simply to utilize my 4 pc w/o shoulders. I'd like to see either another set specific to one or two trees or see things scaled a bit differently. As it stands reforging is just not enough.

■What spells do you use the least?

Expose Armor which we have a warrior tank and to be honest i've not used the skill in literally years and that fact does not bother me. I dont find it usefull at all.
I'll throw cheap shot in here since the diminishing returns have been placed on it.
I'd like to use smokebomb but seeing as its useless in PvE I dont. (Only in pvp)
I use distract very very rarely.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Purchasing and Applying poisons. We're the only remaining class in the game that must spend money to stay competitive in battle. Make all types of poisons into class abilities, instant cast, and change Glyph of Poisons to increase the duration by either 50% or 100%.

Target switching! Redirect isn't really enough to mitigate the pain of having to switch targets in battle.

For Assassination, the fact that our damage is fairly 50/50ish between poison(magic) and physical damage tends to hurt us pretty bad on any fights where one type of damage is totally blocked. We essentially drop to 50% effectiveness on all targets, whereas other classes can keep 100% effectiveness on specific targets. I imagine Combat and Subtlety suffer the same problem, perhaps to a lesser degree.
09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What makes playing your class more fun?

Doing competitive DPS, having utility to deal with many different situations (traps, CC, scouting, etc).
09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
What makes playing your class less fun?

Too many boring non-choice abilities that have to be maintained during battle: Slice and Dice, Rupture, Vendetta, Tricks of the Trade.

Haven't played Combat much in Cataclysm, my only comment here is that Killing Spree is often too dangerous to use (I killed myself with it when fighting Kologarn back in Wrath because it teleported me to the insta-kill zone at his feet.)
09/12/2011 09:00 AMPosted by Nethaera
How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For Assassination:
Keeping Slice and Dice up is boring, it's not a choice, it's a requirement. I'd rather see the 40% melee attack speed rolled into our passive bonuses and Slice and Dice re-imagined as something that makes a more interesting choice when the time comes to spend combo points. Keeping Vendetta up is also boring; it's another requirement due to the flat 20% damage buff. Tricks of the Trade, another boring non-choice ability. I like Rupture in the sense that I have the option to put a DoT on the enemy. I don't like that Rupture is pretty much required to be kept up at all times, like Slice and Dice.

Implement the poison changes outlined above, mitigate some (or all, I'm not picky) of the hassle with having to play "finisher buff uptime juggle". Make some of our boring abilities more interesting and make target switching less painful.
