"What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]"
Both.

"If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]"
Raids and heroic dungeons.

"If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]"
BGs and arenas for points every once in a while

"What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters."
Poisons. It's annoying having to carry around 5 extra stacks of stuff in my bags just to operate at my basic level. If poisons are required for rogues, I don't think they should be all split up. Maybe make just one basic poison reagent that stacks to 100-200 (if even requiring a reagent still at all; unless I just don't know about it, no other class has to buy something that is required for their class to function) and then have rogues learn a poison ability that functions in the UI like a mage's teleport or portal spells with a pull out menu.

"What makes playing your class more fun?"
Control. I enjoy being able to change the flow of the battle by removing a participant from it for a little while or putting an obstacle in the way. I also like being able to choose when and where to attack through stealth. I also like the traditional aspects of rogues like pick pocket and lockpicking too, except they've been really watered down and are semi-worthless. I do like that rogues have our own potions from pick pocketing, however.

"What makes playing your class less fun?"
The reliance on cooldowns. This is more for PVP, but rogues can't survive without cooldowns. If we have them up we can shrug of CC, laugh at attempts to damage us and appear anywhere on the battlefield. If they're down, we get kited into oblivion or swatted by classes with more sustained damage.

"How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)"
I play assassination in PVE, and the rotation feels a little dry. There are no real choices to make, just a laundry list of "is this ability up? No? Use it. Yes? Use the next one" ad naseum. I do really like the rush phase at 35% where you switch to backstab, but that's still only replacing one ability with another, even if it does feel more exciting with the inrush of energy.

"What’s on your wish list for your class?"
Baseline Shadowstep. Sort of related to that, more mobility for Assassination and Combat in PVP.
Combo points building on the rogue instead of on the target. In PVE, it wouldn't really be an issue. It would make us a bit more powerful in PVP, but I think it would be a more evening out with other classes. No other class has a mechanic like that which keeps them from being at full power when switching to another target (other than feral druids, obviously, but they're rogue clones, so what I'm saying here could go for them to, as much as I'd prefer it didn't). The idea of combo points being only on a target doesn't make a ton of sense, either. My idea of a combo is just a string of abilities chained together; why would that have to be only on one target? In a fight with multiple people, I'm often switching targets to sort of manage the fight anyway (a gouge here, a blind there, interrupt a spell over here, shadowstep and throw some more damage on the healer to help finish them off, etc.); that feels a whole lot more like the flavor of a rogue and a "combo" than stabbing one person in the back three or four times and then eviscerating them.

"What spells do you use the least?"
- Expose Armor. I don't know if I've ever used that ability a single time.
- Combat Readiness. There is almost no situation in which I would choose to use CR over Cloak of Shadows. Maybe if I was dueling a warrior. I have used it I think two times since it was put on the same cooldown as cloak, and both were just a sort of "Hm, CR is up? Why not use it, I never do" in fights that were already guaranteed wins.
- Mind Numbing poison. It's obviously not used in PVE at all, and in PVP most of the time I'd rather have Crippling Poison up to help keep from being kited. Most of the time casters use instant cast spells when in range of a rogue, and when they hard cast they're at distance and I'd rather slow them so I can get in range again. I don't really have any suggestions on how to fix it, except maybe add a component that reduces the spell's effectiveness by 10% or something (10% less healing/damage, 10% less time on spell effects like polymorph or curse of weakness, etc.). I might use it then. You could even just take out the cast speed slowing aspect entirely.

On that note, weapon enchants overrule the neat, green poison drip. I'd rather see the poison dripping off my weapons, personally. I am a Rogue, after all.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I'm absolutely in love with Sub PvE's complexity this expansion. It's theoretically a huge DPS output, too. It actually reminds me of my vanilla Combat Daggers Rogue, so it's pretty nostalgic.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The frustration that it's way harder to pull Sub numbers, even when trying your best, than it is as Assassination or Combat. Something I'd like to see changed in a future patch without forfeiting the complexity. I'm not whining about it's current viability, I just think it should be easier for players to get into. i.e. less gear dependent.

Bandit's Guile is hard to manage. I originally thought it would be a little faster than it is- something you wanted to time damaging finishers around rather than DPS cooldowns. This would also mean Bandit's Guile progresses over time on it's own, albeit slowly.

The event would happen like thus: I have 5p's ready for this RS'd Eviscerate and I'm in the yellow for Bandit's Guile. I'm going to wait half a second so I can get a sweet, sweet Deep Insight finisher off.

It would almost deliver that pacing feel of Assassination. It would most certainly have to be done right though.

Subtlety is fun and complex. Combat is okay, but could be (one ability?) more complex. Assassination is way too boring and needs 1-2 more abilities in the mix, though having to pace your energy is cool. Perhaps following an Envenom with a Shiv could yield some kind of bonus. That would also make 4p finishers less likely.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Easier Sub competitiveness without risking it's innovative challenge.

Assassination needs to be more engaging. Having to pace your attacks and pool energy is fun and unique for the spec, but it's the same thing over and over again: 5p Envenom and make sure you have enough energy to Mutilate twice during the proc. That's it.

Combat greatly benefits from having a fast offhand. As the OCD Rogue I am, I really like having matching weapons- whether it be matching swords, fists, axes, and even maces (though I can't figure out why any Rogue would want maces! /shiver). If a second Sword of Coolness drops, I am going to use it regardless of whether I have a dagger or not. I would love to see this made viable. Perhaps by basing the offhand energy gains on damage? A slow weapon won't hit as often, but has more damage, so that could be used to modify the energy return on those slower hits to make up for the lack of speed.

Either that, or allow us to Transmog non-Hunter weapon appearances! I know it was a special case for Hunters, but it does bother me.

What spells do you use the least?

Smoke Bomb and Combat Readiness. Shiv too, I suppose. It's kind of like Tranq Shot in that it's there until the rare occasion I need to hit it. Pick Pocket, Distract.

Smoke Bomb could be a nice emergency threat drop in PvE- similar to Warlock's Shatter.

  • *Combat Energy Regen. Combats energy regen is extremely fast and as such creates a high octane rotation. Though at points there is no way to stop yourself from capping (approx 36.12 energy per second for myself, though will definitely vary according to latency). Capping energy is extremely annoying, and can potentially be a huge loss in dps. There a several potential solutions to 'fix' (I use this word loosely)
    1. Slow down base Energy Regen, or the amount gained from haste. This will ensure there will be less energy capping due to the slower nature of the energy. Though the issue with this, that without using this in conjunction with one of the next methods, it will be a straight dps decrease for rogue kind.
    2. Adjust the energy cost and damage of combo builders (such as Sinister Strike, Revealing Strike and Mutilate) and Finishers (Eviscerate, Envenom, Rupture). Increasing the base energy cost of each of these will ensure that rogues will cap out on energy far less, though unless the damage output is increased and adjusted, this will also be a straight dps loss.
    3. Reduce the GCD on Combo point builders and Finishers. This will ensure that meeting the energy cap will be much less of an issue, and is a potential solution that is a straight dps increase for rogues.
    Another contributing factor to Combats energy regen is Adrenaline Rush. This is an amazing cooldown when used corrctly, though with the constant scaling of gear, it is becoming less and less useful each tier. This can be remedied by adjusting how Adrenaline rush works (perhaps decreasing the energy regen gained from it, as well as adding a straight damage increase modifier to it?).

    *Lack of the ep value of expertise (Assassination). Currently, Expertise is worth less than haste and mastery for Assassination rogues, which makes for a very unstable rotation when it comes to dps. This could be remedied by reducing the damage from poisons, and increasing the damage of yellow abilities.

    *Bad RNG with energy procs/poison procs etc (can't be helped). Haha.

    What makes playing your class more fun?


    Stealth. I've played many MMORPGs before, and the best skill I think for my personality is stealth. I played an Assassin in Ragnarok Online, Rogue in AION and Perfect World. The ability to choose your fight makes it unique. You can become both docile/calm at one second and aggresive on the other. Plus I really like that my opponents can't do anything when you stealth. They get hyped up, push all buttons and blow CDs, just to find out that you disappeared. I want a new stealth animation too, like hunters.

    Energy. Energy management is an innovative idea both for PVE and PVP. Pressing the right skills at the right time when the energy is pooled, and making sure you don't run out of energy for that critical gouge or kick, while making sure you don't go above the energy cap is a skill in itself, and seperates a good rogue from an expert. It took me years to master, and I find it fun. It's like playing a mage low on mana.

    Cooldowns! Many cooldowns to manage intelligently.

    Position. most rogue skills like backstab, ambush requires you to be behind the enemy to initiate the attack. Constantly on the edge making sure you are always behind the enemy is really exhilarating. And the thought that you might not be able to initiate the skill and energy cap yourself when you don't have to, really pumps the adrenaline. It's a unique playstyle that rogues enjoy, that other classes doesn't have. (except those pesky cats)

    Stuns. Since vanilla, stuns and disorientation has been the tool of trade for rogues. It makes the enemy locked out of the fight for a certain duration so that you can do other things. It can be both offensive and defensive. From a PVE point of view, apart from add control, stuns are less used in boss fights.

    Survivability. Now with combat readiness as well as cloak and evasion, rogues has higher survivabilty than olden days. I like having to have recuperate up too, it gives you a low amount of Heal over time to save you from those nasty situations and provides a good amount of energy regen too.
