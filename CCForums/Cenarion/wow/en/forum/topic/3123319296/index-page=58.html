What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Slow ramp up time on damage. Stacking poisons and getting all our self buffs going takes too long.

Vanish as a damage buff. Vanish is a cool rogue ability that often saves a raid from a long run back or at least save you a repair bill. However, since a big damage buff is now tied to this ability and we often use it on cool down, we lose most of the utility.

Very little ability for burst damage, at least in assassination. I have to push buttons mostly to keep my buffs rolling and that is what I spend much of my time doing. Vendetta is the only real burst, but it is pretty weak if I need quick damage and also only applies to single target.

Wish I did not always have to use daggers to play assassination. With the size of daggers these days, there is no real claim that a dagger is needed for a more stealthy attack, the daggers are not much smaller than swords in any case. Seems there is no real need for this limitation and it does not seem to add anything to the game.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Stealth + Vanish. Attacking from stealth on an unsuspecting target. Good interrupts and stuns. Fan of Knives with stacking poison. Evasion tanking a boss for the few seconds it takes to finish it off! Ability to do good damage while moving. Recuperate is great for soloing old world bosses and that sort of thing. It is nice to have a bit of self healing.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Slow damage ramp up.
Mobs that can see us when stealthed.
Reforging to hit cap after every upgrade.
Target switching and having to restack poison and buffs.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Rotation is OK. As I mentioned above, a little too much self buffing and too little actual damage abilities, but over all pretty good. I like the slower assassination rotation as it gives a little more time to think rather than just mashing keys. Nice for weaving in other abilities like TotT. Of course there is a bit of a lack of other things that can be woven in.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Some mechanism to ramp up damage faster. Perhaps a stronger opener that also applied more stacks of poison that can be done from stealth.

Be nice to have a button to generate some extra energy when needed. Seems that when we get into an energy starved situation, like coming off a FoKs spam, it take a long time to recharge. It seems like you are sitting there waiting too long. Probably have to be on a reasonably long cool down.

Separate the damage bonus from Vanish.

Hit being worth while after spell cap, so reforging is less critical.

What spells do you use the least?

Redirect - With the long cool down this is practically useless. With a shorter or no cool down this could be used to solve some of the target switching problems. This would also be a nice fix as it causes the player to use this a the proper time to make it work. If it also transferred poison stacks, it would be cool.

Smoke Bomb - Very situational and not often used.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


For the record, I don't think removing things which give classes their flavor simply for convenience sake is a quality of life improvement when the management of those things is a part of what makes that class unique. I don't think hunters should have had ammo removed from the game, though they could have had ammo made much cheaper, stack much higher, or even just have their own special bag right on the character sheet for ammo where you could put in enough for a weeks worth of adventuring.

That being said, I think rogues don't have any quality of life issues really. Only thing I could really consider as one is that functionality of the Poisoner Addon hasn't yet been made part of the default UI yet, or even just built into the poisons.

For the record: I think removing poisons, making them spells outright, or making them permanent yet replaceable like runeforging would be the opposite of a quality of life change. Flavor and feel counts for a lot in a fantasy game.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    I think the Combo Point system is extremely outdated. With the long awaited addition of Redirect, at this point CPs should honestly just stack on us instead of the target.

    Similarly, Deadly Poison should not stack. In an ideal fight, it's always at 5 stacks anyway. It just creates an extra burden on target switches or ramping up at the start of a fight.

    On a lesser note, Poisons should last indefinitely on a weapon until it is replaced by another. On those rare occasions someone forgets, it's a HUGE issue.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Being solitary, Stealth allows me to pick my targets.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Since vanilla, Rogue mobility has been terrible. This is debatable in PvP, but in PvE having no quick catch up ability like Charge is a hindrance on target switches. Sprint is STILL on too long of a cooldown for it's purpose.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    I dislike the Rogue "rotation," it just doesn't feel like there's enough choices. It's always CP spam to 4+, and pretty much pick the finisher that's most important. I've played since vanilla, and I can guarantee you very little has changed in this regard. We've pretty much gotten a new finisher since then.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    1) Less "auto or proc" based damage, with specials becoming a larger portion of our DMG. Auto-attack and procs are also terrible in a PvP environment where you may only be on a target for a few short moments.

    2) A catch up ability available to all specs.

    3) Better survivability outside of cooldowns.

    What spells do you use the least?

    Expose Armor, Deadly Throw
