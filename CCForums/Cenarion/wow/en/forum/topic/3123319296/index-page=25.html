What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE (Exclusively Combat Spec)


If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics, Raids and Soloing vintage content.


If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Random World PVP (preferably players attacking lowbies and lowbie quest hubs)


What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
I'm gonna go ahead and hop on board with the hordes of people complaining about poisons running out. I remember the days when poisons had charges, and the 1 hour time limit was a much better option. I don't mind so much that I need to apply them, or that they make up a large portion of my damage. What I mind is that if I forget about them, and they end mid-fight, I have to stop attacking/moving and reapply them. And if you haven't made a macro to do it, that means opening your bags, and your character page, mid-fight and applying each one.

If the apply time, and the need to stay still while doing it is a must, I would like to see a little streamlining to the process. Maybe a take off of the totem manager for Shamans. Have individual slots for mainhand, offhand and thrown, have the rogue pick the poison they want for each, and just have 1 button apply all 3 at once. Or make it so they can be applied on the move. Maybe condense the poisons rogues have down to a singular item to sit in the rogues inventory like thieves tools so we don't have to carry 5 different poisons with us.

I'm also going to side with everyone that hates the shared cooldown between Cloak of Shadows and Combat Readiness. With cloak, I can compensate for sluggish/busy cleaners, but with combat readiness, I can compensate for a dead tank, or solo something particularly difficult. Raising the reuse time of cloak is also particularly painful.


What makes playing your class more fun?
Fast paced rotation, meaningful cooldowns, heavy burst damage and occasionally getting to survive to be the last man standing thanks to gamble defensive cooldowns.


What makes playing your class less fun?
Having to keep up expose armor in the absence of a druid or warrior. Having stealth detectors actually spot you from farther away than if you weren't stealthed at all. Switching targets. Anything with AOE point blank knockbacks.


How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I have no love for Bandit's Guile. It looks like its an awesome talent, and it is, but it just feels random, and it has a tendency to get lost in the flurry of other buff procs happening that its hard to watch for. Most of the time, I'm far more concerned with using Killing Spree at a point where its either 1) going to not kill me, or 2) will put me back on the boss/mob to negate a stun/fear/charm/knockback/fast movement mechanic, as opposed to when its going to do the most damage. That aside, I like how it feels to execute the rotation and integrate the cooldowns strategically.

I would also like to echo that it feels a little odd that most of my damage comes from auto attacks and poison, but the combo point builder I'm spamming, and the finishers I'm throwing aren't nearly so impressive overall.


What’s on your wish list for your class?
More sword/fist itemization. No offhand sword for rogues at all so far in Cataclysm? And just who were the Claws of Torment/Agony itemized for anyways? But I digress...

I'd love a faster ramp up on maximum damage output. Instant strikes feel fast, but Bandits Guile and Deadly poison slow it down considerably.

I'd love to see Ravenholdt or SI:7 play a bigger role in the life of a rogue. Ravenholdt just feels like an obscure reputation to collect, and nothing more. It should be like some kind of training academy (think of the movie "Wanted") to give the rogue various challenges to complete to utilize more of their skills, or utilize them better. Maybe the same could be done for other classes? (Nessingwary's Camp for hunters, Hearthglen for paladins, Moonglade for druids, Mannoroc Coven for warlocks, etc).

Another cooldown I could see being useful would be a distraction mechanic like the target dummy, crossed with blink. Drop the dummy in front, switch the attacker's target to it, port the rogue behind them, or X distance in whatever direction they're facing, something like that.

I'd love to see the old Killing Spree glyph come back. The reduced cooldown was wonderful.


What spells do you use the least?
Smoke Bomb, Dismantle, Deadly Throw, Envenom, Rupture, Gouge, Backstab

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
- Rogue Poisons - Rogues seem to be the only ones who are required to purchase and keep on hand something from vendors in able to put out their DPS, since hunters no longer need Ammo. Have poisons be applied via a skill instead of by an item would free up 2-5 bag slots for Rogues, and save them from having to stop between or even during a fight if their Poison's 60 minutes are up.

What makes playing your class more fun?

- Stealth. This is my second rogue main, and both I leveled using subtlety, just because it makes rogues, rogues. It does make me wish that subtlety was more powerful for soloing.

What makes playing your class less fun?

- Vanishing resetting a target. As a solo player, there's nothing worse than wanting to vanish to get behind a target so you can perform a second opener, but knowing you can't because it'll just reset the mob. Maybe a Glyph that will stun a mob when you go into stealth, instead of automatically resetting it. Giving you a couple of seconds to maybe shadowstep then ambush or rupture, like you would if you were part of a group.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

- With this toon I've focused on a Mutilate spec just to maximize my dps, and have found the rotation to be good and interesting, though less rewarding when soloing, as items like backstab and vanish + second openings are unable to be used effectively.


What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Skills for longevity. Perhaps a vanish that will leave a decoy, which would help to keep the fight going for someone solo to get behind a mob, but also will have utility in PvP.
- Poison elimination - As described earlier
- A new type of poison. One idea would be a vampiric blood one that may not do any damage but instead heal part of the damage that was dealt by the weapon it's applied to.
- Help with switching targets - Since Deadly Poisons have to get restacked on new targets, perhaps crits by weapons coated in Deadly Poisons will add 2 stacks of poison to the target instead of one. Getting the stack of 5 up quicker on multiple targets.
- Possible Tanking Talent Tree - With the death of the "Hybrid Tax", Rogues and other Pure DPS classes are hurting for use. Without adding a fourth "Utility" role, Rogues could be tanks via a form of the old "Evasion" tanking. Have a branch that focuses on dodging and parrying, maybe even allowing for the dual wielding of shields to hits that aren't dodged to be blocked. Make the rogues hard to hit, but also with abysmally low dps and where they'd take a lot of damage if they did get hit (since they'll still be in leather).
- Raid or Group buffs. Honor among thieves is good, but a base line one would be even better! :)

What spells do you use the least?

- Trap disarm. Perhaps adding traps to a raid or dungeon that can only be disarmed by rogues, may help to get the skill more use, and will give rogues more of a utility in raids and dungeons.
- Expose Armor - Just not in the rotation.

    ■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    Both (mainly PvE, casual PvP)

    ■If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

    10 man raids

    ■If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

    Arenas, BGs - server faction population is too small for there to be a rated BG group I can schedule with, so I do honor BGs instead

    ■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    Poisons using up bag space could be changed to temp weapon enchants (ala shaman design) from the spell book for a quality of life improvement.

    Target switching is both a quality of life and performance issue for rogues.

    Deadly poison falling off during tank and spank fights as everything but assassination (fixable by making shiv do enough damage that losing your whole stack isn't a still better dps than shivving to keep it up, or make non assassination specs run double wound (helps with ramp up and further distinguishes assassination from combat/sub)).

    ■What makes playing your class more fun?

    In PvP, the noticeable difference between playing well and playing badly (high skill cap).

    In PvE, the active use of a variety of our abilities (including survival tools) in encounters. For example - Heroic V&T made cloak, fient, recuperate, and sprint all useful (the fact that you pretty much used a gimmick spec was less fun however)

    ■What makes playing your class less fun?

    The fact that melee in general feels like a burden to your raid in some encounters without ever feeling like a benefit.

    Our reliance on relatively long cooldowns for mobility, burst, and survival.

    Our heavy reliance on other players buffs to make our dps competitive while we bring so few buffs

    ■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    As combat - spammy, but actually enjoyable as you are constantly doing something. Revealing Strike feels underpowered for how rarely you get to use it (at 4 and only 4 cps). I wouldn't mind seeing rupture fall out of combat's rotation completely, just to help differentiate it more from the other rogue rotations.

    Mutilate - while simple, I like mutilate's deliberate pacing and the preplanning involved in maintaining maximum envenom time and maximum rupture uptime.

    Sub - I tried raiding as sub, and I found the rotation overly punishing in PvE. There's a lot to juggle and dropping any of the balls penalizes you harshly. Making mastery affect the duration of recuperate and slice and dice for sub, rather than their power, would probably smooth this out and reduce the massive difference between sub on a tank and spank and sub on the majority of fights.

    ■What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Better target switching. We have too many factors that penalize for switching targets and redirect didn't help nearly enough. If I have to build combo points on my target, do something about poison stacking and (for combat) bandit's guile

    Burst on demand - killing spree is unreliable, 5 pt finishers take too long to build to, and with the possible exception of shadow dance ambush spam, we have no massive burst. To put it in perspective, we may be the only dps class that couldn't break Rageface's face rage ability in one ability (assuming a crit).

    Get rid of Bandit's Guile. It's broken, it doesn't do what it was intended to do, and it's a huge culprit in rogue woes for quality of life and ramp up. It's pretty much ignored in combat rotations as the difference between saving cooldowns for synergy with it and ignoring it all together is miniscule at best. Everyone knows this is the most penalizing and least rewarding talent in the combat tree.

    Ramp up time. This is tied to burst on demand and target switching. We take a long time to build up to our maximum dps output, far longer than the majority of dps classes, which is unfortunate due to current raid mechanics.

    Mobility. This is a pvp issue. While sub has reasonable mobility, both combat and assassination are atrocious and incredibly susceptible to kiting. While our snare is excellent, being close enough to apply it to a frost mage is but a distant dream most of the time.

    ■What spells do you use the least?

    In PvP - combat readiness, expose armor

    In PvE - combat readiness, expose armor (in raids, it is used in 5 mans if there is no druid, warrior, or hunter pet), dismantle, deadly throw, rupture (as combat), shiv (in raids), smoke bomb, sap (in raids), distract

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    - Still dependent on long cooldowns (aside from Shadowstep) in PvP for maintaining decent mobility and survivability.

    - Not being able to use Cloak of Shadows and Combat Readiness at the same time.

    - Not being able to get behind Raid bosses.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    - Assassination PvE Rotation:

    Ramp up time of getting up five stacks of deadly poison, slice and dice, rupture, and envenom buff. Greatly penalizes the rogue for switching targets. Along with Vendetta being a debuff on the target.

    - Combat PvE Rotation:

    Ramp up time of getting up five stacks of deadly poison, slice and dice, bandit's guile, and possibly rupture.

    Revealing strike feels clunky and not that much of a benefit to use. Since you will only be using it if you already have four combo points on your target, and if you are only going to be using Eviscerate or Rupture.

    Restless Blades also feels clunky.

    - Overall the Combat PvE rotation doesn't feel like a "rotation."

    Every "rotation" is different depending on if you get 4 or 5 combo points after your last Sinister Strike, which will determine if you use Revealing Strike.

    If your raid comp has a +30% bleed damage debuff available, then you will also need to use rupture in your "rotation." But if that raid comp changes on the next boss encounter, which it more than likely will in a 10 man raid, then you might not have that bleed damage debuff, and then your "rotation" changes again.

    Also depending on how much up-time you have on a certain raid boss... either due to the encounter itself, or your raid group's strategy. This will result in how many damaging finishing moves you can chain together, which effects Restless Blades, and will change the pacing of your cooldown usage and "rotation" for each boss encounter.

    - Subtlety PvE Rotation:

    There are too many abilities that you have to keep up all the time. Such as Find Weakness, Expose Armor, 5 Deadly Poison stacks, Slice and Dice, Recuperate, Garrote, Rupture, Hemorrhage debuff, Hemorrhage bleed, Shadowstep damage buff, Master of Subtlety damage buff, and Tricks of the Trade.

    - Change or removal of Bandit's Guile. This talent makes the Combat spec's ramp-up time very slow. Plus it hurts having to target switch with this talent, when you don't have Redirect available.

    - Change or removal of Main Gauche (Combat Mastery). This mastery feels so meaningless, and is one of the lease desired stats for a Combat spec rogue. I would like to see this changed to something where the stat actually effects our spellbook abilities.
