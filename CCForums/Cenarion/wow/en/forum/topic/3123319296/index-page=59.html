What type of content do you focus on?

5s, 10s pve. Arena, BGs pvp.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

Reapplying poisons every hour is a mild inconvenience at worst.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The rogue class is fun because we have an incredible tool set; sap, blind, gouge, stuns, disarm, evasion etc let us react to almost any unforeseen complication in pvp or 5 man dungeons to get things under control.

What makes playing your class less fun?

All of our great tools are mostly useless in a raid setting. Why is Shannox immune to distract, for instance? Is having him hold still for 5 seconds to set up the pull really that overpowering? Only certain mobs aren't immune to disarm, and they hit just as hard with their bare hands. Smoke bomb doesn't prevent being targeted by almost anything. Mechanics like Baleroc's crystals or Domo's scorpion cleave hit us through Cloak of Shadows. I'm not suggesting that we should be stunning dragons. I'd just like to see some consistency regarding what does and does not work instead of it being ad hoc.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

In general, I think all three rotations are far too similar. The formula for all specs is spam a combo generator, then either refresh whatever is falling off or do some extra damage. The only differences are which damage cooldowns they use every 2 minutes.

More specifically, I think Assassination has the smoothest rotation. The keys to success are managing energy rather than always mashing an attack every global, and paying attention to the envenom debuff. It's a bit slower and more meticulous, but I like it.

Combat's gimmick is bandit's guile, which is a terrible, terrible mechanic and is universally hated. The theory seems to be that it's a way to make AR and KS count for more when timed correctly, but the reality is that it's tedious, unfun, and an unnecessary penalty for switching targets. Mastery leads to problems with energy capping, and Killing Spree is too often a pseudonym for spectacular suicide.

Subtlety has the biggest numbers from active damage, which is appealing, but it can be a nightmare to try to keep everything up and it's ruthlessly unforgiving of any mistake. It's much more rewarding to a player to use his combo points to do damage rather than to keep up buffs. Making Serrated Blades function the same way as Cut to the Chase would be an easy way to make the spec more playable.



What's on your wish list for your class?

1. Making our utility abilities useful in a raid
2. Making the specs all play more differently from each other
3. A much higher portion of damage coming from active abilities
4. CoS and CR back on their own timers.
5. Faster ramp up time when switching targets. Having to actually run to the new target is a lot of wasted time we're doing 0 damage as it is.
6. A much lower cooldown on Redirect. Moving points to an add is fine, but I'd like to be able to put them back on the boss once it's dead.
7. End the senseless Killing Spree deaths
8. Assassination getting more use out of crit, haste, and expertise. The reliance on poison damage makes mastery much too strong compared to the other stats, so itemization will favor the other specs unless it's chock full of mastery.
9. Shadow Step becoming a baseline ability, for the sake of having more than one viable pvp spec. The lack of mobility in Assassination and Combat make them entirely unusable in arenas.
10. Some variation in the poisons used in pve, rather than instant, deadly, deadly being the default for all 3 specs.

What spells do you use the least?

1. Combat Readiness. CoS is a much better ability, and there's no justification for ever wasting its cooldown on CR when evasion would likely work just as well. Totally useless.
2. Expose Armor. In pve your tank is going to be putting it on for free, and in pvp the effect isn't worth wasting a finisher for. Totally useless.
3. Pick Lock. Friends and guildmates mail me lockboxes to pick, but I promise you both I and they wish they'd just gotten a green item drop instead. Totally useless.
4. Deadly Throw. If you're being kited, you're already snared. Snaring the kiter isn't going to get you any closer to him. It can interrupt a spell cast once in a while, but that's about all it's good for. Totally useless in pve.
5. Shiv. Getting rid of enrage isn't needed very often. It can be useful against warriors in an arena, or in pve if your deadly poison stack is about to fall off after a target switch. Totally useless in pve outside of one boss in ZA and BWD trash.
6. Pick Pocket. Rogue's draught is a good start, but I'd like to see a more compelling reason to use it than an extra couple silver per mob. In the time it takes to pickpocket ten mobs before killing them, I could have killed an eleventh for more money and exp.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.:
(From a Combat standpoint):
First, the biggest QoL issue I have is Combo points staying on the target and dropping if I place one on another target.
Secondly, losing the Bandit's Guile buff if I hit another target.
These both punish me for switching targets(not to mention my poisons staying on the first target), Combo points should be on the player(like a Paladin's Holy Power) and Bandit's Guile should simply be a buff that is procced rather than treating it as though it were a debuff on the enemy. I understand that Redirect is supposed to alleviate this issue, but the Cooldown is too big(even with Restless Blades) and it takes a Global Cooldown as well.
Last QoL issue I would say is Poisons, I'm perfectly fine with how they work at the moment, but it should be an Ability like Shamans' Imbue Weapon spells rather than taking up bag space.

What makes playing your class more fun?:
(From a Combat standpoint): The general fast-paced playstyle. Fast energy regeneration paired with Blade Flurry call for more cautious use and closer monitoring of my Cooldowns and Ability refreshes.

What makes playing your class less fun?:
Generally just my Quality-of-Life issues.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.):
(From a Combat standpoint): We don't have much of a "rotation", just spam Sinister Strike, occasionally use Revealing Strike, keep Slice and Dice up, and Eviscerate a lot (unless there is an Arms Warrior, Feral Druid, or a certain Hunter Pet present to encourage the use of Rupture, but my raid composition never has these class/spec combos). That being said, the Cooldowns such as Adrenaline Rush, Killing Spree, and Tricks of the Trade(with Tier 12 4pc) force you to play smarter and use them at appropriate times to avoid energy capping. In general I really like how Combat plays but making Rupture more attractive without those other class/spec combos would be nice.

What’s on your wish list for your class?:
Combo points on the player, not the target.
Bandit's Guile working as a conventional buff rather than acting like your target's debuff.
Poisons being Abilities instead of items.
Making Rupture more attractive to Combat Rogues.
Less PvP oriented talents in the Combat tree(since Combat isn't even viable in competitive PvP).

What spells do you use the least?:
Redirect, Shiv, Expose Armor, Pick Pocket, Distract, Gouge, and Deadly Throw.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


    I agree with the consensus among the posts that I have read that the "mechanic" of poisons seems outdated and (at this point) pointless. Back when we made our own poisons and had a poison "skill" the idea of carrying them around and re-applying them made a good amount of sense. With the progression of the game I think making our poisons purchasable was a good decision because the amount of time it took to actually brew them was considerable and was a pain unique to Rogues. Now, with Hunters not needing ammunition for their weapons Rogues again have that unique pain of many extra bag slots that could be used for something else being taken up by an item that could just as easily be an ability. Another unique thing about poisons is the hour long duration persisting through death. Don't get me wrong, I would loathe having to re-app poisons with every attempt of a boss, however, what that does is force us to remember to apply them. After an hour of wiping or killing or whatever when buffs are constantly being redistributed I find that poisons often get forgotten, drop off mid fight, and cause a huge loss in DPS.
    A suitable solution that I think would serve everyone would be to have poisons work in a way where you apply each one, and it lasts until either cancelled or you apply a different one over top.

    *takes a breath*

The only thing I can think that could be interesting is to have a seperate storage place for rogues to have poisons on their person so it doesn't take up bag space..     What makes playing your class more fun?

    Sneaking around in the shadows, overall being a shady character... There's just something cool about being a rogue in that regard. I also quite like the fact that there's a fairly visible cutoff point between skilled rogues and not so skilled rogues - the class is not as easy to play as some people may think and those who do think that are often missing some important points about their rotation (and are probably standing in fire often too).

    I play as combat and honestly I really love the current rotation.     There is only one big change that I would definitely like to see though. One of our key abilities that is currently on the global cooldown has absolutely no business being there whatsoever - Tricks of the Trade. Rogues are the only class left in the game that bring nothing to a raid aside from their damage (and interrupts I suppose but lots of classes can interrupt). The only thing we can really say we have is ToTT to give another raid member more damage or to help the tank keep threat. Yes, the tier 12 4 piece gave rogues more incentive to be using ToTT on cooldown, but honestly I find far too often that I can't use it on cooldown because 9 times out of 10 it will energy cap me due to it being on the GCD thereby preventing me to use an ability to spend that subsequently wasted energy. To sum this paragraph up: Seeing as that ToTT the only unique raid utility we can claim to have don't you think it would make sense to design it so it doesn't interfere with the most important thing we bring to a raid - our dps?

    Like I said above, I really would like to see ToTT come off the GCD.
    I would like to see some attempts made to help with energy capping BUT they should be minor changes, not a reworking of the entire rotation. I don't want to see the spec watered down so that every Tom, Richard and Harry can come in, click a few buttons and do crazy dps. The learning curve is perfect as it is.

    What spells do you use the least?

    Smoke Bomb, Combat readiness. Neither really have that much use in PvE, although smoke bomb does have some uses during the occasional encounter.
