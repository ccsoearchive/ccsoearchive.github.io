My comments are specifically concerning Sub rogues in PvE. The spec almost doesn't work at all in progression content.

I think Blizzard's primary concern for rogues in 6.0 is making all three viable for PvE content. PvP content may get a lot of attention on fan sites and message boards, but far more people are PvE-only than PvP-centric. It's what drives the storylines and the game itself.

Having said that, Sub needs two things:

1) Huge buffs to AoE damage,
2) A final decision on positional requirements in the game, not just for rogues but for kitty druids as well.

Fixing No. 1 is easy: Fan of Knives for Sub rogues gets a huge buff. As in, double or triple of what it current does, and only for the Sub spec.

Fixing No. 2 is tougher. My suggestion is to either throw out positional requirements, or let Shadow Dance affect outbound damage across the board by a major number, say an additional 30-50 percent. You would then probably have to nerf Hemo or Backstab when not used in stealth or Shadow Dance, but that's OK.

If you're concerned about PvP balance, that's the easiest fix in the world. Have these changes/abilities only affect NPCs.

I've been suggesting this for ages now, and I may sound like a broken record.

Rework the basis of stealth as we know it. Make it more dynamic and interactive than it is now. Allow more access into stealth, while reducing the initial burst from opening from stealth. This will in turn solve three problems that has plagued the class on a hold:-

With a more interactive stealth, it offers further differentiation among the specs. Subtlety for example can have it's mechanics more centered around stealth accessibility. Assassination can still be about the poisons, while combat is all about the physical combat prowess we knew it to be.
Secondly, it solves the age problem of Rogue's initial burst breaking PvP or being the determining factor in victory or loss. It will in turn be less punishing if you get screwed as you exit the gate by randomly LARGE AOEs.
Thirdly, it adds a unique theme back to the class on a hold. While every other class has been walking around with our niche ability (some to a lesser extent than others), this would be essentially giving rogues back something that RIGHTFULLY belongs to them.

PS: Stealth will be denoted by a small which regens as energy does over time (slower ofc), and drains as you stay in stealth/use abilities in stealth. This regeneration will be affected by haste.

Since Stealth is the highlight of my suggestion, of course it cannot be mentioned without changes to the spec that is to be centered around it.

SUBTLETY

Firstly, Sanguinary veins would no longer be needed. Since the spec's burst would be adjusted around being in a 'stealth' mode more often. The rotation would be almost the same in a nutshell:-
Backstab: Does X amount of damage, however when used from behind, it hastens the regeneration of your stealth bar.
Shadow dance: Your stealth regeneration is increased by 100%, and your backstab 's special effect no longer has a positional requirement.

Abilties like Slice and dice, hemorrhage and rupture would remain as is, perhaps with numbers adjusted. Meanwhile, Subtlety becomes SUBTLE with it's mechanic centered around diving into 'stealth'.

I just wanted to add some my thoughts besides specific changes I'd like to see. While I think they highlight some direction, and I gave a few points about it here and there I wanted to step back and look at the whole picture as I see it.

I've stayed with the rogues over the years because one word.

Finesse.

No other class has it quite like the rogue does. And in some ways it's a liability in comparison to other classes. I'm not going to whine here about how other classes are better and whatnot. What I mean is that having to make minute decisions with every single combo and energy point is a strength for the class. Many other classes care about just filling GCDs or cooldown timing comparatively.

It can make sense thematically to have the warrior smashing buttons for instance. But it isn't very good gameplay. It's not a deep interaction in the game world. And I don't think that such gameplay should be rewarded. I get that different classes speak to different people in a variety of ways, and sometime, trading a little simplicity here allows for a better experience elsewhere. I get that.

Rogues used to be the kings of CC, especially stuns and incapacitates. They are thrown around the classes and specs like crazy now. It's not unique. It's not a draw to the class. And it devalues the required finesse for a rogue, when you could simply have another class just blow a mild cooldown and not have to make any other decision about the ability. Not have to sacrifice in some other area. It makes the rogue feel weak by comparison. Rogues are fantastic fun by themselves and the system is great, but they don't play in a vacuum.

I miss openers that hurt. CC that was powerful. We shouldn't ever, ever, ever be ignoring ambush if we have the opportunity to use it. It shouldn't be a question. Opening with mutilate or Revealing Strike is just sad.

Builders should have Umph! You shouldn't wonder if that was a builder or an autoattack/poison crit if you turn off the combat text and sound. You should see it on the health bar, especially solo. Part of this is removing some passive damage. I think that moving SnD to combat only would be a good thing. Combat uses slower weapons, they got the attack fast theme going on. Give to them and remove some bloat from the bars.

Which brings me to finishers. There should be no question how many combo points you finish at. It's 5 cp where possible. This has mostly been held standard with Anticipation, and the talent is fantastic, which why I strongly want this to be baseline in 6.0. It just make the class feel much better. With this change, I would additionally request that instead of keeping CP on dead bodies, that they instead stack anticpation charges on the rogue on death. Not killing blow, and fix the weird issues with despawned corpses throwing our cp into the void.

Those points are precious, and I do believe that target stacking combo points add more to rogue gameplay than it restricts it. It's good to have limitations and the mindfulness that rogues have of their combo points is unique to the rogues and kitties. But losing CP feels really bad. Additionally, consider allowing redirect to move anticipation charges to the current target. This allows redirect to remain useful, along with its hopefully coming glyph to remove the cooldown. I would also consider allowing the buff finishers to be able to utilize anticipation charges first, and then whatever CP is on the target to a max of 5, the remaining CP left on the target.

Alternatively, simplify anticipation to allow them to pool at max CP, but instead only applying them on a finisher, allow them to "fill in" with whatever cp is on the target. This can simplify the UI somewhat and give them an representation on the target directly. Show them as a different color combo point and simply show how many points a finisher would use if activated.

Say, I have 2 Anticipation charges, they show as 2 white/glowy frames around the CP on the target frame. I can finish with only those two "white cp", or I can add combo points. I choose to mutilate and gain 3 CP, Now my finisher will finish at "5 cp". Which shows as 3 red cp, followed by 2 empty glowy/white "CP" frames. I mutilate once again and gain 3 CP. The frame now shows 5 red dots for the 5 CP that I have on the target. I also now have 3 anticpation charges, and they show as 3 glowing frames of red CP. I finish with envenom, those three glowy cp become regular red cp. A moment later the mob dies and immediately despawns as part of a raid encounter. Now again, I have 3 glowy CP when I click a hostile target.

Just don't make us look at the top right of the screen. There's nothing interesting going on there. It's not pretty to look at and its cluttered like crazy. It's not fun, we are almost a decade into this game. I'm pretty sure we find better ways to communicate essential mechanics to the player this many years into the game without making them stare away from the action.

The other concern that I want to address is button bloat. Most of the stuff we've gotten since BC has been nothing but bloat at this point. It's only serves to steal key bindings and action bar slots, at the expense of sanity. Lets not turn into the many clones you have inspire that got the wrong ideas about how to build and MMO. We all know they game isn't successful because it strains the physical interface the game depends on, keyboard and mouse. It is simply the result of power creep and class homogenization.

I can safely say that most of my skill, what little there may be, is directly the result of a clever honing of keybinding across a nostromo/orb weaver and a multibutton mouse like the wow mouse or the naga, and an in-depth knowledge of the macro system honed over years of trial and error. I wouldn't play wow if I no longer had access to those devices. I simply wouldn't. The standard keyboard/mouse is woefully inadequate to play this class at high performance with a reasonable level of comfort and finger acrobatics. Especially for newer players.

I given both my old nostromo and wow mouse to a friend simply to help entice him to play the game again. Last time we played together in Cata, I could see the contortions he was having to do to keep up on the keyboard with his shaman. He'd hit wrong buttons all the time. The game, and the interface requires frustrated him more than anything, although he's thought the game had become too hard. It hadn't. WoW forgot what interface it was designed for.

Just to add this as a quick follow up to my post at the top of pg. 2...

The biggest problem for the class as a whole is that you have 2 specs very viable in all facets of the game, and 1 spec that has serious viability issues in progression content. There isn't another class in the game that suffers from this problem. Mages will typically have the "go-to spec" for a particular expansion, but the other two will still be viable. Same for locks, warriors, etc.

If Blizzard wants people to fall in love with their toons and play something they actually LIKE to play, the onus is on the developers to make sure one spec doesn't drag behind the other two so badly as to dishearten the devotees of that spec. The reason I play Sub is it's fun. Assassination is milquetoast, and Combat is an outright chore for me to deal with. My preference, as stated earlier, is for Sub to be the hyper little pinsticker, the haste-built killer in the shadows. But at this point I almost don't care what he eventually becomes, so long as he actually works.

How do you feel about the current damage distribution (where the bulk of your damage comes from) for each spec? What would you like it to be like? Please make note of whether this is in PvE or PvP.

I think that parts of rogue damage are too passive. I'm not specifically opposed to passive damage in and of itself, but I feel that each spec has become too reliant on it. Having one spec with high autoattacks/poisons would be fine if the other two specs were notably different.

At first look Sub seems good (top 4 for me are usually: Melee, Evis, Backstab, Ambush - in that order) but even with the active sources being 3 of the top 4 I still do ~40% of overall as passive through autoattacks and poison procs. More if you include the Meta.

What are your thoughts on each spec's rotation?

I like the pace and feel of both Assassination and Subtlety. With Assassination I like Dispatch because it's a button you always WANT to push but can't because it's not always available - above 35% at least.

For Sub I like having a 1min CD instead of the typical 2-3 minutes. Once a minute I get to do something cool and really increase my DPS with effective play. The only part of Sub I don't like is the reliance on Vanish/Prep for DPS. If they could remove FW from Openers without breaking PvP I'd definitely be appreciative.

A Lilian Voss type spec for rogues. The way that DK might be consider a combining of Warrior and Warlock I'd like to see a combining of Rogue and Shadow Priest.

Shadowstep.

Least favorite?

Combat post 4.0
