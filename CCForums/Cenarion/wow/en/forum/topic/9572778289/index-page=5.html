To those who don't know me very well, subtlety PvE is kinda my thing. With that in mind, I have a few thoughts on it. I'm going to open by saying that there's a good chance I will think of some things later and add them in as new posts so they are easily visible.

I think to a beginner, subtlety must seem very confusing. The spec apparently has three themes going on - bleeds, openers, and finishers. Due to SV, bleeds are required for damage, but Find Weakness, a significant damage boost and the reason the spec can be called an opener spec, does not affect our bleeds. I think openers and finishers go together pretty decently, but bleeds seem very out of place with the spec and I think to a lot of players might make it seem very unfocused to the point where they themselves may not know what to do.

I get the impression that there's some discussion about removing finishers from specs, such that would involve subtlety not using either rupture or SnD. To those who enjoy playing sub, one of its main benefits is the more interesting rotation. With HaT and its mastery being finisher based, if subtlety were to lose a finisher, it would need to gain another. To me, this would be fine, but it would also have to be affected by mastery. I'm going to throw out some ideas that I think are worth considering. Keep in mind that I'm not fully behind any of them.

Stealth finisher: This was an interesting idea brought up by Vinya over on the EU version of my "Rogue Refinements in 6.0" thread. Here's the link, but I will summarize: http://eu.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/7860657859?page=1#4
Basically the idea is that rogues would get a finisher on a short (30 sec?) CD that would cause them to enter stealth. It would be usable in combat and probably be a normal stealth, but the point is more that it would allow us the use of an opener and proc FW. Having such a cooldown would cause a few issues, including a high penalty for messing up the rotation, and not scaling with well mastery, so perhaps that isn't the best idea.

FW Finisher: Rather than the stealth finisher idea, it might be preferable to have a finisher that applied Find Weakness, and probably did some damage. I'm not sure about the idea of a cooldown, simply because if you had this with, say a 30 second CD, you'd be able to get quite high uptime on FW and I think the current uptime on FW is good. (It is pretty high, but you definitely still feel it when you have/don't have it up. Adding higher uptime would probably mean general nerfs which would make the spec feel weaker. Also, I think it is bad to have an expectation of anything close to 100% uptime.) Another possibly better way of doing something like this would be to make a proc for Eviscerate applying FW, but then that doesn't fix the issue of not enough finishers.

Crimson Tempest: I would absolutely love for this to be a skill ability, having it do across its duration maybe 1.25-1.5x the amount of damage an eviscerate single target, making it worthwhile for good rogues to use it always. This might make sub's AoE a bit strong, since while most rogues - myself included - would say it needs a buff, it should be lower than the other specs' AoE as I think the spec works better as a single target oriented one. One thought is to remove or nerf FoK for sub, but that might go too far. I would like to point out that when CT was double dipping into mastery and SV (and thus did more damage than eviscerate), as well as currently on cleave fights, adding CT to my rotation is and has been very fun. While perhaps a bit too much for the average player as a 4th finisher, if you removed rupture or SnD from sub's arsenal having CT would be very manageable and probably be considered more interesting than either of those abilities due to the shorter window where it does its damage and the instant damage.

I'm done for now, although I'll leave with one thing: A lot of people have suggested a change to stealth where it has a short CD and shorter duration. I like that idea a lot in theory, but I worry that such a change would make ShD feel less powerful, and that the increased FW uptime would make the change less meaningful as a whole.

Unrelated to sub, but a short duration, usable in combat stealth might be perceived as weaker than the current one, which might risk alienating rogues. What do you think, would it? (There's a strong argument for rogues needing our damage to be less front-loaded as it is but I'm not sure this is the answer as stealth can be a touchy subject.)

I am posting my own personal thoughts on MY rogue, which don't necessarily reflect where I think the rogue class (and all of you wonderful rogue players) should be going.

What is your ideal, glorified, rogue? This one can be a bit unrealistic, and doesn't have to be one answer (You could, for instance, say 3 - 1 per spec), but basically, if you had a dream you were the perfect OP rogue, what would it be like?


A PvP DPS spec, a PvE DPS spec, and a tanking spec (PvE). I think that having multiple specs vying for relevancy in PvE/PvP only serves to dilute each spec's uniqueness and fun. Or, a separate PvP ruleset with very simple balancing (everyone has 1,000 life, abilities do 50-100 damage, etc).

How do you feel about the current damage distribution (where the bulk of your damage comes from) for each spec? What would you like it to be like? Please make note of whether this is in PvE or PvP.


Active damage should be greatly increased across the board, and breaking up the specs into PvE/PvP could help balance that as well. A pure active damage class (like the Monk was originally intended to be) is just too strong in PvP, but in PvE, what's wrong with hitting for a million damage????

What are your thoughts on each spec's rotation?


  • Assassination has a relatively easy rotation with almost no tough decisions
  • Combat has a very spammy rotation, which is fine by me, but also suffers from being pretty easy (not a lot of thinking/reaction).
  • Subtlety has a fun rotation, but at the same time, a lot of it just feels like busywork outside of Shadow Dance.


If you could pitch just one idea to the devs, what would it be?


I want to see something REALLY engaging added to our rotation. I want my rotation to be more work than just watching EventHorizon without any surprises. Maybe some procs, maybe some special movement or action we have to take, etc. Just something so that I don't fall asleep in heroics. I was thinking of maybe something where we can like 'find a weak spot' and have to take two steps to the left and then our next attack deals +20% damage or something. Then we have to jump and hit a special "Air Strike" button to give us extra energy. Keeping us on our feet and attacking the boss in interesting ways.

What is your favorite part about your rogue in MoP, and what has been your favorite historically?
Least favorite?


  • Favorite in MoP: Great damage most of the time
  • Favorite historically: Stealth (unfortunately Stealth in MoP feels more useless than ever, I have ganked fewer people in MoP than ever before, world just feels empty outside of heavily-populated daily zones)
  • Least favorite in MoP: Really boring rotations
  • Least favorite historically: Any time we're not doing well with damage


What should rogues be able to do better than every other class/melee?


Damage, by about 5% when played perfectly. I am fine with damage being our "thing".

What should rogues be able to do worse than every other class/melee?


Soak damage. I have always liked the slippery glass cannon/cooldown spam/dive-for-the-kill/wear-PvE-gear-in-PvP style of rogues. We shouldn't be able to survive getting nuked by 2-3 classes at once. People always say "rogues need survivability!!!!" without realizing that comes at the cost of dealing more damage.

What do you think about the class's role in PvE/PvP? If you would like to see it change, in what direction?


We are becoming the control class, definitely. We've got a wide variety of CC on different DRs and we're mobile and slippery enough to get the right CC in the right place at the right time. PvP is going towards a slower, more drawn-out battle, which doesn't suit the damage-first idea that I have of the rogue.

What that rogues used to have do you miss?


People in the open world to kill. Part of this is my own fault (transferred to Mal'Ganis) but I think flying mounts + sprawling zones + LFR/LFD have killed world PvP for rogues. Connected realms and a new expansion without flying mounts (for a while) can't come soon enough.

Its a good thing no one likes combat as a dps spec so no one would mind gutting a perfectly good dps spec with unique play style for a tank spec.


At the point where Blizzard is making these changes (6.0) combat and assassination will have existed in an incarnation similar to their modern incarnation for three expansions and sub for one and half expansions. During this time people have become naturally attached to different spec play styles and to tell someone "sorry the playstyle you like is being deleted" would cause a huge outcry. GC mentions constantly on twitter that one of the big worries with lock style spec overhauls is that the changes may alienate more players than it helps and killing the playstyle of two dps specs seems like exactly what he is talking about.

Further I reject the underlying assumption that it is difficult to create specs of the same role with unique play styles. The obvious counter example are warlocks but using locks as an example opens up the potential objection that locks were completely redesigned. Look instead to mages, there is a resource management spec (arcane), a cooldown spec with some procs mixed in (fire) and a reactive proc heavy spec (frost) that are all balanced reasonably well with each other. DKs are another, albeit slightly less successful example, frost is a proc based/reactive spec and unholy is a dot management spec. DKs aren't as good of an example because frost is pretty well behind unholy right now but that is mostly a matter of numbers tuning. Again we have a class with two specs of the same roll that are both unique.

Another problem with the idea of the rogue tank spec is how do rogue tanks contribute to tank uniqueness? The game already has two to three avoidance tanks (depending on how you want to consider dks) and monks in many ways have a very similar toolkit to what rogue tank would look like. Monks AM mechanics are increasing avoidance and the ability to "shrug off" some amount of damage taken, if you remove the word brew from a lot of monk tanking abilities you basically have a rogue tank.

Finally I think many people are underestimating just how complex of an issue tank balance is, its probably more complex than dps balance which isn't a simple issue by any stretch of the imagination. Raids only bring two tanks so weaknesses tend to be magnified whereas with dps balance you can often afford a weaker spec or two so long as you also have stronger specs to cover. No tank can be too weak at any one mechanic or they risk being sat en-masse and tank toolkits can't contain tools that other tanks lack lest that tool starts trivializing encounters (hi monks on ra-den).

TLDR: I see no upside to making rogue tanks that would overcome the massive outcry from many within the community at having their preferred spec deleted.

Historically, playing Sub during DS. The rotation was very complex but INCREDIBLY satisfying to execute (close to) correctly, and being able to "cheat" on some mechanics like the morchok blood phase was very fun.


Least favorite?


The spec homogenization this expansion. I feel rogues lost a ton with the MoP changes in this regard.

Historically, leveling. Compared to every other class I've played, leveling a rogue is really rough. I always feel like I'm getting the snot kicked out of me and do really poor damage back. All the other classes seem to either do significantly more damage or be more survivable (or both).


What should rogues be able to do better than every other class/melee?


Single target damage, "cheating" at mechanics, and avoiding damage (things like feint count as avoiding damage).


What should rogues be able to do worse than every other class/melee?


Target switching, actually taking damage (should be squishy if you get hit), utility, and to a lesser extent aoe damage.


What do you think about the class's role in PvE/PvP? If you would like to see it change, in what direction?


PvE - we should be THE premier damage dealers, assuming equal gear and skill a rogue should outperform other classes (except when fight mechanics say otherwise, like frequent target switching)

PvP - I'd like to see rogues be tweaked to be even better healer killers. I'd personally be fine being worse against other dps if it means we tend to wipe the floor with healers.


What that rogues used to have do you miss?


The sub rotation from DS, I greatly enjoyed the complexity.

Also, Glyph of Disguise persisting through combat when it was on the ptr. I'd love to be able to kill bosses while looking like other bosses :D

In regards to alternate resources and other mechanics tweaks for rogues that people are discussing (broken into sections for each spec):

  • Assassination
    • I'd like to see finisher damage scale with energy (but not spend extra) to reward proper pooling even further. Maybe something like at 40-79 energy finishers do 20% more damage and at 80+ energy finishers do 40% more damage. Combine this with removing SnD from assassination and I think the spec would be perfect.

  • Combat
    • I'd like to see combat have "oh !@#$" off-tank capability. Think symbiosis growl getting rolled into the spec, with cd usage you could temporarily help stabilize the raid when things go wrong. I personally feel this fits really well with the swashbuckler theme of combat and would just augment its existing ability, rather than annoying a ton of people by doing something like turning it into a full tank spec.

  • Subtlety
    • I really like the suggestion of having a stealth form that functions like metamorphosis, having the spec actually leverage stealth in a significant way would be a HUGE win for the class as a whole.

    Thinking about the the underlying spec theme I think a demo lock style system fits better for combat as a BG replacement than for sub. The idea of ramping into bit burst seems to fit logically into a cooldown centric design which combat is clearly aiming for. Additionally the most common complaint against BG is its fundamentally uncontrollable nature, if it lines up badly with player movement you can be in trouble. Switching to a demo lock style system would help deal with that complaint.

    Sub admittedly doesn't have a coherent underlying theme but I looking at combat's cooldown centric system and assassination's timing centric system rogues could certainly use a reactive proc based spec. Sub potentially has the makings of a reactive system with HaT which with a few small changes could provide a more dynamic CP income. To do this sub would probably need a "shadow dance" proc to give it an out of stealth openers and probably another couple procs like a "next ability free" and/or "next ability auto crits/does more damage." The obvious design inspiration is a frost DK.

    A demo lock is fraught with risk for PvP because it can easily create a situation with really scary burst that probably isn't balanced. Given the current design of combat and sub in PvP I think it is less likely for combat to end up with out of control burst (killing spree excepted) with a demo lock style design than it is for sub.

Or perhaps bring something like Overkill back, which gives you a temporary buff upon exiting stealth. Although I think the application of an additional poison is more exciting.
