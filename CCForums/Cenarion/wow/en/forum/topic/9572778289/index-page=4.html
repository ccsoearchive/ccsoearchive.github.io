Something important to keep in mind when thinking about possible rogue refinements is the difference between aesthetic and play style. Play style defines how your press your buttons, aesthetics define how you contextualize those button presses.

To emphasize this difference consider two statements:
1) Assassination is about poisons.
2) Assassination is about envenom.

Statement (1) is an aesthetic comment and statement (2) is a play style comment. The difference is subtle but consider if envenom all of sudden became fire damage instead of nature damage (assume damage remains the same) would how you play assassination in terms of how you press buttons change at all? By contrast consider if envenom was replaced by eviscerate (again assume damage remains the same) this loss of the envenom buff would change how you press buttons because timing you would no longer need to worry about timing envenom to maximize the buff uptime.

This is not to say that aesthetics are unimportant. A coherent aesthetic helps players contextualize their experience and ideally the play style of a spec will reflect the aesthetic properties of a spec. However if the goal is to address the issue that rogues play too similarly that requires play style rather than aesthetic considerations.

To give an example, a number of people in this thread have talked about replacing CT with a poison bomb AoE or rupture with a poison dot for assassination. However none of these proposals explained how this poison bomb or injection would actually change the play style of assassination. That isn't to say these ideas are bad but they do not solve the underlying issue os spec similarity on a play style level.

Specific ability problems/ideas:
As a pure combat rogue (pve and pvp) for ~8 years, I've included a couple of special breakdowns to try to communicate my feelings on 2 important abilities

Blade flurry
Problems:

  • The energy regen penalty slows the fight down a lot and feels like I am doing less damage even when I am doing more
  • The current damage split also, has lost a lot of the feel it had when you would move around to make sure it hits the target you want it to.

Ideas:
  • Abilities no longer require a target during blade flurry. instead the damage is evenly split across up to x targets in front of you.
  • Allow blade flurry to proc poisons on secondary targets (I have no idea if this will help with the feel, it just seems strange that we are hitting multiple targets with each attack but only 1 ever gets poisoned from the blades)


Adrenaline rush
Problem:
When combined with haste, combat potency procs and Relentless Strikes, I feel that every ms of latency is costing me a lot of damage, as it is very easy to cap your energy during this phase, and we are locked on an already very short gcd.
Ideas:
  • Change adrenaline rush so that instead of doubling energy generation, halve ability costs.
    - This allows a similar number of abilities to be used as the current method (a few more with pooling), but is more forgiving because it takes slightly longer for energy to cap.
  • Change combat potency from a 20% chance for 15 energy to a 100% chance for 3 energy (or somewhere in between).
    - This would make energy generation less spiky, restoring our control over energy usage and pooling.
    - If the weapon scaling was also moved from the proc chance to the energy amount, it would give players control over how spiky their energy is.
  • Give us a way to increase our energy pool size
    - I hesitated to suggest this one, because I don't think that it is a good big-picture solution, but the suggestion may help to understand the issue
    - Could be restricted to only in combat or only while Recup/Snd/??? is up to prevent pooling of too much energy in pvp.
