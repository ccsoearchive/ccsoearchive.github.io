What would I like to see? OH BOY!
2 scenarios:
A) (preferred) get rid of passive damage and bring rogues back to ability-based damage. would require 2 parts IMO.

1) get rid of right clicking damage:
- remove SnD
- remove haste effecting energy regen
- redesign poisons, make them operate solely off of shiv (same/similar effects) EG shiv w/ deadly would deal damage and give a CP, shiv w/ wounding would deal less damage, not give a CP but vastly reduce healing for a small amount of time (to anticipate those big heals or set up for burst). Envenom would need a rework too, most likely to interact with a new shiv in some way (increased damage? lowering CD? I don't know!).

reasoning for poisons: the current design of poisons strongly supports the passive damage model, in order to get rid of passive damage and thusly make rogues have a more involving rotation, poisons need to be reworked, yet removing them entirely is detrimental to the identity of the class. By making shiv a relevant ability for both PvE and PvP, rogues can have an interesting ability, non-passive and involving damage, yet still maintain the image that we all love.

2) abilities need to be tuned/changed to balance DPS in the absence of passive damage
- now that right-clicking does less damage, our abilities should do more damage and be adjusted in such a way that haste is still relevant.
- could follow ret pallies and have haste effect GCD/ability CDs

B) (less preferred) keep the high passive (right click damage) in which case I would hope:

- SnD is made baseline, this would a very slight increase in dps and be far more helpful to rogues
- Muti rotation changed, possibly make shiv give one combo point, allowing anticipation to be passed for MfD, giving us a bit more burst (not to mention I'd love to be able to use MfD in raids)

additional thoughts:

- vanish: right now vanish is used by muti and combat as an almost free 2CP and by sub as a dps cooldown with subterfuge, although forcing rogues to choose between keeping it for defense or using it for DPS is interesting, it doesn't really work for PvE, when fighting bosses we shouldn't have to use a defensive cooldown to be at 100%.
- for muti and combat, something else should take the place of shadow focus so that rogues shouldn't be forced to pop vanish just for DPS, either give us fights/balancing where we can use MfD or something else.
- for sub, combine the combat uses of shadow dance and vanish. Right now the best spec involves using both vanishes and shadow dance (30s CD with AoC) with subterfuge for about 14s of stealth per period, with SD having a ~25% up-time and subterfuge having a ~5% up-time with AoC and prep. Why not give shadow dance 3 charges lasting 4.5s each and minute to fully recharge ( keeping about the same total up-time in stealth, yet allowing us to better spread FW and MoS across our rotation). In addition to this, subterfuge could be redisigned to only work off of natural stealth (IE entering combat from stealth and not vanishing). Adjust the numbers a bit and nothing should change for DPS, yet we'll get a more streamlined design and not have to sacrifice a defensive cooldown just for numbers.

Tl;DR: either get rid of passive damage and give us new stuff, or get rid of SnD cus it sucks. Also vanish needs some work because using it for DPS is shoddy design.

I guess I'll put in some more in-depth feedback while I'm here; I'm not very current on the PvP meta game these days so these will largely be PvE suggestions

Assassination I feel like is in an alright spot, most Assassination players I know seem to be pretty content with it (although some say it's a little easy and maybe it could use another mechanic); it never really had much of an identity prior to this expansion beyond "poisons matter" (and it sort of had a finishers thing going on until they gave that to Subtlety in Cataclysm) but I feel like the addition of the Execute mechanic helped define it in a way that it needed. I kind of miss Mutilate being tied to poisons personally, I don't know how it would really be done anymore in a meaningful way but "just a move that hits twice" feels a little bland. Also, I don't think Slice and Dice just being made baseline for Assassination is a very compelling change; I personally think Slice and Dice (for all specs) should either be removed entirely or made a little more interesting, it currently just feels like a nuisance to have to maintain it.

Combat I feel like is much more interesting now than it's ever been and I'm glad you can dual wield heavy weapons again. My only real gripe with Combat spec as of now is how slow it is with the 50 energy Sin Strikes; I think everybody hates that (I also think Sin Strike could afford to be a little more than "an attack that gives a combo point"). I feel like Combat even though it uses the bigger weapons is supposed to be the fastest of the specs, like back when it had double attacks with Sword Specialization and Blade Flurry's old effect, plus Adrenaline Rush. Overall though, it's a lot more involved now than in the past and I think it's a lot of people's favorite now. Oh, also, Revealing Strike feels like something that would fit better in Subtlety; "stronger finishers" seems like it shouldn't be a Combat thing but minor gripe.

Subtlety, my personal favorite, has finally gotten a real identity past "just the utility tree" and again I think it's largely in a good spot. I think a lot of people find the "stealth moves matter" aspect and Find Weakness mechanic to be a lot of fun, and strong finishers are nice too. I think it's a little too hectic in multi target at the moment mainly because you manage a lot of debuffs; Find Weakness tied to the player might help a lot with that but it might be too strong in PvP so I don't know. Redirect glyph is pretty much mandatory in these situations and I think there's a pretty strong case to make it baseline. I also feel like Hemorrhage "just being another bleed to manage" is a little bit boring and that it could be elaborated a little (although I do think if it's called "Hemorrhage" it should be a bleed; it always bothered me before). Also, Sanguinary Veins could maybe increase the damage of Garrote and Crimson Tempest like how it does for Rupture, but that might be too strong, I don't know. And of course, positionals (especially on Ambush) seem like an extra flaming hoop to jump through to punish you for playing with inexperienced tanks

I do like how you can specialize in pretty much whatever you prefer now, and that there aren't really any "useless" stats anymore

All of this just my opinion of course

Very well. I shall create a TL;DR from my posts

1) Stealth is bugged. This happens most often when transitioning from one area to another (intrazone and interzone travel). I hope that these issues will be addressed. It likely has something to do with a character being loaded into the new area, while stealth is not loaded

2) Stealth is either hard-countered, or not countered at all. Stealth needs a soft-counter that allows the Rogue to open on someone using their stealth abilities while giving up their invisibility (and not having to talent for it)

3) Poisons should become more flavorful. I would like more options and a greater impact of poison choice, for both MH and OH poisons. In essence, I want them to take on the same role as Runeforging and Shaman Imbues

4) Assassination is an amalgam of burst damage during execute phases, pressure DPS if allowed uptime, minor to moderate DoT damage, and CC that interferes with their ability to be effective. Versatility is good, however, it's not working for Assassination. Here are issues I see with the spec and ways to fix it
a) Too much damage is cornered into an execute phase. This is fine, unless the Rogue cannot bring a target into the execute range. This is a simple remedy of pulling damage from Dispatch and spreading it evenly over the rest of their damage
b) The spec is not fun. The rotation is Dispatch > Mutilate, Rupture > (SnD) > Envenom, and use Vendetta on CD. I believe this can be manipulated by allowing more procs and opportunities to use Dispatch outside of the execute range and being able to store Blindside charges for significant burst damage
c) To be effective, the spec requires too much uptime. Unlike an Aff Lock or a Feral, Assassination does not DoT-cleave well, as they're not strong enough. The spec can't seem to put enough pressure on a single target either, as they're damage is too spread out. Assassination also lacks the tools to consistently stick to a target

5) Combat needs to be looked at. Restless Blades is a fun mechanic, however, I dislike the CD dependency. It also makes the spec highly gear dependent. Outside of their CDs, Combat is very weak. Combat cannot pressure a target, nor is the spec very interesting without their long CDs. Here are some fixes
a) As planned for WoD, allow the player to have greater control of Bandit's Guile
b) Rather than a CD, turn Adrenaline Rush into a proc from events during a fight. These procs could be health and energy regen, speed increases, haste buffs, etc....
c) In order to maintain pressure, Combat needs more mobility and/or ability to get their opponent from a range, even if only a little bit

6) Combat lacks any real defensives. I, amongst other Rogues, would like to see Combat return to its tanky style of play. Whether this be through a defensive "stance" of sorts, passive mitigation buffs, or abilities, I believe that to live up to its name, Combat needs more defense

7) Subtlety is a great spec. There are some issues with it
a) We rely too much on positional requirements. Hemorrhage hits for about 2/3 the strength of Backstab, even with a slow MH equipped. Subtlety either needs more ways to deal damage from the front, or a way to manipulate players and mobs into giving the Rogue their back
b) Stealth is either hard-countered, or not at all. This means that a Subtlety Rogue either starts the fight well, or they have no chance. I would like to see more opportunities for light burst and pressure during a fight
