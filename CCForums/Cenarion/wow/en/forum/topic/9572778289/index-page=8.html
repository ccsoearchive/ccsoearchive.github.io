What drew me to the rogue class in vanilla was the idea of playing a character who is really different from the other classes. Granted a druid can prowl, but no one else really gets to live in and get comfortable with stealth like we do. I like going sightseeing, getting to places where other players can't, and pickpocketing an entire town before disappearing without a trace. I also like getting to pull off a tricky maneuver in combat, which means having the ability to do something impressive and memorable (e.g. the old cold blood talent).

However, this game isn't about memorable, one-time fights. This is a grinding MMO, not a single-player RPG. And that's fine. But there needs to be some compensation for that with our class. Either we get the tools to grind through mobs like everyone else, or we get the tools to complete more objectives rogue style.

As an example, I have been faithfully working on lockpicking since forever. Leveling that skill is one of the few things I really miss from vanilla. So why can't I lockpick prison doors, chests, etc. in quests? I could maybe understand if the key drops from a boss, but if every other grunt has a key it can't be a very fancy lock. Why not let us sap and lockpick instead? Same with quest items that aren't heads and ears - give them a chance to drop from pickpocketing. How about a disguise cooldown that lets us interact with guards?

When it comes to combat, relying on abilities like disarm, gouge, etc. are what make the class so fun for me. The problem comes when I need to rely on special abilities while a similarly geared character from another class can just faceroll their way through. I see this on the Timeless Isle a lot - DKs, druids, hunters, warlocks, warriors, pallies and shamans are just knocking them down and healing themselves when necessary, but if I don't manage my cooldowns I can wind up dead. Hunters in particular are a good example of design done well - their pets let them pull off insane tricks when soloing, but that's because they use a mechanic like threat which doesn't affect PVP or buff DPS. I think that squishier classes like priests and rogues could benefit from some threat mechanics in solo encounters. My priest can fade and have his shadow fiend tank for a few moments, but that doesn't compare. How about making apparitions draw aggro the way a mage's mirror image does? How about rogues having distract create a really obvious target dummy that pulls aggro on all mobs in a 5 yard radius (like the old stoneclaw totem), but that won't help in PVP since it looks nothing like us? I believe you can make a game where the classes are balanced, but they reach that balance in different ways. Unfortunately rogues are falling behind in the solo game, as well as PVP a bit.

One last thing: please get rid of slice and dice and weapon requirements. It's never been fun, and it led to all the issues which killed combat daggers. Let us equip whatever we want in whatever spec, and let us use our combo points on big, sexy eviscerates/envenoms.

I personally, feel that each spec needs to be more varied from the others. I enjoyed the differences that each spec provided in previous expansions as well as options as far as gearing goes. Really just how they are now just more specialized. Most of these opinions are from a PvE perspective.

Assassination should focus more on poison damage and debuffs/advantages gained through them. More viable Poisons should be available and there should be more comparable choices in terms of dps and utility for both PvE and PvP. (Paralytic Poison needs to be replaced with something better/different). Mutilate is fine and should be left alone, along with Envenom. Daggers should continue to be the weapon requirement for the spec, maybe add another poison based finisher? or CP builder.

Combat should revert to being focused on alternative weapon specialization. I enjoyed the differences in the spec with maces/fists/daggers/swords when talent trees were a thing and i think that should be implemented in another way that maybe isn't just a passive % increase to damage while wielding the weapon specialization you are using. Armor reduction that isn't hurt by other classes abilities and buffing weapon damage of CP builders i think would help this spec a lot. Weapon choices should stick to slower/heavier weapons imo. Swords/Axes/Fists/Maces. Though dagger representation would be thematic to the class.

Sub should continue to focus on stealth abilities and bleed's. I enjoyed playing a Hemo Rogue in classic and think it should be a viable choice as a CP builder for the spec. Backstab should also be supported as a CP builder. Maybe another, stronger finisher bleed other than rupture that also debuff's the target in some way would be welcome here. Thematic cooldowns to the class are represented in this spec heavily and it should stay that way. Positioning should be more forgiving in this spec as it is with the others, however that can be achieved.

Redirect should be a 10 sec cd without a glyph. Crimson Tempest needs to be reworked somehow to make it more attractive as an AoE finisher. Glyph of Tricks of the Trade needs to be reworked to make it a more attractive choice in glyphing. Paralytic Poison needs to be reworked to make the talent more attractive. Expose Armor needs to be reworked to be worthwhile in group PvE situations. Glyph of Cheap Shot should be reworked to make it a more attractive choice in both PvP and PvE, 0.5 more seconds is a little low with all the DR present.

My unlikely wishlist:
Leaching Poison as a base ability for Assassination. Another new survival talent to replace it.
Paralytic Poison as a base ability and replaced with a new talent choice or reworked to be worth the talent point.
Slice and Dice and/or Rupture refreshed on current target after using appropriate finisher dependent on spec.
More loot variation in gem slots/stat weights/trinket itemization for the class. (less "agility based dps" gear. i like to be considered a Rogue, not agility based melee dps)

Hope some of you agree with these concepts, i think it would make the class stronger and more interesting to play overall.
