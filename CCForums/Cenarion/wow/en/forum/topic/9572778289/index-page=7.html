Alright, let's do this.

I'm gonna try to answer these on a personal level like I requested you all too, but I might slip since I've been pretty deep into figuring out what the community as a whole wants.

What is your ideal, glorified, rogue?

I think each spec should have slightly different identities. My personal glorified rogue is one that can play what depict Subtlety and Combat as, though all seem very appealing. This answer is mostly for PvP since I think that is where class identities, especially the rogue's, are allowed to shine. (I'd prefer my Combat archetype for PvP and my Sub archetype for PvE.)

Assassination: Constant, dangerous, pressure. An Assassination rogue has burst, but rarely needs it. They will get their foe below 35% health eventually, and when they do, Dispatch and their lethal poison (Same as Deadly but w/ Wound's MS effect) will ensure that unless the rogue is CC'd, no amount of healing will save its prey.

Combat: Master of control, a Combat rogue's job is to protect their team with stuns, incapacitations, and kicks. To facilitate this Combat would be the most mobile spec. When the enemy is sufficiently worn out by the rogue's teammate/s, the rogue will suddenly unleash its on-demand, long-CD burst and finish off its foe.

Subtlety: With its damage loaded in openers and finishers, the enemy's worst fear should be not having a cooldown for the Subtlety rogue's openers. With increased mobility and AoE protection in Stealth, a Subtlety rogue is almost guaranteed to get an opener. While Subtlety's damage outside of Find Weakness is minor and it has two important finishers to maintain (Slide and Dice and another, not Rupture), the short cooldown on Shadow Dance will keep the enemy constantly on their toes.

What are your thoughts on each spec's rotation?

This one is PvE centered.

Assassination: It is far too easy to play this at a level where you can do strong DPS in almost every situation. I've played Subtlety almost exclusively in PvE since Cata, and haven't played Mut since I got sick of it at the end of WotLK. Well, except for when I tried it out last week. The fact that I was able to pull nearly the same DPS as Mut when I had not played it for so long as I did with Subtlety, the spec I have played for 1.5 expansions and am a leading "authority" on says a lot. Single target DPS was close, trash and short fights Assassination won hands down (this likely will chance with Glyph of Hemo Veins though). AoE and Cleave as Mut seemed much simpler to the point where I could pretty easily pull more DPS than I could as Sub. If a spec is going to have the potential to do well or better in single target, cleave, AoE, and execute situations, then it should be vastly harder to play. Either make the rotation harder, give the other specs better access to important niches, or nerf the hell out of some of Assassination's many niches. Seriously. GAH!!!!!

Combat: Combat's pretty good in PvE at the moment from a rotational standpoint, I like it as the medium-difficulty spec (btw also like Mut as the easy spec but it shouldn't have as many niches). The one problem with Combat in PvE is that AR makes the spec have chronic energy capping issues later in expansions. Combat should play fast, and as such I think increasing the energy cost of SS was a bad call. That said, AR makes it so that at its fastest Combat is ridiculous. The solution in my mind is to nerf the effect and cooldown of Adrenaline Rush, so as to make the energy more manageable. This would have the effect of making Combat a much healthier spec in PvP, since as it is Combat's only real Cooldown is Killing Spree, and that is either OP or simply not enough depending on its damage output. Giving AR a shorter CD would - Hrm, wait, did I ask people not to make suggestions? Well !@#$ it I'm narcissistic and can't sleep. - hopefully make the energy from AR more useful for things like laying on the control or progressing Bandit's Guile at more opportune times.

Subtlety: I believe I've already spoken about Subtlety's confused identity. Basically Sub seems to be about openers, finishers, and bleeds all at once. This is confusing to players, especially ones new to the spec. Subtlety should be solely about openers and finishers, and as such SV and Rupture should be removed. Hemo can stay, I guess. Because of Sub's high CP income it needs something to replace Rupture. This needs to be an over time effect that provides a noticeable effect. I suggest something that increases the damage of finishers, but that specific effect is just an off the top of my head musing. Another option would be to buff Crimson Tempest such that it can be used single target. SnD is already good for Sub since it is buffed by FW (The core mechanic that Sub should be concerned with optimizing) and, most importantly, provides energy which noticeably hastens (hehe) the rotation. As I mentioned, Sub's main goal should be maximizing Find Weakness uptime. Right now the frequency of ShD and Vanishes is great, as it has high uptime but not so much that the buff isn't noticeable. I really like how there are different ways to apply it, and along those lines am loving Shadowmeld. Subtlety needs a niche, but I've gone into that in detail. Also, Subtlety's positional requirements are toxic to the spec. Remove them. I've also gone over that in detail.

What is your favorite part about your rogue in MoP, and what has been your favorite historically?

Honestly I'm not sure, I like MoP a lot more but I don't know if that's because of my rogue or not. Crimson Tempest is a pretty great addition to the Subtlety arsenal. While a bit weak, it's nice to have something to do in multi-target situations.

What should rogues be able to do worse than every other class/melee?

Live without cooldowns, AoE (NOT cleave).

What do you think about the class's role in PvE/PvP? If you would like to see it change, in what direction?

It's pretty good, I think I already covered this in questions 1 (PvP) and 2 (PvE). The general ideas are good, but the specs should be able to own them better or, where they don't have a clear identity or niche, they should be given them.

What that rogues used to have do you miss?


Poisons (profession)

Poison Quests (SERIOUSLY.)

Thistle Tea!!!

Babes hangin on your arms all day 'cause you were SO OP. (For a rogue, the night is no times for babes on arms.)

Spec-only bonuses (were talents) like Improved Gouge, Improved Sprint, and that Sub ability that reduced AoE damage taken passively (Enveloping Shadows?). This is actually something I think really should be brought back.

What popular suggestion(s) do you vehemently disagree with, and why?

Semi-removal of positional requirements: Giving BS a +damage buff when attacking from behind but allowing it from the front will not remove and of the problems with positional requirements unless the penalty for facing the boss is so small as to be negligible. Just remove them.

It would be cool to have a mechanic that conflicts with FW as Fierydemise will hopefully get around to sharing with us, but Find Weakness right now is at a happy medium between being a noticeable damage increase and being up frequently enough to make it interesting and interactive. An increase to uptime is fine as a set bonus, but if it lasts an entire expansion then damage would need to be nerfed to the point where FW not being up will become a penalty, rather than FW being up being a sexy bonus.

A few points for anyone who wants to suggest abilities:

1) You are not a genius.
2) It has already been thought of.
3) Just because it's cool doesn't mean it's good.
4) Just because it's good doesn't mean rogues should have it.
5) Just because classes A, B, ..., N have it doesn't mean rogues should have it.
6) The devs have said this game needs fewer abilities, and I agree. Most top PvPers will have upwards of 50 keybinds.

Here's the takeaway: There are definitely times when new abilities should be added, and I think it would be silly not to assume we will be getting at least one new ability in 6.0. However, if you are going to suggest an ability, you really have to do it right. First, what type of ability should rogues get? Should we be expanding our niches to be better rounded? Or perhaps should we be getting something that empowers one of our existing niches so reclaim identity?

There are TONS of cool new abilities the devs could give us, but if you actually want an ability, you have to understand the dev-player dynamic and work from there. Think of us as being in a department store full of all the possible abilities. We as players are going to want to go to certain sections. Maybe we want something extra roguey so we gravitate towards the "Rogue" section. Maybe we want more healing so we go to the "Bear" section. Maybe we want something under "OP" or "Cool" or you get the point. Fact is though, we aren't the ones buying the abilities. In fact, we aren't the only ones there. In reality, we're there with our family. All the other classes are our siblings, and our parents/guardians are the devs. What's more is that the devs saw our rooms before coming and decided that we have too much junk as it is. You aren't going to get anywhere by just saying "OMG Dax can you buy me the Leap-n-Stab please?", so don't, unless you do a pretty damn good job of explaining why we need it.

I don't want to prevent anyone from posting, but please read through the OP before doing so so that you can give the most productive feedback possible.

            Hm�

            Probably how gutless assassination feels to play right now. I thought there was a nice flow to it in 4.0, but most of those mechanics no longer exist (overlapping garrote and rupture, vanishing for overkill, etc.). Blindside procs are nice, but the execute phase is particularly uneventful.

            Historically�

            Probably Vendetta. I think it's a lazily-designed DPS cooldown that doesn't add anything interesting to the spec. I feel like it'd be more interesting if Vendetta was more like a controllable execute phase we could use at any time that had some kind of ramp-up damaging mechanic.

What should Rogues be better at than Everyone?

            Mitigating/avoiding damage (as a DPS), and manipulation.

What should Rogues be worse at than Everyone?

            Recovering from big hits (that should've been mitigated/avoided).

Abstract/Introduction:

            I like the design philosophy behind each spec, and how well they're being balanced for PvE. I dislike our takes on AoE and ranged DPS, and I think assassination is getting particularly stale of late. I also have an unsettled, internal monologue about utility skills that require energy.

            And at the end I want to blow !@#$ up. I feel like I'm writing a Michael Bay movie here.

Assassination:

            Assassination is our maintenance spec, every one of its finishers (slice and dice, rupture, and envenom) has a significant, indirect damage component. Assassination doesn't deal direct damage so much as it monitors and maintains a consistent flow of passive DPS into the target.

            I don't have a gripe with this, in many ways I think it's a similar playstyle to a DoT class except we're buffing ourselves instead of debuffing our enemies (attack speed boost from slice and dice, poison damage boost from envenom, venomous wounds procs from rupture ticks, etc.).

            The drawbacks, I think, are in the lacklustered execution, not the idea itself. There's never any danger of not being able to keep rupture or slice and dice up, and while you can definitely optimize your performance as assassination, all it amounts to is glorified micromanagement.

            There's never a moment where you wonder "should I envenom, or refresh rupture here?" All the answers are obvious, with the only upward blips in your steady stream of DPS coming from external factors or trinket procs (or vendetta, which is basically an on-use DPS trinket by itself).

            Blindside procs help by giving you something to react to in an optimal manner, but those become a nonfactor during the execute phase as well. Like I said before, I think assassination would benefit from Vendetta being a controllable execute window and axing the general execute phase.

            I just feel like there should be additional variables for assassination that influence gameplay.

Combat:

            Combat is our active spec; sure it uses slice and dice and factors in revealing strike, but it's all about wanting to know what the best thing to do is RIGHT NOW and then doing it, which sets it apart from assassination's monocularly-visaged steady and respectable DPS tabulations.

            While the 0.5-second-GCD-mashing can get insane, I like how fast and in-the-moment combat plays. And how restless blades rewards you for playing quickly by letting you play even quicker, shoving your cooldowns back in your face so you can do fast things faster than ever before.

            I also like the hierarchy of optimization; you learn the basic rotation, then add rupture, and learn to optimize around bandit's guile. There's a nice amount of depth without too much complexity weighing it down, and then there's the utility from skills like blade flurry and killing spree.

            Combat's probably my favorite spec to play in dungeons or LFR, because it shakes things up and has such a variable toolkit that lets me punch different buttons for different situations. Compared to assassination, which goes "Oh my GOD, AoE? I get to MULTI-DOT RUPTURES!"

            In any case I think it's a step in the right direction, and I wouldn't fiddle with it too badly. I did like blade flurry back when it was a more limited cleave, though; I'd prefer if they reverted that change and just fixed the damage component so it wasn't as game-breakingly ridiculous.

            Combat might be too much fun, but I'll let that slide when it's not breaking things.


Subtlety:

            My impression of subtlety is that it's reactive; the combination of honor among thieves, positional DPS requirements and setting up appropriate shadowdance opportunities means subtlety is probably the most aware of its environment at any given time among our specs.

            But without assassination's ease or combat's spreeing cleavage, I get the idea that it's a bugger to play in current content, which is further exacerbated by DPS modeling difficulties. It still has the reputation as our most expert spec though, and I'd like to experiment with it if I were playing.

            I don't have much to add since I haven't played subtlety since 4.3, but I like what I remember of it (particularly shadowdance). If there are ways to give subtlety more utility in current raiding scenarios without destroying its uniqueness or overpowering its DPS, I'd be game.

            I guess to me subtlety is like Schindler's List; it might be rewarding, but it's not exactly "fun".

            I'd like to revisit it whenever I get back into the game, though.

Why Carpenter's like Rogue AoE:

Energy, Cooldowns, and Utility Skills:

            Rogues have a metric ton of personal utility skills which fall into three camps: either they have an energy cost, a cooldown, or both (personally, I think shiv having both is a sign that its current implementation isn't very well mechanically balanced, but that's tangential).

            Now, there are a lot of times in raids when I would like to feint; especially as assassination, if only to interrupt the boring monotony of my life. But feint happens to cost TWENTY ENERGY. So basically using it is a direct sacrifice in DPS, which is our common decision denominator.

            This basically means that you're not being a good rogue if you feint a lot, a good rogue tries to get out of feinting whenever humanly possible. And that's just� %^-*ty. It basically amounts to a trap ability in almost all instances (like the Glyph of Tricks of the Trade).

            I think it'd be better design for us to want to optimize the usage of our personal utility abilities to help ourselves (and the raid indirectly thereby), not to avoid using them unless at the utmost extremity. I think this might also factor into how we play in PvP, but in PvE I find it distasteful.

            Anyway, here's a list:

            Energy: Cooldown: Both:

            Expose Armor Cloak of Shadows Blind
            Feint Evasion Dismantle
            Kick Gouge
            Redirect Shiv
            Smoke Bomb Tricks of the Trade
            Sprint
            Vanish            I just think less of these things should cost energy so we can use them more often.

Rogues and Range:

            I am not a fan of shuriken toss. I actually hate it. If I expanded on that creatively I'd probably get the person posting this for me in trouble with the powers that be, but basically I think it's bad design; it either means our toolkit is insufficient, or other toolkits are outrageously overpowered.

            But either is a failure in counterplay.

            I realize this is more of a PvP issue, so I don't have much to say that's useful. But I feel strongly that rogues should be like the postal service: no matter where we have to go to make a delivery, we get there and then stab you repeatedly with all your letters if you tried to run away.

            I'd be cool with a ranged slow or stun on a reasonable CD that used a concealed, wrist-mounted crossbow or dart mechanism, but none of this spammable nonsense. And shurikens? Really? Why does this feel like something we got because monks decided they didn't need it.

            In summary, please fix PvP counterplay Blizzard. I don't like it butting into my PvE.

An Explosive Idea:

            And finally, bombs.

            I'm not the only person with this idea, but I really want to see it happen. Basically using a bomb would incur a short CD on all other bombs while some would have higher cooldowns (so Smoke Bomb would put itself on CD for 3 minutes, and all other bombs on CD for 15 seconds, say).

            We'd have a shrapnel AoE/DPS grenade.
            Smoke Bomb would still be a thing.

            Then there'd be one modeled on our active primary and secondary poison effects that'd create a patch of poison on the ground for a number of seconds and affect anything standing in them. Cripple could slow, Mind-Numbing would screw up casters, Paralytic could randomly stun, etc.

            It'd basically be like a shiv grenade.

            We could also make Distract an in-combat ability; you throw it, there's a buff of smoke, and a temporary illusion that taunts nearby monsters to attack it; after a certain amount of time (or damage) the illusion disperses. And it should be usable on hunter and other class pets.

            I don't think this should be a large single-target DPS gain, it'd be more about AoE and utility.

And a more Final Finally:
