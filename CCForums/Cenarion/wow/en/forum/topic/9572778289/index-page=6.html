08/22/2013 05:22 PMPosted by Sanosuke
Not to sound like an anime nerd, but something that created shadows clones (ughh i hate naruto)

I gotta say you aren't doing a very good job at not seeming like an anime nerd ;) That said there are so many on this forum it is crazy. Especially when Cloaked and Verelyse were here.

I do have some feedback actually. I wrote this as a pretend letter so if it comes out a bit formal then there ya go. The feedback is exclusively about Sub PvE, and references this thread :/

I am going to start by bringing up the strengths and weaknesses of the spec. The more subjective ones will be based off of feedback from the “Rogue Refinements in 6.0” thread <http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/9572778289>. I am then going to end by suggesting some changes.

Strengths:

Rotation: Almost everybody I talk to who likes Subtlety does so in large part because of its complex rotation relative to the other specs. In fact, many preferred the rotation in Cataclysm because it was harder. Having Subtlety as a sort of hard-mode spec for rogues pleases a subsection of players, and simplifying the spec, even if that would please a majority, would hurt the spec and class as a whole.

Shadow Dance: On the official forums a thread proposed the question, “What is your favorite and least favorite rogue cooldown?” Consistently people’s favorite was Shadow Dance. The reasons were that it changes the rotation, the damage is clearly noticeable, it is hard to get the most out of it (maximizing Eviscerates during Find Weakness uptime and pooling energy), and its short cooldown. PvPers also likes it ability to be used defensively or offensively. I personally think more cooldowns should be as noticeable as Shadow Dance, though uniqueness is important.

Active Damage: People complain a lot about their damage being passive, which I interpret as them not seeing as many big numbers on their screen. Subtlety does not have this issue, and that appeals to a great many people.

Weaknesses:

Required Glyphs: Right now Glyphs of Hemorrhaging Veins and Vanish (it is still bugged to proc an extra Subterfuge) are required as they directly increase DPS. Glyph of Redirect will be too for any add fight.

Lack of Theme: Subtlety is at once a bleed, opener, and finisher oriented spec due to Sanguinary Vein and Hemorrhage, Find Weakness and Shadow Dance, and Honor Among Thieves and Mastery (Executioner) respectively. While this does not bother me personally, removing a niche is worth considering. Focusing on openers and finishers makes the most sense to me since Find Weakness, a core mechanic, does not empower bleeds and finishers help keep Subtlety complex.

Low AoE/Cleave and Lack of Niche: Compared to the other two specs Subtlety is very weak when it comes to multi-target fights. While I think the spec works well as a primarily single target spec, the difference in DPS has caused many theorycrafters and forum rogues to not feel like they can call Subtlety viable for progression raiding. At the moment single target DPS is believed to be similar enough that niches are where one spec will get ahead of another, and currently subtlety lacks a useful niche. I say useful because while burst is a niche, there are very few fights where it truly matters compared to mechanics like AoE and cleave. It’s worth mentioning that a lot of people liked it when Subtlety monopolized survivability. In my opinion while survivability was cool, it was not a useful PvE niche.

Positional Requirements: I wrote a guest post on Fierydemise’s blog on positional requirements <http://roguechat.wordpress.com/2013/07/10/haileaus-on-subtlety-positional-requirements/> but I will summarize the issue. I hear a lot of bad reasons people give for wanting them removed, so what I am saying comes from me. Also note that when I say Backstab, I also mean Ambush. Similarly, Hemorrhage includes Garrote. First, being able to Backstab is not a perk. In group PvE, being behind the enemy is the norm, and thus when we are forced to use Hemorrhage it actively feels bad. Even if dropping Backstab from the rotation was neutral in terms of DPS potential, it would feel wrong. Second, because of the significant loss of DPS when attacking from the front, rogues feel a lot of pressure to not spec Subtlety on certain fights. In this way positional requirements are toxic to Subtlety in a similar way that two-target Blade Flurry was toxic to rogues as a whole by forcing raiders to have a Combat spec. Please read my post for more and more detailed analysis and arguments.

Single Target DPS: Because the Subtlety rotation is difficult, many rogues find they do less single target DPS as Subtlety than when they play other specs. I think this is good as at low skill Subtlety should do less DPS than the other specs. That said, it is only fair that at high skill Sub should do more DPS. This may already be the case; I have not seen very many players I know are skilled try out every spec, and none recently.

Proposed Changes:
Bake in Glyph of Sanguinary Veins or remove Sanguinary Vein’s damage component and buff accordingly: The main effect of this would be to make it so Subtlety rogues have one fewer required glyph in PvE. Removing the damage component of SV would also address the spec’s perceived lack of theme, making it about finishers and openers.

Fix the bug where the Vanish buff procs Subterfuge so that Glyph of Vanish is no longer required.

Buff Crimson Tempest: Ideally the damage of Crimson Tempest through the course of its duration would be 125% that of Eviscerate. This change would allow more skilled rogues to add Crimson Tempest to their rotation and separate them from the run of the mill. This would also bring up Subtlety’s AoE and cleave to a level where it can compete with the other specs. Fan of Knives could be nerfed to compensate, as Subtlety should still have the lowest AoE and cleave of the rogue specs – just not as low as it is right now.

Remove the Positional Requirements of Backstab and Ambush: It’s time. Perhaps make it a glyph so that PvP rogues have to make a choice, although that would bring another required glyph to PvE.
