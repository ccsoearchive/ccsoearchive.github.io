If you don't mind me asking, what kind of button presses, what makes a press meaningful, and how does it promote spec differentiation?


-I would say the most meaningful button presses are the ones that have a near immediate and noticeable effect in proportion to the cost required to press them. Have you ever pressed a button in any game that you immediately wanted to press again after one use? that's a meaningful press.

- If each spec has different skills/buttons that are meaningful in unique ways beyond "this is your builder, this is your finisher, this is your maintenance buff/debuff" then a diversity would be promoted, because each spec would have truly different priorities, instead of different names for the same priorities.

When I first began to play rogue many moons ago, I did not use slice and dice much, if at all. I only started using it because more intelligent rogues told me it was better dps. If the effect of an ability is so unnoticeable, it isn't going to be fun to press, regardless of how powerful it is. Slice and Dice only recently began to actually have an effect on gameplay(extra energy for Sub) instead of just being 'that button you press to raise dps.'

The problem is, abilities like Slice and Dice make up far too much of rogue damage, and it's like that for all three specs. I should feel excited about putting rupture up for the damage rupture does, not because my attacks do 20% more. (BTW, deadly poison still ticks harder than a 5 point Master of Subtlety Rupture.)

Currently, all rogue specs function on a 2 builder, ~2 finisher model rotation. If you open your spellbook and check your core abilities, all three specs list slice and dice, two out of three list rupture, and two out of three list eviscerate. While other classes may have common core skills between specs(hunters actually have it worse than we do) the majority of other dps specs still boast significant differences in their rotation/playstyle.

I would suggest making Slice and Dice, Rupture, and poison procs meaningful. There are plenty of ideas on all three that have already been suggested by others.

I would take a hard look at combo points and introduce real choices for how to spend them. They should have different weights between the specs, or maybe they shouldn't even exist for Assassination/Subtlety.

An idea I've toyed with for a while is having a sort of momentum effect associated with Energy and tied to openers, builders, and finishers. At first I was thinking it could be a secondary(tertiary) resource beneath the energy bar that worked similar to rage- certain attacks built it, others consumed it, it emptied slowly on its own(even during combat) and added a bonus to damage/energy regeneration based on how high it was. Perhaps it could be baked directly into energy itself, as the concepts are similar. The framework for such changes is already there- abilities like restless blades come to mind, continued efficient play is rewarded by more frequently available cooldowns.

Assassination: using a finisher would grant a huge amount of momentum, and finishers would gain the most damage for having a full bar of it. Combo builders would naturally drain the bar, while active poison abilities(newly introduced, exclusive to Assassination) would pause the drain, or even temporarily allow builders to increase momentum.

Combat: each builder used would grant momentum- different builders in different combinations would cause even greater increases as well as secondary effects. Combat would rely less on finishers, perhaps going so far as to avoid using them at certain periods to maintain high momentum and essentially develop an extended/endless combo playstyle. To that end, combo points might stack higher for Combat, with greater numbers of points decreasing the detriment finishers pose to high momentum.

Subtlety: momentum would be gained through openers. To that end, Subtlety would enjoy an increased number of opening attacks and restealth(partial or complete) abilities. The playstyle would revolve around extending periods of target vulnerability via MoS and Find Weakness, with finishers pausing momentum drain and potentially offering a chance to reopen(Even in PvE)

Something like this would differentiate the specs because there would be rewards for using some aspects of the current Universal Rogue Rotation(Has that been coined yet? If not, I call dibs) and bonuses for avoiding other parts of it. Likewise, it would allow each spec to develop its own separate toolkit with few abilities shared between them, and drastically different core abilities.

Note that this is all pretty much from a PvE perspective.

What is your ideal, glorified, rogue?


Honestly, the general feel that rogues have now. A higher skill capped class capable of pulling massive damage, with lots of utility to boot. Makes me feel like I have multiple tricks up my sleeve to deal with any situation.

How do you feel about the current damage distribution for each spec? What would you like it to be like? Please make note of whether this is in PvE or PvP.


PvE. I feel as though its unfair that Combat can only do well with the Tier15 4piece bonus, and while its strange to me that Mut isnt falling behind right now, I can deal with that. Sub definitely could use a buff. All things coming next patch I know :P. I feel like Cata's damage distribution, but with Mut more on par with Sub, would work; With Combat>Mut>Sub for AoE dps and Sub>Mut>Combat for Single Target dps, but with all being acceptable at either role.

What are your thoughts on each spec's rotation?


Mut's rotation is definitely a bit nicer this time around, with the availability of CT for better AoE damage as well as Dispatch procs to break the monotony of Mut Mut Envenom repeat works well. With the added bonus of anticipation and pooling energy for the envenom buff for mut, I believe the spec has enough wiggle room that the greats can distinguish themselves.

Combat, Love the AoE potential, I feel slowed down and more powerful with BF up, and I love the added complexity with Anticipation, but im hoping the changes to SS will help with the spam, yet make it not feel as slow as Mut.

Sub, I honestly dont have any real clue, I haven't had much experience playing it. I know a lot of people are complaining about Ambush/Backstab position requirements, but I feel like thats just part of the game. Perhaps it could be reworked that using Hemo would be the normal rotation, and Backstab could be the harder yet more damaging option.

If you could pitch just one idea to the devs, what would it be?


Not having "necessary" glyphs. You can tell with how the AR glyph is now becoming baseline that some glyphs are in fact needed for better performance. I like the premise behind glyphs allowing you to customize your character a little bit, and to have part of that taken away by mechanical issues of the class isnt fun.

What is your favorite part about your rogue in MoP, and what has been your favorite historically?


My favorite has to be soloing rare mobs. Seeing as I havent raided at all this Xpac, Soloing has become my new outlet, and to have not only the access to, but the need to use my full range of abilities makes it more rewarding when the scouts go down. Kinda wish we had enough damage reduction to kill Warbringers tho.

Historically, my favorite part about my rogue was when I first switched to combat in 4.2. I absolutely fell in love with the rotation's mechanics with cooldown management rather than poison and envenom buff management when I was mut.

Least favorite?


Even though I didnt necessarily take it as bad as I feel some did, theres a lot from Cata that I miss. Mainly, I miss the super long SnD uptime, having rupture actually be useful (It was still preferable in some situations by the last patch, not all but ignored in the middle of the expansion) as well as the ability to pick between things such as improved armor, sprint, recoup, and other things.

What should rogues be able to do better than every other class/melee?


Utility. To be able to self heal somewhat competently, toss out CCs and stuns, perform interrupts, or even off-tank if necessary. To be the jack-of-all-trades essentially.

What should rogues be able to do worse than every other class/melee?


Nothing comes strongly to mind, but I would say putting out significant amounts of damage out over time passively. With how much burst rogues can toss out, its not all that interesting to just sit there and wait for rupture/poison to drop someones health then blow everything and win.

What do you think about the class's role in PvE/PvP? If you would like to see it change, in what direction?


I think we're headed in the right direction, and just need some more tweaking. I like how we are now, and while im not against ideas like a ranged rogue spec or a tanking spec, I wouldn't want to lose what we already have to do so.

What that rogues used to have do you miss?


Legendary Daggers!

For real tho, I would say a significant presence. With how dominant it feels ranged classes are in terms of dpsing, I would like to see more desirability for rogues. In cata, this mostly came through massive numbers with the legendaries, but If we were better at raid utility (getting better now, with our own buff and everything) I think it would help.
