Problem is, it's not just deadly poison.

As combat I need to stack Dp, it's passive, but since we generally don't go near the spell hit cap, it's rough going.

Then we're getting SnD active, there's 5 cp, ~200 energy's worth, @ about 20 energy a second.
Then we've got to get rupture up. There's another 5 CP, another 200 or so energy, at the same regen rates.
Then you're watching Bandit's Guile. You can't hit Killing Spree outside deep insight without a potential dps loss. You've got to line up procs and make sure you're energy is low before you even hit KsP or it'll cap out in that 2.5sec. After getting both snd and rupture up, you'll be at about moderate insight, so give or take another 160-200 energy worth of CP builders to get to that deep insight.

With AR blown, it's about half that time frame to build insight / rupture / snd. but even with AR blown, that's still almost 15 seconds. Closer to 30 without it (truthfully more like 22-25 since we start with 100 energy ofc).

And when we want to swap targets, if redirect ain't available, we gotta start from scratch, possibly with low energy. If redirect IS available, we'll still need to reapply rupture and rebuild our rotation from there, monitoring snd/insight, and restack DP.

Now that all sounds horribly complicated - it's not - it's just REALLY tedious.

While I agree with some of what you say, you are making it seem a lot worse than it is, and some of that may be from the shoddy opening rotation you have.

Start with 1xSS > SnD > 3xSS > KsP > get 5 CPs > AR > continue normal rotation. This maximizes the use of your prepot and KsP will come off of cooldown in your next deep insight.

SnD doesn't always need to be 5cp. Hell, I use 2-3 CP SnD's all the time. Also, KsP is only a DPS loss if it's used with no insight. Try and always use KsP before AR because the amount of finishers you'll get during AR will shorten the KsP cooldown by another 40-50 seconds depending on haste.

Yes, combat needs some work and Bandit's Guile is terrible, but I'm consistently one of my guilds top DPS on fights that don't screw over melee. Also, I can't see your gear, but don't go combat until you have a 372 weapon or it will be a dps loss to Mut.
