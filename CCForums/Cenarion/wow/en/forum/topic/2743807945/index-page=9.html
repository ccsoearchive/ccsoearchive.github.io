What I like about "100% on yellow, 0% on white" is that poison procs become active content. Mut Rogues would still proc more often (ala Improved Poisons) because Mut hits with both hands. The ramp up would depend on energy regeneration and energy cost (which is why it'd have to change for Mut & Sub to something nearer Combat).

In the end though, you'd get a hit:

Ambush crit for A damage (+B instant poison damage) C total.
Backstab hit for X damage (+Y instant poison damage) Z total.
etc,

Sub and Combat could MH DP, and after 5 (including openers/finishers) they are proccing poison from their OH. If they wanted to proc poison from their OH before that, they could just Shiv. Likewise, they'd have a lot more control over which poison they procced in PvP.

It's greater player input, and greater player output.

Of course, passive (white) damage would have to be reduced to compensate. But who cares, right?

EDIT: You know, thinking more about it, that makes all poison proccing less RNG, not just OH (shiv) proccing. If DP is on your main hand, you don't have to think "now, I need at least 3 seconds to ensure a good chance of not losing the whole stack." Instead, you know you have to have enough energy for a MH attack to keep that 5 stack (or Crippling or MN or Wound debuff) up by the very last second it's on the target, and it is up to you to make sure it happens. I kinda like that too.

- It takes sooooooo long (even for 2 finishers),
- there's lots of down-time between hits,
- the damage built up to isn't "unleashed," instead it's:
- a passive (white hit) bonus and barely adequate, or
- unusable because that bonus (EA) is already applied by someone else, or
- hits as hard as ... the weak builders you used to build up, and overall it
- severely limits target swapping.

There's no reason both DP stacking and combo building can't be used while being both enjoyable and balanced. Guile probably has to go though.
