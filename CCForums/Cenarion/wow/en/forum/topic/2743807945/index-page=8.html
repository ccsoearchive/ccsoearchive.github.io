Mut and Sub energy regeneration both flatten out to 14e/s (over time).

Backstab and Hemo each award 1 combo point and cost 40/30e respectively when fully talented. Thats 1 Backstab every 2.8ish seconds (not bad) or 1 Hemo every 2.1ish seconds (better). Mut awards 2 combo points at a cost of 55e glyphed, so 1 Mut per 3.6ish seconds.

For simplicity, lets say HaT or Seal Fate/Ruthlessness procs on average once per 5pt finisher, that means 4 Backstabs (11.2s), 4 Hemos (8.4s) or 2 Mutilates (7.2s!) ... to get off a single finisher.

Here's the kicker. A Rogue needs to get off (and maintain) at least 2 finishers to maintain some sort of sustained DPS, SnD and either Rupture (Mut) or Recuperate (Sub). Between the energy regen of the GCD used on the finisher and 25e from Relentless Strikes, we'll call all finishers "1 second," again, for simplicity's sake.

So for a minimum rotation, we're looking at 24.4s for Backstab Sub, 18.8s for Hemo Sub, or 16.4s for Mut.

16.4 seconds isn't that bad ... since no one is using Expose Armor anyway. It's certainly longer than it takes for a 5 stack of DP. 18.8 and 24.4 seconds on target is harder (why are people worried about Sub sustained DPS in PvP again?), though Sub does have a lower DP proc chance.

Afterwards Mut can maintain SnD with Envenom(CttC). Sub can maintain Rupture by using Eviscerate ... but Rupture isn't in Sub's basic (2 finisher) rotation. This is definitely an issue for Sub Rogue PvE, but is not at the center of our discussion ... which centers around target swapping due to initial ramp-up.

In some ways, I've heard that Combat has the opposite problem. With their energy regeneration (RNG as it supposedly is) and comparatively low cost of Sinister Strike, they are "mashing a button every single GCD." I can't begin to write a simplification of it, and I honestly haven't tried it, so I'll take their word for it.

Either way, Bandit's Guile is the ramp-up they are complaining about. If I remember right (it's in the Combat PvP Guide), it's 4 strikes per level, and 3 levels. That's at least 12 seconds of combo point generators and finishers. Not too bad, right?

As a non-Combat Rogue, what kills me about Bandit's Guile isn't it's long ramp-up. Nor is it that it only swaps on Redirect (and then, requires a combo point to be swapped). What kills me is that it ramps back down afterwards. Combo-points/finishers don't ramp down. Deadly Poison doesn't ramp down. Why does Bandit's Guile? I mean, WHAT? Build up to ... build down?

------------

As Mut, stacking DP and throwing Envenoms is an interesting mechanic. I'm OK with it. If there was a little less time between Mutilates (ie. Mutilate cost less energy or Assassination energy regeneration was a bit faster after Overkill buff ended), it'd be a bit easier to stand combo points being built on the target. I also dislike that Mut (and Combat) doesn't have as many active talents as Sub.

As Sub (off-spec), DP isn't quite as important. I like having two "special" builders, a hard hitting positional and a bleed, and I like having active talents (Shadow Step, Prep, Premed, & Shadow Dance; even "semi-active" talents like Cheat Death, Initiative, and Find Weakness change play-style) ... the other two trees don't have as many active talents. The longer ramp-up is annoying though. I hate that many talents (Initiative, Find Weakness, Premed) are almost unavailable on long boss fights.

I don't enjoy the Combat tree (though some do), largely because I don't enjoy just standing there spamming the same button while letting the WoW server account for most of my (passive) damage. Yes, I know this is the usual critique of Mutilate ... but I use Mut to solo where it's not quite as repetitive.

Rogue IS the most enjoyable class (to me), not matter the spec. Still, I think each would be more fun if player action was optimized (maybe not "maximized" as in Combat's SS spam).
- 1-2.5ish seconds between interesting combo point builders => 10-15s for 2 finisher basic ramp-up.
- Poison procs tied to yellow attacks (100%) not white attacks (0%) => 5-10s ramp-up ^.
- White damage can be reduced appropriately.
- Kidney Shot is already on a CD and DR, it could be made more expensive if ramp-up is too short.
- SnD modifying yellow damage (changing play-style) instead of modifying white damage.
- Expose Armor useful again (lower debuff %, but stackable with other armor debuffs).
- Deadly Throw should be deadly ... or at least moderately threatening.
- Throw should be useful (and would be as an instant ... ranged CP = mobility for Mut/'bat).
- Combat needs something wittier than "Guile."
- Sub should be able to access all of it's talents, even in PvE fights.
- All three trees should be equally appealing for off-specing, both in damage and utility.

Player action and choice make for a more enjoyable game, and don't have to come at the expense of other (class/spec) players.

Combat only has energy capping issues during AR, and even then that shouldn't happen unless you get some really nice rng on combat potency.

You don't spam SS every gcd, not even close.

Factoring in a reasonable amount of haste + vitality, + Combat potency (and mastery potentially proc'ing it, although the rates become VERY small there), combat sits around 18 - 20 energy/sec.

SS costs 39 energy, RvS 40. Finishers also cost energy (although 25 refunded through RS).

12 combo builders + 2 or 3 finishers is going to take quite some time outside of AR. It's really very slow stacking. As I said, you definitely don't spam SS every gcd.

Taking the top combat rogue parse on baleroc (about the closest thing we'll see to tank and spank in firelands I'd think), in a 261 second fight, there were 120 sinister strikes and 24 RvS's. Give or take 1 every 2 seconds, that's best case scenario with restless blades chain procing due to uptime giving as much AR time as possible. That's also going to factor in some good rng, being the top parse and all, you'd expect good rng to play its part.
