Okay, for those of you who were still unable to play with the Update button giving you only an error message, I found a way to get mine to work. It may not involve all the above steps, as was the case for me. Click on the 'Options' menu under the WoW logo after you open and log into Battle.net app, then 'Scan and Repair'. Depending on your system, this might take 30 minutes or over an hour, but be patient. After the repair, my button changed from 'Update' to 'Play', and began the download immediately, no need for me to put any folders on the desktop.

After the update finally went thru, the game became playable. When I exited the game, the 6.2 pre-patch started, then the patch actually downloaded. It took about 25 minutes total, and when the game opened again, I didn't notice any of the visual problems I've seen posted in other threads. I never had to change permissions, I never had to place folders on the desktop, I never had to move or delete any files, just the scan and repair corrected it.

I don't know if this will work for others, but it's worth a shot. I tried all the other fixes several times, and after the last patch, couldn't even use the exe. file to open the game. I figured I had nothing to lose trying the repair, and it ended up working for me. I know this may not work for everyone, but for those that get the game running again, happy hunting! Or maybe I should say ship building?
