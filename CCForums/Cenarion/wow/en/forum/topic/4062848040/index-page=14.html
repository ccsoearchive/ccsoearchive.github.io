Amen to that. That's not to say that if we have concerns, we shouldn't express them, and frankly it can be pretty fun to go back and forth over the impact we think various changes might have, or how they'll play off one another, or ways in which we think those changes might be further tweaked to be even cooler, more interesting, better thought-out or more balanced (as much as we can know such a thing at this stage, anyway).

But as has been said many times in many, many, many threads, a lot of the knee-jerk negative reaction to some of these changes is the sheer fact that they're changes. They represent a departure from what we've long known our class and spec to be, or from the ways we've long expected our class/spec to perform. That kind of thing can feel very unsettling, even catastrophic. (Cataclysmic?)

The more we talk among ourselves about these changes -- and by that I mean, the more we actually listen to each other, think about what we're feeling and why, and try to actually reason through the proposed class, spec and gamewide adjustments we see being laid out before us -- the more we help Blizz developers do the same. The more we talk *at* each other, or the more we simply dismiss a change as awful without considering the context in which the change is being made, the more likely Blizz *and* our fellow rogues will be to just ignore the conversation entirely, which just clutters up the forum and doesn't help anybody.

I should edit to add that we've had a good number of threads this week that *have* been pretty awesome to read through and participate in. I mean, I can't speak for the rest of you, but some of the back-and-forth and the really thoughtful comments several folks have made in this forum have really opened up the way I think about many of the changes that have been announced, and have helped me better understand why they might work (or not), as well as what questions remain as to how these various talents, abilities, yada yada, will all come together.

This may have been the sappiest thing I've ever written on the forums.

Not to sound rude, but I don't think you guys learn from your mistakes. This sounds like Combat Maces all over again.

Being off the DR seems incredibly OP to me for a class that already has a ton of tools (stuns, silence, disorient) to deal with other classes.

It is going to create a problem because it is going to be the best poison hands down for PVP, but Rogues will pay the price of a LOSS of utility in PVP as it will actually end up taking the place of a poison we currently have that we are able to equip to our ranges wep and FoK on our target.

I do really like Shiv being viable again. My other problem with Shiv in general is it is just another way to dilute Rogue DPS in PVP. Rogues have to generate CPs to do both utility AND Dps. These both come at the expense of Energy. Energy spent towards utility is a double loss towards DPS.

For example -- to build up to a 5 sec Kidney Shot a Rogue has to spend enough energy to get 4 CPs a GCD on using KS and an additional 25 energy on top of that to use it. A Ret Pali or Arms War (Or any other class with a 5 sec stun) simply has to roll up on you in melee and instantly stun you. There's no setup. There's virtual no cost to resources for either of these classes and it doesn't burn their ability to DPS to do so.

I know comparing different classes is comparing apples and oranges. I'm not saying Rogues are handicapped in PVP. In fact, I think they are incredibly strong.

But when it comes to design -- please take into consideration that Rogues pay a huge price choosing between utility and DPS, as it costs us 2 sets of resources.

P.S. And for those of you that don't understand -- Imagine if Hammer of Justice cost 3 Holy Power and mana actually mattered. These are the choices Rogues constantly have to make.
