Not many lines on Prot warriors, but I did glean the following, as it now stands:

1) Stances may have their penalties removed, and arms will be the single target stance while fury becomes the AoE stance (prot assumedly the tanking stance). Also worthy of note is that the question asked about abilities being usable only in certain stances, and the response was "removing all stance penalties and restrictions".

My take on it? For the stances part I'd be happy if as a prot warrior I could switch to Battle stance when we tank swap or when my target dies to get a single target dps boost, or help aoe down spawns with a zerker swap (not sure if the stances are just going to boost AoE abilities vs boosting single target abilities?)

The idea that we can finally lose all the restrictions on Warrior abilities and stances (if it's not a miswording by blizzard) would have me very excited though. I've always wanted to use Execute at the end of a fight when I have nothing better to do, and throwing a hamstring in PVP would be especially nice with the recent loss of shield bash's daze.

2) Arms and Prot will get Blood and Thunder

Yay! By far the best improvement in cataclysm for us. Warriors may end up being a bit OP on AoE though if Arms can get B&T, Shockwave, cleaves, whirlwind, and so on all in one go? Probably going to have to get rid of or nerf parts of the arsenal.

3) Intercept probly gone

Meh. I'm assuming the "charge twice" talent is to make up for this, though I don't understand how the charge time reduction talent is ever going to compete?

4) Stuff like Shield Slam may generate rage, while shield block can be used no cooldown but costs rage.

Not sure about this "active mitigation" idea at all. The last thing we need is another Spammy button that we push whenever we can (Weren't we supposed to get rid of heroic strike on a 1.5 sec...oh right, %&@(*ing inner rage).

I'd be far happier with a shield block that say, gave higher amounts of mitigation for rage used, maybe with a cap of 50 or something. That way you have to use it strategically and balance it against other abilities that cost rage (Yes, I'm aware that this makes us more "Deathknighty", deal with it, other tanks have cloned all of our stuff!). I'd also like it completely unhooked with actually blocking, make block and block chance their own thing while Shield Block is a "mini shield wall" like we keep getting told.

I figure it's best to put this here.

Rage on damage taken is going away. Rage will be generated via autoattacks and some special attacks (perhaps one). I don't think it's a big leap of faith to expect that our special shares the Ret Paladin's Crusader Strike mechanic - that is, X cooldown reduced by Haste.

I can also anticipate that our Mastery will change completely. In exchange, it's not a great leap to guess that the Critical Strike rating will, for Protection Warriors and Paladins, increase our Critical Block chance (I assume Critical Block will become a universal feature of all block tanks, so that crit has it's place for a block tank).

So statistically here's a rough idea of how I think it's all going to pan out:

Hit/Exp -> resource generation (autoattacks and specials don't miss)
Haste -> resource generation (accelerate autoattacks, reduce special cooldown)
Crit -> increases critical block chance
Dodge/Parry -> as before
Mastery -> something new?

The only question is, what can crit do for DK's. And with Haste and Crit having a use for tanks is it time to simply eliminate tank plate? Perhaps Mastery could provide Dodge/Parry rating for tanks (which would be subject to diminishing returns, unlike block chance).

I don't know what's going to happen to our Block chance. I'm of the opinion it should be some low chance (say, uh 30%) with no stat you can stack to change that, but that's what I thought they'd do in Cata.. and they didn't.

eg.

Mastery: Avoidance
Your gain Dodge and Parry rating equal to your Mastery rating.

.

Consider that right now I have 3630 Mastery rating. 3630 Dodge and Parry rating is substantially more than I have now (in the ~2000 range) but thanks to diminishing returns, I have absolutely no chance of reaching unhittable, or heck, even 50% combined avoidance.

I've not been a fan of Cataclysm, for a number of reasons that I won't get into here, but I am a fan of the game and I want it to continue to succeed.

All in all, I think the expansion has potential to be good. I like the idea of basically overhauling everything and starting over, which is essentially what is happening; they're scaling stats back, reinventing the talent trees and skills, essentially scrapping everything and starting over while using the general existing structure of the game (lore, concept, etc).

I am also of the opinion that complaining about things to come in 5.0 and beyond while 4.3 is still awaiting release is extremely premature. However, I'm incredibly bored at work right now, so I've been thinking about this a bit.

All that said, here are my concerns regarding Warriors moving forward:

1: Loss of mobility.

At this point, we have Charge, which can be used in combat, and Intervene. This gives us the ability to zoom all over Hell and back, often. The way the new talents read, there is nothing that makes Charge usable in combat (Warbringer changed). If Intervene is going away as indicated, we're really getting hosed, especially considering mobility has been our single strong-point for two expansions. Now, that said, maybe they're making Charge usable in combat by default, which would make this a bit less painful. However, with the options being either a shorter cooldown on Charge (12 instead of 20 seconds) or the use-twice-before-incurring-CD, it seems like we're still losing mobility.

2: Level 90 talent choices.

I hate the fact that I've had Shockwave since the early part of Wrath, and now it's not going to be available while leveling in MoP. And then, instead of getting a cool new tanking ability at 90, we get to take...an ability we will have had for 4 years (beginning of Wrath until MoP release). The alternatives to taking Shockwave are Bladestorm (another ability that we've had for 4 years) or the single new Warrior talent, Avatar. The only reason I can see for taking Bladestorm or Avatar is for the Freedom effect, and they may be situationally better for boss tanking. Regardless, the choice we get to make at level 90 is between two talents that don't have anything to do with tanking at all and what has been a cornerstone to our AOE tanking for a long time.

All in all, I'm not thoroughly excited about the outlook for Warriors at the moment. Fortunately, with one of each tanking class, I can pick something more appealing when the time comes, but truth be told, I like my warrior, and I want to keep playing my warrior. I don't want to have to move her down to my alt list just because Blizzard fails to make the class at all interesting in MoP.

But again - WAY TOO EARLY TO SAY ANYTHING FOR CERTAIN.

/shrug
