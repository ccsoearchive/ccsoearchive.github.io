The following was originally posted by Dr. Hrook. All credit goes to him.

Here are some suggestions for ways to use these lists. Please feel free to add more.

1) Find a long lost relative - scan through the descriptions until you find a couple of people that might be a cousin or even closer - add them to your friends list, then whisper them in game ((OOC)) and see if they are looking for a relative. This may take some time - not everyone will be open to this - but when you find people that agree it can add new depth to your character.

2) Rivalries and Hunteds: Scan through the list and find people with similar dispositions, classes and levels, approach them OOC and see if they want to have a rivalry or other confrontational relationship. Why are there not more "best mages" of Azeroth competitions?

3) Lost and found: Pick a name of someone whose writing you like and send them an OOC mail that says you lost item X and they found it. Suggest that there may be a reward if they return it to you. This can be as mysterious and complicated or as plain and simple as you like (suggestion, keep it simple).

4) Recruitment: Looking to recruit quality RPers to your guild? Here is a list pre-made - lots of folks are already attached, but that doesn't mean there aren't some in there looking.

5) Apprentices and Journeymen: Perhaps you've reached 60 (85) and there's little left to learn other than what rain feels like on your skin - why not take on an apprentice? Scan the list for low seasoned characters in your class and approach them with the opportunity to learn your collected wisdom - this can be win/win (*guilty smirk* I get all of my fish scales and fish oil from students in Da Doctas).

6) (From Seodlotu) Start a cause or movement. Not a guild per say, but a group of people with similiar idealogies. (i.e. Light Fundamentalists, 100% water shamans, Scarlet Crusade sympathizers, etc etc.). Sometimes one guild cant encompass one character's entire idealogy. So perhaps similiar minds can be sought out a channel dedicated to this communication and perhaps spawn some community RP as this idea/movement takes hold.

7) Look for patterns: Many characters on the RPers lists have similar backgrounds, either intentionally or unintentionally. You could start a very simple plotline across guilds with a possibly wide audience by appealing to "Any Forsaken raised a year ago", "All those that are exalted with the Argent Dawn" or "Anyone that lost a parent to the Scourge". Who knows how many bites such a hook would get.

I am sure there are lots more ideas, so I will stop there and open the floor.

---Glippin Quite often folks ask me, Glippin, you were such an inspiring fellow; a master mage, an engineer of world renown, a commander in the elite guild, Pia Presidium. What happened? What made you turn into the dark chocolate evil soul that you have become?

Actually this question is usually posed as, Hey you raggedy gnome, get out of the gutter. What the #@%! is wrong with you? But then such nervous commentary is to be expected when average folks meet someone as famous as I.

And so to my legions of fans, I feel that I owe it to them to take full responsibility for my actions by blaming everything on my parents.

I come from a broken home. The bottom drawer of our kitchen cabinet never opened right.

My mother was a make-up artist for female dwarven !@#$ stars. As there never have been any, my father had to work extra hard to make up for the lack of income.

My father was a shoe salesman and that would have been fine had it not been for his gullible mind. He came to the conclusion that selling shoes to giant insects would be amazingly profitable since they have so many feet. And so he entered into a dark pact with the insect overlords in Silithus. It was only later that he learned that giant insects dont wear shoes. But my father always kept his word and so Im sure that he is still working hard to uphold his part of the bargain; something about convincing the Alliance to open some gate which would destroy the world in a deluge of ravenous insects. Fortunately, Im certain no one will listen to him.

So my father was not around much during my childhood. I entertained myself by running about Gnomegan mixing all the pretty colored vials of liquid that were left about in the labs. One day, however, there was an explosion and..

Um... nevermind.

But it was my grandfather that placed upon me the heavy burden of greatness. For you see, it is through him that I am connected to the Proudmoores. Jania Proudmoores 2nd cousins roommate met my grandfather while riding the tram one day and relayed to him that Janias first cousin once overheard Jania say that sometimes she wished her family were not the military leaders that they were. My grandfather grandly took up this leadership and passed it down to me since my father had run off to serve insect overlords. Therefore I am the grand general of Stormwind.

I tried to keep a low profile but eventually the pressure grew too great and I succumbed to evil chocolate temptation.

And that is my almost entirely true tale of family woe.
