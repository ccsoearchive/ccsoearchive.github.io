When you place weapons in the raids, don't place any one's class best/only weapon drop(s) on the last boss(es) of an instance. They need something in between, to keep competitive.

This kind of itemization isn't that unusual, nor is it peculiar to this situation. As others have pointed out, there are slow off-hands available which aren't broadly useful.

There's enough room in itemization for some flexibility, and that allows us to create niche items like slow off-hands or 1-handed swords with agility on occasion. Of course, if a particular item has a comparatively narrow niche, then it's more likely that fewer of those items will be introduced. Or, perhaps we'll place an item like Stormwake, the Tempest's Reach which potentially appeals to several different classes.


While I understand your point here, there is something to be said of the very limited loot tables in Firelands (there are nearly twice as many bosses in Tier11 compared to Tier12).

It's akin to how all Hunter/Feral Druid weapons this expansion and the better part of last have been Staves/Polearms. You could make a 2h Agi Sword or Mace, but there's 0 advantage to doing so over making it a Staff/Polearm, since the only effective result is to cut the number of classes that can use it in half.


I dig that there are a few items out there with limited use, but still have to be included. There needs to be tanking guns and intellect plate and so on, even if those are 1-spec exclusives. We get it. The difference here is, if there's no intellect plate for a spot, somebody has a slot they can't fill. If all of the 1h/MH agi weapons are Axes/Maces/Fists instead of Swords... nobody loses anything.

Why make the problem worse when it's totally avoidable? Why does the itemization team get that when it comes to 2h agi weapons, but not 1h ones?

First I want to say thank you for the blue. No matter what the content of it, at least it is something.

Until support for our itemization can be tied to something more than the existence of enhance, we will not be safe. The last 6 1/2 years of playing has taught me that the only way our loot tables will be reasonable is if the gear we want is also useful to more popular classes/specs. Whether this be by full stat re-forging, opening up swords, or a realignment of our core stats.

In the weapon situation, it is sad that we get painted into a corner time and time again by sparse/buried tables. When we do find ourselves in a position where we can open ourselves up to another loot table weapon set (i.e. spell power weapons) we repeatedly see them come along and shut that door in our faces. All the while we have been asking for and staring at a simple solution that is as easy as opening swords up to us or just moving away from them and going with axe/mace/fist. I know there are some who would wax nostalgic for the loss of their pretty swords, but call me selfish if I think making some one look pretty should not trump me not being able to put a proper weapon in my hands.

Now we have yet another avenue for improvement in full stat re-forging for Str Agil. With all of this out there, the ongoing lack of positive action coupled with repeated punitive reactions is just down right puzzling and feels cold.

I don’t like twisting the words of blues, but it is down right frustrating for them to use a sword in their example of their itemization that appeals to several different classes. The whole point is we have itemization issues and we have a lack of options that appeal to several different classes. When you force us into a situation where our itemization options are only attractive to us as a fraction while others enjoy much broader selection, you end up with loot tables that reflect the same. This forces us to work that much harder (RNG aside) to get what we need.

I am sorry but it is a hard thing to swallow that tier after tier I find myself staring at statistics that say I should expect to have to do wait months longer and do many times the number of runs as my piers to even get a chance at a drop simply because you won’t allow us to have access to reasonable itemization. That has nothing to do with balance or an action that hasn’t been done for other classes, what it ends up being is just plain cruel.

P.S. Quirk, it is not the data mined loot tables that worries me... it is that it doesnt tell a tale any different than the last 6 1/2 years of itemization I have experienced so why would I expect anything different? I saw literally 20 or more swords get DE'd before I managed to even get my axes and that has been the story as long as I can remember.

Heck there have been entire tiers where the best weapons I even had were the pvp axes I got to tide me over till a pve option dropped. That wasn’t for lack of pve raiding, it was for pathetic loot tables, low drop%, lack of items, buried items, and on top of all of it consistently bad RNG. That is just a silly problem to be forced to have given the alternatives.
