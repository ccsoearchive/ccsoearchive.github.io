Quickly before the 1H discussion we have 6 specs that like 2H STR weapons and they all share similar wants in speed and stats. So far all weapon types that have been itemizted between Warriors, Paladins, and DK's are perfectly shared: Swords, Maces, and Axes (there is no STR polearm). Perfect harmony and balance achieved here as well.

4 specs look for 2H AGI weapons and just like the 2H STR weapon classes, they both share similar wants in speed and stats and the same weapon types itemized are available for both of them as well: Polearms and Staffs.

There are a whopping 11 specs looking for either a Spellpower Staff or a 1H Spellpower MH/OH combo. Out out the 1Handers there are 3 possible weapon types: Daggers, Maces, and Swords and every class which can desires these can use exactly 2/3 of each of those types. Perfect harmony and balance achieved here as well with a little flair available to separate different classes.


Now we come to 1H STR Weapons...

2 Tank Specs use 1H STR weapons with tanking stats: Prot Paladins and Warriors and so far they share perfectly the same weapon classes (since there has never been a Fist tanking weapon): Swords, Maces, and Axes.

2 DPS Specs use 1H STR weapons with DPS stats: Frost DK's and SMF Warriors. SMF is already a very lowly represented % of raiding classes, but at least both specs desire the same basic design of weapon speed and stats. DK's cannot wield Fists which places them at a minor disadvantage from an itemization standpoint, but they can at least all share: Swords, Axes, and Maces. There has been 1 Fist weapon that has snubbed SMF Warriors, the OH trash BoE claw but since the MH was Agility based the overall set has been seen by all the useable specs as subpar (yay itemization team!) so I'm really not feeling like there has been that much strife in 1H DPS STR weapon land.

Non tanking 1H Strength weapon represent not a very large slice of the overall raiding structure as so far I see in 4.2 a 365 BS crafted one and 378 BoE apparent trash drop. Both DPS specs of course want 2 of these, so far it's looking to be a bit of a pain for 1H STR users as they, so far, are relying on trash drops with at least a bit of a boost through a tradeskill.

Now let's look at 1H AGI weapons...

We have 4 specs who want 1H AGI weapons: All 3 Rogue specs and Enhancement Shaman. I've left Hunters out of this discussion since there is no competitive 1H enchants so all Hunters go 2H Agility for stat sticks.

2 of those Rogue specs are Dagger based which are the only melee Dagger using specs as well, yet like all tiers since Classic WoW, it's literally swimming with Daggers. We already find 5 non-heroic mode Daggers datamined (6 if you count the new Brewfest holiday dagger): http://ptr.wowhead.com/items=2?filter=qu=4;ty=15;cr=82:123;crs=1:3;crv=40100:0#0+2+1

Yet Subtlety is a complete dead-end for raiding so all that itemization is for 1 spec of 1 class (Assassination) that literally nobody else cares about. Glad to see Blizzard still loves their Rogues...

So moving on to the non Dagger 1H Agility world we immediately have a huge disparity between the 1 remaining Rogue spec (Combat) and Enhancement (contrast this with how close Frost DK's and SMF Warriors share speed, stats, and weapon types):

Combat Rogues need a slow MH and a fast OH and Enhancement needs slow for both. Right away we have an itemization split. Now Combat Rogues can use all available AGI weapon types itemized so far: Swords, Maces, Fists, and Axes yet Shaman get 3/4: Maces, Fists, and Axes. Now let's see what we find so far in 4.2... http://ptr.wowhead.com/items=2?filter=qu=4;ty=13:0:4:7;cr=82:21;crs=1:1;crv=40100:1#0+2+1

Okay 2 slow AGI Swords, one crafted the other a holiday drop, I can accept this even though I think slow AGI Swords should exist as JP style vendor goods. And then we see 1 slow AGI Axe (good) and 2.1 speed Mace which I'm baffled as neither Rogues or Enhance would want this piece of crap; let's assume it's going to turn in to a slow AGI Mace. So the Combat Rogues and Enhancement Shaman are a little better off than Frost DK's and SMF Warriors but we are all still staring at some very poor itemization so far.

Non Dagger AGI offhands are apparently dead, so Combat is stuck with a fast Dagger OH, and now combine this with the fact that Combat generally lags behind Assassination so there is now exactly 1 spec out of 1 class who really would ever need a slow 1H AGI Sword that is not even a highly represented raiding class, and tell me really... why the hell is there still 1H AGI Swords being created. Die hard Combat Rogues can use 3 other weapons that share fine with Enhancement (except perhaps Fist weapons which have traditionally been a problem since Blizzard can't seem to fix a design flaw making them unable to be one-handed in nature due to graphical issues). What has to happen is either Shaman get the ability to use Swords or you completely STOP itemizing 1H AGI Swords there is no other logical course. IDK either way.

I sure hope there is more to be datamined as right now all 1H DPS specs, except Assassination, is shaping up to be a frustrating tier of upgrades.

Everyone saying enhancement itemization (particularly weapons) has been bad for a while (forever?) are quite correct.
The mind-boggling part of it is that it's not just a side effect of being a less-played spec now...there's really no mechanical reason for it at all.
Rogues were given axes, so more agility axes could be made...and yet, agility swords still abound. The logic given for the addition was followed for less than half an expansion, it seems.
Changing agility swords to NOT swords wouldn't have any negative impact on item usability; all it would do is help tighten up loot tables, and actually make things available for use.
Other inexplicable choices abound....Maimgor's Bite is off-hand only. Yet Soul Blade (strength based BoE sword) is one-hand. Why? What possible reason is there to limit a non-fist, non-spellpower weapon's handedness? It's one thing when it's done quite foolishly in Karazhan. Hey, that was a long time ago. We were barely out of the truly horrendous item choices so often made in vanilla. And even that was...eventually...fixed. But now? What really goes on in itemization meetings? Clearly, there's a need for an overall plan and clear vision with itemization; how things are designed, what's actually added, and where and how things are made available. Blizzard needs an Itemization Czar. Call me.
