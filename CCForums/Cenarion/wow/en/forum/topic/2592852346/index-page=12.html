Shamans should never be allowed to use swords and even daggers. Anyone who insists or asks that they should, since there are agility swords in the game, is in the wrong.

Swords just like daggers require more sophisticated combat skills, not only because they offer an extra way of inflicting damage which is through piercing but because their sharp double edge can inflict self wounds if the weapon is not used by someone who is familiar with it. And by familiar I don't mean going to the trainer and instantly learning it. No.

Axes and maces on the other hand are not that hard to master. Which is why shamans are more comfortable with using those. Shaman's combat is based around using spells and for the melee shamans the weapons serve to enhance that, hence the name enhancement. This explains why enh shamans have only two melee ability - Stormstrike and Lava lash (and thats if they choose to dual wield).

Shamans combat philosophy is about controlling the elements not mastering the weapons all the weapons. Let that be the niche for warriors and rogues.

I'm not too keen on wielding swords, to be honest.. though I don't really care about the lore, I just think adding swords to our arsenal is further degradation of the class by way of homogenization.

If they're going to go the way of full weapon availability, then they should just give in and make weapons in the old model - with attack power and the occasional damage proc. How cool was Black Bruise? ...and Raging Deathbringer? Or what about some 'old school' Syphon of the Nathrezim and Dragonstrike? Were those items really that imbalanced? Damage/Debuff procs used to be a much more prevalent system 'back in the day' - it's about time they made a return.

With the amount of loot being thrown around per boss kill and how easy normal mode raiding has become, it's nothing like in the past where you waited months to put a set of gear together. Sure, there are people with terrible luck who never see particular items drop - but doesn't that make it so much more rewarding when you get it?

It's just like when you only have one option for a piece of gear.. how gratifying is that, really? Why not just let raid bosses drop tokens instead of gear, and let us pick out what we want at a vendor? That's what raiding is coming to. Loot pinatas with gift cards instead of presents inside.

Edit: Sorry, went on an old-timer tyraid. What I was getting at was.. they should just bone up and make more items, period. Pay some needy artists to draw up some sketches and make it happen. I'd settle for something named "The Ugly Club" which just looks like a ham hock with a nail or two sticking out of the fat end... as long as it means I get a decent mace this tier.
