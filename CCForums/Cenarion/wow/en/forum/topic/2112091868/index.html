Hot Topics & Resources
====================

What are the latest things that Blizzard has said about rogues?

  • Direct communication from WoW designers on rogue issues dried up on Twitter and the official forums during the Warlords of Draenor expansion. Chatter picked up a bit starting November 2015, when the class preview for the upcoming expansion, Legion, was released.
  • When it comes to class news, Ravenholdt.net is a solid source of new information. I may be slightly biased, though -- I cofounded the site. :)
  • In addition, Ravenholdt compiles an ongoing list of rogue-related tweets from WoW designers and community managers: http://ravenholdt.net/index.php/guides/blue-rogue-official-wow-rogue-tweets-posts


Where all my guides at?

    If you're still leveling your rogue, you can't beat Aeriwen's stickied "Rogue Leveling FAQ" thread right in this here forum: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/1083749119

    If you're at Level 100, there are a few sources of pretty good rogue guides. I should know: I've written some of them. :)

  • Beginners (PvE): My Wowhead guide will get you started: http://www.wowhead.com/guide=2158
  • Also worth reading: For Assassination specifically, Jokaste's guide features extensive detail and explanations: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/9753835417

    • Intermediate-Plus (PvE): Ravenholdt's guides are helpful if you're looking to optimize your gear and DPS rotation for raiding.
      • Assassination: http://ravenholdt.net/index.php/guides/assassination
      • Combat: http://ravenholdt.net/index.php/guides/combat
      • Subtlety: http://ravenholdt.net/index.php/guides/subtlety

    • Intermediate-Plus (PvE): Icy Veins also has a quality set of guides for the raiding-inclined rogue, but be wary of the stat-weight info.
      • Assassination: http://www.icy-veins.com/wow/assassination-rogue-pve-dps-guide
      • Combat: http://www.icy-veins.com/wow/combat-rogue-pve-dps-guide
      • Subtlety: http://www.icy-veins.com/wow/subtlety-rogue-pve-dps-guide

    • PvP: Sativ has been producing a series of YouTube videos aimed at answering specific questions about various aspects of PvP in Draenor: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLBMButhAK6tt8Fo3R2E4JqD8UVKCCUiTr


    What do we know about how rogues will change in the next expansion (Legion)?

    • Quite a bit, although Legion is still only in its alpha testing phase and we are many months away from release.
    • I'll set up an FAQ of highlights as soon as I'm able; for now, I recommend browsing the recent rogue articles published on Blizzard Watch for a rundown of what we know so far: http://blizzardwatch.com/category/world-of-warcraft/rogue/


    What changed for rogues between the last expansion (Mists of Pandaria) and this one (Warlords of Draenor)?

    • In some ways, a lot, but fundamentally the class isn't much different than it was during Mists. In terms of basic mechanics, easily the biggest change is that combo points now stack on us, not on our targets.
    • The best overall summary around is on Wowhead: http://www.wowhead.com/guide=2289/warlords-of-draenor-rogue-changes
    • A quicker FAQ aimed at folks concerned about Level 100 is on Ravenholdt: http://ravenholdt.net/index.php/blogs/roguechat/47-warlords-rogue-changes-faq

PvE at Level 100: Basic Questions and Answers
=======================================

I'll keep this small to start, and I'll add stuff as I spot new questions that feel common or relevant enough. (And as time allows. :) ) Please nominate questions you'd like to see added!

How are rogues looking in end-game PvE?

    In raids, they're looking quite strong overall. At least one rogue spec averages at or near the top of DPS charts in most Hellfire Citadel raid fights.

    Generally speaking, a person who can play their rogue well, regardless of spec, should find themselves around the middle of the pack at the very least.

    That said, remember that mileage will vary. Averages are just that; from raid to raid, and from fight to fight, you personally may do extremely well or extremely badly depending on a whole host of factors, including how well you're geared, how well you play, what your raid group's strategy is, how much experience you have on that raid fight, what spec you choose, and so on.


What's the best spec for raiding (in terms of DPS)?

    Spec choice really doesn't matter too much unless you're in a competitive raid group (which usually means you're running Mythic-difficulty raids) that pushes people to deal the most damage they can. However, of the three rogue specs,
    • Assassination is an all-around safe bet, particularly on single-target fights. It will often pull slightly worse DPS numbers compared to Subtlety, but if played well it holds its own compared to other DPS classes.
    • Combat tends to do best on fights requiring a lot of multi-target cleave damage. In Hellfire Citadel, that primarily means Hellfire Assault, Shadow-Lord Iskar and Xhul'horac. In Blackrock Foundry, I'm talking about Beastlord Darmac, Blast Furnace, Iron Maidens and Operator Thogar.
    • Subtlety tends to do best on single-target fights, including the majority of encounters in Hellfire Citadel and fights like Blackhand, Gruul, Kromog and Oregorger in Blackrock Foundry.

    All three specs are usually within about 10% of one another in terms of performance, though, so you'll usually be OK whichever one you choose.


How about dungeons?

    Unless you're doing Challenge Modes, it really doesn't matter. All three specs are strong enough that any should do well in dungeons -- even heroic dungeons.

    If all that matters to you is looking good on DPS meters while killing trash... well, firstly, stop being so vain and petty, that kind of thing doesn't matter and doesn't make you more likely to be respected by others. But Combat with Blade Flurry on will usually rack up the highest numbers whenever you're fighting several non-boss mobs at once.


Proving Grounds?

    In my experience, the easiest way to get silver and gold is to go with Combat, since it's the simplest way to cleave (i.e., damage several targets at once). With practice, though, It's achievable with any spec.


What if I'm just questing or killing stuff by myself?

    Depends a little on what you're trying to do. Combat is great for going toe-to-toe with two or more mobs at the same time, especially when you can use Killing Spree and Adrenaline Rush for huge bursts of damage. Assassination and Subtlety are probably about equally strong at fighting a single target (Assassination may even pull ahead since you can't usually Backstab things when you're playing by yourself), so I'd tend to go with those if you're going to mess with elite mobs with a lot of health.


What weapons should I use?

    For Assassination, it's always daggers in both hands -- that's an absolute requirement.

    For Combat, it's generally best to have a "slow weapon" -- e.g., one-handed axes, fist weapons, maces or swords -- in each hand. (If you played Combat in Mists, you'll remember that daggers were almost interchangeable with slow weapons in your off-hand throughout much of the expansion. That's not really the case anymore in Warlords.)

    For Subtlety, it's best to have a dagger in both hands. If you have only one decent dagger, though, put your slow weapon in the *off-hand* when you raid -- you'll lose some poison damage, but because you can only Backstab with a dagger (and Backstab is much stronger than Hemorrhage), you want to keep a dagger in your main-hand if it's at all possible.


What stats do I care about?

    Always make sure to use gear with agility on it; that's always your most important stat.

    Among the secondary stats -- critical strike (crit), haste, mastery, multistrike and versatility -- all of them are good, but some are better than others depending on your spec and the gear your rogue is wearing.

    Use ShadowCraft -- http://shadowcraft.mmo-mumble.com -- to find out which stats are currently the most important for your rogue.

    For the most part, the "best" secondary stats are mastery or crit for Assassination, haste for Combat and multistrike for Subtlety. But again, use ShadowCraft to determine the best stats for your rogue at the moment -- and to help decide whether new gear drops are upgrades or not, as well as which enchants and gems are best to use.

    If you switch specs often and don't want to set up different sets of gear, consider going with multistrike for your enchants and gems. It's the most solid secondary stat across the three specs, whereas other secondary stats tend to be relatively weak for at least one spec.
