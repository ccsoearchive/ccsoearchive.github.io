Aloha, Hasäshi! Your English is much better than you think it is (in fact, it seems to be better -- at least when you're typing -- than many native-English speakers I've seen in the forums); I had no trouble at all understanding your question.

That said, I don't have a great answer for you, since 1) I personally avoid PvP because it terrifies me and 2) it may still be a little too early in the new arena season to say how strong Combat is looking. There was definitely a lot of buzz, in the weeks before Patch 5.4 went live, that Combat was likely to make a comeback (see http://lastrogue.com/combat-returns/, for example), but I haven’t done a whole lot of research yet into how much that prediction is coming true.

You talk about burst as though it’s a bad thing, but keep in mind that almost all competitive PvP revolves around making strategic use of burst to get a kill (and a win). You don’t often see high-end PvP in which the approach is to slowly wear down a team over time through sustained damage -- although good sustained damage can make it easier to set up a kill with burst. All rogue specs are capable of putting out light-to-moderate amounts of sustained damage in PvP (though arguably Assassination is best at this) and a significant amount of burst (though arguably Assassination is worst at this, which offsets its strength at sustained damage). The real question is: What do you want to do with your rogue in PvP? What role do you want to fill, and what goals do you want to achieve?

(If you'd like the opinions of folks other than lil' old me, you may want to start a new thread in this forum, or read/post to an existing thread like http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/10014141475 where discussions about Combat PvP are already ongoing. I don't think many of the people who visit this forum regularly read what people post in stickied threads like this one. Either that or they assume that the questions people ask are meant to be answered by the person who started the thread, and thus they stay silent.)

I like your approach. This is thing with PvP (and to an extent, with PvE as well): This game is big enough, and has enough variety, that you can pretty much play however the heck you want to play and still get a lot of enjoyment out of it -- and, just as important, you can do so without making other people unhappy in the process.

If you want to play highly competitive PvP, yes, your options become pretty limited -- the same goes for if you want to be part of a high-end raid group or push for records on challenge-mode dungeons or solo old raids efficiently. But if your goal is just to go out there and have fun playing the game? A lot of our "rules" go right out the window.

There's always more you can learn and always ways you can improve. But the most important thing is always to make sure you're having a good time -- and that your good time isn't coming at the expense of others. Sounds like you're doing that, so keep it up!

ShadowCraft can be trusted on this -- at least as much as (probably more than) any other tool can be.

The 2pT16 does increase the value of crit for Assassination, because it makes Seal Fate more important.

Keep an eye on the actual DPS numbers ShadowCraft is showing you, though. The difference in the amount of damage you deal when you're reforging for mastery instead of reforging for crit (or haste, for that matter) is so small that you probably won't even notice it. So while doing the mathemetically right thing is probably good, it ain't like your DPS will tank because you choose to go another route.

I've been away (RL stuffies) for a good while, so to those of you who posted in July: I apologize for my callous neglect.

Not sure how relevant deep-in-the-weeds answers are given how much time as passed since you guys asked your questions (and since you may not return to see this reply), so I'm gonna answer generally:

07/19/2014 04:56 PMPosted by Toxicsmog
at what time do i switch out 4 pieces?

When/wether to replace tier gear with Heroic Warforged gear (and thus break up a set bonus) is a decision best made with the help of ShadowCraft (shadowcraft.mmo-mumble.com). It's gonna depend on spec, on what else you've got equipped (and how your stat weights shake out), on how much you've spent on gear upgrades -- on a number of factors that make it a frustrating hassle (at best) to try to napkin-math or guesstimate it. Plug yourself into ShadowCraft, start swapping out pieces and experimenting.

I've got an overview of ShadowCraft here for the uninitiated: http://wow.joystiq.com/2014/02/05/encrypted-text-shadowcraft-a-raiding-rogues-best-friend/

And Aeriwen made a tutorial vid a good while back that's still highly useful: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2wSvib0aCEY
