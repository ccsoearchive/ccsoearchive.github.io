It's really like writing a novel...

You can have critical success and poor sales... AWESOME! You're a published author, but no publishing house wants to take a chance on publishing your next novel because you're not profitable.

You can have fantastic sales and the critics can think you're a hack... Awesome, you just made a lot of money and publishing houses are beating down your door to publish your next book, even though its about hamsters saving the world from giant robots from outer space.

In the first example, you're staying true to your ability, sales be damned... but what good is all that talent if no one ever realizes it exists?

In the second example, you're making oodles of money, and get to keep trying to write that piece of Great American Literature you know is inside you.

I'd imagine most people would prefer the latter if it means having the money to do what you really want. Choosing the former is just bad business sense being dictated by ego.
