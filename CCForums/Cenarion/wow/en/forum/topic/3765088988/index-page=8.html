I think the key that is missing from this discussion is that a lot of players live vicariously through the best guilds in the world.

Just because a few of the top guilds in the world run roughshod through content doesn't mean the content is easy. They spend hours and hours of time trying new tactics, gearing up, and failing miserably. Then, after all that time they get it right and things click. Boom goes the dynamite. Less than one percent of all guilds represent this level of hard core.

There are few fundamental truths to all of this in my opinion:

1. There are likely very few bosses that the developers could put out there that the Blood Legions, KIN Raiders (congrats on DW btw), and Paragons of the world wouldn't dispatch of quickly.
2. The bosses that are "impossible" are likely the result of a) bugs that prevent the boss from being killable b) over tuning or bosses that are mathematically impossible to kill with the gear/skills available c) designed to be impossible. Let's just say very few are every designed to be unkillable. The whole point of the content being released is that it's killable...which leads me to my next point.

Why does a hardmode have to exist in a vacuum?

What does LFR take away from a hard mode guild? Nothing. All it does is provide players an extra option to help spread the content out. As someone who played back in Vanilla, I can tell you LFR is a godsend that we should all be thankful for. Content back then was extraordinarily exclusive (more because of the logistics and time involved in setting up 40 players, but there were hard bosses too) so much so that it was almost laughable. Know how many guilds killed the original version of Kel'Thuzad? Not many at all. Know how many killed the 4 Horsemen? Not many at all.

So my advice would be for the community to stop looking towards top guilds as the example that sets the stage for the rest of us. Those guilds represent the razors edge of talent and coordination. It's almost the equivalent of saying a bronze league player in Star Craft 2 should be held to the same standard as a Grand Master GSL player. None of us are GSL players, haha.

Just because one exists doesn't mean the other can't.
