In this case I wouldn't call that bad person "bad", rather I'd say they're unintelligent. Of course, unintelligent people are probably bad at this game so I guess in a way they're synonymous.

My personal differentiation between a bad and a casual is that a bad player is the person who can't follow mechanics on fights and don't understand their class at all. A casual is a person who is competent for the most part but isn't talented at the game.

A bad in my opinion is a person who has an extremely slow learning curve or is too arrogant to listen to peers who give them advice. Bad's are the group of people who screw up on a mechanic/mechanics in a fight even though they've already done the fight and wiped multiple times.

Bad's stand in fire and die, casuals move out of the fire.

In Arathi Basin, bad's who are defending a flag will attack a player while ignoring an opposing player who is capping the flag. A casual will focus less on killing, and more on preventing the player from capping the flag. If one of your allies is capping a flag, a bad won't try and prevent an opposing player from interrupting the capping of a flag. A casual will run up to the opposing player and instead of trying to kill them, they will CC the player as much as possible until the flag has been capped.

Bad's think DPS is much more important than following the mechanics of a fight. Casuals think following the mechanics of a fight is more crucial than doing as much DPS as possible.

Bad's only think of themselves in a raid, for example a bad would stand in fire while they're DPSing because they only worry about themselves and doing as much DPS as possible. A casual thinks of the entire raid and understands that if they move out of the fire, they may lose an opportunity to do damage, but despite this, they will save themselves in the long run and they will allow the healer to focus on damage that isn't preventable. Therefore even though the casual may appear less competent than the bad on DPS charts, the casual is actually a better player because they care about the entire team rather than a stupid chart.

Of course, lots of what I've said goes hand in hand of what you said about bads and casuals. Bad's care about appearance, casuals care more about substance. Bad's care more about appearing good through higher ilevels, higher DPS, etc, etc. Casuals care more about wearing gear that actually benefits them the most, doing the objectives and mechanics, and thinking about the team as a whole rather than just themselves individually.

That's just my opinion though.
