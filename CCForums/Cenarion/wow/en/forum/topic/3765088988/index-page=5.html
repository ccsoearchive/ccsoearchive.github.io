Are you a hardcore player? Awesome, do heroic modes.

I've played since Vanilla, and I've never seen this friendly community you keep talking about. All I remember is waiting 2 hours or more just to find 4 other people willing to do a 5 man with me (and usually, it was 3 people because I did then with my brother). That was just a 5 man. It could take forever to get people for raids. Even in my guilds that I was in, we wouldn't always have all the players we needed and so we had to pug. Very rarely was there ever a person I found helpful and enjoyable enough to add to my friends list, I pugged most of the time anyway for 5 mans and heroics and then I never saw that person again.

WoW never really had a good community, at all. But the point is, if you don't want to use the LFR tool, don't use it. Just because it's not your cup of tea doesn't mean that it's ruining the game. All it means is that you can do it your way and the people who want to do it their way can do it. I raided in BC for quite a long time. We were one of the fewguilds on our server working our way through Black Temple, and we still never stepped foot into Sunwell Plateau or even downed Illidan (we ended up getting stuck on RoS). Why would Blizzard want to waste so much time designing raids and encounters when less than 5% of the player base even sees it? That's a complete waste of resources. The LFR lets people say that they killed Deathwing, even if it was an easier version.
