As much as I dread stepping into one of these "casual vs. hardcore" arguments, the OP is overlooking a pretty simple correlation:

1. TBC was more casual friendly and story-driven than Vanilla - subscriptions went up

2. Wrath was more casual friendly and story-driven than TBC - subscriptions went up even more

3. Blizzard unfortunately listened to a very vocal minority during Wrath that complained things were "too easy" and decided for Cataclysm to make the dungeons and raids less casual-friendly with an expansion lacking in story.

Result: Blizzard lost subscriptions during the first three months after launch and continued to lose subscriptions. That had never happened before — you gain subscriptions after the launch of an expansion and typically lose them the following year. This meant something was wrong with the expansion from the get-go for many players.

Having the game present different difficulty levels, LFR, normal, and heroic or whatever doesn't mean the game is "too easy." It means that players with different play styles and time frames can play the way they want. By accommodating different types of players, Blizzard is doing the right thing for its player base.
