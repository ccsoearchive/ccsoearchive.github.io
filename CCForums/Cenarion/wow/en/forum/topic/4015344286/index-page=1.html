Tap.

Tap. Tap.

Taptaptap.

A blue troll, Xenarr, leaned aside on an oversized silithid which chittered and shifted its weight, the insectoid creature agitated. It hated the damp chill of eastern Lordaeron. It hated Alliance. It particularly hated when the troll tapped on top of its shell in abject boredom.

"One dead an' they go home?" A harsh voice, Lightsworn's, crackled from a gadget held in the troll's hand.

"I certainly hope not." The more refined, subtly Dalaran accented tones of Archmage Zharikov answered.

The troll himself spoke up in a snickering growl, flicking a switch back and forth on the device. "Doubt it."

He hardly cared enough about some abandoned island to really have his interest piqued by sniping poorly organized wolves in the woods. It was for posterity. Assaults against Horde territory could simply not be allowed to go unanswered. Announced attacks near a major city were to be dealt with swiftly, brutally, and with cunning efficiency.

While the would-be attackers bored him nearly to tears, listening to the clipped communication of his own comrades grew entertaining. Concise, short, and organized, they called locations and swept out to follow targets of interest. It started to become a game. Found-one-got-one.

"Bad positionin'. They're surrounded, an' we have th' high ground. Poor plannin'." Arothand Lightsworn's observations were honestly right on the mark, when he had a moment free to make them. He stood on a paraphet directly ahead of the blue troll, peering down over the edge of keep's wall to where one of the bodies had fallen.

Xenarr shifted his weight against the silithid, half lounging himself onto it. It hissed in agitation again, clawed, scythe-like limbs clicking against the stone of the rampart as it pushed back against the added load. "I'm thinkin' these fellas ain't gonna get very far. Dunno why 'Liance gather up here. Awful location."

Next it was Raoul's turn to sound bored, even as flashes of magic lit up one of the walls on the far side of Fenris Keep. Leave it to an Archmage of the Kirin Tor to sound perfectly composed even while calling fire down on an enemy. "You would think they would learn to stop launching attacks from here." There was a soft 'tsk' from the man over that communication line, and the slightest pause as flickering flames again leapt from the other side of the wall. "Not very creative."

Eventually Xenarr moved to join in the fun, but by far and large kept his distance, using the opportunity to just be an irritation. Silithid in someone's face here, an annoying block of ice there. Barely any need to go out of his way, in as much as the others had it so easily handled. It was better that way, anyway, as far as the hunter was concerned. His reactions were not half so quick as they used to be, and years were gradually starting to catch up on him.

Still. It was fun in the end.

More amusing yet when someone got the wise idea that, if only to add insult to injury, some of the illusions that the Archmage wove be drawn in the striking images of notable Gilneans who were taken by the Forsaken. What better way to drive in the lesson that the wolves would never have a chance at putting any true scratches into Lordaeron's outlier territories? Such satirical jabbing apparently did not go unnoticed, either.

That was it.


That -paladin-.


Despite all his time working with the Crusade, the very sight of that !@#$% - especially in his Dark Lady's lands - made Arothand seethe, fists clenching, teeth long ago sharpened by cannibalistic tendencies tearing at his lower lip.


The taste of blood, after so many months of swearing off his old habits, was the last straw. Arothand all but shot off of his black drake, letting loose a carnal roar as he flew onto the docks.


He vaguely heard Raoul call after him - that accent was almost as hard to miss as his own -, but even the voice of his old friend did not soothe him at this point. There was only one target. The old teachings of undeath were kicking in.

Disarm.

Maim.

Kill.


Reactions proved automatic for Arothand. He did not even take notice of the others wailing on his armor, tearing at him as he all but flung himself at the Paladin.


At some point, he was fairly certain the other Razortalons landed, picking the others off of him before they inflicted too much damage.

That hardly mattered, however. He struck until she fell, and then he struck again, and again, and again. It took every ounce of self control he had to pull himself away from ripping into flesh with his teeth - that, and a good ounce of help from the Archmage Zharikov, shooshing and patting the man.


Everything after that was automatic. A blur. More corpses, more blood. Nothing noteworthy.

Eventually, Arothand took a moment to rest atop the hill next to Xenarr. This new training tired him out quickly, and for the first time in years, he needed to stop to catch his - very metaphorical - breath.

The older Troll had been taking the higher ground throughout the entire course of all the fighting, and while Aro had not exactly been in the mindset to pay attention, the hunter did not seem to be doing much.

From the way the man drummed his fingers along the top of his knee-padding, as he boredly tossed a frost trap at a fleeing Dwarf, his assumption was probably fairly accurate.


"'Ow many is that, Lightsworn?" The blue hunter half-crooned, half-chuckled, watching the frozen Dwarf with a distinct pride. He even went so far as to mumble something about a 'good shot' beneath his breath.


"Thirteen, sir. Countin' those who have ressurected once, na' countin' those who have come back more." Aro muttered in return, lifting a hand to rub at his face, as if trying to clear a migraine. He turned to watch the Dwarf as well, grunting. "Let 'em go."


"Wot? Why?" In an oddly catlike motion, the Troll tipped his head to the side, scratching at his chin.


"Wildhammer. Explains the stubborn-^-* behavior. Keeps on attacking us no matter how many times we kick his !@#. Also, he's usin' th' spirits. Let 'em go."


Shrugging, Xenarr gave the order over the Razortalons' comms to let the Dwarf flee with the battered remnants of his companions. Arothand nodded in approval, letting out a final, barked roar at the fleeing Alliance.

"VICTORY FOR SYLVANAS!"
