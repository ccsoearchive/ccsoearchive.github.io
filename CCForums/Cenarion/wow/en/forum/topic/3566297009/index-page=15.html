Okay, ignoring Mastery for a bit (since it doesn't apply pre 80 and getting a big overhalll) let's look at potential Mists tanking stats.

Dodge, Parry, Expertise, Hit
Sort of Strength (it gives a little parry plus threat)

For the sake of making things a little easier to look at, let's throw out some made up numbers and scaling. We have no idea how things will be balanced. For all any of us know I could be right.

Let's say that Expertise (up to soft cap) overtakes Dodge and Parry when they hit around 12% due to diminishing returns. Hit overtakes Dodge/Parry sometime later, like around 14%. The numbers might be lower, higher, heck I have no idea.

Yes, I know we should be looking at ratings and not percent, but I'm trying to keep it simple.

What does this mean for low level tanks without heirlooms?

Dodge and Parry are probably going to be better for you anyway. You get that first "of the Champion" item, it's an upgrade!

So, what about heirloom tanks that already have a good chunk of avoidance? Dodge and parry items won't be far behind hit / expertise.

If you get a hit or expertise item somewhere in late vanilla / BC, it may not as good for survival as avoidance but it's not far behind either, depending on gear. It also has the benefit of giving you more dps. Let's face it, for sub current expansion dungeons a tank is really just a sturdy dpser.

If AM is required to tank a 5 man, and I'm not sure that it will be, the real danger will be leveling tanks getting tossed in a random with orange or red level bosses. Thanks to vengeance and 500% threat I could still hold agro, but the misses ...
