Unfortunately that's just how things go. Each team and group has assigned tasks and goals to meet while having creative freedom (of varying degrees) to achieve said goals and tasks in a timely manner. How these tasks are assigned comes from a number of meetings and discussions as to how to allocate resources and time and what would be the best direction to go with this and that and no no no the forums have been erupting over THIS thing so we should... naw... and so on.

The management have to distill the billion good ideas into the 50 good ideas Blizzard is going to actually do and then set people to work on them. Sometimes an idea goes south... not because the idea was bad but because the idea was a lot harder to implement than initially expected and the 5 people assigned just couldn't make it work very well... oh well keep at it and we'll polish it more down the line since we've already invested 4 months of time into it.

I guarantee you every single artist and developer and staff member at Blizzard has some pet idea or project in the back of their mind that if given 100% absolute freedom and no deadline to finish... they'd drop what they're doing and work on it. Would these ideas make WoW better overall? Probably. Would they get the most bang for their buck doing so and would everyone agree with 9 months spent polishing up X? Probably not. The point being, if Blizzard staff probably has good ideas gone unused but has to bite the bullet, it is no wonder we the players with no sense of development time or effort feel the same way about our own personal wants.

They will never be able to fully please themselves, much less us, when deciding on what to put effort into and what to NOT put effort into. That's just the nature of limited resources and judgment calls that don't have a right answer.
