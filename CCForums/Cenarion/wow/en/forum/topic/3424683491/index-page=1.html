This is a general write-up on each talent and what I see as being the purpose of
that talents for PvP PvE, and general purposes.

If I missed anything, please tell me - This is a work in progress.

Also, many people have complained about the lack of "PvE talents," which I take to mean direct dps increasing abilities. I say they are wrong to complain...

The problem with direct dps increasing talents is that it is impossible to have the overall damage of the talents be the same for any spec. (potentially you could do it with one spec, but it would be very hard and potentially make it OP for other specs) This means there is literally no choice in, for example, the third tier, unless you are OK with playing suboptimally, which is not what PvE is about.
Instead, by having the talents mainly focused on utility, a rogue will be able to customize much better, without having to deal with suboptimal and therefore evil playstyle.

Consider the current trees (PvE only): Assassination has 1 free talents, Combat has 4, and sub has -1. Assassination's talents is generally obvious, and combat's...well that isn't bad. Still though, if you want to maximize both dps and choices, you are playing combat spec and get a total of two (all utility talents are 2 pts) choices.

Now with the new system, there *should* be more choices involved. Even if there are clearly optimal talents (especially in the last tier), you will have at least one choice in most tiers. Say I wanted to have a sub pve build that I could also solo old content with...I could fairly easily without killing my dps (though backstab would make that a bad idea).

As far as the usual dps increasing talents that we never really had a choice for, we WILL get those as we level. So, does filling in a box that you have to fill in really make you happy? In my opinion, the standardization of most pve abilities is simiply the removal of an illusion of choice.


Tier 1:

Nightstalker: +20% stealthed movement speed.
PvP: Good for catching up to people running away (knowingly or not).
PvE: Gives a movements speed boost for vanish (making it useful if you need to actually run to a mob), and makes getting to a boss/mob faster (therefore more dps). Especially useful for bosses/mobs that see through stealth.

Subterfuge: Stealth breaks 3 sec after dealing/taking damage.
PvP: More opening damage, amazing for ganking.
PvE: More openers! Especially good for subtlety.
General: Vanishing gives more time before being seen, maybe giving time for find cover or get to the mob if you got AoE'd on.

Shadow Focus: Abilities don't cost energy while stealthed.
PvP: Slightly more damage from stealth.
PvE: Slightly more damage from stealth.

Tier 2:

Deadly Throw: Finisher: Deals damage per CP and interrupts the target for 6 sec. Also 50% movement speed reduction for 6 sec.
PvP: Ranged snare and nothing aside from...
PvE: A tiny bit of damage and a...
General: RANGED INTERRUPT!!!! (especially amazing for sub, or if you have Vendetta up)

Nerve Strike: Successful KS and CSs reduce damage dealt by the enemy by 50% for 6 sec after the stun fades.
PvP: Reduces damage taken, allows you to train on an enemy while they are stunned without (ok, with less) fear of being clobbered once it ends.
PvE: --
General: Good for killing lots of mobs with minimal heals.

Combat Readiness: Same as current version: Reduced damage taken by 10% per stack, starts with 1 stack, goes up to 5 stacks, gain 1 stack when hit by a physical blow. Lasts 20 sec but if you don't get a stack/refresh for 10 sec, it's over.
PvP: Increases survivability against physical damage.
PvE: ^
General: ^

Tier 3:

Cheat Death: Same as current version: Instead of getting killed, you go to 10% HP. Damage reduced by 80% for 3 sec. 1.5 min CD.
PvP: One last chance to kill an enemy before you die, or run away.
PvE: Free life every 1.5 mins, which translates to more uptime if you mess up.
General: --

Leeching Poison: New poison: Creates a debuff that gives you HP equal to 10% of damage dealt.
PvP: More living.
PvE: More living, helps healers and therefore raid on healer-intensive fights. Might save you, meaning more uptime. Could actually be used if the duration is long enough that wep-swapping and shiving it on is worth it.
General: Yay for more life!

Imp Recup: Same as current version: +1% of your max hp per tick, and reduces damage taken by 6%.
PvP: More living.
PvE: Slightly more living, reduced strain on healers meaning generally better for raids.
General: More living.

Tier 4:

Preparation: Finishes CD on Sprint, Vanish, CoS, Evasion, Dismantle, and Smokebomb. 5 min CD.
PvP: Extra of all the abilities that are useful in PvP, which is every one of those.
PvE: Extra of all the abilities that are useful in PvE, which is sprint (uptime) vanish (damage for assassination and sub), and CoS (survivability, aka uptime)
General: Reduced repair costs and increased runspeed indoors!

Shadowstep: Teleport behind the target. 25 yrd range, 24 sec CD.
PvP: Mobility!!!
PvE: Increased uptime (aka damage).
General: Increase to sexyness, can avoid fall damage (with proper use of mad skills)

Burst of Speed: +70% movement speed for 4 secs, removes and grants immunity to movement impairing effects for its duration. 60 energy, No CD.
PvP: Mobility!!!
PvE: Increased uptime at the cost of energy that may be useable in that uptime.
General: Increased runspeed indoors.


Tier 5:

Deadly Brew: When you apply Instant, Ming-Numbing, or Wound Poison to your target, Crippling Poison is also applied.
PvP: Control.
PvE: --
General: Easier to kite without sacrificing damage. Too bad it doesn't work on bosses.

Paralytic Poison: New Poison: Chance to apply PP to the target. When it reaches 5 stacks, stuns target for 4 sec.
PvP: Even more stuns! (do they share DR with normal stuns? Probably not. How often will it occur? TBD!)
PvE: --
General: Could be useful if you want a survivability spec against stunable mobs.

Dirty Tricks: Gouge and Blind no longer are broken by the rogue's bleeds and poisons.
PvP: Much increased control if you are using those types of abilities.
PvE: Can use blind without it breaking from our DoTs...
General: Helps kiting mobs that are blind/gougeable.


Tier 6:

Killing Spree: Hit an enemy within 10 yrds dealing damage and givign you 5 CP. You can use this as often as you'd like within 5 sec after you start. 2 min CD.
PvP: Damage, and teleportation/sticking to one guy if you are slowed/rooted.
PvE: Damage (instant and finisher), and staying behind/with the mob your annoying tank is kiting/rotating for no apparent reason.
General: --

Shadow Dance: Same as current version: Can use stealth-only abilities outside of stealth for 6 sec. 1 min CD.
PvP: Damage, control in the form of CS, silence in the form of garrote.
PvE: Damage, especially for sub.
General: Disarm trap, pick pockets if you didn't get to open.

Vendetta: Puts a debuff on the target, increasing damage dealt to it by 20% and allowing use of Vengeful Strike for 15 sec. 30 yrd range, 2 min CD
Vengeful Strike: Deals damage and generates 1 CP. 30 energy, 30 yard range.
PvP: Ranged Damage? No WAY! Also general dps increase.
PvE: ^
General: Wait, really? Ranged damage? Whoh...

Rindairo put that wonderfully. There are two ways you can look at this as a raider:

1) Damnit, Blizz made basically all my talents pointless!

2) Awesome, Blizz made basically all my talents pointless!

This idea feels like the closest one I've ever seen to achieving the goal Blizz has said it wished it could have achieved with talents from the beginning: Have them not feel like a required part of creating an "optimal" rogue (or whatever class).

They're trying to take all of the talents that, for so long now, a raider would be labeled an idiot for not taking, and just bake them all right into the class/spec. What's left is, by definition, fluff. A person can judge that harshly if they choose; I prefer to look at it the way Hail did above, by being happy that -- again, if this actually works out the way Blizz envisions it -- I'll have one less set of icons I need to click not because I had a choice, but because I felt I had to do it or I was gimping my raid.

And, as Rindario and others above have noted, even if you're a raider there are other parts of this game to play. There will be even more of them with the new expansion. And these talents could prove pretty useful -- even, dare I say it, FUN? -- to have for those purposes.
