Does it really matter how much or how little someone roleplays? I would assume we're all here for the benefits of the realm type. I do not see how someone - despite not engaging in the same type of activity as another - could possibly negatively impact another should their paths never cross.


Actually, yes. Having the heavy, medium, casual, and non designations really does make finding RP akin to your playstyle much easier.

A casual RPer may not like the depth that a heavy RPer expects from an interaction, and on the flip-side, a heavy RPer may not want to spend weeks chatting on-and-off with someone and building a storyline just to learn that they are a casual RPer who secretly plays an evil dragon. (DISCLAIMER: Not to say all casual RPers or even most are terrible. Just that a heavy expects more lore-depth than a casual would.)

It's only when someone turns the designations into a slur that things go South.
"Casuals are ruining this server!"
"Heavy RPers are nerds with no lives!"
"Non-RPers should all just leave!"

None of those are acceptable, under any circumstance.
