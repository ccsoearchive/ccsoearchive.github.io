Here is my attempt to finish this (which I know will fail horribly and only spark a new rage, but hey, gotta take a stab at it. =/):

Non-roleplayers do not get "support" on this realm. If you need support, there are literally dozens of other servers that will fit just fine with your tastes.

*That said*, however, as far as I am concerned, you are welcome to stay so long as you keep the disruption to the server's purpose to a minimum. If you disrupt roleplay? No, get out. End of story. If you are polite, respect the rules, and actually contribute to the community? Fine, welcome aboard. Please, just understand that you really are the minority when it comes to the server. Enjoy the community, make friends, but don't start believing that this server has any higher priority than RP.

And for the love of the gods, please do not start pointing fingers at the roleplayers for problems. This "roleplayers are big drama queens" fad needs to die. Every single human-gathering has drama. My high-school had cliques that would ignore you for weeks on end if you so much as smirked in their general direction (not an exaggeration.), and people were constantly shunned from their own groups for the smallest of infractions. My college had fraternities and sororities, and if you were not a part of them, good luck finding friends or even a study group.

Even BOOT CAMP was a big drama fest. I was very close to several of the other women there, and only halfway through the program did I find out my "best friend" had been bashing me behind my back the entire time. This is NOT exclusive, or even prevalent in roleplayers. It is prevalent in humanity.

When presented with hostility, from roleplayers or nonroleplayers, do what I did all those times. Shut up, smile, and walk away. Find someone who you can tolerate instead of causing MORE DRAMA about the drama!
