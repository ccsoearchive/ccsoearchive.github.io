I don't like that there are folks (including one in this topic) who diss roleplayers on their own server, and make them the cause of everything wrong with the community, when it's roleplayers who are the backbone of the community. We made it what it is, and that inspired non-RPers to come seek us out.

That doesn't mean non-RPers aren't welcome, because new roleplayers have to come from somewhere.

My view on it is pretty much this: There are some really cool people who've showed up who aren't RPers. But if you come here, why NOT give RP a try? I'm not talking big, complicated events, or scenes that last all day. You're playing a character-- who are they? What's their story? Short and simple? Fine, not everyone has to have a novel of angst. EMote at folks-- if someone salutes you or waves or talks to you, answer back instead of just saying, 'I don't RP'! Even if it's the most casual of roleplay, the thinnest of IC veneers, why not give it a shot? Even if it's just a few lines of banter back and forth, why not answer like your character would?

Every little bit adds up to the theme, immersion, lore, and feeling of the server as a living community. I don't often have long RP scenes, but if someone RPs at me, even just a line or two, I answer in character before I'm off to the next BG.

"Well, I don't want to!" Well, some people say that because they blank. But the ones who are saying it to be contrary -- and folks know who they are -- what's the point? Chill out, relax, RPers are not your enemy. We just think WoW is awesome!

I, personally, prefer some level of literacy if people RP at me. It doesn't have to be novels, it doesn't have to be ripped out of a thesaurus, but basic punctuation, capitalisation, etc, are something I like to see. Why? Because english is my second language and I am legally blind. I already guesstimate words and a jumbled run-on sentence is actually really hard for me to understand. So, if that makes me an elitist, resent me, I guess.

All of it is to say, I am not opposed to non-RPers coming here. What I wish happened more is that those non-RPers opened themselves up to roleplaying in some form. Especially Horde side, which does have much less than the Alliance, at least in terms of walkups.
