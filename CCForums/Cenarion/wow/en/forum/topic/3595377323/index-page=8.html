How do you deal with the fifty violations of the RP Realm policy you see per day when you can only have one Ticket open at a time? I'll tell you how you deal with it...you stop writing up Tickets.

As a result RP gets driven underground by the troll pressure. Soon RP in the streets and public places stops completely. What RP happens, happens in /Guild and /Party. IC events stop being posted on the forums because it becomes a beacon for griefers.

And yes, when a new RP Realm opens up the RP'ers tend to flock to it. And for six months its RP heaven on Azeroth. Then the "RP? Lawl!" crowd start moving in, and the cycle repeats itself. Except Roleplayers are making their last stand here on WrA. There is nowhere left for us to go, and its doubtful Blizzard will open any new RP Realms.

They just randomly walked in on their own, so no one was associated with each other. A problem happened in the rp that was taking place where there was a difference of opinion on what had just occured/it's relation to lore. Taking it to /general would then subject *everyone in orgrimmar* to the dispute as it was being worked out, including people who were trying to rp elsewhere and had nothing to do with this. Talking in /party wouldn't have worked because there were a. fifteen of us, b. some didn't like each other and had no desire to party with each other, and c. others were walking in and out and creating a raid just for a moment of ooc would be silly as well as disrupt the dungeon queue two people were waiting for. /whispers has the problem of only being able to address one person at a time and everyone couldn't participate. It moved to brackets in /say. It got resolved after a few minutes, then the rp rolled on.

It's moments like that which are why blizzard stopped enforcing that policy. They realized that it's not reasonable and does not account for events that may require dropping into ooc for a moment.

HOWEVER. If someone is griefing you oocly, then by all means report them. Blizzard *does* enforce that rule when someone is abusing /say.

If I see someone rping I make a point to move away from them so that I don't disturb them and ruin their fun (which is why I moved to the front of orgrimmar instead of out by the lake in valley of honor, since I didn't want to inadvertantly spoil someone's rp atmosphere).

I would be very happy if there was a channel similar to /say, that had a limited reach so you could have casual conversations with people while not subjecting the entire zone to your chitchat. This was a wish both when I am rping and when I am not, since I'm one of those people who likes to talk with the other player about the rp and babble oocly about the direction the story is going, my reactions on their character, etc. Doesn't make me less of a role player, it just means my brain needs more to hold my attention then JUST sitting and posting.

I'd like to place my input on this here thread, regardless whether it's been repeated or not (only read OP, going through 7 pages of replies is lame).

I'm a non-RPer. It's not my thing. I'm here because of the community and friends. However, I also respect RPers and do not bash them for it, so long as I am not bashed for being a raider.

I enjoy this community a hell of a lot more than most other servers I've been on. I've been in Blackrock, Illidan, Sargeras, Black Dragonflight, Emerald Dream and Korgath (all PvP servers) filled with the biggest concentration of ungrateful and stupid idiots I have ever seen. I was on the verge of quitting this game because the communities I've been a part of were just that bad.

I had heard about RP realms being generally nicer than regular servers, and I was basically given either Moon Guard or Wyrmrest Accord. Now, MG has the whole ERP stuff going on, and I'm not into that, so I went with Wyrmrest Accord. This server basically revived all sense of fun I had lost. I had never met such an awesome community in my years of playing WoW (Granted, the PvE scene could be a bit better, but the community more than makes up for it).

It was basically a shock seeing mature people in WoW (save for the few trolls here and there). I had never RPed before prior to transferring here, but a few friends got me to give it a shot. While it is enjoyable to a certain extent, it's not my thing. I'm still a raider at heart, but just because I am a said raider, I should be shunned from this server by a few vocal minority people here? No thanks. As Quintus said, RP realms are normal PvE servers with RP policies implemented. There is no reason why I can't be here, and I'd rather not go back to the whole other PvP/PvE servers after being here. Proudmoore is the only other server I'd transfer to that has a friendly community on par or better than WrA, but I've made too many friends here to leave them behind, and I'm leading a guild on top of that.

No reason why RPers/non-RPers can't get along. After all, we're playing the same game, but have different aspects as to how we play. Respect one another, and don't worry about so-called "percentages", population, and such. If you want RP to thrive here, RPers as a collective must ensure that it remains a strong force, which does not including shooing away non-RPers from the server.
