A thriving community has a foundation of respect and consideration. When you lose sight of this, the cookie crumbles.

I have had alts and such on this server for about a year. This is the server I consider my alliance-side RP server, while MG is my horde-side. I haven't been on here in quite some time, and my alts have since been deleted due to that fact. My bf and I were thinking of going almost fulltime alliance, instead of horde like we have been, so naturally I came to the forums to look up how it's been doing, race guilds, guilds in general, ect.

And honestly this forum as a whole is just a nasty pit of drama and cat fight wounds.

I mean, come on! I know for a fact the alliance-side RP on MG is great once you get past GS. But I've come here again and again for the fact I prefer this community. But what I'm seeing here is making me, a partial member, want to just walk away. How do you guys think a complete newcommer would react?

I agree with the OP, and I agree with those who are against her point of view. (Can't remember all your names, sorry!) But I can't stress enough that there is never -one- reason RP servers die. There is never -one- group of people who cause drama. There is never -one- reason people choose not to come here. There is never -one- way of thinking, or acting, that is right, or wrong.
A quote from this thread, “ It happens because RP dwindles.” In referring to why RP servers die. So, if the reason RP servers die, is because RP dwindles, I need to ask you, why does RP dwindle? And none of that, “Because non-rpers join..” stuff. We all know very well that if you have 5 red apples in a basket, and you put in 3 green ones, you still have 5 red apples. (AKA, 70% vs 30%) And if the red apples are being taken away, maybe you need to figure out why the red apples are leaving the basket, rather then why there are green ones in there at all.

RP servers die not because elitist RPers scare them away, or because non-RPers flag the server theirs, or because new RP servers open. It's a mix of the three, plus the fact that RPers give up, get disheartned, ect. I've had a ticket posted for Blizz, and a few other names and such I needed to report. You know what I did? I wrote the ticket as such:

"I've noticed a good amount of people who are going against the policies you've set up for my server, and I'd like to alert you of them, so the community can remain the way you've designed it."

When I was answered I proceeded to give my lengthy list.

On another note, I saw a post about the park/fair thing, remember it? The comment went along the lines of, “And if you don't dress in Renaissance gear you should be the minority not the majority, and maybe even looked at a bit strangely by the people in dress code..” I don't remember exactly, but all I can think of is a quote my teacher once told me, “Just because you can, doesn’t mean you should, and just because most people do, doesn’t mean it’s right.”

Remember those times where there was segregation? Where if an African American walked into a room or area where the majority of the people were white, he was treated like garbage, and pretty much kicked out? It was the social norm, it was what nearly everyone did, but did that justify it? Was that –right-? Morally, or in general? I don’t believe so. If a non-RPer rolls on a RP server, and causes no trouble, supports RPers, even downloads a friggin’ addon to further let us know, “Hey, I <3 you guys, but I don’t rp, so don’t waste your time, instead go about your normal RP lives.”, and pretty much does nothing but further the community, is that bad? I don’t think so.

I do understand that you guys love this community, and it’s RP, and are fearful of it perhaps falling. That’s normal so am I sometimes. But chasing away productive people, even if it’s not the way you’d rather have it, is not the right method. There are many things that kill servers, and focusing on only one, and the least troublesome imo, is just ridiculous.

One more random thing to those saying, “I don’t RP here because of the RPers and how jerky they are.” That’s all fine and dandy, I can get that. But the non-RPers here are saying they’re here because of the community, which is comprised of mostly the people you just called jerky. So, in essence, you’re the people I see as having less of a reason to stay here. That may not be the case, but pulling that line doesn’t say anything to me except that.

So all in all, why don’t we just shush-up, make-up, and try to promote -all- positive aspects of this server, whether it be RP, PvE, PvP, or the community. If we do that, the server will thrive, in a way that will make anyone and everyone happy to be here, and happy to join.
