I just want to be extremely careful not to instigate bad habits.


This is the reason I, too, would against remotely encouraging (or even implicitly condoning) people to stack expertise. I think Aldriana was harsh about it on the EJ forums, but I think the nugget of truth in his anti-expertise rantings is that the Cataclysm-era rogue needs to learn how to play without expertise. That means avoiding the front whenever possible, it means pooling energy so that a missed Envenom doesn't leave you watching your SnD tick into oblivion, and it means knowing instinctively what to do when a finisher misses during those one or two occasions per night when it will.

I empathize with the difficulty of that adjustment. And I empathize with those who want to stack expertise because it gives them a safety net of sorts. But I don't think that translates into teaching new level 85 rogues that expertise is a good thing to have. Sure, it doesn't hurt to mention that there are some who feel expertise capping makes a rotation feel more solid. But if the point of this quiz is to help a max-level rogue get to a place where they can maximize DPS, the best way it seems to do that in the game at present is not to encourage them to rely on expertise.
