This list is meant to be an update for returning players to get them up to speed quickly on rogue changes over Wrath going into Cataclysm. I am only going to list ability/mechanic changes here unless I find a lot of questions posted on the forums.

Thanks to Zodar for some excellent suggestions.

This is a work in progress. Please feel free to comment.

General Game Changes.

  • You can now train a second spec at level 30. Dual spec costs 10g. (Down from level 40 and 1000g.)

  • You must spend 31 points in specialization before you can spend points in another tree.

  • You gain one talent point every even level until 80 when you gain one point every level for a total of 41 points at 85.

  • Weapon skills have been removed from the game. No more "practice required".

  • Emblems (heroism, conquest, valor, etc.) have been removed from the game. They have been converted to justice points or gold.

  • The various BG marks have been converted to honor.
    There are two tiers of points for PvE (Justice and Valor) and two for PvP (Honor and Conquest).

  • Justice and Honor are the grindy points with no daily/weekly caps.

  • Valor and Conquest are the raid/arena(rated BG) points. They have a weekly cap.

  • It is possible to obtain Valor without attending raids. Though it is much slower and you will not reach each weekly cap. (I need confirmation on that last bit.)

  • All points will cap at 4000 each.

  • Top end Arena gear no longer requires rating. Conquest gear is purely cosmetic. (I need confirmation on this as well.)

  • Dungeons can be que'd to enter from anywhere vie the dungeon finder tool.

  • You can que for specific dungeons as well as a random dungeon

  • The first random dungeon awards additional points for the day.

  • Ques for battlegrounds and dungeon finder are now region wide instead of just battlegroup.


General Rogue Changes:

  • Haste now increases the rate at which energy is regenerated in addition to weapon speed.

  • Mutilate, Blade Flurry, and Shadow Step are now available at level 10 for specializing in the respective tree. Along with other spec specific bonuses and masteries.

  • You can use combo points on dead mobs (even untargeted) to refresh Slice and Dice and Recuperate.

  • Lockpicking now levels automaticly as you level. You don't have to practice.

  • Master of Deception (+ stealth level) is now baked into the class itself. (There are no longer +stealth talents.)

  • Sprint is now a 8 second duration, 1 minute CD (Down from 15 second duration, 3 minute CD)

  • Cloak of Shadows is now 200% spell resistance. (Up from 90%)

  • Vanish no longer breaks from damage for 3 seconds. (No longer a .5 second immunity.)

  • Prep is only available to Subtlety.

  • All poisons are rankless; they scale to your level now. (Like all the spells in the game.)

  • Wound poison (and all MS effects) now reduce healing by 10%. (Down from 25%/50%.)

  • Instant and Wound are now on proc per minute (PPM) mechanics. This makes them weapon speed agnostic.

  • Deadly Poison will refresh itself and now proc your other weapon's poison instead when it is as 5 stacks. (Same with Mind Numbing? I honestly never use it.)

  • All abilities automatically scale to your level. You don't retrain the abilities at different ranks anymore.

  • Tricks of the Trade now transfers threat temporarily for 30 seconds. (It was permanent before.) Not attacking after 30 seconds will no longer set the cooldown either.

  • Mastery rating is a new stat that effects your spec's mastery which is unique for each spec and has different value depending on spec.

  • Agility once again grants 2 attack power and still crit. It no longer grants armor.

  • The difference between armor and health/stamina between different types of armor (cloth vs plate) is now much less severe.

  • Expose Armor (and all armor debuffs) are now only 12%. (Not sure what it was before, but I'm sure it was more.)

  • Recuperate is a new heal over time finisher.

  • Shiv now removes one enrage effect. (Anesthetic poison has been removed from the game.)

  • Kidney Shot and Cheap Shot now share diminishing returns.

  • Ambush can be used with non-daggers (and damage is "normalized" for non-daggers.)

  • Poisons can be applied to our ranged weapons.

  • Fan of Knives now uses our thrown weapon instead of main and off hand weapons. It's energy cost has been drastically reduced.

Spec Specific changes.

Assassination:

  • Master Poisoner prevents Envenom form consuming Deadly Poison stacks on the target.

  • Cut to the Chase makes it so that Envenom and Eviscerate refresh Slice and Dice to its 5CP maximum.

  • Vile Poison will allow your mainhand and offhand poisons to be applied when using Fan of Knives.

  • Focused Attacks was removed. Instead Venomous Wounds is our energy regeneration mechanic.

  • Hunger for Blood was replaced with Vendetta.

  • Backstab is now used (when talented with Murderous Intent.) as an "execute mode" when your target is below 35% health.


Combat:

  • Revealing Strike is Combat's second combo point builder. (All trees now use two combo point builders to varying degrees.

  • There are no weapon specializations anymore.

  • Even without weapon specialization, us combat daggers fans are still not supported because abilities that favor slow weapons. :(

  • Ok, so its not really a change, but its my post so I can be snarky. :)


Subtlety:

  • Hemorrhage's damage is "normalized" for daggers.

  • Hemorrhage how applys a 30% buff to bleed damage on the target. It lasts one minute.

  • Cheat Death no longer scales off of resilience.

  • Recuperate also returns energy periodicly with Energetic Recovery.

  • Waylay now snares the target of Backstab and Ambush for 50%. (Before it only proc'd on critical hits for 70% before.)

  • Recuperate does not benefit from mastery. (que dev hate QQ)

  • Slice and Dice's haste is effected by mastery.

Regarding Revealing Strike: If you integrate it perfectly into your damage rotation (that is, use it when you've got 4 combo points and you're about to use a finishing move that deals damage,) it will add ~100-200 DPS.

In other words, the only way you or anyone else will even notice you've used the ability is by reading a combat log with spell names turned on. As of this posting, its contribution to your damage output is negligible at best. It's still helpful to include it in your rotation, but only in the grander scheme of things, as mountains of data that demonstrate its weakness will provide a compelling argument for buffing it.

Nice list. Here are some more changes you may want to highlight:


  • Mutilate, Blade Flurry and Shadowstep are now available in their respective talent trees starting at level 10.

  • Talent points are now earned every other level, and must be assigned to a single, primary tree all the way through the 31st point before placing points in other trees.

  • Instant Poison can now be purchased and used at level 10.

  • Weapon skills no longer require skilling up and will be automatically known when starting a new character.

  • Costs for talent respecs and Dual Talent Specialization (10g at level 30) have been significantly reduced, so you can now easily try out different specs to see what works best.

  • Enchant Cloak - Stealth now provides 8 Agility and 8 Dodge Rating, making it the best cloak enchant for all low level Rogues.
