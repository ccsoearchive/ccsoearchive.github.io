Holy necrognomica batgnome! Oh well as long as its here...

1. Gnomes have the most criminally incomplete and slap-dash lore of any playable race in the game hands down. That said, this can be a boon as much as a bane. This gives the experienced roleplayer a great deal of freedom to flesh out their Gnome character without being shoe-horned into a lore straight-jacket the way some races are.

2. Its highly unlikely that the WoW team will add any significant Gnome lore to the game barring a Gnome themed expansion, and the odds of THAT happening are what we Gnomes call "Mathematically Negligible". More's the pity too, because Gnomes have more amazing story potential than any other race in the game IMO, and that potential has been sitting completely untapped for over a decade.

3. There has been no significant Gnome lore since the Gelbin Mekkatorque Leader story was posted on the forums. This isnt surprising considering Cameron Dayton (the guy who wrote it) left Blizzard to pursue greater opportunities soon afterward. I think when that happened, the WoW team lost the only staff writer they had who actually understood how Gnomes think.

4. The High Tinker will apparently make a cameo appearance on the Broken Shore in Legion. Normally I'd be ecstatic to see Gelbin do anything more exciting than tapping a Brewfest keg. But I think it will be a hollow victory. Every Alliance race will be there, so actual Gnomish screentime is likely to be minimal. Further, once the story advances beyond the Broken Shore, and into all the Night Elf and Legion stuff...I'd be surprised if we encounter any Gnome NPC's at all apart from Engineering trainers.

IIRC, Goblins were going to be the 4th Alliance race, but Blizzard decided they were "too green" for the Alliance, and they made them be Gnomes instead. They were going to have their capital connect to Ironforge, but decided that would make the cities too big, so they came up with a backstory about the city being ruined which we're all very familiar with now.

They've fleshed it out a little bit, but otherwise, they haven't done much with the race. Like Goblins, they fit a niche as the tech workers of their faction, unlike Goblins, there aren't entire factions of neutral members of their race that can be relied on for additional lore. For instance: There was no Bilgewater lore outside of what we see in Ashran, but we had the Pinchwhistle faction (which I still haven't determined the proper faction of, there are valid arguments for them being either Venture Co., Steamwheedle, or just completely independent - I think Blizzard intentionally scrapped a bit of data here, so we'll likely never know). So, with a few major exceptions here and there, Blizzard hasn't really been sure what to do with Gnomes.

Unlike some races, which can have their story driven by repetitive conflicts (humans vs orcs), Gnome lore generally needs to be driven by some kind of new idea (and they already played the Titan card, though they COULD end up doing something with the secret underground Blingtron army and their struggles).

There are two main ways I can see of advancing the Alliance Gnomes story:
1. Clean up Gnomeregon.
2. Give the Gnomes a new capital.
But both of those would require work I don't think Blizzard is willing to put in. Plus, after that, I don't think Blizzard would know what to do with them besides continue to use them for tech niches and silly stuff.
