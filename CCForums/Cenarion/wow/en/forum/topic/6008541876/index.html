Nostalgia Raid Nights!

Add a reply

Profile
Ragefang

As things are winding down at the end of Cataclysm, it's time to turn our eyes to fun things for the sake of fun. ;) So I'm proposing Nostalgia Raid Nights. Once a week, we'll run a raid, starting with Molten Core and ending with Dragon Soul. For the short raids (things like Onyxia's Lair or Gruul's Lair) we'll likely do two in one night. I have no doubt we'll find interested parties, I just want to get a feel for what night of the week would work best for folks.

I encourage someone to put something together Horde-side too; we'd love to have you join us, but your Warchief spanked us when we asked, then sent us back to King Wrynn with gnomes stuffed in our mouths as a warning. The notes accompanying this atrocity amounted to "Orcs no play with humans. You no play first Warcraft? Nubs." :(

The order is:

Molten Core
Onyxia's Lair & Blackwing Lair
Temple of Ahn'Qiraj (AQ 40)
A moment of silence for the lost Naxx 40
Karazhan
Magtheridon's Lair
Gruul's Lair & Tempest Keep
Serpentshrine Caverns
Black Temple
Sunwell Plateau
Naxxramas
Obsidian Sanctum & Ulduar
Trial of the Crusader
Icecrown Citadel
Ruby Sanctum & Blackwing Descent
Throne of the Four Winds
Bastion of Twilight
Firelands
Dragon Soul

In Wrath raids and forward, we'll choose 10-man or 25-man depending on how many we have that night. If we have more than 10 but less than 25, we can likely split into two groups and run two 10-mans or two 25-mans, as the earlier raids don't require a full compliment of players if you're a raid-geared 85.

With Cataclysm raids, they should be easy at least up to Firelands, but experienced raid leaders who are comfortable talking in Vent might be useful to help those of us (like me) who haven't been in anything prior to Dragon Soul for Cata raids. There's a good chance we'll need full raids for the Cataclysm raids.

Let me know what nights work out!
