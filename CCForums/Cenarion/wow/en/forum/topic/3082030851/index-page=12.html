Use the petopia flowchart when it comes to pets.

http://forums.wow-petopia.com/viewtopic.php?f=3&t=4753

Unless you need to supply a critical raid buff that only Cunning can bring (basically just Curse of Elements,) always use a Ferocity. All Ferocity perform equal. Just bring whichever your raid needs and if they don't need a buff, bring whichever you think is prettiest. I'm fond of my purple Tallstrider.

Bringing a Cunning pet when you don't need to bring Curse of Elements (Dragonhawk, Wind Serpent) or +4% physical damage (Ravager, mainly for 25m) will drop your DPS about 1.5k, widening as your gear increases.

Note there are two exceptions to this rule being Heroic Ragnaros and Madness of Deathwing where higher execute phase DPS is extremely important, though you can just swap pets before jumping to the last platform or while waiting for Rag to emerge as well....

Your guide is informative and great :). One issue: you severely downplay the use of mastery.
It doesn't just boost your pets damage, it boosts your prime shot, which is kill command, because even though you trigger it, it is still considered a pet attack. Do some tests to determine this if you wish.
Mastery, as a priority, will ALWAYS be the highest priority where and when you can get it, although, not from taking too much away from your haste and crit. Your haste is important, but to a limited degree. You really, tbh, don't need too much haste priority to make your haste plateaus, and those haste plateaus aren't too useful if your crit and mastery can't back your dps up. At the current gear point, if your rotation is where and what it should be, you don't need any higher than 11% (unbuffed, standing as you are with your pet out).

The real trick to Beast Mastery is getting the rotations smooth like flowing water (your kill command should be triggered right as your cobra shot is done its cast time, or rather, the cd of it should be right near the end of a cobra shot and/or an arcane shot. In short, a tight as an air drum rotation. Practice makes this perfect.

If you learn how to manage your focus properly, you'll find raid buffs plus focus fire will be more than enough for your focus dumping and whatnot. The rest falls into place and CD management. And hitting some of those higher haste plateaus often times hurt your other stats if you reforge into it too generously.

As an avid player of BM for many years exclusively, I can say firsthand it does not deserve the rap it has, and furthermore, if mastered and played properly, is more than capable of holding its own against MM and Surv, easily. I've proved this many times over, and have never met my equal with BM, and few in surv or marks.
