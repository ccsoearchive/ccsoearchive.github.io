Once you factor in various talent procs that give you more focus, you're able to dump more into Arcane Shot over the course of a fight. All said and done, Arcane shot will end up getting fired roughly twice as often as Kill Command. That's still not even factoring in all those other hunter shots. It's also not taking into account pet travel time if it's not a straight tank and spank fight. At the time of this writing, regardless of gear level, Crit is always better than Mastery for a BM hunter.

Up front, let me just say, please don't take any of my comments on semantics or word usage as me being rude. I'm just being direct. Also, don't take anything I say as patronizing. A lot of what I'm about to say is for the benefit of anybody else reading this in the future as well.

I know I've used the word "rotation" here and there. But technically speaking, I've never said anything about a true rotation as such. I've mentioned priorities and cycles.

Priorities:
A priority is that you use the more important ability if and when it is ready. If you cannot, you use the next ability in the priority. Kill shot is a good example for describing this. Obviously you cannot use Kill Shot til the mob is almost dead. Therefor Kill Command becomes your next highest priority and so on.

Cycles:
A shot cycle is basically what (and how many) shots you can fit into a given time window. As it pertains to haste plateaus, these shot cycles are based around how many cobra shots one can fit in between Kill Command cooldowns. This is where I believe you're misunderstanding what was written. Those cycles were not determined by how much focus one is getting (or using) with Cobra Shot. They are determined by how many can be fit due to their cast time. In other words, the shorter the cast time, the more that can be fit between Kill Commands.

While it is only briefly mentioned, anytime you're high on focus, you should be exchanging extra Cobra Shots with Arcane Shots to dump focus. But that doesn't change your cycle altogether. It just changes one shot every few cycles. A higher focus regen should simply mean you're going to alter that shot more often.

As far as me making any amendments to the guide for this, I honestly hadn't given it thought til reading your comments. Due to the holidays, I haven't had as much free time for such things. But now that it's been brought to my attention, I will look into it and do some reading.

As for you personally, if you're able to maintain focus for that 'rotation' every single cycle, then by all means do so. That would fit perfectly into a 6 second window for anybody at a base plateau.
