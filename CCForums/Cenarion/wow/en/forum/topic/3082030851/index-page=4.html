My thoughts:

Despite your responses, I would still change Boar's Speed in the Ferocity tree to Optional. Reason being is that the only other reasonable place to put that point is going 2/2 Bloodthirsty, which has no DPS benefit yet is still considered Optional. If you do find yourself in a movement heavy fight, Boar's Speed at least offers some potential DPS gain. Saying "you shouldn't be running BM spec" is counter productive to the point of the guide.

In the Cunning Tree, I would probably go ahead and change Dash to Required, as you need it to move to the next tier. I would change Boar's Speed to Optional for the same line of reasoning as Ferocity - the only other talent that could possibly increase DPS is Bullheaded if it reduces down time, which you've already marked Optional. If it's optional, there must be another option, and I would choose Boar's Speed over Carrion Feeder 100% of the time.

It would be helpful to have the amount of Haste Rating needed for each of the Haste plateau's. Getting to the second plateau is not something people will reach even in full 378 gear unless they sacrifice more Crit than they should, so I would change the wording about not needing "a great deal of gear". Getting to the third plateau is impossible unless you're rocking 4pc T11 and still reforging poorly.

Your probably planning this already, but if your goal is helping people maximize DPS with BM, then definitely at least add sections on shot priority, reforging, and tips on cool down stacking. Gems/Enchants are going to be the same for all Hunter specs, so not sure if that's worth maybe a small mention.

Shot Priority (since Kevert asked):
Serpent Sting -> Kill Shot -> Kill Command -> Arcane Shot -> Cobra Shot

The point is to always have enough Focus to use KC on cool down. Use Cobra if you need more Focus, Arcane if you don't. You don't want to use Cobra at all during BW so make sure you get plenty of Focus beforehand.

T12 4pc bonus: For the most part you just ignore it. It's either going to catch Kill Command and save 37 Focus (27 if Killing Streak is popped) or Arcane and save 22 Focus. If you have the presence of mind to notice when it triggers and KC is nearly off cool down, it might serve you better to switch in a Cobra Shot and save it (unless you're going to be Focus capped).

Reforging:
As with the other specs, get Hit to 961 first. After that, Crit and Haste are valued very similarly and a lot of it comes down to personal preference. As you get closer to that second Haste plateau, I'd say Haste comes out slightly ahead. Mastery is worse then both.

Cooldowns:
Don't stack BW with Rapid Fire/Focus Fire when possible. Try not to stack any of them with Hero/BL. Use BW before Rapid Fire because you're more likely to miss a BW over the course of a fight if you delay it. Use Fervor early and often.

Shoot for 2 Kill Command's and 8 Arcane Shots during each BW. You can get 9 Arcane's in without Serpent dropping in a straight forward button-smash if you have high enough Haste, but Serpent needs to be at max duration when you pop BW.

If you know Hero/BL is going to be used at the start of a fight and you can get the timing right, you can pull off a decent chain of BW -> Hero -> Rapid Fire -> BW and reasonably delay using Focus Fire as long as possible (thus keeping Frenzy stacks on the pet). If the stars align (as well as your cool downs) you can obviously do this mid fight as well, it's just much harder to time right.

First off, I apologize for not getting this done as soon as I originally planned. I've had some unexpected circumstances recently that interfered with finishing this (as well as participating in guild raids). But I appreciate all the positive feedback.

I just put up the Haste Plateaus earlier this evening. I hope to add more information on stats, shots, and pets after the weekend (real life permitting). That said, there's something else that needs to be addressed.

I really don't know how I can say this without coming off rude. So I'm just going to say it. If the people this is more directed at are offended, I'm sorry. But apparently my previous responses weren't clear enough. There's been some discrepancies on how some of the talents have been deemed. So I feel I need to put this out there.

Right in the middle of the introduction, I mentioned that one of the reasons for writing this guide was that some other sources of information can be a little too opinionated on the information they provide. So my intention as one of the key elements of this guide was to avoid opinions as much as possible. So when it comes to the talents (no matter what some might think) none of the recommendations given are based on my own (or anybody's) personal opinion.

Here are those levels of recommendation.

    Required: Needed; essential; obligatory.

    Optional: Available to be chosen but not obligatory.

    Recommended: Put forward (someone or something) with approval as being suitable for a particular purpose or role.

    Not recommended: The opposite of recommended.


Considering the fact that this guide is focused on getting more damage from a BM spec, here's how they pertain to the talents in this guide.

    Required: If the talent directly affects damage in any way, it's required. For the most part, I don't think there's any real disagreement here. The common misunderstandings seem to be with the other levels of recommendation. That said, technically speaking, the word optional applies to all of the talents that are not labeled as required. I think that's the main part that's misunderstood. So in short, if it's not required, it's optional but to varying degrees. They are as follows.

    Optional: Generally speaking, if I labeled it as optional, it means you probably will have an extra talent point and that talent point might need to be spent around that tier in order to continue to the rest of the talents. So while the talent isn't necessary, it's not a bad choice either. In fact, it's probably one that gets used by many other players.

    Recommended: This is similar to just optional. But in this case, there definitely is a talent point that needs to be spent in that tier in order to go deeper in the tree. So of the talents available, that's probably the best spot.

    Not recommended: Obviously, talents labeled thuswise have nothing to do with damage in any way. But more importantly, even if you have extra talent points, these shouldn't be one of the first choices to spend said points. That said, points could still be spent here if you have some other extenuating circumstances.


Now to address this as it concerns Boar's Speed. First and foremost, the speed bonus does NOT stack on top of the other bonuses. So the only time it would ever come into play is if you don't have Dash/Dive (or it's on CD) and you don't have charge either. Since both of the other movement talents are recommended, this one cannot be. In fact, if you actually do have both the other 2 movement talents, this would be quite a wasted point. When it comes to a cunning pet, it's even more of a waste. If you have just 1 point in Mobility (and this is pointed out clearly in that section) Dive/Dash will be up nearly 100% of the time.


I hope you will now see that nothing I've said here is based on anybody's opinion. Simply put, when it comes to DPS, either something contributes or it doesn't. That's a fact; it's not an opinion. Since this guide is about gaining DPS, everything mentioned is focused towards that end.
This isn't a guide on how to keep your pet alive so you can continue to do damage because if it dies you will lose a big chunk of your damage so you should take this talent so it doesn't die.
So talents that may or may not have an effect on your pet's survival are not being addressed in such a way.

At no point was I ever trying to have a debate on that subject nor am I going to now. I'm simply telling you that the topic was never intended to be addressed in the first place and I hope it doesn't need to be addressed again. The time I spent to explain this simple matter (which I honestly thought didn't even need it to begin with) is time I could've have spent writing something more important. So if you still feel the need to discuss this, at least take the time to think about it in its proper context and not how it pertains to you personally.
