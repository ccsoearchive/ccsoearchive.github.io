Everyone seems to have glossed over my WoG suggestion on the previous page. Sadpants.

There needs to be a clear choice. To my mind that choice is best framed as physical vs magical.

Position Holy Shield (no longer procced by WoG) as physical mitigation. Change WoG overhealing shield into magical only.

This creates a clear choice: block physical (HS) vs absorb magical (WoG), and you use the right finisher in the right situation. HS has one niche, WoG has the other. Right now, HS has one niche, but WoG absorbs everything, so naturally it's not very hard for WoG to be the obvious choice in nearly every situation.

Assuming you position WoG as magic only, a minimalist approach might be:

1. WoG protective shield absorbs magic only
2. Holy Shield proc removed from GbtL
3. Holy Shield duration based on HP consumed

Low level Paladin tanking will still be, um, flavourful.

I'm still not a fan of the above as I dont think it goes far enough but... /shrug

Thank you for putting together this spreadsheet. I found it useful for correcting a few misconceptions I had about how crit block was working.

I noticed that you used a 1.02 armor modifier for the ASD meta. I'm curious to know if there is a reason you chose ASD over ESD (+5% block value). By playing around in your spreadsheet, I attempted to model a switch in metas. Specifically, I modified:

-Non crit blocks reduce damage by 35%, not 30% (Summary, B30)
-Crit blocks reduce damage by 70%*, not 60% (Summary, B32)
-Armor no longer gets a 1.02 modifier (Warrior stats, E9)
*I'm not sure if 70% instead of 65% is legit. Does anybody know for certain?

Doing this, I found that total damage taken declined from 1,817,223 to 1,682,716 while net healing required declined from 1,647,187 to 1,517,523. It seems safe to conclude from these numbers that ESD might be the better meta gem choice for warrior tanks at the gear level you described.

Do the changes I have described above accurately shift the model from ASD to ESD? If so, this could affect choices for meta gems as well as the desirability of mastery as a mitigation stat.
