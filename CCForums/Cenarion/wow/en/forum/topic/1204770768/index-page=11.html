I'm having trouble understanding why it matters that it's a tradeoff.

The problem is that paladins have the option at all, whereas other tanks do not. In the very worst case scenario where a paladin can't sacrifice any threat for survivability, they are about equal to the other tanks.

Very few encounters will be the worst case scenario. Everything beyond than that is gravy, and let's just say that the gravy boat (i.e. the maximum amount of survivability a paladin can gain) is pretty deep.

It would be one thing if every other tank had the option of sacrificing some threat for survivability. We could all have a merry old time crunching numbers about how much threat each class has to give up for X amount of survivability and whether it's balanced.

The reality is that only paladins can do it, so it's not really balanced. Whether it requires giving up 25% of your threat to get those WoG numbers or whether it takes 75% of your threat to get them is irrelvant; the numbers shouldn't be attainable at all.

12/06/2010 8:54 PMPosted by Twenynge
If all tanks can produce roughly the same threat and not all fights are threat sensitive what you say makes perfect sense. If (when) threat is an issue and paladins cannot use WoG and SoI, my paladin is taking 13% more damage than your warrior. That is the other side of the coin.


No, you're mistaken - the difference is much smaller than you claim. The healing number for paladins presented in Zarko's post also includes Divinity, which is totally unrelated to threat generation.

If you can't afford to use WoG or SoI because of threat, then odds are we can't afford to use Impending Victory (and indeed, probably wouldn't even be specced into Impending Victory) because of threat. In that scenario we only get Field Dressing healing.

Looking at Zarko's spreadsheet, that puts warriors at 1,817,223 damage taken and 65,420 healing (1,751,803 damage taken total). Paladins are at 1,898,473 damage taken and 68,345 healing (1,830,128 damage taken total. This is a difference of about 80k damage or 4.3%. We're talking about less than two boss swings here. And heck, frankly, it doesn't include magical damage - where paladins come out ahead by a pretty similar (and also trivially small) margin.

If paladin threat generation is bad for some reason, that's an independent problem they should fix. It still wouldn't justify WoG cranking out as much healing in a raid-tanking scenario as it currently does, because eventually some encounter comes along where threat is not important but survivability is, and paladin tanks are kings.

What Prot needs is a compelling alternative for spending Holy Power to compete with WoG.


That should be ShoR, but it isn't. Why? Because Shield of the Righteous and Holy Shield are talents and not granted at level 10. Therefore you can't make anything revolve around them, as you don't have them until a reasonably high level.

Honestly, it's bad design. ShoR should be the level 10 ability (in place of Avenger's Shield) and HS should be a passive. It's simply stupid that you have to talent into Holy Shield. They are nothing more than talent point soaks; there's no way a Prot Paladin won't take that entire line of talents, most of you would immediately drop a 5 man if you inspected your level 80/85 Prot Paladin tank and saw they didn't talent into Holy Shield. Quite frankly the entire center line of the Paladin Protection tree is poorly designed and does nothing more than hold the spec back from the skills and passives it needs until a higher level.

If it was up to me, some of Divine Bulwark's block chance would shift to Holy Shield, as would Holy Shield and Shield of Righteousness. Holy Shield Bash (an interrupt) would be where ShoR is now, and Avenger's Shield would be where Holy Shield is now. So lets play a game where we pretend that stuff like this actually happens.

Edit: something like this - http://www.war-tools.com/t71330.html

The meaningful choice offered then becomes very simple.

ShoR and Inquisition proc HS which is block chance and value, that is, physical absorbtion. I would change it so that Mastery: Divine Bulwark only functions if HS is active, and base the duration on Holy Power. That way if you dont ShoR or Inquisition, you your chance to block is 5% and your block value is 30%.

Word of Glory should no longer proc HS, and the overhealing shield should be magic-damage-only absorb. Maybe have it create some kind of absorb shield baseline (size based on Holy Power) and then do a heal, with any overhealing added to the shield. Extend the duration if you have to.

Your choice then becomes straightforward - you use your finisher either to mitigate physical or magical damage. The Eternal Glory talent gives you a chance to have both physical and magical overlapping... but because they're fundamentally different types of damage, it's no longer simply a case of "whichever is better, i'll spam that all the time".

WoG needs to have it's scope narrowed, and in a sense, it can't, because that's all a young Prot Paladin has for 40 or so levels they can spec into ShoR and HS. Also, it's very dumb that Prot Paladins can't block worth a damn until high levels.

Revisit the design. Change things around. Make the choice of defensive finishers a choice between physical and magical mitigation. ShoR vs Inquisition is supposed to be single target threat vs ae threat, I get it. ShoR vs WoG is not even a question. Fix it.

Change the decisions that paint you into a design corner with Prot Paladins. It's not too late and i'm pretty sure Prot Paladins dont want to be stuck on a WoG buff/nerf rollercoaster because it's the best choice or the worst choice on any given tuesday.
