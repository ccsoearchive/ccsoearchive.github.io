I just don't see any way it can work that is likely to make you happy. There are basically only three options:

1) WoG is an integral part of your survivability. You're expected to use it and balanced around using it. When you do not use it, you will be harder to keep alive than the other tanks. The problem is that if you balance paladins around this, then their threat is also balanced around using WoG...which means they will put out more threat than the other tanks when they use ShoR. For an expansion where Blizzard ostensibly wants to make threat relevant, that doesn't seem like a good idea.

2) WoG is a negligible amount of healing. It's a waste to use it in most situations; when you do use it, it's because threat is totally irrelevant (and even there it's *still* a negligible amount of healing, it's just that threat is worthless so it's a slight gain to use it). This sounds like what you don't want.

3) WoG is a sizeable amount of healing but isn't included when they "run the numbers for paladins." This is basically what the status quo appears to be, and it ends up, as you'd expect, with paladins looking overpowered while using WoG. This is where we are now, and the result is that paladins are hands down the best tank (unless you count bugged armor druids.)

Something that occurs to me having read the last few interesting posts on WoG (actually, it occurred to me a while ago but with travel and stuff I forgot about it until now) is that maybe we're looking at balancing Paladin survivability the wrong way, basically as "Warriors plus X and minus Y".

For the reasons people have brought up (and a couple others I'll get to in a sec) WoG plus HoPo in general is a relatively difficult genie to stuff back into the bottle. I mean, as one point among many: if WoG gets a long cooldown, what is a Prot paladin supposed to do with HoPo for the first 40 or so levels?

So, maybe instead Paladins should get more of a DK self-healing driven model. Someone mentioned balancing paladins around damage reduction abilities a week or two ago which originally got me thinking of it. Instead of nerfing WoG (well, yeah, take out vengeance scaling, but not the more major changes we've discussed) instead take a big whack at passive damage reduction. Paladins take more damage, but are then balanced around healing themselves with maybe 80/20 WoG/ShoR usage. Paladins take a bunch of damage, then heal themselves back up.

Yes, yes, I know that lots of people hate this model (and to be fully honest, I don't hate this model, I think its cool and was begging GC not to kill it back in like early Beta) but I also think that lots of people DON'T hate it. More importantly, ITS EASIER TO BALANCE.

The problem we run into is that we generally think of it as "Paladins are balanced for survivability, but when they don't need threat they can turn it into more survivability" which, given the current raid environment, raises a number of problems. However, if the model was "Paladins are balanced for survivability, but when they don't need survivability they can turn it into more threat/dps" then it opens things up a bit because tank dps (within reasonable boundaries) is less of a make or break issue.

A tank being able to shift priorities to survive a lot more damage than another is a balance problem. A tank being able to shift priorities to deal like 30% more dps is a pale imitation of a druid, and vastly less of a problem.
