He includes Rune Tap. I don't think he includes Dancing Rune Weapon but I would be fine with him doing so.

The point is that Shield Block and Barkskin/DP/etc are very different. Barkskin/DP/etc have 10s durations/60s cooldowns (~16% uptime) and prevent magical damage while they're up. Shield Block has a 10s duration/30s cooldown (33% uptime) but does nothing against magical damage.

The shorter an ability's cooldown, the more pressure there is to use it when it comes available, because the more of its uptime you lose for each second it goes unused. Furthermore, it doesn't stop magical damage anyway.

Again, I'm not making a balance argument about Shield Block or needing a tool against magical burst (I happen to believe that Shield Block sucks *and* that warriors need a tool to deal with magical burst, but that's beside the point). I just think it "the warrior uses Shield Block on cooldown for damage reduction, the paladin/druid/dk save their 60s cooldowns to handle burst" accurately reflects how tanks will actually behave on encounters.
