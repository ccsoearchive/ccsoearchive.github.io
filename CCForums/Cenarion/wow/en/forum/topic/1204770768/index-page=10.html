I have enormous respect for Theck and his work, but I simply don't believe that 25% number for threat loss is in any way possible. I'm no Theck, but I did a lot of number crunching on this quite some time ago trying to figure out the most efficient hps/tps tradeoffs and whether a WoG centric build was viable for threat (this was before the beta raids opened up. Turns out Vengeance made threat a joke so I stopped).

The 10% number is entirely possible, though a decent chunk of that loss is because of the loss of the overpowered SoT glyph. Exp is our #1 threat stat up to soft-cap and #2 up to hard-cap, so gear assumptions play a big role here as the different builds will have different stat-weightings and targets. Assuming identical gear is the easier way to model, but it won't give good results.

Another issue is glyphing. You obviously have to replace the SoT glyph with something, and if you're running a primarily WoG build, then the seal of SoI (+5% healing) becomes attractive for both healing and threat. But if you're not using WoG, then SoI becomes worse, making the swap look even worse.

Finally, I found the SoT vs SoI threat differential significant but smaller than I expected. Both scale (well, used to scale anyway, haven't tested coefficients lately, though that was also during the very brief period when Paladins were up at 0.5 threat/heal like everyone else, before we got dunked back down to 0.25) very comparably with AP and are both boosted by Vengeance. What really drives SoI threat is your level of overhealing. At 100% overhealing, its useless, while at 0% is surprisingly decent (plus is obviously superior in AoE situations).

That's one reason that trading threat for survivability isn't really a tradeoff you can let tanks make to any significant extent. @Twenynge

You've stated that you are new to raiding and I'll just explain the scenario to you so you understand it better. In a 5 man, the mobs die so fast and hit for so little, you almost never have vengeance. You're right that you can't afford the threat loss. However, on a boss fight you'll notice that holding threat is easier than when you have multiple mobs. In a 5 man, you still won't have much vengeance but the full rotation and single target make threat easier.

In a true raid (which is what this thread talks about), there are situations where the raid does other stuff instead of fighting the boss. That happens all the time. That means you have more threat than you need. For example, on Sarth 3d (the most challenging fight at the beginning of the x-pac), one tank would literally sit there on Sarth for at least 5 minutes while the rest of the raid was busy with adds. That tank had no worries on threat. Similar circumstances happen a lot (though they usually don't stay away for that long).

In addition, end game raiding means full vengeance. If the tank, like yourself, can hold aggro in 5 mans where they never get it, imagine your threat when you have double your normal attack power. That literally happens with vengeance. In a raid, vengeance goes up and you hit like a truck (on any tank). The trade off of 25% threat ceases to be an issue because of it. That only happens in end game. In a five man, threat is a huge concern. Vengeance trivializes it in raids.
