Also, does threat REALLY seem to be that big an issue on the fights we've seen so far? Ok, for the first 30 seconds maybe, but its not like a paladin is going to even approach what any other tank is going to be able to accomplish with a full stack of Vengeance.


I agree with you that single target threat isn't an issue so far. That said, they have said they want threat to matter, and presumably they also want survivability to matter. You really can't tune paladins to meet both of those goals if they could dramatically shift their rotation to focus on one or the other.


I think Zarko's idea - removing HS from WoG - is probably one of the better solutions, because it makes WoG vs. ShoR a survivability vs. survivability choice (instead of a survivability vs. threat choice) that ShoR usually, but not always, wins. As he's mentioned in the past, there will still be niche uses for WoG (primarily in response to magical damage, where hopefully it'll just be a small advantage and not something totally broken) but by and large if you want survivability you'll be using ShoR.

Of course, that'll gimp some of the WoG-related survivability talents for Prot considerably, but, well...not every talent gets to be a keeper. :/

Now base survival is mapped out. How about comparing all cds for all tanks. And mapping potential survival via cd usage. So we can get a real picture of how tanks are stacking up by combining this with that.


This is a lot easier to do once we see how often bosses do burst abilities and what that burst actually looks like in terms of how much total damage it's doing and what proportion of that damage is magical vs. physical.

That said, though, it's not hard to recognize that when CDs "matter", DKs are likely to be the best tank, followed by paladins and druids, followed by warriors. It takes some very odd encounter design for that *not* to be the case.

If they design well, there won't be encounters where burst damage is a huge factor, because they haven't made a significant effort to make tanks equal in that regard. Ditto for enormous amounts of magical damage. Obviously some burst/some magical is fine, but given that they've clearly put more time into making sure tanks take similar amounts of overall physical damage, that's what I'd hope encounters are actually testing.
