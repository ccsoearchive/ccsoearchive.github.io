Hello all.

In case you're not familiar, I put a thread together on the old forums with a lot of discussion about tanks and how they compare.

The original purpose was to compare the tanks at a high level of mastery to show what other tanks looked like when Paladins were able to block cap. Blizzard took away the ability for paladins to block cap in the first tier without using heroic gear.

The purpose has morphed at this point to be a discussion about how the various tanks look at 85, decked out in 359 epics--and how they compare to each other.

There have been a lot of changes recently, so it's helpful to have one spot to speak about them in order to keep the rest of the threads free from hijacks.

Here's what we all look like at 85:


Paladins
Total health: 168,527
Total Armor Estimate: 40,507 (including Devo)
Stance Reduction: 10%
Miss: 5% (baseline)
Dodge: 15.95%
Parry: 12.66%
Block chance (from mastery, including base mastery): 50.4%
Total combined Miss/Dodge/Parry/Block: 84.01%

Warriors:
Total Guesstimate at health: 168,550
Total Armor Estimate: 40,507 (including Devo)
Stance Reduction: 10%
Miss: 5% (baseline)
Dodge: 15.15%
Parry: 12.05%
Block chance (from mastery): 48.46%
Crit Block Chance (assuming 60% HTL Uptime): 34.46%
Total combined Miss/Dodge/Parry/Block: 80.66%

Deathknights
Total Guesstimate at health: 176,153 health
Total Armor Estimate: 34,296, including Devo
Stance Reduction: 13.5% (Stance and Blade Barrier)
Miss: 5% (baseline)
Dodge: 14.65%
Parry: 12.71%
Mastery: 21 (including base mastery)
Total combined Miss/Dodge/Parry/Block: 32.36%

DK Mastery:
Heals the DK for 29% of the damage dealt to him over the last 5 seconds, and places a shield for38.06% of the damage dealt to him over the last 5 seconds. Usable, if you are using DS as much as possible, once per 5.5-6 seconds.

Druids: For druids I did 2 builds, one with Mastery, like the other tanks . . .
Total Guesstimate at health: 171,637 health
Total Armor Estimate: 49,967, including Devo. If Druid armor is currently bugged, 38,540 is correct
Stance Reduction: 12%
Miss: 5% (Baseline)
Dodge: 34.08%
Mastery: 20 Mastery points

Druids: And then this one, gearing for agility, since it's more damage reduction per point than mastery
Total Guesstimate at health: 169,409 health
Total Armor Estimate: 49,967, including Devo. If Druid armor is currently bugged, 38,540 is correct
Stance Reduction: 12%
Miss: 5% (Baseline)
Dodge: 37.06%
Mastery: 15 Mastery points

Savage Defense:
Each time you deal a critical strike while in Bear Form, you have a 50% chance to gain Savage Defense, reducing the damage taken from the next physical attack that strikes you by (35 + 4*Mastery) of your attack power.

Attack power: A druid will have roughly 13,226-16,333 AP from buffs/bear form and gear. If stacked to full vengeance, this increases to 30,594 Attack power or more.

That means your savage defense shield is 19K-22K, depending on how you gear, for mastery or for Agility. Agility gearing has higher base AP, but you benefit less from vengeance.

Now here's the second part of this post:

I put our tanks through a fight with a Patchwerk style boss, who just hits hard, and slowly. Think of him like Gamon.

Our boss hits for 110,000 on a 2.0 second swing speed timer (reduced to 2.4 seconds after Attack Speed debuff)

That gives the following results per hit:

Damage per hit by class:
Paladin: 39,713
Warrior: 39,713
Deathknight: 41,241
Bear: 34,380--with high armor
Bear: 39,904--with 33% thick hide

Let's take a look at how they stack up just in damage taken, so no healing:
AgiBear: 1,384,124--with high armor
MasteryBear: 1,392,485--with high armor
AgiBear: 1,713,048--with 33% thick hide
MasteryBear: 1,737,871--with 33% thick hide
Warrior (w/1 SB per minute): 1,879,506
Paladin: 1,931,246
Deathknight: 2,067,994

As you can see, DKs take the most damage (which they make up for later with healing), while Druids take the least damage. With their current armor value, they take *by far* the least damage.

If you'd like an explanation of how Savage Defense and Blood Shield work, I'm more than happy to give them to you, but it's too long to fit here. I'd like to thank Fasc, for his excellent Druid SS, and Lichloathe, for BloodSim, his excellent DK simulator. I'll include links at the end for those.

Now, here's that same list, but with healing included. All self healing affects are assumed to suffer 50% overheal, while healing increasing affects are assumed to suffer 40% overheal.

Paladin (with 3 minutes of WoG, Seal of Insight and Divinity): 1,166,261
AgiBear: 1,292,643--with high armor
MasteryBear: 1,299,501--with high armor
AgiBear: 1,621,567--with 33% thick hide
Paladin (with no WoG): 1,632,095
MasteryBear: 1,645,187--with 33% thick hide
Deathknight (with DS and RT on CD): 1,661,835
Warrior (w/1 SB per minute): 1,707,325

I wanted to highlight how close things are if WoG is not used and Bear armor is set back to pre-4.03a levels.

I'd like to heartily thank Anothriel, Dosvidaniya, Wrathblood, Warstehdruid, Lichloathe, Fasc and others for their help on this project. Please let me know if I missed something, or if you can think of a way to improve things.

Quick Q&A:
Why didn't you include Blood Craze?
Because it's really, really, really bad. If you'd like, subtract 40K from the warrior's healing, and you'll have Blood Craze included.

How did you handle Savage Defense/Blood Shield?
By using the SS from this thread: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/1083360391 for Savage Defense
And by using Lichloathe's BloodSim, found here: http://bloodsim.codeplex.com/
They're both excellent resources, and improved upon my own very weak early results. The only changes I made was subtracting 5% of the savage defense procs due to latency.

Which version of Shield Block are you using?

I'm using the implementation described here: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/1196140528 I'm doing it because it describes what's going on in the game, and because it seems to me to be the most likely implementation.

Why do you use 1 Shield Block per minute?

I explain this more on the second page, but I don't believe that the major function of a cooldown is to reduce damage over time. It's to be used when you need it. However, SB is available twice as often as other short duration cooldowns. Given that, I decided to resolve that tension by using one Shield Block per minute. It's the best compromise, to my mind, between demonstrating that SB is available more, but most of the other cooldowns want to be saved for use. I give a more thorough breakdown on the second page.

Other resources used:
http://elitistjerks.com/f15/t29453-combat_ratings_level_85_cataclysm/


Thanks for looking, please don't QQ and please don't troll.
