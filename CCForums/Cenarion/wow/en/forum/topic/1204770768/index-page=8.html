I think you did something wrong with your warrior damage reduction calculation.

Warriors have high base block: 5% base + 15% from prot spec.

That means that the avoidance/block mitigation for a warrior with the stats you specify would be the following:
Miss: 5% (baseline)
Dodge: 15.15%
Parry: 12.05%
Block chance (base): 20%
Block chance (from 21 Mastery): 31.5%
Crit Block Chance (from 21 Mastery): 31.5%
Total combined Miss/Dodge/Parry/Block: 83.7%

The total damage that gets to armor is:
16.3% from chance to be hit at all,
70% of 20% when just regular block and,
40% of 31.5% when critical block.
The avoidance mitigation averages to taking only 42.9% of incoming swing damage.

If you gem for 5% more block value (as a warrior really should), then the damage taken becomes 38.75%.

This didn't take into account any block chance or crit block chance boosts because they're just icing.

2) I like the idea of scenarios where using WoG back-to-back for a short interval is smart play.


Honestly, I think what it boils down to is that other tanks just don't have this option. Nobody else can just decide to switch to their survivability mode for a short interval. I mean, maybe DKs can kind of do it with Death Strikes? But it seems like they're paying through the nose for that privilege in terms of their overall damage taken.

That's the basic problem. Any scenario where a paladin can get some significant gain from using WoG as much as possible is a scenario where the other tanks are basically limited to their 'normal' survivability options.

It would be one thing if paladins paid for this privilege with some tradeoff. Perhaps they could have fewer cooldowns than other tanks, or take more damage overall, but heal it up with WoG; that'd be balanced. But they don't seem to, at the moment, and frankly nobody seems to be proposing that they should (understandably).

As such I think WoG should probably be a very small advantage in limited situations, and not used the rest of the time.
