To be honest, I actually like low population realms.

First, it's less lag.

Second, it's less of pointless competition for things that simply don't matter (like when new chain quests come out).

Third, you appreciate resources more because you can't just go to auction house and get what you want. I always thought professions like leather working were useless because you spent more time and money making an item than just buying something like it or better off of auction house. Now, leveling a new hunter, I can't appreciate professions enough.

Fourth, with random battle grounds, dungeon finder, and now raid finder, it's not a big issue to find a pick up ground. And there are enough people on the server to do arena with.. since you won't need more than a few.

Fifth, you always have an option to transfer off the server. Even if money is tight, I'm sure you can floor a $25 bill saving for a month or two.

There is a saying in Russian, "Menshe Narodu - Bolshe Kislorodu"... (Less People - More Oxygen)... and I'd like to keep it that way.

My 2 cents.

It's been a while since these threads have been posted... all I can throw in now is a relevant Blue Post from Zarhym, and a few reflections. I pulled this from http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/3988400920?page=6#103

02/02/2012 03:41 PMPosted by Zarhym
Having said all that, yesterday we discussed low-population and faction-imbalanced realms with our developers. They have some pretty bold and spectacular plans for addressing this in anticipation of implementing some of the features we plan to in Mists. I just don't have a lot of information to share with you at this stage of programming and development.


I'm glad they've finally acknowledged this issue (and that they've been working on it behind the scenes for quite some time). I take it from the wording that whatever they're planning will be coming in a 4.x patch ("in anticipation of implementing... features we plan to in Mists"). Fingers crossed.

* * * * *

Being a Horde man playing low-pop, I posted much earlier in the thread about my trying out Alliance leveling on a high-pop server, vs my usual experience. It was ten times faster and better. (edit: hyperpole acknowledged and not removed.) Even without Heirlooms or any other experience boosters, I went at my own pace, took a break in my early 70s to wait for Patch 4.3, and then blazed ahead to endgame and 8/8 Raid Finder (3 days after hitting level cap!) ...on a low-pop server I would not have been able to solo the content at that pace without alt/guild help (a previously-established support network, that most new players don't have access to). Granted, I did have the Perks from a Level 25 guild, but it died after its raid team imploded and left, and I didn't lean very hard on anyone in it.

High-pop means a healthy Auction House with lots of demand and a fast churn rate, and lower prices driven down by healthy competition. Low-pop means watching your AH postings languish because no one wants or needs them, and astronomical AH prices due to low demand and little other choice.

High-pop means a steady stream of quest buddies and active players. Low-pop means empty landscapes devoid of friends (and foes, on PvP servers, aka "real servers" if you'll forgive me for saying so).

I'm disappointed that the problem has gone on this long, and I'm anticipating the solution(s) with glee.
