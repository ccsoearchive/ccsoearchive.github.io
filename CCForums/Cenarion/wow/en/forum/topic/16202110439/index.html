A fresh page of vellum was added to the various grouping of tattered and faded pieces of parchment that plastered the multiple public notice boards of Stormwind, Ironforge, and Darnassus. Upon it, a single image dominated the letterhead: A full moon, perched behind the heads of two wolves howling toward the sky.

In lettering that at once looked printed by machine, yet bearing the odd quirks of type written by hand, its message read:

“Adventurers seek out fortune, and fame couched in the lyrics of songs. Heroes get awards, and have accolades bestowed upon them with ceremony, and glory. Both are the inheritors of pomp, with all the earmarks of boosting public morale.

The patriot will find none of this renown, for loyalists seek nothing but doing the right thing for its own sake. Moreover, they're willing to take on the risks, without distinction, that send others fleeing to the relative safety of the rank and file brand of career advancement.

You've proven yourself capable, willing, and most importantly determined enough to fight for the goals of the Alliance, yet perhaps you're unsatisfied with being just one more soldier; one more draftee; one more piece on the board without the wherewithal to make your own moves. You crave the opportunity to do some real good for the world without the hand at your back.

You desire true agency. You want to count yourself among those that fight because there is a battle to win.

This then, is a call to the patriots of the Alliance.

If you think you have the verve to join a group of talented loyalists in the pursuit of Alliance victory; if you have the fortitude necessary to operate in the areas left fallow by traditional forces; if you've the courage to do what is necessary to achieve what is right, then we can offer you exactly what you're seeking: true agency.

You need only seek us out. Or, perhaps, we're already actively finding you.”

------------------------------------------------

<Eclipse Agency> has opened recruitment! We are a new RP-PvE-PvP guild with a dedicated core of experienced roleplayers, raiders and PvPers boasting a decade of experience (yeah, we've been playing for a while - don't call us old!).

RP:
Eclipse Agency is a lore-based, medium-heavy, paramilitary RP guild with a loose command structure. We believe that every voice counts, both in and out of character! The guild facilitates in-game missions, monthly meetings/debriefs, community building events, etc.

Also, we have a liquor cabinet. And possibly grand, epic battles to lay claim to the crunchy snacks.

PvE:
Presently, we are working on leveling/gearing characters for Heroics. We are open to all classes, and are always happy to lend a hand whenever/wherever we can!

PvP:
BGs! Ashran! World PvP! Arenas! We love it all, and love it even more when we can run with fellow guildies!

Those interested in our guild can hit up our site for more information at http://eclipseagency.enjin.com or contact Anwynn, Aenlahn, Regharis, Caseiri or Ralleigh in game with any questions.

The streets were filled with roses, and for Meradhel, it couldn't be lovelier. Oh, sure, there were rumors of some vile conspiracy about this holiday floating about, but there were ROSES and there were smiles and there were hearts upon sleeves.

The priestess floated through the dwarven district of Stormwind, reaching up to touch her own rose, lovingly nestled against her bun of black hair. She had lost count of how many such roses she had received over the past year, each one delivered with poem or by hand from her now-husband, since the two had first started dating. A new rose every day, as he had promised her that balmy summer evening that seemed a lifetime ago, or perhaps just yesterday.

A splash of red caught her eye and Meradhel paused in her procession, eyeing the poor, lost rose laying upon the cobblestones. Whoever it had parted from seemed nowhere in sight, and it was a miracle the flower had not yet been trampled in to a pulp. Plucking it up from it's near doom, the priestess consider the flower for a moment before a squawk called her attention.

Her gaze moved over to the notice board nearby, and the raven perched upon it, who squawked at her once more.

"Oh, what is it, Gilly?" she sighed, moving over to the bird, holding her hand out for him to hop on to. "Did you want this rose?"

The bird canted it's head to the side, squawked again, and then hopped back to the board, bending down to bite the edge of one of the notices pinned there. Meradhel couldn't help but smile, her gaze quickly flickering over the notice of recruitment the Agency had posted.

"That is a fine idea, Gilly," she told the raven, as though it could understand her. Plucking the advertisement down, she worked the pushpin through the stem of the rose before returning it to the advertisement and board. A few minor adjustments so that the rose would not cover any of the carefully written text, and she nodded in satisfaction. Her raven, Gilly, flitted to her shoulder, seeming to observe her work as well.

Glancing over at the bird, she smiled. "Such a smart bird," she cooed. "Let's go find you a treat."

Life had been hard since Ralleigh had been released from the Stormwind military. Her father had been discovered as a traitor in his support of the Defias Brotherhood, and without him, the responsibility of taking care of her younger brother had fallen upon the woman's shoulders. Their mother had disappeared following the capture of Ralleigh's father, and not word nor sight of the woman had been given to the children in the months that soon passed into years. Ralleigh had been fortunate enough to earn pity from her former subordinates in the military, however, and had managed to rent out a small cottage from one such person in Elwynn for her and her brother. It made surviving more manageable, yet still, things weren't easy.

Her brother was quite useless. Though he vastly succeeded their father as a blacksmith, the young man was bored by his craft, and instead took to wandering Stormwind instead of doing something productive. Though Ralleigh loved her brother and his desire to see the world, he was sometimes a heavy burden and having to feed the both of them took a toll on her. It was exhausting both physically and mentally, yes, but it was the finances that were the hardest. Money was a bit easier to come by, of course, being that the Alliance was always trying to fund some kind of conflict, but with the recent damage to the city and campaigns being driven against the Horde, Ralleigh began to struggle to find work that didn't threaten her life on a daily basis.

One day when she had come home from clearing a band of troggs out of a mineshaft, she was surprised to see a letter pinned to the wooden frame of her cottage door. Taking care not to tear the letter, she reached up and gently pulled it off the nail that secured it and her eyes fell upon the first words:

"We've had our eye on you."

Her brows creased out of curiosity, and she continued to read further. The letter spoke of her accomplishments under the banner of the Alliance, yet specifically outlined where Ralleigh had acted outside of her orders to get the job done, in a way that while some may describe as "reckless" or "costly", others might describe as "clever", or "brave".

Still, someone had apparently been keeping tabs on her, and if she was reading the letter right, her work was desired. If the letter had been from a band of mercenaries, she may have stopped reading, but these people who deemed themselves the "Eclipse Agency" seemed more like an Alliance support group the further into the note that she read. She continued:

"We recognize your skill set, but we also recognize that you're lacking two essential things that truly allow you to do the kind of good you're wanting to effect. Direction, and funding."

"Funding," the woman said aloud.

Ralleigh was a little unnerved that someone had been watching her, but the promise of being able to live a little bit more comfortably was enough to glean her interest. Plus, if she could convince them to give her brother Ollivan a job, then perhaps they could move out of this damp cottage and into somewhere a bit more livable.

And no more killing vermin for coppers, she thought. Why not?

"Your contacts, should you be so inclined to take that first step, are Anwynn Augustine, or Aenlahn Brennan. Seek them out when you are ready."

The young woman pursed her lips to the side.

"Ready as I'll ever be," she said aloud, before turning on her heel and heading back to Stormwind.

Cas thumbed through the notices of sold and unsold items delivered by the auctioneers as she walked slowly through the Stormwind Trade District. Money and buying trends engaged her in a way that swords and sorcery just failed to do...which would be a strange thing to hear from a warlock of her caliber. Of course, being a warlock was a matter of necessity rather than choice.

As if on cue, the wrathguard bound to her grumbled an epithet in demonic behind her, his arms full of items on their way to the auction house instead of the large swords he much preferred. The armor and weapons jostled a bit, and he cursed louder.

"If any of those are damaged, Nix-ithak, I will take their cost from your hide," Cas off-handedly mumbled as she flipped to the next page. Nix, to his credit, did not grumble in reply.

Cas allowed herself a small, uncharacteristic smile. It was definitely nice to have muscle that did not require compensation.

Just before she made it to the auction house, the bulletin board caught her eye. Under other newer pages, she spotted the corner of the moon and wolves emblem she currently bore on her chest. She tsked softly, then rearranged the pages so that the Agency's call for recruits was now fully visible. She nodded, then turned crisply back toward the auction house. "Come, Nix-ithak. Money doesn't make itself."

This time, Nix grumbled. But just a little.
