The Anytime, Anywhere Messenger Service is once again pleased to announce our annual Armistice Day Ball!

First held nine years ago, the Armistice Day Ball commemorates the spirit of cooperation between the Horde and the Alliance when, faced with a greater threat, they came together to jointly defend our realm at the Battle of Mt. Hyjal.

All persons are welcome to attend our celebration and participate in a night of dancing, socialization, and the honoring of those who came before us. Dress code is formal, and we ask that you leave all conflict at the door.


When: Saturday, Oct. 4, at 7pm
Location: Seradane (Emerald Dream portal in Hinterlands)
Transportation will be provided for those who need assistance.

Cordially,
Derscha Kettlebomb,
CEO
Anytime, Anywhere Messenger Service

-----
OOC:
The Armistice Day Ball is one of Cenarion Circle's long standing traditions, and a signature event for the AAMS. It is a cross-faction event that everyone can attend. We've moved it up a little bit earlier in October than normal in hopes of avoiding hitting the 6.0 launch this year.

We usually have a welcoming speech, some food, then some free time for mingling; AAMS translators will be present for those who need the help! We are also planning fireworks.

We do ask that no one attends flagged for PvP. The AAMS hosts peaceful events and it's very easy to misclick someone by mistake. (We look forward to that being fixed soon.)

I hope to see you all there!

The AAMS' dormitory was the most comfortable place to stay, but Kezrin felt safer there than going back to her home. She didn't have any desire to be out in the public, not after her picture had appeared in that magazine, and she felt even less inclined to be at her own house after glimpsing the photo Mormel had shown her. So she laid in one of the cots after her shift, staring at the ceiling.

Thump, thump.

"Eh?" She lifted her head, staring at the wall. It continued to do an admirable job of being a wall and preventing her from seeing the source of the thumps. Curious, she left the dorms and poked her head into the next room over, a storage closet.

Olu was casually setting down several large crates. She waved at Kezrin.

"What's all that?" asked the goblin.

"Supplies for the ball. Miss Kettlebomb ordered some surprises for all of our guests." The tauren chuckled.

"Uh... don't those labels read, 'Dangerous, Risk of Explosion'?"

Olu peered closer at one of the crates. "So they do! Best not store them next to the fireworks, then." She picked up a crate labeled 'fireworks' and put them on the far side of the storage closet from the prize crates.

Kezrin stepped back into the hall.

She looked at the dorms.

She watched Olu stack a few more 'explosive' crates into the adjacent store room.

Perhaps she'd find another place to hide.

Kel sighed, staring balefully at the notice on her desk. The fact that she'd yet to make it to ANY of the things irked her something fierce, and the doubtless fact that the presence of a number of intriguing characters made it even more of a thorn in her side.

But she couldn't go.

The paladin and noble bit her lip and chewed thoughtfully, tugging the color from her flesh with white teeth. A grunt broke through her concentration as a child careened around the threshold of her study to connect solidly with the back of her leg. Another, nearly identical, impact almost knocked her over.

With two toddles clinging to her calves, the tall Blood Elf pretended to gasp in surprise, feigning unawareness of her assaulters as she dramatically slid to her knees.

"Nooo!"

"Yess!" returned the gleeful squeals of her twin children, Kier'Ali and Alysara.

Some things, she decided, were better than dances.
