Also the problem only arises when that civilized race is incredibly ignorant of how to manage their water resources. For example the Roma (Also known as Gypsies) have an ethnic custom that goes to the effect of always drink water from further upstream then where you bathe and never from a stagnant pool. Simple as that and behold they are still around and very numerous. These are by the way semi-nomadic people. Literally people who have no established settlements for much of their history seem at least crudely aware that water can become dirty.

Also you speak mostly of the Middle Ages, plenty of civilizations bathed (The Romans for example and the Greeks) bathed frequently. Probably because they had heated bath houses where as a peasant in the 1300's had a cold stream. Also as for beer drinking, that is primarily why Northern Europeans drank beer. As they lacked any knowledge of sanitation or sewer systems or what microbes were. Also they had a tendency to not do as the Roma do but instead drink from the same places they emptied the chamber pots.

Also consider that in the Middle East, North Africa and elsewhere Alcohol was forbidden completely (As per dictates of Islam since the year 600 or there abouts) at the exact same time that the Europeans were chugging booze instead of water. Yet at that time period we could argue the dry sober culture was probably far more sophisticated and well off then it's European counterpart. So actually this idea of not drinking much water since the dawn of civilization is a bit misleading.

As for washing with water as opposed to paper, The Romans used a sponge on a stick, and they were pretty much the pinnacle of European civilization until modern times. How about that for a fair compromise?
