Exploring the world is fine, experiencing other cultures is 'odd' for a Kaldorei. The big thing is that you have to understand the cultural norms explained in the OP. If your elf chooses to turn his back on those teachings they still have some play in his life (such as, as you said, Elune worship being drilled into him). Hundreds of years of doing things one way results in very ingrained habits.

In other words don't ignore the culture he comes from, let it influence him, even if he chooses to run from it and try and live with another race.

what im getting at is that my rogue is not a traditional night elf and maybe that makes him strange to some ppl but i dont think hes a purple human


You're equating 'non-traditional' meaning 'purple human'.

A 'non-traditional' Kaldorei still has some of their culture, behavior and history embedded in them. This is a change, remember. This is similar to immigrants, five or six years after being in a different country and many still don't or won't fully convert. You wouldn't except every Kaldorei trope to be defied.

A 'purple human' effectively has -no- tie in to the Kaldorei culture.

So if your Kaldorei likes living in Stormwind... there'd still likely be something he dislikes. He may dislike the cobblestones, the loud noises or he may prefer to be near the harbor, where there is wide open areas. Why? Because that's what he's been used to for a few hundred years.

Think about it like you IRL were suddenly dropped in the middle of France. You may like it there... but there would be difficulties you'd have adjusting and just living there period.
