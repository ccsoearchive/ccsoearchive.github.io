Sounds like alot of thought went into this. Here are my two cents:

You need something to dynamically balance out the sides. I think we can all agree Tenacity in Wintergrasp was a bust and the new system in Tol Barad is too restricting for something as flowing as Gilneas. Instead we need a new system that helps the zone flow without it being overpowering like Tenacity.

My main concern with this system is it has to allow healers to be able to work alone and still capture objectives. Too many times I would go into Wintergrasp and have to follow the angry mob to the front gates instead of defending the towers like I wanted to because I couldn't kill the vehicles on my own. So onward to my idea....

It is mentioned that it will focus on a npc war of such and that to capture a part of the city you must kill a boss. A simple way to balance this is to increase the number of enemy units whenever the amount of players goes up. Say an alliance player strolls into the zone, inside one of the Horde controlled zones a new unit of Forsaken Blight Spreaders appears to patrol the area, leaving little gas mines as they go as well a new unit of Forsaken Soldiers goes on the offensive. Now it is harder for the Alliance to attack/defend but they have another person to help out. As well that lone Horde healer who's getting out numbered say 100 to 1 will have an army of NPCs on his back whereas the Alliance will only have a few but alot of players to counter. Once the zone becomes filled with 200+ players, the NPCs will no longer be steamrolled out of the way like those in AV but instead will actually fight back with decent pressure.

The exact scaling would be up to Blizzard but I would imagine that increasing per player would be demanding and instead per 10 players would make more sense. Simply walking into the zone to spawn more guys would be difficult programming, instead maybe have a check in point where you talk to some guy and you gain a buff. Once talking to the NPC it ques the server to spawn more guards. If you give the buff a duration, it stops players from just AFKing in the middle and allows for a quick break once and a while. An hour duration should work and of course losing the buff once you zone out.

The most important of this "Time-card" system is making sure the addition of the buff out weighs the adding of more guards. One way to do so is to make the guards have something like 300k each and deal a crap ton of damage then let the buff be something like "Take 90% less damage from NPCs and deal 100% more damage to NPCs as well you heal NPCs for 120% more". The pvp side is unaffected whereas it makes it possible to kill the gaurds.

Finally this shines well on pvp healers because they can simply follow a patrol and heal them on defense or stick with an assault group on offense.

TL;DR? Quick summary: Add more NPCs to one faction when more of the enemy faction arrives in the zone to balance out the playing field.

Just a side note: One of the rewards you can add is mounts. Who doesnt love mounts? I am personally still trying to get the Talbuks from the Halaa rewards.
