Addition: With concerns for off hour times and server imbalance. One solution could be to set a limit to how long a faction can hold the city. So battles could start at any time but if one side holds for to long the city reverts after a set period of like 2-4 hours.

Or add a debuff to the holder after a set amount of time. That way if one side holds it for a long amount of time it would give the other faction a shot at taking it.


Just skipped through a lot of the posts and really love the idea, but the idea of a debuff for whoever controls the city for too long is boring and overused. Instead, add something like reinforcements. Lets say, the leader of the attack has called in for reinforcements to break the stalemate.

"A unit from Ironforge's Siege Brigade should be arriving in three hours."
Every hour, a different unit comes to assist the attackers.

1 hour - Airborne Medic unit arrives: Friendly player corpses become interactive, by clicking on the body of a fallen player (while out of combat), you shoot a flare into the air. A goblin/gnome Airborne Medic parachutes down and shocks the player back to life. The use is instant and the rez is instant because it takes a short time for the Medic to land and he can be killed.

2 hours - Something :D

3 hours - Siege Unit arrives: Attackers can receive an on-use item from the Siege Unit leader which can be used to place a ground-target AoE or circle, which will call in artillery from the Siege Units. Destructive if attacking players coordinate it's use. Increased damage vs enemy defenses such as turrets.

4 hours - Elite Unit arrives: When a section is taken, a special elite unit will protect it.. for example, for the Horde: Gob Squad, or some elite Forsaken unit. This would allow for an outnumbered attacker to attack other sections, as the NPC's would be sufficient guards for already taken sections.

Which units, what they do, and how powerful they are is debatable, but would be an improvement over a flat out debuff for the defender and would prove to be more interesting.

I am terrible at ideas or a sense of balance, but anything would be better than throwing in a debuff.
