Is festering to only be used to renew diseases right before they fall off?


You'll use it whenever you have Blood/Frost pair of runes up. The main goal, for single target, is to bridge the gap between the duration of your diseases and Outbreak's cooldown. It also converts the rune pairs into Death Runes, which you'll use on Scourge Strikes.

01/03/2012 01:46 PMPosted by Larthi
Scourge strike is my "obliterate"- do I use it every time I have the runes for it?


Yes, unless you're either a) Overcapping Runic Power or b) Stacking Shadow Infusion on your ghoul via Death Coil.

Lets say Timmy is in Superman mode and my runic power is capped- do I still keep launching out deathcoils or do I wait till Timmy goes back to normal then death coil?


Normally you'll keep launching them, but it's also a good idea to start hording RP (assuming you don't grossly overcap) right before DT falls off. Assuming you have at least 1 point in Runic Power Master (and you should), a nearly full RP bar should allow you to quickly build up 3 stacks of Shadow Infusion.

Where to begin?

First of all, the last time we checked on the sim, it was bugged and granting Festering Strike and Scourge Strike far too much Crit (as in 16% each).
Secondly, it also appears to be granting the tentacle proc off Gurthalak large amounts of Crit too. Skygrass came in at 22.8%, I think.
Thirdly, the sim does not account for AMS soaking- a tactic that automatically inflates the value of Mastery over Crit.

Finally, any fight with cleaves/AoE or target swapping will place Mastery at a higher value due to stronger diseases, DnD, etc.

I have been running LFR*s a good chunk of today with both a crit build and a mastery build to test dps numbers and have seen consistent dps increases going crit, going alongside all my sim feedback.


This is where you lose me.
You have to realize that anecdotal evidence isn't much to go on in the first place.
But saying that you've noticed a consistent DPS increase from Crit to Mastery? That's not plausible. By "consistent", I'm assuming you mean an increase in the hundreds, if not the thousands. The difference between Crit and Mastery for Unholy, is far smaller than this.

Even if we end up being wrong and Crit slightly outweighs Mastery on single target, it won't be a noticeable increase to switch reforging priorities. There's literally a 0.1 DPS per point difference between the two on single target.

To provide an example, in full BiS gear we have around 500 stats of leeway (thanks Tsukiyuri!) to reforge to either Crit or Mastery. That's literally a 50 DPS gain, either way, assuming Crit outweighs Mastery.
The fact that they are currently so close to each other in value with Gurthalak is probably why there is a debate amongst the Unholy DK community in the first place.

BLF vs Creche (same version for each) Which is better and why? Given that ive changed my stat priority to weigh with myself using Gurth, does this mean the crit proc is worse than it already was as UH dks dont have alot of crit to begin with to make it noticeable? And with BLF being a 25 - 30 sec ICD with a 1:1 ratio with AP does it make it a better choice for Unholy with us being given 25% increased str?


Interesting question.

To begin with, no- the Crit proc from Creche does not get worse. It's not that Crit's value as a stat went down with Gurth. Mastery's value simply went up.

I think we did some rudimentry modeling, and estimated Creche to be very slight DPS increase over BLF on single target. This is, of course, assuming you have similar iLevels for both.
However, something we didn't account for was that BLF's procs can and will proc Gurth tentacles. Furthermore, BLF's value is also inflated during any AoE scenario. The LFR version of the trinket did a whopping 7% of my damage during our first heroic Yor'sahj kill.

The good news is that they're relatively close to one another. Personally, I prefer BLF due to not being able to "waste" the proc (compared to Creche which, if you're unlucky, can proc during periods of movement) and due to its obvious superiority on AoE/cleave fights.
