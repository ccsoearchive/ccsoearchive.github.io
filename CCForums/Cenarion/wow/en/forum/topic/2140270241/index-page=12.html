Hi Bloodglas,

While crit does operate on a percentage of attacks, it doesn't necessarily mean that it's a risky stat to invest in.

One aspect to think about is that, over time, the ability to double one's damage becomes a large benefit for certain specs (ability synergy, talents, pets, etc.). Crit was only a weak stat for us before the patch because our minions had a low, unalterable crit rating of their own. However, after minions received crit inheritance in 4.3, the damage potential per point of rating significantly increased in value for us since the effect was shared by player and pet alike.

Another important consideration is that our stats do not operate in a vacuum. Realistically, you will have accompanying amounts of strength, haste, hit, mastery, expertise, and so on. So for example, whatever shadow damage your mastery provides also has the potential to be doubled relative to your crit percentage. That is to simply say, whatever reliability mastery brings you becomes magnified by crit.

There are many instances of interplay which ultimately leads us to our stat priorities and weights. When we were gauging stat values for this patch, we ran 50,000 iterations of the same simulated fight for the maximum amount of certainty and the lowest amount of variability. The results were abundantly clear that crit is no longer an afterthought, but rather, a valuable investment which will only increase in benefit. :)

Yes, that is my raid setup for expertise. The gear I have acquired to date has had little to no expertise and what expertise there is I reforge away.

And why do that?

Expertise's lack of desirability is really a two-part answer.

Firstly, expertise affects some melee attacks for Unholy. These include Plague Strike, Festering Strike, Scourge Strike (melee portion), and white swings. These do not include Death Coil, Unholy Blight, Death and Decay, either disease, minion melee or special attacks (they instead equate your hit as their expertise, which is very important to note!), and Icy Touch. So, if many of our overall damage sources including several of the highest contributors are not being affected by the stat, the overall value within the scope of our particular spec is diminished. The key issue then of 'just how much is it diminished?' wraps into the next part...

The other issue is a matter of relativity. Expertise has worth, but it's real usefulness can only be gauged when you factor in what the other stats provide, i.e. the alternatives. Choosing what stats you want for your gear or reforges is like picking teams for dodgeball: given the available options, you choose who will bring you the most benefit. Naturally, in gear, the qualities you compare would be haste, crit, hit, mastery, and expertise. So which stats are best and to what degree or limits? To make the choice abundantly simple, we have the benefit of weights. Stat weights indicate as accurately as possible how much dps you would receive for an additional point in said stat.

Stat Weights

Strength 3.65
Hit (to cap) 3.00
Haste 1.89
Crit 1.85
Mastery 1.55
Expertise 0.99


Through 50,000 iterations of the same simulated fight, we see the dps gains when each stat was increased individually. As you notice, expertise does have value of around 0.99 dps per point, but that places it well below haste, crit, hit (in reaching cap) or mastery. So, while there is value in having our (qualifying) strikes being dodged less often, it is not as much of a dps increase as attacking and regenerating resources faster, critting more, or having a multiplier added to your shadow damage for example.

Anecdotally, I don't miss having expertise in the Dragonsoul encounters. I'm a rather competitive person, so I invest a lot of effort and planning into how I rank within my raid for each fight. Truly, I find that itemizing and performing reforges that align with the stat weights are successful not just in theory but in real, live play where it matters most.

I did some number crunching based on a singular log (http://worldoflogs.com/reports/tt5x7gtbdbbizu6t/details/0/?s=997&e=1179).

(Note: In reality the numbers for tier set bonuses would vary slightly based on the gear slot swaps being made, but for the sake of a basis of comparison we'll ignore that, RNG, small changes in attack figures, and other individiual variables.)

T12 T13
2pc 262 dps 443 dps
4pc 455 dps 506 dps


I then loaded our stat weights into Wowhead's item comparison. I inserted the most advantageous gems (new ones from Dragonsoul) and made the optimal value reforges (meaning the actual amount of hit on a real set would be slightly different). Also, enchants and other profession augments were ignored since they won't impact the difference overall. I have assumed that Tier 12 is heroic and that the pieces from T13 are only ones that you specifically have available from LFR.

Here are the results of the 3 possible sets:

Set Gear Score Tier Bonus Total
1) (H) T12 Full 12,956 717 13,673
2) (H) T12 4pc + (LFR) T13 Legs 13,048 717 13,765
3) (H) T12 2pc + (LFR) T13 3pc 13,063 705 13,768


Let me just say 'wow', these are all ridiculously close. I feel this is due to to several instances of near equal trade-offs that occur by switching between tiers. For example, Tier 12 heroic has a ton more base strength but Tier 13 has additional sockets that can receive the new gems. It pretty much goes toe to toe.

So what would be best to do?

On any given fight, your contribution from set procs could vary for better or worse, so it would be hard to say based on that alone. What is more decisive is the current availability and cost of the Queen's Garnets, Lava Corals, and Shadow Spinels used in the comparisons. I've found these to be very rare and expensive on my server. Going back to my earlier statement that Tier 12 heroic has more base strength on the gear, I might personally go with set # 2. In that set, your strength bonuses are largely 'built-in' already without the requirement to make difficult purchases and you maintain a very strong 4 piece set bonus.

If you are able to acquire any normal mode Tier 13 gear, then I would expect to see a Tier 12/13 2pc combo being the most successful until your new 4pc.

Hope that gives you some things to consider!
