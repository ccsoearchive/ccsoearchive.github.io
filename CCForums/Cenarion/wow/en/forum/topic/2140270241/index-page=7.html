Sure! If there aren't any lower valued stats that can be reforged on an item, you will want to exchange mastery for haste. It's a slight gain, but a gain nonetheless.
This would happen when:
  • an item has only mastery and you don't need hit rating
  • an item that has hit and mastery and you require the full hit rating that item provides

The single point in RPM isn't to keep you from capping but rather to let you save up enough RP during DT to immediately build 3 stacks of Shadow Infusion after it fades. It's recommended since that strategy provides practical DPS benefit in every scenario. AMZ is still a possibility in that spec with slight modification in the spare point expenditures if you are running fights where that is a frequent request/boon that you enjoy.

Unfortunately, I don't foresee Blizzard modifying our spec significantly. Judging by the lack of changes in 4.1, it seems they're satisfied leaving it with the small RoR un-nerf. It's reminiscent of how Frost was put on the back burner in 4.0.3-4.0.6 while all the core work was being done with Dreadblade. Unholy may not be as handicapped as Frost was at that time, but I welcome the day when perhaps DC or the ghoul is augmented just a bit more to keep pace. If not for the sheer enjoyment of playing the spec, having near equal DPS options brings diversity to raids.
