    • For the sake of time, I would cherry pick which stats you would like to analyze. At higher iterations, it can take a very long time calculate if you choose all (like 'get a sandwich' slow) :)

    Your priority list in Simcraft will depend on which profile you are loading. Just for example, I'll paste some of the priority code from SIMC 4.3.2 (Release 2) within the 'Death_Knight_Unholy_2h_T13H' profile. (BTW - You can open any of these .simc profile files within Notepad/SimC to take a gander at what action list is being used.)

    (...)
    actions+=/scourge_strike,if=unholy=2&runic_power<110
    actions+=/festering_strike,if=blood=2&frost=2&runic_power<110
    actions+=/death_coil,if=runic_power>90
    actions+=/death_coil,if=buff.sudden_doom.react
    actions+=/death_and_decay,not_flying=1
    actions+=/scourge_strike
    actions+=/festering_strike
    actions+=/death_coil
    (...)

    You can see in the excerpt above how the conditionals are set. The code is pretty relate-able; nothing too fancy :). Conditions include for example if unholy/blood/frost runes equal a certain number, if runic power is a certain threshold, if you have a proc and so forth. So in this particular profile, you can see that Death Coil is going to have its priority adjusted higher when runic power > 90 and if Sudden Doom procs. Scourge Strike's priority is adjusted when you have unholy=2 and less than 110 RP. If those conditions don't exist, then it has a basic prio of certain attacks before others.

    One thing that the sim is not doing (at least in this profile) is making any adjustments for Death Coils during Dark Transformation. It won't eschew Death Coil from the base rotation just to have high RP available at a certain time period. It may, just by following the logical program of actions, have RP pooled near the end of DT but not design and probably not very often. So definitely, your live performance would vary from the sim if you use the pooling technique. However, imo, this would not likely be a reason for a deficiency (i.e. your results being lower than the sim). On the contrary, it should be observed as a boon in the DT buff uptimes. In a similar fashion, AMS soaking would be another sentient choice that could outperform the program.

    Sims are a fine way to gauge how well you're doing so long as you also consider your contribution to the raid and the circumstances surrounding live results.

    Sims use a RNG (each test being different) so there are always 'luck' elements to note. Iterations and presets are also very common and very sensitive variables. Deficits could also come from the rare sim errors or certain calculations being skewed (though the developers are always working to catch these). It's good to look at the profiles carefully and ask these questions about priorities and why they've selected the gear they've attached. If the profile and dev notes looks acceptable then we have to look at us, the player, and the encounter itself.
    What's understandable is that each player and fight is different and there's a list of flaws that enter into real encounters. Humans have slow responses and naturally make small flubs when it comes to other priorities. Or maybe it won't be our fault at all: other characters requiring our attention (rez me!), AIs, bugs, role requirement, or even other variables we can't observe directly.

    How much of a gap is reasonable in real vs. tested DPS then? It's only as important as your personal goal or the competitive spirit within your raid group. World of Logs, barring specials mechanics (like vulnerabilities/sploits), are a good way to see real, high-end potential, going well beyond the Sims. Find who ranked, their gear, their uptimes and skill uses. Sometimes seeing the possibilities will help to critique yourself. :)
