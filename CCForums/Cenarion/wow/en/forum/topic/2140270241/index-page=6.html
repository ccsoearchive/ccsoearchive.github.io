Fyury, sure, I'll run the DPS values of both weapons for you and your Orc guildie. I hope people with similar questions can use it as a template for their comparisons too.

===== WEAPON 1 ======
Shalug'doom, the Axe of Unmaking
I am going to assume a reforge of crit -> haste, making this weapon's stats:
Strength 341
Crit 137
Haste 91
Expertise 228 + 90.0816 with his Orc racial [1]
[1] Orc proficiency with axes adds 3 expertise. At 30.0272 rating per point, that's 90.0816 extra expertise rating.

Using our spec's stat weights, Shalug'doom, the Axe of Unmaking's value is determined as:
(341 x 2.96) + (137 x 0.66) + (91 x 0.86) + (318.0816 x .72) = 1316.638752

===== WEAPON 2 ======
Akirus the Worm-Breaker
I am going to assume a reforge of expertise -> haste, making this weapon's stats:
Strength 341
Mastery 228
Haste 91
Expertise 137
Using our spec's stat weights, Akirus the Worm-Breaker's value is determined as:
(341 x 2.96) + (228 x 0.82) + (91 x 0.86) + (137 x .72) = 1373.22
Our result then, is that Akirus the Worm-Breaker is indeed the superior weapon for him by a decent margin.

---------------------

Regarding the logs, WOL has some key buffs, uptimes and power gains to review:

  • Disease uptime (Viewable in: Damage by Spell/Damage Done/Uptime)

  • Dark Transformation uptime for the ghoul (Viewable in: Buffs Cast/Buffs)

  • Unholy Frenzy uses (Viewable in: Buffs Cast/Buffs)

  • Summon Gargoyle uses (Viewable in: Buffs Cast/Debuffs)

  • Runic Corruption uptime (Viewable in: Buffs Cast/Buffs)

  • (fight depending) Anti-Magic Shell uses (Viewable in: Buffs Cast/Buffs and Buffs Cast/Power Gains)


Low usage and uptime statistics for Diseases and DT would be a red flag for either poor melee conditions or a bad performance. Some of the statistics for Runic Corruption and Anti-Magic Shell could conversely indicate above-average class knowledge. Those things, in addition to the polychotomy of attacks (what they chose to attack with) would be some of the best starting places for character evaluation.

If you have any follow-up questions, I'll be happy to help.

Hi Lyria!

When you're 1) not capped on RP, 2) don't have Sudden Doom, and 3) your Timmy is transformed then yes, absolutely, you can pool your RP in anticipation for new Shadow Infusion stacks. If you have 1 point in Runic Power Mastery, you'll be able to aggregate enough RP for 3 successive Death Coils following the end of Dark Transformation (102RP with Runic Corruption).

How soon you begin to save RP into his transformation is going to be a little subjective. AMS RP gains and Runic Corruption will enhance your power generation to varying degrees. If you find yourself capping RP with a lot left on Dark Transformation, you can always throw out an odd Death Coil so that the RP isn't wasted and rebuilds during the remaining time. The idea is to let it cap right about the moment you are going to spend the Death Coils so there's maximum gain with minimum waste. Ultimately, though, whatever RP you manage to prepare efficiently is going to be a boon.

Beyond that, the best activity during Dark Transformation is to keep runes on cooldown using the highest damage per rune cost abilities first (DnD, SS, FeS) and using the Sudden Doom procs if they happen to occur (as normal). You may encounter an occasional lull while pooling runic power in this way, but consider it a well placed investment in buff management. You can also use Blood Tap if it's available to buy you an extra attack during this rune-power focused window.

Thanks for the kind words! Much appreciated.
