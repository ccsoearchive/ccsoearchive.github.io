Yes, it's a priority system.

There is no longer a set rotation like in the days of Wrath (i.e. where you would do A, B, C, D - and repeat). Instead, we have a list of goals and abilities to maintain which are in a state of flux based on our resources and what's happening around us (i.e. If X, do A. If Y, do B. Etc.). Your sequences will be reactionary instead of repetitive which, all around, makes for more fun and dynamic play (yay!).

Dark Transformation is therefore read as a goal, *not* the second attack (which would be impossible in a 'fresh' pull!).

Dark Transformation
Your Death Coils, asides damaging your target, will apply a Shadow Infusion buff on your Ghoul which is capable of stacking up to 5 times. The player can consume the 5 charges of Shadow Infusions on the Ghoul to transform it into a powerful monstrosity for 30 seconds. A huge factor in your overall DPS is maximizing DT uptime (see the Mods section for suggestions about monitoring DT and Shadow Infusion). It is key that you super-size Timmy as often and as quickly as you can.


So, an easy analogy is to turn Shadow Infusion stacking into a race. Do whatever attacks allow you to build Runic Power the quickest so you can Death Coil. Eventually that will culminate in the goal of DT for your ghoul.

The wisest way to approach priorities, and play in general, is asking "What is the best possible action I can take at this very moment?".

I hope that helps answer your question.

Crit
Naturally, the fixes applied to minion stat inheritance has modified our stat priority selection. Crit will now overtake mastery as a preferred stat.

I know I definitely missed every topic about said changes. As such I still feel like crit seems too random while mastery is straight shadow dmg boost, and we do a lot of that. Could you explain these changes or point me to somewhere they have been already explained?


Hi Bloodglas,

While crit does operate on a percentage of attacks, it doesn't necessarily mean that it's a risky stat to invest in.

One aspect to think about is that, over time, the ability to double one's damage becomes a large benefit for certain specs (ability synergy, talents, pets, etc.). Crit was only a weak stat for us before the patch because our minions had a low, unalterable crit rating of their own. However, after minions received crit inheritance in 4.3, the damage potential per point of rating significantly increased in value for us since the effect was shared by player and pet alike.

Another important consideration is that our stats do not operate in a vacuum. Realistically, you will have accompanying amounts of strength, haste, hit, mastery, expertise, and so on. So for example, whatever shadow damage your mastery provides also has the potential to be doubled relative to your crit percentage. That is to simply say, whatever reliability mastery brings you becomes magnified by crit.

There are many instances of interplay which ultimately leads us to our stat priorities and weights. When we were gauging stat values for this patch, we ran 50,000 iterations of the same simulated fight for the maximum amount of certainty and the lowest amount of variability. The results were abundantly clear that crit is no longer an afterthought, but rather, a valuable investment which will only increase in benefit. :)

Xeltes,

The differences in posted numbers will be mostly dependent on the fight mechanics rather than any inherent weaknesses of the specs. One can reasonably assume that a DW Frost spec'd Death Knight will peform higher on fights where there are multiple targets or bursts of activity and, conversely, Unholy will perform higher on more stable, single or low target fights. So the balance will teeter back and forth depending on which boss you are fighting at the time.

If you're curious for the sake of which you should choose, either spec would be a great honestly! If your raid has a niche or you have a personal favorite that you like to play, that would be my recommendation.
