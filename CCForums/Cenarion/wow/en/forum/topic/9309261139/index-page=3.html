“At least mine is prettier.”

“Hmph.”

Two packages sat side by side on top of the finely sanded table. One was wrapped in brightly colored paper with a fancy red bow on top. In contrast, the other was covered only in plain brown paper.

The gnome and the dwarf had been staring at the dissimilar parcels for the better part of an hour. Occasionally, one or both of them would briefly glance at the dwarf’s missing appendage and shudder. There was no way they were going to open the packages.

“How much longer?”

“Well, Nabbi, since it’s only been two minutes since ya last asked…”

Nabbi sighed. “I wanna know what’s in those! I’m DYING from curiosity.”

“Keep yer britches on. It’s not like she could come straight away.” Gertha paused to give Nabbi a small frown. “She had ta go pick up the X-ray goggles you left at the farm. It’s yer fault we have ta wait.”

Nabbi stuck her tongue out at Gertha, but went back to sitting and impatiently staring. Fortunately, Azheira arrived a few minutes later.

Ash entered the room, wearing her traveling clothes as opposed to her normal armor. She appeared calm and collected, as if she hadn’t a care in the world. Her gaze briefly checked her two employees. Finding no fresh injuries, she turned her steely eyes toward the packages.

“Has any sound come from inside the boxes?” she asked while walking around the table, looking at the containers from all angles.

“No ma’am,” Lrigknab squeaked.

Ash nodded and squatted down so that her eye level was even with the surface of the table. “And when did they arrive?”

Gertha glanced at the clock on the wall. “Just about an hour ago, ma’am. Just a few moments afore we called ya on the hearthstone. Nabbi’s came with a note. Mine came as ya see it.” She pointed at each package respectively.

Wordlessly, Ash stood. She merely held out her right hand. Lrigknab immediately pulled the note out of her vest pocket and placed it in the waiting palm.

The stoic paladin read the note to herself and then spent a few moments silently considering Noikona’s words.

“It’s a false insinuation. She has no intentions of hurting me directly. She might have once, but not since Rachel threatened the Blades.” She folded the note and put it in her satchel. “After witnessing her connection to the Blades in general and to Adrazel in particular, I have no doubts about that.”

“It seems the goat has turned her attention toward you two instead.” Ash turned to face her employees. “That is certainly something for all of us to consider. I would understand if you both prefer to leave my employ.”

Immediately Lrigknab and Gertha began to protest, but Ash cut them off with a wave of her hand. “Stop. We can discuss that later. We have more pressing matters to attend at the moment.” She nodded at Noikona’s ‘gifts’ sitting on the table, waiting to be opened as if it were a holiday.

An odd looking pair of spectacles were pulled from her bag and she put them on. Ash zeroed in on the pretty package first.

“Hm. Appears to be a flower. Seemingly harmless and probably is considering the note that came with it. You never know though, it could be poisonous.”

She turned slightly and focused on the plain package. “Damn it.” Turning to the two seated nearby she spoke harshly. “Get out. Now.”

Her tone was such that no arguments were put forth. Gertha and Lrignab immediately left the room.

Ash sighed took off the spectacles and moved to the far corner of the room. After casting Divine Shield she immediately threw a judgment onto the box with the red ribbon. The package was flattened into a brightly colored puddle of ribbon and petals.

“Not very spectacular, Noikona.” She paused for a moment to make sure there were no after effects before turning her attention to the plain box.

Belatedly, Ash realized she had paused to long. Her bubble wore off just as she cast another judgment. The second package exploded, sending fire everywhere. Ash immediately threw her shield in front of her, but was only able to cover most of her head, torso and thighs in time.

A fireball singed her hair as it flew passed. Another burned through the soft leather covering her right shin instantly upon contact. The flesh underneath melted like wax. Ash held back a scream, but struggled to breathe as the pain seared through her lower leg.

The explosion was over quickly, but the smoke lingered. Ash dropped to her knees, coughing uncontrollably, as she made her way to the door. The agony in her leg hampered her efforts. Sheer determination forced her to get to the door and open it. She wanted to collapse on the floor of the hallway, but knew she needed to assess her injury.

Sitting up against the wall, she looked at her leg. Leather and flesh were melted together in a disturbing combination of colors. The smell was horrendous. Dizziness threatened, but Ash’s fortitude was strong and she was able to remain conscious.

“Ah, fel!”

She was able to muster the strength to cast a few healing spells on herself in order to be able to stand. Using the wall for leverage, she propped herself up.

“That damn goat is going to pay for ruining my favorite pair of leggings,” Ash muttered as she limped down the hall.

Noikona Thulane Momlir from the Terradoctyle Peninsula on Argus

Born 5478 B.N. (Before Naaru) – Died the year 622 by the King’s Calendar

Survived by her now 6 month old daughter in the care a Naaru named F’tal

Noikona was one of the only known Man’ari that rebelled against Sargeras and sought refuge within Velen’s encampment. The Naaru knowing her for what she was put her into stasis until the Oshu’gun crashed on what is now called Draenor. Due to her unique nature the Naaru once more put her into stasis on the Exodar before it attempted to escape Draenor. Her stasis pod was jettisoned over Azeroth as the Exodar entered Azeroth real space.

With Noikona’s inability to lie and unnatural drive to live by her word, she became the security officer of the Crimson Blades with a rank of Tactical. Over the two years she was with the Crimson Blades she learned love and friendship, but her evil core routinely got her into much trouble.

Her body now rests in the Cathedral of Light.
