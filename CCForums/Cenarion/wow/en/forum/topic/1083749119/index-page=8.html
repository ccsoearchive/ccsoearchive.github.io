Rogues aren't complicated beasts. You have one main combo point builder for each tree, and later you get a secondary one. Finishers have a priority system. But mostly it is just good judgement. Do you need a nuke? Eviscerate. Do you need a stun? Kidney Shot. Do you need to buff? SnD or Recuperate.

You want to try and keep SnD up, unless things are dying really fast. You want to keep Recuperate up when taking lots of damage, or you need the energy regen as subtlety. You want to keep rupture up unless things are dying too fast, or you are assassination for the energy returns.

02/08/2011 8:36 AMPosted by Drwarwick
Given things are dying so fast, is it best to use a finisher regardless of cp?


This is one the decisions that rogues make when playing the mini-game that is combo points. Guessing when the right time to finish, and with which finisher, is a central part of playing a rogue.

To answer more directly, finish when you have enough cp and energy to kill the mob. Finisher damage scale linearly with cp, meaning the damage/cp is constant. The main optimization is damage/energy, which isn't an issue if the mob is going to do die and your energy will regen before you get to the next mob.
