- Assassination - Slow dagger mainhand, fast dagger offhand.


I'll use this for an example.

DPS = damage over time. A weapon that hits once per second for 10 (a fast weapon) and a weapon that hits every 10 seconds for 100 (slow weapon) do the same DPS. 10x10 = 100x1. This is an exaggeration, of course, but makes for a simple example. The rule is that slower weapons tend to have bigger top end numbers, while faster weapons have smaller top end numbers.

Special attacks do a multiple of main weapon damage. Say, Backstab, which does 1.5x your normal main hand damage.

In the example above, the fast weapon (10 once per second) in the main hand would do 10 x 1.5 on a backstab - 15 points. The slow weapon (100 once per 10 seconds) would do 100 x 1.5 - or 150 points. That's why you want a slow weapon in your main hand, to maximize the damage from special attacks.

Putting a fast weapon in your offhand increases your overall attack speed, as it is averaged with your main hand weapon.

@Acaelus (edit: and Yukiirin too, somewhat): Something to keep in mind here is that a lot of this math is mostly relevant only to people who are really trying to squeeze every last drop out of their damage. If you're a PvP twink, a high-end arena junkie, or if you're raiding, then this kind of thing can potentially matter a great deal. If you're leveling or playing casually, though, it's much less of an issue.

I won't crunch the numbers, but logically the answer to your q is probably yes: Dal'Rend on the main, Scarlet Kris on the off. But at lower levels the distinction is far less significant, particularly for that off-hand weapon. Two reasons for this:

-- One, you're generally not fighting things that live very long, which means you likely don't need Deadly Poison on your off-hand weapon (since it doesn't reach its maximum effectiveness until you have a full five stacks of it on your enemy); if you put Instant Poison on that weapon instead, then it actually makes a slower weapon more attractive, because the way IP works it'll proc more often with a slower weapon. (Don't think you can even get Deadly Poison till level 30 anyway.)

-- Two, in the Combat tree, the big reason you want a fast off-hand is the Combat Potency talent, which I believe you won't be able to get until around level 60 or so. Before that point the speed of your off-hand weapon is a lot less important.

Ultimately, you'll probably do just fine for yourself whatever combination of weapons you choose to go with. The twinkier you want to be on your way up the level ladder, though, the more you'll want to care about your specific choices.
