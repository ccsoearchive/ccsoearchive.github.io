i saw you mentioned slow dagger main hand and fat off hand for assassination. i currently have 2 hieirloom daggers balanced heartseeker. should i use them both or use one for off hand and get a slow main hand from drops?


A fast offhand will make deadly stack faster and consquently, faster IP procs. However, Mutilate will hit harder with two slow daggers. Your daggers level with you. Considering the short nature of the fights while solo, and even in lower level dungeons, I would be using the slow daggers because most of your damage is physical.

It is that way even while I do dailies at level cap.

what glyphs do you recommend for Assassin lvling. i am 63. ty


Mutilate is a must. I would also pick up SnD and Rupture (after you get Venomous Wounds).

The rule I use with glyphs is to glyph what I use the most. This is still true at cap. Use a little judgement, and you'll probably be right with the prime glyphs, the rest are mostly subjective.

Combat is getting some aoe love in .6, for those of you concerned with dungeon dps.

All spec will benefit greatly from keeping SnD up as much as possible, since a large percentage of damage comes from autoattacks.

And I'm going to reiterate that rogues are very weak until we get our specs at 10 and the finishers start working properly. Last time I checked, they had reduced energy cost and damage, but still required the full normal amount of energy to activate. By level 20, those problems should be gone.

Poisons can be tracked with powerauras, however if macros are too complicated, then it is unlikely that working with powerauras will be of any help. I believe poisoner is still being maintained.

Would combat using Sword/Dagger outperform assassination in a dungeon setting?


It has the most aoe at lower levels, which is all low level dungeon runs are, so yes, combat will preform well in that setting. Its not too much of an issue though, because tank threat is very low, almost anything the rogue does will draw agro, so be mindful.

02/04/2011 9:57 PMPosted by Knicked
Is there some kind of special timing when it comes to keeping your energy rolling?


There is nothing magical or arcane about our energy regen. After a while, you get used to the rhythm of your energy regen. That said, haste will increase your rate, and combat has less stingy energy rates. If you like pressing buttons fast, combat would likely be more your style.

As far as talents go, I see you've already skipped the very talent that can help your problem. Relentless strikes is in every single viable rogue build for a reason: It's fantastic energy returns.
