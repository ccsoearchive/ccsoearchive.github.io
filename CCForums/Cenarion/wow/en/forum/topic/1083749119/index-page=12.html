Sinister strike can pretty much be removed from your bar completely when you get Hemo. Backstab is your primary attack, use hemo if you can't get behind and to keep up the debuff if you/someone is using bleeds. Recup is your energy buff, you keep it up along with SnD when in rotation.

Sub has the most difficult rotation, and it is highly unstable. Try to keep Rupture up, but mainly try not to waste energy or combo points as a priority.

Most of this is not a core issue to you until you get to level cap though.

While leveling, its mostly ambush>eviscerate>next target. Don't forget about gouge to regain energy and reposition.

06/21/2011 06:03 PMPosted by Freerunner
Whats about facing higher level enemys? I always found that annoying...


At your level, high level enemies are problematic. In fact, at every level, higher level enemies are difficult, for rogues.

We prey on the weak or ill-prepared.

Evasion, well timed potions, and later a blind, bandage, Gouge, Kidney Shot and much later combat readiness helps, but we aren't meant to face out opponents on equal footing. Suprise and exploitation are our strong suits. It is not for everyone, skill-wise or stylistically.

Assuming that you are a new player, you will learn more about the game, faster and more comprehensively playing a rogue than any other class.

Well, at least, that is in my experience. The only other parts you really don't learn are healer mechanics (which really are quite variable) and tanking tactics (which you really need to roll a tank to learn) for pve. But in PvP, there is nothing like the crucible that is rogue pvp for learning other classes because we have at least a limited counter to nearly every class in the game, we can optimize our tactics to suit the situation.

The optimal solution is nearly never, "just beat the crap out of it".
