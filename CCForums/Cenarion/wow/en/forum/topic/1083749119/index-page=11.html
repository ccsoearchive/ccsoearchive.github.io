I couldn't find the answer to this anywhere else so here goes. I'm leveling combat, completely in 5 mans. When should I swap Wound/Wound out for Instant/Deadly? I've had trouble measuring the DPS myself.

I'm gonna guess after I can max out Savage Combat, or gain access to Envenom, I should use Instant/Deadly...but I'm not sure if it'll even be worth it then. Thanks in advance!


Instead of giving you direct answer, I'll explain the reasoning behind poison choice.

Wound procs more than Instant.
Deadly stacks based on weapon speed. The faster the weapon, the faster it applies.
Deadly will also proc the other hands poison after it has a full stack of 5.
Instant does more damage than Wound.

So your choice of poisons depends on fight length. Longer fights (long enough to stack deadly to 5) and you'll want to go Instant/Deadly. You won't typically encounter this scenario outside of high level dungeons and raids.

So my advice would be to stay wound/wound until your fights are lasting over a minute or two, consistently. I doubt that will happen until after you are level 80 and running the new dungeons. Before that, things just die too fast.

ALL THAT SAID.

The difference should you choose to go instant/deadly, it still marginal. Those optimizations only really have much effect at level cap.

Also to be clear, you'll never use Envenom as combat.

Hunters, warriors and mages level ridiculously fast. Survivability starts to be an issue in later levels for rogues, which is why I put the damage reduction talents in the leveling builds. Make sure you are using poisons at all times.

The difference is that those classes have great front loaded damage and control/range. My prot warrior is 74 and his health bar doesn't move, and if it does, he has victory rush to heal 40 plus percent of his health back instantly. I get annoyed when I can't gather more than 4 or 5 mobs at once to fight.

This is where you start learning to strategize as a rogue. Before the changes, nearly ever fight was like that as you leveled. Figuring out how you were going to break the spawn at an npc camp was a big part of learning to play a rogue.
