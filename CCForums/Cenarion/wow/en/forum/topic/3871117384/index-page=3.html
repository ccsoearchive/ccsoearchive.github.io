I have a question or at least a comment I would like to see addressed.

I have a friend who recently lost her rp partner. Now this happens. And it does for a variety of reasons. The person loses the ability to play due to financial or personal issues. The one she was rping with let her down rather harshly I thought. And at a very bad time, since she had just lost her mother to a long bout of illness.

He did not give a hoot that she was going through some tough emotional times...he complained that he was not into it anymore...after almost 2 years of solid rp. Just dropped it into her lap like it was nothing.

This happened to me a few years ago...and it hurts. Please people remember something very important. This is a fantasy...not reality...do not become attached...keep a distance...a buffer zone...do not rp exclusively with one person and ignore everyone else. Keep your rp fresher and more interesting by mixing it up with a variety of people.

Its ok to make friends, its ok to have fun and have a character fall in like with another...but do NOT become so attached you lose your head when and if your partner has to leave the game. Keep it light and friendly, if you have to break off an in game relationship, at least be civil about it and gentle.

I think I can see what you two are getting it, but this thing isn't really all into love...is about friendship, companionship, teacher/student, and other mild relationships that can spark into something else...that's what gets to ya, that possible spark...you know it's there, somewhere, but then the disappointment comes out when that spark is denied, even after years of RPing.

While I agree that it is better to let your emotions out, after all...that's how I RP...I put my heart, mind, body, and soul into every single post that I make (and THAT, is not exaggerating people), and I have more, don't hit me!, friends online than I do in RL...cuz, in honest truth, nearly everyone I know IRL are a bunch of !@#$%^-s that aren't worthy to like a bum's boot! But, I tend to make easy friends online and have a damn good time doing it!

That's what RP is a little about, having fun with friends. If you RP and don't make friends, something is up there...but do keep it professional. After all, it is still online, don't go about asking for private information and what not...that's just rude. If you want a true RP friend, respect the person on the other computer, just go with the flow...and if WoW becomes too expensive or you aren't into the game anymore (me and my ex-gf used to play WoW, it was how we met...on the RP forums in fact, and she left WoW after losing interest...I still play WoW cuz, hey, I spent my entire life trying to get this game, I'm not going to stop playing it for a good long while!), at least give the courtesy to tell all your friends as to why it happened.

After all, it's your game and your money and your time, you have the right to come and go as please on the game, don't let others tell you where and when you're suppose to be at or what character you need to be...have fun with the game yourself before going off to others.

And, granted, there is the risk you have the 'he/she overweight, 40 yrs older, and is married with kids' person on the other side of the computer...but don't go on about it all. After all, there is no test or something to see who is on the other screen...just keep relationships between characters and things will be fine. I tend to fall in love with the characters, not the person (except that ONE time :P).

It may not be the exact answer, but it is still an answer, no? Just enjoy the game, enjoy the RP, and make friends...but don't expect the 'relationship' to go beyond friends, and don't try to make it...just let it flow. It's not very wise to give out personal info. over the web, everyone knows that and they don't do it unless they trust the other person. Me and my two RP friends met on here, exchanged e-mails, and then BOOM!

I ended up with a GF and a pretty cool guy as a friend. You'd be surprised how things end up. But, as I said, don't expect much, and don't go looking for much. Just have fun.
