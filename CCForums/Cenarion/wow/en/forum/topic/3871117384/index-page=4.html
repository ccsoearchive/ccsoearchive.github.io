Well, Roleplaying with more than one character on a single thread is not an easy thing to do, as I'm sure you know already. I think I've done enough to classify myself as an adept towards the field of multiple character RPing.

Between their own characters? Well, I can imagine the reason they have more than two characters is because they are related in some way. Family, friends, lovers, bitter enemies, and all in between...so I think some kind of communication between the 2+ would be common...but there are others in the thread! Acknowledge them, damn it!

And on the matter of 'glorifying' the main character with the secondary character? Well that's a plot device that has been used for years since the first book was ever written. That would be the first thing I would expect one to do when doing more than one character. But I would suggest to not use it too much...a side-kick can only go so far on the matter.

Swaying arguments with strength of numbers? Well, I suppose that could be a reason/method...But I wouldn't let it get to the you, or the OP, too much. After all, it's still one person, and, who knows, maybe the person who has the more than one character has the characters as two opposites that won't agree on a damn thing. One can only pray :P, besides...from where I see most of it, the 'more involved' character usually makes the decision while the 'secondary/tag along' character just nods along, so *shrug*

And romance? Well, I think that says it enough...maybe the person isn't comfortable with doing romantic scenes with other people? Maybe the RPer has a significant other and they feel like cheating if getting into a RP relationship, or the significant other finds that as cheating...or maybe the person just created the two and figured they make an amazing couple and just goes for it...

I got a couple of...couples, like that and it all works out pretty fine. But I do also have characters that is open for romance...even more so now that my two fav RP pals (Barnette and Grimmrat) don't do WoW RP forums anymore.

Okay, so there's a few pros and cons I see to RPing multiple characters. Firstly, posting can be easier. If you have a cast of 2-4 characters, you will rarely find yourself lost for what to post as there will always be some relevant thought or action you can take in most situations. In addition to this, if bored you can simply have dialogues between your own characters and you can do this during a singular post. This is one incentive to RPing multiple characters. I think that a large selection of different personalities and appearances can be fun to roleplay too, as long as you don't end up RPing with yourself.

A con? Posting can be harder. Yes, following the same vein if you want to RP multiple characters properly it can be harder. If you treat each character as in individual and attempt to post their reactions to each event it will take more time/effort. In addition, if you engage in interaction with more than one poster, you'll find yourself having to closely read and respond to ALL these posters. So you can easily overwhelm yourself if you overextend yourself. People who cannot handle it will either fall behind in threads, or as I sometimes do simply forget characters and leave them as small unnoticed additions only brought in when relevant.

This is sort of what gets me. A character should be a fully fleshed out personality, it should be a part in the story. It should not be some tagged on “lockpicker” or something. A character you only ever mention when you need something small done, and then they drift back into the shadows after their moment in the light. This is fine for GMPCs, characters that a TM will use simply to progress the plot, but not for posters.

I've always had a reasonably strong belief that your relative power in a thread, meaning what you can be allowed to do or achieve or how powerful a spell is etc, is directly proportional to effort and length, when writing a post. Length doesn't immediately mean skill or even effort, however often times length implies more time was dumped into a post and therefore more effort. So with this, the more effort and length put into a post, the more I let people get away with.

For example, if someone wrote their character diving headfirst into a battle, slaying a monster then drawing back to heal an ally, recover and then rally once more into the battle, and they did this in one or two paragraphs, I'd not be impressed. However, if someone took the time to write a whole 1-2 page post of the exact same events, I'd be happy to let it pass. I believe if someone puts the effort in, they get a healthy dose of leniency.

So, in relevance to this, I think if you RP more than one character you should CONSTANTLY update them, don't let them slip into the shadows. You should have to put the effort in to keep them a realistic and active member of the thread, only then do you deserve to use their perks or traits as part of the roleplay. I don't think its fair to just pull characters out of nowhere to advance some personal goal, I've had bad experiences with stuff like that.

In general, when you are roleplaying you are -not- writing a story. You are playing a part in the director/TMs story. It is not your place to have several side characters you use to bring up plot points or shine a spotlight on your character. I'd honestly like to explain myself better, but I can't I've just had several occasions where someone RPing multiple characters has -infuriated- me.

In short, I basically apply the same logic I apply to everything roleplaying, the more effort you put in the more I will let you get away with. I've been in rps in the past where someone would bring in a character just to be like “(Posters Main) is so great! They are so nice and saved my life one time!” Or if their character made a bad call and people got angry, they'd introduce a character that would agree with them. They use random additional characters so that they can cover for their mains falls from grace, or any sort of disagreement. These are the type of people who refuse to ever take a dive out of the spotlight, refuse to lose, etc. THAT is what is infuriating.

- - -

Multiple Characters is almost exactly the same as “Special” Characters. As long as you can RP them properly, and only use them as a different flavour not in order to be “better” than any other poster, I am fine with it.
