I figured, upon hopefully what will be a lasting return to the world of WoW and it's wonderful RP opportunities, I would type up this...statement...guide...whatever it is you may call it.

But, before I begin, I would to give respects to the RPers of old that I am proud to call...friends. Whether they still be RPing, off or on the WoW forums, or no longer.

But, back to the topic. Now, I don't call myself the best RPer ever, far from it. But I am good at what I do. If there is one thing that has always kept me going both in and out of WoW, was my passion and love for the written (and yes even typed!) word. That is what drove me from my raiding days into the world of RP...the chance to mingle with my fellow writers, and WoW players in the art of roleplaying...creating stories with good friends, making interesting characters that seem like real people, and, most importantly, just having good fun.

That's what it is all about, really, just having fun. Take pride with your characters, consider the time it takes to make a person. Making their appearance, their background, their personalities...preferences, romance, and even their family and who they would consider friends (if they ever desired for them). Make them, if you want, and extension of yourself...after all, everyone will related to your skill in total based on your characters and RP style.

You could have the raging berserk Orc, the cool headed human rogue, the dashing, yet scheming, Blood Elf Warlock, or the indifferent Forsaken wanderer. Whoever you make, will be on your head, in several terms. For is not the duty and joy of a writer to think of their character and think of ways to improve him/her/it?

And don't be afraid to be a bit 'affectionate' with your characters. After all, if you have spent some time to consider this and that, finding pieces to the puzzle that is the character, and find ways to make them who they are, one should find pride and love their final piece. But one should also brace themselves for any possible criticisms one could face once unveiling their character. But, you should also accept and consider these second thoughts, for wisdom does say that making due and adapting...can lead to many rewards.

And their is ever the 'troll' threat. And no, not the tall, lanky, tusked beings who live in tribes and talk about the power of the Voodoo in Jamaican accents. No, I'm talking of the wretched, annoying, got-nothing-better-to-do 'writers' that like to get on people's nerves, or purposefully do things that would otherwise ruin and perfectly good story, or just plain out hate for the sake of it. Though, don't confuse this with simple 'newish' inexperience or people who don't have a way with words. There are differences and it takes one to know how to spot them.

And if there is another wise move, is to seek aid where it is most welcomed. Humility and honesty goes a long way, and in the field of Roleplaying, like any other classroom subject, loves those who are, and accept, education. After all, what's the point in doing something that involves writing when you appear as someone who doesn't know how to construct a coherent sentence or thought or setting. It takes some forethought.

A sudden thought had just occurred to me that would make a fine addition to this. It's a rather simple matter, of Roleplay itself. I've already mentioned that with Roleplaying, it takes a bit of heart, passion, and forethought to get things started...but what about actually doing it?

Well, for one thing, serious forethought is needed. For with starting a thread, you have many things to consider.

One, type of RP! Should it be open for everybody or if it should be a closed RP only for yourself, for friends, or for those you have deemed worthy, via a 'sign-up' thread that has a bit of a sneak peak of what the thread will be about? And also, what exactly is the RP? War, kingdom, academy, daily-life, politic? What is it?

Two, active story line! Wouldn't do well if you are just bluntly starting something with no basis to it. Though, stories like that are common and do make excellent character development. But, it would be wise to have a story line.

Three, active people! You might want to prepare yourself for in case there are some people that don't stick around for long. Many ways can fix this problem...You could wait it out, maybe he/she will come back. Or, you could write them out somehow, granted giving them respected time limit. That can be done by continuing the story beyond catching up, killing them, sending them away, or carry them along for the ride till they get back. Either way, can be useful.

Four, keep it entertaining! People like it when their story seems like one. Add a twist or two, make some surprises, have a fight to break out, whatever you think the situation calls for. Keep things diverse and interesting, keep people wanting to stay in the RP you got rolling.

Five, make the people count! No one likes being ignored. If a person is in the RP and is decent enough that you don't have to stop the RP just to criticize them, interact with them! Make them feel welcome, and offer some friendly advice and some constructive criticism. After all, like any art...Roleplaying requires a gentle touch, and a firm hand.

Six, when the RP is good, make a good ending! If there is one thing I hate the most...is when they take something good, end it, and then drag it on until it is just plain annoying. I know, I know...if you like a RP so much, you just don't want it to end...but sadly, all things come to an end. And at least give the RP the respect and love it deserves and stop it when the end comes. There are plenty of RPs to do, don't ruin it.

That's six fundamental rules of a good, maybe even excellent, Roleplay. And also don't forget to have a pretty damn good balance of humor and seriousness, people like a good mix. Also make sure that the two sides of the story (Good-Evil, Alliance-Horde, etc) are somewhat equal and have fair story-play so all can be well than welcome to take part in both, if they desire.

Also, a good thing to keep in mind...is the romance part of the story. Speaking of which, I think that will make a fine excellent next post.

Now, I have mentioned the idea of romance in the post above. I'm not going to sugar-coat this topic:

IT WILL HAPPEN!

I'm not sure you'll see a thread and not find a romance, or hint of it, within it. But, that's not necessarily a bad thing. It adds depth to not only the characters involved, but the story itself. It shows that it really is a story being written. After all, unless there is something preventing them from it, you don't expect a living being to not fell love, or lust, for another, right?

Now, there is a remote difference between romance and ERP. ERP, is just something most people don't generally like to talk about. But romance is fairly accepted. Though, the line between the two have been a matter that has been under multiple, serious debates.

Be respectful, is all I can say. No one wants to hear bed-side antics on a public thread, or even in-game on public chats. Keep things down-aged people. If you make it obvious a 'sex-scene' is going to happen...fade it to black. I think the message will come across.

But, on the topic of romance, it is rather simple. Now, I think people would appreciate the gentle hug...the tender kiss...the symbolic hand-holding...the playful winking. But don't go too far beyond that. Or, Hell, if you play the demonic or unloving kind...you might be able to take the extra step to show it by stating that the character feels lust...not love. And there are safe ways to doing that without getting hate. But, most of it will fall to the above methods of romance, only a bit more wicked and 'suggestive'.

Again, nothing wrong with a bit of romance or the lustful attraction...it should be welcomed, quite honestly. It's a healthy means of expressing the characters and their personalities. But, do keep it somewhat realistic, and keep a few borderlines in mind. After all, romance is a very sensitive topic in the field of Roleplay and should be used in the up most care.

Actually express love, or the subtle lustful moves, but don't be acting out the characters multiplying like bunnies...just ain't right for the atmosphere, and certainly not right for your reputation.

But, if you certainly must act that stuff out...Then I would suggest talking to a friend in-game that you know does this sort of stuff and act it out in the private chats of party or whispers. You'll be better off for it, and will experience a much safer, better, and funner Roleplaying environment for it.

Oh yes, and if there is one thing that I forgot to mention, it the quality of the post, that makes the man/woman! Above all else, you should be at least somewhat descriptive of your post. Show the world how your character feels, breaths, and thinks...give them appeal, give them breath...give them life. Add some touches...glasses, pens in the hair, some scars, tattoos/paint, a missing tooth, maybe an eye. Give them the best story you can for history, but an even greater appearance.

If there is one trick I have learned with writing...is that if you feel that your posts are inadequate...that you post one or two paragraphs while others are posting...say seven or ten...then, waste some space!

Put in as much detail as much as possible...mention the way his eye twitches when he gets annoyed, how crocked his lips get when he grins, how her eyes flutter about when flattering someone, how blood soaked his axe gets when cleaving his enemies on the battlefield. That's a sure way to rise the character count and make yourself seem more like an accomplished writer and more competent Roleplayer.

Even now I am demonstrating this advise by putting in as many words as possible. Details, details, details...they do make wonders in a RP, and can grab many eyes. Sure, this does sound basic and you probably already know this trick...but you'd be surprised at what I have seen. And I'm sure you might know I am talking about as well.

But, trust me, the trick really does work. Hell, if you even think you're up for it...

When the character is outside, even describe how the wind ruffles her golden blonde hair upon the breeze as the morning sun reflects off her silky strands, giving her blowing hair the appeal as if it was spun from the purest of gold as she twirls in joy upon the morning radiance. The simple hair of a country girl turning into a rich halo around the pale, and lovely frame of a mortal-turned angel.

See? Really makes a difference, does it? But, don't make it so descriptive that you have to post multiple times for it and make people terribly lost or have someone else post before you complete your train of typing, post it, see that the post above yours make yours redundant, delete it, and then start all over again. All things in moderation, but do try and make it adequate, at least.

Now, you've got a thread made, got the pals you want to come along, got the story line in your head (hopefully), and you are ready to take it out for a spin! Now, the one people you'll be dealing with besides the occasional dropping of a person, is how to keep your thread on the right track and how to keep it top of the line.

As stated in one of my previous posts. Being very descriptive, especially on the first post, is very important. Hell, making the scene and other important facts (whether you take control of one or more) can be very lengthy...I say that it should be, and it is, alright to go beyond the character limit and have to do more than one post. Makes it seem like you really did think things through for the thread (and you better have!).

At any rate, if there is one thing you should do is to keep everyone together. No need to be on next week while everyone else is still doing stuff the day before. I understand, some times you get impatient and want to do the infamous 'time-skip' to bypass inevitable waits for...well, something. But, just going out bluntly doing it can cause some serious hate. After all, some people were probably still doing a scene before the time skip, so be considerate and ask for permission and the OK from everyone else.

And, some topics the friendly priestess had mentioned was:

A) Creating and easing tension
B) Keeping people up to date on time/weather
C) How to build a scene for entrances

On the topic of the time and weather topic...well, all I can say is trying your best to make the stuff obvious...repeatedly state the time of day (afternoon, sundown, night time, etc) and the weather (bright and sunny, gloomy and rainy, etc) and continue to make it obvious so other people can get the hint and post accordingly. Some times, people can't get the hint...but that is when you go well out of your way and pop an OOC bracket statement saying what is going on. After all, not good to have everyone under grey clouds and one guy manages to find the one spot to get some sun. If they don't get the OOC hint, then they are uncooperative, and a person who doesn't follow a story line...certainly not a person you want in your thread, and everyone else will back you up with warning the guy/gal to play along or hit the road.

And for creating and relieving tension...well, that takes a bit of skill. After all, anyone can have some random people go out in a full-out brawl outside of a tavern...but it takes one with a bit of skill and know-how to make a full scale war and make it fit the story-arch.

Much like the romance topic, CONFLICT WILL HAPPEN! So, might as well get prepared for it, whether it is caused by you or one of the other RPers in your story. But it would be wise to not go straight into the action, it would be best to save it for the middle and last bits of the Roleplay.

A good way for that is a successful build up. Bring in some classic action movie sense...bring in some thriller blood-boiling suspense. Wait it out, plan ahead, and then unleash the hounds of war with a mighty roar of blood-curdling triumph! (sorry, I get worked up easy!)

People will appreciate and enjoy to take part, and read, a RP that has exciting twists and turns and have a bit of depth into it. A good mix of conflict, resolution, and over all good cheer and fun.

But, don't make every conflict a war...sometimes a sporting duel, a gentlemen's challenge, a toe-to-toe spare, or random bandit attack can cure a group of its 'tension-illness'. Just keep it a moderate balance and keep it reasonable.

You don't have to go through such precautions, but hey...it really helps, and it is the goal of a Roleplayer to make their RPs seem like a story. After all, what we do is a form of storytelling...only others can have a say to what is happening, too, and looking for excellent fun!

And then, the matter of creating the first scene for an entrance. As I have stated at the beginning, being very descriptive can go a long, long way. But what else is needed? What else can you put at the very first post of the thread?

Well, for one thing, you can put an easy means for other people to introduce themselves. Whether they be good, evil, Alliance, Hord, or whatever, put into consideration that some people may not be good at introducing their own characters by themselves, so give them the window of opportunity to join in, they'll love ya for it and make the RP just that more enjoyable.

And, depending on the type of RP and the sides it has, don't be afraid to describe the sides and the goals of what is to come. Don't bluntly say what is going to be happening all the time, but a bit of a warning of what is going on can be helpful. I'm sure they would like to know, in a War RP, when things turn from semi-calm to full out bloody battle. Though, hehe, a subtle touch every so often might be a slight bit fun.
