Hello again, everyone. This thread didn't have any posts in a bit, but, due to an unfortunate event that had happened fairly recent and it got my thinking...of the VERY, MOST IMPORTANT, rule of Roleplay.

Respect.

That's right, respect. Just like life, respect is held in very high regard within the Roleplay community. This goes for the person making the Roleplay thread and those participating in the Roleplay.

Now, if you are the one making the thread, generally it means you get to pass the rules and regulations that is WITHIN your control. Now, that doesn't mean go out and kill whoever you want or start making monstrous things that go against WoW's lore, just means you call the shots. Because, you were the one that went through the trouble to start the thread, and you put every bit of yourself into making the story line and bothered to let other people enjoy it.

And, if you have made a closed 'sign-up' oriented RP, that means you can pass and fail whoever you want within your thread. And, if you make an open one, generally you are also within your power to tell someone to get off the thread if no one, including you, likes a person that is RPing, but typically the other people would voice their dislike first and then you'll have to be going out of your way to stop any arguments before something gruesome happens and then half a hundred people starts getting reported. Best to deal with trouble before it starts.

But, as the person participating the RP, you'll have to respect the thread maker and your fellow RPers. One way to respect the thread maker, is to not beg/demand for something to happen, no matter how major or minor it is. You could make suggestions, and then the person who had started the thread will decide (either by themselves or by voting with the other RPers) if the vent can come by.

But, the one thing...the one thing you should never, EVER do...is go on a rage and start a tantrum or do the ever famous 'I quit' phrase. That is just a sure fire way of saying "I'm a little kid, and I won't do this unless I get MY way! RAAAAAWWWWWWWR!". And it can give off a very, very consequential ripples that will just look badly onto you. And, if anyone ever sees this 'event'...then oh boy you might as well not come onto the forums for a good bit, because it is very unlikely people will like to see you on their threads.

Anyways, just as the wise words of Maroon 5 in the song 'She Will be Loved':

It's not rainbows and butterflies, it's compromise that moves us along.

So, just remember people, respect others and you'll get respect. And, as I have said before, respect is held in very high regard.

And, for a bit of a personal note. I will not stand disrespect AT ALL! I don't care if it's on my thread or another person's thread...be sure to hear about it FROM ME. Even if it is on a thread I am not participating in...if the disrespectful event has happened recently...I will raise Hell about it.

*Dusts self off* I apologize for that, I am a very impassioned person with very, very strong connections to my emotions. I would never make it to be a Jedi...and even though I got a mind of one, I couldn't find the heart to be a Sith either. Oh well, there is always conundrums...

I digress, I'll be thinking of new topics to type down and will be eagerly waiting for any suggestions or comments that y'all may have for me. Peace

Quite suddenly, I think I have found a very excellent 4 step process that will anyone became a great Roleplayer.

R.E.P.P

Respect
Effort
Preparation
Play

I already talked about all four of the topics. Respect your fellow RPers and the forums, put effort into your projects, prepare a coherent and sensible story line and for criticism, and have fun while Roleplaying.

When you do this, you'll find your 'Repp', rep, or reputation (whichever you want to use) as a Roleplayer on the forums to be very good and will make lots of friends along the way.

So, remember. Mind your R.E.P.P!

Dear Vasdrim,

I am afraid we have all come into situations such as this, and sometimes, it nearly impossibly to deal with such people or find a solution to them. And the thing with people like that...Let me say it in a slightly modified words of Ben Kenobi from Star Wars:

"The Troll-Men are easy to frighten, but they will be back...in greater numbers."

Where you get rid of one, more of them will come, but one can take many precautions and have a way to deal with such people deemed 'trolls', or 'Mary/Gary Sues', or 'Flamers', or whatever you call people of this caliber...me? I just call them...jackasses and idiots.

But, onto getting rid of them...

There is no sure fire way to get rid of them, but one can do many things to at least try.

One of them is to put up the general rules of RP. That way, people will get a warning that such things will not be tolerated.

Another is to have a sorta of 'Three Strike' system. After all, you don't want to look like a douche and just kick somebody out for a slip...tell them to fix it up and make it look pretty for the sake of RP, yourself, others, and the RPer who messed up.

And, when the person doesn't want to fix there mistakes or just keeps on 'godmodding', tell them one more time to fix their mistakes and then, after getting a sort of 'vote' from the rest of the RPers in the thread, request them to leave.

But, if they don't want to leave...we do have the lovely report button to make sure they won't be posting for awhile. But, only do that as a last resort...try to teach them their mistakes first and help get them to a state of being a better RPer...helps the community like that, makes you look cool, and earns you a friend.

As I said, there is no sure fire way to get rid of them and the 'report' button should only be used as a last resort.

The things you shouldn't do, repeat shouldn't do, are these:

1)Get angry
2)Act unprofessional
3)Stoke the 'flames'
4)Go into full 'elitist' mode
5)Curse the man/woman out
6)Start insulting the 'godmodder'

And many others, but those are the main six. Do any of these plus anything closely relating to it will only make things worse. Just act professional about it and things will be for the better.

Dear Oraira,

I dare say, it is rare one meets a person who went through so many lengths for their character. It brings a tear to my eye and a joyous leap in my heart to see what you have gone through for your character. For that, I applaud you!

Now, on terms with your character. A sophisticated Orc? I must say, I haven't seen one of those before, or maybe too long to remember. Though, I think I got a Orc shaman that I suppose you could call 'sophisticated'...and Thrall himself might as well be sophisticated, thanks to his Human education, I suppose.

At any rate, I don't see many things wrong with a sophisticated Orc, so I suppose it'll be safe to continue as she is.

And as for the best way to handle her?

Practice!

Practice makes perfect. Especially with Roleplay. The more you learn, the more you apply, and the more you try, you find that you get better and better at what it is that you are doing while Roleplay.

Just keep on playing your character. Get rid of some stuff that you find that you don't need or has been proven to be impossible, add some new juicy details, modify a few things, and what else have you!

The more you practice with and flesh out your characters, the better they will become. There are a few lorewise 'sophisticated' Orcs, so you shouldn't have a problem with people complaining about your character. Just make sure that your research is true and get to enjoying your character!
