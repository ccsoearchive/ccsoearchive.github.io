I'm am gonna try to create a guide to help all the rogues out there get this quest done with minimal frustration. Sorry for the wall of text.

I have soloed this quest in worse gear then what i have on now.

First off I'd like to mention this is the first guide i have ever written so give me the benefit of the doubt, I'm tryin to help. Also I recommend you do this on your own before you look for tips, I know it can be frustrating but (atleast for me) it is very fun.

One thing that can help ALOT with this quest is the right spec. Personally i have seen it soloed most in sub due to its cooldown reductions and damage reductions. Although I am sure it can be done in any.

This was the spec and glyphs i used for the quest http://www.wowhead.com/talent#fZbhZcGcfduIRho:mVaimf

A few talents can be swaped out or ignored:

1. [ Enveloping Shadows] is optional, but i find it helps with Creed's ability [Consuming Darkness]
2. [Cheat Death] is also optional, I chose it because it can save you from costly repair bills should something go wrong.
3. [Precision] Last but not least, this talent is absolutely optional. I had to put three points on it because i could not reach the hit cap other wise.

Next thing to do is prepare for the fight.

Start off by farming some Rogue's Draught by Pickpocketing lvl 80 and above mobs. I recommend going to the beach in Twilight Highlands and farming off of them.
You can find a good pickpocket farming macro here: http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2267588004

If you can't get your thieving little hands on those you can get Mythical Healing Potion from alchemists or on your local auction house

Also try to get any food or flask buffs you can.

Next thing is to stock up on First Aid bandages
Dense Embersilk Bandage (These will be the best ones to use.)
Heavy Embersilk Bandage (next best bandages)

These aren't required, I didn't use them, but are drastically helpful. Use them after you use blind and aren't taking damage.

Next stock up on poisons. i recommend using:

Crippling Poison and Mind-Numbing Poison
The crippling poison can give you the chance to kite Creed while Recuperate is healing you. Mind-Numbing Poison Will slow his casting, giving you more time to interrupt his [Blackhowl's Will].

Though you can use any poison you want, I really suggest you don't use Deadly Poison as it will break your Gouge and Blind* (If not glyphed)

Last thing is, if you can, get some friends to buff you up before you start. Gilneas is phased when you are doing this quest so you will have to be buffed before you enter it.

Next job is to come up with a strategy to get into the city.

There are tons of videos out there to help you come up with a path into gilneas but i found this one to be the best http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AMdhDwdQHys

You will want to zoom out fairly far so you can see the whole circle.
Tip: The sleeping guards and dogs dont see you if you walk by them.

You will have to use sap and distract alot to get threw the guards. It is gonna take a few tries unless you are an uber rogue, just have patience and you will get there.

Next will come the actual fight. Again this may take a few tries but keep at it. If all else fails find a friend to help.

Strategy:

Number one thing to remember is, this isn't a dps fight. So no matter your gear you can do it.

For this fight you will need to have a few things up all the time or as much as possible.

First is keep Recuperate up 100% of the time. This will be your main way to survive so watch your buffs.

Next if you are sub and you decided to get the glyph of Hemmorage, you will want to keep the dot up as much as possible. It doesn't do tons of damage but something is better then nothing when you are kiting Creed.

After that keep rupture up as much as possible, again some damage is better then none while you are kiting.

That covers the buffs/debuffs, next is your control abilities.

Number one thing on your priority is to interrupt Creed's [Blackhowl's Will] This ability hits for 18,000-21,000 damage and drains 100% of your energy. If you don't interrupt this one ability it can be very hard to kill him.

Next is to stay out of his [Consuming Darkness] This ability will leave a purple AoE on the ground that will deal damage to you. This ability isn't interruptable by kick. It shouldn't be too terribly hard to avoid but stay aware of where you are standing.

At about 80% health? (Not quite sure about this one) he will transform into a dragonkin which will increase his damage done and grant him the ability, [Shadow Breath] which does 13,000-16,000 damage in a cone infront of him. Don't waste kick or gouge on this, you can just run behind him to avoid it.

If you use Smoke Bomb to stop his [Blackhowl's Will] or [Consuming Shadows] Creed will gain a buff [Blackhowl's Rage] incresaing his damage by 100%. Make sure you have room between you and him to let this buff fall off.

On this fight Gouge and Kidney Shot will be your best friends. You will want to use gouge on CD to stop casts and give your recup that much more time to heal you. Kidney Shot does have diminishing returns so use it wisely.

Use blind on cooldown. I cannot stress how important this is. Blind will give you a chance to use your bandages and let your recup heal you. Plus the bonus to being sub is blind will have a two min cd rather then three, letting you heal more often.

Use Shadow Dance when ever possible. Use ambush and Premeditation during this time. (make sure you are able to stun him for a full 6 second kidney shot before you use it) Because ambush gives you three combo points you can use this time to quickly get recup back up if it fell off.

Next bit is your Survivability skills.

Use Evasion on cooldown. This will give you a few seconds to go head to head with him with out the risk of getting wrecked.

In this case Disarm does actually reduce his melee damage by quite a bit, so use it often.

Next is Cloak of Shadows. I don't believe this stops his [Blackhowl's Will] but it does stop this [Consuming Shadows]. Plus if you have it glyphed it is a 40% damage reduction. Try and use it when possible but its not too important.

Last is Combat Readiness. This one is your least important survival skill especially because you can't use evasion.

After that it's up to you to kill him.

Like i said it isn't easy but you will get it if you keep at it, so keep trying.

Hope this guide helps some people.

Fen.
