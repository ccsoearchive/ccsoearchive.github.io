Thanks, Jarluz. I'm glad you explained why you were being negative. I appreciate you being concerned that me and the others here might be disappointed and you were trying to protect us from that with your Logic. I really do think thats a kind thing for you to try and do.

I've got a pretty thick skin though. Its something you develop over time, I guess. When life throws you disappointment, or people criticize what you want to do with your time, you have a choice.

You can just stop.

Or you can pick yourself up and decide its not going to stop you from having your own kind of fun. My choice (obviously) is to just look at the bright side of life (if you will) and do what I like doing. In this case, what I like to do is come up with ideas. Its like daydreaming to me. I've always liked taking ideas and seeing if I could make them real.

You told me your age and I'll tell you a secret too. Its something I've never said on these boards before. I'm older than most of the people who play Warcraft. A lot older.

And I've has a long career. I used to design new clinical programs for hospitals all over the country. It was my job to do just what I'm doing right now here on this Warcraft board - I used to help other people come up with ideas. We'd sit down and think of things we could do that we'd never done before. We dreamed up things like wings for HIV and cancer and transplant patients that used special positive pressure air handling systems to protect these immunodeficient people from the dangerous infections that run rampant through medical facilities. We did a lot of other stuff too.

The reason I mention it, is that when I had that job, a lot of people used to say things like you did earlier. That our ideas would never get anywhere. That medicine was all about money and the board of directors would never buy in.

But you know what? They did sometimes. They key was something I think you are beginning to grasp... that you have to put it in terms that they understand.

Does Blizzard see dollar signs?

Good! Than you know what they want.

So the job now is to show them that RP players bring in dollars. How many dollars? Well, we don't know that exactly, and they don't either. But I'll bet we can make them start wondering about that. Maybe wondering enough about it that they will hire someone to do for their gaming company what I used to do for my hospital company.

You see, it was not only my job to come up with good ideas, but it was also my job to sell those ideas to senior management. So I had to figure out how much money a particular kind of patient was worth. That may not sound like the most humanitarian way to look at caring for people, but thats how I sold my ideas (and made things happen that made patients well)

Someone was saying earlier that if there was one thing Blizzard could do to push RP in Warcraft it would be to have an RP Czar.

I'm coming around to agreeing with that. It would be that persons job to find out the answer to the question you brought up - how many players of WoW care about the RP aspects of the game (even a little) and how much do these players value that aspect of the game.

Once the "Czar" figured that out, the rest is simple math. How much would Blizzard make for every dollar invested in RP enhancements.

Does that fit in with your rational way of looking at things Jarluz?

If so - see? You sparked a good idea! Maybe you're more creative than you give yourself credit for. Y

The problem isn't the number of us, the problem is the number of us who are vocal. This thread, and the EU thread? Changing that. Which is wonderful. So pitch in, and be vocal about what you want as an RPer.

And, if Blizz were to start listening to RPers more, more RPers would find their way to WoW -- I'm sure of it. That increases Blizz's overall subscribers and -- you got it -- gets them more of the customers that they don't have to work as hard to please, which increases profit even more.

All we have to do is start being more vocal (nicely!). Blizzard will take notice eventually. And if Blizz takes notice with the small suggestions we've made, and sees how well that's turning out, it's almost a given they'll start including more and more for roleplayers.

That, by the way, means not just being vocal about what we want, but being grateful for what they give us!
