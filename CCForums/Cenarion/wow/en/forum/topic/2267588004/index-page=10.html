1) Macros don't do what you seem to want them to do. Once an ability is used that in on the GCD, whether successful or not, the macro stops. Basically every ability save a few you listed (i.e. Cold Blood) are on the GCD.

These macro ideas are automation. It's the game playing itself for you. What macros are meant to do is combine simple and generally mundane tasks like ability+starting autoattacks, or other things that are off the GCD.

ex.

#showtooltip
/startattack
/use 14
/cast Mutilate

starts autoattacks, uses your trinket (item slot 14 on your character sheet) and attempts mutilate if you are in range and have the energy to do so.

2) Even if these macros did work they are pretty terrible. PVP is a lot more fluid than that. You can't just dump all of your abilities all at once and expect good things.

3) You've listed some questionable abilities (e.g. Eviscerate) you should never, ever use this as assassination. Envenom is assassinations damaging finisher.

#showtooltip
/cast [@focus, help, nodead]; Tricks of the Trade; [@targettarget, help, nodead] Tricks of the Trade; Tricks of the Trade

Casts TotT at your focus if you have one, at your enemys target if its targeting an ally, or on the targeted party member.


This may have been brought up, but this macro has an extra semi-colon in it and won't work if you copy/paste it.

It is the one right after the first bracketed condition: [@focus, help, nodead]; just remove that semi-colon at the end and it works.

The corrected one, if you want to copy:
#showtooltip
/cast [@focus, help, nodead] Tricks of the Trade; [@targettarget, help, nodead] Tricks of the Trade; Tricks of the Trade

#showtooltip
/startattack
/cast Cold Blood
/cast [mod:shift] Rupture; Envenom

Personally, if I need to Rupture, I just do that and save Cold Blood for Envenom

#showtooltip
/cast Cold Blood
/cast Envenom

Just make sure you have the energy for Envenom. I don't think you get the 25 energy from Cold Blood until you cast your next move. You can accidentally use Cold Blood on a Mutilate, if you're not careful and that can lower your damage output.

I'll post a few of the macros I often use. Everyone's welcome to try them out.

Assassination Build:

Finisher-
#showtooltip
/cast [modifier:alt] Slice and Dice; [modifier:shift] Rupture; Envenom

Combo Builder-
#showtooltip
/startattack
/cast [modifier:shift] Backstab; [modifier:alt] Shiv; Mutilate

Vendetta-
#showtooltip
/cast [modifier:shift] Cold Blood; Vendetta

Poisons-
#showtooltip
/use [button:1] Instant Poison; [button:2] Deadly Poison; [button:3] Deadly Poison
/use [button:1] 16; [button:2] 17; [button:3] 18
/click StaticPopup1Button1


Sub Build:

Finisher-
#showtooltip
/cast [modifier:alt] Slice and Dice; [modifier:shift] Rupture; Eviscerate

Combo Builder-
#showtooltip
/startattack
/cast [modifier:alt] Shiv; [modifier:shift] Backstab; Hemorrhage

Shadows
#showtooltip
/cast [modifier:alt] Preparation; [modifier:shift] Shadow Dance; Shadowstep

Sap
#showtooltip
/cast [modifier:alt] Distract; [modifier:shift] Premeditation; Sap

Stun
#showtooltip
/cast [modifier:shift] Deadly Throw; Kidney Shot



As you can see i'm a fan of binding multiple actions to 1 main button configuration. I found it much easier this way to use the ability you want to use.



Holding down the modifier button and pressing the number you have macro slotted to uses the desired ability.

I'll post more if requested.
