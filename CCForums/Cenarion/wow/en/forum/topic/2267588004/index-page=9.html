Tricks of the Trade is probably one of the most unused skills by Rogues. These macros might help you use it a little more often.

I have 2 macros that will tie my Tricks of the Trade macro to a specific target I setup ahead of time.

First Macro named "TotT"
#showtooltip
/cast [target=bleh] Tricks of the Trade

That first macro (above) will be the macro that is edited by one of the two following macros. So naming the first macro is important.

2nd Macro to edit the "TotT" to and sets target for Tricks of Trade macro to the current target:
/run ToT = UnitName("target")
/run if not InCombatLockdown() then EditMacro('TotT', nil, nil, '#showtooltip\n/cast [target='.. ToT ..'] Tricks of the Trade', nil); print('Tricks of the Trade set to : ' .. ToT); else print('Cannot change TotT now!'); end;


The above macro is useful for me in battle grounds when there is another DPS handing around that I might want to cast TotT on.

The next macro is used to edit the TotT macro to /assist a predetermined target and cast TotT if it is available. Very useful if you know ahead of time a fellow dps you will be /assiting consistantly.

3rd Macro:
/run ToT = UnitName("target")
/run if not InCombatLockdown() then EditMacro('TotT', nil, nil, '#showtooltip\n/cast [target='.. ToT ..'] Tricks of the Trade\n/assist [target='.. ToT ..']', nil); print('ToT set : ' .. ToT); else print('ToT fail'); end;


So for example, I am running a BG with a warrior friend who I will be assisting often. I use the 3rd macro which modifies my TotT macro to /assist my friend and also cast Tricks if it is up.

Or if I am in a PUG and see another dps near me that I want to hit with the buff, but maybe not assist them then I can use the 2nd macro to set them as my target. Do note that the macros that edit macros can not be used if you are in combat.

You'd asked me to bump my macros in this thread (http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/2267486906) but I didn't read it until now (going through your and Rfeann's threads).

I'll answer a couple questions from above and post my new/condensed macros later on if interest is shown.

Note: Abilities are considered spells. so SPELLNAME means whatever ability you want to use.

Edit: Macros

@Isaputap No, but you can make a weapon swap macro:

/equipslot 16 WEAPON1
/equipslot 16 WEAPON2

tap it before dancing, tap it again after. However, you'll have to wait for one GCD (Global Cooldown) after each switch before you can attack.

@RocknuWhat do you mean? To apply the combo points to a new target, or to apply the redirect buff (and thus, the combo points)? Pressing Redirect should automatically swap your combo points to your current target (or another if you macro one).

Here's one of mine:
#showtooltip Redirect
/cast [@focus, mod:shift] Redirect; Redirect
/cast [@focus, mod:shift] Kidney Shot

Tap once to cast Redirect on your current target. Tap a second time to Kidney Shot.

Hold shift and tap once to cast Redirect on your Focus target, if you have one.
Hold shift and tap a second time to Kidney Shot your Focus target.

This isn't my Kidney Shot keybind, but it's not far away. The macro saves a half second or so of searching, which can mean the difference when you need to stop someone NOW.

@Tragick

#showtooltip
/cast Lifeblood
/use 13 (for the upper trinket slot, /use 14 for the lower)
/cast Vendetta

I don't remember if Lifeblood is on the GCD or not, but if it is this macro will require a second key press for Vendetta.

Also, Mahiko has a great video on keybinds/macros for paid subscribers on skill-capped's website. I don't mean to be an advertising !@#$%, but I can honestly say it's the most informative 4 dollars I spend every month - in terms of learning the other classes, their play-styles and the intricacies of high-end PvP.

Sap and Distract are both on the GCD, once a macro encounters an action that requires a GCD that fails to happen the macro ceases. Therefore you cannot just pile abilities attached to the GCD into a one-button spam without ever having to think macro.

Personally I have Distract bound to the same key as my Sap with a shift mod, similar to:

#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift] Distract; Sap
