That macro remove all the text when you are spamming is there a way to get rid of that anoying voice that says.."i need a target?
This is my macro template. Whenever I roll a new character/need a new macro I just re-write/append the middle section as necessary:

#showtooltip
/run sfx=GetCVar("Sound_EnableSFX");
/console Sound_EnableSFX 0
/cast
/run UIErrorsFrame:Clear()
/run SetCVar("Sound_EnableSFX",sfx)

The script(s) before and aft the actual macro remove the error text/sound effects. With this you don't need to bother running that script of yours every time you log in. If you want to keep doing it however, just omit the UIErrorsFrame line.

One of three things is happening:

1) default keybind involving Shift (since you say you've covered this, move right along)

2) Bartender keybind superseding your macro (If you keybind 'Shift+3' on a button then use a keybind involving Shift on your actual natural '3' key the actual keybind involving Shift will take precedence, even if there's nothing in that bar slot).

Ex. On my '1' keybind (in Bartender) I have a macro with abilities using nomod, shift, and ctrl. Separately I have a button bound to 'Alt+1' (in Bartender), if I added an Alt modifier onto the macro on '1' it would not function, the 'Alt+1' button is of a higher order than the alt modifier on the '1' key and would trigger instead.

3) Broken macro formatting.

Only reasons Shift would stop working as a modifier.

#showtooltip
/cleartarget
/targetenemyplayer
/cast Sap

You can edit out the /cleartarget line if you want it to stick to your current target, but that takes away from the macros spammability. Or you should be able to make it /cleartarget [mod] so that it will only clear target (hence be spammable) if you hold down any of the modifier keys along with it.
