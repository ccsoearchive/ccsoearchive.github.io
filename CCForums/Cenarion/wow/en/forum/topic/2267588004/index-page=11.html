Not sure if this one is in here, I saw a sort of type but wanted to add

#showtooltip redirect
/cast [nomod] redirect
/castsequence [mod:shift, @focus] reset=20 Redirect, Kidney Shot

This is a regular redirect with shift on focus as well as the kidney. This works even if you just want to focus redirect and not kidney since you have to press it a 2nd time for the kidney, so you could just redirect and not press it a 2nd time thus doing only an @focus redirect.

This is the best Tricks macro I've ever used by far. This macro (macro A) requires a second blank macro called TotT (macro B). By targeting a player and using macro A, macro B will be edited such that it will cast Tricks of the Trade on the person targeted with macro A regardless of who your current target is.

/run ToT = UnitName("target")
/run if not InCombatLockdown() then EditMacro('TotT', nil, nil, '#showtooltip\n/cast [target='.. ToT ..'] Tricks of the Trade', nil); print('Tricks of the Trade set to : ' .. ToT); else print('Cannot change TotT now!'); end;

Copy and paste the above text into a macro and name it whatever you like, I named mine SeTTotT

Then create a BLANK macro and name it TotT (if you want to name it something else, you have to replace every occurrence of TotT [note the small t] with whatever you would like to name it in the above text)

Simply target who you want to set your Tricks to, click the SeTTotT macro, and a line of text will appear in your chat log saying, "Tricks of the Trade set to [your target]". Now, all you have to do is press the blank macro (which now contains /cast [target='.. ToT ..'] Tricks of the Trade', nil) and you will cast Tricks on the person you set it to without ever having to target him.

You can't set your Tricks target in combat, since you can't edit macros in combat, and that's what the SeTTotT macro is doing, it's editing your blank TotT macro on the fly. So just remember to set your Tricks target at the beginning of your raid/dungeon/boss and you're peachy.

Here are the Sub [mostly] macros I currently have. I'm always looking for new ones, but I prefer to understand the meat of the macro before I put it into place [as my mind works like a programmer's]. I would love to either a) find out what some of the syntax meanings are [like the ! before a spell, how/when to use IF, THEN, ELSEs, etc] or b) find either a basic macro-grammar structure thread or at least learn what "language" the macros are made in so I can go read up and learn stuffs, but I'm a patient goblin [albeit greedy].


#showtooltip [nomod] Envenom; [mod:shift] Expose Armor
/cast [nomod] Envenom; [mod:shift] Expose Armor

#showtooltip [nomod] Evasion; [mod:shift] Cloak of Shadows
/cast [nomod] Evasion; [mod:shift] Cloak of Shadows

#showtooltip [mod:ctrl] Kidney Shot; [nomod] Slice and Dice; [mod:shift] Rupture
/cast [mod:ctrl] Kidney Shot; [nomod] Slice and Dice; [mod:shift] Rupture

#showtooltip [nomod] Shadowstep; [mod:ctrl] Shadow Dance
/cast [nomod] Shadowstep; [mod:ctrl] Shadow Dance

#showtooltip [nomod] Premeditation; [mod:shift] Preparation
/cast [nomod] Premeditation; [mod:shift] Preparation

#showtooltip [mod:shift] Sinister Strike; Hemorrhage
/start attack
/cast [mod:shift] Sinister Strike; Hemorrhage

my ONE Assassination-specific macro ;) -

#showtooltip [nomod] Mutilate; [mod:shift]Sinister Strike; [mod:ctrl] Vendetta
/startattack
/cast [nomod] Mutilate; [mod:shift] Sinister Strike; [mod:ctrl] Vendetta

None of these are particularly revolutionary or mindboggling. The reason I post them here, other than adding to the already stellar thread is to help people who need to save action bar space. With these macros, I have about 20% empty space on my "show all" action bars. At minimum, I hope this helps someone[s].
