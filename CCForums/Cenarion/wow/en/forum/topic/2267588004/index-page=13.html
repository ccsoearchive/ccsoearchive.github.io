/cast vendetta
/cast cold blood
/use potion of the tol'vir
/cast [@focus]tricks of the trade
This one's not very good. You should only be using a combat pot in conjunction with Bloodlust. So that means having separate keybinds for Vendetta/Cold Blood as well as a separate Tricks macro, since you'll use that every 30sec, or 4 times as much as you'll use Vendetta/Cold Blood. So there's really no reason to have Tricks in there since you already have tricks bound. and you're going to use Vendetta/CB at least once in a fight before you lust. The only way you could use this is if you also include the other 2-3 macros you'd need to do all these things separately as the situation called for. Of course, if you've already got those macros, it kind of makes this one redundant. Doesn't it?

Here's what makes more sense

#showtooltip
/use Potion of the Tol'vir
/cast Stealth

- Good for pre-potting and immediately getting into stealth as the tank pulls, and good for combat potion use as Stealth will fail because of it's no combat requisite so it won't interrupt anything and the macro will simply use a potion when in combat.

#showtooltip
/cast [combat] Vanish; Stealth

- Casts Stealth outside combat, but when in combat switches to Vanish so that you can use it as a DPS cooldown to get in extra Garrotes and restart Overkill. Requires some diligence that you aren't accidentally in combat when you mean to Stealth and use a 3min CD. It's uncommon in PVE for your combat state to randomly jump in and out of, so it's not a huge concern when raiding. Thus cutting down on the need to have a separate keybind for one or the other. Since when you need Vanish you cannot Stealth, and when you can Stealth you should never use Vanish.

#showtooltip
/cast Vendetta
/cast Cold Blood

- I know many people macro CB to Mutilate, but since the CD is identical to Vendetta and you'll want to use Vendetta as close to on CD as possible to get as many uses per encounter in, adding a little extra punch of a guranteed crit and a balloon of energy isn't so bad to the start of your burst phase(s).

#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift, @NameTank1][mod:ctrl, @NameTank2][@focus] Tricks of the Trade

- Tricks my chosen DPS target w/o mod, the main tank w/ shift and the off-tank w/ ctrl. Good for increasing raid DPS, or helping with snap threat on adds, new targets, etc. Whatever is required.

The trick with good macro use is not creating fire and forget buttons that do everything all-in-one without you having to think of anything. Those aren't good macros. They're overly restrictive and increase the potential that you're going to waste things. You need to strike a balance when coupling multiple commands together between efficiency and flexibility.

Using the one macro I quoted, sure, it's only a single key press. That makes it "easier" than the four I wrote. However, using those three gives you incredibly better flexibility. Which translates to better performance for you. They still automate key functions for you, but they do so in smaller bites to cut down on waste as much as possible by being able to mix and match or use any and all individually.

That's because it's trying to use Shadow Dance and Garrote simultaneously. You aren't in Shadow Dance until after it's cast.

Why would you Garrote immediately after you Shadow Dance anyway? Your target should be stunned when you're going for a burst. Which means Garrote should be used toward the end so they are silenced as they come out of the stun giving you an extended period where they cannot retaliate.

I realize mages can Blink a stun, but getting them to burn Blink before you set up a ShD or using ShS to counter would be more effective than wasting Garrote early.

Also, there's no reason to have Shadow Dance on the tooltip line. That macro would display Shadow Dance's tooltip with just #showtooltip.

I honestly don't see why people waste macro space on poisons.

http://www.curse.com/addons/wow/poisoner

It does everything those macro(s) do intuitively, w/o wasting bar space, as a small ui element attached to your mini map (or wherever you happen to move it if you'd rather), and it has added functionality the macro(s) couldn't come close to touching.

-Text/Audio alerts for poison expiration

-Automatic re-purchasing of poisons
