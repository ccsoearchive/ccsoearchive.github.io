Specifically, during tests against a Training Dummy, activating the macro will successfully cast stealth, premeditation, and then shadowstep, but then fails to deliver the promised opener/attack. For the life of me, I can't figure out why.

Im sorry to hear that, Im pretty sure it worked when I used i though... Im not longer Sublety so I cant test it out for myself however. I cant think of why it wouldnt work; Premed and SS are off of the GCD so it shouldnt interfere with the other commands. The only thing I can think of is to change some conditionals.

If you have the time, see if this one works:

#showtooltip Shadowstep
/cast [nocombat] !Stealth
/cast Premeditation
/cast [stealth] Shadowstep; [combat] Shadowstep
/cast [stealth] Ambush; [combat] Backstab

I didnt change much. And as a note, I used this macro to SS, the other commands are just there to make my life easier.

Not 100% if this has been covered; only briefly skimmed the titles of the subsections, but I'll add one of my favorites. This one's for Engineers with Synapse Springs tinkered onto their gloves.

#showtooltip
/cast Mutilate (or Sinister Strike, accordingly)
/cast [Name of Gloves or # of gloves item slot]

being that the bonus from synapse springs isn't significant, I don't bother to save it for any special moments like one would with some trinkets. I believe the cooldown is only one minute, so its a pretty frequent proc and you probably won't ever notice the cast being that mut or SS is such a spammed button.
