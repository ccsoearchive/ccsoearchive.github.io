Something Blizzard might not have accounted for with the stage 1 daggers is Combat's lost damage on Killing Spree. I've set up a macro to fix that.


#showtooltip
/cast killing spree
/equip Spine of the Thousand Cuts

#showtooltip
/cast [equipped: dagger] sinister strike
/autoattack
/equip [equipped: sword] Fear
/cast [equipped: fishing pole] fishing


Change Spine of the Thousand Cuts to whatever your 2.6 MH weapon is. The second macro is set to do this: If you have a dagger equipped, it'll cast Sinister Strike. If you have a sword (or whatever type of weapon your 2.6 is) equipped (usually after hitting the Killing Spree macro) it'll switch back to your dagger. If you have a fishing pole equipped, it'll fish.

I find this to be a nice quality of life issue. And after some testing, switching weapons to do KS and switching back on SS does not cause a GCD, if it's put on a macro.

@Fluor

I made some minor changes to Salamme's macro and it seems to work the way you requested:
#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift, @focus, harm] Cheap Shot; [@target] Premeditation
/cast [nomod, @target] Cheap Shot
As for the "near me" sap modification, try this:
#showtooltip
/cast [mod:shift, @focus, harm] Sap
/targetenemy [nomod, @harm, nodead]
/cast [nomod] Sap
/cast [nomod] Pick Pocket
/cleartarget
I'm not quite sure what you want from this macro, but this should allow you to use the "near me" sap by default, while pressing shift will only sap your focus.

Something thats simple yet effective, don't know if anyone will actually use this but I find it very helpful.

activating Shadow Dance causes about a 1 second delay where it changes from your out of stealth abilities to your in stealth abilities, this actually effects your damage output during shadow dance.
Before when not using this macro to then using this macro you really notice the difference.

So something to help me with this is these macros.

#Showtooltip Shadow dance
/Cast Shadow Dance
/Cast ambush

This gets rid of that one second that you're not attacking for can also be a big help to people who play with a fair bit of latency.

It can also work for other abilities such as cheap Shot or Garrotte just replace the ambush with "Garrotte" so the macro looks like this.

#Showtooltip Shadow Dance
/Cast Shadow Dance
/Cast Garrote

another that helps is something that's called a Pre-Garrotte

It just opens with a Premeditation and a garrotte straight away.

#showtooltip Garrote
/Cast Premeditation
/Cast Garrote

The same can be done with Recuperate.

Hope this helped anyone.
