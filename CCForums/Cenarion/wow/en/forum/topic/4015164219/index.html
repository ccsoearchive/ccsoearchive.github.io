The Maggot Lord looked about him. A smal team of Horde had gathered for this assault. Raoul was present, of course. Lord Aziel was there, along with Thelinna and TYrexus. Bloodgrief, Filuz, Yrkkh, Nocaclaw, Kunlokku, and Mialera the naga lady rounded things out. This would be good.

Raoul went ahead and secured a safe summoning spot, high in the Dun Morogh peaks. With the force gathered, Abominus spoke.

"Tonight we attack the Alliance clinnic, yesss. We hear tell thatGentyl ssshould be there to guard, asss well asss many sssick and injured Allaince warriorsss, yesss. thisss will ssserve to clear out sssome of them, and damage their capacitiesss, yesss. Alssso, we need fresssh dwarf liversss, yesss. I will capture one alive for my experimentsss, too, yesss. A few of you alssso want to sssup on Draenai, yesss, which we encourage, yesss." Abominus looks at Raoul, who is busy muttering about beautiful purple blood. "Alssso, we know Lord aziel will no doubt sssow ssosme chaosss or hatch a plan or three, yesss..."

" We will be fassst, yesss! We will be precissse, yesss! Our only target isss the clinic, any Pia who ssshow, and any who dare to try to ssstop usss, yesss. You know the targetsss, and you know what to do, yesss! Now, fly, yesss!"

"AAAIIIEEE!!!"

The strike team flew into ironforge, dodging the aerial patrols. A swift flight was all it took, and the Clinic was in sight. Many wounded Alliance soldiers were being tended, as well as other diverse members of that faction. Suddenly, flame and ice fell from the sky, with Abominus and the melee members dropping into their midst, slashing and hacking. Chaos was immediate! Scores fell in the first rush, and the surviving members retreated to a small home, trying desperately to pull the wounded with them and form some defense. Screams issued forth over their hearthstones.

Blood!

Death!

Maggots feasting!

It was Glorious!

As casualties mounted, Abominus looked up from Mir'as corpse, as Bloodgrief laid low Gentyl. "Carve me sssome of her liver and ssspleen, yesss!" The elf nodded, and bent to his task. Abominus finished stuffing his maw with fresh draenai liver, saving some for Raoul. Lord Aziel directed his powers at Alliance forces, his fellow Modas Tyrexus and Thelinna destroying large formations of dwarf guards as they rushed in. Mialera directed Naga's, of all things, into attacking wounded night elves. Novaclaw and Kunlokku slashed their way through Alliance response. Pillar of iron forces were cut down, Yrkkh seen cackling madly over their corpses.

It was time to go. Their tasks completed, the horde flew off, their triumphant screams and mocking laughter fading into the distance. The Alliance could only gather to help the wounded, and bury the dead.

Rhudran was...gone.

The Horde attack had come fast, then had left just as quickly. It seemed strange to Lahkin, but as more and more Alliance were accounted for and Rhudran was still not among them, it clicked into place.

They had come for something specific. Perhaps...it had been the dwarf.

But why had they taken Rhudran?

And more importantly, who and where?

A familiar voice buzzed, and the paladin jumped and swung around. It took a few moments for him to realize the mischievious not-dragon druid was NOT behind him, but in his pocket. Or rather, her hearthstone was.

Lahkin pulled it out. As if on cue, Novaclaw started babbling happily at him, something about who he was and what he was and how he had found one of them. Erelyn walked up beside him, staring at the talking hearthstone in disbelief.

Lahkin had an idea. "She might know who attacked us, Eri," he said to Erelyn. "I saw her with them."

"That's easy. Abominus did," snapped Mithara from the other side of the room, where she was tending a cranky night elf with long blue hair. The night elf started cursing, and Mithara turned around and cursed right back.

"Who else, I mean. If we know names, we can start tracking them down," Lahkin said. He and Erelyn looked at each other, then looked at the hearthstone. Novaclaw was helpful enough as they took turns weedling information out of her, but she was also...

"She's an airhead," said Erelyn flatly at his shoulder.

"We've got three names and two half-names, at least." Lahkin answered. "Abominus, Raoul, her, someone called 'Tyraxis' from Modas, and a big black druid bear."

Mithara snorted doubtfully, but it was the best he could do. Lahkin called Ronnad over and asked her to contact the AAMS. Using the names, surely they could start an investigation to find out where Rhudran got taken. Maybe they could even negotiate his return through the couriers, too. Sure, the Horde had showed their hostile intent, but that was no reason to be rude to them, was it?

Mithara muttered something about getting drunk and talking to her Forsaken friend Abby, necessarily in that order. Lahkin sighed to himself, staring into space as the clinic wound down. It was his first day as commander and things were already going wrong.

Or maybe they were going right. Lahkin rolled Novaclaw's hearthstone around on his palm, then pocketed it.

It seemed like ages since the last time she'd been in Dalaran, but really, it had only been as long as the admittedly unending trip to Storm Peaks. Derscha was glad to be, if not on ground level, at least somewhere that wasn't snowing.

She and Arietha were making their way to A Hero's Welcome for a much-deserved break when the dwarf intercepted them, alighting in front of them on a white gryphon.

Ronnad peered at the duo. "Yer AAMS?"

Derscha looked down at her arm, as if to double check. Yes, they were still in their uniforms.

"Looks like it. Can we help you?"

She pulled out a crumpled parchment sheet. "Lahkin o' Terra Icognita's lookin' fer a meetin' with Tyrexus, Raoul, an' Abominus.'

The night elf pulled out a journal to write the request, while Derscha pondered the ramifications. She sniffed a little at the mention of the Maggot Lord; he did horrible things to white uniforms.

"Do ye think ye can set something' up? On neutral ground o' course. Wouldn' want any accidents."

Arietha looked at Derscha for an answer. With a start, the gnome remembered that Ari hadn't been with AAMS that long, and probably didn't recognize the names.

She slowly nodded to Ronnad. "A small party from each side, only, if you want us there to translate," she stipulated. While she hadn't been part of the AAMS during the Boot Bay incident, she'd heard stories of the disaster.

"Aye, how many?"

"Those three alone is 'bout dangerous enough…"

"Aye, I jes' be doin' my job." Ronnad pulled out a timepiece and grimaces a bit. She peered at Derscha. " 's that all ye need lass? 've another appointment tae keep."

"Five," she answered in her rare "stern" voice. "No more than five from each side allowed."

"Weapons?"

"No weapons."

The dwarf accepted it without objection. "Where an' when?"

"We'll contact you with that information. I can't promise anything right now."

"Thank ye lass. I 'ppreciate it. Good travel tae th' both o' ye."

Arietha smiled. "Thank you for contacting us."

The gryphon rider took off, and Derscha sighed as it disappeared into the cityscape. "Sometimes it's dangerous to be in the AAMS."

Arietha nodded hesitantly.

"Do you know who those people were?"

The night elf shrugged. "I'm pretty sure I've heard some of the names."

"Abominus is crazy. Tyrexeus is threatening Pia Presidium. And Raoul…"

She paused. "Well, I've heard Raoul has a nice hat."

"A... nice hat?"

The gnome shook her head. "Nevermind. It's been a long day. Let's get those drinks."

She just hoped this didn't lead to further trouble.

Gods, she wanders away for a few minutes to pick up her mail while she waited for Mira and Rhudran to show up and all hell breaks loose.

The clinic was under attack from Modas, Grims and who knew what other abominations. Gentyl spurred Traveller into a gallop across the stone floors of Ironforge toward the sound of battle. His shod hooves were now sliding across those same stones and she bowled over a rotter she didn't recognize. She vaulted off and shoved her way through the fight toward Abominus.

His attack was weakening and at last she dropped him. Tyrexus was lying in a heap of rotten flesh and armor. How many eyes are you admiring today, huh?

Aziel! What the fel. Surely it couldn't be Aziel. He was dead. Permanently dead. She shoved past a worgen fighting another Modas she didn't recognize. Gods, there was another old Modas. They were raising their old dead.

Her eyes flew open in surprise when someone stabbed her in the back and then she stumbled forward. Before she could recover she was surrounded. Valacar fought toward her, but she had fallen. She lay there for a moment, paralyzed, watching the milling feet dancing impossibly slow steps to the waltz de morte.

There was a bell tolling in the distance. The strikes seemed to take forever, but on they rang over and over. She frowned. What time was it? It wasn't a bell. Those were sword strikes.

A shadow consumed her. She saw it advance slowly like ethereal tar and gradually overtake her. Her breath slipped away like a butterfly's whisper and she closed her eyes.

Mira had called her back. Bless her.

Raoul. Drop that rotter before he had a chance to--

She was immediately surrounded again. She heard Harmarth howl in rage. He was trying to get to her.

She lost count of how many times she was pulled back from the brink, but at last they fell back.

Her armor, once bright silver with pale blue stones of enchantment, looked like something she expected to see on a demon in the outlands. It was red. Not the vibrant poppy red she liked, but dark and foreboding. Part of the dried and darkening stains were hers, no doubt. It swirled and mingled with the ichor of undeath and gore until she looked like walking death itself.

She took off her gauntlets and poured out the blood pooled within them. Blood had already caked on her arms, wrists and hands. It cracked like mud in a drying streambed when she moved her fingers.

Her breastplate straps had been cut away, probably after she had fallen. The edges from a gaping stomach wound were still pink and raw. No doubt Abominus had feasted on her liver again. It was too bad Icharis wasn't around. The rotter had once proclaimed Gentyl's blood tasted like fine wine to him. He could have drunk his fill that night.

"Rhudran is gone," Mira whispered.

Gentyl ran through all the nearby buildings searching for him. He was nowhere to be found.

"Come, Genny," Valacar said.

Gentyl laughed. he was new and hadn't quite got the hang of Sepha. She thought years hence he probably still wouldn't. To him, he would always be Genny.

He called his sand drake they flew through Ironforge, searching everywhere. It was true. Rhudran was gone.

They mounted an attack against the Sepulcher, hoping to draw the war party out. When they failed to respond, they moved the attack to Undercity and the zeps. Limited resistance was brought against them. There were a few surprised horde in Orgrimmar. Some fought very well, but they fell eventually. They switched to another zep, hoping to get at least one of the war party to answer and take them captive, but they refused to answer the call.

Belpha, Prom, Tyr, Valacar, Foxria, Kod, Maethi, Harmarth and others answered the call. They all fought bravely and did their best to lay waste, but to no avail. Maethi, bless him. he was a tiny bundle of arcane energy and death and he was always willing to answer the call. Loyal unto death whenever she asked for help.

Tyr had surprised her. She made to secret of her dislike for Pia and yet she had jumped into the war party without hesitation when she heard Rhudran had been taken by the horde. She was a very skilled hunter and wiped out horde so quickly they were often dead before Gentyl could even get to them.
