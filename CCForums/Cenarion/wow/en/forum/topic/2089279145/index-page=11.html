What would be a very good thing to know is how much of a dps loss using hemo instead of backstab would be. The variables/factors I can think of would be:

2. Slightly less dismal damage/energy, meaning one can get more of these off in any fight.

3. Because of lower dpe, the CP/energy is lower, meaning more finishers and a smoother rotation (less gap between refreshes...)

Well, according to my spreadsheet Backstab makes up about 35% of dps.

And going from my previous numbers, but adding in Puncutring Wounds and a 30% crit our new values are:
Hemorrhage only: 117.74 normalized weapon damage a minute
Backstab: 196.85 normalized weapon damage a minute.

117.74/196.85 = .598 % of Backstab damage is done by Hemorrhage.
.35 * .598 = .2093 Hemorrhage spams contribution to dps in comparison to Backstab spams total.
.35 - .2093 = .1407% damage loss.

Backstab's bonus damage
1634* 2.08 (Puncutring Wounds + Lethatlity) = 3398.72 * 26 = 88366.72 / 60 = 1472.77 additional dps loss from using Hemorrhage (% will vary, but anyone can tell that it's a good size chunk)

Eviscerate makes up about 10% of total damage, adding roughly 1% more of total dps.

Without parries, you lose out on approximately 13% + 1472 dps.

Now with a 7.5% chance to parry, you lose a small bit of energy (though only enough to lose out on one Hemorrhage, so that's about a .6% additional dps loss) and that equals about a 9% dps loss on auto attacks. Getting lazy now so lets just say 30% damage are auto attacks, adding 3% total dps loss.

Total estimated dps loss from being in front: 16.6% + 1472.

Now these calculations are probably far from exact, but it reinforces the idea of "Front bad". And I'm getting lazy.
