I was checking something about expertise (if the % gains from rating are smooth or if they only go up by .25s) as combat and noticed something interesting while spamming rupture for a shorter CD on adrenaline rush.

As we all know, rupture damage scales with attack power. Now lets say you have Fluid Death and Landslide on both of your weapons. If you want to maximize rupture dps, you generally want it in the MoS window so the buff carries through refreshes. However, if you use rupture as soon as possible, fluid death may not have stacked up, meaning you will have lower max rupture damage. Now the exact number depends on gear, but in order to maximize rupture it may be better to wait for a double landslide proc and ensure maximum stacks of the Fluid Death buff before using Rupture. That, or wait until these 3 buffs are up, dump your current CP, and vanish for MoS. I have yet to test this in sub with all of the variables accounted for (my guild made me try combat for a bit), and I only have one landslide enchant. However I thought I would mention it.

Alright, I have done a few tests. Number time:

AP ----> damage (non crit) ---> difference from unbuffed (AP/Damage)

12239 (norm) ---> 979 ---> 0/0
13239 (LndSld) ---> 1031 ---> 1000/52
13275 (FldDth) ---> 1032 ---> 1036/53
12239 (MoS) ---> 1077 ---> 0/98
14275 (LS/FD) ---> 1085 ---> 2036/106

Analysis:

It appears that the damage from AP part of Rupture is linear, with approximately .52 damage/AP. Damage regardless of buffs/lack there of was the same for each rupture when refreshed, just as MoS does. The tests I did were with my current PvE gear (mostly 359 epics). I had no other buffs/flasks, so in a raid environment Rupture would have a higher base damage due to AP/damage increasing buffs. Additionally, there would be a few Fluid Death procs, meaning the 10% damage bonus would be considerably higher.

While I only have Landslide on one wep and was not attacking as frequently as I would in a raid, the proc was far from instant, meaning it would likely take a while to line up rupture with the two landslides required to make it better than MoS'd. In that time it is likely that the damage lossed by not having rupture ticking would be impossible to make up in a limited-duration fight.

Basically, use rupture with MoS. If possible wait until HaT is about to proc a 6th (wasted) CP before using it to maximize FD stacks and chances of an AP (or crit if you have that trinket) proc. If rupture and another ability fall off during a fight, it could be optimal to refresh the other before rupture if there are no AP/crit procs up and hope one comes around when it is time to refresh Rupture, but that is very situational. Also this assumes another bleed on the enemy.

IF you are confident that you can fit a Rupture into MoS after using Recup after opening then it would be a dps increase, but that is quite difficult and requires luck with HaT procs.

Something like this. And perhaps more. Gotta go now though, feel free to add or suggest stuff. Also I havn't edited ShD v. Evisc for a while and I didnt have time to read it so I just copied it.

BiS Gear
Just use your set, mine went away and I forgot to remake it remember the belt though, and make not of the ranged slot.

Glyph of ShD vs. Evis (Kyrrion, my friends, my target dummy)
The glyphs are quite close, but both my tests and EJ's voodoo say Eviscerate has a slight edge (~1k dps in the gear I tested it on). However, use ShD if your job is to put up Expose Armor, or if you have the 4 pc t11 bonus. It is also worth mentioning that ShD scales worse with crit, so at lower gear levels they are likely closer. The gear I tested in was about lvl 350. ShD also has the benefit of offering more burst, so if the burn phase of an encounter is extra important (or there are periods of boss-weakness which ShD can be lined up with), ShD would likely be better. If you are prone to having rupture fall off and need direct reapplications, the value of glyph of eviscerate will drop significantly every time you reapply rupture.

Shadowdance Optimization
Pool energy before using ShD, try to have ample uptime on SnD/recup so you can get more eviscs into FW. Use SS on second, and I find using prem after the first finisher helps ensure no wasted CP. Try to make the last ambush as close to the end as possible to maximize FW uptime. It might be worth it to wait a bit for buffs such as landslide and UH, but only to the point of not using ShD if nothing special is up.
