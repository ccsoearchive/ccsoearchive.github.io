For awhile I have been toying with the use of Envenom within the normal rotation, using it only when Recuperate and Slice and Dice were above 6 seconds in duration and Rupture wasn't past the 50% duration yet. After I've concluded that I am not able to get a consistent group to take accurate and plenty samples, I'm just going to do a rough mathing of it right here using my stats.

Damage comparison on Envenom vs Eviscerate (with no buffs/trinket procs, just plain stats. Assuming 30% base crit however, with 16% haste + Slice and Dice).

Eviscerate: 11176 to 11694, averaged at 11435.
Eviscerate with crit (no glyph): 14865 total average damage, roughly 10182 at unmodified boss armor.

Envenom: 8659 average damage, with crit 11256.

Poisons modified by Envenom:
Instant Poison proc rate increased to 44.99% from 25.71%, a 19.28% increase.
Instant Poison damage (assuming 15% spell crit): 1311 per proc, 1409 with crit.
About 7.42 hits every 6 seconds including abilities means 3.34 procs up from 1.91. So almost a whole Instant Poison and a half procs tied with Envenom.

Deadly Poison: 912 dps with 5 stacks, 182 per stack.
About 6 hits within Envenom buff with off hand. , application rate of 45% compared to 30%.
An extra .9 deadly poison is proc in the 6 seconds, the normal 1.8 procs up to 2.7.

DPS loss from Deadly Poison stack reset:
Time with 0 stacks: 2.18 seconds (912*2.18=1988 damage loss)
Time with 1 stack: 2.18 seconds (2.18 * (912*.8) + 1988 = 3578 total damage loss)
Time with 2 stacks: 2.45 seconds (2.45 * (912*.6) + 3578 = 4918 total damage loss)
Time with 3 stacks: 3.27 (6110 total damage loss)
Time with 4 stacks: 3.27 (6706 total damage loss)
Total time = 13.35 seconds.

13.35 / 3.27 = 4.08 Instant procs from 6th Deadly Poison stack if Eviscerate was used.

Total Eviscerate damage: 10182 + 5748 = 15930
Total Envenom damage: 11256 + 2014 - 6709 = 6561


Pretty clear it's not a good idea to use Envenom, restarting the Deadly Poison stack is too costly as I thought it'd be. As always, I could've easily messed up somewhere so blah blah disclaimer of accuracy blah.

You might want to ask Verelyse about Envenom in his sticky, guys; I'm unsure which hit rating it requires. But as you noted, Haileaus, it does spell damage (nature, specifically) and only spell damage, so it doesn't care about weapon DPS.
