Anyone have any hypothetical stat values for a full Hemo build?

I have been working this out. First of all, a hemo build would work around finishers, meaning you would perhaps go into combat for imp SnD (more eviscerates). It would also mean having a slow MH for non-ambush phases since it would do more damage with eviscerate.

Crit's value would plummet w/o the backstab glyph or extra damage.
Mastery would grow considerably (more and harder hitting eviscerates)
Haste's value would likely increase with more mastery due to how SnD stacks with haste. (More haste the better mastery looks and vice-versa)
Expertise, ??? possibly slightly lower since a higher % of damage would be from rupture, but not sure. Likely minimal change.
Spell hit: lower value, although white hit might be slightly more. Overall would be less than mastery at most.

Hemorrhage glyph for 4.2 (using data from the last post on page 17)

One Hemorrhage = 4.37 weapon damage * .4 = 1.75 extra from the glyph, * 1.3 (crit, Lethatlity does not effect the bleed) * 1.3 (Bleed debuff) = 2.96 extra weapon damage per Hemorrhage.

Total Hemorrhage with glyph = 7.33 weapon damage - 6.47 from 1 Backstab = .62 normalized weapon damage gained from replacing a Backstab, then subtract 2908.

Using my gear, using Hemorrhage with the glyph is losing 1633 damage from the Backstab alone (improving gear will barely effect it as the the .86 weapon damage has to get up to the 2908, which will increase with crit). Yes, you generate combo points slightly (and I do mean very very slightly, barely one combo point over 2 minutes).

Basically it's still not worth anything other than the luxury of an on demand bleed in PvP.

No 5% crit bonus stacks unless it is exclusive to spells or melee (and then only stacking alongside, rather than with).

Basically, the HAT crit buff might "stack" only to generate CP for the Rogue. The crit itself certainly does not.

I've been looking at this alternate more easy play version of Sub using the Combat tree's talent Improved Slice and Dice. This, combined with the glyph, produces a 40 second SnD at 5 CP, which is to say the least a very long time. That's enough time to generate at least 5 maybe 6 5 CP finishers.

Set 1: SnD, Evis, Evis, Recup, Evis, Evis
Set 2: SnD, Recup, Evis, Evis, Evis, Recup
And repeat.

Something like that. Might get an extra Evis somewhere in there on occasion, but I don't know Rogues or Sub well enough yet to be accurate, just trying to give a picture of why it might possibly be a workable spec.

In any case, I don't know how well this spec can sustain DPS compared to the typical Assassination sub specced version, but it certainly holds its own I would think. I put out my best single target DPS just a few minutes ago on Halazzi in ZG, about 17k sustained over 2 minutes (admittedly short but what can you do). Group makeup had 10% AP, 10% stats, a DPS DK and a Hunter, so very well set up with buffs anyway. *shrug*

Advantages: easier to maintain, more CP / time for Eviscerates, 6% extra damage reduction and some extra Recup healing.
Disadvantages: Significantly less Backstab damage (though it gained back 20% damage via Aggression, as well as a gain of 6% for Eviscerates), loss of possible alternative point spending such as Enveloping Shadows or Cheat Death.

What do you think? Has this been looked at enough already to ignore?
