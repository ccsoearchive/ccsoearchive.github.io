You sidestepped the point - the intent of the talent is to increase damage, and if it is not increasing damage the same way that is a problem.

Whether you increase the damage done to the target, or increase the damage of the ability before any multipliers take effect - in all cases it is a percentage gain which translates through all previous and all further modifiers.

Its simple multiplication. If you are correct, and I don't feel the need to test it to prove myself here, you're essentially saying that one talent or the other is extremely bugged and is not working properly (if at all).

It's not necessarily a problem. Sometimes it's just the way %'s stack (sometimes they are added together then multiply the damage, other times they multiply each other). And I did a little more testing into it and the tooltip lies. Eviscerates with 3/3 Aggression were hitting harder than 2/3 Coup De Grace. Only by 300 though (on a raid dummy, no buffs or bleed but that won't change anything significantly). If I had to guess, it would seem Aggression adds onto Mastery while Coup De Grace multiplies with it (don't quote me at all, no research has been done at all, this is merely a thought).

06/26/2011 01:36 AMPosted by Eandar
Assume 1.8 Eviscerates per minute gained, for the hell of it. That is 1.8 multiplied by say, a 5 minute fight length, or 9 Eviscerates. The DPS gain is probably 500-800 DPS depending on talent differences and gear levels. Now, you're doing 30% or more with Backstab and you're losing 30% crit damage, and 30% crit. That alone on someone doing 20,000 DPS is about 2500 DPS.

Let's be more generous and say you get 2 Eviscerates per minute. Let's just say that's 30k damage per minute (counting the minor <500 difference from talents).

Now here's Backstab comparisons (30% base crit)
4.2 * 1.3 = 5.46 weapon damage
3.64 * 1.78 = 6.47 weapon damage

With my crappy pvp gear, that comes out to 12,011 vs 14,232 for the Combat orient spec vs the Assassination oriented spec respectively (keep in mind my average Eviscerate damage is no where near that 30k average given unless both manage to crit but that fights the whole idea of averaging).

Say we gain 16 energy per second (20% haste + Energectic Recovery). Out of 4,900 (+100 base) energy gained through 5 minutes, 1,000 of that is spent towards Ambush (The beginning Ambush, 4 from 5 Shadowdances equalling 20, 3 through Vanish and 1 from Preparation equalling 25 Ambushes), 50 of that is spent on Recuperate (after Relentless Strikes and of course 2 per minute), and 145 is spent on Hemorrhage (1 per minute) leaving you with 3705 energy.

HaT procs 150 points and with Ambush and Premeditation is another 50 and 20 totaling at 220 points, so 44 full finishers. Recuperate is refreshed 10 times, leaving 34 finishers. Slice and Dice is refreshed at 35 and 25 seconds (Combat vs Assassination respectively, 9 refreshes vs 12), leaving 25 and 22 finishers respectively. Rupture takes away one and you get 24 vs 21 open for Eviscerates. That's an extra 240 and 210 energy, leaving spare energy for Backstabs at 3,465 and 3,495.

With the Backstab glyph, that makes Backstab cost roughly 38.5 and 37 energy (for your Combat oriented spec vs Assassination oriented spec respectively at 30% base crit). Adding 2 energy to the base cost of Backstab lets us account for the 10 total energy spent on Eviscerate. That's a total of 85 Backstabs vs 89. Total Eviscerates end up as 41 vs 38.

Backstab for Combat oriented spec is 4.2 * 1.3 = 5.46 vs Assassination oriented spec's 3.64 * 1.78 = 6.4792. Total normalized damage is 464.1 vs 576.6488 (Combat and Assassination orientated respectively). Total Backstab difference is 112.5488 normalized weapon damage. With 14k attack power and a 359 weapon, that's 206,189.4016 damage over the 5 minutes lost.

Say Combat's Eviscerate hits 1k harder overall (remember the damage difference is very slight and probably won't hit 1k in difference), so you get 41k + 3 more Eviscerates.

I know I did not reduce the Backstab damage by boss armor, but that'd still be at least 140k (100k after subtracting the 41ish-k from a very slightly harder Eviscerate) damage that needs to be made up.
