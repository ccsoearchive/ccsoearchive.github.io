Regarding the quote from EJ:

Yeah, glyph of hemo is only good though if you simply don't have time to rupture, since it goes from "Gives a bad DoT" to "Gives a 16% damage boost (and a bad DoT)." According to some of my calculations though, rupture is better than eviscerate after about 12 secs with a bleed already applied in both scenarios. If you add in the 16% damage boost, the number would be quite lower. Also, it could be worth it to use Garrote (18 sec bleed) as an opener for the bleed, and use glyph of Eviscerate for trash. I say Evisc because though I generally prefer the ShD one, they are close and when talking about trash (or generally mobs that will die before rupture becomes worth it) you likely won't be using ShD. Also, during the FW debuff phase, eviscerate would be more powerful (rupture ignores armor anyway) that it would anyway, so the average length of rupture could be more like 14 secs. If you used garrote for a bleed you might see yourself vanishing/ShDing for trash more simply to apply the bleed, which is actually good practice assuming you ALWAYS HAVE A VANISH ON HAND IF THINGS GO WRONG.

Of course, for most people (myself included) paying for reglyphs each time I am dealing with sufficiently easy trash is a waste of time/gold, so I would stick with my normal setup (with ShD as my third). Thinking I will try opening with garrote for proper duration fights, would be interesting to see the window of when it would pay off (need time for the bleed/debuff to be worth it, but too much time and you should have opened with Ambush->Rupture).
