I am curious about the possibility of a PvE Sub Hemo build using a No'Kaled in both the main hand and off hand. I know it is usually quite taboo to even suggest a slow offhand in any rogue build, and I understand (quite well) the mechanics that go into each rogue spec, and what is required in the gearing department.

What has my interest is the proc. Yes, Deadly Poison, and thus Instant Poison will obviously proc less often with a slow offhand, but Sub has nothing outside of DP stacking that relies on a fast offhand, such as Combat Potency, or the Mutilate talent itself. I have read that some have suggested the No'Kaled proc would perhaps more than make up for the lost DP stacking. It is also my understanding that at higher gear levels Hemo starts to scale up to Backstab's damage potential, at least in a build that specifically supports Hemo (more accurately, one that forgoes Puncturing Wounds), removing the requirement of a dagger in the main hand.

Shadowcraft does not seem to model slow offhand weapons for Sub, and I have read it doesn't model No'Kaled as well as it could anyways. Any feedback on this? At the very least, it is a curiosity to me.


I will start by saying this probably wouldn't work, but let's analyze it fully:

For poisons you would probably use double Wound since DP would probably fall off.

Shadowcraft was updated to model the procs better, but I believe it is set up only to proc off MH strikes.

While Hemo does scale better with gear, it is generally not enough to make a full hemo build worth it over a full BS one or a soft-Hemo (using it only for the bleed) build. Possibly with near BiS gear, or if you had a heroic axe and normal daggers it would be better to use a full hemo build.

As for the OH axe, you are trading poison procs for wep procs. Note that the OH would get far fewer procs than the MH. You could try to do this, but I personally doubt you will have much luck with the slow OH.

All that said, this is speculation, and you should feel free to get the appropriate setup and test your DPS against LFR bosses (or just the first). If you do this you will probably want to get Expertise capped (more procs) and use glyphs of SnD, Hemo, and Evisc.

I posted this on EJ, and now am doing it here. Once this information is used I will get this into my guide. For now it will lie in the more advanced stuff section. If you wanna do math for me, feel free, but post all of the info.

NOTE: This is all with no armor debuff up (like Sunder Armor). The actual increase would be slightly lower, I will do a test using EA when I have time.

I haven't seen this anywhere and figured I'd do a test to find how much of a damage boost the FW debuff is. Looks like about 28% but here is what I did and all of my calculations.

This uses Hemo (With bleed up already) on the boss training dummy with no proc gear on. The only wep I used was [Gutgore Ripper] with the Fiery Weapon enchant on it.


Norm:
Avg Min Max Difference
Hits (200) 4819 4752 4888 136
Crits (72) 11480 11304 11625 321


FW:
Avg Min Max Difference
Hits (200) 6185 6096 6269 173
Crits (64) 14716 14503 14908 403

Note: For the FW buffs I checked the numbers at 86 hits (? crits) and the averages I believe both changed by only 1 since then.

Avg damage bonus:

Hits:
(FW-Norm)/Norm = (6185-4819)/4819 = 0.283461299 = 28% Damage bonus (no armor debuff)

Crits:
(FW-Norm)/Norm = (14716-11480)/11480 = 0.281881533 = 28% Damage bonus (no armor debuff)


Next comes the calcs for when you should refresh a non-FW hemo bleed if you have the buff, as well as hopefully whether or not it is best to wait for Prem and SS to be off CD before using Vanish after a ShD, or if it should be used right away. (I'd guess the former looking at this but depends on how good Prem is.)
