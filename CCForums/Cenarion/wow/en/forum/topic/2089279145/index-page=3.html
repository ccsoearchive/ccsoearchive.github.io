The thing is the glyph doesnt just mean 1 extra ambush. You have to consider these factors:
It is possible to get more if you luck out on haste buffs (Heroism definately or possibly Tri Hurricane)

A: You are using ambush INSTEAD of backstab, so you are gaining a combo-point and a bit of damage (depends on your avg difference btwn ambush and BS), and actually costing a bit more energy (glyph of BS).
Let's assume assume Backstab's total crit is 60% (30% base + 30% from talents). 5 (energy refund from glyph) * .6 (crit rate) = 3 total energy on average per Backstab.
3.64 * 2.08 (the 60% crit * 30% extra crit damage) = 7.57 (average Backstab damage)
3.98 * 1.9 (30% crit base + 60 from talents) = 7.562 (average Ambush damage)
1634 (bonus Backstab damage) * 2.08 = 3398 average bonus damage for Backstab
2125 (bonus Ambush damage) * 1.9 = 4037 average bonus damage for Ambush
Damage per energy:
Backstab = .204 + 91.8
Ambush = .189 + 107

The 2 are close. With more gear Backstab will be doing more per energy, especially with more crit. However, this is not accounting for the extra combo point, which you could factor in as, eh, 40% of an 5 point Eviscerate compared to a 20% chunk of it. Blunt damage Backstab will probably shine brighter, but the subtle boost from combo points will pretty much guarantee Ambush being all around better.

B: FW would ideally be up for 2 extra secs, but that may not quite pan out, even if it should. Depends on when you use finishers in your cycle.
Actually, it pretty much will always pan out that way. My dummy tests go like
Ambush > Finisher > Ambush > Ambush > Finisher > Ambush. That's a 2 second gap between the last 2 Ambushes from the GCD. Honestly with regen times, a non glyph Shadowdance isn't getting the "full" 16 seconds of Exposed Weakness normally, while with the glyph you can wait for that last Ambush at the last second for optimal Exposed Weakness uptime.

C: the added CP from an extra ambush would mean you get closer to an extra finisher.
Which again means another 20% from Eviscerate.

D: (aww sad face) Depending on your build, ShD might be favored more if you use Initiative (I am not atm because I like the utility of ES, but I might switch, this could affect ShD fairly significantly).
Initiative means crap in Shadowdance.
1st Ambush = 2 + 2 (Premed) + 1 (HaT) = 5. No go for initiative.
2nd + 3rd Ambush = 2+ 2 + 1 (HaT). If initiative were to proc on either, you'd still be wasting one point.
4th Ambush = The only one that Initiative would really benefit.
Some of these factors are for and against ShD, and as for evis, its pretty simple. I'll try to run a more exact test if I can get the people, and I'll try out both specs.

Thanks for your patience in this matter, the fact is the glyphs are damn close.

I'm still not convinced.

Alright, here's what I've come up with.

No Armor Debuff (with 25% crit chance):
7.363 (Average Ambush damage with crit) * .879 (armor reduction with Exposed) - 7.3346 (Average Backstab damage with crit)* .685 (normal armor reduction) = 1.44% weapon damage + 1200 (the flat damage bonus difference between the two) increase from the glyph.

Armor Debuff:
7.363 * .891 - 7.3346 * .712 = 1.33% weapon damage + 1158 increase.

Then with the extra combo points (counting a 50% Initiative proc) you could probably add a 30% Eviscerate for the Shadowdance glyph as well (for 1.5 combo points per minute).

The main problem is taking into consideration of Exposed Weeakness uptime and the number of Eviscerates you manage to fit inside. Your results could still be skewed due to refreshing Slice and Dice or Recuperate during Shadowdance/Exposed Weakness. I would say that yes, the two glyphs are fairly close though.

I still beleive Shadowdance has the upperhand. The weapon damage + extra bonus ended up to be 34% of an Eviscerate (both calculated at Exposed Weakness armor reduction values, so this is the worst situation for the Shadowdance glyph), a total of 64% of an Eviscerate per minute (from the extra Ambush combo point and 50% Initiative proc). You'd need to pull off a finisher at an average of 5 per second to get a 70% boost from the glyph to make it more optimal. So if you think you can do that go for it. Of course the amount of mastery you have left over will change things, though I don't think it'll make much of a difference.
