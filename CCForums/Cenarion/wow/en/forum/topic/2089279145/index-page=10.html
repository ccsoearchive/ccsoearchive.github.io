Well that could skew the results slightly (RNG can do some crazy things).

But the easiest way to do it would be:

2.23 (Hemorrhage damage) * 1.69 (30 crit with Lethatlity) = 3.77 (average Hemorrhage damage) * .4 = 1.51 (bleed damage in weapon damage value) * 1.3 * 1.1 * 1.3 (bleed debuff, Sanguiary Vein, then crit and Lethality does not effect it) = 2.16 (weapon damage from glyph) / 24 = .09 normalized weapon damage per second with Hemorrhage glyph.

3.68 (Backstab) * 2.08 (30 crit + 30 from Puncturing Wounds and Lethatily in) = 7.65 / 60 = .1275 weapon damage per second.

It costs an additional 43.5 energy per minute for 100% Hemorrhage glyph bleed. Since the bleed only adds .09 weapon damage per second, it takes away .1275 weapon damage per second. However it does grant an extra combo point but that's not saying much. It hurts a lot more if you're with a Feral Druid or Arms Warrior. And since it's in terms of normalized weapon damage, it's a lot easier to carry over to another Rogue.
