Damage per energy formula:

(Overall damage - .71(White Hits - (Instant poison procs from white including DP refreshes - 5) - (Deadly Poison) - (White/poison from white hits Crit damage * .4 (IF you have t12 2pc)))) / (Energy from RS + Energy from BS Crits + Energy from Recup + (Energy/sec*duration of battle))

This is the overall damage minus the damage from white hits that were not created by SnD (71.4...%) and all of the damage that came from white hits. For poisons, use the % they have to proc and assume that it held throughout the fight. That way, each you can simply take the % that *should* have procced form your Specials that can Proc away from the total poison damage. We divide the damage from energy by the total amount of energy gained throughout the fight (there will be some energy that could have been wasted or left over when the fight ended, as well as the 100 energy that was used when the fight began, but these are minor and will offset eachother).

I began doing this with a fight I pulled from WoL but the fact is the stats for that fight would be way different from the ones you used, so you really can't compare. Of course, the number is still probaby w/e amount of dpe is needed.

Did you factor in the increased number of finishers you would get?

Lets do some quick math on Hemo+Glyph vs Backstab, and ignore IP as its proc chance would be the same regardless of which ability is used (assuming you're using a 1.8mh).

Hemo+Glyph base, 14425 for 35 energy will have a 40.5% crit chance for 2x damage will average to:
(14425 * .595)noncrit / 29en + ( (14425 * 2) * .405)crit / 29en = ~8583 noncrit / 29en + ~11684 crit / 29en = ~699 DPE

Backstab base, 13915 for 39 energy, with a 30% buff to crit and 30% increased modifier on crits through talents will have a 70.5% crit chance for 2.3x damage and 5 reduced energy will average to:
(13915 * .295)noncrit / 39en + ( (13915 * 2.3) * .705)crit / 34en = ~4105 noncrit / 39en + ~22563 / 34en = ~769 DPE

I'm not 100% sure to that being the way to solve Backstab's DPE but I believe it is. Based on this it would seem that in a single target fight, it is not worth it to drop a Backstab every 24sec for Hemo instead when it comes purely to damage per energy. Of course, this is purely on a Hemo vs Backstab case, and like said above, may be weighed also by the energy saved by using Hemo instead of Backstab allowing more finishers.

Generally yes, however you want to avoid having to refresh SnD and Recup when FW is up. This means if you have 4sec left on SnD after you open, then you should refresh SnD before going into ShD. In Kyrrion's first post (#4 I believe, though it is the third one since #2 was removed) he outlines the optimal way to open. Of course, you would probably want to save Prep for Lust (unless it will have finished its CD by then), but optimally that is what you want to do.

Also, if there is a phase where the boss will take extra damage, you want to ensure that all of your CDs are available for that part, so on, say, Alysrazor, using vanish in p1 (or ShD later in the phase) might not be a good idea.

Basically, you want to maximize FW uptime (which is the main benefit of out CDs) and the eviscerates used in FW, but if there is a time where your finishers will be more effective, they should be saved.
