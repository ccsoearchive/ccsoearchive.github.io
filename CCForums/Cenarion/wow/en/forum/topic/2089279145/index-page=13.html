Regardless of when you refresh the tick timer does not reset (it used to) so any over-time effect will continue along merrily without interruption. If you refresh with more than 1 tick left in it's duration all those ticks from the point you refreshed until expiration are lost and you are now working off the new application plus time remaining on the current tick. If however, you refresh with less than 1 tick left, then you are now working off the new application plus time remaining on the current tick, and again it continues along it's merry without disruption to the tick timer and there have been no 'lost' ticks.

Also, if you're using Instant Poison, put it on the off hand and Deadly on the main. You heard me. And no, Combat should not do it. Only Subtlety Rogues.

I think this deserves an explanation.
I assume it is because 1 DP = 1 IP, so you can count them as the same, and instant poison is PPM based on wep speed, with a smaller chance to proc than DP. Lets see...

In a 1 min fight with all averages (haste doesn't matter), with wep speeds of 1.8/1.4:

Instant (25.71% chance on hit = 8.57 ppm) / Deadly(30% chance on hit = 12.86 ppm)
Total procs = 21.43

Deadly(30% chance on hit = 10.00 ppm) / Instant (20.00% chance on hit = 8.57 ppm)
Total Procs = 18.57

So you need (21.43-18.57) 2.86 procs from attacks in 1 min with a 4.29% chance to proc.
Judging by my last Argolath fight on World of Logs (all behind him, 100% uptime), taking the total special hits and dividing by the time to get hits per min we have...

(109 hits/fight)*(1/(3.58333(repeating of course) mins/fight)) = 30.42 hits/min

(30.42 hits/min)*(.0429 procs/hit) = 1.30 additional procs/min from specials

So we have a difference of 2.86-1.3= 1.55 procs/min fewer if you use Deadly/Instant, by my math, judging by an optimal fight (ok, i may have made mistakes but it was my best personal dps on that fight so error should be small, I was on the boss the whole time, and the difference would need to be over twice the number of special attacks I made, which would indicate that a fuller lust (was barely up when the battle ended), more optimal play, and more haste would still not compensate.
Of course, I could have missed something, and cannot wait for Kyrrion to tell me what I did wrong.
