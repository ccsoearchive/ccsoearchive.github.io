As much as the post you are quoting "addresses the problem" it actually works under a bunch of incorrect information.

It says that Prot Paladins take more damage than warriors. Not true, We get more block per mastery AND we block 40% instead of 30%. This averages out fairly well, with paladins being better than warriors overall early, but warriors just beating out paladins as you get to the very highest levels of mastery. They blocking is actually very balanced in terms of damage mitigation.

Next up, the poster says that GbtL was made so that we could ALWAYS use WoG instead of SotR to keep up Holy Shield.

This isn't true at all. The Holy Shield was added later, after a lot of feedback saying that in terms of pure mitigation, you actually lose more damage reduction by letting Holy Shield drop off because you used WoG instead of SotR, which made it look VERY undesirable to prot paladins at the time.

Talents catered to WoG.

There are 2 talents that are catered to WoG, 1 is in the first tier, available to all specs. The second is very deep in the Prot tree, and is completely catered to Prot.

Guided by the Light is an excellent talent still, it makes 100% sure if you do use WoG, you don't take a huge penalty for it, and it's impossible to waste. It also increases the benefit of your WoG's. Fantastic!

Then there's Eternal Glory, still good for Ret PvP and Holy PvE, and potentially Prot PvP if you swing that way. It allows you to get your necessary burst heal, with the opportunity to still put on some dps pressure.

For Prot PvE, it's one of our worst threat talents now, so you're probably better off putting those points in SotP, unless you almost exclusively use SoI.

That said, the highest dps prot rotation, thanks to AS increasing our HoPo generation has inquisition in it now. We have a very ret like priority queue now which involves watching for SD procs and Inquisition uptime while and using your SotR's at the right opportunity. So there are still choices that need to be made.


Players really need to get away from the spamming of WoG, and trying to use it as effectively as possible. Some situations I can see it being very useful.


1) Heroic Chimaeron - Top ups/overheal shield in anticipation of eating a double attack.
2) Heroic Maloriak - helping reduce the extremely large damage spikes from his black phase or red phases.
3) Nefarian - Helping provide more buffer/topping up in anticipation of electrocutes.


There are all sorts of areas where it's beneficial to wait. We really need to treat it a lot more like how DK's build up blood shields in anticipation of large melee damage spikes, and less like an overall mitigation tool.

It was overpowered, and I can show you why.

http://www.worldoflogs.com/reports/rt-kt7zqy26frrhm0yt/analyze/hd/source/?s=8365&e=8896

Look at our 3 tanks, Amuck, myself, and Dasphunk.

Dasphunk MT'd Nefarian, putting out 2500 HPS with Seal of Insight and Heavy WoG use.

I put out 1900 tanking the adds in P1, and the adds in P3.

Amuck tanked Onyxia in P1, and Adds in P3, he put out 492.3 HPS, less than a HEALING STREAM TOTEM.

That's a pretty large differential between the 3 tanks, and the paladins especially compared to the warrior. It's a VERY large survivability difference.

Even without WoG, if you have a heavy SoI usage build, you will still put out a pretty large amount of self healing as a prot pally.

It's all about optimizing your WoG use. Adapt, and be a good tank, or don't and continue being mediocre. That's what it's all about.

Prot paladins take less damage than warriors even while not using WoG at all.
Care to point me to where this is proven?

Paladins used to edge out a little more dodge due to agility->dodge ratios, but now that there are 353 str/mastery/avoidance tanking items that replace nearly all of the agility items that PLD's were using, paladins and warriors in equal tanking gear have the exact same parry and dodge.

So let's compare mastery.
Warrior gets 15% base block from Sentinel, 12% base block and 12% crit block from Mastery. So at only base mastery, they're looking at 27% chance to block 30% of damage, and then you add an additional 12% of that 27% chance to block an added 30% of damage.
That's (.27*.3=.081) + (.27*.12*.3=.00972) = .09072 Or 9.72% damage reduction from base block.

Paladin gets 18% base block from Mastery and as long as you keep holy shield up, it blocks 40% of damage.
So that's .18*.4=.072 Or 7.2% damage reduction from base.

Obviously War mastery starts better, so let's look at scaling.
Paladin scaling is linear. 1 Mastery always provides 2.25% chance to block 40% of the damage. That's .0225*.4=.009 Or 0.9% damage reduction per 1 mastery.

Warrior scaling is slightly non-linear.
1 Mastery grants 1.5% block and 1.5% crit block. Inserted into the formula above, an additional 1 Mastery would grant ((.285*.3=.0855) + (.285*.135*.3=.0115425) = .0970425) - .09072 = .0063225 Or 0.63225% damage reduction. That is worse than 1 Mastery for Paladin, but when you combine it with the base damage reduction, you're still ahead as a Warrior.

Let's graph a comparison between Paladin and War Mastery:
Paladin y = .072+(.009*x)
Warrior y = .09072+((.27+.015*x)*.3)+(((.27+.015*x)*(.12+.015*x))*.3)
Here's a link to the graph: http://img232.imageshack.us/i/pldvswarmastery.jpg/
According to my math there, War mastery starts better and continues to be better all the way to cap.

So what's left that is making PLD take less damage than War without WoG?
Cooldowns?
Did I make a math mistake above somewhere?
