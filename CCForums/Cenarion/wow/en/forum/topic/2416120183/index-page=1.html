PvE protection paladins are okay in 4.1. In some ways we are actually even better. The WoG nerf is warranted for Prot PvE. The capacity to heal 15k to 30k of health instantly made us noticeably better than other tanks in some respects. True, our single target threat can be said to be weaker than other tanks but pre-nerf WoG in conjunction with our damage mitigation spells and GbtL more than compensated for it. Right now, we basically traded WoG spamming for better single target threat. In this respect we actually came out of 4.1 better.

I know that it takes some getting used to. We have to rely on our healers more. This, however, is little different from what most tanks have been dealing with. We have to make more judicious use of our WoG. This is, on some levels, a good thing. It actually forces paladins to optimize their usage of WoG and not just use WoG haphazardly.

I'm certain that most if not all of us have managed to survive some encounters because of WoG chaining but these encounters were rare. Moreover, most of these close calls would never have occurred if the healer or all of the DPS were doing what they were supposed to do. Think of the WoG nerf as the democratization of dungeon/raid responsibility and you'll get what I mean.

So please, if you are a protection paladin invested in PvE and not a retribution paladin invested in PvP don't complain about the WoG nerf.

The problem is that there used to be a functional distinction between Prot Warriors and Prot Paladins and it used to be balanced. Prot Warriors can critical block so they have 'spike mitigation', where a critical block is almost as good as a dodge/parry. Prot Paladins can't critical block, but they block more often. They have constant mitigation, overall taking more damage but without as much spike as a warrior. This matters to the tanks themselves and to their healers because they present different types of survivability. The paladin will eat the big damage ability and be easier to heal up afterwards, but the warrior might mitigate more of the big damage through a critical block or he might not block it at all and take a bigger hit and be harder to heal up.

Now, as a Paladin overall takes more damage but on a smoother curve, this was made up for by the self-healing supplied by WoG. This is no longer the case, as WoG usage has been cut by 50% or more.

Talents around WoG in the Protection tree, which mind you was clearly and obviously designed around WoG being a staple ability in the Protection rotation, are subsequently also depreciated in value by 50% or more: Eternal Glory and Guarded by the Light. What was the point of having Guarded by the Light cause WoG to proc Holy Shield if not to allow a paladin to completely forgo the use of Shield of the Righteous? Since you can use WoG less often, the value of increasing its effect by 10% is depreciated. Since you can use WoG less often and you no longer have a choice between WoG and ShotR with an Eternal Glory proc, Eternal Glory is also depreciated.

You want to talk about optimizing WoG usage? You have to make sure that you have a full 3 Holy Power every 20 seconds, which means paying extremely close attention to your WoG cooldown. But the new Grand Crusader functionality is a double-edged sword. Holy Power generation has increased, so you can't just sit on all that extra Holy Power waiting for your WoG to come off cooldown, can you? So you use ShotR and then based on miss/dodge/parry and the randomness of getting another GC proc, you don't necessarily have your 3 HoPo back when your WoG is available again.

Optimizing your WoG usage now is a royal pain in the ass and largely a waste of 4 talent points. Optimizing your Holy Power usage, on the other hand, is simple: Spam ShotR.

And they wanted prot paladins to have an interesting choice? It's not a tank's job to do damage. It's a tank's job to generate threat and to otherwise direct as much damage as possible off of others and onto himself and then to mitigate or avoid as much of that damage as possible. If you're one of those special people who Blizzard claims said that "WoG spam didn't 'feel' right as part of a tanking role" then, quite frankly, you should have rolled a dps. WoG spam was both indirect and direct mitigation, which is a tank's primary job.
