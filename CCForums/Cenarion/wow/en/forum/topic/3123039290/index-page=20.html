First, I need to apologize if some of my answers are the same as above, but I have not read this thread in it's entirety.

  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    • Both.

  • If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    • Mostly raiding, but I also run heroics.

  • If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    • Battlegrounds mostly, but only because Arenas do not favor hunters, which is a shame, because I prefer arenas to battlegrounds.

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    • When I summon my pet, he doesn't appear with his proper level 85 stats immediately, it takes a second or two for him to jump up to the proper health and whatnot, as a result, he can be one-shot.

    Also, when the pet is running along side of me while I'm on a mount, say in Firelands approaching Lord Rhyolith, and I jump over the lava and onto Rhyolith's little island, my pet does not...and he gets killed in the lava. (This is small, and I can live it.)

  • What makes playing your class more fun?
    • The fact that movement doesn't really affect my DPS and that I'm awesome.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?
    • In PVE, I'm comfortable with pretty much everything. In PVP, I feel that hunters don't measure up to most classes--specifically arena--where our minimum range is a huge disadvantage when anyone can just sit on us and prevent us from attacking. And top it all off, the way focus works, I have to be casting Cobra Shot or Steady Shot in order to regenerate focus, but I can't if my target is within my minimum range, meaning most hunters find themselves resource starved.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    • I wish Marksman was a little more forgiving, at least the Improved Steady Shot buff--otherwise it's fine. Survival is fine, but far too unpredictable--which can be fun--but often times leaves the hunter with very few Lock n' Load procs, leaving their overall DPS sporatic at best. Beast Mastery is simple, and boring.

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?
    • I would like to see differences within the specs...for the most part, no matter the spec, a hunter plays the same--you just change which spells are your 1,2,3, etc buttons and boom...no difference, other than rotation.

    Now, I get it, we're hunters, mages all play like mages, they stand there and cast...but I feel that there is a giant missed opportunity here with the hunters.

    Beast Mastery should be all about the pets, there should be better talent points for them that Marksman and Survival hunters can't access (as it is, all the DPS points can be grabbed by them and it makes the 31 point talent of BM a little pointless). Perhaps a second pet? I don't know.

    Marksman should be about shooting! This is the sniper spec. I would even be okay if this spec lost the pet entirely and went dual-wielding pistols as their melee weapons allowing them to DPS within the minimum-range.

    Survival should be about survival. I love that they do a lot of elemental damage (though it counts as physical in the game). What about making this the Damage over Time and mitigation spec, where through our traps and dots we do damage like a Warlock has affliction. Still attacking from range, but applying their damage in a much different way.

    I think the hunter has the possibility of being one of the funnest pure DPS classes out there, simply by making each spec have a different play style while remaining true to the ranged weapon damage dealing.

  • What spells do you use the least?
    • Counter Attack, Widow Venom, Eagle Eye, Beast Lore, Aspect of the Wild, and Scare Beast (has some pvp applications, but it's easier to try to kite a druid than "scare" him).

    Thanks again for taking the time to put these types of threads up here on the forums.

    * Yes, I know "funnest" isn't a word.

    Disclaimer: I will keep this short and to the point on all areas of the formfill.

    What type of content do you focus on?
    More PVE than PVP, but I cap my VP and CP every week.

    If PvE, what type of PvE?
    Raiding, heroics, materials grind, in that order.

    If PvP, what type of PvP?
    3v3, random BGs, world PVP.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
    1. Camouflage: we're the only stealth class that has to continually reapply their stealth ability. Camo should be a toggle.
    2a. Traps and Rogues: being able to disarm my traps from 60 feet away. I suggest that if they close to within melee range of a trap, that they could destroy it, but if a rogue uses disarm trap further than melee, the trap ought to deploy as if someone stepped on it.
    2b. 1v1 vs Rogue: with an easy sap - cheap shot - kidney-shot combo, and with gouge and blind to fall back on, a rogue will annihilate virtually any hunter 1v1, regardless of gear level. This is not balanced. Possible solution: make Master's Call activate automatically?
    3. Deadzone. Hunters are the only class with a deadzone, which has been eliminated in many places like ZA/ZG and still needs to be eliminated in other areas like the Firelands boss fights. Going from blasting someone with 100% hit chance IRL at zero range to not even able to pull the trigger in the game at zero range seems counter-intuitive.
    4. Single-up freezing trap. I took great pride in my ability to maintain 2 simultaneous traps and potentially 3-trap on a pull (without even using trap launcher). How dare you remove my multitrap ability and say it was that you didn't want to have a best-possible CC class? Less than 1% of hunters could ice like i could in BC, and at the time, no one thought I could even CC, let alone beat a mage at it. Because of the decision to nerf hunter freezing traps, all that is now legend. Shame, devs, shame!

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    I enjoy ranged physical DPS. Being a mail-wearer means that I am able to take a few hits, and Distract--FD allows me to actually buy a tank a few extra heals, distinguishing me from any other class.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Deadzone, being so weak to rogues, and unable to pierce a priest's flag-carrier's bubbles.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”?
    I enjoy the BM cooldown management, actually. MM is way too sensitive to the reality of combat. Survival seems like a mix of the two, between cooldown management and shot priority. I wouldn't change anything, off the top of my head.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    1. Implement an Exsanguination Trap, in the place of Immolation Trap. Let it root the target and cause a bleed DoT for 30sec, and a slow debuff for 30sec after. Just a standard metal bear trap, only usable on things that travel on the ground (not ghosts, for example)
    2. Maybe change the effect of Feed Pet from damage recovery (easier done with mend pet) to a class-based 1-hour buff, similar to a rogue poison. (A pet damage nerf would be in order if different buffs were implemented, of course)
    3. I like eagle eye for scouting. Don't remove this, instead, please buff it to use it without breaking Camo.
    4. Flare range. Unfair that a rogue can disarm a trap from 20 yards and Flare is only 10. Seems like the numbers there got switched.
    5. A super-secret viable melee hunter spec, for PVP obviously.

    What spells do you use the least?
    1. Immolation Trap. Absolutely useless. Replace it with the Exsanguination Trap mentioned above.
    2. Feed Pet. With the loss of pet happiness, the present mechanic is worthless.
    3. Raptor Strike. I only use it on Ozruk because it's my only ability that will trigger the bleed dot.

    What type of content do you focus on?

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
    AoE/Pets/Buffs
    Multishot hits more mobs if you are strafing while it is fired. It's quite irritating to -have- to strafe while Multishotting even when you'd rather be standing still, just to increase DPS. This can be easily confirmed on Orgrimmar target dummies, up on top of the rampart between the drag and valley of honor. Stand between the single, elevated dummy, and the line of 4, target the 2nd furthest dummy on the line of 4. Stand still and multishot, try as many different spots as you like. You hit 3 targets. Now do it while strafing. You hit 4 targets. It's a frustrating and annoying mechanic that I'd love to see fixed (ideally with the higher target amount, since if you're basing class balance off what you see in raids, it's likely a lot of the better hunters know and are using this).
    Pets...I assume everything that can be done has been done for the most part. Firelands added a new level of frustration on trash with mobs putting high dmg melee range dots on pets. Make pets exempt from these attacks like you have so many others. Healers still don't like healing pets, and we don't like having to constantly rez ours (or turn off your new assist function, or have it not attack, etc). The small dog packs and the 1+3 elemental packs are the first ones that come to mind. Lava damage on the bridge to Rhyolith is irritating also, though usually doesn't kill the pet. Also, my 2nd character is a priest, and this may be a QoL issue for priest more than hunter, but I know multiple priests who complain about Prayer of Mending with pets. I like the fact smart heals can target pets, but please please please make Prayer of Mending unable to target pets. It ends up timing out more often than not, which is just frustrating for the priest, and if the pet needs a heal, smart heals should cover it.
    Finally, buffs in a talent tree should always provide a personal benefit. MOST raid buffs do this, including Hunting Party, Improved Icy Talons, Abominations Might, etc etc etc. Give Trueshot 1-2% AP or something, even if you lower dmg elsewhere to compensate, it's irritating not having the buff covered cause someone missed a raid, it's irritating to have to respec for that because it's a DPS loss to pick it up 95% of the time. This should be extended to all classes.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I think Marks is pretty solid all around. The only annoyance in it is the delay time on multishot to wait for Bombardment to proc. It actually feels interactive, like I need to watch cooldowns on shots as well as focus and buff procs.
    I haven't played much beast, so I won't comment on it. Survival rotation is garbage. Explosive Shot damage is so high relative to other uses of GCD/cast time, that it -has- to be used on CD. In theory this is okay, except you hit the point where more haste doesn't help because delaying an explosive shot .5 sec is detrimental, and even trying to fit arcane shots into your rotation gives so little benefit that it isn't worth the risk or effort incase you even barely misjudge focus. 4pc t12 makes playing Survival a little more interesting because you get so much focus at times you -can't- mess it up, but even when you do the gain is so minor it hardly even matters. My best suggestion would be to make ISS work on Cobra, somehow make arcane shot more relevant, and compensate any damage gains by lowering explosive shot, making it a little less of a loss if it's delayed by half a GCD or something. Realize that survival picking up ISS would currently mean a loss of a good talent (Sic em or One with Nature). The spec is boring to play, its Exp>Cobra>Cobra>Cobra>Exp, rinse repeat, change it slightly with a LnL proc, replace a Cobra with a BA when it procs. You're perfectly fine ignoring focus, and on a lot of fights are even better ignoring focus or you'll get screwed on a target swap.
