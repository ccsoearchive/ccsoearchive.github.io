What type of content do you focus on? PvE.

If PvE, what type of PvE? Mostly heroic dungeons.

If PvP, what type of PvP? N/A.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? Traps are one of my biggest gripes about playing a hunter. Traps are awesome in the fact that they can be placed on almost any mob, but the 3-second "arm time" is bugged or something, because I have placed a trap and watched a mob (even when the trap was placed there for more than 3 seconds) run right over it and it never went off. Also, Immolation trap does so little damage it might as well not even be in the spellbook. It's probably a DPS loss to take the time to put Immolation trap up.

What makes playing your class more fun? I love pure DPS classes in general because of the lack of "responsibility" I have when I am playing them. That being said, I also love looking for opportunities to help my group out with CC and interrupts. I also love buff-juggling with my pets.

What makes playing your class less fun? Worrying about being kicked because my damage is too low or something even though I am just starting out with gearing up. Another thing that is slightly irritating is the rate at which my bow degrades. I can barely go three dungeons without having to repair it. I can't imagine what it's like (money- and convenience-wise) to have to raid for long periods of time.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? It's a pretty solid rotation I think, not too many buttons to worry about (but not too little either) and I can still think about interrupting and CC-ing without messing up my rotation.

What’s on your wish list for your class? I wish for traps to be less clunky.

What spells do you use the least? Immolation Trap, Snake Trap, Cobra Shot (because I am Marksmanship), Kill Command, Distracting Shot, Raptor Strike, Aspect of the Wild, Beast Lore. Also Counterattack in the Survival tree is one of the most useless abilities in the game. Hunters shouldn't be in melee range in the first place and have a very low chance to parry anyway.

There was another comment early on in this thread that mentioned having a spec with no pet. I think this would be an awesome thing to put into effect for a hunter. Solely relying on oneself instead of a pet would be an awesome feeling.

What type of content do you focus on? PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? I have done a lot of quests. I like to solo old content, or run it with groups much smaller than intended. I love heroics. I hope to raid more.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? Ozruk still hates hunters, last I checked. Tenacity pets using thunderstomp should be able to hold down mobs when I'm keeping Mend Pet on. Kill command only works if my pet is standing completely still? It seems like it does.

What makes playing your class more fun? I love the versatility and independence. I love being able to pull 5 different class buffs out of my pocket. I love being able to off tank with my pet. I love soloing old content. I loved soloing quests that recommended 3 players.

What makes playing your class less fun? There are situations in which my pet is blamed for pulling more mobs by attacking something from behind while everyone else is attacking from the front to avoid getting too close to another pack. This has happened with my raptor, who has no aoe to possibly use, in Stonecore, in Lost City, and recently in Zul'Aman. I don't control where the pet stands, and I don't see any easy way to fix it, except to give another pet's length between packs of mobs. Not everyone is willing to kite mobs away from their location in order keep "stupid huntard pets" from wiping them; although this problem would melt away if tank would do so. Struggling to regen focus after a couple of multishots when everyone is using aoe and 3 mobs die in a row while I'm trying to cobra shot them. Struggling to regen focus when I have a death knight in my face who doesn't let me cobra shot him.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? As mentioned before, Kill Command has a habit of messing up my rotation if the target is moving and my pet isn't standing still when I give the command. On the other hand, it seems like my standard, recommended rotation includes only skills that are available to both other specs: kill command, arcane shot, cobra, cobra (and cobra if KC and haste allow). I pop bestial wrath when available, and I love it, but I feel like we're missing something.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Since this is a wish list, the following might be varying levels of crazy. Glyph for Deterrence that allows some amount of self heal maybe, or a glyph for deterrence that regens some focus for attacks deflected by deterrence. For Devilsaurs to be less noisy. Scatter Shot working as an interrupt on spells that can be interrupted. A single target knock back, possibly sharing a cool down with disengage. For my pet to remember how much Stamina I have, or to at least spawn with a more reasonable amount of hp based on level--I haven't had 6k since sometime in Outlands, and at 85 I have around 30k without gear, so why is the default hp so low? Hunters seem to disagree, does Kill Command benefit from Mastery, since it is a pet attack and all that Mastery does for me is increase pet damage? My "special skill" Intimidation doesn't go into my rotation, so I'm using Kill Command, a skill that other specs get to ignore in favor of their special skills. The whole point of Beast Mastery is using your pet. Crazy Wish: Beast Mastery Special=Kill Command 2.0 with appropriate name. Please?

What spells do you use the least?
Intimidation. I have to say, my special gift for being Beast Mastery never ever gets used unless I'm absolutely desperate for a stun; if I'm solo, I really never need a single target taunt to get a single target off me--Charge/Kill Command combo does the same thing with a heap of damage, and feign death will wipe my threat in a hurry, and misdirection does its job. In a group, the tank needs to keep threat, and intimidation doesn't even work as an interrupt, and so is useless unless the tank dies on us--and even then, I just don't use it. I've probably gone more than a month without using it. It has absolutely no place in my rotation. Aimed shot? Explosive shot? Do other specs use their special skill?

Scare Beast: I'm too afraid the target will wander into more mobs, or else the target is immune. Although there was a really amusing Beauty kill in BRC where I cc'd both pups and somehow maintained decent dps.

I know there are times when I could possibly use Master's Call in pve, I just usually don't. I'll have to work on using it more.

Immolation trap. Seriously, it does absolutely nothing that Explosive trap doesn't do better, unless I'm missing something.

Raptor strike. Hunters are ranged. When our other couple of melee skills vanished, I thought you wanted to enforce this, and then I met Ozruk. He is the only reason Raptor Strike is up where I can click it. More useful in pvp with a glyph I think.

Eagle Eye. I just don't feel a need for it.

Have I mentioned I don't use Intimidation? Because I don't use intimidation.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    Both.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Heroics, raids, questing, & soloing old content

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    Arena & non-rated BG's

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    Love not having to worry about ammo or pet happiness. However, pets being summoned at full health would improve hunter quality of life dramatically. There have been lots of times where I needed to make an emergency pet change, either for pvp cooldowns or to pick up a dungeon boss when the tank dies, and it just gets one-shotted. This makes the change a costly waste, as I've spent the time to dismiss my first pet, but get a dead pet as a result. Especially costly in arena, as you usually can't get the time to revive pet. Also happens on occasion when questing, which is less costly, but just as annoying.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    Being able to do my rotation on the run is amazing. Being able to tank with my pet not only saves my groups from wipes, but is impressive to boot. Camouflage is a fun ability. I love having to CC in instances, as it gives me something to do with all my utility & makes dps feel like a bigger part of the team, rather than just pew pew machines. Being able to provide pet raid buffs helps with that as well.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Minimum range. Hate it.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I run SV for instances and BM for questing & pvp. I absolutely love the SV rotation. The BM rotation feels too simple to me, though. There are only a few buttons to hit - it really seems like they could use another ability.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    I'd like a better distinction between specs. Right now, MM is statistically the best spec for anything you want to do. That irks me to no end. Especially when I get raid leaders & pvp partners scoffing at me for not being MM. I prefer the play style of SV & BM, and I'd like to see changes that really define what each spec is best at, rather than MM being the catch-all for power gamers.

    I'd especially like for BM to feel more unique. Every hunter spec relies on their pets, but I'd imagine BM to be the spec that gets the most use out of them. Instead, they seem to be handicapped by their pets. BM hunters get perks to their pets, but they don't seem powerful enough to really distinguish a BM pet from normal pet. When I spec out my pets, I can get every talent I really want in SV, so the extra pet talents are just going to more trivial abilities. The biggest handicap I see is that BM hunters are useless once their pet dies, whereas MM is actually the spec that buffs its pet the most, but is just fine if it dies. BM needs some sort of overhaul to make it feel like a true master of beasts, rather than just "that other hunter spec."

    Also, taming more creatures. I would love to be able to tame almost everything, and the idea of engineers having mechanical pets is an interesting one. I've also seen a suggestion for letting hunters use pets as mounts, and I have to say that would rock.

    What spells do you use the least?
    Counterattack, Raptor Strike, Eagle Eye, Immolation Trap, Beast Lore.

    I have never, in all my years of playing, found a use for Counterattack. I used to use Raptor Strike (and the old Mongoose Strike) back when we had ammo, to conserve some on weak enemies, but it's been useless since ammo was taken out. I only touch Eagle Eye when I'm terribly bored defending a flag - never actually found a tactical use for it. Immolation Trap is *very* situational - I only touch it when I'm dueling, and I don't duel much at all. I used to use Beast Lore to see if I could tame interesting looking creatures, but nowadays I already know what I'm planning to tame, via Petopia.
