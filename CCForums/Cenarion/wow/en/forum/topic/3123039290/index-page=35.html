What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

My biggest quality of life issue is the remaining bug with focus. I realize that server lag is a concern but because the issue is intermittent this seems unlikely. What i mean is sometimes a shot will cost approximately 10 more focus than expected (almost as if a steady shots focus gain was correctly displayed by the client but not recognized by the server. For example, i fire two steady shots in a row, landing me at 53 focus - if i am using aimed shot as a filler i will now attempt to hard cast aimed shot - however sometimes i will get not enough focus as an error until i reach 60 focus at which point the cast will begin, consuming all 60 focus. Each of the steady shots cast did hit and do damage, and each rewarded 10 focus, but the server didn't recognize them. A frustrating issue.
Another frustrating issue is that every so often when i swap pets out for a new call of the wild my new pet (which was rezzed prior to the fight and should be alive), will not come out. Not only that but after dismissing and attempting to resummon to get the new pet, I lose the ability to summon any pet, dismiss any pet, or rez any pet. Relogging seems to resolve the issue (but i cant relog during heroic rag =P).

What makes playing your class more fun?

Playing hunter is made more fun (for me) by fights that require very focused movement (aspect tabbing) in order to maximize dps. This allows me to take advantage of the mobility of my class, allowing me to push ahead of my guilds dks, demo locks, and arcane mages on damage done =).

What makes playing your class less fun?

Frankly? The terrifying randomness of the t-12 4 piece. Unfortunately enough, although it is a dps increase, it is SO inconsistant that it is not really a great indiciator of skill. If i get lucky and proc 8 times during CA phase back to back i gain a huge amount of dps. However, some attempts i dont proc at all, or i proc RIGHT as i am casting arcane shot. This is compared to the enhancement shaman 4 piece which rewards great play by increasing overall damage by 6%. Instead, we have a bonus that effectively stops us from playing well and tests our ability to have good luck and handle randomness as best as possible. Simply put, just too much rng - i think this is reflected in the difference between end game hunters and single target demo locks/spriests/arcane mages/enh shamans.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Rotation is fine (at 1825 haste CS SS SS AS AS SS) however, it is frustrating that agility is so overblown statwise - upgrades are simply higher ilevel gear almost regardless of if they have good stats.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

My wishlist includes some things that I doubt I will get -
1. Make the hunter dps rotation more punishing (not with huge rng like the set bonus) build in more reliable procs that will allow strong players to be rewarded to a greater extent than poor ones. I think demo locks are a good example of what i mean here - the skill cap is much larger, allowing for very strong players to always destroy weaker players who are more lucky. With hunter the rotation is so simple that there is no real difficulty AT ALL in a rotation that involves standing still.
2. A set bonus for next tier that again, rewards good play. A proc for agility that comes from using Chimera Shot - rewarding people for using it on cooldown? A set bonus that increases the damage dealt by arcane shot, rewarding proper focus management? Most of the pvp ramifications of a pve set bonus dont need to be worried about anymore because of the strength of resilience.
3. A reduced relience and or scared ness of CA/hero. When my guild decides that heroing on the pull is optimal, I can effectively right off any dps records at all. The time the boss is at 100%-90% is shortened, and using rapid fire with hero is also sub optimal, but i must call with hero while CA is active or I will waste the phase. Simply but, I feel that CA is a crutch for making leveling hunters feel "good" about getting crits with aimed shot. On any long fight (heroic rag for example), that heals back up, my dps is destroyed because i spend less time with +60% crit. Its a clunky mechanic and i would like to see it be packed away and the dps gain rolled into something else for marks.

What spells do you use the least?

Nothing in particular (snake trap i guess)

A lot of things that made hunters viable and desired in wrath and bc disappeared in cataclysm (ranged MS, viper sting, reliable burst due to higher crit chance). Most other classes got useful new abilities while hunters mostly got tools to do the same things but with focus instead.

As for PvP the passive focus regen is way too slow, and since it's harder to get all our steady shots off, most hunters feel focused starved outside of rapid fire. Master marksman procs are way too random. They should either be 100% chance per steady, or the required stack reduced to 3 or given the maelstrom weapon treatment and each stack reduces the cast time.

Aspect swapping between fox and hawk is annoying and does not add skill, most people just macro them into shots. Aspects should either be given more attention or removed entirely, they feel outdated and only two of five are used heavily.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
There are many little things that could be done (that also don't affect PvE) but most hunters would be satisfied with a few major changes.

The way we generate focus needs improvement in PvP. Attack speed debuffs (frost fever etc) should not affect the cast time of steady shot. It's hard enough casting steady in PvP, those debuffs are overkill. Our passive regen is hilariously slow, to the point of it being nonexistent. Steady shot needs to have it's cast time reduced, or it needs to give back more than 9 focus, or our passive regen should be buffed. All these could be done through the PvP set bonus, not affecting PvE.

As most people will point out, our self healing is basically nonexistent. It would be nice to get some kind of reliable self heal ability but what's far more important is some kind of defense against dots. Deterrence is a perfectly fine defensive cd half the time, the other half it's completely useless. Dot heavy teams can easily beat hunter teams by hugging pillars and spreading dots. Without any defense at all against dots it puts major pressure on our healers to keep us up. A dot removal or a percent damage reduction would help a lot.

Finally, I think most hunters would just like to believe that their class is actually being paid attention to. A lot of hunters have good ideas, a lot also have terrible ones, but it's disheartening to rarely see any blue posts or changes in patch notes when there is definite room for improvement.

First, I should thank you for the many QoL improvements you've given hunters: No more bullets to stack up in a dedicated bag slot, twenty stable slots in addition to the five stand-by, many of the raid bosses are shootable in our deadzone, every hunter spec is balanced with each other, and you let our pets run along side us again while we're on the ground.

I think that the melee range cut off for our ranged attacks (aka the deadzone) remains the flaw in our masterpeice. I understand why it's there, but I am required as a hunter to resent it.

The distribution of pet buffs is another thing. While there are 9 possible buffs any hunter can bring to a group, it often comes down to only 4 that a hunter needs to fill almost any pug's gap. Of those four, ravagers seem to have the least diversity, coming in original and extra spikey, though many different combinations of colors. Sporebats have only three colors to them. I'm not sure what i'm asking, more colors, switch some of the buffs around, perhaps new families? There are herds of deer and wild equines out there *hint hint*.

•What makes playing your class more fun?
The pets, definitely. Finding and taming a rare you want is one of the best thrills still in the game. Going back to old content or just soloing quests that other classes need whole groups to finish. The sheer diversity of companions we can have by our side (no matter how few the game mechanics allow for) is just fantastic. Our unique role as physical dps allows us to keep pouring out arrows while mages and locks are budgetting their mana or sitting out a silence effect.

•What makes playing your class less fun?
When the raid calls for stacking up on the boss (or nasty sub-boss trash) and i'm still a ways out because of the boss's massive hit box, that's when the dead zone is less fun. When Shannox requires hunters to be Marksman spec because nothing else hits Rageface hard enough to save a raider's life, that's less fun. When a dragon's air phase leaves our pets staring blankly at the sky, that's less fun (as BM). When Nefarion used to break our weapons in half while mages only casted Polymorph: Giraffe, that was a not fun at all. So aside from Shannox, I guess it's not fun when bosses play to our weaknesses and nothing can be done about it. Oh, and that Aspect of the Cheetah still dazes us even with druids and shaman in the indoor 30% business. Have only one spec that can inteurrupt can make a fella feel helpless sometimes, and three pets with different cooldowns doesn't help.

•How do you feel about your “rotation”?
BM's rotation is simple, almost too simple. If they had another button to hit while kill command was on cooldown that wasn't arcane shot, then it'd feel right. Survival's Lock and Load proc makes it pure chaos, which isn't a bad thing. You're constantly trying to use up your focus bar, and then suddenly it's gone and it's the scramble to fill it back up and start all over. Marksman in comparison is refined, you have big expensive shots and fill in the gaps refilling your focus with pairs of steady shots, the procs are pleasant bonuses, not priority shifting change-ups. I do think that MM stole a few of BM's talents, though. What does a marksman need with a free kill command?

•What’s on your wish list for your class?
More stable slots please (if there was room for one of every animal in the world (of warcraft) then hunters would still ask for more ;P), more pet families, more options within each family, more raid level guns, dual-wielding pistols as an option to rifles, riding certain pet families as mounts, a "Stampede" button (for BM), Beaster Mastery (the talent) moved lower, Misdirection trained much MUCH earlier, and pets as a viable fifth tanking class (might need to bring back Eyes of the Beast for that one).

•What spells do you use the least?
Flare, Master's Call, Widow's Venom, Scare beast, Wing Clip but those are typically pvp spells. On the pve side, Beast Lore and Eagle Eye aren't used often. But i wouldn't want to give them up. As BM, Intimidation is mostly an after thought. SV's Counter Attack is a wasted talent point, in my opinion, though my pvp brethren might disagree. All of MM's toolkit gets a workout in raids.

You guys have done a fantastic job getting hunters to where they are today and it's hard to think of things that aren't simply "gimme more" or long standing gripes with built-in weaknesses. Thanks for your time.

Pre-casting trap launcher is a valid tactic, but often results in a wasted GCD and the focus spent. Lastly, sometimes you simply want to put a trap by your feet, and have to find a way to cancel the trap launcher pre-emptively.

  • A sense of control in PvP, and a sense of usefulness in PvE. Kiting a warrior or MDing to the tank for a complicated pull, for example.

Manual management of fox is, of course, the best, but is a button we press several times every cycle and requires only to not forget it.
