Arenas (2s for Conquest) and BGs at times.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
My biggest quality of life issue by far is focus as far as PvP goes. Focus makes it difficult for me to keep pressure on a target, especially if pressure is being applied on me. Switching between Aspect of the Hawk and Fox feels very clumsy for me, even after playing hunter in Cata for several months.
When playing as Beast Master in Arenas, pet pathing is a quality of life issue. Say I'm fighting someone on the bridge in Blade's Edge Arena or near the ledge in Dalaran Sewer, my opponent simply needs to jump down to cut my damage down to a fraction, as my pet needs to run around and down the ramp, then back to my opponent. Then if I need my pet to assist me back up top, I need to wait for it to run around and back up to me.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Hunter has been my main off and on since I started playing towards the end of BC. The aspects I find more fun are having a pet (along with a variety of pets for various jobs) and being able to deal good burst and sustained damage (at least in a PvE setting) at range without having to rely on spells.

What makes playing your class less fun?
As stated before, I'm not a fan of focus overall. Also, in a pvp setting, I'm not fond of having to depend on others simply to avoid being an easy kill more or less. Thirdly, I don't like Marksman's lack of mobility, especially on fights like Heroic Shannox. I need to be mobile to break face rages and kite Rageface, but need to stand still in order to pull decent dps.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I'm happy with my dps rotations overall. I don't like having to stand still to achieve maximum effectiveness in Marksman, but I guess that's just something I'll have to cope with.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Flying pets that could actually fly... would solve the pathing issue I stated earlier. Also, a semi-strong melee attack would be nice. I remember the devs discussing the idea at one point or another.

How about 100yd range on all shots and the ability to fire around corners? I can dream, can't I?

What spells do you use the least?
Aspect of the Wild - extremely situational
Beast Lore - WoWhead or Petopia are much better sources
Feed Pet - Mend pet does just fine
Scare Beast - situational again, much better skills for crowd control
Widow Venom - good if you're by yourself, I guess, but enough other classes have the healing debuff, so I don't need to use it hardly at all
Immolation Trap - weak compared to Explosive Trap
Raptor Strike - situational, only used for the damage reduction while glyphed
Wing Clip - Concussive Shot does the job in most cases
Counterattack - while useful, Survival is less desirable to use in PvP and the talent is too deep into the Survival tree for other specs to take it.
Distracting Shot - while it has potential, I've only used it a handful of times in my hunter career

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

The largest quality of life improvement for the hunter class would be removing the dead zone. The hunter is the only ranged class that cannot deal damage in melee range, and this cuases problems in both PVE and PVP. I still recall being yelled at by a tank who had never played a hunter to stack up for the bonespikes on Marrowgar back in ICC days and trying to explain the hunter deadzone to him. The present mechanics in Cata dungeons that allow ranged abilites to be used at close range during certain phases of certain fights is a good start, however, we need a stronger counter for PVP. One of the largest reasons I don't pvp much is due to the fact that it seems far too easy for other clasases to get up in my face and stay there, thus gimping my damage. If the deadzone were removed, this wouldn't be such a problem. The incentive would still remain to stay at range for survivability, but it would prevent other classes from rendering me essentially helpless and greatly improve hunter pvp gameplay.
The new Assist pet stance is problematic because the pet will try to run back to me while I am switching targets, thus losing dps, and Defensive stance is basically useless.
The current trap launcher mechanic is clunky, difficult, and causes me to lose valuble dps during fights that require cc on the fly because I have to stop me rotation, hit the trap laucher, selcet my trap, and then position it.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The greatest part of the hunter class is the wonderful customization available to us in the form of our pets. I love and cherish the ability to name and spec each one fo my pets. I level primarily through solo questing, and having a pet at my side makes the experience far more enjoyable, which is probably the reason I have far more hunter alts than alts of any other classes.
The ability to solo old content with my own personal tank at my side is another great feature of the hunter class, as well as sucessfully kiting someone in PVP!

The deadzone, as stated above, as well as the diasappearing pet bar bug. The poor state of hunter pvp is a big drawback and turns off alot of new hunters.
