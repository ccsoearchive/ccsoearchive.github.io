What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Lack of a sustainable self-heal and minimum range. While most classes have an effective way of staying-off the black hand of death, hunters only have a 5% recovery with Chimera shot on a 10-second cool down. That is only if we even specialize in Marksmanship.

Lack of stuns, where our traps last only 4 seconds, our scatter shot lasts 4, and our Silencing shot 4 - when a fear, Cyclone, and hex lasts a strong 5-10 seconds each.

Our minimum range was intuitive except for the fact that no other ranged DPS class has that restriction. We are rendered useless in so many cases, it severely limits the strength of our class in comparison to others. Example:

-Ragnaros's huge hit box and the need to stack near him in order to AOE adds.
-Ragnaros's adds which we can not even attack while stacked
-Eyes of Occultar, we become seemingly useless in the AOE
-Bael'Roc's huge hit box and the inability to continue DPS while taking a shard
-Positioning of Beth'ilac in 2nd phase - being forced to stand off-stack when burning the boss
-Unable to attack Spirit of the Flames during scorpion phase of the Majordomo fight.

Essentially, in any case with Hunters, we're being limited when others are not. This makes us less useful of a class and hinders all players using Hunters. These are just a small list of severe problems with the minimum range you've inflicted upon the Hunter population.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I enjoy the flexibility in a hunter and my pet. I have a motley of abilities which serve well in both a PVE and PVP environment.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The honest fact that everything we can do, some one has a clear edge over us. We're OK at almost everything, but excel in almost nothing.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I'm content with my rotation and what I've developed for myself.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Removal of minimum range
Better trap-launching-interface
Better stuns/silences

What spells do you use the least?

Eagle Eye, Venomous Shot

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

There are multiple problems with hunters in PvP in my opinion. Our damage, crowd control, and defensive abilities are all mediocre compared to other classes.

The removal of not being able to shoot in melee range is a main issue. It's so easy for melee to get on a hunter and us not be able to do anything about it. For example: if I am getting hit by a warrior I disengage, then the warrior just charges back to me and im right back where I was without being able to put out damage to pressure the warrior. Another reason why hunters should be able to shoot in melee range is the problem of line of sighting a hunter. In arena its so easy for a target to get behind something and hunters cant do anything. If the person just runs circles around a pole we can't do anything about it. Traps help with this but mainly the people that do this are healers and they just cleanse themselves or shift to get out of slows. The hunter can't do damage to them without following but we get outrun because if we stay close to them we can't shoot them. The worst map is Dalaran Arena for this. It's so annoying. Unless hunters melee attacks get buffed so that they can pressure someone (which is not a great idea) then hunters need to be able to shoot in melee range.

Another major issue with hunters is our crowd control in PvP. Traps are great if they land and with the randomness of scatter shot trapping it can cause a player to move and landing a trap on them doesn't always work because they can randomly move away from it in the scatter shot. I propose changing freezing trap into a frost arrow. The frost arrow freezes a target when it hits them but it is actually a shot not a trap so you dont have to select a place on the ground to set it. I think it should not be placed on the ground rather fired at your target and always hit. This would allow hunters to have a good crowd control ability that would be easier and more efficient to use. It would make hunters alot more viable in arenas because we would have a good crowd control. This would allow hunters to use scatter shot defensively which would help greatly for us to help us escape from melee or interrupt enemy casts.

Hunters defensive cooldowns are not so great. If you disengage you normally get charged by a warrior or death gripped by a death knight or another class uses it's gap closer on you. It's a good skill but only gives you a few seconds of not getting hit and they are right back on you. Detterence is great but dots still can kill you. I think if camouflage was changed so that it was usuable in combat and dropped stuns, bleeds, and dots with around a two minute cooldown it would go a great way to improve hunters defensively and make them more comparable to other classes defensive abilities. Besides camouflage is almost useless anyway except to start an arena with but they can see you anyway and just throw an aoe your way which knocks you out. This way camouflage is improved and hunters can live a little bit longer than usual.

There are also some smaller changes that I think would help hunters out in PvP. For example I feel that the removal of scorpid sting was unnecessary. I liked how it had a change combined with chimera shot to disarm a target. I think it would be cool to have scorpid sting reintroduced or have a similar ability like a disarming shot or something to have a ten second disarm. Maybe a two to three minute cooldown would justify this. It would help hunters out alot when getting facerolled by a melee class.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    Battlegrounds. May do arenas and rated BGs at a later date.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    The disappearing pet bar is a nuisance, but not a terrible one. Pet pathing, though, is probably the biggest quality-of-life issue for me at the moment; they have a propensity to get stuck or dissapear. This is less prevalent in PvE, but other players in PvP have a very easy time using level geometry to nullify my pet.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    The abundance of mobility is definitely an enjoyable thing; being able to disengage or give other people a speed boost with Absence of the Pack gives me a measure of battlefield navigation that's hard to find in the other classes I've played. Of course, the simple fact of being a ranged class is also fun as it can put me in a unique position to target exposed or alone players. As far as specific abilities, I really enjoy Camo in BGs; having an enemy team walk right by me to leave me behind their lines with their healers is satisfying.

    In terms of PvE, it feels like precise timing is rewarded with tangible gains in DPS, and feign death and misdirect are both very useful. It's also fun to stop the party from wiping in a heroic if the tank goes down by having my pet take over.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    The focus regen can seem quite on the slow side at times, particularly in PvP; having to continually cast Steady Shot repeatedly (as I play principally MM spec) can be limiting, as it's not very proactive; Aspect of the Fox alleviates this somewhat, but it's still frustrating getting, say, a healer down low, using your killshot and other DPS abilities, and finding you have to start using SS to continue fighting- The DPS on which is low enough that they can heal up through it.

    In terms of more general features of the class rather than spec, it feels like there isn't as much utility to the class as there could be in PvP. Our traps are hard to target compared to other classes' direct fire or AoE roots, and though there are an abundance of slows they don't seem particularly useful, as in any instance where slowing an enemy is beneficial outside of 1v1 kiting (for instance, a flag carrier), doing damage is much more beneficial.

    The LoS limitations for the class can be quite frustrating, at times, as maneuvering yourself so that an enemy cannot simply run out of your sight when you are midway through a steady shot or aimed shot often puts you close enough that they can simple run into melee range; disengaging from them or otherwise putting more distance between yourself and them then leaves them free to hide from your shots again. I've come to accept this as a limitation of the class, unless this becomes Wanted and we can start doing trick-shots around corners, but it can still be quite frustrating at times.

    As for PvE, truth be told outside of the pet niggles I mentioned before there isn't a whole lot to complain about. If anything, it feels like crit is so important to our damage that there isn't much benefit to taking any haste, but that could just be my playstyle.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    In both PvE and PvP, I feel conflicted; though I do only play MM and can thus only give feedback on that, the sheer volume of Steady Shots we have to cast can get monotonous. While it's not difficult, and it feels a little odd arguing against something that's relatively effortless, it feels like most of my time spent doing damage is watching the SS cast meter move and anticipating how cool it's going to be when I get to finally launch my Chimera Shot/Aimed Shot MIRV nuke at the enemy. While the other two classes I've played have had ramp-up as well (My 85 ret paladin's HoPo, and my level 40 rogue's combo system), both of those had multiple abilities that allowed them to regenerate their respective nuke meter, and felt a bit more dynamic because of it.

    Aside from that, the change to Careful Aim has made it feel a little disappointing; I know raids are different, but in the case of heroic bosses and PvP enemies drop below 90 percent so fast I rarely get to use more than one Aimed Shot. That may be intentional, but the Careful Aim phase gave a bit more variety to fighting.
