What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
--PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
--Primarily 10-man raids. But I also do a lot of Heroics/Questing/Crafting/Old World content/etc.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
--In regards to family-specific pet abilities:

There's a plethora of different tameable pet styles out there, and that's great, but I still manage to feel pidgeon-holed into using the same types of pets in a raid setting. Perhaps players could have a few choices available for their pet's ability whether via trainer or talent tree. In larger raids, most of the pet-specific buffs/debuffs are naturally covered, so pet choice is more vanity than needs-based. But for 10-mans (and downard) there's usually a hole or two that needs filling. Especially in the case of rare or challenge tames, it would be nice to bring a much-loved spirit beast to my raid and still cover a phys damage buff as opposed to agi/str instead of Herpderp the ravager I tamed 5 seconds ago.

There could still be restrictions in place, of course, with the types of abilities available dependant on whether they're ferocity/cunning/tenacity. But still, a little flexibility is a nice thought.

Also, seconded for the Shoveltusk tame suggestion I read above.

What makes playing your class more fun?
--I'm crazy, but CC. I loved the vanilla role of hunters. I like triangulating traps, snaring, stunning, generally feeling like I've got the ability to sway the fight in our favor even though I'm not tanking or healing. I liked that CC used to also require a little skill, but I also (painfully) understand the reason why that was eliminated.

What makes playing your class less fun?
--Not having Eyes of the Beast anymore :(

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
--Apologies in advance if I wall of text.

I spec Surv for raid, but I'm BM for everything else. I love BM. I love it hard. It's how I feel the hunter class should be played both from a gameplay and lore standpoint. But it's an unforgiving spec, and as such gets little love in a raid setting.

The BM rotation is crisp and clean. Bestial Wrath on a 1-ish minute cooldown lines up nicely with my 1min engineering gloves, or (to a lesser extent) a 2min trinket. I enter into a fight with a BW burn, then continue right into a Rapid Fire phase in conjunction with my 3.5min Call of the Wild. Exiting that brings me up nearly on the start of another BW.

Kill Command is satisfying and movement-friendly, Frenzy when I'm not hasted by other buffs, and simply dumping excess focus on arcane shots. The downside to this rotation is, if I'm not utilizing my cooldowns as I should be, I see a far greater decrease in DPS than I do when I'm slacking on my other specs. I'm assuming that's why I still see few BM hunters, and I even get periodically asked why I'm not MM (even when I'm doing 17k MORE dps than the other folks in the raid.)

It's also a great rotation for questing/heroics/generally any mobs that need to die quickly without much setup. Plus exotic pets are fun. I'm honestly surprised this wasn't built as the 'main' spec for PvE hunters.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
--ONE set of hunter tier that doesn't look like I picked up something dead and decided to wear it. Please. Think of the humans. We make stupid-looking murlocs.

More difficult hunter challenge tames. Perhaps summon-based vs. spawn-based with difficult to obtain quest items ala vanilla demon kiting.

What spells do you use the least?
--Immolation Trap, Raptor Strike, Eagle Eye

    ■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    -Focus generation is a pretty big issue in PvP. It's hard to make a ranged class with uptime issues balanced without a slowly depleting linear resource system, like mana. Perhaps tieing some (more) of our focus generation into our pets and solving some of their survivability / kiteability problems would help solve this.
    -The shared trap CD. Trap types is a thing of the past. In both PvE and PvP, 1-2 (if any) different kinds of traps are used on any given encounter, and their effects can be replecated in much less broken ways by other classes. Most of these classes don't have these abilities tied to the cooldowns of other abilities. Its just not as much fun playing the binary system of using 1 trap every 30secs (or slightly shorter), when other classes are enjoying dynamic CC and bonus dps through much more accessible means.

    ■What makes playing your class more fun?
    -Taming rare creatures, being the only class to frequently and effectively employ technology to their weapons (guns, crossbows), control 2 characters at once, enjoying the flavour of the hunter class in non-competitive environments (aspects, tracking, soloing content, etc)

    ■What makes playing your class less fun?
    -Losing flavourful abilities like 'Eyes of the Beast' for perpetually no reason
    -Managing focus against opponents who are effective at countering it
    -Arming time on traps
    -Virtually no melee viability

    ■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    -Not as challenging and dynamic as it could be. At least we're not arcane mages, right?

    ■What’s on your wish list for your class?
    -A solution to focus
    -More pets, but not as in more models, I'd LOVE to see more families added to our selection (hydras, basilisks, perhaps some dragonkin or mammoth)
    -More time spent on developing an interesting AND viable survival tree.

    ■What spells do you use the least?
    -Widow Venom, Scare beast, Aspect of the Wild, Wing Clip, Serpent Sting, Immolation Trap, Explosive Trap

    The pet talent trees are too front-loaded. Most of the later talents aren't very useful at all, leading to the first two tiers being completely filled in while very few of the later-tier talents are picked up.

    # What makes playing your class more fun?
    Having a large variety of potential pets to tame and tamed pets to adventure with - there's a kind of bond between hunter and pet, and that comes across in-game very well. The tracking spells are incredibly useful.

    # What makes playing your class less fun?
    I feel that focus doesn't regenerate fast enough, which leads to constantly using the same two shots to restore it (see below).

    # How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    It's decent, though I feel like I spend half my rotation using Steady Shot or Cobra Shot to restore focus. I understand that's part of how it's supposed to work, but it's neither fun nor interesting.

    # What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Pistols. Lots of pistols. I love dual-wielding, even if it's not best for the hunter's choice of melee weapon (aka "stat stick"), so shooting two pistols would be awesome.

    Being able to use tamed pets as mounts. I know not all would be available, but monster types that already have mounts in game (cats, wolves, raptors, rhinos, etc) should be usable as mounts for hunters.

    A spell similar to the mage's Mirror Image that allows the hunter to call upon all the pets they currently have with them. The additional pets wouldn't be controlled, but it would be an excellent cooldown and very fun to see.

    Displaying the hunter's ranged weapon instead of their melee weapon. I don't care what my staff looks like, but I'd like to see my gun, bow, or crossbow slung over my back. Bringing back the quiver would also be great if someone's using a bow or crossbow.

    # What spells do you use the least?
    The melee attacks, Scatter Shot, Tranquilizing Shot, Deterrence.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    As a hunter, I don't like the fact that feeding your pet no longer has any value. Hunter pets were better than demons or elementals because we could pump up their DPS by keeping them fed and happy. Now that pet happiness has been taken away, it feels like we're just another pet class.

    On the positive side, I LOVE the fact that hunters no longer require intellect. It was such a pain having to compete with every Shaman I grouped with over gear when I started this character. Focus is a great alternative to the old system.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    DPS from a safe distance in PVE, and the fact that there are still tameable pets with truly unique skins, i.e. Pogeyan

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Spirit beasts being exotic... blah.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    My rotation may not be the right one, but I'm happy with it. I open with Serpent Sting and arcane shot until I'm out of focus. I then spam Steady Shot until my focus is 100%, hit with a Chimera Shot and then AS again until focus is gone. Aimed Shot whenever it procs, two kill shots at a time.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    - More interesting minor glyphs, maybe some that actually do something. "Lesser Proportion" is fun for a giggle, but doesn't help.
    - Beast Mastery still seems to have less damage output than Survival and Marksmanship. I'd like to see something implemented that would make it more competitive, rather than just "You get cooler pets."
    - As an interrupt, Silencing Shot is pretty imbalanced. That 20 second cooldown is debilitating compared to those of melee classes (Rebuke, Pummel and Kick all have a 10 second cooldown).

    What spells do you use the least?
    Snake Trap, Immolation Trap, Distracting Shot, Feed Pet
