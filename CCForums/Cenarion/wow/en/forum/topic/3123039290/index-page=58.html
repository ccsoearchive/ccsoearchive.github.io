What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

1) pvp

2) Too many un-kill-able classes now in BGs. Takes 3 or 4 to kill some pallys, druids, shamans, and priests. And even then it is slow. Or make make the rest of us un-kill-able too.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

1) Just what pve dungeons I have to in order to get gear, enchantment, or rep for pvp.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

1) Arena is too small for hunter. Arena has too many line of sight issues for hunter. Arena still has hunter pet bugs. Hunters always have been and always will be the least represented in area: it is like fighting in a closet for a hunter. Only do arena because have to for pvp gear.

2) BGs is where I spend my time. Most of the BGs are big enough to work for a hunter. Still waiting for AV map to be made into a mirror-map. This issue will not go away until finally fixed. North map has many more choke points.

3) Rated BGs means you have to depend on others who don’t know how to play and won’t listen, so not much fun. Pug Rated BG teams are worthless.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

1) Being able to summon a new pet when one pet dies without having to first resurrect the old pet. I think warlocks can do this now.

2) Getting a pet that does range damage again. The old windserpents that did lighting breath magical-damage is an example or the old serpents that did spit magical-damage.

3) Not being flagged for pvp when coming out of a BG. This way I could quest or farm mats while I waited. No reason to be flagged from a BG. Yes, keep being flagged from world pvp and make it longer even. But just waste time having to wait out BG five minutes and knowing if you are doing anything, even archeology, that you can get ganked if you go in a BG without being in a safe city. So much time is unnecessarily lost to this. I do not have this problem when I play Rift.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Good pets.
Good escape abilities.
Good damage
Good slows.

What makes playing your class less fun?

1) DKs have no cooldown on their cc known as chains of ice. They can stupidly spam chains of ice and my pvp trinket is then worthless because it does have a cooldown.

2) Smart mages and shamans just rush and rush again a hunter knowing the 15 feet of dead space makes the hunter an easy target if you can keep that distance closed. The dead zone still exists and is still exploited.

3) Obviously all the pet bugs: (a) disappearing pet bar; (b) disappearing pet when getting off tanks, turrets, or cannons; (c) pet coming in with low health so one shot in both pve and pvp; (d) disappearing pet when you are running away and need it most.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

1) Need to provide pre-made macros. Perhaps for sale on the ah or a vendor. Or, need to make a graphical interface for macros so we don’t have to all be programmers.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

1) Being able to shoot at point blank range (PBR) like real-life hunters.

2) Having a pet that does range magical-damage like serpents or windserpents used to do.

3) Having aoe capability back again.

4) Shots requiring less focus so we don’t run out in a fight with a paladin, druid, or shaman who never runs out of mana and always has power for best attacks. Or, faster focus regeneration. You cannot use focus generating shots in a BG because they are so slow you are dead.

5) Put any pet I want into any class of pet I want, with the few exceptions that would be silly. Give any pet I want any pet special-ability I want, with the few exceptions that would be silly.

What spells do you use the least?

Listed in order of least used from top to bottom:
Distracting Shot
Raptor Strike
Widow Venom
Steady Shot

    ■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    Pet Bar bug and new Pet stances. Assist is fine for a lot of things, but I miss the 'old defensive' stance, where my pet would attack my target until it died, then change to my new target. Currently, if I use assist during Alysrazor, my pet will spend most of its time running between the hatchling and the druids since I switch targets often. To solve this I have to use defensive stance and tell my pet to attack the hatchling. Then when the hatchling dies, I then have to command it to attack Alysrazor, else it just stands there until I take damage. The old Defensive stance would make this a non-issue.

    Minimum range in group-up AOE phases. Occuthar, Rhyolith, Rag, etc. Having the minimum range on Multishot is pretty frustrating. Before it was removed, these would be prime spots for trap + Volley. Removing minimum range on multishot would help greatly, but eventually our focus fizzles out and we cant recoup it while in minimum range, which brings me too...

    Fervor should be a class skill, not just for BM.

    Focus regen outside of combat, between trash pulls is a bit discouraging. Having to start a fight using your focus regen shot is not very fun, and it lessens the usefulness of Misdirect.

    Disengage only usable while in combat. I can't think of a good reason why we can't be allowed to disengage out of combat. Other classes/races have instant movement without the combat restriction.

    Pet tame interrupted by damage.

    For SV, Arcane Shot shouldn't use up LnL charges. We often need a focus dump when LnL procs along with ToTH, and Kill Command is clunky and unwanted on an already full plate of damage abilities.

    ■What makes playing your class more fun?

    Pets. Instant, ranged shots. Versatile CC. Aspect of the Fox.

    ■What makes playing your class less fun?

    Current Pet stances. Minimum range when forced to group up for certain mechanics. Lack of point-blank AoE outside of Traps. Focus regen rate between trash pulls.

    ■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    Rotation is OK for BM. MM can get messy depending on haste and procs. SV can get GCD locked when ToTH and LnL proc together.

    ■What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Bring back old Defensive stance for Pets. Ferver for all specs. Give BM a better lvl-10, spec-defining ability than Intimidation. Point-Blank Multishot or Volley. Allow us to tame Exotics without having to be BM at the time. Take Arcane Shot out of LnL.

    Buld-a-pet workshop. Since the pets are so homogenized now, let us pick the skin, the tree, and the extra ability.

    ■What spells do you use the least?

    Eagle Eye, Widow Venom, Immolation Trap, Non-human/beast Tracking.

    # What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    Focus in PvP is a HUGE problem. I find myself running out of focus way too many times when I'm about to finish off player and having to stop and regenerate focus while they heal and I have to start all over again only to end up having to stop and get more focus.... and well it's a vicious cycle that never seems to end. It's not that big of an issue when I have assistance, but solo it's very stressful to deal with.

    I understand that having a minimum range is part of playing the class, but not being able to do anything when I have to be in minimum range of a boss or enemy player due to mechanics is very frustrating. At least in PvP I can scatter shot then disengage to get away from my enemies but in PvE I can't do that. My best examples are fights like Baleroc and Shannox. On Baleroc when it's my turn to be on the crystal, I have to ask the tanks to move the boss for me otherwise my dps drops to zero and that loss in dps is enough to make us hit the enrage timer or come close by only a few seconds. On Shannox, when the dog is in my face, I can only beat him with my polearm, kite him around into immolation traps, and loose all that time DPSing (unless I feign and dump him on one of my friends which tends to be a squishy clothie). Not being able to shoot at point blank range can make or break a fight sometimes and I wish it was not that way. I like making sure I'm not standing in bad stuff rather than cursing under my breath about not being able to shoot my target cause it's beating my face in.

    My second issue is not as big, but I think that trap launcher should not be on a global cooldown or separate spell. Increase the focus cost of traps by the cost of trap launcher, change the glyph to make it reduce the focus cost of trapping all together, and make the trap spell a one click button to bring up the targeting area to place the trap. That will remove the need of having trap launcher as a separate spell.

    Also scattershot should be an interrupt universal to all hunter speccs so if my raid wants me to interrupt a spell (like the adds on Nef or Alysrazor. I really wish scattershot worked on those fights instead of relying on my pet to interrupt for me once every 40 secs) I can do so without having to go MM and pick up silence shot. Not being able to interrupt can make or break a fight, especially if the group comp isn't built for multiple interrupts.

    # What makes playing your class more fun?
    I love the utility hunters bring to raid. As Survival I may not do as much DPS as Marks, but I have my improved traps, bring 10% haste to my 10man raid since no one else does, have a smooth shooting rotation that I can weave into heavy movement fights, and great area of effect damage for multiple adds. As a hunter, I know what I bring to the raid and what my raid can expect of me while doing big numbers.

    # What makes playing your class less fun?
    Having a minimum range and not having a reliable interrupt.

    I find that with practice my "rotation" is pretty smooth and I can adjust on the fly pretty quick.

Also change taming mechanics to make mobs immune to outside interference while being tamed. I see way too many hunters loose the chance to tame a beast (including myself many times) because someone else wants to grief them and kill a mob while it's in the process of being tamed. I understand some players will be jerks just because they can, but it would be nice to make it more difficult for grievers to get away with doing so. More stable slots would be nice too. I never thought I'd come close to using up all the slots we were given until I started taming pets to get those buffs the raid was missing filled.

    # What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    -Fix all of the pet bugs. Pets being summoned/rezzed with 6k health is a bug that should have been fixed over a year ago. Pets should have been able to jump off of ledges (such as in Blade's Edge Arena and on the second level of the WSG bases) years ago. Also, the new stances are worse than the old ones (I still just leave mine on defensive at all times).

    -Our ranged weapon needs to stop taking damage at such an increased rate compared to other item slots, especially when using multishot.

    -We absolutely need a better way to revive pets than having a 10 second rez.

    # What makes playing your class more fun?

    I play quite a few classes, and hunter is just the most fun due to its overall complexity and reliance on quick reaction times to succeed. When you beat someone competent as a hunter, you really feel like it was due to your skill and not just overpowered class mechanics. Kiting is mainly what has always been so fun about this class.

    # What makes playing your class less fun?

    -We're back to the TBC scenario where hunters just aren't wanted in PvP. At least back then we had a small niche by playing drain games with viper sting, but these days there is literally no reason to bring a hunter onto your arena team over any other class. We don't have a niche in RBGs either other than defending nodes, but it's not as if teams specifically say "we're not playing unless we get a hunter!" like they do with rogues and frost death knights. Even our slight "niche" is often overlooked in favor of classes that are simply better than hunters are. We are the worst PvP class again, which is disappointing because I never thought we'd have to relive the TBC situation.

    -The ease of completely shutting hunters down in arena, be it deadzoning them, line-of-sighting them, killing their pet so they can't use master's call and do anything at all, and simply training them because they're the squishiest class in the game. This class simply has too many weaknesses compared to others with no real great strengths other than kiting (but even that's impossible in the presence of two melee on you and an offensive dispeller).

    -Retarded mechanics, such as snake trap placing a dot on random targets, thus preventing us from being able to use our weak cc. Having to use scatter shot just to get a freezing trap off (and oftentimes watching the target walk disoriented out of the trap range as he's scattered) will make you want to punch your monitor. Also, smart opponents in arena will stack on their healer so that they wind up taking the trap when their healer gets scattered instead of the healer (the intended target), thus making us unable to cc them again for another 30 seconds. Again, there are too many ways to shut hunters down compared to other classes.

    # How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    It's fine.

    # What’s on your wish list for your class?

    -To simply be a PvP viable class again. It would be awfully nice to have people actively seek out hunters for teams again, like in WotLK.

    -FIX ALL OF THE BUGS AND RETARDED MECHANICS

    -I also always wanted to see the marks spec become a petless spec, but I doubt that'll ever happen.

    -Aspect of the Cheetah/Pack
    -Eagle Eye
