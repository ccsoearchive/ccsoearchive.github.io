What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both] Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other] Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs] BGs and Rated BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Hunter minimum range. With all of the changes WoW has gone through, the minimum range is an out of date mechanic previously meant to help keep Hunters in check in PvP. However, given the current state of the game, it gimps the class more than helps balance out the playing field. In PvP there is enough HP on players now that a Hunter standing and shooting say a Warrior at point blank range, is still going to die before he could do enough to kill the Warrior so we will still need to kite. The biggest concern for the minimum range comes from Raids. On fights such as Baleroc, the minimum range forces us to stand out farther than what is comfortable causing a significant hindrance to the our raid group.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The dynamic rotation of our shots, having the ability to tame any beast we want for a pet, being ranged and having our damage abilities change in both power and appearance whenever we upgrade/change our weapon like a melee class (new glow effect, new animations etc unlike a caster where the weapons stay on their back never changing their casting animations)

What makes playing your class less fun?

Minimum Range, weapon breaking far more quickly than any other class, having to constantly keep an eye on our pet else taking a substantial loss to our dps.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Very good, has the right amount of dynamic mechanics too it as well as a set rotation mixed in (speaking of the Marksman rotation here). Has just the right mix of both to not make it overly complex but still provides a high degree of enjoyment to the game.


What’s on your wish list for your class?

More fun to use abilities such as the old Aspect of the Beast or Eyes of the Beast. Abilities that may or may not add some value in combat situations but add to the quality of life while playing the game. BM Hunters gain the ability to tame Drakes (not full fledged Dragons because then that would be a bit... weird)

What spells do you use the least?

Widow Venom, the low amount of healing reduction and minimal impact it has in PvP makes it a wasted GCD. Raptor Strike, would rather use the GCD to Wing Clip or drop a trap to get out of range and use my main damaging abilities.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Hunters are incredibly unique in the way they operate; it's a nice change of pace if you've played this game for a long time. Taming pets of many skins and styles, as well as the archer motif, are pretty cool, too.

The subtle mechanics of the class feel overall out-dated or awkward. There's lots of little stupid things that I figure would be either looked at or out-right fixed by now.

The rotation for MM is perfectly fine and fun compared to some of the other DPS specs out there. I do wish that the other specs were more viable. MM has been top dog for the past 9 or so months and has generally been top for a lot of WoW's lifespan. The same rotation gets old.


What’s on your wish list for your class?

Tweak the pet stances. The mechanics are very cumbersome and just plain not enjoyable if the player knows what they specifically want out of their pet but can't consistently achieve it.

Change the sporadic pacing effect that Scatter Shot has and replace it with the gouge effect where they stand in the stun animation, disoriented. Sometimes the scatter pathing effect, not the players themselves, will walk players out of a scatter+freezing trap for them. I understand that scatter shot, trap launcher and traps are the way they are because the game needs class flavor, but my own scatter shot walking my opponents out of my own CC is a bit ridiculous.

Re-work Widow's Venom. Compared to other mortal strike effects, the hunter one feels like a waste of a global cooldown. Maybe give it some damage or incorporate the MS effect into serpent sting or some other spell widely used across the 3 hunter specs. Anything but how it is now.

Give us a reason to use Raptor Strike. My gear is pretty amazing and mine hits for around 2700. I miss how it was in vanilla and TBC where, if you passed by someone and raptor striked them, it was completely worth it and they actually felt the damage. Make it hit hard enough so that it feels smart to use, or possibly make the raptor strike glyph baseline for all hunters. To keep the ranged-feel of the class, possibly have raptor strike, on use, give you a buff where your next shot costs half the focus or something similar to that. As of right now, raptor strike is only used because hunters in melee situations have literally nothing else to do. (let's say soaking a Torment for Baleroc from a crystal that's too close to the boss)

Remove Deadly Poison from Snake Trap. The snakes having a DoT would be fine if it weren't for the fact that they DoT absolutely everything they can see from here to the horizon, instead of just DoTing the target that activated the trap, making it impossible to CC mobs or players in the area. I can't believe that one of my own traps (snake) completely negates one of my other traps (freezing).

This hurts hunters greatly since we can't get our resource if people are standing on us or if they line of sight us. To get a full focus bar it takes 2 minutes by passive regen. This makes the class feel unplayable.

Our set bonus for pvp is aweful right now providing 1 free arcane shot every 2-3 minutes (not including the attack/casting speed reductions). Another way would be to make steady/cobra castable while moving the whole time and make hawk for damage and fox for defense/healing.

I remember hearing about when a hunter switches to his t2 weapon its a 2% upgrade. That seems too low for a weapon upgrade. It also appears BM and SV are slipping in damage and MM will eventually too.

The hunter community feels ignored by no major pvp help or concern since the launch of Cataclysm and there is a lot that needs to be done in order for us to not be the least represented class in rated pvp.
