What’s on your wish list for your class?


Bind an additional effect to the Beast Mastery (31-point) or Kindred Spirits (26-point, 2 rank) talent which causes the Hunter to gain a non-trivial additional passive focus regeneration iff (if and only if) a pet is within 5 yards of the Hunter.

The effect could perhaps be capped by pet the focus regeneration rate with the additional stipulation that for each measure of focus gained by the Hunter, a modified [multiplied] measure of focus is lost by/from the pet. The effectiveness could be tuned by adjusting this multiplier (which may be more or less than 1.0). This additional focus regeneration would allow BM hunters to trade some focus-regeneration shots for pure-damage shots and therefore generate significantly higher personal output (despite the loss of Focus Fire availability) but at the expense of pet damage output.

In practice, this would mean that a BM Hunter with his/her pet in a passive stance could maintain full combat effectiveness regardless of pet family used (i.o.w. a bringing tenacity pet would not necessarily damage a Hunter's raid output potential, and still allow the hunter to have the tenacity pet's special abilities and group buffs available for situational requirements). This effect would mitigate a large liability for BM hunters in non-pet-friendly PvE encounters.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Primarily PvE with some PvP occasionally.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

BGs and Arenas. I'd like to try Rated BGs once I get enough resilience to get accepted to a group.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

First, minimum range is a huge problem. While you guys have made efforts to cut down on the problems caused by it in PvE, there are still many issues. You tend to fix some bosses, like Cho'Gall, but not others, like the dragon in Vortex Pinnacle, and adds often have hitbox issues as well, such as the dogs on Shannox. In PvP, it goes from being a sometimes problem to an always problem. While I see many complaints about focus in PvP, I think the true issue lies in minimum range. It doesn't matter if you're at 0 focus or 100 focus if someone is in minimum range, because you can't do anything. Yes, we have many ways to get away from people, but many classes have just as many closers these days. Additionally, hunters are easier to line of sight than other classes because of our having to be farther back in the first place. Both hugging us and LOSing us end up with the same problem. We can't get our focus back because we don't have time to get a cast time shot off before we can't attack and even if we have focus, we can't do anything anyway. From a logical standpoint, it makes no sense anyway. Since when is a person incapable of shooting a gun/bow/crossbow at something that's right next to them? A crossbow bolt is likely to not just hit them, but go right through them at that point!

Secondly, as a BM hunter, many of the boss mechanics you choose to use inhibit or completely remove pets working, which hurts immensely. At least some of these could be solved by letting flying pets actually fly within the limit that ranged players can reach with their abilities. This would solve issues on fights like Atramedes and Alysrazor without trivializing encounters like Sindragosa where no damage is supposed to be occurring on the boss during the air phase.

Thirdly, pets are just horribly, horribly buggy. They were at a pretty good place at the start of the expansion, but a lot of the changes you've introduced since then have caused major issues. Passive stance doesn't work properly at all anymore. If I accidentally hit Kill Command because that's what I'm used to doing, but I don't want my pet on something...my pet runs in to start attacking. The pet bar issue has been mentioned many, many times. I'm not going to complain about the health "bug," as I understand the reason for it and haven't actually encountered any of these times where it supposedly won't update for minutes at a time despite having three 85 hunters, but the problems with the "fix" such as pets lagging behind when mounted and in combat are an issue. Pet pathing is horrible and if you have to jump down from something, you'd better dismiss your pet, because they usually won't work right if you don't, such as multiple times on Nefarian. While I don't have the technical know-how or the patience to go through combat logs and confirm the bugs people are reporting with delays in pets using their abilities such as their basic attacks and Rabid, I have no reason to disbelieve knowledgeable people like Zeherah who say it's occurring.

Kill Command could really use some tweaks. The range should be increased or it needs to get a treatment like Intimidate where the move is saved as a buff on the pet that discharges once it is in range of the target again. As it currently stands, even with Boar's Speed talented, the jerky way pets follow their target when it's being kited often prevents it from going off. This hurts BM a lot, as it leaves the spec with the worst disadvantages of melee (especially since pet AI is so stupid) when it comes to the spec's main attack. And of course, it then makes BM undesirable on any fight where their main attack can't hit while MM or SV's still could.
