What is you current spec?
    Marksman with a Survival offspec


What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    PvE


If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    10-man Heroics, Raids, Heroic Raids


If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    I occasionally do Tol Barad to help the poor, failing Alliance on my server.


What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    I’m ridiculously happy to have the additional bag space that comes with not carrying ammo anymore. However, I feel that having a minimum range on most of my abilities and garbage for melee DPS puts me at a disadvantage for fights where we’re forced into close range. Occu’thar comes to mind with this, along with the Molten Elementals in the Ragnaros fight.


What makes playing your class more fun?
    The abilities of pets to balance buffs in a 10-man casual raid. We don’t always have every buff, and I’m happy to be able to bring something more to the table.

    Focus/Aspect of the Fox has definitely made playing a Hunter better. Aside from no longer needing to carry water, I love that it is faster & easier to regen. I’m also happy that there is a solid difference and necessity to change between Fox and Hawk. Back when Dragonhawk was in the game, there was really no need to change between the two Aspects.


What makes playing your class less fun?
    Lack of diversity in talents and specs. The differences between MM and SV hunter are great and provide an excellent sense of balance. That said, I cannot imagine why anyone would ever want to play a BM hunter unless they want to treat the game like Pokemon and “catch ‘em all.” Even with the two classes differing in skills and shots, every hunter is essentially built the same way, with talents that never get used by anyone for any reason sitting there by the wayside. When trees were larger, hunters could spec for additional range when they had a boss they needed to get away from. They could spec to extra speed or less of a damage dropoff while moving. It gave you an actual choice.

    Target selection is always a problem, with the start of Cata seeing hunters constantly pulling additional mobs they did not need. To fix this, a new form of sticky targeting was put in place that actually hinders my DPS in multi-mob situations, as I find myself tabbing to target the next mob, but having them slightly out of front view, so I don’t grab them as a target. I then start a rotation and fire a few shots like an idiot before realizing I’m not firing and haven’t targeted anything.


How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I love the current hunter rotation. It gives you a necessary variety and allows you to hit your GCD’s if you’re good. It’s not like Paladins with their single spell… “Win.” I will say I’m not a fan of Steady Shot being slightly over the GCD, as it can certainly throw off a rotation


What’s on your wish list for your class?
    More and different pet buffs. I really feel these contribute well to a raid dynamic. Perhaps add a few pet buffs that no other class offers?

    Offer new and different traps, or the ability to better-augment traps with glyphs.

    The ability to Dual Wield Ranged Weapons and/or use ranged weapons as melee weapons. You can then do things like hitting an enemy with the butt of a rifle or pistol whipping them. I’m not gonna lie, if the animation looked cool, I would do that even if the damage was lower than ranged combat.


What spells do you use the least?
    All melee abilities (Raptor Strike, Wing Clip) – I am never close enough to actually hit them with anything, so they’re not even worth space on my bars.

    Aspect of the pack/cheetah – They’re just too risky with their stuns.

    Beast Lore – I just use the internet for that.

    Kill Command – It’s not worth the focus.

    Eagle Eye – Since Camouflage, I’ll just walk up and look myself.

    Scare Beast – Even when I’m soloing, I just keep aggro on my pet, so having beasts running off into other packs isn’t necessary to me.

    Widow Venom – I assume this is mostly for PVP.

    Flare – Again, I’m sure it’s mostly for PVP.

    Concussive Shot – Not necessary when DPS is so high. It really just lowers the DPS.

    Scatter Shot – It reduces DPS because it stops your attacks when used.

    ■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    Some arena, mostly BGs, no rated BGs

    ■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    Biggest issue would be minimum ranged distance. There are just too many times when this min distance is crippling. Obviously PvP would be the poster child issue here, but on several encounters like crystal soaking on Baleroc, Magmaw, Marrowgar comes to mind, oh yeah - don't let me get started on Aeonax... <sigh>, the inability to fire up close makes these either needlessly more difficult, or just plain un-fun for hunters. As a suggestion: how about Aspect of the Fox not only lets you move around while firing steady/cobra shot, but gets rid of min distance (for all shots) as well...

    ■What makes playing your class more fun?
    I love the pet aspect of this class - it makes leveling and exploring so much more fun and I've really gotten used to having that companion there with me (just wish he wouldn't keep getting lost when I get off my vehicle/mount all the time...) and miss his presence when I'm on another character.

    I just love being my own tank/healer/dps combo and the increadible versatility it gives me to go where I want and experience aspects of the game that several other classes would find nearly impossible alone. With Blood DKs, Feral Druids, and maybe Protection Paladins now able to solo extreme content too (forgive me if I left out too many other classes/specs from this list), I think the PvE part of the game is going in a good direction.

    ■What makes playing your class less fun?
    Min shot distance and buggy pet/targeting issues.

    ■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I'm pretty darned happy with SV. But I think MM gets a little bit too complicated to use efficiently and effectively when in panic mode (for me, most new raid content) because of the excessively tight timing constraints with Improved Steady Shot. I find that I can keep up a fairly high dps output with SV no matter the situation, but with MM it's more like a roller coaster ride until I've got the fight on no-brainer mode and can concentrate on output only. Hmmm - I'm still working on BM rotation - feels like lots of buttons to push and all the right pets at just the right times to max this one out. Anyway, for me SV is really in a nice place and right where it should be (for Wrath, I would have said MM was in that place).

    ■What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Eliminate the min shot distance somehow.
    Ease up MM's Improved Steady Shot timing window (add a couple seconds to it's duration).

    Reduce the button pushing of BM by about 2 buttons. Oh - and do something about the timing for Killing Streak. It's duration starts when Kill Command goes on cool down, but ends only a second or two after it comes off cd. It just feels wrong having to wait and wait and wait for KC to come up again, then before you have a chance to realize it's ready, the buff is gone. Best way to take care of this that I can think of is to light up the KC button only once it is off cd, then allow, say, 6 seconds from that time for it to be used. That's how all the other procs that I can think of work - make this consistent with those.

    Give me a Survival spec talent that lets me self-heal (Really - SV is the hunter spec that has almost nothing to do with survival... go figure - Well, perhaps I'm overstating this a bit, but there does seem to be just a bit too much irony in this one.) Maybe a HOT from Black Arrow ticks, or direct heal from LnL, or something...

    ■What spells do you use the least?
    Um - if I could remember what it was, I'd tell you... <grin> Let's see, I think I used Eagle Sight (was that what it was called?) once last spring.

    Scare Beast rarely ever sees any use - although if there was a minor glyph to replace the rather useless Glyph of Leser Proportion that locked the scared beasts in place ala the 'lock fear glyph, now THEN I'd use it a lot more.

    Until I met Baleroc, I would have said Counter Strike, but glyphed it gets use on every crystal soak that I'm stuck in melee range. If I was better at PvP, it would get used there a lot more too, me thinks...
