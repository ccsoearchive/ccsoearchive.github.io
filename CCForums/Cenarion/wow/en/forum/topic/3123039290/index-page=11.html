■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

Raids - Hard-mode encounters

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

1) Minimum range. There are countless times where I am standing where I am supposed to be, but because someone else messes up and the target is dragged on top of me I can no longer do damage. Other times we want to all stack up for AoE damage or healing, and the hunters have to stand farther back because otherwise we won't be able to hit the boss. (H Beth'tilac/Occu'thar for example)

2) Aspect dancing. It's not fun. I'm sorry, but it isn't. I know some people manually dance, and some people bind aspect switches in to all of their abilities. It's just overly complicated and unneccesary. I see no real reason for Aspect of the Fox to exist. Put in a major glyph (lord knows we need some decent ones) that lets you cast Cobra and Steady while moving.

■What makes playing your class more fun?

I like the instant cast abilities that we have like Chimera, Arcane, and Aimed procs. They make it so that you can kite, and burst on the run.

■What makes playing your class less fun?

Pet management. Pets die all the time for stupid reasons, and then you lose the dps from a dead pet, and your own dps for having to cast a 10 second res. One of the perks of the hunter class is that you can swap targets pretty much instantly without even moving, but then your pet has to run 80 yards tohelp you out, and if you are swapping a lot your pet can have a hard time keeping up. A lot of the times on a boss your pet goes in at the start and you forget about them. I would much rather have a spec that forgoes having a pet (much like the difference between Frost and Unholy Death Knights).

■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The rotation is pretty fun, but it's extremely unforgiving, and requires pretty much constant spamming of abilities. I think this is a flaw of having shots that build focus. Rogues don't have a strike that builds energy so they can sometimes have some down time in a rotation where it doesn't take 100% attention to pull off. Having focus builders pretty much means you will always be casting something always forever. Also, the third Steady Shot doesn't refresh ISS. I think this should be changed as it would allow for a lot more freedom in rotations.

■What’s on your wish list for your class?

1) Remove Aspect of the Fox. Bake the functionality either in to the two shots, or in to a Major Glyph since we have no real useful ones.
2) Trap Launcher. Having the ability to shoot traps is awesome. Having to manage two trap cooldowns is not so awesome. On more than one occasion I've tried to trap a second early and hit my Launcher cd, nothing happens obviously, then I drop a trap at my feet and trigger a long trap cooldown.
3) A more forgiving rotation.
4) A way to either make having a pet more fun, or to offer a spec that plays without one. You basically can put your pet on assist and forget about it. I enjoy having a pet when I'm soloing and questing, but in raids and dungeons I'd prefer not to have some of my dps baked in to an animal that does it's own thing a lot of the time.
5) Just remove minimum range.

■What spells do you use the least?

All tracking spells
Beast Lore

Aspect of the Wild

# What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both

# If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

# I enjoy doing raids with friends, but I mostly solo content such as current heroics and old raid content.

# If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

I enjoy all kinds of PVP. This includes Arenas, Rated BGs, BGs, and my favorite being World PVP.

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

I feel that a lot of hunter mechanics tend to be a bit clunky or are very frustrating to use in PVP (and sometimes PVE as well). This includes the current trap system (Ice trap breaking on snake dots for example), the minimum range (not usually an issue, but it is frustrating at times), and how easy it is for others to shut down our focus regen (quickly LOSing a steady shot near the end of the cast isn't too hard for most players to do). These are just a few issues I see with hunters that feel awkward, or I feel that needs work.

# What makes playing your class more fun?

I enjoy being able to use many of my abilities on instant cast, and I love the feel of the hunter class as a whole. I love feeling like an archer who fights beside his faithful pet, or as a beast master who's spirit is linked to their pet and they fight as one.

# What makes playing your class less fun?

I hate that there aren't many very viable hunter comps for arena, and most people will opt for another class for RBGs as well. I understand controlling my pet is part of being a hunter, but when I see my pet die because I had to dismiss and summon him again, only to see he has 7K health for 1-10 sec and is instantly killed is very frustrating. It hurts me as a hunter if my pet dies, and if this happens there's nothing I can do to remedy the problem.

# How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For PVP I enjoy my rotation and I feel that it's fine as MM and Surv (although I feel that Black Arrow is rather lack luster in PVP, which makes the rotation different than what it should be because there's no reason to take Black Arrow). BM is a bit off with kill command being a clunky attack. On a Z-Axis map in arenas if the enemy jumps down my pet has to go all the way around to attack them, making kill command useless for at least 10 seconds. Also the killing streak proc is more of a hassle rather than a "Yay, my KC can hit harder this time around!". The talent is odd because it doesn't last very long and allows no room for error. You either have to use Kill Command the moment it's off CD, or lose out on the proc. In PVE this can be dificult, and in PVP the talent isn't taken in a lot of BM talent builds.

For PVE I feel that my dps rotation is fine, but again, I feel BM needs a bit of work on the KC mechanic. I don't have any real complaints about PVE rotation because I do mostly soloing, and so I expect my rotation to be awkward (constantly using MD on my pet, keeping mend pet up, etc).

# What’s on your wish list for your class?

I want many of our clunky mechanics to be smoothed out. Having to use Scatter shot to (almost) insure a trap works in pvp is frustrating because scatter shot is a great spell to peel for my partners, but if I use it then my chances of trapping my targets are reduced dramatically. The other thing I'd like to see is for many of our bugs to be fixed. The minimum range can be annoying, although I feel that if the other "wishes" could be met, I would gladly keep my minimum range without complaint.

# What spells do you use the least?

I have not found a single use for Aspect of the Wild, and I feel Widow's Venom is a waste of a GCD and 15 focus.

Thanks for wanting to hear everyone's concerns and feeling on their class btw :D

What type of content do you focus on? PvE primarily, and very rarely PvP

If PvE, what type of PvE? Current end-game content (at the moment, Firelands), and the occasional heroic.

If PvP, what type of PvP? BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? 2 current issues I see are both pet-related. The issues where upon summoning a pet, they do not have all their health for 5-10 seconds. The second issue being the bug where if you zone into an area on a mount (or get summoned in) and then dismount, you do not have pet control buttons. This happens on a modded UI and a standard one. To correct, pets have to be dismissed and re-summoned.

What makes playing your class more fun? DPSing on the move is a big part of why I enjoy playing a hunter, and being able to add so much versatility to a group. Everything from interrupts (albeit with a really long cooldown), filling in buffs, to crowd control, to "kite this thing."

What makes playing your class less fun?

Lack of viability in world PVP scenarios. The way all classes ultimately beat a hunter is to stand on top of them. Minimum range must be achieved for any significant damage, and requiring those shots to regain Focus creates a challenge, not only in PvP, but also in PvE where there are a number of fights that require the entire raid group to group up on the boss to receive AOE heals or mitigate damage, putting the hunters in the group to a disadvantage because there are no very short range shots, or usable melee abilities to maintain DPS.

Haste has created a very complicated rotation for Marksman hunters, and less so to Survival and Beastmastery hunters. It seems to overly complicate optimizing gear and causes problems in adjusting shot priorities as gear changes and upgrades. It's almost like having to re-learn how to play the spec somewhat every time you replace a piece of gear and change the amount of haste you have. For an average player who might not be playing in Firelands yet, or only has a couple hours to play -- I'm sure it's really daunting to understand how it works.

Aspect dancing is junky, just as it was when we used to jump between Monkey and Hawk back in the day. IIRC, that's the whole reason we ended up with Dragonhawk to begin with, so why did we bring back the need to swap around again? I get that it's to allow for focus regeneration and channeled casts during movement, but it sucks when many of the fights in current raiding content require heavy movement, and the notion of maintaining high DPS.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For Marksmanship - the way it's currently designed, there is 0 room for error, which is a little frightening by itself. If you let Improved Steady Shot drop, your rotation goes bust, and it takes you a bit to recover. Making this last a little longer, or have other ways to refresh it may take some of the pressure off. Just to understand exactly how the MM rotation works, I can personally say I've spent no less than probably 15 hours over a 3-4 week period to get it right -- and that's standing still. Boss fights are hardly ever that simple. Most people would not be willing to make that time commitment, and should likely be considered when looking to review this spec.

For Survival - This one is reasonably easy to play, but difficult to do well. It is far more forgiving than Marksmanship for movement heavy fights, and doesn't take nearly as long to recover from a flub in the rotation. Burst damage on this spec is lacking; however, and may benefit from either a refresh on Rapid Fire or a new DPS cooldown all together.

What’s on your wish list for your class? I would love to see Survival scale better with gear -- since that spec is designed to do magic damage, make the stats that scale for physical output convert to improve the SV magic damage output.

I'd love to see the minimum range go away, or give hunters something that can be used in close range to regenerate focus, and do some kind of real damage. Disengage, while the easy answer to regain range, doesn't solve the problem when boss fights require a "stack on the boss" mechanic.

Make each spec feel more different in play-style. I've heard this elsewhere, but giving Beastmasters the ability to call several pets to battle during DPS cooldown phases, giving Marksman the ability to dual weild (provided crossbows or guns), and survival a self-heal and something they can use as a better burst damage phase.

What spells do you use the least?
Counterattack, Widow's Venom, Immolation Trap, Eagle Eye, and Camouflage is only really valuable during PVP, or for flavor, but does not really have any viable PvE use. Raptor Strike is only used because of the glyph.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE, Heroics & 10-man raiding

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Minimum range (combined with Cobra/Steady as our focus regen) and lack of any self-heal. I'll talk about minimum range later, but the self-heal is something I wish for as a longtime raider. On fights with predictable raid damage, I can use Deterrence to save my healers' mana, but when it's not predictable there's very little that I can do. Combine this with the fact that I have to stand farther away, can't always get the AoE heals, and I'm a low healing priority, and you end up with a dead hunter sometimes. I just wish I could do something - even on a long cooldown - to help the raid in those situations.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I like fights that require some mobility. With Aspect of the Fox we are the one ranged class that can really put out damage while moving, and it's a nice challenge to layer that movement onto our shot priorities.
I also love the fact that I can tailor my pet choice to fill a hole in our raid buffs. It makes me feel useful, compared to the old days when I basically offered no raid buffs that mattered.

What makes playing your class less fun?

When the minimum range combined with the fight mechanics combine to completely make me useless. This happens a few different ways. Sometimes I'm forced into melee range through mechanics or positioning, and it's frustrating to sit there and swing my polearm. Crystals on Baleroc have this problem - I'm not free to run outside the minimum range, so I just have to sit there.

Another case of this: as Survival, I'm a natural choice for AoE add control. But in some cases the positioning is infuriating, making it hard to ensure that I can hit the adds while not pulling them away from the tank. On Rhyolith, I can either cluster on the tank with the rest of the raid - in which case I do no DPS - or I can back off from the cluster, and make the tank's job harder when adds pull or spawn on me. The same sort of thing is true on Occu'thar.

In other cases, Cobra Shot itself is the problem: I'm frequently on the Spiderlings on Beth'tilac. The problem is when I run out of Focus, but can't get another Cobra/Steady off to gain focus back because my target dies. This happens when either my own DoTs or a teammate finish off my target. I've been stuck casting 4 or 5 attempted Cobra Shots in a row and had them all fail as target after target dies. The adds are dying anyway, but it's super-frustrating to just keep getting failed casts and be bottomed out on Focus, and obviously my overall DPS is going to suffer if I'm spending so much time failing to regen my resource.

Another flavor of this problem is when adds swarm into melee range and you're starved for Focus - you can't Cobra/Steady your assigned target because it's beating on you, so you either have to hope you have Feign/Disengage off cooldown or you have to find a target further away to Cobra, just to get the focus to deal with whatever's in your face.

Overall it seems like tier 11 and 12 had a lot of fights where I have to stand in a goofy spot compared to the rest of the raid in order to be viable and not get min-ranged. At best, I have to talk to the raid leader and we all have to adjust to make sure I still get healed, etc. At worst, a hunter gets labeled a 'huntard' because they didn't stack up like the RL said, when it's really the RL that doesn't understand.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I like the Survival priority a lot. It hits my sweet spot for complexity during chaotic raiding situations.

The Marksman priority/rotation made me feel stupid upon Cata's release - I could not correctly manage keeping my buff up with juggling my other abilities. These days it's easier, either because I’m used to Focus, or because I now have enough Haste that pulling off 2 Steadies leaves me more time for other stuff.

The BM priority/rotation utterly fails to grab me for some reason. I think partly this is because hitting Kill Command feels super-anticlimactic compared to the big ol' special shot shooting at the boss I get in SV and MM. Kill Command's inherent limitation of my pet needing to be on the correct target is annoying, too.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

More gun drops in raids!
Easier-to-get shoulders! Putting the only 359 shoulders for Hunters on Nefarian and Cho'gall was frustrating. And of course Beth'tilac won't give up the Firelands ones for me :P

What spells do you use the least?
Aspect of the Wild, Snake Trap, Immolation Trap, the melee stuff (although glyphed Raptor Strike on Nefarian made me feel like a badass)
