-I need an add-on to manage my focus and spells together well. All I do is put the spells I use the most up in the center of my screen in an arc, so that I'm looking at my focus and the world, and not my action bars.
-The pet health bug (when a pet is summoned, it starts with strangely low health) and the AoE no-damage pet attack bug (it hits critters, but does not kill them, putting them on your pet's aggro table) are bugs that are serious enough for me to consider them a quality of life issue.

What makes playing your class more fun?
-Feeling like my pet is an active part of my character. I truly enjoyed pet loyalty, food buffs, and other interactions with my pets. Most of these have gone away in the name of streamlining, and I find I miss it more and more.

What makes playing your class less fun?
-Being made to feel like my pet is a burden, such as air phases or interesting pathing issues. Healers don't like healing my pets, damage meters don't like counting them, and melee unfairly complain about big pets next to mobs with them.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

What’s on your wish list for your class?
-More stable slots. Face it, we'll never be satisfied.
-RP/Vanity brought back to the pet-hunter relationship.
-More reasonable shoulders. Yeah, I know you guys at Blizz dig giant pauldrons. I get that. Put them on the people who aren't supposed to be the guys scouting and sniping. Y'know, the people who wear plate and couldn't see out of their visor anyway.
-I miss seeing a quiver on my back.

What spells do you use the least?
-Raptor Strike. I have found a use for everything else at least once in a while, but never this. Its sole purpose seems to be tricking new players into thinking hunters might fight in melee.

■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both] - PvE

■If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other] - Everything except raids for the moment, hopefully changing soon.

■If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs] - Blech. Random BGs, but only when asked or if I happen to need TB for some reason.

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters. - The only real "QoL" issues I can honestly think of are the minimum range issue and the pet's Kill Command range issue. It's quite frustrating to be in the middle of a fight and suddenly see my DPS drop to null because my pet caught up to them at 4 yards out. It's even more frustrating when I realize that that's the same mob that my pet's Kill Command didn't trigger on even though, based on my display, the pet was on top of the mob. Oh, and let's not forget the Kill Shot Glyph forgetting to proc or failing to proc because of healing. Nothing really game-breaking, but it is a bit annoying.

■What makes playing your class more fun? - The feel of having an always-on group. If I slip up or am slightly distracted for a moment my DPS only suffers as much as I do. I also like being far more utilitarian than some classes. If I take the time and have a healer, I can tank, I can heal my pet for lighter fights, I can kite, I can sit and button-mash. I love not being absolutely pidgeon-holed into one format!

■What makes playing your class less fun? - The minimum range issue, but not horribly so. I'm not specifically endorsing the removal of it, maybe just adding potential workarounds. Also, the "Assist" mode seems to cause the pets to take an extra second or two before they react, and that time is affected by latency. I understand the delay in switching targets when you do (although I wasn't entirely happy about switching to this format, L2Adapt, I know...), but for the initial attack it forces the player to specifically send the pet prior to your shots, else seriously risk gathering too much aggro for the pet to mitigate. Most other issues feel more, "Grr, curse the luck," than anything else.

■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) - Beast Mastery doesn't seem to have much of a rotation. Not complaining, but occasionally it feels as if I should be trying harder. I've taken to trying to throw up a trap (the various fire traps and snake trap) launched into the fight to compare damage vs. without.

■What’s on your wish list for your class? - An interrupt for BM. Intimidation seems to be what we're supposed to use, but being a stun it doesn't quite cut it for boss fights where I'd be most likely to need it. Also improve Snake Trap to scale better, either by level and via Mastery (BM-only, since Snakes are Beasts...). Outside of that, I've been rather happy!

■What spells do you use the least? - Widow Venom. It feels rather useless outside of PvP. Since I pretty much don't PvP, it tends to simply sit. I like the idea behind it, but it needs to be improved upon slightly. Making it a "15-40% Chance to Interrupt on Hit" (but not a silencing) would greatly improve its usability.

p.s. - Please, don't give in to the "Mana plz" people! I've come to greatly enjoy the Focus mechanics and I would really dislike going back. If there must be something done to appease the "I can mana-pot and go forever" mindset, I got some kind of potion in OL that gave back something to the effect of 300 mana, 30 rage, or 10 energy as a part of a quest payment. Maybe make something like that but for Focus, like a Ginseng Shot, with a 3-minute cooldown would be something to consider, but please, no mana.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both, although I probably lean 75% PvE and 25% PvP.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Regular and heroic dungeons at the moment; once I'm geared a little bit better, raids.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
So far, just random battlegrounds. Might be interested in rated BG's eventually. Planning to avoid the horrorshow that I have heard is the life of a hunter in arena PvP.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Please please oh PLEASE, fix the disappearing pet bar. Also, the "your pet zones in with 5% health bug" is fantastically annoying.

What makes playing your class more fun?
I love the general "feel" of the class; attacking from distance, dealing heavy damage, and (if done right) taking no damage myself, is really good fun. I also very much enjoy the various pets a hunter can use, and the flavor they add.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Minimum range. In a PvE situation, it's bad enough. In a PvP situation, it is really REALLY frustrating. Part of the PvP frustration could certainly be resolved by me increasing my skill (since starting random battlegrounds, I've re-learned skills such as deterrence, disengage, master's call, etc)... but it seems like many times, I have 3 ways to escape, and the other guy has 7 ways to get back in my face.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I really enjoy the hunter rotation; I play marksman almost exclusively, and having to manage my focus cost of my hard-hitting shots, with focus regen from steady shot... all the while, being ready for situational use of silencing shot, concussive shot, multi-shot, etc... quite fun.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Honestly, I don't have a problem with hunters having a minimum range to shoot; I just wish we had a more varied (and less clunky) set of tools to re-establish minimum range, especially in PvP. Also, a better self-heal would be a godsend. Chimera shot can be used once every 10 seconds (9 if glyphed), and restores 5% of health. In PvP, this amount of healing is laughably insignificant. Maybe hunters could have some kind of shot which not only does damage over time, but returns some/all of the damage done as a heal. Say for instance, if 50% of our serpent sting's DoT was used to heal us. Or, a similar melee ability... someone gets in our face, we use "Paw of the Beast" melee attack, which does modest DoT, but returns equal healing-over-time to the hunter.

What spells do you use the least?
Aspect of the Wild, Widow Venom.

THANKS for the topic; hopefully some of the concerns of the hunter community get addressed.
