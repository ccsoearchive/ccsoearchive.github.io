  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]:
    • PvE mostly, though I have been considering dipping my toe in the PvP waters.


  • If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]:
    • Heroics and raids, both.


  • If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]:
    • Most likely BGs and world-events likes Wintergrasp and Tol Barad.


  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? (For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.):
    • While the Glyph of Misdirect is awesome in as much as that it allows me to solo content while making certain the critters stay on my pet as is the intention, I wish there were a way to make that happen without having to consume that glyph slot. It gets costly to replace the glyph when the situation, i.e. progression raids, ect., demand I use something else to maximize performance. Though this is a very, very minor complaint, I admit. Hunters are pretty darned good in the quality of life department right now. I'm very pleased when I play my hunter. About the only other thing that I can think of is that Trap Launcher makes it sometimes very difficult to CC mobs that are moving, though I really don't know what could be done to fix that. If you really wanted to make every hunter squee with joy, you could make Camouflage a genuine stealth the whole time it's used, but I know that's reaching. ;c)


  • What makes playing your class more fun?:
    • I love the way the pets allow us to bring nearly any buff or debuff to a situation. It's truly awesome and makes me always feel as though I have value to contribute to a group, beyond the fact that I have Freezing Trap. I also love the versatility the pets give me and how much I can contribute with a little party awareness.

    Just last evening during a heroic run, I noticed the tank was going down and the healer was running very low on mana during a boss fight. Because I knew the boss, I quickly took a glance at my threat meter which told me that I was second on threat, then the rogue. I quickly Feigned Death and let the rogue evasion tank the boss just long enough for me to dismiss my Spirit Beast and summon my Worm. A quick misdirect and some blown cooldowns, mixed with Mend Pet worked every couple of GCD's and heals from the priest once he had recouped some mana and we were able to salvage the boss attempt. Because I'd experience with the boss, I was able to time the Worm's Last Stand to be of really good use and between the healer and I, we kept it in peak fighting condition. That was really epic and felt very cool. That's what makes hunter fun to play for me.


  • What makes playing your class less fun?:
    • Hmn. This is actually tougher, as I enjoy hunter overall. If I had to say anything, I'd say what makes it less fun is that I get tired of people asking me why I play Beast Mastery and telling me I'm doing it wrong just because I'm not Survival or Marksmanship. BM is very slightly behind the other two hunter specs in damage, but not so much that I notice it on most boss fights. I can top out around 22k DPS fully raid buffed with an iLVL of 366. I can average about 20k fully raid buffed. I feel that's pretty darned respectable, but I still get grief all the time about playing BM.

    Maybe a very mild buff to bring BM more in line with the other two hunter specs would be nice, though I don't really think we need one honestly. (I certainly wouldn't complain!) Maybe you could lower the cooldown of Fervor by fifteen to thirty seconds? That would reward an skilled BM hunter with a bit of extra DPS without damaging the fundamentals of the spec as a whole? Not sure, just kind of tossing ideas out there.

    But yeah, if anything makes hunter not fun, it's constantly being told my spec is terrible when it's not.

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?:
    • This is fairly pie-in-the-sky, I know -- but I'd love for Beast Mastery to become a dual tanking/DPS tree like the Feral talent tree is for druids, supported by a tanking mastery that takes effect only when you have a Tenacity pet out. The actual hunter talent build could feasibly remain exactly as it is, while the the pet's talent tree could be tweaked to allow more fundamental ability for raid boss tanking/ect. As so much damage is delivered through our pet, tenacity pets could be tweaked lower in raw damage (with additional threat generated by the hunter through Misdirect with the Glyph -- the hunter version of Vengance) so that their damage while using a Tenacity pet would be more in line with that of the other tanking specs, while DPS with a Ferocity or Cunning pet can remain the same. I know that's not very likely, but I think it would be awesome to be able to collect a more mastery-heavy set of gear and queue up for dungeon or raid as a main tank.


  • What spells do you use the least?:
    • I'm going to assume that this is what spell do I use the least that I wish I used more? Snake Trap. It's usually not worth the DPS loss of two+ CDs/focus to activate Trap Launcher and throw it. While I don't really want it to become a standard part of my rotation, per se (like I said before, I like my rotation fairly clean and crisp -- though adding a thirty to sixty second trap to the list certainly would be terrible, all things considered), I'd really like for it to have more situational use (maybe glyphs that make the snake bites silence?) or to do enough damage that using it beyond occasionally tossing it at the very beginning of a fight where the GDC loss is negligible and when you remember to. It's a really cool ability with a really distinct, 'cool' effect. There's undeniable flair when you see all those snakes slither out and attack something, for sure. I wish I had more incentive to find a way to fit it in to my play.
