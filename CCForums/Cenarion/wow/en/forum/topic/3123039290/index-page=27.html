    ■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    Both

    ■If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Raids

    ■If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    Arenas and RBGs

    ■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
    Related to Both - Numerous quality-of-life issues have already been addressed through recent changes and that is greatly appreciated. However, the pet stance changes have odd behavior at times. I usually rely on manual control due to this ... which is probably not the design intent. Trap launching for all traps is a nice improvement to utility, but the implementation is cumbersome. It requires two "actions" to activate, requires targeting time and in-flight time, followed by a small arming time. This makes crowd control traps by far the most difficult form of crowd control to utilize effectively during combat. Finally, there are some pet abilities that require simultaneous line-of-sight between hunter and pet, hunter and target, and pet and target. This seems a little too restrictive at times.

    Related to PvE - Several bosses that require a "stacking" mechanic are exceptionally punishing to hunters due to minimum range requirements. Some bosses, but not all, have been "tweaked" to avoid this issue.

    Related to PvP - Honestly, PvP feels much better than in the past. Slight modifications to trap functionality mentioned above would be a significant improvement.

    ■What makes playing your class more fun?
    Related to PvE - The utility of the class is amazing in PvE. It allows for a high skill cap for the class without being overly punishing to new players.

    Related to PvP - The "run and gun" PvP play feels alot better than in the past. It is an extremely fun playstyle and a major step in the right direction for the class.

    ■What makes playing your class less fun?
    Related to Both - Situations where minimum range comes into play can be very frustrating both in PvP and PvE. I don't necessarily think this needs significant changes, there are currently ways to manage this, but it is still frustrating and unavoidable at times.

    ■How do you feel about your “rotation”?
    Related to all Specs - Currently the three specs feel more in balance with rotation and output than they have ever been in the past.

    Survival - The rotation feels good. There is room for errors in timing which, while not ideal, do not overly punish the hunter.

    Marksman - The rotation is much tighter and much less forgiving of timing errors. Slightly extending the window for Steady Shot procs would alleviate this significantly.

    Beastmastery - The rotation seems overly simplistic and can suffer greatly in situations where the target is not attackable by the pet.

    ■What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Quality of Life - I would really like to see a more elegant and less cumbersome solution to Trap Launching. Making this a toggle or either "always active" would be the two most straight-forward solutions. Also, re-implementing the original version of Defensive Stance for pets would help some of the oddness in pet AI.

    Redesigns - I would like to see all traps re-evaluated significantly. Snake trap is utilized primary for entrapment purposes and not for the poisons, in fact, the poison component makes them dangerous where crowd control is desired. Both fire traps seem exceptionally weak. Freeze trap breaks too easily and can not be immediately reapplied like many other forms of crowd control that break immediately on damage. I realize the ability to trap virtually anything is a significant PvE benefit, but there is no "recovery" mechanism for mistakes and this is a very significant disadvantage in PvP (and potentially in PvE as well). Aspect of the Cheetah/Pack daze effect being removed is another big request for me. There may have been a need for this restriction in the past, but I no longer feel this is the case. The ability is un-usable or un-necessary in almost all situations now with the restriction in place.

    Fun Stuff - I would really like to see the visible quiver model come back when one of these "bag types" is equipped. An additional "just for fun" pet ability like Play added to each pet type would also be cool.

    New Stuff - A cooldown based "Point-Blank" ability that allows temporary firing of ranged weapons in melee range. This should be a short duration with a long cooldown attached to it. This would address most of the minimum range issues without removing the mechanic completely. As frustrating as minimum range can be at times, I am not on the "remove minimum range" bandwagon yet.

    ■What spells do you use the least?
    Never Used - Beast Lore, Feed Pet

    Almost Never Used - Immolation Trap, Raptor Strike, Eagle Eye, Aspect of the Cheetah/Pack, Aspect of the Wild

    Seldom Used - Widow Venom, Explosive Trap, Snake Trap, Distracting Shot

    What type of content do you focus on?
    PvE mostly. I have attempted a couple of times to start PvP'ing with friends, but it just isn't worth the headache of becoming proficient at hunter PvP.

    If PvE, what type of PvE?
    Heroic Raids

    If PvP, what type of PvP?
    The couple of times I started up PvP before quitting, the intention was to do Arena with friends (2s, 3s).

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
    Minimum range and aspect dancing. Aspect Dancing is largely taken care of by the vast majority of hunters by simply macro'ing shots; something that rather pointlessly differentiates between "good" hunters (i.e. hunters who know how to make a macro) and "bad" hunters (i.e. hunters that don't)

    There are several posts in my post history outlaying my stance on minimum range; I think it should be gone because not only does it cause frustration for essentially everyone remotely involved in a hunter's game experience (I'm certain it frustrates everyone from the hunter himself to the quest and encounter designers), but it's largest inhibition to the hunter class comes in an area where hunters have historically been both underpowered and underrepresented (Arenas). "Class flavor" that makes you bang your head on the wall every time you experience it and makes you weaker in an area that you're already the weakest class in is not good flavor.

    Hell, encounter designers have started specifically coding certain bosses to not have a minimum range (seemingly arbitrarily, too. Rhyolith still wears Marrowgars for leg warmers, apparently). If that doesn't tell you that the mechanic itself is outdated and needs to go, I don't know what will be convincing.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    The classic archer/ranger theme, the pets, the little extra tools we have to handle various things (e.g. Misdirect, traps, pet tanking, etc.), and (obviously) the fact that it's competitive in a raiding environment.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    The fact that about half of its mechanics remain (or at least feel this way while playing) undeveloped from Vanilla Beta, including all the clunk. We still have min range. Traps are still the clunkiest CC in the game. We are still the only class without any legitimate self heal. Pet AI, pathing, and control are still terrible (understand there's just not much that can be done here a lot of times). Steady/Cobra shot are still affected by cast time and attack speed reductions, despite them being integral to focus regen and despite the debuffs being way too prevalent and easily spammed. Snake trap still applies some worthless 5-damage-per-tick deadly poison that only serves to break CC.

    We're the only class that has to blow two 30s CDs and 4-5s worth of GCD, travel, reticule placement, and latency wait time only to hit someone with a CC that breaks on our own DoT damage in PvP.


    How do you feel about your “rotation”?
    I think the specs work pretty well. The only thing is I believe Kill Command could be redone much better to make BM less frustrating when your pet is out of range of your target, even by a very marginal amount.

    Overall, I'd say between the 3 specs of hunter, there's a rotation that everyone can enjoy playing....

    ....if only they had a choice of playing anything other than Marksman.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    -Revisit clunky mechanics and "class flavor" like minimum range that literally only serves to frustrate anyone playing the class.
    -Lower the skill floor for hunters in PvP, it is ridiculous what a hunter has to learn how to do just to play on the same level as a warrior, mage, etc.
    -Get PvE specs balanced remotely close to one another. I'm a heroic raider; it doesn't bother me too much to switch to whatever FotM spec does the most damage. That said, even I realize that you promised BM hunters you wouldn't fail them in Cataclysm, yet you still get laughed out of raids for trying to play BM seriously.


    What spells do you use the least?

    There are many in the hunter arsenal. Widow Venom, Immolation Trap, Beast Lore, etc.

    However, I think the most important ability that is only on my bars as an "lol, hey look at this, guys" ability: Camouflage. It's our level 85 ability and not only does it only get used to camp a node in BGs, with a 1 minute duration and 1 minute CD, it's pathetically terrible at even that.

    edit: Also. Counterattack. I mean COME ON.
