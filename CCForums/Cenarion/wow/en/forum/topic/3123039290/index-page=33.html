What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
I focus on PvE.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
I am a raider.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
One area that I think could use improvement, though, is the AoE situation. Last night I was attempting Ragnaros in the Firelands on 25-player difficulty. During phase 2, there are the adds that spawn from the Molten Seeds, and to deal with this, my raid group collapses on a set point and destroys these adds with our AoE abilities. However, as a Hunter, I felt useless because my primary, hard-hitting Multishot has a minimum range. I was dropping my Explosive Trap, but it hit like a handful of confetti compared to what my Multishot could have been doing. Making Multishot viable in all kinds of AoE situations would be a quality of life improvement, at least for me.

I miss Eyes of the Beast. It allowed me to have fun with my pet in unconventional ways, and having this taken out negatively impacted my quality of life.

What makes playing your class more fun?
The pets make playing my class fun! I like having to micromanage cooldowns and execute my shots with expert precision to maximize my damage dealing capabilities. Also, abilities like Feign Death also make it fun to play a Hunter. As I play on an RP server it is nice to be able to "die" when someone "kills" you.

What makes playing your class less fun?
The thing that really detracts the most from the fun level of my class are my survivability cooldowns, Deterrence and Disengage. Sometimes, it feels like I have to pray for a miracle so that I might have Deterrence actually deflect damage that I neglected to move out of, or for Disengage to actually move me. Both abilities feel fickle and finicky, and it makes playing my Hunter not fun when I can't rely on abilities designed to get me out of sticky situations to actually follow through with getting me out of aforementioned sticky situations.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I really enjoy the rotation for Marksmanship. It requires a lot of precision timing and revolves a lot around cooldowns like Rapid Fire, and managing buffs like Improved Steady Shot, and maintaining all of this keeps me engaged.

I like the Survival rotation, too. It feels monotonous at times, though, with only two shots to use outside of Explosive Shot and Black Arrow, which are usually on cooldown. I really like Lock and Load because during that proc, it's all about maximizing the uptime of Explosive Shot without clipping the DoT effect, and it the rotation becomes so exact during that time.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
I would like to see some more major and minor glyph variety for Marksmanship and Survival. Either I am not in that spec, or the ability is inconsequential/impractical that glyphing it doesn't provide a real benefit for me, regardless of the situation.

Also, I want to tame a druid. Please?

And why do I cast a shadow in Camouflage? I'm not supposed to be seen!

What spells do you use the least?
I don't use Widow Venom. Period.

Immolation or Snake Trap are laughable and I see no real use for them. They don't even have any situational benefits, at least to me.

Wing Clip is impractical. If I needed to slow something, I would rather use Concussive Shot or Ice Trap.

Eagle Eye has little to no use, except to see how far you can see from a given point. I'd much rather have Eyes of the Beast to take control of my pet and play Frogger in Naxxramas than Eagle Eye.

I don't use Master's Call. It's probably because I don't play Beast Mastery and having my pet rooted is nothing more than a minor inconvenience.

Scare Beast is also an ability I don't use. When I am called on to CC something, I use a Freezing Trap, even if my target is a beast.

I hope this helps in some way!

Content? Both

PvE? All. 10 mans mostly.

PvP?
All. Prefer RBGs. Don't care for arenas, but for ease to cap they still beat RBGs.

Quality-of-life?
For pvp I have different pets than pve. It's annoying to forget and ask people to wait while I find a stable. Need Call Stabled Pet back.

Glyph of Misdirect. It's a great glyph, but it's major. Have to constantly re-glyph. Our minors are mostly awful. With the changes to tank threat, can't see a downside to making this minor.

Camo that stays if you don't move. We have to hit it every minute. When moving or if the duration ends in combat that's one thing, but if stationary and out of combat it seems silly.

Fun?
Mobility and utility.

Movement, jump shooting, kiting, disengaging...fun.

While our utility abilities are situational, knowing how to use them is a lot of the fun of being a hunter.

Scare beast on a druid or as CC on trash, eagle eye to spy on an enemy, saving a healer with misdirect, saving a tank with distract + deterrence, CCing, slowing everything, saving healer mana with raptor strike...the more options the more fun.

Less fun?
Pet AI is screwed up. They summon without health and get squashed. They don't obey defensive stance, running back while dragging trash. They get confused with dots on multiple targets, changing direction as they tic. Action bars disappear. Pets go inactive, mostly when you've jumped off something. They no longer take long routes to a target, example is the WSG flag room, stand on the ledge and send the pet to attack a target in the room...pet ignores the command. I've given up and leave the pet on passive. I have to manage the pet more now than before the changes.

Focus works great on bosses. Not bad on 85 trash, though as gear gets better it gets worse. Rapid recup means 85 questing isn't bad. But when you go to lower levels or pvp it tends to fall apart. Low level soloing and some 85 trash dies too fast. You don't have time to get steady off. In pvp it's awful. No one else has a resource that's so easy to shut down. Steady is affected by los, range, haste debuffs, and defensive cooldowns. Even if you do no damage (pally bubble) you should still get focus. No haste debuff should affect steady. Either steady must have its min range removed, or a melee focus move added. Change steady to generate focus while firing...not if it hits.

I don't think the min range should be removed entirely. I don't mind us having to be at range to do our best. But we're far too easy to shut down. Removing Mongoose and dodge was bad. We need MORE melee ability, not less. Give us back Mongoose as a focus generator that does as much damage as steady, or just remove min range from steady. Give us back dodge. There should be a tradeoff for getting inside a hunter's melee range. Currently, aside from wingclip and raptor strike, there's no downside. Wing clip is great, and a glyphed raptor strike can be a lifesaver...but neither are enough to make up for all we lose.

I don't like being unable to dual wield due to lower stats and lack of enchants.

Other classes got glyphs for their CC to work despite dots. We're the one class with a CC that applies a dot and we don't have the option. We can't fix it ourselves anymore since you took scorpid sting. I was miffed when we lost multiple freeze traps. Our CC is so wonky that was one thing that made it bearable. To pull off our CCs we have to CC... Scatter/launcher/trap is ridiculous, especially since you can't macro launcher and a trap. We were better off with freezing arrow.

SV has no reliable option for a 30K shot on Shannox because Explosive counts as three. Arcane glyphed can work but not reliably. This isn't the first time you've had a mechanic with a damage threshold and it's annoying as SV every time.

Rotation
Fine for 85 pve...can fall apart everywhere else.

Wish list
A heal. Everyone else seems to have one. Chimera heal is not great. When it was 15% with widow's venom it was better.

It would be nice to get back the venom/chimera system.

Eyes of the Beast back. Why not?

Ability to viably dual wield again.

Better damage in melee and dodge wouldn't hurt.

What spells do you use the least?
Counterattack. If there's a worse talent I'm not aware of it. Tie it to a stat we don't get. Then to fix deterrence you made its parry a miss...so even if we used it for counterattack, we can't now.

I use everything else and miss the stuff removed in Cata. I miss the extra venoms, eyes of the beast, mongoose, aspect of the beast (for tracking prevention), aspect of the monkey (glyphed monkey was awesome...which is I suppose why you removed it so fast)...I don't like have options removed.

Only thing you removed I don't mind was volley. Multi-shot is much better. It's instant so it doesn't affect mobility and it has spec options for utility. That one gets a big thumbs up.

I suggest increasing the range of both, as they are quite small.


What makes playing your class more fun?
Big hits are fun. Seeing large yellow numbers fly across your screen while you have every buff possibly achievable in the game is fun. As gear progresses forward, Aimed Shot is becoming more viable over Arcane Shot as a focus dump. And I think it should stay that way, in fact, I think it should have always been this way for Marksman Hunters. Aimed shot is way more fun than this dinky pea-shooter Arcane Shot. In an action video game, you make abilities fun by having a build-up, and then a result.
Aimed Shot accomplishes this by the long cast and focus requirement: where you have to strategically plan when you use it, and every single shot is a small reward for getting it off successfully.

I strongly believe other specs (and classes!) should rely on 'build-up" mechanics. And DEFINITELY not these clunky RNG dependent procs. Contrary to whoever designed the Ret Paladin, RNG is not fun. Strategic planning and tactics is fun.
For instance, perhaps Kill Command could have its damage buffed for each consecutive Arcane Shot that you fire, and having KC as the follow-up finisher.
On the side of Survival, Lock and Load has such ridiculous RNG, I have seen my damage float 10% with no/very little outside variables. Replace Lock and Load with a fun 'build-up' mechanic. (Perhaps having to do with Cobra/Arcane Shot or even the "Bombs" I will suggest later on).

What makes playing your class less fun?
Trap launcher is not fun to use at all. If one opens a modern dictionary and looks up the word 'clunky' you'll see an icon of a trap launcher. Pet types are too homogenous. Ferocity is the top damage spec, followed far behind by cunning, and a raiding hunter would never dare bring one of those "tenacity" pets.
(Despite the recent buff to intervene being very appealing to me as an awesome survival and tank utility)
I would like to have unique attacks to each pet type brought back.
