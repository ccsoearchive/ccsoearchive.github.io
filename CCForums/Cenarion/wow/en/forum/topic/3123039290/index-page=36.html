What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Minimum range is aggravating beyond belief (Baleroc, for one current example.) Focus regen in multi-target situations is difficult when mobs die too quickly to get off a regen shot or two.

What makes playing your class more fun?
I love the pet buffs, trap launcher is a lot of fun and very useful (but it would be nice if it were smoother to execute.), being able to shot on the move is fun too. Hunter tracking is fantastic and I'm really enjoying the large stable!

What makes playing your class less fun?
The above mentioned minimum range and focus regen issues. I also hate how my pet disappears when I do certain quests, making it difficult to re-establish my fighting mode when I'm attacked by a mob (the Hyjal turtle-tossing daily quest comes to mind.) I'm also not thrilled with the new pet stances, it feels like I have less control over what my pet is doing at any given time. I really liked the stances/control bar right after the Cataclysm release. I miss that.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I play MM primarily and I really like the feel of the rotation, it would be nice if haste were a little more straightforward. I'm not a theorycrafting brainiac so things like haste plateaus make my head hurt.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
I love the idea of the new pet taming challenges in the Firelands zone but I'm a girl and I'd love to see something a little more fluffy and cute with special taming challenges. Grin.

What spells do you use the least?
I don't ever use Eagle Eye.

    • Managing traps, focus and pets. To clarify, I find traps and their interaction with trap launcher to be clunky. Trap launcher is said to be off the GCD in reality there is some lag time in between activating it and being able to select a trap. This is in addition to trap travel time, after aiming it, and finally arming time of the actual trap. It is needlessly complex even more so in pvp.

    Focus in general is a mess. If I am aoeing adds I need steady/cobra shot adds to keep my resources up but I find, sometimes, that the add I am targeting dies before my SS/CS finishes which leaves me with not enough focus to continue to AoE it chains like this especially in raids. To continue with focus if another caster needs to move they lose dps but not resources, hunters lose both unless they stance dance. I have more problems with focus. Suffice to say I didn't think mana was a problem and if blizz was dead set on changing it the fact that the mess hunters are left with is in the same game where energy exists is embarrassing.

    Pets have a variety of bugs they are plagued with, from spawning with low health to pet bars disappearing, to los issues with master's call. The new pet stances feel worse than the old ones, assist is especially annoying on multi target fights, tbh it seems like a case of fixing something that wasn't broken in the first place.

    In addition to these I'd have to say ISS is particularly annoying to keep up.

  • What makes playing your class more fun?


    • Soloing strength. I enjoy trying to old content, or current content (I was soloing FL trash while it was still possible and was doing the same in places like BT and ICC when the content was current.) I enjoyed being mobile though that is less true currently. I like the hunter archetype. TBH though hunter is probably the least fun it's ever been at the momment for me.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?


    • Focus. I was determined to give it a chance but I despise it with a passion. The idea of restoring resource with a 2s base cast spell, coupled with the hilariously low base regen annoy me to no end. The way in pvp any class can shut hunters down by getting in melee range and kill my resource generation. This is an annoyance in pve due to some bosses hitboxes. I am not exaggerating when I say it has lowered my enjoyment of every area in the game. I haven't pvp'd substantially in months and pve can be really frustrating at times.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


    • Clunky.

    Aside from careful aim it may not be obvious to new players when Aimed shot needs to be hardcasted. ISS is a pain to keep up, just look at the options, you gave rogues the ability to refresh SnD yet hunters need to worry about this sub ~10s buff. Master marksmen procs are inconsistent especially in PvP. Extremely punishing when movement is needed unless you stance dance. At low boss health percentages with KS, MMM procs, T12 4pc and the necessity to keep ISS up sometimes it is difficult to spend focus.

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?


    • Never happen wish: revert back to mana
    Hopefully will happen wish: Blizz takes a serious look a focus and puts more work on making it feel less clunky and flow a lot better.
    Needs to happen: Blizz takes a look a both BM and survival and puts serious work into making them competitive and scale properly.
    Other: better pet control, a point blank aoe, fixing KC, selfish component for TSA

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    Primarily PvE at the moment, though I have had my share of fun in BGs.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Heroics and Raiding

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    Mostly BGs.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    While I really, really enjoy the new Focus system (as I feel that it's far more believable than hunters having Mana), I feel that Hunters need changes to the Trap system. I think this can be considered a quality-of-life issues, because as of right now Hunters are the only Class in the game who's CC and, essentially, Mele combat systems rely on buggy, poorly executed Traps.

    For instance, when a hunter drops a trap (not using the Trap Launcher system), it's VERY obvious that they've just dropped a trap. We have to crouch down and drop it, and even then it takes a second for the trap to activate. When you're talking in terms of PvP, this is a very poor situation, and can mean the difference between life and death. Once someone sees a hunter has dropped a trap, it's easy to then avoid that spot.

    Not only this, but lets look at PvE for a second. In order for a hunter to CC, we have to press two buttons, and again rely on a drop-system to trap our mob. This can, at times, be inaccurate in cases of patrolling mobs (where you might miss altogether or hit the wrong mob).

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    Well, I love being a Hunter. This was my first class, and to this day remains my favorite. This also isn't my first hunter, I played a Horde hunter all through Wrath. Anyway. I really like the fact that I have a lot of control. With minimal cool downs and lots of situational abilities, it allows me to be able to be useful in most situations.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    There are a lot of bugs and glitches with Hunters. These are known issues that have yet to be fixed. Such as pet path finding, disappearing pet bars and the Trap Launcher. Sometimes when I play Hunter it feels like I'm having to play through a lot of disabilities to make my class work right. To me, Hunter has always been a class where, with skill, you can really be amazing. Without skill, you really notice it.

    When Blizz took away pet growls and aggressive stance for instance. In the right situations, these were tools to us. In PvP, I could put my pet on Aggressive, and when a rogue gets on my back (I had a Ravager for PvP), that nice stun was enough to save me more than once. In PvE, when a tank went down and no one else could off tank, more than a few times I could control my pet to pick up the aggro and save the group. Now, unless I have a tanking pet out, this just means that my healer gets his face munched on.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    See, this is an issue for me. Back in Wrath, I didn't have or need a 'Rotation.' That's the beauty of Hunters, most of our abilities don't have cool downs, so you could use what was needed and when. At least, that's how I played it and I had perfectly fine DPS.

    This is also the reason that I play Survival today, instead of Marksman. Playing MM, I feel like I have to have a rotation where I shouldn't. Survival, I feel, gives me a little more freedom and I can still pull very nice numbers.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    I would very much like to see an alternate way of trapping. Maybe a shot that can be fired at any range (much like Scatter Shot), that does not trigger your pets attack (so Hunters who have no idea how to control their pets don't derp out in every group). This would save us from the three click trap launcher (I use macros and keybindings and I still find this very slow and greatly annoying), and also with the queuing bug. For anyone who doesn't know what that bug is, it's simply where sometimes when you hit the trap launcher, it doesn't queue properly (even though your traps get highlighted) and you end up wasting a trap cool down because it drops at your feet instead.

    I would also like to see some of the known Hunter Bugs to be fixed.

    What spells do you use the least?
    Aspect of the Pack/Cheetah/Nature
    Immolation Trap (since I play Surv, this is useless with Black Arrow)

Shamans get to choose between ranged dps, melee dps, or healing. Druids get 4 options! All three hunter specs are ranged dps. Not to get off-topic from hunters, but this is a problem for all four of the pure-dps classes.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”?
    -I really enjoy focus management. It was a big improvement for hunters to make the switch from mana, and feels far more meaningful as a resource.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    -For one of the hunter specs to be modified into an alternate role. Such as:
    -Beast Mastery Tanking. True pet tanking will never be an elegant fit into the game, since a pet will never possess the ease of mobility that a directly controlled toon has, but what if the BM hunter could go melee and tank directly. Aspect of the Beast could return as the tanking aspect (alternatively: Aspect of the Bear, Aspect of the Turtle.) There could be a talent called ‘point blank’ that removes the hunter tank’s ranged weapon restrictions. There could be a cooldown that allows the hunter to split incoming damage with their pet, or a ‘guardian spirit’ pet sacrifice ability that saves the hunter from a lethal blow, or perhaps a passive ability that causes the pet to block, parry, or otherwise diminish incoming damage. Perhaps the hunter tank has a smaller health pool, and is concerned with ‘juggling’ incoming damage between themself and their pet by taunting back and forth. Defensive traps that apply tanking debuffs could be added. There are already pets that apply tanking debuffs, and hunters already have a taunt and a defensive CD. If possible, the BM tree could work similarly to the feral druid tree, allowing those that enjoy BM dps to keep doing their thing. This one gets me excited. The BM tank would turn the most popular class into a tanking class, potentially contributing to less waiting in the dungeon finder for all.
    -The fabled melee Survival hunter. Dual wielding, powerful traps, combo attacks with pets. Give the people who like pets and melee an option aside from the unholy DK (it’s not like ghouls are particularly charismatic.)
    -Petless Marksman. The Marks hunter could be the spec that trades its pet for a powerful alternate resource system, or perhaps a more stealthy camoflage.
    -Hunter Healing! Just throw out a talent tree and allow hunters to tame a unicorn or some other benevolent mythical beast that can mend wounds. BRK half jokingly suggested this already, but it makes sense in a way. The hunter could help too, firing off ‘Bandage Blast,’ an AOE heal that launches insta-tourniquets into the raid. There’s a lot of healing mail getting disenchanted. This would help item crossover between classes.

    -I think 5.0 should make all the classes hybrid classes. There is a lot of design space available in the pure dps classes. Rogue evasion tanking. Warlock necromancer heals that punish overhealing by slowly turning the healed target undead. Frost mages tanking with some seriously buffed Frost Armor, or healing in the arcane tree by using ‘localized time reversal’ to undo wounds
