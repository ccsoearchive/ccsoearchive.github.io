What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
If I had to choose one, I'd say my focus is PvP. I don't enjoy the time commitment PvE requires either waiting is DPS in the random dungeon queue or raiding. Although, as an aside, I am looking forward to testing the random raid finder.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Mostly heroics

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Since cataclysm, BGs. I need to give Arenas another shake.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
I REALLY like not having any ammo. I'm really happy to not have to feed my pet anymore.

What makes playing your class more fun?
I like an interesting shot rotation. Being self-reliant is also something I enjoy. Not that I need to overpower content, but I like having a pet to tank, even choices between which pet I need best to tank, and then the escapability when things go wrong.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Traps. And hunter's mark. The two things I hate most. Traps have gotten better with the shot launcher... and I LOVE that mobs are not pulled when CCing immediately anymore, but why are hunters still the only CC class that can't simply target their mob and shot? Let me shot the frost trap at the CC target... Or make the target template easier to use. It's too slow and cumbersome.

And Hunter's mark. It feels arbitrary, so I don't like using it in my rotation. Not sure I can articulate it better - I have no problem keeping serpent sting alive... but leading off with Hunter's Mark feels like work.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I haven't tried MM in Cata. I really enjoy the Survival rotation. It's a good blend of reaction to outside forces and regular rotation. I've only started playing with BM. BM seems a little too simplistic and I've been able to tie some of BM to macros to make it even easier. Think there needs to be more situational decisions.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
From a cosmetic perspective, I'd love dual pistols. Maybe dual pistols would be a MM tree option. I'd also like pet customization - Doesn't need to have a stat difference. I'd also like to see more weapon differentiation. In earlier expacs, you'd see hunters dual wielding swords, or whatever. Now, our weapon is just a 2H stat stick.

What spells do you use the least?
Hunter's Mark
Master's Call
Raptor Strike

Leveling, I used Steady Shot nonstop. Now, I almost never use it. As survival, I almost never use Arcane Shot, but I use it all the time as BM.

Hope this helps.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    BGs. Hunters generally do poorly in arenas, so I only run 2s for conquest points.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    At the moment, the LOS requirement for anything concerning the pet, including Master's Call, Revive, and Kill Command.

    Taking Arcane Shot off the Lock and Load procs. Now that Explosive Shot is available at level 10, there's no need to have arcane shot linked to LNL.

    Also, would be nice if the hunter did not have a directional requirement for scatter shot.

    Lowering the frequency of the cat's yawning by about 75%. It is by far one of the most annoying sounds in the game, and it occurs every 10 seconds.

    Displaying a ranged weapon on the hunter's back.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Blizzard.

    More range, more mobility, smarter pet AI for times I don't think manual control is warranted. I loved it when I was out at 46 yards where nothing could reach me, now that range has been 'normalized,' hunters are no longer as fun as they used to be in PVP.

    What makes playing your class less fun?


    Blizzard.

    Less effective range, areas where hunters are cramped and ineffective (like the towers in Alterac Valley, any spiral staircases that play hell with the hunter's LOS). Being shoved into a single spec for both PVE and PVP. Live beta testing the pain that is known as 'assist stance.'

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    SV: lazy but fun.
    MM: too mechanical.
    BM: It would be fun, but Killing Streak is probably one of the worst-designed talents in the game. It procs for seven seconds, yet the cooldown on Kill Command is six, giving the hunter a single solitary second to use their Kill Command critical, which unlike the procs from SV and MM, is not free.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    It's a long list, but here's what came off the top of my head.

    Restore hunter maximum range to 45-46 yards while keeping the 5 yard minimum range.

    Proper weapon scaling on Survival and Beast Mastery.

    There is a long list of complaints about camouflage, but I would start with the duration: camo should either be a toggle or its duration should be longer than its cooldown. Would also appreciate if camo didn't break on dismissing/summoning pets and didn't break until shots like steady and aimed finish their cast time.

    True stealth would also be nice.

    Trap Launcher is clunky, should be a toggle that makes launched traps cost 10 focus.

    Take Deadly Poison out of Snake Traps. Snakes also need more base life.

    Add a component to Black Arrow that either prevents it from being dispelled or dissuades healers from doing the same. Lengthen the duration of Black Arrow to match its cooldown.

    Disconnect the CDs on Black Arrow and fire traps. Disconnect the CDs on Ice and Freezing Trap. Incidentally, Explosive and Immolation traps now need a buffing.

    Add a component to Serpent Sting when used by a Survival Hunter that makes it more difficult to dispel or dissuades healers from doing the same.

    The return of Viper and Scorpid Sting, as well as their effects on Chimera Shot. In order to make Serpent Sting more useful, combine its effects with Widow Venom.

    With the introduction of Aspect of the Fox, hunters are back to stance dancing. Aspect of the Fox is useful for two mutually-exclusive shots only, so just get rid of it. At level 81, a hunter can now use steady/cobra shot while moving under Aspect of the Hawk.

    Cobra Shot triggers Improved Serpent Sting.

    Master Call should not be spellstolen.

    Lower the cooldown on Roar of Sacrifice to something like 60 seconds: 30 focus every 3 minutes is a joke.

    Silence on hunters should not have any effect on their abilities.

    Steady/Cobra Shots are immune to attack speed debuffs.

    Freezing Trap glyph that either purges or suspends all DOTs on the target. Alternatively, redesign Freezing Trap so that it will not break until a certain amount of damage has been inflicted upon the target.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both] --Both.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    --Better pet AI would be great; making sure both myself AND my pet aren't in a ZG Blood Drinker's whirlwind is annoying.

    --I've seen improvements to pets following when jumping off ledges, could still use work in some areas (lava caverns in Molten Front come to mind; if I jump onto a solitary rock to attack something, pet can get stuck there until I'm out of combat sometimes).

    --Biggest issue is pet control bar disappearing lately and pet appearing with less than max health for upwards of 10 seconds. Eventually the health will fix itself but that means I can't swoop in right away, misdirect the pet, and attack a target w/out that target 1-shotting my pet. Having pet out while land mounted helps somewhat but doesn't completely alleviate the problem. For the pet bar, I have to dismiss pet entirely to make the bar reappear.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    --Not having to be right up on the target like melee. Having a pet, feign death, and excellent aoe as survival. Being able to lock down multiple targets with CC.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    --Minimum range kills my fun the moment it rears its head (Occu'thar in BH, if I have to do crystal duty on Baleroc, etc.). I understand hunters would be OP in PVP w/out a minimum range, but it's time to do away with it for PVE. Look at Jin'do in ZG, there's no minimum range on him and the hunter has to stack in the bubble with everyone else. Why shouldn't I be able to shoot that close on Occu'thar, or Baleroc, or any other non-player character?

    --Hunter's disadvantages in arena. All someone has to do is pillarhump; not only does this prevent them from taking damage from the hunter, it seriously hampers the hunter's ability to regen their Focus. If I can't shoot the enemy, I can't get Focus from Cobra/Steady, and passive regen is far too slow to rely on in arena. Only BM gets a +Focus on-use ability, but again that's not something I'd rely on.

    --Not having self-healing (good and reliable at that) like virtually every other class. Paladin/druid/shaman/priest all always have access to their heals, regardless of their spec; yes a druid or priest might have to drop form, but they still have the choice. DK can spam Deathstrike. Rogue can hit Recuperate. I'll admit I'm fuzzy on Warrior's self-healing, but I know for Hunter if I want any healing period, I have to spec Marks or BM; Survival has zero self-healing. Marks gets Chimera, BM gets Spirit Beasts.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    --Rotation is fine; I like to stay occupied during a fight. If I want tedious repetition, I'll hop on my arcane mage.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    --Hunter (and for that matter rogue/mage/warlock) dps brought back up solidly to the top where it should be. Bring the player not the class is great and all, but it doesn't negate the argument that hybrids shouldn't be as powerful as pures. If they are, what's the point of the pures? "Because I enjoy the class" isn't good enough for me.

    And when I'm talking about top dps, I mean in regards to bosses; aoe trash is irrelevant imo since bosses are the ones who drop the good loot.

    What spells do you use the least?

    --Distracting shot. Useless in PVP, and I can't remember the last time I needed it to peel a mob off a healer to save them. It's a direct taunt to the hunter; if we're not going to be given an actual tanking spec, then we don't really need this ability.
    --Widow Venom, by far. I'm sure healers just lol at it in PVP.

Why in the world am I unable to fire at a target right in front of me? Any class gains a permanent 'Silencing Aura' as long as they are next to me which is not hard to do and effectively makes my dps zero. You guys have been so good over the years at ironing out bad mechanics, and this one really should be on the chopping block now.
    2) Not having an aggressive pet stance anymore is annoying and the introduction of the new pet stances that apparently also made my pets braindead.

    ■What makes playing your class more fun?
    The capability to solo so much content is great.

    ■What makes playing your class less fun?
    1) Being unable to fire my weapons in melee range and then having pathetic melee abilities.
    2) If a quest reward or NPC vendor offers ranged slot items, there will be relics, wands, shields, thrown, but never guns/bows/xbows and there will never be a 2hander which is our off-slot. I'm always feeling left out.
    3) Polearms, Polearms, Polearms. We used to use 2H axes, 2H swords, staves, fist weapons, daggers, 1h axes, 1h swords and now nothing but Polearms. I'm greatly saddened by this as I have 6 years of collected weapons that were all used at one time or another on hunters that are unable to be transmogrified according to the current rules/parameters :(
    4) Please let me turn off gun sounds.

    ■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    We don't have a rotation like some melee classes do that have the resources to hit some ability on every GCD, we have a mandatory filler (Steady/Cobra). I don't know of any class that has a mandatory filler like we do and it makes us unique which is good. However, it also feels really mundane and while the rotation is not broken or bad, it can be incredibly unreasonable to expect us to maintain it in skilled PvP situations. An optional focus filler would be great. Or perhaps every time we take melee damage in melee range we get focus like a warrior does rage.

    ■What’s on your wish list for your class?
    1) Being able to use all those incredible axes, swords, staves, daggers, and fist weapons for transmogrification that we have saved for years hoping something like this would come along. We are forced to use Polearms only now. It would honor the artists that spent hours and days of their lives designing such beautiful weaponry to display them again. This is me saying I WANT to display cool Blizzard art, please let me do it, we both win. Or just let me transmog a polearm into my Arcanite Ripper, Ashkandi, etc. please :)
    2) The ability to tame an existing beast model for a mount that was mentioned at blizzcon a few years back. Just one is all I need, not a stable of mounts. You would go actively 'hunt' for a new one if you wanted to change it.
    3) Beast Mastery 31pt talent being useful, as well as the class getting a diffferent unique spell. You will use Explosive shot and Aimed shot countless times in a boss fight, you will NEVER use Intimidation in a raid for the most part. It doesn't feel very 'core.'

    ■What spells do you use the least?
    Beast Lore, Feed Pet, Raptor Strike, Widow Venom
