This is from a BM spec'ed hunter, always have been, always will be. I have two BM specs, one for pvp/solo geared to pet survivability and one for DPS for raiding/dungeoning.
Started playing WoW after WotLK expansion. I would call myself a casual player, due to RL commitments, and consider myself reasonably skilled.

# What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PVE mostly. I did some PVP in WotLK in Wintergrasp, and then in TB when Cata launched, since I could be comparably geared to others with crafted or affordable stuff. I don't have time to grind PVP, so now that I'm way out geared, not fun and I don't do it.

# If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Used to do some 25m raiding in WotLK, when our guild did 10 man progression and 25 man alt/casual raids, supported (not carried!!!) by our raid leads. No raiding now with the shared lockouts and RL does not allow me to commit to a several-day-a-week raiding team.
* of note, I could stay reasonably well geared in WotLK, so I did some fill-in raiding when needed by the guild, hence random boss kills like Heroic Gunship. I can't even do that any more, since its so difficult/grindy to get gear now.
I did some heroics early in Cata, but once I realized I wasn't going to see raid content, it didn't seem worth the effort to dungeon grind either. Especially given the bad behavior in LFD groups.
Now I mostly do solo dungeons (BC/WotLK for rare drops and the challenge), some rep grinding for achieves/rewards and questing/dungeoning on lower level alts.
TB and MF dailies/rewards were reasonably fun and satisfying (both for gear and fun stuff)

# If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Not much now, but used to do Wintergrasp/TB. Some rated BGs with guild team as fill in, early in Cata.

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
FOCUS!!! hate it, hate it, hate it.
Minimum range
Losing time on Cobra Shot casts when the target dies

# What makes playing your class more fun?
I like the mobility of hunters, while attacking.
Rare pets (and not a bunch of spiders! ugh! though I liked the idea of taming challenges, like King Krush).
Getting good at managing a pet in challenging circumstances, like solo dungeons, tanking bosses/trash, etc.

# What makes playing your class less fun?
I *really* hate the change to focus! While I understand that managing mana was a non-issue, esp. in raids, it makes playing my hunter much less fun now. I recently leveled a Frost Mage for our guild's Classy achieve, and it reminded me how much fun I used to have playing a hunter, as it felt a lot like hunter used to.

No useful AoE shot. Multi shot feels entirely like a MD-many-mobs shot, and not really useful for taking stuff down without risking threat, losing range and then being useless. Worm's burrow AoE was a nice compromise for BM spec, until you made that drop threat too.

Unpredictability of Disengage depending on terrain. Make the jump more vertical as it goes back to fix?


# How do you feel about your “rotation”?
I understand the best rotation for my class, but again, the fact that it is so strongly determined by focus levels (as opposed to CDs), and I have to gear around haste so much is really un-fun. Not as big a deal in single boss fights, but really annoying with multiple targets.
I do enjoy the "burstieness" of BM spec. Fun and challenging to time that for just the right circumstances, both in PVE and PVP. The long hauls in between CDs, and building up focus again are tedious though.

# What’s on your wish list for your class?
Ability to "ride" my pet as a mount or tame mounts (not asking for unlimited mount tames, but maybe one or two "tamed mount" slots, usable on creatures that are already set up to be mounts, like rhino, drake, cat - my hordie wants a cat mount!!!).


# What spells do you use the least?
Raptor Strike

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
- Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
- Heroic 10 man raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
- BGs, Rated BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
- Attack speed slows (Frost fever, mage slow, etc) easily cut my damage in half in PvP. Not only do they reduce attack speed, but they cripple focus regen, which easily increases the ~3 seconds needed to gain focus for a chimera shot close to 6 seconds.
- Flare does not land if you leave LoS from the location that you launched it to while it is in the air.
- Pet AI and pathing could still use a lot of work
- Trap launcher triggering a 1.5 second cooldown when the buff fades seems almost like an oversight. It doesn't play nicely with a 1 second GCD at all.
- Pets spawning with 6k health and getting instantly killed by a random AoE in PvP.
- Freezing Trap doesn't remove DoTs from its victim, but Sheep/Blind do.

What makes playing your class more fun?
- The ability to do damage on the move
- Many useful niche abilities (scare beast, distracting shot, etc)

What makes playing your class less fun?
- Too many abilities with little use. See the last question.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
- The ability for MM to choose between using pure aimed shot and a combo of arcane and aimed based on boss health/haste as the focus dump is a very nice touch that adds depth to the spec.
- Survival's rotation is enjoyable because of the focus management needed to coordinate 2 DoTs and explosive shot, and I have always preferred it over MM in both WotLK and Cata, but the spec isn't very viable right now for pushing endgame heroic progression.
- BM has been dead for the whole expansion, but that isn't really a bad thing because the rotation isn't very fun or interesting to play. Kill command on cooldown, arcane shot as a focus dump, and Focus Fire every so often isn't a very fun or exciting playstyle.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
- A 5 second decrease on the cooldown of Camouflage, or a 5 second increase in the duration of the effect. Having a short lapse between Camouflages that is completely dependent on latency seems clunky, and to an extent, unfair.
- Increased diversity in aspects. Aspect of the Wild just seems completely out of place, but it does have its uses. Aspect of the Cheetah and Pack are also fine as they are. What I would like to see is a greater selection of combat aspects (currently only Hawk and Fox).
- Greater diversity in buff/debuffs offered by pets. Even though 10% haste is brought by survival, a pet should still be able to offer it because MM specs that used aimed shot as a focus dump are so dependent on haste that a raid without the buff can become significantly crippled.

What spells do you use the least?
- I honestly cannot remember the last time that I used an immolation trap.
- I have not used the new Feed Pet (50% heal) at all.
- Widow Venom has its uses (Anshal in TotFW, for example), but it needs something else. It is the only mortal strike effect in the game that requires a GCD and resources but does not offer any damage or other effect.
- Raptor Strike is still useless, with the exception of using the glyph in fights with predictable massive damage spikes.
- I used to use Wing Clip all the time back in BC, but a combo of Entrapment/Disengage/Concussive Shot is a much more interesting and fun style of play that can completely replace Wing Clip.
- Oh, and I almost forgot that counterattack even existed.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

--Deadzone issues. (mobs + players too close). In PvE this becomes an issue in such fights that require stacking for AoE (Ragnaros adds, etc.), as well as filling in for a Baleroc soaker who has died. In PvP, it becomes an issue when multiple melee are on you (cooldowns become exhausted). Obviously the pvp element is difficult to address.
--Also, INCREDIBLY annoying, is the issue of resurrecting pets. While avoiding the obvious issue that other classes have much better means of summoning killed pets (instant for other pet classes, on cooldown, of course), it is very annoying to resurrect a pet once it has been killed. The pet talent Heart of the Phoenix is available, sure, but takes away from much better talents that pets can use, AND it is on a MUCH longer cooldown than the cooldowns of the other pet classes. Ten second cast time is absurd.

What makes playing your class more fun?

--The mobility and the ability misdirect. Yes, misdirecting someone who has gone afk for trash is very satisfying.

What makes playing your class less fun?

--Dying from dots in PvP, with no viable defense (they tick through deterrence).

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

--Both survival and marksman specs are very fun in terms of "rotation", however Beast Mastery is not very fun at all. Perhaps including another spell. Focus Fire is a very interesting ability, such as it provides an increase to DPS, but not using it does not hinder DPS too much. It seems to be an activated, more skill-oriented Improved Steady Shot, albeit not much more "skill" is needed.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

--As previous posters have cited, an updated usefulness of traps (snake trap most importantly). This could be done, as also said previously, through added glyphs to remove deadly poison from the snakes, or possibly just increasing the percentage the snakes receive from the hunter's attack power. Or both! Also, while Ice trap and Freezing trap are on the same cool down, perhaps an alteration could be made to allow both to be used within a short amount of time. Perhaps not one immediately after the other, but instead a short cool down placed on one right after the other has been used. This would allow both traps to be active if placed before combat is initiated, but still spread out their time of use during combat. If that makes sense.
--Also, a significantly reduced cast time on pet resurrection. Or a talent or spell that reduces cast time. Not to mention fixing the "bug" that summons pets at level one health pools and allows opponents in PvP, and some spells in PvE to immediately kill the pet.

What spells do you use the least?

--Widow venom. It is not worth the global cooldown or focus cost. It is complete garbage as it stands. Many other classes have this debuff paired with another spell or talent, making it not as annoying as Widow Venom. An increased amount of healing reduced (even 20%), or better yet, a talent that incorporates the healing debuff, would be excellent. Using chimera shot, explosive shot, kill command, reduces healing done to that target by x% for the duration of the spells cooldown (10sec for chimera shot, 6sec for explosive and kill command).

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

I do both PvE and PvP, but PvP is my core.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters

The minimum range on Hunters is horrible.

In PvP, outside of the fact is it very possible to shoot someone with a gun at point blank range, it gives us a massive disadvantage against...well, everybody. Melee can charge a Hunter to kill...and so can casters. The fact that casters can beat Hunters by standing in melee range is a bit absurd.

In PvE, bosses with large hitboxes force us out of the raid's groups, causing the entire raid to have to re-position because of an antiquated feature one class has.

I know the development team says this is a "feature" and not a flaw...but I've got to ask for whom? If no one that plays the class thinks it's a good idea, is it a feature for the development team as opposed to the actual playerbase?

I hate to write so much to one question, but this is really the single largest glaring flaw with the entire class, and it can honestly be pretty gamebreaking.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Pets are an interesting mechanic, but honestly only a few of them are truly useful. Don't let any Hunter lie though...we ALL love cool looking pets. Some just can't admit to it.

Hunter's are at their best when you hit-and-run with strong instants, or turret down and crank out some damage. The gameplay at the end of WotLK is a good example of this. Several Hunter moves were FAR too powerful at that point, but that's just tweaking numbers. That style of gameplay was very fun.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I hate to sound pessimistic, but pretty much every single change introduced this expansion.

In PvE, focus regeneration has led to Hunter's doing ridiculous damage, because they are the only ranged class that essentially has unlimited resources in that enviroment. That in itself can seem like a quality of life change, but it honestly cheapens some of what you have to watch to play your class well. All in all though, I think focus in PvE has done well enough. Not a ton of complaints there, move of a lateral move.

PvP...oh, dear Lord PvP...where to even begin...

I know the development does not care about high endgame representation for classes. Hunter's are the least represented class in that area for a reason, though.

You have effectively made the class that was built around instant attacks the only class that is now FORCED to cast nearly 100% of the time. Not only that, Steady Shot/Cobra Shot is our only way to truly regenerate resources. Something as simple as hitting a Hunter with Icy Touch reduces their resource regeneration by 20%. No other classes is crippled by such an easily exploited and defeated mechanic.

Beyond that, if you start in attack range with a cast, and the opponent charges you, guess what? You can't attack anymore, because a class that is forced to cast coupled with a minimum range equals death in nearly any competitive environment. All you have to do is charge the Hunter, no matter what classes you are. HEALERS can beat a Hunter by charging them. I know I touched on the minimum range already, but I have to state again how heinously game-destroying the mechanic has become due to forced casting being shoehorned into the class.

Between LoS, forced casting, and cast speed debuffs, it feels like we are constantly out of resources in PvP. It's more than not fun...it's a chore to do.

Not having a reliable self heal. Chimera's healing ability isn't overly helpful in a PvP setting. In a PvE setting, it would be nice to be able to mitigate some of the unavoidable damage to make healer's lives easier.

Pet pathing issues. For certain encounters (Nefarion comes to mind), there are issues with pet pathing bugging out (if summoned too early while jumping down, the pet will just stand on the hunter rather than engage the boss). Pet's will occasionally get hung up on terrain.

Pet disappearing. Occassionally, when zoning into an instance or BG, the pet bar will no longer display. I realize that this is a bug, but it is a hassle to have to dismiss and resummon the pet.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I enjoy the changes made to pets at the start of Cata (pets providing different buffs/debuffs). It provides an element of decision making that wasn't present before.

I like all of our unique survival abilities-Feign Death, Deterrence, Misdirection.

I enjoy hunter's niche of being the only DPS class that uses range weapons as their primary source of damage.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The variable hit boxes on mobs makes it difficult at times to do PvE effectively. I find that I have to experiment a bit to figure out how close I can get to a particular boss.
