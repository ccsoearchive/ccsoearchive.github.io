What’s on your wish list for your class?
Please remove explosive trap from the game and GIVE HUNTERS BOMBS.
I don't care if it would belong to one spec, give us bombs and make them fun to use, and while you're at it, may as well give us a boomerang, chain hook, and ocarina.

On that note, what ever happened to 'Frost Arrow'. This mechanic was far superior to the ridiculously clunky launcher mechanic which I previously mentioned. Having to shoot your traps at a targeted enemy from your bow is much more fun.

Thrown weapons should be viable for one of the 3 specs. I believe Survival would be best.
I honestly don't think survival should necessarily have to use a bow. When I think of the Survival spec, I think of a hunter who relies on contraptions and cunning, rather than strength of their bow-arm to inflict damage. IE: Give SV a direct damage trap (bear trap, yeah?) Also, bring on the bombs and boomerangs.
You don't even have to be that creative to increase hunter's weapon choice. Having to use only polearms and staves is so dull. Increase the agility budget for 1-handers and introduce a solid 1-hand agility enchant. My guild shards at least 3 unique, heroic raid level, agility weapons a week, whilst my other hunter friend waits patiently for the BoE trash polearm with Crystallized Firestone in hand...

Melee viable spec: While I don't believe Survival is necessarily a bow-reliant spec.
The iconic BEAST MASTER in my mind is a warrior who fights face-to-face with melee weapons, alongside their faithful beast. And in response you devs will probably say (But guys! We have so many melee classes already!)
To that I say: Well that is your own damn fault! You developers think too linearly and give classes and mechanics 1-way trains.
Why doesn't a Beast Master prefer to fight beside their closest ally?
Why can't a Death Knight suffocate its enemies from afar akin to the Sith Lord Vader?
Why can't a Rogue inflict mayhem with poisoned shurikens a la ninjutsu?

What spells do you use the least?
I think this is a poor question. I use whatever spell is necessary to the situation, be that single target damage, area of effect, crowd control, or threat management.
It is up to the developers to help dictate what abilities I will find useful.

What type of content do you focus on?

PVE

What type of PvE?

Raid

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

Dead Zone - I feel the dead zone has among the largest impacts in my ability to conduct mechanics in fights. I've seen many tuned in such ways as to help alleviate this, but it still bears itself as a problem frequently. Baleroc is a good example, my guild needs me to be involved in the crystal rotation. Thus I spend upwards of 15 seconds each time my turn comes up unable to participate in dps'ing the fight short of white hits and occasional Raptor Strike. I'll detail a personal concept solution in my wishlist.

Aspects - I think the aspects being part of the global cooldown can make sudden decisions about stance changes difficult to accommodate.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I enjoy being a ranged class that is theoretically and mechanically more like a melee. Hunters have always felt to me like a very fluid class in their priorities. I thought the focus system would break that feeling but quite in contrast it improved it.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Functional limitation limited only to hunters known as the dead zone, and limited role responsibility in combat (only dps).

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

Rotations are fluid and intuitive.

What’s on your wishlist for your class?

Dead Zone - Turn the dead zone into a melee conversion. When an enemy is within that range instead of simply terminating all ranged abilities have them act like melee equivalents of their ranged effects.. This could come at the cost of some % reduced damage compared to conventional ranged abilities, but would at least give hunters some limited viability when forced into close encounters.

Beastmaster Revision - I have limited personal experience with BM mostly due to its lack of acceptance as a standard raiding spec through most of my time in WoW. In correlation with my desire to see hunter capable of more than just DPS I've thought a lot about what other roles hunter could manage. To that pursuit I believe BM could function expertly as druidic style Tank/DPS spec. The way envision this, the hunter could either lock themselves into a version of the old Eyes of the Beast ability, or perhaps even more entertainingly mount their own pet to accomplish this. The Hunter could in effect take direct control of the pet. Depending on the pets classification they would either be a Tank, DPS or some form of utilitarian hybrid.

"It's a Trap" - I don't want to see traps made into an integral part of the standard rotation. I like most of them now, although the immolation trap seems largely useless. I like them as split second decisions to help keep you alive, or momentary diversions. (oooo can anyone say in combat target dummie distraction? lol) The trap launcher is a pretty slow mechanism, and although I like it for certain situations I'm afraid there could be some push to bring traps into standard rotation, which would be incredibly annoying.

What spells do you use the least?

As far as pve/dps related:

Raptor Strike - Almost never
Immolation Trap - Never
