Kind of regret discovering this thread for a while, the chance to express oneself regarding this kind of thing doesn't come often, and I am glad that this thread has been made. Here are my answers to the OP's questions:

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

I love to do both, however, I and many players have to decide which they really want to focus on. Right now I have been a serious PvE raider for a while, and I use two specs often - Marks and Surv during raiding... This leaves me without a pvp spec unless I want to respec every week. Which is time consuming and costly.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Heroic raid content t12 and t11

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

My guild does a few Rated BGs every week depending on the time and who is online, but its nothing we take seriously and very few of us are very skilled at it.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Aspect of the Cheetah / Pack turning off when you mount... Why? The other aspects don't turn off when you mount anymore, why should these? Just disable the stun on hit affect while mounted with these turned on.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I love my hunter, I have loved him (now her) for years. I've cancelled my subscription many times but I always come back to play my hunter. I think by now I've gotten to a point where the game is no longer boring for me at times, and I could continue playing forever. There are a lot of things that make playing a hunter more fun than playing a different class. I'm not too sure on what things, but I do know that my hunter has been way more fun to play than any other character I have. I just love the whole idea - I'm a hunter, I use bows and guns and I have a loyal and fierce companion with me... I could say more but I think is enough.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Well to say these least our class has always been in the foreground of PvP. We are so gimped in that area of the game its not even funny. It takes a LOT of skill to play good as a hunter in PvP, because other classes have it so incredibly easy - like how a Paladin never dies, or how a Mage literally only presses three buttons on their keyboard and wins. This is less true today but its still a big problem... plus, our CC abilities are terrible, and camouflage is useless in PvP.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I like it, I raid frequently as Marks and Surv and they both are fun... although I prefer the MM rotation over SV by a longshot, the SV rotation is fun, too. I like to take pride in my ability to top the charts while having one of the hardest rotation.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Make BM a viable and fun spec for raiding! Take away our retarded 5 yard minimum range, this is so annoying in both PvE and PvP! And... I don't know what they are called but... there is one animal in the world that we aren't able to tame that we should be able to! I'm talking about those giant snakes you can find in western Nagrand and other places, those things are really neat!

What spells do you use the least?

Melee abilities, Beast Lore, Immolation Trap. I still use Eagle Eye in pvp (Particularly in AB) to see how many enemies are at other bases.

Thanks for reading and taking the time to soak up player feedback, it IS appreciated! Keep the Hunter fun!

Self heal that costs focus to use, but does not require us to shoot in order to activate.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both] Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other] I was 13/13 t11 and 2/7h t12 before I stopped playing on my hunter. I did heroics when they were relevant content. I still do old-world raids, dailies, etc on occasion.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs] I do bgs with friends or to grind honor at the start of the season. I do Arenas on several characters every week, ranging from 1600 to 2100 rating. I have done rated bgs a few times but not often.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters. In pvp, my biggest issue is focus being tied to steady shot. Between minimum range, cast speed slows, and how weak it is, it feels entirely frustrating and unfun to use steady shot in pvp and to rely on it to the extent that we do. In pve, my biggest qol issue is minimum range. I feel as though on certain encounters that raid positioning is "melee stand here, ranged stand here, and hunters over there ;(". Minimum range, at least in pve, feels as outdated to me as laying one totem at a time or 5 minute blessings.

What makes playing your class more fun? This will sound bad but the only enjoyment I get from my hunter now is seeing big numbers. I like topping meters in pve when I can; I love popping everything at the start of the fight in pve during CA phase and just sit at 60k dps for the first 30 seconds. I love going into random bgs and oneshotting people with aimed+chimera. I love when I get into a 1v1 with a melee and perfectly kite them while pumping out decent damage. I like that lots of numbers pop up [autoshot, wild quiver, pet melee, pet abilities, hunter abilites], but unlike other classes with "lots of numbers" we still put a lot of our damage in big shots with hard hitting chimeras.

What makes playing your class less fun? Feeling helpless in group pvp. I feel powerful in 1v1, and in random bgs, where either I am left alone or the opponents are not experienced enough to use their cooldowns appropriately to prevent me from kiting them. However, in 3v3 and 5v5 arenas as well as rated battlegrounds, I feel almost entirely useless. My damage is not higher than any of the other classes and much lower than some; if trained my damage and pressure disappears; my CC is easily negated in high-skill environments; and I provide no special utility that makes me valuable to the team.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) The marksmanship rotation is fun; I enjoy maintaining imp steady shot as well as having to switch between hasted/unhasted rotations and >80% boss health and <20% health rotations. Master marksman feels a little too RNG but I don't think it's a huge problem. The survival rotation is solid, though I do think more damage needs to be put into arcane shot to make focus capping a more serious concern for surv. The BM rotation is enjoyable, although I find that the range limitations of kill command can be frustrating and unenjoyable.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1) Remove minimum range
2) Make trap launch have a 30 sec cd but actually target an enemy like every other cc
3) Increase our passive focus regen and reduce our steady/cobra regen
4) Update our 4pc pvp bonus
5) Consolidate some of our abilities. List of abilities:
a. Chimera applies Serpent Sting
b. Arcane gives 10 sec of widow venom
c. Give tranq shot the same effects of curse of weakness
d. Combine Raptor Strike and Wing Clip
e. Combine Roar of Sacrifice and Master's Call, make undispellable
f. Combine fox and hawk, make Wild a new 'defensive' bonus aspect
g. Combine trap launch with traps, just give us 3 new abilities
h. Combine immolation and explosive trap
6) Remove hunter's mark, just give us the attack power baseline
7) Remove counterattack, give us a wingclip glyph for a 4sec root on 30 sec cd
8) Give us a healing while deterrenced glyph
9) Give heart of the phoenix baseline, 2 min cd
10) Imp revive pet should reduce cast to 3 sec
11) Glyph to remove dots on trap launch targets
12) Unlink ice trap and frost trap cooldown

What spells do you use the least? Immolation trap is entirely 100% useless. Eagle eye. Tranq shot is too expensive for what it does too often in pvp. Snake trap breaking CC makes it less useful than it should be. Frost trap sharing a cooldown with ice trap makes it often a mistake to use in pvp.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
I play both with a focus on PvE aspects of the game.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Major focus on PvE content.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Battlegrounds on occasion.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Trap Launching is still as clunky as it ever was. An example of this 'clunk' would be the interrupt of the Target Reticle that occurs on a new action unless a stopcast style macro is applied.

Along this line, traps are probably more of a hassle now than they have ever been in the game. I suggest a Trap 'Bar' that allows double clicking to simply drop a trap at the Hunters feet and single click to use the Targeted trap feature. The focus cost should be applied to the trap rather than the launch.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Being one of (if not the) most versatile class in terms of group function.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Beast Mastery continues to focus less on the Pet aspect of the class. Making Kill Command hit harder does not promote pet usage. Giving BM Hunters 4 extra talent points for their pets is almost a mute point given the homogenization of pet stats and damage. Rather than focusing on making Beast Mastery competitive as a damage spec I believe that Blizzard was more on track in giving it many of the class buffs and spells. Perhaps making it a true middle-range dps and off-tank (via pet) spec would benefit it the most.

While I feel that this might be seen as sort of heresy by some players, I really feel that trying to force Beast Mastery to 'perform' on damage meters has hurt what made it a unique tree of the Hunter class.


How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I feel that Survival has (never really) had a good place in the class. It really *really* seems like it should be the Hunter melee tree but that the devs are completly against the idea of a melee Hunter. As a result, many abilities that would be interesting if applied to a "is the Hunter in Melee range" situation are simply boring. Lock and Load doesn't mitigate the many lame aspects of the tree that I have mentioned.

I do realize there are Hunters that enjoy Survival, it has simply never interested me and appears to not be going in a direction that will do so in the future.

I enjoyed MM's playstyle in Wrath and further enjoyed the 'Damage Phases' and need to keep up ISS that Cata brought to the tree. Of the three tree's, probably the most interesting damage tree at the moment.

Per my previous answers, I believe that BM suffers from the misconception that all three Hunter trees "should be damage trees". It is clearly a hybrid class by this games standards and is suffering from that misconception. That aside, the 'rotation' is fine.


What’s on your wish list for your class?
Spirit Beasts - I love that there are a ton of them but I would really like to explore the old idea of having newly tamed Spirit Beasts add to a stockpile of "Skins" that the Hunter can choose from for their specific Spirit Beast. The additional Stable Slots are nice, but when the majority of the slots are occupied by essentially the same animal with a different model it begins to get repetitive.
This could be addressed by making the spirit beast a separate call pet with it's own unique stable to choose from or by some other means (I am just tossing an idea out for the programmers really) I do not know.

Pistols. *Assuming* we will never have Survival as a melee tree. Take the time to remove the Melee slot from Hunter character sheets and give us a scaling damage Pistol attack for melee purposes. Offset the lack of an ability to enchant the melee slot by further increasing Hunter ranged base damage. If the need to offset the lack of sparkly enchants on melee weapons is *really* there, then give scopes a visual effect.

Ammo. Don't bring back the admittedly clunky need to carry around thousands of bullets or arrows. DO bring a drop-down ammo selection option that gives us Fire (occasional additional damage and slows to attack speed) Ice (occasional slows to castings and run speed) and Poison (mortal strike debuff and dot that can be kept up via Chimera or Cobra).
Just for kicks, remove Tranq shot and have the Poison Ammo count as Tranqs.
Have Ammo cost a small amount of Focus to offset the debuffs and give an option for Normal ammo that doesn't consume Focus. This gives Hunters a fun and unique management option!

What spells do you use the least?
Beast Lore. The pets health, damage and diet are not really A)Interesting or B)Applicable given the current state of pets.

*** What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

- Aspect Swapping. It is easy enough to do with a macro and even manually, but isn't fun and does not feel meaningful.

- Hunter's Mark. On most classes, I have become used to jumping into an engagement with a target. Hunter's Mark is another global cooldown of wait time.

- If I want to be optimal, I cannot use the pets I actually favor or enjoy. I must use a pet that provides the best buff/debuff for my current group. I do not mind choosing between ferocity/cunning/tenacity in terms of sensibility, but I have essentially been a Hunter with a raptor or wolf for the majority of this expansion.

*** What makes playing your class more fun?

- The utility. From the variety of traps, to the kiting via slowing down my targets, to using my pet to distract or tank - the limitation on situational ingenuity is far lower on hunters than it appears to be on most if not all other classes.

- Being able to have a pet. While a massive irritation or even dead weight at times, I love having a companion and the utility it can some times offer.

*** What makes playing your class less fun?

- Steady/Cobra Shot. While I don't mind focus in theory, it is very frustrating to have to cast to regenerate my resource. This is especially problematic when switching targets or dealing with scattered low-health targets. Focus Shots also make the decision to use a cooldown lack fluidity, as a higher focus pool to begin a cooldown with is more effective.

Aside from the frustrating aspects of needing to use a (notably weak) ability to regenerate focus, my initial draw to hunter was that it played something more like a melee (a lot of instant abilities) at ranged. This was all but abolished with the introduction of focus shots - I feel much more like a caster now, and it is very discouraging. Hunter's adaptability and spontaneity excited me, but this is no longer the case.

- The dead zone feels like a very unnecessary weight to the class. It neither makes sense within the context of the game nor makes me feel as though it is a challenge to work with; it is simply an inconvenience that will pop up at random. It burdens my raid groups at times and forces me to engage in awkward movement "around" targets when all other players can simply run through them or stack up/stick together. It also makes questing at higher gear levels wholely annoying.

- The sheer amount of constant class bugs. My pet often becomes confused as where to go, is killed immediately upon release, freezes in place on some terrain, Master's Call does not appear to work in many instances that it should (especially confusing since I have successfully used Protection Warrior's Intervene in many of the same situations), the Kill Shot glyph will some times not respond properly and does not reset immediately, so on and so forth.

- The damage mitigating mechanisms of my class are of poor quality. The healing offered by Chimera Shot is helpful over time, but seemingly useless (and unreliable, were it useful) in any situation which demands immediate attention. Deterrence removes me from combat and only interacts with attacks which very intently target me, making it useful in a very low number of situations and making me useless for its duration.

*** How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

- Survival and Beast Mastery are both excessively simple, in my opinion. They offer very little to break or demand my concentration - their execution and maintenance of AoE is more complex to me than their single target focus. While I appreciate their very frank approach to a priority system, it would be wonderful to have a greater number of steps to look after.

- Marksmanship, while simple and even repetitive at first glance, is overly complex in a negative way. The spec's actions depend entirely too much on dynamic haste effects and the target's current level of HP. Because your target's HP decay depends heavily on your group's performance, and at least one dynamic haste effect does as well (bloodlust/heroism), it becomes immensely difficult to predict when and what actions you should use at a given interval. While you could "play it safe" and simply use your cooldowns at typical periods - Careful Aim, Termination phase - your group will often make these phases too short, and you may gain more from synchronizing your cooldowns with trinket procs or cooldowns. A group will also some times use bloodlust immediately after you have used rapid fire, thereby diminishing its returns greatly. I find my output often decreases as my group's increases because I cannot exploit HP phases. I would like my damage to depend much more on what I choose to do each moment of a fight as opposed to what I choose to do in variable, unpredictable moments of a fight.

I also find myself not enjoying the sudden-spike approach of Marksmanship. I use Chimera Shot, a hard-hitting ability, and then proceed to use (for the majority of the fight - Kill Shot phase excluded) very weak abilities until that ability is available again. It makes my character's output feel very volatile and unstable. While some players may enjoy this, I certainly do not, because as I stated in an above response to another question, I was drawn to hunter as a flexible and fluid class. I can only describe Marksmanship as clunky and stubborn.

In addition, the spec relies too much on "haste plateaus." While this initially appeared to be an interesting way to itemize to me, it quickly lost its value of interest - whenever I attain a new piece of equipment and need to reforge, I find myself building around a particular haste value, then leaning in for as much crit as possible all the while trying to push mastery to the side. With the availability of reforge calculators, this is a very easy process, but makes my gear gains feel very linear and predictable.

*** What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Misdirect to come off of the global cooldown.

- Trap Launcher allowing the use of traps as shots (i.e. when you have a target and activate a trap).

- No more dead zone.

- A much more fluid and interactive priority system for the class in general (not necessarily similar to the execution, but feeling of, a frost death knight; their priority system is complex, stable, and dynamic).

- Being able to use the pets I favor for group activity.

-Visible guns/bows/crossbows, and to a lesser extent, quivers/pouches.

-Dual wield becoming just as viable as 2-handed weapons. 2-handed weapons get a little old and don't fit every look. Light melee weapons also seem to just make more sense for a Hunter on the prowl.

*** What spells do you use the least?

- Snake Trap, Widow Venom, Immolation Trap, Raptor Strike - though I find myself using Raptor Strike more than the others; i.e. when I'm forced into a dead zone.
