What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

- Using 3 buttons to effectively CC a target (Scatter/Trap Launcher/Freezing Trap) needs adjustment
- Not being able to use ranged attacks in melee range - yes we have tricks to get range, but everyone else has just as many tricks to close the range.
- More durability on ranged weapons to compensate for the extra durability taken by Wild Quiver
- CD on Camo is too long for what effect it has
- If Scare Beast gets interrupted i cannot use some of my shots. Ex. Chimera Shot
- Cast time debuffs severely hamper focus regen in PvP
- Having to cast 2x Steady Shot to gain Improved Steady Shot effect in PvP situations(mostly because of cast time debuffs)

What makes playing your class more fun?

Kiting, Pet Choices(although limiting at the same time), Lots of utility

What makes playing your class less fun?

- Having someone LoS you, and your only choices are to chase them and very likely get caught in melee range or let them self heal/get healed to full
- (PvP) Slow resource regen even while casting Steady Shot

- KC needs adjustment for range - perhaps make it "on next attack" like

dont waste a KC
Marksmanship - Although I manage these rotations fine they do change significantly based
what haste buff you have at the time. Too complicated.
Survival - Not too hard and not too simple

What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Use of Camo in combat or allow us to FD and Camo- perhaps also adjust it also into some sort of in combat defensive CD
- A better self heal
- Aspect of the Fox to allow casting of Aimed Shot while moving
- Better focus regen in PvP that doesnt rely on casting shots (like energy regen for rogues)
- Better CC mechanics
- Update Snake Trap to do competitive damage
- More consistent poisons from Snake Trap - perhaps adding a Glyph to always get crippling poison or always get Mind Numbing in addition to Deadly Poison
- Changing Glyph of Freezing Trap to remove DoTs would be nice
- Giving us a healing debuff that is already attached to an ability we use all the time like a warriors Mortal Strike
- Bringing Disengage's CD more in line with other classes without the use of talents/glyphs
- Aspect of the Cheetah/Pack - remove the daze effect and perhaps add a -% dmg component so its not overpowered
- Glyph of Raptor Strike - duration is too low considering how long it takes to cast a Steady Shot with/without a cast time debuff
- Knocking down Improved Steady Shot to a 1x cast would be nice for pvp situations
- Having Frost/Freezing on separate CDs - One is essential to CC, and one is essential to help gain distance. With how many buttons you need to CC a target with Freezing trap and length of CD I dont see how this would be OP. Perhaps Have a 30sec CD for either trap when used, but a 15 sec shared CD between them

What type of content do you focus on? Exclusively PvE.

If PvE, what type of PvE? Raiding is my primary focus, but I also enjoy 5-mans(when new) and older content raids.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? If I had to pick a single issue, it would be the current trap launcher mechanics. The concept is fantastic, but the mechanic of loading the launcher, using the launcher and then waiting for the trap to arm is very cumbersome. Please consider streamlining this process so that traps can be placed more quickly.

The other issue that comes immediately to mind is the Exotic pet restriction. I understand why the decision was made to limit these pets to BM, but many raiding hunters would love to be able to use their Spirit Beasts without being limited to a single spec. BM hunters should still get extra talent points for pets, but pet classes themselves shouldn't be restricted. It's very frustrating to be lucky enough to acquire a rare pet, only to stable it because your raiding/PvP spces can't use it.

There are other quality of life issues, but these are the two biggies, in my opinion.

What makes playing your class more fun? I've always loved fighting alongside my pet and being able to fill missing buffs/debuffs based on pet selection is a great addition. I like playing a "niche" class, since we're the only physical ranged damage dealers. Focus is far more enjoyable to manage than mana was and the combination of Feign Death + Mass Resurrection is amazing. I also like the utility that traps and special shots(Tranq, Silence, Concussive, etc.) provide, plus extras like Disengage and Misdirection. I was worried about the change from Volley to Multi-Shot, but I think it's worked out well.

What makes playing your class less fun? The hunter "dead zone" can be frustrating at times. I can't comment about PvP, but even in PvE it sometimes limits positioning during boss fights, especially if the raid wants to group up close to the mob.

Pet management is always something that could be improved. I enjoy the sense of working together with my pet, but the recent changes to pet stances make it more difficult than it needs to be. I often have to adjust my pet's stance during a fight in order to ensure that my pet attacks the desired target. I realize that the changes were supposed to make pet management more intuitive, but it's quite the opposite for me because it limits the amount of direction that I can give my pet. Sometimes I want my pet to attack my target; other times, I want it to attack a different target. Above all, I never want my pet to run back to me while mobs are still active unless I direct it otherwise. In Defensive stance now, my pet wastes a lot of time running back and forth between myself and the mobs, such that I don't even bother using my pet on AoE packs. In Assist stance, my pet attacks my target even if I command it to attack something else. This restricts much of the pet's utility to the hunter.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? It's been quite some time since I've played BM, so I'm only going to comment on SV and MM. I really enjoy the SV rotation; it feels very fluid and it's fun to have a more DoT-oriented spec. Lock 'n Load could be a little more involved, although I don't have any suggestions at the moment.

MM definitely feels less fluid than SV, mainly because of the way Improved Steady Shot works compared to the DoTs of SV. The purpose of Aimed Shot in the rotation still seems unclear. Using Chimera Shot to refresh Serpent Sting is still the highlight of the rotation though, and I like that.

What’s on your wish list for your class? Well, I would love to see some better racials for Tauren hunters, but I realize that's a very low priority wish. Aside from that, I would very much like to see the Exotic pet restriction abolished and Eyes of the Beast brought back. Oh, and being able to tame Druids. You guys should totally look into that. ;-P

What spells do you use the least? Definitely the melee abilities like Wing Clip and Raptor Strike(remember, I don't PvP). Beast Lore and Immolation Trap are also rarely used.


Thanks very much for taking the time to read our feedback. =-)

Sincerely,
A dedicated hunter

  • What type of content do you focus on?
    • PvE: Heroics, Normal Raids

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
    • Pet Health not applying properly when summoned
    • Minimum Range
    • Trap Launcher mechanics
    • Lack of Non-Talented Interrupt
    • Temporary CC Immunities (see Zul'Aman; NPCs charging into position as scripted)

  • What makes playing your class more fun?
    I love the mobility that Hunters have. Being able to kite and maintain our rotation on the move is great. The freedom to choose amongst so many pets, and also play the Wild Card role for Buffs and Debuffs is empowering as a raid and party member. Extreme soloing is also a great option to have.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?
    Pretty much everything in the Quality of Life section.
    • Pets are periodically one-shot because maximum health takes a moment to register.
      Hunters are the only class in the game that are penalized for being up-close, and there is no range at which other (ranged) attacks cannot hit us. It's a penalty without a trade-off when compared to other ranged classes.
    • Trap Launcher is kind of clunky in that it requires us to press two buttons to use one ability, and a third action to deploy it. Add that to travel time and arming time once the trap lands, and you have yourself (at best) an inconvenience.
    • The lack of an interrupt for two out of three talent trees is annoying, considering that most (or all) tanks and other DPS have baseline abilities (excluding hybrids like and Shadow Priests, which requires a talent so that the DPS portion of the class has an interrupt).
    • The Temporary CC Immunities introduced in Zul'Aman are mentioned because Hunters are the only class that requires players to place their CC ahead of time, and having NPCs charge across them into a scripted position affects us more than any other class, which requires little or no preparation, and certainly no delay in travel or arming time.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”?
    As a Survival Hunter, I feel like the core rotation is in a good spot, but it's lacking in the Burst department. We can use Rapid Fire on a 5-minute cooldown, but it often feels like a one-trick pony. Compare that to the Beast Mastery tree, who is constantly juggling cooldowns, and you've gone from too little to what may feel like too much for someone who isn't acquainted with the spec.
    On the Marksman front, the changes which encouraged the hard-cast spamming of Aimed Shot for the first 10% (formerly 20%) of the fight feel very cheesy. It's a DPS gain to ignore key abilities for the first part of a fight because of how much better Aimed Shot is for that specific health range.

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?
    I don't have any dreams of dual-wielding pistols or calling five pets at once; I will be happy if we could see the items listed in the Quality of Life section resolved.
    I do miss Eyes of the Beast at times, but I can understand if it was removed so that there is room for other abilities on the technical side.

  • What spells do you use the least?
    • Counterattack
    • Eagle Eye (Though still very useful in specific circumstances)
    • Raptor Strike
    • Immolation Trap
    • Aspect of the Wild
    • Feed Pet

If PvP, what type of PvP?

Arenas and RBG's.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Changing from mana to focus was difficult, but we all seem to have adjusted well enough. Being able to have 5 pets on hand was a true gift from the devs. Aspect of the Fox is extremely nice for fights that require a lot of movement. Although we miss Volley, Serpent Sting application with Survival spec and the correct talent point placement is nice. Camouflage definitely has its own unique helpfulness, and Trap Launcher is amazing.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Prowl - "Ability not ready". It's not a major issue, but is still a little annoying when we have to put our pet into combat just to get it out of Prowl. Not exactly something to put as the top "Things to Fix" priority.

Chimaera Shot (Self-healing) - Lovely ability, great damage, laughable healing. Hunters are the only class without at least a decent self heal ability useable with every spec (bandaids don't count!). I suggest taking Serpent Sting and making it into a sort of multi-use ability. For each point of nature damage caused, you will get +2 health. It would be enough to keep us alive and fairly easy to keep constantly up as it is already a high-priority shot.

10 second pet ressurection - In close-combat situations like Arena and RBG's, successfully ressurecting your pet is nearly impossible with no way to shorten the cast time. The pushback glyph is a step in the right direction, but we have more important glyphs to worry about. I would suggest a 5 second cast time. It's still semi-long, but at least it would be more possible.

Steady/Cobra Shot - Yes, these are good to gain focus, but the damage that they do versus their cast time is depressing (IF your target doesn't just LoS you or cast a slow on you, leaving you empty-handed when it comes to focus regen). LoS is perfectly fine. I suggest buffing the damage by a small amount and making it immune to slowing effects (cast time, not movement speed).

Trap Launcher - Good idea, but in reality it takes too much time when it's really needed. I suggest not making it trigger GCD.

Aimed Shot - The damage is great! The cast time...not so much. With a base cast time of 3 seconds, this easily-avoidable shot can make it or break it. I would suggest cutting the cast time in half but giving it an individual cooldown, somewhat comparable to the cooldown of a Warrior's Collossus Smash ability. I believe that most hunters would agree with me that sacrificing a cooldown for a shorter cast time would be worth it. For those that don't like the idea, it could be made into a talent so that you can choose if you want a long or short cast time.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Black Arrow is hardly worth putting a point into anymore due to its high focus cost. Hard-casting Aimed Shot is still putting out more dps than using Arcane Shot for a focus dump in MM. Focus regeneration overall is terrible, especially in PvP when it's hard to keep enough distance to cast a couple of Cobra or Steady Shots.

What spells do you use the least?

Widow Venom, Eagle Eye, Immolation Trap, Raptor Strike - all good in theory, but they're just not good enough to waste a global cooldown on.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Set Bonuses - T11 vs T12 - Laughable. Also, the s10 4pc bonus (5% extra focus regen) takes way too much time to even amount to anything.

Point Blank - Look! A hunter! *Mage stands beside hunter and kills it* Isn't it about time that our hunters figure out how to defend itself? Give us a buff to Raptor Strike or Wing Clip and scale our traps correctly.

Mobility - Aspect of the Fox, even after "fixed" by Blizzard, still is not worth using much in PvP and is a significant dps loss in PvE. Get rid of it, and give us Glyph of Mobility (I know, the name isn't that good) - "Allows the hunter to cast Steady Shot and Cobra Shot while moving." *Credit to Cätälyst of Malorne for the idea*

Pet health - By now we've all encountered the glitch. You manage to get off a Revive Pet cast only to have your faithful companion come back to life with ~5k health, making it an easy target for someone (or something) who acts quickly.

Master's Call - 50/50, lose-lose. When it does work, we get a bit of freedom, only to have it dispelled away, leaving us with a big cooldown. When it doesn't work...well, we're screwed. I suggest that has 100% success rate and non-dispellable, keep the cooldown as-is.

Glyph of Kill Shot - Amazing glyph! But recently we haven't always been getting our "instant" cooldown, making this glorious glyph questionable for its reliability. In many situations, relying on this glyph can now be your entire downfall.

Sidekick - We were all overjoyed to have our faithful companions finally at our sides while mounted! This was a great idea and I am happy to see it. What I am not happy to see, however, is that 50% of the time when I dismount, my pet bar has vanished. This can only be solved by Dismiss then Call Pet or reloading UI, both of which can be the death of your hunter.

Not Enough Focus - Not enough focus. Not enough focus. Not enough focus. We're all tired of seeing this error message and "firing blanks", so to speak, while staring at our abilities that are "ready". I've been told that this is a communication error with the server, and personally I don't know how complicated it would be to fix, but it's driving us insane.

Arena - Need I say more? It is a combination of all of the above that is our downfall in the wonderous Arena. A hunter can only dream of not being laughed at in high-stakes arena matches ("a hunter" meaning everyone but the handful that are lucky enough to even see high-stakes arena). Between all of our glitches, lack of true defensive cooldowns, and lack of self-healing outside of BM...I'll just stop there, you get the point.


Thank you so much for finally giving hunters your much needed attention, and we are all looking forward to having our mechanics fixed and updated.
