Pet glitches (i.e. disappearing pet bar, summoning a pet only to have it one shot cause of health bug)

What makes playing your class more fun?
Mobility, Pet control, dynamic CC ability (trapping, scattering, monkey stun), unique pet abilities like roar of sacrifice

What makes playing your class less fun?
Specifically in Arena, being absolutely useless if there is line of sight and having ridiculous damage if there is someone in the open (the difference between a map like sewers and ring of valor). More than anything though, not being able to be Survival is my biggest issue. It has no dispel protection and cannot begin to compete with BM and MM hunters. I feel like survival should be the primary pvp spec for hunters and should play similar to a dot class with a hunter twist but in its current state it has no real Damage over Time in PVP and has NO survivability. I also hate having very few viable pets with wanted abilities to tame unless I am BM.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I pvp so rotations are not really something I do but in general I think the play of hunters are very fun, even if there abilities aren't up to par with other classes.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
To take out some of the "glass cannon" mechanics of the class. Make surviving in a PvP environment obtainable for a hunter. I feel like some creative defensive cooldowns or healing ablities are needed. PLEASE revive the survival tree. I have leveled and played every single class in this game and never have had as much fun as being a survival hunter, but due to is current state, I cannot be one. This makes me a sad panda

What spells do you use the least?
been said but widows venom. also camo. not being able to feign-->camo is not fair. camo officially is used like once every encounter.

P.S.
With all that being said, I wanna say that I really want to give Blizzard props for being so involved with its community. You took a lot of risks in Cata and have been getting a lot of flack (particularly from the PvP community) but your involvement with us and your constant effort to tweak, change, and satisfy this HUGE WOW community is awesome and it is going to pay off (already is but more is needed of course). Thanks so much for letting us have input (good or bad) and please keep up the awesome work :)

What type of content do you focus on? There's something other than raiding?

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? Focus, as a concept, is a good idea. The actual mechanics of it are terrible. We're ranged rogues with no finishing moves? Very situationally focus is very detremental. Im not talking about efficiently using focus, but rather the NEED to use a certain ability (like silencing shot, tranq) at a certain time and really not being able to due to focus starvation. The interplay with haste is terrible and almost unnoticable from one extreme to the other.
In fights where there is an add (or adds) such as rag, I find myself overly concerned with focus as its one of those mechanics where if you cannot do what you need to (in this case, kill all the adds before they hit the hammer) the raid wipes, no prevention. So thus Im HEAVILY reliant on Fire! procs or 4pc procs.

I personally feel that the whole focus debacle has been a failure since its implementation. Other than sitting on a single target for an entire fight, it really interferes with the play of the class. It just has not been fun to play since. I hated mana, but at least you had the freedom to change up rotations or shots with little to no interference. Now, as soon as you have to adjust to something, you have to re-evaluate several factors just to get back to your "normal" rotation.

What makes playing your class more fun? Nothing. Since vanilla I have played a hunter as well as other classes and it has always felt like the class was always in steady decline (especially from TBC and on) in regards to "how fun" the class was. Pigeonholing raiders like me into 1 spec (BM, SV or MM) just to compete was never fun. Being forced to play an arcane mage, oops, I mean 1 Button BM was\is the most infuriating thing but conversely the overly complicated MM rotations we see today are almost as disappointing. I do like the fact I get to play the spec I want, which is a step towards being "fun", but I wouldnt mind being able to swap to SV for fight A, MM for Fight B,C & D then back to SV for fight E.
I believe that a "pure" dps class's 3 trees should each have their niches. Just as an example, MM is the single target, heavy hitting shots spec, SV the aoe, specialty/utility spec and BM the "pvp" spec. But instead theyre all pretty much the same and all feel as if theyre trying to be the same. There's no real different flavor between the three specs.

What makes playing your class less fun? Focus. Not having ammo anymore. The homogenization of item slots (see previous posts of guns/bows replacing 2h weapons; dual guns etc all of which are AMAZING ideas). Homogenization of pets and the lackluster feel of most of them now. You're basically not bringing your favorite pet(s) to raids anymore, just the ones who can provide you the right buffs for your comp.
A note about Ammo, costs aside, I always felt it added an extra level of fun to the class. An idea would be (relating to the gun/bow as a 2h weapon instead of a stat-stick and ranged weapon) to have your weapon in your hands whilst in your "relic" slot you equip a type of ammo. Where you'd find "flaming arrows" or "Ice arrows", equip them into your relic slot and it would add dmg, a special graphic, some type of elemental conversion to the attack (making your white shots do say frost dmg instead of physical) etc.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) We have a rotation? I dislike the word as in theory a rotation works, but in practice there's no real such thing.

What’s on your wish list for your class? Fix focus. Fix focus' interplay with haste. Cast times' interplay with haste. Balancing stat weights to actually affect all our abilities instead of whichever ones class developers think it should affect that week. Make pets mean something other than hey, he brings this buff we dont have and forget about the pet the rest of the time. I really, REALLY, liked the idea of (for maybe MM) getting a bonus (cuz MM is more the "sniper" spec) for NOT using a pet. Brilliant.
