# What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE, though I have a lot of PvP exp.

# If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Raiding, Heroics, and L85 dailies.

# If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Mostly BGs, some Arenas, can't get into Rated BG groups...

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
- Having lots of keybinds has been the main issue for me, doing some tasks seems to be more complex then it needs to be.
- CCing a foe that's on the loose (either a wayward PvE mob or a PvP opponent) takes 3 skills to acomplish, scatter shot, trap launcher, and Freeze Trap. The freeze trap part has to be manually aimed with a small circle, takes 2 seconds to activate, and could miss it's target by either another wayward mob that was too close or an aware PVP partner.
- To do a simple snare, there's a keybind for melee snareing and a keybind for ranged snareing.
- I need keybinds for at least 3 traps, one of each category. For the most part, there's only 4 useful traps (Freeze, Ice, Explosion, Snake. Havn't found any reason to deal with binding Immo).
- The necessity for Side-Strafeing to shoot while kiting (a requirement to do anything to a PvE or PvP foe that's attacking the Hunter) is also quite a workout. w/o Side Strafeing, the Hunter is a sitting duck if preasured at all, PvE or PvP.
- Having to dedicate key binds for aspect swapping can be fun but it tends to pile up on the many keys I need to remember.

# What makes playing your class more fun?
- Pets! Pets are more customizable then anything any other class can offer. You can 'choose' their skin, their name, and talents. It's rare to see two - Hunters with the same pets out.
- I'm also into being a mobile shooter. Hunters are easily the most mobile ranged out there with a high amount of instants and a Fox'd steady.
- I like aspect swapping a bit.
- Disengage. A fun and universally useful skill. Whee! There's a reason I'm a goblin...

# What makes playing your class less fun?
- Keybinds! I feel like I still can't control my Hunter fully.
- Being a strictly DPS class has it's pros but it also hurts a lot when it comes to finding groups.
- The difficultly in playing Arenas. Hunts have a high skill floor.

# How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
- In PvE the rotations are good, they can be improved. As I specalize in BM, I can comment most on that rotation. I don't have an issue with BM's rotation being simplier then MM's or Survs. However, the rotation itself has no dynanic elements, it needs something to react to as Killing Streak lighting up just informs me that I'll have a bigger number pop up on my screen, instead of "Fire!" which makes me fit in the extra Aimed or Surv's LnL which changes my next 4-5 shots.
- I like MM and BM's CDs though (Rapid Fire + Aimed, Bestial Wrath). They change up the rotation making the CDs more then just "bigger numbers!"

# What’s on your wish list for your class?
1) Revamped Talent Trees for pets to make them more customizable, mostly for the DPS pets. There's little room to customize your DPS pet.
2) More streamlined keybinds so I can concentrate on controlling my character instead of trying to figure out how to controll my character.
3) Less mathy choices in the first 2 tier of talents for all 3 trees. It makes me go to EJ just to max DPS.
4) More RP stuff. I would love a /pet command that would do a pet emote, such as "Fluffy yawns." Would also love the return of Eyes of the Beast. Overall would like a bit more ability to RP even for a non-RP realm.

# What spells do you use the least?
- Wingclip. Mostly because I simply couldn't find a spot on my bar where it'd be easy enough to reach for the moments I'm in melee range, I just try to strafe/disengage away and concussive instead. Clicking isn't an option as my mouse in situations like that is fully dedicated to movement.
- Immolation Trap. I forgot this existed until I started typing that trap paragraph above.
Snakes if Entrapment isn't speced.
- Counter Attack. Specing into a skill that has a 5% chance of activating in melee range which requires me to still be in melee range to root someone for a tiny bit is not worth it. Especially in the same tree that has Entrapment.

1.) What type of content do you focus on?
I primarily focus on PvE, but I do not ignore the PvP element of the game.

2.) If PvE, what type of PvE?
I find I spend most of my PvE time doing dailies, quests, and Heroics. I most certainly got my moneys worth out of the first Raid tier of Cataclysm, but time being what it is, there is only a finite amount of it to raid.

3.) If PvP, what type of PvP?
When I participate, always Battlegrounds. I hop into the guild RBG runs when they can occur.

4.) What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
As primarily a Hunter, I would say the number of clicks necessary for throwing traps. While the mechanics are interesting, it seems more onerous than difficult to click throw and then the trap. Maybe mix trap throwing with an aspect of some sort?

5.) What makes playing your class more fun?
The dynamic uses of Hunters is what has kept this guy as my main since vanilla. Being able to switch (without switching major specs) between different functions, whether it be single target, AoE, CC, or kiting, has given me the most enjoyment out of this class.

6.) What makes playing your class less fun?
Aside from the standard answer of resists, etc, perhaps the relative similarity between specs means that there are really only two ways to play a Hunter - either ranged or with a pet.

7.) How do you feel about your “rotation”?
As I have always been a Marksman Hunter since Vanilla, I can say with utmost certainty that the standard rotation really has not changed much since the introduction of Steady Shot. Before focus, you used it whenever everything else was on cooldown, and honestly its returned to that once a Hunter achieves decent focus regeneration. Before Steady Shot, you basically had nothing to do between cooldowns. Aside from the Aimed shot proc, there does not seem to be much choice or dynamic interactions once you begin turret mode.

8.) What’s on your wish list for your class?
Alternatives to Arcane shot, either through glyphs or talents, and possible glyphs or talents to vary our frost trap graphic (Warden chain trap/shackle or Shannox crystal). Alternative ways to use the Master Marksman system, which can be improved and changed in a variety of interesting ways. A change to the Master Marksman buff icon, maybe something interactive like the Moonkin pendulum. A way to differentiate the Survival and Marksman play style.

9.) What spells do you use the least?
While its not unused, likely Kill Command (As a Marks Hunter) and Raptor Strike. Eagle Eye, and Snake Trap.

    Fix or redesign interrupt pets. Right now, the spell-interrupt pets are useless because unless you turn off -all- of their abilities, it's impossible to utilize their interrupt with any reliability. The AI isn't bright enough to wait until the target is actually casting, so its interrupts are wasted 90% of the time... these would be far more useful if the interrupt was taken off the pet's GCD so you can actually use it when needed instead of stopping your own rotation and furiously mashing the interrupt button in the blind hope it will register between spams of Bite.

    Fix Intimidate. It's useful... except when things are immune to it, which is a lot in late-game. I have no problem with mobs being immune to the taunt side of it, but the interrupt should still work on any mob that's interruptable. There has to be some way to separate the effects, but right now, it's an interrupt that works about 20% of the time.
