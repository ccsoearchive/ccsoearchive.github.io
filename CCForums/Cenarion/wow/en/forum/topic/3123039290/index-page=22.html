What type of content do you focus on?
-PVP

If PvP, what type of PvP?
-Random battlegrounds
(I wouldn't even try arena as a hunter right now.)

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

-Freezing trap

Freezing trap is the only non-AoE CC in the game that does not directly target a player or NPC. It is extremely frustrating to be unable to crowd control a healer (or anyone else) because I am not close enough to scatter shot first, and they simply move while the trap is being launched.

The other problem is that frost trap shares a cooldown with freezing trap. Using earthbind totem won't prevent shamans from using hex for 30 seconds. Howling fear won't prevent a warlock from using fear for 30 seconds. Frost nova doesn't prevent a mage from using sheep for 30 seconds. Crippling poison doesn't make a rogue lose access to sap, blind or stuns for 30 seconds. I could throw out numerous more examples, but hopefully that gets my point across.

-Pet bugs and clunky mechanics
Having the pet get stuck on scenery and be unable to access kill command, pet abilities, or trigger talents that rely on the pet being able to damage something. The pet bar disappearing. The pet getting dazed while I am mounted and getting dismissed by distance. The pet getting dismissed by entering a vehicle. The pet being resummoned with extremely low health, thus leading to instances in which my pet is killed immediately after summoning. (This, by the way, has happened in PVP and PVE.)

What makes playing your class more fun?
-Kiting, tracking, and using a ranged weapon for damage.
This, not the pets, is what makes the hunter class feel unique from other ranged classes as far as I'm concerned.

What makes playing your class less fun?
-The quality of life issues I described above
-Too many abilities requiring too many keybinds in PVP. I shouldn't feel like I need a special gaming mouse just to be able to access all the basic and necessary abilities that I use constantly. (It's not quite as bad in PVE.)
-Focus and cobra shot mechanics. I've played casters and rogues/ferals and managing mana and energy never felt as frustrating as managing focus.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
-PVP requires too much flexibility to be able to stick to a rotation. In what little PVE I've done, the rotation seems okay.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
-Freezing Arrow
-Bug fixes

What spells do you use the least?
-Widow Venom (waste of focus)
-Beast Lore (if I want to find out if something is tameable, I check Petopia)
-Immolation trap (I won't put explosive trap on cooldown for it)
-Eagle eye (I never need it in PVE, and I can't risk losing track of my immediate surroundings to use it in PVP)
-Aspect of the cheetah / pack (I use this sometimes in PVE but it is far too risky to be beneficial for me in PVP)
-Feed pet (I forgot I had this)
-Counter attack (This is a waste of a talent point now, with hunters having just 5% parry, and being unable to dodge or parry blows that come from behind.)
-Raptor Strike (I just didn't have an accessible spot to keybind it, and can't take the time to click it.)

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

A: PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

A: Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

N/A

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

A: I think the main one has been ammo, but obviously that has been taken care of. I can't think of anything too major other than that.

What makes playing your class more fun?

A: Having unique, fun abilities that most other classes don't have. Also, theorycrafting and the complexity of figuring out when to start stacking one stat over another has been pretty enjoyable.



What makes playing your class less fun?

A: Dead zones, not having able to have any damage reduction cooldowns (other than something you need to glyph for), the removal of fun abilities (Eyes of the Beast).

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

A: I feel like each spec has a fairly decent rotation at the moment. So far, I still prefer Survival. It just feels more smooth and not as "clunky", as some people would say, as Marksman. I haven't played as Beast Mastery very much, because it doesn't have as high numbers as Marksman or Survival. Beast Mastery's rotation feels a little too simple-minded. I think there could be some sort of tweak to the spec that could make a noticeable change in numbers between someone who really understands the class and has mastered it, than someone who plays casually and doesn't really care to push their class and abilities to the limit. I believe Survival and especially Marksman do this. You can separate the "masters" from the novices.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

A: The re-implementation of Eyes of the Beast. It was a VERY fun ability that made the Hunter class feel a little more unique.

Deadzones can kind of get a tad annoying, but I understand the reasoning behind them. Instead of getting rid of deadzones, maybe make bosses not have such huge hit boxes so that if there was a moment during a fight where the raid need to stack, the Hunter doesn't suddenly become useless because he can't attack the target with a ranged weapon. (Lord Marrowgar is a good example)

One more wish list item would be for Silencing shot to be on a shorter cooldown.

What spells do you use the least?

Camouflage, Immolation Trap.



EDIT:

An addition to least used spells: Widow venom, counterattack.

An addition to what makes my class fun: Eagle eye is a talent just like Eyes of the Beast that makes my class unique and fun. Realistically, the ability doesn't do anything for me. It doesn't give me a boost in PvE or PvP. It's just a FUN ability.
