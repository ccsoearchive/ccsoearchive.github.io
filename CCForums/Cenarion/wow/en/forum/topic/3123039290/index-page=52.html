Here is a post I made on my main a while regarding hunters, so the data may be a little dated.

"I've noticed that Beast Mastery hunters have had quite a hard time since the end of The Burning Crusade, and understand that this is the best place to bring feedback regarding changes we'd like to see to the game.

If we take a look at the top parses, we can see that the Beast Mastery hunter is pretty lack luster compared to other classes.

For 25H they are sitting at 3rd from the bottom.
For 25N they are sitting at 3rd from the bottom.
For 10H they are sitting at 5th from the bottom.
For 10N they are sitting at 4th from the bottom.

If we look at similar classes at the bottom, we see Unholy Death Knights, Frost Mages, and Subtlety Rogues.

The difference between these 3 class/specs and Beast Mastery hunters is this: although they are near the bottom for DPS, they are also extremely powerful in PvP, something Beast Mastery hunters are not.

Clearly I would like to see some changes implemented to make Beast Mastery a viable spec to play for more than just soloing old content. While I'm no developer I do have a few ideas that could help balance out the spec and make it a bit better.

1. Change to Bestial Wrath and Beast Mastery.

Here I propose replacing Intimidation as the "Beast Mastery defining ability" with Bestial Wrath. If we look at every other class' "defining ability" we are given moves that truly show what it means to be a Frost Mage, Enhance Shaman, Demonology Warlock, etc. However, Intimidation seems a bit lack luster in that regard. Changing it to Beastial Wrath not only makes the Beast Mastery tree feel more like a Beast Master from the beginning of level 10, but it frees up an option for a talent further down the tree.

On the same note, adding Beast Mastery to the current "Passive" abilities that each Specialization gets would show the world which Hunter was a Beast Master as early as level 10. Sure, players won't get Devilsaurs until later on, but they can still get Worms, Chimaeras, and Core Hounds, as early as level 11 and 12.

This again frees up a talent point slot where something can be added to increase the overall performance of the spec.

Now we have an empty slot where BW was, and where BM was. Simply put Fervor where Bestial Wrath was, and Intimidation where Fervor was, and implement a new 31 point to really drive the spec home.

2. Implement a new 31 point talent.

There are several things that can be done here: a new shot, a new pet focused ability like Kill Command -but what I suggest really would make a Beast Master feel like a Beast Master.

Grant an ability similar to Feral Spirit: Stampede.

At first glance, this sounds like an ability to summon all the hunter’s pets at once, but that is not the case. I understand that would be extremely difficult to balance. Below is the proposed 31 point talent I feel would help Beast Masters perform.

Stampede
40 Focus
40 Yard Range
10 Second Cooldown
Assault your foe with a Stampede of your controlled pets dealing X damage and causing the target to bleed for 20% of the damage dealt for 8 seconds.

(X could be equal the amount of damage their collective Focus Dump abilities would be + a % of your RAP)

The animation of this ability would be all of your current controlled pets charging your target and attacking once before disappearing.

The ability, to me at least, seems unique enough and balance-able enough where it's a win-win for everyone."

After reading some of the other responses to this thread, I wanted to add/reinforce a couple of wish list things:

- Switch hunters' weapon slots: give us a single 2h melee slot where our current ranged slot is, two ranged weapon slots where our melee slots are, and let our ranged weapons be displayed outside of combat instead of our melee weapons. Sure, the second ranged slot would only be used by SV, but one spec using that slot is still better than the current situation of no specs using that slot.

- Make Beast Mastery (the talent) be the reward you get for choosing the BM tree, and make the final BM (tree) slot be summoning two pets. I understand if you don't have the tech to support two pet bars - if the compromise is that we have to control both pets via one bar and just choose which skills they auto-use via a separate spell book sheet for each one, so be it.

- Give dual-wielding pistols to Survival hunters as their final talent. I know that would require some new animations for every gender and race, but it would really be worth it. I personally never play SV, but if they could DW I would definitely consider giving it a shot every now and then.

- Let MM be the petless tree. Since every MM hunter I know just regards their pet as an extraneous damage/buff satellite anyway, why force them to include something in their playstyle that they find un-fun and tedious? Just give them a little extra damage and/or buff/debuff to bring to the group to make up for it when they don't have a pet out.
