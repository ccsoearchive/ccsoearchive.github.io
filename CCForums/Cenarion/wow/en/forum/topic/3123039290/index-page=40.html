What type of content do you focus on? A little of everything

If PvE, what type of PvE? Questing, heroics, some raiding.

If PvP, what type of PvP? BGs, general world pvp if it happens

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? Complete lack of any melee competency makes me feel entirely too weak. My favorite days of playing as a hunter were in the early/mid Burning Crusade timeframe and as a Survival spec back then. Also, lack of any hunter applicable one hand weapon enchants. I like the look of two one-handers over a two hander, but none of the Cata enchants seem to apply to ranged attacks. Lastly, a lack of feeling of much difference between marksman and survival. I think it would be good to try to make those two trees have a more drastically different play style between them.

Also.. the lack of any truly spammable AOE.


What makes playing your class more fun? The removal of mana in made many things more fun since it eliminated downtime (viper aspect for mana regen was fine for raiding/boss encounters but extremely annoying for BGs and general questing/farming). So that's nice. The old school trapping method (dragging things into a trap) used to be a fun challenge.

What makes playing your class less fun? No way to avoid/clear/remove debuffs on myself nor any self heal capabilities. In general my hunter feels like a glass cannon. Also, the fact that it's extremely difficult to detect a stealthed rogue before they get into deadly range. I'd think a hunter would excel at detecting things that are trying to hide. As above, having pitiful melee capability is a big downer at times.



How do you feel about your “rotation”? It's okay as a marksman. It makes me pay enough attention and I always seem to have some button I can mash. I do wish the double steady shot buff lasted a bit longer though to allow some more variety in what I could use in between double steady shots.


What’s on your wish list for your class? I really wish that marksman and survival played more differently. They both just seem like mostly the same pew pew ranged shooters. Personally, I wish that there was a ranger type mixed melee/ranged hunter possibly without a pet. Or maybe they could temporarily tame or summon wild creatures to help instead of permanently. One way to handle the mixed melee/ranged could be to have abilities that buff/increase their ranged or melee capabilities (separate buff types) so that they couldn't continuously maintain equal proficiency in both.. they'd have to choose which to focus on for a given fight/encounter. That way they could choose either (and potentially switch if applicable) for long fights like a boss but wouldn't be overpowered in situations like PvP. It would be quite a challenge to design but I think would provide for a distinctive talent tree option.. resulting in a choice of Beast stuff (BM), ranged pew pew, or the ranger style.

What spells do you use the least? Cobra shot (I'm marksman), Flare, Kill Command.. not sure what else. Scare Beast, because I forget about it.

With the intention to swap to hunter for my main (from mage) for the rest of Cataclysm and (hopefully) into the next expansion, and although I know the spirit of this thread might be such that "non-level capped hunters need not apply" I'd like to give my input as well.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Although I am still levelling I intend to run Heroics and occasional Raids, as I do on my main."

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

I don't engage in much structured PvP aside from Tol Barad. However, as a player on a PvP server I constantly have to contend with the possibility of sudden, unannounced combat.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo would be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Minimum range. In PvP, I get why it might exist, but in PvE and in general, it's an annoyance and just plain not fun. I'll document my concerns more below in the "less fun" section.

Disengage not being useable outside of combat. I understand the original intention here was likely to avoid exploitation, but with goblins gaining Rocket Jump our inability to use Disengage seems mostly just a relic of the past limiting our ability to use one of our most fun toys.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Disengage and our high mobility.

Of course the big one is pets! It adds an extra activity to the game, encouraging us to go on Adventures to tame the pets we want or like, and ensuring that there is a sentimental attachment to each one. Every tame is a story. That's not just my pet who happens to be a boar, that is CHOPS and I tamed him in Redridge and raised him from a piglet, thank you very much.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Broadly speaking, whenever I feel like playing the spec that I like best will hurt the group, or be less than ideal. With regards to Hunter, that means that I'm pretty bummed about not being able to take Beast Mastery wherever I want, as it's what I like best.

Minimum range, as sometimes we're just plain going to have to stand close to avoid the fire or to follow a collapse in mechanic, and it sucks to be penalized for it.

Getting focus back in the last bit of a trash pull, especially AoE pulls, can be a pain because everything is at so low health that nothing lives long enough for focus generating shots to go off, leaving you standing around tossing out the odd Kill Shot with not a lot to do.

Kill Command is a little wonky, as it doesn't order your pet to move to the target and then attempt to use the ability, instead only trying to use it and failing outright if it can't execute.

Trap Launcher is pretty unwieldy as you need to use it, use the trap, target and place trap and wait for it to "load in" before a target even has a chance of setting it off. In this time, the target often ends up passing the originally targeted location and the trap fails to trigger.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I have only had extensive experience with Beast Mastery's "rotation" (read, priority system) but I have enjoyed it quite a bit. Focus fire may just work better as a triggered buff though.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Disengage useable outside of combat.
Trap Launcher tweaked to be more straightforward to use.
"Clicked" pet abilities having priority over ones set to auto.

...dual wielding weapons for stats rather than using a stat stick. It just looks cooler!

More pets! Of course we can usually expect new pets and pet families in each expansion, but I wanted to specifically note on my wish list some sort of Deer family. A lot of people joke about how menacing "Bambi" really is, but it's necessary to note that there are some great "Deer" models in the world that would fit right in as tameable beasts. Of particular note are the stag models used in Azuremyst and Felwood (Shadowhorn Stags in particular) and of course, Talbuks and my personal favourite Shoveltusks.


What spells do you use the least?

Widow Venom and Distracting Shot. While Distracting Shot at least has a place every once in awhile in an encounter, Widow Venom seems too expensive and ineffective to justify using instead of other abilities, either in PvE or PvP.


Thanks a bunch for making threads like these! It's a great gesture on your guys' part!

-Mobility and flexibility in battle; When you can get it to work, it's awesome. Disengage, trap some goon, Readiness and trap him again when he trinkets. It's neat.
-Tactical flexibility. I can hide, wait for people to go past, then open up on some unsuspecting squishy when they get into a fight ahead of me. I can place my traps strategically to cover avenues of approach, or to prepare a preplanned escape route. It's fun to have a wide variety of tools.

What makes playing your class less fun?
-playing a mobility class where ALL of the melee classes/specs in the game can neutralize you. Warriors, Paladins, Rogues, Shamans... any melee spec can close with me and either stay there or deal so much damage that I can't make up the difference kiting them until their closers come off cooldown again. Don't get me started on Feral Druids. Feral v. Hunter isn't a fight: it's a free honor kill for the Feral.
-line of sight
-Complete inability to hurt healers: Hunter damage is barely a tickle to any healer in like gear. They laugh at you. Seriously, they queue the heal, and type /laugh.
-complete helplessness in melee.
-having important abilities or talents from our specs -negate- other class abilities. Survival Hunters can't cast Serpent Sting, OR explosive shot, against a target they're -attempting- to freeze; Marksman Hunter's Mortal Shots talent will break CC.
-easily exploitable or avoidable abilities: Feral druids who I actually manage to fear (the ones that are paying attention shift out before the cast completes) can manually cancel their animal form to clear Scare Beast's fear. Any melee worth his salt knows what my trap animation looks like.
-The overwhelming power of stealth, and my helplessness before it. Seriously, why do I even HAVE Track Hidden? I cannot detect a rogue without literally walking over him, and if I do see him, any skilled rogue will simply sap me before I can react. With the huge boost to movement speed available to rogues in stealth, Flare is a terribly inadequate tool.
-draconian penalties to dps for dispelling, without the ability to sustain the dispels.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
-I'm quite happy with my rotation in PvE, but PvP introduces many situations where the steady shot requirement cripples me. The entire playerbase knows that they can interrupt my dps by moving next to me, or around corners, and so they do this regularly. This causes my focus regeneration to plummet, and often makes me helpless to DO damage again when I achieve distance/LoS. Similarly, the loss of haste from Improved Steady Shot is a heavy blow, as it requires me to cast two unhasted steady shots in a row in order to recover the bonus. Fighting inside buildings is like getting lost on a field trip to Hell.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
-break the unfair power of stealth, or give me some meaningful ability to combat it.
-Give me some kind of balance between melee's closers and my escapes. A melee's closers should be either more powerful than my closers, or more numerous. Currently, melee closers are both: an Arms warrior, for example, has a closer to counter each of my escapes, and several additional abilities to hold me in place. Furthermore, his closers are far more powerful: as an example, I cannot Disengage out of Improved Hamstring, but that Warrior can Charge out of my Entrapment. Combine this with the fact that all melee have at LEAST 115% base movement speed and spammable snares; even in an open area, it can be impossible to gain more than a few seconds of time at range... And your damage output at ranged is crippled while kiting.
-Concussive Shot, while a powerful tool, requires a huge drop in focus regeneration at range, which works out to a crippling cost in dps. The need to cast a global every 5 seconds to refresh the snare interferes with Steady shot.
-Make Trap Launcher like the pet summon mechanic, or at least take it off the global. Either change would be awesome.

What spells do you use the least?
-Kill Command: maybe it's good for Beast Mastery...
-Aspect of the Cheetah: the daze is far too much of a liability
-Widow Venom: Focus cost and a global for negligible healing.
