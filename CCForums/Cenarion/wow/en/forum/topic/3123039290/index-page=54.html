What type of content do you focus on?

both pretty often

If PvE, what type of PvE?

raids, heroic raids

If PvP, what type of PvP?

Arenas, sometimes guild queues rated bg's for funsies

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

-*Clunky pet swaps, and if the new pet happens to be dead for whatever reason, we are the only class required to hardcast a 10s res. Don't know if that's really a QOL issue or not?

-**When we are being focused, deterrence innately reduces our focus regen to the baseline level, since casting can't happen. So when the effect ends, and we are still being trained, it's incredibly dangerous to be in anyone's LOS for 2s intervals trying to get a SS into missile time -- not to mention that dodge/parry drop to 0 for these required cast times.

-*** Trapping has always been awkward, and just about any form of cc will interrupt the trapping process -- leaving us with scatter on cooldown.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Solo'ing and being useful in raid!

What makes playing your class less fun?

**** The obvious ones have been mentioned, so I'll talk about an awkward hunter problem: required LOS time. With other ranged classes, playing defensively against other ranged classes typically requires hiding behind pillars/boxes and flinging cheap, effective spells during that hiding time. 2s poly cast, 2s fear, a hex, and icelance are good examples of this.

But, hunter focus regen requires at least 2s in LOS (the trapping process requires up to 4s) during which we endanger ourselves significantly more than other ranged classes, while still having one of the lowest effective health pools and the lowest passive mitigation.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

For raids, the rotation is actually pretty fun? Arenas obviously don't give much time for an ideal dps rotation, and there are problems with shots costing far more than their actual dps worth.

In raid, your SS's will always go through without anything contesting them. In arena, we have so little focus to spare that our shots are all fired while we squint at the screen hoping for a crit.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

From the problems mentioned earlier:

* A lowered cast time on Revive Pet. 10 seconds seems far too long, especially with the removal of the (rarely seen but still technically attainable) Improved Revive Pet talent.

* An active HP monitoring system for stabled pets that could appear beneath the character's health bar? Similar to the marvel vs capcom style, the health pools would already be loaded to prevent the frustration of pets dying instantly when called.

** Deterrence providing a reasonable amount of focus regen when active (10-20/s), similar to Dispersion? Hard to exploit in raid, while still providing us with a few shots once we leave deterrence?

*** Trapping is awkward, one of its problems being that the entire process is fragile. Scatter and the Trap itself both break on 1 damage, with the hunter being susceptible to any form of CC in the game and they will all stop the process.

To help the fragility, I wouldn't oppose a small damage threshold on either of the abilities. While that would help, the primary issue seems to be that trapping becomes borderline impossible when being trained (imagine a mage being interrupted during polymorph, and not having access to it or frost nova for 30seconds)?

I would like to see a mechanic in place that supresses CC-effects after using scatter shot. Similar to priest shields, this would greatly help the fluidity of our control and defensive utility in general.

**** This is a stretch... but... consider making steady-cobra instant cast, triggering a global cooldown that matches current cast time calculation. Assuming this went through, Fox would need to be re-worked but, it would clear up several of the problems in the PVP theaters while leaving the raid world untouched.

What spells do you use the least?

Widow's Venom (really wish this was passive with serpent sting)
Immolation Trap

Thanks for reading, can't imagine you guys have fun looking through these all day. But, on the off-chance you read this one, I hope you take some of these ideas into consideration - or if you're working on a different mechanic, I'm looking forward to seeing your ideas.

Cheers,
-Schoolias
