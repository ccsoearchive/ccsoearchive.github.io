What type of content do you focus on?
Both, but primarily PvE.

If PvE, what type of PvE?
Mostly raids. 5 man queues are still too long for me to devote any serious time to them. I prefer 25man raids, but they've been hard to put together, so I generally do 10s. Strangely, I also enjoy questing. You guys did a great job promoting the story telling aspects of quests this expansion. I loved the Firelands dailies and all of the achievements involved. Step in the right direction, in my oppinion. I wear my Flamebreaker title with pride. :D

If PvP, what type of PvP?
I've tried all of the above, but primarily focus on rated BGs with this character. Hunters have too many handicaps to be "fun" in arenas. I find it difficult to prevent getting slowed, and once slowed, it's difficult to get back to a spot where I can actually hit anything. Right now, my arena toon is a Holy Paladin, which is infinitely easier. Rated BGs are okay because very few people bother to target a hunter. BGs are fun too, but once you're done with honour gear, they serve little purpose.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Absence of stable masters. Obviously it's our responsibility to bring the right pets to the raid, but sometimes the group composition changes 2-3 times during the run. This currently means hearthing back to your home city to switch pets. You guys have been kind enough to add repair people to the raids, repair bots carry reagents, and flasks/food can be obtained from the guild bank virtually anywhere, but stable masters are few and far between. Please consider adding a stable master to the next raid or at least placing one in a nearby town. :)

What makes playing your class more fun?
Strong DPS in a raid environment. Very maneuverable. Lots of ways to avoid damage and help the raid in PvE. Our gear is primarily our own, and we don't need to fight 3 other dps and a healer to get it. Having a "companion" who sticks with you from lowly noobdom to raid content is fun and makes questing a breeze.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Slows and stuns up the yazoo in PvP combined with limited damage mitigation and an inability to dps or use chimera shot when I'm inevitably caught. Lack of damage mitigation on raid damage-heavy fights, particularly fights where the raid takes a lot of damage at once. Bosses with large hitboxes that require stacking in melee. (You've certainly come a long way from Marrowgar, but a few bosses seem to have missed the memo... *cough* Baleroc *cough*)
Oh, and no self-respecting war/rogue will raid as arms/sub so I'm forced to bring my hyena (ugliest pet in the game) to every raid.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
SV is like wack-a-mole. MM is about guessing whether or not to slip an arcane shot in to bleed focus. It's okay once you get used to it, although having to interrupt or tranq between steadies is annoying. MM gets a little messed up in pvp since you have to do a lot more than just dps. Double steadies aren't always viable.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
As a raider? Not much. Hunters are pretty solid. Just keep letting us dps bosses when stacked and we'll get along just fine.
As a pvper? You could do a lot of things, but I'm going to suggest a talented "aspect mastery" similar to the holy paladin's "aura mastery". Back in wrath BM had its passive aspect buff but this would be an ability you had to trigger, with a cooldown and a limited duration. Hiting this while in cheetah would grant you 6 seconds of undazable run speed (though it would not remove a daze and you could still be stunned). Wild would give you an increased nature resistance, fox would increase your focus regen, and hawk would either give you increased attack power. Increased AP would give the ability PvE uses (which might be okay if it were a talent deep in the BM tree, for example, which has historically lagged behind in terms of DPS but been the go-to tree for PvP). If this would be too "game breaking" you might also consider allowing hawk to remove our minimum range for 6 seconds. This has limited PvE uses, but would be a godsend in PvP when melee and ranged alike delight in simply standing on top of us and cycling slows on us.

What spells do you use the least?
Aspect of the Wild;
Trueshot Aura (everyone has it...);
Track Hidden (ineffective vs rogues & druids);
Flare (also ineffective vs rogues & druids unless you're at a choke point);
Immolation Trap;
and Tranquillizing Shot (please add more bosses with enrages because I loves me some Tranq Shot)

....Part I of III....

Hi Blizz, I’m Jagier! I’ve been playing WoW since, oh, way back in Vanilla. I've played other classes in the game, but by far the hunter is my most favorite class. I'm a hunter at heart, so this post is also near and dear to my heart. It took me awhile to compose this; and to that end, I want to thank you for giving us the opportunity to discuss what we feel are some of the issues we have with our class. I do hope you enjoy reading my post as much as I enjoyed composing it.

Without further adieu…

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

I focus primarily on PvE and dabble a bit in PvP, not really because of choice, but primarily because of my class’s ineffectiveness in PvP. More on this to follow; but due to class ineffectiveness, we find that not many hunters PvP. For example, as to date, out of the 2000 results displayed on rated battlegrounds, only 82 hunters are ranked. This means only 4.1% of those that do rated battlegrounds are ranked hunters, the lowest class represented in rated battlegrounds. The second worst are warlocks at 8.6%. But that’s for the warlock thread. For arenas the numbers are even worse. To date, the percentage of hunters that arena with a rating of 2000+ is only 4%.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

In PvE, I personally focus primarily on raids and heroics. However, old content instances and raids are just as fun.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

In PvP, when I do do PvP, mostly just BGs at this time.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Doing away with manna has been the greatest benefit to us hunters ever, primarily because we are not casters, we are hunters. To that end, having hunter abilities scale with weapon damage would also be the greatest benefit to us instead of spell power/penetration. No more so would this benefit the most than the Survival spec, especially in PvP. This does not mean, however, that our current resource, Focus, is the hunter’s salvation. With the exception of Cobra/Stead Shot, unless we are doing nothing, we have no other way to regenerate our Focus.

The hunter’s resource is mostly akin the to the rogue’s energy, and to that I end I would propose a system similar to that of the rogue. For instance, passively a rogue regenerates 10 energy per second. A hunter, only around 4.58 Focus per second. Do not forget that rogues also have Recuperate, which generates 12 energy per tick. We hunters have nothing akin. Perhaps redesigning Aspect of the Fox to where instead of only in the instance of a melee damage does a hunter regenerate 2 Focus, it regenerates 2 Focus per second regardless of the situation, like Aspect of the Viper to manna.

With the exception of the Beast Master’s Spirit Bond talent and Marksman's Chimera Shot, we hunters also have no self-heal abilities. The 2% health regeneration of total health is almost useless. The Chimera Shot's 5% total health self-heal can only happen once every 10 seconds (9 with glyph). Quite ineffective, and that's only if you're MM spec. One suggestion that I would make in this regard is to convert Widow’s Venom to not just deny to the enemy healing but also to heal the hunter, something akin to the warlock’s Siphon Life ability.

The hunter’s pet is the most important asset to the hunter. No other class in the game in which uses pets utilizes a cast ability to revive their pet from death. With the exception of perhaps a cooldown, the hunter’s Revive Pet ability should be instant, like every other class in the game that uses pets.

CC and traps all together are just simply clunky and user unfriendly. Though it is a big improvement from the olden days, the system of laying the trap on the ground is where the inherent problem resides. With exception to the AoE traps, to perhaps actually shoot a trap on the target itself would make things so much better both for CC and damage.

In regards to our CC, compared to other classes, there's hardly CC balance in the PvP setting where our CCs only last 5 seconds compared to a warlock's fear which lasts 10 seconds, to just identify one example. Additionally, Silencing Shot is only effective for 3 seconds with a 20 second cooldown, compared to a DK's Strangulate which is effective for 4 seconds with a 10 second cooldown.

The dead zone of the hunter is also a big issue. Yes, we can ask for the dead zone to be removed all together, but there’s other ways to work around it. For instance, remove the shared cooldown between Freezing and Ice traps, making them their own separate cooldowns. ....

....Part II of III....

....Redesign the Crouching Tiger, Hidden Chimera talent in some way to it make it accessible to all hunter talent specs, such as by making it into a glyph instead of a talent. Redesign Disengage to where the hunter will always leap back 40 yards, or reduce the cooldown of Scatter Shot. However, this is only applicable during PvP. There are instances during PvE, in raiding scenarios, where the minimum range becomes a really, really big problem. For example, to identify a few, with the exception of Immolation Trap, we have no way to DPS the eyes of Occultar; we lose all DPS ability due to the close proximity of the shard to the boss during the Bael'Roc fight; and we have to be off-stacked from the rest of the group during the second phase of the Bath'ilac fight, depriving us of druid/shaman AoE heals.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Utility is what makes the hunter the most fun class to play in the game. We’re like a Swiss Army knife with so many different features in which makes the hunter a truly invaluable asset to the game. Leveling on a mage or some other class, there are times I realize how useful traps, feign death, and misdirect are, or having a pet when I’m on my DK or Warrior. And then there is the DPS. I enjoy being a top DPS class. It makes me all warm and fuzzy inside when I see that I’m topping the meters in a raid.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Clunky features such as with traps (discussed above), constant aspect changing, and resource management issues (discussed above). The biggest issue is survivability, especially in PvP. As mentioned above, the dead zone is one of the biggest issues. But frankly, we are just too easy to take down. Most classes have some form of damage reduction/self-healing abilities. We hunters have no self-healing or damage reduction abilities, especially when stunned or snared. The only exception is Deterrence, but with a cooldown of 2 minutes, its usefulness is very, very limited. There is also Counterattack in the Survival tree, but with a 5% parry rating, it never ever procs.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Rotation wise, I’m content. I really do not have any complaints there. For us hunters, “rotation” is all based upon Focus management and haste. It does take a whole lot of haste, I mean a whole lot of haste, to get to the best/most ideal rotation while yielding the most damage and highest DPS, even with the help of tier gear and reforging. I also truly detest the Careful Aim nerf reducing it from 80% to 90%. Indeed, I understand where Blizz is coming from on this issue as on certain raid bosses 90% does take a while. However, in heroics or regular instances the window of opportunity is so very small, if it even exists at all.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Along with the suggestions I have already made, I would love to have returned to us Eyes of the Beast and Volley. Sure there is multi-shot, and speced right, it can be very, very damage/DPS satisfying. However, Volley was just cool and not so resource dependent as multi-shot. Eyes of the Beast was really cool because it was nice to scout the area out first before committing to something, without having to put the actual hunter at risk.

I would also love to see Deterrence used when we are stunned, and the hunter not being CCable when using Bestial Wrath again.

Having some sacrifice pet ability like what warlocks have also would be really cool, something that either heals the hunter instantly or gives us a bubble of some sort.

I would also like to have more control over Piercing Shots since it breaks my CCs by either making it an actual shot that I can shoot or to redesign it where it won’t break CCs.

To have Silencing Shot and Tranq Shot both at 40 yards instead of 35.

I would love to go back to dual wielding again. But there’s no useful dual wielding hunter enchants. Heck, maybe even dual wielding ranged weapons??? Now that’ll be cool.

....Part III of III....

What spells do you use the least?

I could list those “spells/abilities/shots” that I don’t use very often, but each of these has its usefulness for its own place and time. I would hate to put us hunters in a comprisable situation in which we lose those abilities. Instead, I would like to highlight some talent abilities that I feel are useless, talents that you very rarely see a hunter use. I’ve already mentioned a few like Crouching Tiger, Hidden Chimera and how I think it should be made into a glyph, the Spirt Bond hunter healing part is virtually useless, and how Counterattack how it never procs with our 5% parry rating.

For BM:
1. Pathfinding. A basically well rounded worthless talent. For mount speeds, guild perks are faster than the talent. In regards to class balance, paladins get 15% and DKs get 20% speed increases. While doing so, their CC and snare effects are reduced. To make it worthwhile, perhaps redesign the talent to where the speed increase is similar to paladins and DKs and hinders CC/Snare abilities in some way (like by removing the dazed effect caused when hit in Aspect of the Pack/Chaeta).
2. Killing Streak. The effect lasts 7 seconds but the cooldown on kill command is 6 seconds. Leaves us one second.

For MM:
1. Rapid Killing. With the exception of leveling or PvP, this talent is pretty much useless. And unless the hunter is feeling adventurous to level with MM instead of BM, it really only makes it useful for PvP. But, to make it all the worse, it only lasts for 20 seconds, and doesn’t benefit our biggest damage contributors in PvP, Chimera and Arcane Shot.
2. Resistance is Futile. We MM hunters never use kill command. Completely worthless to the MM hunter. Even if we do spec into it to get the return, it doesn’t last very long to where when we have an opportunity in our rotation to use it, we can.
3. Termination. I use this talent only to get to my other talents. Unless it’s a raid boss, this is really quite useless as to what like the nerf to Careful Aim did to that talent. Certainly it’s useful to get off more special shots within the window provided, but by no way does this help our signature shot, Chimera.

For SV:
1. Survival Tactics. The second part is nice, but the first part not so much considering traps do not scale with weapon damage.
2. Sniper Training. If we’re lucky enough not to be moving around in raids, instances, PvP.

The type of content I focus on is both pvp and pve. As for pve that includes mainly heroics and endgame quest chains.

As for PVP Its mainly BGs as i feel there is substantially more factors involved than arena's.
My biggest quality of life Issues revolve around pet abilities and ability verities. I believe you should be able to train specific pets in specific abilites.

About the ammo removal, i actually was against that. I believe that Ammunition should be reinstated not as a requirement to be able to use our range weapons but as an alternative to boring and basic gliphs such as glyph of arcane shot and glyph of serpent sting.

Furthermore, what makes my class so fun to play is the unique relation my class shares with the game environment itself. As for every other class, a wondering beast, regardless the lvl is a potential threat, yet for the hunter that wondering beast will 95% of the time be a potential pet.

What makes playing my class less fun is our inability to properly defend ourself in melee range do the removal of our defensive aspects which granted use the dodge necessary to escape attackers and the lack of abilites tied to our melee weapon so that we may finish off dying targets in melee range.

As for the rotation of other specs I find the MM spec most boring, yet I have not invested enough time into cata mm to really give a logical answer to that spec. However, the Bm rotation as far as i can see is very ideal for a high burst spec and i would not like to see it change very far in the near future. Though that does not mean their is no room for improvement.

As for the wish list for my class, I would like to see more veriety between the three specs. What I mean by this is not in spec talents but in specializations based on weapon and pet types and not just talent/pet builds. This kind of specialization would not work like the current spec/racial specializations, these would provide specific damage/crit/crit-damage bonus or tactical effects when using a designated ability selected from a special chart similar to the glyph chart we have today. What this would do is not only make it important for a hunter to go after specific types of ranged and melee weapon combinations. But also make it significant for a hunter of a specific spec to choose a specific ability that he/she would enhance with the sugested new weapon/ammo bonuses per attack.

A good example of this would be the mm ability Chim shot. If an mm hunter chose the X- bow specialization and was carying razer arrows he/she would immediately recieve a buff that granted their Chim Shot a reasionable chance to allow their Chimaera Shot to strike (X) amount of targets directly behind the primary target relative to the direction the hunter had fired his/her shot for (X)% weapon damage.

What this would do is allow a hunter of any spec to choose their own signature ability that delivers a signature blow be it for aoe, single target, burst or survivability in those environments.

As for spells I use the least I use Widow's Venom. Do to its very high focus cost and nominal healing debuff and zero damage there is little or no use for this ability at all. Especially when I'm running with a Devilsaur.
