■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE, since Wrath. I just pug around since I'm on a high-pop server, but lately I've been trying to arena and its slightly frustrating since I'm not a fully veteran hunter yet and it's hard to get high ratings when other classes stomp you with mostly ease. Learning curve is kinda big. I'm having trouble to get past 2200.

■If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Troll heroics, and I'm tired of em. For the rest, Firelands.

■If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs

Arenas, mostly. Since I was done doing my honor farming, I've been trying to, but see above ^.

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Pet aggro, and the pet as it is. If I lose my pet during an encounter, I have no chance whatsoever to compete with the other raid dps. If I have to be constantly moving, dpsing, and controlling my pet at the same time, sometimes it just can't be done and I end up wasting 10 seconds + to rezz my pet. How about an instant rezz, once every 10 mins or so, like Warlocks?

Also, pet aggro. With decent gear I find it very hard to solo stuff, and I constantly need to MD non-stop on my pet if I want it to keep aggro. If I want him to keep threat on multiple targets, forget it, it's over. I remember in BC, when I leveled as BM, whatever I would do, my pet (with growl) would ALWAYS have the main aggro, no matter how hard I tried. In most cases, thats what we want.

■What makes playing your class more fun?

Having multiple ranged options when it comes to shots. I love having a slowing shot, a "damaging" shot, a trap shot, a filler shot, and I'd like to see more shots with utilities. Shot that stuns the target (ex: petrifies him) on a long CD, a shot that pushes back the target, etc.

■What makes playing your class less fun?

When your pet dies, you're done, in a lot of situations (too much). Maybe give us a temporary boost when your pet dies so you can at least make a last stand or something. Hunters already have a non-stop rotation with no free GCDs & a boatload of buttons. Sometimes we just can't always be managing our pets, which influences a lot the tides of a fight for the amount of dmg it does.

■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

It's good, but I'd like to see, if possible, a free GCD every 10 seconds or so, a bit like Enh Shamans & Death Knights hit one every once in a while. Hunters need to control their pets, lay down traps, etc, and it takes our dps time to do it while some other classes just sit there auto attacking (doing practically nothing more). We need to stop our dps and do what we're asked, then provide even more dps to compete in meters.

■What’s on your wish list for your class?

Dual-weilding ranged weapons. Two guns, crossbows, or such. For bows it might be hard to do, but maybe try to see it like the Fury Warrior talent, but where guns could be dual weilded (faster aspd, etc), and bow/crossbows/2h gun used with more effiency (hit harder), just like Arms vs Fury.

Also, maybe a situational big cooldown where it would negate the minimum range effects for 10 seconds or so. A bit like Spiritwalker's Grace, but for minimum range. Every once in a while, for instance in arenas, we could shoot in melee for those times where we just can't escape (or do anything). It would be a long cooldown so not spammable, but I can see this ability being useful in both PvE & PvP.

That would be plain and simple fun.

■What spells do you use the least?

Widow Venom, I didn't even remember it existed. Even at 30% reduced healing I'm not even sure I'd use it. It takes a GCD, focus, what's the point, really?

  • What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]


    • Both.

  • If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]


    • Other (questing, 5-mans), but I also used to raid every now and then.

  • If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]


    • BGs. I have not gotten into Arena yet.

  • What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


    • Pet pathing when jumping off of cliffs, or sending flying pets to tag flying mobs (they still act as if they were grounded). Too many keybinds needed to access all abilities, such as debuff shots (Tranquilizing Shot, Widow Venom) and traps, leading to many of them not getting used. Damage traps and Survival shots not scaling properly.

  • What makes playing your class more fun?


    • Pet management.

  • What makes playing your class less fun?


    • Focus regeneration is too slow at the moment, requiring me to break form frequently just to Steady Shot. This is pretty evident especially in dungeons/raids where caster classes can often outpace us due to this resource limitation.

  • How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


    • The way Focus is currently implemented makes rotations very hard to manage. Certain abilities take a huge chunk out of the resource bar too quickly that we are forced to stagger the rotation about half of the time before it comes back to acceptable levels. Aimed Shot is a case in point: a non-hasted Aimed Shot keeps me locked down for 2.9 seconds with my auto attack turned off, and takes half of my resource bar away in one go.

    This leads to scenarios in which I am forced to a) run and try to do countermeasures in PvP, all the while waiting for my Focus to get back on track, which leaves me a bit more vulnerable (this is why my PvP spec is BM as it tends to be more focus efficient than the other two), or b) spam Steady Shots in PvE until I have enough focus again, by which time other classes have already worn the targets down, if not outright killed them, just because I waited for my Focus to regenerate.

    In PvE, this often leads to scenarios in which I find my Steady Shot getting interrupted just because the target was already dead, leaving me resource-starved for the next pull and forcing me to play a bit more conservatively. But when I play more conservatively, I lose a bit more damage and mess up my rotation in the process, or in PvP, leave an opening for the other player to outburst and possibly kill me. Now there ARE talents to try and mitigate this, including Rapid Recuperation and all, but often we don't get that killing blow because of the above, and not all Hunters have access to Rapid Recuperation.

  • What’s on your wish list for your class?


    • Please work out the Focus bar more. This change alone was enough to throw the Hunter class out of whack. My suggestion is to balance the Hunter against the Rogue in terms of resource usage and regeneration, since those are the two classes closest to each other in that respect.

    More consolidated and efficient abilities. We really need a bit less keybinds.

    There have also been sentiments floating around that the Hunter class is currently having an identity crisis. Are we DPS? What kind of DPS, burst or sustained? Are we a CC and threat management class? This is a combination of all of the above factors which have led to scenarios like the hypothetical one described in Ghostcrawler's latest blog entry on class mechanic changes. I believe that this is actually caused by the emphasis on "game balance" which the WoW development team has been trying to address for the longest time, but have recently been stepping up.

    My suggestion? Focus the Hunter class more. Give us a certain flavor and definite duties so that people won't push us out of 5-mans, raids or RBGs just because other classes can do the same jobs we do so much better. I personally believe that when this is finally solved, most of the problems we have been seeing will be addressed. Now there will be complaints, to be sure. We can't please everybody. But I'd rather that the Hunter class be focused rather than "balanced".

    Also, please remove or change Counterattack. Many hunters have been complaining about this ability as it is the single most useless ability in all the Hunter trees, excluding probably Pathfinding in the BM tree which nobody takes either.
