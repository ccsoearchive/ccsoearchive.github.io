Q: What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

A: Both.

Q: If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

A: All PvE aspects, albeit "casually", I tend to be a couple tiers behind in experiencing raid content.

Q: If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

A: BG's - but as with PvE, "casually" - 22K lifetime HK's, but I'm familiar with all the Battlegrounds.

Q: What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

A:
- Pet pathing/AI improvements.
- Pet-spawning with full (or previous) health.
- Remove minimum range.
- Auto-dismiss on summoning an alternative pet.
- Shorter/instant cast for Revive Pet.
- Ability to summon an alternative pet without reviving a deceased pet first.
- More stable space ;)

Q: What makes playing your class less fun?

A: PvP - Lack of sufficient gap-openers relative to the available number (or frequency of) gap-closers/CC's used by other classes ... or, minimum range restriction - if we're in melee range of anything (melee or casters) we're getting pummeled regardless, despite wearing mail we have few damage mitigation abilities - at lease we can do some damage while we're being beat on (instead of the current <best-case> scenario where we're BM and our pet is active, and then we're still only doing 30% damage at best).
PvE - Pet pathing/behavior issues. BM gear scaling.

Q: How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

A: We're sill hanging on to the 'caster' Hunter legacy in that Cobra/Steady Shot spam dominates much of the 'rotation' and we're Almost always 'casting' those. Instead of a 2 second cast, an instant-cast CS/SS with a 2 second CD - then we'd feel more like Rogues/DK's/Ferals - which is what we were led to believe the transition to the Focus system was supposed to achieve.

Q: What’s on your wish list for your class?

A:
Better Beast Mastery scaling with equipment level.

Interrupts -
MM - Shorter CD for Silencing Shot (6/10 seconds), i.o.w. parity with other class' interrupts
BM - Any true interrupt not bound to specific pet families (e.g. Nether Shock/Serenity/Pummel), perhaps [pet] talented, and with a <much> shorter CD - again on par with other interrupters.

Kill Command - Make this a pet buff again, have the buff consumed when the pet is in range to attack. The same attack effect as it exists as of 4.2 can be applied, but would not be consumed until the pet is on target - this would mitigate some of the effects of less than ideal pet pathing/positioning on movement fights. The buff could potentially allow multiple stacks for burst damage purposes, consumed either sequentially on multiple attacks, or as a multiplier to a single attack.

Traps - No arming time. The arming delay seriously diminishes the CC utility of a traps for dynamic situations (i.o.w. anything after an initial pull in PvE, or any time at all in PvP).

Trap Launcher - Have this launch a trap at the current target's location (if a target is selected, otherwise fall back to the targeting circle).

Multi-shot - Make this analogous to Fan of Knives, a point-blank 360-degree AoE spread of bullets/arrows not requiring a target. If a target is selected, retain the effect, but locate the center of the AoE at the target's location.

Aspects - should be switchable during cast/channeled actions (ex. AiS, CS/SS).

Aspects - "Hunter-only" aspect (a new "Aspect of the Beast") - Perhaps BM-specific for pet non-friendly encounters. "Merge" with pet (limited duration?, pet is dismissed) for an attack power bonus, a pet-specific buff and/or additional "pet-like" abilities (yes, it smacks of Druid-like shapeshifting). The buff could vary based on pet family and make use of existing pet skills/abilities or variations:
- Wind Serpent : on-hit lightning damage
- Spirit Beast : on-use heal (similar to Spirit Mend)
- Turtle : stamina buff, active damage shield [mitigation] ability

#What makes playing your class more fun?

More emphasis on the three aspects of our class (pets, shooting, traps)

I like the idea of hunters having to kite and not being able to shoot in their dead zone.

Feeling like there are always ways to improve your gameplay. In some situations currently in pvp I feel that I am severely limited in the ways I can help allies.

#What makes playing your class less fun?

Kiting a target forever but dying from dots or massive dmg that a class does to you from range or within the few seconds the target touches you.

The new focus system has brought good things and bad things. For example focus is really nice for pve, but in pvp it has put hunters in a bad place. Attack speed slows on our steady shots can cripple hunters damage and focus regen.

Dalaran Sewers is the bane of the hunters existence. It is one of the worst maps for hunters and often it means a win vs a hunter if you happen to play them on the map. What I am confused about is in the loading screen for dalaran sewers there is a stair case on the corner where the boxes are. I think adding this into the game would allow hunters room to move behind the boxes without being forced to run laps to the other stairs.

#How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The rotation has moved more toward rogues in pvp, where you want to pool your focus in order to do maximum burst. I think this idea is fine, but you have to really have burst or cool downs to produce the burst needed to help your team mates bring down the target. Hunters can do a lot of burst but we have to crit. So maybe a short duration crit bonus ability would help.

#What’s on your wish list for your class?

With other classes all having some form of glyph to get cc out whether the target has dots or not I think brings down hunters ability to work with certain comps. I would like to see this eliminated from the game all together or add something in for hunters as well. ie Glyph of scatter shot: removes all dots on the target when used while trap launcher is active. Since scatter shot is almost required to get traps off.
or
Bring back freezing arrow to be a targetable CC and change scatter shot to a stun. This would bring a lot of versatility to hunters.

Trap cooldown: I would like to see freezing and ice trap taken off the same cool down. Being forced to either CC and sacrifice our ability to maintain distance on our target, or sacrifice our CC to keep our distance on our targets is something that weakens hunters a lot.

Counter attack redesign - This ability is horrible now that deterrence doesn't even allow you to parry anymore. I would like to see this ability gone in place of something else or changed into a cool down based ability.

Pet rez: Is nearly impossible to get off in most pvp situations. Bring it more in line with other pet rezzing mechanics.

Camo change: change camo into a defensive ability or something more useful that what it currently is. I had high hopes for this ability but was let down substantially.

Defensive/healing: Hunters lost all of their dodge with the aspect of the hawk change, but were given nothing in return for this defensively. I would like to see some form of additional survival via baseline defense or heal.

#What spells do you use the least?

Widow venom, Aspect of the wild, and wing clip.

What type of content do you focus on?
- PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE?

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

- Minimum Range is always an issue, while in new content this has been largely addressed, it still represents the major reason I find questing to be less fun on my hunter than alts, I have to always worry about range and pet threat. If removing minimum range isn't on the table, at least making melee somewhat useful, even well geared raptor strikes hit for a pathetic amount. Hunter shouldn't be a melee class, but dealing with adds that get close is the least fun part of the class.

- BM pet stabling! I needed to make room in my stables recently but as I am currently specced into MM and SV, I couldn't abandon any BM pets!

What makes playing your class more fun?

- Procs that are pure rewards. The free aimed shot off of master marksman is great, it's a pure reward proc that allows you to stay in a rhythm while feeling like something positive happened. Lock and Load is the opposite, more often than not I'm in the middle of dumping focus when a LnL procs and I waste half of the proc on arcane shots, it changes the rotation when the proc happens (not necessarily a bad thing) but it's so much less predictable than the marks proc. For example on Heroic Alysrazor I like to charge a few master marksman stacks on the casting adds before the meteor comes down.

- Pet variety, I like rare spawn pets that are usable by specs other than BM, the 4.3 taming challenges were fun, but I'd like a greater variety of model MORE than taming challenges, for me the fun part isn't the tame itself but having a cool unique pet. Give hunters a reason to go back and check out some zones and adding a rare spawn wouldn't have to change questlines etc.


What makes playing your class less fun?

- Pet issues are always frustrating, please bring back the old defensive stance! I also miss aggressive stance for the few times I do battlegrounds or for doing archaeology. Assist stance is nice, but I wish it had been an addition rather than a replacement.

- Trap launching needs to be improved, it's become a core part of the expectations groups have of hunters, but it is proportionately much more difficult than other CC's in terms of execution due to the travel time and then arming time of the traps. Perhaps in addition to the targeting circle you could make it a targeted ability as well, if a walking add is targeted and you launched a freezing trap, it would automatically freeze the targeted add like any other ranged CC. Also I would LOVE the ability to macro my traps into launcher to make a one button launchable trap instead of arm launcher then select trap.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

- Focus makes rotations really tricky, for example practicing marks rotation on the dummy is really misleading for many reasons, no haste buff can totally change it, and you get an unrealistic representation of your performance due to careful aim. I wish the focus bar would show predictive gains of focus from shots, much like how you can see predictive health gains on your health bar while a heal is being cast on you. Focus seems to be a more punishing resource system than mana as well, it's very easy to focus starve yourself in an attempt to maximize damage delaying your major shot by even a few seconds each cooldown resulting in substantially lower dps.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Some sort of change to the focus system, perhaps making bigger shots cost less but recharging shots regenerate less as well, basically making the system more predictable for lower level players, casters looking at a mana bar can get an idea of how the next 30+ seconds will play out for them and plan abilities accordingly, and rage/energy classes are more moment to moment based but can't starve themselves for extended periods the same way a novice hunter might.

- Disengage out of combat! Nuff said.

- More definitive rules for Deterrence, it always seems to be a strange trial and error game with deterrence, some boss abilities seem to work, some don't, why?

- Consistency with Feign Death, with Mass Rez it's become an invaluable tool to decrease time between attempts, but some boss mechanics still totally ignore it, like rhyo's volcano blasts or the alys hatchlings and it seems like it's a bit inconsistent when it works and when it doesn't.

What spells do you use the least?

- While all of the spells have some use, Master's Call, Tranq Shot, and Scare Beast probably get the least use. Raptor strike made a big comeback with the glyph.

I'm just going to skip to the rotation section because it covers a lot of these questions:

The idea of focus and a "finisher shot" (chimera/explosive/kill command) was a great idea to make the hunter's rotation more dynamic. I came up with the term finisher shot because they have high focus costs, high cooldown, and do high damage. These finisher shots are the basis of the rotations because you need to use them as soon as they come off cooldown. What you do inbetween the cooldowns is a balancing act which makes the rotation have a great dynamic. Do i use a shot to generate focus, or do i think i will have enough focus to use my finisher when its off cd so i can do a focus dump shot instead. Throw in procs to this mix, and you have one of the most dynamic rotations out there. However, it only really worked well with MM's finisher, Chimera shot. Chimera shot is powerful enough that i feel like a i am spending 9 seconds building up to this finisher and then boom the big number pops up. I played a balancing game for 9 seconds- use steady to have eough focus for chimera when it comes off cd or dump focus into arcane, and the payoff is seeing that big number. Then you throw MMM procs and kill shots into the mix, and it becomes even more dynamic.

BM and SV simply didnt have that excitement. Explosive just isnt explosive enough to seem worth all those cobras I just did. Plus, the cooldown is 4 seconds shorter than chimera, yet it has the same focus cost. There is no decision to focus dump vs. focus regen, you are forced to focus regen all the time. The same issues plague BM and kill command.

imo, heres what we should see in our adorable panda-filled expansion:
Intimidation should be baseline (pets become more useful in pvp) and switched with kill command. Aimed shot should be baseline* and replaced with with chimera shot. kill command and explosive shot should have their cooldowns increased and their damage increased. That way every specialization gets their "finisher" ability as the first ability in the tree and these finishers become more fleshed out as the powerhouses behind the spec.

*Aimed shot is not punishing enough for the amount of effort it takes to pull off (half your focus and 3 second cast). also, the long cast time and the fact that you can't move just makes it an oddball in terms of the regular rotation. Give it a long cooldown so you can't just spam it in pve but increase the damage so it can be a significant threat in pvp if you can pull it off
