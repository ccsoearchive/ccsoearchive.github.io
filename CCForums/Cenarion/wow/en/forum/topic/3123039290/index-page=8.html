What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Mostly PvE, but PvP occasionally

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics, Raids, taming pets, achievements

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
BGs, although I would like to do Rated BGs if our guild could get a group together to do it

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
I absolutely hate the minimum range for hunters. It makes playing at times 'not fun' and is always frustrating. I love pets - almost all pets - and would love to have some mechanic to have stable slots store each type of pet. For instance, I have all the spirits beasts and would like for all of them to only take one stable slot, with maybe a expandable list for that slot to pick which one to use. I love a lot of cats, etc. Maybe this wouldn't be worth it for most, but pets are what make me happy playing WoW. Also, it's too easy to run out of focus using muli-shot, especially doing lower content. If the mob dies too fast, there's no way to regen focus, which is very frustrating. I would love to have a better way to aoe in those situations.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Pets!! :) I also enjoy being high on the dps meters. I want to have the chance to top the meters if I play correctly.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Minimum range!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I don't like the MM rotation right now of having to hard cast aimed shot for the first 10% of a boss fight. It doesn't feel right to not be able to take advantage of artisan quiver while using aimed shot, and the long cast time is just a pain to use. I feel that the serpent sting can fall off too easily. It would be nice to have a little more time. I enjoy playing SV but would love to have a bigger dps shot included somewhere in the rotation. I always feel underpowered playing SV, although overall dps is good, so that makes it 'not fun' to play. During burn phases there doesn't seem to be much to do to push dps. I would love to play BM so that I could use my spirit beasts, but always go with the highest dps specs, both to help the guild in raids but also to try to personally top the dps meters.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Some way of being able to tame more pets/pet skins. I LOVE pets!! :) Having 25 pets slots was huge, but I want more! Also, please, please, please do away with minimum range. Also, I would love to be able to change guns into bows, since I hate to loud gun sound. Overall, most of the changes for hunters over the past few years have made the game more fun to play. It would be nice to figure out some way to make the trap launcher work more easily. I have a hard time fitting all the skills on my bars, and some skills I'm slow just because I can't access them quickly, or find them on my bars fast enough.

What spells do you use the least?
The melee skills.

What type of content do you focus on? [Both]

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Raids, heroics only at the start of the xpac to get gear for raids or when people ask me to dps for bear mounts etc.]

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, I do BGs on other characters]

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Pets... having the pets follow you around while mounted and then despawning when they fall behind if you get into combat, simply make them travel at mount speed regardless of being in or out of combat. This is not a huge issue for hunters since it's an instant resummon, but for other classes such as warlocks, they could soulburn summon... but it really is a pain. Other classes such as unholy dk's and frost mages have cd's on their summons and they may be on cd which can happen when doing dailies on pvp servers.

Hunter mobility relies too heavily on pets in PVP, one of the main strats for arenas is to cc the healer, kill the hunter pet, then train the hunter. Without the pet, the hunter loses 2 means of getting out of melee range(if using a spider or some other root/stun pet). The argument is to use a pet with a 10min cd instant pet res, but I believe all of those only have 1 means of getting out of melee range (masters call). This wouldn't be as big of an issue if it wasn't for the 10sec cast on pet res without haste, and the fact that it is 1 of 2 interruptable spells(that I know of, the other being to catch a pet).

What makes playing your class more fun? It's very unique being the only physical ranged class, also the class has a lot of utility in raiding environments.

What makes playing your class less fun?
5 yard dead zone(I realize the dead zone is in reference to the old 5-10yards, but who honestly believes that a hunter does anything useful in his 5 yard dead zone that couldn't be done better at range?) The 5 yard dead zone results in a lot of hunter hugging in pvp, we get lots of love from our enemies but none from blizzard in terms of our 5yard useless zone. I have played other classes in pvp v. hunters, they attack me, I run into their dead zone, slow them, and proceed to kill them. Even as a caster, getting into a hunter's 5 yard dead zone usually results in winning the game.

The dead zone is an interesting concept, but in reality it proves to be very limited in usefulness to get your point across that the class is using a bow/gun/crossbow and thus they "theoretically" should have a tough time shooting someone point blank range. Has anyone in blizzard seen the Lord of the Rings series, Legolas totally owned face by strait up stabbing orcs in the face with his arrows if they got too close, or at least was still able to shoot until about 3 yards.

Besides that, lag/latency (I play with I believe 50ms wired connection, fiber optics internet) plays a huge role into the limiting factor of the 5yard dead zone. Playing as a hunter, sometimes you run into situations where you are 10-15 yards away from someone, kiting them through a frost trap and they (as a melee class) are hitting you, but you cannot hit them(I have seen warriors doing this, they then take 1 step backwards, sometimes half a step back from being able to attack me and are able to charge which I believe is 10yards+). The reverse happens as well where as a melee you may or may not be hitting the hunter within the 5 yard dead zone, while he is still attacking you. Granted the second situation is not an issue that needs to be addressed, it was simply an example of latency at work. The first example is very demoralizing to hunters, when they play right kiting and getting to range and still not being able to attack.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
the rotation is fine for PVE
For PVP, I feel like marks is less than what it should be in terms of potency when dealing with melee. Hunters cannot hard cast aimed shot, I'm ok with that, but having a proc chance one steady shot that has to get to 5 stacks in order to use aimed shot isn't that amazing when you cannot cast a steady shot due to being loved(hugged) by the enemy team. As survival, I have played games where I have received so much love from the enemy team that my serpent sting fell off(did not get refreshed once) of my main target because I could not cobra shot it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Losing the 5yard dead zone. Fixing hunter mobility in terms of relying on pets.

What spells do you use the least?
Widows venom, there's others but I can't think of them at the moment, since I don't use them too often.

I do unrated BGs. When my boyfriend hits 85 we'll do arenas.

4. What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
--- Too many keybindings. As a marks hunters I have to have the following abilities available to me at all times: Hunters Mark with pet attack macro (because having pet attack used separately is annoying), Silencing Shot, Kill Shot, Mend Pet, Readiness, Rapid Fire, Master's Call, Freezing Trap, Frost Trap, Trap Launcher, Steady Shot, Serpent Sting, Chimera Shot, Aimed Shot, Arcane Shot, Disengage, Deterrence, Feign Death, Camo, flying mount, ground mount, and my racial (in this case Shadowmeld). I don't have enough keybindings nor fingers to fit the last two traps. That's 25 keybindings total, 20 if you don't include mounts, the last two traps, and camo.

Compared to my other most commonly played classes, my mage, who only has 8 keybindings, paladin who only has 9, and priest who has 7, that's an absurd number of keybindings. I'd love it if we could find a way to consolidate abilities.

--- Serpent Sting seems useless. I completely understand the mechanics behind it, but I apply it just to get a sweet damage buff when I use Chimera. Why not make it like the paladin level 85 ability and give you a nice buff depending on your spec? BM could have a % increase in pet damage, marks could have a % increase in chimera damage, and SV could have... something. (I don't know, I don't play SV at all.) All of these buffs would be activated by the spec's specialty shot so it's not a constant thing.

--- I hate how focus regen is linked to two abilities. It should regenerate like energy or runic power.

--- Min. range. We should be able to deal ranged damage from zero to 40 yards. Since hunters are not meleers, we shouldn't have any melee abilities. Trust me, we'll still try and run away because we're still squishy in mail.

--- Allow us to choose our pet's spec. I'd love to have my pet nether ray out more often, but he's so squishy compared to my cat and scorpion.

--- Daze on Pack/Cheetah. If we have it then druids should have it on travel form. Since they don't have it, we shouldn't either.

--- The new pet bar is odd. I preferred the old method of passive, defensive, and aggressive.

5. What makes playing your class more fun?
--- I enjoy playing the class because I know the inside and outs of my spec. I love to kite things around, being able to see the whole fight without having my face in the boss's crotch/butt, and I take advantage of having a pet (let them fend off mobs while I mine, for instance). I enjoy being able to save the healer with a well placed trap, distract mobs and keep them away from people, and solo old content.

6. What makes playing your class less fun?
--- Pretty much everything I stated in the quality of life question.

7. How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
-- It's fine, I just wish that steady wasn't required for focus regen. I'm fine with it being related to a haste buff, but I hate the focus regen aspect of it.

8. What’s on your wish list for your class?
--- A dual pet ability on a medium length cooldown that lets you have two pets out at once for a short time.

--- No melee range.

--- Hunters being able to effectively tank using their pet and special shots dedicated to tanking. (I'm aware this is a pipe dream, but that's why I love my pally, and I'd play my hunter a lot more if she could tank.)

--- Removal of useless skills (Eyes of the beast, eagle eye, beast lore, raptor strike, etc.)

--- Ability to choose the spec of our pet.

--- Let us see our ranged weapon on our back instead of our melee weapons.

--- Consolidation of skills.

9. What spells do you use the least?
Beast Lore (do we still have that?), Eye of the beast, that venom shot that I can't remember, Eagle Eye... I'm sure there's more but I've forgotten.

Marksmanship: Hunter's Mark-Serpent Sting-Rapid Fire-Aimed Shot-Steady Shot until the boss reaches below 90% HP (Make sure to refresh Chimera Shot throughout this rotation). I play a marks hunter in Firelands normal modes (hopefully moving into heroics soon, the guild’s pretty casual). I pvp casually (read: once a month, if that), and avoid arenas like the plague.

I agree that the pet bar bug can be annoying, but in terms of quality of life issues, my biggest concern, particularly for marks, is how gearing up, based on the availability of stats in the current tier, can drastically impact your rotation and, ultimately, your DPS. I feel like with every other upgrade I’m trying to siphon off haste without dropping below the hit cap (or stacking a useless amount of hit above it) and juggling my chimaera shot glyph in order to reconstruct some semblance of a rotation. If I have too much haste, I do less dps due to the way chim shot won’t line up with the necessary arcane shot/steadyx2 in order to keep up improved steady shot and dump excess focus.

Granted, the more I play at these high haste levels, the easier it gets, but adjusting to every upgrade initially feels like a downgrade. I understand that the class design philosophy is intentionally drifting toward a priority cycle, but it feels at times as if the priority becomes harder to reach based on how much (or how little) haste you have. Others may not agree, but I’ve played a lot of specs and roles this expansion (I have 5 other 85s that all have dual specs that see regular play) but I feel like no other class is as drastically impacted by individual gear changes as the marks hunter.

One minor gripe, I don’t like resummoning my pet every time I die in pve, especially if the pet doesn’t die. I get that it feels a little less interactive and more of a third weapon type if the pet is just there, all the time, but after 15-20 Rag wipes in a given night, that’s one button I’m really tired of hitting.

I would like BM to be a viable pve spec again, please. :) I love including my pet in a rotation, and being able to use the unique models only available to BM hunters.

While I’m thinking about it, give us a unique rare wolf model that isn’t a spirit beast!

Spells I use the least: immolation trap. Really have not found a situation in pve where it was useful. I know I technically have serpent sting up the entire fight, but with chim shot I only actually cast it once. So… it’s not a spell I ‘use’ regularly. Otherwise I’m pretty happy with the array of abilities used for my particular spec, and I feel like there’s a variety based on how much health the boss has, if there are adds out, and if I’m kiting anything.

I might note that overall I am very happy with my chosen class, even though I haven't been playing it very long in the grand scheme of things.
