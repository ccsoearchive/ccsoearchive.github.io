What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raid, heroics, you name it.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

RBGs, Arena, Random BG's.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

By far, the most invigorating thing a Hunter faces currently is the 5 yard dead zone( call it dead zone because you DPS literally decimates). Just about all of the new Cataclysm fights require Hunters to either be in the bosses hit box, or in fact, the mobs (Bosses, adds etc) themselves chase after us. For example, lets take the Shannox encounter. The only thing that remains 100% shoot able for hunter this whole fight guaranteed is Shannox ,however this is no way viable. In most cases we need to be on the dog at one time or another and when the dung hits the fan here comes the dog. We can no longer hit the dog (although from earlier post some still melee him? O_o), yes we could simply swap targets, but there goes 3-4 seconds of raw DPS. Including the fact that we would be on a target that is not the primary kill target further decreases our usefulness. This is only for PVE. For PVP, we are talking about a whole new ballpark. Hunters are one of the least represented classes in RBGs and Arenas. This is largely due to the fact that just about anyone can abuse our dead zone in many ways. They can snare us and stand in our hit box, making it so we have to become melee hunters for 5+ seconds while they stand there and cast in our faces or mow us down with that big hammer. For example, in arena I spend probably 70-80% of the game chasing someone who is LOSing my damage or sitting there and looking pretty while I am snared. Masters Call doesn't really counter this as it is on a 45 second cooldown and last for less then 8, compared to say Mages who can use their snare ample times by the time I could use my Masters Call again(Disengage also does little because I can easily be snared again and then can be on my toes within 2-4 seconds as I fly only 10-15 yards). Also you have to consider the fact that the only slow we have, (not including frost trap because 90% of the time freezing is used for CC) can be countered by just about any class besides a warrior. You could argue that Chimera Shot, when talented does automatically slow the target. This is correct to an extend. The slow lasts less than half the cooldown of the shot and only applies if it goes off successfully. Successfully meaning doing damage because when it is absorbed the slow will not apply at all.Yes sorry for the big rant but I think this 5 yard dead zone is very unfair to hunters as we have little to no abilities to use in melee range or dispatch from the combat and being at ranged in imperative to our DPS.

What makes playing your class more fun?

The wide array of hunters perk abilities ( E.G. Silencing Shot, Concussive barrage etc.) make us more of a tactician based class and make PVE content more enjoyable.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The above rant :s^

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Each tree of the Hunter class offers a different feel to the rotation, each also having more room for error than the other. MM is enjoyable to me because there is little room for mistakes and when played well pulls some pretty numbers.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

All I want for Christmas is my dead zone gone :).

What spells do you use the least?

Widows venom seems weak as other classes apply it automatically (Mortal Strike for example). Immolation trap is seldom used as explosive does better DPS.

    What type of content do you focus on?


    Mostly PvE; questing in particular


    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.


    I have no concerns about the "quality-of-life" for Hunters. In fact, I think many times things have been made too simple. For example, I miss the sense of accomplishment and engagement involved with raising my pet's loyalty. Also, I feel ammo could be reintroduced as a means of enhancing our Auto-Attacks.


    What makes playing your class more fun?


    I particularly like the general pace of the class in PvE. When questing, I can easily prepare for combat and take things at my own pace; the class rewards thinking ahead and being proactive. With other classes I feel I have to keep on my toes and can't take as many steps to avoid or reduce danger.


    What makes playing your class less fun?


    Quest mechanics that aren't designed with pet classes in mind... For example, I remember a quest in Blade's Edge Mountains where you needed to get hit with a wind serpent's lightning. For other classes, you'd end up getting hit with it anyway; with my Hunter I had to go out of my way to make sure I was hit with the lightning and not my pet.


    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)


    Good. I really like to change to Focus. My only concern with the BM rotation is that it feels a bit too frantic; to get the most out of my character, I have to be constantly watching my Focus bar and the various procs.


    What’s on your wish list for your class?


    A few things I'd like to see:

    1.) More baseline abilities for generating focus. Focus is an interesting mechanic, but before talents, all you can use is Steady or Cobra shot to regain it. I feel a Hunter's means of regaining Focus is too dependent on their talent spec.

    An example of a new ability would be a sting that does less damage than Serpent Sting, but regenerates Focus with each tick.

    2.) More of an emphasis on the synergy between the Hunter and pet in the Beast Mastery talent tree. It's good now, but could use another interesting mechanic that would encourage players to better manage their pets. (Right now I feel I can just fire and forget with my pet.)

    3.) Some high focus cost ability (60-80 Focus) for the Beast Mastery talent tree. With Furor, Bestial Wrath, and other abilities, there's the potential to generate/converse a lot of Focus but nothing to take advantage of that fact.


    What spells do you use the least?


    I tend to use all of the abilities from time to time as needed. I do, however, tend not to use Concussive Shot or Frost Trap since - as a BM Hunter - I can keep targets on my pet.
