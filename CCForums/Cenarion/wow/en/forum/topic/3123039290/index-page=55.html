What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids mostly, some heroics as well. Still enjoy doing dailies and doing old quests, pet/mount collecting, and various achievements as well.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

The lack of any sort of self heals. Even something like gaining back health a little faster while in camo would be nice while soloing and remove the need to carry any non buff food.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I have enjoyed this class from the very beginning and it's still my main class. I enjoy the versatility the class brings (several buffs from pets, several abilities like tranq shot or traps that are useful to a raid), not to mention the simple fact of having a pet makes playing any other non pet classes feel odd to me at this point. The ability to output high amounts of damage while Misdirecting the resulting threat is nice too. The addition of camo is great although getting it a little earlier would be nice for leveling.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I'm still not totally sold on the focus system introduced for cata. Although I do enjoy not having to worry about "mana" anymore, I never feel like I have enough focus in fights that require the hunter to use AOE like multi-shot (beth or magmaw for instance). Minor gripe though, I'm not sure if there is a better option and I understand the need to avoid being able to spam AOE attacks.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I really like it short of the mentions above. Overall though it would be nice as an MM Hunter to have something other than arcane shot to use as filler.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Some sort of self heal even if only usable out of combat. I'd also like to see new traps even if they are only available within a specific spec (or simply glyphs to make traps looks different like scorpions instead of snakes, a magical cage instead of a freezing trap, etc). Lastly I'd love to see some ways to customize the look of pets or the addition of pet armor or something even if mostly cosmetic.

What spells do you use the least?

wing clip, scatter shot (aside from on cho'gal), widow venom (never used), aspect of the wild (used on nef, other than that never), cobra shot (never use). Many of these may be PvP abilities, but I rarely if ever use them in PvE.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Of the three pure dps classes, hunter has the smallest difference between their specs. You must always choose by which is performing best. With mages, warlocks, and rogues, the specs are different enough to merit some loyalty to one, despite minor dps disparities.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? It's one of the few specs in the game that very literally abides by a rotation instead of a priority list, and yet I enjoy it very much. Boss fights are exciting enough that they keep my attention over maximizing my dps.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
I want to be able to transmogrify pre-level 40 leather, as hunters do wear leather at that level range.

Also, remove the combat restriction from disengage, please. I have at times enjoyed the depth of that restriction, (for example jumping off the cliff down to mine, shooting someone to allow me to disengage before I die from the fall) but the limitation has frustrated far more times than not.

Also, distance conditional in macros. Then I could macro raptor strike with arcane shot, and wing clip with concussive shot, and ice trap with something else, etc.

What spells do you use the least?
aspects of the cheetah and pack - the daze restriction is cool, but it completely limits it from being viable in a raid setting other than running back to the bosses after a wipe. aspect of the wild - probably because there's just too much fire and shadow in this expansion, so there's no need to use it. eagle eye and beast lore - these are fine though, I wouldn't want it to change

aspect of the fox - okay, I DO use this, but the strategy is to use it as little as possible, which is a bit bizarre to me. I would appreciate it more if steady shot just simply did drastically less damage if you cast it while moving (since it's called "steady" shot, after all), and everything else did the same damage.

Basically, all the aspects just seem like arbitrary extra game mechanics with little depth involved, and I opt for their removal.

Widow venom - I literally have no need for this in a pve setting, and even if I did, it is very easily arguably the worst version of an MS debuff in the game.

Cobra shot - no surprise here - I'm marksman.

Raptor strike - because it is negligible damage in almost any environment.

Wing clip - I seriously don't see the purpose to this ability existing when you could just make concussive shot usable in melee range.

Scatter shot - I cannot off the top of my head think of a useful place to use this spell in a raiding environment. Anything you'd want to use it on is immune to it, and anything that is susceptible to it doesn't need to be disoriented. I would like to see this be an interrupt or something, since I can only interrupt once per add that spawns on Alysrazor.

A PVP fight for me is most fun when I have time to evaluate, prepare then engage.
