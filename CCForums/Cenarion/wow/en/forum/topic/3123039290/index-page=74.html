What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]: PVE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]: Raids, Heroics on alts.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

I'm frustrated with how throwing traps works. Having to target an area on the ground in a 25 man raid with spell effects flying everywhere can be difficult and it's easy to waste a GCD (or two!) doing so. I'll also sometimes find that I have selected a friendly player by accident and then I have to re-target the boss, all the while losing damage time.

I like to be able to easily drop a trap at my feet on the run so I need button space for both the regular traps and the trap thrower. There is no possible way I can fit macros for throwing each particular trap in my UI so I'm left with awkward combos like Q (trap thrower) - F12 (snake trap). In general we have too many spells that are frequently used. Traps are a particular problem but not the only culprit.

Minimum range is frustrating on some bosses. I hate H Ryolith purely for this reason; I'm constantly having to move around the edge just to be able to fire on him while everyone else is safe together getting AOE heals. I don't understand why some bosses are still like this since others can be fired on when sitting right on their !@#. The easy (and best) fix is to just take away the minimum range already! I promise we will still try to run away if you get in our face in PVP.

On the pro side, I love cauldrons and feasts! Mass rez is great too although I wish the debuff time was cut in half. Camouflage is awesome.

Not a hunter thing but frequent Rocket Fuel Leaks have ruined much of the enjoyment I once got from Engineering. :(


What makes playing your class more fun?

I like when I can stand still, blow all my cooldowns, and top the meters. I also like being useful for Misdirects or tricky pulls. Using Disengage is almost always fun. I like to drink an alchemist's potion next to a Moonkin and then Feign Death.


What makes playing your class less fun?

Boss fights where I'm constantly needing to move for some arbitrary reason - I mentioned H Ryolith, H Alysrazor is also kind of annoying. Any fight mechanic that kills pets. Huge boss hit boxes. Needing to completely stop DPS to use my defensive cooldown (Deterrence). Trying to Disengage uphill.


How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The Marksman rotation is pretty nice right now. I like that there is the option of using hard casted Aimed; it brings an element of skill to know when it's safe to do so. I thought ISS was an annoying mechanic when I first switched from Survival but I'm used to it now and it brings a nice rhythm to the rotation. I did enjoy the Survival rotation a bit more in early Cataclysm; not needing to put that half second pause into every lock and load really made that spec less stressful to play at a high level.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Fix Trap Thrower including a way to throw a trap at our target without clicking on the ground.
- Get rid of minimum range.
- Get rid of Counterattack already.
- Consolidate some spells so we don't have so many that need to be right under hand.

What spells do you use the least?

Aspect of the Wild (other classes cover this), Aspect of the Cheetah (use Pack instead), Beast Lore, Scare Beast, Widow Venom (other classes cover in their normal rotation), Immo trap (Explosive better).

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

The Trap launcher is clunky. Having to push two separate buttons and then a third time to fling the trap accross the screen eats up a lot of time in the middle of a pull. It's also unreliable unless you take more time to place the aim. This is nearly impossible if the target is on the move, for example the last pull before Nalorak. It's almost be better if all traps had the integrated ability to launch them. that'd cut down the buttons to smash at least by one. Besides, we're rangers, we should have the accuracy to fling a trap with our bare hands.

The pet AI is still a bit odd. I like how you have improved the hunter pet choices a little, but I do miss the old Aggressive, Passive, Defensive system, especially since the new Assist can make you pet run around doing nothing for excessive times in fights where you need to rapidly swap targets, e.g. Nefarion, Beth'tilac, Shannox. This leaves putting your pet on Passive and then manually controlling it the best option, though it adds a bit more hassle to your action bars.

Melee distance. I understand that the style of our class is to be pure ranged, however functioning in melee distance is nothing but painful. I'm aware we have tools to get back to range, like Disengage, but it's unreliable unless the area you are standing at is completely flat, and other classes, most have some sort of an ability to get back on your skin in a blink of an eye. Some boss fights are really tedious because of the min. range too. I don't hate the system, but I don't like it either. Our melee abilities, not counting Counter Attack, which is useless, is exactly 6, 4 of which are traps. Out of the two remaining Wing Clip is some what useful and Raptor strike is so laughable I don't even understand what's its purpose. It's a difficult issue, and I like the unique feeling it gives, but I think it needs some polishing.

I'm not also agreeing completely with at what level you learn some of your skills. As an example I'd like to bring out Misdirection. It's one of the most important skills a hunter needs, yet we only learn it as late as level 76. If I recall correctly, it was originally available at level 70. In a way I can understand having the ability of this caliber being saved as a treat, but considering how many times I run into people who don't know how to use it, I think it should be available earlier, just for sheer practise.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I like the general feel of hunters, relaxed and fluid both alone and in a group. I really enjoy the fact our pets were made more useable by allowing them to fill in for buffs. We hunters are just plain awesome.

I mostly play Surv. and MM. Surv. rotation in my opinion is great. It's fluid, efficient and fits the style of a Surv. hunter perfectly. MM hunter rotation is somehow a bit.. How to say.. Tacky? But it's doable.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

More variation to the pet talent trees. There aren't really many talent combinations you can come up with if you want to push out max dps or if you want to tank. You just pick up the cookie cutter talents each time and the rest in the tree just feel like they are there for show. And while all the pet families have their signature buff or ability they use, they are otherwise all similar with their talents if they are in the same pet group, e.g. Ferocity pets.

I'd also like if the pet models were more lively. I remember the old Wolf model who was howling, scratching its ear, sniffling, it was really cool. It felt like you had an actual pet walking next to you. The cats mainly just wave their heads from side to side and strech from time to time with an annoying sound. Outland and Northrend wolf models just stand there doing nothing. Same with raptors and ravagers and most other pets. It's more like you have a stuffed animal you're dragging along with you. It'd be fun if e.g. your pets would sometimes curl up and take a nap, if you're staying stationary for longer, or dogs would chase their tails, cats could go after rodents, etc. Some new pets have a bonus ability for Trick or Play: Something similar to that, but without having to push a button.

What spells do you use the least?

Counter Strike, Widow Venom, Raptor Strike, Wing Clip, Scare Beast, Eagle Eye.
