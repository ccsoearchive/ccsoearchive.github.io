What type of content do you focus on? PVE

If PvE, what type of PvE? Raids

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? Pet bugs and oversights. A lot of these bugs have been in forever (disappearing pet bar, pet suddenly forgetting what he's doing and standing around in the middle of the room) and i don't see them getting fixed, but I really feel like some of the pet killers in Cata raiding have just been unfair. Pet getting summoned at 6k HP and immediately getting wiped out by electrocute on Nef, pet dying to fire pools not properly flagged as AOE on Maloriak, or pet repeatedly dying to things like pathing through lava in Firelands leads to the incredibly not fun mechanic of rezzing your pet. I understand that pet rezzing has to be balanced for PVP, but can't you just put a cooldown on it instead of an 8 second cast time?

What makes playing your class more fun? Fights that reward me for understanding the mechanics of playing a hunter. Al'akir P1 was a blast for hunters, likewise Ragnaros is also a lot of fun for us, and Beth'tilac can be. Situations where creative disengage use or good pet management are rewarded always lead to more fun encounters for us. Any encounter we can use traps on is good times. For what it's worth, most of cata's encounters have been really friendly and rewarding for hunters.

What makes playing your class less fun? The hunter dead zone. (This mechanic really, really makes some fights a chore. Majordomo, Beth, Rhyolith and Baelroc can all have serious problems with the deadzone. Any point where I basically have to stop damaging the target when the rest of the raid gets to continue fighting is Not Fun.) Having to choose between completely hamstringing my dps to cast rez pet on a fight when I realize my pet died to something stupid (like electrocute on Nef) or completely hamstringing my dps by not rezzing my pet at all.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) - I am fairly satisfied with hunter rotations. The actual rotation of each spec is interesting and focus has given hunters a unique playstyle which is honestly very interesting and fun. If I had any disappointment about current rotations and playstyles, I feel that Survival and Marksman are essentially just mathematically different variations on the same spec. Some changes to Survival could make it much more interesting and fun - as it stands, right now there is simply no reason to play Survival over the mathematically superior Marksman, or the functionally unique Beast Mastery in a raid setting.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1. No more hunter dead zone, full stop. This is an outdated mechanic. Focus generation prevents us from spamming high damage instant shots up close. The dead zone simply serves to marginalize hunters in PVP and give raid leaders a headache in PVE when their hunters have to explain to them why they can't fully get into the stack on any fight where the raid has to cluster on the boss' butt. There is simply no reason for it to exist anymore
2. A hunter legendary. Thor'idal was in the hands of players for about a month before it was made utterly irrelevant by the Wrath launch. Additionally, the item was a drop, rather than an epic and engaging quest line. Melee DPS (warriors specifically) have now had 3 'hard' legendaries and casters have had two. I understand that asking for a legendary for a single class is asking a lot, but couldn't we have a quest line like what we saw in the ICC 5 mans with multiple different rewards?
3. An overhaul of Beast Mastery. BM feels contrary to the design direction of Cataclysm, to be honest. They are based largely in part on outdated mechanics, their signature ability is completely useless in the overwhelming majority of the content in the game, their end tree talent is a lousy passive linked off of another lousy passive, and the placement of certain abilities that are absolutely vital to the success of an endgame BM player come so late that many BM hunters never realize they're worth using. Bestial Wrath (and TBW) should come earlier in the tree and exotic pet mastery should be baseline. I think it would be better to swap BW with Intimidate, put TBW in as a T3 talent and overhaul the late parts of the talent tree. I understand that BM is an extremely strong spec in PVP (at least relative to the other hunter specs) but it really only has utility going for it in PVE now. Changes to the tree (and better availability of exotic pets) would lead to a lot fewer hunters in heroics and raids with maxed BM, no core hound or spirit beast and an inability to break 13k dps.
4. Flying pets. This is mostly a BM complaint, but it applies to marks and survival too - we are essentially losing 10-50% of our dps on any fight where our pet cannot reach them when we can. On most of these fights we can usually repurpose our pets to add management, but on fights like Atramedes where there is only one target, or Alysrazor where we are out of range of our pets and cannot issue commands efficiently, it just feels like we are being denied a feature of our class. I am not suggesting flying cats, but if a pet has wings, is it entirely unreasonable to expect it to make an aerial attack on a boss?
5. More pets! Gyreworms and hydras come to mind. Or those nifty core hounds with all the eyes.

What spells do you use the least?
1. Trap launcher - the focus cost on this ability makes it nearly useless. It is OK in 5 man heroics when you need to CC, but in a raid environment there is almost no reason to use it over simply walking to the location and dropping the trap.
2. Raptor Strike - this ability is embarrassing. Why remove volley and not this? The glyphed version would be useful if the actual damage reduction was meaningful (and didn't require you to be standing in melee range of the boss!)
3. Widow's Venom - the duration and low quality of the effect make this shot useless in pvp and pve
4. Intimidate (When BM specced) - this ability is unusable in PVE past 5 man normals. This is the signature ability for BM. This ability should not be this bad!
5. Dual Wield - there is no comparable enchant for dual wield hunters to the 2h agility enchant. This is a shame, because we've had to disenchant so many agility weapons and I really like the way dual wield looks.
6. Tranquilizing shot - Focus mechanics make using this ability hard. A major glyph that reduced the focus cost to 0 would make this ability a lot easier for us to use.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

At first I was on board for the transition to Focus, I thought to myself "This will fix the horrible downtime hunters have, and make us more mobile". Unfortunately at early levels Focus seems to hamstring us and wedge the downtime right in the middle of our rotation. At 85 Focus becomes far manageable, but early on common rotation is Arcane Shot dump, Steady Shot to recup, Arcane Shot dump, as you level add other abilities, primary shot for spec if Marks or Surv, Serpent Sting, but at the end of the day you're going to run out of Focus and start the tedious process of regaining it.

It's not that it kills DPS, nothing of the sort, it's just that it's boring, and at 85 Focus has shown that it can work, thanks to various talents and haste, but it should be just as feasible and flowing when at lower levels. This makes leveling Hunters not just restrictive, but out right boring.

I personally either would like to see improvements to the Focus system for lower levels, or a return to the days when we had Mana, and actually had a nice rotation where Arcane Shot wasn't a resource dump.

One more thing, I find our AoE still lacks as much as you try to emphasize our quasi-AoE abilities. I would really appreciate having Volley back. Everyone else gets a nice, easy to cast AoE, please do us the same justice.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Instant shots most definitely, anything that involves casting seems silly for a hunter. I can understand Aimed Shot and Steady Shot, but I feel these break up the flow of the hunter, which I think should operate more off of cooldowns on most shots and prioritizing what abilities should be used in order and when. I liked the addition of being able to shoot and run, although I had already mastered hunter movement to the point where that never restricted me from being able to wedge a shot in on a pursuing a opponent, player or otherwise.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Focus recuperation and cast shots to regain. Overall uninteresting leveling experience.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

It's a mix of the old with some changes to cater to Focus. It's okay, again my problem isn't with the rotation, but the resource.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I miss old Beast Mastery, it used to be a great leveling spec, but now has become nothing more but ostensibly spamming Kill Command when it's available, and wedging shots in. I feel that despite having three specs, there really isn't much separating the specs save a few special abilities and overall DPS. This is standard for a class that can do nothing but DPS, but I feel other DPS centric classes like Rogues have a lot of uniqueness between all three specs all the same. But my concerns may be a tad on the frivolous side, I don't know. A simplistic wishlist for me would be a funner leveling experience, and Volley. That's it.

What spells do you use the least?

Widow Venom. I'll use it in PVP every once in a while on self-healers, but most often than not it goes unused.
