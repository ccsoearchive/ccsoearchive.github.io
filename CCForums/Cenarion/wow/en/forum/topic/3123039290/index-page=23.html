What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE, I occasionally do PvP, but only as a means to further my PvE goals (pvping on tol barad to kill the boss there, for instance).

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Raids, i have raided since tbc (got up to kil'jaeden before the 3.0 nerf), gotten the first five pve proto drakes in WotLK, and killed Anub'arak on heroic 25 man. I got somewhat into heroic 25/10 content in cataclysm, but havent raided the firelands.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
As a Pve Raider, i fancy myself a strategist for my guild. The biggest quality-of-life issue for me is the range at which hunters are unable to use ranged attacks. For every boss strategy i employ, i need to create 'exceptions' for hunters if the strategy requires stacking up anywhere close to a boss. I know this mechanic is long-used one, but so were the ammo requirement and the pet happiness.

a second quality-of-life issue is one that's been long said on the hunter forums. Note i didnt use the word debate, because the issue is almost universally agreed upon to be a worthwhile change. This issue is the idea that our ranged weapons should be given an option to show on our back, either with our statsticks, or instead of. This is a purely artistic change, and would not affect mechanics in any way.

A third quality-of-life issue has to do with trap launcher. I am quite fond of this ability, even if many hunter's are less than enthralled with it. My only problem with it is that every so often, usually once or more on a fight with heavy trapping components (maloriak, magmaw, nefarian, etc), trap launcer will randomly lock itself out for a few seconds. These few seconds are, while not necessarily game breaking, extremely frustrating to deal with. They are not consistent, and delay the use of important traps by an amount of time that can be significant. The issue occurs when i chain-trap adds, such as throwing a frost trap, then an explosive trap, then a snake trap (for entrapment).

What makes playing your class more fun?
I like the wide array of hunter abilities that give me a swiss-army type of feel. The dynamic pet stable adheres to this feel as well. I like to be useful in raids, and being able to bring many different things to the table is fun, to me.

What makes playing your class less fun?
As a class with so many abilities, being able to do.. nothing, is not fun at all. Feeling Useless is the epitome of un-fun, to me.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
the survival and Marksmanship rotations are sufficiently dynamic and interesting to use in raiding environments. The MM rotation is very fun to execute, having three distinct stages in which you need to use different rotations during a fight. The SV one is less engaging, but it is very fluid and feels right.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Making BM a viable raiding alternative (this requires finding a way to have pet uptime on fights with 'air' phases).
Giving Survival some sort of self heal.
Removing the daze component from aspect of the cheetah. (i believe there is enough of an opportunity cost NOT being in hawk or fox now to warrant this change)
Giving some sort of cast time reduction to revive pet, and/or reviving/spawning pets with more % of maximum hp.
Allowing survival to scale with weapon damage so as to stay competitive in dps.
Working aspect of the wild into either another aspect, or buffing it to provide the nature elemental resistances. (or both?)



What spells do you use the least?
Widow venom, aspect of the wild, scare beast, Eagle eye, immolation trap.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Being able to tame incredibly cool new pets and play in a way that no other class in the game offers. Also being able to bring buffs that the group might be missing, like Kings or Heroism. All the stuff I said above. Instead of 'Surv or GTFO' like it was in Wrath, now its 'Surv or Marks or GTFO' in Catac. Being forced to play 1 of 2 specs I don't enjoy = not fun.

Doing a fight like Ozumat in Throne of the Tides, only to have it completely LOS my pet and eventually despawn.

My interest in my hunter is all but gone because of the combination of these sorts of things.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Rotation/efficiency feel fine, until something moves out of range of my pet, or causes my pet to despawn, or spams my pets with knockbacks, etc etc etc.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

No longer being the only class in the game that is punished by having a target in melee range. A ranged weapon is MORE accurate at close range, not less. I read the post that said Blizz is afraid of hunters having melee AND ranged attack options in melee range - ok, np. Get rid of the mostly useless melee attacks and just let us shoot. Problem solved.

>>Being able to choose the talent tree our pets use<< (Maybe I'd like a ferocity scorp or a spider that tanks!). Being forced to use whichever one they already are takes a great deal of the fun out of them having talent trees in the first place. This is a big, big deal for me. I have pets that I am extremely attached to that have no use at all in a raid or in most dungeon situations. Because they happen to be tanking pets.

Choice is good. 4.3 proves more than ever that Blizzard gets that, hence Transmogrification.

BM spec getting a shot that shares a cooldown with KC, that we can use when a mob is out of range of the pet. There are just too many things that interfere with the pet reliably attacking a mob in many encounters, and the AI is too simplistic to compensate.

In general, make BM spec engaging and a truly viable alternative to Marks and Surv, instead of the spec that is ok some of the time. I don't like the playstyle of Surv or Marks and yet I feel like I'm gimping my raid if I play as BM. That.Isn't.Fun.

Pets once again made immune to knockbacks and other silliness, like Blizz did in Wrath for fights like Onyxia.

Trap Launcher off the GCD. Huge QoL improvement right there.

What spells do you use the least?

Hm, can't think of many I don't use. Some are situational, like healing debuff sting, but they still get their use.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? I think minimum range is probably the biggest one. Its not world-ending problem but I think it causes some issues, and is probably the major reason for the high skill requirements called for in rated hunter PvP. And even from a common sense perspective, it doesn't make sense. Wouldn't a gun (or even a bow) do just as well if fired right in some bad guy's face? Some other issues I personally find are pets dieing from certain mechanics in boss fights or trash (a lot of firelands trash have this issue) where they should be taking reduced damage or something, and maybe Feign Death failing sometimes. I mean I understand it, as its such a powerful threat drop on such a short cooldown, that there should be some risk involved, but I think that most other classes have received threat drops with little to no risk involved. It just seems a bit outdated, but I could be wrong. Oh, and buggy pet following, where your pet will sometimes get stuck instead of following you when traveling difficult terrain. A prime example is the Beth'ilac fight in Firelands, where your pet will stay on the web if you go up there on the first phase, for the entire fight.

What makes playing your class more fun? The idea of being a ranged death dealer is pretty cool. Having pets that are actual companions rather than slaves (I'm looking at you, warlocks) is also a fun part of the class experience. Play wise, I think having such a toolbox of abilities, a great capacity for damage that benefits from skill (especially when moving), and an involved resource system all make playing a hunter fun. Also, always being able to bring a buff! Makes raids love you more, good stuff.

What makes playing your class less fun? Mostly being excluded from certain duties or being kept from playing optimally because of minimum range. Baelroc in Firelands is a prime example of this. Also having no real emergency heal, and the halfhearted immunity that Deterrence gives us. Ice block or Divine Protection, Rogue Cloak, Warlock immunity all stop at the very least DoT effects and AoE most of the time, while Deterrence can't save us from an AoE or a DoT already on us. Better than nothing I guess, but it only enhances the fragility hunters have, especially in PvP.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) I think the rotation and rotation varieties are great. I even like how one spec has a harder rotation and one has an easier one and then there is one in between! I think that the MM rotation could use a little leeway - I've seen longer ISS buff times to add an extra couple seconds of duration as a suggestion, which is a really good idea. And survival, while basically pretty smooth, has some random focus generation issues, especially with 4piece t-12. But overall a very good system.

What’s on your wish list for your class? Removing of the minimum range, of course. Maybe more pet varieties (ability to tame feral druids???) More guns. And removing of the racial buffs that make hunters (dwarves/trolls) be forced to use a certain weapon type, which can be a big problem when there are no viable weapons of a certain type available, like guns in patch 4.2. Oh, and dual wielding pistols or hand crossbows as weapons (maybe with shorter range than two handers?) That would be pretty awesome.

What spells do you use the least? Probably Widow Venom, which is just not worth the focus cost except for in very specific situations, Immolation Trap, Snake Trap, Aspect of the Wild, and the melee abilities - Raptor Strike and Wing Clip.

Thanks for listening!
