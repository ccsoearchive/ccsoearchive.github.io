■What type of content do you focus on?

- Both.

■If PvE, what type of PvE?

- Heroics and raids.

■If PvP, what type of PvP?

- Arena, battlegrounds and the occasional RBG.

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

- The durability loss rate of my ranged weapon. While the rest of my gear is at 80% my gun's yellow pushing red.
- My pet spawing at very low HP.
- Lag while swapping pets. As in, I dismiss one and attempt to call another. However, the server thinks I still have one out.
- If I want to swap a dead pet, having to first revive then dismiss. This is exasperated by the above issue. Especially if I have caused the dead pet to despawn for whatever reason, as it causes me to have to call the dead pet first. With a second or two of waiting between each step for lag.
- My pet remaining in combat, even while not attacking or being attacked, after an FD preventing me from going to Camo.
- After dropping combat, Mend Pet keeping me in combat. ( I originally assumed my pet was still in combat, but when there's no enemies within 100 yards, it's strange.)
- Camo being inexplicably unusable. Not greyed out, no in-combat error message. Just completely ineffective.
- The clunkiness of Trap Launcher, especially with macros. It takes a second or two for Trap Launcher to register as activated. With macros this frequently results in you planting one at your feet instead of getting the reticule. I've ceased using macros for this reason. To deploy a trap with Launcher I must activate Launcher, wait 1-2 sec for lag, select my trap, mouseover the targeted area of ground and click, wait for the travel time for the trap to land, then wait another two seconds for the trap to activate and hope they're within it's trigger range. All in all, I'm having to anticipate where my target will be in 4-5sec. Much of the time I'm simply not using Launcher, as it's more effective and time/focus efficient to deploy the trap the old fashioned way.

There's more, however this list is getting long.


■What makes playing your class more fun?

- The running, jumping and twirling dance which is kiting.
- To crush your enemies, see them driven before you, and to hear the lamentation of their women.

■What makes playing your class less fun?

- In pvp, regardless of ranged or melee, the standard tactic being to stack on me. Hell, If I'm fighting a hunter and any other class, I'll stack on the hunter and attack the other guy.
- Dealing with pillar hugging in arena. I must run a circle 5yrds in radius larger than any other class in order to stay in range. If I go too far away, or get too close, I can't deal damage or gain focus. If I can't gain focus the majority of my tool-set is useless.

■How do you feel about your “rotation”?

- Rotations are fine for all specs in my opinion.

■What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Some incentive for ranged classes to not stack on the hunter.
- A more effective self heal available to all specs.
- Some sort of DoT protection for our CCs.
- Some means to wipe DoTs from ourselves.
- Slightly larger activation range for our traps with a decreased activation time.
- Timer removed from Camo. It should stay active until cancelled or broken. Then the 1 min CD should be initiated.
- A reason to use Immolation Trap.
- Widow Venom rolled into another shot.
- Counter-Attack either made useful or replaced with something that is.

■What spells do you use the least?

- Immolation Trap
- Feed Pet
- Beastlore
- Widow Venom
- Counter-Attack (I have never used this ability since 4.0 landed. I highly doubt anyone else has either.)

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

n/a

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Lack of a true plantable AOE, honestly. The traps just don't cut it, though I love trap launcher, it's awkward to use mid-rotation when I want to boost my damage by just that much. When I started playing WoW (less than 2 years ago), and this character first hit 40, I fell in love with our erstwhile AOE ability, Volley. It was just fun to rain arrows down onto a target, or even just an area where it won't hurt anyone because it looks cool.

Correct me if I'm wrong, but I think Hunters are the only ranged pure DPS class that doesn't have a true AOE. Multishot just doesn't cut it - and is still sometimes occasionally glitchy. Last night, in Heroic Grim Batol, one of my multishots broke a CC (shammy frog hex'd), when everyone was certain it couldn't hit CC'd mobs. But that's happened repeatedly. It's also hit things that are not IN the battle and drawn them in (monkeys in Heroic Deadmines, anyone?!), which is frustrating. With a plantable AOE, I could aim it directly over where the Tank is, and if anything wandered too close, a quick jump would interrupt the channel and stop that from happening.

The other big quality-of-life issue for me is pet buffs. Not every pet family comes with a buff (IE, Cat: Roar Of Courage, Wolf: Ferocious Howl, Corehound: Ancient Hysteria...). Now, this doesn't sound like a big deal, but....

I caught Steelsnap, the old Thousand Needles Hyena boss, back when *I* was within 2 levels of him. So at something like L35 or so. He's been with me ever since, and I named him Hatari. He was a FANTASTIC pet to level with, and we ran dungeons together and everything. Now, here at endgame... because he didn't have a buff to offer the group or any way to boost his own damage (besides having a slowing ability, but that isn't really something that's sought after in dungeons), and the fact that there are 2 hunters in our regular group and the other one uses a cat... I was asked by my guild's dungeon leader to please pick up a pet that had a buff the group needed. I ended up with a wolf, but I almost cried IRL when I saw what a huge difference the buff made in my and my pet's damage. I have stabled Hatari, probably for good. And part of me feels... really bad about that. But even in solo PVE at end-game level, hyenas just don't do as well as cats and wolves do. So my beloved pet is retired, and I'm stuck with a pet I am not particularly fond of, both for solo (I use a cat now), and dungeoning (the aforementioned wolf). So... if a buff-like ability could be given to all pet families, just something to make them a bit more competitive, it would be nice.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Having and feeling bonded to a particular pet or set of pets. Also the visuals of using a bow are very nice on pretty much every race.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The (unfortunately deserved) reputation that my entire class has for being "huntards". Yes, we can solo content earlier than non-pet classes. Yes, that gives SOME hunters swelled egos and they go into a dungeon thinking they can perform any role and shoot at whatever the hell they want to. But they either learn, or they get kicked enough that it gets drummed into them.

I'm tired of rolling into a random dungeon group and having someone go, "Ugh, hunter," and leave. Usually the tank. I understand the frustration - I've played a tank and I know how annoying hunters can BE! But seriously... I've never seen anyone roll into a random group, go, "Ugh, druid." and leave... Well, ok, once, but that person apparently had a prejudice against bear tanks.

- What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both

- If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids

- If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

Everything

- What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Deadzone is the first thing that comes to mind, hunters either need strong melee damage or the end of minimum range. After a rotation of cooldowns (Master's Call, Deterrance, Disengage, Feign Death...) I'll most likely get in a position where I can't dps my target nor can I stop my target from dpsing me. This is extremely frustrating, aspect of the fox doesn't help much.

Cast speed/Attack speed slows affecting Steady Shot. Using Steady Shot in PvP is awful enough, it does no damage on plate, it has a huge cast time and hits like a wet noodle. The much needed focus regen is lost when hard casting is not an option. Having to get a 3.1 second steady shot off is silly, having to macro /stopcasting to stop channeling it annoys me too.

Scatter -> Trap is annoying, Scatter pathing is really random and traps are pretty silly as is. Snake Trap is complete RNG and annoying, immolation trap is useless, explosive trap is almost useless. Both frost traps are useful but still annoying. Having to use Scatter to CC is awful too, I wish I could save it to peel targets off of myself, like it was it's initial design (given the 0 mininum range).

Pet mechanics should be more intuitive, I wish I could just drag their skills to my normal bars. Pets as a whole always felt pretty weird and awkward, I think they could be designed better but I really can't put it to words. I mean, I think hunters should have more abilities like "Master's Call". Kill Command is almost it, but it really is pretty weird to use. Kill Command should have a bigger range (hunters are range anyways, what problem would it be if pets had a 15yd range kill command?).

I wish Disengage dispelled slows.

-What makes playing your class more fun?

The PvE rotation is great, it's pretty dynamic and intuitive. Marksman is probably one of the coolest dps specs I've played in this game.

The large arsenal of different abilities is also fun. Hunters are really underrated because most players don't even acknowledge the existence of Roar of Sacrifice or non-instant cast web.

Having a ranged taunt, feign death, pet, ccs and all the other survivability/mobiity abilities also make it one of the most fun experiences in both group and solo PvE. Feign Death is great.

- What makes playing your class less fun?

PvP feels frustrating at a lot of times. Pets dying are also annoying, 10 second pet ress feels like too much.

- How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I love it. I enjoy Careful Aim, I like the idea of having both hard cast aimed shot, arcane shot and a mix or both as options, I like steady shot in PvE (as oposed to just faster focus regen), it's in PvP that focus annoys me. I don't really like spending points on Pet Talents, though. I wish Resistance is Futile was better (easier to use and better rewarded). I'd love to get it over Frenzy or whatever.

- What’s on your wish list for your class?

Deadzone disappearance or strong/unique melee ability, like a turtle mode or strong offensive melee cds. Or even making rapid fire drop min range while on it's duration (so I can at least dps my target when I blow cooldowns). Trap/pet rework would be great, too.

- What spells do you use the least?

Snake Trap, while I know it's useful, specially with entrapment, I simply detest this spell's design, same thing happened to me with Mirror Image and mages, even when they Polymorphed I still didn't use them (well, specially with polymorph).

Immolation Trap: never used it.

Cobra Shot: I hardly ever spec anything that is not MM.

Camouflage: other than using it early in arenas/to stealth in bgs I don't see me using this much. I wish it was a short duration vanish that dropped debuffs (not only magic, would love it dispelling physical slows/bleeds). That'd be great.

Raptor Strike looks ok with the glyph but I also hardly ever use it.

Widom Venom, yeah, well...

Kill Command, yeah, I wish Resistance is Futile increased it's damage by 100%, that'd make LoSing a lot less annoying and would also give the PvE rotation a little more good RNG.

Oh, I'd also love to see Master Marksman being better to build up in PvP, 60% per steady shot is already weird, needing 5 stacks to get a short duration buff is also annoying.

Well, that's pretty much it, thanks for the attention.

For Marksmanship Hunters, one QOL issue is the fact that our ranged weapons take a hefty amount of durability damage between our normal shots and the Wild Quiver effect. In addition, the T12 4-piece is often more of an annoyance than a bonus, as it will cap our Focus at a time when we can't spare the shot for fear of losing Improved Steady Shot.

Another major QOL issue for us is minimum range. I appreciate Blizzard's experimentation with the new "double hitbox" for bosses, but it seems not every boss has one of these. We have utterly no recourse for mechanics that require us to be at melee range. Our Focus regeneration basically screeches to a halt, and we can stand there and set pitiful traps and hit Raptor Strike every 8 seconds (which hits for about 3k damage at level 85).

If we could even just cast Steady/Cobra shot in minimum range, it would be SO helpful to both PvE and PvP DPS.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Collecting awesome ranged weapons, hunting down new pets, figuring out innovative ways to solo older raids and improve DPS in current ones.

Always fun is pulling out BIG YELLOW NUMBERS, and we tend to be pretty good at that when we know what we are doing!

What makes playing your class less fun?

Buggy pet mechanics, issues with traps and trap launcher, minimum range and its impact on casting/Focus regen in certain situations.

Next to minimum range, pet AI is probably the biggest issue here. The pathing and attacking are often...strange. Especially with the new stances and target switching. It's frustrating to have your pet bouncing between a set of adds, barely attacking either, because its AI can't figure out which one you want it to stay on. This proves especially frustrating for BM Hunters, as so much of their DPS is reliant on those buggy pet pathing/AI mechanics.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I'm pretty happy with all three Hunter specs, as far as rotation goes. The only thing I might change would be to extend the duration of the Improved Steady Shot buff by 2-4 seconds. Currently, it allows very little flexibility for movement and utility shots (Silencing Shot, Multi, etc), and even using the proc from the T12 4-piece can prove to be a hindrance if it causes ISS to fall off.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

- Removal of the minimum range for Steady/Cobra shots. The others need not be changed, but those two in particular desperately need this.

- Survival and Beast Mastery becoming competitive. Both specs are fun, intuitive, and have their own benefits to bring to a raid. Unfortunately, nearly all progression-raiding Hunters have been pigeonholed into Marksmanship for the past 3+ years, simply due to the fact that it scales so much better than the other two specs. BM got horribly nerfed at the beginning of Wrath, and never completely recovered. SV got the same treatment at the beginning of Cata, and it was honestly unwarranted. Both specs are several thousand DPS behind MM, even on a perfect fight.

- Buyable stable slots in addition to the ones we currently have. Between all the utility/buff pets we must carry, it doesn't give a lot of choice as far as personal preference and vanity/exotic pets.

What spells do you use the least?

Raptor Strike, Counterattack, Eagle Eye, Beast Lore, Widow Venom. In particular, Widow Venom and Counterattack are completely and utterly useless.
