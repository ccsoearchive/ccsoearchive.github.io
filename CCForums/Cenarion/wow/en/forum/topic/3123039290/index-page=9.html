2. Longest-casting pet rez (which makes it impossible to get a pet back after it dies in PvP, and nearly impossible during a raid encounter)
3. A Hunter's PvP revolves entirely around kiting. Hunters that don't have godly kiting skills are just plain worthless.
4. Being useless in melee range (casters can be interrupted by certain other classes, Hunters can be interrupted by ANYTHING so long as they get into melee range). Traps don't count as having a use... in arena, we can't use any of them unless we really need to peel people off ourselves.
5. Our best peel shares a cooldown with our CC ability (Ice trap + Freezing Trap).
6. Traps can be taken by an unintended target, even a pet. I'd be fine with this if #5 was fixed.
7. Our CC doesn't remove DoTs and breaks on any damage, unlike the CC of other classes.
What makes playing your class more fun?
- Mobility is pretty awesome, You can 1v1 pretty decently, and smart Hunters can use their surroundings to greatly their advantage (although it is hard to do)
What makes playing your class less fun?
- 1. You can be shut down very easily just by getting melee-ranged (Dalaran Arena boxes.....).
2. Vs casters and other Hunters, it's only a fight to do more damage than the other guy (many casters can win against me just by abandoning any defensive measures and going full offensive on me)
3. Hunters are entirely reliant on having their cooldowns in PvP, which is why Marksmanship is the best spec by far of any other spec due to Readiness.
How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
- My rotation is pretty interesting in PvE, and easy enough to keep up in PvP.
What’s on your wish list for your class?
- 1. A hard-hitting melee attack (that might interrupt)
2. A DoT-cleanser/HoT to help with DoT classes and with self-sustaining in raid... since we can't very well defend against DoT classes and we're often one of the squishiest classes when it comes to taking damage (IE, we require the most healing in arenas, on fights like Baleroc and Majordomo where damage has to be taken on purpose, on fights like Nefarian where we have little to do about the massive raid AoE damage, and on fights like Heroic Shannox where we'll often be kiting Rageface out of range of the healers). It's frustrating when I finish up a fight with a DoT class and I'll die of dots unless I Feign->Eat. It also doesn't make sense for a Hunter to not be carrying around medicine. First Aid doesn't count... since every class in the game has a DoT that will prevent that.

- 1. Widow Venom - Never
2. Immolation Trap - Only when another Hunter in my raid is laying down an Explosive Trap for the boss pull, so they don't overlap.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
-Both, emphasis on PvP

BGs and Rated BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
-Trap Launcher, no matter how it's in a macro, requiring two button presses. Pet bar disappearing.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Highly mobile ranged class. A lot of instant casts. Amazing kiting ability against most melee classes.

What makes playing your class less fun?
Poor defensive cooldowns. Not being able to use Deterrence while carrying the flag. If that remains the case, Deterrence should remove DoTs in addition to it's current effect, or allow 50% Dps like the paladin Divine Shield. Basically the only class in the game without some form of decent self healing ability. Our method of resource regen can be slowed, from abilities like Frost Fever. No other class is hindered this way.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Decent. I never liked the new Kill Command. I though it put a little too much emphasis on the pet. So I switched to Marks and Survival. I like Marks playstyle. My only complaint is trying to get off Aimed Shots for burst in PvP. I also really like Survival playstyle, and would make it my primary spec but I find Black Arrow completely lackluster compared to Chimera Shot, and no spec feels "complete" without the final tier talent (most PvP Surv builds don't bother taking BA). Ways to fix Black Arrow: Put dispel protection on it, like a Spriest or Affliction Lock. Make the dispeller take damage from removing it. It should do more damage than either Fire Trap on single targets.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Better CC. Scatter is on a long cooldown, and Marks has an interrupt on an almost as long cooldown. Freezing Trap is easily dodged by careful players, and shares a cooldown with our best kiting tool, Frost Trap. I would like to see more interrupts, such as one for all specs, or a better on demand CC. Like turn Freezing Trap into Freezing Arrow: If Hit capped, never misses, and lower/remove the cooldown, but put it on DR with things like Poly and Hex. Make Snake Trap scale, they still hit for less than 50 damage each.

What spells do you use the least?
Easy. Widow's Venom and Immolation Trap. Also Aspect of the Wild (this would be a good place to start with self healing ability) and Feed Pet.

About Immolation Trap: It failed to scale in Cata. And that's fine. Surv has Black Arrow. If Freezing and Frost were separated, as proposed above, simply remove Immolation Trap. Hunters would then have three traps: Frost, Explosive, and Snake. They would all get Freezing Arrow, and Marks and BM would continue using Explosive in raids or PvP.

•What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Raids, mostly, then Heroics and Quests

•If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

When I do PVP, it's usually Arenas or non-rated BGs. However, I'm not currently PVPing.

•What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Quality of life is pretty good right now. I think my biggest concern at this point is that Hunters have been turn into the ultimate utility role and that causes some aspects of the life to be overwhelming. More on this below.

•What makes playing your class more fun?

I like that instant shots are the majority of our shots; I like that we can stand back and watch how things are unfolding and respond accordingly (and without necessarily gimping our dps). And I like the synergy with the pet.

•What makes playing your class less fun?

Minimum range, as I'm sure you'll hear a million times in this thread.
Always trying to determine the optimum pet to bring (I know there are add-ons and such, but I feel that Blizzard should provide a better means in-game for us to check whether our pets are being as useful as they can).
Having to keep a dozen shots hot-keyed on the rare chance I'll need them.
It's also not fun being the only class in the game where the "stat stick" is a main hand weapon. As a result, we're last to get upgrades against melee classes even though the stat disparity between main hand weapons and ranged weapons is significant (i.e., when a Hunter upgrades his stat stick he gets a much larger DPS gain than when another class upgrades their stat stick), and Hunters have to pay a significant higher cost in points towards PVP main hand weapons since other classes are getting a damage benefit from them.

•How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

For Survival I love it. For Marks, I feel like it's a little less forgiving; keeping Improved Steady Shot up seems harder than it should be. I also think the signature shot having 10 seconds (9 seconds glyphed) between castings is a little long, especially compared to the other specs. For BM, I dislike the "wait" with Kill Command, i.e. the pet needs to be in range, but I understand its requirement.

•What’s on your wish list for your class?

Eliminate minimum range for all shots.
Eliminate a few of the shots and traps based on least-used on the survey; eliminating minumum range means you can eliminate all the melee attacks right off the bat.
Add an interrupt (Tremor Arrow?) that every spec can use. Remove Silencing Shot and Wyvern Sting from Marks and Survival as part of this. If necessary, give it a high focus cost in order to give it a lower cooldown (10 seconds or less) to make it raid-viable.
Rework the stat stick mechanic for Hunters. Either make stat sticks akin to relics and wands where they can be purchased with points (and fewer points in PVP), or come up with a new system where ranged weapons have higher stats for Hunters and stat sticks have lower.

•What spells do you use the least?
Counterattack
Immolation Trap
Eagle Eye
Beast Lore
Scare Beast (although I understand this has PVP utility)
Widow Venom
Snake Trap
