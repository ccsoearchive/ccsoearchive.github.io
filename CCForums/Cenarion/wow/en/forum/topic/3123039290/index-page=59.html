If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated Bgs]

Arenas and BG s, some Rateds and World PvP

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Currently, pet bugs are a constant annoyance. Pet bars tend to disappear when zoning or logging. Also pet pathing issues are a major problem in the Nefarian encounter. Even if I summon my pet after jumping down at the start of the fight, it still remains frozen and unresponsive. Pets are also often low on health once summoned and killed instantly.

I am concerned about focus regeneration. In Pvp especially, there seems to be low burst damage compared to the focus cost of higher damaging shots and a very slow recovery of that resource. Steady shot/Cobra shot have a cast time that allows for great opportunity to interrupt or close and although Aspect of the Fox is a good idea, in practical use the damage loss is not worth it. Key binding shots to aspects is clunky and aspect dancing is just another thing to keep track of in PvP while playing a class that already is intolerant of errors and difficult to master.


What makes playing your class more fun?

The unique mobile play style of the hunter class is one of the most fun things for me. Being able to kite, DPS, pull, track, and solo are all things that keep the hunter class my favorite and my hunter my main for six years. I also really enjoy the accomplishment of finding ways to make the hunter class viable, valuable and effective in spite of the various issues with and remakes of the class. I often say the best thing about playing a hunter is being under estimated. What makes a hunter fun to play is being vindicated by being top DPS, winning a PvP fight against the ubiquitous FotM class, nailing an impossible pull, kiting like a pro, and not being one of those “huntards”.

What makes playing your class less fun?

The homogenization of pets is a bummer. Outside of skin differences and buff utility, pets are less fun than they used to be. Broken Tooth used to be the fastest attacking pet in the game and worth the extensive camp it took to find him. The Bloodaxe worgs were the only source of Bite 8 and Furious Howl 4 back in the day AND they changed colors on summon. There was a wolf that did shadow damage, pets that had natural resistances and higher run speed. The accomplishment of finding these mobs and taming them was rewarding and very hunterly. The recent taming challenge pets made up for this somewhat, but spiders or spirit beasts are not much to choose from.

Losing double trap is not fun. I miss it immensely. Back in BC with the dungeon set four bonus, I could sometimes pull off three ice traps at once and that was cool.

In fact, losing the things that make hunters stand out as that ranger sort of class has been a consistent let down. I miss the quiver graphic on my character's back. I miss having to feed my pet and remember arrows. I miss having elite mobs out in the world to play with. :( Remember kiting King Mosh to Orgrimaar? That was fun.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

In PvE Survival seems to be the more effective and less awkward than Marks for me. Especially with all the motion needed in current content. Survival rotation is working well except AoE rotation on the move is hard to maintain as kited or advancing mob groups lose health because of the cobra shot cast time being just long enough so a target mob will die before it goes off, leaving me focus starved and unable finish off those darn spiderlings, worms, slimes, draconid skeletons or whatever before the next little marching group of doom arrives. :(

I have been able to compensate for this by really monitoring my focus, switching targets to higher health mobs when firing focus regenerating shots and some creative trap use, but its awkward and frustrating.

I have not been Beast specced except for in Wrath mainly for RP reasons. I am currently Marks for PvP and the rotation is more of a priority list. I think Aimed shot procs are too rare to make this big burst shot useful. I use it as an opener, and sometimes I get another off in the course of a fight but not often, which is a shame because I still think of Aimed shot as the “signature” shot for the Marks tree. I find aspect dancing uncomfortable and annoying and not a good solution.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

1.Another epic and/or legendary quest line. Nothing says bad !@# like earning a singularly awesome weapon. I am really looking forward to wearing Rhok'delar again should transmogrification allow it.

2.The ability to tame mounts would be awesome.
3.Pets that are one of a kind in appearance and ability either as rares, quest mobs or dungeon dwelling creatures.
4.Race appropriate looking armor and weapon choices.
5.A dev that plays a hunter religiously and loves the class as much as I do.

What spells do you use the least?

1.Widow's Venom
2.Raptor Strike
3.Aspect of the Wild

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Issues with pet summoning persist, and are minor, but need attention. The petbar disappears now and then, and a freshly-summoned pet has very little health and is sometimes one-shotted. Also, the little rock island onto Lord Rhyolith's area still cooks any pet that runs across it.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I play as survival most of the time, and I enjoy the fact that while still doing acceptable dps, the ability to be a cc and kiting machine is still there.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Minimum range is bothersome in some cases. Mostly a matter of boss hitboxes that are so large that I may well be at range when I look at my screen, but find myself poking the boss with a stick.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Survival rotation I think is about right, although we could use a dps cooldown perhaps. MM rotation is slightly too unforgiving, a small extension on the duration of improved steady shot? I can't speak to beastmastery, since I never play it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I wish there was some reason for hunters to care about mastery. It mostly shows up on our gear to be forged away to anything else. Perhaps we could have an ammo option in place of widow's venom. For example: frangible ammo, which occasionally procs extra damage; or leeching ammo which procs a self-heal; and window's ammo, which features a healing debuff. Why not make the stat which governs ammo's special effects be mastery? As it stands now, mastery for hunters might as well not exist at all, so why not make it relevant? A cobra shot glyph would also be dandy.

What spells do you use the least?

Beast lore, widow's venom, and eagle eye never get used. None of the melee abilities are ever used.

I like that the Disengage cooldown can be shortened, and wonder if more of the glyphs could be modified to improve maneuverability—perhaps a glyph to lengthen Deterrence by 2 secs or a glyph to remove the stun when hit drawback of Aspect of the Cheetah (the AP hit you take from not using Aspect of the Hawk should be drawback enough).

What makes playing your class more fun?
I love how dynamic the class can be—creating damage on the run, engaging multiple enemies, cc’ing one foe while damaging another—these are a huge challenges that I would like to see more of in the hunter class.

What makes playing your class less fun?
I get frustrated that too many classes seem to infringe on the hunter’s unique qualities—warlocks and DK’s have pets, warriors throw their swords for physical damage, paladins can damage on the run with the bonus of self-heals—I’d like the hunter to be more unique, I guess. Also, what the hell is the Survival tree for?

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
Pretty great, actually, at least for the MM spec. I usually lead with Chim Shot and Arcane Shot, keeping my Serpent Sting and Mark of the Hunter on the target, Steady Shot when focus is needed. But all of this get’s fracked if I’m engaging two opponents, because I am cc’ing in the middle of this and popping cooldowns. So I love that it’s pretty complicated, but intuitive at the same time.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
1. More maneuverability vis a vis the glyph changes I suggested (one to lengthen Deterrence by 2 secs and a glyph to remove the stun when hit drawback of Aspect of the Cheetah (the AP hit you take from not using Aspect of the Hawk should be drawback enough).
2. Minimum Distance modification—unlike most hunters I understand and agree with the Minimum Distance, however, it should end when in melee range. I should be able to use my knives if I can’t use my bow.
3. I’d like to be able to macro my Trap Launcher into my traps so that I could, for instance, click F1 to set down my Freezing Trap and SHIFT-F1 to launch it.

What spells do you use the least?
But hunters don’t have spells! But seriously, Track Dragonkin, Track Elementals, Track Giants, Track Demons, Track Undead, Track Humanoids, and Scare Beast. I do use Track Hidden, and unlike most hunters, I like to throw out Widow Venom on priests and pallys, but I don’t really notice much of a difference.
