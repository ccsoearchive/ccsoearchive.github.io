What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
All

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
BGs

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Various pet pathing bugs, pet spawning with 5k hp and getting 1 shot, minimum range.

What makes playing your class more fun?
Being able to solo content when my pet decides to work.

What makes playing your class less fun?
The new pet stances, there are times when my pet is running between mobs rather than attacking. Sometimes my pet will run all the way back to me instead of just switching targets. When people decide to attack you in PVP, you're pretty much done once all your traps/disengage is on CD, and focus is too slow to regen unless you're not getting focused.

PVP in general makes it less fun.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
The MM rotation is nice and tight, but pretty boring. Survival and BM's rotations are fine.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
Make PVP as fun as it was in Wrath. In Wrath we had 3 instant shots and didn't have to rely on steady to regen our mana, it made kiting much easier and PVP was more fun in general. Since Cata, we disengage away without having time to fire another shot. Hopefully devs can find a way to bring the focus mechanic similar to the level mana was in PVP.

Revert pet stances, the new ones were supposed to make pet control less difficult, but it has done the opposite.

A good self-heal ability, many classes can save themselves from death by popping some self-heal, and hunters just don't have that.

What spells do you use the least?
Camouflage

# What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]PvE

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters. I dislike how I have more then 40+ spells. But, my biggest issue, is my focus regen. Rogues and druids regen more focus then I do. To get some of my focus back, i have to rely mostly on cobra shot or steady shot, but those two abilities suffer from lost cast time if I'm hit by an ability and that includes an AoE. These days it's rare for someone to kill a raid boss without taking at least some damage.

# What makes playing your class more fun? Cobra shot. No longer having to worry about reapplying serpent sting by pressing the serpent sting is awesome. Also, chucking an exploding trap at a boss and causing it to either despawn, where in someone in the group asks something like, "Wasn't there a boss here?" Or, "Where did the boss go?" I think is awesome. Although, it's not as awesome as that one time, out in duskwood, when I caused a world boss to spam the entire zone for 5+ minutes.

# What makes playing your class less fun? Having 14+ buttons I have to press at least once every five minutes in a raid. I don't want more then TWELVE buttons to press

# How do you feel about your “rotation”? As far as single target fights go, it's okay. I feel like if I want to effectively AoE i need to switch to marksmen. As a beast master, if I want to AoE, I can't spare any regen for anything else. Thus, my 'rotation' goes out the window.

# What’s on your wish list for your class?
Chimera (the pet type)'s AoE is difficult, very tedious, and clunky to use. You can easily and accidentally interupt it's AoE by ordering it to move somewhere. Furthermore, you can't use it's AoE near CC - there is just too much chance the AoE will accidentally break CC. While the Chimera is casting it's AoE, it doesn't auto attack - which prevents the beastmaster from gaining any focus from the pet.
A slightly larger bar for pet abilities, or to merge the abilities into things. For example, as a beast master with a turtle with tank spec, I can have 6+ pet abilities - but only four spots to put them on my pet bar.
The pet system tends to lag a lot. I can order my pet to move to a spot, and in the time it takes for it to realize I just ordered it to move, I can successfully cast 2-4 cobra shots.
I'd like the ability to cast misdirect on someone, and while i'm redirecting threat to that person, if I cast distracting shot the distracting shot acts as if the person taunted the target, thereby causing the target to go after the person, instead of me.
And, finally, I want a shot that lets me one shot everything within 100,000 yards of me. Oh, and it must also cause me to instantly autoloot everything.Wait a minute. Did I write this paragraph down? Opps!

# What spells do you use the least? Eagle eye. Back in vanilla it was rather useful to me. I could use it to scout an area for something. Back then I didn't have things like an in game quest helper, or quest tracker, or the ability to raid mark a monster, or the ability to fly around on a flying mount. Back then mobs could severely punish you stray to far into a place you shouldn't be. Now that I have those things, eagle eye is very rarely useful to me.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Resurrect pet takes way too long to cast and there is no way to speed it up or make it instant. Compare this to options that Warlocks have...

What makes playing your class more fun?
Disengage is great and so is the increased range on Master's Call.

What makes playing your class less fun?
In PvE it's constant switching between Aspect of the Hawk and Fox on movement intensive fights, you need to do it to max your DPS but it is pretty tedious.
In PvP having absolutely no way to cleanse any DoTs is pretty lame, it feels like if you get a large stack of DoTs on you unless a healer is around you're just doomed, that isn't the case with most other classes who have better ways to heal through it or cleanse off the DoTs.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?
I think the rotations are pretty good but the Aimed Shot nerf a while back has made MM less exciting to play. As I mentioned, having to switch Aspects Hawk/Fox on movement fights as part of a "rotation" is tedious.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
More defensive options (heals, shields, DoT cleanse, etc.) I think that the hunter class needs some kind of baseline (for all specs) self heal similar to recuperate that rogues have, or the healing options warriors or DKs have. Right now you can only heal if you're BM or MM, survival has zero self heals! To get the most self heals you have to be BM and use a Spirit Beast pet, which limits options for using other pets. I have also thought it highly ironic that a class with a spec called "survival" can't even mitigate or cleanse poison DoTs.

What spells do you use the least?
Raptor Strike, Immolation Trap (this trap is very very weak). I also don't use dismiss pet that much due to the long cast requiring you to be stationary. I believe dismiss pet should be instant or at least channeled while moving, a lot of times in a BG if you want to dismiss your pet, it's faster (or more practical) to send it off the opposite way of where you are going and just "run it off" to get it unsummoned.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    PvE. While the change from Mana to Focus was a positive change, I feel the burst damage is no longer there for us in PvP. The Focus restriction in PvP limits our burst output whereas Mana allowed us to unload everything we had at a moment's notice so long as we had the mana for it. That change has made PvP a lot more unattractive for me as a result.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

    Progression Raiding, Heroics, Farming.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

    As mentioned above, I no longer PvP as much as I once did. Aside from the Mana->Focus issue, the way classes operate is not even across the board, and that's where the fundamental flaw lies. In Arena, for example, you have classes that are able to reapply their Crowd Control repeatedly as there is no cooldown on those abilities (Warlock Fear, Mage Polymorph, Druid Cyclone). "Diminishing Returns" or not, they're still able to repeat them back-to-back to even reach a DR, whereas other classes have a longer cooldown on their Crowd Control abilities (Paladin Hammer of Justice, Hunter Freezing Trap, Priest Psychic Scream, etc.). It creates an immediate disadvantage based on the PvP composition you've chosen.

    Furthermore, the way classes deal damage can also be brought into question given how arena works. Classes that are able to make heavy use of Damage Over Time (DoTs) effects have abilities that chase their opponent around Line of Sight mechanics. As a primarily Direct Damage class, catching an opponent and doing as much damage as possible to them while they are caught out in the open is the only way to chip them down. However, in order to do damage to them, I must be in Line of Sight, leaving me open to their DoTs. If the DoT class retreats, I'm no longer damaging them with my primary source of damage. If -I- retreat, they're still doing the majority of their damage to me. Cleansing is certainly a wise idea, but now you are redirecting my healer's focus while their healer simply needs to heal through direct damage.

    Just some food for thought when balancing is considered.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

    The hunter "Dead Zone" no longer exists, but stacking in a raid and then attempting to do AoE damage with enemies in your "Melee Zone" is still annoying. Ex. Eye of Occu'thar or the Molten Seeds on Ragnaros.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    Doing impressive damage while maintaining decent mobility and utility.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    The aforementioned "Melee Zone".

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Feign Death should be converted back to an earlier iteration. It used to be an instant threat drop. Now it seems like there's a 2 second delay.

    Trap Launcher should be turned into Trap Shot so we can fire our traps directly at the target instead of flinging them at a location and hoping the enemy walks into them. Easy for PvE, many times more difficult in PvP.

    Scare Beast, Immolation Trap, Beast Lore.
