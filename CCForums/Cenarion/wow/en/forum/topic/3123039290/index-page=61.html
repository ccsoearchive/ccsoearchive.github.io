I for one find that the removal of the hunter's mana pool has caused a loss of power control. With the mana pool, we were able to build our pool with Int stacks, as well as regenerate with switching the former aspects. It was faster, allowed more hunter control, and made play easier. Likewise the removal of auto shot, especially for the pvp'ers has had a profound effect on hunter's attack abilities. Now we have to stop and manually click targets, use tab, or other key in attack, which often delays attacks and defenses to the point we have sustained high damage before getting an attack off. As hunter's go in Cata, if a rogue gets on you, u may as well stand there and die, the poisons, stuns, disarms make a standing defense nearly impossible. And when u do get a chance to fight back, they just vanish and run away to hide till they get the next side step in on you. Once sidestepped, backstabbed and disarmed, hunter's have no close quaters defense. Traps, disengage, and melee all stop working. This is an extreme issue that needs addressing. The pre-cata hunter's were much more efficient, practical and fun.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

Both. I have always enjoyed both.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Heroics and Raids.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

BGs, occasional Arena and Rated BGs; haven't had the personal time to do it since Cata started.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

I'll be honest? Although there are work-arounds, it is almost certainly weapon durability and minimum range. There is nothing quite as embarassing as having your bow break in the middle of a fight (I'm looking at you Festergut) due to the lower Durability then your stat stick.

As for minimum range, there was a time it truly was necessary, especially in PvP. However, with the rise of so many ways for classes to get within that range and stay there with no penalty to them at all, perhaps it is time to consider removing this years-old mechanic. I'm sorry, it shouldn't be THAT easy to render me helpless. I mean, do hunters suddenly forget to fire when they enemy is that close or something? Do mages forget to cast spells when they're that close??? "Oh no, they're close enough to count my teeth, all I have is this GIANT @#$%ING GUN! Somebody, anybody, save me!"

I know there is concerns that this will encourage hunters to NOT learn kiting, but that simply isn't true. Even if we could shoot in melee range, you're in trouble if you can't get that rogue off your butt or that warrior out of your face. And you are CERTAINLY dead if you cannot kite the spirits in Jin'do's heroic fight or any of Shannox's hounds, and do so correctly.

Also, steady and cobra being able to be slowed... no other class has this glaring vulnerability in their regen mechanics, even mages. Please, look at it.

The pet bar bug, ahh the pet bar bug. I know you guys at Blizz HQ are probably already working on this as this isn't just a hunter issue but a game issue, but it can be really annoying when you have your pet out in PvP, need to use their ability and look down and wonder why it's not working only to go "oh. Well @#%@."

The pet health bug is very annoying as well in PvP; less so in PvE. It seems to me it has something to do with how spawn timers work, but when he pet constitutes 7-10% of my DPS or acts as a tool to help me in PvP, it's very annoying when smarter players will exploit this mechanic to snipe the pet or it gets killed by a weak hit. Please look at this as soon as you can.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Camo, the ability to lay traps, the ability to track my opponents movements and the ability to tame beasts to do my bidding. I feel like a true tactician of the battlefield.
