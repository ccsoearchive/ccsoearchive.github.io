- What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Launching traps (PvP & PvE) : Why can't we get rid of trap launcher and add in 5 new traps that cost 20 focus that are single click that give targetting circles (very similar to freezing arrow)?

Fox/Hawk Aspect Swapping (PvP) : My Steady shot button is macro'd in with Aspect of the Fox, all my other buttons are macro'd with Aspect of the Hawk. IMO, Aspect of the Hawk should only effect Steady or Cobra shot (Just like Aspect of the Fox only effects Steady or Cobra shot).

Immolation/Explosive trap sharing cooldown & Freezing/Frost trap sharing cooldown (PvP & PvE) : I just don't see the logic in this. DKs drop desecration just by swinging their weapon. We have to arm traps once every 30 seconds to get the same effect?

Freezing trap (all traps) having a cooldown (PvP & PvE) : I don't understand this either. Polymorph has no cooldown and much easier to use. Why can't Freezing have no cooldown? It's still a ton harder to use and you can't multi-trap anymore.

Camouflage 1 min (PvP) : Why is this a 1 minute ability with a 1 minute cooldown? All other stealth's (which are far superior) last for ever. Even if the cooldown was changed to (lets just say) 45 seconds, it would make the ability 100% better.

Camouflage not usable in combat (PvP) : makes this ability worse than the nightelf racial

Kill Command range (PvE & PvP) : Can we just make this an 8 yard ranged talent? Server lag, internet lag, client lag, opponent's lag makes this talent suck.

Killing Streak Talent (PvE & PvP) : Needs buff time doubled.

The Beast Within Talent (PvP) : Too many ways to negate this effect. CC, LOS, Disarm, Minimum Range. It should return to being un-CC'able.

Improved Steady Shot (PvE & PvP) : Should last for at least 12 seconds. Also, each steady shot after the first two consecutive steadys should refresh the buff effect. In other words, if you cast 2 steadys in a row, you get a buff that lasts 8 seconds. If your next shot is a steady, it doesn't refresh the buff to 8 seconds.

Silencing Shot (PvP) : Should be attainable by all specs to make PvP much more viable. Swap location of Silencing Shot and Improved Steady shot so BM & SV will work better in PvP

Trueshot Aura (PvE) : I go back and forth on my spec occasionally dropping this talent. So many classes/specs provide this buff that it isn't really worth picking up (unless you're using a single spec to pvp & pve, like my current build). It needs something for hunter only... like reduces all AE (or dot) damage taken by 15%.

Resistance is Futile talent issues (PvE & PvP) : First issue is that it isn't effected by Marked for Death talent. The scynchronation of these 2 talents would make this a compelling talent.
The second issue is returned focus after being used. Even when I got this talent and put up hunter's mark and the talent proc'd (rarely in pve, hard to use in pvp), it added yet another factor for me to consider while trying to do my rotation, watch my dots, watch fires, watch DBM, watch what's hitting me and some how add it to my rotation if my focus was greater than 40. Just too hard to use.

Master Marksman effect is random (PvP & PvE) : It makes it impossible to predict when Aimed shot will be insta-cast. Also, the randomness of it makes it not fun becuase it doesn't proc often enough. In PvP, this effect almost never takes places because of being CC'd or LOS'd. I wish this talent would reduce cast time by 20/40/60/80/100% of aimed shot rather than only giving instant aimed shot.

Serpent Sting (PvP) : Too easily Dispelled.

■What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
A good mix of both. I mainly focused on PvE back in wrath and the beginning of cata, but after the long queue times(not really an issue anymore, except Zandalari's) and lack of getting into raids since everyone and their mother plays dps, I went to some PvP, which I'm having fun with.

■If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Mainly heroic dungeons. Normal heroics, or Zandalari's when the queue time is decent. I rarely get to raid, and when I do, its older content (BWD, BoT). Part of this reason is my wild work schedule and a good portion of it is everyone playing dps. Main reason I started playing tanks and healers.

■If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
Mainly BGs. If I get enough gear, I plan to start doing arenas with a friend.

■What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
Having no melee utility. While I don't want to be able to dish out amazing damage in melee, or have tons of utility being a range class (unless they bring in a melee hunter!), but it would be nice to have more things to do that raptor strike, wing clip, then deterrence to help run away to range.

■What makes playing your class more fun?
Tanking old school dungeons with next to no repair bill. Solo'ing just about everything, with again, next to no repair bill. Being able to stand at range, fire everything in the book, be able to move around and having the great mobility of a hunter while still being able to dish out plenty of damage. Aspect of the Fox was a GREAT addition, and while some people think its OP, I find it very fun and a good addition, especially since I run out of focus in 3 shots and need to spam Cobra Shot to get it back.

■What makes playing your class less fun?
Nothing too much. Some bugs, like pet bar disappearing, having to dismount to use aspect of the cheetah, and not having more melee utility.

■How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I find my rotation great. It's simple, yet requires your focus. And by switching to Aspect of the Fox, I can do my rotation on the move. No real complaints here.

■What’s on your wish list for your class?
Being able to use Aspect of the Cheetah while mounted, thus dismounting you into a fast run.

Have more melee utility, like maybe give Raptor Strike give you some small buff or small debuff to the target (but not slow them, we have Wing Clip for that).

Buffing the damage of Survival's DoTs. Black Arrow got a decent damage buff before, but I think it could use just a tiny bit more damage, and give Explosive Shot its 15% back that was taken away earlier in the expansion (or at least 10%, any buff to it would help tremendously).

Buff Serpent Sting for all specs. Its damage isn't exactly wonderful, and could use a decent buff. Then maybe a talent in Survival to buff it a little more, since our burst damage bites and we focus on our DoTs more than anything.

Buff our traps. Seriously. Make them all have their own CD (except keep Black Arrow and Immolation Trap on the same CD, since it's always been that way for Survival and just makes sense). Immolation trap should be buffed for other specs, and Snake Trap needs to be buffed in a very big way. Sure, I've got 12 snakes, but they hit for around 13-20 damage and don't last very long. Maybe make them last about 10 seconds longer, and once the trap has been triggered, they follow you like pets and use the Assist stance. Also, buff their damage by about 100%, for both hits and their DoT.

Last, but not least, GIVE CATS THEIR RAKE BACK!

■What spells do you use the least?
Widow Venom. It's never really useful, just a bit helpful when someone's trying to heal and unless you have someone helping you, it isn't noticeable.

Raptor Strike. I only use it in raid fights that make me get up close to the boss, and when someone gets in my face in PvP and everything I have to get away is on CD.

Feed Pet. I mean, come on, what was the point of even leaving it in the game? I took it off my bar and everything.

I primarily raid with my hunter (hot dps is hot).
What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Removal of ammo and our pet's happiness/ loyalty were great quality of life improvements, however the "assist" stance is almost completely useless in AOE situations. The moment I start unloading multi-shots, my pet runs into the mob and stands there with no clue who to attack. In addition, while the trap launcher is a godsend for CC purposes, I rarely use it in combat (though in retrospect it's also viable to Camo then run up to the mobs to drop it at their feet). It uses focus I could put towards my signature shots. It's also wasted time setting the reticle, meanwhile I'm missing out on doing my rotation and am falling behind in the meters or am not looking out for something important (like my turn on one of Baleroc's Shards of Torment). Finally, I wish that my Silencing Shot had a reduced cooldown, I know hunters aren't the first choice for interrupts in a fight, but I've been able to save a tank's life numerous times in the past with it (such as the Worshipping mechanic in Cho'galll). Primarily I think it would be better to have a 15 second CD for Silencing Shot, and to reduce the CD on Scatter shot to 20 seconds (PVP can be balanced with this by removing a second or two from the shot's daze effect, besides I only use it as my "oh god" interrupt for when things get hairy).
What makes playing your class more fun?
I bring a lot of utility to any group I join. I can kite like no other, I bring whatever de/buff is needed or missing, and still push out great numbers when I can just sit still and dps. CC's are a snap to apply, my pet can tank in a pinch while a tank gets brezzed and picked back up, and my armor looks cool (except for T2.5-- what were you guys smoking?).
What makes playing your class less fun?
I'm always called on to do the !@#$% work in any fight. While I'm more than adequate at handling these challenges, I hate that I'm the de facto work horse of my raid/ heroic. I can still get killed by boss mechanics even though I more than likely won't be fighting him/ her. All I'm really asking for is bring some utility to the other classes-- I don't wanna be the only support class for every boss and encounter that needs it.
How do you feel about your “rotation”?
Hunter rotations are really good reflectors of each spec's flavor-- for better or worse.
--BM is boring. I've raided with it & it's not fun. Healers tend to leave my pet alone and Mend Pet just isn't enough sometimes. If my pet dies, that's a huge chunk of my DPS lost while I'm stuck reving it (which isn’t always possible). Meanwhile, I don't feel like I'm the badass because everything I'm doing is tied to my pet.
--MM is tight and engaging. It's my main spec, not for the DPS, but because it's easy enough to adapt to most situations. Movement phases are no problem and the rotation & mechanics behind it are easy to understand and execute even if I can't sit and tunnel vision over it. Great job!
--SV is weird. The focus requirements and timing are very strange to me with SV. You almost never get to use a focus dump shot unless you're in a cooldown phase, such as Rapid Fire or Bloodlust. The cooldown time and focus requirements of Explosive Shot just don't mesh well with the time/ focus requirements of Black Arrow. Also, I can go through numerous Black Arrow shots and never proc Lock 'N Load, which is very frustrating (SV = the fire mage spec of hunters). It's just lopsided-- which is why I only use SV for add control in boss fights (remember how I'm the work horse of the group?) or trash.
What’s on your wish list for your class?
A cleaner SV spec (and talent trees in general) that has a signature skill I'll actually use (Counterattack makes no sense for hunters to have when the whole point of our class is to keep things at distance seeing as we're THE ONLY CLASS THAT HAS A MINIMUM RANGE).
What spells do you use the least?
I never use my Kill Command. Can that be made the signature skill for BM and have Intimidation replaced with something...useful? Threat isn't my concern (especially with the changes to threat and one or two well-timed Misdirections paired with a Feign Death) and a stun is pretty bland too. Perhaps replace Intimidation with Kill Command for BM's signature skill, make Silencing Shot baseline for all specs w/ a 15 second cooldown, and plug Intimidation into the MM tree-- except have it be something like.... a deterrence buff to reduce AOE damage or allowing arcane shot crits to regen some extra focus (like 3-5, enough to take the edge off of Chimaera Shot's cooldown in relation to the haste plateaus).

Great job with hunters so far (I notice we've mostly been left alone since Cata seeing as we're so well-built and awesome in general)! Please give us more lore though? =3

This rotation is like 50% casting and 50% instants.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I love hunter's versatility. While they are a "pure DPS" class, I feel they have some of the best reactive skill sets in the game, a taunt, a snare, a cc, a second cc when specced, dots, hard damage, and a "living" resource, one that we can react too, not just wait to fill...

What makes playing your class less fun?

Having issues with an effect not working as you might expect, such as frost cauldrons not proccing when you use shots to freeze enemies. And while the idea of exotic pets is cool, the final BM tree trait falls short of what one might expect from it. 4 extra points into many pets is only a small increase in the pet DPS, or survivability, it doesn't offer access to a "cool" ability like one who had "Mastered" the pet might expect.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Having the most experience with Surv atm, I feel it's a solid priority system. A friend of mine who plays BM says he's mostly happy with the priority there, as well... only thing I think I might ask for would be a better, more efficient way of including traps in the rotation.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

I have two things very much on my wish list for my hunter.
1. I would love a "dual-weapon" option for my hunter. Warriors get it, lol, and DKs, and rogues.I don't expect rifles, but I think that pistols or hand-crossbows would be an awesome addition to the game. What's more, they would most likely be more easily identifiable as "Hunter" weapons since they would most likely have lower stats than their one-weapon counterparts.
2. I would love a "BM tank" tree option. Hunters can already, if properly specced, tank instances and even some raid bosses. While this may not be by design, there's no denying that to the hunters that try it, it's a lot of management, and a lot of fun! ^^

What spells do you use the least?

Like many others here, I've never used Widow venom. I don't use beast lore, either, and feel like it might be better passive. I don't use wing clip, or couter attack, and feel like the only way they might become useful is if you were to ever design a "Melee hunter" spec.
