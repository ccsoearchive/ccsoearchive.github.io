What type of content do you focus on? Both

If PvE, what type of PvE? Raids/Soloing/5-mans

If PvP, what type of PvP? Battlegrounds

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? Our "dead zone" gets frustrating when doing combat against classes with numerous gap closers. The pet health glitch causes a few problems, but having them remain by our side while mounted has helped quite a bit. Aspect dancing is also very clunky. I rely far more heavily on macros than I would like to. Trap Launcher is a great addition, but it requires some odd keybinding and the travel time makes it unreliable.

What makes playing your class more fun? Compared to other classes, I like the way hunters work as a ranged damage-dealing class. We normally don't rely too heavily on hard casting and work quite well in movement-heavy scenarios.

What makes playing your class less fun? The glitches that seem to plague the cast, the downtime when pets die, our slow passive focus regeneration (if steady shot gets LoS'd or the target goes out of range, we lose out on our resource).

How do you feel about your “rotation”? I love the way hunters work PVE-wise, or else I wouldn't be playing one! In PVE, hunters work quite well and I'm quite comfortable with the class's rotation.

What’s on your wish list for your class? A more reliable Trap Launcher, higher dodge rating, somewhat better self-healing (at least out of combat), a DoT wipe, removal of aspect dancing, and a faster way of resurrecting our pet outside of Heart of the Phoenix. This list is, of course, perhaps asking a bit too much. Less clunkiness would be a large step in the right direction, though; this class is very keybind-heavy (I have over 80 keybinds at the moment) and is not all that easy to master in PVP.

What spells do you use the least? Widow Venom due to its "high" focus cost, Serpent Sting in PVP, Distracting Shot (although when I use it, it's usually for a darned good reason), Immolation Trap, Wing Clip (I have it macro'd to disengage for PVP), Aspect of the Wild.

-My most prominent quality-of-life issue is the waiting queues for heroics. I hate, hate, hate having to wait half an hour for a single troll heroic only to either miss the dungeon pop because I AFKed out for only a minute or to get stuck with a lousy tank. Repair bills suck, but thank you for Feign Death!

What makes playing your class more fun?
-Pets, Feign Death, Disengage, traps, and being able to /cackle at Horde after I've frozen them, waiting for their death to strike.... >D

Roleplaying aside, I do very much love taming pets. The 4.2 pets have been absolutely fantastic. It's awesome when my friends come over to wait until the rare pet spawns. We talk, and even one time my former raid leader told a really creepy story. (Former because he gave raid leader to another person, not because I/he quit.)

Also, I love the quirky things that make hunters unique to all of the other ranged dps. Who else is able to make people think you're dead when you're really spying on them, waiting for the best moment to strike? Who else can stop you in your tracks before flying backwards, unleashing a torrent of arrows (or bullets, if that's your preference) at your face?

To say the least, I really do love my class. A lot. To add the icing on the cake, there's so much lore with night elven hunters, so I don't cringe like with holy undead priests.

What makes playing your class less fun?
-Lack of an OSHI button. (Feign Death not counted. That's a wipe scenario.) There's no self-heal besides Chimera shot (I'm MM), so I feel a bit screwed when my healer is too busy to heal me and there's nothing I can press to keep myself alive. Chimera is okay, but it'll only keep you alive for maybe a few seconds before you're down. I know potions can solve this, but potions tend to be expensive and even then I'd prefer to use one to help my dps. If worse comes to worse, I'll probably just start investing in healing potions, but a hunter can still hope, right?

Pets are also a bit of a problem, as I can't really use my spirit beasts in raiding very well as I'm MM. It'd be nice to have them out, and that would solve my self-heal problem, but I'm afraid it would hurt my dps very badly.

Another concern of mine is close-range. This isn't much a problem for me, as most bossfights do not require me to be close to them, but when I am as someone else stated my dps shoots to zero. I guess it's fair play with the melee, though, as they tend to have to move around a lot.

Oh, and queues suck as mentioned above, but they're more likely to go back to being a druid. This is for things that would be nice, but I can work around.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
-My rotation is perfectly fine at the moment. It's simple enough for me to be able to pay attention to other things, but not button-mash worthy.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
-A self-heal (hopefully not one that will unbalance the class, as I'd hate to be nerfed), Volley, Eyes of the Beast, and to have a snake trap / fire trap that hits a bit more.

What spells do you use the least?
-Snake trap (tentatively), Cobra Shot, Distracting Shot, Scare Beast, Raptor Strike, and Deterrence.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Being able to dps on the move, and the ability to highly customize the pet to be a very strong and meaningful addition to the group. Instant shots, mobility tricks, and badass pets are among the things that make hunters awesome.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Minimum range! Both in PvE and PvP this causes constant problems, often when it feels like the designers forgot about hunters’ minimum range when designing quests or boss encounters (though Cataclysm has been far better in the boss category — usually by removing our minimum range on bosses where we have to clump). It is, of course, a nightmare of related issues in PvP, which I think heavily contributes to the poor hunter representation and harsh skill cutoff in pvp at this time. I honestly would be willing to have 0 self healing or lower damage then every class in the game if it meant minimum range was gone.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I feel like the MM rotation is a little bit tight and I’d love to see a bit more breathing room, perhaps by extending the duration of the Improved Steady Shot buff by just 2 more seconds so it was less punishing of small 1-second errors. Survival in particular suffers from a lack of dps coolowns, which makes it hard to burst on command in raid burn phases.

Please please please switch Beastial Wrath and Intimidation in the BM tree, intimidation is never used in PvE at max level, and BW makes much more sense as the 'signature ability' of BM, as the first thing everyone thinks of is Big Red Pet
