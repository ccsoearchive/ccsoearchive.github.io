What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

Raids

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

Definitely the minimum range. It's extremely frustrating to be unable to do anything if a fight requires being in melee range.

It would be nice to have an emergency focus regen ability with a moderate cooldown (like Fervor in the BM tree) available to all hunters.

I miss Eyes of the Beast. It was a nice and unique flavour ability. Please bring it back.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Kiting and, until recently, multi-trapping. I enjoyed being able to keep multiple mobs frozen, and was very upset when the ability to do so was taken away. It was fun, unique and challenging, and it did not meaningfully affect PvP or PvE apart from making some trash easier.

I like the threat managing tools that hunters have (FD, MD, Distracting Shot).

What makes playing your class less fun?

Again, minimum range.

No self healing ability (apart from the Draenei racial).

Trap launcher is often clunky and slow to activate. Making it a toggle ability instead of a temporary buff would help with this. That way, if you want to launch traps, you toggle your launcher on, and all traps will be launched until you turn your launcher off.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

Survival

For the most part, Survival is my favourite. However, there are a few issues I have with it.

First: If a boss fight requires both single target damage and AoE damage at different times, having Black Arrow tied to the fire traps does not allow the hunter to maximize both types of dps, since one or the other will usually be on cooldown.

Second: Requiring the hunter to stand still to maximize dps (Sniper Training) can be frustrating, as many boss fights require constant or near constant movement to survive.

Third: Survival feels more focus starved than Marksmanship, especially on aoe fights

Fourth: Arcane Shot using up a Lock and Load proc. Extremely irritating and no longer necessary.

Marksmanship

The MM rotation is good, though it can get a bit complicated when "haste plateaus" are taken into consideration.

Beast Mastery

Haven't really tried Beast Mastery enough to comment. The reason for this is that Kill Command is very picky about pet positioning, and I often found myself having to press the button for it several times before it would finally activate. Making Kill Command a 6 second buff on the pet that causes the pet to cast KC as soon as it is in range instead of requiring the pet to be in range first would go a long way to alleviate this.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Bring multi-trapping back.
Remove minimum range.
Make Fervor a base ability instead of a talent.
Give hunters a self healing ability.
Make trap launcher able to be toggled on/off as necessary instead of being a temporary buff.
Give Freezing Trap a bit more durability like Fear and Hex so it doesn't break on small hits or DoTs.
And, as others have said, dual pistols would be pretty cool.

What spells do you use the least?

Base abilities

Widow Venom
Raptor Strike
Wing Clip (could be removed altogether if the minimum range is removed)
Immolation Trap
Snake Trap (I think the last time I used this seriously was to help absorb the green slime explosion on Putricide)
Aspect of the Cheetah/Pack (daze makes these pretty much useless except for running back after a wipe)

Talents

Counter Attack. I don't think anyone has taken this talent since Deterrence was changed, and even then it likely saw very low use. Any hunter in melee range long enough for Counter Attack to activate is a very dead hunter.

Resistance is Futile. This does not belong in the Marksmanship tree, in my opinion. In fact, RiF could take the place of Fervor in the BM tree, and Fervor could be made a base ability for all hunters.

Might as well be able to use it whenever we want before a fight starts IMO, or be able to RP with it at will.

In a stage where 30 second dots can be applied by spammable abilities from other classes this make hunter CC obsolete in large environments.

10) ISS timer is too short for the cast times in pvp. Make it longer please.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both] PvE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other] Heroics, Raids, Soloing old Heroics and Raids

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? Pet death while in combat. Sometimes constant glyphed mend pet is not enough (this happens quite a bit with Firelands trash) and the Revive Pet cast time outweighs the benefit, because most raids and dungeons have some manner of movement mechanic that will interrupt it. Beast Mastery at the very least could do with Heart of the Phoenix being moved off of Ferocity's talent tree and moved in into BM's.

Outdated pet talent trees. These were the only talent trees in the entire game that didn't get the 'Cataclysm' treatment in 4.0 and it shows. There are a lot of 'must have' and 'must never have talents' in all three. That and a handful of talents that really didn't make a smooth transition from the mana to focus change.

Another issue: lack of situationally beneficial self healing. Beast Mastery's self healing is so small that it might as well not be there, the added 10% to all healing effects is nice for raid survivability, but it means more to someone actively getting damaged like a tank than it does to a ranged dps class which typically is going to be standing away from most sources of damage. Chimera shot provides decent healing over time, but since the shot itself is integral to a Marksmanship Hunter's rotation that healing cannot be used in a clutch situation. If, for some reason, the Hunter takes a large amount of spike damage Chimera shot doesn't provide enough healing to bring him out of the woods (ie: a couple of DoTs will still be able to finish him off) and even then it probably will not be off cooldown since it is at the heart of the Marksmanship rotation. Survival remains ironically the weakest spec in the game at survival since it has no damage mitigation of its own.

Let's also talk about being interrupted in focus generation. There are a lot of things that can do this. For instance, monster dies during your Steady/Cobra. Wasted time and no focus. This happens a lot when attempting to regain focus for AoE and another DD is on the same target. In order to squeeze another Multishot in you have to pick another target that can survive a Steady/Cobra Shot, and that someone else's AoE won't kill during it's cast. Moving on, some add is eating you during Steady/Cobra? Focus generation is delayed. Slow effect? Sitting Duck. Target breaks line of sight (especially when it shouldn't Ie: uneven floor, stairs)? More time and focus wasted. It would be really nice if some focus were returned, such as a percent based on how far along the Steady/Cobra was before it was interrupted. The point is to maintain some flow to your shots instead of waiting for the yellow bar to fill.

What makes playing your class more fun? Being a hunter is all about providing damage while being able to adapt quickly to changes in combat. Mobility and flexibility. Or more simply, running around and shooting things in the face.
People claim that Marksmanship is harder than the other two specializations, but I really think that it just requires more discipline. It has a very tight rotation that can easily be disrupted, but it is a fine thing when chaos is going on all around you and you can still manage to keep that rotation going.

I have the pleasure of raiding with a very decent Beast Master. I find the spec odd because in most situations where melee do well so does BM. They're an oxymoron, a ranged melee class. I think that concept works well.

I've stated before that BM is a ranged melee class, good concept, but they suffer from all of the same problems that melee suffer from and a few more. The biggest issue I think is that they don't have a unique ability like Aimed Shot or Explosive Shot. Intimidation had only one real way to shine, and that's been eclipsed by a glyph. It makes the spec feel a little bland, BM really needs something more than the big red angry pet to define it.

There's also again the issue of pet death, which hurts BM as much as say being disarmed of a gun. Only the disarm lasts ten seconds (minus haste!). And the disarm can be refreshed by any sort of movement. I find it odd that not only is there no talent to shorten Revive Pet, but that BM doesn't have better pet swapping. An ability that makes it so the BM can choose to use a certain pet for a certain phase of a boss fight for example. How about Chimera for an AoE phase and Spirit Beast for a raid damage phase? Would that not be in the spirit of the specialization?

Beast Mastery's talent tree has some bad points too. Pathfinding is archaic and even then was of very little use. I've already gone over why Spirit Bond is lackluster. Kindred Spirits would be nice for the other two specs, but BM is by far the easiest spec on focus generation, and so the talent isn't all that spectacular. The biggest disappointment however is actual Beast Mastery talent.

As I've stated before the pet talent trees are in need of renovation. Four extra points really doesn't do much more for any pet. However the biggest drawback is that exotic pets aren't that useful. What raid utility does any exotic pet bring that isn't already pretty common. Corehounds are the most useful by virtue of the fact that Shaman and Mages are less plentiful than Hunters. Not so with the Shale Spider whose buff is provided by Paladins and Druids. Chimera would be nice if their cone weren't hard to aim. The problem is that pets are used to fill missing buffs and Exotic Pets don't fill any more holes than the other common pets. An exotic pet would have to do something genuinely exotic in order to be worth taking. For instance, an ability that boosts BM's AoE on par with MM and Survival, or how about a non-melee pet for those pesky air phases?

Moving onto Survival, Black Arrow used to be fun. What happened? It was more than just another DoT when it provided a straight damage boost. You saved it on cooldown if you were coming up on Bloodlust, or procced Lock and Load and could spare a couple second to wait to use it. It turned into a watered down DoT that was buffed midway through Cata into a less watered down DoT. I don't care if it's damage is weak, I'd just like it to be a fun ability again.

Why is Arcane Shot still part of Lock and Load? Arcane Shot is now the focus dump for the class. That's a given. If you're near full focus when Lock and Load procs you have two options: one, hope your pet is in range and Kill Command (only viable use for it that Survival has), two Fire off a Cobra Shot and hope it doesn't cap your focus. Neither are particularly great options as they waste too much focus in one direction or the other. A single tic of Explosive Shot will hit as hard as Arcane Shot. Even when something is about to die it is still a better idea to fire an Explosive Shot and clip a tic than firing Arcane due to the fact that you might misjudge the monster's health and the remaining tics will finish it off. Wouldn't it be nice to use our focus dump as it was intended?

Survival has the dubious distinction of having one of the worst talents in the game. Counterattack. Aside from the fact that it's tooltip still lists it's mana cost, it also suffers from relying on the Hunter to parry in order to trigger. First, the chances of a Hunter parrying are phenomenally low, and second if by some miracle it does trigger and you've already used up every other kiting option you have the root only lasts 5 seconds. About the same amount of time that all those myriad slowing effects other classes have on you would last.

Hunter vs. Wild might actually be a halfway useful talent were it not for one problem. You must be able to heal in order to take advantage of more stamina. There are much better talents for raiding and any type of soloing the Hunter is going to avoid damage. The stamina would carry over to your pet, but there are better talents for it's health as well.

Mirrored Blades looks a lot better on paper than in reality. Yes it reflects. What caster is not going to stop casting on you when they see the telltale deterrence blade visual effect? The one that looks like a big neon sign that says 'don't attack me, I'm immune'.

Wyvern Sting, love it and hate it. Love it for the extra CC in dungeons where you can finish off trash just in time for it to wake up. Hate it when you use it and then need to CC the target again. How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.) I feel that Improved Steady Shot is the only reason I don't like Marksmanship. With the exception of AoE there is no reason not to keep the buff from this up at all times.
