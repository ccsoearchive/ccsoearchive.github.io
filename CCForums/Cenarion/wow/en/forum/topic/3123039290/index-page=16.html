* What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

PVE and PVP.


* If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

25 man normals and 25 man heroics

Rated arena, Battlegrounds, world PvP.


* What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo
could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

After playing a hunter with mana for 3 years, and having focused implemented, it is unanimous that focus regeneration in PvP is a huge problem, and the main focal point that causes our other issues.
-Focus Regeneration is TOO SLOW in PvP.
-It is too slow because of attack speed reductions (icy touch, infected wounds)
-Steady Shot is our ONLY real source of focus, yet its cast time is too slow in PvP, especially with previously mentioned attack speed and haste speed reductions.
-In raids, our focus is absolutely fine, which poses a problem because our focus is absolutely NOT fine in PvP.

* What makes playing your class more fun?

Nobody expects a Hunter to do too well, so actually out-playing someone is very rewarding.

* What makes playing your class less fun?

Trap Launcher: Last expansion, Hunters had a nice new ability called Freezing Arrow. You clicked freezing arrow, then you clicked the place on the ground, and the arrow would fire toward that spot and the trap would be laid down. It was a decent spell. Cataclysm removed it. You replaced it with Trap Launcher, which is ten times worse than Freezing Arrow. You CANNOT macro Trap Launcher to any traps, even though Trap Launcher is not on the global cooldown. Once you macro it to a trap and try to use it, the Trap Launcher is the only thing to activate, and it triggers a global-cooldown that is not supposed to exist. Then you have to click it a second time, and then click the spot on the ground.

For short, you removed Freezing Arrow even though it was far better and easier to use than Trap Launcher, which is un-fun, clunky and could be fixed if it was looked at.


WE ARE THE ONLY CLASS IN THE GAME WITH A RELIABLE SELF-HEAL, AND/OR MULTIPLE DEFENSIVE COOLDOWNS. IT HAS BEEN THIS WAY FOREVER, AND WITH HIGH HEALTH POOLS, I DO NOT NEED TO TELL YOU HOW HORRIBLE IT IS IN SMALL PVP SETTINGS.
-Sorry for the caps, but this is the worst part about playing a Hunter.


* How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
Marksmanship Rotation (PVE) - Fun and reactive, not bad at all.
Survival Rotation (PVE) - Not bad, but survival abilities don't scale with weapon damage.
Beast Mastery (PVE) - Not a forgiving rotation, and not fun at all.
Marksmanship (PVP) - Horrific. Our main attacks cost too much focus and then we have to sit there for 10 seconds trying to regain some focus and get some steady shots off (assuming your opponent does not LoS you, go into your melee range, etc.)


* What’s on your wish list for your class?
-Better focus regeneration in PvP. The only reason focus works in PvE is because of attack speed buffs from raids and being able to stand still for so long.
-EYES OF THE BEAST. PUT IT BACK IN THE GAME. That is what we call killing fun.

-A self-heal on par with Recuperate, Enraged Regeneration, or something completely new. Many many hunters want the Spirit Beast ability 'Spirit Mend' to be a baseline hunter ability. It is a fun, unique heal that a Hunter's pet can use on the hunter, or his allies. It heals for a set amount instantly, and applies a healing-over-time effect on them. It is dispellable as well, should you think it is overpowered.

* What spells do you use the least?
-Widow Venom
-Widow Venom
-Widow Venom
-Counterattack. (Seriously, this is ability is NOT USABLE.)

Not a single Hunter that plays in rated PvP EVER thinks about putting Widow Venom on his/her bars. It costs focus, a global cooldown, and ALL it does is do a healing debuff. It should be applied passively by Arcane Shot, like Mortal Strike, Frostbolt, Mind Blast or rogue auto-attack does passively.

I also liked certain animals having unique abilites, so that finding and taming a certain rarespawn gace you a unique animation or ability; now all pets in a family are the same and do the same damage, only the buff matters. I'd like to see pets go back to having individual flavor.


■What makes playing your class more fun? The abilty to solo old content is fun. Finding and taming rare pets is fun too. A reliance on pet management and having the pet play a significant part. Even though Marksman ends up always being max DPS, I don't run it because it almost an Archer spec and the pet is almost meangless for DPS.

Don't like MM, espeically having a shot that doesn't make sense to hardcast (aimed shot). I would like to see more shot synergy (e.g. having an armor reducing shot that enables the next physical damamge shot to be more effective, or something that provides an elemental damage enhancement). I'd like it to be more situational and not same rotation for every fight.


■What’s on your wish list for your class?

Dump Arcane Shot and make the shots more unique to specs. Arcane if for mages, not "Hunter-ish" at all. All out stuff should be natue, elemental or physical.

Make the specs even more unique. MM is an archer/sniper. BM is all about the pet and hunter ability to support and enhance the pet. SV is middle ground beweeen shooting and pet management and is all about traps and, well, survivabilty. Move Sniper Training talent to MM from SV. Give MM back the Volley ability, make it less damage than Multi-shot but much wider are, perhaps like Starfall.

BM needs a lot of work. It is only spec with its signature shot available to all specs (Kill command). I'd give BM a signature ability, perhaps like DK Army - Call Stable, which unleashes all 5 pets in the mobile stable at once to ravage the target for 30 seconds. That would be awesome ... or, make BM the Engineering of Hunters - let it stay last in DPS but give it fun stuff, like:
* a "Fetch" command that has your pet loot a distant corpse, like a loot-a-rang
* ability to ride your pet like a mount

Since BM is low man on DPS, give the spec some hybrid feel. Give BM an ability to possess their pet and melee like a druid, or even tank in an emergency. That could really provide something different for the hunter experience.

For SV, I'd like to see an increased role for traps. Perhaps all specs get the freezing and frost traps, but leave the other traps to SV and make them count. Immoliate should be nasty single target DPS while explosive should be a solid AOE. SV lacks a self heal, perhaps a Vampirc AOE trap, that channels damage to healing the hunter. An AOE CC trap, like a suspended net that snares all mobs within area.

I will preface this whole post saying it'll be from a MM PoV, with a BM subspec. I don't really have enough experience with the other specs to comment.

1. What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
Primarily PVE nowadays

2. If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
Heroics with a sprinkling of raids. Though I used to be a very active raider (my lack of raiding is due to work issues, not issues with WoW)

3. If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
If I were to PvP it would be BGs

4. What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Although not a specific deficiency, my issue is that the theoretically optional talents don't exist in the way I think was intended. Since most of the lower tier hunter talents are DPS increases, it doesn't make the choices nearly as difficult. What I'm suggesting is moving more the DPS increasing talents out of the lower tiers and leaving the more interesting ones. For example, moving Frenzy from the beast mastery tree down a tier so we'd be left with three "flavour" talents, if we were to be a subspec BM hunter, with either survival or MM as our main spec. My understanding was that "flavour" talents were not intended to compete with DPS talents.

5. What makes playing your class more fun?
The recently increased mobility with the moving autoshot is more fun, although less challenging to manage.

6. What makes playing your class less fun?
The change from mana to focus has still left a sour taste in my mouth. I've gotten used to it, rotationally, but it's clunky to be generous. If I wanted to play a rogue I would have re-rolled one.

7. How do you feel about your “rotation”?
The AoE rotation is a little iffy on trash pulls with relatively low HP, or once you're farming the content. The interaction of multishot, steady shot and rapid killing is nice when you land a killing blow midish way though, but if you don't you can end up focus starved and ineffective for the last 5 or so seconds or an AoE. I am comfortable with the single target rotation, considering I've mostly adjusted to using focus.

8. What’s on your wish list for your class?
Go back to more of a CD management/priority system such as the ret pally uses now/the way the system was throughout the previous expansion and move away from the focus system.

9. What spells do you use the least?
- Aspect of the Wild. For almost all PvE environments there's almost always a better way for the group to get resistances, much less just one type of resistance.

Thanks for reading! Good luck!

5. What makes playing your class more fun?

I started and stayed as a hunter because of the pet taming ability. It seems that with the hunter's pet, there are more variations then other classes might have.

6. What makes playing your class less fun?

I realize that hunters can, by definition, fight "hand-to-hand", but you quickly learn that this is not an effective option.

Larger stable for BM; larger entourage of pets a BM hunter can carry with him; ability to shoot at melee range.

Eagle eye. The rest I use fairly regularly.

# What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Minimum Range:
I believe my hunter companions have summed up any gripe I can think of in previous posts on this forum. However, I do offer solutions. In PvE the solution is simple: remove minimum range from bosses, or change where range begins, such as starting from the NPC graphic, not his hitbox. A warrior at minimum distance from a boss (for example, Marrowgar) can be over 5 yards away from me, but I am still unable to attack the boss (who is, graphically, 5 yards or more away from me.)
For PvP, minimum range is a handicap no other class is forced to experience. Removing it would be entirely counterproductive though, and would change class dynamic and cause us to be OP. To fix this, I think Aspect of the Fox should be removed and Aspect of the Dragonhawk brought back into the game. Trap damage should scale, especially snake damage, allowing us to do at least some form of damage.

The NEED to aspect swap:
Aspect dancing was, in theory, a mediocre idea at best. This tedious skill requires unnecessary macros in order to be properly executed, and I find can be a bit buggy. For example, when stacking steady shot pairs: I may be moving for the first one, but still for the second one. Lets say I want to switch aspects (off the GCD) between the 2 shots, while still stacking them as tightly as possible. 99% of the time when I attempt this skill, the server is unable to swap aspects between my shots, and instead of just staying in fox, it glitches and de-selects my aspect, effectively removing the buff from my player.
In my opinion, Aspect of the Fox should be removed completely, and instead replaced with a glyph much like ele shaman, allowing hunters to cast steady and cobra on the run (aimed would be OP). Or, remove the One with Nature talent (add the 2600 to the base AP, as well as make it a % buff so it scales with gear) and create talents allowing you to cast on the move.


Trap Launcher and CC:
Trap launcher is arguably one of the largest wastes of a GCD, as well as sometimes glitchy. IMO the ability should be removed from the Global CD, as this makes it very difficult to macro. I liked the idea back in wrath of an (Ice?) Arrow that shot a trap on the ground. Also, traps take too long to activate in PvP....high end arena is about reaction time, and waiting a full second on a trap to activate can be the difference between a killing blow. Continuing on the idea of CC, the need to Scatter before trapping is a waste of my scatter shot. It's stupid. I have to CC twice, using 4 clicks (if un-macro'd) to lay down an 8 second CC, which is easily broken if there is a DoT on the target. Fix it...I don't need to give you ideas.

This post is already long, but to make it short and sweet, they're messed up. Simple recon will show how bad pet movement is at the moment. Micro managing is annoying. I'll let the other hunters describe it in detail.
