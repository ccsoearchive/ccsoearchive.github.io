What makes playing your class less fun?

Given the fact that Camo is called Camo, WHY IS IT SO STUPIDLY EASY TO SEE? Why can't it get rid of our nameplate while it is active, and why can't the graphic be made to be similar to... I dunno, the cloak effect on Observers, Dark Templar and Banshees in Starcraft 2? I mean, you guys could do it there, so why not here? It would assure that somebody could spot the hunter if they were paying attention, and yet if the other player isn't paying attention the Hunter will have the advantage of surprise.

I like the direction you guys took with the trap launcher, but while it is a step in the right direction, it feels very clunky. I have to first load the trap launcher, then I must select the trap I want to drop, and THEN I need to aim it with complete accuracy. It certainly beats running into mob packs and risking death to drop traps, but it doesn't feel very smooth. Maybe toggling Trap Launcher to be an autocast would help or just a rework or traps entirely is in order.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

SV by far has the smoothest rotation. It feels the most fun, it has the right amount of buttons, and it has explosive shot. I can shoot exploding arrows and poison-tipped arrows at my enemies... that is awesome, 'nuff said. :)

Marksman feels a little bit both clunkier and a little bit more jumpy. Since most of its damage is physical, it feels strange to have to alternate to a different focus giving shot every time the opponent has high armor or high magical resistance to a certain type.

As for BM, I haven't played the spec since TBC so I can't comment on it.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

More exclusive weapons, mounts and abilities! One thing I would love to see if the ability for hunters to transmogrify their melee weapons into whatever they wish; I mean, our ONLY melee abilities hit like a wet blanket, does it really matter whether it's transmogrified into Farmer's Broom (http://us.battle.net/wow/en/item/3335) or the Edge of Agony? (http://us.battle.net/wow/en/item/47884) It's not like you're going to see Raptor Strike critting somebody for 100k... what gives? *shrug*

What spells do you use the least?

Widow's Venom. Not only is the focus cost stupidly high, but it is easily dispelled or ignored altogether in PvP. If there IS any effect, I barely notice in BGs.

Aspect of the Wild? We still have this in the game??? Honestly, I think with all the buffs and such in the game now, the usage and place of this ability in the Hunter spellbook is questionable at best.

Aspect of the Pack... given its groupwide daze, I rarely if ever use this. Maybe only if the group is alone in PvP or if we're running back from a wipe. That's it.

Beast Lore. Honestly, at this point? I think this should be something that Hunters can turn on or off like an Aspect ability; what's the harm in letting hunters being able to see whether they can tame something or not constantly or allowing them to see their pet's diet constantly? "Oh by the way, the fact that wolves eat meat means your pet gets a +50% damage bonus against Tauren." While it doesn't do that, I see no harm in this.

Distracting Shot, the only one of two shots that I do'nt have on my bars. It is too situational, and there is so much catastrophy that can result from its misuse (such as pulling the boss by mistake) that I keep it off of my bindings.

Raptor Strike. Even with the glyph, the usage I get out of this ability is nothing like it's less hard-hitting, snaring cousin Wing Clip. If you're spamming this at melee range, you're only delaying the inevitable downfall and hitting like a wet blanket. Other then that, I only use it if I'm passing close to somebody just to bleed a little focus away or to try getting a pole kill if I'm feeling obnoxious.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    PvP. I also happen to be quite good at PvE but I don't really care about it.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Whatever provides a challenge with the minimum group size. The heroics were great when they were hard for awhile.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
    Arenas and non-rated BGs. I'd prefer to focus on 2s, but those don't award titles and have a nasty amount of 40 minute matches sometimes.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    BM is the pet spec, but oddly, it has fewer pet choices if you want to optimize. I'd like all the families to have exotic abilities, and as many as possible to be different from each other and arena viable. Right now it feels like there's Spirit Beast or Devilsaur depending on your group's buffs, and then Silithids for defensive play.
    Rhinos would be awesome if commanding your pet to go to a location worked while it was still fighting, and the pet kept its autoattack on and strafed. Telling it to stop fighting, then stand at this spot, THEN punt the guy is too much work and telegraphs your intention of using an ability easily countered by movement.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    The fact that I move more than other classes and shoot at long range. All the other ranged classes feel like sticks in the mud (non-mages) or hyperactive annoying little dipwads (mages). Also, the fact that I can cover whatever raid buff we're missing and I actually add to the flexibility of our raid comp.
    Also, Disengage -> Kill Shot before they can find the charge button on a low health melee hugging my face is awesome.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    The "eatability" of my traps.

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    With PvE, I like my Survival rotation.
    In PvP (as marks or BM) I find the emphasis on range-only channeled spells very limiting. Letting us Steady or Cobra shot at point blank range, or just regen a little focus on a failed cast, would help a lot.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    Survival: Black Arrow is no longer a magic effect. (It's the most useless spec in arena right now partially because of its hideous dispel vulnerability.)
    BM: All pet families now have an exotic ability unlocked when their owner has the Beast Mastery talent.
    Pet Management: Utility of the "move to" command has been improved. Your pet now strafes and turns with the mouse!
    Rhino: The bleed buff has been replaced with something good in PvP (interrupt, stun, root, disorient... some might be very strange for an animal that size though). This is a Tenacity pet with a potentially awesome PvP utility exotic ability. The Tenacity part makes it need the help though - BM is very dependent on pet damage.

    What spells do you use the least?
    Snake Trap, because the damage is so tiny - I use it only as a root or as a casting debuff when I'm on the enemy healer and don't have to worry about my freezing trap breaking on damage. A glyph for Freezing Trap letting it absorb ~2k damage even before breaking could fix that - so could a buff to Snake Trap's damage.
    Immolation Trap, because it's not worth the GCD.
    Raptor Strike, but I SHOULD use it in arena for the glyph. I'm just lazy.
    Eagle Eye, because my graphics settings are good enough to see at range anyway.
    Widow Venom, it just doesn't do enough - although I suppose it could be dispel cover.
