What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both] PVE

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other] Raids

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs] Casual bgs, arenas

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Pet AI, traps, and the new aspects. I dont see the logic in changing the stances when the old one were more versatile and useful. Hunters already have the most buttons to press if you want to be the most effective. MD, traps, aspects, and all our shots makes a prepared hunter a thing to behold, but at the same time, the average person cant reach that level.

Most people choose the hunter class either because they love pets or it's the closest thing to an archer class there is. The pet AI kills both. The pet takes far too long for start attacking to make any difference while leveling since most hunters (avg) will not know to macro /petattack, at best, binding a key to pet attack. BM levels way too slow for the revamped experience since they dont get any pet speed increase buffs for a while With the changes to threat, pets are useless for MM and SV leveling as well since the pet cant get aggro fast enough.

Kill command as BM "main" move feels second class since other specs get it and do use it on occasion.

Trap launcher is a good idea, sucks in execution, 2 gcds for some utility or aoe? steep price to pay dps-wise.

Asepct dancing sucks and is done with a macro, does not add depth, all it did was have me play with the sound effects off.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Attacking on the move, finding new pets to tame, pets, pro trapping, massive amounts of utility. Being rewarded for good play (not so much now, feels more like punishment for not having enough macros or research online)

What makes playing your class less fun?

So many macros, not having the "downtime" as we are usually moving during steady/cobra. Having to micromanage a slow/stupid dot that is inconsistent.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

I'm ok with it, but the first and second tier talents tend toward confusion due to the nature of their placement.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Smart pet UI, separate buttons for launching our traps since most hunters i know macro em together anyways, but still have to have the original keybound for speed/close range traps.

A bow without a face.

I would love for the old MM style to make a comeback, The various stings made pvp fun without being OP and was true utility. Since "magic" is SV's flavor, you can go old school rpg and have MM aim at body parts for diff effects.

SV should have their serpent spread replaced by pro trap launcher and marks a spot on the ground that the next 3 traps you shoot will land with0.5gcd in between traps. New mastery to increase trap damage and a ability on ~1min CD to bring any trap off CD.

Serpent spread should be given to BM with a new flavor for the talent "you befriend the snakes in your snake trap and ...etc, but you lose the ability to use snake trap"

Which is a great idea for MM aoe, "Your bowarm is massive, you can now shoot immolation traps with your multishot and chimera shot, but your massive bicepts cant properly place a puny trap on the group any longer"

A real dps cooldown for BM hunters, I'm thinking an ability to attack with all your current pets a la Arcade fighting games "assist". That can be rolled in to a baseline ability that let pets attack flying mobs or at least a talent for pets to make up for lost time on target: "when your pet is out of range, it simmers with impotent rage and increasing the power of its next attack by XX"


What spells do you use the least?
Aspect of the Pack, utter crap for utility due to the daze, I get it for cheetah, we should be pro kiters, but why even have it if it can daze the party? currently only used for slightly faster wipe recovery.
Aspect of the Wild: redundant in most cases and not an option for most as the dps loss is too signifcant for the amount of resist.

What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

N/A

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

1. Pet health when dismounting. This is the number one QoL problem I experience.
2. Pet bar disappearing when zoning.
3. New pet stances. I dislike the loss of aggressive mode. While it's mostly useless, there are a few PvE encounters where it is extremely useful, and from what I've read it has some place in PvP. I would also like a way to get the old defensive mode, which would be a combination of the current defensive and assist modes.

What makes playing your class more fun?

I like the versatility of the Hunter, we can pull our weight in a raid, and yet we are also one of the best soloing classes.

What makes playing your class less fun?

I understand the "Bring the player, not the class" philosophy, but this has lead to the situation where a lot of the "utility" that hunters have, especially survival (my main spec), is no longer used.

Oh yeah, one other "situational gripe." The "minimum range override" has been applied to quite a few mobs where it makes a great deal of sense, e.g. Erudax during shadow gale. Please, please please, pretty please with sugar and a cherry on top, apply it to Magmaw. Please. The lack of that override makes this encounter less fun for me as a hunter due to the strategy that we use.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

The one minor gripe I have, and no idea how to fix it ,is a sense that there are "Haste sweet spots". Getting cobra's cast time to 1.66 (repeating, of course) means you get exactly three cobras between each explosive. The next "sweet spot" here is 1.25 cast, which would fit in four. I'm not sure it's possible to get that much haste, but without it, there's a sense of dead time waiting for explosive. "Do I put in another cobra, and delay explosive, or do I just sit and twiddle my thumbs waiting for it to come off CD?"

What’s on your wish list for your class?

Fixing QoL issue #1. Period.

What spells do you use the least?

Counterattack, Raptor strike, Mongoose bite TBH, I'm not sure how many of those are even still available to me after the Cata changes. Granted, that's from a PvE perspective, I suspect that a PvP player might disagree with me, and I can respect that.

  • All - even War Games.




What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

Minimum Range:

  • PVE - Almost ignored on Raids as of late, changing the Hit Box Mechanics of bosses to allow hunters to use Steady and Cobra Shot whilst within the natural character model is a welcomed change. Exception - Baleroc. This fight has caused me to die or take too many stacks of the Shard debuff due to hunters' inability to damage Baleroc IF the shard spawns close to his Hit Box (as it has a tendency to do). The result is that I was simply removed from the shard rotation for my raid team for the sake of convenience and smoothness. This removal I feel is a consequence of an oversight on Blizzard's part for the mechanics of the fight, especially for 10-man Raiding teams where DPS selection is more limited.


  • PVP - Minimum range, even with movement and defensive abilities such as Disengage, Deterrence, and the effect of the Glyph of Raptor Strike, this limiting factor seems more like an exploit on part of enemies (ESPECIALLY in Arena) then a legitimate class mechanic that should exist.


Focus Regeneration:

    • PVE - Golden here. Rotation has it covered.


    • PVP - Defense against ranged and melee? Hiding behind a pole. Defense from a Hunter? Let them hide behind a pole. With almost no focus regeneration beyond using Steady or Cobra Shot, even with BM's Fervor, Hunters are crippled by pole humping or LoSing


    • What makes playing your class more fun?

    • The pet system is unique to Hunters in that unlike with Mages and Warlocks, where their pets seem like personality-less, slaves, Hunter Pets have a person and loving relationship with their masters. Being able to add custom names to our pets add that little level of personal touch that I understand many WoW players are calling for, and Blizzard seems to be responding in greatly positive way (i.e. Transmogrification). This is a great uniqueness to the class.


    • If you want ranged, you have a wealth of choices! Magic users and Hunters. As the only non-magic (mostly) ranged class, and the closest thing to the archer archetype, Hunters are the only choice for a class of players whose preferences exist in all Fantasy MMOs.



    • Unlike other classes that can solo a lot of content in the game, Hunters are all inherently DPS, Tanking, and Healing classes! Switching to Beast Mastery whilst wearing Hunter Tier 5 (2 set bonus) allows for soloing of even Wrath of the Lich King Raid content! These are feats only available to Death Knights for the most part. Pure awesomeness.




    What makes playing your class less fun?


    • Consistent bugs that persist between patches (and expansions), that provide minor inconveniences for very specific instances and circumstances in the game. Example: Pet health deficit when summoning or reviving (initially).


    • Removal of Eyes of the Beast.


    • Talent Tree builds are very much simplified with little to no wiggle room within the range of both maximizing pure DPS builds (BM, MM, SV) or even PVP utility/DPS (BM, MM, SV -we all know SV is terrible for PVP)




    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    • I find absolutely nothing wrong with Hunter rotations, using varied haste ratings and specializations, and builds.




    What’s on your wish list for your class?


    • Bug fixes.


    • Dual wielding single handed ranged weapons for either MM or SV specs as an end Talent Tree option (much like how Single Minded Fury works for Warriors).


    • "Stampede" ability for BM, which would call pets to the hunter (or the current available 5) which would either just do damage, or apply use of all of their abilities, utilizing dormant mechanics not currently being used.


    • Revival of the Talent Tree for SV. In particular the removal of Counterattack with something either useful or something fun, at least something that would actually be based off of a stat that Hunters use.



    What spells do you use the least?


    • Counterattack


    • Beast Lore - Not asking to remove it.


    • Immolation Trap



    -------------------------------------------------

    I've been playing since The Burning Crusade. Blizzard is doing/has done a great job balancing and listening to Player feedback. Keep up the good work.

    -What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
    PvE only.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
    Right now due to my work schedule, sadly only heroics. But I absolutely adore raiding; I've done it extensively and at mid-to-upper levels in the past, and I'm looking to get back into it when I can.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
    No more ammo was nice. My biggest issues are our dead zone and pet death. While it's true we don't have much of a "dead zone" where someone or something can stand and we can neither melee nor ranged attack it (it's there but it's about a millimeter wide)--we can do so little in melee that it is functionally dead for us. Some bosses are adjusted to compensate for this, so we can stack when we need to stack, and that's fantastic! Others are decidedly not. For example, Daakara in ZA--when he goes into bear form and charges me, others often aren't standing far enough out, so it's a choice between getting charged with the Surge debuff already on me because my other groupmates just aren't thinking about my deadzone or swinging at him with my staff because his hitbox is way more massive than it has any right to be.

    As for pet deaths, they're more of an issue in heroics trash than on raids. Even when I assist the tank religiously, and even when I have /petattack macroed into my shots with my pet on passive so I know it's only ever attacking the tank's target, I'll still look up and find my pet at a sliver of health if not dead. And when my pet dies, I have to make that decision--do I stop dps for 10 seconds, which is an eternity in this game, or do I continue with gimped dps and no pet buff?

    Another minor issue is the fact that pets spawn with a tiny health pool while its proper health level is calculated--a poorly-timed strike can easily one-shot a freshly called pet.

    A final minor issue is the fact that hunters slip through the cracks on boss strategies every now and then. For example, Ozruk gives you two ways to avoid the um... the freezy petrification debuff, it's been a while since I ran Stonecore. You can either melee him or get a dot reflected at you. I can't melee without doing a silly dance, and I can't get Serpent Sting reflected, so that sucks. It's rare! But it happens.

    What makes playing your class more fun?
    I've always loved ranged classes. I'll be happy as long as I am one.

    What makes playing your class less fun?
    Range-dancing as discussed in quality of life issues. "Oh, it's on me... feign. Oh, it's on me again... um... strafe in circles, annoy the melee... deterrence and stand there doing nothing... herp derp"

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
    I absolutely love the Surv rotation of watching for LnL procs for ultra-dps, although it's REALLY annoying accidentally burning a charge with an Arcane Shot I was queuing up before the proc. I'm not a fan of MM's micromanagement of Improved Steady Shot, and BM's constant need to "err, did that KC go off or not?" irks the snot out of me.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?
    I mean, we're hunters. We're ranged. You can't just make it so LOL OK YOU CAN BE IN MELEE ALL THE TIME NOW. But your continued attention to bosses that require stacking would be fantastic, as would a way to burn a short "Point Blank" cooldown (10sec every 2min or so) that would allow us to fire in melee range. I get that this has PvP considerations, but you're smart, you can make it work. Please. D:

    What spells do you use the least?
    Beast Lore, Master's Call, Flare, Immolation Trap, Raptor Strike, and Scare Beast.

    What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]

    Both, but mainly PvE.

    If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]

    Predominantly Raids, some "Heroics," the occasional Solo of old raid content, and anything with an achievement.

    If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]

    Arenas and BG's, Rated BG's when I have enough guildmates.

    What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

    Fixing the pet control bar bug and the bug that causes my pet to be summoned with hardly any health is VERY annoying, nothing is worse than having a pet just summoned be one-shot by a mob. Removal of the minimum range would be a dream come true, the skill curve on hunter PvP is outrageous, a decent hunter versus a decent player of any other class automatically puts the hunter at a significant disadvantage. When the no-brainer solution to fighting a hunter becomes "get in close" regardless of the situation it suggests that there is a flaw with the game design; players need to be forced to think about their actions and have repercussions as a result. Regarding traps, if they all were usable like Freezing Arrow back in Wrath that would be FAR and away better than the current system where we have to waste 2 global cool downs and then target the area... oh how I miss you Freezing Arrow. And if Hunters could dual wield ranged weapons that would be outstanding as well. Aside from those issues I would say hunters have never had it so good as we currently enjoy.

    What makes playing your class more fun?

    The feel of playing a Hunter can't be beat by any other class. Being able to fire off an Explosive Shot, Concussive Shot then being able to jump Disengage and land going running in the same direction (away from a mob) and watch it slowly die... Priceless.

    What makes playing your class less fun?

    Summoning a tanking pet to pick up a mob after the tank dies and having it get one-shot because it has no health as a result of a bug is VERY annoying. Also, watching something eat my face off and not being able to hit it back or do anything about it can be infuriating. And yes I am fully aware of the utility of Feign Death, Deterrence, all our slows etc...

    How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)

    I don't think that the Survival rotation could get any better, at times it can be boring but personally I enjoy the reactionary aspect of the rotation. Marksman seems rushed to me, if there was a bit more free time to do things without worrying about the Improved Steady Shot buff wearing off. As for the Beast Mastery rotation, I can't truly say; it seemed very awkward to me the few times I tried BM.

    What’s on your wish list for your class?

    Fix the pet bugs mentions above, remove the minimum range, grant dual wielding of ranged weapons, making traps less clunky by reverting back to the one button launching ability that we so fondly embraced in Wrath. Being able to tame druids would always be nice as well.

    What spells do you use the least?

    I never use Immolation Trap, Widow Venom, Counterattack; occasionally Beast Lore but even that is a very rare occasion and really the only one of those listed that poses any real value to me. Seeing a how my stables are full it is only used to tell other players if a potential pet is tamable for them on their hunter alts.
