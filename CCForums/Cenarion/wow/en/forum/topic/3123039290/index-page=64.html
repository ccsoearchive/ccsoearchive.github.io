I pve and pvp. Some raiding, bg’s and some arena.

Quality of life issues:

I’ve never been comfortable with focus, it’s really clunky and messy and feels unfinished/primitive. It would be helped with something like instant shot cobras, maybe doing a fraction of the regen/damage but I’d rather shoot off 5 instants to get the focus I need for the big shots. This would also help the problem of casting a cobra then having the target die before it finishes which then leaves you to start over. That is so very, very annoying. Hunters just should never have to stop and stand still / have cast times.

Multi shot is a mess for trying to target. It should also cost less focus even if that meant nerfing the damage a bit. Trying to cobra shot and having the target die just before it finishes is bang your head on the keyboard time.

Disengage – would like to be able to disengage more often. Shorter distance but more often? Unlimited but costs focus and/or on the gcd? Maybe get rid of Aspect of the Wild and instead put in Aspect of the Hummingbird. This aspect would let you constantly disengage but your distance and damage would only be ½ normal? That would be in addition to the usual disengage, not in place of it. Or maybe instead of that, some kind of melee range push back/shove.

I want pet buff food again. It was really fun to make these treats and then feed my pet with them. Hunters have almost no active interaction with their pets any longer. It’s sad and really takes away from the hunter experience. For lol’s…how bout a pet groomer, where you can change the color, cut, etc. And also finally add that collar with gem slots I’ve heard mentioned long ago….maybe with a glyph too.

Kill shot shouldn’t be two ½ strength shots. Bring it back as a real finisher like it was before.
Widow’s venom is so poor. Would be nice to have it mean something. Reduces healing by 50 percent for 10 seconds. Only usable every 20 seconds. Or reduces healing by 75 percent for 10 seconds, shares cooldown with explosive, kill command, etc. Only usable every 30 seconds.

Bring back the old pet stances, including Aggressive. Any good pet should be able to tell when there is a threat nearby and act to stop it.

Pets should jump with us...follow closely...not take the scenic / slow and often dangerous route, pvp and pve.

I really miss freezing arrow. Or have it be a glyph so that you can have freezing arrow instead of trap.

I've spent most of my time using survival for pve. However, I think it could be cool to change that spec to become a primarily defensive spec, designed to mitigate the damage from other players in pvp or from bosses, mobs in pve. Damage could be secondary to defense/suppression. Wow...maybe that could be a new class role....suppressor or equalizer...

The way our class has been modified, nerfed, broken and often ignored, I have to admit any new redesign scares the crap out of me. You almost lost me for good with the launch of Cata and I’m not sure how many more heavy changes I can stand. Be careful with hunters. We’ve been through a lot and deserve careful, thoughtful and gentle treatment for a long while to come.

Answering the original questions by Nethaera in order:


- I focus both on PvE and PvP.

- In PvE, I tend to favor raids.

- In PvP I favor Rated BGs and Arenas, that is ranked PvP.

- Some quality of life improvements could be things like a more favorable variety for pets rather than the current way pets are handled as buffs and debuffs. Regardless of the Cataclysm changes, which looked good on paper but didn't quite execute the way I'd hoped it be, most builds and raid makeup favor only a very limited selection of pets or the actual variety of pets feels a little too bloated with certain ones feeling useless in certain situations. If the goal of the new pets systems was to encourage a greater variety of pets in PvE and PvP, it did not quite work. Yes, the end of Wrath of the Lich King had the Hunter mantra "Wolf or bust!" but it's now only up to 3-4 pets with all the others out there being left in the stables collecting the proverbial e-dust. I believe that if pets instead provided a more unique variety of spells and utility, a larger variety of them would be favored compared to every other Hunter in PvE using a Wolf or Wind Serpent / Ravager and in PvP using a Spider or Silithid. The only other quality-of-life issue I see being a major problem is the restriction of range on Shots and Stings. All other ranged classes have no range restriction in combat with a target while Hunters still must negotiate a 3-5 yard gap between a target to be effective. If someone reaches melee range on a Hunter, they are left dead in the water with only a small amount of spells to rely on. If Hunters did not have a range restriction, I believe they would be more desirable in PvP situations or certain PvE encounters that favor a point-blank range during the fight. (i.e Chimaeron, Valiona and Theralion, Lord Rhyolith, Beth'tilac, etc.).

- The things that make playing Hunters more fun is the variety of pets available for the player to tame so that they have a unique pet rather than the typical small handful of pets like a Warlock is restricted to right now. I also find it enjoyable certain pets have emergency spells for high-pressure situations compared to other classes.

- The least fun thing about Hunters is the lack of unlimited range on Shots and Stings, for the most part. It is not the most enjoyable thing to be in Arenas or BGs where you are forced to deal with melee players and attempting to negotiate that minimum gap between you and the target to keep the damage rolling. Yes, it adds a layer of challenge, but I believe it's a layer of challenge our class can do without. It is also not as enjoyable being a Hunter and feeling somewhat pigeonholed into certain pet choices to keep competitive in PvP or PvE.

- Rearding rotations, I will cover each spec respectively as I have used all 3 in both PvE and PvP:

-In regards to Marksman rotation, I feel that the current version is almost in that perfect ground between routine and sporadic so that it stays interesting during long periods of quiet moments during an encounter, however certain talents feel forced in Marksman (Master Marksman, Careful Aim, Improved Steady Shot) during the rotation in that it does not allow for some play during a movement-heavy encounter so that the player can compensate for instant shot spamming while on the move or having to Aspect-dance between Fox and Hawk while on the move so forcefully.

- Beast Master rotations I think border on too simplistic and not enough interesting talents and Cooldowns or special procs to keep the player on their toes. The rotation feels too blunt to the point that all it is is "Build up Focus, then dump it with one attack." If there were more interesting CDs to pop that cost focus or more than just the Focus Fire proc, I think the spec would become much more enjoyable.

- Survivalist rotations are a little TOO sporadic to the point during a Lock N' Load proc, it feels like playing Whack-A-Mole. I feel that the current version of Survivalist relies too much on bouncing between Explosive Shot and Black Arrow, even in PvP, to the point that waiting for a Lock N' Load so that you could whittle down that target faster in that it leaves no room to blow a CD to either attack or defend.



- If I were too boil it do some simplistic requests, Hunters for any upcoming Cataclysm patches just need less restrictions on min-max range and adjusting the specs to add more utility during PvE and PvP than just a simple Freeze Trap or an armor reduction debuff. I have a much longer wish list for Hunters, but it wouldn't fit in this post.


- My least used spells, in no particular order, are Raptor Strike, Snake Trap, Beast Lore, Eagle Eye, and Feed Pet (is there really a point to using Feed Pet when Mend Pet takes under 10 seconds for your pet to get to full health after a rez or heavy combat?).

 What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.

Minimum Range is one of the biggest QOL issues for me. We are the only range class that is required to be at a minimum range and while I understand where the need for this came from, currently melee classes have better gap closers than we do escapes. If we focus on nothing but escaping we can maybe get away but end up doing no dps. Either better ability to escape melee range or no minimum range would be nice.

4pc PvP set bonus is disappointing. 5% extra focus regen takes way too much time to even amount to anything.

Taken from Katalyst's A Cry For Help thread:
Steady/Cobra Shot - Yes, these are good to gain focus, but the damage that they do versus their cast time is depressing (IF your target doesn't just LoS you or cast a slow on you, leaving you empty-handed when it comes to focus regen). LoS is perfectly fine as it is part of the art of playing PvP. I suggest buffing the damage by a small amount and making it immune to slowing effects (cast time, not movement speed).

Pet health - By now we've all encountered the glitch. You manage to get off a Revive Pet cast only to have your faithful companion come back to life with ~5k health, making it an easy target for someone (or something) who acts quickly.

Glyph of Kill Shot - Amazing glyph! But recently we haven't always been getting our "instant" cooldown, making this glorious glyph questionable for its reliability. In many situations, relying on this glyph can now be your entire downfall.


Focus Regen – Tying into the Steady/Cobra shot issue. In PvP where you are constantly slowed and LoS’d relying on a 2 sec cast time ability(is more like 2.5-4 sec if slowed) to regen focus is pretty harsh and results in constant focus starvation. Oh and the fact that our passive regen is slowed by effects as well is just…I don’t even have words for it. I like the core idea of the shots but think that Focus would work better with a higher passive regen and a lower gain from the shots.

Traps/Trap Launcher – Fire and Snake traps do not scale and hit for pitiful amounts of damage. The DoTs snake trap puts up only cause us to break our only CC. Freezing and Frost traps sharing a cooldown is also an issue. One of our only CC’s is tied to the same timer as an important kiting ability. This also ties in with the minimum range issue, where if we have to use Frost trap to kite away from melee we now have no CC ability. As far as trap launcher goes it takes too long to for the trap to travel and arm causing us to have to use scatter shot followed by trap launcher followed by Freezing trap in order to CC (If the random pathing of scatter shot doesn’t just make the person run away from said trap).

Deterrence – The general idea of this ability is great. It is supposed to be an oh **** button and allow us to survive for a few seconds and put some distance between us and the enemy (and hunter’s definitely need survivability). However, with its current mechanics we often don’t live. The ability does not remove DoTs or bleeds and is unusable while we are CC’d resulting in us taking tons of damage before being able to use the ability and then dying to DoTs while in it.



Basically with all of these issues it boils down to hunters not having a place in PvP. There is no utility we bring to the table that would make someone want to take a hunter. After all, everything that a hunter can do a mage can do better; whether its damage, kiting, CC, Slows, and survivability, all are performed better by a mage.
