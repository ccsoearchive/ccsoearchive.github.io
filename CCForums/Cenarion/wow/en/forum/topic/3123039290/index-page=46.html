What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
PvE and casually PvP

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
-normally just random BGs, have done some arena, never got into it really

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
-Having to be at a minimum range away from mobs is pretty annoying. No other ranged class has to so i dont understand, from a PvE perspective, what the issues would be if we didn't have that handicap. Honestly i cant think of much more than that as a quality issue, personally with the removal of ammo and the addition of focus, the hunter class has felt much more dynamic to me than it has in the past where buying ammo was a necessity and carrying mana potions wasn't a silly thing to do.

What makes playing your class more fun?
-Playing a class that is ranged, can utilize instant attacks instead of having to cast everything, being able to channel steady/cobra shot while running, able to pump out high damage, and has the utility to do almost anything has been what keeps me into this game above all. Hunter was my first class i chose and is and probably always will be my favorite.

What makes playing your class less fun?
-Honestly not much, its less fun when i have less to do. I play a night elf hunter so i have shadowmeld and when 2 aggro drops (Smeld and FD), and a Misdirect, doesn't get an add out of my face (tank issue as well) then i cant dps it and it can be annoying.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
-I've always had fun with it, the tools given to hunters make for a fairly easy PvE experience in my opinion versus other classes such as a warlock. I have never felt overwhelmed with having to use 19 different abilities before it rolls over back to the first, but also have never fell asleep because i bound all my keys to one shot and drummed my fingers for an hour, so i have also never felt as though i played a generic class. Always exciting basically.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
-Maybe some new trap types such as shadow traps or lightning traps, possibly a new DoT to work with, and the above mentioned frost arrow idea, though i'm sure it would be a bit anti-pvp. Also possibly pets that bring different buffs/debuffs to the raid, or possibly healing. It would be nice to remove the NEED for a pet and replace it with the CHOICE of a pet, such as a passive buff if you do not have one out for solo or 5 mans, and the normal benefit from having one for things such as raiding.

What spells do you use the least?
-In PvE i find i have never felt the need to use Widow Venom on anything, or a snake trap. I can count on one hand the amount of times i have used Scare Beast. Other classes have made it so i never use nature resist aspect.

What type of content do you focus on?

PVE

If PvE, what type of PvE?

Raids (normal)

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?

The melee range zone on some bosses is limiting. Beth'tilac and Rhyolith I am forced to move out of Healing Rain and other AOE heals sometimes so that I can shoot. Baleroc is just not much fun since I have to move in to take a crystal.

Ranged weapons taking huge, quick durability hits. I like how there's a repair NPC at the start of Firelands, but before, I'd need to repair my bow before anyone else needed to.

What makes playing your class more fun?

Disengage. I've mastered the Jump-Disengage to use it as a hunter charge and it's just fun. I love jump-shot kiting (and kiting in general).

I love taming rare and unique pets and this is the huge draw for me. I love the challenge tames. The amount and variety of pets available keeps the game fresh.

I absolutely love hearing the sound of a gun and I prefer them over sissy elf bows. I miss having a gun in both tiers of Cataclysm so far.

I really enjoy my rotations and being able to do them while keeping my mobility up is really what's kept me raiding on my hunter. I thoroughly enjoy the instant cast ranged physical dps playstyle. I absolutely love the acrobatic feel and the fast pace the class presents.

I actually love the change from Mana to Focus (Focus just "makes sense" for hunters) for PVE. For PVP, it's just a major pain in the butt.

What makes playing your class less fun?

Melee range, especially on boss mechanics that want it. Alysrazor and Staghelm were great but Baleroc, Beth, and Shannox's dog are all sucking the fun out of pew pewing.

While I enjoy the tight rotation based on Focus, I despise having Improved Steady Shot fall off because I had to move during Steady Shot or it clipped off at the end as I moved.

The lack of defensive survival abilities has me saddened, especially on fights like Baleroc or Staghelm. I've got nothing to stave off some boss damage such as Shannox, Beth, or Rhyo. Deterrence doesn't work on a lot of boss abilities. I like to think that dps players can be more responsible for their survival but hunter's don't have that open to them.

How do you feel about your “rotation”?

I enjoy all three specs. I find them interesting and fun in different ways. BM is my favourite just because I always see hunters having a strong and special bond with their favourite pet.

The MM rotation can get skewed by procs, buffs, and ISS falling off at weird times. Otherwise, I love it.

I find the SV rotation far too proc dependent on Lock and Load. The spec seems to have no focus or flavour like the BM and MM trees obviously have. Is it a melee and trap focus spec with Entrapment, Black Arrow, and Counterattack? Is it a poison based spec with Wyvern Sting and Improved Serpent Sting? Is it a fire spec with Explosive Shot? I just can't figure out what its flavour is.

BM is fairly simple but the short cooldown on Bestial Wrath makes it fun. My gripe is that pet control has been very poor lately and there's a few bosses with pet pathing issues or more chance of pet death which makes BM a very poor choice.

What’s on your wish list for your class?

More pet families, the ability to choose the talent tree, and more rare and unique pets. I love a good camp and a good challenge tame.

Defensive capabilities in raiding. I love that we can bring different pets to fill buffs, but as a hunter, I personally don't offer anything besides dps.

Disengaging out of combat. I just love this ability and the loss of it being only in combat was very disheartening. I like to practice my jump-disengage and can't do that out of combat.

Silencing Shot is great! Except it has a longer CD so I can't be a reliable interrupter. It's MM only so that's more limiting. There's a pet in each family that has an interrupt, but they're on a 1 minute CD. I'd like to see Silencing Shot a base ability (or earlier in the tree) and with a shorter CD.

I'd also like to be able to use 2 one handed weapons. There's some that are really cool looking and with great stat optimization but we can't use them since there's no enchant that's worth it.

What spells do you use the least?

Aspect of the Cheetah, Pack, and Wild. Cheetah and Pack are for running back from wipes in places you don't have mounts.There's just too much raid wide AOE and stuff going on to be able to use the Daze aspects. I usually just Jump-Disengage. I'd love if the Daze aspects were instead run speed boosts for our raid in combat.

I rarely lay down traps. Snakes just suck period. I hate Trap Launcher. The launch + arming time makes it silly to use on moving targets.

I don't often use Deterrence, Counterattack, Scatter Shot, Scare Beast, Flare, Camouflage, Beast Lore, Master's Call, Distracting Shot, Widow's Venom, Wing Clip, Raptor Strike.

What type of content do you focus on?
Personally, I do a mix, Raiding, PvP in arenas and Bgs. I recently changed guilds, and with that, rated Bg's are on the table.
If PvE, what type of PvE?
I raid, and I do heroics, but not happily. I feel that an alternative means of gaining points might be needed, or at least there need to be greater variety in encounters. I don't like taking on Jin'do only an hour after the last time I beat him, I don't like taking him on each day, but I also don't want to queue knowing that I'll have to burn through easy fights seven times with almost no chance at a substantial reward as a DPSer once I've out geared the content of old heroics.
What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
Pet pathing would be the #1 issue. I was on a 2v2 arena team for some time with another BM hunter, and we knew that if we got Blade's Edge arena, we would almost certainly lose, because a smart player can simply jump down and force our pets to run all the way down to the ground, and then simply do it again. In addition, the removal of aggressive stance was unnecessary and honestly was a small blow to me in situations where I vastly overlevel content, such as clearing an old dungeon for a particular objective, and must power through hordes of trash groups that I have little interest in. I would suggest remaking aggressive stance, making assist the new defensive, establish the current defensive as a third stance called 'selective target' and keep passive.
As far as that goes, also change the current system of pet aggro. I frequently find myself fighting my pet for control of a target I wanted to attack, while my pet is now fighting me for aggro of the target I TOLD him to attack. I don't want a pet that 'interoperates' my targeting as commands, but sticks to the commands I give him until ordered, with the stance perimeters as guidelines.
What makes playing your class more fun?
More schools of traps, a self-heal available to all hunters, a true 'signature shot' for beastmasters available at spec selection, a glyph to increase pet size in addition to the one that reduces it.
Now for the pie-in-the-sky wish list: allowing the use, if temporary, of multiple pets simultaneously for beastmasters, making traps a key part of survival's rotation in PvE content, allowing Marksmen to duel-wield ranged weapons.
What makes playing your class less fun?
Armor designs in recent PvE content, the at-times arbitrary nature of taming limitations (I.e.: being unable to tame a particular named bear when it's nearer, unnamed neighbor is tamable).

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
I think Marksmen and Survival hunters are just fine for their basic rotations; beastmasters need either for kill-command to be spec-specific. All three specs need a greater variable bonus from mastery, and BM hunters need mastery to affect pet survivability in addition to damage.
What’s on your wish list for your class?
Full stealth for Camouflage, to match mages. An on-demand self-heal for all hunter specs. Control of multiple pets simultaneously for beastmasters; at this point, I feel less like a beastmaster, and more like 'ranger who shopped smart and bought his kibble at S-Mart. Duel-wielding ranged weapons for Marksmen. A buff to survival hunter's traps for use in the basic rotation.
Now the pets: Remove improved cower. For BM hunters, have spirit bond give additional aspects: Tenacity pets get more damage reduction, ferocity get a damage increase, cunning gets reduced cooldowns. Allow BM hunters to choose their pet's spec. Buff the number of extra talent points from Beast Mastery from 4 to six, seeing as we had four in Wrath, but in Cataclysm, it's still four.
Make beastmaster hunters tank. Give an ability called 'eyes of the beast' that puts the hunter into the 'pet' mode, and allows the player to drive the pet to tank. The same could be applied to demonology warlocks.
Give Exotic pets a third ability and non-exotic ones a second ability. Give raptors heroic leap, give cats a bleed enhancer, give corehounds a cauterize to reduce healing, give devilsaurs a disarm, give Gorillas an AoE knockback and give the same to rhinos, who also have a thick-fur-shield.
Above all: Re-implement quivers and ammo pouches bags an average of 4+ more bag slots than what is usually available, optional visibility (I.E. the same way you can toggle helms and cloaks), and make them hunter-unique. With that in mind, re-implement max-level class quests, for hunters if nobody else, offering a special 24-28 slot quiver for completion. Hell, just Make the old one with the same requirements of Rhok'delar, just so us Cata-vets to be can say 'yea, I was there and I earned this badge' to the players of later years, and us real old-timers can pat our Rhok’delar and put them in our quiver-bag, and know we did it not once, but twice, and we did it ourselves.

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues? For instance, no longer requiring ammo could be considered a quality-of-life improvement for hunters.
--- I can't truly think of one that's worth writing about. The majority of the problems hunters have faced or that I personally had a problem with are already fixed.

--- If anything, I used to actually think about what pets to bring and how to best utilize them in terms of raiding while in a 10man scenario. Now that I raid 25man though almost every buff is covered and I feel like I'm just using a cunning pet strictly for the focus talent. If hunters, being a pet class, could have more selection in either the buffs their pets use or having a pet that gives a unique buff (Could be a hunter only buff or a raid wide buff) Id feel more like a pet class again instead of just another ranged dps with an additional function.

What makes playing your class more fun?
--- Extreme soloing. The fact that every raid boss I tackle is like trying to figure out a Rubik's cube is alot of fun. Its challenging and it makes me think outside of the box.
--- The rotational differences between each spec while at the same time staying reasonable competitive in raid dps. Of course, MM is in my opinion best and as BM I just cant compete on the meters as with the other specs (I think that's a skill problem I have, not a spec problem) Its nice to switch it up once in awhile to do something new and keep it fresh.

What makes playing your class less fun?
--- The fact that I have to be at least 5 yards away from a target to do anything. Any closer and i'm useless. Granted, there's so many things to be done that allows you to get out of range, but it is a slight annoyance from time to time.

How do you feel about your “rotation”? (Rotation is the accepted order in which abilities are used to maximum efficiency.)
--- I think its a little stale in Survival but Marksmens rotation is enjoyable. Its fluid and requires quick decision making to keep a proc from going to waste or losing a buff from a talent. When I play survival im basically hitting 3 buttons real quick, then focusing on 2 of those while I'm refreshing one at the same time and waiting for the rest to be off cooldown.
Beastmaster is not overly hard and its enjoyable to use.

--- Duel Wielding would be nice as well, I've never used it accept for when I was leveling and that didn't last very long either.
--- I enjoyed the new tames that were brought in with Firelands, what was disappointing was that if you had one spider you had them all. There was no reason to tame those other ones except for flavor. Keep the unique tames coming but don't do rehashes of the same species with just a different color.
