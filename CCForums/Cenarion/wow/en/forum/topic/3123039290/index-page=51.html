What type of content do you focus on? [PvE/PvP/Both]
- I participate in both PvE and PvP content, including world PvP and solo PvE content.

If PvE, what type of PvE? [Heroics/Raids/Other]
-I raid slightly, I do heroic 5mans, I do reg 5mans, I solo reg 5mans, I solo quests, I do rep grinds, etc. I pretty much have tasted, and taste daily, everything WoW has to offer to me.

If PvP, what type of PvP? [Arenas, BGs, Rated BGs]
-Mostly world PvP and battlegrounds, non-rated. I quit doing arena in Cataclysm because of the abysmal state of hunters in PvP at the moment (that is to say, low survivability and non-fluid PvP and a giant red target over our heads because of that.)

What are your biggest quality-of-life issues?
- ASSIST STANCE. I hate assist stance with a burning passion. There was no reason to take away old defensive stance.

As a person who frequently solos content as well as levels new hunters quite a bit (currently flying over Nagrand on my freshly 65 worgen hunter as I type this), I can say assist stance makes hunter pets more annoying than ever. The reason it is so annoying is because it forces the pet onto targets I don't want it to be on. For example, while leveling, I'll frequently pull 5ish mobs, throw down an ice trap and an explosive trap, and kite them in circles. I am used to my pet nuking down whichever one I sent it after first. However, since assist stance is annoying, having serpent sting (and thus, damage) on all those mobs causes my pet to run around targetting a bunch of random crap, ruining the positioning and causing, more than once, deaths on my part. Passive is the next solution, right? Nope! Because kiting using q, e, and mouse means hitting ctrl-1 is nigh-impossible. New defensive stance? But why? It won't attack the new things because they can't hit me because I am kiting them.

This is also an issue at 85. I solo H UP every day for a blue proto. Skadi's fight has annoying little adds. i can nuke them down in a second, yes, but for my shale spider to turn around and try to hit the dang things is obnoxious as all heck because it moves the boss and then she'll growl the almost-dead add and aggro gets screwed up. So, the answer is passive (since the new defensive stance is utterly useless aside from going AFK while stading alone, unmounted, with your pet out in a hostile zone for whatever reason.) But passive is all about super-micro management. I hate that. I just want my pet to be on one target and move to a new target when the previous one dies. Heck, you can keep assist, I'd just be ecstatic if defensive went back to how it used to be. The whole problem with serpent sting ticks making my pet dart around like a lunatic under assist stance's administration is really, really frustrating.

- I don't know if this counts as quality of life, but I'd like to bring up rare pets. Nowhere have I ever seen such an source for hatred of a class to be hammered out aside from in battlegrounds when I target the rogues first.

It's not infrequent for people on my server to throw up links to tears of loyalty just to troll other players. It's also not uncommon to be in mid-tame and have someone kill the pet you were farming and then sneer at you and laugh in your face. It's hard enough to camp these pets and out-compete other hunters, but to have griefers just kill them in front of you or to have them purposefully tell you about such a tale is heartbreaking.

A good solution that you've already done is to make the pets neutral and also unkillable, such as Ankha, Magria, and Ghostcrawler and Ban'thalos, to name a few. I know it's kind of unfair to make the rares that give great exp boosts straight unkillable, and I know that's just part of them being rare, but the harassment towards hunters is disgusting, and this dates all the way back to when people would kill your demons for the Rhok'delar/Lhok'delar chain. I feel as though if you want to make rares marked for hunters, instead of giving them a 25g loot piece, maybe they should have a different dragon portrait or be made simply unkillable.

- Short one: Ancient Hysteria is on the GCD. I wish it wasn't so that I wouldn't have to leash my corehound about 6 seconds in advance to bloodlust being called. And do not get me started on how assist screws with said leashing.

- Pet happiness. When you guys removed it, it felt like you took the soul out of my pets. The one thing that sets apart hunters from the other pet classes is how much personality and customization can go into our pets, but removing happiness made them into little robots without feelings. I feel like, now, they are more of a tool such as warlock minions. Something about it feels... detached. It really struck a blow into my heart when my favorite pet, Clyde, couldn't tell me he was happy anymore.

What’s on your wish list for your class?
- More stable slots. I am not a rare collector, allow me to say that first and foremost - the only rare pets I have legitimately farmed are Silithid Ravager (pre-cata ofc), Loque'nahak, Karoma, Skoll, and Karkin. The rest have fallen into my hands as random "Hey look, blahblah is up" moments. But I know a lot of my hunter brothers farm rares, and I'd like it if I would be able to tank a raid on my druid with a hunter who can actually provide buffs to my raid instead of responding with "No I HAVE to use this wolf because it's the only non-BM ferocity pet I have. GOTTA CATCH EM ALL!" I'd also, personally, like more slots because I easily get attached to my pets and it breaks my heart to tame a new thing and have to toss my snake because "Well the raptor does the same buff and is fero vs cunn so I might as well just get rid of the serpent." I REALLY liked that snake, but now Amadeus is out wandering the streets with no one to feed him!

- Baseline self heal. Survival hunters get jack, BM hunters are forced into using spirit beasts, and MM hunters only get 5% every 10 seconds (9 if glyphed.) This is CRIPPLING in PvP. I'd really like to be able to use my devilsaur for a free mortal strike, but no, if I don't use my spirit beast I am absolutely toast. I wish I could use a bird of prey to disarm that warrior in my deadzone, but nope, gotta get that spirit beast for the heal or else I wouldn't have even made it THIS far. Forcing a hunter to use a spirit beast in pvp is like telling a rogue, "Here's the ultimatum: Kidney shot or recup. Choose. You can't have both."

- Improved revive pet's return. With the whole dismounting in combat instagibbing your pet thing going on, spending 10 seconds on something that wasn't your fault seems mundane and is actually really obnoxious. And in PvP? It's downright gamebreaking. I can't tell you how many times my pets have been 1-shot by clever people who know how that system works because when they walk out they have 6k HP. Or even worse, when you get in combat on a ground mount, then your pet slows down and despawns, and then while on the same run you get into combat again, get dazed off your mount, call the pet in to come help you, and they are immediately killed by whatever dazed you while their HP adjusts to your gear. This is, as far as I am aware, an unavoidable part of the game because it would take a ridiculous amount of server space or other technical crap I know nothing about to remember your pet's individual stats. But it would be nice if you could lessen the burden on us by giving us our talent back that counteracted this.

- After giving it some thought, I'd kind of like to see a BM dual-wield pets 31 point talent. As is, our 31 point talent is severely lackluster in comparison to others (as is our level 10 spec perk, but I digress.) Consider it a BM titan's grip: Both pets work as one entity and both do 50% reduced dmg across the board. They cannot stack spirit bond, call of the wild, or two of the same buff (ex. cat and spirit beast would both throw out battle shout, can't stack culling the herd, no double-frenzy for focused fire procs.) But then you'd be able to use a wolf and cat for both of their individual group buffs, as I know a lot of people go BM solely for their love of the pets and so would be ecstatic to dual-wield them. That said, this sounds like a coding and upkeep nightmare. Just a thought, though, and it would make the spec feel much, much more unique than it does now. (Right now I just feel like a lame little hunter with shiny pets and no cool abilities to make up for it.)

What spells do you use the least?
- Widow venom. 10% heals reduction feels very inconsequential for the duration of the debuff and the fact that it's on GCD.
- Wing clip and raptor strike. I should probably use clip more, but RS is just... meh...
- Feed pet. It actually has worked its way off of my bars because of how almost useless it is.

Thanks for taking the time for this, guys. This is vastly appreciated by all of us. I'm done. ;)
