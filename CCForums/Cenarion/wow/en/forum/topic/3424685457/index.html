Every guild recruit I see these days says "tight-knit community" and/or "laid-back casual". In particular, I'm sick of seeing "tight-knit community". Tight-knit means they have their own clique of people they hang with, and if you're not similar to them in any way, then you will be treated as an outcast.

Tight-knit: Have known each other a long time on the server, always hang out together. Sure, they'll be nice and polite to newcomers, but you'll never actually be part of their "tight-knit community". There are a couple of hardcore guilds, several average progression guilds and TONS of social/casual/laid-back guilds.

As with any server in WoW, there is always a few jerkwads that like to sit in city and spam trade with useless nonsense.

My advice to you is this: start a lowbie toon, browse the guilds and start asking questions. If you find a number of guilds that offer what you're looking for, join on a trial basis. See how members interact with each other daily. It should take barely a week for you to see how active they are, who the cliquey members are, who the attention-hog is, etc.

I've been on this server for 2 years. I have yet to find a guild that does not have a clique, that does not have at least one oober-popular attention-hog, and most everything done guild-wise is decided by the 'clique', and not the GM.

My experience with this server, as a whole, has been positive. I've made friends here and brought over friends of my own from other servers. I do truly believe that there is something here for everyone. I'm very happy that I made the decision to move here.

When I was in high school, one of my teachers used to say, "This school would be perfect, if not for all the people." Cenarion Circle is wonderful, but it is not perfect. There is no such thing as a "perfect" server, because the players that inhabit the game are not perfect themselves - what determines how much fun you have is your attitude.

Just as with any other social interaction, you will have ups and downs. There will be someone who doesn't like you and the things you do, but that shouldn't stop you from finding like-minded people and having fun.

I encourage you to make a trial character here and to get your feet wet. Look at things in a positive light, and I'm sure you will find, like I have, that Cenarion Circle is a wonderful place to call home.

I think the best way to find out if you would like it here is to roll your trial character. Join allianceooc or hordeooc to get to know people. There are also ic channels Horde and allianceic. Hang out a bit.

People will help you get there if you're too low. Thursday is Feathers Inn at Booty Bay. Events are usually broadcast in Allianceooc and they are open to all.

Poke me or any other Pia member and we'll give you our chat channel so you have people to visit with while you get a feel for the server. Also, we'll send you a welcome to CC care package.

Drop in at the Blue Recluse. There's usually someone there.
