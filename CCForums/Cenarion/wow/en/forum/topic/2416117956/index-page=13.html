There's no real solid math here because it depends on your skill for both.

In a hardcore raiding guild, you could clear 12/12 normal in a night. 4 hours or so for 840 points (70 points per boss) and of course BH is another 70, but that doesn't put you anywhere near cap.

So you have to run daily heroics to get capped. 70 VP for each dungeon finished, which can take anywhere from half hour to an hour and a half if you have a painfully bad group.

The other thing to mention is that clearing 12/12 in a night is for a *hardcore raiding* guild, which means...they won't be clearing 12/12 in a night. They'll be clearing half the bosses on heroic then working on progression. Wiping on the same fight for 3 nights. The "average" raiding group probably wouldn't be able to do that. And even if they were, that's only 840 valor points.

TL;DR arena is faster because if you're good at arena you'll get the wins faster than you can clear instances full of trash and bosses, and if you're bad at arena your rating will be low enough that you only need 10 wins.

Exactly this; well said.

I could care less if bad/newer players get their gear fast, as much as people want to deny it there's still a skill factor involved in the game that even fully geared players playing OP classes can't break 1600 somehow, so the gear issue doesn't bother me.

PvE'ers seem to think everything should be a stupid long grind. PvE'ers play the game for gear and/or the PvE content itself. Trying to justify the change saying "NOW YOU HAVE TO WORK AS HARD AS US TO GET IT" doesn't make any sense whatsoever.

If you enjoy the act of PvE, then you're getting pleasure from PvE, you chose that playstyle so continue to enjoy your choice.

On the other hand, if you PvE because you like collecting epics, doesn't the fact that the epics come slowly rather than swiftly make each one all the more satisfying and "epic" feeling when you receive it? If you got a full set of <insert current best Tier set here> after downing a boss once, would you be satisfied with that system? I sincerely doubt it.

PvP'ers on the other hand don't PvP because they just want to collect a suit of epics. PvP'ers want to get the suit of epics as fast as they possibly can so they can not be at a disadvantage doing what they enjoy.

The argument of "if you like PvP so much you shouldn't mind doing more games every week".

The problem with that short sighted argument is, for someone playing a single character, this usually isn't a problem. For people with jobs/real life obligations who enjoy playing multiple classes, this is a huge time constraint problem. Just because someone enjoys PvP'ing doesn't mean they can afford the time to do it 40~ games per character per week, as much as they'd like to.

The beautiful thing about the current system is if you only get enough time in to play 5-10 games a week on each character you aren't put at a significant gear disadvantage for having a life; it's a beautiful casual system. I'm sorry if people hate "casualization" of aspects of the MMO, but the sad reality is some people have obligations they can't get rid of and want to enjoy the game still.

This current system doesn't hurt anybody, so complaining about it is retarded. If you chose a life of PvE and virtual gear obsession, that's fine; most of us PvP'ers would rather start the season in a full set of gear equal to all of our PvP opponents, so we dont' have to worry about weekly points/gear disadvantages and just enjoy the game for 1, 5, or 30 games per week, whatever we amount our individual life schedules allow, or that we desire.

The key point is, this system I'm talking about hurts nobody, so nobody should complain about it, and I don't see the point in changing a system everyone enjoys.

I do of course realize everyone queuing more games per week will mean more total games played in arena every week, which would help rejuvenate the arena scene and reduce queue times, so 10 games a week is a fine middle ground I don't think anyone would complain too hard about.

However, I think cross-BG Queues will solve a lot of the "dead queue" issues itself.

/endrant
