I have some changes that I'd like to see made

Talents Tier 1: Nightstalker VS Shadow Focus
Shadow Focus is the better talent by far. This is tied in with the actual opening ability. Mutilate>=Ambush>Garrote
Garrote won't stack VW procs with Rupture, only grants 1 CP, and deals crap damage
Ambush has a lot of up-front damage, but Mutilate is better because...
Mutilate has 2 chances to proc SF, has a chance to proc Blindside, and deals more poison damage

The opener is a very minor DPS issue for Mut and Combat, but it still counts! =D

Talents Tier 3
Cheat Death is good for when you have the bosses on farm. But for progression, Elusiveness is just plain better.
I can walk through death beams and stomp on crawler mines without breaking a sweat with Feint up. I get healed up, then do it again 5 seconds later!
Leeching Poison is the talent choice for Malkorok

Glyphs
Vendetta is required
Smoke Bomb is darn near required for Norm and Heroic
Redirect is also darn near required

Feint is really the only other option I would ever consider replacing

Stat Priority
Thank you for linking Shadowcraft. I would include that using Shadowcraft to find out which stat weights are best, and then plugging those into AMR is a minor DPS increase over using only Shadowcraft

As for static values, I would go with Crit>Haste, rather than the other way around. Haste isn't much of a benefit until you get your legendary gem and cloak

I would disagree here. When you have content on farm, you pretty much know fairly well what will happen ahead of time and with your raids strategy. Thus you can plan when to use Feint.

When you're progressing, you don't always know what will happen or if your raid changes or uses a different strategy for your next attempt. It's also a great talent for progression since you're more likely to make mistakes with a fight you've never done before as opposed to a fight you've completed 4-5 times already. I'd also like to mention again that Elusiveness only confers a 65% damage reduction when using it on AoE such as Immerseus' Swirl or Norushen's Blind Hatred because the 30% damage reduction stacks multiplicative with the 50% AoE damage reduction and 30% of 50% is 15%. That devalues the talent slightly.

I didn't think about soaking bombs with it though so I'll have to take a look at that!

Redirect isn't something you'd need in your glyph book for every fight, so I wouldn't really say it's required. A few fights with frequent target switching cheese it for us as the mobs disappear rather than falling down dead and the CPs disappear with them. (Protectors and Immerseus have a few situations like this.) I will add Redirect to my reccomended section though. (I'm not sure why it isn't already there.)

Similarily, Vendetta isn't required either because there are a few fights where you can't sit on a target for longer than 24 seconds. Heroic Immerseus comes to mind, as he stacks a very nasty DoT on you the longer you deal single target damage to him, at which point you should switch to the tiny adds he summons to let your DoT wear off (Or for a healer to dispel you!) and avoid further stacks. It also becomes progressively less useful as the fight wears on due to his shrinking health pool. Protectors is also another fight where the Glyph wouldn't be so great due to the frequency of target swaps.

I would agree that Smoke Bomb is darn near required too though. Feint is debatable as it's only useful when you're going to refresh Feint back to back while attacking, which really only happens on Thok, Juggernaut and Malkorok to an extent.

02/05/2014 04:27 PMPosted by Alcone
Although there is some room for playstyle differences here, I still think Elusiveness is better than Cheat Death by a significant margin


I was converted to Team Cheat Death when writing my Combat guide. I switched over and haven't really looked back. But then, we have a paladin for Hand of Purity/Protection, a disc priest to lessen the front-end damage, and a resto druid to passively keep me up. In general, if I die, it's not something Elusiveness would've saved me from. Actually, most heavy hitters that aren't DoTs this tier can be mitigated with non-talented feint alone.

So the question becomes: Can my healers handle the DoT I'm taking without me using Feint? If so - if you flat out -aren't- dying to DoTs - Elusiveness doesn't do you any good, as it's only saving healer mana (which regenerates very fast at the end of the expansion.)

EDIT: On the subject of Juggernaut bombs. With a decent amount of health (~600k? Could be wrong) you can take normal mode bombs with Elusiveness alone, which is great. On Heroic, I switched to Cheat Death, which lets me take a bomb every wave (Cloak of Shadows wave 1, Cheat Death wave 2, repeat until dead.)

On the subject of Malkorok and leeching poison. The healing I personally get, at 570 with a heroic warforged mainhand and a heroic offhand, averages out at about 25k personal HPS when none of it overheals (Malkorok). Everybody in the raid who has self-healing outheals me. That's not to say 25k HPS isn't more than we have normally, but it's decently insignificant in terms of your shield size, especially since the shield CAN cap out. Cheat Death provides a small buffer if somebody misses the pool, or Elusiveness can help with the DoT if it's a problem during phase 2. I don't see Leeching as all that useful on that fight.
