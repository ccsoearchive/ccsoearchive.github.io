What do you disagree with? If you let me know and present compelling arguments and thoughts, I'm not afraid to change things.

The big problem I have in general (in addition to all the specific changes I'll mention below) is that a guide should be much more clinical. You shouldn't present specific recommendations rather you should describe the strengths and weaknesses of talents and abilities and let players make their own choices.

Specific Critiques:
T45: You are still somewhat undervaluing cheat death. I’m glad you changed the recommendation to either of them however the text suggests that elusiveness is strictly superior to cheat death.

Just to quickly elaborate on my point earlier about DRs not being created equal, the typical explanation for wanting more DR is healer mana but this is something of a canard given modern healer design. There are broadly speaking two cases where you use feint to reduce damage,
1) AoE Burst/Pulse: In this case healers are going to be using AoE heals and possibly raid CDs which do not substantially increase in efficiency when healing 9 targets vs. 10.

2) Small group Mechanic Soaking: This is a good use of Elusiveness in general because that additional DR may prevent healers from having to use fast or big heals which are less mana efficient on you. In general Elusiveness tends to be a good choice for single target soaking because it tends to the case that you will be doing a lot of single target soaking if you are called on to do this quite frequently.

T75: If you consider most raid adds that get stunned the big idea is they need to be stunned ASAP not in 10-20 seconds depending on poison procs. I agree all talents are situational but Prey is clearly the strongest choice on the tier overall because 10% damage for the raid is very powerful especially on targets that need to be burned really fast (cracklers on ra-den, embodieds on tsulong). Its easy to see the utility to prey on fights that require a stun, much harder with paralytic.

Glyphs:
Glyph of Feint: Glyph of Feint only makes feint more energy efficient if you are trying to keep it up 100% of the time which generally is a waste. Sure on fights like Megaera or Ra-den its pretty nice but for most other fights you are feinting against a single bug burst and that extra time doesn’t do much for you.

Glyph of Smoke Bomb: There is no way this is the weakest of the three glyphs, this is almost certainly the more important glyph we have. There seems to be an inconsistency between the explanation for this glyph and the glyph of feint above. For pulsing aura fights an addition 2 seconds of DR for the entire raid is almost certainly more important that a small amount of additional energy. Obviously for single big burst fights this glyph doesn’t matter much. The other big consideration with this glyph is your raid leader may be counting on smoke bomb being 7 seconds rather than 5 so you may want to check with your raid leader before you replace this glyph.

Glyph of Sprint: Sprint has a nice use case on fights with Pushback mechanics (which blizzard seems to love these past couple tiers) in that it makes you basically immune to them with glyphed sprint.

Finally for the gearing section just link Aeriwan's great ShadowCraft tutorial and ShadowCraft itself.

    There's some debate as to what you should open with while using Subterfuge and Shadow Focus. Some people are in favor of Mutilate, giving you a nearly free Mutilate (With Shadow Focus at least.) and 2-3 combo points - letting you get a 5 CP Rup up sooner - as well as good damage and a chance to proc your poisons twice. Others are in favor of Garrote, giving you quick access to Venomous Wound procs, allowing you to focus on getting SnD up and your first Envenom. The downside is that Garrote does far less damage than Mutilate and only generates 1 CP.
    I may be wrong in this understanding, but I believe Mutilate > Rupture > Mutilate is the better opener.

    Garrote might let you start procing Venomous Wounds faster, but it ticks every 3 sec. compared to Rupture ticking every 2 sec, meaning a 2-3 point Rupture would tick just as much, if not more than a Garrote opener in less time (12-16 second duration, 16-20 with T15 2p), leading to more chances at VM procs. Likewise, any Ruptures applied after (with the Garrote DoT still ticking) won't proc VM until after Garrote falls off the target 18 seconds later.
