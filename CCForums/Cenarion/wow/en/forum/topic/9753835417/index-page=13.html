You can't just blanket apply raw statistics to individual human beings like this. It would be like the Seattle Seahawks telling Russel Wilson to become a pure pocket passer because the statistics bare out the increased success of teams with QB's who play in the pocket. You work with the skill set you have. Not every individual is going to be both a 90th percentile mut rogue AND a 90th percentile sub rogue. We're also ignoring the inherent bias in the data. If we only look at parses for mythic gruul kills for example we're only getting data from a subset of probably the more skilled rogues. Most of them can probably learn and excel at any of the three specs and most of the best players will probably gravitate toward sub since it is the best spec mathematically.

Now one can not definitively say that there are more skilled rogues playing sub than Mut or combat on ST but it's impossible to quantify skill or even know what the distribution of skilled players amongst parses is. There are so many factors beyond that that effect a parse anyway. It's not as simple as how good you are or how good your spec is. I can use my own personal recent parses as good examples. I parses lower this week on gruul than last week not because I played any worse this week but simply because I got poor RNG on my 4 PC procs. My blackhand parse was significantly worse because both tanks died in phase 3 and the hunter pet was kite tanking the boss for the last 10%. I had a low operator parse last week because the tanks were being dicks and wouldn't keep the boss near the adds. There are a number of factors that can affect a parse that aren't readily apparent.

This isn't to say that the data is useless. We can look at it and determine a lot of things. We can see which spec has the highest ceiling. We can see which specs perform better on average overall and on specific fights. What we can't do is look at an individual and say, "sub is better you should be playing sub." I don't disagree with your sentiment, fierydemise. I think if you can definitely help your raid by learning sub if it will net you more dps and I agree that learning sub isn't nearly as difficult as a lot of these elitests who want to hide behind the notion that simply playing sub makes them better players would have you believe.

We have to recognize that all players aren't doing the same content at the same skill level. Every rogue isn't 90th percentile pushing mythic progression. Some rogues are mediocre or average doing average content. Some guilds are struggling on average content and having their players switch to the higher theoretical dps spec isn't the answer. If you're having issues blowing up slag bombs, or missing interrupts, or switching targets, or if you're just dragging your raid down for any number of other mechanical issues you should probably just play the spec you're most comfortable with, the spec that feels the most natural to you. This isn't just saying if you're bad play an easier spec. For every Mut rogue who's having a hard time playing sub on normal blackhand and not messing up mechanics, there is probably a sub rogue who's struggling to put enough damage on the elemenalist trying to playing combat on blast furnace.

Ultimately my point is this. I agree, if your concern is raw damage output and you're a skilled player you can probably benefit from learning sub, especially if you're doing the highest level of content and you need to be performing at the highest levels. However I don't think the solution for every individual rogue is simply to learn sub. Specific problems require specific solutions and its not accurate to simply look at parses and say "sub does better average dps so everyone should play sub."
