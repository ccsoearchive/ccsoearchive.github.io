02/02/2015 10:13 PMPosted by Niridianlol

Hey! Thanks for posting and sorry for the late reply.

Right now with SR, you can get a total of 3 finishers in on average. You don't want to spend your SR doing a lot of Mutilates and DIspatches. Instead, you want to squeeze as much damage into that little period as possible since it's basically a 100% damage increase and, like all damage increases, you get more of a return during their duration on harder hitting attacks. The best option is to pool 10 CP and near full energy before using SR as this means you can immediately open with two finishers, such as Envenom. You can then Mut/Dispatch and should be able to get a third finisher in before the initial 8 seconds of SR expires. Yes, chaining Envenoms back to back is bad for your damage overall (Due to loss of the Envenom buff) but as the clone doesn't apply poisons, it will only be beneficial for your Shadow Reflection.

As for using Rupture vs. Envenom during SR, this is something I've been looking into myself lately. Right now, I'm not really recommending one over the other but am starting to lean more on the side of Envenom. If you don't have around 1.1k+ Mastery, I would say go Rupture. If you have 1.1k+ Mastery, then it's really a toss up between either one. If your clone's second Envenom crits then Envenom is the definite winner. If it doesn't, then Rupture is the winner. This is due to a variety of reasons but mostly boils down to crit % and multi % and how it interacts with Envenom and Rupture during SR. So right now if you feel like Envenom is the stronger option then go for it. The only situation I would definitely recommend Envenom over Rupture during SR is if you have or are close to the 1.1k Mastery mark and have a sizable Mastery trinket proc for the duration of SR. But I'm still starting to look into this so take what I say with a grain of salt.

02/02/2015 10:13 PMPosted by Niridianlol
Also the best example for the Elusiveness talent would be the Twin Ogron fight, seeing as how you can cut all the damaging abilities they put out in half, I say this because on mythic butcher you dont want to be using Feint because you want to be DPSing 99.99% of the time.


Elusiveness doesn't stack additively with Feint, so you're only reducing AoE damage by 65%, not 80%. Remember that Feint on it's own is 50% AoE reduction. For the most part, 50% AoE reduction will get you through the damage being dished. If the damage wont kill you and your healers wont oom before the boss dies, extra damage reduction is pointless, especially when it's tied to the same resource you use for dealing damage. I believe Cheat Death is the better option on Twins (And most fights) because it offers a valuable buffer between life and death, which occurs most of the time from mistakes or incidents that Elusiveness could not have saved you from. This is in part due to the nature of Feint and Elusiveness; It's only valuable if you can predict damage and raids tend to have plenty of options for dealing with predictable damage. It's what you can't predict that will kill you and that's why Cheat Death shines.

I do agree that Elusiveness is not very valuable on Butcher (Due to recent experiences) but disagree that Feint isn't valuable on Butcher. If you find yourself low after a recent cleave then you're better off playing it safe and Feinting, especially if Cheat Death is on CD. It may hurt your damage, yes, but you're not going to be doing any damage if you die.

02/02/2015 10:13 PMPosted by Niridianlol
Also I myself feel Crimson tempest is completely useless spell, the damage and bleed damage is just to week. Personally I would just pool some energy and re envenom or continue rupture rotation on my targets. I think the only time I would use Crimson tempest is a fight where their is 8+ targets.


The real appeal of Crimson Tempest currently isn't really it's DoT or direct damage (Although this will outpace Envenom once you hit enough targets.) but is the Enhanced Crimson Tempest effect, which increases poison damage (Deadly Poison and Venomous Wounds specifically.) targets take by 30% for 6 seconds (Assuming a 5 CP CT.) after casting. This is a very sizable increase to your AoE damage as it's mostly all Deadly Poison and Venomous Wounds. The downside is that it's not very feasible to use until you have Rupture on several targets, meaning that Assassination takes much longer than Combat or Sub to reach full AoE potential.

for that guy that said cheat death is better than elusiveness talent..............................

just think about what you said for a second


The issue with Elusiveness is that it's tied to an active source, Feint. This means that Elusiveness is only good when you can predict incoming damage. When the raid is taking predictable AoE, you have a lot of options for dealing with it, most of it on the healer. Adding Feint on top of that, the ability that makes Rogues one of the most durable classes in raiding, predictable AoE damage isn't likely to kill you until it's a wipe, your not getting any heals etc. Basically, if you're dying to predictable AoE damage with Feint up, regardless of Elusiveness, then there's a problem other than the amount of damage you're taking. You're either doing something wrong or someone else is. Elusiveness isn't going to save you at this point.

Cheat Death, on the other hand, is kind of like your second chance. No matter how good you or your teammates are, something unpredictable will happen. An add will explode on the melee on Mythic Butcher. You won't make it out of the way of Oregorger in time. You'll miss a Feint or Cloak of Shadows on Gruul. A Reverberation will spawn right on top of you on Kromog. And so on. Unless you're somehow perfect and all your teammates are perfect, then things like this will happen, especially on progression. This is where Cheat Death shines. Every two minutes, you get to live through a fatal mistake, whether it's your mistake, another's, or, more importantly, just plain RNG.
