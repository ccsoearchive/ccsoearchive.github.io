I started in vanilla with a rogue. Deleted it at level 45 when I decided to leave Alliance for Horde. I started one up again about two weeks ago and play it now and then. I've been playing it the last two days a good bit and I'm as of right now level 45 again.

At this point, so far, I feel like my ability to PVP has increased as a rogue (with levels). I'm still 2-3 shotting people here and there. If you know how to pick your targets, when to pick them, when to vanish and re-enter combat you will do well.

You also have to know who to avoid and when to avoid them. Hunters for example. I generally walk around the "agro/stealth detection bubble" as I call it (an invisible circle which is smaller or larger depending on your level and the enemies level) or shadowstep in and do as much damage as possible.

As a rogue I feel that one job is to stun targets at opportune times. For example to allow time for your team to move in and assist you. Or to stun a straggler once or twice to spread him or her apart from his team mates. Separation is a valuable thing and so is confusion. Especially when you can't get to a fight nearby quickly enough but you can prevent that enemy from getting to the fight and assisting instead.

And it's not often smart to simply pick the closest target. I have learned to move behind the groups and pick those targets that are standing back (and they usually are the most defenseless). Also, it will take those up front valuable time to realize that those behind them are in combat. Time you can use to take them out. Also when you have the fight coming from all sides, it can confuse people enough to slow them down, cause them to panic or simply make mistakes when hitting keys.

The problem is that most people don't care to use strategy in PVP. They want a win button. Which is absolutely pathetic.
