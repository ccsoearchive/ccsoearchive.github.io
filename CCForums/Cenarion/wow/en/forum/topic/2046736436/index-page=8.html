Do take into consideratioon that even though death knights, warriors, paladins, and druids can all tank, not all of them are tanks nor are all priests or shaman healers. Break down the classes by possible role type in a raid/dungeon.

Druids are effectively 3 roles in one, paladins are 3, DKs/warriors/priests/shamans/ each are 2. Even though the overall population of each class isn't a perfect split among their roles, each of the hybrids roles should fall roughly within the same population range of warlocks/rogues.

Now we can argue that mages and hunters are overpopulated when compared to all others when broken down by their role.


We have 6 melee class here: DK, War, Pally, Shaman, Druid, Rogue.
2 of them can heal or tank: Pally, Druid
2 of them can spec into tank only: DK, War
1 of them can heal: shaman

Only rogue is pure melee dps class.

This is very good reason to roll into another melee dps since all of them (except rogue) can fit into different role in raid/dungeon/guild.

I just don't feel rogues are in a good place right now conceptually. Optimization and the power pendulum aside rogues just feel off and honestly I think it's because they want us to play more like a shock trooper and less like a specialized controller.

The advent of recuperate and combat readiness, while both powerful and useful, really hammers this home for me.

This forced shock trooper mentality also doesn't even work correctly with our class mechanic of energy and combo points. We are designed around being a controller in the sense that we are supposed to take a target and force a choice on a response (generally to ignore us, which was the bad choice, or find a way to peel or destroy) and instead now we are less mobile (the duration our target is out of stuns counts toward our lack of mobility, imo) which means the likelihood of a target switch is higher, a strike against combo points, and we're also less immediately threatening since our relative damage (dmg vs health pools) to energy ratio is dramatically less, so our pacing mechanic has essentially turned us into hunter pets and we act as pressure.

But at the same time we're still expected to BE controllers because we still have lingering abilities that lend itself to the role. Also our exposure (less time in stealth/less time enemy is incapacitated) is much higher due to, again, relative damage, less control and relative mobility. And this is bad because our wearing leather (not that armor class REALLY makes a difference, but for sake of discussing design flaws here...) was supposed to be our penalty for our limited exposure.

So we're being pulled in like 3 directions here. Control, pressure, and exposure.

It's like we're confused front line fighters with a few dirty tactics. This makes playing a rogue just feel weird. Again our relative power notwithstanding, as that's just a numbers game and not a design problem.


If we really must be forced into this change in role than at the very least Blizzard owes us a mechanics update. CPs staying on the rogue would be the best place to start. Otherwise the only way for a rogue to feel like a rogue again is to scrap the survivability tools we got and give us back burst and control.
