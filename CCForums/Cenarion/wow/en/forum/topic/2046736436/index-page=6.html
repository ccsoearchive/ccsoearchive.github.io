Masaq a couple post back had quite a few good points.




In PvE, even accounting for active modifiers like Slice and Dice and Envenom, a very large portion of the rogue's damage is attributable to passive sources of damage. Yes, they are using abilities for the entire duration of a fight, but we want to reduce the percentage of rogue damage that comes from auto-attacks and poisons. More of their damage will be coming from active abilities and special attacks.




Pre-cata 70% or greater of a rogues damage was essentially passive. For assassination rogues, roughly 60-70%of the damage is essentially passive. Great change there.


i would say it very hard to change this without improving rogues burst ability in pvp.


In PvP, we want to reduce the rogue's dependency on binary cooldowns and "stun-locks," and give them more passive survivability in return. One major change is that we'll put Cheap Shot on the same diminishing return as other stuns. The increase to Armor and Stamina on cloth, leather, and mail gear will help with this goal as well.




Rogues have less effective passive defenses now than ever before, unless you're combat. They have more binary cooldowns that they rely on more than ever before. The armor and health changes were a net nothing since they applied equally to everyone. Rogue dodge was crippled.


couldn't agree more with you Masaq, but combat passive defense really sucks. my PRIEST has more armor then my rogue by a long shot. this is using inner fire without glyph even.

if they nerf rogues control then they could buff our abilites for pve. since this would hopefully result in a zero change in rogue over in pvp, the only problem is that this would take a lot of time and research to fully balance. still doable i would like to point out.


The Assassination and Combat talent trees currently have a lot of passive bonuses. We plan to dial back the amount of Critical Strike Rating provided by these trees so that rogues still want it on their gear.




Assassination lost 5% crit, Sub gained 5% crit in HaT, combat lost 5% crit with daggers (lol) and fist weapons. But as 5% crit is now a raid wide buff several classes give, there wasn't really a net change except sub sort of gained an extra 5% crit.


and look at how useful crit is to rogues now lol






i would also like to point out this last patch buffs to combat and sub. both buffs added to the respected specs scaling abilites to gear, yet it did not address the under line problem, synthesis.

Why is assassination doing so well with far inferior AP? the simple answer is the synthesis of assassination talents and abilities. EVERYTHING they do increases the effect of another aspect of their dps.

Combat is next in line with synthesis and thus look at how well its performing along with mut. pretty good i would say but thats after 25% more AP added to their arsenal. im not even going into sub besides pointing out why this spec is not viable.
