You're welcome. Usually it's best to make a thread or tack onto a thread asking about armory checking for quicker replies, but I'll go over what I saw.

-Glove enchant, Greater Mastery.
-Wrist enchant, Agility.
-Cut 1pt from Coup de Grace (Lethality if you run a Ruptureless rotation) and 1pt from -Quickening and put them into Ruthlessness.
-You're below the Expertise cap, which for a Gnome is 691, that's an imperative for Combat. And you've reforged Expertise to Crit. Never reforge anything to Crit its value is down around or below (depending on your gear) white hit. It's essentially your least valued stat.

Glyph of Blade Flurry is requisite. Blade Flurry is a powerhouse of an ability for Combat, but it needs the glyph to function to full potential.

Glyph of Tricks of the Trade is requisite. You should be using TotT as close to on CD as you're able while taking advantage of the 2pc set bonus. Given that, it is a DPS bonus to glyph it.

Glyph of Feint is requisite. This is an extremely powerful mitigation cooldown and the frequency of use makes removing the energy cost a DPS bonus.

You shouldn't need glyph of Evasion of Blind in PVE as CC should be organized and clearly defined and if your group hasn't stabilized with you Evade Tanking for the base duration of it, the glyph isn't going to save you. And so long as the tank is alive there's very little use for Evasion. Fan of Knives is right out. As a Combat rogue, you have Blade Flurry and Killing Spree. In any situation where there are multiple targets it is always a DPS loss to use FoK instead.

Drop Reinforced Leather for, pretty much anything else. The extra armour is not enough to keep you alive if a tank dies and you have aggro. Evasion is your best defense in that case, and has the added bonus of allowing you to effectively tank for its duration while your raid brings up and stabilizes the tank to taunt back from you. Imp Recuperate is a very good choice to give it extra power in the few situations you have cause to use Recuperate. But pretty much anywhere else you could put those two points would serve you better. Since very little damage you'll actually be taking in a raid is physical.

Shadow Dance is a simple and easy to use cooldown. How it works is, as you already know, it allows you to you use your stealth bar in combat. There is a use to it that is just as important as vanish and your opener in pvp/pve-- it triggers find weakness when using garrote, ambush and a few other skills.

Where Shadow Dance fits into this is using it for the reason of Find Weakness and to provide high burst on a 1 minute cd when lined up with Shadow Step.

Shadow Step -> Shadow Dance -> Premed -> Ambush -> Finisher depending on importance. As an example of how to use it.

To sum it up its one of those CDs that provide high burst when used correctly, mostly when near energy capped so you can make full use of it.

Shadow Step -> Shadow Dance -> Premed -> Ambush -> Finisher depending on importance. As an example of how to use it.
You actually want to save Shadowstep. The first Ambush during Shadow Dance is going to trigger Find Weakness, but not itself benefit from Find Weakness. So Shadowstepping the second Ambush will put the bonus damage on an ability that also has 70% arpen meaning it will hit harder than if you had applied the bonus to the initial Ambush.

Also if you have the 2pc-T13 you'll want to use Tricks of the Trade as close to on CD as possible (so that every second use lines up with ShD).

As ShD is coming off CD make sure both your SnD and Recuperate have sufficient duration to last the length of your FW burst. As Eviscerate obviously has more to benefit when being cast under FW than either SnD or Recuperate do. You'll still need to refresh them as necessary if they don't last the entirety of it.

Dump all your CP into a final finisher, cast TotT while pooling up some energy. You'll need to find an ideal spot to do this with your gear. Your goal is to get close to capping without capping either directly or during the ShD to give you as much burst as possible without costing yourself potential DPS by capping.

Pop ShD/Premed > Ambush > Eviscerate > Shadowstep > Ambush > Ambush > Eviscerate > Ambush

The goal is to get as many Ambushes as possible in the ShD and also to make sure that you Ambush just before the buff ends to give you the total maximum up-time on FW. Use Eviscerate when you have 5CP as a filler to bridge time while you regen energy for the next Ambush, since you will not have sufficient energy to Ambush every GCD for the full duration. Between the 2pc and current energy regen levels 4 Ambushes is an ideal number to aim for, with as many Eviscerate fillers as you can get. Eviscerate costs 35 energy, at 5CP refunds 25 energy (via Relentless Strikes), and you regen ~12-14 energy during the GCD, -35+25+12=2 So using Eviscerate instead of just waiting for energy to further Ambush is fairly energy neutral, while at the same time netting you extra damage utilizing the FW debuff.

For general Sub CD use (assuming also presence of 2pc-T13):

Premeditation: 20sec
Shadowstep: 24sec
TotT: 30sec
Shadow Dance: 60sec
Vanish: 120sec
Preparation: 300sec

On the pull, Ticks the tank > Premed > Shadowstep > Ambush - This provides you with 10 FW and 5CP to get the start of your rotation going

From here, you will have Premed first then ShS, then TotT back in 30sec, or 20sec after FW ends. At which point do the above mentioned sequence for Shadowdance.

From here, again, you will have Premed, ShS, TotT back in 30sec.

TotT > Vanish > Shadowstep > Premed > Ambush

From here, Premed, ShS, TotT, and now Shadow Dance are back in 30sec. So that you can once again use the above Shadow Dance sequence.

From here TotT will be back in 30 sec. You can choose to use Preparation at this stage in your CD rotation or save it for a double burst of FW via Vanish later during a Bloodlust or an otherwise burn phase. If you use Prep at this point of your rotation, both Vanish and Shadowstep are reset so their CDs are not factors. Only Premed and TotT are.

From here (if you used Prep/Vanish), Premed, ShS, TotT, ShD are back in 30sec. Eventually you will get to a point after this where on the 30sec mark you will not have a DPS CD to use for FW, but it is still important to TotT (which with the 2pc is still burst DPS and therefore a DPS CD in its own right due to the reduced energy costs) such that it does not fall out of sync with Shadow Dance. So until Vanish is next off CD you'll be alternating between Tricks'ing for a ShD and just Tricks'ing alone every 30sec.

So what you see is that as Sub, you rotate through a CD order over 30sec. Priority-wise if more than one is available at any given time you give priority first to: 1) Shadow Dance 2) Vanish 3) Preparation

Like with ShD ideally set up a SnD and Recuperate before using Vanish/Prep as Eviscerate will again benefit more being cast during FW burts than the other two finishers. But that does not mean let either lapse just because you have FW.

All that said, thank you kindly Pekti. It's always nice to know something you wrote had a positive impact on another person. I hope this clears up the ambiguity on Sub's CD management.
