This isnt a QQ post, it's simply an insight into why a player of 7 years is choosing to finaly call it quits with this game. I think Blizzard is a great company and hopefully someone there reads this and they can benefit from it.

This game should be for both casual and hardcore players, yes I agree 100%. However, tailoring almost 100% of game design to casual players is the underlying reason that alot of people are leaving. Why? Because making the game so easy that anyone can clear any "heroic" dungeon, kill raid bosses for sligthly lesser gear than normal raid loot (not even worth noting how slight the stat diff is, it's still top gear in game, just not bis), and giving the false confidence that "everyone" can and does play games at the same basic level of hand-eye cordination and information processing speeds, has created an enviroment of poor quality "gamers".

Most everytime I try to play or participate in raid/heroics/etc, the majority of the group doesnt know thier class's basics because they leveled it in a matter of days now and have never developed reflex responses in tandom with thier class abilities. Infact the overall quality of players in general in this game is now at a level that the overall game experience is just unnacceptable to most anyone with a gameing background.

The current solution of lfr/normal/hard mode's for raid is redundant and fails to keep me entertained for extended periods of play, ie progression. Doing the SAME FIGHT with one or two new mechanics and TUNING BOSS HEALTH/DMG higher is about the most unentertaining thing I could ever think of for giving players a different choice of "harder" content to play. This along with the same mechanics, only mixxed together differently with different bosses, can only go so far and the wall has been hit. Oh, and same boss models for 7 years (void for instance) is pathetic and simply not worth a subscription fee, I mean really, a subscription fee for a game that gives u the same boss graphic xpac after xpac?

This game grew in popularity because of the vanilla/bc style of game design. Yes, yes sub numbers were at its highest in wrath but think about it..... that was the games "peak", that's when friends were playing because wow was the "in thing", and ofcourse a substantial portion would eventualy drop out. But what got wow to that point of exposure was not the "casual" style of catering, it was the enjoyment of a challenge you had to work with your friends to overcome something. By banking on the casual style, blizzard has chosen wrongly. Why? Because the casual's are come and go, the "gamers" are long term players. It's either a base of sub players of gamers at decent amounts, or the gamble that somehow casual players will somehow become hardcore about a game they casualy play and pay a monthly fee for...... see the irony in that statement? "casuals paying monthly fees regulary for a game they casualy play"

It's been a great run and I have had great memories with people I've met! Thank you Blizzard for the hours of enjoyment over the years you have given me and others. I am greatful to have been a part of cultural phenom since launch and tho its bittersweet to leave, all things move towards thier end.
