Asking for unique utility is probably futile; the devs don't want to give any class significant unique utility. A minor raid cooldown like rallying cry or even something more meaningful like healthstones can't justify taking an entire class.

It's perfectly reasonable for pures to expect all (or at least two) of their specs to be at least situationally competitive in raiding content. If only one spec is competitive, as with hunters, why bring one?

Take rogues for example. Combat is the best single-target and cleave spec, assassination is the best AE spec, subtlety offers the best mobility. If AE really mattered this tier, assassination would be much higher on the rankings.

Same deal with hunters; marks has always been the best single-target, survival the AE spec, and BM... well, BM has never been any good, but that's because it's always tried to make the pet itself the center of the spec rather than having synergies with the pet be the main spec mechanic.

3) one spec optimized for AE, with very little rampup and minimized target-switching costs (raid encounters with AE components, trash, 5man content, soloing)

Not great, but within 10% of optimal, and you'd get all kinds of neat utility that PvPers like that could make it situationally optimal in PvE too.
