After doing a quick scan on raidbots today, noticed something quite peculiar.

Marksmanship, Hunter's best spec, has slipped to midpack after being overtaken by Elemental Shaman and Fury Warrior. This is kind of a scary thought as Hunters have always been one of the higher up competing classes.

It also means that Hunter's BEST spec is mid pack while the others are sitting at bottom of the barrel. Marksmanship is looking to be overtaken by Frost DK in the coming weeks. When our best spec can only compete for middle pack and our other 2 specs can't compete at all, there's something that needs to be addressed in regards to scaling. Even Enhancement Shaman, commonly thought of on this forum as the terribad spec, are within 1.5% of taking over Marksmanship.

What does this all mean? Hunters are going to need their scaling addressed for all 3 specs, especially the bottom 2 of Beast Mastery and Survival. MM is supposed to be the our scaling monster, but in relation to other classes, it's not as great as everyone believe. It also means that Hybrids have almost pushed back every single Pure spec in game. The only ones still up are Affliction and Combat. Every Hunter spec, every Mage spec, Assassination and Sub, Destruction, and soon to be Demonology will appear to lose out in the coming weeks if the trend continues.

I'm not saying that we need to be the top or starting a hybrid vs pure debate or any of that nonsense. I'm stating that the trend that the charts are showing with showing Hunters, and Pures in general, as slipping behind our Hybrid compatriots each and every week.

Additional note....even Arcane Mages that everyone looks at as the meter topper are pulling in behind Enh and with the 3% buff to Ret, will be overtaken by Ret.

I haven't looked at raid bots or how the info is collected and presented but keep in mind that one thing that's keeping hunters from topping fights is multi-dotting.

Regardless of the boss, or the mechanics I used to be able to top my guild meters every single week, without fail, because I was able to eek out every possible ounce of damage while avoiding death mechanics and the like.

It's no longer remotely possible when considering the potential that exists for Shadow Priests, Warlocks and Boomkin. Even on single target fights the aforementioned classes can be lethal, but give them superchickens to dot for the duration of the fight, or a couple of dogs with eleventymillion health, and they can pad the meters all day.

I suspect this is going to make it even more difficult to balance. It would require that the damage be broken into effective and inconsequential, similar to healing and overhealing. Sadly much of what makes up the top parses is fluff damage and is surely skewing the meters.
