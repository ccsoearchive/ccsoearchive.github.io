Hmm, that's a good question.

Do I log off in Silvermoon, where I realized what a beautiful game WoW really was?

Or at the Dark Portal, where I discovered the freedom of flight?

Or Outland, where I (to this day, probably) have spent the most time exploring, adventuring?

Or do I log off in Deathknell, where my first-ever toon (who never made it past level 11, if I remember correctly), was first dragged into the World of Warcraft to pledge eternal service to his Dark Lady? Maybe even the Ruins of Lordaeron; I dunno, endings and new beginnings seem to be a theme in that place.

But I don't want WoW to end like that.

I've always imagined the very final ending to be the return of Saregras or all the Old Gods or the Titans, tbh. Cliche, I know, but, hey. I don't want my characters to go wither away quietly in some graveyard or inn. I want them to be out there, doing what they do best: dealing furious, delicious enmity to their enemies.

At the end of Warcraft, I want to see Orcs rend their foes with the honor of the blade. I want to see humans bestow righteous, holy fury to the darkness. I want to see the Forsaken bequeath the cold, amoral kiss of the grave and unleash the most twisted, vile horrors they can. I want to see the Blood Elves unleash the greatest secrets of the Arcane, I want to see Goblins and Gnomes work together to create the greatest weapons ever built, I want to see the Night Elves and the Tauren let loose the powers of the elements of nature, I want it all. Everyone on Azeroth working together as one, for the sole purpose of beating back a force which can do nothing but destroy and unmake.

Needless to say, we fail and the planet is destroyed, ending the game. A sequel, WoW 2: Brave New World, is released about a year and a half later, featuring a handful of survivors who escaped on The Exodar and landed on some other planet somewhere in space once they ran out of fuel.

...What?

Realistically, I think I might log off kneeling at the feet of Sylvanas, the Great and Terrible Banshee Queen. When I first began this game, she was my character's purpose, his drive. Even though I play a Blood Elf now, it seems fitting to pledge final allegiance to both my new main's Ranger-General and my old main's Dark Lady at the very end.
