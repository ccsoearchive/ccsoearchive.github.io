This really makes one think, doesn't it...?

These are all for my max level characters, and all from an RP perspective.

For my blood elf warlock, I'd probably 'lay her to rest' at the Wrathgate. She was the first of my characters to finish that quest chain Horde-side, and there will always be that part of me that feels indirectly responsible for what transpired there... so my warlock gazing at the Wrathgate one last time before disappearing into the frozen wastes of Northrend... fitting.

For my human priest, she was born in Arathi, and she ran away from home to escape an arranged marriage, even going so far as to change her name. Seems fair that she would only return to Arathi when all was said and done, never to be seen or heard from again... maybe she assaults Hammerfall alone, and if she happens to die in the ensuing onslaught, so be it.

My night elf druid would probably just find a cozy place to settle down with her loved ones. There's a nice place in Northern Stranglethorn that she's particularly fond of, but Shattrath has also become a place of importance to her, as well...

My night elf mage would probably just retire and live out the rest of her days in Dalaran, teaching young and aspiring (not to mention NPC) magi their craft, doing the occasional jeweler commission here and there.

My human hunter would have loved to retire in Theramore, but because Blizzard has to go and take that from us... *grumbles under breath* Tarren Mill. She was born there, she was raised there. Forsaken-occupied or not, it's her home, and if they kill her because she drew too close... at least she dies at home.

My night elf rogue, I'm not completely sure about. Maybe she heads to Felwood, where she was born many years ago, and just isolates herself from everyone else.

But Vyranne... oh dear gods. The cemetery south, southeast of Andorhal, paying homage to Lord Uther one last time. Lord Uther the Lightbringer is, and always will be her hero and idol, and he was the reason she became a paladin - the shining image of all that is good and right in the world. After laying her weapon, shield and armor to rest in front of his shrine as a sort of silent tribute, she'll probably wander off into the area that is now the Eastern Plaguelands... whether or not she lives beyond that, only time will tell...

(My dwarf warrior would probably fly his flying machine into the middle of some epic battle, expecting to win it and have yet another tale to boast about... except that this particular battle would be the old dwarf's final battle. All that would matter to him is that he died doing something he enjoyed - fighting for the sake of others. I didn't include him above though because he's not max level yet - only level 67 or 68, can't remember exactly...)
