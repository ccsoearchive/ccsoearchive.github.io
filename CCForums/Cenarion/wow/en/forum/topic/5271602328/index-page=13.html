Who knows what the future holds?

If you knew me IRL that's my answer for anyone that starts a conversation related to what i plan on in the future, "where to go from here'....type talk.

Why? Because its true. First I highly doubt I'll still be playing or even interested in WoW in another ten years....(of course in Feb 2005 I never would have guessed i'd still be playing in 2012 either)...

But for starters...there needs to be MASSIVE and I mean MASSIVE change in the game to drive interest for that much longer. They need a severe influx of new content, I'd like them to invent a way to actually address once and for all all the 'game too easy/ game too hard' bickering ... But unless they can make the game have a 'toggle switch' of sorts that tunes up the difficulty across the board (I'm talking quest, random mob, dungeon, raid --- all of it ) I don't think the community will ever be satisified.

I'd need to see the graphic engine in WoW overhauled....and with that the all the player race models (and of both genders for each) re done....

Only with that kind of massive change will most people have any hope of even being able stick around for another 5 years yet alone another 10.

I think in reality no will CARE enough about WoW in 10 years to be bothered if Blizzard warns of the servers shutting down for good or not...why? You really mean to tell me you think in 10 *years* time there will be no WoW killer MMORPG that comes about and REALLY honestly just crushes the crap out of WoW to the point people are pretty much going "What was WoW?"....

(Keep in mind Blizzard could also be the maker of the 'WoW killer' as well..lol)

In EverQuest I was left sleeping in Siren's Grotto.

In wow? Well, back before flying, a friend and I used to spend hours climbing just for the challenge. One of our favorite spots, the "Wetlands farm" could be reached by slow-fall from Ironforge Airport. There you'll find small farmland plots around a pond at the base of a tall waterfall.

We used to sit there for hours just fishing and talking, listening to the sound of the water lapping the shore and the chirping of birds. My friend died of cancer just after Thanksgiving two years ago.

When I saw this thread a couple hours ago I started to write this post. Then stopped. I logged on and flew up there for the first time in a long while. And what do I see but two nelf NPC's, Ranadae and Kartanus, sitting right where we used to.

It gave me a chill.

Thats where I'll log.
