If Dalaran was still around, I'd log off while standing next to Applebough. Some of my best times were had in that spot during Wrath, just goofing off with people.

If it's not there anymore, I'd log off a few feet away from the Darnassus bankers.

Edit: since this was bumped up recently, I feel the need to edit my post for my own sake.

I would most definitely log off in Dalaran right next to Applebough - literally on his right side. Not only was Wrath my favorite expansion, but that spot holds so many memories.

It's where I would stand when the old school Wintergrasp raids were forming up, when I was looking for people to run a dungeon with, when LFG came out and I queued for dungeons, where I /danced and cracked quirky jokes for about 3 hours straight with chill people from a guild that no longer exists, where I did a mass Tranquility spell with a bunch of other restoration druids when we were saying goodbye to Treant form, where I met my first real best friend (on here) who also happened to be the player I looked up to most in all regards, where I'd go AFK while looking up druid healing tips or watching boss strategy videos for fun, where I'd hunker down and talk in guild chat for hours about anything and everything, where I stood when I first discovered the global OOC channels on the server.

All these memories are so fuzzy and warm.

I still make a stop next to Applebough when I happen to be in Dalaran now.
I would rather be near no other NPC and in no other city.

I'll angle my camera view the way I always angled it back in Wrath era - facing my character and Applebough from across the cobbled street so I can see our faces.

Then I'll probably cry a little over all the memories and extremely powerful emotions that were (and still are) tugged from me in this game.

I have only one level capped alt. I'd log into my death knight, have her fight what ever is a powerful and easy accessible boss at the time, and let it kill her.
What inspired me to make her died long ago but I can't let myself delete her. So I'd just let her perish and rest forever.

My other alts, all very low level, would simply be deleted.

I concur that this thread is pretty emotional, and am surprised no blues have posted.

My first character ever was a Blood Elf Mage named Dalamagus, named after Dalamar the Black from the Dragon Lance Chronicles and Magus from Chrono-Trigger. He died on a nameless low-pop pvp server. I rerolled him as a Forsaken Mage named Ashdeliche on Turalyon when I transferred here to be with friends. Though his journey was longer and more arduous than any other of my clan, he would still be the one I chose to end where it all began...again. He must finally accept his undead nature, and I would log him in the mausoleum that he was reborn into.

His son, this mage Ashvandal, was his son that he didn't meet until long after his rebirth. Ashvandal surpassed Ashdeliche in power and skill and has always been a better mage than his father. Ashvandal was my main until the launch of MoP, when I switched to using my DK witht he limited amount of time I had. Despite this, my heart has always been with Ashvandal, and most of my best memories are with him. I would like to finish his storyline with him surpassing Grand Magister Rommath as the leader of Silvermoon's mages. I would spend the weeks preceding the server shutdowns trying to solo all the old vanilla, tbc and wrath raids. The points I would like to hit are Karazahn, Black Temple, Sunwell, Ulduar and Icecrown Citadel. I would /salute Lor'themar Theron and log off in the center of the magical circle in the Mage's room.

My Deathknight, would spend the preceding weeks soloing Naxx, Firelands, and DS, and whatever raids we have going forward from here that make good memories for me. Regardless, I'm pretty sure he will log off the final time /kneeling /saluting the Banshee Queen one last time, the Horde's True Warchief.

My other character's I'd probably gather together in tavern in some city, toasting the adventures they had, wearing the heirlooms bequeathed to them from their legendary forefathers.
