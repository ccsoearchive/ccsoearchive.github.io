Right now, feral is pretty solid on the PvE side of things, but there's been a lot of complaints about Feral being too strong in PvP.

Overall, feral's sustained damage isn't overpowering, but it's the combination of bleeds rolling and shreds with the Unheeded Warning Proc up that cause a lot of damage.

If you remember Wrath as a feral druid, we were grossly underpowered in PvP, however a good player was capable of topping the charts in PvE every time.


I think the key to balancing feral's damage in PvP, but keeping us viable in PvE is Savage Roar.

Currently Savage Roar increases auto-attack damage by 80%. It's a modest increase in damage in PvE, but with the lack of uptime on target in PvP, it falls short of Ferocious Bite and Rip.

The change I suggest is returning Savage Roar to what is was in Wrath. A 30% damage increase, while nerfing our damage overall by about 23-25% (To account for the damage loss from what Savage Roar is currently.)

This would require feral to use this ability in PvP before applying bleeds or burst damage, ultimately resulting in the same sustained damage but reducing our burst by a recognizable margin.
