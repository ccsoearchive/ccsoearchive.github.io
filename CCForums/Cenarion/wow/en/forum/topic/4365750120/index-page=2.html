The sad tale of Nicias

Nicias brought down his sword down on the wretched urchin. It cleaved the urchin, leaving a bloody wound. Yet the urchin fought on. Nicias struck again, showing no mercy in his emotionless stare. After having slain the urchin, he saw his foe, Thaelas the hungerer. Without hesitation, Nicias charged. The suprised Thaelas was quick to retaliate though. He called two of his guards and they attacked Nicias. Without fear of any kind Nicias brought down his mighty sword crippling the first urchin. But Nicias was outnumbered. He gritted his teeth as he took a sword blow against his chainmail. Nicias showed no sign of flinching as he swung his sword again knocking the urchin to his knees. Yet as Nicias pulled out his blade from the bloody corpse, Thaelas stabbed him in the side with his pike. It penetrated his mail and caused a gushing wound. Nicias stumbled, almsot falling to the ground. He knew he was badly injured and he knew he wouldn't last much longer, but victory was in his grasp, he knew it. He let loose with all his furry a rush attack. He stuck Thaelas and gored him through the stomach. Thaelas struck back in retaliation, but missed. Nicias twisted the blade and kicked off the corpse, ready to face the final foe.
Nicias cut off the head off of thaelas as he stood over the three corpses. He was injured, but he had come out victorious as always. He looked over the horizan to the rising sun. He knew nothing would stand between him and his goal. The Scourge would pay. Nicias strapped the claymore to his back and like silent death, walked on.

Tranquillion

The smell of Scourge, the rotting flesh and the rattle of bones. It gave her nightmares, but she had a job to do. She could not do it alone, it was a good opportunity to test out the Fellowship...but would they answer the call?

Kara knew that Tyrael was ready to assault Deatholme. He had been training for weeks, his thirst for glory made him push himself relentlessly. She had not seen Kriest this day except for a very brief time. He seemed preoccupied, his dedication was admirable. His movements full of style and grace, like a dancer he went fluidly through his exercises.

She had not seen Telord in several days. She wondered what had kept him from the Fellowship, perhaps he had urgent business in Dalaran. Kel'Tira was happy to see another paladin and welcomed her aid. They made a good team. Would it be enough?

Kara felt bad about Ikthael. She had given him no chance to prove himself and now he seemed to have disappeared. Of course he could be sitting in a cell in Silvermoon, his fingers were quite nimble. She sighed, she had no proof of his wrongdoing. Agility, combined with weapons in each hand were deadly. Perhaps she should seek him out and invite him to accompany her on her training exercises.

There was another warrior she had not met yet, Tyrael had invited him to join the fellowship. His name was Nicias and she thought she should at least acknowledge him if and when he came to Tranquillion. She trusted Tyrael's judgement, she would be glad when they had more healers.

Thinking of healers she wondered what had become of the priest Reylin? Her heart nearly stopped when she remembered she had not seen him in a couple of days. Maybe he was injured and in need of help...somewhere in the ruins of the scar. She made a note to seek him out.

Walking along the trail deep in thought she saw a huge apparition traveling down the Scar. Her heart thumped wildly, it was so big, how could anyone even hope to best it? But her determination prevailed and she would get a few others and attack thes giant. They would triumph! Glory for the Sindorei!

Deathholme part 1- outnumbered
Nicias stared up at the gates of Deathholme. His blank stare showed no fear. "No" he thought to himself, "I have no fear, I will never run away again." Nicias drew his twin blades from his waist and charged in. Nicias battled his way facing many undead, but he left many of the battles unscathed.
Nicias Drew his blade out of the undead corpse, the black rotten flesh came off with it. Nicias swung his sword and threw the rotten flesh off. He looked around for his next victim and saw one with its back to him. He charged. Yet, this was a foolish move. Unbeknown to Nicias, there was far more than one undead creature. Nicias saw the error in his judgment as soon as he engaged the monster. Four other nearby undead saw him and began to attack him as well. Nicias was outnumber one to five. Nicias gritted his teeth and swung his swords.
Nicias swung his swords in rapid succession trying to fell one of the undead as fast as possible. Nicias tried to dodge as many of the attacks as he could, but he was being swarmed. As he fell the first creature, he backed up and quickly looked over his wounds. He had taken a few minor hits, but had also taken two direct hits that had penetrated his mail and caused his flesh to spew fourth his warm blood. He quickly grabbed a health potion he kept at his waist and in one gulp downed the bitter contents. He threw the empty vial on the ground shattering it into hundreds of pieces, he looked back up and readied himself just in time. The swarm was upon him.
The four remaining undead hacked and clawed at Nicias, but this time he was lucky. His armor protected him from harm and he only got a minor gash across his side. He fell another and smiled. Victory was his.
The three remaining did not falter their attacks at the fall of their comrade. They kept attacking with furry only the undead possessed. Nicias kept swinging his swords and hacking away at the large spider like undead creature. Nicias fell the monster with such speed that the other two undead had only hit him twice.
Nicias raised his sword again and sliced the abdomen of the other spider creature, causing it to spew fourth black blood, yet it did not even flinch. It retaliated with a swing of its claw. Nicias had just dodged an attack from the banshee and was caught off balance. The claw caught him in the leg, bringing him to one knee, but Nicias had flung his sword into the skull of the spider, slaying it on his way down.
Nicias grunted as the pain from his leg tingled his spine. The banshee flew in to what seemed like his last moments. Nicias had a quick flashback, remembering his family and what had happened. Renewed fury filled Nicias and as the banshee brought its claw down on Nicias' head, he raised his sword and blocked it.
"I cannot DIE! NOT HERE! Fell my fury, my sorrow and all my ANGER!" He shouted at the top of his lungs. He lunged forward gutting the banshee and pushing it back. He quickly pulled his sword out and began to slice away. The banshee slowly moved back. As it went back even farther, Nicias grabbed his other sword from the skull of the spider creature, and used that as well. The banshee, heavily wounded fell on both knees, staring at Nicias with its eyes filled of hate. Nicias jsut stared back. He crossed his swords and put them to the throat of the banshee. "My hate is greater than yours foul creature, now die!" he said as he executed the banshee, removing its head.
Nicias looked at the five corpses at his feet and sheathed his bloody swords. "Sister, I will avenge you." he said as he walked out of Deathholme and back to Tranquillian to rest and heal is injuries.

Farstrider Enclave

The Farstriders and my family have never really seen eye-to-eye, but these ones seem nice enough. They fight back our common enemies, and have work for me to do, so I fight. Trolls, lynxes, bats, scourge, spirits, on and on. Night Elves.

I had a close brush with one of the Troll leaders, but I am learning from my mistakes, and from the bodies around his tower.

I realized, while I was sitting here, thinking, resting, reveling in the luxury of not looking over my shoulder every few seconds, why I am keeping this journal.

It is less a commemoration of my life than it is an outlet for the emotions I dare not show. Fear, grief, loneliness. Terror, pain, remorse. I have to be strong for the people around me, that is part of what I accepted when I shouldered the burden of protector. Honestly? I am scared. I am afraid. I am saddened by the death that surrounds me. My sister-- (The next few words are rendered illegible by a water mark on the page) --memory that I honor.

Last day at home
The whole village was in silence as a hawkstrider rode into town. The rider dismounted and approached a young woman and handed over a letter before mounting and ridding off into the distance.
Like so many before her the letter arrived signalling that it was her time to leave. But unlike the others she vowed that she would one day return. Sure the others returned as great stories of triumph and glory or as faint whispers of death and demise. But she, she would return in the flesh. This was her home and her home it will be once again for what did she have but that?

Sunstrider Isle
Upon arriving at the Sunspire she sighed heavily and looked for Ranger Sallina. She thought to herself once she spotted her “A huntress so this is what’s to become of my life.” Sallina was kinder than she thought a trainer would be. The ranger lent her a Dragonhawk and gave her a bow and taught her, her very first lesson.

A night in an inn
Leather boots rest at the foot of the bed in which the young huntress lay sleeping. A pile of parchment on the table beside her gets blown over by a light breeze. Some of the loose pages are now visible all of which have the same random squiggle at the bottom. No, not a random squiggle, a feather?

Lesson 1: Sallina reminds me that it’s not always a race to the death. Sometimes one must stop and take a moment to breathe deeply, then aim to regain focus during a fight. Loosing ones focus can cost you your life.
-----------------------------
Day 2: I’d never seen so many people in one place, or bunnies for that matter. Falconwing square was buzzing with people of all races and class. What were they doing? Running around the square looking in bushes and under chairs. What was lost that required so many people to look for it? As I walked up for a closer inspection there was a crunch under my feet. My heart raced as I thought I had just broken whatever it was that everyone was after. Checking to see if anyone was watching me I slowly bent down to see what I had done. Crushed chocolate and egg shell? What was going on here?
I dodged my way through the crowd of people to get to the inn as I intended. As I got to the door I was offered the cutest little basket if I would only gather some of the chocolate eggs that were hidden about the square. Before long I had joined the masses in the hunt for eggs.
I was just picking up an egg when ‘poof’ I was suddenly a pink bunny! Not something I would like to experience again anytime soon. After spending some time gathering eggs for the basket among other things. In the spirit of noblegarden I bought some black bunny ears which I donned before continuing with more meaningful tasks for the people of falconwing square.
-----------------------------
Lesson 2: Hannovia of Falconwing Square taught me about how important learning to use my melee weapon can be. Once a target gets too close I will no longer be able to use my bow and I will be forced to do some close combat. If I don’t master this as well it could very well mean death.
-----------------------------
Day 5: I was now ready to train my own pet. I had always heard stories of big cats that were found in the barrens. Before I was to head out on my journey for a lion I wanted to stop in and thank Ranger Sallina for the use of her dragonhawk. Once it was returned there was only one thing to do, head to Silvermoon.
The city was awe inspiring. I worked my way to the Inner Sanctum of the Sunfury Spire to take the orb of translocation to Undercity. The feeling was very similar to that which I had experienced in Duskwither Spire. Once I arrived in the outer parts of Undercity it was a short walk to the towers on the outskirts of Brill. I caught a zeppelin to Orgrimmar. The city smelled or orc.
I passed a bulletin board which had a request for help on the road between Durotar and the Northern Barrens. I might as well make myself useful while in search of a cat worth my attention. To my huge disappointment all of the lions were beyond my reach. No worries I will work for the people in the Barrens until I can tame a cat of my own.
