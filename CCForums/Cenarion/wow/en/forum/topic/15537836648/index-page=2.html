I'll preface my post by saying that the concept behind this guild seems very innovative and community driven.

In fact, especially given all of the enthusiastic comments left here by both members and people who've worked with Yielding Veil Services, I was fairly impressed! I'd even considered getting more information and putting in an app if it seemed a good fit.

Unfortunately, before I did so, I ended up in a situation with one of your members that left a bit of a sour taste in my mouth. I was having an open, public RP with a friend near Stormwind Harbor when a member of the guild popped into the scene. (Logged into the scene, in fact. lol) Obviously I won't mention names, since negative call-outs are a no-no on these forums, and I wouldn't do so on a public forum like this even it was acceptable.

My friend and I thought it'd be fun to remain IC and address this person to encourage some walk-up RP, which we did and it seemed like everything was going alright. Within maybe ten minutes or so, this person left the two of us, and my friend and I resumed our RP. A few minutes later, my friend is telling me that this person enthusiastically contacted her OOCly in whispers to invite them to join Yielding Veil Services, provided she hurries her character to level 100 and sent an accompanying IC letter to her mailbox with a formal invitation to join. (Also, I see that not all of your members are lv100, so I don't know why he asked her to hurry up like that in order to join...) I know, it's nothing monstrous, but I'm getting to my point now.

I hadn't been given any such invitation or correspondence, and ICly, his reactions to my character were a lot more chilly than those he'd shown upon my friend. This is ordinarily fine with me due to normal RP chemistry, especially where walk-ups are concerned. But I suppose the difference was, my friend's character was practically fawning over his and showing him plenty of attention and mine wasn't. The entire interaction was friendly, interactive and I didn't act "Betsy Badass" either. (In fact, I find the whole "pained and distant" act pretty cliche and old hat, but that's just my opinion.) But regardless, that was the only difference between myself and my friend ICly that we'd both observed about the entire interaction.

I'm very confident in my ability as a role-player so this isn't about validation. (Even if it was, I know that random RP certainly isn't the place for that anyway.) I RP my characters the way I've conceived of them, as I expect most everyone worth their salt to do, I don't RP to cater to anyone else's ego, especially for someone who I don't even know personally. But that experience left me feeling as though perhaps - at least with this particular person - that's considered an acceptable 'in' to the guild. So, if that's an acceptable form of entry to your guild, then it calls the whole thing into question.

I'm sure you're all very nice people and quite good at what you do. You've got two pages of people here praising and applauding Yielding Veil Services. Know that I don't hold any grudges here, and should I ever run into another member of your guild, I will act just as naturally ICly as I would to anyone else.

I really dislike being "that gal", but I saw this thread was front-paged and decided to add my two coppers. Do with the information what you like, or do nothing at all with it. But as with any good business IRL, you get good reviews and bad reviews, regardless of how top-notch you might be 99.5% of the time. This isn't even a "bad review" post for Yielding Veil Services, actually.

The finer point of this post is a friendly warning that you've got at least one person out there, representing your guild "business", who clearly makes recruitment decisions based upon who appeals to their ego the most. We included this person in an open RP, not an impromptu guild interview. Nothing so overly impressive occurred during this ten minute walk-up that would've reasonably caused someone to say "Oh man, I have to recruit this level 20ish hunter NOW!".

I'm open-minded enough not to blame an entire group for one person, but let's face it, most people don't operate that way and will dismiss an entire guild because of a single person's conduct. It's all about reputation after all, isn't it?

Please don't mistake any of this as hostility. I felt that this merited the guild's attention particularly, and that of our fellow WyrmrestAccordians who might be planning to patronize your services ICly, or have done so in the past. This was also far more than 'send me a whisper' content. Please feel free to contact me though, if you have any questions, concerns or reservations (pun intended :P) about what I've said here.

I also apologize for the length of this post, but I felt it was necessary to paint the entire picture for context.

Well, if nothing else, free bump for Yielding Veil Services. :P
