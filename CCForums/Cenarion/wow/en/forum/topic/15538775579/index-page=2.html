Last night the Servitors investigated reports of strange events at a mine in the Eastern Plaguelands. After dodging (or in Eth's case falling down and getting run over by) a stampede of rats, the Servitors discovered an oubliette hidden deep within the mine, with evidence that something had been imprisoned there for a long time. It's imprisoned no longer.

The Servitors don't know it yet, but this will play into a storyline involving an old enemy from earlier this year, and there will be intrigues and dangers to come! The best part is that this is only one of many stories we're planning, and now is a great time to join the adventures, if you're looking for an RP guild that:

1) Welcomes and encourages member contribution.
2) Is active, takes RP outside of the garrison, and plays with others.
3) Offers a mature, safe, and friendly OOC atmosphere.

Uh oh.

I took a team to the mine. We prepared ourselves to face mind-magic, but what we found was far stranger. There was a weight to the darkness, an oppressive and claustrophobic feeling that grew exponentially as we descended the shaft. The torches seemed to burn dimmer, and within the darkness we felt things moving just on the edge of the peripheral vision--I don't know if it was real or imagined.

Then came the rat stampede.

It sounds like a funny thing, but it was not funny when it happened. There was nothing remotely funny about it. The ground under our feet boiled with a swarm of living, squirming rats. I toppled right over and I can still feel their tiny, clawed paws--hundreds of them--on my body, my face. I can still smell the musty-filth stink of their fur. I've bathed several times since then and each time I scrub myself extra hard for fear of some lingering taint. Luckily the little beasts weren't of a mind to attack. Rather, they seemed bent on escape.

I didn't notice it, but some of the other Servitors did, and reported it: the rats all appeared to have red eyes. Not the retina-reflection of normal eyes, but something unnatural and unholy.

The stampede probably lasted only a few seconds, though it felt much longer. When it was over, we proceeded further down the shaft but didn't have far to go--this was as deep as the excavation went. Nelmadge was the first to spot it--a hole in the rock wall. When we took a closer look and shined a light into it, we discovered a small chamber not much bigger than a coffin. An oubliette, really. The walls were hewn from solid rock, but smooth except for what appeared to be many, many claw marks. On each wall, and the roof and floor, there were sigils carved into the rock and inlaid with mithril.

On each wall except for the one that the miners had breached, of course. Jo didn't recognize the specific sigils but she confirmed they had the feel of a containment spell.


What did those miners unleash to run free around Azeroth? We don't know yet, but we're pretty sure the Servitors will have to clean it up. To join in the adventure, apply at http://servitorsoflothar.guildlaunch.com and come share your RP talents with us!

Things are heating up for the Servitors. Last night they were in Hearthglen to investigate a strange malady, a waking coma that seemed to strike people at random. This appears to be linked to the presence of red-eyed rats that swarmed from the sewers and escaped into the hills last night. The Servitors managed to kill some specimens but...

They wouldn't stay dead. Not after stabbing, stomping, shooting, freezing, and burning with fire.

If the rats continue on their westward track, they'll enter Tirisfal. Is Undercity their destination? Are the Forsaken their masters...or their next victims?

Now is an excellent time to get your RP on with the Servitors. The door is even open for cross-faction RP with Forsaken characters! If you're interested, you can poke a Servitor in game, post here, or send us a message through our website, http://servitorsoflothar.guildlaunch.com; we'd love to hear from you.

Etharion's journal takes a self-reflective turn in his latest entry, as he considers how he's changed over the past few years:

Meri told me, more than once, that one day I'd have to learn to value myself and not look to others to do it for me. Last night as Thoran ran down the list of things he loved about me, instead of denying it in my heart, I thought, okay. Sometimes I screw up, or get enraged with myself or things happening around me, and Elune knows I still "get the sad" as Meri used to say. And sometimes I just don't know what's the right thing to do. But all things considered maybe I'm starting to think Etharion Longsight isn't such a terrible thing to be, after all.


Etharion's growth as a character could not and would not have happened without the incredible RP that I was able to find with <Servitors of Lothar>. This guild has been the experience I'd hoped to find when I first tried my hand at roleplaying in WoW. Eth has been challenged and hurt, he's found love and camaraderie, and bit by bit he's grown in directions I never expected. At the same time, SoL offered a safe, friendly, and supportive OOC community full of amazing people.

This has been my experience, and it's the experience I want to pass on to other Servitors, veteran members and newcomers alike. So do you have a character that's sitting in limbo, and you want them to grow and change? Do you have storylines you want to share with others? Do you want an OOC community of open-minded, smart, fun-loving, mature players? (Okay, sometimes we're not always mature. Pun wars and fart jokes may happen.) Do you want a guild that works to keep the drama IC only, where it belongs? Then I welcome you to visit our website at http://servitorsoflothar.guildlaunch.com and check us out.

I am very pleased to say we've had an influx of exciting new people since we re-opened recruitment, and some of them are already proposing and running their own events! We have a new member running sparring practices, and another who came up with an idea to help build emergency housing in Talador for some Draenei who were burned out of their homes. We also have a new chaplain, a new aide-de-camp, and as the Academe fills out we look forward to grooming and promoting a new officer.

One thing we'd love to see more of, though, is more druids and shaman. In <Servitors of Lothar> we're not just interested in combat missions. Historically we've also strived to heal damage wrought on our world by the Legion, the Scourge, and the magical or biological weapons of madmen (and madwomen--looking at you, Sylvanas). At one time we even had an entire division devoted exclusively to druids and shaman and their mission to protect the natural world.

So do you have a shaman or druid character looking for a home? Even if they're not militarily inclined, they might still find their place among the Servitors of Lothar! Come visit us at http://servitorsoflothar.guildlaunch.com and if you like what you find, hit the "apply" link to send us your IC letter of interest. We hope to hear from you!
