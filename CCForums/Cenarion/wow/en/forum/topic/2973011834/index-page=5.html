Everyone always complains about their class as if it was the most broken piece of crap they've ever had when there's a few problems with it. But of course when they end up on top it's "We're fine, lrn2play kk stop the QQ". Just sayin'.
Kiting is also something that gets thrown out there a lot as to why hunters are the weakest ranged class to damage. It's not exactly the answer.

First and foremost, kiting requires the cooperation of your opponent. If he wants to stay behind a LoS object, then kiting class or not, you aren't kiting anything. This is a weakness of all range, but it's something the supposed king of kiting classes shares meaning there's no advantage at all to the kiting class. Now if there were some ability the hunter had to force someone out of hiding that no other class had like say perhaps lobbing a grenade through the object, then maybe.

Second, the advantage of being at ranged as their damage mitigation is completely nullified versus other ranged opponents. Here the hunters lack of basic defense versus something as basic as a DoT is front and center. They have no DoT wipe and no absorb mechanic. When every ranged class can duck behind a LoS object to mitigate damage, the hunter can't even use their heal.

Third, between ranged and melee, kiting should be a 50/50 proposition overall. As Ghostcrawler says, nobody likes to be helpless and kited all day long. So a melee should have chances to get at the hunter, and the hunter should have chances to get away. This works fine and dandy in a 1v1 situation, in fact based on the skill of either opponent, it isn't a 50/50 situation. When you take this to NvN situations, the hunter's kiting ability is largely nullified. For example, a warrior's Charge and then a dk's Death Grip. This is something that can plague all ranged, but hunters above all ranged are the most vulnerable at melee despite being the most heavily armored off all of them, well besides elemental shaman.

Ghostcrawler also once said hunters, being one of the heavier armor classes, should not be so weak in melee. This was way back in TBC when Raptor Strike hit like a truck. A hard hitting Raptor Strike isn't the answer in today's PvP environment. Hunters need to be an undesireable target. A class that takes disproportionate amount of time and effort to kill. They can't do much else. Their CC is among the worse of all the damage specs. Their damage isn't really heads and shoulders better than any other damage specs. Their auxiliary control abilities like interrupts or snares aren't much better than any other damage spec.
