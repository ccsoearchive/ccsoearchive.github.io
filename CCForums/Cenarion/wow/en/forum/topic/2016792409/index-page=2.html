I agree, deleting whole threads is not the answer. It is dismissive and heavy-handed. Deleting a thread with no reason given will just encourage more threads like it. How can you correct a behavior if you remove all references to what was bad about it?

If a decent criticism or idea is clobbered by trolls, take action on the trolls. If the idea is echoing similar threads at the time, lock it and let it drift. At least then the community would have an idea of where they stand. And you could actually find it later.

I followed several threads today that were Four-Oh-Four'd, and it's frustrating. It really feels like the integrity of the discussion environment here is lacking. Edit: Not just lacking, but actively undermined.

It'd be nice if the search feature was more intuitive to use. It's more useful than the old one, but only if you know the search parameters. The problem is that I don't think there's any link in the forums to those parameters.

Indeed. The loss of the "Advanced" search button was a definite step backwards (even though the search system itself is now significantly better).

There are actually quite a few little UI tweaks that could help improve things:

1. Why are the vote up/down buttons hidden by default?
2. Why is "REPLY" spelled out in full, but "QUOTE" is instead a cryptic bubble icon?
3. Why isn't there a box for "Search in this thread" to see if a specific point has already been made in a given discussion?
4. Why isn't there either an Advanced search link, or at least a "Search Help" link pointing to the blogpost above?

That's off the top of my head as an occasional forum user - I'm sure there are plenty of other minor things that could be improved.
