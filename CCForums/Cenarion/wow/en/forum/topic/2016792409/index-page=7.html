After seeing 2 threads recently locked, one with a redirect here, i suppose ill throw my thoughts out, doubt it will matter tho.


We do see lots of repeated threads not only in the general section, but in most... if not all sections of the WoW boards. I see 2 big reasons for such unrest and repetition.

First, players are very passionate about the games they play, and when something is brought up its generally because the players are searching for answers about something. Posting in a 47 page thread, while that does keep it in one place, generally leads to lots of bickering and many many posts get buried in the garbage and really never seen, especially if it happens to fall at the bottom of any particular page. Players want to be heard and find out all the whats and whys, getting your post burried doesnt seem like the best way to go about seeking those answers.

Second, Ghostcrawler used to post pretty frequently, keeping dialogue with the players, explaining things and answering questions. Kinda sucks for us all now because he doesnt do that anymore and nobody has really stepped up to fill that void, so the communication from blue to player has dropped considerably.

IMO if a person can take the time to read and lock a thread(s) they could also take a minute to actually answer the question, to respond to any of the threads on the subject. That alone would give players something that they came here for and because of the big blue BLIZZ tag on the thread they responded to, it would help eliminate much of the over posting of the same stuff. Of course not every question from every person is going to be answered, but when there are multiple threads about a topic, would it really kill you guys to just suck it up and reply instead of deleting or locking?

Just to follow-up with everyone. We are always looking at ways to improve communications and moderation. We will continue to lock threads and delete as we feel are necessary, but will try to be better about communicating why a post was locked. Please do not create new posts asking why.


There appears to be a blanket policy in effect that while it seems that you (or any other CM) are trying to be helpful when it comes to the deletion of a post or a thread, I have been in this situation several times recently on the forums - that is posts vanishing, and in one case a whole thread - and unfortunately the policy and procedure you currently have makes it impossible to actually verify whether a post was deleted for an actual violation or because the CM was having a bad day.

I am not suggesting that every CM deletes posts or threads for the wrong reason, or even whether it is a common or rare occurance. What I can say is that just recently, it was confirmed in email that a thread that was deleted of mine was done so in error and actually did not violate any aspect of the code of conduct.

One aspect of your policy that needs to be looked at (at least from what CM's have told me) is that once a post/thread is deleted there is no way for you to recover or even see what the post contained. In addition there is no policy of notification as to a deletion.

Not every post/thread that is deleted obviously needs to be sent a notification to that account, many are self explanatory, however if you want positive progress in communication and relating to the day to day pruning, a deletion of a post/thread shoud by default cause a history/save of that post and require a reason be entered by the CM doing the deletion.

Should someone who is "very passionate" about the post they created, or the thread they made, wish to enquire as to why the post/thread was removed, the history would allow the CM to investigate to ensure that it was done so correctly and additionally adds a layer of protection for the CM and the player involved.

Unfortunately as a result of the deletions of posts and the thread that I had made, not to mention the inability to access what the post/thread was by Blizzard, the only option left was to initiate a complaint against the CM staff involved. This is something that can easily be avoided on Blizzards part.

Lastly, there is not an expectation that CM staff should or will comment on each and every question/problem/feedback etc on the forums. Part of the detriment of the forums recently however have been in part caused by the absence of a response in discussions or issue threads that are both topical and considered by many players important and dirctly affecting game play.

The example I can use here is Archeology. I can not even begin to guess at the sheer volume of posts in relation to this issue, or the number of threads or the number of complaints. In addition, after considerable period of time not responding, changes were announced via patch notes that would in some respects, cause further irritation and heart ache and the hole fiasco, from our side, appears to have been ignored. Obviously I assume that i not the case, but it is understandable that the lack of interest in any blue posting anything on the subject in such a considerable amount of time, and then posting what can be seen as "nerfs" all the while seeing posts on just about every other change in the game, is very disheartening and that can be seen in the multitude of posters expressing their feelings that Blizzards essentially couldn't give 2 pence for the profession.

Threads that go this big are the perfect example of where the posts such as "we're listening" etc are the most helpful and where they should be applied.

All of the above are aspects that if dealt with could improve the forums.

Additionally, with the retraction of Real ID had with it an announcement that the WoW forums would launch with Cataclysm, a Unique posting ID - that is that you would have a unique, user generated (so you chose it yourself) ID that would be your "posting name" on the forums thereby assisting in preventing quite the level of trolling. Obviously it would not remove it, but it was a step in the right direction.

I note however that to date that feature appears to have been lost and my last request as to what happened with it tried to have a very slippery GM weasel his way out of the wording of the Real ID retraction to make it sound like it was a "possible" feature.

What happened to this feature?

I will say that I am not just offering suggestions, I did say to a GM in game recently that I would happy to volunteer my time online to actually assist in the implementation and several of these fixes however was told that Blizzard do not take volunteers after an incident where "volunteers" sued Blizzard because they weren't paid.......which I can't quite get over that.

But the offer still stands.
