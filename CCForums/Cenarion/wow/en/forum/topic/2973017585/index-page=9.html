I've played this hunter for almost 6 years now. I used to love battle grounds in vanilla. As time went on, however, I felt less and less useful in pvp. Now I only ever did pvp casually but even then you tend to notice things.

I've always felt hunters get the poor-man's version of other skills or really weak skills altogether.

Master's Call - dependent on a pet which can be killed, taunted out of los or out of range all causing the spell to fail or unable to be casted. Compare that to HoF.

Disengage - not usable out of combat, sensitive to elevations, goes backwards, doesn't break stuns or roots. Compare that to blink.

Counterattack - 5% chance to root someone. But really people can attack you from behind so they can drop that to a 0%. It amazes me that this talent is still in the game. I think it is hands down the worst talent in the game and it boggles my mind that it made it past the remake.

Resurrect pet - 10s base cast the BM tree can't even lower the cast time anymore. For a class that is so dependent on it's pet for damage and utility it is absurd that this cannot be shortened in any manner.

Camo - I'm not entirely sure what this skill is supposed to be for. Mages get Ring of frost, rogues get a portable LoS in smoke bomb. I have found no situation in which I thought to myself, wow camo was really useful there.

Trap launcher - Blizzard took a neat skill in frost arrow and actively made it worse for us. Trap launcher, for cc purposes, is inferior to frost arrow in every way. It's clunky and for what purpose? Only 2 of our traps are worth using in pvp(the ice ones), snake trap is a slot machine. Imagine if a mage's frostbolt randomly, instead of slowing their target, put a dot on their target that they could wipe rendering them un-ccable.

Speaking of cc - Hunters need serpent sting for damage, especially marks hunters, but we can't cc with it up. Not a problem I'll just wipe my own dot off by applying scorpid sting. Oh what's that? You're removing that skill? Well that sucks I guess but I suppose cc should be a choice right? Dot damage or cc? See hex, cyclone, stuns, poly glyph, fear damage threshold etc...

It goes on and on. The absurd idea of a class with a minimum range having to cast to it's resource back in an environment full of snares and dispels which makes getting out of melee range if trained a heroic feat.

Having played this class for 6 years I can say one thing. Mechanic wise I feel very little has changed over that time regarding this class play style wise. We have gained new mechanics and new resources but we're still fight the same battles and it actually feels worse.

I've never played much competitive pvp but it doesn't take a genius to realize when something isn't working, to know when you're at a disadvantage.
