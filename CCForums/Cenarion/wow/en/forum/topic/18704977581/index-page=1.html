I. Summary

IC: The Terenas Foundation is a charitable investment firm founded by the Alteraci knight Astianel Katrignis Senior. Named in honor of Lordaeron's late king, Terenas Menethil II, and funded by a fortune amassed following the events of the Second War, the Foundation seeks to ease the suffering of the Alliance's general population. Carrying out a number of public works, the most notable being the distribution of storage capable devices, the Foundation continues to honor the example set by those long gone and provide assistance to those who need it most.

OOC: With some friendships stretching back nearly nine years and guild experience lasting just as long, we intend to provide a stable foundation for those desiring a fun, drama-free environment that caters to roleplay, PvP, PVE, and general shenanigans.

II. Basic Rules

1.) Be good to eachother -- and to everyone else -- if you cannot, then be the least bad you can be.

2.) Try not to depend entirely on others to fulfill your own wants.

3.) If what we do here ceases to appeal to you, then make a change.

4.) Listen to the designated leader.

5.) Refrain from griefing slain or living enemies and comrades (tea-bagging, spitting, camping, etc).


Failure to uphold these standards will result in punitive action.

III. Activities

Bag-Folk Outreach
Near daily dispensing of Netherweave Bags to any and all in need of storage capable devices.

Total Bags Given: 9,000+

This takes place typically in Stormwind City next to the Canal Tailor and Fit Shop (Between Trade District and Cathedral Square) and it can happen at any time of day by any member(s).

Raid Team
Casual. Loot is set to Personal. Master Loot in special cases.

Normal Hellfire Citadel: 13/13
Heroic Hellfire Citadel: 5/13

Tuesdays @ 7:00pm-10:00pm Server.
Thursdays @ 7:00pm-10:00pm Server.

Currently seeking ranged DPS x2 (any class) that can push 40k+ and a Healer (Monk, Paladin, Shaman, or Priest).

Point(s) of Contact: Edwint or Katrignis

IV. RP

Our current roleplay model is rather open and inclusive as we aim to cater to all types of roleplayers. Stories seem to naturally spawn from the charitable acts of the Foundation. Many of our storylines are open-ended and everyone, in-guild and out, is free to contribute to them.

Who knew giving away bags could be so exciting?

Branches Within the Guild:

    Triage Unit
    A moderately like-minded group of individuals made up of Menders, Healers, and Surgeons. They hold a purpose of lending medical aid to those in need free of any expected reciprocation.

    Bag Knights
    The Foundation's order of adventurers. A collective force that operates together in attempt to achieve reasonable goals.

    Pathfinders
    A group within the Foundation which locates trade routes, expands merchandising and discovers products.

    Commonwealth
    Assist in the upkeep of the Foundation's assets, personnel, and public image.

V. Ranks

Trial < Alt = Pledge < Beneficiary < Shareholder = Director < Trustee < Chairman

Ascension is handled through a member's consistent activity, level of participation and time spent within the Guild.

Inactivity: If a person does not login for a month without giving ANY prior notice of their absence, then they will be removed from the Guild. If one does supply notice, then only their alts will be purged.

Any and all inactive parties will be readmitted upon return to the game as necessary.


Trial
--------------------
One who is new to the guild and has yet to prove their intentions/reasons for being here IC or OOC.

Alt
--------------------
An alternative character for members above Trial.

Pledge
--------------------
At least four weeks in the Guild.

A member who has proven their intentions and reasons for being here IC or OOC. They should fit in nicely and seem to mesh well with the population.

Beneficiary
--------------------
A member who has been around for a while (at least 8 weeks). They are an integral part of what we're doing and have signed up on the Guild forum.

Shareholder
--------------------
A veteran (at least six months in the Guild). This person has somehow managed to endure all of the nonsense and proven their tenacity time and time again.

Director
--------------------
Someone that leads/manages specific fields of development within the Guild. A sub-officer.

Individual heads of respective branches: Triage Unit, Bag Knights, Pathfinders, and Commonwealth.

Trustee
--------------------
An organizational leader of the Guild. They uphold certain aspects while maintaining a positive presence. Capable of making decisions when other officials are not around. Officer.

RP Focus:

    Organize and host general events for the Guild; both free form and non.
    Provide lore assistance as necessary
    Aid new role players as necessary

PvE Focus:

    Organize and host PVE related content within the Guild; Dungeons and/or Raids
    Provide DPS and Healing assistance upon request

PvP Focus:

    Organize and host PvP related content within the Guild; random and rated battlegrounds
    Provide DPS and Healing assistance upon request

General Focus:

    Administrative tasks as necessary
    Provide assistance to other focuses upon request/as necessary

Chairman & Co-GM
--------------------
Provide assistance and food-for-thought toward other members of the Guild. Administrative tasks and general upkeep of Guild operations. Fostering and cultivating leadership and growth in member base.

Out of Character Information:

Most of our members are 18+. It is not a requirement.

Many of the Guild members are enrolled in academic pursuits at various institutions.
Others are people with families who enjoy the good vibes.

We have, roughly, 12 active members. The "core" group is made up of about 7. We use this term lightly as it often implies a disparity between the members. We do our best to include everyone. There needs to be initiative on both sides for our new relationships to work out.

Our hours of activity are between 5:00pm - 12:00am Server (Pacific Time) and often earlier/later than that on the weekends.

We hold a firm stance on being a no drama Guild and will respond accordingly to people who try to cause upheaval inside or outside the Guild.

There is a brief period of meshing a new member into our group and this where many people have the greatest concern:

How do I mold into a group that has been together for such a long time?

It can be stressful for a time, especially for shy people. Many of us are very shy ourselves, but we do appreciate having fresh faces around. We give each other a lot of flak and can often argue, but it is all part of the process. We have developed an interesting relationship with each other. We are dysfunctional. I caution those who are sensitive as sometimes things can get heated, but it is all with the most loving of intentions. A person may appear like an arrogant tikbalang, but I (as the GM) firmly believe everyone is a nice, worthwhile individual behind our seemingly coarse exteriors.

With that said, we have had little to no serious drama. We never truly had much to talk about in all of our years of existence. And we will try our best to keep it that way.
