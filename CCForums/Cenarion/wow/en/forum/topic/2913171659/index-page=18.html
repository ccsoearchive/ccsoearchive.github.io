You're an Enhancement Shaman without a gap closer outside of Frost Shock + Frozen Power. That's why you have issues.

For other melee like Rogues and Warriors, it's hard for the Hunter to get away due to the constant slow effects active on top of their gap closers like Charge, Leap, Shadowstep, Sprint, etc on cooldowns that can near directly counter our defensive toolkit

Hunter damage is also quite pitiful on Plate targets or anyone with physical mitigation. Mail is currently the weakest armor in the current design. Enhancement gets hit harder by our Steadies than most clothies, and does pathetic amounts of damage to plate. Aimed Shot is also pretty weak against plate. This forces the majority of Hunter damage into a predictable very very short burst window every 10 seconds minimum. Said burst window can have the gap between them increased greatly by sitting on the Hunter or LoSing to prevent Focus generation, or use of Thunderclap/Frost Fever to greatly slow generation.

Also keep in mind that traps can only be reliably used on a target via Scatter Trap which is also quite predictable. As a melee, you should barely ever actually hit a trap laid by the Hunter while on top of him as it has an incredibly small trigger radius on players and simple strafing around the Hunter completely avoids triggering the trap unless they want to take a severe pounding trying to force a trigger, which means they aren't kiting to avoid further damage taken.

The Hunter toolkit is essentially still using the design of kite to win, but the majority of classes have been given tools to counter that, and the game overall has evolved beyond it. The Hunter toolkit is essentially the kiting toolkit we've had since vanilla and BC when kiting was effective, but in today's game with melee having as many gap closers, the plethora of movement impairing effects on classes, and the amount of ranged CC available have made the toolkit a clunky and obsolete mess with little true effectiveness except on the odd spec like Enhancement, Blood, or Ret.

The removal of the minimum range is Blizzard finally making an attempt to modernize the class after its become so apparent this expansion especially that it just isn't working. I expect a lot of changes to come.
