Just adding my 2c to this, because of all the Hunter problems the OP title best summarizes it for me. "Not fun."

I have alts of every single class. My lowest alt is a 60 Lock. I have 5 85s, 3 80+, and a 75 DK I started last weekend. I've raided as MT, as heals, as melee dps, and as caster dps, and I've PvP'd on a lot of toons as well.

Every single alt I have is "more fun" to play than my sad current main. This one, with 10k+ achievement points and several server firsts from LK.

Why is that? Sure there are loads of bugs and I know Blizzard will get around to fixing them eventually. Those bugs aren't really the problem.

It's that our shots are so uneven. Our focus-generating attack hits for too little, and our focus-dumping attacks hit for too much.

Nerf the hell out of Chimera shot and buff the hell out of Steady and Cobra.

That way, I won't feel like a 6-year old boy struggling to tread water in the local urine-filled community swimming pool, while everyone else is swimming laps around me in the fun dept.

Hell, the DK is walking on water next to me.

Resource starvation is not fun. Sure, it's "roughly" the same rotation we had prior to 4.0, but instead of an even pace, we're constantly drowning and fighting for air.

I also think it's a flippant answer to say that because 1% does well the class is fine. You can easily make major QoL improvements that don't make that 1% OP.

if 99% of my customers can't figure out how to sit comfortably on a chair that I've designed, that's ultimately my fault, not the people sitting incorrectly.

Feel free to explain to me why any of that is overpowered.

The change to Camo would finally give us something vs dot classes instead of being a free kill.
Besides that it's our lvl85 ability, it should be somewhat powerful. In comparison to several other classes 85 abilities right now, Camo is clown shoes.

Adding 20% dodge to Fox isn't OP by any stretch, we had a good deal more than that ripped from us in 4.0 with ZERO compensation. In PVE we're typically right after tanks in heals required, and in PVP no class goes down faster than a hunter, none.

The MMM change has a 2 fold benefit. In PVE it would relegate casted aimed shot where it belongs as an opener and smooth out MM's rotation considerably.
In pvp it would in effect provide an instant AS on an 8 sec CD, allowing for a much more sustainable level of damage output, a DPS needs to be able to provide pressure to be viable.

And lastly, giving traps some damage absorbtion would mean SURV hunters, since it's to far in the tree for the other specs, would be able to use BA and Serp and still have the ability to trap, once again enhancing sustainable damage output.


There's nothing OP about this stuff, for pete's sake, Hunters are so beat down and used to being gimped that they think viability would make them OP.

Would some of the things I propose need tuning, possibly, but look at it in comparison to other classes abilities, why is talent symetry OP for Hunters?
