The reason there is an insane number of hunter threads popping up is because as soon as a hunter finishes leveling up and decides they want to pvp they find out the reality of the hunter situation and try to get an answer to blizzard about why hunters are so outdated.

People don’t understand that the hunter that just killed you in a BG doesn’t represent the entire class at all times in all given situations.

Basically people think playing a hunter is face roll because they got killed by a hunter they think shouldn’t have been able to do so. This leads to a lot of "huntard" talk and general disregard for complaints a hunter should have, because they are obviously ok. Being kited is a frustrating thing, and it tends to leave people with a feeling of complete domination, which in turn creates hatred towards that class.

But you have to look at this in the eyes of the hunter. Yes we can avoid taking damage but for how long. And as we are avoiding this damage how much damage are we doing to you?

I don’t need to repeat what Audaces already posted because he said it way better then I could have. The only thing that I would definitely like to point out is that this isn’t something that popped up the last few weeks. This has been an ongoing issue since season 1 in TBC.

Hunters are under represented because they are so difficult to perform the same tasks other classes do with relative ease.

I don’t want to belittle a skilled warrior but let’s just say to be a 2400+ warrior, mage, lock, shaman, druid you need to be pretty good. To be a 2400+ hunter you need to be great.
