Warriors and mages are the most identifiable fantasy archetypes. Hunters all-to-easily fit into other genres for it to be true about hunters.

You don't typically get fantasy hunter movies without a mage of some sort in it. Heck, you don't really get fantasy hunter movies. On the other hand, you might get a wizard or mage in 100 fantasy movies without a hunter-type character in it.

Blizzard wants to make sure their class development caters to this socially accepted phenomenon by ensuring that the mage class is one of the strongest, easiest to play classes because they know there's a good chance it's going to be one of the top two choices for an inexperienced player.

If the two most popular archetypes are hard to play, or underpowered, you're going to turn off a lot of players who need that shallow learning curve in order to stick with the game and to graduate to a higher level of understanding.

With each passing day the game goes from being one that survives and prospers on its own merits to one that is using every trick in the marketing manual to satisfy the lowest common denominator.
