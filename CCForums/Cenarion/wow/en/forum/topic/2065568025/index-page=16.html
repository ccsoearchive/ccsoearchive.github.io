I can only repeat what has already been said while attempting to be even more incredulous than other hunters.

1. Cobra/Steady/Aimed/etc etc etc are still not affected by the haste buffs you said were going into this patch. I want you to really stop and think about this for a second: you are issuing nerfs to something you have no real data on because you haven't even fixed it yet! How can you have possibly "monitored the data?" You're not even staring at the appropriate numbers. This is ignorant at best and imbecilic at worst.

2. I'm glad a lot of my hunter brethren got to this before me, but it's worth repeating: I spend the vast majority of my time in Aspect of the Fox. It is the optimal aspect for PVE and literally the only option in PVP. If you made Hawk give me five dollars every time I activated it, it still would not be better than Fox in a PVP situation. How, therefore, can you buff Hawk and act like you're making a smart decision?

3. Masters Call...you can dispel it now? Possibly spellsteal it? WHAT? How can you make my only freedom cooldown a detriment to my PVP partners? Now some mage is running around with my freedom while he frost novas my teammates and steals my girlfriend (sorry, that's new in 4.1, right?)

4. Survival is unplayable. Do you understand what I just said? Survival, one of your three hunter specializations, is completely unplayable. You throw traps and spam arcane shot. Remember all the racket you made in development about how powerful and special you wanted our signature shots to be? Yeah, up until 4.0.6 BM Hunters weren't using Kill Command and MM Hunters didn't exist. Now in this patch, Black Arrow and Explosive shot are DPS losses. You literally do less damage using your glorious signature shots than you would just spamming arcane/cobra. How is this acceptable? This is ignoring the fact that you made survival abilities not scale with weapon damage and therefore not scale with gear, which means they're just going to continue being terrible when you eventually ham-handedly "fix" them anyway.

5. One last thought. You had the PTR running for a good while (a month, maybe?) before this patch came out. Last I checked, this was a public test realm, designed to gather data from the playerbase and help make decisions about class balance. How could you, the people who actually make the game and crunch the numbers not see the effects before they happen? I'm going to link the development team to a forum they might have heard of, it's located at www.elitistjerks.com. Here, they have various thread designed to crunch the numbers (also known as "theorycrafting") and simulate the data as it would appear in live in order to inform people of the best DPS specs and their numbers. From what I can tell, the amateur developers working on the WoW team currently are outskilled on a daily basis by a bunch of dudes from Mal'Ganis wielding simple scripts and number-crunching programs.

That's right, Blizzard: you're being outclassed by kids with calculators. I was able to figure out I'd need to hardcast aimed shot in about a minute and 45 seconds while apparently it slipped under the noses of the people I pay to balance my game. Your ham-handed, unnecessary and often completely ridiculous stream of endless buffs and nerfs has severely shaken my customer loyalty. I expect consistency from the guy who washes my car or cooks my burger: why can I not expect the same level of competence from a well-paid game developer with nigh unlimited resources? I'm crying foul, here, because I think you're taking my fifteen dollars a month and using it on hookers and blow instead of making World of Warcraft a game worth playing.

And sure, I'm just one guy with a loud mouth and a lot of opinions. It won't matter if I leave, you'll just have 11,999,999 people to screw with instead. But can you honestly look at your fixes, look at the responses already piling up in a young, young thread, and conclude that you're doing a good job? This is one loud mouth that can answer that for you: No.
