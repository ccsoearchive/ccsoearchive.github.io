    I do think that Dark Intent is the actual problem, and nerfing baseline Shadow Priest damage is a serious mistake.

    Speaking anecdotally from my own raids, we run a 10-man group that never includes a warlock. You know, bring the player not the class, that old line of thinking. We just don't have any active warlock players right now...

    And I can say that my current output is VERY much in line with the others in the group. Sometimes I'm on the lower end, sometimes I'm on the higher end - generally the ranking varies based on the specifics of the fight. But not once have any of my guildmates commented on absurdly high numbers, because they simply aren't absurdly high by any respect.

    So now, hearing about a fairly massive nerf coming along such as this one...

    I have little doubt that I'm now going to end up underperforming on most fights. This because Dark Intent is scaling too highly, for those lucky enough to have a warlock in their group - skewing the numbers in ways that make it seem like shadow priests themselves are the problem.

    Given that I'm surely not alone in this, and that I don't think it's unreasonable to expect shadow priests in 10-mans to often not have a warlock around... Same as it was realized that shamans having a monopoly on Bloodlust wasn't true to the "bring the player" idea either...

    Wouldn't it just make more sense to nerf the cross-class buff (Dark Intent), rather than the baseline damage of the class (Shadow Power)?

    I'm not claiming to know what a better version of DI would look like...

    But I can say that if the current plans go forward, I'm going to be left behind and feel like I'm the weak link in my raid group. And that obviously makes me sad.

    Hopefully you take a look at this proposal, and come to a similar conclusion as to the right thing to do. Thanks for your time.
