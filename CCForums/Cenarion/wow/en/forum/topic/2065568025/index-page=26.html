Shadow is just affected too much by Dark Intent. That's an issue with Dark Intent (on the recipient end rather than the Warlock's end), not us. Due to our class mechanics and stat priorities, we're a very popular choice to receive the buff.


Given how similar the size of the nerf is (it's 8% of our overall damage after some math) to the bonus we get from DI (9%), I rather get the impression that you ran numbers in PTR to balance us assuming we didn't have DI, and then are looking at parses from live game data on players who are under DI.

Here's why it's an 8% nerf (not 10%) to our damage, assuming 10000 dps before Shadow Power:

10000 * 1.25 = 12500 w/pre-nerf Shadow Power
10000 * 1.15 = 11500 w/post-nerf Shadow Power
11500 / 12500 = .92 an 8% overall reduction



And here's why I think we were balanced for the patch around no DI and that's not being considered now, assuming 10K dps before Dark Intent's damage bonus:

10000 * 1.09 = 10900 w/Dark Intent, pre-Shadow Power nerf
10900 * .92 = 10028 w/Dark Intent, post-Shadow Power nerf

That is remarkably close, as close as you can get while using the round numbers Blizz prefers! We're too strong by exactly the amount of power Dark Intent gives us??



Please, please take another look at Dark Intent. Currently this is just going to leave us more dependent on it.

The problem isn't Shadowpriest, it's that Dark Intent is far, far too strong a buff to its recipient. 3% haste remains a nice buff, we don't need the extra damage on top of it. Please don't balance us around an external buff whose presence we can't guarantee.



Also, this effectively undoes the very necessary buff to Mind Sear.
