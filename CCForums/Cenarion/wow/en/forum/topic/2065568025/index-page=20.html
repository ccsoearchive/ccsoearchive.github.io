Before the patch our numbers fell middle of the road and that is fine. The overall numbers are relatively close and so balancing them to within 100 dps isn't a reasonable use of your time at that point. In 4.0.6 we received a mild mastery buff and a slight single target starfall buff. It's possible this buff was implemented to move mastery higher up on our priority list and that is also reasonable. If that was the intent then of course a damage nerf is expected after enough numbers have rolled in.

The problem arises when we look at the implementation of the correction to starfall. The bug at 4.0.6's release was outrageously inflating our numbers and so of course that had to be fixed. The first announced nerf which rebalanced our numbers around the bug was of course reduced yielding an overall 5% nerf instead of 9%, but I wonder if the final reduction is still a bit severe. It appears that starfall's corrected behavior is doing less damage than before the patch due to only a fraction of the stars falling even on multiple targets. Not including the net change in starfall's output, it would seem that changing the moonfury damage from 15% to 10% is a bit drastic unless the mastery buff actually increased moonkin dps by over 5%. If indeed that mastery buff increased our output by 5% then really there is nothing wrong with nerfing us by that much to bring us back to where we were prior to the patch (or slightly lower until starfall can get sorted out). It just feels too soon to make such a large change in light of the recent bug that undoubtedly skewed a good deal of the data.
