In all honesty, we seemed powerful in PvE. We had to know that a nerf was coming.

That said, I'm disappointed by how ham-handedly Blizzard has implemented this change to the spec. I do 10-mans without a warlock, and my DPS was never disproportionately high. Generally our mage tops the meters and I come second, unless we're facing an encounter where I can multi-DoT targets.

I don't know whether the Blizzard developers actually test these changes in a variety of raid environments or merely feed the numbers through a simcrafter, but I do feel that this nerf is a clumsy approach to fixing the synergy of Dark Intent. I'm hopeful that Blizzard is still willing to reexamine the real reasoning behind abnormally high shadow DPS, as the announced changes run counter both to Blizzard's belief that priest DPS was too low prior to 4.0.6, and to the decision to make Cataclysm raid comps less rigid. We're a tight-knit group; I don't want to excuse myself from a raid, nor find a way to swap in a warlock, just so I don't feel like I'm holding back the other 9 players.

I remember the 33% nerf to Shadow Weaving, after it was shown to unfairly buff warlock damage. I ask Blizzard to please apply the same logic in this case. If one spell is nerfed, I can take my lumps and relearn casting priorities to adapt. With a flat 8% damage nerf, I can't do anything but slip a few steps down the meters.

I enjoy playing a Shadow Priest; ever since the days of BWL I remember being on my warrior and thinking how cool that guy with Anathema was. The priest community is a curious one; players of the class always seem to be interesting individuals, and I run across quite a few who have played this game since classic. Unlike many here, I won't lie: If this nerf is implemented (at least in this manner) I will still login and play my priest, probably every day. But it will be hugely disappointing to have watched needed buffs survive a month of PTR testing, only to disappear after a few hours on live.

Please respond to this or any other plea for clarification regarding Shadow DPS without the benefit of Dark Intent. So many once-unique buffs have been distributed to other classes; if this change is implemented, I don't see why Dark Intent couldn't also fall under that category.

And thank you for making World of Warcraft. It may not always seem that way, but I doubt there are too many people wishing the game never existed :)

You literally do less damage using your glorious signature shots than you would just spamming arcane/cobra. How is this acceptable? I'm crying foul, here, because I think you're taking my fifteen dollars a month and using it on hookers and blow instead of making World of Warcraft a game worth playing.

And sure, I'm just one guy with a loud mouth and a lot of opinions. It won't matter if I leave, you'll just have 11,999,999 people to screw with instead. But can you honestly look at your fixes, look at the responses already piling up in a young, young thread, and conclude that you're doing a good job?
