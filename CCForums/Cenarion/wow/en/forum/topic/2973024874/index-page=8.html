This is slightly off topic but can see the issue with pug runs is that players like myself will pug to fill in gaps when the guild isn't running them but I don't want to wipe all night. I just need the tokens from Cho or Nef and if we can't get past the first boss without wiping there's little chance of making it past the end boss. Therefore there's no incentive for players who already know the bosses to want pugs who don't know them ie haven't completed the achieve.

I think pugs who haven't done those raids need to pug together as they're the only ones who will have patience to learn and teach the fights to inexperienced raiders.


This is a very astute observation. I also want to add that people who are willing to form a raid and lead it without experiencing the fights beforehand are rare and likely already are your guild and raid leaders on the server. There is a large barrier to entry for raid leading - people don't feel comfortable leading or they worry about failing or don't know how to distribute loot with the ML system or are flat out horrible at leading, etc.

I run two different T11 raids for my guild and Nef, Ascendant Council, Cho'gall, and Alak'ir are not guaranteed wins. They still require a greater degree of skill and ability than some players have even with the current nerfs. So I'm not surprised people still don't have the title, and I'm not surprised people balk at leading since some of these fights require a bit of pre-planning to set up and knowledge of class abilities and players with the skill to perform an unusual role (add kiting, chains, etc).
