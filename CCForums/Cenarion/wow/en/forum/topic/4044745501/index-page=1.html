MACROS

I’m going to start out with a few essential macros to get things rolling. First is my ShD macro, which is designed to pop a burst trinket and ShD simultaneously, granting me a great deal more burst damage in my dance, which is fantastic.

#showtooltip Shadow Dance
/use Cataclysmic Gladiator’s Badge of Conquest
/cast Shadow Dance

Next is a Trix macro, which I find very helpful. It needs refining, if anyone would like to help it out, though. Change my name for whoever you plan to give Trix to, since 15% damage is nice. Although this does you no good 1v1, scenarios arise in BG’s where you and a friend might be 1v1’ing different targets, and this will help your friend. Replace my name with your friend’s name. If your friend is not in the party, it will allow you to Trix whomever you wish by simply clicking on them or their Unit Frame.

Do NOT use your focus for Trix in PvP. Your focus is for CC. Take the extra 17 seconds to write a proper macro.

#showtooltip Tricks of The Trade
/cast [@Notgolleniel] Tricks of The Trade; Tricks of the Trade

Clarification: This macro is for someone else to use. You cannot trix yourself. I put my name here so that others would see that you're supposed to cast it on another person, not attempt to cast it on yourself.

(Thank you, Jamalia, for pointing this out to me!)

After that is a FGouge macro. This is wonderful for fighting things like Unholy Deathknights. I know a lot of people who prefer shift modifier macros for their gouge, but I’ve got 2 entirely different keybinds for mine. (Regular gouge= Q, FGouge= #pad 2 = Naga Mouse 2).

#showtooltip Gouge
/cast [@focus, exists] Gouge; Gouge

Simple but effective.

Same for kick. It’s nice against frost mages if you’re out of range and want to stop a pet nova for the next 5 seconds.

#showtooltip Kick
/cast [@focus] Kick; Kick

Sap macro for stealthies and to use in general. I love it.

#showtooltip Sap
/console targetnearestdistance 10.00000000
/targetenemy [noharm][dead]
/console targetnearestdistance 40.00000000
/cast [harm,nodead] sap

Complicated but effective.

I’m not going to put my Blind macro up here because it’s terrible, I’m too lazy to fix it, and I’ve gotten too used to it to fix it.

If you have a CoS+ Vanish macro, delete it. Same goes for CoS+ Evasion and CR+ Evasion. They’re all giant wastes of essential cooldowns.

GENERAL STRATEGIES

Frost mages:

DO NOT CLOAK UNTIL THEY BLOCK UNLESS YOU ARE GOING TO DIE.

Mind numbing poison is MANDATORY. To correctly execute the following opener, your garrote must be done at a 70 degree or wider angle. It’s tricky and takes practice, but once you get the hang of it, you’ll be grateful you took the time to learn it.

Open with a sap > premed > garrote> kidney, then Hemo to 4-5 CP’s. Use those points on a recup immediately. If you have enough pressure, trinket the first deep freeze. Most of the time, you’re fine to sit in it. If they pop cooldowns, trinket. If not, “chill” in it.

First big Shatter they try to throw at you, if they’re far away, Smoke Bomb it. If not, Vanish and ambush. Why ambush? It doesn’t put your stuns or silences on DR, so you can save them for when you’re forcing a block or trinket and it puts Find Weakness on your target.

Next time you’re in a long nova, use whichever cooldown you haven’t used yet, but SAVE CoS.

Stay on them. If you need to ShS, get in front of them immediately and Gouge. A lot of times, they’ll blink and be Gouged where they land. If you see a chance to reset, take it. If you see LoS, use it. That’s about it.

Oh. One more thing. Combine sprint and CoS. Nothing’s worse than getting a CoS kited.

Rogues when you get opened on:

You’ve got two choices: eat the opener and risk death or trinket and risk being reset on later. 95%+ or more of the time, you will not die in an opener. You will get low, but you can come back easily. I like to turn around and gouge out of the opener, then with about 2 seconds left in the gouge, Dismantle, hemo, shiv, vanish.

Recup those points off, then premed cheap shot backstab and get ready to kidney. If they trinket, you can blind them, but your stuns and saps will be on DR if you do so immediately, so only blind them right here if you are going to get crapped on if you don't.

Once the bleed debuff you've applied to your opponent has a longer duration than the one on you, kite them to hell and back, utilizing deadly throw, shadowsteps, shivs, and even regular throw. If they get close, gouge them, then you can dance cheap shot if your stuns are off dr, recup, slice or evisc with those points and go into your shadowdance burst rotation. If you have not blinded and they have no trinket, wait about 5 seconds after the dance and blind them. This will reset your sap DR and you will be able to open on them again.

I cannot stress enough how important it is to watch your recuperate buff. Try to not let it fall below 15 seconds. Being caught in a blind reopen without a recup is lethal.

If you blind your opponent with a bleed on you, watch it tick. Hemorrhage ticks every 3 seconds, starting at 24, then 21, 18, 15... etc. You'll want to stealth for a sap at one of those intervals + 1 second. (22, 19, 16, 13... etc.) Why? Because the INSTANT you've resapped them, you're back out of stealth (assuming you have no nightstalker, you've got 6 seconds of out of stealth time. This will enable you to restealth and cheap shot safely right before the sap falls off). Watch your opponent's recup time, aiming to blind them with at MOST 15 seconds left on their recup.

Also: OP opener for rogues if you get the opener:

(They’re sapped)

Premed, wait for full energy. Recup at full energy, then open right as recup ticks (9 seconds). This simple move gives you a guaranteed extra Ambush in your ShD.

Cheap Shot, Hemo and start spamming ShD as Hemo goes off, then Ambush. KS as CS falls off, then ShS Ambush (this is for the added damage and because some people will trinket the KS and you can get away much quicker with the 70% speed buff) and Ambush again if they do not trinket. If you’ve got the time, Evisc. A lot of times, you will not have the GCD to Evisc, so you’ll want to vanish out.

When you reopen, sap just after the second to last tick of their Hemo debuff ticks, then sap again after it ticks. Recup with the 5 points you should have, then premed, slice and open with CS. Hemo, Backstab, Backstab and KS. If they haven’t trinketed something, Cheat Death should proc by now, then they die. EZPZ.

If they trinket something, wait a while and Blind them. Then reopen and put the hurting in their face.

Positioning for Saps:

You want to be outside your opponent. You want to form a ring around where you think they are and encircle them, spamming the hell out of your sap macro.

This looks dirty, but it’s the best diagram I’ve got. []=you, ()= opponent. [()]

That’s what you want. Predict where they are, and get a TINY bit (5 yards or so) outside of where you think they are.

Blood DK's:

Not winnable against a competent Blood DK anymore, but you shouldn’t ever die, either.

Premed> Garrote> Disarm. Hemo, Recup, Backstab til 5 combo points, then KS. They’ll probably IBF, so be ready to kite.

ShS every grip, shiv frequently, especially if they’ve got Vengeance.

It’s a battle of attrition. It shouldn’t be won, nor lost. I’m not going to go to in-depth on this one, since I see no point in helping draw out a stalemate. It’s never going to be won or lost unless you make huge mistakes. The DK can make as many mistakes as he wants. You don’t have to know how to play at all to be effective with Blood.


BM Hunters:

You can't premed recup, so you want to try to jump the flare and cheap shot right away, popping readiness right off the bat.

This is a HARD fight to win, since they can disengage so often due to a certain talent in the BM tree.

Save step for disengage and disarm for BW.

Smoke bomb on their pet with 5 stacks of readiness up, get 5 combos on the pet, redirect into a kidney > dance.

Hard to win but winnable.


Retribution Paladins:

Tricky fight. I open dance. Not sure if this is what most do, but I like it.

Premed recup, cheap shot, hemo, dance ambush, kidney, ambush, GARROTE and get a full recup up out of that. You can kite them while they're garroted, but come back in for a disarm right as the garrote ends.

Gouge freedoms. ShS and use that speed boost to get in front of them if sprint is on cd. Most rets will trinket nothing but a blind, so I like to try to bait it by blinding with 5 combo points up and stuns off DR. Put them into a kidney the instant they trinket.


I’ve started doing another one that’s similar to the Blood DK strat recently and I like it a lot, especially if the Ret is an idiot and doesn’t have a chain.

Premed> Garrote> Disarm> SHIV if not slowed> Hemo if crip applied already> Recup> Backstab spam, KS right before Find Weakness falls off, throw ‘em into a ShD.

Most will bubble your ShD. That’s what you want. Kite the bubble, come back in with Evasion popped as bubble ends. Don’t let them get behind you for Crusader Strikes. The fewer Word of Glory’s with 3 HP they get off, the better. Same goes with Templar’s Verdict.

Don’t trinket anything but a HoJ. If they’ve yet to get you to CoS or CR by this time, you’re in good shape. If they HoJ you and burn wings, trinket, Vanish THEN CoS. This is so he doesn’t get Censure on you in Vanish, since that would defeat the purpose of using CoS and Vanish together.

Blind to bait trinkets or for resets once his trinket is down. Play like a sissy and the fight’s yours.

I prefer the second one, but I’ve had success with both.

Shadow Priests:

Spriests are generally a very easy fight.

Stay on him with recup up and you win, basically. If I'm going for high pressure against an inexperienced priest, I will trinket the first fear and immediately cloak, preventing him from Horror'ing me. He will probably disperse at that point, which means you need to gouge him. If you've got a dance and your stuns/ garrote are off dr, this would be a lovely time to use it. I prefer to cheap shot, ambush, slice, garrote, ambush kidney. This keeps them locked down for a long time while you get some burst in as well as getting slice up. It may also force a trinket, in which case, you'll want to wait until the DoTs on you reach low duration and your DR's will fall off within the blind. Throw him into a blind with no trinket and no dispersion and the fight is yours.

You can also bait a trinket with a blind when you've got 5 combo points up, then throw him straight into a kidney, then backstab once and wait a bit to see if he disperses (provided it's up) and gouge the disperse. At this point, the fight really is entirely in your control.

As for cloak times, I try to use mine when I'm getting low and he's just refreshed DoTs. (but not so low that Cheat Death procs). Around 30% or so, allowing my recup to tick me back up close to 40 , then gouging and kidney'ing to buy myself more time for it to tick. This is assuming, of course, that (for whatever reason) a spriest actually manages to get you to 30%. If you can combine sprint and cloak and stay on him, then vanish and reopen with less than a second left on cloak, you'll put out a metric crap load (new standardized form of measurement) of pressure.

You can also (as stated earlier) CoS offensively after trinketing or WoTF’ing the first fear, since they’ll often try to Horror you right there. If you do this (and it’s kinda risky) you need to make sure you can sit on them for the entire duration of your CoS, or you’ve wasted a cooldown and you suck.

Okay, maybe you don’t suck. But you’ve wasted arguably your most valuable cooldown.

Unholy DK's.

By far the trickiest DK if they know what they're doing.

My opener against Unholy is questionable, weird sounding, and doesn’t sound effective, but I’ve found it to work well.

I focus sap the DK then open on his pet. Recup with the premed> CD> Hemo points, then hemo spam for CP’s, redirect kidney to DK just as sap ends and keep wailing on the pet. Switch to the DK just as kidney ends and swap your focus target to his pet.

If you get kited, switch to the pet. Just keep switching back and forth. When he pops Gargoyle, Smoke Bomb and CR the rest of the damage, then switch your focus to the Gargoyle. (I do this via a focus mouseover macro) The Gargoyle will be forced into your Smoke Bomb and you can kick its next cast. That’s 10 seconds of his Gargoyle wasted.

Once you survive the Garg and kill or force a sac on the pet, this fight becomes crazy easy. Until then, it’s the hardest DK fight of the three by far.

Just keep switching, use your focus target wisely, if the DK grips you off his minion and doesn’t have it leap to you immediately, ShS back to the minion and go for a kill. If he comes in to stop you or do damage to you, switch onto the DK. Make him kite you, then go back on the pet.

Enhance Shaman:

This fight is not difficult at all if you know what you’re doing.

I like a strat that I’ve developed to take care of Searing Totem and get them sapped at the same time.

Set the Shaman as your focus target. Then, target him and premed, but DO NOT recup yet. Target his Searing Totem, and focus sap the Shaman, auto attacking the Searing Totem the exact millisecond your sap goes off.

(#showtooltip Sap
/cast [@focus] Sap; Sap) –much easier than the stealthy sap macro

You have two options here. Both involve burning ShD.

You can sap again, then Garrote+ KS + ShD (they cannot use Shamanistic Rage [+30% damage reduction] while silenced, but they CAN use it while stunned) then Ambush> Ambush> Recup (the amount of Ambushes you are able to get off depends on if Relentless Strikes proc’d on your KS)

You can use your premed points on a recup just as you are ready to open, then ShD, garrote> CS> Evisc> Ambushx2> Recup

I prefer the first. Either way, you wanna open as hard as possible to force a trinket.

Save CR for wolves, Disarm out of your opener, Evasion out of Disarm.

Auto attack the Searing and Earthbind totems if you get near them. If you’re in a Frost Shock Nova, target one of those totems and use throw.

Vanish a Frost Shock Nova, ShS and Garrote or CS (depending on if they’re slowed—if they are, Garrote, if not, CS) and keep the pressure up. Trinket a Hex, especially if they’re going to use it to heal. If they’re at higher HP, you might consider sitting in the Hex, as they’re probably not going to attack you while you’re hexed.

Of course, if you get in trouble, play like a sissy. Kite, kite, kite, kite, gouge, kite, kite, KIDNEY DANCE OMFG HE DROPPED 70% OF HIS HP IN THAT KIDNEY. That’s about it.

Wait. I lied. Stoneclaw shield makes your Gouge function as a stun, IIRC. At least, until it break. So you can Gouge that, then Backstab and unless you break the shield, they’ll still be stuck there.


Affliction Warlocks:

Affliction locks are actually one of the easier things for rogues to kill.

Open with a premed recup garrote then build 5 combos and go into a full kidney.

Dance during the kidney for pressure. If they trinket, you can blind them later.

If they don't trinket, cloak out of the kidney. This will force a port. You then ShS and stay on them.

It's a simple fight to win. Don't trinket the first howl unless you're going for huge pressure right off the bat. I like to chill in that to give them some false hope, then come back and roflstomp them. It's also fun to let them fully dot you, then just cloak them off once you're out of the fear and next to them.
Another way:

Premed> Recup> CS> Hemo> Dance> Ambush> Slice and Dice> Garrote> Ambush> KS> More damaging stuff.

Cloak out of that, combine Sprint with CoS, don’t ShS the port, sit in the Howl when they fear you, then ShS back, trinket when they Death Coil, Gouge, Vanish, Garrote into full KS. Simple fight.

Feral Druids:

Ferals are a hard fight.

They have the advantage, (Their hitbox is further back, meaning they get an extra yard or so over us where we can see them but not sap them. Since a rogue's hitbox [and center of mass] is directly up front, while a feral's is almost a full yard back, they have a clear advantage when opening. If you catch a feral, start singing praises to the heavens)but you can win it if you play it well.

If you get opened on, you almost have to trinket it to have any shot at surviving. Follow your trinket with a gouge, then vanish, cheap shot, recup, hemo. Now he's got a bleed on him, you don't have FF, you've only got pounce bleed and you have a full recup running. Yes, you had to burn some cooldowns. Gotta do what you gotta do.

From there, it's all about only engaging when you can get the upper hand. Abuse the hell out of gouge. Yes, it'll get dodged a lot. Shiv or hemo or try for backstabs if you're at 60-70 energy or less. I find that if I try for backstabs at higher than 70 energy, I often end up capping energy simply 'cuz I can't get behind a feral due to feralag (that teleporty crap that happens whenever a feral moves). Try to bait a barkskin with a kidney, then dance on the next kidney. If you can land a dance on him when he's got no barkskin and he's in cat form, you're going to crap all over him, forcing a trinket or making him go defensive.

Should he go defensive: your first priority is to SHIV THAT VENGEANCE. This will gimp the living hell out of his bear damage. Also, if he goes bear and you've got evasion up, pop it now. He will likely bash you. Plus, he'll be getting fewer Savage Defense procs (since he'll need to crit to gain them, and critting you requires you not to dodge it in the first place.) Not only does evasion gimp his offense, it gimps his defense, as well. VERY powerful cooldown.

Since you've vanished at the start of the fight, you can combat readiness whenever you want. A great time to use it is when you think he's going to pop offensive cooldowns. If you can pop readiness just before Berserking, you're in good shape. If you make him prolong berserking to go defensive and get you off of him, you're also in good shape.

Keep your hemo bleed on him at all times. Watch for Faerie Fire on yourself. If it drops to 7 seconds or less, BLIND HIM, especially if he has no trinket. Why 7 seconds or less? Blind lasts 8 seconds, so you'll be able to stealth and sap him right as Faerie Fire falls off, allowing his bleeds to tick you out of stealth (provided they're on you) or giving you time to clear stun DR's (if they're not cleared already.)

Play it smart. If he gets a restealth, but still has a bleed, consider dropping a smoke bomb on yourself, ensuring he will no be able to feral charge and reopen. If you find yourself with a faerie fire on you while he's healing and it's got 5 seconds on it or less, pop a smoke bomb. Free restealth.

Use your cooldowns wisely, pace yourself, reset the fight as many times as you possibly can. But most importantly: do not panic and do not give up.

Warriors:

Simple, simple, simple fight once you get it down. Until then, it’s frustrating as hell.

First off: overpowered opener. This will ONLY work in duels, not world PvP, as nobody’s going to stick around after a pseudo-opener.

Get in stealth, then stand about 20 yards away in FRONT of him. Yes, FRONT. Come towards him, sprint when you’re getting closer, then premed> CS> gouge and run away behind him. DO NOT use these CPs yet.

Get back in stealth. Wait on DR’s. Sap him to bait a zerk. If he doesn’t zerk, oh well, you’ve got more prep time. Recup with those 5 combo points, Premed and Slice next. Open HARD.

CS> Hemo> Dance> Ambush> Kidney> Ambush> Ambush> Evisc> Evasion.

If he fears you, DO NOT trinket it. That’s asking for him to Charge, Throwdown, pop Reck, his burst trinket, and whatever else he’s got, and smash your face in. Bad idea. Don’t do it.

5-8 kite like a boss. If you can, ShS a charge when you think he’s going to.

If you don’t get the charge, you’re still behind him and he’s still not hitting you.

You can also ShS his heroic leap to keep him from gaining any momentum at all.

If he trinkets your opener, you’re free to blind and reset later. You can sap out of the blind, but CS quickly afterwards, hoping to catch a Berserker Rage that you can Shiv off in the CS.

Another thing I’ve come to love against Warriors is Feint. If he bladestorms, don’t disarm him. Pop CR and Feint and /giggle as your Recup heals you through the Damage he deals. Disarm when he pops CDs such as Reck or when he gets low so he can’t Sword/ Board and Shield Wall.

Fire Mages! (Very, very rough guide) -- Please do feel free to add things to this, I am still in the testing stages, but I've got enough to write a very basic guide.

There are not very many good mages on my server, but I found the best mage I know and had him duel me as fire a few times. I had a basic idea of what to do against fire mages, but this really helped a lot.

Things to Know
You'll probably need to Shadow Step before they blink fairly often. That's okay.
Do not CoS before Combustion. You will lose. Period.
Do not trinket unless you're in a Dragon's Breath with Combustion on you. If you trinket before this, you will be utterly screwed, because then it doesn't matter if you've saved cloak, cuz you can't even use it.
Casting while moving ruined the game. No seriously. It did.
Fire mages, when played well, are absolutely obnoxious. I'll take a good frost mage over a good fire mage any day.

So far, the most effective way I've found to open is HARDER THAN HELL. No seriously, get him as low as you can the first time you're on him.

This means Premed> Recup in stealth when he's Sapped, Garrote without breaking the Sap (Garrote from 70 degree or wider angle) burn ShD, CS him, Evisc, Ambush, Ambush, RECUP. You need Recup desperately against a Fire Mage, as well as a Hemo bleed on him.

That being said, sometimes you're going to need to ShS just to get a Hemo bleed on him for combo points. And, ya know what? That's fine. You need a Recup up while being kited, and Blazing Speed is annoying. 40 yard range on cast-while-moving Scorch means you'll be spending a lot of time off your target.

When you ShS, do not waste it. Spam Hemo as you ShS, then get in front and go for a Gouge on the Blink. If you can get that, you're golden.

Shiv often. Especially if he gets a Blazing Speed proc.

While it may seem like combining Stuns and Silences is not as crucial against a Fire Mage, you do not want to get your Garrote kited because of a lucky Blazing Speed proc. It's frustrating.

If you've got a Recup up, a Hemo bleed on your target, he is not slowed, and you've got a combo point or two (I suggest three points being the absolute most and that's if you're desperate), you should Deadly Throw to get a slow on him.

He can't keep you slowed forever, so as long as you keep Recup up and a slow of some sort on him, you'll catch up eventually.

Another thing you'll want to avoid is getting Shattered, since any decent Fire Mage will have taken the Shatter talent. This gives him more instant Pyro procs. Those hurt.

The same principal applies to frost mages as it does to fire. GET THE HELL OUT OF THE NOVA. Smoke Bomb if it he's at range, Vanish and reopen (I've not yet determined the best reopener yet, though I'm leaning towards Cheap Shot, since it's easier to land than Garrote or Ambush and will force a Blink--but be aware, since you may get Dragon's Breath'd right after the Blink. As I said, the guide's still rough and VERY imperfect.)

Once you've got him low, you're going to think you've killed him. If he's already Ice Blocked, you're probably right. He's got a popsicle's chance in Hell of living against you if he's really low with no Ice Block up and you're at high HP. Watch out for Cauterize, though. (A good Mage will Block right as it procs, leaving himself with very nearly 40% of his maximum HP.) When Cauterize procs, spam damage into him to get him as low as possible. If he's still got a block, the worst thing that can happen is that you'll lose some energy. If he's got no Block, you're going to score the kill very, very soon.

Be patient; this is not an easy fight if the Mage is smart. Like I said, I'll duel a Frost Mage over a Fire Mage any day.

Vengeance is a stacking attack power buff that tank specs (and Feral Druids in Bear Form) receive for taking damage. Its initial purpose was to help generate threat for PvE, however, it has become crucial in PvP for tank specs.

It is also an enrage effect, meaning you can Shiv it off. This is fantastic for Rogues! It only needs to be Shiv'd once every 20 seconds, but it does NEED to be dealt with.

With 4.3, it has become increasingly vital to Shiv Vengeance as SOON as you see it. It stacks much more rapidly than it used to, and I've seen tank specs getting around 14-15THOUSAND extra attack power from it. Basically, if you see a tank, Shiv them fast. If you're out of stealth already, open with a Shiv, even.

Hello, again.

I don't even have Expose bound in my PvP spec. Here's why: that damage gain is very minimal. It's our lowest DPE finisher. There is almost no situation which I would use it. Rupture I might use against a rogue when expecting a Blind to make it harder for them to resap. (resapping with multiple dots is hard) and maybe a Feral when they go Bear Form. Other than that, I wouldn't Rupture.

And even then, I still prefer to use my combo points on other things. I'd say for sure get a Recup and Slice up before you even think about Rupturing for damage. Weigh your options and if you plan on kiting a lot (such as Bear Form Druids) then you might want to Rupture.

However, the damage is so low, you might just be better refreshing a Recup, depending on what duration is left.

Basically, I have no definitive answer. Rupture is crap damage, but damage is damage while kiting. Use whichever option you see best fit at the time being. If you find yourself putting out more damage with Rupture kiting things that are hard to kill, by all means, go for it. If you don't like it as much, don't use it. It's more of a preferential thing at this point, as up 'til recently I've been firm against using Rupture in PvP, but I have come to realize recently that there are times when Rupture is a better finisher than your other alternatives.
