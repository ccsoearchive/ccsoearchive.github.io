I have a problem with both of those builds, both lack imp recup which makes them both no go's. In addition to that let's start with sub. You don't take Sanguinary Vein which is a straight across the board 10% damage increase. Are you serious? Terrible idea. It's a far better idea to gylph hemo and/or Garrote and open with a bleed on everyone so you get your 10%. !0% across the board damage is better than Puncturing wounds, the extra 2 in HaT, you name it. 10% beats it, handily. It's a far better idea to go 1/3 in hat and 2/2 in Sanguinary Vein. You'll also need to give up 2 points in Puncturing wounds to get imp recup. It needs to constantly be up at all times, there is no reason not to buff it to it's max.

CttC and ruthlessness are fine talents, the problem is that there are noticeably better ones so they need to be sacrificed. The same is true of Elusiveness and that one hurts the most and is one reason why mut is more of an arena spec and less of a bg one. You simply need dispels as mut or your mobility is nearly nothing. There's no real reason to glyph Vendetta, you won't be on a target that long at this stage of the game. Rupture is simply better, particularly since you're going to use it asap and then build for recup and hopefully have enough time on it to envenom. For mut the spec I use(secondary) is the best compromise between what you need and what you can take. Not including cd's, Open on everyone with sap->garrote->mut->rupture. Doesn't matter if you have 3 or 4 cp on the rupture hit it anyway. You'll have double venomous vim going and the guy will be doing 70% less damage + silenced for 5 seconds. That's an incredible opener and gives you all the time you need to setup on the target. Once you have rupture up build for recup and by the time the guy can fight back you'll be taking 16% less across the board damage(Deadened nerves and imp recup stack additively which is amazing.) and healing yourself for about 5k every 3 seconds.

Against everyone not playing mage this is enough to clinch the fight in your favor, assuming it's 1v1. Bad mages will beat you senseless, it's disheartening, but it simply is what it is. You have no mobility but you are a fine tank. Venomous wounds, envenom, deadly, instant, and all bleeds go through armor so it isn't as big a deal as it is with sub. Sub needs to keep up Expose weakness or it simply hits for nothing. You don't have that problem as mut, you just need some support to keep yourself mobile and ripping someone up will come in due time.

I could post some more but overall this is a fine start for a guide.

I'm assuming you say Slice & Dice glyph because it can be difficult to keep so many finishers up in PvP without Overkill going, but I actually don't find myself having trouble keeping Slice & Dice and Rupture up, and Recuperate/Kidney Shot when I need to, without Overkill active. Maybe that's just me. I also find that the sustained damage I maintain with the Vendetta glyph does a lot during my current arena matches as Assassination Rogue/Restoration Shaman.


I'm more referring to the fact that one of the two main strengths of CttC, besides psecifically refreshing SnD via Envenom, is that it will do so off of any duration SnD.

To put it another way, if I use a high cp SnD to ensure I'll get a CttC off an Envenom before SnD runs out, then one of the points of CttC is obviated. Might as well have just gone without CttC and popped a high CP SnD to begin with really. Any class that can kite will make you work for the CttC proc, especially if you're popping the SnD off the Garrote. You have 9 seconds to Mut x 2 and Envenom before the 1pt SnD falls off. This can be close to problematic even in PvE if you don't get a RS proc and/or you don't get a Ruth proc + crit Mut--it gets tight.

This doesn't even account for how many stacks of DP yuou will have when you first Envenom, which can lead to a pretty awful conversion of CP and energy into dmg. We still haven't touched upon what happens when the poisons gets cleansed/dispelled etc and you're forced to wait for stacks to build back up before you Envenom and before SnD falls iff.

I haven't really seen anyone post Cata or even really post shattering for that matter supporting vendetta--it was a PbvE glyph that people got over real fast. 6 seconds extra for Vendetta just doesn't seem worth it. maybe it is for your comp, but I dunno.

I never really liked CttC for PvP because I find it restrictive and forces you to make bad choices/decisions at times to get the proc. I saw that reckful was running CttC, but with SnD prime glyph and it made more sense to me. Having that extra SnD time alleviates some of the logistical issues I have with CttC as a talent because it gives you more breathing room when you are getting kited, when DP stacks aren't procc'ing or are getting cleansed, or when you needed to dump the cp's immediately for a KS or Recup instead of the Envenom.
