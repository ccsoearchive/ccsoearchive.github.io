10/11/2012 08:37 PMPosted by Yougotmooed
So, order is cap hit to yellow (not spell hit cap, it used to be spell hit cap for poisons did it not?).

Yeah. Poisons used to go by spell hit, but they go by special/yellow hit now (whichever you want to call it).

Then get expertise capped though mastery does have a higher EP value, we just want to make sure we make most use of that poison damage by ensuring we don't have our attacks dodged?

After that reforge to mastery.

If mastery has the higher EP value then you'll prioritize it over expertise. But you'll end up with a lot of gear that has mastery and X stat on it, where X ends up being reforged to expertise since it's the next best thing. So you'll probably end up capping expertise anyway even when maximizing mastery.

10/12/2012 01:26 AMPosted by Korilz
I think I remember that envenom used to refund energy by 80% when it got dodged, so now rogue needs expertise also? am I right?

I believe all our dodged attacks for all specs refund 80% of their energy cost currently. I -could- be mistaken, but I think I remember checking on that at some point after hitting 90.

Just be careful transferring conventional wisdom from one expansion to the next; EP values are relative rather than absolute, so changes in the values of other stats can impact expertise's value as well.

10/18/2012 12:32 PMPosted by Saresh
Now that you mention it, I'm not sure if it has to be in MH to get the buff or not. The tooltip is not clear, just says if dagger is equipped. Guess I'll be doing some dummy testing later.


Unless it was changed for MoP...

The tooltip is oriented around the weapon you have in the main-hand. If a dagger is in the main-hand, you get the bonus. If a dagger is only in the off-hand, you get no bonus.

It works this way because you're not getting the weapon damage from both hands, but only the main-hand when you use the ability.

It works the same way for Hemorrhage in the subtlety spec. The change was added to normalize the damage between people using a dagger main-hand and those using a slower main-hand weapon. So, the penalty for using the weapon you prefer wouldn't be quite as steep as it was before.
