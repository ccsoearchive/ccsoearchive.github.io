03/11/2012 02:30 PMPosted by Valarisa
I have a couple questions in mind. I'm an aspiring novelist/fan-fictionist, but I've always wanted to give RP a try. Only problem, the character I can act the most upon is a half-breed (Human/High Elf). Is that allowed in the WoW community? Also, in the fiction I have concerning this toon, she is close kin to Alleria Windrunner(who has been missing for a good number of years). Is that allowed? I'm not sure what the serious RPers feel about deals like that.


Generally, half-breeds are frowned upon, typically because they add little to a character, and most people think that people use it as a means to be "snowflakey" or a poor attempt to be unique. Non-playable races like high elves or alliance side goblins or something similar are generally frowned upon as well, mostly because people without addons won't be able to immediately recognize it (same applies to females who look like a boy playing a male character, and vice versa). Similar "off-shoot" races, like Wildhammer or Dark Iron dwarves, Mag'har and Dragonmaw orcs, Steamwheedle goblins, etc., are seen as alright for the most part.

The general rule being, "would being a half-breed/non-playable race make this character more compelling and not special snowflake-ish?" And most of the time the answer is no, and you can accomplish whatever it is you set out for with an existing race. Feel free to do whatever you like, you're just more likely to come across resistance to the idea.

And for "close kin", that's also heavily frowned upon, even openly mocked, when it pertains to major lore characters. "Long lost brother to Thrall," and what have you, is almost always a bad idea. Again, does it make the character more interesting aside from being able to namedrop the lore character at every turn? If yes, proceed with caution, but again, you won't find many people who are willing to accept it.
