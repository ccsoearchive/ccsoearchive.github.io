I don't think it's meant to be canon that the night elves are experiencing accelerated aging as all the time that passed while they were immortal somehow "catches up" to them. I think it was just an instance of bad writing...or more bad communication between Blizzard and one of their writers, who 1, forgot or was not properly aware that even without immortality, night elves have vastly extended lifespans compared to humans, 2, didn't realize that only maybe a decade or so had passed between WCIII and Wolfheart, and/or wasn't aware that at the time of the sundering according to war of the ancients, Malfurion and Tyrande were really only like the biological equivalent of teenagers, or at least...maybe early 20s? Which, I fully admit is rather odd given that they've all been immortal in the intervening time and yet the Malfurion we see in WCIII seems middle aged and wizened. It's also worth noting that Blizzard has not changed the art for Malfurion or Tyrande to depict them with any sudden wrinkles or visible signs of aging, the art for Seeds of Faith shows Malfurion every bit as musclebound and vigorous as ever, and Tyrande doesn't seem to have sagged a millimeter. So I'm gonna chock the "rapid aging" in Wolfheart up to incongruous writing until and unless similar things start being mentioned again elsewhere. So I very much doubt that the night elves will just waste away before our eyes without immortality, I can't imagine Blizzard would go so far as to put them in a position where they biologically NEED it and still not restore it. After all, they gave the blood elves back the sunwell when their society was depicted as falling apart without it.

Responding to a few points brought up directly to me by Veloran:

1: Velen is directly stated to be immortal by the post-cata draenei intro narration. Normal draenei (as in, not Velen) over 25,000 years old are depicted in game. Jessera of Mac'aree, a questgiver in bloodmyst isle speaks of Argus from personal memory, similarly, Hakmud of Argus, the vendor on the tundra mammoth is, most likely, of Argus, which would put both over the 25k mark. As neither shows visible signs of aging, it seems likely that they are immortal, or if not, close enough that the distinction seems moot. And while many people have put forth theories like the draenei spending most of their time flying between worlds in stasis pods that explain their long lifespans, there is no actual canon evidence (to my knowledge) that this is the case or that such stasis pods even exist.

2: What power does immortality give them over the mortal races? I mean it's awesome, don't get me wrong, and logically it SHOULD convey thousands of years of combat experience and developed skills that put their fighters on a level of expertise no mortal could compete with, but they are never depicted as such and seem to be entirely fightable one on one by most other species. And if it WERE the case, the damage would already be done, they'd already HAVE a ten thousand year advantage. It's not gonna get measurably WORSE within the scope of the game, immortal or not. But beyond experience, their immortality is simply depicted as protection from age and perhaps from natural diseases, it has never been depicted as god-like or even vampire-like immortality which conveys immunity to harm or the ability to rapidly heal from injuries like Wolverine. And please elaborate on this "previous misuse" of the night elves immortality, because I don't know what you mean and see no evidence that they somehow abused it when they had it. Are you referring to the events of Azshara's reign where they went all demony and magic crazy? That was abuse of arcane magic, not immortality specifically, and it was perpetrated by only a specific few among their numbers. Punishing all night elves for the crimes of some night elves seems absurd to me, especially when the actual perpetrators have already been punished, either by outright death, horrific mutations, or thousands of years of exile and shunning.

3: You make a fair assessment of Blizzard's apparent views on immortality. However, that being the case, they should have simply put immortality in the rear view mirror after WCIII and moved the night elves on to other stories. As they did it, consistently teasing night elf fans with storylines about how they've found hope for the restoration of their immortality, only to then promptly dash those hopes at the finish line every time, and this has been their main story arc for the past NINE YEARS real world time, Blizzard are the ones who have built up in players an expectation for immortality to be restored so that the main story their race has had since vanilla can finally be paid off. It's not like the players are just coming out of left field with this desire. I don't think it's reasonable to expect night elf players to be satisfied with constant teasing and failure while they see the other races achieve their goals and progress their story. It's not like it's within the power of night elf players to simply accept immortality as lost and move on to another story, Blizzard keeps bringing it up!
