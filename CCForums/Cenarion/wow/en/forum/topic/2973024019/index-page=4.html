> The very fact that two different sets of gear have to exist for PvE and PvP is already an indication of how severely this game needs a total separation with abilities/talents as well.


I really don't agree with this. I think to much focus is put on this. What really hurt arena and started the downfall was the "Bring the Player not the class" crap that started in WoTLK beta. That is really what started all this.

In TBC you had buff/utility classes and you had control heavy specs and then you had high damage classes, and then healers.

Because of this it was very easy to see when a class was overpowered. When they start spreading extremely powerful raid buffs to all specs and stripping things that classes had for years it caused new problems.

Example... In TBC a Elemental Shaman was brought to a raid for their buffs. They had pretty poor damage in 25mans but great damage in 10mans like ZA. They had wicked burst in group pvp and that was there thing.

When they did the bring the player stuff, it put the microscope on balancing big time because now if Elemental Shaman are undertuned in pve they are not worth bringing because other classes have their buffs. This is what started the out of control burst in pvp. It's like what can you give a Elemental Shaman to make people want them? I mean multiple classes have their buffs now so that isn't enough to warrant adding them to a team. So they gave them retardo burst damage.

I mean that is just one example but it's been a trend.

Also i think the talent point rebalancing has been a failure and sucked the life out of the last bit of fun and choice we had left. There is virtually no choice when it comes to picking talents anymore. I honestly don't even know why we have talent trees at all. They are so limited and boring now.


I didn't realize just how big of an issue the talent tree change was til I went and played Rift for a month and found myself creating crazy builds just because I could. With the way things are set up now they might as well just have buttons you press that are " standard PvP spec" or " Standard PvE spec". I know trees have always had cookie cutter specs, but at least before you could create some wierd hybrid spec and it made for some fun.

As far as patch 4.3 and beyond, its simply going to be New Raid, New 5 man, more dailies with PvP getting more jacked up due to some crazy PvE item. I said coming into the expansion that Cata would be the one to determine just how big it stays. I certainly believe so far it has been a flop in a large percentage of players minds.
