see the thing i like about pvp is i know im better then any ignorant pver, like granny for example


And you wonder why "PVEers" look at "PVPers" like they're the big, dumb jocks of WoW.

I came into this game back in '08 for the idea of PVP. I loved PVP in Guild Wars, and I love team-oriented, objective-based games that require strategy and planning in not just the zones you play in, but the characters you create as well. WoW doesn't have that.

Every spec has the same basic build, and requires the same set up of stats, talent points, gear, rotations, etc. Guild Wars (and I think Rift did this as well) allowed HUGE freedom as far as creating your own character that functioned how you want it to, or how your team needed it to. As I played a Ranger, if my team needed a silencer/interrupter, I would go back to town and create a silence- and interrupt-heavy build. If my team needed extra tanking, I'd go back spec for my pet for maximum pwn donorship. I usually preferred to be the (what WoW players would call) multi-shotting juggernaut that'd have half the enemies down (in PVE, in PVP I'd simply be harassing the living hell out of the opponents, while picking off random ones) before they reached our tank. However, there was no ability to one-shot, nor did I ever find myself getting slowed, stunned, and CCed for minutes on end, while I limped my way away from a Paladin or Mage.

The fact of the matter is, we could choose. We have about three choices in this game- race, class, and class role. To be honest, it's the reason I like Enhancement so much- it's not painfully tough to play, but it's somewhat complicated (in some areas) and requires quick-thinking to be good at it. That, and I don't have to sit there and cast all the time.

Oh, the four disco lights we get are awesome too.

PVP and PVE need to function separately- it's not really an option. Resilience needs to be scrapped from having effect in the PVE areas (meaning, it's a stat that works solely in arenas, battlegrounds, etc.), skills need to function differently against players, and things like one-shotting, constant stun-locks, or being stuck in CC for 10-15 seconds need to go. I would love to get into PVP in a game like WoW, but as it stands, WoW's PVP just doesn't work as it should.
