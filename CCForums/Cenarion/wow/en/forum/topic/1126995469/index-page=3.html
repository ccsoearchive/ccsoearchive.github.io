I loved your guide and agree on a lot of the points, but I disagree on one part. Most assassins throughout history, to this day even, know how to defend themselves extremely well in close quarters combat. Assassins are generally extremely intelligent or else they'd just be strongarms or caught their first day out. They don't get into a situation thinking that they'd automatically succeed and they train thusly. The martial art "Ninjutsu" is an effective martial art and it was created for the sole purpose of "when things go wrong". People have this odd notion that just because an assassin works at night and under the cover of dark that automatically means they are weak as all get out. That's simply not true.

There are a lot of exceptions though. Snipers obviously would have very little training in close quarters combat, but they wouldn't be in your house while you're sleeping anyway. The ones that primarily mix and use poisons: They might be in your house to add poisons to your favorite brownies (the ones with the sprinkles) and would definitely flee at the slightest hint of danger. Generally the close combat expertise applies to those who willfully sneak into someone's house to kill them in their sleep with a dagger or some such. They are generally trained for the "oh crap" times. Obviously if there are 4 guards with the person, that goes out the window and they too would flee. Also a lot of assassins are in contract of the military and they too would have received above-par training.

Close quarters combat doesn't require a lot of strength/ bulk at all (In real life it's actually a hindrance). The bigger you are, the harder it is to move while agility fairs much better than swinging around a giant axe the size of a car. In my opinion, dodging attacks is much more preferable than getting beat on without damage.

Though, I just wanted to reiterate, I loved the guide and thought it to be a nice resource for a lot of the RPers that sit around in bars with their daggers out, talking about the good ole day's where they killed their mom (or a clown). Keep up the good work.

Wow. So in other words you're saying "If you don't Roleplay something right/cool then you're not a good Roleplayer".

I don't roleplay. I don't like my little pony. But what makes a good roleplay is making a good role and a cohesive character that flows with a story you are creating. A lot like writing basically, but more "control" and "differentiation" put into how the story and characters move along.

So no, deciding to Roleplay as an openly aggressive and loud mouthed assassin doesn't instantly mean no one else will roleplay with you or that you're "bad at it". Roleplaying My Little Pony might be dumb, but it CERTAINLY doesn't mean you're not going find people to roleplay with and it definitely doesn't just mean you're innately bad at it.

It's roleplay. Roleplay the character how you want them to be written. A "good assassin" character can be a speechless, silent creeper who never does anything spontaneous, or they could be a snarky loudmouth who's open about their occupation.

You can't just say "This character isn't written how I think an assassin should be written, so it's bad."

Well, you can, but it'd make you a narrow-minded !@#$%^-. But maybe that's the kind of person you want to be, so I dunno.


Well no, if you want to be a loud mouthed and arrogant assassin, sure you could RP that out just fine, but you would have been killed long ago or imprisoned unless you RPed your character as ridiculously god-like and powerful. I think I mis-worded if you assume that I say everyone has to be just right. On RP servers though, god-moding is very bad and not setting limitations is also very bad. An assassin that told everyone about how he killed people would have been killed or imprisoned after a short period of time.

On an RP server, most people will not RP with that person because it leads to a LOT of issues between character. When someone RPs a guard (and plenty do) and someone RPs an assassin, neither wants their character to fail. It leads to a rock and hard place situation which is good RP for the beginning then horrible infighting toward the end when someone has to win or lose.

I was specifically talking about those situations with the AC and MLP references. If you want to be unique, you can. If you don't accept the consequences for being unique, then that's bad RP. If you RP a loudmouthed assassin, you have to expect others to dislike it and want to put a stop to that person. If you pretend you are omnipotent and can't be beaten, then you're a bad rper. It just isn't an intelligent move on your character's part to be a loudmouthed assassin. I personally like to make a depth to my character and not make them bludgeoningly stupid enough to walk into populated areas and announce that he's a cold-blooded killer. You have to expect someone that RPs a guard to jump in and try to put a stop to you. You also have to realize that on RP servers (which is what I'm being specific to) your character's story doesn't end at the end of the session.

If you're in a fight and no one wants to lose, you don't just stop RPing and move on to the next RP session like it never happened. You develop a history. If you're taking it as me saying you're a bad RPer, I'm not. I'm simply stating that unless your character is an idiot (or totally drunk at the time) he wouldn't do that. If he did, it's safe to assume something would have stopped him. You can't just think about your own character on RP servers, you have to be courteous and realize that others RP as well and your omnipotent and !@#$-like character WILL clash with them and it may not turn out how you want (unless you like being blacklisted by every serious RPer around).

I'd also prefer not to get into a flamewar / argument on this guy's guide as it's disrespectful. I hear your opinion, understand it, and I'm not invalidating it. I just have a differing opinion and would appreciate it not being invalidated as well. Thank you.
