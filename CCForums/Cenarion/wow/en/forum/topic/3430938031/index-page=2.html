Well, you only cited a single expansion in your examples, but I agree that in the case of talent trees at least, we have heard this before. But Blizzard acknowledges this: It was said during BlizzCon that they *did* try to make them less cookie-cutter for Cata, but that they didn't do it right. This is their next shot at doing it right. Will it work? Duno yet, but it certainly has promise.

I can't comment on world PvP promises for Cata because I didn't pay attention to such statements at the time. I think many of those who play on PvP servers, though, would agree that Molten Front, as boring a grind as it was for PvE, did wonders for reinvigorating world PvP on their servers.

Re: your comment on utility, I think rogues *do* have more utility than they did in LK. All classes did, by virtue of the return of crowd control to dungeons (before we began to overgear them, anyway) and the increased complexity of some raid boss fights (compared to in late Wrath) that required us to use such abilities. I never considered disabling those gimmicky traps in ICC to be "utility"; few raid groups, if any, brought rogues specifically to disable them, and they felt to me more like Blizzard throwing us a bone than actually incorporating our abilities into the raid in a meaningful way.

It's fine to be disappointed that some things didn't come to pass that you, or Blizz, hoped would happen. But don't let yourself slip into the spiral of despair that leads you to conclude that "every expansion fails to deliver on its promises," because it's just not true. It's easy to forget about the good when the bad registers such stronger emotions.
