Ummm after reading some of this guide there is some misinformation in here that should be corrected.

First off, the prime glyph choices you presented are incomplete and inaccurate as to what is most important. Shadow Dance glyph is far more important than the backstab glyph. 2 Extra seconds is enough time to put a garrote at the end of a stun chain on a healer which can guarantee kills, this is non-negotiable in my book. For some reason, you also saw it fit not to include the Eviscerate glyph? For a sword sub rogue, Evis/ShDance/Hemo are hands down best glyphs. For Dagger, ShDance/Hemo with SnD, Backstab, OR Evis glyph ALL work.

Also, for major glyphs, while there are some rogues who use glyph of cloak of shadows, it is generally considered far inferior to Blind/Preperation/Garrote glyphs. Only time I could see somebody using Cloak glyph is if they are playing with a class that has a silence that lasts as long as garrote that you don't want to DR.

Additionally, I am not sure if you understand the importance of WOUND poison for a sword-hemo rogue. Playing with mind-numbing as a sword rogue is going to SEVERELY hamper your damage, as the main reason to use swords is increased poison procs which lead to more sustained damage. Also still not sure if you understand spell pen? Envenom is not affected by spell pen, period. Only poisons that proc off weapon swings (i.e. wound) are affected. Typically when a poison procs it does X damage with Y damage resisted. Spell pen reduces the damage resisted.

Overall I think there is some useful info in here, but some of what you wrote shows a deep misunderstanding of fundamental rogue mechanics.

Just to clarify, I'm 2400+ in 3s and have played a rogue since vanilla.
