Question: Why is Waylay excluded from the dagger and sword build? is there a reason that its better to take something else?

Edit: Um im a bit confused on key bindings, do you HAVE to use them? i started wow as a caster, so ive come to learn to click my abilities and using the arrow keys. ive tried the hotkeys, but it just dosnt seem natural to me, my hands always get misplaced on the keyboard, any help on how i can learn this easily?


Well if you picked up Waylay in your Hemo build the Backstabs wouldn't gain the Crit Strike buff from Puncturing Wounds. If you then chose to pick up Puncturing Wounds you'd lose survival for more Backstab damage, which is the opposite to the reasoning behind the Hemo build.

No you don't have to use keybinding, although you will notice a drastic change in both reaction time and gameplay. Your reaction times and movement will be a lot sharper and getting behind players will be leaps and bounds easier. Having said that, there are still lots of players that have hit high ratings in arena through clicking.

There are no real huge tips that I can give for keybinding apart from keeping it simple. Try and keep the keybound keys close to the natural positioning of your left hand (WASD). I.E. Q-T,F-G,Z-B,1-5 etc. the further away the key (E.G 9/0/-/=) the longer it takes to find/feel for, since it's so far away.

If it's any help I generally have my left hand pinky on the shift key, with my thumb on the spacebar and my three middle fingers on a/w/d.

A few things i'd like to add that you should update for 4.2:

A new spec has arisen using Hemo in the stead of BS and is very competitive right now.

Take the "Balance" spec he listed above and do the following:

Puncturing Wounds > Coup de Grace.
Waylay + Nightstalker > Cheat Death.

This gives you a bit of extra survivability.

The Glyphs for such a spec are a based on preference. You MUST use Hemo/ShD. But for a 3rd you can take either SnD or Evis. Most will prefer SnD. This spec requires Daggers. Testing has been done using a slower 2.6 in the MH but it's a loss. Don't do it.

The next thing I was to bring up is Expertise.

Currently, Rogues have become a more viable train target. This requires us to take 20/20 Expertise as to not miss a Gouge, KS, etc... When we can't be behind the target/are being trained. After your 5% hit, grab 20/20 Expertise (It isn't hard), and don't EVER let anyone tell you that you need 7% hit for Night Elfs. You don't. 5% is it. Period... Crit is still the king > Haste.

Gemming:

A lot of Rogues are using Agi in all sockets excluding yellow, where they take Agi/Res or Agi/Crit on preference. Use this to match yellow sockets. If a blue is needed, don't do it (although one Agi/Hit gem is recommended to get as close to 5% as possible using full Vic).

And since i'm on this last page anyway...

What's more effective? 8/2/31 or 10/0/31?


The former is for more survivability. Many top rated Rogues are currently running with it because we're train targets this season.

The latter is for more sustained damage. Use it when you know you will not be trained and can stay behind your target.
