I'm positive that the ambush damage done with a slower weapon is still ahead of dagger ambush. Ask Tssarlol


It doesn't. It's slightly behind, but it's also fairly negligible. I've finally gotten around to enchanting my T2-fist and I can honestly say I really don't notice a difference either way. With resilience factored in, the differences between the weapons are very minimal. Still not sure which one is 100% optimal, but I'm inclined to say for pure, raw burst the dagger would be ahead.

Just have to look at the tooltip to confirm that:

256% weapon + 1272, 370% weapon + 1841 (using my spec, which is probably different from most people).

256% weapon damage with a sword is equivalent to 370% weapon damage with a dagger. It's equal. The static modifier got multiplied by the same coefficient to scale weapon damage up for a dagger, and thus it's always going to be 44.7% ahead of the sword. It's generally not much extra damage (with armor, resilience, etc), but it is something.

Also: if you use a 2.6 weapon, unheeded warning is quite a bit worse than our agility trinkets.

How to effectively flip a Rogue opening:

Presuming they open with Cheap Shot (If they don't just Gouge and start controlling like usual) trinket the Cheap Shot and Blind them, it's very important to trinket very quickly, if they get a bleed up you will be putting yourself at a disadvantage. From there wait the Blind out, if they trinket that then Vanish and open with Cheap Shot, if they don't trinket the Blind then continue with your normal control methods while being ready to use Cloak when you feel they will Blind.


Blind is physical. Cloaking doesn't do anything to keep you from getting blinded.


Getting a clean Kidney Shot:

Using Kidney Shot from face on can sometimes miss if you have low Expertise, however if you Gouge first then get behind your opponent and Kidney Shot there is a 100% chance it will not miss. Moves from behind cannot be dodge/parried/missed/blocked in PvP.


No, no, no.
Hit Rating affects whether you miss or not.
Expertise Rating reduces the chance for your attacks to get dodged/parried/blocked.
No matter where you attack your enemy from, your attacks can still miss with inadequate hit rating.

when 4.1 rolls around dont you think it would be smarter to drop a point out of waylay and toss it into nightstalker?


Every spec should have at least 1 point in waylay no matter what. Crippling poison is very easy to dispel and is a crucial part of rogues control, if crippling gets dispelled you can fall back on waylay.

Nightstalkers main strong point is opening on other stealth users and chasing down flag carriers, aside from that its uncommon to be needing that stealth cooldown so rigorously.

I was having a look at your spec and would recommend you drop the 3 points from Cheat Death and put either 1 in Night stalker and 1 in Waylay or 2 in Waylay. In either scenario put the third point into Puncturing Wounds, from here you can decide if you want to drop points from Quickening and get more damage from Puncturing Wounds or not.

As it stands Cheat Death in most specs is already less useful than picking up the control and additional damage, the one exception is higher-end arena where you're more likely to encounter well formed cleave-style teams that favour fast switching to the rogue once your cooldowns have been used up.

Furthermore, in 4.1 Cheat Death is going to be severely nerfed and even at high-end arena against cleave-style teams taking the option of more control and damage will be the preferred option.*

* Chance I may be wrong, if anyone would expand on that I'd appreciate.
