Okay, I know it's really early and we shouldn't jump to conclusions about anything. But, that said...

Okay first off, talents aside, you're taking away the out of combat rez from feral and balance druids? Please, PLEASE tell me this is another one of those database mistakes. There was a reason you gave that to use in the first place, please remember what it was like before and don't send us back there.

As for talents... for druids they really seem to focus on the idea that "Shifting is fun!". But, well, Okay it IS fun to shift around and turn into animals and what not... but not during combat. The only reason I will ever shift out of cat or bear form is to either A. grab a mob if the tank dies or B. quickly battle rez someone before shifting back.

All our level 90 talents seem pretty worthless aside from disentanglement, but that's only for pvp really. Heart of the wild gives us bonuses we'll probably never use, as does master shapeshifter. I'm not about to shapeshift out of cat form to cast moonfire three times for a meager bonus. Also, if you're wanting us to shapeshift out and cast heals to make use of these bonuses then why are you taking away almost all our heals?

One tier of druid talents has 2 abilities that require you to be in bear form to use, so... that's just great for the 3 other druid specs. Also the other talent from that tier brings all enemies towards you, which is also something I would only think a bear would enjoy.

Meanwhile, there's another tier with 2 awesome abilities for cat druids. Among which is our stampede bonus and another which lets us use stealth abilities while not stealthed. But I can only pick one, and quite frankly the latter seems like the better option (of course I would have to test it). Still, it hurts to see stampede go as it felt like such a core Alpha-strike ability to cats.

Also, unless I missed it we're losing "blood in the water". Now, it's not exactly integral, but without it I really have little incentive to use ferocious bite unless the raid target has 1 or 2% health remaining.

It also seems like nurturing instinct is being changed to another one of those "Hey you should shapeshift a lot" abilities since it's losing the 20% increase to incoming heals in cat form which was what made it worth taking.

All-in-all, it feels like the druid talents were designed with an awesome idea in mind (that druids should be shapeshifting all over the place), that would never work in the actual game.

I like the warrior trees at this stage of the game. It looks like they are doing a good job of making the choice for each tier a playstyle/utility choice rather than a dps or mitigation choice. I'm thinking about the builds for both prot PvE and PvP, which are the two specs I tend to play, and really struggling with build choices. I think this is indicative of good design: choices reflect playstyle and not optimized builds.

As just one example, for the first tier of warrior talents (Juggernaut, Double Time, Warbringer) one could make a very good case of the viability for each for a PvP warrior. I can imagine how each brings different strengths and weaknesses to PvP and see the difficulty of picking just one as optimal. If this translates into making a talent choice based on playstyle, composition, and setting (10v10 RBGs or 3v3 Arenas, for example), then I think Blizzard will have succeeded.

Looking forward to seeing the next few iterations of these talents as MoP approaches.
