Void shift is actually a terrible talent since I can't see how it would work anywhere except a PVP scenario. Even then I'm not sure. You're switching health with a FRIENDLY target. That means you can give them your higher health pool and potentially die yourself, which means surprise, the group is down a healer. I'm assuming this is a little like LOH except bad. You heal whoever it is to maybe max HP while taking a huge risk yourself (Since when has Blizzard not been in love with their AoE damage boss fights?).The only good priest talent in that tier is vampiric dominance since it applies to healing as well as damage. The fake beacon with the priest sharing damage with the person they beacon is terrible as well. What is it with priests and the half-baked paladin spell copies?

I mean some of these talents look really cool on paper, but realistically they will not be used.

ETA: To sum it up, some of the priest spells require healing at the cost of your own healthpool, which seems stupid. Since when are priests meant to be half-tanks? Sure, I'm sure my group wants me to sacrifice myself so that they can continue the boss fights without a healer. Wait.

Keep in mind these talents are designed for you to be able to change from fight to fight. While there may be a setup more versatile than others, you're not meant to try to come up with that generic build you can take to every fight.

There are fights that will have strong tank damage for certain phases with low raid healing needed during said phase. In such a situation, Vow of Unity or Void Shift actually would be pretty handy. Also, ever think that taking Vow of Unity in junction with Final Prayer would be a pretty nifty combo?
Even if there is an AoE heavy phase (Vampiric Embrace would probably prove stronger, but...) coupled with heavy tank damage it would also be a viable choice to Void Shift and immediately Desperate Prayer right after to avoid the next AoE pulse from killing you.
Additionally, Vow of Unity has a mini-Binding Heal effect on it. Discipline Priests who are losing Binding Heal innately will find a tad more use from Vow of Unity than Holy would.

You're phrasing of "I mean some of these talents look really cool on paper, but realistically they will not be used," is quite interesting to me. It's on paper for everyone, including you. You can't say what it'll be realistically until you've actually played with it yourself. You're focusing too much on the shortcomings everything may or may not have. The truth is, these talents are designed for every choice to have a shortcoming, otherwise there'd be no compelling reason to ever have to switch talents which is what we're going to be intended to do.

You're thinking of these talents too narrow-mindedly. Don't look at one tier of talents on its own. Look at how they can work together to get the most from them.

The talents for rogues are either extremely early in design or just plain bad at the moment.

Tier 1: The only really decent option for PVE is Shadow Focus. Subterfuge MIGHT be useful for Sub in PVE. Nightstalker is a complete joke of a talent.

Tier 2: I believe this tier has something to do with defense? Need I mention that two of these spells are currently baseline spells that Rogues currently know. Having them "taken away" is pretty terrible when most classes get to pick MORE utility.

As for performance of these abilities, Nerve Strike is iffy at best in pvp and completely useless in pve since everything is stun immune. Combat Readiness is in its current form which makes it extremely situational in pvp and useless in pve. Deadly Throw is actually weaker than its current form since Combat rogues are currently able to pick up a talent that allows it to interrupt.

For this tier, all of the choices are just bad and need changing.

Tier 3: Compared to the other two choices, Improved Recuerate is almost a waste. With the changes coming to poisons in Pandaria, Leeching Poison isn't a dps loss and provides more healing. In addition, some of the math I read pointed out that Cheat Death may well be all of the healing a rogue will even need on a boss fight.

Tier 4: The only time I could see it being worth it to pickup Burst of Speed is on Deadflame duty on Heroic Ragnaros. For PVE Scenarios, Shadowstep will provide more mobility, bar none. Also, can it please just be possible to get rid of Preparation and balance rogue CDs accordingly. As history has shown, since rogue CDs are balanced around Prep, one HAS to take Prep in pvp. This is why every single competitive rogue spec has involved taking Prep.

Tier 5: Unless Paralytic Poison's stun is off the internal cooldown, taking this talent is just plain rubbish since you in affect also give up Crippling Poison, a rogue's only slow. For anyone not following, for a melee to be competitive in pvp, they need a slow. There a reason why Ret Paladins have been the laughing stock of pvp compared to other melee.

Between Dirty Deeds and Deadly Brew, neither is particular useful in pve. The only determining factor on which one you would take for pvp is pretty simple. Does anyone you're pvping with have a Mortal Strike debuff?:

Yes?: Take Dirty Deeds.
No?: Take Deadly Brew

Not much thought to that.

Tier 6: This tier is a complete slap in the face and unoriginal. All you did was take the dps cooldowns at the bottom of each spec's tree and prevent us from getting them until level 90. This means that rogues literally do not have anything NEW to look forward to at level 90 in form of talents. Even then, the level 90 talents for rogues are pretty meh. You couldn't pick a tier where there will almost always be a talent you MUST take. Already, theorycrafting is being done to pick which spell gives the most dps. Every will be this ability and the other two will be forgotten about.

In terms of how good the level 90 talents are, Shadow Dance is a carbon copy of the current talent.

Vendetta has actually been nerfed both in effectiveness and duration.

Killing Spree, I don't even know what to say. Why does it still randomly teleport the rogue around? I want to attack the mob I'm on, not the dog running by me for only a brief second that I can't even kill.

Overall:

The current talents for Rogues seem like you randomly took current baseline rogue abilities and threw them into the Talent previews. I look at other classes and see that many are getting newer, cooler abilities while most of my baseline stuff is getting stripped and I have to pick some of it that I'm able to get back. Again, I hope that this is just a preview of the current Alpha build that is under internal testing because the rogue talents currently look like something a used car salesman would offer me.

I lied. I looked at the resto druid.

1) You're forcing me to have lifebloom be refreshed by nourish, regrowth, and healing touch? no. just no. I want my lifeblooms to expire. Thanks for the choice.

3) Omen of Clarity might be worded wrong, because it says on damaging spells and attacks. The current malfurion's gift talent gave us Omen of Clarity with our lifeblooms, but it doesn't say it will do that. The proposed malfurion's gift talent doesn't have anything about lifebloom, and it says it will reduce our tranquility spell to 13 minutes. WTF?

4) I have to waste time placing mushrooms to explode to aoe heal people? No.

5) Symbiosis sounds cool. Hope I don't get some cheesy ability.

6) There is nothing mentioned about shifting removing roots/snares like we currently have for resto druids.

7) The LvL30 talent is a no-brainer, Cenarion Ward is way better than the other 2, mainly because of their cooldown.

8) LvL 15 talent, Displacer Beast, is way better than the other two. 3min cooldown for something fun, is a shame.

9) You took my Nature's Grasp and bastardized it into Mass Entanglement, thus providing a super bad ability for PvP (see: Interruptable) and a terrible 8s AoE CC in PvE (8s?). But you gave me the option to have typhoon, which is really wierd to have as resto.

10) For a class that has 4 specs the number of talents you choose from is terrible because you have to have something in there for all 4 at each level. It's not like a hunter choosing how he wants to interrupt (silence, sleep, or stun) for his dps. It's choosing a tank, vs heals, vs dps x2 for totally different goals. Absolutely terrible for druids, pallies, and shamans.

11) Can't comment on Force of Nature because there's nothing there, but at least the LvL 60 talent level tries to address the FACT that there are FOUR specs. (see: #10) This is the ONLY talent level that has 3 abilities, each affecting one of the four specs, essentially having 12 options. This is how they all should be for these multi-role classes.

12) I hope Wild Charge has a 30s, or less, cooldown. It looks fun. If not, it will be easily outmatched by Incarnation.

13) LvL 75 talent level has nothing for PvE cats, balance, or resto. You failed. I picked up an AoE bear-form daze purely for PvP/short CC reasons. Terrible level.

14) LvL 90 talent level finally has my disentanglement ability. Glad no lowbies want to use that for their low level PvP, but Blizz doesn't recognize that now do they. This whole level looks to be for PvP. I picked this up because it'll help me in PvP, and heal me in PvP or PvE if I need to power-shift quick for some reason.

14b) Heart of the Wild. 6 min cooldown for something that is marginally helpful. If you have to use this in PvE you're changing roles for (lose dps and gain healing, for example.) a gain that you shouldn't have had to do in the first place. This tier, and this ability, are not good.

14c) Master Shapeshifter. Good for PvP. nothing else.



Overall, the abilities and talents are a downgrade and provide lackluster choices for things our roles don't do.

Putting in some effort is an understatement. The quality is on par with an average WoW player's post on MMO-Champ coming up with cool new talents and abilities.
