Thanks for the post, Bashiok. Really appreciate everyone wanting to give the players some tangible idea of what's to come for talents and class abilities in MoP, as quickly as you could, even though it's all still a work in progress. It's nice to be able to offer feedback so early on in the process.

I'm pretty excited about the new system. I think it's a nice way to streamline talents, while still offering options to people that will make their individual characters feel unique, compared to others of the same class and even spec. And although, I'm sure that there will eventually be those bulids that are considered the best way to go for PvE and PvP, I still think it's allowing for a bit more creativity on the player's part.

I like the changes that are being made to Warlock talents and abilities, as a whole, and to my favorite spec, Affliction. If I'm remembering correctly, I read that Malefic Grasp will be replacing Shadow Bolt as the filler for afflic 'locks. I never really minded SB, but I like this idea.

It's cool that BoA, Drain Soul, and others will be spec-only; and, I really like that there will be a separate resource systems for each kind of Warlock. Rolling CoW and CoT into Curse of Enfeeblement seems like a fantastic idea, too. Again, I think changes like this are a great way to streamline our abilities for the better. I like the alterations made to Nightfall and Shadow Trance, respectively, as well.

Unending Resolve is a gift and it's a blast to imagine Demonic Portal.

One thing I did notice, though, was the lack of Haunt on the spec list for Affliction. I double checked all Warlock abilities, to be safe, but still had no luck. I know that everything's up in the air right now, though, and that several abilities were left off the lists. So, it's probable that it'll be added again, in the future. Everything's a work in progress right now, so I realize that nothing's near finished. But, if there is the slim chance that Haunt is being removed, then I'll be really bummed. Haunt feels like such an integral ability for Affliction and I know that it's one of my absolute favorite spells. So, I hope that won't be the case.

Other than that, the only thing I'm a little disheartened about is the loss of Soul Harvest. I understand the removal and I realize that we'll be able to regenerate Soul Shards automatically outside of combat and in combat, via Soul Drain, even if our target doesn't die. But, I really enjoy using it as a way of refueling when questing and/or doing several dailies in a row. I like to be able to rip through my dailies and use SH as a way to lessen downtime needed to drink/eat. I'd rather life tap my mana back and then use Soul Harvest to regenerate my health on the fly. It's also nice way to help healers out a bit, when running instances. So, other than being a bit sad to see that ability go, I'm really pleased with what's to come for Warlock talents and abilities.

I think MoP is shaping up wonderfully. I can't wait. :)

We wanted to take a moment to give some context to why the calculators were posted, and the permanence of the data shown. A lot of times we say things like "not final" and "may change" as verbal wiggle room should we decide we need to go a different direction, or are unable to complete something in time. In this case the Mists of Pandaria calculator data is essentially pre-alpha, and will absolutely change in many ways.

It was important for us to get this stuff in front of you as soon as possible though (and as a work in progress as it is) to get your feedback, which will in no small part influence that change. We also wanted to present everything on a single page to give you a fuller context for the initial talent changes we announced at BlizzCon, and to see how the overall structure of core abilities, spec abilities, and talents will function, with concrete examples.

The calculator contains elements that are experimental, still in the process of implementation, or in some cases outright failed experiments that we already intend to revise or replace. Odds are good that if it looks like we’ve forgotten some critical piece of a particular class toolkit, it’s either accounted for elsewhere, or simply a data glitch (e.g., Prayer of Healing is currently absent from the calculator – we are not taking Prayer of Healing away from priests, and Devastate for warriors probably won't sunder armor 453%). Our hope is that revealing the calculator in this state will shed light on the philosophy behind our talent overhaul, and let you get a sense of how pieces of your core rotational gameplay, such as Hot Streak, Riptide, or Sudden Doom, fit into the new system.

The different classes are also at very different stages of design and implementation, and the fact that some mechanics changes we discussed at BlizzCon and in recent Q&As are not yet present does not mean that we have abandoned those ideas.

Any and all feedback is very welcome, but please keep all of the above in mind when considering the information in the talent calculator, and please keep feedback consolidated in the Mists of Pandaria forum

With all due respect, you released this information way too soon. The Shaman forum has exploded into waves of crying over Shaman being "gutted" and many of us forum regulars have thus had to do the job of CMs by trying to put out the fires and explain how incomplete the information is.

It's great to hear stuff about the classes that you HAVE worked on, but if you were going to release this stuff, you should've listed Shaman as "coming soon" the same way you did for Monk. Probably should've done the same for other classes, too, but I haven't checked up on other classes yet.

I know this may come off as incredibly rude, but I really would appreciate it if you could remind your lead developers that quality comes first, not timetables. Rushing out content faster is a worth goal to meet, but not if you're going to sacrifice quality and cause community uproars.

You know how GC said your litmus test is what's fun?
Well what is NOT fun is to see a list of changes posted on information sites that several spells from your class were "removed" in 5.0. To receive incomplete information that could, potentially, cause a panic in posters who tend to quickly react emotionally before they take the time to think it through logically.
That's not fun. Not in the least.
