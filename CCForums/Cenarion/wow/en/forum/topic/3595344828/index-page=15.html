While I understand the motivation behind the new talent trees, I think its important that there are still talents that boost throughput in some way for all three specs. Reading the shaman talent trees is basically like looking through a bunch of PvP/utility talents for Enhance or Ele, and only Resto gets talents that make a raider look forward to taking them.

If you want situational talent choices that players will have to think about, they still have to be useful enough that you absolutely want at least one of them. I can say with utmost confidence right now that as a raiding DPS shaman, I could spend 0/6 talent points in that Shaman tree and lose almost nothing in terms of raid performance. There's a huge gap between that and having a single cookie-cutter spec for most encounters.

For DPS, talents should boost your performance on particular kinds of fights and ask you to use your knowledge of the encounter and your role in it to make a choice, like say one that increases mobility vs another that rewards you for being stationary. One that increases AoE or cleave damage and another that helps single target. That should be the general goal with the talents and the "choices" they offer. Some situational survivability talents are good too, but there needs to be something that impacts the bottom line as a raider, which is your throughput.

The warlock tree seems like a decent starting point. The shaman one, at least for the DPS specs, is poor.
