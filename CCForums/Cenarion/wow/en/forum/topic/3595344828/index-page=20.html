Honestly, I like the new Talent system. That it needs some work is undeniable, but the rough cut has me very excited about the final product. There are several reasons I like this:

1) The old talent system was rarely equalized. Some specs had more options & some specs had less options. While they've been trimming & streamlining for years, I can definitely say that this system has officially discarded the old flaw by opening up options to everyone.

2) The old talent system had a lot of "necessary" mechanics inside of it. Take points in "Talent A" to get more of "Stat B" so that you can fill [insert role here]. Now, they've put the necessary stuff for your role INTO your spec. As such, the talents are actually CHOICES! Sure, there may be certain picks that are more ideal for Tanking (my preferred role), but even if I don't take the "right" talents, I can still perform reasonably well as a Tank. That's because the put the required stuff INTO my spec, instead of making me buy it.

The new system gives me what I need, and then let's me pick from a list of interesting toys I can add to it all! I am in love with this concept!

3) I actually have to make a choice & I actually know I will enjoy my choices!

This may seem complex, but...

I looked at the sample Paladin Talent Tree, and with maybe one exception, I could see myself spending some serious time trying to debate which specific talents I want to utilize. In some places (the level 15 talents is a great example), all three options were very appealing to me. I think I know which one I'd take (if the tree remains the same), but it would be a SERIOUS CHOICE.

And then, when I got my new talents, I'd actually ENJOY my choices. I'd feel like I really got something (more than +X% damage to {ability Y}). I would actually feel like I got to use my new talents, instead of them sitting in the background, modifying the RNG.

And this is an upgrade, b/c while the Old Talents were useful, they were rarely exciting. I can rarely recall an instance where I actually had to debate -which- talent I REALLY wanted. I usually had it mapped out several levels in advance, with no real thought going into it. In fact, the only time I had to think was when I encountered uninteresting talents in the current tier. I've had the circumstances where I don't like the highest level talents, at which point, I had to pick which lower tier talent could fill the time until I got back to the obvious high level talents I was aiming to get.

In the new talent system, I feel like I actually have to make a choice, and like I'm going to actually enjoy my choices made.

______________________________________________________________

I do concede that there are a few locations where I feel the system needs some refining (in some cases, heavy refining), but all-in-all, the new Talent Trees are off to a good start, & I can't wait to see the final product Blizzard releases.

I've looked at a couple trees, and it's definitely going to depend on class.

For druids, I'm in love. Essentially, we got:

Things I keep seeing Druids lacking or asking for, such as CC options in all specs and mitigation CDs usable on other players.

Things that just seem neat, like Symbiosis, which could be brilliant depending on composition and implementation. I find it very hard to imagine that there will be no situation in which it doesn't make me giggle.

And finally, things that get at the heart of why we rolled druids, such as Heart of the Wild, allowing every druid player the ability to fulfill that fantasy of switching roles in a pinch and seeing the raid through in a crisis.

So the druid stuff is very well designed and I'm giddy about all of it. My other 85 is a mage, and is significantly less impressed. I mean, neat idea, sure, but unless Pandarian raids have an incredible number of sheepable adds it overall seems somewhat lackluster. Alter Time has possibilities for creative use, however.

I look at the Druid trees and I go "Wow, I can't WAIT for MoP!" I look at the Mage trees and I just kinda go "Meh" by comparison.

It is impossible to have talent choices that are both meaningful as well as optional.

To be meaningful, they have to be different enough to make a difference by selecting talent A over talent B.

To be Optional, the effects of Talent A are so close to the effects of talent B that it wouldn't hurt you no matter what you chose.

If Blizz wants us to choose the talents that are fun rather than needed, then they need to make talent choices a strictly for fun thing. Like Pallys having the option to sprout golden wings for 5 minutes, dress up in golden armor, or have a unique pet follow them around. Given options like that, there is no impact to your DPS/healing/tanking so you are free to choose what you prefer.

If Blizz wants meaningful talents that matter, then there will always be a cookie cutter spec. If I want to max my DPS I will check online and choose the talents that do that. Hence, a cookie cutter spec is born.
