11/24/2011 10:26 AMPosted by Milsa
All of the work spent on these trees could have been spent on flushing out and balancing the existing trees


You know, that would have been a better idea. Instead of starting new again and possibly heading downhill further, they could have just improved the current trees. Why start fresh? Unless the current trees are that bad. If so, then why risk another failure?


Well see, since their goal with the talent trees is to provide choices for how you play your character, there's a couple of problems with the current trees that they are attempting to address.

First, there's a lot of points that are just so boring and brainless. 3 points for 3% haste? 2 points for some increased armor? All of those talents have to be removed because they aren't actual choices and they make no real mechanical impact either. You just either pick them up because they are theorycrafted to be the best, or you skip them because they aren't.

Then, there's the problem of certain abilities that are must-haves for a spec. Things like Icy Veins, Moonkin Form, Vampiric Touch, etc. You don't really get to "choose" those either. You just take them because you're a balance druid and you can't operate as such without those talents. So again, to make the talent trees achieve their goal, all of those have to be removed and just given to you as a specialization bonus.

By the time you get rid of all the talents that aren't really a choice, you don't really have many actual talents left to choose from. You only have the utility talents like Solar Beam, Selfless Healer, Silence, etc. In some cases (like silence and solar beam), the development team obviously made the decision that they want the spec to have it, but they don't want it to be possible for the other specs to have it, so it gets put with all the stuff you get for your specialization bonus as well.

At this point, if you still had 41 talent points to spend, you'd be able to get everything that's left, and still have nearly all of your talent points left, meaning they either need to create 30-40 new "utility" talents just so you have something to put your points in, then another several talents so you have to choose and can't get everything, or they reduce the number of points you get to spend. They opted for the latter, resulting in a talent selection that is roughly the size of the 5.0 talent system.

To further ensure that you must make a choice, they arrange the remaining talents into "categories" and only allow you to pick one from each category. So ideally, all of the talents in one row will be of the same type (self-healing, for example), and you have to choose how you want your character to handle that (do you want an automatic self-healing proc, a small, short-cooldown heal, or a large, long-cooldown heal, for example), and while one way may be the "best" for a particular situation, none of them should be the best in all situations.

I don't want to speak for blizzard, but I think some of the feedback they are looking for right now is for us to help make sure that 1) each talent within the same row is something you could see needing in certain situations or 2) each talent within the same row handles similar situations in a different manner (such as actively versus passively reducing damage), but that each manner is something you could see someone enjoying, even if you yourself might not find each manner compelling. At this point, I don't think they are looking for people to math out the few talents that can be mathed out to see which is best (balancing that comes later).
