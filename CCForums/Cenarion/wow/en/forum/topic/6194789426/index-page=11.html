A tall, well dressed blood elf with long auburn hair was splayed out in a leather chair, his brooding gaze on the fire. It had been several weeks since he had hired someone to retrieve the woman who had called herself Calandre and he had yet to receive so much as an update. He was considering putting a bounty out on the woman who claimed that she could find his fair prize but had yet to produce her.

A soft knock came on the door of the library.

The man stirred in the chair, “What is it?”

The door opened and there was a soft footstep before one of his associates spoke, “We have word from your business associate. She has the woman in question.”

Eliran Falconsky jumped up from the chair quickly and turned towards the door. “Does she plan on bringing her here?”

“She said that it would be at the end of the week.” The location was detailed and Falconsky shook his head.

“Unacceptable,” he strode towards the door. “See that her room is prepared and call the stables. We will leave tonight. Send word to the Rose that we will collect Calandre at the border.”

“Of course, sir.”

Falconsky turned and looked at the chaise sitting empty by the fire. In his mind, he could already pictured the woman who would be reclined there, her chestnut brown hair gleaming in the firelight and her soft skin illuminated by the flames. He clenched one hand into a fist before walking down the hall, his retainer following.

There were quite a few things to be finished before her arrival and Falconsky wanted to ensure that everything was in place. Calandre had slipped away once; she was not going to do so again. He was more than eager to have her under his roof again, only this time she would understand that she was meant to stay.

He would ensure it.

Progress Report-to the Banshee Queen [b]

My Lady,
You chose well when you chose the Fellowship. They are an honorable people and have accepted me into their ranks. They are not without flaws that the Blood Elven race normally posess. But for the most part, they are an excellent group of people. Most of their members have been tollerant of me even though I am an outsider and I believe it should be noted that they have almot accepted me as one of their own. Dare I say, I've perhaps become friends with some of them.
As far as progress goes, the Fellowship has several noteworthy enemies. A warlock by the name Viragona and a crazed witch they call Ash. Their leader tells me that Viragona has necromantic powers. I have no doubt that you keep excellent records on Necromantic achievements and noteworthy users. Any records you can find about her would be most helpful. As far as this "Ash" goes she seems to be insane. She's kidnapped one of our members and has taken him to an unknown location south of the Undercity. If your scouts find anything, I would appreciate it if you could send word my way.
On a side note their is a Forsaken traitor amongst you. He goes by the name Rattlecage Joe. We have intel that he should be returning to the Undercity soon. I'd suggest having the guards arrest him on sight and hold him for one of our members to come collect him.
My Lady, this group is growing quite powerful. Their members are headstrong, moral, and growing stronger by the day. I would humbly request a steady flow of gold from the Undercity, as well as some extra troops if you can spare them.
Sincerely, Alenthis Sunstrider

Night always seemed darker in the territory held by the Forsaken. The sky hung heavier in the sky and the stars didn’t shine as brightly. The ground seeped fog that curled upwards and laced itself around the limbs of the living and the ambient sounds of nocturnal animals was noticeably missing. The wind still blew; its caress rattling the dry foliage, making the unnerving sound of tiny clattering bones.

The woman with the brown hair was still tied up. She had made the trip with a knife at her throat, seated in front of a rusty looking forsaken who turned out to be an excellent horseman. The blonde blood elf rode on a hawkstrider, and looked suitably nervous for their meeting with whomever had ordered the purchase.

As they moved forward towards the border of the Silverpine Forest and the Arathi Highlands, a horse drawn carriage came into view. Outside the carriage five men stood, all of whom were armed. One had a wolf at his side. One spoke as they came into view.

“State your business!”

“I have the package for your employer. If he will show himself, I will hand her over,” the Rose replied.

The woman with the brown hair bristled at the thought of being called a package.

One of the men rapped on the carriage door before opening it. Out stepped an elegant looking man, his long auburn hair managing to gleam in what little moonlight there was. He was a strikingly handsome man, he also wore a very dangerous expression—a curious mix of disapproval and ownership.

“I see you kept your end of the bargain. Any reason why she needs a blade at her throat?” he spoke quietly, his voice carrying easily through the darkness.

The woman with the brown hair felt her knees go weak. Eliran Falconsky commissioned this? Why her? She couldn’t quite wrap her brain about it but one bright note was that if Tirastrian was still at the manor, he would help her get out.

“I needed to convince her to come,” the Rose said, malice hinting her tone. “She put up quite a fight.”

“Really,” it was a statement, not a question. Falconsky sounded as if he didn’t quite believe the words of the blonde woman. “Calandre, come here my dear.”

It was an order, not a request. The woman with the brown hair gave the wolf a surreptitious glance. She would not be able to outrun it when she had all her faculties working let alone now, where she was still fighting off the effects of the serums she had been given. Slowly she walked forward, schooling her expression to somewhere between disbelief and wariness.

She went through her instructions on Falconsky, given all those weeks ago. He liked to be in charge, she needed to be submissive. She would go along with this game until she and Tirastrian could speak and formulate a way out of this.

‘I’m, sorry, Sol,’ she thought as she came to a halt beside Falconsky and looked at the ground, ‘but if you are ever going to see me again, I need to do what I must to get through this.’

There was one amusing thing, she thought as the business was concluded and final payment arrangements were made. She’d get through this by pretending that Eliran was Solorin. It would certainly irk the powerful weapons trafficker to know that with every kiss, every touch, she was imaging herself with another man.

As the Rose turned to leave, the woman with the brown hair caught her attention. The dark look in the younger woman’s eye caused a shutter to run down the blonde woman’s spine. Of course, if she played her card right, she wouldn’t have to hunt the Rose down. She’d have Eliran take care of the matter for her. That might be right up his alley.

Soon she was bundled in the carriage, sandwiched between Eliran and another man. Across from them sat two more men. Riding shotgun was the teamster and the hunter with his wolf. She gave a small sigh and leaned back against the seat. While she waited for the inevitable questions, she thought about her answers and she peered through the darkness at her injured wrists.

Eliran remained quiet for a good thirty minutes before asking, “Why did you leave?”

“I went downstairs to get new wineglasses after you feel asleep,” she said, not looking at him. Across from her, she could see one of the men nod. “Then there was the fight and someone tried to grab me. I ran outside and hid. But, you had fallen asleep and well… I didn’t think that you were that interested in me so to spare you the trouble, I left.”

Not quite the truth, but it was close enough that he would probably buy it.

“But I did go through a lot of trouble for you,” he replied in an even tone that was laced with displeasure.

“I’m sorry,” she spoke in a quiet voice, biting her lower lip to keep her tone meek.

Eliran saw the gesture through the weak light filtering in through the carriage and patted her hand. He was rewarded with a sharp intake of breath. “It seems there was some miscommunication and… what is wrong?”

“My wrists are raw from where they bound me,” she clasped her hands in front of her.

“Why did they have to bind you?” his voice became suspiciously calm. “Didn’t you want to see me again?”

“They drugged me, knocked me out, told me that someone bought me but didn’t tell me who,” she said with a trace of venom in her tone. “All they had to do was tell me that you were looking for me and I would have come back.”

Again, another lie tied in with the truth.

“I see,” this time, his tone was lighter, more contemplative. She hoped that that was a good sign. “I will deal with the Rose later, for now, let us get you home and in your bed,” he reached out and placed a finger under her chin, tilting her head towards him. “No more miscommunication: you will stay with me.”

“Yes…”

“Yes, you will what?”

“Yes, I will stay with you, Eliran.”

His low, throaty chuckle filled the carriage.

This would take some planning and some careful maneuvering, she knew. The real danger was that if she was left in this situation long enough, she ran the risk of acquiring Stockholm syndrome. She would have to ensure that it didn’t get that far.
