Verronia held her hand against her head, sitting behind her desk at home, and surrounded, as usual, by every manner of scroll and tome. Sitting in front of her though was a nondescript looking brown-covered book, the one for the recent audit.

Kyalin was soon to arrive after she had finished, looking around the organized mess with mild interest, scanning the floor to avoid stepping on a stray thumbtack or something. She stopped in front of the desk, looking at Verronia curiously. "Finished?"

There was an ungrateful sting from that word somehow, and the sideways quirk of Kyalin's head betrayed that she had picked up on the furrowed brow that the word had seemed to produce on Verronia's face. "I have, yes..."

Kyalin just looked at her expectantly.

Sighing, she continued. "Clean on the information I reviewed and clean on compliance. I couldn't find anything that suggested that he did anything wrong either."

"I noticed you've left out controls."

Verronia nodded. "Very poor, but I stepped up my substantive procedures to compensate. The person involved with parts of the custody functions is involved with the person who authorizes the contracts, and the bookkeeper receives the company's revenues."

Kyalin just stood there and stared at the mage. "So, what you're telling me is that they could sneak anything past and you wouldn't be able to tell."

Sighing again, Verronia nodded again. "Yes, the potential for collusion and the mixing of duties is enough to get anything past. An audit isn't designed to detect fraud, after all, and..."

"And it was completely fruitless." Kyalin finished.

"Kyalin..." Verronia started.

Kyalin shook her head, holding her hand up. "It isn't your fault. I've learned enough from you to know that." Her eyes followed some old articles and pronouncements tacked to the wall that Verronia had picked up during her extensive time in Stormwind, detailing years of news and important documents when she learned the trade of a bookkeeper in the human city before the Cataclysm. "I did find something though just the other day: him speaking with another individual about trying to find contacts within the Horde, which corroborated something that a... new friend of mine had said about him playing both sides."

Verronia leaned back in her chair, gesturing with both hands forward, the way that her old boss used to. "Well, there you go, you've got him."

Kyalin slowly nodded, angling her head downward afterward. "I know. I just wanted your opinion on it."

Verronia's previously raised eyebrows dropped, giving way to an attentive, yet unsure expression. "You're not going to act on this, are you?"

Kyalin straightened her posture and stared at Verronia again, her mood as unreadable as usual behind that steel mask. "I'll use my discretion."
