Coughing echoed through the under-dwellings of the building. In her excitement, Yuuko had tried to drink too quickly. Dellis patiently waited until she recovered and coaxed the glass up to her lips. She drank more slowly this time, letting the cool water flow into her. She hadn't expected Dellis to come into her cell nor did she remember when he arrived, all trace of time had vanished.

No words were exchanged as she ate, Dellis waited until she had drank at least three glasses before he stood, taking the tray with him. As silently as he came, he left, locking the cell door and reestablishing the wards to the upward leading stairs.

Hearing footstep coming down the stairs, Yuuko turned hiding her excitement. It could only be Dellis, she was starting to enjoy his company for he was the only person she ever saw. Her heart quickly sank as Trenetir came into view, she turned away.

Trenetir tapped on the door idly with his ax, the soft 'tink tink' of metal upon metal ringing out through the dungeons, "My my, you -are- resilient aren't you?"

Yuuko turned to look at Trenetir, pulling her robe close around her "What do you want?"

Trenetir leaned on the bars grinning maliciously, "I want you to suffer. I want you to die, and so you shall in time. He suffers now because of you."

Yuuko stared at Trenetir "I don't know what you are talking about"

Trenetir replied “There is no point in playing coy Yuuko. He is paying for his failures, just as you are. I never thought that he would turn on me, but that was twice... once for Belore'alah and once for you, and frankly, a man who is not loyal is not a man.”

Yuuko rushed the bars, her eyes blazing "I saved your life!"

Trenetir stepped back from the bars and grinned at her, "And yet you did not have the sense to save your own, to follow orders."

Yuuko retorted “And now you stand here and gloat. I live you bastard, you failed!”

Trenetir smirked, "You really should be thanking me. I am going to keep true to my word. Dellis is banished, and most likely dead by now, and you... you will not be rotting in my dungeon."

Yuuko looked confused at both statements, "What do you mean?"

Trenetir answered “Do you think that I am stupid? That I would not find out that he was feeding you after I ordered you dead? You were a fool to accept his help for now he dies.”

Yuuko exclaimed “Leave him alone, he is much more of a man then you will ever be!”

Trenetir laughed, "It is already done Yuuko. Just as your fate is already sealed."

Yuuko looked around her cell “My fate! Have not you done enough?”

Trenetir 's malicious grin did not go anywhere. "Oh I have ensured that you will live Yuuko, you should thank me. But the quality of your life.. that is another story."

Yuuko shook her head glaring at Trenetir "I will skin you alive!"

Trenetir pulled out a flask and drank from it, "And how do you expect to be able to do that?"

Yuuko leaned into the bars stretching out her arm trying to grab Trenetir, she hissed “I will kill you!”

Trenetir merely shrugged “Hardly”

Trenetir heard the footsteps and looked to the Orc, he had put aside his distaste for the race for this occasion.

Yuuko turned quickly to the noise

Gar'mak gazed about the dungeon from beneath her dark hood. She affixed her glare on the pair of blood elves.

Trenetir clapped his hands together his grin taking over all of his face now, "Wonderful, your new master is here."

Trenetir beckoned Gar'mak over.

Yuuko turned to Trenetir raising her voice "What is this!" as she backed away from the bars.

Trenetir spoke calmly “Gar'mak this is Yuuko, your new slave. As we agreed I will accept no less than” he paused watching Gar'mak.

Gar'mak stalked near to the pair. Brooding she examined the prisoner.

Trenetir sneered at Yuuko as he named the price, "5 copper, that is all that she is worth for she is disloyal and unable to follow even the simplest of orders." He continued enjoying himself “You'll have to beat her often if you want any real work out of her.”

Yuuko's jaw dropped "Slave! I am no slave! I am a citizen of Silvermoon and the Horde!"

Gar'mak glared at the prisoner. "SILENCE!" she howled. "You will speak when spoken to, Maggot." She turned to Trenetir “This... specimen.. Is hardly worth my time, Trenetir.”, she refrained from spiting the name.

Trenetir replied shrewdly “Yes, well a deal -is- a deal isn't it? You're here, she's here. Unless you're not a woman of your word, which wouldn't be surprising given your nature.

Yuuko glared at the orc "I will speak when I damn well please thank you"

Gar'mak examined Yuuko through the bars, then turned to Trenetir, holding out her had “Key”

Trenetir pulled out the key, "This will release the wards as well." He held out his hand for money, "Payment first."

Yuuko shook her head at Trenetir "You bastard. I am not your property!"

Gar'mak growled deeply. She produced a small sack, inside two silver. "Consider the extra a bonus. I'm about to make a mess of your pretty dungeon." She glared through the bars.

Trenetir opened the pouch and examined its contents. He handed the orc the key, "From this moment on she is yours, you are responsible for her actions."

Yuuko shook her head "No, this isn't happening” she continued with a raised voice “You don't realize what you are doing!

Gar'mak held the key tightly in a balled fist. She pulled her hood and dropped her robes beside her revealing a lean hard body, covered with a map of scars.

Yuuko’s eyes rolled up as her body went limp, her breathing stopped.

Darkness surrounded Yuuko, there was no light only fleeting shadows of men and women she could almost recognize before they vanished, it was cold, and a heavy burden felt as if it was pressing down upon her shoulders. She saw other images, her childhood, smiling and laughing, the years progressed quickly. Soon she saw herself training as a paladin, moving quickly up the ranks. She saw her fall, where the Sun Well no longer answered her, causing a slow progression into darkness. Her life as a performer quickly flashed past, a life she wished she never turned away from. The images now became darker, faster; they were her years as a hired sword, the money, power and manipulation all swirling around in a dark formless fog. Faces appeared, they were of the dead, faces she all knew a recognized, faces she’d struck down by her own hand. They beckoned to her to follow, she had no choice; she felt herself being led on. Heat began to build on her shoulder; she turned to see in the distance the light of the Sun Well. She yearned to stretch out a hand to it, but she was slowly being pulled away, into nothing, the light began to dwindle. Howling began to come from everywhere. She was terrified she wanted to scream then…
