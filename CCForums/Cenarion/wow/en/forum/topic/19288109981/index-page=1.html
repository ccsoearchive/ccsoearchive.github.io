“The man of the hour, the man with the power. I am the hit-maker, the record-breaker. I got style and grace, a pretty face. I’ll make your back crack, your liver quiver. If you ain’t into this match, you’re at the wrong address. Superstar, when the other wrestlers are smilin’ and jokin, The Dream be, WOO!, cookin’ and smokin’.” - Dusty Rhodes

If you haven't figured it out by now, I'm going to start the ad by telling you that this is a pro-wrestling RP guild. If that doesn't appeal to you because wrestling's fake, that's okay. Because RP is fake too, and we're going to capitalize on that.

What makes wrestling interesting for people once they figure out its staged and they figure out how 'kayfabe' works? I for one enjoy watching well-choreographed matches and interesting story lines unfolding through the medium of incredibly athletic men and women slamming each other into the floor. The energy and the personality that characters like Macho Man Randy Savage, The American Dream Dusty Rhodes, and The Nature Boy Ric Flair bring to the table can be captivating and inspiring, and I feel like they'd be right at home in the roleplaying community.

Now, a few details about the guild itself. It's called BRAWL, the Burning Rage Azerothian Wrestling League. Over the top, flashy, non-stop action is the name of the game and we're going to perform it. I've been a long time member of other performance guilds and one of the hardest things was making an audience actually pay attention to an act. I feel like the amount of effort put in by the performers was often times inversely proportional to the amount of enjoyment a crowd got from it. So why not try to keep them engaged and entertained constantly?

I'm using this thread as an ad for the performances right now, but frankly, I need people recruited now too. I want long-time wrestling fans and new fans alike, people who might just be getting back into it and people who are exhausted from it. I want people who have been RPing their whole lives and I want people who want to make this their first big foray into the RP world. I want to build a casual, friendly community of people who just enjoy goofy, fun RP.

I'm still developing some basic ideas, but here's a short list of things to expect from us:

-4 Weight Classes, Heavy (Plate), Cruiser (Mail), Medium (Leather), and Light (Cloth). This way, we have warrior wrestling paladins and priests wrestling mages and not death knights breaking druids in half.

-Inter-gender and inter-race matches. No restrictions will be placed on this. I feel like the inter-gender matches of some promotions can be amazing and right now, women's wrestling is better than it has ever been before. Additionally, while it might seem silly to have goblins wrestling trolls or undead wrestling tauren, I think we can get some very interesting match-ups out of that too. It's all in the way the wrestlers work.

-No Magic. Well, no magic legally. That doesn't stop a heel from cheating and lighting their opponent on fire or secretly healing themselves while the referee is distracted.

-No Weapons. Same as above, if your villain decides to conceal a dagger in their boot and slashes their opponent up, or hides a sledgehammer somewhere under the ring...Well, they're the villain, right?

-No Armor. Ring gear should look fun and flashy, and something your character could conceivably run around in. That doesn't mean we can't have cosmetic armor, (AND BELTS, OH BOY THE MASSIVE BELTS ARE PERFECT FOR US) but be creative and don't just use your transmog!

-Have fun. Don't take yourself too serious, because nobody else is going to take it seriously if you do. This is a game that most of us play in our leisure time, and RPing is an even smaller subset of people trying to have fun. That doesn't mean we can't have engaging, serious storylines, but at the end of the day, it's not just RP, it's not just wrestling, it's RP WRESTLING.

Anyway, I've gone on for a while now, but I do hope I've caught some people's attentions. I need wrestlers, promoters, managers, referees, announcers, commentators, and interviewers. We'll work with you to establish your gimmick inside and outside of character! Please don't be afraid to message me in-game or send me a letter.

TL;DR Dumb kid thinks wrestling is real, wants to start a dumb RP guild about how real wrestling is

Heya, thank you very much for the great questions! I'll do my best to clarify them for you.

1. Fights are not pre-planned in their entirety. The wrestlers plan their 'spots' ahead of time, which is to say, a sequence of events they perform in order to get them from one part of the ring to another and then to gauge the crowd's reaction. If we're looking to convey a particular emotion, a spot might change or the order of spots called by the wrestlers to each other might change as well. Ultimately, most outcomes are pre-determined but we're supposed to do our best to make them look like anyone could win.

2. The fights are decided ahead of time by a number of factors. The wrestlers performing are probably the most key part. One of them has to be willing to make the other person 'go over' them. The guild as a whole will probably workshop who should have title belts and who deserves a push the most to bring them to the top. As with real wrestling though, egos might get bruised by this, but I still think it's fun! Also, unfortunately, I'm the General Manager! I have recruited a Paul Heyman-type goblin though, for promotion and management of specific wrestlers. Organizations within organizations!

3. I kind of covered the first bit in 2 but you bring up a good point. I've scouted out some locations with a few others and we've got specific kinds of fights in mind, because gimmick fights are great. We'll be moving and specific characters will be using abilities in beneficial ways (either just visually like Heroic Leap or if a heel is cheating, by using blink or something like that). We want to provide as much ring choreography as possible with it. Monks are especially good with their kicks and rolls!

4. Since we're not doing /roll, we don't really have to do that, but in our matches it would look a little weird for a goblin to go over a tauren in a match clean, wouldn't it? Expect lots and lots of cheating!

5. I am not preventing anyone from betting on these matches, but just like betting on professional wrestling, you're not backing an athlete's ability. You're backing a writer. I would never bet on wrestling, RP or pro. However, if a particular fight seems 'rigged' that has happened a few times in pro wrestling. It might be interesting to have our own Montreal Screwjob! As for bouncers, it might just be the wrestlers who aren't currently wrestling. I don't want to over saturate the guild with non-performance roles.

I hope that's cleared some stuff up, but thank you for the suggestions! I am hoping we find those people who have the charisma and the ability to get others interested!
