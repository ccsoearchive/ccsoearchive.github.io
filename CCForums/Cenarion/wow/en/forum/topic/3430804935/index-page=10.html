He didn't say up to par. He did say that it wasn't that bad and that it was actually pretty good.

I won't touch on the theorycrafted differences between hunter specs. I'm not familiar enough with them to do that.

GC did have a solid point though when he mentioned the self-fulfilling prophecy. When everyone says something is horrible, nobody plays it except those that do so poorly, thus skewing the results you see on World of Logs. It doesn't mean BM is good (or any other spec that shows poorly on logs), it means that you need to be careful in the magnitude of the conclusion you draw.

All that said, I did wince a bit when GC said that. I knew exactly what the response would be.


He said this prior the recent attack power buff to Aspect of the Hawk, which has had a significant impact.

There is a fundamental problem with accepting that a spec's dps is "pretty good", when it should be "competitive". Unless you're taking the spec for a raid buff, you're hindering your group. The less dps you do, the more strain you put on your healers and tanks and other dps. I'm not interested in heroic progression, but it appears the vast majority of players even struggle with normal progression, as is made clear by the progressive DS nerfs.

While a player is completely justified in saying "It's my money, I'll play how I want", that doesn't really carry over well into group play. In group play, if your group is failing and you're intentionally not performing up to your class' potential, you're wasting the time of at least 9 other people. So, it's not so much GC's bullsh!t excuse of "self-fulfilling prophecy" so much as it is "It's unreasonable to expect players to play a spec that under-performs in meaningful content".

Theoretical Patchwerk DPS means nothing when the mechanics of your class spec fail in some way on almost every other encounter.
