Ghostcrawler was right in one aspect. BM dps is pretty good. Not as good as marks, but not a huge gap in pure numbers.

However, he then went on to say in simplified terms that all the good players play marks because that is what they are told to. BM is fine, but noone plays it so how would anyone know?

This statement is grotesquely incorrect. There is a reason the good players don't play BM. It is because BM cannot put out the damage, the utility, and the flexibility of a MM spec. On any fight that is not a pure stand and burn BM falls behind drastically because of Pet AI and damage uptime on said pet.

    - If I need to target switch a lot, I will not play BM. Each switch I can only swap half of my damage while my pet travels to the new target. As Marks the pet is a much more minor role in your overall damage and it doesn't matter as much.
    - Marks also has better AoE. It doesn't have to use a Cunning or Tenacity pet to do so. It can AoE on the move efficiently.
    - Marks also scales with weapon damage and comes out on top with pure DPS.
    - Pets are also not casting their focus dump every 3 seconds and instead it is getting delayed by .2-.3 seconds or more after it comes off CD. This hurts BM dps even more.

The only thing to make BM viable would to give it a MASSIVE numbers buff so that it is semi-viable to use it on anything that isn't Patchwerk-esque, or fix the mechanics of the spec to increase pet uptime and AI. Let's not even get into Survival and its problems.

This is why "pro" raiders play Marks. Not because they like it, but because it is more reliable. The fact that he cannot see that seriously brings into question his ability to balance this game.

PS: I am also terribly disappointed with the 4 hunters who asked questions at Blizzcon. Two of them revolved around appearance or inconsequential !@#$, one was about CC which was promptly dodged by the panel, and the last one was a pokemon question that just happened to trail off into a BM damage answer because Ghostcrawler felt like it needed to be said.

There was no question of Hunters in PvP, Gregg staking his reputation on fixing hunters and failing, Hunter scaling issues, etc. HELL WHERE THE HELL WAS THE QUESTIONS REGARDING COUNTER ATTACK AND IMMOLATION TRAP. I bet half of all hunters don't even know what those two spells are. No, it was about minor things, that while nice to hear, are NOT what I or many others wanted to hear asked. We wanted proof that we will still be a relevant class come 4.3 and even 5.0. Not just the bastard step-son who is only there to play Pokemon Master.

Someone has no clue how the specs work.

Beast Mastery: 56% of your DPS does not scale with weapon damage at all. The few shots that benefit from weapon damage are also increased more from RAP than weapon damage.

Marksmanship: Only 15% of your DPS does not scale from weapon damage.

Survival: 60% of your DPS does not scale from weapon damage.

Now the issue here is that in order for pure RAP scaling to work, they need drastic scaling overhauls which has a chance to be brokenly powerful as AP coefficients scale exponentially and not linear. MM with everything but the pet scaling from weapon damage keeps it at a more linear scaling progression. Granted, MM scaling is still bad, but that's a fault of Hunter class design.

Weapon scaling also double dips from RAP as well as they nto only have RAP scaling coefficients, but RAP and buffs also increase the weapon damage part of the equation. By making shots like Explosive Shot scale from weapon damage, it can give Survival more linear based advancement instead of starting up great then nosediving because armor upgrades (RAP scaling) cannot keep up with weapon based upgrades who advance with both weapons AND armor/stat increases.

Look at Unholy and Frost for example with Unholy's scaling keeping it constantly behind Frost. Unholy = Survival/BM, Frost = MM in the way they're modifed by weapons and armor. Slapping on more AP through their specialization isn't going to magically fix their poor scaling as seen in 4.2 when they buffed Unholy's bonus by 15% and it's still doing bad.

At this point, all we can hope for is a baidaid (which I'm hoping Surv and BM get more of) but weapon based scaling is something that needs to be looked into for MoP or we'll just repeat this exact same nosedive for a 3rd expansion.
