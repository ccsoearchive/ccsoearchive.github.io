GC did have a solid point though when he mentioned the self-fulfilling prophecy. When everyone says something is horrible, nobody plays it except those that do so poorly, thus skewing the results you see on World of Logs.


I call BS on this though. Hunters like Kruf TRY all the specs on fights. Even in T12 where BM was crap, Paragon, the people who brought us the first heroic clear of T12, tried BM specs on heroic. These people don't give a crap what the common concussion is. I mean, fire was universally laughable and would have been booted from even a casual run, but SOMEONE had to try it on Aly to realize it was insane on that fight.

People copy what the best guilds do. The best guilds don't give a crap what the other 99% of the pop believes, they try damn near everything to down a boss. Everyone else copies them. If BM was crap in general and had one fight (like fire had aly) where it owned, say adds had to be bursted down ever min and a half, then top guilds would use it regardless of what joe shmoe the forum monkey spouted about it's dps. If it worked we'd know. Those people basically playtest everything in practice with the highest skill levels in the game.

If BM could top other hunter specs (even on one fight) those guilds would be running it and the rest of us would be going O.o zomg when did BM get teeth? And then people would start running it on fights it was great on. But there aren't any fights like that. So, thanks to the top 1%, there really is no self fulfilling prophecy. The 1% tried it, something else was always better and made chance of downing a boss that much better.

Can you run BM in a casual guild that doesn't care about min./maxing or wiping? Sure, but you are still going to hold the group back. If they don't care then *shrug* it doesn't really matter. But for those who do, BM is not the way to go. It's been proven in practice by those who don't care what the general pop believe.
