Yes, if you define being one of the 3 worst dps specs on H-Rag (by any metric) as "well enough".


Obviously I don't, because raids don’t succeed or fail based on relative dps. I define it as being capable of getting the job done, whatever job that happens to be. What I want is for people to pay attention to what the actual conversation was, so that they realize that the balance implications aren’t what they think they are. I’m convinced that a good number of the people posting in this thread didn’t actually follow the link to the video.

A player first thanked Blizzard for the taming challenges introduced in patch 4.2. (if that’s not a clue that the conversation isn’t going to be about heroic mode dps then I don’t know what is). Then that player said:

“We’re really proud of all the pets that we’ve tamed, particularly the rare exotics. So it seems sort of a shame that we can’t bring them into a dungeon or raid when we switch to our higher dps specs … I can kinda maybe sorta see it in PvP, but when you’re going up against an NPC in a raid or dungeon, why can’t I have my spirit pet [sic] with me when I’m in Marks or Survival spec?”


It’s pretty clear this is a “normal mode” kind of question. The question even references dungeons, which are even easier than normal mode raids. So GC first said that they will probably let hunters chose any pet tree for their pets, which will help with some pet issues, although he acknowledged that didn’t answer the question. So then he said, “ideally the solution is for BM dps to be high enough so that you wouldn’t have to switch and lose that pet.”

That is exactly what everyone in this thread wants, even if they are talking about heroic modes instead of troll dungeons. But for some reason, we’re focusing on the third thing he said, which was meant to reassure that player that it’s probably okay to take that “spirit pet” they were so proud of into a freaking dungeon or LFR raid and do “pretty good”. It’s worth noting that that’s basically what you said too -- that at the “normal” level, skill matters more than the maximum dps capability of a spec.

So no, I’m not shifting the goalposts. I’m asking you guys to stop shifting them.
