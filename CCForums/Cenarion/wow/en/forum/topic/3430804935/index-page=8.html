It depends on how you break down the data for one.

If you're talking simcraft, unless Blizz somehow withholds shot coeficients, then the data is the same as whats available 3rd party within a very small margin. The problem with sims is they don't account for external factors like movement latency etc. However they do give accurate info for a specs potential.

Then you have logged information and statisical breakdowns which can be endlessly debated for everything from sample size to acceptable ranges of variation.


BM falls into a difficult spot, because it sims out as being quite strong in a single target , target dummy encounter, but is subject to severe drop offs with the most basic additional mechanics.

So in essense BM could be buffed to a point significantly higher than MM, but would still be outperformed due to it poor mechanical flexibilty.

Also, Blizz track record to date, atleast in regard to hunters, has offered no evidence of them having a greater understanding of the potentials of this class than the theorycrafting community, whose predictive analysis has been born out again and again.

This would be the case if people were arguing over data in the first place. The issue isn't over what the data are, but how to characterize them. If, for example, Blizzard released data showing that BM hunters averaged 20k dps in normal FL, which is more than the average needed to complete the content and therefore why they said it was "pretty good", then people still would take issue if, for example, MM hunters averaged 25k. It's about different priorities. Blizzard wants to ensure that players have the tools to complete the content. But players don't just want adequate tools, they want the best ones. It's human nature to focus on how what you have falls short of what someone else has instead on whether what you have is "enough."
