Just managed to do Lurker in SSC, pretty easy honestly, should have tried sooner. Like other bosses, he drops 250g and some epics. Some main points would be,

1) Stand just inside the water where he is, while still on the wooden platform so you don't aggro the frenzy. Getting closer to him like this means you won't be knocked off the platform when he Whirls, you'll just land on the platform and you can run back into position.

2) When he does spout, simply keep behind him and continue to DPS. Should you be caught in a bad position, it is cloakable, or you can use a teleport like Killing Spree to save yourself. The main reason you don't want to get hit by this is it knocks you 100yds away, often landing you in the water. You waste a lot of time swimming back and getting eaten by frenzy, and he'll be spamming 10k Frostbolts at you all the way.

3) Periodically he will submerge and spawn 3 sets of adds, 2 ranged and 1 melee each. You want to kill them asap so you can get back into position when Lurker surfaces again. If you're late though, he'll usually start off with a Spout so you can cloak through it on your way back to the platform.

PS: Glyph of Blurred Speed highly recommended.

Is it possible to solo Skadi the Ruthless to farm the drake mount?


Yes. I blow a Potion of the Tol'vir at the start, sprint down and collect all the gauntlet mobs, Blade Flurry/Killing Spree and burn the rest down. After that you take it slower, making your way up to the end and collecting harpoons. Then wait until your CDs are up and bring Skadi down.

(I prefer waiting until there ISN'T a Witch Doctor up as they will range snipe you for major damage comparatively).

Once Skadi is down Disarm and start burning. At the first WW feint and sprint down the gauntlet back to the start and bandage. Run back to him or cloak off his spear and you should be able to get him down after that.

Do note, for Skadi I run with my PvP gear to be uncrittable. Back in Wrath I solo'd bosses in MC, ZG and Kara for mounts and legendaries, but would use a mix of PvP, PvE and tank gear. As I down more content and have more Valor/Conquest to spare I'll probably make up a new tank set.


I'm going to attempt duo/trioing BT and Sunwell this week, maybe even Ony/VoA/Maly 10 for mounts. We'll see.

ZA is pretty simple, and I did this with an alternated assassination build (for pve/soloing, it has imp recuperate and deadened nerves instead of a dps points)

The Lynx Boss is difficult, he can be disarmed when in troll form. When I first did this, I got him to 1 HP, and he didn't die. After I took a bit longer and killed the lynx spirit while it was out, which may or may not have been necessary, either way, it worked. He plops down a totem that casts chain lightening and has fairly low hp. Kill it if you are having trouble staying up, this would be trivial with BF.

Final boss is really annoying in bird form, otherwise he is cake. In eagle-mode, he does nothing and a tornado follows you, pushing you back, and doing a little damage. For combat, make sure KS is up for this phase, as getting quality time on the boss is hard. Removing SnD from the rotation (after KS) wouldn't be a bad idea either, since time on the boss is so low as it is that most of the buff (and energy from procs) is wasted.

Hope this helps,

Though Razorgore isn't soloable (I got my shammy friend to 2 man it with me), the rest of the instance up to and including all trash and Nefarian is certainly soloable. Some notable fights,

Vaelestraz: I'm not 100% sure it's soloable, as I was still 2 manning it at this point, but it's just a matter of putting out enough DPS before he decides to Burning Adrenaline you. Kind of a moot point anyway since you need two people for Razorgore.

Ebonroc: His debuff which heals him actually heals him up pretty quickly, faster than Kazzak's Twisted Reflection. At my current level of gear, my sustained average DPS was able to keep his health roughly neutral against his healing.

Everytime I pop a defensive cd to mitigate the debuff (cloak/evasion) or ramp up my damage with an offensive cd (AR/KS) was when I was able to get his health going negative. Eventually you will kill him, doesn't take too long and you're in no danger of dying, it's just a DPS check.

Chromaggus: Another DPS race, once you get 5 stacks of brood affliction I presume you get MCed and he will reset. Clear bronze affliction with hourglass sand, and cloak once you get dotted up with the remaining four (red/blue/green/black). Again, at my current level of gear I was able to kill him in a comfortable margin of time. Take too long and you will get dotted up to 5 stacks again before your cloak comes off cd for a second use.

Everything else is easy.
