Just soloed Zombiefest for my Reins of the Red Proto-Drake. It was a little hit-or-miss and it would have been really easy to screw up, but it worked. It's just the regular strategy, clear up through the inn, then go back and collect all the zombies (kept combat readiness stacked for most of this, cloak glyph also helped). I couldn't make it through the inn though, the zombies wore me down too much. So I collected all I could and FoK'd them down right in front of the inn (popped evasion here just to be safe), then sprinted (glyphed, imperative) through to the other side and completed the achievement with about 3 seconds left on the timer. Damn that was a sweet adrenaline rush.

PS: A friend told me that if you take the second boss to the inn and tank it for 4-6 minutes over 100 zombies will spawn in the first part of the city, which would make this incredibly easy to pull off. I'm 99% sure this is correct since there were 99 zombies in front of the inn for me, it's possible I even just missed one in a corner somewhere.
The Lynx Boss is difficult, he can be disarmed when in troll form. When I first did this, I got him to 1 HP, and he didn't die. After I took a bit longer and killed the lynx spirit while it was out, which may or may not have been necessary, either way, it worked. He plops down a totem that casts chain lightening and has fairly low hp. Kill it if you are having trouble staying up, this would be trivial with BF.
Lynx and Bear were the hardest, but Lynx was easy as soon as I figured out that you can ignore the add and burn the boss through the phases (reducing either one to 20% induces the next phase). I'm not sure why he got stuck at 1HP for you though, didn't run into that. The bear was just a crazy burn, think I was flasked and used a tol'vir potion to take him down. Final boss was easy, glad I finally got that achievement.

Also tried Hyjal Summit, but I got my face bashed in. Maybe in another tier...

// edit //

Used a 31/2/8 assassination build for all of the above, with 3/3 deadened nerves in place of 2/2 murderous intent and 1/2 deadly brew in the assassination tree, and 2/2 improved recuperate in place of 2/3 precision in the combat tree. I used the vendetta prime glyph instead of backstab, and the cloak/evasion/feint major glyphs usually (swapped feint for sprint above).

The actual gear I use is pretty much troll dungeon drops, valor gear, bs dagger, and some rep stuff from firelands (I haven't really been raiding this expansion...). I ended the fights pretty low on health, so I'm not sure that it could be done with lesser gear. On the other hand, I also did this in the general mut PvE spec; it might be doable in lower gear if someone did it in one of the suggested combat specs.

For cd's, I did evasion and vendetta right at the beginning and made sure to kick phantom blast as many times as possible. If he did mirrored soul, I'd just stop autoattacking. I'd say drop target alltogether, but it's still good to keep him targeted for interrupts.

When he begins doing wailing souls, immediately bandage. The boss can't do anything while he's casting it, and it will take the ghosts a few seconds to reach you. Pop combat readiness once they do hit you. The reason I suggest this instead of evasion is that you'd have to reposition the boss and all of the ghosts to make sure that you're not getting hit from behind by them. Since there are a lot of ghosts and the boss will resume casting phantom blast soon, I found it simpler to just stay in one place with combat readiness running.

I'd also recommend trying to get as big a recoup off during this phase as you can. A healing pot is also helpful here.

Beyond that, it's just a matter of interrupting, not hitting it too hard when mirror is up, and maintaining reocuperation.

The boss never got a second wailing souls off; at best, it might use the laser ability.

I suppose you could try bringing the boss to a corner / wall and seeing if that prevents the ghosts from getting behind you. This would allow for a single blood mirror to be cloaked and for evasion to work on the ghosts. I personally didn't try this, but it'd be worth a try.
