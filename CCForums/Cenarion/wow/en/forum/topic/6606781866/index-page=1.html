Litonya, there's been a recent flare-up of drama (and I'll own my part for participating in it when the more mature approach would have been to likely just remain silent; I blame my "crusader" personality type IRL) that's unfortunately spilled over into a resource the Alliance RPers use to coordinate our activities and give like-minded folks a place to chat without excessive involvement from parties uninterested in RP. Our realm, being on the larger (and older! This realm launched with WoW) side, has a fair-sized non-RPer population, and as a result, Imperon, the guild leader for Lluchduu Ocheliad, started the AllianceOOC channel way back when, as a place for the RPers to hang out. Typically speaking, we welcome everyone in that channel, whether they're actually an RPer or not, as long as they don't actively antagonize RP. :)

The recent flare-ups aside, this realm is awesome enough that, despite the larger RP population on Wyrmrest Accord, I chose to return here after Wrath was done. I first rolled here back in 2005, was Horde all through Burning Crusade, took two years off in Wrath (because we had so many people that we had login queues; free transfers were being offered to Wyrmrest Accord, so I availed myself of them), and returned shortly before Cataclysm launched (and then immediately race-changed to a worgen at its launch).

Why? Because Cenarion Circle is home. Some 7 years after I started playing, the people are what keep me logging in these days; Guild Wars 2 is honestly as fun from a game-play perspective, but the people on Cenarion Circle insure I can't stay gone for long, and to be honest, even after 7 years of playing, WoW is still fun as a game for me, and I'm excited to get to 90th level in a couple weeks.

I don't always like everyone I encounter, which is fairly normal with any group of people that gather. But I can say I like more people I encounter than not, and with the unique personalities RP can sometimes attract, this is honestly a meaningful and pleasantly surprising statistic.

Cenarion Circle is a great place to be, for event-based RP, for creative storytelling, for epic storytelling (some of our really large events have a good deal of behind-the-scenes cooperation from both factions).

Don't let the recent forum squabbling stop you from coming over to check us out. There are some awesome, friendly and fun players on both Alliance and Horde here, and we'd love to have you join one (or both!) of us to enjoy what Cenarion Circle has to offer.
