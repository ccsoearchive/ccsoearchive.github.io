I also think you're overstating how much someone might throttle PW:S. I don't immediately discount putting a shield on a DPS or tank because it'll probably break 1 or 2 seconds before the Rapture ICD is up. I may prioritize a Greater Heal on them before I actually bubble though, as a GHeal and PW:S will amount to more or less the same effective healing regardless of the order you cast them in, but casting GHeal first might shave that final ~2 seconds off Rapture and allow you to get the proc earlier, which in turn increases the number of possible procs you can receive for the rest of the encounter encounter.


Let's say there's a 5% chance that the shield will break within the first second after you cast it and you're not going to be able to cast PW:S again on that target for another 15 sec. Now ask yourself what happens if you cast PW:S one second before Rapture's ICD expires instead of after it expires.

5% of the time, you'll lose 15 seconds (because your shield will break before the ICD and you'll have to wait to recast). 95% of the time, you'll gain one second. So this strategy will reduce the interval between your Rapture procs by 0.2 sec over your wait-for-expiration strategy.

Now, those were completely arbitrary numbers. But if you don't know those numbers for your situation, then you don't know if your approach is right.

What your overlooking is that while you might lose some time due a quick shield break, you're definitely losing some time by delaying. Ultimately it boils down to the simple truth that you can only get a Rapture proc from casting PW:S, so pursuing a strategy where you deliberately cast fewer PW:S than you might otherwise is unlikely to yield better results.


P1 H Rag on 10m while raid healing. Here is what you know....

1. The boss will break a shield if he lands a hit (short of some combination of alternative sources of mitigation, like block, and a large tank CD).

2. You have two reliable sources of Rapture, traps and tanks.

Keep in mind, there are 45 second intervals between traps being hit. Meaning, your only source of Rapture is the tanks for large parts of this phase (unless you count Wrath "guessing".....). You are also running an SoS build on this particular boss, because Atonement is useless on the fight.

Here is a hypothetical. Let's say you threw a shield up on the boss tank at the time, a tank swap had just recently occurred, the shield breaks instantly, the trap debuff has 30 seconds remaining, and you follow up the shield with two GH casts. WS has 8 seconds knocked off of it via SoS and you figure it took maybe 4-4.5ish seconds to cast those two greater heals. So, WS has around 2-3 seconds remaining and the Rapture ICD has around 7-8 seconds remaining.

In this situation you have two options. Re-apply a shield as soon as those 2-3 seconds elapse or wait for around 7-8 seconds. You would take option one. Personally, I would take option two.

If you take option one and the boss does break the shield instantly you are now "stuck" waiting for WS to be off that tank, or a tank swap to occur, to get another Rapture proc. If you take option two and the boss does break the shield instantly you really couldn't have gained a proc any quicker.

Should you prioritize Rapture like this if a player is in risk of death? Definitely not. The point is that without tracking the ICD on Rapture you would never be able to decide one way or the other.

And, forgive me, but why label the Rapture ICD useless information if you're going to admit to using an addon that tracks it? Furthermore, tracking the ICD isn't going to make you play any worse if you have a reasonable attention span.
