This im a little worried about you seem to think i have said i think haste is worth more than int which is NOT the case. First off id like to explain for some that may not realize what psuedo power (PP) is, it is basically an imaginary stat created to compare 2ndary stats in terms of a primary stat IE in this case int therefore int is set to a value of 1 and all other stats are represented on how much theoretical intellect they are worth.

Now to clarify previous statement lets look at some examples. Which would you rather have an increase in 1 intellect or an increase in 500 haste. The answer should be obvious 500 haste is a much greater increase in dps. What about 1 int or 100 haste; 1 int or 50 haste; 1 int 10 haste. In all these cases haste would be a greater increase in dps because the AMOUNT of haste outweights the INDIVIDUAL worth of a small int increase. Int will ALWAYS be what you want to stack, however the MORE int you have the BETTER other stats become. Beyond 6k intellect Haste passes a PP value of .5 meaning for every 1 haste you have it is the PP equivalence of having a little over .5 int. To restate as your intellect increases other stats become more and more valueable. Therefore looking at it mathematically gemming for + 10 int in SOME cases is def a slight increase in dps. So the only cases to match a + 10 int bonus is when the socket is a yellow gem (excluding epic gems, i dont socket non heroic bis gear with epics) so IF the socket is yellow the gem would be 20int/20 haste my last sim done about a month into 4.3 had values of haste being .5633 (given 4 set bonus i would assume this value might be a little lower but still def greater than .5) So 20haste*.5633 = 11.266 PP + 20 int + 10 int bonus = 41.266 PP > 40 PP int red gem. If the socket is anything but yellow 40 int would be better. This example obviously fails when socketed with a 50 int gem.

It's like this, and this is really simple, at certain gear levels 1 point of Haste becomes worth more then .5 Intelligence. Say you reach a point where 1 point of Haste is worth .55 Intelligence. Put another way, say 2 Haste is worth 1.1 Intelligence. In this situation you are better off going with 2 Haste, if possible, over 1 Intelligence. Why? Because that 2 Haste is worth 1.1 Intelligence, which is a larger value than 1 Intelligence.

Let's put the above analogy into real game terms. Say you have a +10 Int socket bonus on a yellow socket item. You have two choices here. You could take the "typical" advice and ignore this socket bonus, gem a brilliant inferno and end up with +40 Intelligence. As an alternative you could gem a +20 Int/+20 Haste gem, take the socket bonus and end up with +30 Int/+20 Haste.

If we're assuming 1 Haste is worth .55 Intelligence, or 2 Haste is worth 1.1 Intelligence we can assign values to these numbers from above. In the first case 40 Int is worth... 40 points. In the second case 30 Int is worth 30 points and 20 Haste is worth 11 points for a total of 41 points. Since 41 is greater in value then 40 we know in this specific situation it actually makes sense to take the socket bonus and gem the +20 Int/+20 Haste gem in the item.

In reality it doesn't make much of a difference. 30 Int/20 Haste or 40 Int aren't going to have that large of a difference in value between them. That doesn't change the fact that technically, in the above situation, 30 Int/20 Haste is the slightly better option. The only way you can tell in-game whether this is worth it is by simming your own character, with their own specific gear setup, and seeing the value for Haste it spits out (the sim values every stat in terms of Intelligence if I am not mistaken). Even that may not be 100 percent foolproof since sims aren't infallible.

And when it comes to H DS gear none of this even matters because the H DS gear either comes with all red sockets or a large enough socket bonus that makes it obvious to gem for it (2 pieces come without all red sockets, the tier robe and legs). For the gear available in N DS/N FL/H FL this decision does come up.

DIcaje is basically saying everything I said above but expanding on it to suggest players that care about gearing their characters should understand these types of concepts. You shouldn't run to a thread that tells you every decision you should make whenever you have a gearing choice. You should understand why it told you to do that in the first place so you can make these decisions yourself. The why of it is the important part.
