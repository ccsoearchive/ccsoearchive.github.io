First, Rapid Renewal. Keep in mind Renew is not the only spell effected by this talent. It reduces the GCD on the Renew spell. Correct me if I am wrong, but I believe this translates to "when I cast Renew, I am stuck waiting on the GCD for less time". This means that anything you cast following a Renew will take less time to "get out" if you have taken this talent. AKA, it effects any spell you cast immediately following Renew.

I do not believe Rapid Renewal to be an amazing, mandatory talent. I just think it's slightly incorrect to suggest the talent is exclusively useful for "renew blanketing".

I would advise against gemming specifically for Haste breakpoints. To make a long story short, Haste breakpoints are often over-valued for Shadow. Many people blindly assume DoT durations always remain at their base value. The issue is Blizzard fibbed when they said this was the case upon the release of Cataclysm.

If you're looking at a yellow socket with a +10 Int bonus it may make sense to gem for the socket with a Reckless Ember Topaz (so, +30 int/20 Haste instead of +40 Int) if it will push you over the break point, provided you have enough gear (that 6k-6.3k Int area). The thing is it would make sense to gem for the socket in that situation anyway (anyone considering this make sure to sim their character first). Sacrificing 40 Intelligence for 40 Haste, on the other hand, just for the sake of a break point never makes sense IMO.


I neither stated nor implied that someone should use a Quick Amberjewel at any time. I gave the acceptable gem choices.

In Sanctuary, 5- tick Renew with all talents/glyphs heals for 5 * 1.1 * 1.06 * 1.1 * 1.15 * 1224 = 10381 before stats. It costs 3150 after 3/3 MA. So it's 3.30 hpm before stats.

Heal with all talents heals for 1.15 * 1.15 * 1.06 * 3390 = 4752 with a cost of 1853 for 2.56 hpm before stats.

Renew also receives an additional 10% boost from Divine Touch. Heal will gain the benefit of Mastery. So with 20% Mastery, Renew is about 18% more efficient than Heal.

Note also that Renew is the highest hpct single target spell even without Rapid Renewal.

The only time I cast Renew is if I'm in Serenity and tank healing. I definitely don't put it on multiple people in a 5man with the intention of keeping it refreshed via Heal. That's what Prayer of Healing is for. And Lightwell. And PoM. In a raid setting it's even less useful. You will never see the full effect of a Renew unless you say, "Everyone stop healing Joe DPS, I have placed Renew on him, so he will be topped off in 10 seconds."


In a 5-man, it is enormously more efficient to heal secondary target damage by casting Renew and then tossing a direct heal 10 secs or so later than it is to just use direct heals. Indeed, if you're in Serenity Chakra, this method works better than PoH for anything but 5 fully damaged targets.

In terms of raids, consider the situation where players are damaged but you don't have the 4+ players in the same group damaged necessary to make casting PoH worthwhile. What are you going to cast? Circle of Healing might end up healing them, but its small size coupled with 10 sec cooldown means it's barely going to move their bar. Prayer of Mending can't help. Binding Heal requires the Priest themselves have damage. Holy Word: Sanctuary is horribly inefficient for such a task.

That leaves single target heals. You want to spend the least amount of time and mana addressing each player you're going to heal. Which means Renew, since it has the highest hpct/hpm of the single target heals.

11/23/2011 12:27 AMPosted by Volios
I don't see how knowing when Rapture is off CD is necessarily a bad thing though. It's much more convenient than "remember to shield the tank every 15 seconds".


How so? You're already tracking Weakened Soul on the tank (or should be). In contrast, the Rapture ICD is just superfluous information that you don't actually need to know.

More importantly, it encourages a bad style of play.

If you're waiting for the ICD to expire before recasting PW:S, the best you can ever do is 12 sec + average break time. This generally means in the 16 - 18 sec range.

The mistake most players make is in presuming that PW:S "instabreaks". But it doesn't. Indeed, it has a fairly low chance of breaking in the 1 - 3 sec range that many players seem to believe is the 'norm'. What this means in practical terms is that you'd actually do better if you recast PW:S a few seconds before the ICD was up - the amount you lose from when it breaks early tends to be smaller than the amount you gain by shaving those seconds off the interval.

But once you're anticipating the delay in breaking even a little bit, you're no longer really 'timing' it because you've run up against Weakened Soul.

Unless, of course, you're increasing the rate at which you can cast PW:S (either through SoS or Weakened Soul). In which case, timing against the ICD starts to become really foolish because the time you lose from anticipating the ICD and getting unlucky is based on the PW:S casting interval.

From a practical standpoint, just mindlessly casting PW:S whenever you can on the tank normally isn't going to perform all that much better than trying to time against ICD. But why would you waste your attention on trying to match the ICD when it just makes you perform slightly worse than simply casting PW:S as often as possible on the tank?

I also think you're overstating how much someone might throttle PW:S. I don't immediately discount putting a shield on a DPS or tank because it'll probably break 1 or 2 seconds before the Rapture ICD is up. I may prioritize a Greater Heal on them before I actually bubble though, as a GHeal and PW:S will amount to more or less the same effective healing regardless of the order you cast them in, but casting GHeal first might shave that final ~2 seconds off Rapture and allow you to get the proc earlier, which in turn increases the number of possible procs you can receive for the rest of the encounter encounter.


Let's say there's a 5% chance that the shield will break within the first second after you cast it and you're not going to be able to cast PW:S again on that target for another 15 sec. Now ask yourself what happens if you cast PW:S one second before Rapture's ICD expires instead of after it expires.

5% of the time, you'll lose 15 seconds (because your shield will break before the ICD and you'll have to wait to recast). 95% of the time, you'll gain one second. So this strategy will reduce the interval between your Rapture procs by 0.2 sec over your wait-for-expiration strategy.

Now, those were completely arbitrary numbers. But if you don't know those numbers for your situation, then you don't know if your approach is right.

What your overlooking is that while you might lose some time due a quick shield break, you're definitely losing some time by delaying. Ultimately it boils down to the simple truth that you can only get a Rapture proc from casting PW:S, so pursuing a strategy where you deliberately cast fewer PW:S than you might otherwise is unlikely to yield better results.
