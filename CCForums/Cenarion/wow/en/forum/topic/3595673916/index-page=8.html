@Maysie: Thanks. ^ ^

@Chayot:

Stick with Serenity + Inner Fire for now. Inner Will really doesn't come into play until you start using Sanctuary and your other (non-Serenity) instants heavily--which shouldn't be until you hit raiding content.

Your basic healing method should look something like this: (Serenity+Inner Fire); Renew on the tank (kept refreshed), HW:Serenity on cooldown (as necessary), Heal as filler.

You can juggle multiple Renews on different players by using Serenity and any other direct heal to keep it refreshed.

With heavier damage, switch up to Greater Heal. Try to use Flash Heal sparingly.

CoH is good to use for light damage, if you have the mana to spare. Otherwise, I would just wait until the damage warrants PoH.

In heavy group damage scenarios, keep PoM out, then PoH>CoH>PoH until PoM/CoH comes off cooldown. Don't forget to use Divine Hymn, either, even if the situation isn't truly dire. The three minute cooldown makes it fantastic even for trash.

@ Kal -

I'm sure Elethia will be by pretty soon to give tips I've missed, but for now, here are my 2 cents' worth.

Ultraxion - never PW:S except on the tank, as his WS falls off (for Rapture). That fight is *all* PoH-spam for us. At least, for Normal modes. Ultraxion is the "exception" fight this tier.

Zon - consider swapping out Glyph of Smite for Glyph of Dispel Magic. You really shouldn't be Smiting all that much (outside of specific moments in fights, like for example, you could Smite a ton when the oozes spawn & are headed towards Yon), and specifically for Zon, Glyph of Dispel Magic will actively make a difference.

You're running *very* low on Spirit. Not many Disc priests ran that low on Spirit in FL, and fewer are doing so now. That being said, ditch the purple gems in your shoulders and hands, and gem them full-on red; +10 Int socket bonus is not worth the +20 Spirit on the purple gem. You should get your gloves chanted with Mastery, and your boots with Lavawalker (for mastery *and* for the run-speed increase).

Keep in mind that Int trinket you're talking about is actually *not* good for AA/A Disc. It would only be somewhat beneficial if you're doing content you outgear (ie, aren't wiping on) and could Smite-spam. You have to dps a target once every 10sec to keep the stacks from falling off, but Holy Fire's cooldown is 10 sec so you would only have started casting your HF when the stack from the previous HF fell off. Anytime you can reliably fit a Smite in between HF cooldowns through a fight, you're outgearing the fightl; meanwhile, if you don't have a number of stacks accumulated with the trinket, it's worthless to you. Avoid it and instead push to better utilize the whole Disc tree, rather than focusing on Smiting enough to get the trinket stacks up & maintain them.

If you have a Paladin on Ultraxion they should get blue. However, never, ever, ever give a Dpriest green. Green does not transfer aegis last I checked so a Dpriest should always get red or blue, or both. In a Hpal/Dpriest healing setup the Dpriest should take red and give green > blue to the Hpal. Green kind of sucks for a Hpal but it is much worse for a Dpriest and the Hpal will be swapping it anyway.

In terms of PW:S, use it for Rapture on Ultraxion and nothing more. Outside of that a Dpriest should be bombing PoH, with the occasional Penance/PoM thrown into the mix. If people start dipping for whatever reason use FH/GH or HF via an Atonement spec. If people are dipping because they are not following mechanics fix the derping.

For gear on this boss Haste/Crit kind of is a better approach. Both stats contribute much more to PoH than Mastery. Since PoH will be responsible for the vast majority of your healing on Ultra it makes sense to gear the stats that provide the largest gains to it. Unfortunately, for most other encounters a high Mastery approach is often more useful.

In addition to all of that, DPS plays the largest role in whether you kill Ultraxion or wipe on him. It is much more important then any choices you would make in regards to healing.

For Zon dark phase the only suggestion I can make is alternate PW:S > PoH and weave PoM/Penance in if able. Or, pre-shield a chunk of the raid around 5s before the phase starts and bomb PoH before transitioning into alternating PW:S > PoH with PoM/Penance weaved in. Use raid cd's on the raid if you need to do so. Pop PI and similar CD's to increase your output.
