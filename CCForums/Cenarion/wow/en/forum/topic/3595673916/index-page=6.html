I do not cease all healing on the tank whilst waiting with bated breath for the Rapture ICD to come off CD so that I might finally be able to use a shield after 12 seconds of bubbleless torment. Thought I pointed that out.


I was discussing the difference between two different strategies. The first, where you refreshed the tank's PW:S as soon as Weakened Soul dropped. The second, where you delayed PW:S casting until after the ICD was up.

The first maximizes your tank healing, no matter what happens. However, it may potentially lead to a situation where you're "locked out" of shielding the tank for Rapture for some time.

The second means you lose a bit of hps by delaying your highest hps single target heal, but also +10% critical if you choose to cast a heal on the tank in that time. But you're guaranteed not to miss the upcoming Rapture proc.

That's the "even if you win, you lose" situation. The only way the second strategy "wins" is if the tank took damage in the gap of time between when Weakened Soul wore off and the Rapture ICD was up. But if this occurs, you also "lose" because you've placed the tank in a worse healing situation.

In no way did I attribute either strategy to you. The reply wasn't to you, and I didn't mention you.

I would add to Constie's point about "information excess," that while an expert may (rightly or wrongly) feel he/she is completely unaffected adversely by unneeded information, that almost certainly isn't true of a beginning discipline priest. It is important for a beginning discipline priest to start maximizing Rapture gains, but to do it in a way that doesn't (implicitly) encourage the wrong habits or overwhelm them. It's probably better advice, to maximize both Rapture gains and their overall performance, to advise shielding the tank every moment that they can and putting shields on as many secondary targets as they can, so long as the shields stand a reasonable chance of being consumed and their mana can support it. It keeps the mental overhead lower with little, if any, drop in performance.

If any benefits can be extracted from explicitly tracking the Rapture ICD (personally, I don't really believe there is, though I won't claim that it is completely impossible), that would probably be something for after a discipline priest has become very comfortable with their play and has the free mental resources for A. tracking the ICD without letting it dominate their decision-making and B. figuring out if it's helping them.
