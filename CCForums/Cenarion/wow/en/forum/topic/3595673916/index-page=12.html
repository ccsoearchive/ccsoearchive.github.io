07/02/2012 09:24 PMPosted by Tereasaa
Please clarify. Your guide says it depends on factors such as raid size, but now you say it depends on which spells you use?


Holy and Disc differ here. In most cases, Holy Priests will use the same spells regardless of raid size (Spine is one fight where this differs), which means that raid composition and size come more into play than for Disc, for whom raid size often determines spell use.

If you read the Holy section, I specify 10-mans without a Resto Druid for a Mastery heavy build, while suggesting Haste for 10-mans (the implication being those with RDruids, but it also works for those without). The reason is two-fold: (1) Holy will compete with Resto Druids with HoTs and can instead provide better burst to counter the stabilizing effect of the Druid's HoTs, and (2) EoL does far less on 10-man than 25 because there are much fewer players and you'll be constantly either overhealing or refreshing EoL (whereas, on 25 man, you will likely be hitting far more players by way of working through the groups with PoH).

Disc, on the other hand, really doesn't have nearly as much of a thought process. On both 10 and 25, you can use shields heavily or opt for PoH use. If a player makes heavy use of PW:Shield, whether it's via pre-shielding both groups on 10, pre-shielding a group or two on 25, or weaving PW:Shield with PoH on either, they want to go with heavier Mastery.

As for the point of the guide: it's a FAQ. Most of the questions we get are answered here. There are two spec posts that specifically covered how to heal (I specify the 5man level, but state that most of what is covered works at the raiding level), as well as the post that covers how to level. So I feel like not only am I explaining the bat, the ball and how to hit the ball with the bat, but the helmet, the uniform, the positions, how to play each position, how to run the bases and even whether baseball is a good fit.

To be blunt, unless I want to add another full page of hand-holding, which is exactly what you seem to be asking for, this is about as good as it will get. This isn't about how to play the game. It's about how to play a priest, and it's a pretty detailed account of how to do just that. I'm not sure what direction you're looking for.

Could I just get some tips on how to improve my Disc spec please?


I would suggest learning how to use archangel and atonement effectively. They're better to have than not in all circumstances at this point, especially with the ability to use them on bosses like ragnaros and al'akir now.

Train of thought is not as good for mana-back as archangel is.

Stength of soul is only good for normal or heroic spine of deathwing. theres really no other time to take it.

improved renew is a BAD talent. you should do yourself a favor and take renew off your bar unless you know the very very few situations where you can use it.

Darkness is a nice talent that I would take over inspiration. Inspiration is 'easily' covered by a resto shaman, so if you're ever paired with one than your inspiration isn't being used as often or as effective and can often be a waste of talent points. The haste however is very nice from darkness for nearly all of your spells.

Picking up veiled shadows is also a + because a lower CD shadowfiend is very very nice, espeically if you mess up your mana management. However if you run a ridiculous amount of spirit or are way too conservative then this talent will just hurt your mana problems more.

Empowered healing only needs 2 points in it, because single target healing isn't something you end up doing a lot because of how strong poh+divine aegis is, so if you want a survival cd like desperate prayer I would go with 2/3 in empowered healing. However its not necessary and you could keep 3/3.

Echo of light will always do a fixed percentage of your healing, based on the amount of mastery you stack. It's a flat % of your total healing done as a HoT, which stacks with itself. Example if you have 20% mastery Echo of Light will do 20/120 or 1/6 or 16.7% of your total healing.
