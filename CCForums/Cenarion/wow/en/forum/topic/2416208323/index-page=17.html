Fair point, nobody WAS complaining, but Blizzard did indicate that they wanted a little more involvement, and didnt want casual arena to be a once-a-week epics vending machine..

For example, as I stated in my first post, compare the amount of time required by a raider to get a piece of gear compared to the amount it takes PvPer..

The CASUAL PvP'r has to:
-Win 5 games (which can take not even 45 minutes sometimes) , collect loot, guaranteed to be there by the vendor.
-If the piece is more expensive, he simply has to wait another week

A GUILD raider has to:
-Wait for 10 (or 25) people to get online, grouped, summoned and organized. (as opposed to 5 at most for arena
-Clear trash
-GET to the boss
-DOWN the boss
-Hope the boss drops what he wants, (which can be DIFFICULT, considering some stuff has a 7-9% drop chance..
-WIN the roll.
All of which can take HOURS, and sometimes multiple days.

Now a CASUAL/PUG raider
-Form a pug
-Do all of the above steps, but with greater chance of failure and disbanding
just to get a CHANCE at ONE piece of gear.

As you can see, the time involved in obtaining gear in PvE vs PvP is greatly different, even though the quality of items is the same.

They want the PvP people to be forced to spend more time to get best in slot pvp gear, and I agree with that.
