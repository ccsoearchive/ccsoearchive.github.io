I know Blizzard only does things they feel are necessary to balance aspects of the game, but I think it would be in everyone's best interest for them to clarify why this change was actually implemented. This certainly comes across to me as an issue of "it wasn't broke, don't fix it". Yet, it got "fixed" anyway.

If I had to guess, it's because Cata made two significant changes:
- Stam + Primary stats only depend on iLvl
- Primary stats are much more valuable than anything else

This suddenly made PvP gear much more interesting for the PvE crowd, especially given that it can be gotten with comparatively little time spend. Compare that to Wrath, were a good deal of the item budget for PvP gear was spend on Stam and primary stats weren't worth as much compared to secondaries, so giving up e.g. haste for resilience was a much harsher choice.

However, if it's a problem that people use PvP gear in raids, the solution is not to make it harder to get PvP gear. PvP is most fun if everyone has similar gear, so it's a good thing if casuals can get the same gear as hardcores. The solution would be to make PvP gear less interesting for raiding. Maybe simply change resilience so that instead of only reducing damage done to you by players, it'll also reduce damage+healing you do while in an PvE instance. This way you can still wear PvP gear while you do quests to protect yourself against gankers, but once you enter an instance, PvP gear will be worth nothing.
