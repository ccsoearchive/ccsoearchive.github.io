Was a 5-game-per-week cap too quick? Perhaps. But for those of us with multiple characters at level 85, it was a core part of the reason we enjoyed rotating the non-mains into our weekly arenas. We had fun with our 5 wins a week at the minimum cap, and that's all we could ask for. Now, it's not that we can't possibly have fun doing this anymore, but for every level capped alt in our player list we need to spend an exponentially longer amount of time pushing to complete our weekly cap. It's difficult to view that as anything except grindy.

In particular, I have trouble understanding why the conquest change had to affect players at and below the minimum conquest cap, the area where you'd naturally find more alts, non-mains, non-serious, and transiently played characters. There's no visibly positive impact in forcing those characters to queue for conquest more often, in terms of itemization balance (especially since you cannot trade conquest for valor) or in terms of queue times. Which makes the decision somewhat frustrating to comprehend.

If the devs thought that earning 2200+ points per five wins felt too generous, that's fair, I do not disagree. But 1350 points per five wins felt just about perfect, especially since most weeks required at least ten games (if not more, especially for the recently leveled and undergeared characters) to compensate for win:loss ratios anyway. Just like the old arenas. But ten wins, (therefore 15-25 games total per week), seems to ask a bit much of the characters we enjoy playing, but don't necessarily want to dedicate ourselves towards.

For those players above the minimum conquest cap, I feel it's safe to assume they're inclined to play beyond the five wins per week, either for sport or achievement or just plain enjoyment. Therefore it isn't asking too much of them to work extra for their conquest. But for those of us who just want to enjoy time on our alts, can we keep the requirements somewhat accommodating?
