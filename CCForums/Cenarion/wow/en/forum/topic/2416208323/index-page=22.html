Arena players are usually players who do not have vast amounts of time to spend on the game. Without taking into consideration their player-base, Blizzard, yet again, implements a design that is flawed.

Just like their decision to prevent Arena groups of 3s and 5s from queueing up in RBG, Blizzard's uncreative solutions always makes nice features into something that is restrictive and not fun.

Much like RBG, watch as the dance studio get through years of development time, many iterations and bureaucracy, then end up as something useless. It always happens.

Blizzard need to start looking at other games like DC Universe Online and see how they made gear customization fun and interesting, then review their barbershop and see the reason why all Blizzard's new additions to the game seems good on paper, but always fails after iteration after iteration.

A perfect example of iterating into a failed design is the Cataclysm's locked-down talent system: The original Cataclysm talent design was interesting: The more points you put into a tree, the more Mastery you get. It was self-regulating as hybrid specs are punished by having a Mastery that do not synergize with other trees. But now, the talent system might as well be deleted since 95% of the "choices" are already set in stone, and the game can automatically assign all the skills to the player. Prime Glyphs are set in stone, so they might as well roll the bonuses into the actual skills. Mastery became just like another Haste / Crit. The original intent was good, then Blizzard starts iterating the design into something that fails.
