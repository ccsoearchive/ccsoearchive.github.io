The main activity I've done in the game since Arenas came out back in BC is Arenas one night a week with friends. It's the only reason I've played the game since then. This change is extremely disappointing.

There's usually only one night a week my arena team can play since we're all busy with work and/or family. We won't always be able to cap each week from that team anymore though we normally play as many games as we can fit in (though the queuing beyond our BG should help with that).

I am very glad that I'd managed to stick with one character this expansion, since otherwise this change would probably force me to only have one character.

Blizzard, you gather feedback on all sorts of changes, and talk about all sorts of things before implementing them... for the future please make sure you do that when you contemplate touching the incentive system for areas of the game people care about so much (conquest, valor points, raiding, etc). So it's not just a simple human failure to include it in patch notes, since it should have been mentioned other than in patch notes too. What makes it even more disappointing is that it wasn't announced at all is that there was so much highlighted about tripling the possibility for valor points by adding the new heroics. And note this isn't just a change on the amount, but on the delivery style, from a percentage based to a fixed amount. I'd actually assumed it was a fixed amount until a little into the expansion, but was much happier with the game when I found out it was a fixed percent.

And as others have pointed out, most anyone who cares already has a full set of arena gear by now, so doing this change in the middle of a season seems pretty harsh on anyone who doesn't... especially without any explanation or discussion.

I have to assume the change wasn't just to make the game more grindy, so I assume that part of the reason for the change is probably that queue times were long sometimes since so many people played 5 games and stopped... but there are a lot of other ways to solve the problem people would have suggested. For instance, letting folks get honor points after their 5 wins (the same as RBGs). I'm also not sure that'd have been an issue after the queuing outside of their BG, since part of we stop playing in a night sometimes is waiting 3-5 minutes to wind up playing people 300-500 rating above or below us (neither is fun).
