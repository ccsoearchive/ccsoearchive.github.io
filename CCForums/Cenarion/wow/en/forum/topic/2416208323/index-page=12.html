My two (or more) cents:

I rarely post on these forums since I usually support Blizzard's design choices. But this is the worst patch ever to hit in my almost 6 years of playing WoW (even though ZA was and still is my mostest favoritest raid ever).

First off, I don't raid or PVE anymore. I refuse to do heroics for conquest points, though I might PVE some to get healing gear for ZA.

I LOVE BGs, so all I would want to do is rated BGs. A few RL buddies of mine (my two arena partners) and I have been setting up rated BG groups for the past few weeks for fun, capping conquest points on Friday nights and playing more games on Saturday for kicks (all 3 of us PVPed together in Vanilla, so it brings back great memories of games vs. Alliance from our old server, Windrunner).

But this is only on our mains, and all 3 of us have alts that we also arena together with, gearing them up in Vicious Gladiator for the next PVP season. Because we all know that doing rated BGs without the best gear (from arena-ing) will just hurt the team.

We didn't want to be stuck with just our mains for the next season (my disc priest, an enhance shaman, a frost mage - just under 1800 and fully VG geared), so we set up our second group (my frost mage, a warlock, and a disc priest - just hit 1550), and we are currently working on a third group (my resto shaman, a hunter, and a DK - terrible rating since we did arena in greens). We only play up to the cap because one of our teammates is also a raider (which takes play time away).

All 3 of us planned for a 4th character to honor-grind/arena with so we would have more options and comps to choose from for next PVP season in both arena and rated BGs (it's not easy being disc/enhance/frost mage).

And now high rated 3s/5s people are tanking their 2v2 ratings for super easy wins, and this also hurts the low, rated 2s bracket players. They will lose more games and have to put in more time for just a *chance* at winning. Not sure how many high rated players are actually doing this, but it sure would suck to go up against people that are more than double or even triple your MMR. Not that I play 2s; I hate 2s since I have 2 healers and only 1 dps at the moment.

So basically, with the current ***TERRIBLE and buggy random bg queues with no fix in sight***, the number of arena games we now have to play a week, and the issue with not getting some wins/losses counted in both arena and rateds, there is no incentive for me to continue playing (what I have deemed), the funnest part of this game, which is PVP. I cannot gear my alts for Rated BGs in a efficient manner, so there is no reason for me to continue playing this game.

Edit: I don't see how this designed to push people into Rated BGs. You have to get 10 people all scheduled on the same night to even get a game going (vs 2 or 3 for arena). And you still have to win the bg(games can take a LONG time). And you only get about 220% more conquest points for 330% (getting 10 people vs getting 3 people) more effort and a longer queue time (at least for horde). It still seems easier to get a 2s team, tank rating, and win.

TLDR:
I arena to get alts geared for rated BGs. Arena takes too long now, so I don't see us doing arenas anymore. So now what? Suck it up, stop playing, or PVE.

EDIT: This was written at the assumption of a 135 flat rate and the game #'s are based on that value.

What exactly does Blizzard want from this change?

People to play more than 5 games? Make the arena more of a grind to prevent "free epics lol"? Slow down people from gearing up?

It doesn't do any of those things... at least not in a way that makes sense.

The Newbie: 1343 Weekly Cap (10 wins).
Just starting & learning, lets assume he wins 40% of his matches. Needs to cap out to gear up & get competitive.
Pre-4.1 = ~9 games average weekly at 40% win rate to cap
Post 4.1 = ~25 games average weekly at 40% win rate to cap

The newbie will be forced to grind out many more games at a discouraging pace. If they're playing in the 2's bracket as healer/dps combo, their games may last 20+ minutes and they could spend many hours trying to cap out for the week.


The Casual: 1750 Weekly Cap (13 wins).
Fairly geared up. Playing with friends rather than a "power team". Has some experience, manages about 60% win rate. Needs to cap out to fill his last few slots to help improve.
Pre-4.1 = ~8 games average weekly at 60% win rate to cap
Post 4.1 = ~22 games average weekly at 60% win rate to cap

The Casual will have to play many more games to cap out and stay competitive, but the matches shouldn't be as drawn out. However, it will hurt 2's brackets where games sometimes go long.


The "Good": 2400 Weekly Cap (18 wins).
Probably has all their gear already. Maybe collecting pieces for an off-set still, or just running arena for the fun of it at this point. 80% win rate.
Pre-4.1 = ~ 6 games average weekly at 80% win rate to cap
Post 4.1 = ~23 games average weekly at 80% win rate to cap

This player is in one of two categories already:
1. Plays a few games a week just to keep their rating up or help gear up their partner
2. Plays a ton of games a week because they like the arena.

This probably won't have much of an impact on either category because at this point, they're barely in it for the points.


The Hardcore:
These guys are in it for the Gladiator title. Amazing win rates, geared out, they don't care about points at all.

They either run a lot of games for the fun and to improve, or just a few to keep their rating active and high for the end of season rewards. This change in 4.1 will not impact either play-style at all.


The Altaholic: 3 characters, 1 Casual, 2 "Good".
This player has 3 level 85s and enjoys the variety of life playing multiple characters. Two of them are doing very well, one is more recently max level and the team is still working out some issues.

This change KILLS the Altaholic. They're looking at 50-60+ games to get their cap on 3 characters.



Overall, this change penalizes people who haven't geared out for the season, and is negligible to anyone who is geared out... which makes it's inclusion mid-season very poorly thought out. If anything, this change should have occurred between seasons.

It forces new to mid-range arena players to play a lot more games, but has no impact on top end players who don't care about points. It won't "slow down" the rate arena players gear up on a weekly basis... it just makes the weekly experience more of a grind.

It will discourage a lot of newer players because of the additional time sink and rather than increasing the amount of games occurring, it could actually decrease them, especially in the 2's bracket where games are likely to go long.

Anyone can see my profile, I'm no arena pro... I like playing arena with a RL buddy, which means playing 2's and playing in a formation that's not a "power group". My buddy is an Altaholic... so this is a major blow to the fun of arena.

If you want people to play a minimum of 10 a week... keep it at 5 wins, but also require 10 games. If you want people to win 10 a week, then change the scaling, but don't make it flat. And whatever you want... you should not have made this change mid-season.


TL;DR?
1. Penalizes lower ranked players who still care about points.
2. Creates a further gap to those still climbing the ladders & those on top of it
3. Could potentially kill 2's bracket where games can go longer
4. Poor judgment to make any type of change mid-season
