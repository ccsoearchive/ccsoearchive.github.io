CC RP is Event based. Which means it has a time to happen, people gather, interact, have fun, further stories and so on. Not like the Openess of MG, but it's less chaotic.

Alliance side there is always a event going on, as well several niche guilds.

Horde side has very big guilds with broad concepts, usually fewer players overall but all my RP experiences H side where great in one way or the other.

If you are looking for some RP alliance side, just join Allianceooc and ask about their guilds, there is always someone willing to list all their guilds.

Horde side, try Hordeooc. Like I said, Horde guilds are usually more broad, like Razortalons, Modas and Homeland. They got this concept and get several RPers under it. Das Doctas are also a great bunch and they are a bit more niche, running a weekly clinic event.

As for me, I'm trying to start a tribal/clan niche RP guild H side. Either way, be welcome!

It isn't Rogues (plural) sapping Gentyl, it's just Pang. He's kind of focused. ;)

RP on our realm is quite alive and well on both sides of the conflict, and we have the peaceful types (the AAMS on both factions, Homeland with the Horde, etc.) as well as the warmongers (me prime among them; building up my RP-PvP guild in anticipation of more world PvP in Mists of Pandaria!), and a lot between those two extremes.

Join AllianceOOC and hang out, talk with people, get to know us (belts are recommended; you'll understand why upon coming in, or within a few hours depending on if a certain someone is online...), and have fun. We tend to be lighter on events at the end of the week and on the weekend, heavier towards the front of the week. But there's always someone in AllianceOOC, and frequently people hanging out in the Blue Recluse in the Mage Quarter (including Horde on occasion; we had quite a bit of fun repeatedly stomping them last night...), and most are always quite happy to start up RP.

Bring gnomes with caution, as there seems to be a current debate on the ethics of munching on them.

The role playing community of Cenarion Circle is always happy to welcome another role player. We are an old server (one of the original RP servers, in fact) so there are lots of non-role players. We do our best to turn them into role players when we can, but they are present.

It's been noted that we don't have the incredible population of role players that Moonguard or Wyrmrest enjoys. I feel that I should add the word "yet" to that statement. We have a rather steady trickle of role players coming in from other servers, as well as former role players trying it out again and new people getting into it. Our community grows slowly.

Gentyl noted it, and I'll mention it again, we have a lot of events on Cenarion Circle. There's pretty much something going on every night. I don't find it particularly hard to find role playing outside the events, but it may mean you're going to the cathedral district of Stormwind or the Blue Recluse. Though I am finding more RP in the trade district lately as well.

Come and join us, Jassun, we're the most welcoming community of role players out there. Do bring extra pants though, there's a pants thief about. Just make sure to /join Allianceooc (or Hordeooc if you end up on that side of the fence) and say hello.

RP on Cenarion Circle is going strong and growing every day. It's event based; people meeting up in an RP hub is essentially our random RP

Okay... admittedly I'm a bit biased over the A-side groups... *cough cough* Anyway...

The RP hubs for Alliance and Horde are the Blue Recluse and Wyvern's Tail, respectively. You also have OOC and IC chat channels like AllianceOOC, HordeOOC, and HearthstoneIC
