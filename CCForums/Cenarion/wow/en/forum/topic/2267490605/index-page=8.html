Back to the Stacking RF + Hero/BL:

Here is a comment from Whitefyst's post on EJ:


Finally, if it can be avoided, you do not want to stack dynamic haste buffs (RF and Bloodlust) since much of the haste will be well above the soft cap. You are better off staggering these effects so that you can be closer to the 1s GCD on SS more often since it gains you 2 shots per 10s CS rotation.


I have still been trying to do some napkin math, research, and some testing to find out if staggering large Dynamic Haste effects is more beneficial than stacking them, but haven't come up with enough proof.

I can add that from what little testing I have done (tried some out last night with the help from some guildies) I wasn't able to effectively use all my focus and get more shots with all Haste buffs stacked thus leading to wasted focus many times. There were several times where I would have to choose to either cast another aimed shot to dump focus or cast 2 steadies to keep ISS up.

It was much more smooth when I tried staggering the haste effects and I was able to maintain a fluid priority. I was able to keep from getting focus capped and maintain ISS with no issues.

I don't have any numbers at this time to back up my claim, but at this time I believe it is better to stagger the Haste effects than to stack them.

If anyone has any math proving different I would love to see it.

If anyone has any math proving different I would love to see it.


I ran a quick FD sim. In one attempt, I was running with RF/Bloodlust at 0%, with the second with Bloodlust at 20% into the fight, or 1 minute in, which is 30 seconds after RF wears off and we are out of CA range, as well as another case at 11%, which is 33 seconds into the fight, 3 seconds after RF wears off.

In case #1, total DPS is 28781, with 34 AiS, 38 ArS and 140 Steadies. Case #2 was 28704 DPS and 30 AiS, 47 ArS, 138 Steadies. Case #3 was 28863 DPS, 32 AiS, 42 ArS and 139 Steadies.

The overall difference between the cases is around 150-160 DPS for myself personally.

I don't think the results are that conclusive, as 150-160 DPS at a 28704 DPS benchmark is a 1% difference. It'd probably depend on comfort and skill to burn off Focus without wasting focus while maintaining ISS.


Awesome feedback Mendia. Thank you for for this. To me it appears that this is one of those "6 of one, half a dozen of another" deals where either way wont really gain/lose you hardly any dps and just comes down to comfort your comfort level in those circumstances.
