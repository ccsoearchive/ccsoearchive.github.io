Long answer:
These sort or questions are going to run rampant in this thread, so I'll do my best to lay out the calculation required for others to figure this out themselves. You may or may not decide to put this in an "Advanced" portion of your guide:

Steady Shot Cast Times
Desired Steady Shot Cast time (I): 1.50 seconds
Desired Steady Shot Cast time (II): 1.25 seconds

Haste Multipliers
Pathing (3 Point Survival Talent): 1.03
Raid Buff (Windfury, Hunting Party, Improved Icy Talons): 1.10
Improved Steady Shot (ISS) (3 Point Marksman Talent): 1.15

Total Haste Multiplier = (Pathing Haste Multiplier)*(Raid Buff Haste Multiplier)*(ISS Haste Multiplier)


To determine your required haste rating, use the appropriate calculation below:

Without T11 4-Piece Bonus
Base Steady Shot Cast Time: 2.0 seconds

To Reach Haste Plateau I (1.50 seconds)
Required Haste Rating = [(2.0/[1.50*Total Haste Multiplier]) - 1] * 12805.70

To Reach Haste Plateau II (1.25 seconds)
Required Haste Rating = [(2.0/[1.25*Total Haste Multiplier]) - 1] * 12805.70

With T11 4-Piece Bonus
Base Steady Shot Cast Time: 1.80 seconds

To Reach Haste Plateau I (1.50 seconds)
Required Haste Rating = [(1.8/[1.50*Total Haste Multiplier]) - 1] * 12805.70

To Reach Haste Plateau II (1.25 seconds)
Required Haste Rating = [(1.8/[1.25*Total Haste Multiplier]) - 1] * 12805.70

7. The hard cap on crit is something like 104.8% percent due to crit suppression. Basically, bosses are higher level (Skull = 83) and are less likely to take a critical from a player. Specifically, they are 4.8% less likely to be critically hit from a player. This was blatantly evident to anybody who test on a raid training dummy at level 80 while they were tuned for 88. Thus, the soft cap should be changed to 44.8%.

Here is the math Whitefyst's math behind Crit:
The hard cap is at 100% crit. In Cataclysm, there is a 0.6% crit depression on a level 88 boss. Thus, we can have 100.6% crit before crit chance is actually capped. Our raw crit chance will never be at the hard cap with the range that crit chance will be in Cataclysm; however, a couple soft caps exist in conjunction with the hard cap.

The first crit soft cap has to do with the talent Careful Aim (CA). CA provides 30/60% bonus crit chance on SS and AI attacks on targets above 80% health. Thus, with 2/2 CA, your SS and AI shots will be crit capped if you have 40.6% crit chance. This is just a soft cap though, you do not want to reduce your base crit chance below 40.6% in order to maximize your CA benefit since it lowers your crit rate for those shots for 80% of the boss health and the rest of your shots for 100% of the boss health. What this soft cap means is either that crit percent above 40.6% is worth a little less or that the second point in CA is not worth a little less.

I have been under the impression that you will get off a higher number of Aimed Shots by staggering the haste effects instead of stacking them.

It is very hard to near impossible maintain ISS and not be focus capped at the same time when under both RF and Hero/Blood lust. This usually leads to just letting ISS fall off because you wont cast Steadies in Pairs to keep ISS up. This is effectively wasting a 15% attack speed buff.

When you stagger the haste effects, it is much easier to keep the ISS buff up and not become constantly focus capped.

Now I dont have any math to back this up, this is just my hunch and my experience. I would like to see some math with focus regeneration and the amount of aimed shots you can fire both under stacked haste effects RF/Hero and with them staggered back to back.

My Hypothesis is that you would see a greater benefit from staggering them, but I could be wrong.

If this can be proven one way or another, I would gladly update the guide. I may post this over on EJ and see if I can get some of the Math Experts to help out with these calculations.

This is somewhat back of the envelope math, but if you're using Rapid Fire and Bloodlust (Heroism/Time Warp/Ancient Hysteria) at the same time, there's a good possibility you're breaking your GCDs. What do I mean?

1347 Haste (for plateau II and 4 piece) = 10.519% haste (1.10519)

1.10519*1.1*1.15*1.03*1.3*1.4 = 2.62
Steady Shot Cast Time = 1.80/2.62 = 0.687 seconds

Correct me if I'm wrong, but our GCD prevents anything cast faster than a 1 second CD.

With Rapid Fire alone:
1.10519*1.1*1.15*1.03*1.4 = 2.016
Steady Shot Cast Time = 1.80/2.62 = 0.893

With Bloodlust Alone:
1.10519*1.1*1.15*1.03*1.3 = 1.87
Steady Shot Cast Time = 1.80/1.87 = 0.962


Now, as you can see if our hard cap for the global cooldown is 1 second, there would be no additional benefit from using Rapid Fire with Bloodlust. However, while in the middle of doing these calculations, I feel as though the cap might be pushed down lower than 1 second for hunters (perhaps even to 0.5 seconds). If this is the case, my argument above is invalid and I'll delete this post =D

Anyone care to add?
