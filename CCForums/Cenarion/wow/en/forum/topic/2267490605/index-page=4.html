The basic Shot Priority for Careful Aim Phase is as follows:

Apply Serpent Sting, only Cast Chimera Shot to refresh SrS before it runs out, Do not cast Chimera on cooldown.


Ok so maybe I misread on EJ, but I could have sworn it was decided to forgo SrS and CS during Careful Aim in favor of the AiS -> SSx2 rotation.
^^^^
Incorrect see below.


Edit: Went back to look and found this post that I must have missed.

"First, concerning using CS to refresh SrS during the CA phase, that is the suggested case to maximize DPS. Concerning the situation where you need to wait to refresh SrS at the last moment is just the worse case scenario to meet the maximum DPS requirement. For the max DPS case during the CA phase (and also the other phases), in some situations it works out best to use CS a little off its CD, while there are others where the maximum theoretical DPS is to cast CS late. If you have trouble with the cast CS late recommendation, then you can go with the alternate cycle that only casts one AI and uses CS sooner. You have to chose the option that fits best for your situation. Not everyone has the same latency, reaction times, or preferrences. I try to make that clear and state that other rotations are possible but that I try to list the cases that fit for the largest percentage of players.

Concerning the free hardcast AI case, once again you have to do what you feel works best for your situation; however, personally, I do not think it is hard to fit in a CS to refresh SrS when you have a 5-6s window to do so. Its an instant cast shot, if you know you are going to be getting close to 5 stacks of MMM late in the window, then just cast CS earlier.

I did not factor in the free hardcast AiS into the analysis, nor do I plan to. I consider it a bonus for those that use it."

I'm glad to see you finally got the haste calculations up there, but I'm curious about one thing:

Haste Rating for the 1.25 Cast Time Plateau (CS -> SSx4 -> ASx3*)
Unless I've misunderstood the math somewhere, we would want 1347 Haste rating to achieve this plateau:

1347 Haste Rating = 10.519% additional haste = 1.10519 Haste Multiplier
Raid Buff = 10% additional haste = 1.10 Haste Multiplier
Imp SS = 15% additional haste = 1.15 Haste Multiplier
Pathing = 3% additional haste = 1.03 Haste Multiplier

(1.10519)*(1.10)*(1.15)*(1.03) = 1.44000
Because the base cast time of SS will be 1.80 seconds (with the T11 4-piece bonus), our steady shot cast time at this haste value will be:
1.80/1.44000 = 1.249996

Obviously this value is mathematically less than 1.25 and should (in a perfect, latency-free world) allow for successful completion of this rotation.

So where did your 1486 haste rating value come from?

A comment on the haste plateaus. The rotation you've presented for the 4pc bonus with a steady shot cast time of 1.25 while the target is not in focused aim and you are not currently under the effects of an extra haste buff is not right. Whitefyst's calculations were based on a rotation of:

Chimera Shot -> Arcane Shot x2 -> Steady Shot x5

Which, based on the original time of his calculations, ended up in a 9.25s focus positive rotation with a 1.25s Steady Shot cast time.

Unfortunately his calculations weren't quite right since we've figured out that Improved Steady Shot does not affect focus regeneration. The rotation still holds itself together based on MMM procs because one of the Arcane Shots is replaced by an Aimed Shot! during the rotation.

Assuming that you have no latency, a Patchwerk style fight, and you get an Aimed Shot! proc every 9th steady shot, you end up focus positive for the rotation. It is not, by any stroke of the imagination, massively focus positive though. As with all Patchwerk rotations, once you move the rotation is purely based on how well you are able to maintain it while moving.

I've played around with haste ratings to see what the net effect is on the rotation. Here are some of the things I've noticed (all assuming 4pc bonus + 3/3 in Pathing, and assumes you're getting the 10% haste buff from the raid):

0 Haste Rating - This nets an always-positive 10s rotation of ChS -> ArS x2 -> StS x5, with an AiS! proc replacing a steady shot instead of an ArS throughout the rotation. The cast time on Steady Shot in this rotation is 1.38s and Aimed Shot at 2.07s. The negative of this rotation is you become slightly over-focus-positive and can't really drop the positive due to how the rotation holds itself together. You will also end up with a (very) slightly lower number of Wild Quiver procs.

1343 Haste Rating - This seems to be the breakeven for the overall 9.25s rotation to work seamlessly. With this, your Steady Shot cast time is ever so slightly over 1.25s and Aimed Shot at 1.875s. If you have extremely low latency (all of network, input, and brain) this is likely the best breakpoint. You end up with 10.487% haste from haste rating.

If you have higher latency, you can adjust your haste rating upwards to compensate for it, but don't go overboard. Based on the MM thread on EJ, haste rating compensation for latency can get completely out of control, requiring a whole lot of haste rating that you simply can't afford to spec into.
