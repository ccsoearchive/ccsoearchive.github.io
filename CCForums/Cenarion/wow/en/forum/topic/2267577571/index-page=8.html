truely hope you didn't make any newbie rogue waste their hard earned conquest points on a 2.6 speed weapon.


Here's the thing, though:

There are builds which can succeed with 2.6 speed weapons. I use a hemorrhage-only build (the same build most people would get a sword for) though my weapon choice is deceiving. I do believe Jailbreaklol also happens to use a similar build on occasion. It isn't a popular choice, but it's T2-capable. Works for me.

Weapon choice really isn't that significant. Let's say I 'waste' my points on a 2.6 weapon and have to use a 346-blue. I'll lose a little bit of damage, but will it impact the other two options?
-Mutilate does hefty burst via poison and envenom, which is largely impacted by stats, not your weapon. Yeah, mutilate also contributes to the burst, but you'll still put out some nice damage with a non-PvP weapon.
-Subtlety does most of its 'kill' power during a Shadow Dance. Eviscerate is based off of your stats and is unaffected by your weapon choice, and Ambush doesn't really diminish that much. Just for anecdotal reason on super small sample space: blue dagger ambush crit: 28k, t2 dagger ambush crit: 30k. Same target, same buffs, all that.

Basically: I could still maintain my rating with a blue weapon if I were to 'waste' my points.

So will that newbie who 'wastes' his points really be in a bad spot? No. Not at all. His weapon isn't winning him matches, and if he doesn't know what he's doing then nothing will really help him out that much. If anything, his gear would be superior to his weapon to greater improve his survivability.

So you know what? Go let the newbie waste his points on whatever weapon he feels like. It won't make a large difference. And if he really feels that he fails at combat at the start, well - he can always return the weapon and buy something else. If the newbie has fun playing Combat then what's the big deal? At least there is now an information source they could follow to hopefully improve.

As for the guide: I skimmed over it when it was still in its testing phase on realm forums, and I skimmed over it again now as a refresher. There's nothing inherently wrong with it, regardless of the rating of the people who wrote it. Could it be more concise? Definitely. I think it does a good job of explaining the skills behind Combat, and it could probably work on defining general playstyles to more encounters. But really, it covers important PvP ideas such as not wasting cooldowns and using abilities for specific purposes rather than blowing your load. So even if Combat didn't work out for someone who reads the guide, it still has some valuable PvP information.

I do have some comments concerning this spec though. We'll see how this evens out come test-day, though.

* Considering the combo point generation is the worst of all specs, managing to get a full-point SnD and Recup up all at the same time in between building for a 9-second kidney shot and eviscerate seems clunky as hell. And managing to stay on top of your target all while they're flailing about like a school girl with a spider in their dress probably won't help either.

* You suggested using CloS for melee. Yes the glyph does grant that physical damage reduction, and we do have that free glyph space because we won't require the blind glyph (but that leads to another problem, which I have listed below concern partners), but that seems like a very situational arena or even 1v1 statement. Not all 2's are going to be dual physical damage brawlers, unfortunately. You would be better off with the blind glyph. Because without the blind glyph, we come to this little issue.

* What if the partner isn't a frost Mage? As y'all said, and most of us rogues are keenly aware, combat's damage is pretty meh because it's sustained damage. The fact that we wouldn't be able to run with a healer limits our utility to double DPS, and even then we'd have a hard time running with anything that has DoTs. Even if combat were loleasymode in PvP, you have to acknowledge that this spec would not function outside of a 1-way type team. I mention this because of the heavy reliance on that really sweet gouge and force-trinket blind. You couldn't force a blind if it broke when you don't have the glyph. It would just become pointless if you ran with a basically anything but a Frost Mage. Don't recommend another combat Rogue, because then DR and minimal burst really would make the spec pointless.

* Our bread n' butter damage dealin' cooldowns are, as y'all said, pretty dang long. Relentless Blades does solve this in theory; however, due to what I mentioned earlier (clunky Combo Point management) you'd find that you wouldn't get as many eviscerates off. That really, really, really hurts combat as far as their burst 'cause Sub has Shadow Dance a one minute cool down compared to KSp's 2 minute cooldown.

I'll add more comments when they come to mind.

On paper, Combat has a lot of bonuses it's looking like. I'm just thinking if the pro's really do out-weigh the con's, and if that compares anything to Sub. Still not having tested this, I have to remain neutral. I'm going to try, but I have feelings that although this may be a fun change of pace for a day or two, it -may- not out perform Sub. You guys do advertise this as, "take it or leave it" and such, and I respect that. I'm just trying to give feed back, not flaming. Expect more when others try the spec.

Edit: As Dert said, this guide does list an excellent amount of good basic PvP advice. Again, anyone that doesn't see that is just pointless flaming. Let those who want to break from the Sub grind for a few days return to Sub after a distraction if it doesn't work.

Also, I'd hate to see this thread be denied a sticky because of you guys feeding a troll. The lack of proper organization of information and indirect dealing with certain classes though, not to mention no gear guide, does remove this guide from becoming a sticky unfortunately. At least, the lack of a gear guide hampers it greatly. Perhaps you guys could post a link to a source where that type of thing is listed, or quote it and site that source so people know where you got it from.
