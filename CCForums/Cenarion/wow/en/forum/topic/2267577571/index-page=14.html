Thanks for the comprehensive guide - great stuff! One question on KSpree weapon swap macro - doesn't changing weapons trigged GCD and re-set auto-swing timer? Does the damage gained from using the slow OH outweigh the damage lost due to these issues?
Thx

Yes, the weapon swap macro triggers a GCD, but consider this:
The swap doubles the damage dealt by your offhand weapon, which is a large ammount (30k raw damage from a slow offhand at maximum, so 15k bonus )
In terms of PvE DPS, I think it`s considered a loss of DPS, but in PvP you don`t do sustained DPS and you want your bursts to deal as much damage as possible during your 30% Bandit`s Guile windows.

For PvP: it`s worth it.


I wanted to point out that you can write the macro in a slightly different way, re-ordering it, and you only then need one button press. This is what I have used when I give combat a shot, this is one with trinket/racial usage for damage.

/startattack
/use 14
/cast Blood Fury
/cast Killing Spree
/equipslot 17 "SLOW OH WEAPON"

You hit it once, and it will use the trinket, use blood fury, start KS and then swap to the slow OH weapon. The downside is that your first OH KS swing occurs with your fast weapon, resulting in a little less damage. I'll take that versus having to wait a GCD.

The only issue occurs in that you have to be off GCD when you hit it. If you are on the GCD, it will activate trinkets and swap the weapon, but not activate KS, but due to the weapon swap, it will activate another GCD. I don't mash buttons, and not that one, so not an issue for me, but preference.

I'll then do the same with my SS macro, so no extra key presses are required.

Also, saw some comments on SnD. The benefit of the talent and glyph is so that you can make it effective with low CP's. Adding the glyph alone makes that 1pt SnD last for 15s, which is big, 5 CP's would get you 27s. If you also talent into it with both points, that 1pt SnD will last 23s. Considering how important SnD is for Combat, I would recommend the glyph at a minimum and at least 1 point in the talent. Save those CP's for other things.

I would agree on the poisons. I fully understand the importance of being able to shiv crippling on, but blade twisting is pretty solid, and can FoK the crippling. There are times where it can cost you, that's true. Mind numbing is so solid, like to keep that on.

This game and this class is too big with too many variables to encompass in 1 guide. Especially when its a off-spec pvp tree. There are 30 diff classes in the game, and the rogue requires a different approach for almost every single tree. The generalites may be same, but the finer points are not. The rogue class is so dependant on "If so, then" split-decision making that it couldnt all be laid out in one single guide.

You could write a full novel with ever possible decision/encounter with all 30 classes. Nobody would read something that long. So, we get 2 people, that love the rogue class and want to help out Combats. They put in the time and effort to write a good, well rounded guide. I dont play Combat, havent since mace stun, but I feel like I have a good working knowledge of the spec after reading. That was the intent of the guide. I have enuff of the basics to go on, and a train of thought, to get started. Im not, but I could if I wanted. :P

We get people here everday looking for a "guide". We routinely refer them to AJ or EJ. Why? Because most people dont want the headache or heartache of going out on a limb and writing a guide for the WOW forums. Why would you? So bitter, know-it-alls can call you out?

@Celine/Spriest: If it was my guide, I wouldnt even mention arena ratings. Its of no consequence. What most people fail to realize is that spec/gem/gear viability in the 2400+ bracket is WAY different than what us Avg Joe's do on a daily basis. You wrote the guide, let it succeed or fail on its own merits. Discussion of points if fine, but do not feel you have to defend it regularly. It speaks for itself. If newbies try it and are successful with it, that will come out eventually. You both did a fine job and I respect the effort. Rememeber, for every person that disagrees or trolls this thread, there are 10+ that got something positive from it.
