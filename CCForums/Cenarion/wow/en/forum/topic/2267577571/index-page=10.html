This guide does a really good job of outlining the basics of Combat PVP, and goes pretty in depth with it's information. You guys have some good points, but you can't attack every doubter by waving them off as trolls.. You make a guide for the general public, and referencing one exceptional player repeatedly doesn't help. We're not all amazing players.



Blade twisting eliminates the need for Crippling poison, allowing you to equip a utility poison in its place on your offhand.



As far as enchants go,
Get a DPS enchant on your MH
it ranks Ava > Hurr > Land



Blade Twisting does not in any way eliminate the need for Crippling poison. Shiv'ing the Crippling is a more reliable snare. You can't count on it proccing for you if you're chasing a target. And for the second part, the arrows go in the other direction.




Throwing Specialization: Makes it easier to keep that pesky feral in combat, preventing a re-stealth, same for a rogue. But beware this also keeps you combat as well. But the neater part of this, is the interrupt attached to it. If you're in a nova and see a poly going for a healer, don't be afraid to throw it. this has no CD, but of course requires CPs to interrupt, and you must be 5 yards away to utilize DThrow.

As Celinne has said, sometimes lining up your buffs, you can land 100k+ damage on a zero-resi target.


I do believe the Deadly Throw interrupt has a travel time, I'm not 100% sure on that. And if you're going to write a guide for PvP, don't fluff it up by listing flashy numbers for situations that never happen. Zero resil targets in PVP outside of ganking daily grinders are unlikely.

Also, I believe you forgot to mention that you still auto attack during Killing Spree, for 20% extra damage no less. Plus if you're going to use it as a gap closer or a finisher for a specific target, try and make sure there's no other target(pet's or players) nearby or could be flying everywhere.

Honestly, because of its true viability among the general players, I don't think this should be stickied. I do, however, think you guys should keep it up in case some players do want to give it a try.

Now I could offer an alternative I don't think I've mentionned in the guide:

Unlike the other two rogue specs, Deadly Throw is much than just an expensive ranged snare to a Combat rogue. ( Although in this case you would in fact be using it for the snare effect >_> ) Ambidextry gives it a 75% damage buff, while Revealing Strike gives it an additionnal 45% damage buff. It lowers the cooldowns on your CD's via Restless Blades and interrupts spells with Throwing Spec. Which means it's not a finisher you feel terrible feeding combo points into, even if it's just to keep your target slowed until you can land a daze on them.


Your second point was regarding Deadly Throw's travel time. Yes, it has one of about half a second or so. This is why Mind-Numbing poison is very helpful, increasing the cast time and the window of opportunity with an interrupt. Some spells and classes are just easy to predict as well.


As for the 100k Killing Spree, it was simply a mention of the raw damage you could hope to do with it at maximum. Factor in Resilience and Armor and you got yourself the number you'll be doing on a PvP target. (Something like 30k on plates and 60k on clothies?)
