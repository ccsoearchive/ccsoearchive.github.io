I've enjoyed combat for awhile, but I've decided to try sub for a little while just so I can get a better picture of what works best for me... I suggest everyone <<time permiting>> to try each talent group out.

What I like about Sub over Ccombat so far:
Combo points--combo points are everywhere,lol, well not that much but the Honor among thieves talent in Sub is huge so far in BG's, while im stealthed I'm gaining combo points, for things like recuperate or whatever.I always felt combo point starved, which is why I probably feel that theres so many now, like a kid at a candy store. I'm not dying half as much as I was, unless of course im doing my fan of knives w/mindnumbing poison on. So, my survivability has gone way up as well.

Damage-- My damage has gone through the roof. Even with using a blue 1.8 dagger&1.4 dagger,I'm still doing more damage than w/ my combat's purple claws of torment&1.4 dagger. I still havent reforged my PVP gear either, I had alot in haste, which worked well for combat for energy regeneration.

More stealth--alot more vanish and then im able to get right back at them or just escape.

What I like about Combat over Sub:
Killing Spree-- I do miss it, nothing was more fun when someone attacking you from behind and hitting KS..It was fun to watch them keep turning around and try and beat the Ks--thinking it was a Sub rogue. Did I mention I really miss that..

Speed--I never felt my movement hampered as I do with Sub. Sometimes with Sub I feel like i want to push the character on the screen because he's moving sooo sloww.

Attack from behind with combat i didnt have to worry as much about getting behind the target for damage. With Sub I feel once you've exhausted most of your bag of tricks and the enemy is not dead or nearly dead, its time to pack it in and escape, because the enemy is just not going to let u get behind them so easily. The last thing you want to do in BG's is do the round and round a character and attract attention to yourself. <on a side note- I did Like EQ's AA=WOW talent(sorta-)that you could backstab from the front, although contrary to the wording it made it so u would strike vital areas in the front of a target.>

I havent noticed my cc hurt by it that much, but then again im still glyphed for gouge for frontal attacks. I know the premier reason in combat is the 9 sec kidney shots, but I always felt that even when I did get them off, I just couldn't do much damage with them in BG's. Now arena fighting I can somewhat see where they may be more important, but perhaps more in 3v3 or 5v5. I havent tried 2x2, or any other arena matches, YET, still getting use to a new talent tree after being dual combat for a long time.(well to me anyway).I'm not a good judge by any means on arena and have only played around with them for some easy conquest points.

I do appreciate this Combat PVP guide and recommend it be stickied. We should all have the option to a style of play that we enjoy. I am still combat for PVE and will be for quite awhile, till I give assassination a try. But for now I'm going to experiment with Sub for a while..So far I like it.

After getting the 5% hit chance, choose either Haste, Mastery, or maybe crit. The effects of mastery and haste are listed in the gearing portion of the guide.

The problem with crit is that Combat has no talents that give additionnal crit, or additionnal damage from crits, so your crit chance is low, even if fully reforged / enchanted / gemmed for crit, and your crits are not boosted in any way.

However, 28-30k evis crits are rather nice, I'm thinking of getting another rogue to 85 and checking what it gives to go full crit.

I already tried combat PvP, and have come across a few things this post did NOT cover.

1. Throwing Specialization. Remember rogue glove pvp bonus in BC? Yah. You can run in throwing range when someone is casting to get a 3 second interrupt. This was in Neilyo 14.
