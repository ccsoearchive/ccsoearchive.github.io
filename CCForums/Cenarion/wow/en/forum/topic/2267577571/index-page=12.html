What does viability in arena have to do with making a guide about Combat PvP? Nothing they wrote in the guide is wrong, it explains pretty accurately how someone, if they should choose, should PvP as combat. Instead of focusing on the content, you and other people trolled their arena ratings. There is no reason NOT to sticky the guide itself. Fact is there are people who don't care if a spec is not top of the line for PvP, they find it fun for whatever reason. Why can't there be a guide from players who play that spec for others out there who choose to run it?

Combat does have some interesting tools for PvP. The way they function is similar to prot warriors, with less survivability but more damage. I've played a healer (Paladin/Druid) since Vanilla and I know how obnoxious it is when a prot warrior is on me with their myriad of stuns and silences. Having a combat rogue on a caster, especially a healer, pretty much guarantees they will not get a cast off. Between 9 sec undispellable kidneys, 6 sec 15 energy undispellable gouges on a 10 sec cooldown, 3 sec silence on a 10 sec cooldown, mind-numbing, and a ranged 3 second interrupt that only requires 1 combo point to use, a combat rogue's lockdown potential is ridiculous.

The only issues with the spec as I see it is Bandits Guile, Killing Spree, and Restless Blades.

Bandit's guile is the backbone of Combat's damage. They do some good damage during moderate and deep insight. The problem is that PvP requires a ton of target switching. Due to the LOOOONG ramp-up time combined with the buff applying to a single target instead of the rogue means that it is hard to build up moderate or deep insight in PvP. Reducing the ramp up time OR making the buff not fall off after switching targets would be a HUGE buff to combat.

Killing Spree is frustrating. I see no reason as to why this ability hits multiple targets. Nothing more frustrating than hitting killing spree on a hunter or lock just to end up on his pet with the lock or hunter free casting on the rogue with a huge smile on their faces. The ability should either just hit the target only or should at the very least put the rogue on his original target on the final attack.

Restless blades lowers the cooldown on the major cooldowns of combat for each damaging finish move he does. The problem is that with combat's combo point generation, between using SnD, kidney, recup combined with lower time on target due to kiting, there just isn't enough combo points to pop out damaging finishing moves outside of Adrenaline Rush and lucky Glyph of SS procs. If they added a glyph of Restless Blades that did this: "Restless Blades is now triggered by Kidney Shot and Recuperate (and possibly SnD) rather than damaging finishing moves", it would tremendously help out Combat Rogues in PvP without affecting PvE, since that glyph would obviously be a DPS loss in PvE. It would greatly help out their mobility by lowering the cooldown of sprint by 20-30 on average rather than 0-10 it does now as well as making it's two major damage cooldowns up more often.

TLDR:
Yes, combat isn't a top tier spec, the players who wrote the guide aren't top tier on the arena ladder, but they are probably top tier at playing this spec for PvP and worth a read for anyone interested in PvPing as a Combat Rogue.
