Yes, I've been experimenting with crit, and you'll notice I'm now fully regemmed / reforged towards crit.

After all, Main Gauche is a chance to deal double damage on a main hand swing.
Crit is also a chance to deal double damage, but not just for your main hand, but on all attacks.

And the chance of a chance to get 15 energy back? Let's just say I crit a 37k evis yesterday on a target dummy and it's much more appealing to me than some tiny ammount of energy regen.

So now the competition of which Stat is best for Combat PvP is between Crit and Haste.
Haste might be troublesome to test seeing as nearly none of the PvP gear gives any of the damn thing.



Obviously the best thing concerning Mastery and Crit would be to have a high percentage of both so that you could not only get extra swings, but have them crit, in addition to all your other crits, but such numbers are not attainable with the current gear sets unless maybe you go in full PvE gear.

Celinne, I'm pretty sure the limit is 500 posts. I used the thumbs-down report feature to request a cap extension for this thread (recommend the same for anyone else who wants to see it live on!), but maybe you're right: Maybe a 2.0 guide in a new thread might be a better bet, and if it's solely yours to maintain and we can avoid the... um... ugliness of the first several pages, maybe it could actually get all stickified. :)

Regnum, Fou is right; if you just hit 85, you are DOA for a while, at least until you really start accumulating significant Bloodthirsty gear and get to like 2500-3000 resilience. I'm only at 2200 now, and I'm just starting to feel like I've got a smidge of survivability.

And as for getting pissed off because you're losing so many duels as Combat: Well, yeah. It's cause you're dueling. :) Combat as a PvP spec is a control spec far more than a damage spec; you will not be able to go toe-to-toe with most classes in a solo battle to the death. Our greatest asset is arguably our ability to keep casters occupied or locked down with repeated interrupts, silences and stuns (along with fair to moderate offensive pressure) while *other* damage dealers dispatch our enemies. In most one-on-one situations, depending on gear of course, the best you can hope for is a stalemate until help arrives. (Though I look forward to stacking the crap out of crit to see if that changes things at all.)

If you can get someone to 30% insight and lock them in a Kidney Shot, that's a perfect time for Adrush + DPS trinket.

When you need quick combo points too, for locking down someone or getting recup running.

When you find yourself outnumbered and hit Combat Readiness, Adrush and Blade Flurry + SnD.

When you really want to get to 30% insight fast and use it to get your strikes off faster, although when you reach 30%, you'll most likely run out of energy unless you popped it near the end of Moderate Inisght.


By the way, if you Evis crit a clothie for 20k, and have Blade Flurry on, you'll also be hitting the warrior next to him for the full 20k.

By the way again, your belt currently has crit reforged to expertise. I suggest reforging the mastery to expertise and keeping the crit instead. And personally I think you're sacrificing a lot of crit for expertise too, PvE purposes?
