Well, a regular SnD lasts 21 seconds, meaning most of the time you'll be busy refreshing everything, your rotation will look like this:

Recup > SnD > Kindey shot > Recup >SnD > Kidney Shot > Recup > SnD > Kidney shot

Having SnD last twice as long allows you to skip refreshing once or twice so you can slip in another finisher, like Expose Armor, or Eviscerate.

Recup > SnD > Kindey shot > Recup > Eviscerate! > Kidney Shot > Recup > SnD > Kidney shot

Having finally reached 85 today, I've been able to stop focusing solely on levelling like a lunatic and start studying combat more closely, and while I've yet to fight anything more dangerous than a target dummy, I've already noticed something in regards to a talented and glyphed SnD...

With us being as CP-starved as we are, would it not be better for us to use 1-CP SnDs? In return for 4CPs, we get the duration cut by less than half. Where a 5-point SnD will last 42 seconds, 2x1CP SnDs will last 44 and save you 3CPs in the process.

Granted, you'll need to spend more time casting SnDs, but you'll also get to use the 4CPs you would have used on an extra recuperate/kidney-shot/eviscerate instead.

Just wanted to add a little trick I noticed leveling this guy up in BGs.

If you want to be clever and take a small risk, you can open up with garrote on frost mages and disc priests for the silence and still not have gouge break early. It turns out that if you gouge them right after they bubble, the bubble will absorb the damage completely (as the garrote bleed isn't really that potent as combat); you essentially use their own move to keep your gouge from breaking.

I find it kind of neat, and it's really only important for combat, as sub doesn't need to worry about it at all (that talent that makes bleeds not break gouge) and assassination isn't using gouge anyways.

Props to you for going through all this effort, and the guide, at least in terms of how combat works, is very thorough and well thought out.

However I would hesitate to call combat remotely close to even with sub in arenas. I think to say so would be extremely misleading.


No matter how you slice it, sub's mobility is better. Shadowstep is a 25 second CD and can be reset with prep. Sprint resets with prep, even if it doesn't break roots. Two vanishes, each of which also clear roots. Lets not forget two dismantles, and prep resetting kick too.

Smoke bomb is probably the most powerful ability in a rogue's arsenal, and sub rogues get two. If they choose to, they can use one after another.

Like combat, sub doesn't do much damage unless it is using a big cooldown (shadowdance) but combining shadowdance and smoke bomb is extremely lethal. I'm sure smokebomb + the long kidney + killing spree is dangerous too, but not in the same way. A sub rogue's kidney is long enough to last the whole duration of smoke bomb anyway, and killing spree divides damage up among all targets in range - not ideal when you're trying to kill one guy.

Last, sub rogues are signifigantly harder to kill than combat rogues. So much so that many teams refuse to target sub rogues at all.


So if you choose combat over sub, you drop a huge amount of actual utility for theoretical utility. Improved gouge is cool, improved sprint is cool, blade twisting is cool, but always remember - there is a reason almost every high rated rogue is sub. Naturally it is your choice if you want to play a less mainstream spec, but arena is difficult enough as it is without weakening yourself. You'll see nothing but sub rogues, and they will have the advantage.

P.S - I would be much more willing to accept combat as being viable in 2s than 3s, but 2s is a rather silly bracket anyway.
