No matter how you slice it, sub's mobility is better. Shadowstep is a 25 second CD and can be reset with prep. Sprint resets with prep, even if it doesn't break roots. Two vanishes, each of which also clear roots. Lets not forget two dismantles, and prep resetting kick too.

Smoke bomb is probably the most powerful ability in a rogue's arsenal, and sub rogues get two. If they choose to, they can use one after another.



This is something that, theoretically anyways, is different between both specs. Sub has that Prep advantage, allowing for a quick burst of mobility or control, which is very powerful.

Combat lacks that ability to "burst" mobility or control, but on the other hand has much more sustained control ( once again in theory ) throughout the entire fight, without blowing any CD's on it.

There is no equalling the power of a double smoke bomb, however Combat rogues can, instead of separating the healer from the kill target, lock down the healer for 18+ seconds with a Kidney, Gouge and Silenced kick, every 20 seconds. Downside: You need to be near the healer, have combo points on him, and if he is far away from the kill target, you need to make your way from one to the other to apply CC and then deal damage.

However, you're right that Combat is slow-paced. To be able to do the "same" ammount of damage sub would do in a similar healer lock-out ( through smoke-bomb instead of stuns / CC's ) a Combat rogue would need Bandit's Guile and either Kspree or Adrush. Both of these are on somewhat long cooldowns which usually means it's a one-time thing, if you miss then you don't get a second chance.
Bandit's Guile, very situational, and takes a considerable ammount of time to get to 30% on any one target, let alone if you're target switching.

Again: this is why Combat needs buffs. An increase in combo point generation would allow Restless Blades to do it's job better in PvP scenarios.
That alone would reduce the cooldowns on Kspree and Adrush, making them accessible more often for damage bursts, while also reducing the cooldown on Sprint to increase mobility.
It would also reduce the cooldown on Redirect, which would facilitate target switching, keeping Bandit's Guile up, and better control through redirected kidney shots and dealy throws.

That alone would be a great step in the right direction. I'm hoping if I post it on the forums enough, the Devs will take a hint.

I keep thinking that a combo point generation increase, one way or another, would be quite beneficial to PvP but may be too beneficial to PvE...

Allowing Offensive Finishers or all finishers to trigger Restless Blades would also help, but then combo point generation would still be slow, eviscerate would hardly ever be used ( Eviscerate, which I've noticed hits quite hard when you're spec'd / glyphed for it )

There could be some ability that allows for better combo point generation but that would hardly work enough in PvE to justify spending talent points into it, like one of my ideas which was kind of made up of different ideas and some old talents:

Move Reinforced Leather to be one of Combat's perks, no talent points need to be invested into this. In it's place, make a talent that would bring back the old Setup and Unfair Advantage, while introducing a mechanic to reduce the cooldown of evasion that would work like Restless Blades:
Every time you dodge, the cooldown on Evasion is reduced by 1.5 / 3 seconds, you strike at opponent dealing 100% main hand weapon damage and every time you dodge or fully resist a spell, you gain one combo point on your active target. This effect cannot happen more than once per second.

This would bring forth Blizzard's view of combat as being the Toe-to-Toe spec that can deal with plate melees better than other specs, while leaving it's combo point generation slow on ranged classes on who the Kick Silence and Deadly Throw already deal a lot of pressure.
