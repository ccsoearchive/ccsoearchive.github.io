Hey forum.

So something i'm wondering about (not for my own sakes, just curious) is if it is generally accepted to roleplay a race you're NOT. Now there are a LOT of races out there, and a lot of combinations and things in between. So the question is if it makes sense to roleplay a race that you might look nothing like.

Now i'm not referring to half-breeds. There are plenty of those and they're usually accepted. I could go all day long listing how many half-elves or half-ogres or half-orcs and so on and so forth. But these are not, in the end, races i'm referring to (although it'd still be ok to hear comments on these races).

A quick list of races that people cannot usually get their models to look like (except in some situations with potions or costumes).

-Ogres
-Murlocs
-Jungle Trolls (can KINDA be done if you're skin is green...but, still)
-Dragons (yeah yeah yeah I know how you all feel about dragons, but they're there)
-Half-breed races (in some cases (such as a half-ogre) a different model might be logically required)
-Wolvar
-Naga
-Tuskar
-Various types of undead (ghouls, zombies, abominations, ghost, liches, etc etc)
-(The bird-men from outland...can't think of the name off the top of my head)
-Broken
-Various demons (again I know how you feel about them...but hey, they exist)
-So on and so forth

So forum- if you make it QUITE clear that you are one of these races despite the lack of a model to say so, is it ok/does it make sense?

Begin discussing!
(gonna add more races as I think of em)

I'm going to disagree with both Galethria and Callatham.

While some restrictions should apply, I see no reason why people could not play some of the plausible races with the limited resources we have. For example: ogres, there are resources in game that change the appearance of a character into an ogre, off the top of my head I know of the Gordok Ogre Suit from tailoring and the Carved Ogre Idol, I believe it's called, from the TCG loot.

Now, does playing an ogre make you grossly overpowered like playing a dragon would? No. In fact, ogres generally just have large physical strength and endurance going for them, similar to the tauren. Can a single ogre level Orgrimmar? No. Is it hard to visually display the fact you are an ogre for more than 20 seconds or so at a time? No.

Now for some of the races listed, then you should go into the consideration of whether or not it will be a challenge to pull off (see: Naga). Then the power of the character comes into question (see: Dragon/Demon). After those are taken into consideration it should then be decided whether or not you'd be skilled enough to pull it off.

After all is said and done, the character races we're given does give us a good... base, it gives the newer RPers a good stepping stone. But it is just silly to say that we should be confined by them, granted it does take as much skill to make an interesting character just out of the models we're given, but the reward from roleplaying an ogre warlord, or a Blood Elf Demon Hunter, and being complimented on how you play these races, classes that are not playable would to me be more fulfilling.

Besides, as we all know, someone can play their character as a human mage, and make him as strong as the Titans themselves. So, it isn't the character concept. It isn't the race, it's the person controlling the character that is a problem.

No, I advise against all those races.

If your roleplay hinges on a transformation item, it's a good idea not to do it. To parrot Palehoof, "my [can] know that there are transformation items out there, and he may distinguish that you are wearing a disguise rather than in a true form."

If your roleplay relies on a story of how you had to become accepted into the Horde/Alliance, because your race is a kill on sight for most or all guards and probably have a record of hostility to deserve that, don't do that either. If anything, be an ambassador or visitor.

And if your roleplay only functions with the use of an addon, you also have a problem. What if I'm on a different computer that doesn't have FlagRSP, or TRP2 got corrupted and I haven't re-downloaded it? Well I guess that means I'll miss the fact your a Val'kyr and not a Blood Elf DK, or that you aren't a real person but you're really a statue. Or a real extreme is when people go overboard with their GHI buffs, or make their own set of spells with "unique" mechanics shared in their MRP.

TL;DR: When your RP only "works" when reliant on some specific thing, something could be wrong and your defense of your character could be seen as flimsy.

It comes down to your audience. The few times I RP in-game, I'm looking for random encounters and don't want to have to keep a notepad or something to remind me that the person playing the gnome wants me to perceive his character as a dragon or a vrykul. The same goes for the myriad of other non-playable races in the game. I assume you're playing the character I see on my screen. If you have an RP add-on that I can access, I'll make an effort to get some details from it.

But when it becomes more of hassle keeping up with who is what, usually I'll just walk away. There are plenty of others RP'ers out there who don't expect others to jump through hoops to interact with their character.

Now, if we're normally RP'ing together and there's an ongoing storyline happening, then it becomes a bit easier to see your character for what you want it to be. But that takes time to develop.

Know your audience. Telling me repeatedly what your toon is, isn't going to work for me. It might work for a group you normally RP with. It might even work with some other random folks. If you wish to RP this way, just understand that some in the audience might not care to participate. Accept that and continue to have fun with those who do want to RP with you.
