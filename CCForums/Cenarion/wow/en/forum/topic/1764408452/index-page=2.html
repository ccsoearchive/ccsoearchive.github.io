Game mechanics =/= Lore

That's a very true axiom, but when you're RP'ing in-game, game mechanics begin to play into part of the immersive atmosphere. As more then a few people have mentioned, when you keep having to remind your audience that what they see on their screens isn't what they're 'supposed' see, then the entire situation becomes more of a hassle, then an enjoyable experience. The in-game part of RP looses its seamless integration into the game-verse.

My character walks around a street and see's people with his/her own eyes. What I see on the screen, is what I assume people are to be. I don't want to consult a notepad to understand that this person's dwarf is really a human/gnome hybrid.

Perhaps with a group of friends, in an ongoing story, that will work. For random encounters, I just don't see it having much of a success rate. To me, it almost seems selfish to expect RP'ers not to assume that the characters on the streets of Stormwind aren't the race that they appear to be.

I don't RP in-game with that level of expectation, regardless of how often you want to remind me, or whether the 'special' race you want your character to be is in all-caps in your MyRolePlay profile.
