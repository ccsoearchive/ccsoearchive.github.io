The other day I was in Org, just doing my sales and rep my gear. Some one screams troll in the tail. I figure I'll go watch for kicks and giggles.

What I say ICly wise was just amazing.. Kor'kron runs a goblin through with his sword.. for trying to make a Dark Spear go back to their part of their city. Now after running a blade through the goblin what does he do? Kindly asks the troll to go back to his part of the city. This here is an example of fail.

Now abuse is a far more touchy item to point out. A level 8 - lets say 60 character. Claiming to be one of the elite of the orc guard. The reason I went as high as 60 is because I've seen death knights saying they are Kor'kron.

Yes I know the saying level should not play a role in RP. But when a person has spent years building their character.. their story their reasons.. and some one spends 2 days leveling an alt with a wordy MRP.. that annoys me.

Now you can tell me I QQ to much, but honestly... for the sake of our servers RP integrity.. People who RP being the elite of the orcish guard.... should at least need to be 80 or higher. I'm tired of lvl 22 chars telling mine what to do.

Also if your going to jump on me for being a DK I RPed him once in Org, and like a good little prisoner he did as he was told. He is a remake of my shaman.. I wont bother you with the story but blah blah blah.. I leveled him.. hes now a DK so im leveling him too.

Kor'kron RPers should remember that the most important part of RP is consent. The only way to construct a compelling conflict is if both parties are involved.

"But we're a gang of thugs in a city on lockdown!" some might argue. "Wouldn't it make sense for us to go around abusing our power?"

Yes, it would, in any other story. But we need to make exceptions for the medium. This is a cooperative storytelling. If not everyone is cooperating, then it becomes hostile and no one wants to play.
