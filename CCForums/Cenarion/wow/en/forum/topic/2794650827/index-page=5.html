Galaire and Clawfyre had retreated a short distance from the fighting around the time when the Warlock turned himself into a demon. The two were in sight of the fight, watching as the fight went on and on. Galaire was a good ten feet away from Clawfyre, and Clawfyre was in his human form since he wasn't fighting anyone. He scratched a spot on his chin while the paladin cast Hammer of Wrath.
"So we wait until they kill each other?" asked Clawfyre.
"The warlock wanted a honorable fight" said Galaire.
"Say, what was that all about back in Stormwind?" asked Clawfyre. "back when...."
"I don't want to talk about it" said Galaire through clenched teeth. Galaire then sat on a wide tree trunk and watched the fight between the warlock and the paladin. Galaire looked up in time to see the mage fly around on her dragon, freezing undead targets on the ground. Clawfyre sat down beside Galaire, and Galaire glanced back at him but said nothing.
When the mage drove the drake to take the paladin in its claws, Galaire sighed.
"Back to Dalaran?" asked Clawfyre.
"That would be a good guess" said Galaire. "I hope you have another mage friend since our friend mage has flown away"
"As a matter of fact..." said Clawfyre, motioning Galaire to follow him. "I do have a mage friend who'll help us get to Dalaran"

Dalaran, The Ledgeremain Lounge


Galaire knew that the warlock was here. Since Dalaran forbade any fight between the Alliance and Horde, she wouldn't pull anything on the warlock or his companions. She and Clawfyre had gone back to the base camp, where a gnome mage created a portal that led them both to Dalaran. At first, Galaire wanted to go to the Underbelly to get some poisons for her weapons but decided to have a good meal at the lounge first. She was sitting at a table, eating from a plate of two turkey legs and a mug of ale. Clawfyre was sitting with her, eating from a plate that had a large helping of tundra berries and a couple of turkey and chicken legs. Clawfyre said that he grew to like the tundra berries that grew all along Borean. Galaire just nodded absently and glanced around to see a pair of Tauren enter the lounge, speaking in their own language to each other as they walked in.
"Excuse me" said a voice. Galaire turned around to see a young man dressed as a courier. "is one of you 'Galaire Moonwalker' ?"
"I am" said Galaire with a brow raised. Few people knew her last name. Very few people.
"I have a letter for you" said the courier, handing over a letter to Galaire and walking off to a table of three blood elf males to deliver more letters.
"Moonwalker....?" asked Clawfyre. "I've heard of that name before..."
Galaire ignored Clawfyre and broke the seal of the letter. She read the letter. It was written in fancy lettering.

My Friend Galaire,

I'm sure you didn't expect to hear from me again. First, let me tell you that I'm alive and well and have moved back to Ironforge. I didn't write you before because I couldn't find out where you were, even in Teldrassil....do you know how many Moonwalkers there are? Anyway, I have to confess that I'm a Death Knight now.....after I was captured, I was tortured for information and eventually killed and brought back to life as a Death Knight by the Lich King. I believe I was among the last Death Knights to have been made by Arthas before he fell. When Arthas died, I joined the Ebon Blade and ended up in one of the Plaguelands, going into Stratholme because I heard a rumor about a possible 'cure' for the Death Knights. The 'cure' was a lie of course. Anyway, meet me in Ironforge sometime and we'll have a drink to old friends.

--Fizbinn Stromander

Galaire's eye went wide after she read the letter. Fizbinn is still alive...... after a fashion. Galaire smiled.

Clawfyre had come back from the madness that all worgen fall prey too. He was no longer a wild worgen, attacking everything that happened to be near him. He had been saved from that madness....that uncontrolable urge to attack whoever was unlucky enough to cross his path. Clawfyre had no memory whatsoever after he became a worgen....he had no idea what he had done, where he had roamed or how many people he had killed that dared to hunt him down. It was a big blank void. And it was probably for the best that he had forgotten. He soon chose a new name and embraced his fate. He was something more than human.
When the worgen was pulling out of Gilneas, Clawfyre had made a friend of a night elf named M'Renien Moonwalker. She was a druid of some renown and she was friendly to Clawfyre now that they were allies.
Not every human that became a worgen escaped that madness, even when given that special potion. The ship that Clawfyre had been on to Darkshore was also carrying cages of wild worgen that would take the potion once they had made it to Darkshore.


Clawfyre suddenly remembered the name Moonwalker when that courier had given that letter to Galaire.
"Your sister was M'Renien......" muttered Clawfyre.
Galaire looked up from the letter she had been reading from and looked at Clawfyre. If looks could kill....
"Don't you dare bring my sister into this" said Galaire, her voice all but growling.
"I knew your sister...." said Clawfyre. "I know why you hate us worgen now.......you....."
" YOU KNOW NOTHING!" yelled Galaire, so loudly that other patrons looked around. " ALL OF YOU WORGEN ARE NOTHING BUT.... " Galaure cut her sentence short, looking as though words had failed her. She stood up and stalked out of the Lounge. Clawfyre looked after Galaire in shock for a couple of moments before he went after her.
Clawfyre walked up and down the streets of Dalaran, even going to the Underbelly and the Violet Citadel but he couldn't find Galaire anywhere. Clawfyre cursed himself that he had said anything out loud, much less blurted out that he knew Galaire's sister............Galaire's dead sister.
Clawfyre then went to the Silver Enclave to ask the others if they had seen Galaire and tell them what just happened.
