One week later

Ashok looked at his newly polished and repaired armor and sighed. It had been a week since he had seen anyone from the Recluse or the warlock. Ashok knew that it was time for him to travel the other continent and hoped to find himself again. He had lost himself to his darker side and decided it was time to travel. He already had his course mapped out it would head to Teldrassil and stop in on the Temple of Elune to hopefully meet with several priestess to help him, with his darker side. He then would travel to Darkshore then to Ashenvale, through Stonetalon and into Desolace. His plan would have him end his journey in Uldum hopefully finding some sort of employment on Kalimdor.

He knew what he was doing, he was exiling himself from the Alliance. It was the least he could do, after losing himself to his lust for vengeance. It was a dark path and one that one other Paladin had followed. 'And look what happened to him, he lost everything that was important to him and his love is still alive,' Ashok thought bitterly. He shook off the dark image and pulled out a picture that he always kept close to his heart, it was a picture of Liz, it smiled at it and ran his right thumb over the face. He sighed, he needed to heal himself and knew that she would expect that of him. So he went through his ritual of arming himself, praying to the Light and blessing his weapons. While in Dalaran, Ashok had a new rune inscribed upon his blade, it said "Never forget." It was a reminder of her, and of what he once was and striving to be once again.

Once Ashok was armed grabbed his pack, and headed outside where Leopold, his gryphon, waited. He mounted him and bid farewell to Northrend once and for all, he hoped. Heading towards Valiance Keep, Ashok hoped that he would be able to go back to the Alliance and reclaim his lost title in the future, for he knew that he would have to fight for them soon enough.

A fortnight later

Teaching had never been his strong suit, but since following in Tazmahn's footsteps and becoming one of the Undercity's warlock masters, he had found a new purpose in life.

"Fel energy is unlike any other, young wizards. If improperly controlled, it will corrupt the user into its service, forcing him to do its bidding and creating an addiction...my apologies to our Blood Elf friend in the corner there." The Felweaver nodded at a dark-haired young elf, who had become crestfallen on the mention of addiction. "But, for those that master it, the energies of the Twisting Nether supply a limitless amount of power, the path to which is blocked only by your unwillingness to try new things. Now let us commence our first lesson: Immolation."

The lesson proceeded smoothly until a Troll apprentice by the name of Zandi almost burnt his hand off. He sent him to the Apothecaries' Quarter, hoping that they would heal the hand instead of chopping it off. It was always a toss-up at the Undercity aid room.

"Alright, that will be it for the day. Extra credit for tomorrow if someone rights a two-scroll paper explaining what that bumbling troll did wrong and how to correct it."

Packing up after class, the warlock noticed two Kor'kron guards heading towards him. As they got closer, he recognized him as the two who had barred his entrance to Grommash Hold a month or so ago.

"Well, got re-assigned, did we?" he chuckled as they got closer.

"Bite your tongue, bone-bag...and yes," the larger one mumbled.

"We have summons for you from the Banshee Queen. She requests your presence immediately."

"Very well. Allow me to finish packing my things, and I will be along shortly."

Arriving in Sylvanas' chambers, he kneeled in front of the Dark Lady. "I have come as you have bidden me, my Queen. What do you ask of your humble servant."

"Rise, Maccabeus Felterror. One of your stature need not kneel before me. But, I have need for you once again. Our agents in the Reliquary have discovered Titan technology in Uldum related to the Curse of Flesh. I ask that you journey there and discern what you can about it, for our Apothecaries feel it may aid our development of a new strain of plague particularly effective against dwarves and gnomes."

"Of course M'lady. May I ask, though, why you are sending a Warlock and not a team of apothecaries down there? Surely they have more expertise on the subject than I," questioned Maccabeus.

"You are a learned man, Master Felterror. I am sure that whatever is down there is not beyond your mental capabilities. Furthermore, the fighting skills of the apothecaries do not begin to rival your own, and we are not sure who, or what, for that matter, is guarding this technology," Sylvanas explained. "We have procured lodgings for you at a recently opened establishment by a Goblin Death Knight called Sam's Bar and Grill. You are expected in three days. Make haste!"

"Your will be done, my Queen." And with that, he bowed again and exited.

Uldum. He had been through there once as he fought his way through the regions affected by the Cataclysm, and once more as he vanquished Al'Akir. He didn't particularly favor the heat much, but at least it was a dry heat, which actually prevented decay. Though it was not as if he had a choice in the matter. Those were his orders, and he would see them through.
