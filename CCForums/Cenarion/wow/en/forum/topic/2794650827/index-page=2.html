After a short nap at her campsite, Galaire gathered her stuff and remounted the horse--whom she decided to call Blackshadow since she had a time seeing the horse in the night even with her superior elf vision. She had only slept for about two hours and felt well-rested after her nap. She rode past the Horde outpost and soon entered Sholazar Basin. She stayed to the road for the most part, heading for the hunter's base camp.
While she rode on, she kept on looking all around as if expecting an attacking to come to her at any moment. No attack came and she rode on and on until she came to the base camp....which was deserted as expected. She dismounted Blackshadow and hitched him into a post nearby. Once that was done, Galaire seemingly vanishes from sight due to her rogue skills and her racial ability to blend into the night. She started to head towards one of the towering Pillars that was in the Basin.
At one point, Galaire heard a horse running from somewhere to her left. When she glanced aside, she saw Ashok riding on a paladin's charger. The paladin went speeding off in the same direction that Galaire was going in. Perfect. She would meet the Paladin somewhere up ahead.

Maccabeus stood high upon the pillar, gazing down upon the milling masses of elementals and undead. The he saw him. A pillar of light cutting through the roiling cauldron of walking corpses. The warlock's withered upper lip curled into a smile. He decided to look more the part of warlock than Horde agent today, and quickly summoned his Dreadsteed, Horror, to him. The demon horse whinnied and dashed off through the field, incinerating the zombies as it streaked by them.

The Paladin had been efficient, creating a fairly large arena for them to play in, though the Warlock had no intention of sticking to the boundaries drawn by the holy knight. Boundaries always favored close quarters fighters versus those who preferred to pelt their enemies from range. Slowing Horror to a saunter, the demoniac entered the clearing.

"Hello, Paladin," Maccabeus called from across the field. "I do not believe we have been properly introduced. I am known as Maccabeus Felterror. An adopted name of course, but one that has suited me well since I was turned to undeath."

Ashok slowly opened his eyes and turned toward the warlock. He seemed different now than when before in the Recluse, more calm and collected. A somewhat terrifying figure sitting astride a massive black charger. His sword Ragnarok humming with power his shoulders glowing with power. "Warlock, I am Ashok Longshadow, former Paladin Defender of the Alliance, and Defender of a Shattered World. I care not for your quips and chosen name. I am simply here to end your miserable existence." His voice was chilling, filled with power and rage yet completely calm and collected. He waited for a response from the warlock, looking down at Ragnarok and feeling it hunger for combat.

'Even if this is the end, I will make him suffer.' Ashok thought. He had become the cold calculated destroyer that he was in the Northrend campaign, the same version of himself that had watched his companions die on this frozen continent, the same person Tyrion warned him against becoming. It was all too late to turn back. Maccabeus would be dead, or Ashok would.
