Sighing as Mara left the room, Ashok pulled the sheets back on the bed and pulled off the bandages off hoping to heal some of his less grevious wounds. He noticed that his legs survived mostly unscathed and turned to set them over the edge of the bed. He took a quick breath and slowly stood, smiling as he realized that walking would not be a huge issue. The serious injuries he noticed were to his left shoulder and side of his body. He sighed realizing that in his attempts to kill the warlock he had almost used his body in an attempt to achieve that goal.

Ashok cast a couple quick Holy Lights to help the wounds heal faster than went to grabbed some of his Dense Embersilk Bandages and bound the wounds. He then healed the lesser injuries allowing the Light to close them up and bring his body to not look so broken. The ribs he knew would require at least a week to properly set and sighed. 'If only I hadn't been so careless' Ashok thought.

Ashok then grabbed a clean white silk shirt out of the dresser and buttoned it up 3/4s of the way. Taking his time to work his left arm into the shirt and get it over his shoulder. He then found a pair of tan cloth pants that he pulled on. Looking half-way decent, Ashok threw on a pair of sandals that he saw laying on the ground and looked around for something he could use to make a sling. He found it in a length of rope laying on top of the table. He then tied it up to where he could sling his left arm to keep him from moving it too much.

Being satisfied with his appearance, Ashok grabbed his mug of mead and headed downstairs to see who all was still here.

Dragonblight.....near Wyrmrest Temple



Galaire had left Dalaran by teleporting herself down to Crystalsong Forest. Once in the forest, Galaire merely picked a direction and went in that direction, little caring of where she might wind up. All she wanted to do was to get away from Clawfyre and all worgen. She had thought she would get over those memories of the past, but clearly she couldn't get over them.
Galaire had no mount either, something of a big no-no in the frozen wastes of Northrend. That was how upset she truly had been....she hadn't been thinking clearly to get a mount and ride whereever the wind would take her. It was a miracle she had made it to Dragonblight without getting into trouble. Little did she know that she was heading right for Wyrmrest Temple. She couldn't see much of anything because there was a very high wind kicking up snow and making visibility be downright zero. She was dressed for cold weather, sure, but even that wouldn't stop her from freezing to death.
Finally, Galaire saw a shape on the horizon. It was a building of some sort. As she walked closer to the building, her strength was sapping from her body. She made another five steps before falling to the ground and the darkness took her.


"I think she's coming around" said a male voice. Galaire opened her eyes to see a rather tall blood elf male dressed in the robes of the Kirin Tor standing over her. She was wrapped under several heavy blankets and seem to be in room open to the air....the room was warmed by huge braziers around the room. She was lying on a soft mattress. She looked again at the blood elf and suddenly realize who he must be.
"Krasus?" asked Galaire, choosing to use the dragon's name when he was in his elven guise. She had met a few members of the Red Dragonflight before....but certainly not the consort of the Dragonqueen herself. She had seen Krasus a few times in Dalaran, always at a distance.
"Yes, I am Krasus" said Krasus. "we found you outside our temple a couple hours ago. I had feared the worst"
Galaire tried to raise up from the bedding, but Krasus pushed her back down. "just relax here for a bit. You should rest more before you do anything else"
Just my luck.... thought Galaire. I end up at the Wyrmrest Temple of all places. But not the worse place to be at certainly.
