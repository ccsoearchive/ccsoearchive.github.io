The pre-dawn air was sticky and wet, and a heavy dew hung in the air as three wind riders arrived at the River's Heart in quick succession. One figure was clad in frigid plate armor, with two runeblades that hung menacingly at his side. The next was wreathed in mail, tall and lanky, and with a rune-scribed shield on his back. The third was cloaked in robes of the shadowflame, the very same energies that the black dragon Nefarian commanded. His dagger hung at his waist opposite and thick tome, both of which glowed with unholy energies.

"We are convened," said Maccabeus.

"I am ready, brother," intoned the Death Knight

The Shaman nodded. "As am I."

"Good. Now, I am an honorable warlock, or as close to honorable as a warlock can get. I agreed to the rules of this engagement, and I intend to abide by them as long as my opponents do the same. What I require of you two is to mount up and follow me through jungle, flying above the canopy. I will shoot a red flare in the air if the rules have been disregarded, which is your cue to fly in and assist me."

Maccabeus looked at them hard. "If they are not broken, you may not assist me in any capacity." They both started to protest, but the demoniac cut them off. "No, I will not scar my honor by calling for help in a fair fight. If I lose, it is my own fault. However, If I am captured, do break me out before I am taken to Stormwind and executed by that pig-headed Varian Wrynn. I will accept defeat, but not capture. Understood."

"Aye, brother."

"Ya mon."

"Good, then I will make my way towards the Shattered Pillar. The plethora of undead there should confuse the paladin's senses and give me the upper hand."

Dalaran...many years ago


The floating mage city of Dalaran amazed Galaire. She had gotten here through the help of a mage at an inn at Howling Fjord. While walking the busy streets of Northrend, she was approached by a gnome rogue. The gnome rogue had bumped into her and muttered apologies while he walked away, soon lost in the crowd. Galaire discovered that the gnome had placed a letter in her hands when he had bumped into her leg. She opened the letter. The letter called for any and all rogues to come to the Underbelly for a daring campaign against the Lich King. Perked by this news, Galaire had traveled down to the sewers of Dalaran and soon found what she had been looking for. It was a meeting of several rogues, conducted by a member of SI:7.
The mission was from SI:7, the rogue guild of Stormwind and it was a bold one. They would sneak into Icecrown Citadel and spy on the Lich King's forces. The mission would likely take several months. It was at this meeting where Galaire met Fizbinn, the gnome rogue that she had ran into earlier. She made friends with the gnome and decided to look over each other.
Breaking into the Citadel was a tricky one, but not impossible. It did take several months to figure out anything useful. Galaire grew to know and ins and outs of the citadel, perhaps better than the Lich King himself. Today, she could probably walk blindfolded and find her way through the citadel. It was also a costly venture, for several members of SI:7 were caught and tortured for information....but nobody appeared to have talked, elsewise the mission would've been a failure. Fizbinn was a good friend to have around.


Present Day...

Galaire made her way to Sholazar Basin on the black horse she had gotten earlier. She had just passed the gnomish airstrip and making good time. She started to wonder where the others from the Recluse where at now.
Suddenly, she came across a dead body on the road. She had dismounted to get a closer look. It was the body of a worgen mage by the looks of his clothing. The worgen had several gashes torn across his body and a thin blood trial led away from the road towards what looked to be a cave. The dead body was stone cold, so it had happened hours before. As she looked over the body, Galaire saw a little bundle lying next to the body. As she moved closer to the bundle, the bundle suddenly opened to reveal a night elven toddler covered from head to toe in blood. The toddler's eyes had been ripped out, as well as most of the head. The toddler stood within the bundle of blanket and looked at Galaire with its arms wide. "Momma?" Startled, Galaire backed up a dozen or so steps. The toddler walked out of the bundle, despite the fact that the toddler had no feet. As it walked towards Galaire, the voice of the toddler took on the voice of a death knight. "Momma, is that you?"
"NO!" yelled Galaire, backing up even more with eyes wide until she tripped over a rock on the side of the road. She instantly sprang back up. The toddler was gone. The body of the dead worgen was still there. The horse looked at Galaire with a look of concern.
"I'm.....alright" said Galaire, her voice shaky. She mounted the horse again and shot forward to the Basin and the warlock.

Near Bor'gorok Outpost


Galaire decided to rest for a bit. It was late in the afternoon when she had rode from Valiance Keep, and now it was approaching early evening. She could see the Horde outpost known as Bor'gorok on the horizon. She knew better than to head for the outpost and look for an inn....she doubted she would make it in the walls before the guards cut her down. She had dismounted from her black horse and ate some flatbread she had gotten earlier. It wasn't the most delicious food, but it was better than nothing. She wrapped herself up in the fur cloak she had also brought at Valiance Keep. The horse snorted and ate the grass that grew along the road.
Galaire had been to Sholazar Basin only once, when she was on her way back to Valiance Keep after the Lich King had fallen to get back to Teldrassil. She had traveled on a night elven ship back to her homeland and there she stayed for a time before going to Stormwind and, eventually, to the Recluse.
Galaire took out her map of Sholazar Basin and studied it, planning her search for the warlock. She decided to begin her search at the Nesingwary Base Camp and then circle outward until she had found the warlock. And then what? The warlock had spells while she didn't, but she could sneak up on him....she was sure if she had catch him unawares, she would take him down with ease. Warlocks had very poor armor that would be easily defeated by her swords.
A sudden noise made her look up from the map. It was a horn blowing that came in the direction of the Horde outpost. While she was looking at the map, dusk had truly fallen and night was on its way fast. The horn was probably the signal for a shift change in the patrol or some such thing. Galaire then led the horse to a somewhat sheltered area near the road. Then she began to make up camp, building a small campfire and cooking a spit of chicken over it. Nights in Northrend were cold indeed.....but not as cold as it was all the way up to Icecrown and the Storm Peaks. As soon as she finished putting up her small tent, the food was ready to eat.

The warlock was getting impatient. it had been four days, and his trigger finger was itchy. "They must not value the lives of their compatriots very much. Three days, and Surwich will fall."

Othrys and Tazmahn, to their credit, had stuck around for the entire ordeal. The comradery helped the situation, as they each recounted their adventures for each other, and ran makeshift competitions to pass the time. They spent the nights camped in atop the the Shattered Pillar, with one standing guard against the angered elementals and ferocious undead, and possibly an Alliance attack.

He resumed his vigil on the surrounding areas, looking for falling swathes of monsters beneath the Paladin's war-hammer. He heard the clatter of bony wings beside him, and saw Othrys dismounting and running over to him.

"I saw a human paladin flying in from the direction of Icecrown. He came down over the Avalanche, but he has not spotted us yet. Good thinking to camouflage ourselves amongst these ravenous dead."

Maccabeus smiled and nodded. So, the Paladin had come. Good. He had been getting restless.

"I must make haste with the final preparations. Alert our shaman friend if he does not already know, and then stay off-station. You remember the signal?"

"Aye, brother. Very well. Good luck."

They clasped hands in a sign of familial solidarity, and parted ways.

The warlock headed to his camp and unwrapped a rather large bundle. Inside contained his gladiatorial vestments, won with his own blood and sweat during the most recent season. Though he would lose some of his power, it was worth it to gain any resistance he could get against the coming Alliance.

Only one thing left to do. The warlock was a man of many talents. He had studied under the fel masters of Azeroth, including one who claimed to be a disciple of Gul'dan himself. In that time, he had learned to partition his mind to accommodate two separate sets of skills. It took brief, but intense meditation to access one half or the other, but, finally, after years of practice, he was finally able to master the technique. This he did now, summoning up his knowledge of the second branch of warlockery, Demonology. He felt the second half of his brain engage, and with it came a flood of new knowledge and spells, different ways to manipulate the shadowy and chaotic energies of the Twisting Nether.

Trading his life force for mana until he was almost dead, he then began to consume souls from the nearby area, forming a purple vortex of the lost around him, until he was once again in fighting shape. He created, once again, a healthstone and a soulstone, though he did not think he would need either.

Lastly, he began a summoning ritual, though this was no simple ritual to summon an Imp or even a Felhunter. This was dangerous, for he was attempting to summon one of the Burning Legion's shock troops: a Felguard. The ground beneath him cracked as he forced it to give up the remaining energies he needed to complete the ritual. A dark cloud of purple smoke accumulated over his head, and, thrusting his hands skyward, he opened the portal through which he would pull the demon.

"By your true name, I bind you to my will...Shaashak!"

The brutal demon stepped through the portal, clutching a blood-soaked battle-axe in its drake-heart sized fist.

"WHO DARES SUMMON ME!" the demon bellowed.

"I do, Shaashak. I have need for your services once again. You see, there is this Paladin..."

The Felguard's eyes narrowed at the mention of its natural enemy.

"Say no more," Shaashak intoned, its voice warped by fel energy. "I will take pleasure in eradicating this bug."

"Good. I thought you might. He has just flown into the area. We will allow him 6 hours to find us."
