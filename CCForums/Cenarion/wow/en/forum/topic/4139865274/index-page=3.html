They are mental, but not because of that! It's actually a smart move. Consider what we were looking at:

Mut's current rotation is Mutilate OR Backstab -> Rupture (20s glyphed) and Envenom. With the exception of the loss of Ruthlessness and 4 extra seconds on Rupture, it isn't changing.

Sub's current rotation is Backstab OR Hemo -> SnD (21s + glyph + mastery), Recuperate (30s), and Eviscerate. MoP's rotation dropped Recuperate and pushed SnD's duration to 45 seconds!!! That is comparatively incredibly simplistic. Sub needed another finished added in and/or a reduced SnD duration. It also needed a shorter SnD duration due to Energetic Recovery moving from Recuperate (30s for 5cp) to SnD (then 45s for 5cp).

It's actually a pretty good move that should help preserve Sub PvE's challenge.

No, there should still be a large number of Step and BoS Rogues. It'll depend a lot on type of PvP (world, arena, random, or rated BG) as well as skill level and probably the composition of the team and their chosen talents.

Step and BoS will be particularly useful for Rogues who will get kited a lot, like Paralytic builds. It's easy to speculate now "All Rogues will go Prep!" but hard to really know how it will turn out. Close, but not exactly. The suggestion was to keep Prep, and offer two alternatives. The three could be put in a tier of the talent tree (you get one of the three), or each in a different spec. The alternatives would modify the same talents as Prep, but not work the same way. They could simply shorten cool-downs (ala old style Elusiveness), reward a player for certain actions (ala Restless Blades), increase ability durations (Your Sprint now lasts 50% longer), or increase the power/usefulness of abilities (your Vanish now allows you to dodge incoming attacks).

The existence of alternatives that every Rogue would have, not matter their spec or talent choices would allow Prep to be balanced ... or so goes our hypothesis.

Blizzard has other ideas. We're not privy to those ideas, and it is their game and their profit/loss to make.
