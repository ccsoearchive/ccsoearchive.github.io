This thread is going to be a running update of the in-game RP "The Lost Clan." It will be updated on the forums not only for the use of the members of the main group RP, but also for anyone who wishes to interact with these characters and be a part of their story.

The main plot of the RP is as follows. Around twenty years ago, during the time when most orcs were still imprisoned in internment camps, one clan was wiped out and stricken from orcish history, with the exception of a few babies who were separated and given to different camps/situations (as lore-appropriate!). These babes were never told of their heritage, but all had been given the clan's brand at birth. Few orcs are left who remember what the brand means, and even fewer who are foolish enough to speak of it.

This brings us to the present-day, when a 22-year-old orc warrior posts the following notice on a board in Orgrimmar:

DO YOU HAVE A BRAND ON YOUR RIGHT SHOULDER AND DO NOT KNOW WHAT IT MEANS? I MAY HAVE ANSWERS. COME TO THE VALLEY OF TRIALS AND MAKE SURE YOUR BRAND IS EXPOSED. I WILL COME TO YOU.

12/7/11 Meeting

One by one, Egar approached four young orcs in the Valley of Trials and furtively instructed them to meet him in Sen'Jin Village. Knowing that his note had been public, he covered his own brand and did his best not to be seen speaking to the others in the Valley. He found four other orcs, all around his age, bearing the mark.

Darmu - a mild-mannered male hunter with a recalcitrant boar.
Rintu - a level-headed female mage, blunt but fair.
Kagorian - an angry female shaman still haunted by the orcs' shared history of oppression.
Mirchi - an amiable female warlock with a particularly difficult and moody imp.

Mirchi and Darmu hit it off well with Egar from the start. Although Rintu and especially Kagorian seemed reluctant to trust Egar or to accept that he might be a clan brother, they were willing to cooperate in the search for information about the mysterious symbol, if only to know more about their own histories.

Egar left the meeting discouraged by the strife already brewing within the group but still secretly thinking of them as clan siblings and determined to find answers by whatever honorable means he could find it.

Rintu threw another pebble in to the lake she sat beside, watching the ripples expand ever outward.

Rintu was an Orc. She knew this. Of course she knew this. As if she could ever forget. Even if the echos of the cat calls, the jeers, the curses and the yells could somehow be finally stilled in her mind, she had the physical scars to remind her of that fact.
She sighed to herself. Yes Rintu was an Orc. But what did that mean? What did it mean to be an Orc? A free Orc? A real Orc, like those she saw in the city, like those she had met in Sen'Jin Village?

She squeezed the parchment in her hand. She had read the posting her first day visiting Orgrimmar. She had dismissed it out of hand and yet, something tickled in the back of her mind.
She woke the next morning with the parchment in her fist. She immediately knew Nani had put it there. She knew she would have to go. There was no arguing with Nani. She knew things, and she was always right. But Rintu didn't have to like it.

She sighed deeply and threw another rock out across the still water. Watching as the reflected sky shattered into a million sparkly pieces only to ever so slowly melt one by one back together into a smooth picture once more. She knew that feeling. All to well.

The first meeting left her confused, left her with un-answered questions and un-explained feelings. Worse it left her with a headache. The headaches were always bad news. They meant Nani wanted something, and Nani couldn't be denied. Rintu owed her too much. Rintu owed her her life.
She did as Nani wanted, she purposely missed the next meeting with the strange Orcs, She watched from afar, while feelings of jealousy and longing warred inside her. She envied their easy manner, she envied the laughter she heard carried on the wind. Oh how she wanted to rush out waving her arms to join them, to be enfolded in the warm camaraderie. But Nani had forbidden it.
So instead she had followed. She had watched. She had waited. She had wished.

12/12/12 Meeting

Egar made his way to Orgrimmar to register a new clan, though he left the name blank for the time being until he could learn the name of the clan he had been born to and why it was no longer in existence. He ran into the mage Rintu there, explained what he was doing, and was pleased to find that she seemed friendly to him and had not been influenced by Kogorian after all. Egar got Darmu and Rintu to sign his clan paper, and then to his surprise, Kogorian herself showed up with a gracious apology. She herself had run into an elder who had identified the mark as Orcish rather than human, and so her theory had been proven incorrect. She willingly offered herself to the clan, and when Mirchi arrived she added the final signature to the paper.

The newly-formed clan then ventured into the chasm beneath Orgrimmar to clear the place of its latest infestation of Searing Blade warlocks, demons, kobolds and elementals. Expecting problems during their first team effort, Egar was stunned to find that the group worked together seamlessly, each playing his or her role to perfection.
