PUBLIC NOTICE: AAMS ANNOUNCES GUILD LIAISON PROGRAM


The Anytime & Anywhere Messaging Service is pleased to announce its latest public outreach program: guild liaisons within both the Horde and the Alliance!


We're offering members of either faction a unique chance to interact more directly with the world's largest neutral business enterprise. By appointing a specific liaison to a guild we're able to bring the AAMS and its services to you instantly, giving you a point of contact for all your business with us and with the other faction -- as well as access to all our accumulated news and knowledge, as soon as it passes our desk cleared for public dissemination. Here's how it all works!


* You, the officer of a licensed guild, organization, or business, request that a liaison be appointed from the AAMS.
* We assign one of our couriers to be your personal liaison and have a brief meeting to introduce the two of you.
* From then on, the liaison will help to keep your guild informed about any events or messages that might touch on your business, as well as passing along any news of general public interest.
* Should you need the services of the AAMS or wish to contact anyone from the other faction, the liaison provides a convenient contact to ensure that your business gets taken care of promptly.
* In the event of business or conflict between two liaisoned guilds the liaisons from both can assist in mediation or negotiation.


It's our hope that we will eventually be able to incorporate many of the larger guilds into our network, ensuring rapid information-sharing between all of them. Confidential information, of course, remains protected by our same policy of absolute discretion as always -- this is simply a way to ensure that news relevant to you makes it to your guild as quickly as possible!


We plan on contacting several guilds with specific invitations this week. However, this program is open to any registered guild, and anyone may feel free to request a liaison from us at any time. Liaisons will usually touch base with guild leaders or officers once or twice a week, or whenever news of note reaches them.


Contact the AAMS to be introduced to your liaison today!

((Ok roleplaying guilds! We'll have a hand-picked AAMS member check in with your chosen officers a couple of times a week with all the latest in server news, projects that might interest your guild based on its main focus, and any in-character gossip we can pull out of the messages we carry without breaching confidentiality.


Because everyone could use a little more gossip and scandal in their lives.


This is a brand-new project for us, so sign up, and then let us know what improvements you'd like to see! Our hope is that it'll give roleplayers another way to interact with the larger community, and we're always looking for new and better ways to do that. If you're in a roleplaying guild and you haven't heard from us yet, let us know that you're interested and we'll get a liaison signed up for you right away!))

"Message for you, Supervisor!"

At first, the mound of paperwork, crates, boxes, engineering whatsis, undelivered love letters dated back to last February, three sleeping prairie dogs, and half a pumpkin...didn't answer.

"Supervisor?" The new courier eyed the pile. It crouched in the back of the tiny Ratchet office, like a bear using a dwarf's ale cellar for its winter den. Like a bear, it didn't seem to like being disturbed, if the rank smell it was giving off was anything to go by.

The courier swallowed and then called out at the top of her lungs. "Supervisor! Yo-ting-go!"

The left side of the mound began shifting and twitching. The courier shrunk back, expecting some bug-eyed monster to appear, but only a three-fingered troll hand reached out and beckoned at her madly.

The courier stared at it for a long time, then remembered what she was there to do. She cautiously set the message scroll in the hand. Hand and scroll disappeared back into the mess. There was much hmming and crunching, and suddenly, the rest of the troll climbed out.

"Missus Poni wants me ta be a liason?" Yotingo asked, picking packing peanus out of his bright blue mohawk.

The courier nodded faintly. "What-what were you doing in there?"

Yotingo picked up a broken lantern, inspected it, then tossed it into the mess. "Winter office cleaning, mon. After all, I be missin' da fall cleanin'. An' da summer cleanin'. An' da spring cleanin' before dat. I still be havin' da memo here dat told me when ta do it!"

The troll stunk a hand into the mound, dug around, then came up a scrap of paper and half a turtle's wishbone.

"Hm, no, no, dat's da invitation to Liko's weddin'. Where DID it go...?"

"Supervisor!" The courier butted in before the troll could get lost in the office files again. "Your liason job?"

"Oh, right mon! Tell her I'd be glad ta. In fact, I'll head out dere right now!" The troll paused, looking the courier up and down. "Ya be new, aren't ya?"

"Yes, sir. I just signed on yesterday."

"Oh ho!" Yotingo grinned. "Welcome to da AAMS! Don't forget ta pick up your tabard an' uniform, an' da Caer Darrow project be every Thursday. Andelia can be showin' ya how ta work da guild vault machine. Oh, and mon..."

"Y-yes?"

"Ya be havin' prairie dog scat on your shoe."
