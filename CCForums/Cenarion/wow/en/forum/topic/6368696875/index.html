One final flick of the scalpel and the basilisk's eye flew out of its socket with a satisfying "POP!"

Knowles clapped his hands eagerly. "This'un's a'goin' long, Boxxy!"

Almost immediately, a long, slimy tongue lashed out of the dusty crate by the Forsaken's feet. It daintily picked the eye out of the air and disappeared back inside.

Knowles carved another angry tally mark in Boxxy's lid and grumbled: "The heck's that old orc even want with all them eyes anyway?" Then he shouldered his bloody shovel and trudged off to find another victim, the crate close at his heels.

"An' why's he always a'pickin' on me? Ain't like we can't use all'a them bodies I dug up."

Later, Knowles marched through the slums of Shattrath's Lower City on his way to his meeting with Arcanist Adyria. A sporeling refugee tried to sidle up to him as he passed through a throng of people, but a warning growl from Boxxy sent her running.

"'preciate it, Boxxy. Bet she were after them eyes..."

Finally, he found the Arcanist. "Got a delivery, ma'am. From Headmaster Oskor. Where'd y'all want it?"

She motioned toward a chest behind her, and Knowles bent to inspect it. "Well, alright, but I don' reckon them're all gonna fit in there."

He snapped his fingers, and the crate began to cough and retch, before finally disgorging a flood of basilisk eyes that quickly filled the chest as well as the floor around their feet.

Arcanist Adryia gathered up her skirts and took a step back. She clicked her tongue in annoyance. "Lovely. though hardly a drop in the bucket. The Seer shall require much, much more for his elixirs.

A scowl crossed Knowles' face. "About how much more, y'all reckon?"

She waved a hand idly. "Say... seven hundred?"

The Forsaken shook his head. "Better make it seven hundred an' fifty, or I won' never hear the end of it."
