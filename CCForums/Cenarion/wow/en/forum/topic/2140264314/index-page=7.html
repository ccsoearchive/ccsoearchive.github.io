How do you get the keybindings to appear on bartender mine dont show what the hotkeys are. Also how do you change size, and how do u move the chat log from the original spot to the midd like that


in the Bartender config, select a bar, under 'Button Look' at the bottom of the 'General Settings' will be options for display/hide keybinds and macro names.

To change the size go to the 'Positioning' tab for the bar and there is a scale slider

To move the chat frame, right click on the tab marked 'General' and select 'Unlock' from there you can move it wherever you like and resize it using the resize tool in the bottom right corner while the frame is unlocked. Follow the same steps to lock it once you've found a new position/size you like.

Burial's makes me wonder where the dividing line tends to be between people who primarily stick with the default UI (or at least the default layout) and those who get more customizable. Do raiders do more UI customization than folks who run heroics? Do arena players do it more than casual BG players? Do people tend not to begin tinkering much with UI until they're at max level, or does it happen much sooner? Or is it more about the personality of the player (and his/her literacy with UI addons) than anything else?

Welcome back and congratulations again on your shiny new sticky.

Nice to see your UI, I admit it's a little busy for my tastes but not nearly as bad as some I've seen around from friends and guildies.

The only combat text I have msbt track is heals on me and my procs for the most part. But any UI is a good UI if it gets you what you need to see, and feels comfortable without getting in your way.

That's an interesting question I have to say. As an answer I don't think there's any hard and fast. But from the other raiders in my guild, it seems like most (again, I can only extrapolate from the raiders I know) tend to use more add-ons, but leaner UIs, more looking like considerably less. Basically using a lot of add-ons to remove a lot of the extraneous elements from the screen while making the crucial info to a raider blatantly obvious.

PVPers I'm learning (never really PVPed before this season) use what seems like fewer but very key add-on, such as tidyplates and gladius. smaller chat frames, no need for threat meters really. So again, lean allowing focus on you, your team, and the opponents. Situational awareness is important to reacting quickly and properly. Have too much on screen and your eyes might be drawing to all your pretty charts frames, and what have you, and not at the mage casting something big and nasty your way. Like monosodium glutamate. That's bad.

I know I didn't start tinkering with add-ons until max level, I had a few running but I found I didn't need much specific until I got to end-game*

I think there's something to be said for using the default UI or only making minor modifications to it. It does give quite a bit, especially these days, of information in easier and easier ways. I don't even use a raid frame add-on now on this toon for instance. It looks fairly nice, and it does give you the information, if not exactly consolidated. If you aren't raiding, getting competitive with PVP or doing anything else specialized I don't think you absolutely need to mod every element of the interface.

Of course that's all my own experiences and the anecdotal experience of those I know and have seen, so is just a generalization and completely subject to the rule of, "to each their own."


*And of course, now that I use add-ons so much it's the first thing I configure on any new alt, and I find them invaluable to making my life easier.
