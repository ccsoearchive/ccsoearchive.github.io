Now, go! Go and get your frivolous things, fun-seekers.

My furchewah of choice is Ayamiss the Hunter, a beautiful wasp with lively colors, from the Ruins of Ahn'Qiraj. She (a bandit in Elwynn called her "missy") is a great pet to have, I think! Her natural ability, as a wasp, is a 2s stun on a 45s base CD, or 31.5 seconds with the Longevity talent.

Most BM hunters might find this excessive or even redundant, but I keep a lot of Ayamiss' abilities off auto-cast and set them to trigger with some of my own, or set them to specific modifier keys. One macro, which I keep on 1, is my signature fazzarawrz for a wasp.

Do pardon me if it's not the most appropriate place to post the following, please, as I'm new to the whole 'forum' business. I figure that, being a BM thread, some may find it helpful or interesting.

#showtooltip Sting
/dismount
/targetenemy [noharm]
/petattack
/cast Rabid
/cast [nomodifier, target=pettarget] Smack
/cast [modifier:shift, target=pettarget] Sting; [modifier:alt] Heart of the Phoenix

First line shows CD left on stun, second dismounts, third targets the nearest enemy, fourth sends pet at it, fifth casts the ferocity talent, Rabid, sixth (I keep this line on nearly every macro to prevent GCD from keeping Sting or something from going off) uses her basic attack, and seventh will stun the target if shift is held or use HotP if alt is held.

Anyway, do what's fun, I love BM, and stuff 'n' stuff.

My main, and the toon I usually play, is BM and has been all along. I tried MM, tried SV, but still keep going back to BM - mainly because of the spirit beasts. That healing comes in handy sometimes. And I just like the way they look. :-D

From observation and experience with other hunters since BC, I think one of the major reasons people are down on BM (and hunters in general) is that so very many hunters simply don't know, never learn, how to use their pet effectively. Sometimes they don't use them at all - it's like the pet is just decorative or just there for a little bit of added damage. Just today I watched a lvl 85 hunter get slaughtered by lvl 83 mobs, simply because his pet was ignored. We are not melee fighters, but I still see hunters out there pulling mobs before they ever even think about sending their pet in. I see pets in groups pulling aggro like crazy, and everybody yelling and blaming hunters in GENERAL, instead of the poor player who can't control his pet. And then, of course, there are those macho players who think that unless you're up in somebody's face when you fight, you're a sissy. These are the same people who overcompensate with objects because they feel so inferior size-wise...er...sorry, that's a different discussion...

My pet is my best weapon and my best armor. My pet is my tank, and occasionally acts as off tank for the group (there is a reason that hunters are the best soloing class.) As for dps, I consistently am in the top five, usually the top three, sometimes first - but I was as MM and Survival too. Your spec is a matter of personal playing style and what you feel most comfortable with. Everybody is different and wants different things from their pets and their rotation, etc. The #3 ranked arena player in the world is a BM hunter. Using dps as a way to score a spec is a little silly. If you're most comfortable playing BM, then that's what will give you the best results; same for the other two specs.

Back when I was about lvl 45, I was running a dungeon and somehow, through some fluke, we ended up with two hunters and a priest - the tank quit, and then the rogue quit, and there we were, waiting, and waiting...and then the priest says, "the heck with this, let's finish this by ourselves. The pets can tank." And that's what we did. We zipped through that dungeon, didn't wipe, killed everything and it was the most fun I ever had in a dungeon - EVER. The two pets were absolutely awesome tanks, the two of us hunters took down everything in seconds, and the priest had very little to do. Two hunters teamed in a battleground, of whatever spec, can be truly formidable. I don't play arenas, so I can't speak for that, but I would imagine it's the same story there: how well you do has very little to do with which spec you are, but a lot to do with how you play it. It's true of any class. Look at the arguments over Frost vs. Fire vs Arcane mage specs. The opinions are just as strong and everyone can offer up just as much "proof" as we can about BM, MM, and SV. A skilled player, a practiced player, one who understands the class and why we have pets to begin with, can compensate for, if not overcome, the various nerfs and buffs and changes that keep coming along. We adapt. That's part of what hunters do. :-D
