Just wondering how many people out there play BMery full time because they enjoy it, and regardless of what others say?

I jumped to survival not long ago just to test it out, and because I was a little tired of joining a group, and before we even started, people saying "dude, you should spec MM, or Surv for the haste". My dps was always fine on bosses as BMery; in the mid 20s and higher at times. I personally enjoy BMery the most simply because I enjoy the focus of that spec being about the pet.

Now, I don't raid with a guild. I do LFD, and LFR, and I understand that in a lot of major raiding guilds, that you want to maximize your dps, and spec in a way that best helps the guild. But what I'm not really understanding, is how come there is such a negative view of the spec in LFD and LFR? Neither of those are hard, and as long as the player is competent to learn their rotations, their dps will be plenty.

I dunno, just curious how many folks play BMery full time out there, and how well they do....

I play BM as a main spec because my 10man raid group needs the 3% dmg buff (plus sometimes we don't have a mage/shaman). I was a bit unhappy with having to switch from MM to BM since I was pulling a lot of DPS with MM. However, I realized that MM and BM's rotation are quite similar with BM having much more mobility than MM.

The one thing that costs BM its DPS is KC. You have to wait for your pet to get to the boss or adds, and those few seconds are DPS lost especially if your KC is off CD.

In LFR, I'm usually top 5 DPS (rogues and casters with legendaries usually top me) and on Madness fights, I am usually top three. I definitely beat all other hunters that I've seen in LFR (most are SV hunters). When my 10man had another hunter (who was SV), I was only about 2 - 3k DPS behind, so it's not too big of a DPS lost, especially with so much mobility.

Personally I like it. I think BM has the less rigid rotation out of all hunter specs, and I like having different pets such as chimeras and worms. :)

PS I like having mini bursts as opposed to a main nuke only at the beginning of fights. :)

I enjoy BM, however it feels kind of off with the 2pc T13 in PvE. I use it off and on but I can definitely say I enjoy it less now than before as it lost a lot of its fluidity. The 2pc T13 coupled with Focus Fire's Haste effect makes the spec feel a lot more all over the place and more like button mashing, similar to how Survival feels on Madness of Deathwing or Survival emphasizing Haste over Mastery.

The relatively rigid shot cycles weren't a bad mechanic imo (such as 3 Cobras > 1 Explosive, 3 Cobras 1 Arcane 1 Kill) as it kept a priority system involved but still had a predictable flow. The 2pc T13 is awesome in DPS potential and is a god send in PvP, but I can definitely say it made the gameplay of Marksmanship (due to ISS as well) and Beast Mastery go downhill. The main reason you don't feel the difference in Survival is that it doesn't have an extra Haste buff and by emphasizing Mastery > Haste, it plays no different than prior patches.

Beast Mastery is just all over the god damn place between 2pc T13, a Haste effect you can't really take advantage of in shot cycles, and Invigoration.
