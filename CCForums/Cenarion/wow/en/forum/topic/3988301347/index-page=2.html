I've been BM/BM ever since patch 4.0 went live. I tried MM spec for one day (and one raid) and didn't enjoy it (it wasn't a DPS gain anyway). I can't stand SV spec. It's not a hard spec to play. I just don't enjoy it.

When I first started playing WoW, I leveled up as MM mostly (with a short stint of BM from 60-70 just for kicks). I went SV when I started raiding but only because it was THE raid spec for the gear I had at the time. I went back to MM as soon as my gear allowed me to.

After ICC was on farm, I went MM/BM and swapped between specs pretty much evenly (depending on the fights). Contrary to popular belief, going BM was never an issue. In fact, since my damage was spread more between my pet and myself, it meant I didn't have to hold back due to tanks lacking threat.

In all honesty, I've always found I have a lot more control over situations by running BM. This was especially true when Cata first went live. Having my own heal when healers were constantly OOM proved quite invaluable. Truth be told, I liked MM MUCH better before Cata. But they took out most of what I liked about it with patch 4.0. So that's another reason why I've been BM since.

If you actually learn how to play the BM spec, there's not a damn thing wrong with it. You just need to understand you're not completely playing a ranged spec. You have to adopt a melee mentality.
