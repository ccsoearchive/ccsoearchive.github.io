The drink hadn't had the effect the death knight had hoped for, and he remained quite bored. Something twinged at his subconscious, if only for a second. A familiar whisper rolled back into his thoughts.

"Your will...is not your own"

When he had first heard it again a few months ago, a terrible feeling had passed over him. Was Ner'Zhul back? Was he going to claim his soul again? Through much searching into these questions, the death knight realized he had an uncanny link to the undead. He left them be for the most part. The mindless wonderers were usually too lost in this world to do much damage; but there were others. The Horde utilized other powerful undead in their armies. The betraying masses led by the traitorous Sylvannas were to be watched closely and avoided often. The feeling he had now was....familiar.

"I am Maracelle Sturmblade, could I trouble you with a small task this rainy night? In the interest of Stormwind security, of course..."

He hadn't even noticed the human approach him...maybe the drink was working afterall. He tossed around her words in his head for a second. He hadn't completed any tasks in Stormwind for some time, and he had a feeling those in charge were beginning to forget his previous exploits. Perhaps tonight would be interesting afterall.

"Hello Maracelle", he said. His words echoing as if being drawn from another plane. "What can I do for Stormwind?'

((@Erdar: Sure, you can! But from the post I read, it seems like your characters are operating on a separate mission. So, it may not be time for them to collide yet. And Mara: Sure you can, but lets make it a somewhat even fight so its still fun and not a steamroll :P))

The rain had soaked the Warlock's shadowflame vestments, and as he stood, they clung to his thin frame, outlining the exposed bones of his decomposed body. 'This will not do' he thought. Felwind had returned from hunting some time ago, so he mounted the drake and dipped behind the hill on which he had been perched. Dismounting, he gritted his teeth, and quickly channeled one tick of his Hellfire spell. The pain was immense, but not unfamiliar, and his clothes were as dry as the day he purchased them from Jamus'Vaz in Orgrimmar.

Now a few measures more comfortable, the Afflictionist began the summoning of his Felhound, Greedrom. Purple runes began swirling around his feet that contained the true name of the demon. Drawing power from those runes, the violet energies traveled through his rotted hands and collected in a sphere above his head. When he sensed the magic-hunter's presence close enough to the Azerothian plane, he thrust his hands up into the air, completing the ritual. With a sound that reminded him of a bubbling cauldron of blood, the demonic canine emerged with a growl beside him.

"Come, minion, the hour is nigh." As he mounted Felwind, the demon disappeared. For all of fel magic he had studied, he could not for the un-life of him figure out how demons disappeared whenever he mounted his drake.

The wyrm beat its great leathery wings, and soon the duo was soaring towards Stormwind Harbor. The warlock took great care to skirt around the edges of the city as long as possible. During his briefing, he had been informed of the skill of the gryphon riders that patrolled the skies over the human capitol, and though the numbers were diminished at this hour of the morning, they were still a force to be reckoned with.

The drake glided silently into the harbor as Maccabeus took out a stone tablet.

"Well, well, these fleshbags have been busy, haven't they."

Alongside Stormwind's venerated wind-powered fleet stood four brand new Turalyon-class battleships, steam-powered with the latest technology from those tasty gnomes over in Ironforge. Flying closer, he noticed that they were already outfitted for war, bristling with 105mm guns crafted in Ironforge as well, but this time by those smelly dwarves.

"We have spies down here, damn it! How could they have missed this! I have to get a report to both Sylvanas and Garrosh immediately."

Then Maccabeus noticed the empty dock next to him, and realized it wasn't quite empty. A small metal tube was sticking up from the water...a submersible! This could spell bad news for the Horde fleet. The only known submersibles for both sides were operating in Vashj'ir, containing the Naga threat. If the Alliance had figured out a way to churn these out regularly, the Horde's shipping lanes were in grave danger.

The warlock burned these notes onto the tablet he was carrying and quickly dumped it back into his satchel. Now, to see if any of the Alliance naval command was lingering around the harbor or any of the taverns. The lights in all the ships seemed to be off, and though the fleet was prepared to sail, the high-ranking officers would be spending the night in the city while their underlings kept watch over the ships. He decided to check the taverns first.

The great gray-furred Worgen strolled through the streets, uncaring of the rain that fell, dampening his fur. He didn't really care where he was going, and as such, found himself walking towards his favorite inn in the Mage Quarter.

'I wonder if anyone else is at the Recluse.' He thought, almost to the door. Glancing up, he noticed in a flash of lightning a hunched figure on top of one of the roofs, rummaging through it's bags. The light faded, and with it, the figure. He shrugged, knowing that all manner of people came to Stormwind.

Stepping through the door to the tavern, Grishmak briefly paused to shake the water from his fur before changing his form to that of a human, knowing that most of the races in the Alliance were still uncomfortable with the Worgen.

Letting his eyes adjust to the light in the tavern, Grishmak noticed that several people had already taken up places in the room. His eyes scanned the faces, and finally alighted on an empty place near the fire. He strolled across the room, sitting down at a table next to a night elf.

"I didn't expect to find another druid here," He began slowly, waiting to see if the elf took notice of him. His voice didn't carry much of an accent, and he wondered if the druid would question his statement that he was a druid
