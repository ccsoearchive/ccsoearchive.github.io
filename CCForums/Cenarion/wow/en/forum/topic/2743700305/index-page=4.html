Maccabeus pulled his mount up abruptly. As an undead, he had quite a distaste for the energies of the holy light, and had since trained himself to detect those disgusting powers wielded by Paladins and Priests, especially those of the Alliance. And he had just felt the twinge of someone activating their Light-given powers near, or possibly in the Recluse.

He was unhappy now. His mission had taken more time than he had thought, and he was now unsure about the circumstances that awaited him at the Blue Recluse, though it was apparent that at least one Light-wielder was present.

Ironclaw landed on a roof near to the Inn, just in time for Maccabeus to see a druid sprinting away in cat form. Ill portent, he thought. Perhaps he had tripped one of the mage wards located around the city. Careless, but most likely unavoidable. It appeared to be a fresh adventurer, and so the warlock persued him.

"Time to have some fun," the undead grinned.

He saw that the Druid had stopped in front of the cathedral. Even better, it was a Worgen. Maccabeus smiled, and summoning his fel power, quickly corrupted and baned the young adventurer with excruciating agony. He waited a moment to make sure the spells had taken effect, then mounted up and scurried back towards the Blue Recluse, eager to see what other challenges awaited him this night.

Though the wind whipped past his decayed ears, he could still hear the shouts coming from the cathedral. Either they had just found the druid dead, or they had found him in time and saved him. Either way, the city would be going into alert mode soon. It was time to cause some havoc.

He grinned, thinking about his plan to release the full might of his magic. Maybe he would even summon his infernal, if he had a chance. Hmm...should it be his opener or his ace in the hole...decisions, decisions. He had some preparation to do at first, however.

He dismounted on the building directly next to the inn, Greedrom appearing by his side. He conjured a healthstone for himself, and then created a soulstone, which would a nasty surprise for whoever thought they finished him off, if they even had finished him off at all. He had not chosen to wear his hood all night, for fear of his silhouette giving him away to the casual onlooker, but he donned it now, the elementium horns twisting and jutting out at odd angles, giving him the appearance of a high-ranking twilight cultist.

He had expended so much fel magic close to the mage district that he had most likely tipped them off to his presence now. Nothing was left to do except make their day a living hell. At his feet, he created a demonic portal, his escape mechanism. Then, he targeted a spot on the roof, spoke one of the most secretive incantations known on Azeroth, and watched, with twisted glee, as a massive meteor crashed through the roof of the Blue Recluse.

A giant crash echoed through the Mage district. Grishmak watched as a meteor crashed through the roof of the Recluse. The Warlock had to be behind this. Grishmak ran through the door, transforming into a Worgen as he did so. The scene that greeted him was one of pandemonium.

The creature was wrecking havoc, destroying the tavern's bottom floor. He saw several people fighting the creature, containing it's progress, if only for the moment. They broke off large chunks from the creature, but he could see that it would take a lot more effort to bring it down.

Grishmak grabbed the barmaid, who had out a pair of daggers. "We need to get everyone out of here. Is there another way out?" He asked, channeling healing magic onto the fighters, doing everything he could to keep them alive. He had a lot to learn, but he could still keep them up and fighting until reinforcements arrived.
