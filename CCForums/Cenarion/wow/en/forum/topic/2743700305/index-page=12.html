It was always confusing to his senses to one moment be in the frigid air of Icecrown, then the balmy streets of Dalaran, and then the arid, dry climate of Orgrimmar, but such was the life of an undead on a mission. Maccabeus had always had an excellent relationship with the Darkspear trolls, and one in particular, which always surprised other members of the Horde when they saw them together.

Walking up the Valley of Spirits, Maccabeus inhaled the heady scent of the bayou on which the trolls now made their home. Making his way towards the Shamanic training center, he spied the long dark dreadlocks of his friend.

"No, mon, you have to feel de elements," the lanky troll was coaching a young orc, who seemed displeased with his results. "De do not listen to demands. It is a symbiotic relationship, ja'know?"

"Wise words, shaman," Maccabeus said.

" 'Ello Macc! What brings you round dese parts?"

"I've come to ask for your aid in something, my friend. But I shall wait patiently until you are done with your lesson." To the orc he said, "It would be wise to heed his words, young shaman. Legend says that he was able to heal a raiding party through an encounter with the Four Horsemen of Naxxramas all by himself."

The orc looked up in awe at Tazmahn, who blushed slightly.

"Now you know I hate when you bring dat up, mon," the lanky troll grinned.

...

An hour later, the troll had finally finished with the young orc, who walked away grinning over his new mastery of the frost shock.

"So, what ya be needin' my help with, my demoniac friend?"

They were sitting in a local tavern, drinking Frog Venom Brew, a troll favorite. Maccabeus explained the situation, what had happened at the Blue Recluse, and the letter he had sent to the patrons the previous day. The Elementalist's eyes went wide and stayed that way for the entirety of the conversation.

"Of course I be helpin' ya, mon," the Shaman said when he finally finished. "But tell me who we be up against first."

"The most powerful of the bunch is a human paladin. One-on-one, I will most likely be able to drive him into the ground, but if he decides to bring back up, I will need your help. In addition, we will probably be facing a semi-powerful mage, a druid healer, and two to three rogues, plus whoever else they recruit for this little expedition."

"Sounds like it could be fun. I be seein' in Sholazar tomorrow then, eh?"

"Excellent. River's Heart before dawn. My brother will be there as well."

"Othrys too? I hope de don't break de rules, Macc. I feel sorry for dem if all t'ree of us come down on em like a wave of mojo."

The warlock cackled. "I, too, my tusked friend. I too. Until tomorrow."

Activating his Hearthstone, Maccabeus vanished out of the tavern. He grinned. Come, then, Alliance dogs, he thought. You shall meet your match amongst the undergrowth of Sholazar.

Galaire entered the docks of Stormwind and looked for a certain captain of a ship called The Drunken Dwarf, the same ship that had taken her to Northrend all those years ago. The captain she was looking for was a dwarf by the name of Crissat Ironfist. She asked around the docks for said captain, but the various dock workers didn't know where the dwarf was. Galaire cursed her luck and continued her search until she spotted the stout dwarf captain walking out of a small bar called the Dancing Mermaid ((yes, I made that bar up ^^;)).
"Ah, Galaire, m' bonnie lass" said Crissat. "how have ya been? It's been a long time, aye?"
"Crissat, I'm on my way to Northrend to hunt down that warlock that attacked the Recluse"
"I heard o' that" said Crissat. "Northrend, eh?......fine, I can have ya at Borean Tundra in a jiffy"
Galaire smiled. "Perfect. The warlock sent word he would be in Sholazar Basin"
"Oh, aye?" asked Crissat.
Galaire told the dwarf about the letter that the warlock had sent.
"Alright" said Crissat. "I can have you at Borean in just 5 days"
"5 days?" asked Galaire. "surely, you can do better than that"
"Give me a few hours to gather my crew" said Crissat, ignoring Galaire's comment and walked away with a slight drunken walk. Galaire sighed and decided to stay in the docks. She walked her mount to a deserted area of the docks and got off. She walked slowly towards the overlook that overlooked the entire docks of Stormwind and enjoyed the view.
She saw the ghostly figure of the worgen again when she turned around. The worgen wasn't alone this time; this time the worgen had brought four other worgen, each with a little bundle in their claws.
"No..." said Galaire aloud. "not now"
The lead worgen sneered at Galaire and looked ready to pounce onto her. Instinct took over as Galaire went for her sword just as the worgen leaped at her. She dodged wildly and......the worgen had vanished along with the other worgen. Starlight looked at Galaire with concern in her eyes.
"I'm alright" said Galaire, putting away her sword and turned to look out over the docks. Starlight came over and nuzzled Galaire's back. Galaire absently rubbed Starlight's head.
"I would've thought I would be rid of those memories...." muttered Galaire.

Ashok headed for his humble room in the graveyards of Stormwind where he had stayed as a boy. He looked at the picture on his bedside table, in it were four men and a woman. Ashok in his splendid gold armor with Ragnarok strapped to his back, the great Bolvar Fordragon, Highlord Tyrion Fodring, Ashok's long time friend and mentor Adrias Slovensky with his tankard and warhammer, Vegeance and finally the lovely priestess Elizabeth Tanner. All of them smiling, the picture was taken right before they all headed off to Northrend. It was a happy day, one Ashok remembered fondly, but soon a darker memory took over...

He was standing with Adrias and Elizabeth looking down on a field filled with the Damned, to his right was Wintergarde Keep and above them was Naxxramas, again the memory jumped and Ashok found himself on his knees as he remembers watching both Adrias and Elizabeth torn to shreds as he cut swathes through the undead hordes. The memory jumped again to watching Bolvar fall victim to the ungodly plague at the Wrathgate. Ashok screamed in rage and pain hoping to drown out the memories of his long lost friends and companions.

"No. I will put an end to this warlock and whoever else he brings. No one but Tyrion and a few others knows of the beast Arthas unleashed within me when his minions killed my friends. Soon this warlock will learn why I left my station!" Ashok said to the air. With that he grabbed his bag and headed for the Castle where he found the Gnome who owed him a favor.

"Ho! Gnome! Its time for that favor! I need to get to Dalaran, think you can help me out?" Ashok called out to him. The gnome, named Charles Biggins, looked up and sighed. "Why do you want to go there again?!?! Oh well its not mine to question I guess... Let me see." The gnome closed his eyes and let the arcane energies engulf him creating a portal to Dalaran. "Have fun!" Charles said sarcastically as Ashok walked through the portal.

Once in Dalaran Ashok went directly to the stables there and acquired his gryphon, Leopold. "So old friend, it looks like we are headed to battle once more." Ashok said to the gryphon as he stroked the gryphon's mane. In response Leopold growled fiercely, he was always up for a fight. Ashok smiled saddled Leopold and headed for Krasus' Landing. When he got there he took flight planning on visiting some grave sites before he actually went to Sholazar.
