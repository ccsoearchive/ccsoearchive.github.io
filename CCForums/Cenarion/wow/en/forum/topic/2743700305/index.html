(OOC-this is my first attempt at forum rp, be kind!)

Standing in the Mage tower in Stormwind, Mara put the heavy book back on the shelf of the library. The day had been long and she rubbed her neck briefly as she turned to go down the ramp.

"I am heading to the Recluse to unwind." she chuckled as she walked down the ramp and stopped at the doorway. Dark clouds covered the sky and lightning flickered.

She took out an umbrella and hurried down the ramp, the wind starting to gust as she made her way to the tavern. Just as she made it to the doorway, the clouds opened up and rain poured down. Sighing with relief she went inside and found a table close to the bar.

"Good evening, Joachim. I will take some of that cherry grog I know you have hidden in the back room, and keep it coming. I think you will have more people soon, as it is starting to rain."

There was no one else in the tavern besides her and the bartender and staff. Idly she wondered if anyone else would come in out of the rain. Perhaps a few tall tales would be shared over the mugs of mead and bottles of wine.

Joachim laughed and brought her cherry grog to the table. "I would welcome the business, and rain is always good for bringing in the crowd. I better get the staff ready, and I hope that leak in the roof was fixed properly. Tonight will be a test of that. Any food for you tonight? I have some lovely roasted boar, with apples and kraut. Baked potatoes and some fresh asparagus."

"That sounds delicious Joachim, I am sure the aroma from the roast will bring in some customers if the rain doesn't!" Mara sipped from the mug of cherry grog and got out her beadwork.

( jump in as any Alliance character, I expect to hear some tall tales of adventure and a modest amount of flirtation. If you come in dry, and not soaked to the skin, you better have a good explanation! Have fun!)

The rain never really bothered him. He would entertain children by freezing the rain on his plate armor then making it shatter to the ground. The children would luagh and and cheer then usually be ushered back into their homes quickly by their parents. Death Knights were not to be played with.

Wondering through Hyjal was always taxing. The forest was growing back quickly and as a result, the foliage always snagged and tore at his armor as he tracked down those vile enemies of the Alliance. He only trusted one blacksmith, which is what brought him back here. His helm would be ready in a day. He had hoped to fish, something he remembered he liked doing before...he died, but the rain would have no part of that. I dim light caught his attention. A tavern...why not.

He walked in, letting the rain remain on his armor and not doing his usual trick, expecting to see jeers and experiance the uncomfortable silence that accompanied him into most areas. To his relief, he only saw two people. A bartender...a dwarf. He always got along with dwarfs. They had nothing to prove; they knew they were tough. The other was a human in cloth. He was about to walk up to the bar when a blur of motion nearly knocked him over. Somebody else rushed into the tavern muttering her apologies to the bartender.

Laughing to himself that this woman almost knocked him over; he was fortunate indeed that the tavern was empty. She was strong though, uncommonly strong. There was something familiar about her.

No time to think too hard right now. He needed to follow suit of the human and get a drink. She had a look in her eyes of concern. Was it about the weather? He always watched those in cloth closely. They studied most of their time, so he learned early in his life that when one of them had a bad feeling, it was often warranted...

"We shall see..." he said to himself.

"Oh, ya want to see the menu lad?" the bartender asked. "Better get some o' tha strong stuff in ya soon afore this whole inn keels over!" He slid a pint of stout across the bar with a hearty laugh. "Welcome to the Recluse! Mayhap more people will be followin' ya in here after all lass!" he said to the human throwing her a wink.

The hills to the north of Stormwind were green, filled with lush grass, and only accessible to those with the rather expensive ability of flight. They also smelled sickly sweet to the figure perched upon the hill.

"Look at them scurry, Greedrom," muttered Maccabeus to his scaly companion. "Those humans and their so-called 'friends' in their self-righteousness."

Behind the warlock and his felhunter stood his means of access to these rather advantageous perches. Felwind, a black drake given to him as a trophy for his participation in the defeat of Sartharion the Onyx Guardian, clawed the ground impatiently. The great beast had grown fond of the conjured healthstones that the Afflictionist could produce. As such, Maccabeus conjured one and tossed it to the obsidian wyrm as a means of keeping it quiet while he did his reconnaissance.

The felhound drooled next to him as it eyed the population of the Alliance capitol city, strands of magic tinged saliva dripping over its myriad razor-sharp teeth.

"Greedrom, Lady Sylvanas aspires to bring the Forsaken back to the prominent place they held in the Horde prior to the traitor Putress' actions at the Wrathgate. To that end, we have been sent to Stormwind to do some aerial reconnaissance of their naval fleet, and, if the opportunity presents itself, do some damage to their naval chain of command."

The sickly-orange demon growled in delight and anticipation. Though subjugated and controlled by this undead, there was still nothing the creature delighted in more than pain and agony.

"I shall summon an Eye of Kilrogg to peform one final sweep of the area. The city looks mostly deserted for this hour, save the night watch, but on my last sweep I noticed a tavern that still had its lights on. I want to make sure we are well-prepared should any Alliance do-gooder come running to try and save the day."

Muttering incantations that no normal man could have ever listened to sanely, Maccabeus formed a glowing green eye in the air in front of him. He exhaled, allowing his sight to flow into the orb, and launched it towards the tiny tavern near the Mage tower.

The eye came to rest right on the roof, just as the warlock had planned. Carefully, so as not to trip any magical wards set out by Stormwind's mages (though he loathed them, Maccabeus had come to respect the power wielded by those humans who commanded the arcane), he maneuvered the eye over towards one of the windows of the ground floor. Inside, he saw six figures: one dwarf, one night elf, and two humans, and one Worgen, although he could not be sure if the humans were actual humans or one of the new mutts to join the Alliance. By this time, however, the fel-master couldn't maintain the incantation any longer, and let his concentration slip. The eye disappeared with a semi-audible *blip*, and ceased to be, leaving only a few faint wisps of green mist behind.

"Six Alliance. Nothing to be too worried about, though that Worgen may give us some trouble if he shows up. Well, the hourglass says we have 2 hours until midnight, which is when we shall launch our attack. I shall call upon you both then. Felwind, you are free to hunt in the Burning Steppes, but do NOT stray into Elwynn or Redridge, or I shall make your scaly hide regret it."

With that Maccabeus closed his faintly glowing eyes and lay down to rest himself. It was going to be a long, arduous night.

As a rogue, Galaire didn't hang around this district of Stormwind often. As a matter of fact, she didn't hang around in Stormwind at all. It wasn't that she hated the human city, but she much preferred her homeland of Teldrassil. She had came to Stormwind City two days earlier purely on a whim, just to see the rest of what Azeroth had to offer. During those two days, she had caught wind of a rumored undead warlock hanging around the area but she couldn't find any trace of the warlock. The information she had gotten was pretty sketchy and besides...why would someone from the Horde go to Stormwind? An army of Horde she could understand, but just one undead? Thunder clashed in the sky as Galaire entered the district of the mages.

Two days wandering the city, Galaire had found a nice little tavern on this side of the city. It was usually a quiet tavern...at least it was quiet the last time she was there. But that had been extremely early then....and Galaire was a bit of a nightowl as night elves tended to be. She was heading for the tavern now just to get out of the storm.

As she approached the tavern, she thought she had caught a glimpse of a shape sitting on one of the roofs. In a flash of lightning, the roof was empty. Must have been a trick of the eyes. Before entering the tavern, she heard voices in the tavern. She paused to consider her options, but then walked into the tavern and headed for the bar, noticing all the people there. She ordered an ale and then took her usual table on the second floor of the tavern. She liked it up there because she had a good vantage point of the whole tavern. And besides...she wasn't really in the mood to talk to anyone at the moment.
