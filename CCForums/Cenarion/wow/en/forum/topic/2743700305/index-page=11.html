The Warlock sat in his room in the Legerdemain, packing his things for the trip. He was excited, but at the same time dubious as to whether they would follow the agreed upon rules of engagement. He had two stops to make before he would arrive in River's Heart...

...

The foot of Icecrown Citadel was quiet now, except for the encampments of the Ebon Blade and the Argent Crusade. A few adventurers lingered about, either aiding in the clean-up of the Scourge or waiting for comrades to arrive so they could use the portal recently installed by the Bronze Flight to fight against the Lich King again. Maccabeus pulled his traveling cloak around him tightly, though nothing could protect against the chill that emanated from that house of the damned.

The fel-weaver walked towards the Ebon Blade encampment where his brother, Othrys was stationed. His brother was a powerful Death Knight, though he did not have the experience of his older brother. Both had been taken by the Scourge during the destruction of Brill, but they had not been re-united until the campaign in Northrend. That moment was the only time he had felt joy in his twisted heart since he had been turned.

"Oth! There you are," shouted Maccabeus against the howling winds.

The Death Knight looked up and grinned. "Macc! By the Banshee Queen, how are you?"

"I am well, brother. And you?"

"I cannot complain. There are still a multitude of Scourge to slaughter, and it keeps my rune-blades busy," he grinned malevolently. Both of them had the gift for destruction, but Othrys was cursed in that if he did not inflict pain on another creature, he would go mad. And so he had to do battle for the rest of his days.

"That is good to hear. Anything to keep you from the darkness," Macc said, clasping him on the shoulder.

"Aye," nodded Othrys, somberly. "Did you hear the news about the Blue Recluse being attacked? I'd like to shake the hand of whomever's handiwork that was...wait, that wasn't you, was it?!" He looked up to see his brother grinning. "By Sylvanas' tits, that was incredible!"

"I'm glad to see you still keep up with the news at this desolate outpost, brother. But, that is actually what I came here to talk to you about. I need your assistance, I'm afraid to say. I have angered the beehive, and I know they will come after me."

Othrys frowned. "I'm sure you can handle it. You are Maccabeus the Kingslayer, after all."

"Yes, but..." The Frostwielder listened as Maccabeus recounted his letter to the patrons of the Blue Recluse. "In short, they're going to send everything they have after me. I've come to you for help, and I will also be going to our shaman friend."

The Death Knight stroked his bony chin. "Of course I will help you. But, I need to know. Will this be for capture or for killing."

"I would personally like to capture them and take them to the Undercity. You, of course can have your pick to take to Highlord Mograine. There is a druid who your Unholy Knights might like to have fun with," the Warlock hinted.

"I could handle that. And the Paladin will be your prize, I assume?"

"Naturally. Now, I must make haste to Orgrimmar. Tazmahn has become a teacher of young Shaman there."

"Good for him. He always had the patience that you and I lacked."

"Farewell brother, I will see you in River's Heart tomorrow."

Icecrown Citadel.....many years ago


Galaire grabbed the missive covertly from Fizbinn, her fellow member of the SI:7 that had sneaked into the frozen citadel itself to spy on the Lich King's forces. Members of SI:7 were dressed like those belonging to the Cult of the Damned if it was called for, and today it was called for. Galaire and Fizbinn--a gnome rogue--were in a fairly active hallway deep within the citadel. As the two walked closer to each other, Fizbinn had made a sign that he was carrying a missive that should be delivered to someone in high command. Galaire grabbed the missive without anybody in the hall noticing. Galaire stashed the missive up the sleeve of her robes, never breaking her stride. Fizbinn was heading down the hallway, towards a towering Tauren that was dressed as a high-ranking member of the cult. Galaire came to a T intersection and turned down an empty hallway. She walked down the hall a little ways, before pulling the missive from her sleeve and opened it, breaking the seal. She read the letter without slowing down.



I found something out. I heard the other cultists talk about someplace called the Halls Of Reflections, the Lich King's private chambers....where he seems to let his guard down. I have yet to discover the location of these halls. I'll write when I can. --Fizbinn




Present day...


Galaire drank the rest of the ale and cursed the memory that had suddenly surfaced within her. Cursed the memory because Fizbinn had been caught later that week when he had passed the letter to her. She never heard what had happened to him. That was the last time she had seen the gnome rogue, and she knew better than to ask what happened to someone that mysteriously vanished within the citadel because it happened like twice a month. Going to Northrend again sounded like revisiting old memories....
