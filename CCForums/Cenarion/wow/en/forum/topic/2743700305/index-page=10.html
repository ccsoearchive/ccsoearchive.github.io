Standing under a tree just outside Stormwind, Taneth Owlhorn looks closely at a butterfly that had just landed on the tips of his fingers. A smile crept onto his face as the tiny bug crawled along from his index finger to his pinky and took off again. Taneth closes his eyes and takes a deep breath, feeling the rain drops run down his face. Oh how he loved the rain, but as a druid, he knew the cruelties of nature all to well. He looked over to the Gates of Stormwind with a frown. He hated the concrete and stone jungles of the other races, they always made him feel sick.

But knowing the dangers of the storm, Taneth put on his hat and wrapped his cloak around his shoulders and headed through the gates of the Human city. It was their he came across an inn. He figured he would rest there for the night, warm up and perhaps get some food in his belly. He walked threw the door and took a seat, taking off his hat and looking about the room. He could already feel his isolation from nature, behind the thick walls and stone walkways. It had made him feel claustrophobic, so he did not quite notice the people inside, or what the inn even looked like. Instead he let his wet, light blue hair hang in front of his face and huddling up in his cloak.

One week earlier

Maccabeus awoke the next morning with a rather large ache in his chest, where the rogues had punched him. Grinning a jawless grin, he recalled the events of the previous night. The Infernal through the roof had been a nice touch, though he was disappointed in himself for not activating any of his soul shards. Ah well, lesson learned.

The spellcaster rolled out of bed and slipped his bony feet into a pair of custom batskin slippers, made from the duskbats in his homeland of Tirisfal Glades, and ambled to the door to pick up copies of the three major newspapers of Azeroth: The Orgrimmar Herald, The Gadgetzan Times, and The Stormwind Courier, Orcish edition. What he saw made him chuckle. Each front page showed a picture of the decimated Recluse. The Herald proclaimed, "Horde strike team lays waste to Alliance inn in Stormwind!"

'Good,' he thought. 'The Horde does not even acknowledge that I was even there.'

The Gadgetzan Times read, "Blue Recluse attacked last night; warlock and Twilight's Hammer suspected"

The Steamwheedle paper was usually a balanced rag, unless someone came calling with bribes. Damnable Goblins.

The Stormwind Courier read: "Brave adventurers defend Blue Recluse from joint Horde/Twilight Hammer assault."

Maccabeus sighed. leave it to the Alliance to make it seem like the Horde and the Twilight nutjobs were working together. Glancing at the time, he saw it was already 8:30 am!

"Damn! I have a meeting with Garrosh in 15 minutes," he swore under his breath.
Hastily, he threw on his battle vestments, including his helm, took the stairs down two at a time, and mounted his Kor'Kron Annihilator. He had debated throwing on his Orgrimmar tabard, but he didn't want to seem like a brown-noser. Besides, the information he had what earn him all the kudos he could ever desire.

Taking the portal in the Sunreaver's Sanctuary, the Afflictionist braced himself for the change from the cool air of Crystalsong Forest to hot, arid conditions of Durotar. He stepped out of the portal into the Cleft of Shadow, and quickly rode out into the Valley of Strength, stopping in front of Grommash Hold.

Two very large Kor'Kron crossed their spears, blocking him from entering the hold. "State your business, undead," the right one sneered.

"Maccabeus the Kingslayer here to see the Warchief. I have an appointment," he growled at the large orc. Grumbling, he stepped to the side, allowing the warlock to pass.

The caster made his way into the throne room, nodding at the Forsaken delegation on the left. He approached Garrosh and knelt. "Greetings Warchief, I bring excellent news."

"Good. from what I have been reading in our newspapers, it sounded as if you forgot your mission for a moment last night, warlock," the Warchief growled.

"Not for a moment, sir," Maccabeus replied, hiding his surprise that the Warchief actually knew how to read. "My mission first and foremost was to collect intelligence on their naval fleet, which I did with excruciating detail."

He reached into his satchel and retrieved one of the two tablets he had created, and presented it to Garrosh, who examined them carefully. If nothing else, the massive orc was an incredible military tactician, if a little bull-headed sometimes.

"These are excellent reports, Maccabeus. Our strategists will greatly appreciate the work you did last night. I will have copies sent to Vol'jin and Baine. I understand that you have a meeting later with the Banshee Queen?"

"Yes, Warchief. The Forsaken Navy, as you are aware, is one of our premier fighting forces. With this knowledge, we will only be able to further secure our hold on Gilneas."

"Very well then. Look for your compensation in the mail soon. You are dismissed."

"By your leave, Warchief."

Maccabeus turned to leave, but Garrosh called back to him. "I almost forgot. While I do not approve of your magical background or talents, I am quite impressed with your dismantling of that inn. I have seen Ashok do battle in Northrend, and he is not a Paladin to be trifled with. You are quite an impressive warrior, Maccabeus."

The warlock bowed deeply. "I am humbled, Warchief. Thank you."

Garrosh grinned. "Now go. You would be wise not to keep Sylvanas waiting." Besides, he didn't add, it was bad enough that three of you rotters were in here all the time. He didn't need a fourth stinking up the place.

One week later ((In line with the rest of the posts))

Maccabeus glided into Dalaran on Felwind, having returned from a hunting trip in the Howling Fjord. There was a rare ice elemental he had been seeking to kill, but alas, no luck. He did get to pay Camp Winterhoof a visit though, which was always a pleasure.

It had been a week since the events at the tavern, and the warlock always enjoyed thinking about that night. Strolling towards the Legerdemain, he decided he would send a courier their way, and thank them for that wonderful engagement, as well as express his disappointment and astonishment that none of them died, though, he remembered, the rogues came quite close.

...

A courier arrived outside the newly rebuilt Blue Recluse, soaked to the bone but for his standard issue, magically-protected letter satchel. Looking up to double check his location, he quickly entered through the door, letting the warmth of the inn wash over him. He was a sandy-haired human teenager, and he wasn't a particular fan of the job, but it was what he needed to get into the Hunter Academy, as far as money was concerned. Tuition wasn't cheap these days.

The inn was lively tonight, with patrons laughing, dancing, and eating and drinking to their hearts content. The mailboy looked at his instructions, and asked out loud, "Anyone here that was present on a stormy night one week ago?"
