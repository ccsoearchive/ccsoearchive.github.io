Lvl 15 - Tier 1: I've always been a fan of Nightstalker, but this is a lackluster version up against two awesome talents, imo. I can think of good uses for both Subterfuge and Shadow Focus. Decent choices for both PvE and PvP in this tier, imo.

Lvl 30 - Tier 2: I'm leaning toward Deadly Throw, but that's probably nostalgia for the ranged interrupt. Nerve Strike could be interesting, especially combined with defensive use of Shadow Dance. Is CR still on a shared CD with CloS? Now that Evasion is back on Prep I can't see myself taking Combat Readiness though. This tier seems largely PvP-based, but who knows what mechanics the MoP PvE fights will bring.

Lvl 45 - Tier 3: Elusiveness - the new Enveloping Shadows, hopefully it still stacks with Feint's current 50% AoE dmg reduction, which appears intact in the MoP Feint version. Decent competitor with Cheat Death. Leeching Poisons along with Shiv seems great for duels, and possibly PvE fights with intense damage, though I think in most cases it will be the weakest choice.

Lvl 60 - Tier 4: Burst of Speed seems like a hefty energy cost, but could combine interestingly with Shadow Focus. However, I think it's the weakest choice in the tier. My fuller thoughts can be found at http://us.battle.net/wow/en/forum/topic/4063168381

Lvl 75 - Tier 5: Dirty Tricks - great for duels, probably meh otherwise due to other people's DoTs. What is the status of the Blind glyph? Deadly Brew we've seen, and I think Paralytic might be my favorite in the tier, especially combined with the new Shiv functionality.

Lvl 90 - Tier 6: Interesting tier to say the least. Anticipation seems like it would combine well with Redirect, or you can choose to eliminate Redirect's CD. Shuriken Toss combined with Deadly Throw will be fantastic in certain situations. Decent choices for different playstyles, and I'm leaning toward Shuriken, at least for PvP. Anticipation could have decent use in PvE for keeping a more stable rotation in some situations.

Waylay is gone, but we have new CC choices.

Deadly Poison - Redesigned and essentially has Instant baked into it now. I like the new approach with the poisons, as it allows us to have more utility in PvE than the current model which forces dual damage poisons.

Shadow Walk - new shiney toy. Looks promising, albeit very situational. Curious to learn more about its functionality.

Shroud of Concealment - again, uncertain functionality, but very promising. Is it usable while moving? Is the granted stealth effect indefinite like ours? Will its effect persist after being cast if a teammate wanders beyond 20 yards from the Rogue? Is it an instantaneous casted effect, or a persistant aura? Can people beyond 20 yards walk into its area of effect while its is active? I feel I won't fully appreciate it until I use it, as was the case for Smoke Bomb.

The new Shiv is awesome, as outlined by our new Blue best friend forever.

Hemorrhage now says it "Replaces Sinister Strike." That's a step in a nice direction for helping people learn how to play. Also, I heard it no longer applies a debuff? What does this mean in regards to Sanguinary Vein - does that still exist?

I like the philosophy of giving us the dps-boosters baseline, such as Relentless Strikes and HAT. HAT being an all-or-nothing single ability which is baseline for Sub is awesome. And we're trading the Crit buff for our new Haste buff, it seems. I assume talents which are missing, like Opportunity, are being baked into the abilities they affected.

Fleet Footed baseline? I like.

The new Killing Spree looks a little gimmicky, but I assume NYI means Not Yet Implemented, so it's a work in progress, so it gets a pass.

Revealing Strike got the Sinister Strike Glyph effect in an attempt to have us use it more often I bet. Instead of using it just when we proc into 4 CPs, we'll probably use it as our 1st, then possibly 5th, combo point.

Energetic Recovery on SnD is a nice change. And it looks like Improved Recuperate is gone, though now we have new survivability choices. I wonder how it will compare to our current survivability, which can be over the top when Recup is fully talented.

Feint - No longer reduces threat. Makes sense considering the new philosophy in regards to threat and tanking and such. 20 energy cost with the glyph functionality unknown, but I don't see a cooldown on it. Interesting how the ability evolved over the years.

Slice and Dice - Another case of "what about the Glyph?" It looks like the current glyph functionality has been baked into the move. Are glyphs going away in MoP?

And Backstab is FINALLY a Subtlety move! All these years of Blizzard saying they didn't intend Combat Daggers while keeping Backstab in the Combat tab of our Spellbook really bugged me.

I'm sure there's stuff I missed, but so far I'm enthusiastic about everything except the level 60 Prep / Step tier.
