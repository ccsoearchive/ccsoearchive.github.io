It's hard to tell yet, there are so many changes. Both definitely have advantages and disadvantages.

Mut burst has actually never been a problem for Cata, it's been lack of Step, lack of Prep, inability to Gouge/Blind main target, and short DP duration (for Venomous Wounds). Some of that's changing ... but we can't really know what burst will be.

Dance is getting the 8s glyphed duration, but losing the 60% talented crit ... yet again, we can't really know what burst will be.

Venomous Wounds is still relying on poison duration, 8-15 seconds in MoP (DP still 12) and 20s unglyphed Rupture (equal to Cata glyphed Rupture) ... both still require a target and melee range.

Sub however gets Energetic Recovery on SnD, which doesn't require a target (except as a place to store combo points at range) or melee range (a pretty big boon) and also lasts up to 40 seconds!!!

On the other hand, Recuperate is no longer part of Sub's damage "rotation." You can argue that it's easer to keep both 40s (SnD) and 30s (Recup) finishers up than 30s and 20s on-target (Rupture) finisher up and be right ... but it's still a step down from where Sub survivability was before.

And of course Sub's been balanced around having both Prep and Step, and two utility poisons with approximately Dirty Tricks equivalent ... not so anymore. Mut simply wasn't balanced high enough (though is losing some utility to poison changes).

All in all, we've got a lot of great new things, but are losing a lot too. It's hard to figure out which spec will be best even when looking at Rogues alone. If you try and put all the other classes and their changes in, who knows? Maybe we'll actually have 2 or 3 viable PvP specs.

I was reading what Aldriana said about Anticipation and wow...

First, it lets us execute perfect cycles. No longer will one have to skip RvS as Combat or finish at 4CP at Mutilate - you will always be able to do 5pt finishers without wasting combo points. And if you're at 5 CP but not ready to refresh Rupture or SnD for another few seconds, you can just throw an extra move or two to kill time instead of having to use a different finisher and refresh your buff/debuff with an undersized finisher. It gives more control than we've ever had about what size finisher you want to use when, and will thus result in a nontrivial increase to our single target DPS.

Additionally, it gives us a degree of controllable burst that we've never had before - you can build up 5 stacks of Anticipation on a target in order to release two finishers in a row. It looks like you may be able to build up Anticipation stacks on one target and then switch to another, thus getting an implicit redirect of 5 extra combo points the first time you do a finisher. Burst of that sort is something we've been lacking for a long time, so its good to see them add something to address it.

All in all, I think Anticipation (and to a lesser extent, Versatility) are the most fun and interesting talents they've proposed to date, and I'm very encouraged to see it. The rest of the tree still needs some work, and Anticipation might need better competition within its tier to keep all PvE rogues from taking it - but its definitely a step in the right direction.
