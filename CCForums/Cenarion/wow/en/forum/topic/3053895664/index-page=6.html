Hunters are OK but still unbalanced in PVP, they need to do a few minor tweaks and fixes to finally balance us.

1.) Merge Hawk, Fox, and Wild into one aspect. We already macro it to make it trivial, (Except wild) tell me how this would be Over Powered? Simplify our class on par with others! PVE Wise, stop complicating our class! It's not hard, but its not fun!

2.) Hunters, still more complicated to succeed in pvp than other classes. I thank Blizzard for the little they have done so far though. But please, make our defensive pet abilities (Roar of Sacrifice, Master's Call) not require LOS, OR just make them reliant on our own LOS, its incredibly frustrating to manage at times. Requiring the pet to be alive / active still a requirement obviously.

3.) Pet bugs, over-discussed.

4.) Traps: Blizzard is WELL aware of trap issues. They are over-discussed. FIX! They are a primary reason for our failures, so im not downplaying it.

5.) Our healing debuff is not cost effective, at all. It's just another thing to complicate our overly complex class.

6.) We are THE ranged class, with minimum range as evidence of this. Redesign it in someway to emphasize the importance of it, by modifying our damage based on how far away we are.

7.) Serpent sting, make it do something in PvP worthwhile besides being <5% of our damage. Maybe our healing debuff?

SUB ISSUE with Serpent Sting: Annoying to accidently press it more than once and waste 25 focus. Suddenly dawned on me that this is a strange clunky mechanic which Blizzard is usually good at avoiding. It rarely ever happens, however... We are the only class with a DoT that is instant. That wastes 25% of our resource, that we will have to spend a good 6 seconds recovering. This is punishing for bad players as well, which im sure Blizzard also doesn't want.

Mana DPS classes have ways to recover so a second cast is merely a waste of mana and one GCD that hardly effects them.

What i'd like to see. 5 Focus Cost, 5 Second Cooldown. Adds a lot of flavor to the ability, and it can't be Overpowered because of how piddly the damage it does.

I have some ideas to address the deadzone and melee handicap problems.

(1) Pet damage could scale up in indirect proportion to the targetted enemy's distance from the hunter; the shorter the distance between the hunter and his target, the higher the pet's damage bonus. BM could have a talent granted ability to remove CC's on a moderate cooldown, like how Warlocks can do so with their Felguard.

(2) Remove our ability to use melee weapons and give us a new unique class slot/item to act as a 'stat stick' such as the icons and sigils other classes use. My idea...

(3) Hunter Class Item: Bola - All bolas would have an ability to fire a (20 yard?) ranged knockdown followed by a short root on a 2 minute cooldown. The knockdown plus root ensures the item is useful on both casters and melee opponents. The bolas would have their own hot keyable action similiar to how wands function with the 'Shoot' command.

(4) In place of current melee weapons and abilities let us fire all skills at point blank range for reduced damage (80 percent?) and give us one no damage utility melee attack - a 'pistol whip' type of attack whereupon we strike with our ranged weapon and stun the opponent. The Shamans and Rogues will like this one, no more fighting over agility-rich melee weapons. Hunters are supposed to be agile and wily - a big two handed axe strapped on our backs looks out of place anyway. Oh, and on the aesthetics tip, have our ranged weapon visible and strapped across our back when we're out of combat.

(5) Get rid of traps and replace them with arrows - Exploding Arrow, Immolation Arrow, Freezing Arrow etc. This would remove the need for Trap Launcher and the awkwardness of a ground targetted CC mechanic.
