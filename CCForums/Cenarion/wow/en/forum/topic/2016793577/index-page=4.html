Overall the guide is a good effort, but I'm going to put in a dissent on one part.

You don't need to be a lore professor to roleplay in this game. Not only do you not need to bone up on Wowpedia, or a like site before you make your first character, I actually strongly advised AGAINST doing so. Boning up like that tends to lead to overexpectation of what you should be pulling off and tends to open yourself to a lot of traps because of those expectations.

And it's not neccessary. The game provides you with an excellent resource in getting into the basic mindset of your character... The NPC's of the staring areas. The quests, the speeches, the dialogues are an excellent launchpoint to set mood and tone.

When I start a new character, I don't even THINK about a backstory. I frame him or her in the present tense and work more on getting a sense on where the character is today, what he or she is doing and what frame of mind. The backstory I let itself fill in piece by piece on it's own. The backstory on Drahliana didn't even start coming into being until she was level 12 and it's development was a slow process.

After you're comfortable with a character then you can plunge into the lore trivia. Don't be too hung up on what you may have gotten wrong, Not everyone in the real world after all is that right about the past or present either. :)

I took a gander at this topic, and I'm going to say most stuff on here is helpful, but I fear most stuff maybe incorrect. As most are aware, I'm not well liked because I'm trying to correct the laughable rp WoW has. This isn't really true. I'm trying to correct the myths being touted as fact in role play. Like needing a character history. This is completely optional and only serves as a personal aide of keeping your character info correct.

I've repeatedly stated the RP i know for the past 6 years isn't the style used in WoW. I've been on hardcore RP MUD And various online games. Hardcore meaning you have to be in character the hole time you log on and must follow strict naming rules, description wording and the like. But one thing is a character history has always been optional.

Role playing in an RPG game or MMORPG game is very simple. All you have to do is act like a person living in this realm. You do not need and shouldn't do scripted plays, like a high school drama club. That is a basic myth on roleplaying.

Basic forms of roleplay are:
Walking instead of running
Using emotes to NPC and players. The reason for this is in role play or being in character those NPC are REAL people to your character.
Having hobbies such as fishing or hunting.


Some basic rules of Roleplaying
1 Keep IC and OOC seperate. Meaning do not judge someones IC, OOC. AkA sending a whisper saying the are roleplaying wrong. Keep incharacter and just simply say you do not believe or agree with this person and walk away, por just simply walk away.

2 Keep to the basic IN GAME lore and history. Do not go off on out of game referances such as RPG books as these tend to be stand alone. Pretty much understand what your character knows of the past, but keep up with the current goings on.

3 Your character will not know every part of history. More so if your from Gilneas. Like knowing who the death knights are and other stuff. Though a worgen death knight is sorta up in the air on this.


Thats what I learned over the course of 6 years.


All in all RP doesn't need to be a big scripted or unscripted story just act like you live in Azeroth. Lore the way i've read it, is up for personal opinion, is there a right or wrong way to understadn it. Also not all races have set codes of play, and people are free to use what ever personality they choose. And have what ever quirks they wish. And no there doesn;t need to be a big epic explination either. We are all different. So are all members of each race. There is a black sheep in every family.
