Ok, so the fix is this....

Create a new admin user on your mac.

Navigate Go>Applications>World of Warcraft.

Now right click and select "Get Info"

Now under Sharing & Permissions add your new Admin account and allow it "Read & Write".

Log into Battle.net and your update will automatically begin.

The terminal commands I recommended are not for use when performing a major installation unless absolutely necessary. However, the Terminal command (and associated download for the executable you're activating) is beneficial as it will basically scrape up every log for every Blizzard installation all at once and let the CS/TS staff view them so they can offer a potential solution. Believe it or not, the actual cause of the BB8 error can be different for every person. It's literally a "catch all" for a specific subset of errors, though 90% of the time they're permissions related. This also tends to happen a lot in El Capitan thanks to how SIP interferes with things, even in /Applications.

What happens if you close the battle.net app, let it sit for 30 seconds (to let Agent auto-quit itself for a clean slate) and relaunch the battle.net app, also letting it idle for a couple of minutes just in case it needs to update? Odds are that you are in the middle of an update and the b.net app was pushed an update and broke the installation process at the same time. I've seen that happen before - the b.net app isn't exactly playing nice when it has an update pending and you're attempting to do something else at the same time.
