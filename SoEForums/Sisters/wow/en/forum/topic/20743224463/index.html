OPEN TO PUBLIC ATTENDANCE

or students to audit for Pass/Fail Grade

*So reads the heading of a flyer in bold. A green ribbon affixed to its upper left corner by wax with the Stormwind University seal stamped into it.*

An extensive review session to be held The TWENTY-NINTH of this month, at SEVEN BELLS o'th'eve, in the Stormwind University Theatre Green Room (( upstairs in the "restaurant" in Lakeshire, Redridge, where the last class was held)).

On docket for review shall be: Theatre Appreciation, script theory, acting basics, and a technical overview.

Any questions, or if you wish to audit please see Priori *there seems to be a second style of handwriting that has squeezed itself in* (Prof.) *Original handwriting resumes* Thinthistle during office hours ((Post here in this forum below, send me an in game mail, either toon [ Fangleford or Fangulford ], OR PST )) Or inquire with the main office (( any Stormwind University guildies should at least be able to direct you to me, if not provide more information)).

If you DO intend to audit the class for a Pass/Fail grade and earn credit, please submit your homework before the lecture as you may be asked to share with the class. Please notate upon your sheet if you would rather not share.

Chose One Prompt, and turn it in to Priori *other handwriting* (Prof.) *old handwriting* Thinthistle's office before class.

PROMPTS:

1: min 200, max 1000 words: "Is art just as necessary to existence as shelter, sustenance & safety? Explain why or why not."

2: Prepare a 1-5 minute monologue, any style allowed. Submit the script with character & blocking notes, try not to exceed 1000 words.

3: min 200, max 1000 words: Describe the last time you assisted with a technical aspect of a performance and why it was a necessary role.

*The bottom of the parchment has a stamp of the Stormwind University Theatre Department Seal. The University Griffon with the comedy/tragedy masks cradled above each wing.*

(( Theatre Arts Master Class. A compounded version of the Master Series from Last semester, for one extended lecture. Friday, April 29th, 7pm server! With drinks at The Ship-In-A-Bottle to follow! ))
