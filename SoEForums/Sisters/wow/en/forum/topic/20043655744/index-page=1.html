The Red Dawn


Part I

Two days had passed since the attack by the human forces of Arathor against Dawnbringer Keep, situated on a lonely island some way south of the former human kingdom of Lordaeron. Memorial services were to be carried out for those that fell in the attack two days before, later in the morning. Many were trying to get back to their daily lives, but the atmosphere around the keep was one of mourning and concern. Many elves had lost someone they loved, or someone they knew in the attack. It felt like the blood of their kin was still wet on the soil of the courtyard.

Sentries were on alert, constantly on patrol and stationed throughout the Keep. To make matters worse, all communication with their sister settlement across the lake in New Alah’dorei had ceased, and their dispatch riders hadn’t made their last return trip. Many feared that the settlement was taken by Arathorian forces on their westerly conquest. The day before, Lord-Commander Silversworn made it clear he did not want to risk more elves to find out their fate, when their own fate was so uncertain. He knew that he would need every single person capable of fighting in the times ahead. Many of his scouts and sentries were battle-hardened after all of the fighting and struggles they endured. Many still longed for their home of Alah’dorei in Quel’thalas, the abundant land and ever greens, back before the scourge came.

That was a different time, in a much different place…

House Dawnbringer, its remaining military forces, and its citizens now found themselves in a ravaged, nearly inhospitable land, far from their home, possibly surrounded by an enemy far greater in number than they. What was once seen as an advantage, being surrounded by water on the island, seemed to turn into a natural prison. Even their farmland settlements to the East in the foothills were unsafe and likely overrun by the humans.

Scout-Captain Lamaenel Lightblade had been in talks with Lady Sephariel over whether to make plans for their escape. If they were to become overwhelmed, surely all would be lost. She was granted permission to begin construction on small vessels to assist their people with an exit from the Island on the northern side. Her thinking was that if some could make it to the forsaken capital now known as the Undercity, they may find some refuge through their mutual affiliation with the Horde. After all, Sephariel and her people were allowed to settle at the keep in the first place as a temporary solution, until she was able to clear up her business with the leadership in Quel’thalas. Sephariel secretly hoped that the forsaken may come to their aid, but she knew there was a good chance the forsaken would just as easily leave them to die.

Since her House’s self-exile from Quel’thalas, when she learned of Lor’themar Theron’s ascension to Lord-Regent, she found herself with few allies, and politically speaking, she knew that the forsaken may find themselves on shaky ground if Silvermoon found out they were aiding her. She missed the security and stability of her true King, Anasterian Sunstrider and viewed the Lord-Regent as a traitor for not advocating for a council to determine the rightful heir to the throne, after Prince Kael’thas’ betrayal. After all, it was their way, she thought. All of this led to where they are now. She knew her people deserved better. It was always in the back of her mind, and she would flirt with the idea of returning home often just to give them that opportunity, despite her own views on those in power.

Lady Sephariel was in attendance at the memorial service, though she did not speak herself. She noticed most able to make it, but did not see the Lord-Commander present. After inquiring with a few of her advisors, no one knew where he was. Most considered he may be grieving on his own. It would be his way to do that. The Lord-Commander was a loner by nature, and did not speak much. The memorial was a somber occasion, filled with tears, and small speeches of remembrance of the departed. I’ve been involved in too many of these, Sephariel thought to herself. She felt scared inside, but knew that no one could be allowed to see that. She had to do her duty, just as her father before her, and his father before him.

The bodies were arranged in two rows from the gateway of the keep, to the steps leading up to the keep itself. Each was adorned with symbolic items, placed there by family members, and other cherished things they loved. The bodies were laid across pyres. Citizens were gathered on each side of the bodies. One by one, as senior members of the Dawnguard walked past the bodies with torches, they lit each of the pyres aflame. Sentries and Rangers were still on high alert. From their posts atop the walls of the keep they could see the Arathorian forces in the distance at the base of the Alterac Mountains. Following their retreat from two nights before, they set up encampments on the mainland. Their forces didn’t seem to be growing in number, but remaining steady. With watchful eyes, they kept their distance.

After the memorial service concluded, Sephariel, her sisters, other high ranking members of their House, and their military forces convened in the Main Hall to discuss the situation. All were present at the meeting, with one exception, Lord-Commander Materon Silversworn. In his place, Scout-Captain Lamaenel Lightblade sat. This wasn’t the first time she had to do this. She once filled in for him when he was granted time away on leave for a fortnight, so she was familiar with the role of Acting-Commander.

Sephariel sat in her usual place with Lamaenel to her right where Materon would normally sit, and her sister, Sepheriah Dawnbringer to her left. Sepheriah, one half of a set of twins with her sister Sephariah, was poorly received by many because of rumors from her past that she was responsible for the deaths of their parents. Nevertheless, she still was cherished by Sephariel, because she knew that everything her sister had been through made her strong. Her mastery over certain magics was well-known as well. She had been given the name, Crimson Phoenix, by the citizenry because many believed she was killed in the scourge war, only to resurface amidst rumors of treachery. It was intended as a pejorative, always spoken of in a mocking fashion, but over time Sepheriah claimed it as her own, and made it something to be proud of. She wore it as a badge of honor. She understood that you only give people the power that you wish for them to have. With a prudish posture, she sat, her icy green hues sharply observing the others gathered before them.

“You all know why we are here.” Sephariel began, “Something must be done about the Arathorian threat. We would be fools to think they mean to do anything but attack again.”

Lamaenel spoke up quickly, “My Lady, we have enough wood to construct four small seaworthy vessels, if we begin deconstructing less vital structures within the walls of the keep, we can produce twice as many.”

Sephariel looked on thinking for a few moments, before turning back to Lamaenel curiously, “How many will fit in one of these vessels?”, to which Lamaenel replied without missing a beat, “Six, my Lady, and that is pushing it. I can’t guarantee they’ll stay afloat beyond that.”

Hearing her words, Sephariel began to nod slowly, “Very well. Do what you need to do. Even with these vessels, that will do nothing for most of the people on the keep grounds.” She began muttering, “Most will be trapped here.” A look of sadness came over her in that moment as she realized that if the keep were to fall, most would die by the sword.

Sephariel’s sister Amarth’anel interjected, “Sister, it will not come to that. They will fall before us like the spineless weaklings they are. None shall stand against us and live. If it comes to that, I request permission to remain behind and destroy them in the name of our House.” Saliea Dawnbringer, sitting next to Amarth’anel chimed in, “As will I.”

Sephariel remained quiet as she sat there in her chair. She drew her hand up to her chin and thought to herself as she looked out across the people gathered before her, studying them. She then gave a reluctant nod of approval to her sister and her sister’s wife, “Very well. I will allow this under the circumstances. You’ve made me proud, Amarth’anel. You always did. Saliea, always know that I have loved you as a sister.” Sephariel gave Amarth’anel a broken smile quickly as she turned to the rest, “Anyone else willing to do what is necessary to ensure some live?”

Sepheriah began to speak, “Sister, I have-“ Sephariel cut her off, “No! I don’t want to hear any more about my family sacrificing themselves. One sister is enough. I know that you want to, and I know that you are able, but I forbid it. At least one of us must survive.” She looked at Sepheriah with a determined, almost desperate expression upon her face, “Do you understand?!”

Sephariel kept thinking to herself about her brother Alenil that was executed, thinking about the death of Amarth’anel, only to be risen by the scourge as a death knight, and that her other sister, the other twin Sephariah was missing, likely dead for all she knew. They both knew that the only person left in the line of succession was Sepheriah. Amarth’anel would be unable to claim the seat of House Dawnbringer due to her state of undeath. Her brother is dead, her other sister missing, and one is risen in undeath. She is all that would remain should something happen to Sephariel.

Sepheriah had to survive.

The various commanders of the Dawnguard and Suntread Rangers, along with other officers, stood up and reaffirmed their allegiance to Sephariel, and that they would lay down their lives if necessary to protect her and ensure that as many as they could, would be sent to safety. Several of the most seasoned officers had been with her since she left Quel’thalas, and even served under her father, Lord Sepharian Dawnbringer, the House patriarch, and many of the Dawnbringer’s namesake.

One of the officers, Commander Nor’drain Windborne stepped forward and kneeled, “With respect, my Lady.” Sephariel nodded approvingly, “Yes, Commander?”

“I served under your father’s command, and it would do me no greater honor to command the remaining forces should something go wrong. I would see you make it to safety as well, my Lady. Let me bear this burden. I’ve served for a long time, more than anyone else here. I will give them hell.” He looked back at those gathered as they all gave a slow nod of acceptance.

Sephariel noted their responses as he finished speaking.

“Commander Windborne, I-“, humbled by his selfless gesture, Sephariel began to fumble for words before the Commander cut in, “Please, Sephariel. You need not say anything. If it looks like the keep will be lost. Get to the boats. Save yourself, and the soul of our people.”

Sephariel said nothing aloud, but the look on her face said more than words could ever say. She closed her eyes and nodded humbly at the Commander’s request. The battle-hardened Commander dressed in his scarred and plated armor rose up from his knee slowly, turned around, and returned to his seat. With no way off the island for everyone, and the Arathorian forces surrounding them, everyone in the room fell silent, knowing that many in the room may not survive should the humans attack again.

After the silence wore off, Commander Windborne began to speak, “We’ll need our best sentries at the front. I want our best rangers posted at all of the fault lines of the keep’s weakened defenses.” He stopped for a moment and looked down a map laid out across the table, “What is left to do as far as reinforcing the walls that were damaged?”

Lead Architect Da’thris Fireblade stepped up to the table from behind one of the Commanders, “We’ve done everything we can. For the most part, the walls are as good as they were before the last attack. We can’t fortify them any better with what we have here. They should hold,” he paused, “for a time. Beyond that, I can give no guarantees, Commander.”

The rugged Commander gave Da’thris a nod of acknowledgement, “Very well.” He then turned to the now acting Scout-Commander Lamaenel Lightblade, “Lamaenel, once you have the vessels ready, come find me. I will need for you to take care of something before anything happens. It’s important.” She could see the seriousness in his eyes and in the tone of his voice, and with a prompt reply, “Yes, Commander.” The Commander then looked to Lady Sephariel, “With your permission, my Lady, I’d like to begin having our soldiers comb through the residents and arm everyone. We’ll need every able body we can get.” With a stern follow-up, “Everyone.”

The Lady knew what he was implying. Flashbacks from the scourge wars in Quel’thalas kept running through her mind, Is this really happening, again? Her eyes met his, “Proceed, Commander. You may continue the rest of your planning for our defenses.” The Commander gave a sharp nod, “As you wish, my Lady.”

She then stood from her seat, and shortly after, the rest of the room rose up to their feet as well. Once everyone was standing, she began to address them,

“Thank you, each of you for your service, for your selfless sacrifices you have already made, and have yet to make. I can never begin to fully express the aching in my heart in this moment. It is with great hesitation, but with the resolve of my father, and every Dawnbringer before him, that I send you to war against an enemy such as this. This time we are not just fighting for our home, or our land. We are fighting for our very lives, our continued existence! May the light guide each of you. Never forget who you are, or where you came from.

If they mean to destroy us, take as many of them with you as possible, so that our children can survive. Your deaths will not be in vain! You will be legends and remembered for all of eternity for what you do here today. May your blades swing swift, and your arrows strike true!

Selama ashal'anore.”

The ranking officers rendered their salutes as Sephariel returned them with a bow. Afterwards, Sephariel, Lamaenel, and Sepheriah exited the room, followed by the rest of those gathered.

It was nearing dusk, as the Sun made its last appearance on the horizon. There was lots of shuffling about, as residents moved hurriedly in various directions carrying supplies, weapons, and tools. It was colder than usual as night fell across the island. Eventually though, all of the moving around slowed, and quietened as some began to rest while others stood watch. They had been on guard day and night in shifts at that point for three days.

Sephariel sat in her bedroom reading through old diaries trying to better understand the frame of mind her father had during the most difficult of times, when suddenly,

Knock, knock, knock.

She quickly put the diary away, and heard her sister Sepheriah ask, “Are you busy?”

“No. Come in.” She replied. Her sister came in and sat next to her on her bed, “How are you feeling?” Sephariel knew that it was unlike her to come in and ask such things, so she went straight to the point, “What’s wrong, Sepheriah?”

Sepheriah looked surprised by the quick, pointed response, “I’m worried about you.” Sephariel sat there for a moment staring at her sister, “I’m worried about all of us.” She quipped reaching over to the diary, “I’ve been reading our father’s writings, trying to understand the way he thought. How he managed to last through so many battles against the trolls, the orcs, and what exactly he did in his final days as the scourge began to bear down on him.” Sepheriah looked at the journal in Sephariel’s hands, “What were you able to learn?” Sephariel shrugged, “Nothing really. Most of his writings were about us. He didn’t seem to write much about his military engagements.” Sepheriah shook her head, “Maybe he’d rather not remember them. I can’t blame him, after what I’ve seen.”

Sephariel nodded, “Nor do I.” Sephariel let out a big sigh and fell back onto her bed, flat on her back, “I keep saying I’m not ready for this, but when is anyone ever ready for something like this?”

At that moment Sephariel began to feel her eyelids get heavy. She heard her sister saying something, but her voice faded and within a minute or two, Sephariel was fast asleep. Her sister walked over and grabbed the journal, “Get some sleep. You probably haven’t slept in weeks.” She then left with it to read on her own as Sephariel lay there, sleeping peacefully.

As she lay there dying on the ground, she watched the unimaginable.

It was Dawnbringer Keep burning to nothing as the Arathorians over ran it. Everything faded to nothing.

She stormed out of bed and got her things together. She ran to the Main Hall and sent a scout to find Lamaenel to inform her that her presence was required, immediately. Within minutes Lamaenel came running through to the Main Hall, “Yes, my Lady? You needed me?”

Sephariel spoke quickly and to the point, “Lamaenel, I have to know right now, if we consumed everything possible to make boats what can we do?”

Lamaenel had a look of confusion on her face, but straightened out as she began thinking, “I-I, I’m not sure. I’d hav-“ Sephariel cut her off abruptly, “We have to get everyone off the island. Now! I want you to go down there and get started immediately. Start loading people onto boats and send them away. We have to begin the evacuation now.”

Lamaenel began to look very concerned. Luckily no others were within earshot at that moment, “At once, my Lady.” Sephariel stopped her as she began to take off, “Begin getting others to tear down everything that can be used to build vessels. We’re getting off this island by dawn.”

Lamaenel paused at her words, “Yes, my Lady.” And then shot off at a full run.

It was nearing midnight across the land. The moon was bright and high in the sky that night. In its light you could make out shadows as they crawled across the courtyard. Sephariel had made her way back to her chamber. Not long had passed since Sephariel sent Lamaenel away, when suddenly her sister Sepheriah stormed in, “I just heard what you ordered. What the hell is going on?”
