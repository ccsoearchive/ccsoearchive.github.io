Last night our nemesis of the Black Needles returned in a big way. How big? More than one Servitor ICly lost their lunch when we got to the lumber mill.

The Servitors have been busy this month:

- They ventured into the Ghostlands to retrieve a book of lost knowledge from a forgotten Quel'dorei library that was overrun by a malignant fungus-slime that, after failing to absorb them, farted a blast of methane and sulfur at them as a final blow.
- They returned to the mine where the forgotten prison was, to examine it more closely, and barely escaped a cave-in engineered by their Unnamed nemesis.
- Kel led a mission on Draenor to rebuild housing for Draenei who'd lost their homes due to fire.
- Sandy introduced the concept of Sunday brunch.
- We have a new Chaplain: Congratulations Initiate Brightiron!
- And a new Chronicler, Initiate Stoneforge!
- Eirian found an Ebon Blade contact who uncovered some vital information for him about some dark dealings in New Hearthglen. There will be trouble down the road....
- Speaking of Eirian, he's done a great job coordinating our sparring practice sessions. Last week it was hand-to-hand, this week we're in full gear.
- The Servitors chased down a gang of bandits in the Arathi Highlands, discovered possible Defias ties and maybe a link to an old antagonist, rescued a farmer's daughter, defused a bomb, and made frag grenades sexy--all in one exciting night of RP.
- In the Prose section of our guild website, our writing-inclined members have been having fun with the Seven Deadly Sins Challenge: name a Servitor, name a sin. That Servitor then must write a drabble about that sin and how it affects their character.

We are looking for well-drawn characters of every class and skill set, created by players who have stories to tell and want to share their talents. We love players who want to seek out RP and create content. Visit us at http://servitorsoflothar.guildlaunch.com to check out our Handbook, read our IC journals, and send your IC letter of interest to the Commander!

Tonight the Servitors faced off against the Iron Knuckles!

The Knucks set a clever trap, baited with several cages full of innocent captives and rigged to explode. The situation only got worse when the Servitors' Gnomish communicators, which they've depended on so often in the field, suddenly began to malfunction, emitting static, earsplitting whines, and even stray radio transmissions, interrupting communication and giving away the positions of their scouts! The Servitors managed to turn the tables on the Knuckles by blowing up their munitions stockpile while "Joy to the World" by Three Worgen Night played over the comm channel.

Last night the Servitors were in Outland, escorting a supply wagon to Wildhammer Stronghold. There was a moment of tension as they hurried past Legion Hold, but they weren't noticed, and continued on their way:

[Etharion]: That was the worst part!
[Shivs]: Really? Because I won't complain if that was as bad as it got.


They spoke too soon.

Above them, a chimaera lazily floats ovehead. One of its heads tilts and notices the talbuks. Uh oh.

The chimaera roars with both heads and makes a dive for the talbuks. It's clearly thinking of dinner!

[Roiya]: Oh bloody--
[Davvì]: OH GOSH DIVEBOMBER!!!
Shivs reaches for her blades, drawing them slowly. "Eyes uP UP UP!!" Her tone makes a sudden jump to alarm as the wyvvern dives.
Roiya gathers shadows between her palms, then sends a blast of pure force of will toward the chimera, hoping it will be enough to somehow divert it from its course.
Etharion nocks an arrow and launches it at the incoming beast!
Davvì crouches down, bracing herself back against the cart's load, and raises her mace-arm to fire a volley of micro missiles up at the swooping chimaera.

The chimaera banks and dodges Roiya's blast of power, but Etharion's arrow cuts through a wing, and Davvi's missiles pepper its soft underbelly. The beast roars in pain.

The talbuks don't want to be dinner! And the onrushing chimaera--now falling more than diving--doesn't help. Spooked, the talbuks take off running. Gunnhild rocks back and forth, trying in vain to pull the talbuks up, but the cart rattles and jounces as the talbuks race off the road and over the broken, ashy landscape!

Roiya swears. A lot.
Davvì: Oh golly mind the acceleration!!!
Shivs holds on as tights as she can to the side of the cart. "-Now- this is feeling like a normal mission!" She calls over the clamor of cart wheels.


Shivs wasn't kidding. Apply at http://servitorsoflothar.guildlaunch.com and you could join us on a wild ride.

Thursday saw a wrap-up to our Children's Week storyline, "The Kids Aren't All Right." And they really weren't.

A bog witch--a shapeshifting nightmare thing that was part horse, part bat, part old hag, and all teeth and hunger--has been preying on the children of Menethil since at least last year, when the Servitors first encountered it as a mean old neighbor lady. Last year they hurt it and drove it away, but it only went into hiding. This time they made sure to finish the job, killing the thing up close and personal. Roiya went all dark side stabby. M got exposed to saronite-tainted blood. And everyone collectively went "EWW!" when they got a look at the bog witch's true form.

It warmed the cockles of this guild leader's heart.

Tonight the Servitors are hired for escort duty, to make sure that Silver Blaze--the favorite for the inaugural Kalimdor Derby, an endurance race across the sands of Tanaris--makes it to the starting line. There's rumors that some bookies in Gadgetzan have a vested interest in seeing to it that Silver Blaze doesn't get there.

The Servitors successfully escorted Silver Blaze to Gadgetzan, but we earned our hazard pay: our ship, the Crunchnut (piloted by one Captain Crunch), came under heavy fire by goblin flyers! Luckily Adjutant Rattleclank was flying escort in her gyrocopter and managed to blast both flyers out of the sky after a thrilling dogfight. Silver Blaze's owner, Madame Gogo Bordello of the Steamwheedle Cartel, was so pleased that she added a bonus on top of the agreed-upon payment, and paid for the injured Servitors to receive full healing once the Crunchnut pulled into port.

Madame Bordello was not so pleased the next day when her Silver Blaze (along with his rival, the Bilgewater Cartel-owned second choice in the betting, Bilge Pump) finished the race behind the eventual winner, longshot Sharknado. But that wasn't our fault.

This past Tuesday, the Servitors wrapped up a storyline that's been a bit over a year in the making by cornering and conquering the Needler...only to open the door to something even worse, a terrifying undead entity known only as the Unnamed. Millennia ago, this Highborne mage went darkside in such a big way, the Quel'dorei not only shunned him, they cursed his True Name and erased it from their records and from all memory. He was so awful even Death didn't want him.

And the Servitors are in possession of a ritual that he wants.

Oh yeah, and some other things happened. Lirren caught the Needler's final minion, Drusilla Drudgebottom, who'd been mind-controlled into an assassination attempt on Etharion. Frovelos lost an eye in the final confrontation with the Needler (but he'll look dashing with an eyepatch). Etharion inhaled cyanide gas. And when making mind contact with the Needler, M. saw a vision that was "Emperor Palpatine-bangs-Voldemort" levels of squick. Really, she threw up in her mouth.

So one story ends, another revs up as the Unnamed is about to take center stage. Now is a great time to get involved. If you want a guild where you can develop your character and take part in long-term storylines, we'd like to meet you. Visit us at http://servitorsoflothar.guildlaunch.com and hit the "apply" link on the left.
