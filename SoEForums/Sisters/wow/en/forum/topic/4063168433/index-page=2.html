Mialera Darkweaver, the Queen of Souls

This chaotic creature barely resembles her original form; once a human woman who prayed for paradise, she has given into her madness of shadow and souls, devouring all life in an attempt to create an imprisoning heaven for all living things. The souls give her immense power and near immortality, only furthering her arrogance and madness. For nearly two centuries this maddened being has fled from frame to frame, hiding her soul within a new body each time... now, she's at her last. Her madness must end.

LOCATION:
This fight takes place within Soulreave Citadel, Mialera's base of operations. The room is Mialera's Throne: it is a large, deep purple stone that floats directly above the twisting nether, in between Netherstorm and Hellfire Peninsula. At the back of the rock lies an ornate golden throne with various soul gems hiding within. The fight does not begin until a player approaches the throne (within 30yd distance).

PHASES: This fight has numerous phases and inter-phases;

PHASE ONE: FOUR SOUL MASTERY

Mialera begins the fight atop her throne, leaping off and landing in the center of the fighting area. In this first phase, she will cast a spell to split her soul into four equal parts of her magical expertise: Frost, Fel, Necrotic and Light. Each soul has a unique name and model to distinguish itself from the others.

Fro'av'ae, the Frost Mistress wears ice blue robes, a blue headband and wields a scythe of frost. She is a mostly melee attacker with high slowing capabilities and Area of Affect attacks.
ABILITIES:

Frost Wave; Fro'av'ae launches a wave of ice at her foes, dealing damage and slowing them temporarily.

Frozen Shackles; Fro'av'ae binds a unit in place, stopping attacks but allowing spellcasting to continue. Haste is reduced by 15% while shackled.

Scythe Leap: Fro'av'ae will leap to a random target, dealing damage and slowing one single target. This ability is an aggro dump.

Mial the Darkweaver is a spellcaster utilizing fel magic (a combination of shadow and flame). She is a ranged DPS character.
ABILITIES:

Felfire Bolt: launches a bolt of fel fire at a single target, dealing damage. Can be interrupted.

Murderous Shade: summons a weak shadow to attack an enemy. This shadow loses health over time and may only survive for ten seconds. This shadow moves very slow.

Black Flame: engulfs nearby enemies in a cone of black fire, dealing damage and reducing hit rating by 40% for the time. Only used upon characters in melee range. Cannot strike units behind her.

Lady Sarath Karkun Nemesis, the Plagueweaver utilizes necromantic energies to fight her enemies. She focuses on DoT abilities.
ABILITIES:

Spread Plague: Lady Nemesis inflicts a disease upon a random player, dealing damage over time. After ten seconds, this disease spreads to any other player near the first afflicted player. This disease cannot be cleansed. Spread has an 8yd range.

Festering Wound: Lady Nemesis infects a wounded ally, reducing healing on them and dealing damage over time. This abilitiy prioritizes Fro'av'ae's or Mial's current targets.

Corruption Aura: Lady Nemesis deals weak damage over time to enemies that stand near her. Deals increased damage the more plagues that are on the target.

De'vella Dennavor is a healer and wielder of minor light magic. She deals very little damage and focuses mainly on healing her allies.
ABILITIES:

Redeeming Light; heals all allies for 10% their maximum health. 6 second cast time. Can be interrupted.

Holy Wave; heals any creature caught in the wave for 12% their maximum health. Can heal allies and enemies. 15yd range, travels in a straight line. Instant cast.

Greater Heal; heals one ally for 15% their total health plus 5% their current missing health. 4 second cast time. Can be interrupted.

NOTE: These four characters do NOT share a health bar, thus the group should prioritize one and then focus down another in sequence. They do not empower each other upon death.

PHASE TWO: THE QUEEN'S FURY

When all four soul fragments are defeated, they will re-unite into Mialera Darkweaver, the Queen of Souls. Mialera begins this fight at 70% health. Mialera gains a new set of abilities and forgoes her previous ones.
NEW MECHANIC: SOUL COUNT

Mialera's entire combat prowess is based upon her current Soul Count. She will gain souls over the course of the fight depending on what abilities she uses, and she is empowered for every soul that reaches her. Soul Count works as such:

Might of Many: Mialera's damage is increased by 1% for every 5 souls in her possession.
Growing Army: At 25% damage increase, Mialera gains access to her second set of abilities.
Eternal Army: At 50% damage increase, Mialera gains access to her third set of abilities and may now devour allied players.
Vast Army: At 75% damage increase, Mialera gains access to one more ability and can now steal spells from other players at random.
Infinite Army: At 100% damage increase, Mialera uses all of her acquired souls to deal instant area of effect damage to the group, essentially wiping the raid. This ability pierces all shields, immunities and forms of resistance.

NEW MECHANIC: Devour Soul
Certain abilities will allow Mialera to devour a player's soul, however this does not kill them. Instead, the player is teleported within Mialera's own soul and must find their way out. While inside, the player will encounter patrolling enemies and trapped souls.
Mialera's Will is an enemy that patrols within the area and will suppress, snare, slow and stun enemy players it encounters. Its damage is very weak.
Soul Tethers are links that connect a soul to Mialera. If this link is destroyed, Mialera loses any soul attached to it. This is one of the few ways to reduce Mialera's Soul Count.
Soul Rips are portals that lead outside of Mialera's imprisonment. Clicking on this portal returns you to your body, but disorients you for four seconds.

ABILITIES:
Soul Count at 0-24%

Shadow Cleave: Deals instant damage to all enemies in a cone directly in front of her. Damage is split among all targets struck.

Send Soul: Mialera sends a soul to torment an ally, dealing continuous damage until that soul is destroyed. Stacks up to five times. At five stacks the ally is devoured by Mialera.

Nightmarish Vision: Mialera sends a soul to strike fear into an ally, slowing spellcasting and interrupting spells until destroyed. Deals no damage. Stacks up to five times. At five stacks the ally is devoured by Mialera.

Soul Count at 25-49%

Soul Wave: Mialera sends a wave of souls enemies in front of her, dealing conical damage. This ability reduces her soul count by an equivalent of 5%, and will not be used if her soul count is at 30% or less.

Spirit Rend: Mialera rends a target, dealing damage over time until the target is fully healed. If the target dies from this effect, they are devoured by Mialera.

Soul Count at 50-74%

Devour: Devours an allied player's soul instantly. Must be within melee range. Applies Mialera's Hunger on the target (Mialera's Hunger: Mialera is chasing you down to devour your soul! Run!).

Arcing Shadow: Deals damage to up to five players within an 8yd range.

Agony and Pain: Deals damage instantly to one target, and then damage over time for five seconds.

Soul Count at 75-99%

Soul Mirror: Mialera steals an ability that was recently used by any target within the raid. Cast periodically every 30 seconds. This can steal only spells. When stolen, target cannot use that spell for the next 5 seconds. If the spell is an aura, this aura is applied to Mialera until 15 seconds have passed.

Possess: Mialera fully possesses an ally, increasing their health by 500% and damage by 20%. Ally becomes tauntable. While possessed, the ally is considered devoured and can shatter soul tethers.

Soul Count at 100%

Wrath of the Queen: Mialera unleashes all of her devoured souls to deal lethal damage to the raid, wiping the raid.

PHASE THREE: THE SELF PROCLAIMED GODDESS

This phase begins when Mialera reaches 40% health. She will scream in frustration and stun the raid, beginning to devour every single soul she has. When this effect ends, Mialera's form becomes half demonic, with a shattered black wing erupting from her back, dark purple cracks forming along her body and her eyes gaining a black flame as a pupil. She will now aggro dump occasionally, and her spells become unpredictable. Her spell roster is drastically reduced for this fight.

MECHANIC: SOUL TETHERS

Mialera's might is connected to her souls. Soul Tethers now appear whether a target is devoured or not and can be destroyed. For each soul tether destroyed, Mialera takes damage and loses some of her power. Mialera can shatter a soul tether herself to heal herself. She cannot heal herself past 40%, and devouring this soul also reduces the damage she can deal until she links a new soul to her.

Link Soul: Mialera creates a soul tether between herself and another soul, increasing damage dealt.

Devour Soul: Mialera shatters a tether to heal herself, reducing damage dealt but healing herself by 2% her total health.

ABILITIES:

Maddened Strike: Mialera will periodically reset her aggro table and focus on a random player. This player becomes her target for the next 20 seconds until aggro is reset again. Applies the buff Queen's Challenge to the target (Queen's Challenge: Mialera is focusing you! Damage done reduced by 50%, damage dealt reduced by 20%, haste increased by 10%, healing received increased by 10%, you are rooted).

Demon's Claw: Deals instant damage to an enemy (May only be used when health is above 35%).

Soul Strike: Deals damage to all enemies within melee range and silences them for two seconds (May only be used when health is above 25%).

Blackflame Blade: Deals damage over time to her current target and spreads damage to two other nearby targets (May only be cast if health is above 15%).

Insane Fury: Mialera's madness fully consumes her, now dashing to a target at random for a single attack every 15 seconds and increasing her attack speed by 20%. Lasts until death.

Tyrynna the Broken

A shell of the being she once was, the telepath known as Tyrynna has been forced into madness, revealing a collection of alternate personalities that manifest against her will. What's left of elf only desires peace, but such is impossible, until a band of heroes should answer her call...

The fight takes place within the elf's mind, a blank white expanse with a paved, black path ahead. On the path, a group of trash mobs will ambush players at certain points. Upon approaching Tyrynna, a black, inky web circles around the raid which deals low damage when touched and prevents their escape. She will not attack until taunted.

PHASE ONE: Within the Shell

When the fight starts, Tyr is in her elven form. She uses a handful of archery abilities, as well as a few of her psionic powers.

Scarlet Arrow: Tyrynna Stormshade fires a blood red arrow in the direction of a random player, dealing moderate damage to all players in the arrow's path.

Scream: Tyrynna lets out a psionic scream, doing moderate AoE damage to anyone within 10 yards of her and stunning them for three seconds. This power is charged and therefore avoidable.

Agony: Tyrynna targets a random player and unleashes a psi barrage, dealing high damage.

PHASE TWO: Are We Demented?

At 75% health, Tyrynna Stormshade casts Dementia, an un-interruptable psionic scream that knocks all players back and stuns them. She then splits into four entities, Dischord, Delicate, Deceit, and Destruction, all of which look like varying copies of her, and each harbor their own set of abilities.

Dischord of Chaos: Dischord takes the appearance of a Black-and-white Tyr wearing garments of scarlet. She carries a bloodstained scythe and deals high physical damage in addition to one psionic ability.

Harvest: Dischord sweeps her scythe in a circle around her, dealing high damage to anyone within 5 yards of her.

Soul Reap: Dischord targets a random player and rushes them, dealing 75% of the player's remaining health as damage and also dealing small damage to anyone who is in her path when she charges. She receives healing for every player damaged by this attack. This power is interruptible.

Nightmare: Dischord afflicts a random player with Unending Nightmare, a curse which inflicts moderate shadow damage every three seconds.

Delicate of Fragility: The creature bound in straps and chains is Delicate, who specializes in mind control and telepathy without the assistance of other weapons.

Half-Pain: Similar to Scream, Delicate lashes out at everyone around her, dealing high damage to everyone within a 15 yard range.

Marionette: Delicate takes control of a random player, as well as all others next to that player, for 30 seconds. This cannot be interrupted or dispelled.

Edge: At 10% health, Delicate loses control and lets out a psionic cry, causing mental shutdown and death to all players within 30 yards. This power is NOT interruptible, however it deals 1% of her health as recoil for each player killed.

Deceit of Shadow: The masked and leather-bound deceit is a master assassin, using very rogue-like melee abilities amplified by her psychic powers.

Deception's Shiv: Deceit attacks her target with both blades, dealing moderate damage and poisoning the target. The poison does high damage every 5 seconds and CAN be dispelled.

Vendetta: At 75% 50% and 25% health, Deceit marks a random player for death. She then disappears, only to reappear behind the target. She channels a series of harsh attacks for 5 seconds, which do fatal damage unless interrupted and high AoE damage to anyone within 5 yards. The targeted player is stunned for the duration of the attack.

Cloak and Dagger: At 60& and 30% health, Deceit uses her abilities to disguise herself as a random player and approaches that player. If she is not spotted and attacked, Deceit will attempt to eliminate the target by dealing high damage to that player.

Destruction of Cataclysm: Destruction is the true name of the being once known as Tyrynna. She uses archery and shares the abilities of her original form, on a smaller scale.

PHASE THREE: Mad World

When all four have been defeated, all four meld together into an entirely different being, known as The Maddened. The Maddened has 50% health upon entering combat, and gains one advanced ability of each of her four forms, in addition to one more.

Vendetta

Marionette

Agony

Nightmare

End of it All: At 1% health, The Maddened starts to break apart the conjured world within her mind, starting with herself. The webs surrounding the fight dissolve, allowing players to escape. All players who have not escaped the webs will die instantly.

Upon escaping, the players will find themselves in a circular stone room, with Tyrynna's corpse laying in the center.

Sathrasa

A black guard in service to lord Imperon Showdah, Sathrasa is an experienced fighter and assassin, as well as a gem magus, wielding spellgems and magical crystals to imbue her own abilities.

There are no trash mobs, and the fight takes place within a war zone, surrounded by other soldiers. Sathrasa is the first boss encountered; she is a combat rogue-type character which utilizes various spells for her abilities. These spells can be stolen and utilized by the raid. There are two phases.

PHASE ONE: Loyal Servant

MECHANIC: Spell Gems
Sathrasa's armor is littered with spell gems of all kinds, each containing a spell or ability of some sort. If a tank strikes a critical blow upon Sathrasa, there is a 15% chance that one of these gems will be knocked off of her armor. A raid member may then gather these gems for their own use. The gems are as follows:

Red, "Might": Increases all damage dealt by 5%. This effect is permanent until phase two. Stacks. If used on Sathrasa, increases her damage by 15% for 20 seconds.

Yellow, "Refresh": Increases mana, energy, rage, focus and ruinic power regeneration by 10%, stacks, lasts until phase two. If used on Sathrasa, she can cast three spells in succession without cost. The spells are randomized.

Blue, "Shield": Reduces all damage the user takes by 10%, stacks until phase two. If used on Sathrasa, increases her defenses, allowing her to parry 50% of all frontal attacks, and she will absorb damage dealt behind her for 10 seconds.

White, "Rejuvenation": Heals the target for 5% their maximum health per second for five seconds. If used on Sathrasa, heals her for 10% health per three seconds, infinite duration, dispellable.

Green, "Acid": All attacks done by the user now apply a DoT, dealing damage over time based upon their highest stat, stacks five times. If used by Sathrasa, she applies a stacking debuff to her current target, reducing damage dealt and increasing damage taken, stacks, lasts 15 seconds, refreshes with each new stack.

In addition to her crystals, Sathrasa can use the following abilities;

Blade Flurry: Sathrasa deals 200% increased damage to a single target. If another target is beside her current target, this damage is split among the two targets.

Riposte: Parries the next attack, stunning the attacker and switching targets, dealing damaged to the stunned unit. Stun lasts 3 seconds.

PHASE TWO: Crystal Ascendant

At 50% health, Sathrasa will use a multicoloured crystal, her body shifting and warping until it is half crystallized. She will attack with 20% increased attack speed, and now uses an arm blade and a crystal sword. Her spell roster changes during this time.

Crystal Spire: Deals damage and impales a target, bleeding them until the Crystal Spine is destroyed.

Crystal Restore: Four second cast, heals Sathrasa for 15% health. Interruptible.

Crystal Yield: Removes all armor and defenses upon a target for 20 seconds and increases the damage they take by 10%. Cast every 23 seconds.

Crystal Charge: Sends a blast wave of energy, dealing damage to anyone fifteen yards in front of Sathrasa. Can only be cast at five stacks of Slice and Dice. When cast, gives the buff "Crystal Mastery".

Slice and Dice: stacking attack speed buff, is reset every time Sathrasa casts Crystal Charge. Applied every 20 seconds.

Crystal Mastery: Increases all damage dealt by 5%. Stacks until death.

Kairesh "Burning" Fury

Blademaster, Master Swordsmith, Fisherman, Drunken Bastard... he was a lot of things. Mostly the latter nowadays. Well he's offered you a fun little challenge; can -you- kick his !@# for free stuff? Win and he'll give you some finely crafted weaponry, armor, some trinkets from his travels, even a bit of gold. Lose... well, you may find yourself naked tied to a post in Thunderbluff.

Kairesh Fury is a three phase fight. He is a fury warrior with protection side abilities and a hefty dose of fire magic. He can also render other players drunk, both an advantage and disadvantage to the inebriated player. The fight takes place in one of three zones, chosen randomly when first encountered:
The Feather's of Iron Tavern (Stranglethorn Vale)
The Grim Guzzler (Blackrock Mountain)
The Firelands

MECHANIC: DRUNKENNESS

Throughout the fight, Kairesh will throw ale at players, which will be instantly quaffed. Players will have a sobriety bar throughout the fight.

100 Sobriety: You are sober. No bonuses/debuffs.
75 Sobriety: You're a wee bit tipsy. 10% increased damage, -3% hit chance, 5% dodge chance.
50 Sobriety: You're kinda drunk. 20% increased damage, -6% hit chance, 10% dodge chance, you may accidentally cast the wrong spell or the spell might fizzle.
25 Sobriety: You are freakin hammered. 30% increased damage, -9% hit chance, 15% dodge chance, you may cast the wrong spell/spell may fizzle, and you may hiccup, interrupting spellcasting.
0 Sobriety: You're down. Vomitting for 5 seconds, unable to act. When completed, Sobriety returns to 50.
Sobriety will slowly increase per second that you are not drinking beer.

PHASE ONE: Shahram the Shield Bearer.

Kairesh Fury will wield a sword known as the Blackblade of Shahram; with it, he can summon a flamewaker to his side. He will also use a gigantic spiked shield.

Summon Shahram: Kairesh will summon an untargetable flamewaker named Shahram. Shahram has no aggro table. Shahram can cast Flamestrike (AoE damage), Cone of Flame (conical fire attack), Burning Fist (DoT on a single melee target) and The Blessing of Shahram (increases all of Kairesh Fury's statistics by 1% for every 20 seconds spent in Phase One).

Spiritual Immunity: While wielding the Blackblade of Shahram, Kairesh Fury is immune to all drain effects; Drain Soul, Drain Life, Drain Mana, Manaburn, Devouring Plague, and related abilities will fizzle when used.

Shield Slam: Kairesh slams his body-sized shield into the melee target, impaling them. Deals damage over time and decreases his attack speed and movement speed by 20%. Kairesh will shake the target off his shield after 10 seconds. Cast every 30 seconds.

Fire Cleave: Kairesh will cleave with his blackblade, dealing damage.

Hilt Bash: Kairesh bashes a target with the hilt of his blackblade. Stuns the target. Cannot be used against tanks.

Blade Toss: Kairesh throws his blackblade at a target spellcaster; the target is silenced for 3 seconds. Further, if the target was casting a spell when struck, they cannot use any spell in that school for 15 seconds. Cast every 30 seconds. May only target spellcasters (Mages, warlocks, priests, druids, shamans).

PHASE TWO: The Twin Maws of Lionheart.

At 65% health, Kairesh knocks back all targets by 15 yards and switches his weapons, desummoning Shahram. He will now wield two Lionheart Executioners, blades the size and width of his body, and use new fire abilities.

Spinning Flame: Kairesh whirls his blades in a dervish, summoning a Flame Tornado. Flame Tornadoes chase a single player for 10 seconds. If caught, player is knocked into the air and dealt damage while in the tornado.

Flame Whirl: Kairesh whirlwinds in a circle, dealing damage in melee range and sending out a blast wave of fire 10 yards around him, dealing damage.

Maw of Lionheart: Kairesh jams his blade into the neck of his foe and stabs it into the ground, pinning the target. He will use his other blade one handed, dealing less damage but using his abilities twice as often. Pinned targets cannot take any action, and take damage over time from Lionheart's flame. An ally can remove the blade to free the pinned target.

Low Blow: Kairesh Fury does an underhanded kick to the loins with his plated boot, stunning his target for 8 seconds. May only be used on the player that saved a pinned target from Maw of Lionheart.

Doctor Vincent H. Wolfe, W.M.D.

As the reclusive head of the Modas il Toralar's Research department, Doctor Wolfe spends most of his time locked in the V.A.U.L.T., building constructs and devices that would benefit Science! (and, as an unintended side effect, the Modas). Headmaster Aziel V'Ghera tolerates his frequently changing eccentricities because some of his ideas last long enough to not detonate if looked at wrong.

Doctor Wolfe is a four-phase boss-fight that would finish the V.A.U.L.T. wing of the Modas il Toralar dungeon/raid. Though he will initially start as a engineer/alchemy warlock hybrid, the combat will change. Mechanically, it is unclear if some of the phases would have to be separated into different entities.
