After briefly talking with Zurom last night about what traits his characters might have if they were followers I thought it would be fun to make a topic about it. The idea is simple. If your characters happened to be garrison followers what abilities and traits would make the most sense thematically for them to have? It is fine to disregard the in-game restrictions if you feel it fits the theme of your character.

If you are unsure of what all the different abilities and traits are then you can find them here:

http://www.wowhead.com/guides/garrisons/followers/warlords-of-draenor-follower-guide

Now I'll start first :3

Gnomebane Mormel
Goblin Destruction Warlock

Abilities
-Rain of Fire [For some insane reason Destro followers can't get this. It's stupid.]
-Summon Infernal

Traits
-Alchemy
-Economist
-Scavenger

Rain of Fire and Summon Infernal are both spells Mormel has used IC. They also appropriately fit her reputation as a "fire mage". Alchemy and Economist are obvious choices since she is an established alchemist who naturally gets along well with other goblins. Finally her business skills and pursuit of profit enables her to obtain more resources via the Scavenger trait.

Gerhilda Colbrew
Dwarven Fire Mage

Abilities
-Conjure Food [Normally limited to Inn Arcane Mages but it fits Gerhilda perfectly]
-Polymorph

Traits
-Hearthstone Pro
-Humanist
-Epic Mount

Conjure Food I feel is self explanatory and Gerhilda has had some... "success" with the Polymorph skill before. Hearthstone Pro is a nod to her sheltered geeky side while Humanist represents her time living in Stormwind and thriving more in human culture than she ever did in Ironforge. Epic Mount is a reference to her "cat" Felicia who has not only enabled Gerhilda to do things she normally couldn't but in a very speedy/efficient manner.

The reasoning: Arjah's not actually a very good mage, and has long relied on comical and non-lethal argument-enders like turning people into turtles or cutting off their speech in lieu of fighting (and probably losing) a duel. Thus, Polymorph/Counterspell for abilities.

Extra Training stems from all her years as a teacher of one sort or another, Voodoo Zealot is obvious given her long activism on behalf of the Horde's trolls (and her dalliances with "Darkspear Nationalism" and other radical troll movements, back before it was cool/patched-in), and Dancer, well...she's never been shy about shaking her fanny. It was that or "High Stamina," if you know what I mean.

That said, Arjah would make a terrible follower. Do you know how many jobs she's been fired from? You're probably better off keeping her Inactive.

I'm guilty. I really don't know the dynamics of follower abilities and traits well enough to choose, so I'm going off of what I've seen some followers do during quests and such.

I really wanted the mage from Talador (mage tower) to be a bodyguard, only because I think it'd be fun to run around on my mage with a mage follower doing magery things. So to that end I made Tink here a bodyguard :)

She's intended to follow a fellow mage (specifically my human mage-Spectral, for RP reasons), but I suppose any other caster could possibly benefit. Meant to be completely utilitarian. idk, maybe she'd be the worst follower ever but I'd have fun with it. :)

As a bodyguard, in combat, ...
  • she'd cast a power boosting buff and throw down arcane runes at your feet all with the intent of boosting mana regen and buffing spell power.
  • If you pulled two or more attackers, she'd randomly select from the ones you weren't currently attacking and use a random polymorph (sheep, rabbit, pig, etc.) for crowd control. She'd also randomly select a caster from the bunch and silence using her Counterspell at every given chance.
  • She'd also offer a few select nifty things depending on your reputation such as more powerful buffs, larger crowd control spells, and a few fun things like randomly turning you into a sprite darter or some such.
    • That's it.
    No offensive spells whatsoever.
  • If she's attacked she'll pop up a frost shield, pop presence of mind, and sheep the one attacking her. If she has more than one assailant she'd then continuously blink back and forth until you pulled them off her or she's defeated.


    • Out of combat you'd be able to click on her for a teleportation dialog, where you could select any major outpost on Draenor and port there, including a neutral flight master in Frostfire.

    While she's not following me around I'd like to have her ever present in my Garrison, and since my mage now Enchants it'd be neat to have Tink hanging around and helping out at my Enchanter's Hut.

    Because it would be humorous to me, everytime the cell populates, no matter where she is, Tink will randomly have a chance of producing a large explosion that will coat her, the area around her and any nearby npcs (including you) in smoking black soot for the next few minutes; thus illustrating why she doesn't use offensive spells.
