Hi there.

This isn't a post to complain about WoW, so much as to provide an exit commentary about why I'm finally leaving the game after so long. This is here to help out WoW developers, and it is also a message to my friends and guild members explaining why I'm leaving.

Quick background. I've been playing my partner since the launch of TBC, and we had a blast at that time. We were very new and never raided, but we enjoyed the game a lot. We liked Wrath ok, and we got into serious raiding at that time (on our little server). We started a raiding guild and ran raids 3-5 nights per week from Ulduar launch through today. We currently have a raid team that just about has DW down on normal, and we just finished our first FL legendary (of which I was the recipient) last Tuesday.

With that out of the way, I've been struggling to stay with WoW for a while now. Cata was interesting at launch. I drove 60 miles to get my box at midnight in rural america, and I was excited about the new content and zones. Over the course of this expansion, I've logged in less and less, until now I'm on 3 nights a week only to raid lead.

I'm burnt out on the game. I have 9 x 85s (most of which had up to date gear at some point during this expansion). I've started a Worgen alt, and did most of archeology, but nothing really seems enjoyable anymore. Raiding slightly fills the bill, but with having to recruit as people leave the game, or have RL issues, it's just not as rewarding for me anymore.

A lot of games on the market have gone F2P, and I have been feeling really resentful at having to pay for WoW lately. I burn through most of what makes a patch interesting in a week or two, and then I struggle to stay interested while I'm stuck paying sub fees that aren't really justified for me (just for a few hours of raiding).

I would probably continue to play if I could unlock each new tier of content for a fee (maybe $5-10) to play through at my leisure with no monthly fees. Another option is to sub only when a new patch comes out, and then to drop it immediately after, so that I only pay to play when I'm interested. If I didn't have raiding commitments, I would have shifted to this approach a long time ago.

Another big factor for me is the upcoming expansion. I can't really see myself putting down $50+ just so that I have the option of subbing/unsubbing when it gets interesting. I may check out free trials (if they're offered to me), but I have no plans to pay for a new box (that requires sub fees), and I won't resub if I can't access the new content without paying for the box first.

In short, I'm thrifty, and on a tighter budget, and WoW just isn't delivering entertainment value anymore. I have not played Skyrimm yet, I have not checked out SWTOR; I'm waiting for those games to come way down in price. I've never played Rift.

At the same time, so many MMOs are shifting to F2P formats, and it works so much better for me. I'm currently playing around with DDO and Poxnora, and I'm playing FF11 for a few months (which does cost a sub fee) now that the box with all the expansions has dropped to $18.00 (which includes a month of sub time). In this kind of market, WoW just can't keep me anymore I'm afraid.

This was a very hard decision to make. I know a lot of awesome people, and I'm going to miss them tons. I've collected all the mounts and pets and achieves and reps and gotten my Loremaster done; we've raided forever, and this means disbanding a sexy raid team that works very well together. This involves disappointing people who count on our raids. Altogether, it wasn't an easy conclusion to come to, but it's the right one.

Goodbye WoW. I will be back for brief periods to check on you (as long as it's free or very cheap to do so), but I'm abandoning my intense devotion to you, and it feels good and right.

---

Some quick thoughts on things, specifically, that I no longer enjoy about the game.

Emphasis on End Game: I like the fact that there's so much support for this, but it's really become unfun for me. I don't enjoy the quality of human interaction I find in LFR/LFD, and I don't particularly want to grind the same 30m or 3hr experience for months and months. It just isn't fun anymore to follow that model

Old Game Irrelevant: In some of the new games I'm trying out, the content is never irrelevant like it is in WoW. Once a tier is replaced, there's very little reason to go back to old tiers. This just isn't fun. If there were 40+ raids in WoW, and you could do any of them, there'd be a lot more breadth and variation in content. Atm people don't do anything but the current tier because the game is very reward-centric; no rewards = no interest. Having all the gear I want from a tier, I'd rather have more options and experiences.

From playing Final Fantasy, one thing I love is level caps on things. You get scaled down to content in many places so it's never irrelevant. Having something like this in place for WoW would e pretty neat. Might not keep me playing though.

The new expansion doesn't seem terrible to me, but there is nothing revolutionary or new in it that promises to change things in a big way. I'm not having fun with WoW atm, and I don't see that changing without a major shift in design.

The game just isn't fun anymore for me.

---

Anyway, long post. Thanks again for reading! Goodbye and good luck wherever your travels take you!

F2P = P2W (pay 2 win). Good luck with that. WoW, despite the terrible economy we've deal/dealt with, has been very consistent on their pricing and hasn't even RAISED rates like I would've expected in my 6+ years playing (literally, not one single thing I can think of hasn't costed me more or been worth less in that same stretch period).

If you're unhappy, you should quit. I kind of have to laugh a bit about you claiming to "blow through the content in 1-2 weeks" yet admit your guild is "just about" to down NORMAL DW. You still have heroic content that you have not yet scratched the surface on. That's hardly clearing "all the content".

Not to try and dismiss your otherwise well thought out speech, if you aren't happy with the service you are paying for, I agree you shouldn't continue to pay for it. Just don't go away thinking the grass is greener on the other side....it isn't. In most/all other cases, there isn't even grass on the other side...it's poison ivy.

Not to say your suggestions are bad either...they're fair enough. I personally think they put too much emphasis on keeping the elite minority catered too and that time should be better spent on more fun solo and small group content for the masses to enjoy. Attendance is always higher during holiday and special events, yet they're too few and far between. We didn't get near enough raids to sate our appetites this expansion and none that were classified as short and sweet (like the Sarth and Maly of Wrath).

The content itself was tuned much too high just starting out too. Early heroics were brutal and the raids much more so. Simply clearing a dungeon felt like a feat of strength those first few weeks. By the time you had a raid force ready to go into BOT/BWD, you found yourself at another mountain getting the reasonably easy but unforgiving mechanics down to a perfection. Fail to get 2 players on Magmaw's head? Wipe. AOEers/kiters too slow on worms and they get into your party? Likely wipe as people puke and worms spawn in the group and just continue to infect people. Too slow to move from flame pillar? Take 3/4ths of your life bar in fire/fall damage in an age where healers didn't have the mana to spare to spend healing you back up to a survivable health level without. And Magmaw was the easiest of the entry bosses.

Oh, I also laugh at those "old school" raiders who think raiding back in the day was soooo much harder...I was there, it wasn't. Raids were more of a gear check than a skill check. Hell, there are heroic dungeon bosses today that have more mechanics today than half of the raid bosses "of old"...the players back then were just as terrible as the ones today, they just got by easier because they were asked to do less by Blizzard once they finally met the gear requirements to go in. Blizzard has reversed that in Cata...low barrier to entry but one hell of a task to follow the crazy song and dance while outputting the numbers needed (and back then we had easy mode rotations, little of this "priority system").
