The last time she had set at the same table as Imperon, Imperon had told her of his plans for Azheira. Plans that she had relayed to Liore via a letter. The contents of that letter had somehow found their way back to Imperon which led to the cutting off of her finger in retaliation of her betrayal. Now she held a contract in her hands that was supposed to make everything better? She gritted her teeth as she read through the contents. Questions arose in her mind. Why was he so willing to release the finger now? Was she too late? Had he discovered more than what she had thought possible? She cringed as she read on. Her inability to lie was meaningless when applying her signature to a contract. Written words meant nothing to her, she was bound only by what passed her own lips, but in all her life she had never broken a written contract, even though the freedom was there to do so.

Slowly she brought her eyes to meet his, “I must commend you on the time you put into such a well-ordered contract, but nay I will not sign it. I care nothing for your current engagement with the Watch. It actually humors me. I will take it as my own conquest that such a person of your stature is harassed by them.”

She rose abruptly snagging the finger off the table, bringing her knee up hard in an attempt to flip the table, she spoke as she bolted for the door “Now I will be taking this and we can leave it at that”

As Noikona rushed up the stairs the door behind her exploded in a shower of splinters. Half-covered in gravy and looking very angry, Imperon strode through the doorway. "That was uncalled for!"

As she fled, Noikona could hear Imperon ascending the stairs behind her. She could hear him a little too well. Each foot fall sounded like it was made by something much larger than a short wizard. "When I catch you, miss Noikona, you will wish you had taken the opportunity to bow at my feet!"

Noikona did not want to risk a glance behind her. The wizard's voice, apart from sounding very angry, was just a little different than usual. Whatever the cause, she wanted to get to the top of the stairs as fast as she already could. Nothing she might see behind her could urge her on faster.

Before she'd ran far enough to be more than a little winded, she abruptly came to the end of the staircase. Above her she could see a rough, stone ceiling like in a cave. Running as fast as she had been didn't give her enough time to stop and she charged out of the stair well...into a room, if it could be called that, without a floor.

All Noikona had was a brief impression of uncut stone walls and the ceiling before her first step off the top stair failed to find purchase on anything and down she fell. The room vanished as she fell and she couldn't see any other walls or anything to suggest that she was still in Mahr Carreg at all.

Without any sensation of having changed location, Noikona rolled to a stop on her back. She was no longer falling, although someone had forgotten to tell her heart that. Blood pounded in her ears as she took stock of her situation.

She lay on grass. Cool grass that felt and smelled entirely ordinary. Trees grew near her. She was in the bottom of a valley that she would have called small, except that the walls of the tower rose up all around her.

Somehow there was an entire mountain valley contained within the strange entity called Mahr Carreg. Closing her eyes and shaking her head didn't make the odd picture vanish. Where the sides of the valley would have risen up into mountains anywhere else, the valley in Mahr Carreg only rose up to join seamlessly with the tower itself.

Within that valley were entire groves of trees, with small streams and even one that was almost large enough to be called a real river flowing in...many different directions. Moss covered boulders lay here and there, some of them flat on the top and low enough to the ground that they could serve as picnic tables.

Indeed, one was being used for that. Two adult humans sat next to each other, trying to keep a trio of children from throwing any of the food on their 'table' at each other. To another side, Noikona saw a pool of water, deep and broad enough that half a dozen boys and girls were swimming. She could hear quiet voices nearby, indicating other people enjoying a place so odd that it made the entryway to Mahr Carreg, with its park like appearance to be entirely normal.

Then Noikona recognized one of the girls with the swimmers. It was Pika, the girl who'd first greeted Noikona when she'd entered the tower. Smiling broadly the girl ran over to where Noikona still sat on the grass. "Hi, Noikona! Did you get lost or are you done with your courier stuff?"

It wasn't hard to find a tunnel leading out of the odd, underground park. Walking swiftly at first, Noikona soon found herself truly spelunking. However most people got into the park, this wasn't it. She had to avoid massive boulders that had fallen out of the ceiling, detour under stalactites and around stalagmites and stalagnates.

Her going wasn't easy and when she finally came to a large, open cavern, she was ecstatic. She'd been hearing the occasional breath of wind, but so far she'd been so far from the entrance of this cavern that it had sounded more like a monster's breathing than anything else.

Occluding the entrance to the cavern she heard more than saw in the darkness was another boulder, with no way around it. Scrambling over it she used some convenient (if rather jagged) stalagmite handholds and scrambled her way up...where she found herself staring into two giant, glowing, reptilian eyes.

"You had better not be who I think you are. I was napping in order to avoid getting involved," said a deep voice from directly beneath where she stood on a very large dragon's snout. One of her hooves slipped and Noikona had to grab hold of the dragon in order to avoid falling off.

Torches around the edge of the cavern suddenly lit up, giving Noikona her first lit view of the creature. It was jet black, as if made out of obsidian from head to tail. As close as she was to it, the dragon's eyes were focused on her. The dragon raised its head several yards into the air and regarded her for a long moment before speaking again.

"Well? Are you who I hope you're not or are you some other problem entirely?"
