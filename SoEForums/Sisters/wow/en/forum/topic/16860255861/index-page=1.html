I agree. We should discuss what CRZ means for RPers. I'll start here with a few comments to keep the discussion local. Perhaps I'll branch out into your WET post later. :) ...if that's ok with you.

I recognize and appreciate the beneficial opportunities CRZ can provide, but personally my interaction has been more to the detrimental side.

Where RP is concerned at best I tend to be ignored, and I'm content with reciprocating that by minding my own business. I'm more than happy to kibitz icly and emote if the other approaches me first, but typically 'they' don't. Rarely does an other-realm rper extend a whisper or ((ooc comment)) and attempt to contact me, the player. Usually I'm stepped over, crowded out of a situation, and generally made to feel unwelcomed as though I were a toddler to be ignored.

On the rare occasion that I do get that ((ooc extension)) there is a fairly good chance that the conversation will go something along the lines of "You're in my space. I'm using this space. You need to leave." In this scenario I would need to comply or else it'll devolve rapidly into a grief fest, as if they're 12yo and they call all their dumb little buddies over to make a nuisance and ruin it for everyone.

On the ooc side of things, we've all had horrible experiences with PUGs. For me, I tend to run more dungeon leveling PUGs more than I do LFR or PVP, but I've had my fair share of similar experiences there as well. The benefit to PUGs is that if/when things devolve and become unproductive I can just leave. It's kinda like stepping into another room and closing the door behind you, a door through which they can not follow. I can dust my hands off and carry on without a second thought, regardless of what may have happened in the PUG.

CRZ doesn't have that "PUG door" benefit. If I'm sitting comfortably in an unused house in IF and a group of two to four WrA toons wander in and are being rude and discourteous, I can't just pop back into a CC-SoE only phase and make them disappear. Would that I could sometimes. Instead I'm forced to either move to accommodate the whims of a bratty 12yo or stand my ground and risk wasting the next several minutes suffering their grieving and that of any other "brahs" they call to their aid.

Needless to say, I'm more than a little jaded where CRZ is involved.

This isn't -every- experience mind you. I did say that I recognize that there are some benefits, and I've had a few good experiences. I also realize that WrA, MG, and the other realms aren't full of idiots, a**hats, and 12yo's... >.> though sometimes I wonder. I have met more than a few good and fair minded people IC and OOC, in PUGs and in CRZ. Unfortunately it seems at times that the bucket of 'bad experiences' tends to outweigh my hand full of 'good experiences'.

I really do appreciate the efforts you are putting in here, Zaranae. Your attempts to open up lines of cross-realm-zone communication is a very good thing. Perhaps it can help me get over my aversion to CRZ by connecting me with helpful like-minded individuals in those other realms.

The Clinic is located in Orgrimmar, which is CRZ-free.

The Lounge is on Saturdays, in Hardwrench Hideaway in the Cape of Stranglethorn, from 7pm to 9pm, the same zone and general time frame as a certain other event. (*eyes Bremmin and demands free bagels as compensation*) We picked it in part because it's a more open space than the BB tavern, and we'd be less likely to get trolls.

We also picked it because it was a Horde-only area, and one of the main complaints I was getting from my guildies at the time was that our weekly cross-faction events turned more into "work" for them rather than a night spent enjoying RP, as they felt obligated to assist with interpreting conversations.

So... as much as I'm not against the trickle of new people we get through word of mouth and happenstance, I am not about to open the floodgates and mass invite our CRZ'd realms to the Lounge. It's a matter of logistics. We can get pretty crowded on a good night already, and there's usually only one AAMSer present on any given week, sometimes two, to monitor what's going to make sure there are no problems and that no one breaks the AAMS' rule concerning violence (which has happened).

Note: I consider "pretty crowded" to be any night when there's enough people that we have three or more conversational groups going. That's about the same time I start getting comments from people that they can't follow what's going on and feel too shy to join in. I end up spending a good chunk of my concentration making sure people aren't being left out or ignored, and I'm not always successful.

So I'd love to see the event expand, but not at the cost of being unable to manage having that many people present.

I'm sorry you've had bad experiences, Odin. Trying to avoid bad experiences is why communicating about this is so important. Blizz never facilitated a discussion before throwing us all together, so it's kind of a mess...

I think if we can reach out to the communities on other servers, we can smooth over our interactions. I think people are initially uncomfortable with RPing with people from other servers. They're like aliens, they come from a different worlds. We've lived in separate spaces for years, and now we sort of run into each other. It's a strange phenomenon. I think simply talking to people and showing examples of positive cross server interactions can help with this initial weirdness and help people open up.

As far as problems with sharing space, there are bad apples anywhere, and some people will not be reasonable...
However, that's another thing we need to communicate about. if we could form a place for Ironforge RPers to talk about the spaces they use, and find ways to share it, like Feathers and Company of Blades did with Booty Bay, I think everyone would be happier and we would have less snobby "mine!" sort of attitudes. It's understandable that being are protective of their RP spaces; there can be strong emotional connections. But some friendly communication can go along way, and I think in the end, most people -are- reasonable if they feel they are being respected. Unfortunately, by showing up in their space, most feel you are automatically disrespecting them. If we communicate first, we can avoid that initial, defensive reaction.
