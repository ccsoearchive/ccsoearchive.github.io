Q&As:
Q: What does your guild do icly?
A: The Wolfheart Warband acts as Homeland security for the Horde by focusing efforts on Azeroth, mainly the two main continents.

Q: Is there a minimum level requirement?
A: Yes, for first timers, you must be level 70 before we consider inviting.(Alts can be opted in after) Some exceptions can be made.

Q: What days do you RP or have events?
A: Due to the busy schedules of our members, we've settled to doing events twice weekly on Friday and Saturday. Due to scheduling, we dont have much sporadic RP, but our events are full of surprises!

Q: What kind of RP do you guys do?
A: The style of RP we do can be gauged as "Adventuring" heavily influenced such universes as Lord of the Rings/ Game of Thrones/ Witcher / Shadows of the Colossus. Certain events you will be asked to limit post length due to high attendance. This is to get the event done on time without too long of a spill over time. People need to go to bed!

Q: What races do you accept?
A: All races are considered except Blood Elves. Do we need a reason? Not really.

Q&A Continued.
Q: Is event attendance and overall activity required?
A: Event attendance? Not in the least! But hey, if you arent planning on attending, why bother, am I right? As for overall activity, we basically provide a home for those that want a drama free experience of roleplay while still allowing the roleplayers a chance to RP outside the guild without deep commitment. Show up to our events to keep up with whats going on! Roleplay whatever else you want to! Members will not be removed for inactivity until the 3 month mark.

Q: Does your guild follow lore?
A: You bet! We have officers well-versed in Lore and kindly speak to those that have mistaken anything. Those spoken to should either provide proof to educate the officers, or change what was pointed out. Though since our RP style writing and story is using the World of Warcraft Universe to play our own ideas, we do sometimes bend lore appropriately that is made only canon within our guild.

Q: How is the Guild Chat monitored?
A: Our guild chat is not monitored, therefore any conversations may happen. Our definition of mature may not be agreeable to others as we prefer an uncensored environment versus most guilds basically booting anyone that offends another person with words. So if you are thin skinned, you may not belong.

(Important, mail Heavycleave Or whisper him In-game with "I understand these terms and conditions of the guild chat policies".)

Q: What is the current state of Drama in your guild?
A: We carry no baggage and expect all guild members to not bring their baggage to us. If this happens, an easy solution is to boot the one bringing the extra baggage. We also do talk things out if the party is understanding and not unreasonable. (We haven't had drama)

Q: What is your policy on ERP?(Yes, ofcourse its brought up)
A: Although ERP is a case by case situation, we do appreciate ERP be kept out of MRPs/Currently's. ERP is not something we bother people about. But when it begins to reflect negatively on the guild as a whole? Thats when we have to step in.

Q: What aspects of a person do you look at when recruiting?
A: Several things. We look for typography, RP ability, Tumblr(Required Review if you have one), Maturity, Attitude. All interested will be asked to complete an RP Prompt(In character continuation post)

Q: Meta-gaming and Powerplay, God modding. Whats your take on these?
A: All 3 are frowned upon. To clarify if you are not familiar with the term Power-play. Its when someone RPs so influentially that it changes the course of a story or roleplay with a single post. Say, Taking a plot device into their own possession. << Practice Consent

Q: How do I get started?
A: Send a message to one of our friendly officers to schedule an Out of Character review and In Character introduction.

Recruitment:
1. Review the Q&A and other information
2. Have a fully filled out MRP/TRP with at least an adequate and preferably structured physical description.
3. Finish the In Character Prompt below and let an officer know.
4. Contact an Officer or Member to redirect you to an officer/recruiter.

Ogrudar narrowed his brow as he watched the slaughter commence. He gritted his jagged teeth as his fingers curled into loose fists, he had no love for Blackrock Orcs and there brutish tactics. Ogrudar swiftly leaped from his vantage point, arms out stretched toward his first two victims. His single eye looked to the female frost wolf they had prepared to ravage as he spoke harshly. "GO!" he ordered, to which she obeyed without question. His attentions would shift back to the captain of the Militia as he slumped into a seemingly relaxed stance. The Captain and had focused on the much smaller orc, yet seemed cautious. "Impressive, little one. But foolish. Rip his heart out; we'll finish with the Frostwolves soon enough!" he commanded of the militia, who charged in response.

The halfblood took in a deep, calming breath before leaning forward slowly. Upon exhaling, he would break into a sprint, charging headlong to meet the larger orc's advance. With a vicious growl, Ogrudar would swing his blades this way and that. Rending flesh, tearing through tendons and splattering gore and viscera about the ground. After some slicing and severing of limbs, Ogrudar stood bathed in the blood of Blackrock Orcs, breathing heavy and hyperfocused on the militia leader and his single remaining henchman. Of the henchmen, he seemed less than enthusiastic to engage Ogrudar. "Sniveling coward!" said the commander as he swung his mace for the side of his subordinates head, which became little more than a pink mist and bone fragments flying through the air. The commander then set his eyes on the smaller, one-eyed, halfblood and grinned menacingly.

"My men lay broken, or dead. You stand covered in their blood." he would swipe his mace through the air, flinging off the chunks of flesh that had clung to it when he dispatched his henchman. Swift as he was however, the smaller Orc managed to avoid the blow by leaping back and allowing the heavy mace to slam into the ground. With his opponent off balance, Ogrudar would leap forward and thrust his knee into the large orc's nose, causing him to reel back in pain as the blood gushed from his nostrils. The commander roared and swung his mace in a low arc, biting into back of Ogrudar's leg and dropping him to his back.

"Now die!" the commander called as he raised his mace and attempted to slam it down onto Ogrudar's chest. The half-orc quickly rolled to the side, avoiding the heavy bludgeoning tool and find himself in a kneeling position. The Commander reared his mace back for one final blow, giving Ogrudar an opportunity. Pushing off of the ground with his uninjured leg, Ogrudar would throw an uppercut toward the underside of the commander's chin. Finding purchase, the blades which extended from his gloves sank into the bottom of the commander's jaw and were now protruding through his mouth. The commander's grunts and groans became muddle by the blood pooling in his throat. Ogrudar gritted his jagged teeth once more before yanking and ripping the commanders mangled mandible from his skull.

The Commander dropped to his knee's, dropping his mace and clutching his gaping maws in a fruitless attempt to stave off the bleed. Ogrudar grunted as he watched his struggle for a moment. When he ceased to struggle, Ogrudar looked to the captive Frostwolves that remained "You're all free...Truly" and with that, he turned away from them and began limping his way home, having had enough adventure for the dat.

Hiroma was still a good distance away, stepping towards the execution site with a slow but steady gait. His curved blade remained sheathed, the sheath held loosely in his left hand - cloak billowing in the cold winds as he made his way forward. It was not long before the Blackrock orcs took notice of him - an uproar of laughter quickly came from those gathered there - distracting them from their executions.

"Look - some overfed beast walks like an orc."
"Overpadded creature."
"One more for the block, then."

The pandaren's right hand rose to remove the grass reed from his mouth, tossing it aside. He spoke with confidence, proclaiming: "I joined the orcs of the Horde because I respect their skills and their honor - I sought to emulate them. It is tragic, then, that your clan chooses to act in such a way. Do you lack the skills to defeat an enemy who can fight back? Do you lack the honor to give them the chance?"

The commander spits - "Words. We're not here to talk." He turns to his comrades: "Fire at will." Riflemen raise their weaponry to aim at the large target, firing upon him - as bullets plink from his armor, the pandaren grunts as each send a shock through his system. One bullet flies true towards his unprotected head - with a practiced motion, the pandaren draws his blade to deflect it, stepping forward as he goes. What was once a killing blow instead cuts into his cheek, dying his fur red around the cut. His sword drawn, the pandaren moves with practiced motion as bullets glance off the blade into the surrounding area, the shock of the attack rippling down into his powerful arms - each concussive strike bringing him ever closer to the commander and troops. He taunts: "I will show you the true meaning of warfare. I have been saving something special for you!"

A quick turn of the blade sends a bullet ricocheting towards the commander, striking him in the armored shoulder unexpectedly. He turns to the riflemen as they begin to reload, ceasing their actions with a wave of his hand. He looks down towards Hiroman: "Hrmph. This... pillow warrior has challenged me directly. You wish to know of orcish honor? Fine." The commander hefts the large blackrock hammer - "I will smash your ridiculous blade to pieces, followed by your head!"

Hiroma tosses the sheath to the side: "Simply terrifying. Just imagining you as my enemy is enough to make me shudder in fear." He raises the blade, taking the time to 'salute' his enemy before the battle begins. With a cacophonous roar from the commander's troops, the fight began. Hiroma launched himself back to dodge a downward swing from the hammer - a powerful strike that would have easily slain the pandaren in a single blow. Pulling the hammer from the ground, the commander swung horizontally - Hiroma ducks under this attack, swinging with his blade only to strike the orc's armored legs. The orc laughs, striking out with gauntleted fist to land a crushing blow on the pandaren's muzzle, both nostrils beginning to bleed profusely. Rolling with the blow, the pandaren spins - keeping both hands on his hilt and raising it to head level. The orc's reaction time is fantastic, but not enough to save his looks as the curved blade takes the cap off of one of his tusks.

The two leap back from each other, circling each other - Hiroma kicks his sheath back into his left hand as he passes by, raising it above his head like a sword while his actual blade is raised to be parallel with his sideburns. With a laugh, the orc charges - seeing the pandaren leave his body open. A moment later, he gurgles in surprise - a quick strike against his temple with the sheath pushed his head back, before a forceful strike with the blade through the commander's throat. It was too quick to even follow - the orc drops his hammer, trying with his last breath to do some sort of damage, punching at the armored belly of the pandaren. Warm blood flows out onto Hiroma's hands - he withdraws his blade, flicking the blood from it with a skilled wrist motion. He glances at the commander's troops, challenging them with a glance.

They met his challenge well - charging at him. The first was side-stepped, his stomach sliced open with the same movement. Riflemen found their bullets deflected towards their allies as they fell, until the ground was awash with blood and corpses - finally, the few that was left broke free and fled. As Hiroma exhaled to ease himself from his adrenaline high, he moved over to free the few surviving captives.

"You let them flee," they accused him.
"Victory is all that matters - there is no need to annihilate the enemy. To pursue total victory is to invite trouble."
