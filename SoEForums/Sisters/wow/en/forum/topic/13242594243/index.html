Miss Pennicka Goghvan followed the realtor through the front door of the building, the tip of her walking stick making a dull ‘thunk’ on the hard, wooden floor. The wrinkles around her faded eyes crimped slightly as she considered the large table in the middle of the room. It would do nicely for soft interrogations and the studying of reports as they came in. Her gaze traveled left and followed the simply carved railing up to a balcony.

Her question to the realtor come out in the typical croak of an old woman, “What is up there?”

“The stairway leads to the balcony you see and over in the corner is a quaint little alcove, perfect for small gatherings.” The man’s smarmy, salesman-like voice oozed with condescension.

It took some effort, but she managed to not roll her eyes as she grasped the bannister for support, and unhurriedly ascended the wide staircase. As she neared the top, she realized the sycophantic realtor was right. A half dozen chairs circled a cozy niche, a war room of sorts for the leadership to gather in and discuss strategy.

“You said there was a basement. I should like to see it as well.”

The realtor nodded and motioned for her to follow him downstairs. Once on the main floor, he led the way to a second staircase, this one leading to the underground room. She sniffed as she entered. It was clean and tidy – no rats, nor did it have the musty smell of a grimy cellar. Miss Goghvan nodded and smiled in approval. It was perfect. The room was large enough to hold a reasonable sized lab as well as an area that could be used for…more intense interrogations.

Soft wrinkles created deep creases around her eyes and lips as she gave the realtor a generous smile. “I’ll take it. How much?”

He named a price and was astounded when she immediately produced a large sack of gold.

That same raspy voice told him what he wanted to hear, “That should cover it as a well as a nice tip for you. Now be gone so I may conduct my business.”

The man shook the bag of gold and nodded, leaving the building in haste.

The old woman shook her head at his departing back. “So easily manipulated by greed.” The lines and furrows of age softened slightly as she climbed the stairs and looked around at the new Stormwind offices of the Underground. A satisfied smile passed over her lips and her old eyes almost seemed to sparkle with anticipation of what was to come.

Kosuzu Felvice watched Miss Goghvan walk away and on up the stars. She turned back to filing cabinet pulling out the last report of the sleeper in question. A puzzled look crossed her face as she read through the document. Every sleeper was given an ID number and tracking device, to circumvent the tracking device was unheard of, there were a number of failsafe’s involved to keep such a thing from happening, none of which were pleasant for the sleeper.

She replaced the document once again and refreshed the wards, it wasn’t like anyone could read the pages but it always gave her an added feeling of security to apply them. The filing cabinet shimmered as its appearance changed to that of a wine barrel. The machines against the walls, hanging maps and desks all faded until once again the basement looked dank and deserted.

She left the cellar, exiting the building to take a walk along the canals. She was sure her colleagues would do their job well, they always did. She just didn’t want any more problems. The pressure for results was already starting to bear down on her, but the foundation must be in place first. She smiled to herself as she walked. The rogue sleeper would be dealt with the collective would gain a sure footing in the city once again.

Muted colors of green and brown undulated slowly back and forth, swaying to a silent tempo. The elongated bodies of aquatic vertebrates weaved in and out of the seaweed arms, tail fins creating ripples threading out like a balls of yarn unfurling behind them. Below the dancing algae, along the seabed, a small benthic crab scuttled over the sand. He moved this way and that, wandering along in search of his next meal. Disengaging himself from a large clump of sea tangle, he stopped in surprise. Before him was a good sized layer of marine snow – a banquet of detritus to last him a long while. The crustacean made a mental note of this area of the aphotic zone as he feasted. He would definitely have to come back here again.

Scurrying sideways, he was too intent on his repast and did not notice the large rock before he slammed into it. Half buried, it lay in his path. On top of the rock, a thicker, even more scrumptious layer of detritus could be observed. Of course, he had to have it. Scrambling, he climbed up and prepared to indulge.

Just as he was settling in, his right claw snagged on something taut. He squirmed and twisted and eventually his claw pulled free. Unrestricted, the rope upon which it had been caught snapped, part of it reeling back to smack the upper exoskeleton of the crab. The rest of the rope slithered upwards as if it were a sea snake chasing after whatever was attached to its other end. At that moment an inch long strip of burlap drifted down, flustering the feast and causing the delicate marine snow to puff up in an atomic plume formation. Disturbed at the commotion, the crustacean skittered away, vowing to come back another time.

Sometime later, the rope, the remains of a large burlap sack and its contents arrived at the water’s surface. Slowly, unhurriedly, the items drifted with the tidal stream toward Stormwind Harbor.
