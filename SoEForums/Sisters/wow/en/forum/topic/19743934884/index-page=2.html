A big thank you to everyone who came out last night. The turn out was honestly just amazing. I can't express how much I appreciate the support you have all shown for this event. I will keep working to make these events better, more engaging and just more fun for everyone to attend.

As such, to facilitate more active RP and to better accommodate the large turn outs the structures of the Sermons are changing. Moving forward we will be using the raid group interface to divide people into parties of 5 to have a more active debate/discussion on the topic. My character will still present the topic and questions to be considered. Any other guest speakers for the lecture portion of the event must contact me 2 days prior to the event. Guest speaker spots will also be limited to 3 people per event.

We hope this helps the event be more engaging for everyone involved. Thank you for your patience last night, as well as moving forward as we test out this new event structure.

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Topic:

The Pursuit of Purpose

Consider what your character defines as the purpose to existence. Do they believe there is a purpose? Do they believe that life (or unlife) is just a series of inconsequential events? Is there a 'master plan' for the universe and their life? Think about what your characters purpose is and how they might interpret that.

Sermon Text:

Greetings to my fellow Forsaken and our allies in the Horde. As this is only our second gathering I will once again introduce myself and state my claim to authority in hosting such events. I am Silaine, High Priest of the Leviathan and leader of the Covenant of Transcendance, a sect under the Cult of the Forgotten Shadows. I welcome you all to the second Sermon where the matters of purpose and existence shall be discussed.

I would like each of you gathered here to reflect on what you think your purpose is-- Why and how do you exist? Do you believe there is an ‘end goal’ for sentient creatures? Do you subscribe to the belief that the actions and events in your life are preordained or just a series of inconsequential events?

I realize that what I have asked of you this evening is a daunting task, prone to induce anxiety in each of you. It is such a monumental question to reflect upon-- to define the very nature of -you-. As such, I will ask that each of you listen to what I have to share, and as you do so contemplate the question of your purpose and existence. Allow my words and insights to guide you-- Whether you agree with what I have to present or disagree, allow it to be a basis for your own knowledge that you may share with us once I have concluded my teachings.

In the prior week we discussed the nature of Will and its fundamental purpose of being the driving force behind ambitions and actions. The concept of freedom was also touched upon and how Will interacts with freedom, that is to say, Will grants us the ability to make deliberate actions which exercise our freedom.

This week will be a continuation of that discussion but incorporating the matter of purpose and existence.

Let us begin by examining what it means to exist. From this point there are many places we may tumble, falling further down the rabbit hole until we begin to question if we exist at all. That is not where we wish to wander today, so I will focus our thinking on the matter: On the assumption that we may recognize our existence, and therefore know that we do in fact exist, what does this existence mean? What is the -purpose- of existence?

I will argue here tonight that there is no objective universal purpose to existence. Sentient beings, like ourselves, are simply birthed into existence, isolated, without any answer as to “why” yet compelled to invent a meaning. The nature of the matter is that every sentient individual and every sentient species is insignificant, without purpose. To attempt to create significance in the insignificant is to delude ourselves of the reality of the sentient condition: There there is no “greater” purpose.

The ‘absurdity’ of existence is that we are born to die; and it is here, without purpose, that you realize that you are nothing but must make yourself of something.

-C-

Through the strength of Will we must mold our essence, the very attributes that fundamentally make us who we are. For there are no objective universal truths of existence to dictate what our character is to be, what goals in life we are to pursue or what moral actions we may take. We are isolated with the burden of our freedom and charged with defining our very being. We only become what we define ourselves as, and we will only be what we make of ourselves.

Existence is a matter of subjectivity, for each of us here will have a different answer to how we may exist or what purpose we may set out for our existence. The one common principle that we all share is the ability to define that individual existence-- to be burdened by our freedom and take the weight of responsibility for our futures. Every ambition, every success and every failure is a measure of your own strength of Will. Do not fall prey to ‘deterministic excuses’. Recognize the self, define the self and take responsibility for all that may come after.

Now, I will open the floor to all in attendance. I ask that volunteers wishing to speak, or to question, simply raise a hand so we may respectfully direct our attention to you.

(What follows is speeches given by volunteers. Due the increased volume of volunteers I had had to cut some of the speeches for easier forum reading. A special thank you to characters: Brasmon, William Hayes, and Karevyn for standing up and speaking last night. )

Dayon Rolav:

Brothers and sisters. It has been a long time since I have beheld such a gathering of our fallen kin. I’m honored to be given the privilege of speaking to you this evening.

The High Priest speak of purposes, of defining what that is to be for ourselves. Any Forsaken who has had the misfortune of coming into this existence with knowledge of their former life has to struggle twice with this burdensome question. Everything that I had in life, everything that was promised to me, was swept away by the cold hand of fate and swallowed. When I woke I struggled to come to terms with everything that had been stolen from me. But I carried with me, through the grace, more than just the memories of who I once was.

With my father’s lessons in mind, I was able to carve for myself a purpose as Forsaken. The Banshee Queen had delivered back to us our Kingdom; And because of her, because of this gift of shadow, we can continue to ensure it remains the domain of her fightful subjects.

My purpose is to preserve this great Kingdom, to continue the rule of our Dark Lady, and to drive back any who dare claim that this land is anything other than -our- birthright. I once believed that undeath was a curse, but now, after so long. I see what it affords us: A vigil eternal. Unending service. Duty everlasting.

I have found my purpose. I only wish that you -all- may find something to propel you forward.. That we will never be defeated, and spit in the face of the fates that dard believe they could take anything from us.


Ievgenius (Feathermoon Server):

Brother’s. sisters, friends I haven’t yet met.. I found our last speaker’s words most inspiring and would like to add some of my own thoughts to our purpose, as living and as Forsaken. I have always been a man of peace. In life I found my purpose in the Light. It guided and nourished me, and through me, it brought hope and inspiration to those I served.

We Forsaken often say that the Light has abandoned us. That the light shrinks from the very notion of our existence. When I awoke, those were the thoughts that tormented me- that I had lost the favour of the thing that had once brought me purpose.

But, if our prerogative is to find purpose, then we must accept when it is time to move onto a new one. When we are “cursed” as is often thought, we this new life. It is time to be grateful for the chance at a new purpose. When we yearn for the things that can no longer fulfill us, we suffer as if our very souls were being from our bodies. When you are grateful for what is given to you, no matter how little, that gratitude will give you the strength to move forward. And so I devote all of mine to the Dark Lady, who grants us life in death, and asks only that we make of ourselves what we will. In that freedom, I choose to serve.

That is my purpose.
