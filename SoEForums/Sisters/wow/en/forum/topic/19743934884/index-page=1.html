ANNOUNCEMENT:

Changing the schedule for sermons.
Sermons will now be held once every two weeks.

Sermons have changed days and times. They are now going to be held on THURSDAY nights at 6 PM sever-time

Location: Sepulcher, Silverpine
Time: 6 PM
Days: Thursdays

Shadow Sermon Info:

Hello everyone, every week I will try to host a Shadow Sermon event. These events will primarily revolve around my characters interpretation of the Cult of the Forgotten Shadow. The nature of the topics will change from week to week but will be designed to allow as much input from attendees as possible. The topics will also be posted at least two days in advance to the Sermon so you have proper time to reflect on them.

What You Can Expect:

You can expect topics to be philosophical or political in nature.Debate and discussions are very much encouraged. Each event is likely to run for at least one hour, please feel free to drop in and have a look even if you can't stay for the entirety of the session.

Location And Time:

The events will take place within the Sepulcher at 8 PM server time. The style of the event will consist of a Lecture and Seminar. The lecture will provide a general outline and background information on the topic, while the seminar serves as the speaking floor. Debate is welcome and encouraged.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Other Information

I do have a channel up and running for those who are interested in religious roleplay focused around the Cult. The channel is purely OOC and is a way to communicate with other people and to announce various events.

Everyone is welcome to /join ShadowCult (case sensitive) and help promote this style of RP and get involved with the Forsaken and Horde community.

For anyone looking to contact me but missing the times when I'm logged in my btag is: Silaine#1824

We had such an amazing turn out last night. I want to thank everyone who made it out and everyone else who showed interest. It was a great start to the Sermon events and I am really looking forward to hosting these every week.

As stated in my OP I will be posting the full contents of the event below. Feel free to have a browse and join us at our next event Sunday, the 22nd of November at 8 PM server time. Whisper Sillain in game for more details of /join ShadowCult (case sensitive).

Once again, thank you all.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Topic:
The Nature of Faith and Will

Think about what drives your characters actions and what they understand faith to be; come prepared to share and engage!

Sermon Text:

Greetings to you, my brothers and sisters of the Forsaken and a warm welcome to our allies of the Horde. For those of whom I am stranger to allow me to begin by introduction: I am Silaine, High Priest of the Leviathan and leader of the Covenant of Transcendance, a sect under the Cult of the Forgotten Shadows.

Tonight’s discussion will focus on the nature of Faith and Will. What do these concepts mean to us? How do we understand them? And how does that understanding impact our actions-- if it does at all? At this time I would like to call upon each of you gathered here to exercise your knowledge and define what Faith and Will mean to you as an individual.

So please, step forward and speak the thoughts invoked by the mention of Faith and Will.

(What follows is every speech given by volunteers)

Edwin Lyddington:

I don’t know about anyone else here, but I wasn’t what you might call a man of faith when I was alive. But now, I find myself seeking answers in the shadows. I’ve never believed there’s something out there that will hand us some great and grand destiny. And now it seems clear to me: we must make our own way in the world, and if we are to achieve greatness, we must reach out and seize it. We must exercise our will to take what we believe rightly belongs to us. Who is going to do it for us? No-one.

That’s what I have to say on the subject of will. On the subject of faith? I came here hoping for answers. Maybe I’ll find them, maybe I won’t. But it seems to me that we should have faith in those things and those people we consider worthy.

Dayon Rolav:

I -was- a man of faith, in life. I was in Stormwind City to take my vows as a paladin when the news of my princes’ return reached me. When word of the slaughter reached our ears. My faith was tested and I began to question. In the absence of the Light, I was driven only by my loyalty to the crown, by a thirst for vengeance, an adherence to duty. I was carried by my will to serve my fallen king. I found, in death, that it was my will that freed me from the shackles of the Lich King’s control.

We are born of shadows, aye, but delivered by our indomitable spirit. Our conviction. Our vindication. If there is -anything- that can have faith in, it is that the Will of the Forsaken is ironclad and will see our foes driven before the march of the damned.

Before I removed myself from this pedestal, I will caution you, my brethren.
The Will of the Forsaken is known and feared in the hearts of the enemy, but do not become overconfident in it. Draw pride, but not comfort, lest you forget that we -can- still be destroyed in our arrogance.

William Hayes:

Like others here who have spoken I am not a man of faith and indeed I never was. To me, faith is to believe in something that is beyond all reason.

Will is another matter entirely. Will is the driving force within us all. It is the possibility of achievement and fulfillment of ambition. Many of us have been through hellish and traumatic lives, even to the point where our will may be broken entirely. For some of these people it is easy to fall into, what I think, is the trap of faith.

But I say to you friends, no matter how broken or destroyed you may feel never stop believing in yourself. Never doubt your Will for it will always be within you.

Keleril Falavir:

Obviously, I am a sin’dorei and my faith was not tested or altered by the experience of death. However, as some others here did, I started out as a practitioner of the Light. I practiced with the Light for a brief time and it also failed me in that brief time. I had lost my faith in that time. One way or another, I was turned towards the Shadow. Which is frowned upon by my people, but regardless I pursued it. Looking to fill the void the Light could not fill.

So as to how I define faith, I define it as following something you believe is right, regardless of opposition. Letting your will, or inner motivation, guide you to what fits you.

Myrtiliah Graveheart:

Faith, to myself, is not a spiritual entity, rather a belief. A belief in others, in friends. That faith connects you , brings you to believe they will have your back in a fight, or in other aspects of this life. Perhaps I am incorrect in it. I do not know. It is simply what I feel is correct.

Will, as so many others have said, is within each of us. It is what we should strive for, the power to conquer our enemies, to reach our goals. It is who we, as Forsaken, are. No disrespect intended on the other races.

Continuing the Sermon: Silaine

Much of what is to be done at these sessions in the future will revolve around what has taken place tonight: engagement. These sermons ought to be a gathering of the minds, where each and every one of us may exercise our knowledge and grow from perspectives otherwise incomprehensible to us.

As such..

I am pleased with the courage of those gathered here tonight, it is no faint task to address your peers while under the fear of being perceived lesser in your opinion or wrong in your thinking. Overcoming the fear of judgment is an important tenet to achieving Enlightenment, but I have digressed as that is a subject for a future Sermon.

You have all spoken of your understanding and in turn have made each of us better for being here, for we have extended upon our knowledge and understanding. I would now ask to present my own interpretation on the nature of Faith and Will. Some of the points, I’m sure you will find, mirror much of what has been said this evening; while other points, I’m sure you will find, offer a completely new interpretation on the matter-- A great testament to the power of minds.

Before I speak of Faith I believe it is important for me to first define what I consider Will to mean: The characteristics of Will is comprised of endless urging, craving, striving, wanting, and desiring. It is a force that can be harnessed and directed, it makes up our voluntary and deliberate actions. When speaking of an individual's Will, I do not mean so much the capacity that person has for the characteristics of Will, but the power the Will gives to act deliberately towards goals under rational thinking. Will, as I understand it, is the driving force behind achievements and ambition. It is the force that allows us to exercise our inherent potential.

Now that I have explained Will, as I see it, allow me to speak on the matter of Faith:

Faith, as I have understood it in life, is the complete surrender of the self. To recognize the insignificance of all that you are and in unison recognize the significance of the powers you serve. In my understanding the individual forfeits their Will to the Will of another, to live a life of servitude and happenstance. Under this definition the individual is no longer responsible for their own Will-- for their own power, or their own consequences. Life ultimately becomes a state of absent responsibility where your actions are no longer regarded as your own, but the Will of another. To put this into context; in my past life I knew a Paladin of the Light who regarded every action he took and every event of his life as a direct result of the Will of the Light. He did not recognize his own power but only that of the Light-- All the good and bad he experienced, as far as he was concerned, was not a result of his exercise of freedom but only of the Will of the Light.

The conclusion I have drawn from this understanding then follows that Faith is a tool used to pacify the individual and bind them to the Will of another. It denies the natural potential and power within each individual and most abhorrently, suppresses the actual freedom of individuals by stripping the consequence of responsibility from actions.

In starting these sessions it was my goal to bring about a renaissance in thinking-- to once again highlight the importance of exercised personal Will and to preach of the responsibility of freedom. Each of us going forward must realize that we are insignificant but that insignificance does not, and should not, mean the surrender of our Will to another. We, as conscious beings, must recognize our Will and recognize the freedom that comes with it. We are the tools of our own destiny and only we decide the nature of our lives-- it is time to cast aside the bindings that hold us and embrace our true power to forge our own futures.

This is the way of the Covenant of Transcendance and this is the flavor in which all my Sermons and Seminars will be carried out. With absolute freedom at the helm.

And that is where I will end my thoughts this evening but I welcome each of you back in a weeks time, where we may explore those concepts further. If there are any questions or closing comments I would hear them now.
