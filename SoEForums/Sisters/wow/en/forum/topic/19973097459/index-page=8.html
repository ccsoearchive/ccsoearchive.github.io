Of late, the Forward Line has been contending with the machinations of a traitor to the Darkspear Tribe, the scornful Shadow Hunter Radazhan. This spiteful veteran of the Darkspear Rebellion has forsaken the namesake of his tribe and has led followers to establish a new world order: The Nafarashi Nation.

Emboldened by unearthed legends of ancient relics of power known as the Icons, these foci allow those who attune to its power to wield god-like power.

After discovering the Icon of the Jungle, Radazhan and his followers usurped Bloodlord Mando'lok, who had garnered power and influence in the power vacuum after the trolls' crushing defeat at the Isle of Thunder. With the remnants of the Gurubashi Tribe swayed to follow Radazhan, the Nafarashi now seek to extend their reach to the Amani, Drakkari, and Farakki by tempting them with the myths of remaining Icons.

In the forests of the Ghostlands, the Forward Line intercepted incumbent warlord Dazaka the Taloned, claiming the Icon of the Forest amidst the ruins of Deatholme.

In Zul'drak, brother of the fallen Frost King Malakk, Rimelord Bo'lakk, had discovered the Icon of the Tundra and nearly succeeded in augmenting his necromantic powers to reanimate the corpse of the slain Loa Akali as an indomitable engine of death. The Loa's spirit overwhelmed Bo'lakk, putting an end to his schemes. The Icon was lost to a Nafarashi follower, Bamongo the Dreamer.

Now, the Forward Line heads to the dunes of Tanaris, where the next Icon is suspected to lay. With luck and resourcefulness, they may find and discover it before Hisaya of the Dunes, an influential leader of the Farakki hoping to find the Icon to secure her bid for supremacy of the remaining Farakki tribe.
