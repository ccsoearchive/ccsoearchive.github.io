There's a meaningful difference between "ribald", which I enjoy, and "obscene".

I don't personally object to ERP, even if I don't participate. If two people want to strip down to their characters' skivvies and have one type /sleep while the other repeatedly hammers their "X" key, that's their thing. I don't care. They're best served to do it where there isn't enough traffic for someone to really see them.

In short, instead of in the town square of Goldshire or in the common room of the Lion's Pride Inn, they could do what two people would ACTUALLY do... and go upstairs into a room. If both rooms are being used, guess what? Inn's full, you go to another inn, like travelers would do.

If two people want to talk dirty, more power to 'em. Doing it in /say where anyone can see it? You're going to get reactions. I don't care if two people are talking about "licking the root of that tree" in whispers; I've heard it on the public transit I use where I live in real life.

I care when two people are blatantly talking about the various private things they're going to do with each others' bodies in a fashion where you really can't help but hear it. Just like that can resort in a police action in real life (public indecency), it can result in action in game, as a violation of the game's Terms of Use. But no one's going to report two people who are talking in /tell, or in party chat, or even in guild chat (if the whole guild is fine with that sort of thing), and those people aren't going to get caught (or are highly unlikely to get caught), because for the most part, Blizzard doesn't actively monitor chat channels. They just don't have the number of employees necessary for that. They rely on players to report violations, just like they rely on people on RP realms to report names that are in violation of an RP realm's naming conventions.

People should feel free to do whatever they like when they're consenting to the action(s). But when they make it public, they remove the ability for others to not consent (as other random consenters are unlikely to care), and consequently, they become a nuisance.

It isn't about conservatism, Dagdason.
