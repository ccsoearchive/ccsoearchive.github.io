Back on the road again...

Zylae was pretty excited for how the day went. There were not a lot of people that seemed to show for the soup night meal at Crossroads. There were more people last night than the previous week. So who knows, maybe next week even more would show for a free meal, friends, and supplies.

Zylae did run across a few new travelers who were starting their journey together among some other lone wolves figuring out what it was they wanted to do with their lives. Nevertheless, she and her tauren friend Anrakni were able to hand out bags and bedrolls to whoever needed them. They also got to enjoy the lovely vegetable soup provided by the tauren clan that operated out of that small town.

The conversations were both friendly and rather enlightening. It was mostly about peoples pasts and how everything works in mysterious ways in order to get people on the right path they belong on. Well, that was how she took the conversations to mean. It warmed her heart a little as she adopted to the life on the road in a completely new area.

Zy was no longer on the caravan route that ran between Silvermoon and Undercity. Arakielith was now leading that route. Zy was given the one between Org and Thunderbluff.

She missed her friends and family she saw all along the old route. She knew she'd see them again and this was a great opportunity to meet even more people. The rather young elf said her goodbyes to the people of Crossroads and went and joined her husband Baelitas back on the caravan.

It was a very simple covered wagon with enough room to sit three people comfortably. A few kodo were the ones that pulled it along the general roads. The wood was in overall pretty decent repair but it was by no means anything fancy to attract too much attention. The material for the top was made of a creamy baeige color and on the sides were the symbols for the guild operating it; two worgs on either side barking at a crimson colored full moon.

It was near dark but they planned to ride for a little bit to get closer to Ratchet which would be their final stop before continuing the route to Orgrimmar. They rode for about an hour before pulling off from the road. Bael made a small fire while Zy took care of the animals. He'd do the cooking and she would get the camp set up and the deck of cards out.

They usually enjoyed a few rounds of games before turning in for the night. Games played with strangers usually filled what could otherwise be a very long and lonely route carting supplies back and forth.

A visit with the grandmother...

The rogue master Vlea had paid a small visit to see a few of the newest members of the guild. They were children and grandchildren of current members. She valued honesty and integrity from her people; but sometimes such things could be a real black mark and a burden.

It was the case of the two twin girls resting in bassinets before the rogue master. They looked elfin enough to likely not drawl to many questions. Those that knew what to look for, likely could tell a human had assisted in their making.

Vlea rested her hand on her forehead and sighed. She wasn't going to outright tell the new mother to lie; but in this case, Vlea would really like to not know.

"I do not need to know, how this came to be, Tae. I have heard stories of humans that have plundered more than they should have. Its unfortunate. There is no shame in that if it had happened to you. However, little one, if you wish for the guild to provide a promising future for them, you must remember your loyalty to the Horde. We can not assist a traitor... So if you wish to continue working, you had best take yourself to the Faire and work a bit. "

Taeti Steam looked down to her two little infants. Her husband had run off once again and this time, she wasn't going to take him back. She had two little mouths to feed and she couldn't let their lives rest in the hands of some human who did not care about them all. She wasn't smart by any means, but she surely did pick up the not to subtle hints dropped by the rogue master.

"Of course, Master. I will be seeing my mother shortly before going there".

Taeti had her eyes still on her children when the rogue left the small home. Tae followed shortly after with both of her children bundled up tight against the cold weather. Tae paid a small visit with her mother Main Stream. They all left to make it to the guild's campsite on the Darkmoon Island.

Tae kissed the foreheads of her two little girls before leaving them in their grandmother's care. Taeti had a small job to do and it was to help hand out bags and assist in whatever efforts the guild needed at the faire.

Oops! What did I forget?

A blonde headed elf with two pig-tails sat outside Silvermoon City on one of the benches. Daeli had been in the area for a few days now, first to see her father Belinor; secondly, she had been given a task to do for the guild. This afternoon had been a lovely time speaking to her lover who had finally shown up once again after a long break where he had been off engaging in the demands of war torn world.

She took out a heart shaped cookie and began eating it. It was sweet and had a cute little message concerning love. It was too tasty to just eat one, so she helped herself to another all the while of trying to remember what it was she was suppose to have done. She figured it was definitely okay to take a break in her task once she had spotted the human who held her heart.

The answer came a little later when she received another page on the guild's communication line. It was the Master inquiring how the last distribution of bags, cookies, and other supplies had gone. "OH shiii" Daeli cursed quietly with her cookie filled mouth.

Daeli glanced down to the box of half eaten cookies. This box was just as good as the other one she had also eaten. The elf had been sent to take them over night to the Crossroads for soup night a few days back. She had done that and enjoyed a rich flavorful onion soup prepared by the tauren tribe in the area. Daeli even enjoyed chatter with a pandarean new to the world and a female orc. Among many other new faces that were friendly enough to not mind a hug or two from the slightly flighty elf. But then her father had called for her and she left quickly to answer; completely forgetting what she was suppose to do with the cookies that had been the point of the trip.

"oh, master, they were delicious.. um " Daesong banged her communication device down on the bench a few times, "master" BANG, BANG, "its kind of hard to hear anything here.." BANG "let me get back to you"

Daesong promptly turned off her radio, took out a small rolled up happy leaf, and enjoyed the quiet. She felt a lot better after just saying goodbye to her beloved not to long ago. She hated the separation, but in their case they had to all to often.

Getting Ready: Toad Jumping Contest

It was the weekend and the usual time in which Vlea visited with her step-son Lore. He was currently receiving training as a page under a knight. This weekend, she would not be seeing him at the usual place. Instead, she had picked him up on Friday to attend the Toad Jump Games that were going on this weekend at Sunrock Retreat.

Lore didn't seem to mind the change of scedule. Vlea had given him a mechanical lifelike toad as a present and told him about the games. A few similar packages had also been put together for the other children under Sir Urak's care.

The trip from the blood elf lands over to the spot of the games had been relatively easy. Vlea was still nursing her children in private; a number of hands had been hired to act as guards, maids, and so on.

She had hired out a new group that would be in charge of refreshments. The crates holding the mechanical toads to be given out had arrived a few days before she did.

The Horde Supply Caravan had sent someone over with a box of bags and other goodies to hand out.

It seemed like everything was in order for the event tonight. Right now, people were set up and the children and adults present were welcomed to talk, practice the games, and enjoy something pleasurable that had nothing to do with war.

There was even an artist set up doing painted tattoos for the children on their faces or hands. She had a few appointments for after the games to do permanent ones on a few adults.

Toad Jumping Contest

Sun Rock Retreat, Stonetalon Mountains

Come join the fun for prizes, refreshments, and a chance to win Big!

February 28th at 6 pm!
