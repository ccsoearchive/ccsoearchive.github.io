Okay, in light of recent RP events - I'm looking at you battle settings! - I've gone ahead and come up with a plan for RPing multiple people fighting in one battle. As we all came to realize in Desolace, the bowls of the Undercity, and wherever else a fight may have gone down with multiple people, with all of us trying to get an attack in, things can become extremely hectic very fast. So, aside from creating multiple parties to have their own OOC for battles, and spacing ourselves out to have enough distance so that we're not interfering with one another's chats, I also came up with an idea for keeping our fights better organized in-game.

As it currently stands, 1v1 fighting is the easiest to pull off: one person attacks, both characters roll, and a defense emote is made which includes a counter-attack; thus allowing the fighters to attack one-another in a back-and-forth bout. However, if we're fighting alongside allies, our characters IC wouldn't naturally stand by and wait until they're needed, they'll be trying to help out. in whatever manner they can. This is what I discussed in Skype to get things making sense for 1v2, 1v3, and so on.

----

I agree that counter-attacking should be something involved when we have only two combatants for a 1v1 fight, but realistically, there's no way that would be going on in the situation our characters are faced with. I know that if I were in a WoW, LOTR, or any other such fantasy setting, I wouldn't stand by and watch my friend engage with a 1v1 fight against an enemy when I'm capable of trying to get behind the man and really keep him on his toes: a grapple, kick to the back of the legs, perhaps grab onto his arms, a stab/punch in the back, etc. Logically, it just doesn't make sense for our characters to line up for a series of 1v1 bouts, even for the sake of simplicity.

The second person rolls for a defence immediately, then emotes the result as a defence of what happened, plus a counter attack and rolls again


This manner of RP combat works for 1v1, but for what we're doing by getting at least two combatants to fight one person, 1v2, we need a different system; otherwise it does become a 1v1 attack-and-respond battle. What I suggest, is to simply make things turn-based for the three RPers so that everyone can be involved with the fight naturally.

To do this, all we need to do is remove one step - when somebody attacks, and another player must emote a defense, they simply don't include a counter-attack, just the effect of the assailant's move against them; the counter-attack will be the responsibility of the next person to fulfill. Perhaps, if a defender wins a roll, and wishes to perform a counter-attack in their emote, then all they must simply do is ask OOC in that group's party chat if anybody minds them doing so, this way we don't skip anybody's turn needlessly.

Example:

Blackmold attacks Baelic with a shield slam, and Baelic would emote his defense for that attack. Then, whoever had the next highest roll when it came to establishing turns, would make their emote: if it was Baelic, he would simply include a counter-attack either after the successful hit, or successful parry/dodge/whatever. After he performs his emote (assume he was next to go) then whoever remains to make their attack, would go, and Baelic responds to that manuever.

All we're doing is removing a person's ability to immediately follow-up with a counter-attack, so that we may pace the fight by making it turn-based rather than free-for-all emote battles, or narrowing it down to dueling while another watches. I feel that it may sound confusing as written text, but if we get together and coach a demonstration for how it works in my head, then it will make VASTLY more sense.
