Just a small critique after reading over it.

1) I wouldn't exactly say they have arrogance that comes with age, as people said before, if racism exist within night elven society it exist within the individual, not the race, and while they may find races like gnomes quirky for their tinkering, and humans quite young, they still respect them greatly as allies.

2) Again, the death knight thing, at first all death knights were hated upon entering into each faction, Alliance and Horde, but overall the Death Knights currenty with player factions, and with the ebon blade and ashen verdict, have proven to be nothing but allies and have worked just as hard since their freedom to take down the Lich King, giving themselves a permanent place into the factions, with maybe only civilians fearing them for what they are, not heroes ( Which the players technically are) not fearing them, finding them as allies needed in this time.

3) The gender status, Night Elves don't seem to follow the typical pattern of one stays home one goes off to fight. Both genders in night elven society seem quite high on equality, and any roles tend to be filled by either gender. And the males when warriors or the such, were just as likely to be freelance or working with the circle as females were, during the war of the ancients it was males actually leading the army as a whole, with males and females fighting side by side, though I don't believe males are generally accepted into the sentinels, that is one of the few fragments of the night elven military. Though up until recently it was mostly males as druids ( Shown by the claw and talon we witnessed in warcraft three ) and mostly women in the role of a priestess, not a single male I can remember during any story was a priest back in the times, please correct me if you know of some, so many class/gender combos are recent things ( Decade wise ). But most roles aside from those two up until recently were equally head by both genders.

Just my two cents, roleplayed a night elf warrior for 4 1/2 years before switching to this guy

After reading Nicholas' post, I did some digging for some information about gender roles when it comes to the family.

Unlike other races, night elf families are loose-knit and tribal despite their civilization. As one put it, to them, your birth family is not as important as the family you adopt yourself. Translation: It doesn’t matter who your parents are, but it does matter who you keep around you. Entire communities raise their children together, forming long and lasting bonds, until everyone is related in spirit to everyone else. Night elves do not traditionally marry, but they do take companions for life. Socially, night elves combine what humans see as traditional matriarchal and patriarchal elements. Traditional belief has it that that the duties of a druid are a man’s job, while women casters are much better suited as priestesses. Women are also the warriors of their race, and few men participate directly as warriors and hunters. Those few men who actively engage in battle are either the fierce druids of the claw, or the enigmatic and misunderstood demon hunters. While this trend has been changing recently, most night elves hold to tradition, as it’s all they had for thousands of years.


Alliance Player's Guide, pages 125-128, as written by Brann Bronzebeard

I must say that this guide is pretty in-depth. I've beenb playing most of my NElves like this anyway, but a few points must be made. The largest pertains to Mages and Death Knights.

Death Knights

While, yes, they would be seen as reminders of unnatural circumstances and times, they would have proven themselves by this point. Some even being revered, despite their circumstance in life. This would be case by case of course, but universally shunned is a bit of a stretch. Pity may enter the equation perhaps more accurately.

Mages

Highborne. Yes, they are back from Eldre'thalas. And they would be seen as the masters of the arcane, moreso than High Elves, moreso than Blood Elves. Draenei would have them beat solely. Each Night Elf mage either comes from Highborne stock, ideally, or trekked from Eldre'thalas, even more ideal.

Overall, the guide is quite good, but we all could find a few knit-picks in any guide, I suppose.
