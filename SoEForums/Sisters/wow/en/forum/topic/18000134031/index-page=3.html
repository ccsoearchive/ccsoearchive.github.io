She plopped down on the ground, the Scar at her back, and built a small campfire. While the water heated for tea, she unwrapped her dumplings and began to eat, taking in the beauty of the woods.

She watched the golden leaves billow in the soft breeze, small animals chittering at the base of the trees, and dragonhawks fluttering in the distance.The scene was idyllic, but the pandaren was plagued with a sense of wrongness, like a prickle between her shoulder blades. She tried to shake it off and enjoy her meal, but her attention kept drifting back to that feeling of unease.

Qipao hefted herself to her feet and moved to the other side of the fire. When she sat back down, she could see the beauty of Eversong spread out ahead of her and to her left, while the Dark Scar intruded from the right. She nodded and returned to her meal. A mere blessing hadn't been enough. She was wrong to turn her back on the Dark Scar.

The terrible wound was as much a part of this land as all its gentle beauty. As painful as it was to behold, this Scar was a part of history that had to be acknowledged and honored, a battle scar to be worn with pride. Like the scars of the Sin'dorei. This land and its people had suffered, but they hadn't given up. They were fewer, but they were stronger for their scars.
