IC:
The Council of Cognition is an institution that believes the pursuit of knowledge to be a never-ending journey. Comprised of accomplished spell-weavers, adventurers, and scholars from far and wide, we consistently look to gain a better understanding of magical pursuits all for Alliance peace of mind and advancement. These pursuits take us on many journeys in discovery of the new and the old.

Under the direction of Arcanist Koira Winthrope, the Council's headquarters are based in Stormwind City where orders and direction is received. Those wishing to advance their education will find their basic needs met here, as well as those who are looking to be of service.

For the last couple of years, the Council has managed to uphold its respectable status as not only a place for educational advancement and research, but for its charitable contributions to the denizens of Stormwind and the Alliance as a whole. The Council's selective membership approach for aspiring scholars, even explorers, as been well-met with keen interest and the formal endorsement of Archmage Ambergin.

OOC:

We were first established in February/March 2012 as an institution devoted to magical pursuits and ideals. Comprised of a variety of people with one common goal, we frequented the community with hosted server-wide events such as magical instruction and a beloved book/coffee dealing, before real life forced our doors shut for some time. We have recently been restored to hold a more permanent presence on WrA as we gear up for the upcoming expansion and get ready to roll out on the scene with new storylines to enjoy (even the return of server-wide events).

The Council of Cognition, simply known as the Council, is a guild focused on a variety of magical pursuits and ideals. We are not only geared towards player characters with scholarly pursuits, but even those who seek to join us during our research and recovery missions. There is something for just about everyone. If your character is handy with a weapon or medically gifted, we could certainly use you as well!

Regardless of class, there is something for just about everyone to enjoy within the guild through our RP and casual content. We accept warlocks, but be advised if you are flashing your demonic loving ways around out in the open, you probably won't be accepted. It's just not something that's openly accepted within the Alliance. Dks are accepted only on special circumstances.

While we may do things outside of the realm of roleplay. Questing. PvP. Exploring PvE content - our main purpose will always be for the story of the guild and its characters. We do not strive to be the biggest or even the best there is out there, but we do strive to have fun and that's what it's all about.

What to Expect:
*Guild-wide story-lines to get involved in.
*Magical lessons/lecture events.
*One-on-one magical instruction option.
*Several DM'd mission events.
*PvE/PvP fun (CASUAL)

Additional Information:
*Applications are required.
*Ages 18 + only.
*Death Knights and Warlocks are only accepted on a case by case basis.
*Experienced RPers only. This is not a beginner's RP guild.

What We Offer:
*Friendly and welcoming guild atmosphere.
*Well thought-out & fun missions/events!
*Several bank tabs/weapon & armor repair.
*Guild webpage for all of your IC and OOC needs out-of-game.

General Ranking System:

Neophyte: Entry level rank for all new members. New members will find themselves placed within this trial rank for about a week - or at least until their first guild meeting.

OOC Alts/Casual: OOC/ALT rank of the guild. Council members are allowed to bring their alts in. We have no limit set at this time. The number of IC alts, however, is limited. Please let us know who will be an additional IC character.

Seeker: Our Seekers are our primary explorers, tradesmen, even our on-call friends of the Council. They are a vital element, especially when we travel abroad. This rank is filled on a case by case basis.

Mender: Our menders are quite important. Without them, injuries on the job would likely go on untreated and headaches would increase from there.

Sage: Mystic spell weavers from cultures far and wide. Practitioners of arts and magic unknown to the common folk. Scholars with a lust for unquenchable knowledge. (Diverse scholars, apprentice-types, accomplished spell weavers)

Aegis: Skilled in the art of combat and survival, you are the men and women in charge of defending the Council. Our guards are the Council's tough as nails meatshields.

Advisor: The elite of the Council, whose will and determination has been tested in the forge of battle and beyond. Their quick wit, skill, and determination has earned them a high ranking seat within the Council. They work directly with the Director and help oversee that the Council is running as it should be.

Director : Holding this rank is none other than, Koira Winthrope. Her word is final and her votes in the Council sessions are the most important. She says it, it gets done and she is not to be questioned.

Darkness Always Comes Before the Dawn

In light of Archmage Ambergin’s passing, several luxuries were gifted upon his most prized pupil who was seen as the daughter he had never had. Rewarding Koira Winthrope with his completed and incomplete research alike, as well as several luxuries he had left behind, his will had given her pretty much everything - save for the small estate and a few personal items for his estranged son, Sanborn Ambergin.

Koira wasn’t quite sure if Sanborn would even return to claim what was left behind for him, as he had outright refused in writing to attend his father’s funeral. And even though his refusal bothered her greatly, she left everything just as the Archmage had requested out of respect for her late mentor and him alone.

For obvious personal reasons and his own research, Sanborn kept to himself deep within the snow-covered territories of the dwarves; distancing himself from whatever rift existed between he and his father. Nobody had really seen him in years. Koira had never known the reasoning behind this estrangement. It was something she had never inquired about as it was not her business. She did, however, keep a curious ear about; eager to listen had her mentor ever decided to share such a personal secret with her.

Archmage Ambergin’s passing wasn’t one that surprised Koira all that much, regardless of how emotionally taxing it truly was. He was quite up there in years and as such, it naturally made his body weaker as time went on. When he had fallen ill in the late spring, it didn’t take longer to realize that after all remedies had failed, that his body just wasn’t strong enough to fight off the infection that had overcome his lungs. By the time the Midsummer Fire Festival had begun, he had finally passed from this world; laid to rest within the large cemetery beyond the Cathedral.

Of course, Ambergin’s passing was rather difficult on Koira, for he was something in the way of a father figure to her. If it hadn’t been for the presence of her elder sister and the continued instruction she conducted within her residence, it might have taken a darker toll on her than she could have ever realized. Perhaps with Brant Fisc’s death in Stranglethorn all those months ago and Rhys Ashbrook’s sudden departure then as well, everything still seemed fresh.

Eventually, she began to find some comfort after she began sorting through all of the things he had left behind for her. A personal letter he had written a few weeks before his passing seemed to help quite a bit as well. If anything, it had awoken a deeper sense of purpose within her. Something she had known before, but was failing to see over the last couple of months or so. His wording had stirred something with her and it made her continue in pursuit of a broader purpose in the world for her Council.

Perhaps there was something within his research left behind for her that she could begin. Something the Council could conduct as a whole and educate - no advance the Alliance as a whole. It is what he would have wanted anyway.

…wasn’t it?
